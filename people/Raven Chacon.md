# Raven Chacon

Diné (Navajo Nation) composer, visual artist, and educator. 2022 Pulitzer Prize for Music — first Indigenous composer to win. Creates scores-as-art, noise performances, and sound installations that engage Indigenous land rights, military occupation, and decolonial futures. VAP at SAIC 2023–24.

## Key Work
- **[Voiceless Mass](https://www.ravenchacon.com/)** (2021) — Pulitzer Prize-winning piece for pipe organ and chamber ensemble
- Graphic scores as visual art — instructions for performance that function as drawings
- Collaborative work with Indigenous communities

## Why Interesting
Sound art with deep political and cosmological stakes. Non-Western knowledge systems embodied in compositional practice. Indigenous futurism adjacent to Amber's interest in non-Western tech theory (cosmotechnics). Connects noise, land, and decolonization.
