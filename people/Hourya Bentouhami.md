# Hourya Bentouhami

French philosopher at the University of Toulouse Jean-Jaurès. Specializes in theories of recognition, cosmopolitanism, and postcolonial thought. Works at the intersection of analytic and continental philosophy — unusual in French-African intellectual circles. Contributor to the **2nd Ateliers de la Pensée anthology** (*Politique des Temps*, Philippe Rey, 2019). Her work connects Honneth's recognition theory with postcolonial critiques, asking how recognition can be decolonized.

## Key Works
- *Race, culture, identité* — on recognition theory and postcolonialism
- Various essays on recognition, justice, and cosmopolitanism from a postcolonial perspective

## Why Interesting (for Amber)
- Her work on **recognition** in postcolonial contexts is relevant to questions of how AI systems recognize (or fail to recognize) non-Western persons, cultures, and values.
- The analytic × continental crossing she does is rare and useful for AI ethics: analytic philosophy has dominated AI ethics, but postcolonial continental critiques are what give it substance.
