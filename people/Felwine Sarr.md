# Felwine Sarr

Senegalese economist, philosopher, musician, and writer. Co-founder with [[Achille Mbembe]] of [Les Ateliers de la Pensée de Dakar](https://ateliersdlapensee.com/) — the premier forum for African critical thought. Now at Duke University.

Best known for:
- **[*Afrotopia*](https://www.philipperey.fr/)** (Philippe Rey, 2016) — major manifesto for African economic and philosophical renewal; argues Africa must invent its own path by drawing on its own epistemic resources
- **The Sarr-Savoy Report** (2018) — co-authored with art historian Bénédicte Savoy; commissioned by French President Macron to study restitution of African cultural heritage held in French museums. Became globally influential and triggered major policy debates across Europe.

Also a musician and poet. Embodies the interdisciplinary African intellectual.

## Why Interesting
One of the most important living African intellectuals. His work on African futures directly connects to AI ethics, epistemic justice, and decolonial knowledge systems. The Sarr-Savoy Report is a model of theory applied to produce real policy outcomes. The Ateliers de la Pensée forum network is the node where African critical theory happens.
