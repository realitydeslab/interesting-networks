# Pujita Guha

Artist, curator, and scholar. PhD Film and Media Studies, UC Santa Barbara. Currently Mahindra Humanities Center Postdoctoral Fellow (2025-26), Environmental Humanities cohort. Research sits at intersection of environmental humanities, media studies, STS, and Global Indigenous studies. 

Work focuses on Indigenous communities in upland Asia (NE India, contiguous SE Asia, SW China) and how they claim sovereignty of forests through artistic, popular, and scientific media. Current book project: *Forested Media: Indigenous Lifeworlds in Upland Asia* — argues that Indigenous relations to the forest are not just cosmological but also "technical and practical" in building everyday non-extractive, non-capitalist life. Strong more-than-human framing.

Co-founded [The Forest Curriculum](https://theforestcurriculum.org/) (2018-2023) — artistic and research platform. Co-founded/co-directed [Hosting Lands](https://hostinglands.com/) (2023-2025) — decentralized land and ecological curatorial project, Denmark. Published in E-flux, Artforum, Cultural Critique, ACT Taiwan. Funded by Sharjah Art Foundation, Arts Council Australia, BikubenFonden Denmark.
