# Nicola Clayton

Professor of Comparative Cognition at the [University of Cambridge](https://www.psychol.cam.ac.uk/staff/professor-nicola-clayton). Leading expert on corvid (crow, jay, rook) cognition — specifically episodic memory, mental time travel, and theory of mind in non-human animals. Her research fundamentally challenges the assumption that these capacities are uniquely human. Also known for her ongoing collaboration with Ramachandran on the relationship between cognitive science and art. Participated in "The Shape of a Circle in the Dream of a Fish" (Edition 5, Porto 2022), curated by [[Lucia Pietroiusti]] and Filipa Ramos.
