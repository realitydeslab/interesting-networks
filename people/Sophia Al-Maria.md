# Sophia Al-Maria

Qatari-American artist, writer, and filmmaker; coined the term "Gulf Futurism" (~2010) with [[Fatima Al Qadiri]]. Contributing editor at Bidoun. Studied comparative literature at American University in Cairo and aural/visual cultures at Goldsmiths.

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-03-03:** Stub created during Gulf Futurism deep research.
- **2025:** Joined development of *The Many Lives of Miss K* TV series about WWII secret agent Toto Koopman.
- **2024:** *Beretta* film script rights acquired by Uri Singer & Aimee Peyronnet after decade on ice.

## Key Works
- [*The Girl Who Fell to Earth: A Memoir*](https://archive.org/details/girlwhofelltoear0000alma) (Harper Perennial, 2012) — first English-language memoir by a Qatari author; growing up between Puyallup WA and Qatar
- *Virgin with a Memory* (novel, 2014) — partially based on the shelved *Beretta* script
- *Black Friday* (installation, Whitney Museum, 2016) — Gulf malls as consumer prisons; defining Gulf Futurism artwork
- *The Watcher #1* (film, 2014)
- *The Magical Slate* (film, 2017)
- *Mirror Cookie* (film, 2018)
- *Little Birds* (UK miniseries, Sky Atlantic, 2020) — co-produced and co-written

## Key Communities
- [[Bidoun]] — contributing editor

- Mathaf Arab Museum of Modern Art — helped open it; formative art education there

## Why Interesting
Primary coiner of Gulf Futurism; her work is the richest entry point into the aesthetic and conceptual field. The memoir is key for understanding the experiential basis of the concept. Her Goldsmiths connection links to the broader UK critical-media-art tradition relevant to Amber.
