# Lev Manovich

Pioneer of **cultural analytics** — the quantitative analysis of culture using computational methods at scale. Author of the foundational media theory text *The Language of New Media* (2001, MIT Press), one of the most cited books in digital humanities. More recently developing "artificial aesthetics" — theory of AI's impact on visual art production.

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Stub created during ISEA deep research. Keynote at ISEA 2025 (Seoul).
- **ISEA 2025 keynote:** "Artificial Aesthetics: Generative AI, Art and Visual Media" — based on research from 2019-2024. Argues AI creates new relationship between "machine" and "creativity," positions AI as co-creator rather than mere tool.
- **Book:** *Artificial Aesthetics: Generative AI, Art and Visual Media* — his latest.

## Background
Born in Moscow, emigrated to USA. PhD in Visual and Cultural Studies, University of Rochester. Taught at UCSD for 20+ years, now at CUNY. Founded the [Cultural Analytics Lab](https://culturalanalytics.org/). Known for applying computational methods to art history, cinema, design at massive scale (analyzing millions of images, films).

## Domains
- **Cultural analytics** — quantitative methods for large-scale cultural data
- **Software theory** — software as cultural logic (Softcinema, cultural interfaces)
- **Media theory** — digital media's effect on representation + aesthetics
- **AI aesthetics** — generative AI as new creative agent
- **Data visualization** — visualizing cultural data at scale

## Key Works
- [*The Language of New Media*](https://mitpress.mit.edu/books/language-new-media) (2001) — foundational. Defines new media's logic through concepts like database, variability, automation, transcoding.
- [*Software Takes Command*](https://www.bloomsbury.com/us/software-takes-command-9781623568498/) (2013) — software as medium, metamedium.
- [*Cultural Analytics*](https://mitpress.mit.edu/books/cultural-analytics) (2020, MIT Press) — methods book for the field he created.
- *Artificial Aesthetics* (in progress/recent) — AI aesthetics theory.
