# Lev Manovich

Pioneer of **cultural analytics** and **AI aesthetics**. Theorist who gave us the conceptual vocabulary for digital culture — databases, variability, transcoding, the "language" of new media. Author of the most-cited book in digital humanities (*The Language of New Media*, 23,000+ Google Scholar citations). Defined four research fields over four decades: new media studies (1991), software studies (2001), cultural analytics (2007), AI aesthetics (2017). Now making generative AI art and building theory to explain what it means. Presidential Professor at CUNY; 48,500+ citations; h-index 64.

## Background
Born in Moscow, where he studied painting, architecture, and computer programming. Moved to New York in **1981**. Received MA in Visual Science and Cognitive Psychology from NYU (1988), then PhD in Visual and Cultural Studies from University of Rochester (1993). Was already making **digital art since 1984** — he was a practitioner before he became a theorist.

Career trajectory:
- 1991–: Begins publishing new media theory; creates field of **digital culture and new media studies**
- 1996–2012: Professor of Visual Arts, **UCSD** (San Diego) — base for major cultural analytics work
- 2001: *The Language of New Media* (MIT Press) — becomes field-defining text
- 2007: Co-founds **Software Studies Initiative** at UCSD (renamed Cultural Analytics Lab 2016)
- 2013+: Joins **CUNY Graduate Center** as Presidential Professor of Computer Science
- 2016–2019: Core faculty, [[Strelka Institute]] for Media, Architecture and Design (Moscow)
- 2009–2017: Faculty, **European Graduate School (EGS)**
- 2020–2022: Visiting faculty, Higher School of Economics (HSE, Moscow)
- 2022+: Launches **[manovich.art](https://manovich.art)** — generative AI art practice

Manovich is the rare humanist who is listed alongside tech CEOs: in 2014, **The Verge** named him in "50 Most Interesting People Building the Future" — he was the *only* humanities scholar alongside CEOs of Google, Apple, Microsoft, Amazon, and Tesla. In 2013, *Complex* listed him in "25 People Shaping the Future of Design" alongside Jonathan Ive and Zaha Hadid.

## Artificial Aesthetics — Book Structure
Co-authored with **[Emanuele Arielli](https://manovich.net/index.php/projects/artificial-aesthetics)** (philosopher, University IUAV of Venice). Combines six disciplines: aesthetics, philosophy of art, psychology of art, media theory, digital culture studies, computer science.

Chapters:
1. **Even an AI Could Do That** (Arielli, 2021) — challenges to human artistic value
2. **Who is an Artist in AI Era?** (Manovich, 2022) — redefining authorship
3. **Techno-animism and the Pygmalion Effect** (Arielli, 2022) — why we anthropomorphize AI
4. **AI and Myths of Creativity** (Manovich, 2022) — dismantling romantic creativity myths
5. **Seven Arguments about AI Images and Generative Media** (Manovich, 2023) — key characteristics of generative visual media
6. **Human Perception and The Artificial Gaze** (Arielli, 2024) — how AI perception differs
7. **Separate and Reassemble: Generative AI and Media History** (Manovich, 2024) — GenAI's place in media history
8. **From Tools to Authors** (Arielli, 2024) — AI as author
9. **Made by and for Humans? The Issue of Aesthetic Alignment** (Arielli, 2024) — alignment in aesthetics

Central thesis: GenAI is not merely a tool — it is a new form of cultural production that creates previously unseen aesthetics while being trained on all prior human culture. Like modernist artists who claimed to reject tradition while secretly absorbing it, AI creates genuinely new work *from* accumulated cultural archives.

[Download full PDF](https://manovich.net/content/04-projects/181-artificial-aesthetics/manovich_and_arielli.artificial_aesthetics.all_chapters_final.pdf)

## Venue Circuit Analysis

Manovich moves in **three distinct circuits**, each revealing different aspects of his influence:

**1. Institutional media art / European circuit:**
ZKM (Karlsruhe), Centre Pompidou (Paris), ICA (London), Walker Art Center (Minneapolis), KIASMA (Helsinki), Museum Guttuso, Évora, EA-AI Venice, ISEA Seoul — these are the premier global media art and cultural institutions. He's not a gallery artist; he's a media art institution figure.

**2. US institutional / NYC circuit:**
MoMA commissions, New York Public Library commissions, CUNY, Sylvia Wald and Po Kim Gallery, LA Center of Photography — academic/museum commissioned work; theory institution prestige.

**3. Digital culture / magazine commissioning circuit:**
Art Basel (essay commission), MoMA magazine (essay commission), European Photography journal, Nam June Paik Art Center publications — commissioned theory writing for high-profile institutions shows how much the field values his framing.

**Signal from venue choices:** Unlike most digital artists who show at galleries, Manovich prefers **institutional commissions** (MoMA, NYPL, Google) and **academic exhibitions** that validate his theoretical work with artistic output. His new AI art practice (2022+) at manovich.art is deliberately personal/small-scale — a return to artistic origins after decades of major projects.

## Why Interesting

Manovich is the **canonical theorist of computational culture**. Anyone working with AI and creativity is working in a field he helped define. But beyond the citation machine:

1. **The "artificial aesthetics" turn**: His move from describing what computers *do* to asking what computers *make beautiful* is exactly the question Amber is grappling with. His answer (GenAI creates genuinely new aesthetics by recombining the entire archive of human culture) is the right framing for generative creative work.

2. **Scholar-artist model**: He's been making digital art since 1984 AND theorizing it. The new manovich.art AI work (Library of Unwritten Manuscripts, Ideal City) is him practicing what he theorizes — rare and credible.

3. **Generative museum concept**: His idea of using AI to create "alternative art histories" — generating what Soviet artists might have created if they'd lived differently — connects to Amber's interest in speculative computation and counterfactual design.

4. **Cultural analytics at scale**: His methodology (analyze millions of Instagram images, Time magazine covers, manga pages) is the quantitative complement to Amber's qualitative speculative practice. Understanding how culture *actually* evolves at scale can inform speculative proposals about where it might go.

5. **Software as culture**: His Software Studies framing — that software isn't neutral but embeds particular ways of seeing and acting — is foundational critical computing theory. Directly relevant to critical AI work.

**Connection to Amber's taste:** He sits at the intersection of Amber's **computational creativity** thread (how AI generates genuinely new aesthetics) and her **critical computing** thread (software as cultural logic). Less aligned with her posthumanism/multispecies work, but deeply relevant to AI aesthetics, generative art theory, and the practical problem of publishing AI art work in serious venues.
