# Payton Croskey

Princeton '23 undergraduate researcher who became Creative Content Director at the [[Ida B. Wells Just Data Lab]]. Won the **FitzRandolph Gate Award** (2022), Princeton's top undergraduate prize, for her thesis "The Augmented Undercommons and the Path to the Sun: An Exploration of Liberatory Technology and other Revolutionary Tools." Co-developed the concept of **"digital marronage"** (with Kenia Hale) — drawing on the history of maroon communities (enslaved people who escaped to build free settlements) as a framework for thinking about digital surveillance resistance and liberatory technology. Panel accepted at [4S/ESOCITE 2022](https://www.4sonline.org/meeting) (Mexico). Featured on [Tech Policy Leaders podcast](https://www.techpolicypodcast.org/payton-croskey-subverting-race-based-surveillance-in-a-digital-age/). Speaker at Twin Cities Innovation Alliance Data 4 Public Good Conference (October 2022).

## Why Interesting
The concept of "digital marronage" is intellectually rich — it bridges African American history (maroon communities as resistance to slavery) with contemporary surveillance studies. This kind of historical analogizing is exactly the kind of conceptual move (like Benjamin's "New Jim Code") that creates citeable, politically charged frameworks. Worth watching where Croskey takes this after Princeton.
