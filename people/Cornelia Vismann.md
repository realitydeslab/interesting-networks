# Cornelia Vismann

German legal scholar and media theorist. Pioneer of the **cultural techniques of law** — an approach that treats legal media (files, dossiers, bureaucratic forms, courtrooms) as the material substrate of legal-political power. Her *Files: Law and Media Technology* (2000/2008) is a foundational text of cultural techniques theory, read alongside [[Bernhard Siegert]]'s work. Died 2010 at age 46.

## Why Interesting
*Files* is essential reading for understanding how documents, archives, and bureaucratic media constitute legal and political subjects. Deeply relevant to anyone working on data infrastructure, protocol studies, or the politics of data representation. Her "cultural techniques and sovereignty" argument — that sovereignty is constituted through media operations, not the other way around — is a key move.
