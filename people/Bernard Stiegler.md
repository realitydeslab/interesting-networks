# Bernard Stiegler

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Created during cosmotechnics research. [[Yuk Hui]]'s doctoral supervisor and key bridge between Simondon and cosmotechnics.

French philosopher (1952-2020) who developed technics as constitutive of human temporality. Doctoral supervisor of [[Yuk Hui]] at Goldsmiths and crucial bridge between [[Gilbert Simondon]]'s technical philosophy and contemporary cosmotechnics.

## Background
Unique intellectual trajectory from armed bank robbery and imprisonment to becoming major philosopher. Developed philosophy of technics during incarceration. Later taught at Goldsmiths and directed philosophy at Centre Pompidou.

## Domains
- Philosophy of technology and technics
- Human temporality and memory
- Critique of digital capitalism and "disadjustment"
- Cultural policy and institutional critique

## Key Works
- **Technics and Time** trilogy (1994-2001) — foundational work on technics as constitutive of human time-consciousness
- **For a New Critique of Political Economy** (2010) — critique of digital capitalism
- **The Neganthropocene** (2018) — ecological and technological critique

## Key Relationships & Collaborators
- **[[Yuk Hui]]** — doctoral student, developed cosmotechnics under Stiegler's supervision
- **[[Gilbert Simondon]]** — major influence and theoretical foundation
- Students and collaborators at Goldsmiths philosophy department

## Key Communities
- [[Goldsmiths University of London]] philosophy department
- Centre Pompidou cultural institution
- Continental philosophy of technology network
- Influenced [[Research Network for Philosophy and Technology]] through Hui

## Why Interesting
Stiegler's *Technics and Time* trilogy provides the immediate theoretical foundation that Hui builds cosmotechnics upon. His argument that technics is constitutive of human consciousness (not just tool use) opens the space for Hui's argument about different cosmotechnics. Essential for understanding the philosophical genealogy.
