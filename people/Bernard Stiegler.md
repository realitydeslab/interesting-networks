# Bernard Stiegler

French philosopher (1952-2020) who developed technics as constitutive of human temporality. His extraordinary intellectual trajectory from armed bank robber to major European philosopher produced groundbreaking work on technology, time, and human consciousness. Doctoral supervisor of [[Yuk Hui]] at Goldsmiths and crucial bridge between [[Gilbert Simondon]]'s technical philosophy and contemporary [[cosmotechnics]]. Founded multiple institutions including the [[Institut de recherche et d'innovation]] (IRI) and [[Ars Industrialis]].

## Background
Born 1 April 1952 in [Villebon-sur-Yvette](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Villebon-sur-Yvette). Extraordinary intellectual transformation: from high school dropout and communist activist to armed bank robber to major European philosopher.

**Early radicalization:** Aligned with left-wing politics and French Communist Party by age 16. Dropped out of high school to participate in student revolts against Charles de Gaulle. Left the Communist Party in 1976, spent his 20s working various jobs including farming, office work, shop clerk, manual labor, and owning a jazz bar.

**Prison self-education (1978-1983):** Incarcerated for armed robbery at Prison Saint-Michel (Toulouse) then Centre de détention (Muret). **This is where philosophy began.** Studied through correspondence with **[[Gérard Granel]]** at Université de Toulouse-Le Mirail. His prison transformation became central to his later philosophy about technics and human temporality. Recounted in *Passer à l'acte* (2003).

**Intellectual emergence:** PhD from EHESS (1993) under **[[Jacques Derrida]]** — connecting his work directly to deconstruction. Habilitation from Université Paris Diderot-Paris 7 (2007). Taught at [[Goldsmiths University of London]] as visiting professor where he supervised [[Yuk Hui]]'s PhD on cosmotechnics.

**Institutional leadership:** Director General at Institut National de l'Audiovisuel (INA), Director General at IRCAM, Director at Collège international de philosophie, professor at Université de Technologie de Compiègne.

## Domains
- **Philosophy of technology and technics** — technics as constitutive of human consciousness, not just tool use
- **Critique of digital capitalism** — "disadjustment," symbolic misery, industrialization of consciousness
- **Temporal philosophy** — human time-consciousness as technically mediated
- **Cultural and institutional critique** — alternative economic models, contributory economy
- **Educational philosophy** — care, attention, and formation in digital age

## Key Works
- **[Technics and Time trilogy](https://www.sup.org/books/title/?id=2681)** (1994-2001) — his foundational philosophical project:

  - *Vol 3: Cinematic Time and the Question of Malaise* (2001) — cinema, consciousness, and technical objects
- **[Echographies of Television](https://www.politybooks.com/book?title=Echographies%20of%20Television)** (1996, with [[Jacques Derrida]]) — television, technics, and hauntology
- **De la misère symbolique** series (2004-2006) — industrialization of consciousness and desire
- **Mécréance et Discrédit** trilogy (2004-2006) — "disbelief and discredit," capitalism and spirit
- **[For a New Critique of Political Economy](https://www.politybooks.com/book?title=For%20a%20New%20Critique%20of%20Political%20Economy)** (2010) — critique of digital capitalism
- **[The Neganthropocene](https://openhumanitiespress.org/books/titles/neganthropocene/)** (2018) — entropy, technology, and ecology
- **Acting Out** (2009) — includes *Passer à l'acte*, his prison memoir
- **Automatic Society** series (2015-) — automation, employment, and social transformation
