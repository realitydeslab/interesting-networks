# Ian Bogost

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-27:** Stub created during Graham Harman deep research.
- **Recent:** Frequent contributor to [The Atlantic](https://www.theatlantic.com/author/ian-bogost/) on media and technology.
- **2021:** Left Georgia Tech for Washington University in St. Louis, partly over COVID safety disputes.

American academic, video game designer, and media theorist. Professor and Director of Film and Media Studies at [Washington University in St. Louis](https://wustl.edu/). Known for: **Alien Phenomenology** (OOO applied to nonhuman experience), **unit operations** (game theory), and [Cow Clicker](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cow_Clicker) (satirical social game critique). Co-founder of [Persuasive Games](http://www.persuasivegames.com/) studio. Frequent writer for *The Atlantic*.

## Domains
- **Object-Oriented Ontology (OOO) applied to media/games** — what is it like to be a thing? "alien phenomenology"
- **Videogame theory** — unit operations; persuasive games; procedural rhetoric
- **Media criticism** — technology critique, platform analysis
- **Carpentry** — OOO's term for "making things that do philosophy" (games as philosophical arguments)

## Why Interesting
Bogost is the bridge between OOO and digital/computational media. His concept of "carpentry" — making things that do philosophy rather than just writing about it — is directly relevant to research-creation. *Alien Phenomenology* is the key OOO text for people thinking about nonhuman computational entities, games, and media. The "what is it like to be a thing?" framing opens OOO to experiential and design questions.
