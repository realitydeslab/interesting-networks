# Emily M. Bender

Linguist and AI critic. Professor of Linguistics at University of Washington. Co-author of [Stochastic Parrots](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3442188.3445922) (FAccT 2021, 11,693+ citations) with [[Timnit Gebru]], Margaret Mitchell, and Angelina McMillan-Major — the paper at the center of Gebru's firing from Google. Co-host of [Mystery AI Hype Theater 3000](https://www.dair-institute.org/projects/mystery-ai-hype-theater-3000/) podcast/stream with Alex Hanna (DAIR). Co-authored [The AI Con](https://thecon.ai/) (May 2025) with Alex Hanna. The only Stochastic Parrots co-author not employed at Google at time of writing — gave the paper external independence.

## Why Interesting
Key figure in critical NLP / critical AI. Her linguistic training gives the Stochastic Parrots critique its rigor — the paper's argument that LLMs are "stochastic parrots" (sophisticated statistical pattern matching, not understanding) comes from linguistic philosophy. Her work connects machine behavior, language, and power in ways directly relevant to Amber's interests.
