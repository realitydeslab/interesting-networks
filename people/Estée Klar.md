# Estée Klar

Artist, filmmaker, critical disability studies scholar, and co-director (with Adam Wolfond) of [[dis assembly]]. PhD candidate at York University in Critical Disability Studies. Mother of [[Adam Wolfond]]. Their collaborative practice ("intraethnography") reframes autism research from a neurodiversity-affirming standpoint, treating Wolfond as co-researcher rather than subject.

## Why Interesting
Central figure in neurodiverse arts practice and disability studies. Co-authored academic articles with Wolfond. The intraethnographic methodology — researcher and researched as mutual collaborators — is a model for participatory research design.
