# Anastasia Kubrak

Dutch-Russian designer, researcher, writer. Works at the intersection of design, data politics, and digital infrastructure critique. Doctoral candidate at the [Critical Media Lab](https://criticalmedialab.ch) at the [Basel Academy of Arts and Design FHNW](https://makesensephd.ch/). Also tutors at [Design Academy Eindhoven](https://www.designacademy.nl/) in MA Social Design and MA Information Design. Previously researcher at Nieuwe Instituut Rotterdam (2018–2021).

## Why Interesting
Discovered through [[Adam Harvey]] deep research — co-creator of DataPools/SkyLift WiFi geolocation spoofing project (2016-2020). Harvey's DataPools is a political-satirical work that uses technical countermeasures against surveillance capitalism; Kubrak's contribution was the graphic design, concept, and art direction. Her own work independently spans digital rights, design politics, and critical infrastructure — she worked on Metahaven research including "The Sprawl" (2015), co-curated the Lithium exhibition at Nieuwe Instituut (2020), and exhibited at Venice Biennale of Architecture (2023), Istanbul Design Biennial (2018), Seoul Mediacity Biennale (2018).
