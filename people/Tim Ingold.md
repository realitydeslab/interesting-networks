# Tim Ingold

Emeritus Professor of Social Anthropology at the University of Aberdeen. Not self-labelled as posthumanist but hugely influential on posthumanist thought through his "dwelling perspective" — the idea that beings are constituted through their engagement with their environment, not prior to it. His "lines" and "meshwork" thinking is widely cited in more-than-human design.

## Why Interesting
Ingold's "wayfaring" vs. "transport" distinction and his "correspondence" concept are directly relevant to more-than-human design and embodied HCI. Aberdeen proximity to UK community. His work underpins a lot of practice-based posthumanism.
