# Raqs Media Collective

New Delhi-based collective of artists, curators, and theorists: **Jeebesh Bagchi**, **Monica Narula**, and **Shuddhabrata Sengupta**. Founded 1992. Co-founders of the influential **Sarai media lab** at the Centre for the Study of Developing Societies (CSDS), Delhi — one of the key nodes of Asian critical media theory in the 2000s. The Sarai Reader series (published 2001–2013) is foundational critical media studies from a Global South perspective. Raqs's art practice engages with archives, time, labour, affect, and the politics of knowledge production. Chief curators of the **11th Shanghai Biennale** "Why Not Ask Again?" (2016–2017) — structuring the biennale into five interrelated formats (Terminals, Infra-Curatorial Platform, Theory Opera, 51 Personae, City Projects) in one of the most formally experimental biennale editions. Co-curators of Documenta 11 (with Okwui Enwezor). Major figures in connecting South Asian critical thought to global contemporary art discourse. For Amber: the Raqs approach to technology, archives, and the politics of infrastructure has deep resonance with both cosmotechnics discourse and critical computing.
