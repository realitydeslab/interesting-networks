# Ian Cheng

American artist. Creates live simulation ecosystems — digital worlds that evolve autonomously in real-time.

## Key Works
- *Emissaries* trilogy (2015–2017) — live simulations exploring mutation and narrative
- *BOB (Bag of Beliefs)* (2018–2019) — AI creature that learns from audience
- *Life After BOB* (2021) — animated film + simulation
- *Worlding Raga* (2022) — writing on world-building

## Domains
- Live simulation as art form
- ALife + game engines as artistic medium
- Worlding — designing evolving systems, not static objects

## Connections
- [[Serpentine R&D Platform]] — exhibited BOB
- MoMA PS1, Whitney, Venice Biennale — exhibited
- [[Artificial Life]] — artistic ALife practice
- Cognitive science + ILM background

## Why Interesting
The most important artist working with ALife today. His simulations are genuinely open-ended — they evolve without predetermined outcomes. BOB is an artwork that is literally a machine behavior experiment. Worlding Raga theorizes simulation as creative practice. Direct connection to Amber's ALife, machine behavior, and speculative design interests.
