# Ian Cheng

Artist creating live simulations that function as autonomous virtual ecosystems. Combines cognitive science, artificial life, and contemporary art to explore mutation, consciousness, and humanity's relationship to change. Pioneer of "live simulation" as art form — works that evolve continuously without authorial control.

## Background
Born 1984 in Los Angeles. Dual degree in Cognitive Science and Fine Arts from UC Berkeley. Worked at Industrial Light & Magic (George Lucas's VFX house) before transitioning to art. This unusual background — cognitive science training plus Hollywood VFX experience — gives him unique technical and conceptual foundation for creating truly autonomous digital life forms. Based in New York.

His theoretical framework draws heavily from cognitive scientist Julian Jaynes' controversial theory about the bicameral mind and the evolution of human consciousness. Sees his simulations as "neurological gym" for exercising feelings of confusion and cognitive dissonance that accompany change.

## Key Works

### Emissaries Trilogy (2015-2017)
Live simulations exploring cognitive evolution, each episode examining different aspects of consciousness and agency:

- **[Emissary in the Squat of Gods](https://fsrr.org/en/mostre-category/ian-cheng/)** (2015) — First episode, premiered at [[Fondazione Sandretto Re Rebaudengo]], Turin. Inspired by Julian Jaynes' bicameral mind theory. Simulation of ancient community facing geological threat + narrative of emerging consciousness occupying same landscape.
- **Emissary Forks for a Bright Future** (2016) — Second episode
- **Emissary Sunsets the Self** (2017) — Final episode

The complete trilogy was acquired by **[Museum of Modern Art, New York](https://www.moma.org/)** in 2018.

### BOB (Bag of Beliefs) (2017-ongoing)
AI creature that learns from audience interaction. Exhibited live at [[Serpentine Galleries]] (2018), where each BOB developed individual personality through visitor encounters. Not a passive artwork — BOBs actively observe, learn from, and react to viewers. Described as "artificial life forms exhibited in museums."

### Life After BOB (2021)
Animated film/simulation exploring consciousness and AI evolution. Extends BOB concept into narrative form.

### Bad Corgi (2015)
[[Serpentine Galleries]] digital commission — mindfulness mobile app featuring demon pup herding sheep. Early exploration of interactive digital art outside traditional exhibition spaces.

Ian Cheng represents the most sophisticated merger of artificial life research and contemporary art practice. His cognitive science background gives theoretical weight to the posthuman speculation inherent in his work.
