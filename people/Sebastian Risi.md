# Sebastian Risi

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Full deep research completed. Professor at IT University of Copenhagen, Research Scientist at Sakana AI, co-founder of modl.ai. ERC Consolidator Grant holder (GROW-AI, €1.9M). Key figure bridging ALIFE/evolutionary computation with modern deep learning. Extensive collaborator network including David Ha (Sakana AI co-founder), Kenneth O. Stanley, Michael Levin, and Julian Togelius.

**Professor of AI at the [IT University of Copenhagen](https://www.itu.dk/) and Research Scientist at [[Sakana AI]].** One of the leading researchers in self-evolving neural networks, neural cellular automata, open-ended evolution, and procedural content generation. His research asks: can we grow machines that continuously learn, adapt, and self-assemble — taking inspiration from biological development rather than human-designed architectures? Co-founder of [modl.ai](https://modl.ai), an AI game-testing company.

## Background
- **Diploma in Computer Science, [Philipps University of Marburg](https://www.uni-marburg.de/en)** (2007) — Germany. Foundation in computer science.

This lineage is critical: Stanley's lab is the intellectual origin point for many key ideas in open-endedness, quality diversity, and indirect encoding of neural networks. Risi's PhD work on adaptive HyperNEAT and developmental encodings directly built on this foundation.

Lipson is a pioneer in evolutionary robotics, self-replicating machines, and 3D printing. This is where Risi's interest in growing/self-assembling physical systems crystallized.

- **Research Scientist at [[Sakana AI]]** (Tokyo) — founded by **[[David Ha]]** (CEO), Llion Jones (CTO, co-inventor of the Transformer), and Ren Ito. Sakana works on nature-inspired AI (evolutionary model merging, the AI Scientist, Darwin Gödel Machine). Risi's role bridges his academic self-assembling systems research with Sakana's commercial nature-inspired AI agenda.
- **Co-founder of [modl.ai](https://modl.ai)** (Copenhagen) — AI-driven automated game testing. Uses AI agents that play games like human testers, finding bugs without code integration. This is the commercialization arm of his game AI research.
- Based in **Copenhagen, Denmark** — short flight from London. ITU is a small, design-oriented CS university, unusual and interesting institutional context.

## Domains
- **Self-assembling neural networks** — Neural networks that grow their own architecture through developmental programs, inspired by biological morphogenesis. The core of his ERC grant (GROW-AI).
- **Neural Cellular Automata (NCA)** — Self-organizing computational systems where local rules produce complex global behavior. Growing 3D structures, regenerating soft robots, variational NCA.
- **Open-ended evolution** — AI systems that continuously generate novelty and complexity, never converging. Connection to artificial life's grand challenge.
- **Neuroevolution & meta-learning** — Evolving neural networks, Hebbian plasticity in random networks, lifelong learning machines.
- **Procedural content generation** — Evolutionary and LLM-driven game level generation (MarioGPT, latent-space evolution of Mario levels).
- **Game AI** — Both academic (player modeling, content adaptation) and commercial (modl.ai automated testing).
- **Evolutionary robotics** — Self-repairing soft robots, bio-inspired controllers, Flora Robotica (EU FET project on plant-robot hybrid systems).
- **Human-AI co-creation** — Computational creativity, casual co-creation platforms, art exploration through object detection.

## Key Works

### Foundational Research
- [*Meta-Learning through Hebbian Plasticity in Random Networks*](https://arxiv.org/abs/2007.02686) (NeurIPS 2020) — Showed that Hebbian learning rules can enable rapid adaptation in randomly initialized networks. Press coverage in [Science](https://www.sciencemag.org/news/2020/12/watch-ai-robot-walk-broken-leg-thanks-brain-never-stops-learning). With Elias Najarro.
- [*Increasing Generality in Machine Learning through Procedural Content Generation*](https://www.nature.com/articles/s42256-020-0208-z) (Nature Machine Intelligence, 2020) — Influential paper arguing PCG is key to training more general AI. Major collaborative effort.
- [*Variational Neural Cellular Automata*](https://arxiv.org/abs/2201.12360) (ICLR 2022) — Brought variational inference to NCA, enabling generative self-organizing systems. With Rasmus Berg Palm.
- [*Growing 3D Artefacts and Functional Machines with Neural Cellular Automata*](https://arxiv.org/abs/2103.08737) (ALIFE 2021) — Growing complex 3D structures in Minecraft using NCA. Press in [Science](https://www.sciencemag.org/news/2021/03/watch-artificial-intelligence-grow-walking-caterpillar-minecraft) and [Fast Company](https://www.fastcompany.com/90618419/minecraft-self-building-structures-cellular-growth-evolution-simulation).

### GROW-AI Project
- [*GROW-AI: Growing Machines Capable of Rapid Learning in Unknown Environments*](https://sebastianrisi.com/grow-ai/) — ERC Consolidator Grant project. €1.9M. Central thesis: biological nervous systems aren't designed, they're grown through a "genomic bottleneck" — a compact developmental program that produces rich capabilities and rapid learning. Can we do the same for AI?
- [*Towards Self-Assembling Artificial Neural Networks through Neural Developmental Programs*](https://arxiv.org/abs/2307.08197) (ALIFE 2023) — With Elias Najarro and Shyam Sudhakaran.
- [*Evolving Self-Assembling Neural Networks: From Spontaneous Activity to Experience-Dependent Learning*](https://arxiv.org/abs/2406.09787) (ALIFE 2024) — With Erwan Plantec, Joachim Pedersen, Milton Montero, Eleni Nisioti.
- [*HyperNCA: Growing Developmental Networks with Neural Cellular Automata*](https://arxiv.org/abs/2204.11674) (ICLR Workshop 2022) — Bridging hypernetworks and NCA.

### Game AI & Procedural Generation
- [*MarioGPT: Open-Ended Text2Level Generation through Large Language Models*](https://arxiv.org/abs/2302.05981) (NeurIPS 2023) — Using GPT to generate Super Mario levels from text descriptions. Wide press coverage: [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2023/02/14/mariogpt-hints-at-a-glorious-ai-generated-future-where-we-will-all-play-nintendo-forever/), [Kotaku](https://kotaku.com/super-mario-bros-mariogpt-ai-text-to-level-generator-1850124134). - [*EvoCraft: A New Challenge for Open-Endedness*](https://arxiv.org/abs/2012.04751) (EvoApplications 2021) — Minecraft as an open-ended evolution benchmark.

## Research Taste Analysis

### Conceptual Strategy
- **Biological metaphor transplant** — Risi's core move is finding a specific biological mechanism (genomic bottleneck, Hebbian plasticity, developmental growth, self-assembly) and creating a precise computational analog. Not vague bio-inspiration, but mechanistic translation. The "genomic bottleneck" from Tony Zador's neuroscience → GROW-AI's neural developmental programs is the purest example.
- **Platform problem framing** — Instead of solving one task, Risi frames platforms: EvoCraft (Minecraft as open-ended evolution sandbox), MarioGPT (text-to-level as a framework), NCA as a substrate. Each becomes a tool others build on.
- **Scaling-contrarian** — Explicitly positions against the "just scale it" paradigm. His ERC grant pitch: "While scaling current neural network approaches has produced ground-breaking results, I'm in doubt that this method alone will get us closer to general AI." This is a bet on a different future.

### Career Architecture
- **Niche-to-mainstream pipeline** — PhD in neuroevolution (niche) → publishes at NeurIPS/ICLR (mainstream) → gets ERC grant (institutional prestige) → joins Sakana AI (industry relevance). Never abandons the ALIFE community but systematically bridges it to bigger stages.
- **Triple positioning** — Professor + Startup co-founder + Industry research scientist simultaneously. Each role feeds the others: academic credibility enables grants, modl.ai demonstrates commercial viability, Sakana AI provides cutting-edge compute and talent.
- **Strategic advisor board** — GROW-AI's advisory board (Kevin Mitchell, Robin Hiesinger, Tony Zador) are the actual neuroscientists whose work inspired the project. This isn't decoration — it's intellectual supply chain management.

### Methodology Signature
- **Grow, don't design** — The signature methodological claim. All of his most cited work involves systems that self-organize: neural networks that grow their architecture, NCA that self-assemble 3D shapes, robots that regenerate.
- **Minecraft as laboratory** — Using Minecraft (EvoCraft) as a substrate for open-ended evolution research is genius: it's complex enough to be meaningful, accessible enough to attract collaborators, and culturally legible enough to get press coverage.
- **Press-worthy demos** — Nearly every major paper has a visual demo that gets media coverage (Science, TechCrunch, Fast Company). The Minecraft caterpillar growing itself, MarioGPT levels, the robot walking on a broken leg. This is deliberate: visual results in media amplify academic impact.

### Network Strategy
- **Lineage exploitation** — Kenneth O. Stanley (PhD advisor) → connected to the entire neuroevolution/open-endedness ecosystem → Uber AI Labs alumni network → Jeff Clune → David Ha → Sakana AI. Risi systematically leverages his academic lineage into industry connections.
- **Large collaborative papers** — The Nature Machine Intelligence papers (2020, 2022) are coalition-building exercises with 20+ authors, establishing community consensus around his research agenda.
- **Student pipeline** — Builds a tight group (Najarro, Sudhakaran, Pedersen, Plantec, Nisioti, Montero) who co-author everything and become the next generation of ALIFE/developmental AI researchers.
