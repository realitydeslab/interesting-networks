# Friedrich Kittler

German media theorist and literary scholar. Pioneer of German media studies and one of the most influential humanists of the late 20th century. His *Discourse Networks 1800/1900* and *Gramophone, Film, Typewriter* established a materialist, technological approach to cultural history that transformed media theory globally. Mentor to [[Bernhard Siegert]], [[Claus Pias]], and others of the Kittler school.

## Background
Born June 12, 1943 in Rochlitz. Studied at Albert-Ludwigs-Universität Freiburg. Professor at Ruhr-Universität Bochum, then at Humboldt University Berlin (Chair for Aesthetics and History of Media, 1993–2008). His approach: media determine our situation. Technologies of inscription (the gramophone, the typewriter, the computer) don't merely record human expression — they constitute the very possibility of what can be thought and said.

## Why Interesting
The foundational figure for all of German media theory. Understanding Kittler is prerequisite for understanding Siegert, Pias, Ernst, and the cultural techniques paradigm. His hardware determinism is the foil against which cultural techniques theory (as softer, more operational) defines itself.
