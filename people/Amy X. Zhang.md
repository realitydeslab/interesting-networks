# Amy X. Zhang

Assistant Professor at UW Allen School. Researcher in social computing focused on **platform governance** — how communities design and enforce rules, moderate content, and manage power dynamics online. Built PolicyKit, a governance toolkit for online communities. Prolific co-author with [[Michael Bernstein]] on social computing infrastructure.

## Background
- PhD at MIT CSAIL
- PostDoc / junior faculty, moved to University of Washington
- Research focused on tools for online community governance and sensemaking

## Key Works
- **[PolicyKit: Building Governance in Online Communities](https://hci.stanford.edu/publications/uist_policykit.pdf)** (UIST 2020) — With Grant Hugh and Bernstein. A governance toolkit allowing online communities to define and enforce their own rules through code. Think "democratic infrastructure for subreddits."
- **[Proceedings of the Knowledge Infrastructure for the Future](https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.05388)** (CSCW 2024) — With Bernstein, David Karger, Mark Ackerman. On building shared knowledge infrastructure.
- Multiple papers on content moderation, group dynamics, and collective decision-making.

## Why Interesting
Platform governance is a deeply political topic: who gets to make rules for online communities? How do algorithmic systems embed power structures? Zhang's work on PolicyKit asks Amber's kind of question — not "how do we build better content moderation AI" but "how do we give communities democratic control over their own governance." This connects to the Aarhus participatory computing tradition. Worth tracking for her political imagination applied to platform design.
