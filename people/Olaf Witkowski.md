# Olaf Witkowski

Co-founder and Director of [[Cross Labs]], a Kyoto-based research institute pursuing the mathematical foundations of intelligence across natural and artificial systems. Vice President of the [[ISAL|International Society for Artificial Life]]. General Chair of [[ALIFE Conference|ALIFE 2025]] in Kyoto. One of the most active institution-builders in the global ALife community — not just doing research, but creating the infrastructure (labs, conferences, journals, workshops, summer schools) that makes the field run.

## Domains
- **Open-Endedness** — algorithms that keep learning forever, like biological evolution. How do you create systems that generate novelty indefinitely?
- **Theory of Agency** — mathematical frameworks for how goals emerge in agents. Credit assignment in its most fundamental form.
- **Collective Learning** — swarm intelligence, multi-agent systems, societies of AI that learn from each other.
- **Neuroscience of Intelligence** — translating biological intelligence processes into mathematical principles.
- **Origins of Life** — the deep connection between ALife and astrobiology.
- **Consciousness & Phenomenology** — publications on autopoiesis, integrated consciousness, and the ethics of AI systems.
- **AI × Symbiosis** — his blog writings argue against the "AI will destroy humanity" narrative, instead framing AI as a symbiotic relationship comparable to mitochondria in eukaryotes. Draws on extended mind theory (Clark & Chalmers), enactivism (Froese), and evolutionary biology.

## Venue Circuit Analysis
Olaf operates across **three distinct circuits**:

1. **Global ALife/Complexity**: ALIFE conference (as VP of ISAL and General Chair), Santa Fe Institute connections (Kempes), IAS Princeton. This is the intellectual core.
2. **Japan Academic-Industrial**: Cross Compass (manufacturing AI), Tokyo Institute of Technology, University of Tokyo, Hokkaido University, Tokyo University of the Arts, Bunkyo University. He's uniquely positioned as a Western researcher deeply embedded in the Japanese academic-industrial system.
3. **Cross Labs Workshop Circuit**: His own series of annual workshops that he's built since 2019 — increasingly ambitious, pulling in major names (Wolfram, Levin, Maes, Stanley, Schwitzgebel). These are intimate (not mega-conferences), discussion-focused, and interdisciplinary. This is exactly the kind of venue Amber values.

**Kyoto as a strategic choice**: Not Tokyo, not Silicon Valley. Kyoto is Japan's cultural and intellectual capital. By placing Cross Labs there, Olaf has created an intellectual outpost that's both rooted in Japanese tradition and globally connected. The choice to host ALIFE 2025 in Kyoto (rather than a US/European city) signals a deliberate repositioning of ALife's center of gravity.
