# David Abram

Philosopher, ecologist, and magician — the person who coined "the more-than-human world." Abram is the most important single figure connecting phenomenology (Merleau-Ponty), ecological science (Gaia hypothesis, Margulis), and Indigenous cosmologies into a unified philosophy of sensuous participation with the animate Earth. His 1996 book *The Spell of the Sensuous* is the foundational text of ecological phenomenology, and his phrase "the more-than-human world" has become the central term of multispecies studies, more-than-human design, and ecological humanities.

## Background
Born on Long Island, New York. Began as a stage magician — literally sleight-of-hand street magic — which gave him a lifelong fascination with perception and misdirection. Studied at Wesleyan University, then traveled through Southeast Asia as an itinerant magician, living with traditional practitioners in Sri Lanka, Indonesia, and Nepal. At Yale School of Forestry (1984), wrote "The Perceptual Implications of Gaia," which brought him into contact with James Lovelock and Lynn Margulis, and subsequently into the Lindisfarne Fellowship. Received his PhD from Stony Brook University (1993, philosophy). Isabelle Stengers translated *The Spell of the Sensuous* into French (2013).

## Key Works
- [*The Spell of the Sensuous: Perception and Language in a More-than-Human World*](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/319/the-spell-of-the-sensuous-by-david-abram) (1996, Pantheon) — Lannan Literary Award for Nonfiction; the foundational text; covers Merleau-Ponty phenomenology, alphabetic writing vs. oral animist cultures, language and landscape
- [*Becoming Animal: An Earthly Cosmology*](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/318/becoming-animal-by-david-abram) (2010, Pantheon) — runner-up for inaugural PEN/E.O. Wilson Literary Science Writing Award; develops the "commonwealth of breath" concept

## Key Concepts
- **"The more-than-human world"** — coined in subtitle of *The Spell of the Sensuous* (1996); designates earthly nature as including and exceeding human culture; now the central term of multispecies studies and more-than-human design
- **The commonwealth of breath** — recent elaboration: the atmosphere as a commons shared by all breathing organisms
- **Ecophenomenology** — application of Merleau-Ponty's phenomenology to human-nature relationships
- **Alphabetic inscription & perceptual narrowing** — thesis that alphabetic literacy pulled human perception away from the animate landscape toward the self-referential page; oral cultures maintained richer ecological perception
- **Animism as viable worldview** — first contemporary philosopher to advocate seriously for animism as a cognitively sophisticated (not naive) philosophy
