# Matteo Pasquinelli

Italian philosopher, media theorist, and historian of AI. Author of [*The Eye of the Master: A Social History of Artificial Intelligence*](https://www.versobooks.com/products/735-the-eye-of-the-master) (Verso, 2023) — the most rigorous Marxist-materialist account of machine intelligence currently available. Associate Professor in Philosophy of Science at Ca' Foscari University Venice. PI of the ERC Consolidator Grant [AIMODELS](https://pric.unive.it/projects/ai-models/home) (2024–2028). Winner of the [Deutscher Memorial Prize 2024](https://www.deutscherprize.org.uk/wp/).

## Background
Italian. Trained in philosophy, media theory, and critical theory at the intersection of the Italian autonomist/operaismo tradition and German media philosophy (Berlin, Karlsruhe). 

Career trajectory:
1. **Pre-2010s**: Emerged from Italian radical left intellectual milieu — associated with journals *Multitudes*, *Rekombinant*, autonomist media collectives. Early work on "animal spirits," immaterial labour, network politics (*Animal Spirits*, Rekombinant).
2. **2010s — Berlin/HfG era**: Moved to Germany. Edited landmark anthology [*Alleys of Your Mind: Augmented Intelligence and Its Traumas*](https://meson.press/books/alleys-of-your-mind/) (Meson Press, 2015). Taught at Pratt Institute New York. Joined HfG Karlsruhe (University of Arts and Design) as professor of Media Philosophy.
3. **2018–2023 — KIM research group**: Founded [KIM (Critical AI Studies)](https://kim.hfg-karlsruhe.de/) at HfG Karlsruhe — landmark research group on AI and media philosophy. Built collaborative research infrastructure across Europe. Co-created the [Nooscope Manifested](https://kim.hfg-karlsruhe.de/nooscope.ai) with Vladan Joler (2020) — became widely taught visual essay on AI as instrument of knowledge extractivism.
4. **2022**: Awarded ERC Consolidator Grant — career-defining institutional recognition.
5. **2023**: Published *The Eye of the Master* (Verso) — immediate global impact. Moved to Ca' Foscari Venice.
6. **2024–present**: Associate Professor at Ca' Foscari. Running 5-year ERC project AIMODELS. Deutscher Prize winner. Book in 14+ languages.

Based in Venice (primary) and Berlin (secondary).

## Why Interesting
Pasquinelli is doing what almost no one else in the AI-critical space does: he connects the **deep history of labor abstraction** (from Babbage to today) with the **internal architecture of machine learning algorithms**, and does so with rigorous philosophical and historical method. This is not "AI ethics" discourse — it's a structural critique of how AI encodes measurement, hierarchy, and extraction.

For Amber's research, the connection is the **"social history" method**: studying machine behavior not as autonomous phenomenon but as encoding of social relations. The Nooscope in particular — an AI cartography that maps both how AI works AND how it fails — is a direct methodological ancestor for any serious work on agent ethology or machine behavior as science. The question "what does AI actually do in society?" that Pasquinelli asks through historical materialism, Amber asks through ethological observation.

## Research Taste Analysis

### Conceptual Strategy

**Labor-theory transplant as field generator**: Pasquinelli's core move is importing 19th-century political economy (Marx, Babbage, General Intellect) into contemporary AI critique. Not "AI is Marxist" superficially — but finding that the actual *architecture* of machine learning (its training on human labor patterns, its abstraction of skill, its division of cognitive labor) is **structurally** isomorphic with industrial labor abstraction. This is framework multiplication: history of technology × political economy × philosophy of mind = new field (labor theory of AI).

**Diagram-manifestos as theoretical objects**: The Nooscope is the signature format. It's not a paper (though it was also published as a paper). It's a map, a manifesto, a diagram — made to circulate visually, to be taught, to be reproduced. This is a key move: make your theory into an *artifact* that travels across disciplines.

**Response-papers as territory**: "Beyond the Schism" (e-flux Notes, June 2024) is a response to a *Critical Inquiry* review. Using public scholarly debate to further clarify and defend your position is territory-building — it shows you're central enough that critics engage with you.

### Career Architecture

**Institute-building as intellectual infrastructure**: KIM (Critical AI Studies) at HfG Karlsruhe was not just a research group — it was a **field declaration** and intellectual hub that produced the Nooscope, hosted symposia, trained PhD students, and coordinated EU projects. When he left HfG, the AIMODELS grant went with him as portable institutional infrastructure. This is how you build influence that outlasts any single institution.

**Verso as platform**: Publishing with Verso Books is a deliberate career choice. Verso is the flagship of the anglophone radical left intellectual scene. It distributes to a global left-academic readership that journals don't reach. The Deutscher Prize win was then the academic validation that followed.

**ERC as legitimation**: ERC Consolidator is the European academic gold standard. It validates the field (historical epistemology of AI) as a fundable research program and provides 5 years of resources to build a school of thought.

**Translation as distribution strategy**: 14+ translations — including Arabic, Farsi, Chinese, Russian — means deliberately targeting non-Western academic markets. This is unusual for European critical theory. It signals: this is not just for the anglophone left, it's for global AI critique.

### What Amber Can Learn
- **Build infrastructure before you publish the book**: KIM came before *Eye of the Master*. The research group generated the ideas, tested them in workshops, produced the Nooscope as an early artifact. Infrastructure produces ideas, not the other way around.
- **Make diagram-manifestos**: The Nooscope is more influential than any single paper. For Amber's own theoretical work on agent ethology or machine behavior, a visual artifact (a map, a diagram, a manifesto) would travel further and be cited more than a 30-page paper.
- **Choose your press strategically**: Verso over MIT Press if you want the left-intellectual-global-south distribution. MIT Press if you want academic libraries and tech-ethics readers. The choice shapes who reads you.
- **Translation = global south penetration**: Pasquinelli's 14-language translation is a deliberate strategy. For Amber working in AI ethics/behavior, translating key essays into Chinese, Arabic, or Portuguese opens entirely new audiences.
- **Response-papers are territory**: When someone critiques your work in *Critical Inquiry*, respond publicly in *e-flux*. This shows you're central, clarifies your position, and models rigorous engagement with critics.
