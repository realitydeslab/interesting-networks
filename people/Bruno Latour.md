# Bruno Latour

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Stub created during Donna Haraway deep research. Latour died October 9, 2022.

French philosopher, anthropologist, and sociologist of science. Co-founder of **Actor-Network Theory (ANT)** with Michel Callon and John Law. One of the most cited social scientists of the 20th-21st century. Central figure in Science & Technology Studies (STS). His *We Have Never Been Modern* (1991) is canonical; *Facing Gaia* (2017) and the *Critical Zones* project at ZKM Karlsruhe are his late-career environmental work. Died October 9, 2022.

## Domains
- **Actor-Network Theory (ANT)** — non-humans (objects, microbes, machines) as actors in social processes; flattens human/non-human hierarchy
- **Science Studies** — how scientific facts are constructed in laboratories (*Laboratory Life* with Woolgar, 1979)
- **Political ecology** — Gaia, Critical Zones, Earthbound vs. Humans
- **Philosophy of modernity** — *We Have Never Been Modern* (1991)

## Key Works
- **[*Laboratory Life: The Construction of Scientific Facts*](https://www.amazon.com/Laboratory-Life-Construction-Scientific-Facts/dp/0691028185)** (with Steve Woolgar, 1979) — Ethnography of a biology lab. Foundational for STS.
- **[*Science in Action*](https://www.hup.harvard.edu/catalog.php?isbn=9780674792913)** (Harvard, 1987) — How science and technology are built through networks.
- **[*We Have Never Been Modern*](https://www.hup.harvard.edu/catalog.php?isbn=9780674948396)** (Harvard, 1993) — The modernity critique. The separation of nature/culture/society is a modern invention — and we've never actually done it.
- **[*Reassembling the Social: An Introduction to Actor-Network-Theory*](https://academic.oup.com/book/37015)** (Oxford, 2005) — The definitive ANT textbook.
- **[*Facing Gaia: Eight Lectures on the New Climatic Regime*](https://www.wiley.com/en-gb/Facing+Gaia%3A+Eight+Lectures+on+the+New+Climatic+Regime-p-9780745684338)** (Polity, 2017) — Late career environmental turn. Gaia as a geopolitical actor.
- **[An Inquiry into Modes of Existence](https://www.modesofexistence.org/)** (Harvard, 2013) — 15 different modes of existence for humans and their assemblages. Digital humanities project.

## Why Interesting
Latour is the ANT co-founder — the theoretical framework that many "things as actors" / non-human agency arguments in design and STS build on. His late work (Critical Zones, Gaia) directly overlaps with multispecies / environmental humanities. His dialogue with Haraway at the Brazil primate conference (see Haraway note) is a key historical moment in feminist STS. Haraway and Latour are often taught together as the two poles of constructivist STS: Latour → actor-network theory; Haraway → feminist materialism. For Amber, understanding this tension is important for positioning her own work.
