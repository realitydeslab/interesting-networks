# John Holland

Computer scientist and psychologist who invented **genetic algorithms** — computational models of Darwinian evolution for optimization and machine learning. Also developed **classifier systems** and the broader framework of **complex adaptive systems (CAS)** at the Santa Fe Institute. Author of *Adaptation in Natural and Artificial Systems* (1975) and *Hidden Order: How Adaptation Builds Complexity* (1995). One of the founding external faculty of SFI.

## Why Interesting
Holland's genetic algorithms are the ancestor of evolutionary computation and a strand of ALife. The CAS framework is still the dominant vocabulary for complexity in social and economic systems. For understanding the SFI intellectual program that shaped Kauffman, Holland is essential.
