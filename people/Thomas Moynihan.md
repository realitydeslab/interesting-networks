# Thomas Moynihan

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Initial deep research. Full profile from website, CSER page, publications. Created stubs for CSER, Anders Sandberg.

Historian of ideas and author. Writes about how worldviews transform over time as humans learn more about the universe and their place within it — specifically the history of how humanity discovered its biggest-picture priorities: the greatest perils and potentials facing our species. Currently working on a new book about how people slowly woke up to the ways present actions can scar the entire future for life on Earth.

## Key Works

### Books
- [*X-Risk: How Humanity Discovered Its Own Extinction*](https://www.urbanomic.com/book/x-risk/) ([MIT Press](https://mitpress.mit.edu/books/x-risk) & [[Urbanomic]], 2020) — The book traces how the idea of human extinction emerged as a modern, secular concept from the Enlightenment onwards, distinct from religious apocalypse narratives. Covers astrobiology, geoscience, forecasting, and existential catastrophe through intellectual history. Praised by Anders Sandberg (FHI) and Milan Ćirković. The definitive intellectual history of the x-risk concept.
- **New book in progress** — on how people through history slowly woke up to the ways present actions and accidents can scar the entire future for life on Earth (climate disruption, resource depletion, mass extinction). Aims to retell the story of how we grasped that the biography of macroevolution and planet Earth itself is shot through with contingency.

## Venue Circuit Analysis
Moynihan operates across **three distinct circuits**:

1. **X-risk / EA institutional circuit**: CSER Cambridge, 80,000 Hours, FHI-adjacent Oxford network. This is the "serious" existential risk world — quantitative, policy-oriented, tied to effective altruism philanthropy.

2. **Speculative philosophy / theory circuit**: Urbanomic, Antikythera, Spike Art Magazine, Tank Magazine, Combinations Magazine. This is the Continental philosophy / speculative realism world — Robin Mackay, Reza Negarestani, Benjamin Bratton. Conceptually adventurous, less policy-oriented.

3. **High-end science communication circuit**: BBC Future, Aeon, Noema, The Guardian, New Scientist, Big Think, MIT Press Reader, Asimov Press, Palladium. This is where he reaches broad audiences with deep intellectual history translated into accessible prose.

The **key insight** is that Moynihan uniquely bridges circuits 1 and 2, which rarely talk to each other. The EA/x-risk world is analytical and utilitarian; the speculative philosophy world is Continental and conceptual. Moynihan's intellectual history work is legible to both. This bridging position is extremely rare and valuable.

## Research Taste Analysis

### Conceptual Strategy
- **Intellectual archaeology** — Rather than proposing new theories, Moynihan excavates the *history* of ideas that others take as givens. He shows that "existential risk" has a rich, surprising prehistory in Enlightenment thought, astrobiology, and geoscience. This reframing gives familiar concepts new depth.
- **Counter-narrative construction** — His recurring move is: "You think X is modern/new, but actually it has a centuries-old history, and understanding that history changes what X means today." Applied to extinction, AI anxiety, space colonization, consciousness, climate.
- **Two-register writing** — Same ideas appear in academic-adjacent form (Urbanomic, Antikythera) and in accessible public form (BBC Future, Big Think). Different framings for different audiences, but the intellectual core is consistent.

### Career Architecture
- **Institutional triangulation** — Rather than one tenure-track position, Moynihan holds three simultaneous affiliations (CSER, Berggruen, Antikythera), each giving him different network access and legitimacy. CSER = x-risk credibility, Berggruen = global intellectual credibility + publication pipeline (Noema), Antikythera = speculative philosophy credibility.
- **Book as credential** — *X-Risk* (2020) was the career-defining move. Published simultaneously by MIT Press (academic legitimacy) and Urbanomic (theory-world credibility). One book, two publishers, two audiences. This dual publication opened both circuits to him.
- **Prolific public writing as visibility engine** — He publishes in 15+ outlets across different registers. This isn't padding — each outlet connects him to a different audience and network.

### Methodology Signature
- **Intellectual history as method** — He doesn't do empirical research, experiments, or fieldwork. His method is reading deeply across centuries and synthesizing narratives about how ideas evolved. This is a low-resource, high-insight method that anyone can adopt.
- **Cross-century poaching** — He imports examples from the 17th-19th centuries into 21st-century debates, making old thinkers speak to current concerns. Paracelsus on "star stuff," medieval theology on modernity, 1780s extinction debates on contemporary x-risk.

### Network Strategy
- **Dual-world bridging** — His most distinctive move: being credible in BOTH the analytical EA/x-risk world AND the Continental speculative philosophy world. These communities rarely overlap. He serves as a translator between them.
- **Co-authorship with key figures** — Writing with Anders Sandberg (FHI) anchors him in the x-risk world; publishing with Urbanomic/Antikythera anchors him in theory-world.

### What Amber Can Learn
- **The intellectual history move**: Any emerging field needs someone to tell its story. Machine behavior, more-than-human computing, computational creativity — these fields are young enough that their intellectual history hasn't been written. Amber could be the person who writes the origin story of her field, just as Moynihan wrote the origin story of x-risk.
- **Institutional triangulation over tenure track**: Multiple affiliations (research affiliate, fellow, affiliate researcher) give more freedom, more networks, and more publication outlets than a single position. This is a viable alternative academic career model.
- **Dual-register publishing**: Same ideas, different framings for different audiences. Academic paper → Aeon essay → BBC Future piece. Each version opens a different door.
- **Timing a book with cultural moment**: X-Risk published in 2020, just as COVID made extinction concerns mainstream. The lesson: identify which of your ideas is about to become culturally relevant, and have the book ready.
- **Bridge rare to each other communities**: The EA world and the speculative philosophy world don't naturally talk. Being legible to both gives Moynihan outsized influence. Amber can do the same: bridge HCI/design research with speculative philosophy, or bridge Western more-than-human thinking with Chinese cosmotechnics.
