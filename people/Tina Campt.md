# Tina Campt

Professor of visual culture and contemporary art. Close friend and intellectual peer of **[[Saidiya Hartman]]**. Author of *Listening to Images* (2017) and *A Black Gaze: Artists Changing How We See* (2021). Her work focuses on quiet photography, the affective dimensions of Black visual culture, and what she calls "quiet" forms of refusal.

## Key Works
- **[*Listening to Images*](https://www.dukeupress.edu/listening-to-images)** (Duke University Press, 2017) — reframes "quiet" photography of everyday Black life as a practice of future-making; argues that images project alternative futures even when depicting mundane presents
- **[*A Black Gaze: Artists Changing How We See*](https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262543477/a-black-gaze/)** (MIT Press, 2021) — on how contemporary Black artists are reshaping the conditions of visuality itself

## Key Connections

## Why Interesting
Campt applies Hartman's archival methods to visual culture — both are thinking about what the archive suppresses, but Campt does so through photographs and film. Her concept of "quiet" refusal resonates with Hartman's "narrative restraint."
