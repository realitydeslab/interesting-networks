# Wendi Yan 闫文迪

Artist, writer, technologist. Constructs speculative epistemologies through research-driven worldbuilding and metafictional simulation. Uses CGI, game engines, and documentary practices to probe the artifice of knowledge. Collaborates with scientists (synthetic biology, astrophysics) to explore the frontier between science and entertainment.

## Background
- Born 1999, Beijing. Grew up wanting to design game characters/environments. Took digital illustration classes in middle school, discovered oil painting in US high school.
- **AB History of Science, [Princeton University](https://www.princeton.edu/)** — won the Horace H. Wilson '25 Senior Thesis Prize in History of Science, Medicine, and Technology. This is the foundation: her speculative work has genuine historiographic rigor because she was trained as a historian of science.
- **MS Fiction and Entertainment, [SCI-Arc](https://sciarc.edu/)** (Southern California Institute of Architecture) — experimental architecture school's fiction programme. Explains the spatial/world-building sophistication and cinematic quality.
- Based in New York.
- Directs **[Research Revival Fund](https://wendiyan.com/)** — her own initiative, an experimental fund restoring illegible research to active circulation. (Worth tracking as a model for experimental funding.)

## Key Works
- **[Dream of Walnut Palaces](https://wendiyan.com/exhibitions)** (2025) — CGI film. A jeweled skeleton of a Chinese scholar on a ghost ship heading west through an imagined 1780s Paris. Fuses Taoist philosophy, science history, and cutting-edge animation. Imagines Eastern and Western knowledge meeting as equals. **Grand Prix, [6th VH Award](https://vhaward.com/6th-award/)**.
- **Visions of Phosphine Earth** (2025) — film imagining microbial life in Venus's sulfuric clouds
- **[The Inner Carbon Classic](https://wendiyan.com/)** (2024) — 3D game + animation film
- **[Biotopy](https://wendiyan.com/)** (2024) — bio-digital interface, game installation, writing. Presented at Unfiguring Conference at Harvard.
- **Tale of the Mammoth Goddess** (2023) — video about a resurrected woolly mammoth growing up in our time
- **A Tiny Museum of Mammoth Technology** (2023) — solo show, Hurley Gallery, Princeton, NJ
- **[Discovering an Antimalarial Drug in Mao's China](https://press.asimov.com/)** — published by [Asimov Press](https://press.asimov.com/), named "Best of Journalism" by [The Syllabus](https://the-syllabus.com/). Serious science history writing, not art-adjacent fluff.

## Venue Choice Analysis
Wendi deliberately moves between **four circuits**:
1. **Asian art-tech**: VH Award (Korean), Art Basel HK, ArtScience Museum Singapore, Kai Tak Art Week, Hyundai Motorstudio Beijing, BANK/TANK Shanghai, X Museum
2. **European media art**: Ars Electronica, HEK Basel, Berlin Art Week, Hamburger Bahnhof, Ujazdowski Castle Warsaw, Expanded Conference
3. **NYC art-tech**: NEW INC, Eyebeam, Rhizome, Powerhouse Arts, Artifice, The Wythe
4. **Silicon Valley/tech-culture**: Interact Fellowship, Steve Jobs Archive, SeedAI×SXSW, Gray Area, FWB Fest, Skywalker Ranch

She also bridges **crypto-culture** (FWB, Ethereum Foundation, Cosmos Ventures) and **serious institutions** (Harvard, Princeton, Hamburger Bahnhof). The small venues she chooses (Trust Berlin, Last Projects LA, Artifice NYC, Slash SF) are all emerging spaces with strong curatorial vision — she's betting on the scene before it becomes mainstream.

## Why Interesting
**[[Sinofuturism]]** angle: her work directly imagines what happens when Chinese and Western knowledge systems collide — not as a political project but as speculative epistemology. Princeton History of Science training gives her an unusual rigor; SCI-Arc Fiction gives her cinematic worldbuilding; together they produce something genuinely new.

The Research Revival Fund is a signal: she's not just making art, she's building infrastructure for illegible knowledge. This is a systems-level thinker operating through art.

Every fellowship/community she joins reveals her network strategy: Steve Jobs Archive (tech-philanthropy), Interact (tech-culture), NEW INC (art-tech), Eyebeam (art-tech), Arctic Circle (art-science), FWB (crypto-culture). She's weaving between all of these deliberately.
