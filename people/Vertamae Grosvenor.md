# Vertamae Grosvenor

(born Vertamae Smart) — Gullah-Geechee author, performer, NPR host, and culinary anthropologist from rural South Carolina. Author of *Vibration Cooking, or The Travel Notes of a Geechee Girl* (1970) — a landmark book blending memoir, food culture, and African diasporic identity, widely considered an early example of Black food writing as cultural criticism.

Appeared in [[Julie Dash]]'s *Daughters of the Dust* (1991), playing a character aligned with her own background as a Gullah woman. Was a longtime NPR commentator and host of *Seasonings* and *The Seasoning of a Cook*. Lived much of her life in Paris and New York.

Currently the subject of Dash's documentary-in-progress, **Travel Notes of a Geechee Girl** — described as a feature-length documentary about her life. Died 2016; the film is an act of posthumous recovery for a figure overlooked by mainstream literary/cultural histories.
