# Matthew Handelman

Scholar of German literature, digital humanities, and the philosophy of mathematics. Associate Professor at Michigan State University's German Department. Author of *[The Mathematical Imagination: On the Origins and Promise of Critical Theory](https://www.fordhampress.com/9780823283149/the-mathematical-imagination/)* (Fordham UP, 2019), which argues that mathematics functions as a form of critical thought in German Jewish modernism (Rosenzweig, Benjamin, Scholem). Co-author of *[Digital Theory](https://www.upress.umn.edu/9781517920197/digital-theory/)* (University of Minnesota Press/meson press, 2026) with [[Alexander Galloway]], [[Beatrice Fazi]], and Leif Weatherby.

## Background
Trained in German studies and critical theory. His *Mathematical Imagination* (2019) makes an unusual argument: that Benjamin, Rosenzweig, and Scholem turned to mathematics (not in spite of but because of) their Jewish modernist context. This bridges Frankfurt School, Jewish studies, and philosophy of mathematics. Co-author of *Digital Theory* with Galloway, Fazi, Weatherby.

## Key Works
- *[The Mathematical Imagination: On the Origins and Promise of Critical Theory](https://www.fordhampress.com/9780823283149/the-mathematical-imagination/)* (Fordham UP, 2019) — critical theory and mathematics in German Jewish modernism
- *[Digital Theory](https://www.upress.umn.edu/9781517920197/digital-theory/)* (co-authored with [[Alexander Galloway]], [[Beatrice Fazi]], Leif Weatherby, UMN Press/meson press, 2026)

## Why Interesting
The mathematics → critical theory pipeline combined with digital humanities is an unusual combination. *Digital Theory* (2026) co-authorship puts him at the center of the new digital philosophy conversation.
