# Tega Brain

Australian-born digital artist and environmental engineer whose work examines issues of ecology, data, automation, and infrastructure. Her practice critiques the engineering mindset through art that reveals the limitations of technological solutions to environmental problems. Known for creating "eccentric" systems that operate according to environmental rather than computational logic.

## Background
Born 1982 in Australia. Trained as an environmental engineer before transitioning into art practice. This engineering background is crucial to understanding her work—she critiques engineering thinking from the inside, having been trained in its methodologies and assumptions. Based in New York since joining NYU faculty.

Her transition from environmental engineering to art represents a deliberate critique of techno-solutionist approaches to environmental problems. Rather than rejecting technology entirely, she creates "eccentric" technological systems that operate according to environmental rather than computational logic.

## Domains
**Environmental Art:** Creates systems controlled by environmental phenomena rather than human users
**Critical Computing:** Questions the assumptions underlying computational thinking and infrastructure  
**Ecological Systems:** Explores more-than-human agency and environmental intelligence
**Network Art:** Builds alternative internet infrastructures powered by renewable energy
**Data Art:** Creates systems for obfuscating personal data and questioning surveillance
**Educational Technology:** Co-authored foundational textbook on creative coding

## Key Works
**[Solar Protocol](https://solarprotocol.net/)** (2020-ongoing) — Network of solar-powered servers around the world that routes internet traffic based on sunshine availability rather than speed. Nominated for Ars Electronica S.T.ARTS prize (2024). Collaborative project exploring energy-centered design with [[Alex Nathanson]] and [[Benedetta Piantella]].

**Being Radiotropic** (2015) — Series of eccentric wireless routers that respond to electromagnetic environments. Created during [[Eyebeam]] residency. Explores human sensitivity to wireless signal landscapes.

**[Code as Creative Medium](https://mitpress.mit.edu/books/code-creative-medium)** (MIT Press, 2021) — Comprehensive textbook co-authored with [[Golan Levin]]. Foundational resource for creative coding education.

**"The Environment is Not a System"** (2018) — Influential essay/paper critiquing the engineering mindset that treats ecology as a system to be optimized. Key theoretical work underlying her practice.

**Smell Dating** — "Wildly popular online smell-based dating service" that uses scent rather than algorithms for matching.
