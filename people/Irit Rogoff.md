# Irit Rogoff

Writer, theorist, teacher, and curator. Professor of Visual Cultures at Goldsmiths. **Founded the Department of Visual Cultures at Goldsmiths in 2002** — the origin point of one of the world's most important art-theory departments. Previously at UC Davis (1989–1997), where she merged graduate programs in Art and Critical Theory under "Visual Culture."

Research: visual culture, contemporary art and critical theory, postcolonialism, geoculture, cultures of education, gender.

Key works: *Terra Infirma: Geography's Visual Culture* (Routledge, 2000); *Visual Cultures as Seriousness* (Sternberg Press, 2013, with Gavin Butt). Guest-edited e-flux journal no. 14 (2010, "Education Actualised").

Co-founded **freethought** (2011) — curatorial and research collective with Stefano Harney, Adrian Heathfield et al. Freethought was artistic director of Bergen Assembly 2016.

## Why Interesting
The architect of the institutional model that Goldsmiths Visual Cultures represents: a department where theory and practice are genuinely entangled, where curatorial practice is a form of philosophical inquiry, where education itself is a critical methodology.
