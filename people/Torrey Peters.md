# Torrey Peters

Trans novelist and one of the most visible voices of contemporary trans literature. Best known for *Detransition, Baby* (2021), a mainstream literary novel published by Random House that was the first trans-authored mainstream literary novel to receive a major publisher push. Part of the Brooklyn trans bohemia documented by [[McKenzie Wark]] in her 2022 *Document Journal* essay "Mapping a modern trans bohemia in the borough of the flesh."

## Background
Before mainstream publication, Peters self-published trans fiction online and shared it in trans communities for free. Her earlier novellas (*Infect Your Friends and Loved Ones*, *The Masker*, *Glamour Boutique*) circulated as PDFs in trans networks. The transition to Random House and mainstream literary success represents an interesting case study in how trans cultural production moves (or doesn't move) from community to market.

## Key Works
- *[Detransition, Baby](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Detransition,_Baby)* (Random House, 2021) — literary novel about three women (two trans, one cis) navigating pregnancy, relationships, and identity. Published by Random House and widely reviewed; finalist for a Pen/Faulkner award. The first major mainstream literary novel by a trans woman to achieve broad cultural traction.
- Earlier self-published novellas shared in trans communities

## Why Interesting
Peters represents one model of trans cultural production: rooted in community (self-publishing, sharing freely in trans networks), then transitioning to mainstream literary infrastructure. The tension between community-oriented cultural production and mainstream publishing economics is played out in her career. Her work is adjacent to [[McKenzie Wark]]'s autotheory but firmly in the fiction tradition — no theory, just character and story, doing the theoretical work obliquely.
