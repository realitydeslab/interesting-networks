# Frank Pasquale

Law professor and scholar of algorithmic opacity, AI regulation, and platform power. Author of *The Black Box Society* (2015), which coined the "black box" concept for algorithmic systems — taking inputs, producing outputs, with no legible interior. Currently at Brooklyn Law School. One of the foundational figures in algorithmic governance and AI law.

## Domains
- Algorithmic accountability and transparency
- Platform regulation and antitrust
- AI law and governance
- Health informatics and AI in medicine

## Key Works
- [*The Black Box Society*](https://www.hup.harvard.edu/catalog.php?isbn=9780674970847) (Harvard University Press, 2015) — foundational text on opacity in finance and search. Coined "black box" as the governing metaphor for algorithmic power.
- [*New Laws of Robotics*](https://www.hup.harvard.edu/catalog.php?isbn=9780674975224) (Harvard University Press, 2020) — argues AI should complement rather than replace human expertise.

## Why Interesting
Core figure in algorithmic governance. His "black box" concept is the foundational metaphor. *New Laws of Robotics* is increasingly relevant as agentic AI raises questions about human-AI complementarity. Connects to [[Algorithmic Governance]], [[AI Safety]], [[Platform Studies]].
