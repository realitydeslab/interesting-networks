# Emanuele Coccia

Philosopher of plant life and vegetal existence. Teaches at EHESS Paris. Author of *[The Life of Plants: A Metaphysics of Mixture](https://www.politybooks.com/bookdetail?book_slug=the-life-of-plants-a-metaphysics-of-mixture--9781509531622)* (Polity, 2018) — a radical philosophy arguing that plants, not animals, are the paradigmatic form of life, and that all life is fundamentally about the coproduction of environments (milieu). Has become a key reference in more-than-human design, plant studies, and ecology philosophy. Italian-Brazilian background, teaches at EHESS Paris.

## Why Interesting
Coccia's philosophy of plant life is directly relevant to more-than-human design and posthuman theory. His idea that plants are not passive but actively produce their environment challenges human-centric assumptions about agency and cognition. His presence at AIC talking about "How to Hijack Cities" suggests extending his plant-milieu framework to urban environments — cities as living systems that inhabitants transform and that transform them.

For Amber: Coccia offers philosophical grounding for more-than-human design work. His framework connects to active inference, free energy principle, and machine behavior research — the idea that all living things (and perhaps machines) are perpetual milieu-producers. He bridges continental philosophy with ecology in ways that are unusual and productive.
