# Ed Halter

Film and video curator, critic, and co-founder of [Light Industry](https://lightindustry.org), an influential Brooklyn-based venue for artists' film, video, and digital work. Co-editor (with [[Lauren Cornell]]) of *[Mass Effect: Art and the Internet in the Twenty-First Century](https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262029261/mass-effect/)* (MIT Press, 2015) — the canonical anthology on the art-internet intersection.

## Domains
- Artists' film and video
- Internet art and net culture history
- Digital media criticism
- Experimental cinema

## Background
Long-time film critic and programmer working in the space between cinema, video art, and internet culture. Co-founded [Light Industry](https://lightindustry.org) in Brooklyn with Thomas Beard — a small, rigorous screening venue focused on film, video, and digital works that operates between the art world and cinema culture.

## Key Works
- **[*Mass Effect: Art and the Internet in the Twenty-First Century*](https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262029261/mass-effect/)** (co-edited with Lauren Cornell, MIT Press, 2015) — essential anthology.
- **[Light Industry](https://lightindustry.org)** (co-founded with Thomas Beard) — Brooklyn screening venue; one of the most important sites for artists' moving image in NYC.

## Key Communities

## Key Relationships

## Why Interesting
Halter and Cornell's *Mass Effect* is the intellectual genealogy document for anyone working in art+technology today. His curatorial work at Light Industry keeps him embedded in experimental film culture while maintaining connections to the contemporary art world.
