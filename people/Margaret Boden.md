# Margaret Boden

Foundational theorist of computational creativity. Her 1990 book *The Creative Mind: Myths and Mechanisms* established the field's core vocabulary and taxonomy. Professor Emerita at Sussex; cognitive scientist and philosopher of mind.

## Key Works
- [*The Creative Mind: Myths and Mechanisms*](https://openlibrary.org/search?q=the+creative+mind+boden) (1990; 2nd ed. 2004, Routledge) — defines the field; introduces combinational/exploratory/transformational creativity taxonomy
- [*Computer Models of Mind*](https://www.cambridge.org/core/books/computer-models-of-mind/D1D5D11E53BB19CDBD7D6C0E9F9FB2D5) (1988, Cambridge) — foundational cognitive AI text
- ["What is Computational Creativity?"](https://doi.org/10.1007/s10339-009-0344-y) (2009, *Cognitive Computation*) — accessible overview

## Why Interesting
Her three-type taxonomy (combinational / exploratory / transformational) is the most-used framework for evaluating whether AI systems are "genuinely creative." Transformational creativity — changing the rules of the conceptual space — remains elusive for all current AI systems. This framework is essential for Amber's work on agent ethology and open-endedness.
