# Xiao Xiao

Musician, researcher, artist working at the intersection of music, tangible interaction, and memory across time. Created MirrorFugue with [[Hiroshi Ishii]] — a player piano that projects life-sized hands of past performers, creating the illusion of a ghostly co-presence. Core TeleAbsence collaborator at MIT Tangible Media Group. TEDxBoston 2013 speaker.

## Domains
Music + computing, telepresence, memory, illusory presence, tangible interfaces for music.

## Background
Research and artistic work at MIT Media Lab with Hiroshi Ishii. Central figure in the TeleAbsence research — her MirrorFugue piano became the core platform for studying "illusory communication with the deceased" (the ReMirrorFugue 2025 CHI paper).

## Key Works
- **MirrorFugue** — player piano augmented with life-sized projections of pianist's hands/body. Creates illusion of a virtual "reflection" playing the instrument. Presented at TEDxBoston 2013, BIF9.
- **[ReMirrorFugue](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3706598.371332)** (CHI 2025) — study of emotional experience of illusory communication with decade-old piano recordings; 28 participants, including recordings of deceased pianists.

## Why Interesting
Xiao Xiao sits at the rare intersection of music performance, tangible computing, and grief/mortality research. Her MirrorFugue work predates but directly enables Ishii's TeleAbsence vision. The ReMirrorFugue paper is one of the few HCI papers that seriously engages with the emotional reality of connecting to deceased loved ones through technology — exactly the territory Amber's mortality research occupies.
