# Janneke Adema

Researcher and practitioner at the **Centre for Postdigital Cultures** at **Coventry University** (Gary Hall's centre), working at the intersection of **experimental open access publishing**, **postdigital humanities**, and **media theory**. Key figure in the **COPIM project** (Community-led Open Publication Infrastructures for Monographs), which develops non-commercial, collectively-owned infrastructure for OA book publishing.

At **[[Open Humanities Press]]**, edits the **Combinatorial Books: Gathering Flowers** sub-series — which invites authors to creatively rewrite/remix/recombine books from the OHP back catalogue. This is one of the most genuinely experimental publishing projects in contemporary academia: it takes CC licensing seriously and treats the academic book as a living, modifiable artefact.

Her research is fundamentally about rethinking what a scholarly book *is* — not just making existing books available OA, but questioning the book as a fixed, single-authored, linearly organized intellectual product.

## Key Works
- *Living Books: Experiments in the Posthumanities* (MIT Press, 2021) — major monograph; OA; maps experimental book forms in the digital-posthuman moment
- Multiple essays on open access, postdigital publishing, experimental scholarship

## COPIM Connection
COPIM (Community-led Open Publication Infrastructures for Monographs) was a major UK-funded project (2019-2023; follow-up: Open Book Futures 2023-2026) developing open infrastructure for scholar-led OA book publishing. Adema was a key researcher on Work Package 6 (experimental publishing). COPIM outputs include Thoth open metadata, Opening the Future subscription model, and Open Book Collective.

## Why Interesting
Adema is doing the infrastructure work that makes scholar-led publishing sustainable. Where Hall and Zylinska theorize radical OA, Adema builds the workflows, platforms, and legal/technical conditions for it. Her work on combinatorial books is the most concrete attempt to take open licensing seriously as a creative practice rather than just a distribution mechanism. *Living Books* (free OA from MIT Press itself) is a genuine contribution to publishing theory.
