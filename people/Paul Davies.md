# Paul Davies

Theoretical physicist, Regents' Professor at Arizona State University, and founder/director of the [[Beyond Center for Fundamental Concepts in Science]]. One of the world's great scientific communicators, with an extraordinary range spanning from cosmology to cancer biology. A natural philosopher in the historical sense — someone for whom physics and metaphysics are not separate.

## Domains
Astrophysics, cosmology, quantum mechanics, origins of life, astrobiology, cancer biology, philosophy of science, SETI.

## Background
- **BSc** Physics (First Class), University College London, 1967
- **PhD** Physics, UCL, 1970
- Career trajectory: Cambridge → King's College London → University of Newcastle upon Tyne → University of Adelaide → Macquarie University, Sydney → **Arizona State University (2006–present)**
- **Director, Beyond Center for Fundamental Concepts in Science, ASU** — founded 2006
- **Regents' Professor, ASU** — 2012 (highest academic rank at ASU)

## Key Works

### Books (Selected)
- *[The Goldilocks Enigma: Why is the Universe just Right for Life?](https://www.amazon.com/Goldilocks-Enigma-Universe-Just-Right/dp/0547053584)* (2006) — The fine-tuning problem and the multiverse
- *[The Eerie Silence: Are We Alone in the Universe?](https://www.amazon.com/Eerie-Silence-Are-Alone-Universe/dp/1846141427)* (2010) — 50 years of SETI and the Fermi paradox
- *[The Demon in the Machine: How Hidden Webs of Information are Solving the Mystery of Life](https://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B07YRL1GK6/)* (2019) — **Most important for Amber.** Information as fundamental to biology; quantum biology; cancer. Argues for a "third revolution" in biology where information joins energy as a fundamental concept.
- *[What's Eating the Universe? And Other Cosmic Questions](https://press.uchicago.edu/ucp/books/book/chicago/W/bo126163512.html)* (2021) — Cosmic mysteries
- *[Uncovering Einstein's New Universe](https://cosmos.asu.edu/publication/uncovering-einsteins-new-universe)* (2022) — Gravitational waves
- *Quantum 2.0: The Past, Present, and Future of Quantum Physics* (Nov 2025, upcoming)

### Key Papers

## Why Interesting for Amber
Davies is one of the great synthesizers — someone who sees how quantum physics, information theory, biology, and cosmology all point to the same deep questions about existence. His framing of biology as fundamentally about *information* (not just chemistry) is directly relevant to how we think about AI, agency, and life. *The Demon in the Machine* is required reading for anyone working at the intersection of information, life, and intelligence. He's also a model of how to do science publicly — his books make foundational questions accessible without dumbing them down.
