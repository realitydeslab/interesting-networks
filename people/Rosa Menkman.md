# Rosa Menkman

Dutch artist and researcher. Pioneer of **glitch art** and founder of **resolution studies** — a framework for understanding the political and aesthetic stakes of image standards, compression artifacts, and the hidden infrastructures that shape what images look like.

Most known for *A Vernacular of File Formats* and the *Glitch Studies Manifesto*. Her book *Beyond Resolution* (2020) maps the concept of resolution as a site of cultural and political negotiation — who sets the standards, who gets seen clearly, who gets pixelated.

Guest lecturer at **Saint Interface Day 2025** (Interface Cultures, December 5, 2025, KAPU Linz). Talk: "Beyond Resolution."

**Connections:** [[Interface Cultures Lab]] · Ars Electronica · transmediale (regular presence)

**Why interesting for Amber:** Glitch as a philosophy of failure and exposure — systems revealing their own seams. Natural overlap with machine behavior, agent ethology, and the question of what AI sees vs. what it renders invisible.
