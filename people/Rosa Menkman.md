# Rosa Menkman

Dutch artist, theorist, and curator. Pioneer of **glitch art** and founder of **resolution studies** — a critical framework that interrogates image standards, compression artifacts, and the hidden infrastructures that determine what can and cannot be seen. Author of the canonical *Glitch Studies Manifesto* (2011) and *Beyond Resolution* (2020). Winner of the **2019 Collide International Barcelona Award** (Arts at CERN). Her work doesn't just aestheticize failure — it uses glitch as forensic evidence of encoded political compromises in media systems.

## Background

Born 1983 in Arnhem, Netherlands. Studied at the University of Amsterdam. Her practice emerged from a deep technical engagement with video formats, signal processing, feedback loops, and compression artifacts — starting with *Acousmatic Videoscapes* (2007–) and crystallizing in *A Vernacular of File Formats* (2010), which achieved cult status in glitch/net art communities.

The intellectual core of her work is the **Angel of History** — borrowed from Paul Klee's *Angelus Novus* (1920) and Walter Benjamin's 1940 conceptualization — as a protagonist navigating a landscape of obsolete technology, upgrade cycles, and resolution standards. This mythological framework allows her to analyze technical infrastructure through storytelling, art, and speculative theory simultaneously.

Her trajectory: artist-researcher → manifesto writer → festival organizer (GLI.TC/H) → gallery exhibitor (Transfer Gallery) → institutional professor (Kunsthochschule Kassel, HEAD Geneve) → CERN-residency researcher → EPFL resident. She has consistently operated at the intersection of technical rigor and critical theory.

She runs the **i.R.D. (institutions for Resolution Disputes)** as both a publishing imprint and a conceptual institution — publishing her books independently and building a framework for critical engagement with image standards.

Funding: **Mondriaan Fund** basis stipend 2018–2021, renewed 2023–2026. **Stimuleringsfonds.nl** (2018, for website). She practices what she calls **"Copy \<it\> Right"**: all her work and syllabi remain freely online, even after institutional acquisition — a clear ethical stance on knowledge commons.

## Key Ideas

### Resolution as Politics
Resolution is not a neutral technical setting — it is a **regime of rendering**, a site of power. Every standard encodes trade-offs: who is visible, who is legible, who is recognized. The famous example: Kodak's original "Shirley Card" calibrated film exposure for white skin tones, embedding racial bias into the very standard that defined what "correct" skin rendering looked like. Standards enforce hierarchy.

### Glitch as Forensic Surface
Glitches are not mere noise — they are **trace evidence**. When an image breaks, it reveals otherwise invisible operations of compression, encoding, and transcoding. Every artifact is a confession about the pipeline that produced it. This reframes "failure" as disclosure.

### The Penrose Staircase of Progress
Technological upgrade cycles are not linear improvement — they are **paradoxical ascent**: each new standard solves some problems while creating new constraints and silencing alternative possibilities. The metaphor of the Penrose Staircase to Nowhere (progress that keeps climbing but never arrives) runs throughout her work.

### Im/Possible Images
Post-CERN: a research programme into images that are impossible to create — due to physics, cost, technology, or decision. The question "what image would you create with no limits?" becomes a way to probe what current standards exclude. Different categories of impossibility: images rendered impossible by technology, by physics, by ideology, by forgotten standards.

## Key Works

- **[*Acousmatic Videoscapes*](https://beyondresolution.info/Videoscapes)** (2007–) — early experimental video/audio work. Collaborations with musician Extraboy. The origin of her practice.

- **[*A Vernacular of File Formats*](https://www.stedelijk.nl/nl/collectie/100175-rosa-menkman-a-vernacular-of-file-formats)** (2010) — catalog of visual artifacts from different compression formats (JPEG, MP3, etc.), presented as a language guide. "Cult status." Acquired by **Stedelijk Museum Amsterdam** and **MOTI Breda** in 2016 (commissioned by Karen Archey for Stedelijk BASE collection). PDF freely available.

- **[*Glitch Studies Manifesto*](http://networkcultures.org/wpmu/portal/publications/inc-readers/video-vortex-ii/)** (2011) — published in *Video Vortex Reader II* (Institute of Network Cultures). Named "best practice" by Virtueel Platform, then sector institute for e-culture in the Netherlands. The foundational text of glitch art theory.

- **[*The Glitch Moment(um)*](http://networkcultures.org/blog/publication/no-04-the-glitch-momentum-rosa-menkman/)** (2011) — INC Network Notebook #04. Uses information theory to understand glitch as genre. ISBN 978-90-816021-6-7. Edited by Geert Lovink and Sabine Niederer. Free PDF at INC.

- **[*The Collapse of PAL*](https://beyondresolution.info/Collapse-of-PAL)** (2010/2011) — mourning the end of the PAL analogue television standard. First performed as live AV on Danish national TV; curated by Kristoffer Gansing and Linda Hilfling Ritasdatter for TV-TV. Rendered version produced at LabMIS São Paulo residency (2011) thanks to IMPAKT (NL). Live performance at Nova festival in Brazil with Defi, Optical Machines, B. Fleischmann; commissioned by David Quiles Guilló / Nova Rojo.

- **[*Un/Resolved*](https://beyondresolution.info/unresolved)** (2016) — 84-metre-long data file encoded pixel-by-pixel and painted on canvas, partially wrapped. Inspired by Beflix's 29 Parallel Stripes. The material realization of a key theoretical insight: digital resolution is a process across multiple axes (space, time, depth, spectrum), not a fixed property.

- **[*DCT:SYPHONING. The 64th Interval*](https://beyondresolution.info/DCT-SYPHONING-1)** (2015–) — first iteration commissioned by **The Photographers' Gallery** London (2015); exhibited at Schafhof Munich (Dec 2015); released as VR in **DiMoDA 2.0** (2016, with Alfredo Salazar-Caro and William Robertson); developed during Oregon Story Board residency (2016–2017) and Schloss Solitude residency. A narrative drama about DCT (Discrete Cosine Transform) algorithms learning to transcode across compression languages.

- **[*i.R.D. (institutions for Resolution Disputes)*](https://beyondresolution.info/Institutions_of_Resolution_Disputes)** (2015–) — launched at **Transfer Gallery** NYC. Operates as a critical institution, publishing imprint, and ongoing conceptual project. First prize at **Crypto Design Challenge**, Museum of the Image (MOTI) Breda, October 2015.

- **[*Behind White Shadows / Pique Nique Pour les Inconnues*](https://beyondresolution.info/0010-GENEALOGY)** (2017–2020) — research into racial bias in colour calibration standards (Kodak "Shirley Card" genealogy). Research undertaken during 2019 JMAF residency in Tokyo. Exposes how white skin-tone dominance in calibration has encoded systemic racism into image processing standards.

- **[*De/Calibration Army*](https://beyondresolution.info/DECALIBRATION-ARMY)** (2019–) — 365 Perfect De/Calibration Army, a daily practice of producing de/calibration images, building an army of forgotten/alternative calibration figures.

- **[*Beyond Resolution*](https://beyondresolution.info/Beyond-Resolution-1)** (2020) — book, self-published by i.R.D. Her major theoretical statement. Describes how standardization of resolutions promotes efficiency while obscuring alternative rendering possibilities. Full framework of resolution studies.

- **[*Whiteout*](https://beyondresolution.info/WHITEOUT)** (2020, 15 min video) — personal narrative about a mountain hike in a snowstorm: sensory deprivation as a way to understand resolution's role in perception.

- **[*BLOB of Im/Possible Images*](https://beyondresolution.info/IMPOSSIBLE)** (2021) — virtual exhibition in New Art City. Collection of "impossible images" gathered from CERN scientists. Commissioned by HEK Basel (HEK Net Works). The physical version: *Latent Space of Im/Possible Images* at Lothringer 13 Halle, Munich.

- **[*Im/Possible Images Reader*](https://beyondresolution.info/IMPOSSIBLE)** (2022) — published by i.R.D. & Lothringer 13 Halle, with support from V2_ Lab. Contributors include Alan Warburton, UCNV, Susan Schuppli, Sasha Engelmann & Sophie Dyer, Daniel Temkin, Memo Akten, Fabian Heller. HQ (330MB) and LQ (57.6MB) editions.

- **[*Refractions*](https://beyondresolution.info/REFRACTIONS)** (2022–) — lightbox and video installation. The Angel of History meets a Cyclops-god; uses actual CyCLOPS.cy satellite corner reflector network in Cyprus as artistic source material. Developed during **EMAP residency at NeMe**, Limassol Cyprus (2022). Soundtrack by Debit. Reviewed in de Witte RAAF #233.
  - Collaboration with **Kimchi and Chips**: *Cyclops Retina* (2023) — commissioned for Thin Air, The Beams, London. Millions of beams of light merged in haze to create floating graphics.

- **[*A Spectrum of Lost and Unnamed Colours*](https://beyondresolution.info/SPECTRUM)** (2024) — mixed-media installation: sigils, rainbow generator, video. Commissioned by **EPFL CDH Artist in Residence Program 2023** in partnership with EPFL Center for Imaging (Edward Andò) and CLIMACT Center for Climate Impact and Action. Sound by Debit. Explores "chromatic antiques," AI misrepresentation of rainbows, lost gamuts, cultural colour knowledge erosion.

- **[*IM/POSSIBLE RAINBOWS*](https://beyondresolution.info/SPECTRUM)** (2025) — 30-screen installation for NXT Amsterdam ("Still Processing").

## Venue Choice Analysis

Rosa's circuit reveals a distinctive European media art axis, with careful incursions into science institutions and a strong independent streak:

**Core circuit (European critical media art):**
- Transmediale (Berlin) → Ars Electronica (Linz) → iMAL (Brussels) → WRO (Wrocław) → RIXC (Riga) → HEK (Basel) → Schafhof (Munich) → Lothringer 13 Halle (Munich)
- This is the established circuit for serious European media art — not the commercial gallery world.

**Science-art institutions (expanding her frame):**
- Arts at CERN (Geneva) → EPFL CDH (Lausanne) → Fabra i Coats (Barcelona science hub) → RMIT (Melbourne)
- She consistently moves toward science institutions to test ideas about resolution with actual scientists, not just art audiences.

**Peripheral/experimental nodes:**
- NeMe (Limassol, Cyprus) — EMAP residency, Eastern Mediterranean
- The Beams (London) — with Kimchi and Chips
- The Photographers' Gallery (London) — not a media art space, deliberate crossover
- Transfer Gallery (NYC) — her New York anchor, experimental digital art space

**Teaching institutions (Europe):**
- Kunsthochschule Kassel → HEAD Geneve — both art schools in German-speaking Europe, both with experimental/critical media programmes

She deliberately **avoids the commercial gallery circuit** and **big-ticket art fairs**, instead choosing venues that engage seriously with her ideas. The pattern is: develop work in residencies, test it with scientists, show it in critical media venues, teach it in art schools, publish it independently.

## Why Interesting for Amber

**Direct resonance with machine behavior / agent ethology:**
Menkman's core insight — that glitches are forensic evidence of what machines actually do, not what they're supposed to do — is essentially a practice of machine behavior study. She watches what image processing pipelines *do* when they fail, and learns from that. This is the same impulse as studying AI systems observationally. Her work could directly inform how Amber thinks about machine-readable vs. human-readable image artifacts in AI systems.

**Resolution as protocol critique:**
Her "Resolution Studies" framework is a **protocol theory** (in the Galloway sense) applied to images. Standards, defaults, and compression regimes as political infrastructure — this connects directly to Amber's interest in Critical Computing and the power structures embedded in technical systems.

**The racial bias angle (Behind White Shadows):**
The Shirley Card genealogy — how white skin tone became the default calibration standard — is a precise, technically grounded example of how infrastructure encodes ideology. This is the kind of work that could appear at FAccT or alongside Amber's more-than-human design concerns.

**Methodology model: forensic aesthetics:**
Treating glitch artifacts as **material evidence** — each compression artifact as a trace of the machine's operations — is a powerful methodology for anyone studying machine behavior. Menkman demonstrates how to read technical artifacts hermeneutically.

**Im/Possible Images as speculative practice:**
The "what image would you create with no limits?" question is a generative speculative research method — essentially Speculative Design applied to scientific imaging. This connects to Amber's interest in design fiction and speculative futures.

**Worldbuilding through theory:**
The Angel of History framework — treating a Walter Benjamin concept as an ongoing artistic protagonist who moves through the history of image technology — is a practice-based approach to media archaeology that bridges rigorous theory and narrative art. Amber's research on machine death and machine consciousness runs parallel tracks.

**The Copy \<it\> Right ethic:**
Her commitment to making all work, syllabi, and research freely available even after institutional acquisition — and her independent publishing through i.R.D. — models a different relationship between art practice and institutional knowledge economy.

**CERN connection:**
The Collide residency opened a direct line to high-energy physics and the extreme edge of scientific imaging. CERN scientists' "impossible images" reveal the limits of human knowledge at a fundamental level — the same edge-of-the-possible territory that interests Amber.
