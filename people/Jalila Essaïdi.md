# Jalila Essaïdi

Dutch artist-entrepreneur, born 1980, Eindhoven. Founder of [[BioArt Laboratories]] and CEO of biotech company Inspidere B.V. Specialized in bio-based materials and biological arts (bio-art). Pioneer of bioart-as-entrepreneurship — she treats living material as both artistic medium and commercial substrate.

## Key Projects
- **2.6g 329m/s / Bulletproof Skin** (2010) — Human skin bioengineered with spider silk from GMOs to stop a slow-speed bullet. Won the 2010 BAD Award. Covered by AP, Reuters, CNN, BBC. Exhibited at V&A London, State Tretyakov Gallery Moscow, NOMA New Orleans, Power Station of Art Shanghai.
- **Mestic®** — Method to transform cow manure into cellulose-derivatives. Transforms the Dutch manure excess problem into a local bio-based economy (fashion, plastics). Won H&M Global Change Award 2017, Chivas Venture Award 2018 (social entrepreneurship), Clim@ competition of Green for Growth Fund.

## Why Interesting
- The anchor figure for European bioart infrastructure — founded [[BioArt Laboratories]] to give other artists access to what she built for herself
- Bulletproof Skin is a canonical more-than-human design provocation: the cyborg body as biotechnology
- Mestic: circular economy through biological transformation — ecological design thinking
- DDW 2019 Ambassador for Dutch Design Week — recognized as a cultural/creative leader for Eindhoven region
