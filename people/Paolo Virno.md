# Paolo Virno

Italian philosopher and post-operaismo theorist. Key work: *Grammar of the Multitude* (2004) — a contemporaneous parallel to [[Michael Hardt]] and [[Antonio Negri]]'s *Multitude*, drawing on Aristotle, linguistics, and Marx to theorize the multitude as a political-linguistic form. Also known for his theorization of the "general intellect" (from Marx's *Grundrisse*) as the central productive force in post-Fordist capitalism.

## Why Interesting

Virno's account of the **multitude** and the **general intellect** is arguably more linguistically grounded and less voluntarist than Hardt/Negri's. His work is directly relevant to understanding collective intelligence, language models as "general intellect," and the political economy of cognitive capitalism. *A Grammar of the Multitude* is widely cited in platform/digital labor studies.

## Key Works
- [*A Grammar of the Multitude*](https://semiotexte.com/?p=510) (Semiotext(e), 2004, trans. Isabella Bertoletti, James Cascaito, Andrea Casson) — the key text. Develops multitude as a political category through Aristotle's categories of intellect, language, and work.
- *Remembrance of the Present: For a Critique of the Present Time* (2015)
- Essays on the general intellect, multitude, bios, potentiality

## Key Relationships
- [[Michael Hardt]] — parallel theorist; Hardt edited *Radical Thought in Italy* anthology which included Virno's work
- [[Antonio Negri]] — intellectual colleague in post-operaismo
- [[Maurizio Lazzarato]] — colleague in autonomist tradition
