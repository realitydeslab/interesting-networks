# Clare Birchall

Professor of Contemporary Culture at **King's College London**. Works on secrecy, radical transparency, surveillance, digital culture. Long-time collaborator of Gary Hall — co-edited *New Cultural Studies: Adventures in Theory* (2006), co-edited the Liquid Books series and Culture Machine Liquid Books series, co-produced Liquid Theory TV, co-authored multiple articles. One of the key figures in UK critical media theory alongside Hall.

## Domains
- Secrecy and transparency as political categories
- Radical (state and corporate) openness — what it conceals
- Surveillance studies, digital governance
- Cultural studies theory; Derrida-inflected
- Open access and academic publishing reform

## Key Works
- *Shareveillance: The Dangers of Openly Sharing and Covertly Collecting Data* (Minnesota UP, 2017) — key work on the politics of sharing/surveillance
- *New Cultural Studies: Adventures in Theory* (Edinburgh UP, 2006) — co-edited with Gary Hall; defining anthology for the field
- Various articles on secrecy, transparency, and the state in *Cultural Studies*, *Theory, Culture & Society*
