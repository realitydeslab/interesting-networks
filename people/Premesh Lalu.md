# Premesh Lalu

South African historian and theorist. Director of the [Center for Humanities Research (CHR)](https://www.uwc.ac.za/) at the University of the Western Cape (UWC) — one of the leading humanities research centres in Africa. Works on postcolonial and decolonial theory, the philosophy of history, archives and memory, and the politics of knowledge in South Africa and the Global South. Key figure in the "decolonising the university" debates.

## Background
Based at [University of the Western Cape](https://www.uwc.ac.za/), one of the historically Black universities in South Africa (founded in the apartheid era as a "coloured" university, now a major research institution). His work addresses how the colonial archive shapes present knowledge formations and how humanistic research can be decolonized.

## Key Works
- *The Deaths of Hintsa: Postapartheid South Africa and the Shape of Recurring Pasts* (HSRC Press, 2009) — on memory, the colonial archive, and South African history
- "Unseating Mastery: The University and the Promise of the New" (with Erich Hörl, *Theory, Culture & Society*, 41(7-8), 2024) — intersection of disruption theory and university decolonization

The Lalu-Hörl collaboration is unusual and productive: it brings together German media/technology philosophy and South African postcolonial theory around questions of the university, knowledge, and disruption.
