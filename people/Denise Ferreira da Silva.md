# Denise Ferreira da Silva

Brazilian philosopher, relational artist, and filmmaker. Author of *Toward a Global Idea of Race* — the foundational text arguing that race is not incidental to but constitutive of post-Enlightenment thought. Bridges critical race theory, continental philosophy, quantum physics, and Black feminist poetics through books, films, and collective art practices.

## Background
Born in the Morro do Pasmado (Botafogo neighborhood), grew up in Vila Aliança (Bangu), Rio de Janeiro. Encountered liberation theology at her church in Vila Aliança. At 22, worked on the successful 1986 campaign of [Benedita da Silva](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Benedita_da_Silva) — the first Black woman elected to Brazil's national Congress. This brought her into the Black peoples' and Black women's movements.

**Academic formation:** BA in Sociology from [Federal University of Rio de Janeiro (UFRJ)](https://ufrj.br/), 1985. MA in Sociology and Anthropology from UFRJ, 1991. Doctorate from [University of Pittsburgh](https://www.pitt.edu/), 1999 — where she encountered Black feminist theory through directed reading with Dr. Brenda Berrian in Africana Studies, including Octavia E. Butler's science fiction that would shape her thinking for decades.

**Career arc:**
- **1999–2010:** Associate Professor, University of California, San Diego — Ethnic Studies Department. Directed the Latin American Studies Program, Brazilian Studies, and the Centre for Iberian and Latin American Studies. Also visiting associate professor at USC (2006–2007).
- **~2010–2015:** Inaugural Chair in Ethics, School of Business and Management, Centre for Ethics and Politics, [Queen Mary University of London](https://www.qmul.ac.uk/) — also director of the centre.
- **2015–2023:** Professor and Director, [Institute for Gender, Race, Sexuality and Social Justice (GRSJ)](https://grsj.arts.ubc.ca/), University of British Columbia, Vancouver.
- **2023–present:** International Chair in Contemporary Philosophy, Department of Philosophy, [University of Paris 8 Vincennes-Saint-Denis](https://www.univ-paris8.fr/) (from 2023).
- **Current:** Professor, [New York University](https://www.nyu.edu/).
- **Additional positions:** Visiting Professor of Law, Birkbeck, University of London; Adjunct Professor of Fine Arts, Monash University, Melbourne; Visiting Professor at U Pennsylvania, NYU, U Toronto, University of São Paulo, University of Copenhagen, European Graduate School.

She started practicing astrology in secondary school and tarot later — decades of study in divinatory arts that now formally inform her philosophical method alongside quantum physics.

## Key Works

### Books
- [*Toward a Global Idea of Race*](https://www.upress.umn.edu/book-division/books/toward-a-global-idea-of-race) (University of Minnesota Press, 2007) — The central monograph. Argues that race is not a scientific error or ideological residue, but constitutive of modern thought itself: the **transparency thesis** (the self-determining European subject defined against an **affectable** Racial Other). Raises the central question: "Why, after more than five hundred years of violence perpetrated by Europeans against people of color, is there no ethical outrage?"
- [*Race, Empire, and the Crisis of the Subprime*](https://jhupbooks.press.jhu.edu/title/race-empire-and-crisis-subprime) (Johns Hopkins University Press, 2013) — Co-edited with Paula Chakravartty. Special issue of *American Quarterly* expanded into book form; analyzes the 2008 financial crisis through race and coloniality.
- [*A Dívida Impagável*](https://www.cobogo.com.br/denise-ferreira-da-silva) (Oficina da Imaginação Política / Living Commons, 2019) — Portuguese-language version, Brazil.
- [*Unpayable Debt*](https://www.sternberg-press.com/product/unpayable-debt/) (Sternberg Press / MIT Press, 2022) — Full theoretical and visual elaboration of the "unpayable debt" concept. Uses Octavia E. Butler's *Kindred*, quantum physics, and the Zapatistas to analyze what debt one owes that cannot be computed within existing political-economic frames. **Winner: Frantz Fanon Award for Outstanding Book in Caribbean Thought, 2024.**
- [*Homo Modernus*](https://www.cobogo.com.br/denise-ferreira-da-silva) (Cobogó, 2022) — Brazilian Portuguese collection/monograph published with Brazilian publisher Cobogó.

### Key Essays & Articles
- ["Fractal Thinking"](https://accessions.org/article2/fractal-thinking/) (*aCCeSsions*, vol. 2, 2016) — Proposes "poethical" or "fractal thinking" as a 4-dimensional alternative to linear modern thought; critiques Badiou and Žižek's handling of raciality and the refugee crisis. Introduces the **plenum ontology** drawing on Leibniz and quantum physics.
- "No-Bodies: Law, Raciality, Violence" (*Griffith Law Review* 18:2, 2009)
- "Reading the Dead" — on the Zapatistas and quantum physics, later developed into *Unpayable Debt*
- "Black Feminist Poethics: The Quest(ion) of Blackness Towards the End of the World" — identifies ontological pillars she finds shattered by Octavia Butler's characters
- Articles in: *Social Text*, *Theory, Culture & Society*, *Social Identities*, *PhiloSOPHIA: A Journal of Continental Feminism*, *The Black Scholar*, *Theory & Event*
- Publications for Liverpool Biennial 2016, São Paulo Biennial 2016, Venice Biennale 2017, Berlin Biennial 2018, Singapore Biennale 2022

## Art Practice

### Films — with Arjuna Neuman
A decade-long collaboration with filmmaker [[Arjuna Neuman]]. Each film takes one of the classical four elements as its subject/method:
- [*Serpent Rain*](https://www.arjunaneuman.com/serpent-rain) (2016, 30 min) — **Element: Water.** Premiered widely. Asks the film's closing question: "What would become of the human being if expressed through the elements?" Central to the Sensing Salon's Lisbon iteration.
- [*4 Waters: Deep Implicancy*](https://www.arjunaneuman.com/4-waters) (2018/2019) — **Element: Water (continued).** Shown at Gallery TPW Toronto (2019), gallery venues.
- [*Soot Breath // Corpus Infinitum*](https://www.arjunaneuman.com/soot-breath) (2020) — **Element: Earth.** Filmed in Death Valley. Explores texture over movement. Shown at MACBA Barcelona, Belkin Art Gallery UBC.
- *Air film* (in progress as of 2024) — **Element: Air.** Includes footage from lithium mines in Chile (Atacama), engaging mining, extraction, and energy as ecological-colonial violence.

### Relational Art — with Valentina Desideri
- **[Sensing Salon](https://www.thesensingsalon.org/)** — collaborative studio practice conceived with [[Valentina Desideri]] that expands art beyond objects to include healing arts. Uses tarot, astrology, reiki, and Political Therapy (Desideri's own practice) to facilitate "deeply implicated existence." Held at: Vancouver, Lisbon (Hangar/Kunsthalle Lissabon, via KADIST), Hamburg, Paris, London, Saint Erme, and other locations.
- **Poethical Readings** — performance/lecture format drawing from Joan Retallack's concept of "poethics." Works through philosophical texts and practices of reading that bypass representational logic.

## Research Taste Analysis

### Conceptual Strategy
**Framework theft from physics.** Da Silva's most distinctive move is treating quantum physics (particularly the ontology of the plenum — everything as entangled field, dark matter acting without being identified as cause) as genuine philosophical resource, not metaphor. This allows her to escape the Kantian categories (space and time as conditions of knowledge) that she identifies as the foundation of racial exclusion. The plenum doesn't just gesture at entanglement — it enables specific philosophical claims about simultaneity, compositionality, and the four-dimensionality of events that directly displace the transparency/affectability binary. This is *rigorous use of physics as philosophy*, not science-fiction-style loose borrowing.

**Genealogical inversion.** Her second major move: show that what modernity claimed to expel (magic, astrology, alchemy) was actually practiced by its founding figures (Bacon, Galileo, Newton). This lets her argue that Western reason's excluded tools actually enable the critique of Western reason — from within, using its own forgotten methods. This is philosophically elegant: she doesn't need to appeal to "traditional knowledge" as an outside critique; the inside is already contaminated with other ways of knowing.

**The two-word concept.** Her key coinages function as conceptual knives: "transparency thesis," "deep implicancy," "fractal thinking," "unpayable debt," "affectability." Each is a minimal formulation that captures a whole philosophical argument. *Transparency thesis* is particularly clean: it says that the modern subject constitutes itself by claiming self-determination (transparency to itself) and projecting external determination (affectability) onto the Racial Other. This is not description but analysis — it names the mechanism.

### Career Architecture
**Field bridging as permanent method.** Rather than declaring a single discipline, da Silva has traversed Sociology → Ethnic Studies → Critical Legal Theory → Gender/Sexuality/Race Studies → Philosophy. Each move has reframed the same core argument. When she was at QMUL, her work on ethics and law developed the "No-Bodies" legal theory. At UBC, the GRSJ directorship gave her institutional infrastructure for interdisciplinary feminist work. At Paris 8 (philosophy), her quantum-physics ontological moves became a philosophy of mind problem. At NYU, she lands in the most visible institutional position. This is not opportunism; it is a sustained strategy of **positioning the same argument in whatever institutional space is most receptive to it at the moment**.

**Art practice as intellectual laboratory.** The films with Arjuna Neuman operate as genuine experiments — not illustrations of pre-existing philosophy, but situations that generate new theoretical positions. The discovery in *Soot Breath* (that earth-element filmmaking is about texture, not movement) was not anticipated and generated a new theoretical direction. This is the opposite of the common academic move of making art to popularize existing ideas.

**Collaborative work as intellectual insurance.** By working with Desideri, Neuman, Mombaça, Winterling, and others, da Silva has built a collaborative intellectual infrastructure that keeps her thinking alive and unpredictable. Each collaborator brings different disciplinary tools (therapy, cinematography, performance). This is how she avoids the trap of writing increasingly elaborate variations on a core argument.

### Key Moves (chronological)
1. **1986 — Political activation before philosophy.** Working on Benedita da Silva's campaign embedded her in the actual political reality of anti-racist organizing *before* she developed theory. This grounded the philosophical project in lived stakes — her academic work has never been only academic.

2. **1999 — Pittsburgh PhD as Black feminist portal.** The directed reading with Brenda Berrian introduced her to Octavia Butler in the 1990s. She didn't use Butler immediately — she let it incubate for fifteen years. This patience is characteristic: she develops ideas slowly and deploys them when fully formed.

3. **2007 — *Toward a Global Idea of Race* as field declaration.** Rather than "race and something else," da Silva claimed to show race as foundational to all modern thought. This was an audacious philosophical claim. It established her as a thinker making genuine arguments about Western metaphysics, not just identity politics.

4. **2012–2013 — The four elements turn.** Starting work with Valentina Desideri and recognizing that tarot/astrology/alchemy weren't opposed to but suppressed by modern philosophy opened a new research program. The Sensing Salon was the institutional form this took. The films with Neuman followed shortly.

5. **2016–2020 — Film trilogy (Serpent Rain → 4 Waters → Soot Breath).** A sustained artistic research program working through the classical elements. Each film was a genuine experiment: not knowing in advance how to make a film about earth (answer: texture), or what the next element would bring. This is research-by-doing at high quality.

6. **2022 — *Unpayable Debt* as synthesis.** Fifteen years after *Toward a Global Idea of Race*, this book brought together the quantum physics ontology, the debt analysis, Butler's *Kindred*, and the political-economic critique into a single framework. Winning the Frantz Fanon Award 2024 confirmed its reach in the Caribbean/decolonial studies field.

7. **2023 — Paris 8 chair → NYU move.** Positioning herself in philosophy proper (Paris 8) and then the most visible US research university (NYU) signals a move toward mainstream philosophical legitimacy while keeping the art practice alive.

### What Amber Can Learn
- **Let ideas incubate long.** Da Silva read Butler in 1990 and didn't fully deploy it until 2013+. The best theoretical moves often need decades of background processing.
- **Refuse translation between formats.** Don't make art that illustrates your writing; make art that generates genuinely different knowledge. The film practice must be an independent source of ideas.
- **Steal from science rigorously.** Don't use quantum physics as metaphor; use it as genuine ontological resource. If you're going to invoke deep learning or information theory in your art/theory, find the actual philosophical claim that the science enables — not just the analogy.
- **Build collaborative infrastructure.** A lasting intellectual partnership (like Neuman x 10 years, Desideri x decade+) is more generative than one-off collaborations. The long collaboration is how you develop a shared conceptual vocabulary.
- **The 4-element framework as organizing principle** — a way of sustaining a multi-year project with built-in structure (four films, four salons) that feels inevitable in retrospect but is actually a design decision.
