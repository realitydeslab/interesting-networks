# Zhang Peili 张培力

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Upgraded from ⭐⭐ stub. Added biographical detail, key works, institutional context, and Amber connection. Needs full deep research.

"Father of Chinese video art." Pioneer who made China's first video artwork *30x30* (1988), founded the New Media Art department at China Academy of Art (where Amber teaches), and trained the next generation of Chinese media artists including [[Lu Yang]]. One of the most important figures in Chinese contemporary art.

## Background
Born 1957 in Hangzhou, China. Studied at the Zhejiang Academy of Fine Arts (now [[China Academy of Art]]), graduated from the Department of Oil Painting in 1984. In **1986**, with fellow artists Geng Jianyi and Song Li, founded the **Pond Society** — an existentialist artist collective organized around public happenings and interventions in Hangzhou. One of the earliest avant-garde art groups in post-Mao China.

His first video work **30x30** (1988) — showing him continuously smashing a mirror and gluing it back together — is widely considered the first Chinese video artwork. It was screened at the 1988 Huangshan Conference, which directly preceded the landmark China/Avant-Garde exhibition at the National Art Museum of China in 1989.

He transitioned almost completely to video art and began shaping Chinese video art as an institutional practice. In the early 2000s, he founded the **New Media Art Department** at China Academy of Art — the first such department at a major Chinese art institution. This grew into the **School of Intermedia Art**.

Currently lives and works in Hangzhou. Dean of the New Media Department / School of Intermedia Art, China Academy of Art.

## Key Works
- **[*30x30*](https://www.artic.edu/exhibitions/2595/zhang-peili-record-repeat)** (1988) — First Chinese video artwork. Shows Zhang repeatedly smashing and mending a mirror. Screened at Huangshan Conference 1988.
- ***Document on Hygiene No. 3*** (1991) — Prolonged washing of a chicken with soap. Themes of political control and repetition.
- ***Water: Standard Version from Cihai Dictionary*** (1991) — CCTV news anchor reading the dictionary definition of "water" with absolute professional affect. Deadpan institutional critique.
- ***Uncertain Pleasure*** (1996) — Multi-channel video installation showing 10 different views of a man scratching himself.
- ***Actor's Lines*** (2002) — Remixed footage from 1964 state-sanctioned film, transforming patriotic dialogue into implied romance, subverting military hierarchy.
- ***Go Ahead, Go Ahead*** (2004) — Dual-channel parallel projection of American and Chinese war films.
- ***Happiness*** (2006) — Edited Cultural Revolution-era film, crowd applauding a speaker.
- ***Last Words*** (2003)
- ***A Gust of Wind*** (2008)
- ***A Scene in Black and White Unfolded Four Times*** (2007)
