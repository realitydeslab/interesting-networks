# Adam Zaretsky

American Wet-Lab Art Practitioner mixing Ecology, Biotechnology, Non-human Relations, Body Performance, and Gastronomy. Founder of VASTAL (The VivoArts School for Transgenic Aesthetics Ltd.) — a hands-on DIY bioethics lab-as-art-school. Former MIT biology researcher. Works at the radical intersection of transgenic art, animal studies, germline ethics, and performance. Known for staging provocative encounters between the public and living systems (organisms, cells, embryos) to force embodied confrontation with bioethics.

## Background
- Former researcher, **MIT Department of Biology** — this is his scientific foundation; gives him genuine wet-lab credibility.
- PhD (field unconfirmed but likely biological arts or related).
- Faculty at **RPI (Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute)**, Arts department (though current status uncertain — not listed on 2026 faculty page).
- Defined "Vivoarts" as a concept in 2005: *"any artistic production that has a living component embedded in it at the time of its exhibition."*
- Coined the VASTAL pedagogy framework and "Unstill Life Studies (ULS)" — theoretical lectures accompanying hands-on biolab performances.
- Collaborates with/associated with **[[SymbioticA]]** (Oron Catts + Ionat Zurr, University of Western Australia) since at least 2009.
- Based in Woodstock, NY USA area.

## Key Works

### VASTAL / VivoArts School
- **[VASTAL (The VivoArts School for Transgenic Aesthetics Ltd.)](https://inflexions.org/n7_zaretsky.html)** (founded 2009, with [[SymbioticA]]) — public hands-on biotech lab as art school. Runs "Unstill Life Studies (ULS)" lectures alongside practical labs. Core method: give non-biologists actual experience with living organisms to unlock genuine bioethical intuition vs. abstract policy.
- **VASTAL Ethology and Art for Non-Humans: Enrichment Arts Lab** (Nov 17, 2009) — first major public lab at **Theatrum Anatomicum, de Waag, Amsterdam**. Included lectures by Kees van Oers (avian ethologist) and Koen Vanmechelen (The Cosmopolitan Chicken Project). Followed by visit to Vanmechelen's solo show at Muziekgebouw.

### Artworks / Projects
- **Transgenic Orange Pheasant Project** (2008) — delivered formal letter to Prince (now King) **Willem-Alexander of the Netherlands** proposing a "Royal Dutch Transgenic Breeding Facility" at Gorleaus Laboratory, Universiteit Leiden, to breed orange pheasants for the royal hunt. Classic Zaretsky: serious scientific proposal + absurdist performance + political provocation.
- **FIST.SAVE.MOP.BAIT** (full title: *Forced Interspecies Symbiosis Transgenic Solar Animal Vegetable Environmental Microinjection Organismic Personality Behavioral Audio Integrity Test*) — visual artwork/collage; published in *[Yes Naturally](https://www.nai010.com/)* ed. Ine Gevers, Nai010 publishers, den Haague, 2013.
- **"On Mutaphobia"** (2008) — Bio Art Workshop at [UCLA EDA + CNSI](https://cnsi.ucla.edu/), [UCLA ART|SCI Center](https://artsci.ucla.edu/), Los Angeles.
- **DNA ID DECON — Problems in Techno-Ancestry in the 1000 Vaginas Project** (2018) — video critique of genetic ancestry testing industry, created during Ars Bioarctica Residency. [Watch on Vimeo](https://vimeo.com/335690825).
- **"Breeding Sentience" panel** (ISEA2020) — engagement with AI consciousness and selective breeding.
- **"Interpersonal Protective Equipment: The Libidinal Economy of Aseptic Containment"** (2020) — PPE + latex fetishism + COVID containment culture. Presented at [TTT Conference](https://avarts.ionio.gr/ttt/2020/en/presentations/171/), Univ. of Applied Arts Vienna.
- **BioARTCAMPh** (2011) — documented by Jeanette Groenendaal & Zoot Derks.
- **DIY Embryology** (2013) — workshop at Mellon Institute, Carnegie Mellon. Editors: Jonathan (Jae) Minard, Elizabeth Buschmann, Sondra Hart.

### BEAK
- **BEAK (Bioart Ethical Advisory Kommission)** — satirical/serious body Zaretsky created to provide "artistic oversight and ethical assessment for bioart applications." Reviews LESLi (Legal, Ethical, Societal and Libidinal implications). Proposes that human gene editing should require *aesthetic review* by artists and art historians, not just bioethicists.

## For Amber

1. **🌍 Areas to know** — Zaretsky's LESLi framework (Legal, Ethical, Societal, **Libidinal** implications) is a provocative expansion of standard ELSI ethics frameworks. The addition of "libidinal" is the key move — it opens bioethics to desire, aesthetics, and bodily economy. Relevant to Amber's interest in more-than-human design.

2. **🔧 Professional inspiration** — VASTAL's DIY bioethics pedagogy model: hands-on lab experience generates better intuitions than abstract policy discussion. This is Zaretsky's core epistemological claim. Relevant to Amber's design/participatory methods.

3. **🤝 Cooperation** — The BEAK (Bioart Ethical Advisory Kommission) satirical oversight body has a structural parallel to Amber's work on AI agent ethics/machine behavior. Could be an interesting provocation/comparison point in papers about AI oversight.

4. **🏠 Residency** — [Ars Bioarctica](https://www.bioartsociety.fi/projects/ars-bioarctica/) (Bioart Society Finland) — Kilpisjärvi Biological Station in arctic Finland. Genuine art-science residency in extreme environment. Worth applying for.

5. **🌍 Areas to know** — SymbioticA at University of Western Australia has artist residency programs. One of the few proper wet-lab residency programs globally. Relevant to anyone doing art-science intersection.

6. **💡 Career inspiration** — Zaretsky demonstrates how to use *satire and absurdism as rigorous critique*. The Transgenic Orange Pheasant letter to the Dutch king is a real document delivered in real institutional context. This kind of performative critique (serious document, absurd object) is a methodology Amber could adapt for work on AI agency/machine rights.

7. **🔮 Future involvement** — Bioart Society Finland's networks (Erich Berger, Piritta Puhto) connect to broader Scandinavian art-science ecology. With Amber's increasing European connections, worth tracking.

## Why Interesting

Zaretsky is interesting because he takes art-science seriously *as ethics*. His claim that "human germline gene editing IS bioart" is not metaphor — it's a philosophical argument that scientism has been doing bioart without art's ethical/aesthetic oversight, and that artists should therefore have regulatory standing. This is genuinely provocative and philosophically defensible.

The VASTAL model — art school using actual transgenic organisms, DIY labs for the public — is radical in form not just content. It enacts what posthumanism means rather than theorizing it from a distance.

For Amber: Zaretsky works the more-than-human question from biology (not computation). The parallels and differences with Amber's computational more-than-human design are productive friction. Where Zaretsky's non-humans are biological organisms shaped by DNA manipulation, Amber's are computational agents shaped by training and architecture. The ethical frameworks overlap.

The "jazz bioinformatics" concept — improvisation between genetic code and flesh — is a nice aesthetic-philosophical frame for thinking about emergent behavior in biological and computational systems alike.
