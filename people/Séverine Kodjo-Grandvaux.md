# Séverine Kodjo-Grandvaux

French philosopher, journalist, and essayist specializing in African philosophy, aesthetics, and contemporary African cultural politics. Regular participant and contributor at [Les Ateliers de la Pensée de Dakar](https://ateliersdlapensee.com/). Author of *Philosophies africaines* (2013), one of the accessible survey texts of African philosophy in French. Former editor and journalist at *Le Monde Afrique*.

## Key Works
- **[*Philosophies africaines*](https://www.editionspresenceafricaine.com/)** (Présence Africaine Éditions, 2013) — survey of African philosophical traditions, accessible introduction to African thought for French-speaking readers.
- Regular essays and journalism on African thought, culture, and intellectual life.

## Why Interesting (for Amber)
- A bridge figure between academic African philosophy and broader publics — she writes for Le Monde, not only for academic journals.
- Her survey work *Philosophies africaines* is potentially a useful shortcut to understanding the landscape of African thought that underpins the Ateliers.
- Connected to the Ateliers core circle; has direct access to Mbembe and Sarr's networks.
