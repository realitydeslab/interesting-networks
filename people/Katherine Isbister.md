# Katherine Isbister

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Stub created. General Chair [[Halfway to the Future]] 2024. Presented "Toward 'Suprahuman' Technology" manifesto at HTTF 2019.

Professor of Computational Media at UC Santa Cruz. Researcher in games, social interaction, wearables, and augmented collective experience. Director of the Center for Research in Digital Play. Presented "Toward 'Suprahuman' Technology: A Manifesto in Support of Computational Augmentation of Mutual Physical Presence and Collective Action" at HTTF 2019 — a sign of long engagement with the HTTF community. Served as General Chair for HTTF 2024 as local host (UCSC Santa Cruz).

## Why Interesting
Isbister's "Suprahuman" framing — computing that augments collective human action rather than individualizing it — resonates with Amber's more-than-human, post-individual design stance. Her work on social presence and wearable social tech is relevant to Amber's interest in multispecies/collective computing. Isbister is also the institutional gateway for future HTTF editions.
