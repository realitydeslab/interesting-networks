# Charles Hirschkind

Anthropologist at UC Berkeley; known for *The Ethical Soundscape* (2006) on Islamic cassette sermon culture in Cairo. Major figure in media/Islam/secularism studies. Co-edited Social Text special issue "Media and the Political Forms of Religion" (2008) with [[Brian Larkin]].

## Domains
- Media and religion (Islamic media, cassette culture)
- Secularism and religious publics
- Egypt, Arab world
- Sensory anthropology / soundscapes

## Key Works
- *The Ethical Soundscape: Cassette Sermons and Islamic Counterpublics* (Columbia UP, 2006) — how Islamic cassette sermons build ethical subjectivity and religious publics
- *A Feeling for the Political: Religion, Affect, and the Arab Spring* (2021)

## Key Relationships
- [[Brian Larkin]] — co-edited Social Text special issue

## Why Interesting
His work on cassette culture as infrastructure for religious community is a direct parallel to Larkin's loudspeaker work — both examining how media creates religious publics. The "ethical soundscape" concept is transferable to any sonic ambient environment.
