# Charles Hirschkind

Anthropologist at UC Berkeley. Author of *The Ethical Soundscape: Cassette Sermons and Islamic Counterpublics* (Columbia UP, 2006) — on how Islamic cassette sermon networks in Egypt constitute an ethical and sensory infrastructure. Co-edited *Social Text* 26(3) (2008) on "Media and the Political Forms of Religion" with [[Brian Larkin]]. Key figure in media anthropology, sensory studies, and Islam/media.

## Domains
- Media and religion (Islam, Egypt, Middle East)
- Sensory anthropology — sound, listening, the body
- Islamic ethics and media
- Counterpublics and political Islam

## Why Interesting
Hirschkind's "ethical soundscape" concept shows how media infrastructure (cassette tapes) becomes a vehicle for ethical formation, not just information transmission. His work parallels Larkin's on Nigeria but in the Egyptian context — together they form a powerful comparative frame for media/religion/infrastructure in the Global South.
