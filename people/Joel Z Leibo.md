# Joel Z Leibo

Senior staff research scientist at Google DeepMind, visiting professor at King's College London. Architect of generative social simulation and leading theorist of AI cooperation. Created Concordia (LLM-based social simulation platform) and Melting Pot (multi-agent RL benchmark). Bridges computational neuroscience, cultural evolution, and institutional economics to understand cooperation at scale.

## Key Works

### Platforms & Infrastructure
- **[Concordia](https://arxiv.org/abs/2312.03664)** (2023) — Generative agent-based modeling platform using LLMs. Works like tabletop RPGs where agents interact in natural language. The foundation for "theory of appropriateness." Open source: [GitHub](https://github.com/google-deepmind/concordia).
- **[Melting Pot](https://arxiv.org/abs/2107.06857)** (2021) — Suite of 50+ multi-agent RL environments testing cooperation, competition, deception. Over 256 test scenarios for generalization. Standard benchmark for multi-agent AI. [GitHub](https://github.com/google-deepmind/meltingpot).
- **[Melting Pot 2.0](https://arxiv.org/abs/2211.13746)** (2022) — Extended version with more complex social scenarios.

### Theoretical Contributions
- **[A theory of appropriateness with applications to generative artificial intelligence](https://arxiv.org/abs/2412.19010)** (2024) — Core theory for context-sensitive AI behavior. How agents determine what's appropriate in different situations. [Blog post](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/S8KYwtg5vZqah4SHF/societal-and-technological-progress-as-sewing-an-ever?utm_campaign=post_share&utm_source=link).
- **[Machine Culture](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41562-023-01742-2)** (2023) — Nature Human Behavior. How machines mediate and generate culture. Co-authored with cultural evolution researchers.
- **[A social path to human-like artificial intelligence](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2405.15815)** (2023) — Nature Machine Intelligence. Argues that social interaction is key to intelligence.
- **[Multi-Agent Risks from Advanced AI](https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.14143)** (2025) — Taxonomy of risks from AI agent interactions.

### Empirical Studies

- **[Multi-agent Reinforcement Learning in Sequential Social Dilemmas](https://arxiv.org/pdf/1702.03037.pdf)** (2017) — AAMAS. Foundational paper on cooperation in RL.
- **[Spurious normativity enhances learning of compliance and enforcement behavior in artificial agents](https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.2106028118)** (2022) — PNAS. How agents learn social norms.
