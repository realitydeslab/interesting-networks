# Jiabao Li 李佳宝

Artist, designer, technologist, professor. Creates multispecies speculative installations that ask what it's like to be a bat, squid, cricket, octopus, or plankton. Bridges rigorous science (marine biology labs, proteomics, bioprinting) with deeply weird, deeply empathetic design. Runs the **[Ecocentric Future Lab](https://www.ecocentricfuture.com/about)** at UT Austin. Former Apple prototyping designer (Vision Pro, LiDAR, ECG Watch, Mental Health). Two TED talks. Published at CHI, SIGGRAPH, IEEE VR, ISEA, HttF.

## Key Works

### Nocturnal Fugue + EchoVision (2024-2025)
With Matt McCorkle and Botao Hu. Inspired by Thomas Nagel's "What Is it Like to Be a Bat?" — immersive installation using AI-discovered bat social calls, spatial sound, projection mapping, haptic transducers. EchoVision: mixed reality bat echolocation simulation using [HoloKit](https://holokit.io/) headset. Published at [CHI '25](https://doi.org/10.1145/3706599.3721192), [IEEE VR '25](https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/10972681), [SIGGRAPH Asia '24 Art Papers](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3680530.3695460), [HttF](https://doi.org/10.1145/3686169.3686212), [UbiComp '24](https://doi.org/10.1145/3675094.3681951), IEEE VIS Art Program '24. Appeared on [Xinhua News](https://english.news.cn/20240426/3e936fb4fbe840f3a327d815492892f0/c.html), [Forbes](https://www.forbes.com/sites/charliefink/2025/03/15/xr-at-sxsw-2025-expanding-the-boundaries-of-immersion/).

### CryoScapes (2025-2026)
With Ziyuan Jiang, Kuan-Ju Wu, [[Yasuaki Kakehi]]. 3D ice printer that captures ice formation; melting as core aesthetic. Camera records ice in real time, AI translates images into haiku. Born during Arctic Circle residency in Svalbard. Published at [SIGGRAPH '25 Art Gallery](https://dl.acm.org/). Shown at [[Ars Electronica]] 2025 "Panic" theme exhibition, SIGGRAPH Asia Hong Kong, Gallery Ether Tokyo, Tokyo Prototype.

### Chthulucene (2022-2024)
Imagining post-human world where octopuses inherit Earth. Contact improvisation + AI style transfer between octopus and dancers. Shown at **Venice Architecture Biennale 2023** (European Culture Center), [[MoMA]] screening, New Museum "Embodied Ecology" 2022, SIGGRAPH Art Gallery '23, ISEA Paris '23, Exploratorium SF, Today Art Museum Beijing. [SIGGRAPH publication](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3588428.3593823). Special thanks to [[Donna Haraway]], Fiona Raby, Margaret McFall-Ngai, Paola Antonelli. Credits: [[NEW INC]], New Museum, [[Serpentine R&D Platform|Serpentine]] Gallery.

### Squid Map (2022-2025)
Hawaiian bobtail squid reinterprets human-made maps and borders. Created during residency at Kewalo Marine Biology Lab, University of Hawaii. Gyotaku prints with squid ink. Deeply personal meditation on immigrant experience. Shown at [[Ars Electronica]] 2024, MoMA screening, Venice Biennale 2023, ISEA Paris/Barcelona, New Museum, Exploratorium, Today Art Museum Beijing, Edge on the Square SF 2025. [ISEA publication](https://isea-archives.siggraph.org/presentation/squid-map-presented-by/). Credits: [[NEW INC]], [[Serpentine R&D Platform|Serpentine]].

### Sentient Clit: The Pussification of BioTech (2024)
With WhiteFeather Hunter. 3D bioprinted clitorises from menstrual stem cells. Challenges male-dominated biotech narratives, exploring posthuman pleasure, consent in bioengineering, feminist methodology. Used OnlyFans as strategic distribution. Shown at [Exploratorium "Sexplorations"](https://www.exploratorium.edu/visit/calendar/after-dark-sexplorations).

### Menstrual Garden (2022-2025)
First-ever stable menstrual blood proteome. 3D printed protein sculptures, olfactory installation (smells of fresh/stagnant menstrual blood and pain). Core77 Design Award. Shown at [Olfactory Art Keller](https://www.olfactoryartkeller.com/exhibitions/bodies-odors-trauma-disease), Duande Art Museum.

### Squeeker: The Mouse Coach (2023-2024)
Running app + installation where a mouse controls your exercise. App on Apple App Store / Google Play. Premiered at [IDFA DocLab](https://www.idfa.nl/en/film/5e56524e-6675-4203-b51f-914ad9ee5df6/squeeker:-the-mouse-coach/) Amsterdam "Phenomenal Friction", also at [[Ars Electronica]] 2024, MoMA screening, Duande Art Museum.

### TransVision (2019)
Three perceptual machines: Hyperallergic Vision, Tactile Vision, Commoditized Vision. Harvard thesis project. [TransVision book](https://www.amazon.com/TransVision-Jiabao-Li/dp/0464514339). SIGGRAPH Art Gallery, ISEA Gwangju, CHI Glasgow.

### ANIMO: WildLife Priority Mode (2023-2025)
Self-driving cars that prioritize wildlife to reduce roadkills. Roadkill animal carpets + Roomba. DESIGNART TOKYO, ISEA Seoul 2025.

### Wings Against the Veil of Light (2024)
Butoh performance about cricket chirping, mating, light pollution. With Rosemary Candelario, Joy Deng. Performed at The Contemporary Austin, Laguna Gloria.

### The Long Fall (2025)
Immersive audiovisual journey with plankton + Earth's carbon cycle. With Stanford's Prakash Lab datasets. Live bioluminescent dinoflagellate tank as living instrument. 64-pad "plankton instrument."

### The Elephant in the Room (2024)
Legal action against Wild Elephant Valley (Yunnan) for elephant abuse. Interactive 3D model of scarred elephant, AR placement, drone sounds during court hearing, viral Elephant Song video. Art as collective political action in China.

## Venue Choice Analysis
Jiabao moves across **five circuits** with remarkable fluidity:

1. **International media art festivals**: [[Ars Electronica]] (twice), [[ISEA]] (Paris, Barcelona, Gwangju, Seoul), [[transmediale]]-adjacent. This is the media art establishment circuit.
2. **Science/tech conferences**: SIGGRAPH (Art Gallery + Art Papers), CHI, IEEE VR, UbiComp, IEEE VIS. She publishes *proper academic papers* at top HCI/graphics venues — unusual for an artist.
3. **Major museums**: MoMA, New Museum, Venice Biennale, Exploratorium, Contemporary Austin, Today Art Museum Beijing, Hamburger Bahnhof-adjacent. The institutional art world.
4. **Documentary/film festivals**: IDFA DocLab, Sheffield DocFest, Vancouver IFF, Millennium DocsAGG Warsaw. Treating her installations as cinema.
5. **Asian art-tech**: TANK Shanghai, West Bund, Wuzhen, DESIGNART Tokyo, Gallery Ether Tokyo, Duande Art Museum, Ming Contemporary Art Museum. She has deep China + Japan circuits.

The **unusual signal**: she bridges **Apple/Silicon Valley** (WWDC talk, formerly designed Vision Pro) and **radical bioart** (menstrual stem cells on OnlyFans). Very few people credibly inhabit both worlds. She also bridges **serious HCI research** (CHI, SIGGRAPH papers) and **performative/experiential art** (Butoh dance, olfactory installations). This dual credibility — engineer who makes art, artist who publishes papers — is her superpower.

The **small venue choices** reveal taste: Olfactory Art Keller, Gallery Ether Tokyo, Edge on the Square SF, Codame, Critical Shifts Istanbul. These are niche, curated, experimental. She's betting on emerging spaces alongside the major institutions.

## Why Interesting
Jiabao Li is the most complete embodiment of **more-than-human design** in our network. She doesn't just theorize about nonhuman perspectives — she literally works with squid, bats, mice, crickets, plankton, elephants, and menstrual stem cells. Every project starts with actual science (proteomics, marine biology, AI animal communication) and becomes speculative design that makes you feel what it's like to be another species.

The **Harvard → Apple → art professor** trajectory is unusual and revealing. She learned to prototype at the highest corporate level (Vision Pro!), then chose to use those skills for radical eco-feminist bioart. The technical sophistication shows — her installations are polished, her papers pass peer review at top venues.

Her **Chinese immigrant lens** gives projects like Squid Map genuine emotional depth — the squid carrying sand between black and white territories is autobiography through interspecies metaphor.

The **menstrual blood research** thread (Menstrual Garden → Gift → Sentient Clit) is genuinely edge-of-chaos: first stable menstrual proteome, 3D bioprinted organs from menstrual stem cells, feminist body politics meets real science. This is the kind of work that's simultaneously advancing scientific knowledge and asking radical political questions.

For Amber: Jiabao's [[Ecocentric Future Lab]] model — a university lab doing art × science × more-than-human design — could be a reference for how to institutionalize this kind of practice. Her venue strategy (publishing at both CHI and Venice Biennale) shows how to be taken seriously in multiple worlds simultaneously.
