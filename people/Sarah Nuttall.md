# Sarah Nuttall

Professor of Literary and Cultural Studies at [[WISER]], University of the Witwatersrand, Johannesburg. Directed the Institute 2013–2023. Author/editor of 15 books. The leading figure in *elemental humanities* — treating water, air, plants, and rain as rigorous theoretical objects for reading literature, art, and politics from the Global South. Spouse and intellectual partner of [[Achille Mbembe]]. Currently convening the *Plant Lives* forum (2025–) with [[Isabel Hofmeyr]].

## Background
Sarah Nuttall is one of the most important humanists working in and from Africa today. She built [[WISER]] into Africa's leading interdisciplinary humanities institute over a decade of directorship (2013–2023), stewarding a strong emphasis on collaborative research, interdisciplinary scholarship, and critical public humanities practice in and for the Global South.

Her trajectory is driven by two intersecting projects: (1) reading post-apartheid South Africa and Johannesburg as a global theoretical object, not just a regional case; and (2) developing an *elemental humanities* — a methodological and theoretical framework for taking water, air, plants, and rain seriously as agents in cultural and political life.

The elemental turn began organically: her work on post-apartheid entanglement and Johannesburg's urban life gave way to a sustained engagement with hydrology, atmosphere, and now plant worlds. She has published seminal articles developing the concept of "pluvial time" (rain as a mode of reckoning with colonial and ecological time) and "reading for water" (a materialist methodology for literary analysis). Now free of administrative duties after her directorship, she is producing her most theoretically ambitious work yet: *On Pluviality*, a book on rain worlds and elemental reading in southern African literature.

Since 2023, she and [[Isabel Hofmeyr]] have co-convened three 6-month online forum series that generate new work from the Global South, each reaching publication as books and special issues:
1. **Heated Conversations** (2023) — a heating planet + overheated pedagogies
2. **Breathing In: Air and Atmospheres** (2024) — non-transparency of air; making air explicative
3. **Plant Lives** (2025–) — material plant lives; postcolonial plantarium; phytopoetics; pre-colonial pharmacopoeias

She has supervised nearly 30 PhDs to completion and delivered almost 50 keynote addresses.

## Key Works

### Books (authored)
- [*Reading for Water: Materiality and Method*](https://www.routledge.com/Reading-for-Water-Materiality-and-Method/Nuttall/p/book/9781032456621) (Routledge) — the methodological manifesto for elemental reading. Develops "reading for water" as a critical practice applicable across literature, art, and politics.
- [*Entanglement: Literary and Cultural Reflections on Post-Apartheid*](https://www.dukeupress.edu/) (Duke UP) — foundational post-apartheid cultural theory. Key concept: entanglement as a way of reading the multiply constituted post-apartheid subject.
- *On Pluviality: Rain Worlds and Elemental Reading in Southern African Literature* (in progress) — the culmination of her elemental humanities project. Develops "pluvial time" as a way of reading rain as a temporal and political register.
- *Postcolonial Air and Atmospheres: Environmental Crisis and Elemental Life* — air as political and literary object. Companion to the water and rain work.
- Essays on literature, art and politics (in progress) — gathering essays developed across her career.

### Books (edited)
- [*Your History With Me: The Films of Penny Siopis*](https://www.dukeupress.edu/your-history-with-me) (Duke UP, October 2024) — comprehensive study of Penny Siopis's short films. Contributors include William Kentridge, Griselda Pollock, John Akomfrah, Zineb Sedira, Achille Mbembe, Mark Gevisser. 504 pages, 294 color illustrations.

Contributors: Arjun Appadurai, AbdouMaliq Simone, William Kentridge, Mark Gevisser, and more. Benchmark text.

- [*Reading from the South: African Print Cultures and Oceanic Turns in the Work of Isabel Hofmeyr*](https://witspress.co.za/) (co-edited with Charne Lavery, Wits University Press, 2023) — festschrift for Isabel Hofmeyr; introduction co-authored with Lavery.
- *Publishing from the South: A Century of Wits University Press* — institutional history and Southern publishing politics.
- *Loadshedding: Writing On and Over the Edge of South Africa* — collection on South African crisis writing.
- *Negotiating the Past: The Making of Memory in South Africa* — memory and post-apartheid transition.
- *Senses of Culture: South African Culture Studies* (co-edited) — foundational South African cultural studies anthology.

## Venue Circuit Analysis
Nuttall operates in an African humanities–Global South–postcolonial literary studies circuit, distinct from art-tech or media-art circuits:

**Core institutional circuit:**
- **WISER/Wits** — home base; generates most research infrastructure
- **Duke University Press** — primary publisher; connects to North American African studies
- **NYU** — Hofmeyr's New York base; occasional connective node
- **Routledge** — major humanities publisher; *Reading for Water* series

**Academic conference circuit:**
- African Studies Association (ASA) annual conference — primary North American venue for African studies
- African Literature Association (ALA) — primary literary studies venue
- MLA (Modern Language Association) — major literary studies conference
- Postcolonial Studies conferences — theoretically adjacent
- African humanities symposia at major universities (Harvard, Columbia, Yale, Princeton, Oxford, Cambridge)

**Forum/workshop circuit:**
- Online format (the Nuttall-Hofmeyr forum series model) — explicitly Global South accessible; 6-month duration; open subscription
- WISER seminar series — weekly public seminars; speakers from across Africa and globally
- African humanities reading groups and workshops (often at WISER or via partner institutions)

**Key observation:** She has *inverted the typical circuit*. Instead of moving to the US/UK and visiting Africa, she stays in Johannesburg and makes the Global South the intellectual center from which theory travels outward. The online forum model is a deliberate strategy for producing work from the South without requiring travel or institutional affiliation in the North.

## Research Taste Analysis

### Conceptual Strategy
**Element-as-concept coining.** Nuttall's key move is taking something physically present but theoretically absent — water, air, rain, plants — and constructing a rigorous methodology for taking it seriously as an agent in cultural and political life. Each element becomes a "reading for X" methodology. This is a form of **framework multiplication**: materialist analysis × postcolonial theory × literary criticism → elemental humanities.

**Concept-to-method pipeline.** She doesn't just coin concepts; she operationalizes them. "Pluviality" becomes "reading for rain in Namwali Serpell" becomes *On Pluviality* becomes a Wits UP book. The arc from concept to method to monograph is consistent across her work.

**Collaborative theory-building.** Almost all her major projects are collaborative (with Mbembe, Hofmeyr, Lavery, Gupta). She does not produce theory in isolation — she generates it through sustained collaboration, which is also why her forum series model works: it's essentially a theory-building collective.

### Career Architecture
**Directorial power as research platform.** Running WISER for 10 years gave her the institutional authority to build research programmes, invite visitors, host PhD students, and run seminar series. She used the directorship as a platform for intellectual community-building, not just administration. The result: WISER has a 25-year record of generating cutting-edge theory that travels globally.

**Publisher relationships as field-building.** Her relationship with Duke University Press (which publishes most of her major works) is a strategic partnership. Duke UP is the premier press for African studies, cultural studies, and postcolonial theory in the North American context. By consistently publishing there, she ensures her work circulates in the key academic networks.

**Field declaration through methodology.** "Elemental humanities" is a field declaration, not just a project. By developing methodology ("reading for water," "reading for air," "plant lives"), she's building infrastructure that other scholars can use and extend. This is **infrastructure-as-field**: you create the tools, others build with them.

### Key Moves (chronological)
1. **The Johannesburg book (2008)** — co-editing a major Duke UP volume that inserted Johannesburg into global urban theory. Established her as a serious international scholarly force, not just a South African specialist.
2. **WISER directorship (2013–2023)** — a decade of institution-building. Gave her the platform to convene, invite, and sustain research communities on her terms.
3. **The elemental turn (2019–)** — "Pluvial Time" articles in high-profile journals (*New Literary History*, *Interventions*, *English Studies in Africa*) established the theoretical framework before the book.
4. **Reading for Water (Routledge)** — the methodological manifesto, published as a Routledge book rather than Duke UP: a deliberate move to reach a broader methodology-focused audience.
5. **The Nuttall-Hofmeyr forum series (2023–)** — a new institutional model for producing Global South scholarship: online, open, 6-month, book-length output. Three series running since 2023; in publication. This is infrastructure-building without requiring a new institution.

### What Amber Can Learn
- **The "reading for X" move**: turn a physical element into a reading methodology. Amber can do "reading for heat," "reading for latency," "reading for bandwidth" in computing contexts — using elemental materiality as a lens for cultural/political analysis of AI.
- **The forum series model**: build research communities through time-limited, topic-focused, open-subscription online forums. 6 months, invite 10-15 scholars, produce a book. No need for a big institution — need good conveners and a publisher relationship.
- **Stay in place, travel theoretically**: Nuttall stayed in Johannesburg and made the world come to her. Amber can stay in Oxford/London and make more-than-human computing travel to global theory, rather than chasing global validation.
- **Concept-to-method-to-monograph pipeline**: the discipline of taking a concept through the full arc (article series → methodology → book) is how you build a field, not just publish.
- **Collaborative theory-building**: the Nuttall-Hofmeyr partnership model (two senior scholars, sustained over years, generating cumulative output) is a career-long collaboration model. Amber should look for intellectual partners for sustained co-work.
