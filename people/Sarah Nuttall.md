# Sarah Nuttall

Professor of Literary and Cultural Studies at [[WISER]], University of the Witwatersrand. Directed the Institute 2013–2023 (two terms). 15 books. The leading figure in a new *elemental humanities* — treating water, air, plants, rain as theoretical objects for reading literature, art, and politics. Spouse and intellectual partner of [[Achille Mbembe]].

**Highest Amber relevance at WISER.** Her elemental/more-than-human turn is the humanities counterpart of Amber's more-than-human design. Currently convening *Plant Lives* forum (2025–) with [[Isabel Hofmeyr]].

## Domains
- African literary and cultural studies
- Postcolonial urban theory (Johannesburg as analytical object)
- **Elemental humanities**: water, air, rain, plants as analytical/theoretical objects
- African aesthetics (film, visual culture)
- Materiality and method — how to read for elements, substances, atmospheres
- Critical public humanities from and for the Global South

## Background
Built WISER into Africa's leading interdisciplinary humanities institute over 10 years of directorship. Strong emphasis on collaborative research — most books are collaborative. Now free of administrative duties and producing her most theoretically ambitious work: *On Pluviality* (rain worlds, in progress), the ongoing forum series with Hofmeyr.

## Key Works
- [*Reading for Water: Materiality and Method*](https://www.routledge.com/Reading-for-Water-Materiality-and-Method/Nuttall/p/book/9781032456621) (Routledge) — the methodological manifesto for elemental reading
- *Your History With Me: The Films of Penny Siopis* (Duke UP) — African cinema as political archive
- *Postcolonial Air and Atmospheres: Environmental Crisis and Elemental Life* — air as political object
- *On Pluviality: Rain Worlds and Elemental Reading in Southern African Literature* (in progress)
- *Entanglement: Literary and Cultural Reflections on Post-Apartheid* — foundational post-apartheid cultural theory
- [*Johannesburg – The Elusive Metropolis*](https://www.dukeupress.edu/Johannesburg) (co-edited with Mbembe, Duke UP, 2008) — Joburg as global theoretical object
- *Beautiful-Ugly: African and Diaspora Aesthetics* (ed.)
- *Planetary Hinterlands: Extraction, Abandonment and Care* (ed.)

### Online Forum Series (with [[Isabel Hofmeyr]])
Three 6-month series generating new work from the Global South:
1. **Heated Conversations** (2023) — heating planet + overheated pedagogies → reaching publication as book/special issue
2. **Breathing In: Air and Atmospheres** (2024) — non-transparency of air; making air explicative → reaching publication
3. **Plant Lives** (2025–) — material plant lives; postcolonial plantarium; phytopoetics; pre-colonial pharmacopoeias → in progress

## Why Interesting
The most direct Amber connection in the WISER network. Nuttall is building elemental humanities — a framework for taking plants, water, air, rain seriously as more-than-human actors in cultural life. This is exactly what Amber's more-than-human computing work needs from the humanities side: a rigorous theoretical framework for elements as actors, developed through 25 years of African scholarship. Her reading method ("reading for water," "reading for air," "plant lives") is transferable to computing — "reading for data center heat," "reading for electromagnetic fields," "reading for atmospheric carbon." The Plant Lives forum (2025–) is happening now.
