# Simon Denny

New Zealand-born Berlin-based artist, professor, and institution-builder. One of the most important figures globally making art about technology ideology — Silicon Valley culture, surveillance capitalism, blockchain, platform power, and the aesthetics of tech companies. Works across sculpture, installation, painting, print, video, and NFTs. His method is investigative: he dissects the visual culture, management rhetoric, and organizational aesthetics of the tech industry to expose its ideological assumptions. Co-founded [[BPA// Berlin Program for Artists]] (2016) with Angela Bulloch and Willem de Rooij. Professor of Time-Based Media at HFBK Hamburg (since 2018). Appointed Member of the New Zealand Order of Merit (MNZM) in 2025.

## Background

Born 1982 in Auckland, New Zealand. Studied at the [Elam School of Fine Arts](https://www.elam.auckland.ac.nz/), University of Auckland (BFA 2004), then moved to Frankfurt for a Meisterschule (master's degree) at the [Städelschule](https://www.staedelschule.de/) under influential professors — graduating 2009. The Städelschule connection put him in the orbit of the European critical-art scene and specifically the Frankfurt-Berlin axis of conceptual and political art practice. After graduating he settled in Berlin, which has been his base ever since.

Early on he co-founded **Gambia Castle**, an artist-run gallery in Auckland — characteristic of his community-building instincts. He first gained international attention at Art Basel 2012 (Baloise Art Prize) and at the 55th Venice Biennale (2013) as a participant in the main exhibition, before being selected to represent New Zealand at the 56th Venice Biennale in 2015, which became his breakthrough moment internationally.

His work takes the position of the investigative journalist applied through the language of contemporary art. He researches primary documents — tech company PowerPoint slides, management handbooks, patents, trade-fair marketing materials, blockchain whitepapers, game design conventions — and transforms them into large-scale installations that make the invisible visible: the ideological assumptions embedded in the visual culture of power.

In 2016 he co-founded BPA// Berlin Program for Artists, a major institutional contribution to Berlin's art scene. In 2018 he became Professor of Time-Based Media at HFBK Hamburg — cementing his institutional footprint. He has curated two landmark blockchain-art exhibitions: *Proof of Work* at Schinkel Pavillon, Berlin (2018) and *Proof of Stake* at Kunstverein Hamburg (2021).

In 2024 he married **Maria Paula Fernandez**, an Argentinian activist, tech entrepreneur, and co-founder of the [Department of Decentralisation](https://www.departmentofDecentralization.org) — a Berlin-based organization that organizes Ethereum-adjacent events and open-source culture. This partnership connects Denny to the active European crypto-culture scene at an institutional level.

## Domains

- **Art & technology ideology** — the aesthetics, rhetoric, and visual culture of tech companies (Apple, Amazon, NSA, Anduril, Palantir, SpaceX)
- **Blockchain & crypto art** — first major artist to bring blockchain art into European museum mainstream; NFTs as critical practice rather than speculation
- **Surveillance capitalism** — NSA visual culture, Five Eyes, Edward Snowden leaks as artistic material
- **Platform capitalism** — labor, extraction, data mining, algorithmic management
- **Geopolitics of tech** — defense tech, Silicon Valley libertarianism, seasteading, crypto ideology
- **Virtual worlds & game design** — MUD/MMORPG history, metaverse land ownership, Minecraft/Roblox as political spaces
- **Machine painting** — new series using modified CNC machines, plotters, and handheld inkjet printers trained on Futurist aesthetics
- **Curatorial practice** — the *Proof of Work/Stake* exhibitions as field-defining moments for blockchain art

## Key Works

### Major Series & Exhibitions

- **[Secret Power](https://simondenny.net/secret-power-2/)** (2015) — *The* breakthrough work. Created for the New Zealand Pavilion at the 56th Venice Biennale. Staged at the Biblioteca Nazionale Marciana and Marco Polo Airport arrivals lounge. A case study in NSA visual culture, responding to the Snowden leaks. Sourced imagery from leaked NSA PowerPoint slides (MYSTIC, FOXACID, QUANTUMTHEORY, TREASUREMAP), commissioned new design work from NSA designer David Darchicourt without telling him the intended use — a form of "reverse espionage." Modded server racks, Herman Miller office furniture, allegorical paintings. Te Papa (Wellington) acquired four works for its permanent collection. A landmark in art responding to surveillance and geopolitics.

- **[The Personal Effects of Kim Dotcom](https://simondenny.net/kim-dotcom/)** (2013) — Replicas of items seized from Kim Dotcom's home in the NZ Police raid. First shown at mumok Vienna (2013), then Firstsite Colchester, Adam Art Gallery Wellington, MoMA PS1 New York. An early example of his method: real world events + forensic reconstruction + conceptual framing.

- **[All You Need Is Data](https://simondenny.net/all-you-need-is-data-main-project/)** (2012) — Based on the DLD (Digital-Life-Design) conference in Munich, 2012. Recreated the conference environment as an art installation. Baloise Art Prize winner. Shown at Kunstverein München, Petzel Gallery, Auckland Art Gallery, Arnolfini Bristol, Hamburger Bahnhof Berlin, Fondazione Sandretto Re Rebaudengo Turin.

- **[New Management](https://simondenny.net/less-stale-attachment-2006-exhibition/)** (2014) — Solo show at Portikus Frankfurt. Examined tech industry management culture.

- **[The Innovator's Dilemma](https://momaps1.org/exhibitions/view/396)** (2015) — First major American solo museum show, at MoMA PS1, Long Island City. Title borrowed from Clayton Christensen's famous business book about disruptive innovation.

- **[Products for Organising](https://www.serpentinegalleries.org/whats-on/simon-denny-products-organising/)** (2015–16) — Solo show at Serpentine Sackler Gallery, London (Nov 2015–Feb 2016). Examined radical management practices and hacker organizational forms. Divided into Products for Emergent Organisations and Products for Formalised Organisations — mapping Apple, Zappos, and GCHQ as analogous organizational structures.

- **[Blockchain Future States](https://simondenny.net/)** (2016) — Solo show at Petzel Gallery, New York. One of the first major gallery exhibitions dedicated to blockchain as artistic subject matter. Positioned Denny as the key bridge between European contemporary art and crypto culture.

- **[The Founder's Paradox](https://simondenny.net/the-founders-paradox-main-project/)** (2017) — Uses board games as framework to examine competing political utopias: Peter Thiel's *Zero to One* (libertarian) vs Max Harris's *The New Zealand Project* (collectivist). Custom versions of Settlers of Catan, Game of Life, Ascent. A figure called "Operation" assembled a full startup-bro outfit (fidget spinner, nootropics, DLD hoodie, Terminator figurine). First shown at Michael Lett Auckland, then Museum of Contemporary Art Cleveland, Christchurch Art Gallery, Gwangju Biennale, Guangzhou Triennial.

- **[Mine](https://simondenny.net/mine-main-project/)** (2019–2021) — Major touring exhibition on the double meaning of "mining" — extracting physical resources from the earth and extracting data from users. Included an Amazon Worker Cage patent rendered at human scale with endangered King Island Brown Thornbill renders superimposed (the "canary in the coalmine"); large-scale board game "Extractor" which doubled as exhibition catalogue; speculative accountability trial courtroom sketches imagining Brisbane Supreme Court cases against autonomous mining robots; and (in 2021 at Petzel) NFT Mine Offsets. Shown at MONA Tasmania, K21 Düsseldorf, Petzel New York, and numerous venues including Kunstmuseum Basel, de Young Museum San Francisco, New Museum New York.

- **[Metaverse Landscapes](https://simondenny.net/metaverse-landscapes-main/)** (2023) — UV-print and oil paintings depicting parcels of land from various metaverses (Decentraland, The Sandbox, Voxels, Somnium Space) — following the conventions these platforms use to represent land in online marketplaces. Each painting came with a corresponding NFT Title Deed tracking actual metaverse land token ownership. References colonial landscape painting and modernist abstraction. Shown at Kunstverein Hannover, HEK Basel, Frans Masereel Centrum Belgium, Art Basel, Antenna Space Shanghai, Altman Siegel San Francisco, National Taiwan Museum of Fine Arts, Espacio Fundación Telefónica Madrid, and more.

- **[Dungeon](https://simondenny.net/dungeon-main/)** (2024) — Exhibition at Petzel Gallery examining the dungeon as organizational logic — from tabletop RPGs to MUDs (Multi-User Dungeons) to contemporary metaverses and social media. Includes "Dungeon artefacts": a Palmer Luckey office display Anduril sword replica (epoxy resin, ground coffee), a Grimes "War is Coming" ex-Twitter Belkin power strip in plexiglas case, HeroQuest bookcase and rack reconstructions. Also curated *Multi-User Dungeon (MUD)* group exhibition at Petzel simultaneously featuring Seth Price and others. Then expanded to ArkDes Stockholm as *Worldglimpsing: Roleplay and the Design of Alternate Realities* (2025).

- **[Machine Paintings](https://simondenny.net/machine-paintings-main/)** (2025–ongoing) — New series using modified CNC machines, reprogrammed plotters, and handheld industrial inkjet printers (originally designed for marking shipping crates). An AI trained on Italian Futurist art and contemporary advertising produces synthetic pointillist-like abstractions in acrylic and gouache. Simultaneously channels the aesthetics of defense tech companies (Anduril, Palantir, SpaceX, Helsing). Recasts early 20th-century Futurist aesthetics through 21st-century automated logic. Shown in *The Future* (Michael Lett Auckland, 2025) and *Forces of the Unknown* (Kraupa-Tuskany Zeidler, Berlin, presented at JW Marriott Hotel Berlin across from the German Defense Department, June 2025).

## Venue Choice Analysis

Denny's circuit reveals a deliberate **three-axis strategy**:

**1. European critical art institutions (Kunstverein axis):**
Portikus Frankfurt, Kunstverein München, Kunstverein Hamburg, Kunstverein Hannover, Heidelberger Kunstverein — the German Kunstverein network is the backbone of critical European contemporary art. By showing consistently here, he maintains legitimacy in the most rigorous intellectual context for contemporary art in Europe.

**2. Major museums for mass legitimation:**
MoMA PS1, Serpentine Galleries, MONA (Tasmania), Hammer Museum, WIELS Brussels, K21 Düsseldorf, MAXXI Rome, de Young San Francisco. These provide the canonical weight that makes his work teachable, collectable, and influential.

**3. Platform-specific deployment:**
His *Proof of Work* at Schinkel Pavillon and *Blockchain Future States* at Petzel were targeted interventions in specific conversations — not just "art shows" but field-defining events. Similarly, *Worldglimpsing* at ArkDes (Sweden's national design museum) positions metaverse/game design within design history.

**The New Zealand vector:** He consistently returns to New Zealand contexts (Michael Lett, Auckland Art Gallery, Christchurch Art Gallery, Te Papa, NZ Pavilion Venice) — maintaining roots while operating globally. This is both loyalty and strategy: NZ's art establishment is small enough that he remains a central figure without competition.

**Small-venue intelligence:** JW Marriott Hotel Berlin for *Forces of the Unknown* — directly across from the German Defense Department and German Resistance Memorial Centre. This is not random: the site selection IS the work's first layer.

## Ideas & Critical Theory

Simon Denny's theoretical contribution can be summarized as **the aesthetics of power as primary document**. His core insight: the visual culture, management rhetoric, and organizational forms of tech companies are not neutral — they are ideological artifacts as rich with meaning as any canonical artwork. By treating them with the tools of conceptual art, he makes their assumptions legible.

**Key threads:**

1. **NSA visual culture as art history**: The Snowden PowerPoint slides, he argues, "have become retroactively some of the most important artistic images created today." They contain "a lot of cultural information that we just haven't been able to unpack." His role: artist as archaeologist of the present.

2. **Silicon Valley ideology as religion**: *The Founder's Paradox* (2017) dissects the ideological framework of tech libertarianism — Thiel's "zero to one" logic, seasteading, the sovereign individual — and maps it against collectivist alternatives using game theory's own language (board games). The game structure IS the argument: these are competing ontologies about how society should work, dressed up as play.

3. **Platform capitalism and extraction**: *Mine* (2019) names the double extraction — of rare earth materials AND of user data — and implicates the labor (Amazon Worker Cage patent), the ecology (endangered King Island Brown Thornbill as canary in coalmine), and the technology (autonomous mining robots, surveillance satellites) in one analytical frame.

4. **Blockchain as speculative epistemology**: The *Proof of Work* (2018) and *Proof of Stake* (2021) exhibitions, plus *Metaverse Landscapes* (2023), treated blockchain not as financial speculation but as an epistemological system — a new way of encoding ownership, value, and trust. *Metaverse Landscapes* specifically frames virtual land ownership through colonial landscape painting, connecting current crypto speculation to centuries of colonial land enclosure.

5. **Defense tech aesthetics and the militarization of Silicon Valley**: *Machine Paintings* (2025) and *Dungeon* (2024) track the convergence of gaming culture (Dungeons & Dragons → Roblox → metaverse), defense tech (Anduril, Palantir, Helsing), and venture capital (Grimes, Palmer Luckey, Elon Musk) into a coherent ideological complex. The Italian Futurist parallel is exact: Futurism celebrated speed, war, and machines in service of national power — today's tech-defense complex does the same.

**Signature quote:** "The question is what to do when you are living in symbiosis with machines that rely on pattern recognition that flattens meaning to the point where conspiracy theories are as strong as any other type of truth logic. One thing to take from the Futurists is that when you use the new without knowing what it is – as you have to do when you use the new – sometimes you don't know what you're getting and sometimes you don't know who's winning."

## Why Interesting for Amber

**Multiple direct resonances:**

1. **Machine behavior as political practice**: Denny doesn't just study machine behavior abstractly — he makes the behavioral norms of tech platforms legible as ideology. His *Mine* boardgame ("Extractor") and *Dungeon* map actually model the logic of digital extraction systems, which connects to Amber's interest in machine behavior as a scientific and critical practice.

2. **Critical Computing realized in art**: Denny is perhaps the most consistently rigorous practitioner of what the Aarhus tradition theorizes. He doesn't just critique tech — he does the structural analysis of how tech ideology reproduces itself through visual culture, organizational forms, and management aesthetics.

3. **Platform capitalism ontology**: His blockchain-focused work asks exactly the question Amber's work on decentralized AI raises: who controls the epistemic infrastructure? *Metaverse Landscapes* maps the colonial logic of virtual land ownership. *Proof of Work/Stake* curations mapped a field where autonomy was supposedly encoded in code but reproduced old hierarchies.

4. **Berlin-base + institutional infrastructure**: As co-founder of BPA// Berlin Program for Artists, he's created one of the best pathways for Berlin-based emerging artists. If Amber ever has a Berlin period, BPA// is worth applying to. And Denny's HFBK position means direct access to time-based media pedagogy in one of Germany's better art schools.

5. **Defense tech × art**: The recent *Machine Paintings* series is exactly Amber's kind of provocation — using aesthetics to reveal the ideological continuity between Italian Futurism (art movement that loved war machines) and Silicon Valley defense tech (startup culture that now loves war machines). The temporal parallel is devastating.

6. **More-than-human angle**: terra0 — the art collective Denny championed through *Proof of Work* — asks whether a forest can own and utilize itself. This is directly in Amber's more-than-human research territory: non-human economic agency, smart contracts as governance, ecological systems as autonomous actors.
