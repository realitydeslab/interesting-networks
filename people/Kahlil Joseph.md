# Kahlil Joseph

Filmmaker and visual artist. Co-directed Beyoncé's visual album *Lemonade* (2016, with Beyoncé). Directed Jay-Z's *m.A.A.d* (inspired by Kendrick Lamar). Founded **BLKNWS** — a mixed-media project that functions as a news channel, artwork, and ongoing collaboration. Regular exhibitor at major art institutions.

Close collaborator of [[Arthur Jafa]]. Together with [[Malik Hassan Sayeed]], Joseph was pivotal in the creation of *APEX* (2013–14) — they took Jafa's image archive and placed it on a timeline in the studio, creating the work. This act of formalizing Jafa's archive is a central story in Jafa's practice.

## Key Works
- *m.A.A.d* (2014) — short film, Jay-Z. Shown at Sundance.
- *Fly Paper* (2017) — short. Shown at Frieze New York.
- *BLKNWS* (2018–ongoing) — mixed-media news channel installation. Shown at [[Venice Biennale]] 2019, New Museum, and internationally.
- *Beyoncé: Lemonade* (2016, co-director)
- *Until the Quiet Comes* (2012) — Kendrick Lamar music video. Won Grammy nomination.

## Why Interesting
BLKNWS demonstrates a unique model: a "news channel" that is simultaneously a critical artwork commenting on how Black news is produced and consumed, a community platform, and an ongoing collaboration with Black voices. It's positioned in museums and galleries but functions as media infrastructure. The model of the "channel" as artwork format is worth tracking.
