# Willem de Rooij

Dutch artist based in Berlin. Works across installation, film, and object. Known for formally rigorous, politically engaged work that interrogates colonial history, representation, and the politics of beauty and display. A key figure in the German/Dutch contemporary art scene. Co-founded [[BPA// Berlin Program for Artists]] (2016) with [[Simon Denny]] and [[Angela Bulloch]].

Represented by Galerie Buchholz — same gallery as Angela Bulloch, and historically Simon Denny. The Buchholz orbit is the institutional spine of the BPA// founding group.

## Why Interesting
Critical practice at the intersection of colonial history, aesthetics, and institutional politics. His work on representation and display has formal overlaps with Amber's interest in how technologies encode and reproduce power. The BPA// co-founding makes him a key Berlin art infrastructure node.
