# Michel Serres

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Stub created during Bruno Latour deep research.

French philosopher of science. One of the most poetic and wide-ranging thinkers in 20th-century philosophy. Influenced Bruno Latour and Actor-Network Theory through his concept of the "quasi-object" and the "parasite." Member of the Académie française (1990). Also a professor at Stanford University for many years.

His philosophy moves across mathematics, thermodynamics, biology, mythology, and communication theory — refusing disciplinary boundaries in a way that directly enabled Latour's "generalized symmetry." Died June 1, 2019, Paris.

Key books: *The Parasite* (1980), *Hermes* series (5 vols, 1969–1980), *Rome: The Book of Foundations* (1983), *Statues* (1987), *The Natural Contract* (1990), *Angels: A Modern Myth* (1993), *The Incandescent* (2003), *Malfeasance* (2011). 

Engaged in extended dialogues with Latour published as *Conversations on Science, Culture, and Time* (Michigan UP, 1995) — a key text for understanding both thinkers.

## Why Interesting
Serres is the philosophical grandfather of ANT — his quasi-objects and "parasite" concept gave Latour a vocabulary for talking about things that are neither subjects nor objects. For Amber, reading Serres (especially *The Parasite* and *The Natural Contract*) alongside Latour illuminates the philosophical depth of ANT.
