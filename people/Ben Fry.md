# Ben Fry

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Created stub note. Discovered through **[[Processing Foundation]]** deep research as co-creator of Processing.

American designer and data visualization pioneer. Co-created **[Processing](https://processing.org/)** with Casey Reas in 2001 at MIT Media Lab's Aesthetics and Computation Group (advisor: John Maeda). Processing became the foundational open-source creative coding environment used globally by artists, designers, and educators. Principal of [Fathom Information Design](https://fathom.info) in Boston — a data visualization and software consultancy.

## Key Works
- **[Processing](https://processing.org/)** (co-created with [[Casey Reas]], 2001–present) — Open-source creative coding language, IDE, and community. 2005 Golden Nica at Prix Ars Electronica.
- **[Visualizing Data](https://www.amazon.com/Visualizing-Data-Ben-Fry/dp/0596514557)** (O'Reilly, 2007) — Seven-stage data visualization process framework
- **[Processing: A Programming Handbook for Visual Designers and Artists](https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262182621/)** (with Casey Reas, MIT Press, 2007)
- **[Getting Started with Processing](https://www.oreilly.com/library/view/getting-started-with/9781449340230/)** (with Reas, O'Reilly, 2010)
- **[Getting Started with p5.js](https://www.oreilly.com/library/view/make-getting-started/9781457186769/)** (with Reas + Lauren McCarthy, O'Reilly, 2015)
- **[All Streets](/allstreets/)** — Data visualization of 26 million road segments in the US. Iconic piece.
- **Computational Information Design** — PhD dissertation at MIT. Introduces seven stages of visualizing data: acquire, parse, filter, mine, represent, refine, interact.

## Awards

- Cooper-Hewitt Design Triennial (2003, 2006)
- Whitney Biennial (2002)

## Why Interesting
Ben Fry's contribution is rarely named but omnipresent: the seven-stage data visualization framework from his PhD thesis is now standard design methodology. Processing — co-created at MIT — democratized visual programming for artists and designers globally. His career shows how academic research (MIT PhD) can directly produce tools with massive real-world impact (4+ million p5.js users alone). Less public than Reas but equally foundational.
