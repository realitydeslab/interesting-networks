# Keith Breckenridge

Deputy Director at [[WISER]]; Standard Bank Chair in African Trust Infrastructures. Historian of biometrics, surveillance, and state formation in Africa. Author of the landmark [*Biometric State*](https://www.amazon.com/Biometric-State-Politics-Identification-Surveillance/dp/1107077842/) (Cambridge UP, 2014), showing how fingerprint ID originated in colonial South Africa and became global surveillance infrastructure. Working on *Biometric Capitalism*.

## Domains
- Biometrics and surveillance history (1850–present)
- Digital population registration and its political effects in Africa
- History of South African gold mining and state formation
- Science and Technology Studies (STS) applied to African contexts
- Trust infrastructures: civil registration, digital ID, credit surveillance

## Key Works
- [*Biometric State*](https://www.amazon.com/Biometric-State-Politics-Identification-Surveillance/dp/1107077842/) (Cambridge UP, 2014) — global politics of identification; colonial origins; 2017 Humanities Book Award from Academy of Science of South Africa
- [*Registration and Recognition*](http://britishacademy.universitypressscholarship.com/view/10.5871/bacad/9780197265314.001.0001/upso-9780197265314) (ed. with Simon Szreter, OUP/British Academy, 2012) — civil registration in world history
- *Biometric Capitalism* (in progress) — global digital ID + credit surveillance in former colonial world

## Key Communities
- [[WISER]] — Deputy Director
- **Bhalisa Network** — founder/host; 200+ researcher network on digital population registration. [Bhalisa list](http://lists.wits.ac.za/mailman/listinfo/bhalisa)

## Why Interesting
The clearest voice connecting colonial history → biometric surveillance → contemporary AI/digital infrastructure. His concept "biometric capitalism" is exactly the theoretical frame for understanding how AI-powered identity systems replicate colonial power. Less directly adjacent to Amber's more-than-human interests, but critical for anyone working on critical AI or digital rights from a Global South perspective.
