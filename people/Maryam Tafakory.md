# Maryam Tafakory مریم تفکری

Iranian-born artist and filmmaker based in London. **2024 Film London Jarman Award winner.** Makes textual and filmic collages — layered, found-footage essay films that excavate the forbidden bodies, queer desires, and erased intimacies buried inside post-revolution Iranian cinema. Her films are not about explaining Iran to the West; they are archival interventions that force the archive to confess what it concealed.

## Background

- Born and raised in Iran. Grew up in a period of relative opening in Iranian cinema and press — but cinema was, as she says, "an escape to a deeper form of repression." There were watchmen with torches in the cinema.
- Dropped out of her university in Iran in 2007. Moved to the UK; dropped out again from Southampton University (computer science). Later found her way to fine art.
- **MFA, [Ruskin School of Art, Oxford University](https://www.rusk.ox.ac.uk/)** (2015–2017) — where she still teaches.
- **PhD in Fine Art (fully funded), [Kingston University](https://www.kingston.ac.uk/)** (2017–2020) — research initiated here became the "rage against the archive" that drives the del series. During the first weeks of the pandemic she watched 417 films made in post-revolution Iran, from which she drew 87 for Nazarbazi.
- Based between London and Shiraz (earlier writing); now primarily London.
- Teaching: **MFA Tutor, [Ruskin School of Art, Oxford University](https://www.rusk.ox.ac.uk/)** (2025–26); previously **Associate Tutor in Fine Art, [Goldsmiths University of London](https://www.gold.ac.uk/)** (2022–24).
- "Compulsive editing" is her self-described methodology: "Editing for me is compulsive — it's the sharing of it that is always disturbing."
- Rarely gives interviews; prefers "to be with a pen and paper." Meticulous with words.
- Her practice is explicitly not didactic for non-Iranian audiences: "I'm not interested in clarifying cinematic codes for non-Iranians."

## Domains

- **Essay film / experimental cinema** — archival collage using mirroring, inversion, superimposition, expressive colour manipulation, sonic textures from found footage
- **Censorship as form** — treating prohibition as a generative constraint; the handbag technique, the play of glances, the negative space where touch should be
- **Queer feminist critique of Iranian cinema** — interrogating both state censorship AND Western misreadings/co-optation of Iranian resistance
- **Text / body interface** — words as the only permissible skin; poetry and silence as the languages of touch
- **Archive as site of violence** — returning to the cinematic archive "to remember not to forget"; forcing images to confess
- **Live performance** — works developed as both films and live film performances (CodeNames, Mast-del series)

## Key Works

### The "Del" Series (heart/desire/censorship — ongoing)
- **Mast-del (مست دل)** (2023, 17', Iran/UK) — Two women in bed; a memory of going to the cinema that cannot be shown in images. Found and original footage superimposed. A love song that would never pass through the censors. Premiered Cannes Quinzaine des Cinéastes. Scored by [[Sarah Davachi|Canadian composer Sarah Davachi]]. Named Film Comment Best Short Films 2023; Sight & Sound Best Essay Films 2023.
- **Razeh-del (راز دل)** (2024, 28', Iran/UK/Italy) — Dense layerings of inky newsprint and appropriated footage retelling the history of *Zan*, Iran's first women's newspaper (1998–99), through two schoolgirls who send a synopsis for an impossible film. Saturated amethyst, crimson, amber. World premiere 77th Locarno Film Festival (Pardi di Domani). Won six Oscar-qualifying awards.
- **Sukhte-del (سوخته دل)** — in production; worked on during [MacDowell Fellowship](https://www.macdowell.org/artists/maryam-tafakory) 2023.

### Archive Intervention Series
- **Nazarbazi (نظربازی)** (2021/22, 19') — *The play of glances.* Love and desire in Iranian cinema where intimacy is prohibited. Built from 87 films drawn from 417 viewed, spanning 28 years (1982–2010). Commissioned by [Film and Video Umbrella](https://www.fvu.co.uk/) as part of BEYOND. The word *nazarbazi* can mean "eye fucking," "lustful glances," even "illusion." Major award circuit.
- **Irani Bag (کیف ایرانی)** (2020, 8', Iran/Singapore/UK) — Split-screen video essay. Bags as touch-prosthetics in post-revolution Iranian cinema: the bag between two people on a motorbike instead of their hands. Commissioned by [Asian Film Archive](https://www.asianfilmarchive.org/) for their *Monographs* series. Sight & Sound Best Video Essays 2020 *and* 2021.

### Earlier Works
- **I Have Sinned a Rapturous Sin (گنه کردم گناهی پر ز لذت)** (2017) — Title taken from [[Forugh Farrokhzad]]'s first published (and scandalous) poem "Sin." Collage around religious advice on suppressing sexual desire; women mockingly lip-sync the advice. Two IFFR 2018 nominations.
- **Absent Wound (زخم)** (2015) — Shot in a Zurkhaneh (traditional men's wrestling space — women prohibited) and a men's bath (before 4am). The filmmaker menstruates inside the bathhouse; blood washes down the drain. Text drawn from religious guidance websites and Simone de Beauvoir's *Second Sex*. DocumentaMadrid Best Short; IFFR nominated.
- **Poem & Stone (شعر و سنگ)** (2015, installation and film) — September in Tehran brought to life through taste, touch, scent. Feet in a white frame; hands pushing earth into circular words. Closes with Rumi's *Masnavi*.
- **Taklif (تکلیف)** (2015) — Inner monologues woven with choreography and daily obligations. BFI Doc Lab funded. Premiered Zurich Film Festival.
- **Fragments (تکهها)** (2014) — Earliest exhibited work. Disembodied hands; Farsi text: *I dreamt you ate me.*
- **I Was Five (پنج سالم بود)** — film

### Live Performance / Performance Films
- **CodeNames (اسم رمز)** (2023) — Solo screening/performance work. Shown at National Gallery of Art Washington DC, e-flux New York, MoMI New York, Images Festival Toronto, Batalha Centro de Cinema, BOZAR Brussels.
- **Daria's Night Flowers (گلهای شب ِدریا)** (2025, 16', Iran/UK/France) — Daria's manuscript about falling in love with a girl called 'abi' [blue]; night flowers hiding secrets; a country that has turned love stories into crime scenes. World premiere FID Marseille (Grand Prix Flash Competition). Then TIFF Wavelengths, NYFF Currents, BFI LFF Experimenta (2025), and 25+ festivals.
- **Gol[e] Sorkh (گل سرخ)** — in production
- **remembering to forget** — performance
- **CodeNames** (also performed as standalone live performance)

### Poets & Writers She Works With / Through
These are not collaborators in the conventional sense — she weaves their words into her films as co-inhabitants of the image:
- **[[Forugh Farrokhzad]]** (1934–67) — Iranian feminist poet. Title of *I Have Sinned a Rapturous Sin* from her poem "Sin." A haunting presence throughout her filmography.
- **Ahmad Shamlou** (1925–2000) — Iranian poet. Used in Nazarbazi (*Anthem for the One Who Left and the One Who Stayed Behind*, tr. Jason Bahbak Mohaghegh).
- **Hafez** (14th–15th century) — Classical Persian poet. Renarrativised by the Iranian state as pious devotee; Tafakory restores his erotic charge.
- **Nima Yushij** — founder of Persian free verse, 1950s. The new wave of Iranian poetry that broke from rigid form; Tafakory cites this as the precursor to her filmic caesura work.
- **Roland Barthes** — French essayist. Text fragments in Nazarbazi and Irani Bag.
- **Jacques Derrida** — Theoretical reference throughout.

## Intellectual Lineage & Key References

Her work sits in explicit dialogue with:
- **[[Laura Mulvey]]** — whose 1975 "Visual Pleasure and Narrative Cinema" she critiques from a queer feminist perspective: the Western voyeurism model does not map onto post-revolution Iranian cinema's specific taboo structures.
- **Negar Mottahedeh** — *Displaced Allegories: Post-Revolutionary Iranian Cinema* (2008) — the scholarly context her films depart from and complicate.
- **[[Audre Lorde]]** — referenced in Mast-del notes on the erotic as life force.
- **Simone de Beauvoir** — *The Second Sex* — fragments in Absent Wound.
- **Avery Gordon** — *Ghostly Matters* — the spectral logic of her cinematic hauntings.
- **Ursula K. Le Guin** — *The Carrier Bag Theory of Fiction* — containers, not weapons, as the hero's vessel.
- **Robin Wall Kimmerer** — *Braiding Sweetgrass* — braiding, ceremony, attention as intention.
- **Adonis** (Syrian poet) — woven into the text of Nazarbazi.

## Venue Choice Analysis

Tafakory operates across **three overlapping circuits**, each with distinct logic:

1. **Major film festival experimental/shorts circuit** — Cannes Quinzaine, NYFF Currents, TIFF Wavelengths, Locarno Pardi di Domani, BFI London, IFFR, Melbourne, True/False. This is where prestige accretes, prizes are won, and the work is positioned in the international cinema conversation. She is not in the narrative feature circuit — she is in the experimental shorts circuit that takes theory seriously.

2. **Art institution circuit** — MoMA, Academy Museum, National Gallery of Art, BOZAR, HKW, Pergamon Museum, Whitechapel, LUX, e-flux, Tate. These are not cinema venues — they are contemporary art spaces treating her work as installation and video art. This crossover is deliberate and rare. The dual citizenship (film world + art world) gives her unusual reach.

3. **UK experimental film community** — LUX, FVU, ICA Frames of Representation, Encounters Festival, London Short Film Festival, Essay Film Festival Birkbeck, Open City Docs. This is her home base and where her work is supported and commissioned. The commissions from Film and Video Umbrella and LUX distribution signal institutional investment before major awards came.

**Small venues she has chosen** (revealing her character): Light Industry NY, Spectacle Theatre NY, DokuFest Kosovo, Courtisane Ghent — the underground experimental film circuit that functions as a taste-making layer below the major festivals. She shows here alongside the most rigorous practitioners.

**What the choices reveal:** She is building a practice that refuses the binary between cinema and art, between Western feminist theory and post-revolution Iranian experience, between the archive and the present. Her venue choices enact this refusal — she won't settle into either the "festival darling" or the "gallery artist" box.
