# Andrew Feenberg

Canadian philosopher of technology, Professor Emerita at [Simon Fraser University](https://www.sfu.ca/philosophy/people/faculty/andrew-feenberg.html), School of Communication. One of the foundational figures in **critical theory of technology** — the tradition that uses Frankfurt School critical theory (Marcuse, Habermas) to analyze technology as a contested site of social and political power. Key concepts: *instrumental rationalization*, *democratic rationalization*, *technical codes*. Distinguished speaker in the [[Research Network for Philosophy and Technology]] Dialogues series.

## Background
Student of Herbert Marcuse (Frankfurt School) at UC San Diego. Developed *critical theory of technology* — distinguishing between "instrumental" (means-end, neutral) and "substantive" (value-laden) theories of technology, and proposing a "critical" theory that sees technology as a site of democratic contestation. His *Technical Code* concept: the embedded social values in technical design that appear neutral but encode power relations. This directly links to design justice and participatory design.

Published extensively on Heidegger, Marcuse, and the philosophy of technology. Also known for work on *online education* and democratization of learning.
