# Dorion Sagan

Essayist, philosopher of ecology, fiction writer, and theorist. Son of Carl Sagan and Lynn Margulis; became Lynn Margulis's primary intellectual collaborator and co-author (~10 books together). His work sits at the intersection of new materialism, nonhuman theory, non-equilibrium thermodynamics, and feminist science studies. Widely cited since the Nonhuman Turn (2015). Co-founded Sciencewriters Books with Margulis (2006).

## Why Interesting
His *Into the Cool* (with Eric Schneider) connects thermodynamics to life — energy dissipation as the driver of life's complexity. His philosophical writings on the nonhuman, symbiosis, and ecology are cited in critical theory. He bridges rigorous science with philosophical and cultural theory in a way that's rare. Works widely cited in Critical AI Studies and new materialism.

## Key Works
- [*Microcosmos*](https://archive.org/details/microcosmosfourl00marg) (with Lynn Margulis, 1986) — best popular account of symbiogenesis
- [*Into the Cool: Energy Flow, Thermodynamics, and Life*](https://press.uchicago.edu/ucp/books/book/chicago/I/bo3637405.html) (with Eric D. Schneider, 2005, U Chicago Press) — non-equilibrium thermodynamics and life
- [*Cosmic Apprentice: Dispatches from the Edges of Science*](https://www.upress.umn.edu/book-division/books/cosmic-apprentice) (2013, University of Minnesota Press)
- [*Lynn Margulis: The Life and Legacy of a Scientific Rebel*](https://www.chelseagreen.com/product/lynn-margulis/) (ed., 2012, Sciencewriters Books)
- *Acquiring Genomes* (with Lynn Margulis, 2002)
