# Felicity Sparrow

British feminist filmmaker and activist; co-founder of [[Circles Cinenova|Circles – Women's Film in Distribution]] (1979). Circles ran initially from her flat. Produced the first Circles catalogue (1980, printed by Only Women Press). Wrote the first touring programme notes for Circles' inaugural touring programme "Her Image Fades as Her Voice Rises" (1980). Co-signed the "Women and the Formal Film" manifesto (published in *Film as Film*, Arts Council, 1979) alongside [[Annabel Nicolson]], [[Lis Rhodes]], Jane Clarke, Jeanette Iljon, Mary Pat Leece, Pat Murphy, Susan Stein.

## Domains
Feminist film distribution; experimental film; feminist infrastructure building.

## Background
Active at the [[London Film-Makers Co-operative (LFMC)]] in the late 1970s as part of the feminist experimental film tendency. One of the four founding members of Circles (with Lis Rhodes, Annabel Nicolson, and Jo Davis).

## Key Communities
- [[Circles Cinenova|Circles]] — co-founder; practical organiser; produced first catalogue; ran distribution from her flat (1979–80)

## Why Interesting
Sparrow was the practical anchor of Circles in its founding phase — the person whose flat it ran from, who produced the first catalogue. The model of distribution collective as collaborative domestic space is significant. Needs further research on her own filmmaking practice.
