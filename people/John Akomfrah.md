# John Akomfrah

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Stub created during The Otolith Group deep research.

British-Ghanaian filmmaker, installation artist, and cultural theorist. Co-founder of **[Black Audio Film Collective](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Black_Audio_Film_Collective)** (1982–1998), arguably the most important British Black arts collective of the 20th century. Former member of The Otolith Group Advisory Board. Works in multi-screen installation exploring memory, diaspora, the archive, and the natural world. Represented by Lisson Gallery, London.

## Key Works
- *Handsworth Songs* (1986) — Landmark documentary about the 1985 Handsworth riots; won BAFTA
- *The Stuart Hall Project* (2013) — Portrait of cultural theorist Stuart Hall
- *Vertigo Sea* (2015) — Three-screen installation on the history of whaling, migration, and ecology
- *Mimosa* (2011) — Three-screen work on exiled political refugees
- *Mnemosyne* (2010) — Triptych on migrant experience

## Why Interesting
Bridges Black cultural politics, essay film, and environmental thinking. His three-screen installation work is formally rigorous and politically committed. Deeply relevant to Amber's interest in moving image as critical form. Black Audio Film Collective is the historical root of much of the essay film tradition in the UK.
