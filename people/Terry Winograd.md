# Terry Winograd

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Stub created. Core panelist at [[Halfway to the Future]] 2024 (reflecting on *Understanding Computers and Cognition*).

Professor Emeritus of Computer Science at Stanford. Pioneer of human-computer interaction, natural language processing, and philosophy of computing. Best known for *Understanding Computers and Cognition: A New Foundation for Design* (1986, with Fernando Flores) — a landmark text applying Heidegger, Wittgenstein, and speech act theory to computing and AI. Google co-founders Larry Page and Sergey Brin were his graduate students.

## Why Interesting
*Understanding Computers and Cognition* is the philosophical backbone of HCI's critique of cognitivist/rationalist AI. At HTTF 2024 the "Reflective Practices with AI" panel used his book as a provocation to interrogate what current LLM/generative AI developments mean for HCI. His framework for thinking about breakdowns, tools, and ready-to-hand vs. present-at-hand connects to ongoing debates about AI design.

## Key Works
- [*Understanding Computers and Cognition: A New Foundation for Design*](https://www.wiley.com/en-us/Understanding+Computers+and+Cognition:+A+New+Foundation+for+Design-p-9780201112978) (1986, with Fernando Flores)
- *Bringing Design to Software* (1996, ed.) — foundational design thinking text
