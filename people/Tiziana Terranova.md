# Tiziana Terranova

Co-founding member of [[Critical Computation Bureau (CCB)]]. Associate Professor of Cultural Studies and New Media in the Department of Human and Social Sciences, **Università degli Studi di Napoli L'Orientale** (the Oriental University of Naples). Leading theorist of network culture, digital labor, platform capitalism, and hypersocial networks. Member of [Euronomade](http://www.euronomade.info) and co-founder of the [Technoculture Research Unit](http://www.technoculture.it).

## Background
- Associate Professor, Cultural Studies and New Media, Napoli L'Orientale
- Publishes in: *Theory Culture and Society*, *Culture Machine*, *New Formations*, *CTheory*
- Connected to Italian autonomist political tradition (through Euronomade)
- Based in Naples — key figure in the Southern Italian critical theory scene

## Key Works
- **[Network Culture: Politics for the Information Age](https://www.plutobooks.com/9780745317298/network-culture/)** (Pluto Press, 2004) — seminal text on digital labor, immaterial labor, network culture. Introduced the concept of **free labor** in digital networks.
- **"Hypersocial"** in *Posthuman Glossary* ed. Braidotti & Hlavajova (Bloomsbury, 2018) — concept of new social formations emerging from platform capitalism
- ***Hypersocial*** (University of Minnesota Press, forthcoming) — major book developing her current concept
- "Inflections of Technoculture: Biodigital Media, Postcolonial Theory and Feminism" (with Iain Chambers) — *Anglistica–AION* 18.2 (2014)
- Multiple essays on digital media, network culture, and postcolonial technocultures

## Key Concepts
- **Free labor**: The unpaid labor performed by internet users that generates value for platforms — foundational concept in digital labor studies
- **Network culture**: How communication networks produce new political and cultural formations
- **Hypersocial**: New social forms emerging from platform capitalism — beyond the "social" of social media into something more intensive and pervasive
- **Postcolonial technocultures**: The entanglement of colonial history and contemporary digital technology, particularly in the Mediterranean context
