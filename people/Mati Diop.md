# Mati Diop

Senegalese-French filmmaker and actress. Grand Prix, Cannes 2019 for *Atlantique* — the first African woman to compete at Cannes. Participated in [[Les Ateliers de la Pensée de Dakar]] (2019 edition). Her film *Dahomey* (2024) is directly about the restitution of African heritage objects from France to Benin — the exact theme of the [[Felwine Sarr]] / Sarr-Savoy Report. César Award winner 2025.

## Domains
- Speculative/documentary filmmaking
- African diaspora narratives
- Heritage and restitution (in film)
- Ghost stories, the spectral, migration

## Key Works
- *Atlantique* (2019) — Grand Prix, Cannes. Senegalese migrants, the sea, the spectral.
- *Dahomey* (2024) — Documentary about 26 Beninese artifacts returned from France. César Best Documentary 2025. Directly continues Sarr-Savoy work in film form.

## Why Interesting
She makes films about the same things Sarr writes about — African heritage, colonial violence, the ghost of the past in the present. *Dahomey* and the Sarr-Savoy Report are companion pieces. The art-theory-policy triangle is fascinating. Also: speculative/supernatural elements in *Atlantique* make her interesting from a more-than-human angle.
