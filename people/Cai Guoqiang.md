# Cai Guoqiang 蔡国强

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Stub created during Xu Bing deep research. Encountered as parallel generational figure.

Major Chinese contemporary artist known for gunpowder drawings, large-scale explosion events, and spectacle-based public art. Contemporary of Xu Bing (born 2 years later); both navigated the post-Tiananmen exile-to-America-to-global-recognition path but with radically different aesthetics: Xu's is cerebral/linguistic; Cai's is sensory/pyrotechnic.

## Background
Born 1957 in Fujian. Studied at Shanghai Theatre Academy. Moved to Japan 1986, US 1995. Known for designing fireworks for Beijing Olympics 2008 opening ceremony (the "footprints" over Beijing). Retrospective at Guggenheim New York 2008. Major works include explosion events, gunpowder drawings on canvas or paper, large installation sculptures from found materials.

## Key Works
- **Black Rainbow** — explosion event
- **Inopportune: Stage One** — 9 cars in explosion sequence, Times Museum, Guangzhou
- **Sky Ladder** (2015) — fireworks ladder 500m into sky over sea off Huiyu Island, Quanzhou
- Designed fireworks for Beijing Olympics 2008 opening ceremony
- **Gravitational Arena** (Museum of Art Pudong, Shanghai) — same space as Xu Bing's later commissioned work

## Key Institutions
- Guggenheim Museum (major retrospective 2008)
- MoMA
- Museum of Contemporary Art, Los Angeles
- Today Art Museum, Beijing

## Why Interesting
Useful as a comparison figure to Xu Bing — same generation, similar exile-and-return arc, but completely different aesthetic strategies. Cai uses spectacle; Xu uses language. Both achieved international recognition and returned to China from positions of Western credibility.
