# Lynette Wallworth

Australian immersive artist and filmmaker working at the intersection of technology, ecology, and Indigenous knowledge. One of the most globally recognized artists in immersive media — her work has been shown at Sundance, Venice, Tribeca, and in formal diplomatic contexts (UN, World Economic Forum). Received the first-ever VR residency from [[Sundance New Frontier]] (~2016). Known for deep, ethics-driven immersive experiences that center non-Western and more-than-human perspectives.

## Domains
- Immersive art (VR, installation, film)
- Indigenous sovereignty and knowledge systems
- Ecology and more-than-human relations
- Planetary imagination and care ethics

## Background
Australian practice rooted in ecology, feminism, and cross-cultural collaboration. Her long-term collaboration with the Martu people of Western Australia (for "Collisions") and the Mu Awi Wati people (for "Awavena") demonstrates sustained relational practice across cultures — making work *with* communities, not about them.

## Key Works
- "Collisions" (2016) — VR documentary co-created with Martu elder Nyarri Morgan; nuclear testing on Aboriginal land; shown at Sundance, Venice, Tribeca; screened for world leaders
- "Awavena" (2018) — VR with Yawanapi shaman Hushahu Yawanapiiti; Amazonian indigenous cosmology; premiered at Davos WEF; now in science contexts
- "Coral: Rekindling Venus" (2012) — interactive installation about coral reef decline
- Various large-scale video installations across galleries worldwide

## Venue Circuit
Sundance New Frontier → Venice VR → Tribeca → World Economic Forum → UN → gallery circuit (MOMA, museums). Crosses between art world and diplomatic/policy contexts — rare.

## Why Interesting
For Amber: Wallworth's work is the best example of how immersive art can operate as **more-than-human diplomacy** — bringing non-Western ecological perspectives into rooms where policy gets made. Her collaboration methodology (long-term, relational, Indigenous-led) is a model for responsible practice with non-human and marginalized-human communities. Her pushback against the "metaverse" in 2022 (*"the metaverse is not meta — it's a mono-verse"*) reflects exactly the critical computing lens Amber values.
