# Layli Long Soldier

Oglala Lakota poet. Author of *WHEREAS* (Graywolf Press, 2017) — a landmark work formally responding to the US government's 2009 "Apology to Native Peoples of the United States." The line "*the space in which to place me*" from her poetry became the title of [[Jeffrey Gibson]]'s 2024 US Pavilion exhibition at the Venice Biennale.

## Background
Oglala Lakota. *WHEREAS* won the PEN/Jean Stein Book Award and the National Book Critics Circle Award (2018). Uses the legal/governmental language of the formal Apology as a formal constraint and subject of critique — turning official language against itself. Important figure in Indigenous poetics and the intersection of language, law, and sovereignty.

## Key Works
- [*WHEREAS*](https://www.graywolfpress.org/books/whereas) (Graywolf Press, 2017) — Major poetry collection responding to US government apology to Native peoples. National Book Critics Circle Award, PEN/Jean Stein Award.

## Why Interesting
Her practice of using official/legal language as poetic material is a critical language strategy analogous to what [[Jeffrey Gibson]] does with materials — using the tools/forms of the oppressor to critique the oppressor. Important for understanding how Indigenous contemporary artists think about language, sovereignty, and form.

## Key Relationships
- [[Jeffrey Gibson]] — Gibson titled his Venice Pavilion *The Space in Which to Place Me* from her poetry
