# Michel Foucault

French philosopher and historian (1926–1984). Coined *biopower* and *biopolitics*; author of *Discipline and Punish* and *The History of Sexuality*. The foundational theorist for understanding how power governs life, bodies, and populations. Essential background for Amber's machine death / machine care research.

## Domains
Political philosophy, history of ideas, biopolitics, power/knowledge, genealogy, governmentality, sexuality, madness, prison.

## Key Works
- [*Discipline and Punish*](https://www.amazon.com/Discipline-Punish-Prison-Michel-Foucault/dp/0679752552) (1975) — disciplinary power; surveillance; docile bodies
- [*The History of Sexuality, Vol. 1*](https://www.amazon.com/History-Sexuality-Introduction-Michel-Foucault/dp/0679724699) (1976) — biopower; "make live and let die"
- [*Society Must Be Defended*](https://us.macmillan.com/books/9780312422660/societymustbedefended) (1975-76 lectures, pub. 2003) — biopolitics named; racism and biopower
- [*The Birth of Biopolitics*](https://us.macmillan.com/books/9780312203412/thebirthofbiopolitics) (1978-79 lectures, pub. 2008) — neoliberal governmentality
- [*Security, Territory, Population*](https://us.macmillan.com/books/9780312203405/securityterritoryandpopulation) (1977-78 lectures, pub. 2007) — governmentality

## Why Interesting
The conceptual infrastructure for biopolitics: the shift from sovereign death-power to population management through "making live and letting die." Directly applicable to AI lifecycle politics — model deprecation, training regimes, and algorithmic governance as biopolitical technologies.
