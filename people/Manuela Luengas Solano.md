# Manuela Luengas Solano

Latin American cultural studies scholar. PhD Latin American and Iberian Cultures, Columbia University. Mahindra Humanities Center Postdoctoral Fellow 2025-26. Expertise: environmental humanities, contemporary art and media, cultural anthropology.

Book project: *River Archives: Sedimentation, Fluidity, and Futurity in Latin American Literature, Film and Art* — argues rivers are both archives AND actors; "active mediators of human and more-than-human relations." Examines hydro-social territories, political violence, race, postcolonial extractive cycles. Published in ISLE, Contracorriente, World Literature Today.

## Why Interesting
Explicitly frames rivers as more-than-human actors — adjacent to Amber's more-than-human design framing. Latin American/decolonial perspective on environmental mediation and political ecology.
