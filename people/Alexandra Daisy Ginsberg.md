# Alexandra Daisy Ginsberg

British artist and designer working at the intersection of **synthetic biology, artificial intelligence, ecology, and speculative design**. Design Interactions (RCA) alumna under [[Anthony Dunne]] and [[Fiona Raby]]. Most known for *E. chromi* (2009, with James King) — engineering bacteria to produce colored health indicators — and *The Substitute* (2019) — an AI reconstruction of the northern white rhino's behavior, meditating on de-extinction. Work shown at MoMA, V&A, Cooper Hewitt, Science Gallery Dublin and London, Center for the Future of Museums.

## Why Interesting
One of the strongest "second-generation" DI practitioners who has pushed speculative biology toward AI and ecology. Her work on de-extinction (*The Substitute*) and machine-generated nature (*Machine Auguries*) directly anticipates more-than-human design questions. Unlike most DI alumni, she has deep scientific collaborations (real biologists, ecologists). The evolution from synthetic biology provocation → AI ecological reconstruction is a trajectory worth watching.

## Key Works
- *E. chromi* (2009) — bacteria engineered to produce colored indicators of digestive health. RSA Student Design Award. Widely cited as foundational biodesign work.
- *The Substitute* (2019) — AI-generated model of northern white rhino behavior and movement. Asks whether digital reconstruction of extinct behavior constitutes a form of preservation.
- *Machine Auguries* (2019) — AI-generated dawn chorus, replacing real birdsong with imagined machine nature.
- *Designing for the Sixth Extinction* (2013) — biodiversity offset market scenarios, creating objects for endangered species.
