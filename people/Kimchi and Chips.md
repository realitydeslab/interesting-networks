# Kimchi and Chips

Duo of Mimi Son (Korean) and Elliot Woods (British). Make large-scale installation works using light as a material — millions of projected beams, optical computation, spatial imaging. Their work often involves physically impossible-seeming effects: light appearing to float in mid-air, haze-projection, and volumetric imaging without screens.

## Why Interesting
They sit at the intersection of optical science, spatial computing, and perception art. The *Cyclops Retina* (2023) collaboration with Rosa Menkman at The Beams London used "millions of beams of light merged in haze to create floating graphics in the air" — directly connecting to questions of how seeing works, what counts as a rendered image, and the materiality of light.

Their work treats light the way Menkman treats compression: as material with properties, constraints, and revealing failures. Both practices are ultimately about the infrastructure of seeing.

## Known Works
