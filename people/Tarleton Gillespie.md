# Tarleton Gillespie

Media scholar and STS researcher at Microsoft Research New England, known for foundational work on platform governance and algorithmic curation. "The Relevance of Algorithms" (2014) is one of the most-cited texts establishing why algorithms matter for social order. *Custodians of the Internet* (2018) is the definitive account of how platforms govern content moderation.

## Domains
- Platform governance and content moderation
- Algorithmic curation and social ordering
- Science and Technology Studies (STS)
- Media and communication studies

## Key Works
- ["The Relevance of Algorithms"](https://tarletongillespie.org/essays/Gillespie%202014%20-%20The%20Relevance%20of%20Algorithms.pdf) in *Media Technologies* (MIT Press, 2014) — seminal essay establishing algorithms as constitutive of social order, not merely tools.
- [*Custodians of the Internet: Platforms, Content Moderation, and the Hidden Decisions That Shape Social Media*](https://yalebooks.yale.edu/book/9780300173130/custodians-of-the-internet/) (Yale University Press, 2018) — authoritative account of how platforms make (hidden) governance decisions about speech, visibility, and behavior.

## Why Interesting
Platform governance is central to [[Algorithmic Governance]]. Gillespie's lens — platforms as norm-setters, not neutral conduits — is essential for understanding how agent ecosystems will be governed. His framing of "custodians" (platforms that take on governance responsibility without claiming it) is directly relevant to thinking about who governs agentic AI on the web.
