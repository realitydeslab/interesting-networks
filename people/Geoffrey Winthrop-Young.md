# Geoffrey Winthrop-Young

Canadian-German scholar and translator. The primary English-language interpreter and advocate for German media theory, especially Friedrich Kittler and the cultural techniques paradigm. Associate Professor at UBC. Translated both [[Bernhard Siegert]]'s *Cultural Techniques* (Fordham UP, 2015) and [[Cornelia Vismann]]'s *Files* (Stanford UP, 2008), among others.

## Key Works
- *Kittler and the Media* (Polity Press, 2011) — the primary English-language introduction to Kittler
- Translation: [[Bernhard Siegert]], *Cultural Techniques* (Fordham UP, 2015)
- Translation: [[Cornelia Vismann]], *Files: Law and Media Technology* (Stanford UP, 2008)
- "Cultural Techniques: Preliminary Remarks," *Theory, Culture & Society* 30:6 (2013) — key introductory essay
- "The Kultur of Cultural Techniques," *Cultural Politics* 10:3 (2014)
- [Conversation: "More Things in Theory Than Heaven and Earth are Dreaming Of"](http://digitalpassage.wordpress.com/2014/12/22/winthrop-young-more-things-in-theory-than-heaven-and-earth-are-dreaming-of-interview/)

## Key Relationships
- [[Bernhard Siegert]] — primary translator and English-language advocate
- [[Friedrich Kittler]] — primary interpreter (wrote the introductory book)
- [[Cornelia Vismann]] — translated *Files*

## Why Interesting
If you want to engage with German media theory, Winthrop-Young is the guide. His translations and introductory texts lower the barrier. His meta-commentary on what cultural techniques theory is *doing* philosophically is often clearer than the original German texts.
