# Jason Edward Lewis

Professor of Computation Arts at Concordia University. Samoan/Hawaiian descent. Co-directs the **Indigenous Futures Research Centre (IFRC)** and **Obx Laboratory for Experimental Media**. Co-founded **Aboriginal Territories in Cyberspace (AbTeC)** with Skawennati.

Key work:
- **Indigenous Protocol and AI workshops** (2019) and position paper — foundational for Indigenous AI ethics
- **Abundant Intelligences** (2024, AI & Society) — major paper reimagining AI through Indigenous knowledge systems. DOI: [10.1007/s00146-024-02099-4](https://doi.org/10.1007/s00146-024-02099-4)
- Multi-year Abundant Intelligences research program at Concordia

**Core argument (Abundant Intelligences)**: AI has an epistemology problem, not just an ethics problem. Western AI bakes in Cartesian duality and computational reductionism. Indigenous knowledge systems offer a *relational* model of intelligence — not a property of individual agents but emergent from a web of relations (human, non-human, territorial, ancestral).

Fellow of the Royal Society of Canada. Stanford BS (Symbolic Systems + German Studies), Royal College of Art MPhil (Design).
