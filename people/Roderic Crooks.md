# Roderic Crooks

Informatics scholar at UCI working on data justice, surveillance, and racialized communities. Co-author (with Morgan Currie) of ["Numbers will not save us: Agonistic data practices"](https://doi.org/10.1080/01972243.2021.1920081) (2021, *The Information Society*). Known for "dataveillance and performativity in urban schools" — studying how surveillance technologies operate in K-12 education serving marginalized communities. Faculty affiliate at [[CREATE — Steckler Center for Responsible, Ethical, and Accessible Technology (UCI)]].

## Domains
- Data justice, surveillance studies
- Racialized communities and technology
- Education technology (EdTech critique)
- Community activism and datafication

## Why Interesting
"Agonistic data practices" framing — not just resistance or refusal, but productive engagement with data by communities who are simultaneously harmed by and dependent on data systems. Relevant to Amber's agentive commoning / participatory design interests. Discovery via [[CREATE — Steckler Center for Responsible, Ethical, and Accessible Technology (UCI)]].
