# Souleymane Bachir Diagne

Senegalese philosopher at Columbia University. Works on African Islamic philosophy, the philosophy of translation, and religious pluralism. Regular participant in [[Les Ateliers de la Pensée de Dakar]]. Author of *African Art as Philosophy* (Seagull Books) on Senghor and Bergson, and *Open to Reason: Muslim Philosophers in Conversation with Western Tradition* (Columbia UP). Major figure in African philosophy.

## Domains
- African Islamic philosophy
- Bergsonism and African aesthetics
- Translation theory and inter-civilizational dialogue
- Religious pluralism

## Why Interesting
Diagne works at the intersection of African tradition and French/Islamic philosophy — the same bridging work as Sarr but with a more theological/textual focus. At Columbia (NYC). If Amber is in NYC, his lectures are accessible.
