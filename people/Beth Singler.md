# Beth Singler

Anthropologist of AI, religion, and digital culture. Studies how AI is constructed as a quasi-religious or spiritual phenomenon — AI as God, the Singularity as Rapture, the AI ethics community as a secular church. Formerly at the Leverhulme Centre for the Future of Intelligence (LCFI), Cambridge. Now at University of Zurich. Did ethnographic fieldwork with AI ethics communities and transhumanist groups.

## Key Ideas
- The AI ethics community as a **secular religion** — with its own prophets (Turing, Minsky), sacred texts (Bostrom, Russell), and eschatological fears (existential risk = secular apocalypse). Her ethnographic lens reveals this as a cultural construction, not a neutral techno-philosophical project.
- The **Singularity as Rapture** — she traces how rapture theology shaped transhumanist AI discourse.
- AI "faces" and anthropomorphism — how we project personhood onto machines reflects deep cultural and spiritual assumptions.
