# Antonio Negri

Italian Marxist political philosopher. Co-author with [[Michael Hardt]] of the *Empire* trilogy and five other books. Central theorist of Italian *operaismo* (workerism/autonomism) — immaterial labor, constituent power, the "refusal of work." Imprisoned in Italy 1979 for alleged leadership of Red Brigades (widely disputed), exiled to Paris, allowed to return to Italy 2003. Died December 2023 in Rome.

## Why Interesting

The Negri/Hardt partnership produced the most ambitious Marxist political theory of the late 20th/early 21st century. Negri's foundational concepts — constituent power (from Spinoza), the "multitude," immaterial/affective labor — are now standard vocabulary in political philosophy. His books *The Savage Anomaly* (Spinoza), *Marx Beyond Marx* (Grundrisse reading), and *Time for Revolution* are philosophical touchstones. Also: his biography is remarkable — philosopher-militant, imprisoned, exiled, kept writing for 40 years.
