# Lisa Gitelman

Media historian at NYU. Author of *Always Already New: Media, History, and the Data of Culture* (MIT Press, 2006) and *Paper Knowledge: Toward a Media History of Documents* (Duke UP, 2014) — on the materiality of documents and how the document form shapes knowledge. Editor of *"Raw Data" Is an Oxymoron* (MIT Press, 2013). Participant in the *AHR* information circulation conversation (2011) with [[Brian Larkin]], Gabrielle Hecht, Paul N. Edwards, Adrian Johns.

## Domains
- Media history and theory
- Document theory — the document as media form
- Data history — how "raw data" is never raw
- History of recorded sound, inscription, and information

## Why Interesting
Gitelman's *"Raw Data" Is an Oxymoron* is essential reading for AI/data researchers — data is always already processed, always the result of infrastructure choices. Her document theory is relevant for understanding how protocols and formats shape knowledge. Strong connection to Amber's interests in how technical systems carry hidden assumptions.
