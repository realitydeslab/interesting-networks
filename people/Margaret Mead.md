# Margaret Mead (1901–1978)

American cultural anthropologist. One of the most famous public intellectuals of the 20th century. Key participant in the Macy Conferences (both Cybernetics and Group Processes series); with [[Gregory Bateson]] (her husband, later divorced), she brought communication theory and cultural analysis into the cybernetics project. Edited the cybernetics Transactions volumes alongside Heinz von Foerster.

## Why Interesting
Mead is often reduced to "she was there" at the Macy conferences. But her contribution was essential: she and Bateson insisted that social and cultural processes *are* feedback systems, that communication *is* cybernetics, and that the science of mind couldn't be built without anthropological observation. She co-edited the Transactions volumes. Her involvement directly shaped second-order cybernetics by insisting the observer is always culturally situated.
