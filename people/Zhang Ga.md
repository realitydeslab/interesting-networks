# Zhang Ga (张嘎)

Media art curator, writer, educator, and institutional architect. **Artistic Director of [[Chronus Art Center]] (CAC) since 2015**, Distinguished Professor at China Central Academy of Fine Arts (CAFA), and the single most strategically positioned individual in the China–global media art circuit. Zhang Ga is not just a curator — he is the principal intellectual architect of how China's media art scene engages with global posthumanist and AI-critical discourse.

- **2025:** CAC continues active programming — *River Biographies* (Lundahl & Seitl) co-produced with West Bund Museum, CHANEL support.
- **2023:** *There Is No Perfect Spell* (We=Link 3, online, with HeK Basel) — exploring crypto/blockchain/occult cultural entanglement.

## Background
- Based in Shanghai, with deep historical roots in New York (where he lived for many years and built his career).
- **Associate Professor of Media Art at [Parsons School of Design](https://www.newschool.edu/parsons/)** (The New School, NYC) — this was his primary role before CAC.
- **Visiting positions** at MIT Media Lab, Stanford University, UC Santa Barbara, Graduate Center CUNY.
- **Consulting Curator of Media Art at the National Art Museum of China (2007–2014)** — curated the landmark international media art triennial series: [*Synthetic Times*](https://chronusartcenter.org/) (2008), [*Translife*](https://chronusartcenter.org/) (2011), and [*thingworld*](https://chronusartcenter.org/) (2014). These were major exhibitions at China's national museum, setting a benchmark for international media art in China well before CAC.
- Active in the net.art scene in New York in the late 1990s–2000s. His 2020 essay "Sideways" reveals first-hand knowledge of Wolfgang Staehle's Thing.net, MoMA's early media art programming under Barbara London, JODI, and Vuk Ćosić's *Documenta Done* — evidence he was present in and around that community.
- **Currently**: Distinguished Professor at China Central Academy of Fine Arts (CAFA); previously Professor at the Academy of Arts and Design, Tsinghua University.
- On the editorial board of [Leonardo Books](https://mitpress.mit.edu/publishers/leonardo/) (MIT Press).
- Has contributed essays to *October*, MIT Press, Liverpool University Press, and Tsinghua University Press.

## Key Works / Curatorial Concepts

### Datumsoria (Curatorial Framework)
[*Datumsoria*](https://chronusartcenter.org/exhibitions/datumsoria-an-exhibition-of-liu-xiaodong-carsten-nicolai-and-nam-june-paik/) — Zhang Ga's own neologism: **datum + sensoria** = "a new perceptual space immanent to the information age." This is the conceptual spine of his entire curatorial practice at CAC. The framework asks: what happens to human perception when reality is increasingly constituted by data and computational processes? Rather than either technophilia or technophobia, he holds open the tension. Three exhibitions under the "Datumsoria / A&T@ (Art & Technology @)" programme:
1. *Datumsoria I*: LIU Xiaodong, Carsten Nicolai, Nam June Paik (2016, CAC; then ZKM Karlsruhe)
2. *Datumsoria: The Return of the Real* (2017–2018, ZKM Karlsruhe) — co-curated with ZKM
3. *Datumsoria*: Jon Kessler + YAN Lei (2018–2019, CAC)

### We=Link Platform (2020–ongoing)
[*We=Link: Ten Easy Pieces*](https://chronusartcenter.org/exhibitions/cac-exhibition-welink-ten-easy-pieces/) — launched March 30, 2020, as an emergency response to COVID-19 lockdown. A network-native online exhibition co-commissioned by [[Chronus Art Center]], [[Art Center Nabi]] (Seoul), and [[Rhizome]] (New Museum, NYC), with 12 co-hosting institutions including Arts at CERN, e-flux, [[HeK (House of Electronic Arts Basel)|HeK]], iMAL, Leonardo/ISAST, MU Hybrid Art House, SETI AIR, [[V2_Lab for the Unstable Media|V2_]]. Artists: Tega Brain & Sam Lavigne, JODI, LI Weiyi, Evan Roth, Slime Engine, XU Wenkai (aaajiao), YE Funa, Yangachi, Raphaël Bastide, Helmut Smits. Title puns on WeChat + Jack Nicholson's *Five Easy Pieces* — "a community of solidarity as a network of empowerment."

Sequels:
- [*We=Link: Sideways*](https://chronusartcenter.org/cac_readings/ensideways/) (2020–2021, online) — accompanied by Zhang Ga's extended essay on net.art history
- [*There Is No Perfect Spell*](https://chronusartcenter.org/exhibitions/cac-hek-online-exhibition-there-is-no-perfect-spell/) (2023, online, CAC × HeK) — We=Link 3rd iteration, exploring crypto/blockchain/occult cultural entanglement; artists: 0xSalon, Cullen Miller & Gabriel Dunne, Ilja Karilampi, Lauren Huret, LI Hanwei; supported by Pro Helvetia Shanghai

### AI Delivered (2021)
Two-part exhibition under the same curatorial framework:
- [*AI Delivered: The Abject*](https://chronusartcenter.org/exhibitions/cac-exhibition-ai-delivered-the-abject/) (Jul–Oct 2021) — Artists: Sofian Audry & Istvan Kantor, HE Zike, Lauren Lee McCarthy, [[Casey Reas]] & Jan St. Werner, Devin Ronneberg & Kite, Tonoptik. Framed through Turing's Imitation Game + Hal Foster's "Return of the Real" — argues the "abject" in contemporary AI is precisely where instrumentality imposed by capital fails to grasp human complexity.
- [*AI Delivered: Redemption*](https://chronusartcenter.org/exhibitions/cac-exhibition-ai-delivered-redemption/) (Nov 2021–Feb 2022) — Artists: Cesar & Lois, Ursula Damm, Interspecifics, Helena Nikonole, ZHENG Da. Imagines AI "freed from the assumed intelligence by a human measure" — machine intelligence as "agentic entity of another order" capable of a posthuman subjectivity and symbiosis.

### Entangled: bio/media (2022–2023)
[*Entangled: bio/media*](https://chronusartcenter.org/exhibitions/cac-exhibition-entangled-biomedia/) (Jul 2022–Feb 2023) — Conceived by Zhang Ga, co-curated with BI Xin and CAO Jiamin. Artists: CAO Shuyi, Mads Bering Christiansen & Jonas Jørgensen, Yunchul Kim, KU Kuang-Yi, YU Chun-LO & TIEN Zong-Yuan, Ani Liu, Iris Xiaoyu Qu, Anastasiia Raina et al., Casey Tang, WANG Yueyue, XU Haomin, XI Lei, Etsuko Yakushimaru. Drawing on Eugene Thacker's *Biomedia* (2004) to argue that biological process is itself a form of mediation — "a process of mediation" in Thacker's sense. The exhibition unfolds in four chapters, released progressively — itself structured as a "living entity."

## Why Interesting
Zhang Ga is interesting for at least five distinct reasons:

1. **The China Gateway Node**: He is the most intellectually serious and internationally well-connected media art curator currently working from China. Other Chinese curators are either more domestically focused or operating in contemporary art (not specifically media/tech). He speaks fluent posthumanism and can negotiate between Chinese state art institutions (NAMOC) and radical European media art (ZKM, Rhizome) — a rare combination.

2. **The Datumsoria Concept**: His "Datumsoria" framework is an original theoretical contribution, not just borrowed critical theory. It's a genuinely interesting concept for thinking about perception in the information age. Relates to Amber's interest in cosmotechnics — Datumsoria is a kind of Chinese-situated information philosophy.

3. **AI Critique from Art**: His AI Delivered curatorial essays are among the most philosophically rigorous pieces of AI-art criticism from a curatorial voice. He deploys Turing, Searle, Heidegger, Thacker, Zylinska, Hal Foster, Daniel Dennett together in a coherent framework — it's not name-dropping, it's actual intellectual synthesis.

4. **Institutional Architecture**: He built something durable — CAC is now 10+ years old and internationally recognized, which is rare for a Chinese media art institution. He did this by forming strategic alliances (ZKM, HeK, Rhizome, Art Center Nabi) rather than by fundraising from Chinese tech companies.

5. **Net.art History from the Inside**: His "Sideways" essay (2020) reveals he was genuinely present in the New York net.art community in the 1990s–2000s. He knew Thing.net, JODI, Vuk Ćosić from that era. This gives him an unusual historical memory that few current curators have.

## Research Taste Analysis (⭐⭐⭐)

### Conceptual Strategy
Zhang Ga's conceptual method is **framework multiplication via concept-coining**:
- He takes a theoretical concept (datum + sensoria → Datumsoria; abject + AI → AI abject; biology + media → bio/media entanglement) and then builds exhibition programmes around the *investigation* of that concept rather than illustration of a fixed position.
- He deploys **philosophical heterodoxy**: Turing alongside Heidegger, Hal Foster alongside Eugene Thacker, Shannon alongside Simondon — but it's not eclectic; it's deliberately using analytical philosophy (Searle's Chinese Room, Dennett) to interrogate continental theory.
- He writes in the mode of **provocation-first**: each curatorial essay opens with a crisis (the abject nature of AI, the failure of Turing's dream, the entanglement of carbon and silicon) and then uses artworks as evidence or counterevidence.

### Career Architecture
**The triumvirate of positions**: He simultaneously holds a curatorial role (CAC), an academic role (CAFA Distinguished Professor, formerly Tsinghua, formerly Parsons), and an editorial/intellectual role (Leonardo Books). This triple positioning means he has:
- Institutional power (the museum)
- Intellectual credibility (the university)
- Publishing reach (MIT Press / Leonardo)

**Strategic migration pattern**: NYC → Beijing/Shanghai (Parsons → NAMOC consulting → CAC). He built his international network while at Parsons, then returned to China with that network as capital. The NAMOC triennial series (2008, 2011, 2014) gave him a platform to exercise international curatorial ambition *within* Chinese state infrastructure — then CAC gave him freedom to pursue a genuinely independent programme.

**Coalition building**: Every major exhibition is co-produced with at least one prestigious international institution. This is a deliberate positioning strategy: it makes CAC look less "regional" and gives Zhang Ga's curatorial vision international validation.

### Key Moves (Chronological)
1. **Parsons Associate Professor (before 2007)** — Built NYC-based art/tech intellectual network while embedded in New York media art scene. Absorbed net.art history firsthand.
2. **NAMOC Consulting Curator + triennial series (2007–2014)** — *Synthetic Times* (2008), *Translife* (2011), *thingworld* (2014). Established himself as the most internationally-connected media art curator within Chinese institutional context. Each triennial brought major international artists to Beijing; each one built his ZKM/HeK/Leonardo/Rhizome relationships.
3. **CAC Artistic Director (2015)** — Takes over a small institution (founded 2013) and repositions it as intellectually ambitious. Immediately establishes international co-production model.
4. **Datumsoria framework (2016)** — Coins his concept; gets ZKM and Nam June Paik Art Center to co-present. This travels the exhibition to three venues: Shanghai → Karlsruhe → Seoul.
5. **Datumsoria at ZKM (2017–2018)** — Zhang Ga curating *at ZKM* (not just with ZKM) is a significant escalation. He's now a trusted curator operating in one of Europe's most prestigious media art institutions.
6. **We=Link (2020)** — Transforms the COVID crisis into a foundational online art platform. Gets 12 top-tier international institutions to simultaneously co-host. This establishes CAC as the Chinese node of a global art network. The platform has three iterations (2020, 2020–21, 2023), meaning it outlasted the crisis.
7. **AI Delivered + extended essay (2021)** — Produces his most substantial intellectual writing. Positions himself as not just a curator but a theoretical voice on AI and art.
8. **Entangled: bio/media (2022–23)** — Moves into bioart/bioinformatics territory with Thacker's *Biomedia* as framework. A 6-month living exhibition with 4 progressive chapters.

### What Amber Can Learn
1. **The "concept coin then exhibition" strategy**: Zhang Ga creates a concept (*Datumsoria*, *AI Delivered*, *Entangled*) and then builds a curatorial programme to investigate it. The concept comes first and structures the exhibition, not the other way around. Amber can use this — coin a conceptual frame, then invite collaborators into it.

2. **The tripled institutional position**: Academic + curatorial + editorial = intellectual security. Each reinforces the others. When the curation fails, the academic credential holds. When the academic writing is too abstract, the curatorial practice grounds it.

3. **Network as legitimacy infrastructure**: We=Link is smart: 12 institutions co-presenting an online exhibition means the exhibition is automatically "international" and "institutional" even though it exists only in browsers. Amber's work on distributed/online platforms could use a similar co-institutional structure to gain legitimacy without a single high-prestige venue.

4. **Return migration as career move**: Building credentials in NYC/US before returning to China (or London → somewhere else) is a strategic amplification loop. The prestige of the outgoing context is reinvested on return.

5. **The "intellectual present in crisis" mode**: Both *We=Link* (COVID) and *AI Delivered* (AI panic) are responses to contemporary crises that deploy the crisis as curatorial occasion rather than merely commenting on it. Amber can adopt this posture: find the crisis in the present that's *most relevant* to your practice, then use an exhibition/platform as the site of investigation.
