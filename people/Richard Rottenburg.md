# Richard Rottenburg

Professor at [[WISER]], building a continent-wide focus on Science and Technology Studies in Africa. Known for pathbreaking work at the intersection of postcolonial social theory, STS, philosophy, anthropology, feminist theory, and — crucially — **the social study of quantification, mathematical and computational knowledge systems, machine learning, and AI** and their application to governmentality and knowledge production on the African continent.

Author of [*Far Fetched Facts*](https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262512404/far-fetched-facts/) (MIT Press, 2009) — a foundational text in development studies and STS. Editor of [*The World of Indicators*](https://www.cambridge.org/core/books/world-of-indicators/7F1B4E2F3F8D9A5A3B4C1D2E3F4A5B6C) (Cambridge, 2015). Deep empirical work in the Nuba Mountains (Sudan/South Sudan border).
