# Yto Barrada يتو برادة

Moroccan-French artist based between New York and Tangier. Founder of the **Cinémathèque de Tanger** (2006), a cinema and cultural center in Tangier. Works in photography, film, sculpture, and textiles — often exploring decolonization, Moroccan modernity, childhood, and vernacular knowledge. One of the most significant artists from the Moroccan/North African contemporary art scene. Featured in [[Meriem Bennani]]'s film *Bouchra* (2024).

## Domains

- Documentary/archive photography
- Postcolonial theory through art
- Cultural infrastructure building (Cinémathèque de Tanger)
- Textiles, children's culture, pedagogical objects

## Key Relationships

- [[Meriem Bennani]] — generational elder figure; starred (voiced a character) in Bennani's *Bouchra* (2024)
- Pace Gallery (represented by)

## Communities

- **Cinémathèque de Tanger** ([cinemathequedetanger.com](https://cinemathequedetanger.com/)) — founded 2006; nonprofit cinema, library, cultural center in Tangier

## Why Interesting

Key node in Moroccan contemporary art world. Her relationship to [[Meriem Bennani]] signals generational continuity in how Moroccan artists build institutional infrastructure as well as art practice. The Cinémathèque de Tanger is a model for artist-run cultural infrastructure in the Global South.
