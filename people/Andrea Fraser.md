# Andrea Fraser

Institutional critique performance artist. ISP alum. Now UCLA professor + ISP faculty. Pioneer of institutional critique in the US — work that uses performance to expose the social/economic underpinnings of art institutions.

## Domains
Institutional critique, performance art, feminist art theory, museum/gallery critique.

## Key Works
- *Museum Highlights* (1989) — posed as museum tour guide at Philadelphia Museum of Art, scripted from Kant + poverty essays
- *Official Welcome* (2001) — mimics art award ceremony language; strips down mid-performance
- *Little Frank and His Carp* (2001)
- *Untitled* (2003)

## Awards & Fellowships
- National Endowment for the Arts Visual Arts Fellowship (1991)
- Anonymous Was A Woman Fellowship (2012)
- Wolfgang Hahn Prize (2013)
- [Oskar Kokoschka Prize](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oskar_Kokoschka_Prize) (2016)

## Key Communities
- [[Whitney Independent Study Program]] — alum + current faculty
- UCLA School of Arts and Architecture (professor + area head, Interdisciplinary Studio)

## Why Interesting
Institutional critique tradition directly relevant to Amber's interest in critiquing AI-art institutions, museum/tech industry relationships, and the social conditions of art production. Fraser's method — using performance to expose institutional dynamics — is a model for art-theoretical practice.
