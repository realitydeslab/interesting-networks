# Tavia Nyong'o

Professor of Theatre at Columbia University. Member of [[Practicing Refusal Collective]]. Performance studies scholar whose work spans Black queer aesthetics, afro-fabulation, speculative fiction, and punk music. One of the key theorists of "afro-fabulation" — Black speculative world-making.

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-27:** Stub created during Practicing Refusal Collective deep research.

## Key Works
- *[Afro-Fabulations: The Queer Drama of Black Life](https://nyupress.org/9781479800131/afro-fabulations/)* (NYU Press, 2019) — on "afro-fabulation" as a mode of Black speculative performance and life-making; weaves together Sun Ra, Nina Simone, Kara Walker, and queer of color theory
- *[The Amalgamation Waltz: Race, Performance, and the Ruses of Memory](https://www.upress.umn.edu/book-division/books/the-amalgamation-waltz)* (Minnesota UP, 2009) — Black performance and the contested memory of racial mixing in the US

## Why Interesting
"Afro-fabulation" is a concept with direct resonance to Amber's interests in speculative design and alternative futures. Nyong'o grounds world-making in Black queer performance rather than tech/design — a different disciplinary angle on similar questions.
