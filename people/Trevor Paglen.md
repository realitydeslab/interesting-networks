# Trevor Paglen

Artist, geographer, author. MacArthur Fellow (2017). Makes the invisible operations of military and corporate power visible through experimental geography, limit telephotography, and AI critique. Pioneer of surveillance art who uses rigorous geographic methods combined with art-making to expose classified landscapes, surveillance satellites, and AI training datasets.

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Complete deep research. Extensive global exhibition schedule through 2026. Major institutional representation through [[Pace Gallery]]. Active art-science collaboration with [[Kate Crawford]].

## Background
Born 1974. **BA Religious Studies, [University of California at Berkeley](https://www.berkeley.edu/)** (1998) — foundation in critical thinking about belief systems and power structures that underlies his work on state secrecy.

**MFA, [School of the Art Institute of Chicago](https://www.saic.edu/)** (2002) — where he developed conceptual art practice combining documentation with abstraction.

**PhD Geography, [University of California at Berkeley](https://www.berkeley.edu/)** (2008) — rare combination of rigorous academic geographic training with art practice. His dissertation work on classified landscapes became the foundation for his art.

Lived in Berkeley Student Cooperative (Chateau, Fenwick, and Rochdale co-ops) during PhD studies. Splits his time among **Berlin, New York, and Berkeley, California** — strategic positioning in global art capitals and academic base.

Coined the term **"Experimental Geography"** to describe practices coupling experimental cultural production with critical human geography. The 2009 book *Experimental Geography: Radical Approaches to Landscape, Cartography, and Urbanism* is largely inspired by his work.

## Domains
**Surveillance Infrastructure** — "Limit Telephotography" series uses telescopes + cameras to photograph secret prisons and military bases at impossible distances. "The Other Night Sky" documents classified satellites orbiting Earth with amateur astronomers.

**AI & Machine Vision** — "Training Humans" with [[Kate Crawford]] at Fondazione Prada examined AI training datasets. ImageNet Roulette revealed biases in AI classification systems. Recent work explores what AI systems "see" when they look at the world.

**Classified Landscapes** — Extensive documentation of CIA detention facilities, NSA headquarters, underwater internet cables, and other infrastructure of state power typically hidden from public view.

**Space Art** — **[Orbital Reflector](https://www.nevadaart.org/exhibition/trevor-paglen-orbital-reflector/)** (2018) launched first "purely artistic" satellite into space via SpaceX — inflatable mylar sculpture intended to be visible from Earth.

## Key Works

- **[*Torture Taxi*](https://www.mhpbooks.com/books/torture-taxi/)** (2006, co-authored with [A.C. Thompson](https://www.propublica.org/people/ac-thompson)) — first comprehensive book on CIA extraordinary rendition program
- **[*Blank Spots on the Map: The Dark Geography of the Pentagon's Secret World*](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/302310/blank-spots-on-the-map-by-trevor-paglen/)** (Dutton, 2009) — broader examination of military secrecy
- **[*The Last Pictures*](https://www.ucpress.edu/book.php?isbn=9780520275003)** (UC Press, 2012) — collection of 100 images launched on EchoStar XVI satellite as repository for future civilizations
- **[*I Could Tell You But Then You Would Have to be Destroyed by Me*](https://www.mhpbooks.com/books/i-could-tell-you/)** (2007) — exploration of classified military programs through unit patches and memorabilia
- **"Training Humans"** (2019) — major exhibition with [[Kate Crawford]] at **[Fondazione Prada](https://www.fondazioneprada.org/)**, Milan, examining AI training datasets and algorithmic bias
- **ImageNet Roulette** — interactive artwork revealing biases in ImageNet classification system by having users upload photos and see how AI classifies them (often problematically)
- **[Autonomy Cube](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Autonomy_Cube)** — sculpture with [[Jacob Appelbaum]] placing Tor network relays in art museums, bridging activism and institutions

## Research Taste Analysis
Paglen operates on multiple strategic axes that offer lessons for Amber:

### Conceptual Strategy — Academic Rigor as Artistic Method
- **Geographic training as artistic tool**: PhD in Geography provides methodological foundation most artists lack. Not just "interdisciplinary" but genuine dual expertise.
- **Documentary + Abstraction paradox**: Images function simultaneously as evidence and art objects. The extreme difficulty/impossibility of his photos (classified bases, satellites) is both conceptual gesture and technical achievement.
- **Language coining**: "Experimental Geography," "Limit Telephotography" — creates terminology that becomes citeable and builds academic legitimacy.

### Career Architecture — Institution Building Through Art
- **Cross-sector validation**: Military/intelligence expertise (through research) + art world success (MacArthur, Pace Gallery) + academic credibility (geography PhD) = unique position no one else occupies.
- **Infrastructure as subject and method**: Doesn't just critique surveillance — uses actual surveillance tools (telescopes, amateur radio networks) to make the work.
- **Exhibition as research publication**: "Training Humans" wasn't just art show but genuine research contribution to AI ethics discourse.

### Methodology Signature — Making the Impossible Visible
- **Technical innovation as conceptual strategy**: Limit telephotography required inventing new photographic techniques. The technical impossibility is the artistic point.
- **Collaboration with non-art communities**: Works with amateur astronomers, investigative journalists, tech researchers — builds expertise networks outside art world.
- **Documentation + Speculation**: Rigorous documentation of existing systems + speculative interventions (Orbital Reflector in space).

### Key Moves (chronological)
1. **Geographic PhD (2008)** — Built academic credibility and methodological foundation while developing art practice. Gave him unique research tools.

2. **"Torture Taxi" (2006)** — First major book combined journalism + art + research. Established him as serious investigator of state power, not just artist.

3. **MacArthur Fellowship (2017)** — Mainstream recognition that surveillance art is legitimate form of scholarship and social contribution.

4. **"Training Humans" with Kate Crawford (2019)** — Major collaboration bridged art world and AI research. Made AI datasets visible through art exhibition at major institution.

5. **Orbital Reflector (2018)** — Most ambitious project: actual satellite in space. Art as infrastructure, not just representation.

### What Amber Can Learn
- **Academic credentials can be artistic assets**: Paglen's PhD isn't separate from his art — it's the foundation. Rigorous training in one field (geography) becomes unique artistic method.
- **Make the technical constraint the conceptual point**: The extreme difficulty of his photographs IS the artwork. Technical limitations become artistic meaning.
- **Collaborate with experts outside art world**: His best work involves amateur astronomers, investigative journalists, AI researchers. Art world connections are necessary but not sufficient.
- **Build expertise that no one else has**: Paglen occupies unique position between geography, art, surveillance research, space technology. Hard to replicate but shows value of deep specialization.
- **Document first, speculate second**: Rigorous documentation of existing power structures provides foundation for speculative interventions.
