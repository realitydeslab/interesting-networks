# Trevor Paglen

American artist, geographer. MacArthur Fellow (2017). Work on surveillance, AI, and hidden infrastructure.

## Key Books/Papers
- *Invisible: Covert Operations and Classified Landscapes* (2010)
- "Training Humans" (2019) — exhibition with [[Kate Crawford]] at Fondazione Prada, examining AI training datasets
- *Orbital Reflector* (2018) — satellite launched as art object
- "ImageNet Roulette" — revealed biases in AI classification

## Key Communities
- Contemporary art world (major galleries)

## Key Venues
- Venice Biennale, Whitney Biennial, Documenta
- [[Ars Electronica]]
- Fondazione Prada (with Crawford)

## Key Relationships
- [[Kate Crawford]] — collaborator (Training Humans, ImageNet Roulette)
- [[Hito Steyerl]] — peer (AI, images, politics)
- [[Vladan Joler]] — peer (infrastructure visualization)

## Why Interesting
MacArthur Fellow doing extraordinary work on the hidden infrastructure of AI and surveillance. "Training Humans" made AI bias visceral. ImageNet Roulette forced a reckoning with how AI sees people. Orbital Reflector — launching art into orbit — is audacious.
