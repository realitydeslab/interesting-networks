# James Evans

Max Palevsky Professor of Sociology at University of Chicago. Director of [Knowledge Lab](https://www.knowledgelab.org/). Faculty Director, [Masters in Computational Social Science](https://macss.uchicago.edu/). External Professor, [[Santa Fe Institute]]. Studies how collectives — human, machine, and hybrid — think, know, and create. His work sits at the exact intersection of sociology, AI, complexity science, and the science of science.

## Background
- Unusual trajectory: BA in **Anthropology** at Brigham Young, then switched to **sociology** at Stanford (MA, PhD). The anthropology training shows — he approaches AI and knowledge systems with an ethnographer's eye.
- Post-doc at **Harvard Business School** (Negotiation, Organizations, and Markets group) — explains the organizational/institutional lens.
- Before academia, **started a private high school** focused on project-based arts education. This is a telling detail — he's not a pure academic; he has a practitioner's sensibility about how learning happens.
- At Chicago since ~2005. Built Knowledge Lab from scratch into a major research centre.
- His intellectual question is fundamentally **sociological**: how does collective cognition work? How do ideas emerge from social structures? But he applies it to everything — science, technology, AI, religion, gossip.
- Now increasingly focused on **AI as both subject and tool** — studying how AI agents form collectives, while using AI to study human knowledge.

## Domains
- **Science of Science** — quantitative study of how science works, what makes discoveries disruptive, how team structure affects innovation
- **Collective Intelligence** — how groups (human, machine, hybrid) think and know

- **AI Societies** — emergent behavior in multi-agent AI systems, collective consciousness in LLMs
- **Computational Creativity** — can algorithms be genuinely creative? What conditions produce novelty?
- **Complementary AI** — AI that augments rather than replaces human cognition
- **Digital Minds** — understanding how AI systems think, their cognitive architecture
- **Metaknowledge** — studying knowledge about knowledge itself
