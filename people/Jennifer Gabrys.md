# Jennifer Gabrys

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Stub created during Feral Atlas deep research. HIGH PRIORITY for full research.

Professor of Sociology at the University of Cambridge. Pioneer of **digital and computational environmental humanities**. Works on how environmental sensing technology, IoT devices, and citizen science platforms reshape ecological knowledge and governance. Field report contributor to **Feral Atlas** (plastic bags in London environments).

Author of *Program Earth: Environmental Sensing Technology and the Making of a Computational Planet* (University of Minnesota Press, 2016) — one of the most important books at the intersection of computation, ecology, and power. Also wrote *Digital Rubbish: A Natural History of Electronics* (2011) and *How to Do Things with Sensors* (2019).

**Direct relevance to Amber**: bridges computational sensing, environmental knowledge, and more-than-human agency. Works on questions of what it means for environments to "compute" themselves through distributed sensor networks — which parallels ALife questions of distributed cognition.

## Key Works
- **[*Program Earth: Environmental Sensing Technology and the Making of a Computational Planet*](https://www.upress.umn.edu/9780816694815/program-earth)** (University of Minnesota Press, 2016) — Masterwork. Examines how sensors, satellite, and citizen-sensing technologies are transforming what counts as environmental knowledge. Argues that environments are increasingly "programmed" through computational systems.
- **[*How to Do Things with Sensors*](https://www.upress.umn.edu/9781517908157/how-to-do-things-with-sensors)** (University of Minnesota Press, 2019) — Practical and theoretical guide to sensor-based environmental research. Covers air quality monitoring, citizen science, and the political dimensions of sensing.
- **[*Digital Rubbish: A Natural History of Electronics*](https://press.umich.edu/Books/D/Digital-Rubbish)** (University of Michigan Press, 2011) — On electronic waste and the material ecology of digital devices.
- **Field Report in *Feral Atlas*** (2020) — On plastic bag ecologies in London environments.
