# Guy Ben-Ary

Bioartist and researcher based at SymbioticA, University of Western Australia. Works with living neurons, cultured biological tissue, and wet computing as artistic medium. Co-creator of *Revivification* (2025), featured on the cover of *Technoetic Arts* Vol 23.2 (2026) — cultured living neurons as installation. Known for CellF (2016), a neural synthesizer made from his own stem-cell-derived neurons.

## Domains
- Bioart / living systems art
- Cultured neurons as artistic medium
- Wet computing / moistmedia
- Machine consciousness (from biology side)

## Key Works
- [*CellF*](https://www.guybenary.com/work/cellf/) (2016) — neural synthesizer made from stem-cell-derived neurons; the "world's first neural synthesizer"
- *Revivification* (2025, with Nathan Thompson, Stuart Hodgetts, Matt Gingold) — installation; living cultured neurons; featured in *Technoetic Arts* 23.2

## Key Communities
- [[SymbioticA]] (UWA) — the bioart research centre
- [[Technoetics]] — published in *Technoetic Arts* journal

## Why Interesting
Works at the intersection of living biology and machine/art systems — directly relevant to Amber's machine consciousness/death themes, but from the bioart side. CellF is a machine whose "mind" is literally living neurons. This is an extreme case of the living/non-living boundary that Amber's ALife and machine death work orbits.
