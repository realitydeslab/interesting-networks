# Guy Ben-Ary

Perth-based bio-artist. Early collaborator with TC&A on Pig Wings, Semi-Living Worry Dolls, Stone Age of Biology, and Extra Ear ¼ Scale. UWA / SymbioticA affiliated. Best known for **cellF** — described as the world's first neural synthesizer: a biological brain made from human induced pluripotent stem cells (iPSCs) that listens to music and responds in real time by generating neural signals converted to sound. Also: **Silent Barrage** and **MEART**. Works at the intersection of neuroscience and musical performance using living neurons as the performer.

## Key Works
- **cellF** (2015) — neural synthesizer. Human iPSCs grown into a neural network in a petri dish. Listens to human musicians and "improvises" by firing electrical signals converted to modular synthesizer output. Live performance worldwide.
- **Silent Barrage** — with TC&A. Neural-robotic installation.
- **MEART** (Multi-Electrode Array ART) — with TC&A. One of the earliest "neural network" artworks using actual cortical neurons.
- **[Pigs Wings](https://tcaproject.net/portfolio/pigs-wings/)** (2000–2001) — with TC&A. Wings grown from pig bone marrow. Developed at Harvard.
- **[Semi-Living Worry Dolls](https://tcaproject.net/portfolio/worry-dolls/)** (2001) — with TC&A. First tissue-engineered sculptures presented alive in a gallery (Ars Electronica 2000).

## Why Interesting
Uses actual living neurons — not simulations — as creative agents. cellF raises profound questions: is the neural network improvising? Is it creative? Who is the author? The work is more rigorous than most "AI art" because the "AI" is literally a living brain.
