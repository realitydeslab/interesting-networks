# Dylan Robinson

Stó:lō scholar, artist, and author. Professor at UBC. Author of *[Hungry Listening: Resonant Theory for Indigenous Sound Studies](https://www.upress.umn.edu/book-division/books/hungry-listening)* (University of Minnesota Press, 2020) — develops "hungry listening" as a decolonial framework for understanding how settler audiences consume Indigenous sound. Co-curator with [[Candice Hopkins]] of *Soundings: An Exhibition in Five Parts* (2020). Contributor to *Native Visual Sovereignty* reader.

## Why Interesting
"Hungry listening" is a genuinely interesting concept — how settler/colonial audiences consume Indigenous cultural production with appetitive desire. This is a form of epistemological critique with potential resonance for how AI systems "consume" human cultural data, and how machine perception relates to colonial extraction logics.
