# Arturo Rosenblueth (1900–1970)

Mexican physiologist and cybernetician. Close collaborator of Norbert Wiener. Co-authored the pivotal 1943 paper **"Behavior, Purpose and Teleology"** with Wiener and Julian Bigelow — the text that formally proposed that goal-directed behavior in animals and machines could be understood through the same feedback framework. The first scientific paper to give teleology a mechanistic explanation.

## Key Works
- **Rosenblueth, Wiener & Bigelow, "Behavior, Purpose and Teleology"** (1943, *Philosophy of Science*) — [DOI: 10.1086/286878](https://doi.org/10.1086/286878) — the foundational paper; coined "teleological mechanism"; argued that feedback loops explain purposive behavior without invoking vitalism

## Why Interesting
The 1943 paper with Wiener and Bigelow is where the cybernetics program begins as a unified claim. It's also one of the rare examples of a Latin American scientist being foundational to a major field later claimed by US/European institutions. Rosenblueth's physiological perspective gave cybernetics its biological grounding.
