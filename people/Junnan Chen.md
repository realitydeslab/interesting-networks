# Junnan Chen

Assistant Professor of Media Theory at [[NYU Shanghai IMA]] and [AIC](https://aic.shanghai.nyu.edu) affiliate. PhD from Princeton University (joint Interdisciplinary Humanities and East Asian Studies). Researches media theory, feminist theory, cybernetic aesthetics, Cold War Japan, extraterrestrial imaginaries, philosophy of time. Also a filmmaker.

## Key Works
- *Expressing Time: Cybernetic Aesthetics and Cold War Japan* — book manuscript in progress. Explores phenomenologies of time in Japanese artworks as transfigurations of Cold War conflicts.
- "The Imperishables: Somatic Remediation, Femininity, and Plasticity in Ōshima Nagisa's Urban Critique" — *[positions: Asia critique](https://read.dukeupress.edu/positions)* 32(2), 2024
- "Mediatic Cosmology: Taki Kōji's Urban Semiotics" — in progress
- Working on documentary about Assassin's Creed and video essay on cosmic exoticism & planetary UFO imaginaries
