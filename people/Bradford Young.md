# Bradford Young

Cinematographer. One of the most significant DPs working in contemporary film. Emerged from the same Howard University / L.A. Rebellion lineage as [[Arthur Jafa]] — trained under Haile Gerima. Known for a painterly, chiaroscuro visual style influenced by Rembrandt and Black vernacular photography. 

Key films: *Arrival* (Denis Villeneuve, 2016) — Academy Award nomination for Best Cinematography; *Solo: A Star Wars Story* (Ron Howard, 2018); *Selma* (Ava DuVernay, second unit DP); *Ain't Them Bodies Saints* (David Lowery, 2013); *Mother of George* (Andrew Dosunmu, 2013, photographed entirely in candlelight).

## Why Interesting
Young represents the generation that took the BVI principles Jafa articulated and applied them within studio film production. He has carried the visual language of Black cinematography — low light, "painting with shadow" — into mainstream Hollywood in a way that remains formally distinctive. His Howard University training connects him directly to the Gerima/Jafa lineage. His work on *Arrival* is the most visible example of a Black visual aesthetic in contemporary sci-fi.
