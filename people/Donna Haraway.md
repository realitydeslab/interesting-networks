# Donna Haraway

American feminist philosopher and STS scholar (born 1944), professor emerita at UC Santa Cruz. Author of *A Cyborg Manifesto* (1985) and *Staying with the Trouble* (2016). One of the most influential theorists of the human-machine-animal boundary. Her cyborg disrupts biopolitics by refusing the clean separation of organism and technology; her multispecies thinking opens care ethics beyond the human.

## Domains
Feminist STS, cyborg theory, multispecies thinking, primatology, posthumanism, care ethics, situated knowledge.

## Key Works
- [*A Cyborg Manifesto*](https://sites.evergreen.edu/politicalshakespeares/wp-content/uploads/sites/226/2015/12/Haraway-Cyborg-Manifesto-2.pdf) (1985/1991) — human/machine/animal hybridity; socialist feminist politics
- [*Primate Visions*](https://www.routledge.com/Primate-Visions-Gender-Race-and-Nature-in-the-World-of-Modern-Science/Haraway/p/book/9780415902946) (1989) — science as storytelling; race/gender in primatology
- [*Modest_Witness*](https://www.routledge.com/Modest_WitnessSecond_MillenniumFemaleManMeets_OncoMouseTM/Haraway/p/book/9780415912457) (1997) — technoscience politics
- [*The Companion Species Manifesto*](https://prickly-paradigm.com/product/companion-species-manifesto/) (2003) — human-dog kinship
- [*Staying with the Trouble*](https://www.dukeupress.edu/staying-with-the-trouble) (2016) — multispecies kin-making; Chthulucene; care politics. URL: https://www.dukeupress.edu/staying-with-the-trouble
