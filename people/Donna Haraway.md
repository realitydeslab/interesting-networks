# Donna Haraway

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Initial deep research. Comprehensive profile created with full CV, collaborator network, and influence analysis.

American scholar emerita. The foundational theorist of posthumanism, feminist technoscience, and multispecies thinking. Her "Cyborg Manifesto" (1985) redefined technology-nature relations; her later work on companion species and the Chthulucene provides conceptual frameworks that underpin the entire more-than-human design movement. First tenured professor in feminist theory in the United States.

## Background
Born 1944 in Denver to Frank O. Haraway (sports writer, The Denver Post) and Dorothy Mcguire Haraway (Irish Catholic background). Mother died when Haraway was 16. Raised Catholic, attended St. Mary's Academy in Cherry Hills Village—Catholicism profoundly influenced her understanding of the material-symbolic nexus through the eucharist.

**Education trajectory:** Zoology major at [Colorado College](https://www.coloradocollege.edu/) on full Boettcher Scholarship, with philosophy and English minors. Post-graduation Fulbright at [Fondation Teilhard de Chardin](https://www.teilhard-de-chardin.org/), Paris, studying evolutionary philosophy and theology. PhD in Biology at [Yale University](https://yale.edu/) (1972), dissertation on metaphor in developmental biology later published as *Crystals, Fabrics, and Fields* (1976).

**Career progression:** Women's studies and history of science at [University of Hawaiʻi](https://www.hawaii.edu/) (1971-1974), [Johns Hopkins University](https://www.jhu.edu/) (1974-1980). [University of California, Santa Cruz](https://www.ucsc.edu/) from 1980, where she became the first tenured professor in feminist theory in the US. Joint appointment in [History of Consciousness](https://histcon.ucsc.edu/) and Feminist Studies departments.

## Domains
Feminist technoscience studies, science and technology studies (STS), multispecies relations, primatology studies, cyborg theory, posthumanism, environmental humanities, companion species theory, speculative fabulation, situated knowledges.

## Key Works
- **[*A Cyborg Manifesto*](https://www.marxists.org/subject/women/authors/haraway/cyborg.htm)** (1985) — The landmark essay that redefined feminism for the digital age. Published initially as "A Manifesto for Cyborgs: Science, Technology, and Socialist-Feminism in the 1980s" in *Socialist Review*. Later expanded in *Simians, Cyborgs and Women* (1991). Argues that the cyborg as boundary figure dissolves nature/culture, mind/body, male/female dualisms. Essential reading for anyone working at technology-society intersections.

- **[*Primate Visions: Gender, Race, and Nature in the World of Modern Science*](https://www.routledge.com/Primate-Visions-Gender-Race-and-Nature-in-the-World-of-Modern-Science/Haraway/p/book/9780415902946)** (1989) — Feminist analysis of primatology that reveals how scientific narratives about primates reflect cultural assumptions about gender, race, and power. Won the [American Sociological Association](https://www.asanet.org/) Robert K. Merton Award (1992). Shows how female primatologists offer radically different observations than male-dominated field.

- **[*Modest_Witness@Second_Millennium.FemaleMan©Meets_OncoMouse™*](https://www.routledge.com/ModestWitnessSecondMillenniumFemaleManMeetsOncoMouse-Feminism-and/Haraway/p/book/9780415912457)** (1997) — Analysis of technoscience through the figure of the genetically engineered OncoMouse. Winner of [Society for Social Studies of Science](https://www.4sonline.org/) Ludwik Fleck Prize (1999). Collaborative work with feminist artist [Lynn Randolph](https://www.lynnrandolph.com/) (1990-1996).

- **[*The Companion Species Manifesto: Dogs, People, and Significant Otherness*](https://press.uchicago.edu/ucp/books/book/distributed/C/bo3628482.html)** (2003) — Extension of cyborg thinking to human-animal relations. Arguments for "significant otherness" and coevolution. Personal document exploring her relationship with her dogs as models for multispecies ethics.

- **[*When Species Meet*](https://www.upress.umn.edu/book-division/books/when-species-meet)** (2008) — Develops companion species thinking. Analysis of human-animal relations through training, breeding, laboratory work, and domestication. Central text for multispecies studies and more-than-human design.

- **[*Staying with the Trouble: Making Kin in the Chthulucene*](https://www.dukeupress.edu/staying-with-the-trouble)** (2016) — Her response to the Anthropocene discourse. Proposes the "Chthulucene" as alternative periodization emphasizing ongoing multispecies entanglement rather than human dominance. Core argument: "make kin, not babies" as strategy for multispecies survival. Develops "tentacular thinking" and "response-ability." Widely cited across environmental humanities, design studies, and art theory.

- **[*Making Kin not Population: Reconceiving Generations*](https://press.uchicago.edu/ucp/books/book/distributed/M/bo28583407.html)** (2018) — Co-edited with [Adele Clarke](https://sociology.ucsf.edu/faculty/adele-clarke). Collection expanding "make kin not babies" framework with feminist population politics.

## Research Taste Analysis
**Conceptual Strategy — Boundary Figure Multiplication:**
Haraway's signature move is creating powerful boundary figures that dissolve established dualisms: cyborgs (nature/culture), companion species (human/animal), Chthulucene (human/geological time). Each figure does multiple theoretical work simultaneously — political, epistemological, aesthetic, ethical. Not metaphors but "material-semiotic" entities that reshape how we think and act.

**Methodological Signature — Situated Knowledge Production:**
Her "situated knowledges" (1988) framework revolutionized how we think about objectivity and perspective. She demonstrates this methodologically by always writing from specific embodied positions — as a dog trainer, as a former Catholic, as a biologist turned feminist theorist. Theory emerges from material practices rather than abstract reasoning.

**Career Architecture — Institutional Boundary-Crossing:**
Built career by refusing disciplinary boundaries: biology → history of science → feminist studies → science and technology studies → environmental humanities. At each stage, she imported methods and frameworks from unexpected sources. Created intellectual legitimacy for boundary-crossing through rigorous scholarship in each field she entered.

**Writing Strategy — Dense Etymological Play:**
Haraway's prose is famously dense and playful. She creates new concepts through etymological excavation (Chthulucene from Greek "chthonic" + "kainos") and wordplay (response-ability, companion species). This isn't ornamental — the linguistic work makes new thinking possible. Her writing performs the boundary-dissolving she theorizes.

### Key Moves (Chronological)
1. **PhD Biology → Feminist Theory (1970s):** Used rigorous biological training to critique masculinist bias in science. Gave feminist theory scientific credibility.
2. **"Cyborg Manifesto" (1985):** Intervention in socialist-feminist debates through technoscience framework. Perfect timing as personal computers entered households.
3. ***Primate Visions* (1989):** Applied feminist analysis to an entire scientific discipline. Showed how to do cultural studies of science without losing scientific rigor.
4. **Companion Species Turn (2000s):** Extended cyborg thinking to human-animal relations. Moved beyond technology to living relations.
5. **Chthulucene Concept (2016):** Provided alternative to Anthropocene discourse at moment of climate crisis urgency. Positioned multispecies thinking as climate response.

### What Amber Can Learn
1. **Boundary Figure Strategy:** Create powerful conceptual entities that do multiple kinds of work. Not just metaphors but "material-semiotic" tools that reshape thinking and practice.

2. **Etymological Concept Development:** Excavate language to create new theoretical tools. Haraway's neologisms (Chthulucene, response-ability) become widely adopted because they capture something previously unnamed.

3. **Methodological Boundary-Crossing:** Import frameworks from unexpected disciplines. Haraway's biology background gives her theory material grounding that pure cultural theory lacks.

4. **Situated Writing Voice:** Write from specific embodied knowledge rather than abstract perspective. Haraway's dog training, laboratory experience, and feminist positioning give her theory credibility and distinctiveness.

5. **Art-Theory Collaboration:** Work directly with artists (*Modest_Witness* with Lynn Randolph) and engage art institutions as theory dissemination venues. Haraway understood early that new concepts need aesthetic as well as analytical expression.
