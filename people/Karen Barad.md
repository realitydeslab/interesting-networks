# Karen Barad

Physicist-philosopher whose "agential realism" framework fuses quantum field theory with feminist epistemology — showing that matter and meaning don't precede their entanglement, they emerge through it. Arguably the most rigorous interdisciplinary thinker of the last 30 years: writes equations *and* deconstructs representationalism. Co-founder of UCSC's Science & Justice Research Center. The person who gave posthumanism a proper ontology.

## Background

Born 29 April 1956. Earned a doctorate in theoretical particle physics and quantum field theory at [Stony Brook University](https://www.stonybrook.edu/). Dissertation focused on computational methods for quantifying properties of quarks, fermions, and other particles using lattice gauge theory — this is real physics, not metaphor.

**Critically: Barad held a tenured appointment in a physics department before moving into more interdisciplinary spaces.** This matters enormously. They are not a humanist appropriating quantum physics — they are a theoretical physicist who turned their technical knowledge toward philosophical and feminist questions. The legitimacy this confers to their work is considerable, and they are careful to maintain it: *Meeting the Universe Halfway* (2007) contains an original discovery in theoretical physics that is largely unprecedented in a book categorized under "gender studies."

Current title: **Distinguished Professor of Feminist Studies, Philosophy, and History of Consciousness** at [UC Santa Cruz](https://www.ucsc.edu/). Three departments simultaneously — signals the scope of the project. They are also co-founder of the **UCSC Science & Justice Research Center** and the **Science & Justice Graduate Training Program**, which are the institutional infrastructure for their ideas.

Research has been supported by the **National Science Foundation**, the **Ford Foundation**, the **Hughes Foundation**, the **Irvine Foundation**, the **Mellon Foundation**, and the **National Endowment for the Humanities** — a rare combination of STEM and humanities funders that reflects the work's genuine reach across disciplines.

Barad uses **they/them** pronouns.

## The Full Framework: Agential Realism Explained

*Because the label is everywhere but the content is rarely explained.*

### Starting Point: The Problem with Representationalism

Barad's entire project begins with a rejection of **representationalism** — the assumption that the world consists of things-in-themselves, and that language, ideas, and knowledge are separate representations of that external reality. This is not just a philosophy-of-language point; Barad argues that representationalism is **built into the practice of science** and **built into humanist critique of science**. Both sides assume a pre-existing world of discrete objects that can then be known or represented. Both are wrong.

The alternative: **performativity**. Ideas, concepts, and practices don't represent the world — they *enact* it. Knowledge production is world-making, not world-reflecting.

### The Bohr Connection

Barad's key inspiration is [Niels Bohr](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Niels_Bohr), one of the founders of quantum physics, and specifically his **philosophy-physics** — his reflections on what quantum mechanics *means* for our understanding of reality and knowledge. 

Bohr's key insight: in quantum physics, you cannot separate the phenomenon being measured from the apparatus doing the measuring. The two are entangled. The "observer effect" isn't about consciousness changing the quantum system — it's about the physical entanglement of measurement apparatus and phenomenon. There is no pre-measurement object "out there" waiting to be found; the phenomenon is the product of the intra-action between the apparatus and the measured system.

Barad extends this from physics into a general philosophy: **phenomena are the ontological inseparability of intra-acting agencies**.

### The Key Concepts

**Intra-action** (vs. interaction): The neologism at the heart of everything. "Interaction" assumes pre-existing separate entities that then act upon each other. "Intra-action" means entities don't pre-exist their relations — they emerge *through* specific intra-actions. There is no "me" before I am in relation with something else. No "electron" before the apparatus creates the conditions for it to be measured. This is not idealism (the electron doesn't cease to exist without being observed) — it's a claim that **determinate properties** only emerge through specific material-discursive practices.

**Phenomena**: For Barad, phenomena are the basic ontological unit — not objects, not subjects, not even relations. Phenomena are "the ontological inseparability of intra-acting agencies." Objects and agencies emerge from phenomena, not the other way around.

**Material-discursive practices**: The apparatus that produces phenomena is always both material (physical, technological, institutional) and discursive (conceptual frameworks, linguistic practices, social norms). Matter and meaning don't exist independently — they co-emerge and co-constitute each other.

**Agential cuts**: Because everything is entangled with everything else, how can we know *anything* determinately? Through "agential cuts" — specific configurations of the apparatus that enact certain distinctions while excluding others. An agential cut creates the illusion of separation (subject/object, nature/culture, this/that) while remaining entangled with what it appears to separate. The cut produces "agential separability" — not absolute separation, but enacted distinctions within an irreducibly entangled field.

**Onto-epistem-ology** (or "ethico-onto-epistem-ology"): The neologism captures Barad's core claim that being, knowing, and ethics cannot be separated. How we know (epistemology) constitutes what is (ontology), and both are always already ethical (the cuts we make have consequences for what is excluded). Knowing is not neutral — it is a form of world-making that carries ethical responsibility.

**Diffraction**: Barad contrasts "diffraction" with "reflection." Reflection implies a mirror relationship — faithful reproduction of what's already there. Diffraction is a wave phenomenon: when waves pass through an aperture or around an obstacle, they spread and interfere, creating new patterns. Diffraction as methodology means reading texts and ideas through one another to produce new insights — not finding what's already there, but creating new patterns of difference that matter.

**The Void**: In later work, Barad rethinks the void not as empty space (Newtonian) but as charged with quantum vacuum fluctuations — inexhaustible dynamism, nothingness as plenum rather than absence. This connects to hauntology, colonial critique, and the question of justice: what is "nothing"? Who is made to be nothing?

### Connection to Bohr's Complementarity

Barad is technically precise about Bohr in a way most humanist readers are not: they carefully distinguish Bohr's principle of **complementarity** from Heisenberg's **uncertainty principle** (see Appendix B in *Meeting the Universe Halfway*). Complementarity says that certain properties (wave/particle, position/momentum) are mutually exclusive — you can set up an experiment to measure one OR the other, but not both. This isn't about observer disturbance; it's about the exhaustiveness of specific experimental arrangements. Barad uses this as a model for how different apparatus produce different phenomena — not as relativism, but as the material basis for specificity.

### What This Has to Do with Gender

The connection to feminist theory is not analogical but structural. Barad argues:

1. **Gender, race, and sexuality are not properties of pre-existing subjects** — they are enacted through material-discursive practices (institutions, medical apparatuses, legal systems, cultural norms). Just as the electron doesn't have a determinate position before measurement, bodies don't have determinate sex/gender before they are "intra-acted" with apparatuses (medical, legal, cultural).

2. **This doesn't mean gender is "constructed" in the sense of being arbitrary or unreal** — it's as real as quantum phenomena. The material consequences of specific intra-actions (medical interventions, legal categories, social practices) are real and have genuine effects on people's lives.

3. **The critique of representationalism applies to feminist critique too**: feminist theory that assumes women are a stable category to be represented is trapped in the same problem as the science it critiques. Posthumanist performativity offers a way out — not by denying that gender matters, but by asking how it comes to matter through specific intra-actions.

4. **Responsibility is built in**: because knowing is a form of intra-action that makes certain things matter and others not, all knowledge practices carry ethical obligations. Feminist critique isn't just about studying women — it's about attending to what is made to not matter, what is excluded by the agential cuts of dominant science.

## Key Works

### Books

**[*Meeting the Universe Halfway: Quantum Physics and the Entanglement of Matter and Meaning*](https://www.dukeupress.edu/meeting-the-universe-halfway)** (Duke University Press, 2007) — The main event. 544 pages developing agential realism through an engagement with Niels Bohr's philosophy-physics. Includes original theoretical physics work (unprecedented for a gender studies text). Three-part structure: (1) Entangled Beginnings [philosophy-physics foundations], (2) Intra-Actions Matter [agential realism developed], (3) Entanglements and Re(Con)figurations [applications]. Contains an original discovery in quantum physics in Chapter 7. Endorsements from Donna Haraway AND Arthur Zajonc (quantum physicist at Amherst). ISBN: 9780822339175.

**[*Agentieller Realismus: Über die Bedeutung materiell-diskursiver Praktiken*](https://www.suhrkamp.de/buecher/agentieller_realismus-karen_barad_47260.html)** (Suhrkamp, 2012) — German edition. Suhrkamp is the prestige publisher of critical theory in German — putting Barad in the Habermas/Adorno tradition. ISBN: 9783518260456.

**[*Verschränkungen*](https://www.merve.de/)** (*Entanglements*) (Merve, 2015) — German compilation of essays. Translated by Jennifer Sophia Theodor. 224 pages.

### Landmark Papers

**["Posthumanist Performativity: Toward an Understanding of How Matter Comes to Matter"](https://doi.org/10.1086/345321)** (*Signs*, 28(3), Spring 2003: 801-831) — The condensed manifesto. Argues against both "linguistic constructivism" (everything is discourse) and "naive realism" (matter is just matter). Develops the performativity framework from Judith Butler but pushes it into the material-discursive. One of the most cited papers in feminist theory. Reprinted widely.

**["Getting Real: Technoscientific Practices and the Materialization of Reality"](https://archive.org/web/20150220092124/http://www.open.ac.uk/researchprojects/iccm/library/39)** (*differences: A Journal of Feminist Cultural Studies*, 10(2), 1998: 87-128) — Early formulation. Develops the idea that reality is materialized through technoscientific practices, not just represented.

**["Agential Realism: Feminist Interventions in Understanding Scientific Practices"](https://www.routledge.com/The-Science-Studies-Reader/Biagioli/p/book/9780415918688)** (in *The Science Studies Reader*, ed. Mario Biagioli, Routledge, 1999) — Key early statement for the science studies community. The formulation "feminist interventions" signals the stakes.

**["On Touching – The Inhuman That Therefore I Am"](https://doi.org/10.1215/10407391-1892943)** (*differences*, 23(3), 2012: 206-223) — On touch, sensation, and quantum indeterminacy. The title riffs on Derrida. Asks: what does it mean for inhuman matter to touch and be touched?

**["Nature's Queer Performativity"](https://doi.org/10.7146/kkf.v0i1-2.28067)** (*Kvinder, Køn & Forskning*, 1-2, 2012: 25-53) — Atoms as "ultraqueer critters." What if queerness is not about the breach of nature/culture but is in the very nature of spacetimemattering? Connects queer theory with quantum indeterminacy.

**["Diffracting Diffraction: Cutting Together-Apart"](https://doi.org/10.1080/13534645.2014.927623)** (*Parallax*, 20(3), 2014: 168-187) — The methodology of diffraction developed as the core analytical practice of agential realism. How to read ideas through each other rather than representing them.

**["Troubling Time/s and Ecologies of Nothingness: Re-turning, Re-membering, and Facing the Incalculable"](https://doi.org/10.3898/NEWF:92.05.2017)** (*New Formations*, 92, 2017: 56-86) — Reads quantum field theory and Kyoko Hayashi's account of the Nagasaki bombing together. Nuclear colonialism, the politics of the void, temporal dis/junction. Most explicitly political major paper.

**["After the End of the World: Entangled Nuclear Colonialisms, Matters of Force, and the Material Force of Justice"](https://muse.jhu.edu/article/729449)** (*Theory & Event*, 22(3), 2019: 524-550) — Nuclear colonialism, entanglement, justice-to-come.

**["Quantum Entanglements and Hauntological Relations of Inheritance: Dis/continuities, Spacetime Enfoldings, and Justice-to-Come"](https://doi.org/10.3366/drt.2010.0206)** (*Derrida Today*, 3(2), 2010: 240-268) — Reads quantum physics through Derrida's hauntology and vice versa.

**["No Small Matter: Mushroom Clouds, Ecologies of Nothingness, and Strange Topologies of Spacetimemattering"](https://www.upress.umn.edu/9781517902377/arts-of-living-on-a-damaged-planet/)** (in *Arts of Living on a Damaged Planet*, ed. ## Key Relationships & Collaborators

**Direct intellectual relationships:**

- **[[Donna Haraway]]** ⭐⭐⭐ — UCSC colleague. Haraway is the most important relationship here. Barad extends Haraway's "situated knowledges" into an ontological framework — Haraway showed that all knowledge is situated (embedded in a perspective), Barad asks what kind of world must exist for situated knowledges to be possible. Haraway wrote the most sophisticated endorsement of *Meeting the Universe Halfway* (for Duke University Press): "Reality is not independent of our explorations of it; and reality is not a matter of opinion, but of the material consequences of some cuts and not others made in the fabric of the world." They are both at UCSC; the intellectual cross-pollination has been central to both projects. See [[Donna Haraway]] note.

- **[[Anna Tsing]]** ⭐⭐⭐ — also UCSC. Contributed to the *Arts of Living on a Damaged Planet* volume. Multispecies thinking, more-than-human ecologies. Tsing's concept of "assemblages" and Barad's "phenomena" operate in related registers.

- **Niels Bohr** (intellectual ancestor) — The explicit progenitor. Barad's relationship to Bohr is scholarly: they provide the most thorough reading of Bohr's philosophy-physics available in contemporary critical theory, including in areas where Bohr was unclear or contradictory.

- **Judith Butler** — Critical interlocutor. Barad extends and critiques Butler's performativity. Butler's *Gender Trouble* argues that gender is performed into existence through repeated acts. Barad accepts the performativity framework but argues Butler is still too linguistic — the "matter" of bodies cannot be reduced to discursive construction. Posthumanist performativity extends Butler's project into the material.

- **Michel Foucault** — Key source. Barad uses Foucault's concept of "apparatus" (dispositif) as the starting point for their own apparatus concept — material-discursive practices that produce subjects and objects. But Foucault's apparatus remains too discursive; Barad materializes it.

- **Jacques Derrida** — hauntology, differance, spacetimemattering. Barad reads Derrida through quantum physics: the void as hauntological, quantum indeterminacy as differing/deferral, the cut as both determining and haunted by what it excludes. The "Quantum Entanglements and Hauntological Relations" paper (2010) and the *Derrida Today* publication are key.

- **Nina Lykke** ⭐⭐ — feminist materialist theorist at Linköping University, Sweden. Co-author of chapter in *Bits of Life* (2008). Co-edited the *Chasing Technoscience* matrix series. One of the key figures in European feminist science studies. Probably worth a stub note.

- **Vicki Kirby** ⭐⭐ — feminist theorist at University of New South Wales. Endorses the *New Materialism* interviews volume. Kirby's work runs parallel to Barad's — *Telling Flesh* (1997) and *Quantum Anthropologies* — but takes a different theoretical path. Valuable critical interlocutor. Worth stub.

- **Rosi Braidotti** ⭐⭐ — feminist posthumanist theorist at Utrecht University. Featured alongside Barad in *New Materialism: Interviews & Cartographies*. Braidotti's posthumanism is more Deleuzian than Barad's quantum-inflected version, but they're the two major figures in European feminist posthumanism. [rosibraidotti.com](https://rosibraidotti.com/). Worth stub.

- **Rick Dolphijn and Iris van der Tuin** — interviewed Barad for *New Materialism: Interviews & Cartographies* (OHP, 2013). Van der Tuin is based at Utrecht University.

- **Nils Bubandt, Elaine Gan, Heather Swanson, Anna Tsing** — co-editors of *Arts of Living on a Damaged Planet* (2017), the volume where Barad contributed "No Small Matter." This is the UCSC multispecies cluster.

- **Andrew Pickering** — philosopher of science at University of Exeter, author of *The Mangle of Practice* (1995). Similar project of thinking scientific practice materially. Haraway's DUP blurb compares them.

- **Evelyn Fox Keller** — feminist philosopher of science. Overlapping intellectual territory (gender, biology, language/matter).

- **Bruno Latour** — Haraway and Barad are often compared to Latour's actor-network theory, though they resist it. Where Latour's actors "interact," Barad's phenomena "intra-act."

- **María Puig de la Bellacasa** ⭐⭐ — feminist STS scholar now at UCSC History of Consciousness. Author of *Matters of Care* (2017). Direct institutional colleague. See current UCSC presence. Worth stub.

- **Isabelle Stengers** — philosopher of science at Université Libre de Bruxelles. Strong intellectual affinity — Stengers' "Cosmopolitics" and Barad's agential realism both ask how scientific practices constitute their objects. Shared attention to the "lure" of practice.

## Venue Circuit Analysis (⭐⭐⭐)

Barad moves in a distinctive circuit that most academics don't navigate:

**1. The UCSC Cluster** — Feminist Studies + History of Consciousness + Science & Justice Center. This is their home base: the most important interdisciplinary humanities cluster in the US for feminist science studies. The circuit includes Donna Haraway, Anna Tsing, Heather Swanson, María Puig de la Bellacasa. A genuine intellectual community, not just proximity.

**2. European Critical Theory** — dOCUMENTA (13), Suhrkamp, Merve, German university lectures. Barad's work has been deeply engaged in German-speaking critical theory circles. The Suhrkamp edition (2012) and the dOCUMENTA (13) inclusion signal integration into continental thought at the highest level. The German feminist studies network (Nina Lykke's Linköping, van der Tuin's Utrecht) also engages heavily.

**3. Feminist Theory Journals** — *Signs*, *differences*, *Catalyst*, *Hypatia*. These are the prestige publications for feminist theory. Being on the advisory boards of *Catalyst* and *Signs* while publishing in *differences* gives Barad simultaneous authority and visibility.

**4. STS / Science Studies** — 4S conferences, History and Philosophy of Science venues, involvement in Lancaster University workshops. This circuit connects to science-studies scholars who are not necessarily feminist or humanist — Pickering, Latour's networks, etc.

**5. Physics-Adjacent** — The original discipline. Barad maintains enough physics credibility to get endorsements from Arthur Zajonc and to have their work reviewed in physics-adjacent journals. This is the credential that authorizes the entire project.

**6. Art-Theory World** — dOCUMENTA (13) is the signal moment. The *Lancaster matterrealities* workshop (2007) combined Barad's talk with art practices. The German Merve press (also publisher of Baudrillard, Virilio, Lyotard) signals crossover into art theory circuits.

**Why this circuit matters:** Barad builds from *legitimacy in physics* → to *authority in philosophy of physics* → to *intellectual leadership in feminist theory* → to *prestige in continental critical theory* → to *recognition in art-theory*. Each step requires the previous one. This is how you create a new intellectual territory that can't be dismissed by any of the constituent disciplines.

## Research Taste Analysis (⭐⭐⭐)

*How Barad builds their research, not just what they research.*

### Conceptual Strategy — Ontological Reconstruction

Barad's defining move is **working at the ontological level before the epistemological**. Most critics of science ask "how do we know?"; Barad asks "what kind of world must exist for knowing to work the way it does?" This reconstructive approach — starting from the physics and building outward — means every intervention is foundational rather than tactical. When Barad critiques representationalism, it's not a cultural critique; it's an argument about the structure of reality.

The secondary strategy is **terminological construction**: intra-action, phenomena, onto-epistem-ology, agential cut, material-discursive. Each term does specific work and is defined with precision. The neologisms are not jargon for its own sake — they mark conceptual distinctions that existing vocabulary can't capture. Compare to Haraway's "situated knowledges" or Foucault's "apparatus": the vocabulary creates the conceptual space.

### Career Architecture — Legitimacy Ladder

Barad built their career on what could be called a **legitimacy ladder**:

1. Earn real credentials in theoretical physics (Stony Brook PhD, tenured physics appointment)
2. Move into interdisciplinary space *from physics outward*, not the reverse
3. Write the major book that crosses all boundaries (*Meeting the Universe Halfway*, 2007)
4. Get endorsed by both a physicist (Arthur Zajonc) AND a feminist theorist (Donna Haraway)
5. Get published by the right houses (Duke UP for humanists, Suhrkamp for Germans)
6. Appear at dOCUMENTA (13) to signal art-theory relevance
7. Build institutional infrastructure (Science & Justice Center, Graduate Training Program)

Each step up the ladder was enabled by the previous ones. The physics credential doesn't diminish — it grows more valuable the more Barad moves into humanities. This is a counter-intuitive career strategy that most academics don't understand: genuine technical expertise becomes *more* valuable the further you go from your home discipline.

### Methodology Signature — Physics as Philosophical Lever

Unlike most humanist engagements with science (which import scientific concepts as metaphors), Barad uses physics as a **philosophical lever**: if the most mathematically precise science we have produces these ontological implications, then the assumptions of representationalism are untenable at the level of the world's most rigorous inquiry. The move is not "quantum physics is like gender" but "the philosophy of physics and the philosophy of gender face structurally analogous problems, and solving them requires the same ontological revision."

The scale of evidence is also methodologically unusual: *Meeting the Universe Halfway* is 544 pages. This is not an essay-as-provocation; it's a sustained argument that must be answered rather than dismissed. The length signals seriousness and makes the work difficult to caricature.

### Network Strategy — The UCSC Cluster + European Continental Theory

Rather than building a personal network of collaborators, Barad has cultivated **place-based intellectual density**: UC Santa Cruz's History of Consciousness and Feminist Studies departments are the single most important cluster for this kind of work. Being there with Haraway, Tsing, Puig de la Bellacasa, and others creates a gravitational field that attracts graduate students, visiting scholars, and collaborative projects.

The European angle is equally strategic: Suhrkamp gives Barad access to the German continental philosophy tradition (crucial for being taken seriously by Derrida/Foucault scholars). The Nordic feminist studies network (Lykke, van der Tuin) provides European feminist theory authority. dOCUMENTA is the capstone that reaches art worlds directly.

### Key Career Moves (Chronological)

1. **Physics PhD → tenured physics appointment (1980s)**: The foundational move. Established genuine disciplinary authority that could never be taken away. *Why it worked:* most interdisciplinary scholars enter from humanities; entering from physics reversed the usual power dynamics.

2. **Moving to interdisciplinary appointment (late 1980s/1990s)**: Left physics tenure to take up the interdisciplinary position at UCSC. *Why it worked:* UCSC had Haraway, one of the few environments where this migration made career sense rather than career suicide. Timing coincided with growth of feminist science studies as a field.

3. **"Getting Real" in *differences* (1998) + "Agential Realism" in Science Studies Reader (1999)**: Established presence simultaneously in feminist theory and science studies before the book. *Why it worked:* seeded the ideas in two different intellectual communities who would then be primed for the major book.

4. **"Posthumanist Performativity" in *Signs* (2003)**: The condensed manifesto before the major work. *Signs* is the most prestigious feminist theory journal. *Why it worked:* established the stakes — this was not a science studies paper but a feminist theory intervention — which expanded the audience for the book.

5. ***Meeting the Universe Halfway* (Duke UP, 2007)**: The career-defining work. 544 pages, original physics, dual endorsement from physicist and feminist theorist. *Why it worked:* arrived at exactly the moment when new materialism was being named as a movement. Became the most rigorous statement of that movement's ontological claims.

6. **dOCUMENTA (13), Note #099 (2012)**: Placed in Kassel's art world alongside 99 other thinkers/artists. *Why it worked:* legitimized the art-theory dimension of the project and introduced Barad to a generation of art practitioners who were looking for rigorous theoretical frameworks for their material/computational practices.

7. **Suhrkamp edition (2012) + Merve edition (2015)**: Translated and published by the prestige imprints of German critical theory. *Why it worked:* completed the continental philosophy integration; now the reference for German-speaking scholars working on materialism, constructivism, and posthumanism.

### What Amber Can Learn

**The dual-credentialing strategy is the most transferable.** Barad shows that the rarest and most valuable position is "has genuine technical competence AND can do rigorous humanistic critique." Most people have one or the other. The combination creates a position that neither "pure" engineers nor "pure" humanities scholars can occupy. Amber is already building this position with AI/computing on the technical side and critical/feminist design theory on the humanistic side.

**Ontology before epistemology.** When facing a new problem, Barad asks "what kind of world must exist for this to work?" rather than "how do we know this?" Starting at the ontological level produces more fundamental interventions. For Amber: don't just ask "how do AI systems create knowledge claims?" — ask "what kind of world do AI systems enact, and who/what is excluded by their agential cuts?"

**The strategic paper before the major work.** Barad planted two key papers in 1998-1999 and one in 2003 before the 2007 book. Each introduced the framework to a specific intellectual community (feminist theory, science studies). By the time the book arrived, there were readers in multiple disciplines already primed for the argument. For major intellectual moves, seed the ideas in targeted venues 2-4 years before the main intervention.

**Build something, don't just publish.** The Science & Justice Research Center and Graduate Training Program at UCSC are Barad building the institutional infrastructure for agential realism as practice, not just theory. The ideas persist because there's a community and a program, not just papers. For Amber: what institutional infrastructure would embed the critical AI/more-than-human design ideas into something that outlasts any single project?
