# Gary Zhexi Zhang

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Initial deep research completed. Comprehensive profile with exhibitions, writings, collaborations, and institutional network.

Chinese-British artist, writer and researcher exploring systemic connections between cosmology, technology, and economy. Works at the intersection of finance, ecology, and speculative knowledge systems. Operates individually, in collaboration, and within organizational frameworks to investigate "unstable knowledge" and the production of geopolitical imagination.

## Background
Chinese-British background. Currently **Tutor (Research) in Contemporary Art Practice, [Royal College of Art](https://www.rca.ac.uk/)** London (2026-present). Previously held teaching positions at **[Goldsmiths](https://www.gold.ac.uk/) MFA** (lecturer in critical studies, 2022-23), **[Parsons School of Design](https://www.newschool.edu/parsons/)** (adjunct lecturer), and served as PhD external examiner at RCA.

Co-founded **[[Foreign Objects]]** design studio with **[[Eliza Collin]]** (now dormant) — received Mozilla Creative Media Award in 2019. Based in London with extensive international exhibition and residency network.

## Domains
- **Financial worlding & temporal critique** — investigating connections between finance, climate, and cosmology
- **Ecology & technology systems** — exploring planetary computation and more-than-human relationships  
- **Sinofuturist speculation** — bridging Eastern and Western knowledge systems
- **Geopolitical imagination** — how technologies mediate cultural and political possibilities

## Key Works
- **[Catastrophe Time!](https://mitpress.mit.edu/9781913689674/catastrophe-time/)** (Strange Attractor Press, 2023) — edited book exploring weird temporalities of finance, economics, insurance, and reality-making technologies. Contributors include Diann Bauer, Suhail Malik, Kei Kreutler, Bahar Noorizadeh.
- **[METAMERS](https://epfl-pavilions.ch/exhibitions/metamers)** (2024) — solo exhibition, EPFL Pavilions, Lausanne. Extended hallucination exploring perceptual knowledge, industry, war, and primordial biological memory using psychological and acoustic descent.

- **[The Tourist](https://thetourist.ggyu.art)** (2023) — film commission for Art Gallery of York University, Toronto. Documentary essay exploring exchange between Afrofuturism and Sinofuturism via life of Zanzibari revolutionary Ali Sultan Issa.
- **[Ocean Briefings](https://www.instagram.com/forecast.space/p/C3TNESsuYCl/?next=%2Fjoseitopercu%2Ftagged%2F&hl=cs&img_index=2)** (2023) — public artwork, Sea Art Festival, Busan

## Research Taste Analysis
**This section analyzes HOW Gary Zhexi Zhang builds research, not just WHAT he researches.**

### Conceptual Strategy
- **Temporal reframing:** Takes familiar systems (finance, ecology, technology) and reveals their "weird temporalities" — making visible the temporal assumptions that organize reality
- **Cosmological scaling:** Constantly moves between planetary and intimate scales — "cosmology, technology and economy" as integrated system rather than separate domains
- **Speculative materialism:** Uses art practice to investigate "unstable knowledge" — knowledge systems that are actively changing or whose foundations are uncertain

### Career Architecture  
- **Multi-institutional positioning:** Simultaneously holds positions at RCA (academic legitimacy), Antikythera (cutting-edge research), Serpentine (cultural authority), TBA21 (climate leadership)
- **Platform multiplication:** Rather than building one institution, participates meaningfully in multiple platforms — Serpentine R&D, Somerset House Studios, Weird Economies, Other Internet
- **Strategic collaboration:** Partners with established practitioners (Victoria Ivanova at Serpentine) while co-creating experimental forms (Waste Paper Opera)

- **Durational projects:** Multi-year touring (Dead Cat Bounce), ongoing series (Future Art Ecosystems), book projects spanning years — builds sustained investigation rather than discrete works
