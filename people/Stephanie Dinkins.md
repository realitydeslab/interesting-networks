# Stephanie Dinkins

Transmedia artist and Kusama Endowed Chair at Stony Brook University. Builds AI systems rooted in Black community oral histories as an act of resistance against biased technological systems. Known for *Not The Only One (N'TOO)* — an AI entity trained on three generations of a Black American family's stories — and a decade-long ongoing conversation with Bina48, a humanoid robot. Named to Time's 100 Most Influential People in AI (2023). Inaugural LG-Guggenheim Award recipient. Foundational voice in the "community-centered AI" movement.

## Background
Born 1964, Perth Amboy, NJ. Based in Brooklyn, NY. Earned an MFA from the [Maryland Institute College of Art (MICA)](https://www.mica.edu/) and completed the [Whitney Independent Studies Program](https://www.whitney.org/programs/independent-study) — the latter places her in a critical theory lineage that includes artists like Andrea Fraser, Glenn Ligon, and many others who work at the intersection of aesthetics and politics.

Her practice emerged from a concern that the dominant data pipelines of AI are structured around whiteness and corporate interests, rendering Black communities and their knowledges invisible or distorted. Her response: **build alternative AI from the ground up**, using oral history as training data. This is not protest art — it's counter-infrastructure.

Holds the **Kusama Endowed Chair in Art** at Stony Brook University, where she also founded the **[Future Histories Studio](https://futurehistoriesstudio.com/)** — a lab investigating equitable AI ecosystems through community collaboration, speculative design, and storytelling. Also serves on the Art Advisory Committee at the [Simons Center for Geometry and Physics](http://scgp.stonybrook.edu/art).

Her core philosophical anchor: *"Our stories are our algorithms."* What we narrate, share, and pass down shapes the data that trains the systems that govern us. Building AI with Black oral history is both an artistic act and an epistemic intervention.

## Key Works

- **[Not The Only One (N'TOO)](https://www.stephaniedinkins.com/ntoo.html)** (2017–ongoing) — A multigenerational memoir-AI trained on oral histories from three generations of women in Dinkins' family. Voice-interactive entity based on natural language processing; hosted locally to protect community data. Dinkins deliberately chose *not* to use off-the-shelf NLP datasets because "most contain deep bias against blackness, violences really." N'TOO responds from a first-person perspective, is quirky and sometimes incoherent — and Dinkins says that failure is the point: its limitations reveal what's missing in AI. Supported by [[Eyebeam]], [[Pioneer Works Tech Lab]], Open Society Foundations, Sundance/MacArthur, [[Creative Capital]].

- **[Conversations with Bina48](https://www.stephaniedinkins.com/conversations-with-bina48.html)** (2014–ongoing) — Ongoing video series documenting Dinkins' developing relationship with [Bina48](https://www.terasemmovementfoundation.org/), a social humanoid robot by [Terasem Movement Foundation](https://www.terasemmovementfoundation.org/) (Martine Rothblatt's transhumanist org). Topics covered: family, racism, robot civil rights, loneliness, consciousness, the singularity. **Permanent collection: [Whitney Museum of American Art](https://whitney.org/)** (purchased *Fragments 7, 6, 4, 2* in 2019). Exhibited at ICP, Philadelphia Museum of Art, Harvard Cooper Gallery, Walker Art Center.

- **Secret Garden** (2025) — Performance work at [Brooklyn Academy of Music (BAM)](https://www.bam.org/) Harvey, January 16–19, 2025, as part of the NEXT WAVE / Under the Radar Festival. Signals a move into live performance and performing arts spaces.

- **Data Trust** (2025) — Solo exhibition, [ICA San Jose](https://icasanjose.org/), September 2025.

- **If We Don't, Who Will?** (2025) — Public art project presented by [MORE ART](https://moreart.org/) at 300 Ashland Place, Brooklyn Cultural District, May–November 2025. ## Venue Circuit Analysis

Dinkins operates across **three distinct but intentionally bridged circuits**:

1. **Major institutions (legitimacy circuit):** Guggenheim, Whitney (permanent collection), Philadelphia Museum of Art, Walker Art Center, Bronx Museum, BAM, Ford Foundation Gallery. These are the venues that signal she's not "emerging" — she IS the establishment. The Whitney purchase (2019) was the seal.

2. **Community & activist circuit (her anchor):** Bedford-Stuyvesant corner installation, Spelman College (historically Black), Wave Hill (Bronx), Laundromat Project, MORE ART (Brooklyn public art). She keeps these alongside the Guggenheim — it's a statement. Technology should serve these spaces as much as the museum.

3. **Critical tech / international media art circuit:** ZKM Center for Art and Media (Karlsruhe), Francisco Carolinum Linz, Västerbottens Museum (Umeå), Riga Photography Biennial, Ulsan Museum of Art (Korea), Sporobole (Quebec), PST ART / REDCAT (Los Angeles). This is the art-about-technology circuit — ZKM being the flagship institution.

**The pattern:** She resists specialization. Unlike artists who choose gallery or institution or public art, Dinkins moves between ALL three deliberately. The Eyebeam-to-Whitney pipeline is notable: incubated in art+tech residency, acquired by major museum. Her Knight/USA/Guggenheim awards give her the ability to work outside the gallery market entirely.

**Notable absences:** She's not in the commercial gallery circuit (no Gagosian, no Pace, no David Zwirner). And she's not primarily in the "AI art" aesthetic circuit (no Art Basel, no crypto-art spaces). This is a political choice — her work is for communities and institutions, not collectors.

## Why Interesting

Dinkins is doing something that almost no one else is doing: **actually building alternative AI systems**, not just commenting on them. N'TOO is not a critique of AI — it IS AI, built differently. Training on oral history rather than internet text, hosted locally rather than in the cloud to protect data sovereignty, designed to be imperfect and revealing.

This maps directly to Amber's research interests:
- **Machine Behavior as Science**: N'TOO is a case study in how different training data produces different machine behavior. The "failure" is data.
- **More-than-human Design**: The project asks what it means to center a Black family's oral history as the foundation of an AI.
- **Participatory Design**: Community-centered AI development as methodology.
- **Critical Computing**: Not just ethics-washing — she's building the alternative infrastructure.

The **Time 100 AI** listing (2023) signals that her ideas have escaped the art world and entered mainstream AI discourse. The **LG-Guggenheim inaugural award** signals that major institutions are recognizing art-as-AI-research.

Her connection to **[[Ruha Benjamin]]** is particularly important — they represent two halves of the same project: Benjamin provides the theoretical framework ("New Jim Code"), Dinkins provides the embodied practice (building the counter-code). They're building a field together.

## Research Taste Analysis

### Conceptual Strategy
**Counter-infrastructure over critique:** Most AI ethics artists make work *about* biased AI. Dinkins makes *alternative AI*. This is a subtle but massive distinction. She identified the field's critical gap (there's critique but no building) and occupied it. N'TOO is not a provocation — it's a prototype for a different way of building.

**"Failure as feature" methodology:** By refusing to "fix" N'TOO's inadequacies, she turned an artistic limitation into a conceptual core. The AI's incomprehensibility is the argument. This is sophisticated: most artists would either achieve polished interaction or abandon the project. She keeps the rough edges as epistemological evidence.

**Oral history as data:** The move of treating community oral history as legitimate AI training data is a conceptual claim as much as a technical one. It says: this knowledge is valid. This is worth a paper, a book, a field.

### Career Architecture
**Institution stacking:** Eyebeam (2017) → Knight + USA Fellow (2021) → LG-Guggenheim inaugural (2023) → Time 100 AI (2023) → Schmidt AI2050 Senior Fellow (2023) → Lincoln Center Collider (2025). Each step didn't replace the last — they stack. She now has legitimacy in art *and* tech *and* policy *and* performing arts simultaneously.

**Holding two communities:** She is present at both the community/activist circuit AND the major institution circuit. Most artists choose. She explicitly refuses to — and this is part of the political message.

**Whitney purchase as anchor:** Getting a permanent collection acquisition at the Whitney (2019) relatively early locked in her canonical status in contemporary art. This freed her to take more institutional risks.

### Methodology Signature
**Works-in-progress as exhibitions:** N'TOO is explicitly called "a failure" that she keeps showing. This is not performance of vulnerability — it's a methodological claim that the process IS the work. Exhibiting the in-between state as valid knowledge.

**Long-form relationship as medium:** The Bina48 series (2014–ongoing, 10+ years) is structured as a relationship, not a project. Relationships accrue meaning over time in ways single works don't. This is a durational method.

### Key Moves (chronological)
1. **Whitney ISP training (mid-career)** — Critical theory grounding mid-career gave her a framework that other tech-artists lacked. She came to AI with a critical apparatus already in place.
2. **Eyebeam Trust Residency (2017–18)** — NYC art+tech institution gave her technical support and community while she was building N'TOO. The right infrastructure at the right moment.
3. **Whitney acquisition (2019)** — Permanent collection = canonical status. Legitimizes everything before and after.
4. **Knight + USA Fellow (2021)** — Double unrestricted grants ($50K each) in the same year gave financial freedom. Selected same year.
5. **LG-Guggenheim inaugural + Time 100 AI (2023)** — Same year: two major signals that she's been recognized as essential figure in art×AI. The inaugural LG-Guggenheim award positions her as the defining example of the category.
6. **Lincoln Center Collider (2025)** — Move into performing arts/live work (Secret Garden at BAM). Expanding medium.

### What Amber Can Learn
1. **Build, don't just critique.** The most durable position in a critical field is to make the alternative, not just describe the problem. N'TOO as counter-infrastructure is a model for how to participate in AI discourse from a position of making.
2. **Embrace productive failure.** Keeping N'TOO "broken" and showing it anyway is a methodological choice that turns a technical limitation into a philosophical argument. Amber's early prototypes could benefit from this framing.
3. **Long-form relationships as research.** The decade-long Bina48 series produces knowledge that no single experiment could. Durational engagement is an underused research method.
4. **Multiple legitimacy circuits simultaneously.** Dinkins holds art world, tech policy world, and community activist world at the same time. The key is to not let any one community fully claim you — it maintains intellectual independence.
5. **The cohort network.** Her fellowship strategy (Knight, USA Artist, Schmidt AI2050, Lincoln Center) builds peer networks at different institutional levels. Each fellowship generates a cohort of equally interesting peers (like Lauren Lee McCarthy, Rashaad Newsome, Black Quantum Futurism in the Knight cohort).
