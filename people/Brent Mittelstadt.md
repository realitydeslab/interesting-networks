# Brent Mittelstadt

Professor of Data Ethics and Policy at the Oxford Internet Institute (OII), University of Oxford. OII Director of Research. Co-leads the Governance of Emerging Technologies (GET) Research Programme with [[Sandra Wachter]] and [[Chris Russell]]. Philosopher/ethicist specializing in AI ethics, professional ethics, and technology law and policy.

## Background
Philosopher/data ethicist. Co-leads GET at OII alongside Wachter (law) and Russell (ML). Together they form the core interdisciplinary unit that has produced some of the most cited AI ethics papers of the 2010s-2020s. Also leads the Trustworthiness Auditing for AI project.

## Key Works
- Co-author on "The ethics of algorithms: Mapping the debate" (Big Data & Society, 2016) — 4,900+ citations
- Co-author on counterfactual explanations paper (Harvard JOLT, 2018) — 4,600+ citations
- Co-author on "Explaining explanations in AI" (FAccT, 2019) — 1,213+ citations

## Why Interesting
The ethics anchor of the GET triumvirate. Has written on why AI cannot just be made "ethical" through technical fixes — it requires institutional and professional accountability structures. The philosophical backbone of the GET programme's critique of AI governance.
