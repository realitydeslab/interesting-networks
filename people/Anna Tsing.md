# Anna Tsing

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Deep research completed. Full profile created with comprehensive CV, collaborations, and network analysis.

Professor of Anthropology at UC Santa Cruz. Pioneer of multispecies ethnography and more-than-human anthropology. Her *The Mushroom at the End of the World* (2015) is one of the most influential anthropological works of the 21st century, following matsutake mushrooms through global supply chains to reveal how life persists in capitalist ruins.

## Background

Born 1952. Educated at Yale (BA Anthropology, 1974) and Stanford (PhD Social Anthropology, 1984). Early career focused on Southeast Asian politics and environmental studies, particularly Indonesia and forest management. Gradually developed her signature approach of following non-human actors (fungi, forests, commodities) to understand human-nature entanglements.

Her intellectual trajectory moves from conventional political anthropology toward what she calls "the arts of noticing" — methodologies for attending to multispecies collaborations and the more-than-human world. This shift parallels the broader "ontological turn" in anthropology but with distinctive attention to political economy and environmental destruction.

## Key Works

- **[*The Mushroom at the End of the World: On the Possibility of Life in Capitalist Ruins*](https://press.princeton.edu/books/paperback/9780691178325/the-mushroom-at-the-end-the-world)** (Princeton University Press, 2015) — Her masterwork. Follows matsutake mushrooms from Oregon forests through Japanese markets to reveal "the possibility of life in capitalist ruins." Winner of multiple awards, translated into numerous languages.

- **[*Arts of Living on a Damaged Planet: Ghosts and Monsters of the Anthropocene*](https://upress.umn.edu/book-division/books/arts-of-living-on-a-damaged-planet)** (University of Minnesota Press, 2017) — Co-edited with Heather Swanson, Elaine Gan, and Nils Bubandt. Anthology of multispecies approaches to Anthropocene challenges.

- **[*In the Realm of the Diamond Queen: Marginality in an Out-of-the-Way Place*](https://press.princeton.edu/books/paperback/9780691006512/in-the-realm-of-the-diamond-queen)** (Princeton University Press, 1993) — Earlier ethnography of the Meratus Dayaks of Indonesia, establishing her reputation in environmental anthropology.

- **[*Friction: An Ethnography of Global Connection*](https://press.princeton.edu/books/paperback/9780691120652/friction)** (Princeton University Press, 2005) — Theorizes "friction" as the awkward, unequal encounters that shape global connections. Studies Indonesian rainforest politics.

- **[*The Feral Atlas*](https://feralatlas.supdigital.org/)** (2020-ongoing) — Digital project mapping multispecies entanglements in the Anthropocene. Collaborative platform with artists, scientists, and scholars.

- **"Unruly Edges: Mushrooms as Companion Species"** (2012) — Seminal paper in multispecies studies

- **"On Nonscalability: The Living World is Not Amenable to Precision-Nested Scales"** (2012) — Important theoretical contribution to scalar politics

- **"Contaminated Diversity in 'Slow Disturbance': Potential Collaborations in Overgrown Landscapes"** (2019) — Recent work on disturbance ecology
