# Ming Smith

African-American photographer. The first Black woman to have her work acquired by the Museum of Modern Art. Member of the [Kamoinge Workshop](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kamoinge_Workshop) — the collective of Black photographers founded in 1963 in New York. Known for atmospheric, painterly black-and-white photographs of Black cultural figures and everyday life. Subjects include Alvin Ailey, Nina Simone, Sun Ra, Grace Jones, and many others.

[[Arthur Jafa]] selected her works for the "A Series of Utterly Improbable, Yet Extraordinary Renditions" exhibition at [[Serpentine Galleries]] and Moderna Museet Stockholm (2019). Jafa's curatorial selection of Smith positions her work within the tradition of Black visual intonation he theorizes.

## Why Interesting
Smith's photography — blurry, painterly, emotionally atmospheric, resistant to the documentary clarity expected of Black subjects — is a photographic equivalent to the "worried note" concept. She photographs how it *feels* to be in a space, not just what was there. Her photographs predate digital blur effects by decades.
