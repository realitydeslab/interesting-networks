# Walter Pitts (1923–1969)

American autodidact mathematical prodigy. Ran away from home at 15 after memorizing Principia Mathematica in a library; found by Bertrand Russell, then taken under Norbert Wiener's wing at MIT. With Warren McCulloch, authored the most important paper in the intellectual prehistory of deep learning.

## Key Works
- **McCulloch & Pitts, "A Logical Calculus of Ideas Immanent in Nervous Activity"** (1943) — [DOI: 10.1007/BF02478259](https://doi.org/10.1007/BF02478259) — the founding paper of neural networks; proved formal neurons can compute boolean logic

## Why Interesting
The story of Pitts is remarkable: a child runaway who became co-author of one of the most consequential papers in 20th-century science. His life is also a tragedy — he burned his later manuscripts and died in obscurity. The McCulloch-Pitts paper launched the neural network tradition that led, via Rosenblatt's perceptrons, to modern deep learning.
