# Achille Mbembe

Cameroonian-South African philosopher and political theorist. Professor at the [Wits Institute for Social and Economic Research (WISER)](https://www.wits.ac.za/wiser/), University of the Witwatersrand, Johannesburg. One of the most important living postcolonial and critical theorists — known for **necropolitics** (the power to expose certain people to death as an extension of Foucault's biopolitics), **On the Postcolony**, **Critique of Black Reason**, and **Out of the Dark Night**. A defining intellectual of the African critical theory scene that overlaps with [[Premesh Lalu]] and the [[Centre for Humanities Research UWC]].

## Domains
- Necropolitics and biopolitics — the thanatopolitical as supplement to Foucault
- African political philosophy — subjectivity, time, and the postcolony
- Afropolitanism — rethinking African identity beyond nativism and nationalism
- Decolonisation of knowledge — critique of Western epistemological universalism
- Technology and the digital condition — recent work on AI, surveillance, and the posthuman from African/African diasporic perspective

## Key Works
- *[On the Postcolony](https://www.ucpress.edu/book/9780520204355/on-the-postcolony)* (UC Press, 2001) — landmark work on postcolonial power, temporality, and subjectivity in Africa
- *[Critique of Black Reason](https://www.dukeupress.edu/critique-of-black-reason)* (Duke UP, 2017; trans. Laurent Dubois) — major philosophical work on race, capital, and the future of humanity
- *[Necropolitics](https://www.dukeupress.edu/necropolitics)* (Duke UP, 2019) — extension and collection of the necropolitics framework. Essential for understanding AI governance from the Global South.
- *[Out of the Dark Night: Essays on Decolonization](https://cup.columbia.edu/book/out-of-the-dark-night/9780231188616)* (Columbia UP, 2021)
- *[Brutalisme](https://www.editionsladecouverte.fr/)* (La Découverte, 2020; French) — on contemporary brutalism as logic of capital and technology

## Why Interesting
Mbembe's necropolitics concept is the most philosophically rigorous framework for understanding how AI systems can be designed to expose certain populations to harm, exclusion, or "social death." His more recent work on AI and African futures connects directly to Amber's Decolonial AI interest. He is the major intellectual figure in the Johannesburg/Wits orbit that connects to CHR/UWC (Lalu's institution).
