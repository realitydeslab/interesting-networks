# Andrew Adamatzky

Director of the Unconventional Computing Laboratory, UWE Bristol. Pioneer of slime mould computing, chemical computers, and fungal electrical computing.

## Domains
- Unconventional computing (biological, chemical, reaction-diffusion)
- Cellular automata
- Slime mould (*Physarum polycephalum*) as computer
- Fungal electrical spiking as information networks

## Key Works
- [*Physarum Machines: Computers from Slime Mould*](https://worldscientific.com/worldscibooks/10.1142/7968) (2010) — definitive text on slime mould computing
- *Fungal Machines* (2022) — fungal electrical spikes as computing substrate
- *Reaction-Diffusion Computers* (with De Lacy Costello and Asai, Elsevier, 2005)
- Founding Editor-in-Chief, *International Journal of Unconventional Computing* (2005–)
- Founding Editor-in-Chief, *Journal of Cellular Automata* (2005–)

## Why Interesting
Adamatzky treats non-human organisms as genuine computing substrates — not metaphorically but literally and experimentally. His slime mould work (it designed road networks for Spain) and fungal work challenge the assumption that computation requires intentional design. Connects to more-than-human design, ALife, and machine behavior. UK-based → geographically accessible for Amber.
