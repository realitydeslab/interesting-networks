# Thecla Schiphorst

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-27:** Stub created during Lian Loke deep research.

Professor at Simon Fraser University (SIAT, School of Interactive Arts and Technology). Pioneer of body-centered, soma-based computing with roots in dance choreography and Laban Movement Analysis. Co-authored "The somatic turn in human-computer interaction" (ACM *Interactions*, 2018) with Lian Loke — the landmark paper that named the field. Has spent 30+ years at the intersection of dance, movement, and computing. Former research collaborator with Myron Krueger (early interactive art). Deep connection to the Laban Movement Analysis tradition.

## Key Works
- [**The somatic turn in human-computer interaction**](https://doi.org/10.1145/3236675) (*ACM Interactions*, 2018) — with Lian Loke. Field-defining paper.
- [**Embracing First-Person Perspectives in Soma-Based Design**](https://doi.org/10.3390/informatics5010008) (*Informatics*, 2018) — 21-author soma design manifesto with Kristina Höök et al.
- "Designing for Sensory Appreciation: Cultivating Somatic Approaches to Experience Design" — CHI 2020 course with Lian Loke and Kristina Höök.

## Key Relationships
- **[[Lian Loke]]** — closest collaborator on somatic turn in HCI
- **[[Kristina Höök]]** — soma design community

## Why Interesting
One of the oldest nodes in the body+computing space. Her dance/Laban background gives the soma design community its movement vocabulary. Understanding her work is understanding the deep history of somatic HCI.
