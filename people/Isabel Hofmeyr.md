# Isabel Hofmeyr

Professor Emeritus at Wits University, based at [[WISER]], and Distinguished Scholar in Residence at NYU. Pioneer of oceanic, transnational, and hydrocolonial literary history. Three decades of reframing how Africa is understood globally through oceanic and transnational cultural flows.

## Domains
- Oceanic Humanities and Indian Ocean studies
- Hydrocolonialism — water, ports, custom houses as sites of colonial power
- Transnational African literary history
- More-than-human/elemental turn (co-convenes Plant Lives, Breathing In forums with [[Sarah Nuttall]])

## Key Works
- [*Dockside Reading: Hydrocolonialism and the Custom House*](https://www.dukeupress.edu/dockside-reading) (Duke UP, 2022) — the definitive hydrocolonialism text; ports and reading as colonial encounter
- *The Portable Bunyan: A Transnational History of The Pilgrim's Progress* (1994)
- *Gandhi's Printing Press: Experiments in Slow Reading* (2013)
- *Ten Books that Shaped the British Empire* (co-edited with Antoinette Burton, 2015)
