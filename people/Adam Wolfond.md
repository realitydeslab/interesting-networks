# Adam Wolfond

Autistic poet and artist based in Toronto, Canada. A key figure in the [[SenseLab]] / [[3 Ecologies Institute]] orbit — not as a research subject but as a co-theorist of neurodiversity. His language-making art is deeply integrated into [[Erin Manning]]'s theoretical work on "minor gestures" and autistic perception.

Wolfond types using facilitated communication to produce poetry and theoretical texts that perform a different relationship to language — one that, Manning argues, enacts rather than just describes the "pre-chunked" relational field she theorizes. His writing resists normative syntactic closure, remaining in motion.

## Work

He appears in:
- [[Inflexions: A Journal for Research-Creation]], Pop-Up Issue 1: "Diversity in Diversity" (2018), alongside Tito Rajarshi Mukhopadhyay and others
- [[Erin Manning]]'s theoretical texts (*The Minor Gesture*, *For a Pragmatics of the Useless*) — cited and engaged with as a co-theorist
- SenseLab events and 3e gatherings

His own written work circulates primarily through these community contexts rather than mainstream publishing.

## Why Interesting

Wolfond is interesting as a demonstration of the knowledge-political claims that Manning makes theoretically. His existence as a poet and community participant in the SenseLab — on equal epistemic footing with the philosophers and artists — is itself an argument about what neurodiversity means as intellectual practice, not just as identity politics.

For anyone thinking about **non-standard cognition, alternative forms of knowledge production, or inclusive research practices**, Wolfond's work is a concrete instantiation of abstract arguments about epistemic diversity.
