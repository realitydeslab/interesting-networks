# Adam Wolfond

Non-speaking autistic poet, artist, and researcher based in Toronto. Co-director (with Estée Klar) of [[dis assembly]], a neurodiverse arts collective. MA student at Concordia University in the Individualized Program. Central collaborator with [[Erin Manning]] and the [[SenseLab]] / [[3 Ecologies Institute]]. Author of two books — *The Wanting Way* (Milkweed, 2022) and *Open Book in Ways of Water* (Punctum Books, 2023). Featured in NYT Magazine, NPR, and Poetry Foundation. Wolfond is not a subject of neurodiversity research — he IS the research, as co-theorist, co-instructor, and co-creator.

## Background
Wolfond types using facilitated communication — his primary mode of producing language for poetry and theoretical text. He grew up in Toronto. His collaboration with his mother, [[Estée Klar]] (PhD candidate at York University in Critical Disability Studies, artist/filmmaker), has been central since 2016, when they co-founded [[dis assembly]].

Since 2016, he has been an artist in residence at [[SenseLab]] (Concordia University) under [[Erin Manning]]. He is now enrolled in Concordia's Individualized Program for an MA — a program that allows non-standard students to design their own graduate curriculum. This makes him both student and collaborator in an unusual but deliberate institutional arrangement.

He uses "man of autism" as a self-identifier, reflecting his alignment with the neurodiversity movement and identity-first language.

## Why Interesting
Wolfond is interesting to Amber on several levels:

**Epistemic politics**: His existence as an MA student, co-instructor, and published poet — not as a "case study" but as a theorist and co-researcher — is a concrete instantiation of arguments Amber likely finds compelling: that knowledge comes in non-standard forms, that "accessibility" in research means allowing different kinds of minds to produce scholarship, that the researcher/researched binary is itself a neurotypical imposition.

**More-than-human perception**: His 2025 video installation uses endoscopic and body cameras to share what *he sees* — to make autistic perception visible and communicate it to neurotypical audiences. This is a form of design-as-epistemology that connects to somaesthetics and embodied interaction.

**Language-making as research**: His writing doesn't "describe" autistic experience — it *performs* it. Sentences resist closure, return to themselves, enact "staying in the question." This is practice-based research at its most radical.

**Crip time / subaltern temporalities**: The Kronoscope article engages directly with temporal difference as a form of knowledge — subaltern temporalities as alternative ontologies. This connects to speculative design's engagement with futures-as-alternative-presents.

**Connects to research-taste.md**: Participatory and democratic design (decentering the neurotypical researcher), embodied interaction (the body as knowledge producer), practice-based research, small over big (community-based work rather than conference circuits), agenda-setting (dis assembly resists operationalization).
