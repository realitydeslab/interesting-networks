# Vladan Joler

Academic, researcher, artist and digital rights activist whose work blends critical design, data investigations, counter-cartography, and data visualization. Explores and visualizes technical and social aspects of algorithmic transparency, digital labor exploitation, invisible infrastructures and contemporary phenomena at the intersection of technology and society. Co-founder and director of SHARE Foundation, one of the leading digital rights organizations in Southeast Europe.

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Initial deep research completed. Recent Silver Lion win at Venice Architecture Biennale 2025 for "Calculating Empires" with Kate Crawford. Active in student protest documentation in Serbia.

## Background
Born 1977 in former Yugoslavia. Studied at Academy of Arts, University of Novi Sad (1996-2000), during which time he engaged in artistic interventions and self-organization during the student uprising against Milošević that began in 1996. From 1996-2000, during extreme poverty, isolation, war and repression, developed low-budget, low-tech, socially and politically engaged art practice out of necessity rather than choice. 

Career evolved from early game hacking and media activism in the early 2000s to becoming a leading voice in critical technology studies. Has pioneered methods of visualizing invisible infrastructures and algorithmic processes that have become influential templates for the field.

## Key Works
- **[Calculating Empires: A Genealogy of Technology and Power Since 1500](https://calculatingempires.net/)** (2023, with **[[Kate Crawford]]**) — Large-scale research visualization exploring how technical and social structures have co-evolved over five centuries. Silver Lion at Venice Architecture Biennale 2025, S+T+ARTS Prize European Commission 2024, Boghossian Foundation International Prize 2025, acquired by Rijks Museum Nederlands 2025, commissioned by Fondazione Prada 2023.

- **[Anatomy of an AI System](https://anatomyof.ai/)** (2018, with **[[Kate Crawford]]**) — Large-scale map and long-form essay investigating the human labor, data, and planetary resources required to build and operate an Amazon Echo device. Design of the Year Award 2019 Design Museum London, permanent collections MoMA New York, V&A Museum, permanent exhibition Ars Electronica Center.

- **[The Nooscope Manifested](https://kim.hfg-karlsruhe.de/nooscope.ai/)** (2020, with Matteo Pasquinelli) — Cartography of the limits of artificial intelligence as provocation to both computer science and humanities. Maps machine learning as "instrument of knowledge magnification" rather than "intelligent machine."

- **[New Extractivism](https://extractivism.online/)** (2020) — Cartographic project outlining interconnections of contemporary extractive capitalism. Maps world-scale infrastructure designed to extract, collect, quantify, analyze and connect all aspects of reality and turn them into capital.

- **[Facebook Algorithmic Factory](https://labs.rs/en/category/facebook-research/)** (2016) — Deep forensic investigation and visual mapping of algorithmic processes behind world's largest social network. Reveals non-transparent algorithms deciding content visibility and defining new forms of labor and exploitation.

- **[Topography of Information Warfare](https://labs.rs/en/mapping-and-quantifying-political-information-warfare/)** (2016) — Map visualizing forms and methods governments and political actors use to control online spheres. Based on 5-year monitoring and 400+ documented cases by SHARE Foundation.

- **[Black Box Cartography](https://www.krisispublishing.com/prodotto/black-box-cartography/)** (2023, book) — First comprehensive collection of his most important projects exploring hidden layers of technological systems through critical cartography.

- **[Reverse Contradictionary](https://reverse-contradictionary.net/)** (2025, with Vuk Ćosić and IOCOSE) — "Dictionary of New Worlds" presenting confrontational neologisms challenging predatory, algorithmic logic embedded in statistical language.

## Research Taste Analysis

### Conceptual Strategy — Counter-Cartographic Method:
Joler's breakthrough insight was applying **critical cartography** to technological systems. Rather than accepting tech companies' self-representation, he developed forensic methods to map actual flows of data, labor, and materials. His maps don't just visualize — they **indict** through radical transparency.

**Two-phase methodology**: (1) Deep forensic investigation using network analysis, patent research, supply chain tracking; (2) Large-scale visualization that makes complexity legible without simplifying it. This creates "evidence-based speculation" — speculation grounded in rigorous documentation.

### Career Architecture — Institutional Bridge-Building:
**Strategic positioning between domains**: Maintains simultaneous legitimacy in art world (Venice, MoMA), policy world (EU Commission, digital rights NGOs), and academy (University of Novi Sad, research partnerships). Never commits fully to one institutional logic — uses each to validate the others.

**Foundation as base of operations**: SHARE Foundation provides independent platform for long-term investigations without institutional constraints. Can take on projects too politically sensitive for academic institutions or too research-intensive for art galleries.

### Methodology Signature — Data Investigation as Art Practice:
**Forensic depth meets aesthetic legibility**: Investigations involve months of technical research (patent analysis, network monitoring, supply chain tracking) but output is designed for general audiences. Creates "beautiful evidence" that functions both as artwork and policy tool.

**Scale jumping**: Projects operate across multiple temporal and spatial scales simultaneously — from microseconds of algorithmic processing to geological time of resource extraction, from intimate voice commands to planetary supply chains.

### Network Strategy — Eastern European as Strategic Position:
**Geographic specificity as analytical advantage**: Position in Serbia provides case studies of algorithmic authoritarianism (facial recognition, information warfare) that reveal global patterns. Eastern European experience of rapid technological change offers critical distance from Silicon Valley techno-optimism.

**Coalition building across regions**: Uses SHARE Monastery to bring together global digital rights networks with local expertise. Creates South-South knowledge exchange bypassing Western institutional gatekeepers.

### Key Moves (chronological)
1. **Game hacking (2000-2004)**: Tactical modification of commercial games as political commentary. Established early relationship between technology critique and artistic practice.

2. **SHARE Foundation founding (2011)**: Created independent platform for long-term digital rights advocacy. Provided stable base for research too sensitive or time-intensive for other institutions.

3. **"Invisible Infrastructures" series (2015-2017)**: Systematic investigation of internet architecture from user perspective. Developed signature methodology of "following the packet" to reveal hidden power structures.

4. **Crawford collaboration launch (2018)**: Partnership with leading AI researcher brought academic credibility and global platform. "Anatomy of AI" became template for planetary-scale technology critique.

5. **EU policy engagement (2019-2024)**: S+T+ARTS prizes created direct line to European Commission policy-making. Positioned critical art as necessary input to technology governance.

6. **Venice legitimation (2025)**: Silver Lion at Architecture Biennale established critical tech visualization as recognized art form. Highest institutional validation enables future institutional experiments.

### What Amber Can Learn
**Methodological innovation over theoretical novelty**: Joler's impact comes not from new theories but from new ways of making existing critique visible and actionable. Focus on **tools** that others can use rather than conclusions they should accept.

**Multi-institutional strategy**: Maintain legitimacy across art, policy, and academy simultaneously. Use each domain to validate work in others. Never fully commit to single institutional logic.

**Geographic specificity as global strategy**: Eastern European position provides analytical advantages — case studies of tech authoritarianism, distance from Silicon Valley mythology, experience of rapid social transformation. Amber's London/Oxford/Cambridge position offers different but equally strategic advantages.

**Foundation-building for independence**: Long-term investigations require institutional independence. Consider creating independent platform that can take on projects too politically sensitive or time-intensive for universities.

**Evidence-based speculation**: Ground speculative work in rigorous documentation. Maps that both reveal and argue. Make invisible infrastructures legible without losing their complexity.
