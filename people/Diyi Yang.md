# Diyi Yang

Assistant Professor of Computer Science at Stanford. Works at the intersection of NLP and human-centered computing. Builds AI systems that align with human social values — analyzing mental health conversations, understanding how language shapes social outcomes, and creating LLM systems that are more genuinely helpful in human contexts. One of the fastest-rising researchers in the [[Stanford HCI Group]].

## Background
- Assistant Professor at Stanford CS (joined recently)
- Works across NLP and social computing
- Prolific co-author with [[Michael Bernstein]] on AI alignment and social computing papers

## Key Works (with Bernstein)
- **[Aligning Language Models with Demonstrated Feedback](https://arxiv.org/abs/2406.00888)** (ICLR 2025) — With Omar Shaikh, Michelle Lam, Joey Hejna, et al.
- **[Creating General User Models from Computer Use](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.10831)** (UIST 2025, Best Paper HM) — With Omar Shaikh, Eric Horvitz, Joon Sung Park, Bernstein.
- **[Knoll: Creating a Knowledge Ecosystem for LLMs](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.19335)** (UIST 2025) — With Dora Zhao, Bernstein.
- **[Measuring Moral Machines](https://arxiv.org/abs/2309.12309)** (CHI 2024) — With Omar Shaikh, Valentino Chai, Michele Gelfand, Bernstein.
