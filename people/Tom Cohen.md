# Tom Cohen

Emeritus Professor of English at the **University at Albany (SUNY)**; **co-director of the Institute on Critical Climate Change** there. Major literary theorist and deconstructionist who turned from Hitchcock studies and de Manian theory toward the Anthropocene and extinction.

Closest intellectual collaborator of [[Claire Colebrook]] — together they co-edit **Critical Climate Change** and **CCC2 Irreversibility** at [[Open Humanities Press]], the premier infrastructure for extinction and Anthropocene critical theory. Third partner in the trilogy: **J. Hillis Miller** (Yale deconstruction, UC Irvine).

## Domains
- Deconstructive literary theory (Paul de Man tradition)
- Hitchcock studies / film theory
- Anthropocene / extinction / critical climate theory
- Rhetoric, language, anthropomorphism
- "Ecocide and Inscription" — how language structures and mediates ecological destruction

## Key Works
- *Anti-Mimesis From Plato to Hitchcock* (Cambridge UP, 1994)
- *Ideology and Inscription: Cultural Studies after Benjamin, De Man, and Bakhtin* (Cambridge UP, 1998)
- *Hitchcock's Cryptonymies, Vols. 1 & 2* (University of Minnesota Press, 2005)
- *Derrida and the Humanities* (ed.) (Cambridge UP, 2003)
- *Material Events: Paul de Man and the Afterlife of Theory* (co-ed. with Barbara Cohen & J. Hillis Miller) (Minnesota, 2001)
- *Theory and the Disappearing Future* with [[Claire Colebrook]] & J. Hillis Miller (Routledge, 2011)
- *Twilight of the Anthropocene Idols* with [[Claire Colebrook]] & J. Hillis Miller (OHP, 2016) — FREE — applies de Manian rhetorical reading to Anthropocene discourse
- *Ecocide and Inscription vol. 1 Black Ops* (OHP, CCC2 series) — "hyper-blackness" beyond racial binaries; inscription and ecocide

## The Institute on Critical Climate Change
Co-directed by Cohen at University at Albany. The institutional infrastructure for the CCC series and related research. Brings together the OHP publishing project with an academic research program.

## Key Relationships
- **[[Claire Colebrook]]** — primary intellectual partner; co-editor OHP CCC and CCC2; co-authored two books
- **J. Hillis Miller** — third collaborator in the Routledge/OHP trilogy; Yale deconstruction lineage
- **Paul de Man** — major intellectual ancestor; Cohen's entire project applies de Man's rhetorical reading to new domains

## Why Interesting
Cohen represents the application of the hardest version of deconstruction — de Man's rhetorical reading, with its insistence that all language is figurative and anthropomorphic — to the hardest possible subject: the Anthropocene. The argument is that we cannot think climate change clearly because the very language we use to think it (tipping points, our future, the planet) is anthropocentric machinery. This is not a comfortable position and it has real theoretical bite. The CCC series at OHP is built on this insight.
