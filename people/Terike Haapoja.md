# Terike Haapoja

Finnish/US artist working with animal rights, nonhuman subjectivity, and ecological justice through art, performance, and writing. Often works in collaboration with Laura Gustafsson as **Gustafsson & Haapoja**. One of the most rigorous practitioners at the intersection of posthumanism, animal ethics, and art.

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-27:** Stub created during Zhang Ga / CAC deep research. *Closed Circuit – Open Duration* shown at [[Chronus Art Center]] (2017) under Zhang Ga's direction.
- **2025:** New major commission for Lönnström Art Museum — *Songs of Struggle, Songs of Life* (opens 2027), exploring singing of humans and other beings as ecological resistance.
- **2025:** *Labor Movement for All Animals* at Stoa gallery, Helsinki.

## Why Interesting
- **Animal rights through art** — not in a sentimental way but through rigorous interrogation of the nonhuman "other" in capitalist/industrial systems
- **Gustafsson & Haapoja** — sustained long-term collaboration exploring what it means to feel, remember, suffer as non-human
- Publications in serious peer-reviewed venues: *Radical History Review* (Duke), *Animals as Experiencing Entities* (Springer)
- Work shown at: National Museum of Contemporary Art Athens, Pragovka Gallery Prague, Beijing Art and Technology Biennale (Synthetic Ecology), Reykjavík Art Museum, Brussels
- Connected to posthumanist/multispecies studies networks
- **[Museum of Nonhumanity](https://www.terikehaapoja.net/)** — an ongoing project on "animalization and the climate crises"
