# Tillmann Ohm

Artist and creative technologist at [[CUDAN Open Lab]]. Builds tools at the intersection of machine learning, network science, and curation — treating the curatorial possibility space itself as a computational object. Creator of the [Collection Space Navigator (CSN)](https://collection-space-navigator.github.io/).

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-27:** Stub created from CUDAN deep research.
- **2025:** [fruit-SALAD](https://github.com/Style-Aligned-Artwork-Datasets/fruit-SALAD) dataset published in *Scientific Data* — 1,000 artworks × 10 styles × 10 artists for probing ML similarity perception.

## Background
Diploma in Fine Arts from Bauhaus University Weimar (2018). Bauhaus Graduation Scholarship for computational AI research in contemporary art. Scholarship for Visual Artists 2019 from the Cultural Foundation of the Free State of Saxony. Joined CUDAN Sept 2020.

## Key Works
- **[Collection Space Navigator (CSN)](https://collection-space-navigator.github.io/)** — browser-based open-source visualization tool for multidimensional cultural datasets. Combines 2D UMAP/t-SNE projections with configurable metadata filters. Launched with classical Western art demo; now used by museum researchers. [GitHub](https://github.com/Collection-Space-Navigator/CSN). Published ACM VINCI 2023.
- **[fruit-SALAD](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41597-025-04529-4)** — Ohm, Karjus, Tamm, Schich (2025, *Scientific Data*). Style Aligned Artwork Dataset: 1K images across 100 artist-style combinations. Tool for understanding how image embedding models perceive visual similarity. [GitHub](https://github.com/Style-Aligned-Artwork-Datasets/fruit-SALAD) [Zenodo](https://zenodo.org/records/11158522)

## Why Interesting
Ohm is the rare artist whose practice produces genuinely useful scientific infrastructure. The CSN is not an art project that happens to be usable — it's a research tool that reflects careful artistic thinking about how we navigate possibility spaces. For Amber: CSN is immediately practical (use it for visual cultural analysis); fruit-SALAD is valuable for probing how AI models perceive style and similarity, directly relevant to questions about machine aesthetics and AI art evaluation.
