# Patricia Hayes

NRF SARChI Chair in Visual History and Theory at the Centre for Humanities Research (CHR), University of the Western Cape, Cape Town, South Africa. Published extensively on colonial and documentary photography in southern Africa. Co-editor with [[Premesh Lalu]] and G. Arunima of *[Love and Revolution in the Twentieth-Century Colonial and Postcolonial World: Perspectives from South Asia and Southern Africa](https://doi.org/10.1007/978-3-030-79580-1)* (Palgrave, 2021). Long-time colleague and institutional partner of Lalu at CHR. Works on visual culture, decolonial history, Namibian and South African photography.

## Why Interesting
Visual history and theory at the intersection of colonial photography, memory, and southern Africa — a "Visual History and Theory" SARChI Chair is a rare institutional position that combines scholarly and curatorial practice. Her work on photography and decolonisation could connect to Amber's interests in more-than-human sensing and computational imaging.
