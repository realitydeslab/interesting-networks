# Meron Estefanos

Researcher at DAIR Institute, journalist, and activist. Studies social media content moderation failures and surveillance, particularly in the context of East African conflicts. Subject of German government intelligence surveillance.

## Domains
- Social media content moderation failures
- State surveillance of journalists
- Ethiopia/Eritrea conflict and genocide documentation
- Platform accountability

## Key Works
- **[CHI 2025 paper on content moderation failures in Ethiopia/Eritrea genocide](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3706598.3714010)** — 3 years in the making. Documents how social media content moderation failures fueled the deadliest genocide of the 21st century.
- [Legal complaint against German government surveillance](https://rsf.org/en/rsf-and-gff-file-complaint-against-germany-and-its-federal-intelligence-service-act-european-court) — Filed with Reporters Without Borders (RSF) and GFF against Germany's Federal Intelligence Service Act at the European Court.

## Key Communities
- **[[DAIR]]** — Researcher
- [Reporters Without Borders](https://rsf.org/) — Legal ally

## Why Interesting
Estefanos combines activist journalism with rigorous ACM research — a rare combination. The German surveillance case puts her at the intersection of state power, press freedom, and AI/digital rights. Her CHI 2025 paper is an exemplar of how DAIR embeds research in the communities most harmed by AI failures.
