# Heinz von Foerster (1911–2002)

Austrian-American physicist and cybernetician. Secretary of the Macy Cybernetics Conferences — he edited the verbatim Transactions (Vols. 6–10) with Margaret Mead and Hans-Lukas Teuber. Founder of the **Biological Computer Laboratory** at UIUC (1958–1975). The key developer of **second-order cybernetics** — the study of observing systems, where the observer is always part of what is being observed.

## Why Interesting
Von Foerster's *second-order cybernetics* is philosophically the most radical contribution of the Macy tradition: **the observer cannot be excluded from the system they observe**. This became the epistemological foundation for constructivism, for Maturana/Varela's autopoiesis, and for much of complexity science. For Amber: this is the principle behind "machine behavior as ecology" — you can't study AI systems from outside them; you are always entangled with them.
