# Bogna Konior

Scholar, writer, media theorist. Assistant Professor of Media Theory at [[NYU Shanghai IMA]], core researcher at the [[AI & Culture Research Center]]. Author of *The Dark Forest Theory of the Internet* (Polity, 2025) — now being translated into five languages. Co-editor with [[Benjamin Bratton]], [[Anna Greenspan]], and [[Amy Ireland]] of *Machine Decision is Not Final: China and the History and Future of Artificial Intelligence* (Urbanomic/MIT Press, 2025). Developing a multimedia project on AI, erotics, theology, and mysticism. Bridges Eastern European (especially Polish and Ukrainian) and Chinese intellectual traditions with contemporary technology theory — a combination almost no one else occupies with equal rigor and depth.

## Background
Born in Poland. Her intellectual trajectory spans three continents, four languages, and multiple traditions — this is not incidental but constitutive of her work:

- **BA Film Studies, [Queen Mary, University of London](https://www.qmul.ac.uk/)** (2008–2011) — visual culture foundation. London exposed her to British cultural studies and continental theory.
- **RMa Media Studies, [University of Amsterdam](https://www.uva.nl/)** (2012–2014) — entry into the Dutch media theory tradition. Amsterdam's program sits at the intersection of media archaeology (Kittler, Parikka, Zielinski) and new media studies. This is where the technical-philosophical rigor comes from.
- **PhD Humanities, [Hong Kong Baptist University](https://www.hkbu.edu.hk/)** (2014–2018) — **the pivotal move**. Choosing Hong Kong over a Western PhD positioned her at the intersection of Eastern and Western academic traditions. Her doctoral work focused on environmental media and posthumanism, producing several peer-reviewed articles on climate media, interspecies technologies, and post-cinematic realism. Funded by the **Hong Kong PhD Fellowship Scheme** (competitive government fellowship).
- **Visiting PhD Fellow, [University of Utrecht](https://www.uu.nl/), Institute for Cultural Inquiry** (2016) — maintained European connections during Hong Kong PhD.
- **Research Fellow, [Bauhaus-Universität Weimar](https://www.uni-weimar.de/), International Research Institute for Cultural Techniques and Media Philosophy** (2018) — post-PhD fellowship at one of Germany's most important media theory institutions. The Weimar media philosophy tradition (Kittler's legacy) is foundational to German media theory.
- **Lecturer, [University of Amsterdam](https://www.uva.nl/), New Media and Digital Culture** (2019) — taught Media Research, Media Theory, Digital Activism, and a graduate seminar "Post-colonial, Post-Soviet, Post-Earth." The seminar title alone reveals her intellectual ambitions.
- **Postdoctoral Fellow in Media Theory, [NYU Shanghai](https://shanghai.nyu.edu/)** (2020–2021) → **Assistant Professor of Media Theory** (2022–present) — Shanghai became her base. This positions her at the center of Chinese AI development while maintaining Western academic connections through the NYU network.

The trajectory tells a story: London → Amsterdam → Hong Kong → Weimar → Amsterdam → Shanghai. Each move was a strategic repositioning that expanded her intellectual range. She never stayed in one tradition long enough to become provincial, but always stayed long enough to absorb it deeply.

## Venue Circuit Analysis
Bogna deliberately operates across **five interconnected circuits**, each serving a strategic function:

### 1. Shanghai AI-Philosophy Hub (home base)
NYU Shanghai, [[AI & Culture Research Center]], [[Chronus Art Center]], TANK Museum, Arts Pioneer Studio, Duke Kunshan University, Institute for Contemporary Arts Shanghai, Royal Asiatic Society Shanghai, Fotografiska Shanghai.
**Function:** Direct access to Chinese AI development, dialogue with Chinese writers and artists, institutional base.

### 2. European Critical Theory Circuit
[[Antikythera]]/[[Berggruen Institute]] (LA but European-rooted), [[Urbanomic]], [[ZKM]], [[Medialab Matadero]], [[Goethe Institute]], Hatje Cantz, Sternberg Press, Conflux Festival Rotterdam, Bauhaus-Universität Weimar, Université Catholique de Lille, University of Southampton London.
**Function:** Continental philosophy meets media arts. This is where her theoretical work gets its authority.

### 3. Eastern European Tech-Culture Network
[[MoMA Warsaw]], [[Ljubljana Biennale]], [[Maribor Computer Arts Festival]], [ŠUM Journal](https://www.sum.si/), [[DATAS Residency]] (CEE consortium), [Unsound Festival](https://www.unsound.pl/), [EastEast Magazine](https://easteast.world/), Yaby Independent Art Space, Legacies of Antihumanism (Ljubljana), School for Materialist Research (Ljubljana), Korporacja Ha!Art (Kraków).
**Function:** Reclaiming Eastern European technological imagination. This is her unique intellectual territory — connecting Polish/Slovenian/Ukrainian traditions to global tech discourse.

### 4. Global Art-Theory Interface
[[e-flux]], [[Singapore Art Museum]], [[Fundação de Serralves]], Ludwig Museum Budapest, *Spike Art Magazine*, New Art City, Onassis Foundation (*Chimeras*), How Museum Shanghai.
**Function:** Where philosophical concepts meet artistic practice. Her catalog essays and commissions position her as a leading art-theory voice.

### 5. Anglo-American Academic Circuit
Cambridge (Leverhulme Centre for the Future of Intelligence), Oxford University Press, MIT Press, University of Chicago Press, Manchester University Press, Harvard Conflux, Aalto University.
**Function:** Academic legitimacy and publishing reach. OUP, MIT Press, and UChicago Press publications establish her in mainstream academic discourse.

**Strategic insight:** She's one of very few scholars simultaneously embedded in Chinese AI development (circuit 1), European critical theory (circuit 2), Eastern European intellectual traditions (circuit 3), global contemporary art (circuit 4), and Anglo-American academia (circuit 5). This five-circuit positioning is almost impossible to replicate and gives her unique intellectual authority.
