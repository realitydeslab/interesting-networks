# Tishan Hsu

Artist (b. 1951, Boston). MIT-trained architect (B.S. Environmental Design 1973, M.Arch 1975). Since the mid-1980s, has built a prescient practice exploring cognitive and physical effects of technology on the human body. Makes enigmatic paintings and sculptures using unusual materials, software, and innovative fabrication that probe the posthuman body's entanglement with screens, networks, and digital systems. Work is prophetic: he was exploring "body-as-interface" decades before the internet existed. Represented by [[Miguel Abreu Gallery]].

## Key Works / Exhibitions
- *skin-screen-grass* (Miguel Abreu Gallery, 2021) — first NYC gallery show in 32 years
- *Liquid Circuit* (SculptureCenter NYC + Hammer Museum LA, 2020) — survey exhibition
- 59th Venice Biennale: *The Milk of Dreams* (2022)
- 58th Carnegie International, Pittsburgh: *Is it morning for you yet?* (2022)
- MAMCO Geneva — first European survey (March 2024)
- Interface Remix (MOCA Toronto, 2025)
- *The Living End: Painting and Other Technologies 1970–2020* (MCA Chicago, 2025)

## Collections
Metropolitan Museum of Art, Whitney Museum, MoMA, Centre Pompidou, Tate Modern, M+, Museum für Moderne Kunst (MMK), Pinault Collection, Dallas Museum of Art.
