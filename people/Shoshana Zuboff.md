# Shoshana Zuboff

American author, social psychologist, philosopher, and professor emerita at Harvard Business School. Originator of the concept of surveillance capitalism.

## Domains
- Political economy of digital capitalism
- History of capitalism and technology
- Social psychology of technology
- Behavioral modification at scale
- Digital rights and democracy

## Key Works
- [*The Age of Surveillance Capitalism: The Fight for a Human Future at the New Frontier of Power*](https://www.publicaffairsbooks.com/titles/shoshana-zuboff/the-age-of-surveillance-capitalism/9781610395694/) (2019) — the canonical 704-page text defining surveillance capitalism
- [Big Other: Surveillance Capitalism and the Prospects of an Information Civilization](https://doi.org/10.1057/jit.2015.5) (2015, *Journal of Information Technology* 30(1): 75–89) — original conceptual paper
- [*In the Age of the Smart Machine: The Future of Work and Power*](https://www.basicbooks.com/titles/shoshana-zuboff/in-the-age-of-the-smart-machine/9780465032051/) (1988) — earlier work on computers and labor

## Why Interesting
Foundational for any critical computing framing of behavioral AI. Her concept of "instrumentarian power" — behavioral modification at scale through environment design — directly parallels questions in machine behavior about how AI systems shape human behavior. The coevolutionary extension (humans and AIs training each other) is an angle Amber could develop.
