# Silvia Lindtner

Associate Professor at the University of Michigan School of Information. PhD student of [[Paul Dourish]] at UCI (2012). Studies maker culture, hackerspaces, fabrication, and innovation ideology — particularly in China. Author of *[Prototype Nation: China and the Contested Promise of Innovation](https://press.princeton.edu/books/hardcover/9780691207674/prototype-nation)* (Princeton UP, 2020) — a critical ethnography of Shenzhen's maker scene as a site of state-capitalism and promise/exploitation. Key figure in critical HCI and feminist technology studies.

## Why Interesting
Lindtner's work on Shenzhen maker culture is directly relevant to understanding Chinese tech innovation ideologies — important for Amber's China connections and cosmotechnics interests. Her feminist + STS approach to "making" critiques the Silicon Valley maker mythology that treats hackerspaces as purely emancipatory. *Prototype Nation* is one of the most important critical tech books of the 2020s.
