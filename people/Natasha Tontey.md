# Natasha Tontey

Indonesian multimedia artist. Minahasan (ethnic group from Sulawesi, North Indonesia). Works with video, installation, performance. Explores posthuman futures, bodily transformation, surveillance subversion, ancestral ritual, and non-Western indigenous cosmologies. Described as working at intersection of technology, animism, and resistance politics.

## Key Works
- **The Phantom Combatants and the Metabolism of Disobedient Organs** (2026) — Largest work to date. Commissioned by [[LAS Art Foundation]] and Amos Rex (Helsinki). At Ateneo Veneto, Venice Biennale (61st International Art Exhibition). Retells story of 1950s Minahasan female resistance fighter Karamoy through shapeshifting avatar, biological enhancement, thermal/infrared military imaging. Minahasan animist ritual of invisibility.

## Key Communities
- [[LAS Art Foundation]] (Berlin) — major commission 2026
- Amos Rex (Helsinki) — co-commissioner 2026

## Why Interesting
Non-Western posthumanism rooted in Minahasan animism. The combination of indigenous ritual + military surveillance technology + bodily transformation is exactly the "weird + rigorous" quality that signals strong fit with Amber's more-than-human / speculative design interests. Minahasan "shapeshifter and trickster" cosmology is a non-Western cosmotechnics lens.
