# Mindy Seu

Artist, technologist, performer, writer, educator. Her practice centers on technology-driven performance and publication, focusing on internet history, digital art, cyberfeminism, and the politics of archives. She moves fluidly between archival research, techno-critical writing, lecture performance, book design, and curatorial work — medium-agnostic, research-first.

## Background
- Born 1991, California. Grew up in Orange County; parents ran a flower shop. Strong early design instincts — edited high school yearbook, designed t-shirts and flyers.
- **BA Design Media Arts, [UCLA](https://arts.ucla.edu/)** — started in Business Economics, transferred to Design Media Arts in second year. Senior thesis was a print-on-demand generator (a concept she's continued to use throughout her career). This is her alma mater; she returned as tenured faculty in 2024.
- First job at **[MoMA](https://www.moma.org/) Design Studio** in NYC, then at **[2×4](https://www.twoxfour.com)** (global design consultancy) working bicoastally between SF and NYC on fashion (Marc Jacobs, Prada), cultural institutions (Pérez Art Museum, Rubin Museum), and corporate clients (Apple, Starbucks, Target). Started feeling it was too much like advertising → pivoted to research.
- Taught interactivity at **[California College of the Arts](https://www.cca.edu/)** while developing independent archival projects focused on countercultural history.
- **MDes, [Harvard Graduate School of Design](https://www.gsd.harvard.edu/)** — focused on design research. Cross-registered at MIT Media Lab, MIT Business School, and Harvard Law School. This is where the Cyberfeminism Index began. Mentors: [Jeffrey Schnapp](https://www.gsd.harvard.edu/person/jeffrey-schnapp/) and [Malkit Shoshan](https://www.gsd.harvard.edu/person/malkit-shoshan/).
- Fellow at **[Berkman Klein Center for Internet & Society](https://cyber.harvard.edu/)** at Harvard Law School — where she realized internet policymakers and internet artists share interests but not methods. Worked on the Moral Labyrinth project with [Sarah Newman](https://cyber.harvard.edu/people/snewman) and [Jie Qi](https://cyber.harvard.edu/people/jqi).
- **Assistant Professor, [Rutgers University](https://www.masongross.rutgers.edu/)** Mason Gross School of the Arts (2020–2023). Chose Rutgers specifically because she supported the ethos of public research universities over private art schools.
- **Critic, [Yale School of Art](https://www.art.yale.edu/)** (2021–2023) — taught MFA courses including "On Gathering," "Core II: Thesis," and "Lecture Performance."
- **Associate Professor, [UCLA Department of Design Media Arts](https://arts.ucla.edu/)** (January 2024–present) — tenured faculty at her alma mater. Teaches MFA tutorials, Senior Capstone, Design Systems, Professional Practice, Typography, and Performing Lectures.

## Key Works

### [*Cyberfeminism Index*](https://cyberfeminismindex.com) (2020–2023)
A pseudo-encyclopedia gathering three decades of online activism and net art (1990–2020). Originally an [online database](https://cyberfeminismindex.com) commissioned by [[Rhizome]] and premiered with [New Museum's First Look](https://www.newmuseum.org/exhibitions/view/first-look-cyberfeminism-index). Then published as a [physical book](http://www.inventorypress.com/product/cyberfeminism-index) by [Inventory Press](http://www.inventorypress.com/), distributed by D.A.P., designed by [Laura Coombs](http://www.lauracoombs.com/), edited by Andrew Scheinman and Eugenia Bell, with image permissions by Danielle Wu. Online database developed by [Angeline Meitzler](https://angeline-meitzler.com/), with frontend support from Janine Rosen and Print CSS by [Charles Broskoski](https://www.charlesbroskoski.com/) (co-founder of Are.na). Research began at [Berkman Klein Center](https://cyber.harvard.edu/) and [Harvard GSD](https://www.gsd.harvard.edu/project/cyberfeminism-catalog-1990-2020/). Recipient of a [Graham Foundation Grant](http://www.grahamfoundation.org/grantees/6259-cyberfeminism-catalog). Featured in Vanity Fair, Frieze, Brooklyn Rail, Dazed, Fast Company, High Snobiety, and more. International book tour: **85 events in 50 cities across 21 countries** (2022–23), with sold-out events at [New Museum](https://www.newmuseum.org/calendar/view/1829/book-launch-cyberfeminism-index) (NYC), [Whitechapel Gallery](https://www.whitechapelgallery.org/events/cyberfeminism-index/) (London), [Lafayette Anticipations](https://www.lafayetteanticipations.com/en/manifestation/mindy-seu-cyberfeminism-index) (Paris), [MCA Australia](https://4a.com.au/events/the-digital-dissent-of-cyberfeminism) (Sydney), and [Amant Foundation](https://www.amant.org/programs/103-cyberfeminism-index-blow-baby-blow) (Brooklyn).

### [*A Sexual History of the Internet*](https://asexualhistoryoftheinternet.com/) (2025–ongoing)
A two-part project: (1) a participatory lecture performance and (2) a 700+ page artist book. Reveals the pervasive and perverted origins of digital tools. In the performance, audience members read citations aloud from a shared script on their mobile phones — "performance as re-citation." The Instagram-Stories-as-Lecture format was first conceived by [Julio Correa](https://www.instagram.com/juliocorreaestrada/) in Seu's Lecture Performance studio course (Fall 2023) at Yale School of Art; she further developed it in collaboration with Correa. The [book](https://darkforest.metalabel.com/asexualhistoryoftheinternet) is published on [Metalabel](https://www.metalabel.com/) as a financial experiment: 45 cited people split 30% of profits through "Citational Splits." Collaborators: [Laura Coombs](https://www.lauracoombs.com/) (design), [Meg Miller](https://megmiller.world/) (editing), [Ven Qiu](https://venqiu.net/) (illustrations), Jon Gacnik (timecodes), Gabriel Noguez (video), Tom Hancocks (3D), [Charles Broskoski](https://www.charlesbroskoski.com/) (web), [Yancey Strickler](https://yanceystrickler.com/) (Metalabel), Gia Kuan (consultant). All 2025 performances sold out: [Pioneer Works](https://pioneerworks.org/programs/mindy-seu-a-sexual-history-of-the-internet) (NYC), [MOCA](https://www.moca.org/program/mindy-seu-a-sexual-history-of-the-internet) (LA), [KW](https://www.kw-berlin.de/en/mindy-seu/) (Berlin), [MOT](https://tokyoartbookfair.com/events/10333/) (Tokyo), [Gray Area](https://grayarea.org/event/mindy-seu-a-sexual-history-of-the-internet/) (SF), and more. 2026 tour continues: MCA Chicago, ICA London, Mutek Montreal, Vivid Minds Sydney, Melbourne, Milan, Seattle, Fort Worth, Riga.

## Venue Circuit Analysis
Mindy Seu moves across **four distinct circuits**, each serving a different strategic purpose:

1. **NYC art-tech/cultural institutions**: Pioneer Works, New Museum/Rhizome, Amant Foundation, Performance Space New York — her home base, where she builds deep institutional relationships.
2. **Global contemporary art museums**: MOCA (LA), KW (Berlin), Whitechapel (London), Lafayette Anticipations (Paris), MCA (Sydney/Chicago), MOT (Tokyo), ICA (London), Kunstverein (Hamburg), OCA (Oslo) — the blue-chip museum circuit. She's moved from independent/alternative venues to major institutions remarkably fast.
3. **Academic institutions**: Harvard GSD, Yale School of Art, UCLA, Rutgers, Columbia, Central Saint Martins, MIT Media Lab — strong academic grounding gives her work intellectual legitimacy that pure art-world figures lack.
4. **Design/tech crossover**: Serpentine, CCA Montreal, Metalabel, Are.na, Internet Archive, Gray Area, Pornhub, SSENSE, Google — she deliberately performs at non-art venues to reach different audiences.

**Key pattern:** She sold out EVERY SINGLE performance of *A Sexual History of the Internet* in 2025. This is extraordinary for a lecture performance — it means she's crossed from niche academic/art audiences into genuine cultural phenomenon status. The LA Times calling her "a public intellectual" is accurate.

**Small venue choices:** La Mosca (Madrid), Section80 (Milan), Kim? (Riga) — she deliberately plays emerging/independent venues alongside major museums, maintaining her alternative credentials while scaling up.

## Why Interesting
Mindy Seu represents a rare synthesis: genuine academic rigor (Harvard GSD, Berkman Klein), radical archival politics (Cyberfeminism Index as counter-history), and mainstream cultural reach (sold-out performances at MOCA, every show selling out). She's turned the spreadsheet — the most banal digital tool — into a site of feminist resistance and performance.

The Cyberfeminism Index makes visible a vast, often-forgotten history of feminist tech engagement from 1990–2020. It's not just a book but an entire ecosystem: online database, physical publication, AR lecture performance, international tour. This is research-as-infrastructure.

*A Sexual History of the Internet* takes this further with the Citational Splits model — a genuine financial experiment in how to credit and compensate the people you cite. This connects to Amber's interest in alternative research economies and critical approaches to knowledge production.

Her movement between internet policy (Berkman Klein), digital art ([[Rhizome]]), planetary computation ([[Antikythera]]), and mainstream institutions (MOCA, Artforum, LA Times) shows someone who deliberately bridges siloed worlds. The LA Times called it: "Her unusual power is the ability to move through different fields, Trojan-horsing her theories across academia, the art world, the lit scene, tech, fashion, et al."

## Research Taste Analysis

### Conceptual Strategy
- **Archive-as-argument** — doesn't write manifestos; builds comprehensive archives that make the argument through sheer accumulation. The Cyberfeminism Index doesn't argue that cyberfeminism matters — it presents 700+ entries and lets the mass of evidence speak.
- **Medium-agnostic research** — starts with research, lets form emerge. Same content becomes database, book, AR performance, lecture, financial experiment. This is a strategy of maximum surface area from single research investments.
- **Counter-history as creative practice** — positions archival work as activism. "To name something is to claim its existence." Naming and indexing as political acts.

### Career Architecture
- **Institutional triangulation** — simultaneously holds positions in art (Rhizome/New Museum), academia (UCLA/Yale), and tech-criticism (Berkman Klein/Antikythera). Each validates her in the others' eyes.
- **Public pedagogy** — all syllabi, courses, and teaching materials publicly archived at [designforthe.net](https://designforthe.net/). This builds reputation far beyond her actual students and positions her as a thought leader in design education.
- **Strategic return** — went back to UCLA (alma mater) as tenured faculty, completing a full circle that signals both loyalty and arrival.

### Methodology Signature
- **Spreadsheet epistemology** — uses Google Sheets and Are.na as primary research tools, turning "mundane" organizational formats into intellectual frameworks. Her CV is a spreadsheet. Her index is a spreadsheet. This is a deliberate aesthetic and political choice.
