# Angelina McMillan-Major

Computational linguist and dataset documentation researcher. PhD from UW Linguistics (2023), supervised by [[Emily Bender]]. Co-author of [Stochastic Parrots](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3442188.3445922) (FAccT 2021) — the only grad student co-author, contributing critical framing on dataset bias. Co-developed the Data Statements framework (Versions 2 and 3) with Bender and Batya Friedman. Her dissertation: [Language Dataset Documentation Design: Learning from Deaf and Indigenous Communities](http://hdl.handle.net/1773/50854) — shifted data statements to include prospective documentation and community co-design.

## Why Interesting
Works at the intersection of critical NLP, Indigenous data sovereignty, and Deaf community needs. One of the few people doing NLP documentation work *with* language communities rather than about them.
