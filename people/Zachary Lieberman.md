# Zachary Lieberman

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Created stub note. Discovered through **[[Golan Levin]]** research as primary creative collaborator (Tmema, 2002–2007).

American new media artist, programmer, educator. Primary creative collaborator with **[[Golan Levin]]** under the name Tmema (2002–2007), producing some of the most significant interactive artworks of the early 2000s. Co-creator of **[openFrameworks](https://openframeworks.cc/)** (the C++ creative coding toolkit used by tens of thousands of artists). Co-founder of the **[[School for Poetic Computation]]** (SFPC). Now leads the Future Sketches group at MIT Media Lab.

## Key Works
- **Messa di Voce** (2003, with [[Golan Levin]]) — Real-time speech visualization performance. Golden touchstone of interactive art.
- **Re:MARK** (2002, with [[Golan Levin]]) — Augmented reality handwriting visualization.
- **The Manual Input Sessions** (2004, with [[Golan Levin]]) — Gestural hand interaction installation.
- **[openFrameworks](https://openframeworks.cc/)** (co-created with Theo Watson and Arturo Castro) — Open-source C++ library for creative coding. Equivalent to Processing but lower-level and more powerful. Used globally.
- **Eyewriter** (2010, with others) — Eye-tracking graffiti tool for paralyzed artist. Golden Nica, Prix Ars Electronica 2010.

## Key Communities

- **[[MIT Media Lab]]** — Future Sketches group leader
- **[[Parsons School of Design]]** — Faculty, graphics programming
- **[[Eyebeam]]** — Residency alum

## Why Interesting
Lieberman is one of the **pillars of the creative coding world** — through openFrameworks (the tool), SFPC (the school), and his art practice. His collaboration with Levin during 2002–2007 produced work that still defines what interactive art can be. Highly networked; central to the computational creativity community.
