# Jolene Rickard

Tuscarora Nation, Turtle Clan curator, scholar, and artist. Professor at Cornell University (Art & Art History). Key theorist of "visual sovereignty" in Indigenous contemporary art — a concept that fundamentally shaped how Indigenous art is understood in academic and curatorial circles. Member of [[Forge Project]]'s Indigenous Steering Council. Contributed to *[Native Visual Sovereignty: A Reader on Art and Performance](https://dfpress.org/books/native-visual-sovereignty/)* (2025, ed. [[Candice Hopkins]]).

## Key Concepts
- **Visual sovereignty** — the right of Indigenous peoples to control their own representation; developed as a counter to dominant museum and curatorial frameworks. *Forge Project's* publication *Native Visual Sovereignty* takes this concept as its central framework.

## Why Interesting
Rickard is the theorist who developed "visual sovereignty" — one of the most influential concepts in contemporary Indigenous art theory. Understanding her work is essential for understanding the intellectual framework that underlies Hopkins's curatorial practice and the Forge Project's mission.
