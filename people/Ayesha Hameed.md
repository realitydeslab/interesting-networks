# Ayesha Hameed

Artist and theorist working at intersection of performance, video, and critical theory. Focuses on borders, topologies, migration, and the underside of modernity. Doctoral work (York University) examined how early modern London's architecture and visual culture manifested colonialism and slavery — using Benjamin's dialectical image and Deleuzian virtuality. Now at Goldsmiths.

## Domains
- Performance art / video installation
- Migration, sans-papiers organizing, migrant subjectivity
- Border theory, topologies
- Deleuzian critical theory applied to colonial history

## Key Communities

## Why Interesting
One of the few figures who bridges the SenseLab affect-theory world with explicitly political migration and border work. Goldsmiths is very proximate to Amber (London). Her methodology (Deleuzian virtuality applied to colonial history) is unusual and productive.
