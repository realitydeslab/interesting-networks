# Anab Jain

Co-founder and Director of [[Superflux]], Professor of Design Investigations at the [[University of Applied Arts Vienna]] (die Angewandte), TED Fellow, and Royal Designer for Industry. One of the most consequential design thinkers alive: she created the vocabulary of **more-than-human-centred design**, built the institutional architecture to propagate it (studio + professorship + biennales + policy), and has done so from a distinctly postcolonial, South Asian perspective that sits uncomfortably inside Western design discourse — which is exactly what makes her interesting.

Grew up in Ahmedabad, India. Trained in filmmaking at the **National Institute of Design (NID)** — this is relevant: she thinks in narrative and experience, not product. Moved to London for a Masters at **Royal College of Art**. Spent time at **Microsoft Research Cambridge** working on machine intelligence. Co-founded Superflux with **Jon Ardern** in 2009. Since 2016, simultaneously leads the **Design Investigations** department at dieAngewandte in Vienna — arguably the only European design school programme explicitly committed to critical, speculative, and more-than-human futures work.

She's London-based day-to-day (dieAngewandte is a short flight). She travels constantly for talks, exhibitions, and biennale commissions.

## Why Interesting

Anab is the clearest example in the design world of someone who has actually moved the Overton window — not just imagined futures but changed what counts as legitimate design practice. She introduced "more-than-human centred design" as a direct critique of HCI's human-centred orthodoxy and made it stick, across studios, schools, and galleries. Her career is built on a few deep bets: that experiential futures change minds more than policy papers, that design has to reckon with ecology, and that speculative practice belongs in governance.

For Amber specifically: her intellectual project is the closest thing in design to what Amber is building in computing. Both are asking: what happens when we take non-human agency seriously — not as a metaphor but as a design and governance constraint? Amber does it through machine behavior and agent ethology; Anab does it through speculative design and ecological AI. The conceptual terrain is nearly identical. The tools are different.

She's also interesting as a **South Asian woman in European design institutions** — a position she navigates openly. The De Volkskrant interview (the Dutch national newspaper) covered her thinking on colonial histories and whose futures get imagined. This thread in her work — who does futures thinking serve, and who gets excluded — connects directly to Amber's interest in power and technology.

## Intellectual Contributions

### 1. Design for the New Normal (2012)
Early concept: design should engage with mess, disruption, and uncertainty rather than optimize existing systems. Influential in critical design circles before "speculative design" became mainstream.
→ [Essay on Superflux](https://superflux.in/index.php/work/design-new-normal/)

### 2. More-than-Human Centred Design (2018)
Keynote at **Interaction Design Conference (IXDA)**, Lyon 2018. Direct challenge to the HCI/UX orthodoxy of "human-centred design" — argues that centering only humans in the design process has caused ecological destruction. Design must account for multispecies, ecological, and non-human perspectives.
→ [Video: Vimeo](https://vimeo.com/255010942) | [Wired/Beyond the Beyond writeup](https://www.wired.com/beyond-the-beyond/2018/04/anab-jain-human-centred-design/)

### 3. Calling for a More-Than-Human Politics (2019)
Talk at **Tentacular Festival**, November 2019. Extends the design concept into politics — argues for institutional and governance structures that represent non-human interests. Written up and published on Superflux site.
→ [Superflux: Talk transcript](https://superflux.in/index.php/work/calling-for-a-more-than-human-politics-a-talk-by-anab-jain-at-tentacular-festival-november-2019/)

### 4. Ancillary Design (2022)
A newer concept: designing *alongside* or *adjacent to* rather than *for*. Less anthropocentric framing for design agency.
→ [YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LMHB4XJ3vHw)

### 5. Ecological AI (2023–)
Superflux's emerging framework: AI systems designed *in service of* ecological intelligence — trained on indigenous wisdom, folklore, and environmental sensing rather than data extraction. The *Ecological Intelligence Agency* (Policy Lab/DEFRA 2023) and *Nobody Told Me Rivers Dream* (Design Museum 2025) are the two major demonstrations so far. Conceptually: AI that *amplifies* the intelligence of ecosystems rather than flattening it. The inversion — "environment prompts AI, AI prompts humans" — is the cleanest formulation of this.

### 6. A More Than Human Manifesto (2021)
Published for Dezeen 15 festival. Six moves: from **fixing to caring**, **planning to gardening**, **systems to assemblages**, **innovation to resurgence**, **independence to interdependence**, **extinction to precarity**. Widely circulated.
→ [Dezeen](https://www.dezeen.com/2021/11/18/superflux-dezeen-15-manifesto-human-exceptionalism/) | [Superflux](https://superflux.in/index.php/a-more-than-human-manifesto/)

## Key Projects

(Full studio portfolio in [[Superflux]] note. Below: projects where Anab's personal intellectual voice is most visible.)

### Nobody Told Me Rivers Dream (2025)
**Design Museum London** — "More than Human: Making with the Living World" (from 11 July 2025). Three handcrafted sculptural sensor objects positioned along the Thames: a birdsong sensor, a water flow sensor, a weather sensor. AI trained not just on scientific data but on *folklore, oral traditions, and indigenous ecological wisdom*. The outputs are poetic prompts for visitors — not data dashboards. Philosophical position: "Rather than asking humans to prompt AI, the project flips this relationship: the environment itself prompts the AI, which in turn prompts humans."
→ [Superflux](https://superflux.in/index.php/work/nobody-told-me-rivers-dream/)

Anab's quote: *"AI is often criticised as an extractive, disembodied technology — and rightly so. But what if AI could be used in the service of ecological intelligence, rather than overriding it?"*

### Refuge for Resurgence (2021)
**Venice Architecture Biennale** (La Biennale di Venezia, Arsenale), 22 May – 21 November 2021. A multispecies banquet: fox, rat, wasp, pigeon, cow, human adults and child, wild boar, snake, beaver, wolf, raven and mushroom gathered around a shared table in the ruins of the Anthropocene. *"The scene lays bare a conversation between the paralysis of fear and the audacity of hope."* Curated by Hashim Sarkis. Photography by Giorgio Lazzaro. Funded by EU Horizon 2020 / CreaTures project.
→ [Superflux](https://superflux.in/index.php/work/refuge-for-resurgence/) | [Dezeen](https://www.dezeen.com/2021/05/19/superflux-refuge-for-resurgence-venice-architecture-biennale/) | [FT Weekend](https://www.ft.com/content/c885297f-db9f-4f93-b626-13773103a61b)

### Invocation for Hope (2021)
**Vienna Biennale at MAK** (Museum der Angewandten Kunst), 28 May – 3 October 2021. 400+ fire-blackened pine trees arranged in a perfect grid — a carbonized, calamitous landscape — with a living resurgent forest hidden at its heart. Soundscape by **Cosmo Sheldrake**. Described as "one of the most impressive installations of recent times" (ORF). Commissioner: MAK Director Christoph Thun-Hohenstein. Curator: Marlies Wirth.
→ [Superflux](https://superflux.in/index.php/work/invocation-for-hope-3/) | [Fast Company](https://www.fastcompany.com/90641912/this-haunting-forest-offers-hope-in-the-face-of-climate-change) | [Dezeen](https://www.dezeen.com/2021/06/04/superflux-invocation-for-hope-forest/)

### The Ecological Intelligence Agency (2023)
**UK Policy Lab + Defra Futures**. Commissioned for the "Changing Course" forum. Imagines an autonomous inter-departmental government agency composed of localised AI models — each trained on indigenous ecological knowledge, community stewards, and scientific data — that advocate for ecological flourishing. AI giving voice to the River Roding: lamenting forever chemicals, imagining harmonious futures. Presented to 80+ policymakers, academics, NGOs. Precursor to *Nobody Told Me Rivers Dream*.
→ [Superflux](https://superflux.in/index.php/work/the-ecological-intelligence-agency/)

### Mitigation of Shock: London (2017)
**CCCB Barcelona** "After the End of the World" — then widely traveled. A fully-built future London apartment set in 2050. Food-insecure, climate-disrupted world: fogponics food stacks, urban animal trapping, improvised recipes, post-collapse barter economy. Every detail worked through. Described by Julian Oliver as "a reference piece in the coming years." Research involved NASA, UK Met Office, Forum for the Future. Traveled to ArtScience Museum Singapore (*Mitigation of Shock Singapore*).

This is the project that established Superflux's signature mode: *actual rooms you walk through*, not renders or prototypes. The experience as epistemology.
→ [Superflux](https://superflux.in/index.php/work/mitigation-of-shock/) | [TED Ideas](https://ideas.ted.com/an-unsettling-peek-into-the-reality-of-life-in-2050/)

### After Abundance (2018)
**London Design Biennale 2018** — Austria's official entry, shown at Somerset House. Co-curated with **Gerald Bast** (President of dieAngewandte). Book published by **Birkhäuser**, co-edited with **Thomas Geisler**. Explores climate change in the Alps, speculative design as national pavilion strategy. Covered in New York Times, Zeit im Bild; Top Ten picks from Metropolis Magazine and Abitare.
→ [DesignInvestigations.at](https://designinvestigations.at/2018/08/22/presenting-at-the-london-design-biennale-2018/)

### The Quiet Enchanting (2024)
Speculative digital frieze wrapping the façade of **Bush House, Strand, London**. Commissioned by **King's Culture, King's College London**. Year-long research residency (Cascade Inquiry) preceded it — deep-listening conversations with King's professors across neuroscience, geography, war studies, governance, and finance. Result: a mythological narrative of a rewilded London, referencing pagan folklore and animist belief systems, asking *"How can we rewild ourselves?"* The frieze became the basis for a Citizens Assembly (September 2024) on positive climate action.
→ [Superflux](https://superflux.in/index.php/work/the-quiet-enchanting/)

### It's Easier to Imagine the End of Capitalism than the End of the World (2022)
**Serpentine North Gallery**, Back to Earth show. A quiet intervention in the gallery's "shop": books by Kate Raworth, Robin Wall Kimmerer, Merlin Sheldrake, Aaron Bastani — displayed on Enzo Mari's open-source *Autoprogettazione* furniture (1974). The title flips Mark Fisher's famous formulation. Collaboration with designer **Ghazaal Vojdani** (Studio Ghazaal Vojdani). Online companion: [endofcapitalism.shop](http://endofcapitalism.shop).

The Artistic Director of Back to Earth was **Hans Ulrich Obrist**; General Ecology founder **Lucia Pietroiusti**. Good signal of institutional relationships.
→ [Superflux](https://superflux.in/index.php/work/its-easier-to-imagine-the-end-of-capitalism-than-the-end-of-the-world/)

### How Will We Work? (Vienna Biennale 2017)
Exhibition at **Angewandte Innovation Lab**, co-curated with **Gerald Bast** (President, dieAngewandte). Investigated robots and the future of work for MAK's Vienna Biennale. Early academic-studio integration work.

## Teaching: Design Investigations, dieAngewandte Vienna

Since 2016, Anab leads the **Design Investigations** department at the University of Applied Arts Vienna (die Angewandte). Previous professor in the same chair: **Fiona Raby** (of Dunne & Raby). This continuity matters: she inherited the most explicitly critical/speculative design programme in continental Europe and has expanded it.

The course: *"for the curious and the critically engaged... for the responsible radicals who want to challenge and change the world around them."* Students work on speculative projects dealing with climate, AI, ecology, democracy, energy. Annual themes have included "Hungry Ghosts" (food in the Anthropocene), "Jelly Worlds" (deep time and alternate evolutionary histories), "Futures of Democracy?", "Design for Agency".

Student work shows at **Vienna Design Week**, **Global Grad Show (Dubai)**, and the school's own exhibitons. The programme hosted **Fiona Raby** for a guest lecture in 2019; also hosted a futures-of-design panel in December 2023 ("Possible Futures of Design Education").

The programme's website ([designinvestigations.at](https://designinvestigations.at/)) is an archive of student projects — worth browsing for the current generation of critical designers coming out of Europe.
→ [[Design Investigations — dieAngewandte Vienna]]

## For Amber

### Why ⭐⭐⭐

The intellectual overlap is almost total. Amber's research threads — more-than-human design, speculative design, machine behavior, agent ethology, posthumanism, ALife — map directly onto Anab's life work. Anab's **Ecological AI** framing (EIA 2023, Nobody Told Me Rivers Dream 2025) is essentially the design-and-art version of what Amber is building through computation: AI systems that don't just serve human interests but are *of* the ecological system they operate in.

Three more specific reasons:

1. **More-than-human as shared vocabulary.** Amber teaches "somaesthetic realities" and "speculative realities" at CAA. Anab runs the flagship European programme for exactly this. The vocabulary they share — more-than-human, speculative futures, design fiction — creates immediate common ground. An introduction would not require context-setting.

2. **Ecological AI / machine behavior overlap.** Anab's *Ecological Intelligence Agency* and *Nobody Told Me Rivers Dream* are art and policy instantiations of an argument Amber makes through research: non-human agents deserve representation in our systems. Amber's agent ethology / machine behavior work is the scientific/STS framing; Anab's is the design/gallery/policy framing. Together they could be mutually reinforcing.

3. **London proximity + accessible via academic channels.** Anab is reachable via dieAngewandte Vienna (her official academic affiliation) or through Superflux. She's publicly active. She's in London. Amber is in Oxford. There's no geographic barrier.
