# Shari Frilot

Founder and Chief Curator of [[Sundance New Frontier]] (2007–present). Senior Programmer at Sundance Film Festival. The most influential curator in immersive and new media art in the US — her taste has shaped what "serious" VR/AR art means institutionally for nearly two decades. Previously programmed queer cinema at Sundance.

## Domains
- Immersive media curation (VR/AR/installation)
- Queer cinema and politics
- Technology × social justice intersection
- New media art theory

## Background
Rose through Sundance as a queer film programmer before founding New Frontier in 2007. Her background in queer film — which was itself once considered fringe and is now mainstream — shapes her approach to immersive tech: she sees VR/AR as art forms fighting for legitimacy, centered on identity, embodiment, and social justice. Connected to Afrofuturist and feminist networks through years of curation.

## Key Works (Curatorial)
- [[Sundance New Frontier]] (2007–present) — founded and runs the program
- First VR residency: gave Lynette Wallworth the first Sundance VR residency (~2016)
- SPHERES (2018) — curated Eliza McNitt's seven-figure VR deal
- NeuroSpeculative AfroFeminism (2017) — Hyphen-Labs breakthrough

## Why Interesting
She IS the taste of New Frontier. Understanding Frilot = understanding what gets into the world's most prestigious immersive art exhibition. Her consistent centering of feminist, Afrofuturist, and non-Western perspectives in technology art is intellectually rigorous and ethically serious.
