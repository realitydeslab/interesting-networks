# Ingrid Burrington

Writer, artist, and researcher who maps the physical and political infrastructure of the internet. Author of *[Networks of New York: An Illustrated Field Guide to Urban Internet Infrastructure](https://www.mhpbooks.com/books/networks-of-new-york/)* (Melville House, 2016). Founding member of [[Deep Lab]]. Represented by [NOME Gallery](https://nomegallery.com/artists/ingrid-burrington/), Berlin. Recently completed doctorate at University of Delaware (January 2026). Participates in [[Rhizome]] Seven on Seven (2016) paired with [[Meredith Whittaker]].

## Background
Born 1987. BFA from [Maryland Institute College of Art (MICA)](https://www.mica.edu/). Career began with data visualization, shifted toward internet infrastructure studies after Snowden revelations (2013). Has since built a practice combining illustration, writing, mapping, and critical theory to make internet infrastructure visible and politically legible. Represented by NOME Gallery, Berlin. Completed PhD at University of Delaware's Department of Geography and Spatial Sciences (January 2026).

Teaching positions: Cooper Union (2018), Rhode Island School of Design (2017), [School for Poetic Computation](https://sfpc.study/) (2015–2016), NYU Tisch ITP (2020).

## Why Interesting
Burrington's practice sits at the intersection of critical infrastructure studies, art, and feminist tech critique. Her *Networks of New York* is the canonical artistic-critical work on physical internet infrastructure — a model for how illustration and mapping can make invisible power visible. The Seven on Seven pairing with Whittaker shows how art and activism can productively cross-pollinate in critical tech.

Her completed PhD (Geography/Spatial Sciences, Delaware) is interesting: she moved from art practice toward rigorous spatial theory — a trajectory that bridges art-tech and academic worlds.
