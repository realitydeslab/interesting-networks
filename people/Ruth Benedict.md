# Ruth Benedict (1887–1948)

American cultural anthropologist. Trained under Franz Boas at Columbia. Mentor and intimate of [[Margaret Mead]]. *Patterns of Culture* (1934) is the classic statement of cultural relativism: cultures are integrated wholes, each with its own logic, none objectively superior. Her *The Chrysanthemum and the Sword* (1946) studied Japanese culture at a distance for the US government during WWII. Co-directed the wartime Research in Contemporary Culture project with Mead at Columbia.

## Why Interesting

The Boasian school (Boas → Benedict → Mead) is the intellectual genealogy of cultural constructivism. Benedict's argument that cultures are integrated, self-consistent wholes (not hierarchically ranked) is the same move that more-than-human design makes: don't rank human vs. nonhuman, understand each on its own terms. Her influence on Mead — and through Mead on feminist theory and cultural studies — is foundational.
