# Stefano Harney

Professor of Strategic Management at Singapore University of Technology and Design (SUTD). Co-author (with Fred Moten) of *The Undercommons: Fugitive Planning and Black Study* (Minor Compositions, 2013) — one of the most downloaded and circulated texts in Black radical theory, education theory, and post-academic philosophy.

His academic home is management/organization studies, but his work with Moten has been adopted far beyond that. The *Undercommons* is assigned in art schools, performance studies programs, architectural programs, and radical pedagogy contexts worldwide.

## The Undercommons

[*The Undercommons: Fugitive Planning and Black Study*](http://www.minorcompositions.info/wp-content/uploads/2013/04/undercommons-web.pdf) (with [[Fred Moten]]; Minor Compositions, 2013) — free download. The undercommons is the space of Black study, collective life, and fugitive planning that happens around, below, and despite universities, corporations, and state institutions. Key concepts: the undercommons vs. the commons (the commons is captured; the undercommons escapes), fugitive planning (planning without a plan, collectivity without institution), the "undercommons of the university" (the students and staff who live in it but aren't captured by its logics).

The book is notable for its form as much as its content: it was published by the anarchist/radical press Minor Compositions (Anticopyright) and made freely downloadable from the start. This was itself a demonstration of the undercommons logic.

## Connection to Manning

Harney blurbed [[Erin Manning]]'s *For a Pragmatics of the Useless* (Duke UP, 2020) and is engaged throughout that book's argument about alter-pedagogy and alter-economy. His concept of "the undercommons of the university" — the ungovernable space of learning that happens outside the official curriculum — directly feeds Manning's "useless" practices that generate surplus value of life outside capitalist capture.

## Other Work

- *State/Flow: Political Economy and the Arts* (2002) — earlier work on arts and political economy
- *All Incomplete* (with Fred Moten; Minor Compositions, 2021) — continuation of the *Undercommons* project; logistics, finance, and study
- *The Undercommons and Destituent Power* (Minor Compositions, 2023) — further extension

## Why Interesting

Harney is interesting at the intersection of management theory (logistics, organization) and radical theory (Black study, fugitivity). This unusual disciplinary position means he brings concepts from management/logistics (supply chain, planning, infrastructure) into critical theory in ways that are genuinely novel.

His publishing choices (Minor Compositions, free downloads) are themselves demonstrations of his theory — you can't argue for the undercommons while keeping your arguments inside paywalled journals.
