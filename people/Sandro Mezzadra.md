# Sandro Mezzadra

Italian political theorist and activist. Specializes in migration theory, borders, postcolonialism, and the political economy of capitalism. Co-authored *Border as Method, or the Multiplication of Labor* (2013, with Brett Neilson) and *The Politics of Operations: Excavating Contemporary Capitalism* (2019, with Neilson). Emerging as [[Michael Hardt]]'s most active intellectual collaborator after Antonio Negri's death.

## Why Interesting

His border/migration frame intersects with AI governance (biometric borders, surveillance at checkpoints) and platform labor (migrant workers, logistics). With Hardt, he co-authored ["A Global War Regime"](https://newleftreview.org/sidecar/posts/a-global-war-regime) (NLR Sidecar, 2024) — one of the clearest syntheses of how capitalism and war have merged in the 2020s. His forthcoming *The Rest and the West: Capital and Power in a Multipolar World* (Verso, with Brett Neilson) is highly anticipated.

## Key Works
- *Border as Method, or the Multiplication of Labor* (2013, with Brett Neilson) — Duke UP. Major rethinking of borders as productive forces in global capitalism.
- *The Politics of Operations: Excavating Contemporary Capitalism* (2019, with Brett Neilson) — Duke UP. On financial operations, logistics, and the transformation of capitalism.
- *The Rest and the West: Capital and Power in a Multipolar World* (forthcoming, Verso, with Brett Neilson)
