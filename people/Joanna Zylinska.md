# Joanna Zylinska

Artist, writer, curator, and **Professor of Media Philosophy + Critical Digital Practice** at King's College London (since 2021). Director of the **Centre for Attention Studies** at King's. Co-investigator of the **Creative AI Lab** (King's Department of Digital Humanities + Serpentine Galleries). Born Joanna Żylińska in 1971 in Poland; PhD from the University of Wrocław; moved to UK for graduate studies. Previously Professor of New Media and Communications at Goldsmiths, University of London (Co-Head of Department 2017–2020). Holds visiting chairs at Shandong University (China), University of Minnesota, and McGill University.

Author of **nine books** on philosophy, technology, and art. Key advocate of "radical open access" through the Open Humanities Press and the Radical Open Access Collective. One of the most rigorous thinkers at the intersection of AI, photography, creativity, and posthumanism — combining theory with her own image-based art practice.

---

## Core Intellectual Project

Zylinska's work interrogates the **constitution of the human** as both a species and a historical subject, adopting a "deep time" geological probe. She traces the emergence of the human in conjunction with technologies — tools, artefacts, communication, language, art, media. The planetary perspective of her work is anchored in the socio-political concerns of the here and now: ecological and economic crises, and the gendering and racialisation of apocalyptic narratives.

Three major threads:
1. **Nonhuman Photography / Perception Machine** — redefining image-making beyond human-centrism; includes automated astronomy, computational photography, Google Earth, AI-generated imagery, fossils. Argues that photography is no longer exclusively a human act.
2. **Feminist Counterapocalypse** — challenging masculinist and technicist "Exit Man" narratives (Silicon Valley transhumanism, Musk-to-Mars etc.); proposes an ethics of entanglement, coexistence, and collaboration across species and ecologies.
3. **AI Art Criticism** — critiques "candy crush" generative AI aesthetics; argues that understanding AI creativity requires addressing labour, roboticisation, power structures, and extinction — not just aesthetics.

Recent focus: **"Diffused Seeing"** (2024) — how generative AI (Stable Diffusion, DALL·E, Midjourney) transforms the relationship between seeing and understanding; whether the current sociotechnical moment offers emancipatory possibilities alongside its nihilism.

## Art Practice

Zylinska holds an **MA in photography** (completed while teaching at Goldsmiths) which pivotally shifted her from pure theory to a hybrid theory-practice mode. Her art is image-based and photomedia-centric.

**Key works:**
- **"Exit Man"** (2017) — 6-minute photo-film companion to *The End of Man*; black-and-white dissolving images, pitch-shifted voice, feminist counter-narrative to the apocalypse; featured at Vimeo
- **"View from the Window"** (2020) — AI art project in *AI Art* book; explores human and nonhuman forms of intelligence, perception, and action
- **Nonhuman Photography series** — companion to MIT Press book; website nonhuman.photography includes "Always Digital," "Autographer," "iEarth," "Googled," "Wires," "Museum," "Perception Machine" series
- **Photomediations** (2016) — collaborative experimental publishing project

Quote: *"I prefer my nature remanufactured."* (echoing Haraway — "I would rather be a cyborg than a goddess.")

## Ideas Worth Exploring (for Amber)

1. **Nonhuman Photography** — Zylinska's argument that fossils, satellite imagery, and AI images are all "photographs" radically expands what counts as image-making. Directly relevant to Amber's more-than-human + nonhuman agency threads.

2. **Feminist Counterapocalypse as method** — Not just content, but a *method* for challenging techno-masculinist narratives (AI hype, longtermism, transhumanism). Amber's critical computing interest meets this squarely.

3. **Minimal Ethics for the Anthropocene** — A "non-normative" ethics of responsibility that doesn't collapse into individualism or solutionism. The non-prescriptive "ethics of entanglement" is interesting for design ethics.

4. **Creative AI Lab model** — Partnership between university (King's) and gallery (Serpentine) as a research infrastructure model. Amber is interested in communities as research infrastructure; this is a good case study.

5. **PhD students she's supervised** — *Fungi Media* (Piotr Bockowski, published in her series), *Geological Filmmaking* (Sasha Litvintseva), *Light at Night* — unusual material/elemental media projects that align with Amber's interests.
