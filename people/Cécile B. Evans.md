# Cécile B. Evans

American-Belgian artist (they/them) examining the value of emotion and its rebellion when it encounters ideological, physical, and technological structures. Multi-channel video, film, installation. Collections: MoMA NY, Whitney, Centre Pompidou, Louisiana Museum of Modern Art, National Museum of Modern and Contemporary Art Seoul. Commissioned by Tate Liverpool, Tramway, Serpentine Galleries, Castello di Rivoli, Museum Abteiberg. Films screened at New York Film Festival and Rotterdam International. Was visiting artist-professor at Le Fresnoy for Panorama 25 (2023).

## Why Interesting
Works directly on agency, emotion, and technological/ideological structures — relevant to Amber's interests in machine behavior, agent ethology, and more-than-human design. The Serpentine + Centre Pompidou + MoMA circuit is useful institutional context.
