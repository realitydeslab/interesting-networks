# Martyna Marciniak

Polish artist and researcher, Berlin-based. Practice: sculpture, 3D art, animation, film — using spatial storytelling, speculative fiction, and 3D reconstruction to interrogate how design and technology mirror and shape ideologies and social biases. Trained in architecture at the Bartlett School of Architecture (London). Work intersects architecture with human rights investigations.

Collaborated with media outlets (CNN, BBC) and NGOs (**Forensic Architecture**, Amnesty International, Human Rights Watch). Co-founded **Border Emergency Collective** (2022), documenting migrants' experiences at the Polish-Belarusian border.

**2025 Collide Copenhagen winner** (Arts at CERN × Copenhagen Contemporary).

Exhibited at Ars Electronica, Warsaw Biennale, Kinema Icon (Bucharest), Haus Gropius (Dessau), deTour Festival (Hong Kong).

**For Amber:** interesting figure at intersection of architecture, forensics, 3D/digital art and political investigation — different from typical digital art, grounded in physical/political reality.
