# Julien Devaux

Belgian filmmaker based in Mexico City; primary technical collaborator on [[Francis Alÿs]]'s long-form documentary and experimental film works. Key credit: co-director, cinematographer, and editor on *Sandlines, the Story of History* (Iraq, 2018–2020) — a 61-minute documentary about children near Mosul reenacting a century of Iraqi history. Also co-filmed *Tornado* (2000–2010) and *The Green Line* (2004, Jerusalem).

## Background
Belgian-origin filmmaker relocated to Mexico City. Works in art/documentary film. Part of the Belgium–Mexico City circuit that [[Francis Alÿs]] inhabits (both Belgian-born, both Mexico City-based).

## Key Works / Collaborations with Francis Alÿs
- **[Sandlines, the Story of History](https://francisalys.com/sandlines/)** (Iraq, 2018–2020, 61 min) — co-director, cinematographer, editor. Children near Mosul reenact a century of Iraqi history in sand drawings. Premiered January 2020; 16 festivals, 10 countries; Best Feature Documentary Award (children's jury).
- **[Tornado](https://francisalys.com/tornado/)** (2000–2010, Milpa Alta) — cinematographer. Ten years filming Alÿs entering tornado funnels.
- **[The Green Line](https://francisalys.com/the-green-line/)** (2004, Jerusalem) — cinematographer. 24km walk tracing the 1948 Jerusalem armistice line with green paint.

## Key Relationships
- **[[Francis Alÿs]]** — 20+ year primary collaboration
- **Tamara Chalabi / [[Ruya Foundation]]** — co-production of *Sandlines*
- **Felix Blume** — sound designer (*Sandlines*)

## Why Interesting
Devaux's work at the intersection of documentary, conflict-zone filmmaking, and conceptual art is underexplored. *Sandlines* — children near Mosul reenacting history through play — is one of the most formally innovative and ethically rigorous documentary films of the last decade. His long-term commitment to the same collaborator (Alÿs) over 20+ years in conditions ranging from tornado fields to Mosul suggests a practice built on sustained trust rather than portfolio-building.
