# Sascha Pohflepp

German artist and researcher. **Died March 2019**, aged 38, in New York. [[Design Interactions (RCA)]] alumnus. Worked at the intersection of speculative design, media art, and philosophy — particularly around **photography, time, memory, and alternative histories of technology**.

Most known for *Buttons* (2008) — a camera that connects to the past, searching for photographs taken at the exact same millisecond elsewhere in the world, making a portrait of a simultaneous global moment. Also *The Golden Institute* (2010) — an alternative history of US energy policy in which Jimmy Carter invested heavily in renewable energy, imagining a counterfactual present. Collaborated with [[Anab Jain]] and Chris Woebken.

His work exemplified the DI approach at its most philosophical: not proposals for products, but probes into how technology shapes our sense of time, history, and possibility. His death at 38 was a significant loss to the speculative design field.

## Why Interesting
A historically important DI figure whose work on time and counterfactual history was more philosophical than most DI peers. *Buttons* remains one of the most cited DI student projects. His early death means the work is now archival — but understanding his approach is part of understanding the full spectrum of DI's intellectual range.
