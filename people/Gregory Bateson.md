# Gregory Bateson

Anthropologist, cybernetics theorist, biologist, and philosopher — the rare thinker who built a unified theory of mind, ecology, and communication. His concept of "the pattern that connects" is the proto-statement of more-than-human thinking. Core figure in the Lindisfarne Association. Father of Mary Catherine Bateson; formerly married to Margaret Mead.

## Why Interesting
Bateson's central claim — that "mind" is not confined to individual organisms but is a property of ecological systems — is the direct precursor to Varela's enactivism, Abram's more-than-human philosophy, and the whole more-than-human design tradition. His concept of "double bind" (contradictory injunctions that cause pathology) is underused in AI/agent design theory. *Steps to an Ecology of Mind* is one of the most cited unread books in design and STS.

## Key Works
- [*Steps to an Ecology of Mind*](https://archive.org/details/stepstoecologyof00bate) (1972, University of Chicago Press) — collected essays spanning anthropology, cybernetics, psychiatry, and ecology; the most comprehensive statement of his thinking
- [*Mind and Nature: A Necessary Unity*](https://archive.org/details/mindnature00bate) (1979, Dutton) — written during his Lindisfarne period; argues that the "mind" is the pattern of organization characterizing all living systems
- *Naven* (1936) — early anthropological monograph on ritual in Papua New Guinea, pioneering reflexive methodology
- *Communication: The Social Matrix of Psychiatry* (with Jürgen Ruesch, 1951) — applied cybernetics to psychiatry

## Key Concepts
- **Double bind** — contradictory injunctions that entrap and cause psychological distress; influential in psychiatry, family therapy, and communications theory
- **The pattern that connects** — his late work's central claim: all living things share structural patterns of relationship; the pattern IS the mind
- **Deutero-learning** — learning how to learn; meta-level adaptation
- **Ecology of mind** — mind is not in the individual brain but in the whole system of organism + environment
- **Levels of logical types** — from Bertrand Russell; Bateson used this to explain communication, play, and pathology

## Key Relationships
- [[Francisco Varela]] — Varela's enactivism is in direct dialogue with Bateson's ecology of mind; met at Lindisfarne
- [[Humberto Maturana]] — autopoiesis theory engages directly with Bateson's concepts
- [[William Irwin Thompson]] — Lindisfarne founder; recruited Bateson as core intellectual
- [[Lynn Margulis]] — fellow Lindisfarne member; shared interest in symbiosis and living systems
- [[David Abram]] — Abram's "more-than-human world" concept is a phenomenological translation of Bateson's ecology of mind
- Margaret Mead — former wife; major early influence

## Key Communities
- [[Lindisfarne Association]] — core intellectual presence; *Mind and Nature* was substantially developed here
- Esalen Institute — frequent presenter
- Macy Conferences (1941-1960) — early cybernetics conferences; Bateson was a key participant alongside Norbert Wiener, John von Neumann
- University of Hawaii; University of California Santa Cruz (late career)

## For Amber
- *Steps to an Ecology of Mind* is essential background for more-than-human design — specifically the essays on "The Cybernetics of Self" and "Form, Substance and Difference"
- His concept of "ecology of mind" is what Varela turned into enactivism — reading Bateson helps understand where Varela started
- The "pattern that connects" is a direct ancestor of more-than-human design's core claim: design must attend to the relational patterns between organisms, not just individual needs
- Double bind theory has direct applications to AI agent design — agents constrained by contradictory institutional imperatives exhibit precisely this pathology
