# Ronaldo Lemos

Co-founder and Chief Science Officer of [[ITS Rio]]. Architect of Brazil's Marco Civil da Internet (internet civil rights framework). Bridges Global South tech governance, cosmotechnics discourse, and mainstream policy — visiting scholar at Oxford, Princeton, MIT Media Lab; professor at Columbia University and Schwarzman College at Tsinghua University. World Economic Forum Young Global Leader.

## Background
Law degree from University of São Paulo, master's from Harvard, PhD from USP. Founding figure of Brazilian internet governance. Created Marco Civil da Internet (2014) — Brazil's landmark internet civil rights law. Currently holds simultaneous positions at Columbia (law professor), Tsinghua/Schwarzman College (professor), and visits Oxford and Princeton. President of the OAB-SP Technology Committee. Former member of Brazil's National Council for Combating Piracy. Vice president of the National Council on Social Communication of the Brazilian Congress.

## Key Works
- **Marco Civil da Internet** (2014) — Brazil's "Internet Constitution." Landmark legislation protecting privacy, net neutrality, and freedom of expression online. Internationally cited as a model.
- **Technodiversity** concept — ITS Rio whitepaper (2024) — philosophical challenge to Silicon Valley universalism, resonant with Yuk Hui's cosmotechnics.
