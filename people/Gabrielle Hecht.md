# Gabrielle Hecht

Historian of technology at Duke University. Author of *Being Nuclear: Africans and the Global Uranium Trade* (MIT Press, 2012) — on how African uranium workers were rendered invisible in the global nuclear industry. Also *The Radiance of France: Nuclear Power and National Identity after World War II*. Key figure in postcolonial STS and African technology history. Participant in the *AHR* information circulation conversation (2011) with [[Brian Larkin]], Lisa Gitelman, Paul N. Edwards, Adrian Johns.

## Domains
- History of technology in Africa
- Postcolonial STS
- Nuclear technology and its social/racial dimensions
- Infrastructure and invisibility

## Why Interesting
Hecht's concept of "nuclearity" — who/what counts as nuclear is determined by politics, not physics — is a model for how to analyze how infrastructure systems are differentially visible and invisible across contexts. Her postcolonial STS framework is essential for non-Euro-American tech theory.
