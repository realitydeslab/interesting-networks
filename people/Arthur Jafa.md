# Arthur Jafa

Artist and filmmaker working primarily with video and found footage. One of the most important artists in contemporary art, period. His work excavates the emotional, aesthetic, and political dimensions of Black life through film, video installation, and photography. Close friend and collaborator of **[[Saidiya Hartman]]**.

## Background
- Born 1960, Lamar, Mississippi
- Cinematographer on spike Lee's *Crooklyn* (1994) and Julie Dash's *Daughters of the Dust* (1991) — one of the most important Black films in American cinema
- Directed Jay-Z's **"4:44" short film** (2017) — featured Saidiya Hartman, James Baldwin archive footage, and other Black cultural figures; received massive attention
- Turner Prize nominee; shows at Sprüth Magers, Serpentine, and major institutions worldwide

## Key Works
- **"Love Is The Message, The Message Is Death"** (2016) — 7-minute video collage of Black American life, joy, and violence; one of the most important artworks of the 2010s; [Gavin Brown's Enterprise](https://gavinbrown.biz/), New York
- **"4:44 Short Film"** (2017) — directed for Jay-Z's album release; featured Saidiya Hartman, Hannah Giorgis, Mahershala Ali; distributed via Tidal
- Cinematography on Julie Dash's *Daughters of the Dust* (1991) — landmark of Black cinema

## Key Connections
- **[[Saidiya Hartman]]** — close friend; the Serpentine day of study centered on his show
- **[[Tina Campt]]** — part of the same intellectual-artistic orbit
- Turner Prize network; Serpentine; Sprüth Magers

## Why Interesting
Jafa is at the intersection of film theory, Black aesthetics, and contemporary art in a way that almost no one else occupies. His friendship with Hartman produced one of the most striking crossings of Black scholarship and popular culture in recent memory (the Jay-Z film). Deep-research required.
