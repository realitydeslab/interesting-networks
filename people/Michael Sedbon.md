# Michael Sedbon

Artist working at the intersection of biology, computation, and political philosophy. BAD Award winner 2019 for *CMD: Experiments in Bio-Algorithmic-Politics* — one of the most conceptually precise ALife-political artworks in recent memory.

## Key Work
- **CMD: Experiments in Bio-Algorithmic-Politics** (2019, BAD Award × VU Amsterdam) — Artificial ecosystem comprising photosynthetic bacterial cultures competing for light resources governed by a political AI algorithm. Twelve bioreactors. Each bacterial colony earns light-access credits via oxygen production. An auction algorithm empowers each bioreactor with "agency" over the financial system. The system experiments with political configurations (selfishness vs. communitarianism, fair distribution vs. power imbalance). The algorithm optimizes for "the greater good." Sedbon built the entire installation from scratch — custom data collection, automated pump arrays, -80°C to incubator environments. Described as "an evolutionary computer" and "a life support machine." Quote: *"The post-Anthropocene will be marked by the agency of everything non-human."*

## Why Interesting
**Extremely relevant to Amber.** CMD sits at the exact intersection of:
- **ALife** — bacterial cultures as computational agents with emergent, evolutionary behavior
- **Machine behavior** — AI algorithm governing non-human agents, studying how political systems emerge from non-human life
- **More-than-human design** — organisms as active participants in governance experiments
- **Cyborg sociology** — human political frameworks dissolving into biological substrate
- **Agent ethology** — observing how bacterial "agents" behave under different incentive structures

The conceptual frame (*"bio-algorithmic politics," "evolutionary computer," non-human agency*) anticipates the language Amber uses in 2025-26 research.
