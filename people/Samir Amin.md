# Samir Amin

Egyptian-Senegalese Marxist economist and political theorist (1931–2018). First Executive Secretary of [[CODESRIA]] (1973–1975) and its intellectual godfather. Founder of the [Third World Forum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Third_World_Forum) in Dakar, which he ran until his death. One of the architects of dependency theory and world-systems analysis from a Global South perspective — working in parallel with Immanuel Wallerstein but from Africa rather than the US academy. Key books: *Accumulation on a World Scale* (1974), *Eurocentrism* (1988), *Delinking: Towards a Polycentric World* (1990). Concept of "delinking" — African/Third World economies developing autonomously from the world capitalist system — remains influential. Based in Dakar from the 1970s until death, which positioned Senegal as a node of Global South critical theory.
