# Sadie Plant

British philosopher and cultural theorist. Co-founded the [[CCRU]] with [[Nick Land]] at University of Warwick (1995). Cyberfeminist theorist. "The most famous media academic in Britain" in the late 1990s. Departed academia in March 1997, leaving Land to take the CCRU in more extreme directions. Has largely withdrawn from public life since ~2000.

## Background
- Grew up in Birmingham. Philosophy PhD at Manchester University on the Situationist International. Lecturer at Birmingham University's Department of Cultural Studies.
- Long-standing creative and personal partner of Nick Land before and during early CCRU.
- Offered a Research Fellowship at Warwick's Philosophy Department 1995. Brought Birmingham students with her. Formed the CCRU. Her comparison to Germaine Greer is apt — both left Warwick (Greer taught English there) and the Vice Chancellor commented on both departures.
- Left Warwick unexpectedly March 1997 to become a "media academic" (regular newspaper columns, BBC Radio "Start the Week"). 
- After *Zeros + Ones*, largely retreated from academic and public life.

## Key Works
- *[The Most Radical Gesture: The Situationist International in a Postmodern Age](https://www.routledge.com/The-Most-Radical-Gesture-The-Situationist-International-in-a-Postmodern/Plant/p/book/9780415062220)* (Routledge, 1992) — Her PhD-turned-book. Situationist theory.
- *[Zeros + Ones: Digital Women + The New Technoculture](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/154877/zeros-ones-by-sadie-plant/)* (Doubleday, 1997) — Landmark cyberfeminism. Ada Lovelace, women and computing, machines and femininity. "A Nineties cyberfeminist equivalent to The Female Eunuch."
- *[Writing on Drugs](https://www.faber.co.uk/product/9780571193585-writing-on-drugs/)* (Faber, 1999) — Cultural history of drugs.

## Why Interesting
Historically significant as CCRU's originator and cyberfeminist synthesizer. *Zeros + Ones* remains a landmark text connecting feminism, computing history (Ada Lovelace), and cultural theory. Her departure created the conditions for CCRU's later extremism — her cyberfeminist frame was more politically grounded than Land's accelerationism. The cyberfeminist thread she started was later picked up by [[Laboria Cuboniks]] and the xenofeminist movement.
