# Christina Sharpe

Scholar of African American literature, visual culture, and critical theory. Author of *In the Wake: On Blackness and Being* (2016) — one of the most important works of Black studies in the last decade. Her concept of "the wake" extends and deepens **[[Saidiya Hartman]]**'s "afterlife of slavery" into a phenomenology of Black life lived in the ongoing wake of the slave ship.

## Key Works
- **[*In the Wake: On Blackness and Being*](https://www.dukeupress.edu/in-the-wake)** (Duke University Press, 2016) — the wake (of a ship), the wake (a vigil for the dead), the wake (being awake), "wake work" (the ongoing labor of living with and against slavery's afterlives). A masterwork of critical theory and elegy.
- **[*Monstrous Intimacies: Making Post-Slavery Subjects*](https://www.dukeupress.edu/monstrous-intimacies)** (Duke University Press, 2010) — earlier work on the intimate violence of post-slavery.
- **[*Ordinary Notes*](https://www.dukeupress.edu/ordinary-notes)** (Graywolf Press, 2023) — a book of fragmented notes on Black life, aesthetics, and care.

## Key Connections
- **[[Saidiya Hartman]]** — direct intellectual lineage; "afterlife of slavery" → "wake"

## Why Interesting
Sharpe's "wake work" is the methodological successor to Hartman's critical fabulation — both are about sustaining attention to what is systematically ignored. Her work is increasingly influential in design, media studies, and critical AI studies. Deep-research required.
