# Judith Revel

French philosopher (born 1966), professor of French Contemporary Philosophy at Université Paris 1 Panthéon-Sorbonne (since 2023); member of Institut Universitaire de France. Daughter of historian Jacques Revel (former EHESS president). Married to [[Antonio Negri]] until his death in December 2023.

**Specialist in contemporary French and Italian thought**, particularly Michel Foucault (biopolitics, subjectivation, philosophy of language) and Italian operaismo/post-operaismo. Formerly professor at University Paris-Nanterre (2014–2023), where she headed the Sophiapol research team. Member of the Michel Foucault Center; head of IMEC scientific council.

Her philosophical work bridges Foucauldian biopolitics and Italian radical thought: the "common" as alternative to public/private; political ontology of the present; philosophy of history. Her Negri connection is not merely biographical — she actively worked at the intersection of French post-structuralism and Italian autonomism.

Published a Foucault dictionary and multiple books on Foucault's work on language, anti-idealism, and the transition from biopolitics to subjectivation.

## Why Interesting
A living connector between the Foucault tradition and the Negri/autonomist legacy. Her work on biopolitics and the "common" is relevant to posthuman and AI ethics frameworks. As Negri's partner and intellectual interlocutor for two decades, she has unique access to his late thought. Paris 1 Panthéon-Sorbonne context makes her part of a major node of French political philosophy. She is currently the most active living intellectual at the intersection of Negri's legacy and French theory.
