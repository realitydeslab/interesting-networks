# Nadia Bianchi-Berthouze

Professor at [[UCL Interaction Centre]], Academic Director of the [Global Disability Innovation Hub (GDI Hub)](https://www.disabilityinnovation.com/). Affective computing via body movement and posture — using non-facial signals to infer emotional and pain states in real time. Major work on chronic pain recognition through movement analysis; also VR/games for pain management. The GDI Hub is the WHO Collaborating Centre for Assistive Technology.
