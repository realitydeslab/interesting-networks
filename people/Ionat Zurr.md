# Ionat Zurr

Artist, researcher, curator, and bio-art theoretician. Co-founded the **[Tissue Culture & Art Project (TC&A)](https://tcaproject.net/)** with [[Oron Catts]] in 1996 — together they pioneered tissue-engineered living art and coined the philosophical category "Semi-Living." Academic Coordinator of **[[SymbioticA]]** (world's first university-based biological arts lab) and **Chair of Fine Arts Discipline, School of Design, University of Western Australia**. Instigated the **[Master of Science (Biological Arts)](https://symbiotica.uwa.edu.au/)** at UWA. Her individual research thread — separate from TC&A — pushes into ectogenesis, reproductive biology, and the politics of technology-mediated life. Visiting tutor at Royal College of Art London's Design Interactions programme. Her 2008 PhD thesis *Growing Semi-Living Art* remains the canonical inside account of what it means to grow living sculpture.

## Background

- Based in **Perth, Western Australia**. Grew up in Israel; moved to Australia where she has been based since the 1990s.
- **Research Fellow, Tissue Engineering and Organ Fabrication Laboratory, [Massachusetts General Hospital](https://www.massgeneral.org/) / [Harvard Medical School](https://hms.harvard.edu/)** (2000–2001) — the same scientific residency as Catts; this is where Pig Wings and related experiments were physically realised. Access to genuine wet lab infrastructure as an artist.

- **PhD: *[Growing Semi-Living Art](https://tcaproject.net/wp-content/uploads/2019/01/ionat_zurr-phd-final.pdf)* (UWA, 2008)** — 333-page thesis. The first internal, artist-authored account of what biological art practice involves from the inside: ethics committee negotiations, wet lab methodology, philosophical questions about care, stewardship, and authorship of living objects. A rare document.
- **Co-founded [[SymbioticA]]** at UWA in 2000 with [[Oron Catts]], biologist Miranda Grounds, and neuroscientist Stuart Bunt.
- **Visiting Professor, [Aalto University](https://www.aalto.fi/)**, Helsinki (2012–2013) — set up **Biofilia – Base for Biological Art and Design** within the School of Art, Design and Architecture (together with Catts). First biological art lab in Finland.
- **Visiting tutor in Design Interactions, [Royal College of Art](https://www.rca.ac.uk/)**, London — contributed to the programme that became the epicenter of European speculative/critical design.
- **Academic Coordinator of [[SymbioticA]]** — where Catts is Director, Zurr runs the academic infrastructure: curriculum, student supervision, degree architecture.
- **Chair of Fine Arts Discipline, [School of Design, UWA](https://www.uwa.edu.au/schools/Design)** — institutional authority enabling her to reshape fine art education from the ground up.
- **Instigated the [Master of Science (Biological Arts)](https://symbiotica.uwa.edu.au/)** — an entirely new kind of degree, merging art and science credentials into a formal qualification. This is the move that trains the next generation.

## Individual Research Focus

While TC&A is the core practice, Ionat Zurr has an increasingly distinct individual research thread:

**Ectogenesis & Reproductive Futures**  
Zurr's own research, often in collaboration with scientists outside the TC&A framework, probes technology-mediated reproduction — artificial uteruses, placenta mimicry, externalized gestation. This moves the questions from animal ethics (TC&A) to reproductive politics, feminist bioethics, and the politics of bodily sovereignty. The *Ex Utero* and *Placen-Tech* projects (2024–2025) make this the most developed individual thread in her practice.

**Curatorial Writing & Theorizing Bio-Art**  
Zurr has solo-authored influential survey and theoretical writing: "In Continuous State of Semi-living: A Partial Survey of Contemporary Artists Working with Living Tissue" (2013); guest-edited two issues of *[Leonardo](https://direct.mit.edu/leon/)* (MIT Press, 2014–2015) titled "Agency in Movement" — a sectional journal editorship that marks her as a field-shaper, not just a practitioner.

**Ethics & Institutional Politics of Art-Science**  
Her solo commentary "On Instrumentalisation of Life and the Vitality of Matter" (*Dialogues in Human Geography*, 2012) and the Springer chapter on ethics committees are Zurr working independently as a theorist of the bureaucratic and ethical machinery that governs biological art — distinct from Catts's more scientific/philosophical framing.

## Venue Circuit Analysis

Zurr operates across **four overlapping circuits**, distinguishable from Catts's because she has a stronger curatorial and institutional-design dimension:

### 1. Scientific Infrastructure
Harvard Medical School, CERN, SynthSys Edinburgh, Penn State Huck Institutes, Aalto Biofilia — Zurr, like Catts, is literally IN labs. But her Harvard fellowship is more explicitly framed in her CV as enabling ectogenesis and reproductive biology research (Ex Utero lineage), not just structural biology (Catts's Pig Wings lineage). This foreshadows her individual research thread.

### 2. European Media Art Festivals
Ars Electronica, ISEA, transmediale, L'Art Biotech Nantes, Laznia Gdansk, Touch Me Zagreb — the circuit that *validates* biological art as a serious critical practice. The 2007 Golden Nica was field-defining: it said SymbioticA's model of artists-in-labs is a legitimate form of practice worth the highest honors in media art.

### 3. Major Collecting Institutions
MoMA, Mori, NGV, GoMA, Pompidou, National Art Museum of China — the circuit that *canonizes* the work. MoMA collecting Pig Wings and exhibiting Victimless Leather puts TC&A in the permanent art-historical record.

### 4. Academic / Institutional Design
RCA Design Interactions, Aalto University, Stanford, Harvard — this is distinctly Zurr's circuit. While Catts held formal visiting professorships at RCA, Zurr's role as *visiting tutor in Design Interactions* was embedded in the pedagogical work of the programme. Her academic coordinator role at SymbioticA, the MBiolArts degree, and her PhD thesis make her the institutional designer of the next generation of biological artists. **This circuit is where Zurr differs most from Catts.**

**Small/niche venues that matter:**
- L'Art Biotech at Le Lieu Unique (Nantes, 2003) — first cultured meat performance. Small French new-media venue; globally significant act.
- Shuffle Festival (Tower Hamlets Cemetery Park, London, 2016) — community-embedded, site-specific. The Golem installed in a cemetery: life, death, biological material, local community. Unexpected venue for TC&A.
- GYRE Gallery, Harajuku Tokyo (2025) — fashion/lifestyle cultural center. Zurr's ectogenesis work lands in a consumer-culture space. Deliberate: the "Bio-Gucci" aesthetic from Biomess extended to reproductive futures.

### Intellectual Threads (Zurr's distinctive emphases)
1. **Ethics bureaucracy as art medium**: The fight to get ethics clearance for living-organism art is, for Zurr, philosophically productive material — she writes about it and incorporates it into the work. "Unnatural Relations" is the key text.
2. **Agency as the central question**: The "Agency in Movement" guest editorship reveals her organizing concept: who (or what) has agency when the art object is alive? This connects to posthuman and STS debates but Zurr grounds it in laboratory practice.
3. **Ectogenesis as feminist bioethics**: Ex Utero and Placen-Tech extend the TC&A logic (what if life were grown outside the body?) into specifically feminist territory — who controls reproduction? What does ectogenesis mean for bodily autonomy?
4. **Incubator as socio-political apparatus**: The Compostcubator, Futile Labor bioreactor, and Ex Utero incubator share a thread: the incubator is not just a technical container but a *biopolitical surrogate* for bodies, wombs, and care systems.

## Why Interesting

**The theorist inside the artist inside the institution-builder.** TC&A is usually framed as Catts-and-Zurr-together, and that's accurate, but Zurr's individual contributions are distinct: she wrote the PhD thesis (Catts didn't), she guest-edited Leonardo (he didn't), and she holds the academic coordination role at SymbioticA that makes the institution actually function as a degree-granting entity. The MBiolArts degree is hers — she instigated it.

**The ectogenesis thread is the sleeper.** Ex Utero and Placen-Tech are not TC&A work — they're Zurr's own research program, developed with a different set of collaborators (Millett, White, Penn State scientists). This work takes the TC&A logic (life grown outside the body) and applies it to human reproduction with explicitly feminist implications. This is the thread that makes Zurr independently interesting beyond the TC&A partnership.

**Relevance to Amber's core threads:**
- **More-than-human**: Semi-Living entities are literally "more-than-human" — chimeric, partially alive, between categories. Zurr didn't just practice this; she built the institution and degree programme that trains practitioners in it.
- **Speculative design as ethics**: Every TC&A project poses a speculative question and then grows the artifact, puts it in a gallery, and lets the audience live with the consequences. This is speculative design before the term existed.
- **Art-science integration**: SymbioticA under Zurr's academic coordination is THE model for artists embedded in scientific laboratories. If Amber wants to understand how art-science integration works institutionally, Zurr's academic architecture is the canonical example.
- **Critical biology / posthumanism**: The "instrumentalisation of life" is Zurr's framing — biotechnology instrumentalizes life as raw material; art can interrupt that instrumentalization by making the consequences visible and uncomfortable.

## Research Taste Analysis (⭐⭐⭐)

### What Makes Zurr Distinctive from Catts
Most profiles treat TC&A as an undifferentiated collaboration. Closer reading reveals two different intellectual orientations:

- **Catts** is more interested in the *philosophy of life* — what "Semi-Living" reveals about categories of being, biological ethics, the history of biotechnology.
- **Zurr** is more interested in the *politics of life-management* — who controls life, how institutions mediate access to biological material, what reproduction and care systems look like in a post-biological world.

This distinction shows up in their solo publications: Catts writes about Neolifism and synthetic biology critique; Zurr writes about ethics committees, reproductive technologies, and the agency of semi-living things.

### Career Architecture
**Platform-building through curriculum design**: SymbioticA's MBiolArts degree is Zurr's most significant individual institution-building act. A degree programme is infrastructure that outlasts any exhibition. It trains practitioners, employs researchers, justifies lab budgets, and positions UWA as the global centre of biological arts. Every graduating MBiolArts student is a node in Zurr's network.
