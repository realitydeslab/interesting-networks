# Ani Liu

Internationally exhibiting research-based artist working at the intersection of art and technoscience. Uses emerging biological technologies (galvanotaxis, cell culture, synthetic scent chemistry, EEG brain-computer interfaces) as feminist critical instruments, investigating how technoscience co-constructs the body — especially the female reproductive body — with culture. Studio in Queens, New York. Currently Carrafiell Assistant Professor of Fine Arts at University of Pennsylvania's Weitzman School of Design.

## Background

Ani Liu's trajectory is rare: three elite institutions in three different disciplines, each adding a new technical vocabulary. Dartmouth College (B.A. High Honors, 2008) gave her liberal arts and first art prizes. Harvard GSD (M.Arch Thesis Distinction, 2014) gave her spatial and material intelligence. MIT Media Lab (M.S. 5.0 GPA, 2017) gave her lab-grade technical access — working under [Pattie Maes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pattie_Maes) in the [Fluid Interfaces group](http://fluid.media.mit.edu/) and connecting to the MIT Space Initiative and iGEM communities.

She is Chinese-American (born in the US, Dartmouth class of 2008). Her studio is in Queens, NY. She became a mother in 2019, which initiated a major thematic pivot into reproductive labor, care work, and what she calls "matrescence" — the process of becoming a mother, which she intellectualizes with the same rigor she applies to synthetic biology.

Career arc: art prizes at Dartmouth → architecture at Harvard → bioart/tech at MIT → Princeton Arts Fellow (2019-21) → University of Pennsylvania faculty (2021-present). She also explicitly self-categorizes her own practice into three methodological registers: **biological / digital / analog** — a conceptual taxonomy that itself reveals her thinking.

## Domains

- **Biopolitics of reproduction**: pregnancy, surrogacy, lactation, birth as materials and subjects
- **Feminist technoscience**: how technologies re-encode gender norms; body as vessel for competing ideologies
- **Synthetic biology + bioart**: cell culture, galvanotaxis, microbiology, microbiome, iGEM
- **AI and gender**: algorithmic gaze, gender socialization through ML, AI toys
- **Olfaction and memory**: scent chemistry as perceptual prosthetics and time capsules
- **Quantified self + data portraiture**: biological data (feeds, diaper changes, milk volume) as sculpture
- **Brain-computer interfaces + somatic augmentation**: EEG, skin sensors, neuroscience art
- **Labor theory**: care work, reproductive labor, the breast pump as biopolitical device

## Key Works

Works are organized by Ani's own three methodological registers — **biological**, **digital**, and **analog** — with major series spanning multiple.

### 🔬 Biological Register

- **[Mind-Controlled Spermatozoa](https://www.ani-liu.com/pussygrabsback)** (2016–2017) — *The central early work.* A system allowing women to direct sperm movement using brain-EEG (galvanotaxis: cells migrate toward charge in electric fields). Made as feminist provocation in response to Trump's "grab them by the pussy." Brain EEG → custom circuit on glass slide → sperm controlled via XY axis. Single-channel film 3:12. *Theoretical backbone*: Foucault on biopolitics, Roosth & Schrader on feminist theory out of science. Shown at Mana Contemporary, Boston Center for the Arts, MU Hybrid Arthouse. Press: VICE, Fox News, PC Magazine.

- **[Kisses from the Future](https://www.ani-liu.com/kisses-from-the-future)** (2015–2022) — Speculative love notes: petri dishes of custom-molded LB agar with microorganisms cultured from a kiss. Reflects on microbiome identity research (we have 2M bacterial genes vs 23K human genes). Raises question: if microbial wealth replaces genetic fitness as partner selection criterion, what does intimacy become? Shown at Paul Robeson Gallery at Rutgers, Boston Center for the Arts.

- **[A Search for Ghosts in the Meat Machine](https://www.ani-liu.com/the-ghosts-you-havent-been)** (2018–2020) — Nine sculptures, each the artist's height and volume in liquid form (62"×29"×21"). Materials: SLS 3D-printed nylon, glass vials, antennas from cellular phones, **cow brains**, custom electronics, silicone, fluorescent lights, oxidized steel. Each explores one frame for personhood: anatomical, psychological, genetic, biochemical, behavioral, algorithmic, personal narrative, memory. Series title: "A Search for Ghosts in the Meat Machine." **Funded by Biological Art & Design Award (€25,000).** Scientific collaborators: **Prof. Mario Maas, Matthias Cabri, Onno Baur** (Dept. of Radiology & Nuclear Medicine, Amsterdam UMC). Shown at MU Hybrid Arthouse, State Studio Berlin, Elijah Wheat Showroom / NADA Miami, **Chronus Art Center Shanghai** (2022), Chengdu Biennale (2023).

- **[The Surrogacy (Bodies Are Not Factories)](https://www.ani-liu.com/surrogacy)** (2022–) — A pig uterus depicted pregnant with human fetuses. Inspired by real research on interspecific pregnancies (endangered species incubation) and pig-to-human organ transplants. Core question: does the ability to defer pregnancy to technology liberate women, or does systemic policy need re-innovating? Shown at YveYANG Gallery NYC (*Offworlds*, 2023), Today Art Museum Beijing (*To Your Eternity*, 2023), KORA Italy (*Design for the Unthinkable World*, 2023), Macau International Art Biennale (2023).

- **[Consumerist Pregnancy](https://www.ani-liu.com/consumerist-pregnancy)** (2019) — Suite of speculative products: *Consumerist Pregnancy Reports™* (ritual devices for simulating pregnancy), *Untitled (Small Inconveniences)* (silk/nylon garment simulating incontinence), *Untitled (Woman Pains)* (custom electronics garment simulating labor pains via electrode array). Garment fabrication with **Randi Shandroski**. Sponsored by Adobe's Festival of the Impossible. Published essay in [Biodesigned](https://www.biodesigned.org/ani-liu/the-consumerist-pregnancy). Epigraph: Shulamith Firestone, *The Dialectic of Sex* (1970).

- **[Untitled (Labor of Love)](https://www.ani-liu.com/untitled-labor-of-love)** (2022) — Data sculpture: a 30-day postpartum ledger of every feed and diaper change. Each vial (marking 30 mins) is filled with breast milk, formula, or diaper fragments. 24 rows (hours) × 30 columns (days). Sometimes 12+ feeds per day; diaper changes at 2am. Shown at Design Museum den Bosch (Netherlands, 2025), MAD Museum NYC (*Designing Motherhood*, 2025), James Gallery CUNY (*Textures of Feminist Perseverance*, 2024).

- **[Untitled (pumping at work)](https://www.ani-liu.com/untitled-pumping)** (2022) — A week's worth of breast milk (by volume) circulated through clear coiled tubes snaking across a gallery floor and around a front desk. Programmed to the artist's breast pump rhythm. Patented 1854, the breast pump is simultaneously liberatory (return to workforce) and coercive (obligating postpartum bodies back into capitalism). Shown at 601 Artspace (*In Absentia*, 2024).

- **[Olfactory Time Capsule for Space](https://www.ani-liu.com/olfactory-for-space)** (2017) — Scent amulet for astronauts: three Earth memories (loved one, home, natural resource) chemically embedded in slow-releasing polymer. Dial-selectable, immersive olfactive experience. "An emotional Golden Record for smells." Collaboration with **MIT Space Initiative** and **IFF (International Flavors and Fragrances)**. Shown aboard zero-gravity shuttle. Photos: Steve Boxall.

- **[Biota Beats](https://www.ani-liu.com/biota-beats)** (2016) — Microbial record player converting human body microbiome into sound. Collaborative project with **EMW StreetBio iGEM team**; demoed at Ginkgo Bioworks Youth Science Initiative. Bronze medal, iGEM Competition (community lab category), Oct 2016. Full competition site: [biotabeats.com](http://biotabeats.com/index.html). Covered in Gizmodo.

- **[Microbial We](https://www.ani-liu.com/microbial-we)** — Microbiome as collective subjectivity.

- **[Kisses from the Future / Forget Me Not / Laboratory of Longings](https://www.ani-liu.com/laboratory-of-longings-longings)** (2018) — Solo exhibition "Laboratory of Longings: The Experiments of Ani Liu" at Mills Gallery, Boston Center for the Arts.

### 💻 Digital Register

- **[Biologically Algorithmic / Algorithmic Animal Gaze](https://www.ani-liu.com/biologically-algorithmic)** (2018) — Images of the artist's body sorted by algorithmic "unpredictability" (compression-based analysis). Finds the computational eye focuses on the same features as the human eye: eyes, lips, breasts, vulva, fingers. Presented in LED panels at artist's height. Technical collaborator: **Doug von Kohorn**. BAD Award winner. Shown at MU Hybrid Arthouse and Science Gallery Rotterdam (*UNREAL*, 2020).

- **[A.I. Toys](https://www.ani-liu.com/ai-toys)** (2020–2021) — ML model trained on Amazon/Target toy data labeled "boys" and "girls." Generates fictional toys that reflect gendered societal values. Website: [ai-toys.net](http://ai-toys.net/). Software: **Ryan Thorpe**. Design assistance: **Michelle Lim, Andrea Li**. Also *A.I. Toys (Unboxing Mania)* and *A.I. Toys (screens)* sub-series.

- **[Shapes & Ladders: Battles of Bias and Bureaucracy](https://www.ani-liu.com/shapes-ladders-battles-of-bias-and-bureaucracy)** (2021) — Video game critiquing bias in systems. S+T+ARTS Prize Nomination 2021.

- **[Alone Together / Eyeris](https://www.ani-liu.com/eyeris2)** — Digital series on surveillance and togetherness.

- **[Networked Sensorium](https://www.ani-liu.com/networked-sensorium)** (2013–2014) — Devices allowing two people to swap vision (video glasses, welding masks, HD micro camera). Exploring McLuhan's Global Village and transubjectivity. Shown at 40 Kirkland Gallery. Press: National Geographic.

- **[To Engineer a Gut Feeling](https://www.ani-liu.com/engineer-gut-feelings)** — Iowa Gambling Task inspired biofeedback skin that amplifies subliminal stress signals (galvanic skin response → visible goosebumps). Collaboration with **Thomas Lengeling**. Presented to [Pattie Maes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pattie_Maes) (Fluid Interfaces, MIT Media Lab).

- **[Psyche in the Age of Mechanical Production / Mind in the Machine](https://www.ani-liu.com/mind-in-machine)** — Factory worker portrait created by programming her machine to knit according to her emotional state (EEG brainwave data). Explores mechanized vs. human labor. Featured in Queens Museum Biennial.

## Research Taste Analysis ⭐⭐⭐

### Conceptual Strategy

**Multi-register body politics**: Liu's core move is to identify a *political claim about bodies* (female reproductive body is controlled/colonized/made into labor instrument) and then *enact the counter-claim using the exact scientific apparatus that enables the original control*. In *Mind-Controlled Spermatozoa*, the controlling apparatus (galvanotaxis) is repurposed from fertility medicine into feminist performance. The form IS the argument.

**Scientific-theory bootstrapping**: She reads the scientific literature seriously and finds speculative art premises in it. The microbiome-identity research becomes *Kisses from the Future*. The Iowa Gambling Task becomes *To Engineer a Gut Feeling*. She is doing what good speculative designers do — finding the art in the science, not the science in the art.

**Data as autobiography, not abstraction**: Her recent work makes data portraits of her own body's labor (30 days postpartum data, one week of pumped milk). This is different from data art that abstracts — she is making data *intimate*, showing what quantification does to lived experience.

### Career Architecture

**Credential stacking → community building**: She used three elite institutions not just for skills but for *networks and communities*. Dartmouth = art community foundation. Harvard GSD = architecture + spatial communities. MIT Media Lab = biotech + science credibility + the MIT Space Initiative + iGEM world. Each institution gave her a community that followed her into practice.

**Fellowship as protection**: Princeton Arts Fellowship (2019–2021) was two years of protected time and institutional cover during a transitional period (just before UPenn, post-MIT). The BAD Award €25,000 funded an entire major work series. She has been adept at using fellowships to protect long-term project development.

**Teaching as creative infrastructure**: She's always teaching — Harvard GSD, Princeton, Columbia, now UPenn. But not as compromise — as community building. Her students are in her network; her talks at these schools are her intellectual circuit.

### Methodology Signature

**Art-science bridging at the technical level**: This is the key signature. She doesn't *reference* galvanotaxis — she *builds the circuit*. She doesn't *reference* microbiome research — she *cultures the organisms*. Technical depth is not decoration; it IS the practice. This is what separates her from most artists "working with science."

**Serial investigation**: Each major theme (identity + neuroscience → then gender + biotech → then pregnancy/labor/motherhood) is investigated through multiple works over years. She doesn't make one work per idea; she makes a *series*, developing the idea across different materials, scales, and angles. *A Search for Ghosts* is nine sculptures. *AI Toys* has three sub-series. *Labor of Love* + *Pumping at Work* + *Milk Fat Globules* are a motherhood cluster.

**Essay as extension of practice**: The *Biodesigned* essay on Consumerist Pregnancy and the Venice Biennale essay on Everlasting Plastics show she moves between making and writing as a single practice. The writing theorizes the making; the making embodies the writing.

### Network Strategy

**Dual-circuit credibility**: She plays both the bioart circuit (MU Hybrid, Science Gallery, BAD Award, Ars Electronica) and the feminist design/art circuit (MoMA, Designing Motherhood, Artforum). Being taken seriously in both means she can't be dismissed as "just a scientist doing art" or "just an artist referencing science."

**Institutional ally cultivation**: Paola Antonelli (MoMA) is her most significant institutional ally — MoMA R&D Salon invitation + moderated MoMA Life Cycles panel = MoMA as amplification platform even without a solo show there. This is strategic: it's better to be *on MoMA's platform* through an ally than to be in a group show.
