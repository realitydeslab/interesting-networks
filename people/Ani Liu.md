# Ani Liu

Artist working at the intersection of biological systems, AI, and bodily experience. Her practice uses biological processes — pregnancy, lactation, reproduction, microbiomes — as artistic materials and critical lenses. One of the most intellectually compelling artists working at the biology/technology/feminism interface.

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-27:** Stub created during Zhang Ga / CAC deep research. *A Search for Ghosts in the Meat Machine* (2018–2020) shown at [[Chronus Art Center]] as part of *Entangled: bio/media* (2022).

## Why Interesting (⭐⭐⭐)
- **Body as medium, not metaphor** — she works with actual biological materials (milk fat globules, spermatozoa, pregnancy) not just representations of body
- Strong feminist critique embedded in rigorous biological/technological practice
- Work titles signal depth: *Biologically Algorithmic*, *Mind-Controlled Spermatozoa*, *The Surrogacy (Bodies Are Not Factories)*, *Consumerist Pregnancy*, *Corpus Callosum*
- Three methodological registers: biological / digital / analog — she explicitly codes her own work this way
- Directly relevant to Amber's interests in posthumanism, machine-body co-constitution, speculative biology
