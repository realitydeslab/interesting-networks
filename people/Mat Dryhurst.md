# Mat Dryhurst

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Full deep research. Expanded from stub to comprehensive profile.

British artist, musician, technologist, and cultural theorist based in Berlin. Pioneered practical artist data sovereignty through co-founding [[Spawning AI]] and [Have I Been Trained?](https://haveibeentrained.com/). Builds critical infrastructure for AI ethics rather than just writing about it. Long-term creative partner and life partner of [[Holly Herndon]]. Listed in Art Review Power 100 (2021, 2022).

## Background
Born 1984 in Birmingham, England. No confirmed formal degree — his trajectory is that of a self-taught practitioner who moved fluidly between music, technology, and theory. Early career in the Berlin experimental music scene; began collaborating with Holly Herndon in 2013. The Berlin context is essential: the city's experimental electronic scene shaped his thinking about independent infrastructure and decentralization.

Key intellectual trajectory: started from music distribution and decentralization politics, moved into AI ethics and data sovereignty as AI became central to creative practice. The throughline is: *who controls the infrastructure of cultural production?* Whether that's platform distribution (Saga, 2015), AI training data (Spawning, 2022), or voice synthesis (Holly+), Dryhurst consistently asks the power question.

ZKM described him (2015) as working on "online distribution," interested in how artists can own the online spaces where their work is hosted. Now he's doing the same thing for AI training data. Consistent logic, evolving context.

## Key Works & Projects

### Spawning AI + Have I Been Trained (2022–present)
- **[Spawning AI](https://spawning.ai/)** — Co-founded September 2022 with [[Holly Herndon]], Jordan Meyer, and Patrick Hoepner. Name references the reproductive process AI uses to create infinite new works from training data.
- **[Have I Been Trained?](https://haveibeentrained.com/)** — Website allowing artists to search the ~5.8 billion images in the LAION-5B dataset used to train Stable Diffusion and Midjourney. Artists can check if their work was used and opt out of future training.
- By March 2023: 80 million artworks opted out of Stable Diffusion v3.0 via Spawning (partnering with Shutterstock and Artstation).
- Spawning's approach cited as instructive for EU policy on text and data mining.
- **[Source.plus](https://source.plus/)** (2023) — Tool to search and curate image collections into tailored training datasets. Flips the paradigm: not just opt-out but curate-in.

### Holly+ (2021–present)
- **[Holly+](https://holly.plus/)** — AI music generator that lets the public upload polyphonic tracks, performed by a deepfaked version of Holly Herndon's voice. Premiered at [Sónar Festival](https://sonar.es/), Barcelona (2021) with Maria Arnal and Tarta Relena.
- **TED2022: A New Era** (April 2022) — Holly+ presented with Pher
- **[Ars Electronica STARTS Prize](https://starts-prize.aec.at/) 2022** — Won for digital art with Holly+
- Cover of Dolly Parton's "Jolene" (2022) using Holly+ — with Ryan Norris on guitar

### Spawn (2019)
- Developed during Proto with [[Holly Herndon]] and Jules LaPlace
- Artificial neural network trained on vocal samples. Taught via "training ceremonies" — live call-and-response singing where participants train Spawn by singing to it. First sound Spawn produced became foundation of "Birth" (track 1 on Proto)

### Interdependence Podcast (2020–present)
- **[Interdependence](https://interdependence.fm/)** — Launched April 30, 2020 with Holly Herndon, broadcasting from their Berlin studio
- Conversations with artists, thinkers working at arts/technology frontier
- Notable guests: [James Bridle](https://jamesbridle.com/), Simon Denny, Harm van den Dorpel, [Emad Mostaque](https://www.stability.ai/) (founder of Stable Diffusion)

### Saga (2015)
- Embeddable self-hosting and publishing framework. Lets content creators claim ownership in every space where their work is embedded online
- Allows creatives to control how their work looks when displayed on different websites
- Articulated DIY music industry logic transposed to online media infrastructure

### MUSTER
- Audio play derived entirely from data mining the listenership of Deutschlandradio Kultur
- Example of using audience data as creative material — flipping surveillance logic

### Musical Collaborations with Holly Herndon
- **[Platform](https://hollyherndon.bandcamp.com/album/platform)** (2015, 4AD) — Co-produced. Explores DAOs, internet decentralization, digital intimacy
- **[Proto](https://hollyherndon.bandcamp.com/album/proto)** (2019, 4AD) — Co-produced. Features Spawn as credited band member
- Supported Radiohead on tour (May 2016)
- Recruit (2015) — 11-minute track for British menswear line Cottweiler
- Sonic Movement (2013, with [Semcon](https://semcon.com/)) — Sound design for electric vehicle pedestrian safety; premiered at Frankfurt Motorshow 2013

## Why Interesting
Dryhurst is one of the clearest examples of **artist as infrastructure builder**. He doesn't just critique AI; he builds practical tools for artists to protect their rights within AI systems. This is the critical computing tradition (computing as political act) carried out through artistic practice.

The specific intersection that makes him unique for Amber: he bridges **experimental music/sound art**, **political theory of the internet** (decentralization, DAOs, sovereignty), and **practical AI ethics policy**. This is a rare combination. Most AI ethics people are academics; most experimental musicians aren't policy-relevant. Dryhurst is both.

Also: the **Interdependence podcast** is a living index of his intellectual network. Every episode guest is a node worth knowing. James Bridle, Emad Mostaque, Simon Denny, Harm van den Dorpel — these are real connection points for Amber's research interests.

His position on the Spawning/opt-out question is also genuinely interesting intellectually: he says "more will ultimately opt in than out, but first we have to establish common respect." This is not a simple anti-AI stance but a nuanced position about establishing norms before building scale. That kind of thinking — infrastructure ethics before adoption — connects to Amber's critical computing interests.

## Research Taste Analysis

### Conceptual Strategy
Dryhurst's core intellectual move is **infrastructure critique**: instead of writing about the politics of a technology, he builds an alternative that makes the politics visible. This is a form of **provocation-first thinking** — Have I Been Trained? didn't just argue that artist consent matters; it showed the scale of non-consent by letting any artist search 5.8 billion images.

The pattern: identify a site where power is exercised invisibly → build a tool that makes the power visible → use the tool as leverage for policy change. Saga → Spawning → Source.plus. Each step extends the logic.

### Career Architecture
- **Not institution-hopping but institution-straddling**: simultaneously holds positions at NYU (US music industry), Strelka (Russian critical theory/urbanism), EGS (European continental philosophy). These aren't sequential — they're concurrent identities that legitimize him in different registers.
- **Platform as statement**: every platform he builds (Saga, Spawning, Source.plus) is also an argument. The medium IS the message in the McLuhan sense.
- **Collaboration as methodology**: the Holly Herndon partnership isn't just romantic — it's a research methodology. They think together in public (Interdependence), build together (Spawning), perform together (Proto, Holly+). This couple-as-research-unit model is unusual and effective.

### Key Moves (Chronological)
1. **2013: Enter through sound design** — Sonic Movement for Semcon/Frankfurt Motorshow. Not pure art world, not pure tech — industrial client, artistic approach. Established the hybrid identity early.
2. **2015: Saga** — First infrastructure move. Addressed distribution ownership. Too early for mainstream adoption, but established the conceptual logic.
3. **2015: Platform album** — Co-produced with Herndon. Made the decentralization/DAO politics explicit in a music context. Art world distribution of critical computing ideas.
4. **2019: Proto + Spawn** — Crossed into AI proper. "Training ceremonies" as performance art that's also a machine learning protocol. Made AI legible through ritual/community.
5. **2020: Interdependence podcast** — Built a public intellectual forum. Guests include people from Stability AI to sound artists. Created a space that bridges worlds.
6. **2021: Holly+** — Made the consent question visceral: *here is a tool that lets anyone deepfake Holly's voice*. The point isn't violation — it's showing what's now possible and demanding governance.
7. **2022: Spawning** — Scaled the consent question to infrastructure. From one artist's voice to 5.8 billion images. Policy leverage through scale.
8. **2023: Source.plus** — Flipped from opt-out to opt-in curation. Moves from defense to construction.

### What Amber Can Learn

1. **Infrastructure as argument**: Don't just write about the problem — build something that makes the problem undeniable. Amber could apply this in critical computing contexts: build tools that make invisible power visible in research/academic settings.

2. **The consent framework**: Dryhurst's framing — "establish consent norms before scale, more will opt in than out" — is a generalizable template for thinking about any new technology where power asymmetries exist. Applicable to genomics, surveillance, educational data mining.

3. **Multiple simultaneous legitimacies**: He's credible in Berlin experimental music, in critical theory (EGS), in music industry (NYU Clive Davis), and in AI policy. Each legitimacy amplifies the others. Amber has art + science + theory + design — she should think about how to keep all of these active rather than specializing.

4. **Podcast as research methodology**: The Interdependence model — regular public conversations with people working at the frontier — is a form of research that also builds community. It's faster than writing, more networked than seminars. Amber could consider a similar format.

5. **Artist-as-policy-actor**: Dryhurst shows that artists can be cited in EU policy debates if they've built something real. The path from art to policy goes through infrastructure, not just advocacy.
