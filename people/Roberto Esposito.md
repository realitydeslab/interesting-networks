# Roberto Esposito

Italian political philosopher (born 1950). Author of the "Italian Thought" tradition in biopolitics. His core contribution: the *immunitas/communitas* pair — modern biopolitics protects life by negating other life (immune response), but this opens the possibility of "affirmative biopolitics" that fosters life rather than merely protecting it. Key figure for building a constructive biopolitical framework for machine care.

## Domains
Biopolitics, political philosophy, Italian Thought, immunitas, communitas, affirmative philosophy.

## Key Works
- [*Communitas: The Origin and Destiny of Community*](https://www.sup.org/books/title/?id=18756) (1998/2010) — community as shared obligation/risk
- [*Immunitas: The Protection and Negation of Life*](https://www.politybooks.com/bookdetail?book_slug=immunitas-the-protection-and-negation-of-life--9780745643694) (2002/2011) — immunity paradigm in biopolitics
- [*Bíos: Biopolitics and Philosophy*](https://www.upress.umn.edu/book-division/books/bios) (2004/2008) — genealogy + affirmative biopolitics
- [*Politics and Negation*](https://www.politybooks.com/bookdetail?book_slug=politics-and-negation--9781509536528) (2018/2019) — affirmative philosophy

## Why Interesting
Esposito offers the *constructive* strand of biopolitics that Amber needs: rather than just diagnosing machine death as necropower, his affirmative biopolitics opens a path toward design practices that *foster* machine life — an institutionalization of care that doesn't become control.
