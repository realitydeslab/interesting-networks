Ethiopian-Irish cognitive scientist and AI accountability researcher. Founder and PI of the [AI Accountability Lab (AIAL)](https://aial.ie/) at Trinity College Dublin. Former Senior Adviser in AI Accountability at Mozilla Foundation. One of the most rigorous and combative critical ML voices working today — simultaneously technically proficient (empirical audits, NeurIPS papers) and philosophically grounded (enactivism, relational ethics, algorithmic colonization). ## Background

Born in Ethiopia. Studied psychology and philosophy as an undergraduate through The Open University (UK's distance-learning institution — an interesting institutional choice, suggesting financial or geographic constraints). Moved to Ireland. MSc Cognitive Science at UCD 2015. PhD at UCD's Complex Software Lab (School of Computer Science and Statistics), supervised within a machine learning context despite being a cognitive scientist. This institutional positioning — a cognitive scientist embedded in CS — is core to her unusual perspective.

PhD (defended 2021) focused on computational models of human behavior: [thesis available on arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2206.04179). During PhD, began noticing that ML researchers weren't critically examining their training data — nobody else was doing it, so she "almost got forced into it."

Post-PhD: Mozilla Foundation Senior Adviser in AI Accountability, adjunct assistant professor at Trinity. In 2024, established the AI Accountability Lab (AIAL) at Trinity College Dublin — a full research group, not just a personal label. She is now PI.

Ethiopian-Irish identity is central to her work: her critique of "algorithmic colonization of Africa" comes from lived experience and intellectual positioning. She applies Ubuntu philosophy ("a person is a person through other persons") as a counter to Cartesian computational individualism.

## Domains

- **Empirical AI auditing**: systematic examination of training datasets, model behavior, benchmark evaluation
- **Algorithmic bias & fairness**: going beyond "fix the bias" to asking "fix the bias for whom?"  
- **Enactivist / 4E cognition applied to AI critique**: using embodied, embedded, enacted, extended cognition frameworks to show why ML models fail
- **Algorithmic colonization**: how AI systems replicate colonial power structures, especially toward Africa and the Global South
- **Research culture critique**: auditing the values and ideologies embedded in ML research itself
- **AI governance & policy**: translating research into EU/global AI regulation discourse
- **Relational ethics**: Ubuntu/relational approach to ethics as alternative to individualist frameworks

## Key Works

- **[Computer-vision research powers surveillance technology](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-025-08972-6)** (Nature, 2025) — with Pratyusha Ria Kalluri, William Agnew, Myra Cheng, Kentrell Owens, Luca Soldaini. Found 5-fold surge in computer vision research linked to surveillance-enabling patents. Major media coverage across Nature, The Register, El País, etc.

## Research Taste Analysis

### Conceptual Strategy

**Provocation-first**: Birhane's signature move is the counterintuitive empirical finding that becomes a provocation. "Scale makes things worse, not better" (TIME100 interview, 2023 paper). "The impossibility of automating ambiguity." "Large image datasets: a pyrrhic win." The provocation leads the paper; the empirical evidence follows. This is rhetorically effective: it forces the ML community to respond to the challenge.

**Framework multiplication**: Ubuntu philosophy × computational science = relational ethics for AI. Enactivism × ML criticism = impossibility arguments. History of colonialism × AI datasets = algorithmic colonization. She consistently imports frameworks from outside ML (philosophy, African philosophy, cognitive science, postcolonial theory) and collides them with ML problems.

**Two-word concept coining**: "Algorithmic colonization," "relational ethics," "pyrrhic win" — she creates memorable shorthand that becomes citable. Compare to how "dataset bias" became "structural racism in ML."

### Career Architecture

**Outsider-to-insider trajectory**: Started outside the ML establishment (cognitive scientist, African, female, Global South background, distance learning). Used that outsiderness as a *methodological asset* — she could see what insiders were normalizing. Made the establishment come to her (NeurIPS, Nature, FAccT) rather than adapting to their norms.

**Dataset audit as a new discipline**: She effectively founded/co-founded empirical AI dataset auditing as a recognized field. By the time others started doing it, she had the founding papers (2020-2021). This is the "field declaration" move.

**Institutional positioning**: PhD in CS but trained as cognitive scientist → unique position to critique CS from inside. Mozilla fellowship → policy credibility + public reach. Trinity PI → institutional independence + ability to build lab. AIAL launch → own research group as infrastructure.

**Coalition papers as territory claims**: Her multi-author papers (FAccT 2022 values paper with 6 authors; Taxonomy of LM risks with DeepMind; Nature surveillance paper with 5 authors) function as coalition-building. Each co-author brings their network. The DeepMind paper especially — getting inside the most powerful AI lab's research while remaining critical of it.

### Key Moves

1. **2017 — Aeon "Descartes was wrong" essay**: First public declaration of her Ubuntu/relational framework. Not academic paper — public essay. Established her as a public intellectual before she had a PhD. Built audience outside academia.

2. **2020 — ImageNet/80M Tiny Images paper (with Prabhu)**: First empirical audit. Caused MIT to take down a major dataset. Proved that critical ML work could have real-world consequences. Made her name.

3. **2021 — "Impossibility of automating ambiguity" in Artificial Life**: Published in ALife journal, not a CS venue. Signals she's maintaining cognitive science identity and cross-disciplinary range. Philosophically rigorous, not just empirically activist.

4. **2021-2022 — FAccT presence**: Multiple papers in the central critical ML venue. Established as a permanent fixture of the FAccT scene, not a one-hit wonder.

5. **2023 — TIME100 AI**: The leap from researcher to public figure. Now she has access to policy conversations, mainstream media, board rooms.

6. **2024 — AI Accountability Lab at TCD**: Converts her individual credibility into institutional infrastructure. Can now hire, fund, produce a lab's worth of output.

7. **2025 — Censored at ITU, documents it publicly**: Converts censorship into credibility. The documentation of the AI for Good censorship is a classic "speak truth to power" move that increases her moral authority precisely because it cost her something.

### What Amber Can Learn

1. **The provocation-first structure**: Lead with the counterintuitive finding. Let the empirical work follow. This is more citeable and more memorable than "we studied X and found Y."

2. **Cross-venue publishing as identity maintenance**: She publishes in ALife, Cognitive Science, Nature, FAccT, Real Life Magazine simultaneously. This prevents being captured by any one disciplinary identity. Amber should keep publishing across venues (art venues, ALife, CS venues, policy).

3. **The outsider-as-asset positioning**: Birhane never pretended to be a core ML researcher. She positioned her outsiderness (philosopher of mind, African, non-traditional institution) as her distinctive methodological contribution. Amber's designer/artist background in HCI research is analogous.

4. **Building infrastructure before you're famous**: The AIAL launched when she was already established. But she was building collaborator networks (Prabhu, Abebe, Kalluri) from the beginning. Network before infrastructure, infrastructure after the name is made.

5. **Public essays as audience-building**: Her 2017 Aeon piece, 2020 Real Life, 2020 Noema essays built a public audience *before* her academic career took off. They're what made her NeurIPS best paper matter beyond NeurIPS.

6. **Documented confrontation as credibility**: The ITU censorship story is a masterclass in turning institutional suppression into public trust. She didn't complain privately — she documented it, published it, with slides. Authentic integrity.
