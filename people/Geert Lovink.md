Dutch media theorist, internet critic, and publisher (b. 1959, Amsterdam). Co-coined "tactical media" with David Garcia (1997). Founding director of the [[Institute of Network Cultures]] (INC, 2004–2026) at Amsterdam University of Applied Sciences (HvA). Retiring September 1, 2026 at mandatory age 67. INC transitioning to autonomous NGO. Most recent book: *[Platform Brutality](https://networkcultures.org/geert/2026/01/21/interview-with-geert-lovink-about-platform-brutality/)* (Valiz, 2025).

## Key Works
- *Platform Brutality* (Valiz, 2025) — platforms as necropolitical infrastructure; inspired by Mbembe
- *Sad by Design* (2019) — social media as melancholy infrastructure
- *Social Media Abyss* (2016) — critical theory of social media
- *Networks Without a Cause* (2012) — organized networks, net nihilism
- *Zero Comments* (2007) — blogging, comment culture, decline of the internet public
- *Dark Fiber: Tracking Critical Internet Culture* (2002) — early internet critique
- Co-edited: Unlike Us Reader, MoneyLab Readers

## Key Concepts
- **Organized networks** — networks that self-organize around issues, not institutions
- **Platform brutality** — the political violence embedded in platform extractivism
- **Net nihilism** — critical engagement without utopian escape
- **Tactical media** — media as political intervention (with David Garcia, 1997)
- **Unlike Us** — unity of social media critique and alternatives
- **Urgency publishing** — fast, open-access, practice-embedded publishing

## Why Interesting
Essential context for Amber's protocol studies and decentralized AI work. Lovink asks: who controls digital infrastructure? What violence is embedded in platform logics? His "organized networks" concept is the media theory precursor to Amber's [[Agentive Commoning]]. His MoneyLab work critiques crypto-as-liberation — essential adversarial context for agent economies. Retiring in 2026; INC transitioning. Window to engage before the institutional reorganization.
