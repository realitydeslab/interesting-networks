# Anna Ridler

London-based artist working with AI and machine learning, particularly **GANs (Generative Adversarial Networks)**, to explore relationships between data, nature, and time. Best known for *Myriad (Tulips)* and *Mosaic Virus* — works that map tulip datasets to financial derivatives, connecting natural cycles to speculation. Member of the **[[Future Art Ecosystems]]** network (Serpentine Arts Technologies).

Her practice is notable for insisting on creating her own training datasets — she doesn't use pre-existing image banks but grows, photographs, and curates images herself, often of plants and flowers. This methodological commitment to the dataset as an artistic choice (not an engineering given) is what distinguishes her from most AI artists.

## Key Works
- **Myriad (Tulips)** — 10,000 photographs of tulips, hand-taken, used as training data for GANs. The dataset itself is the artwork.
- **Mosaic Virus** — video installation mapping tulip price to GAN output. When the price rises (per historical tulip mania data), the virus appears. Finance → nature → AI loop.
- Various explorations of plant life cycles, botany, and generative processes
