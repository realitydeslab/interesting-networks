# Homi K. Bhabha

Indian-British literary and cultural theorist. Anne F. Rothenberg Professor of the Humanities at Harvard University; Director of the [Mahindra Humanities Center](https://mahindrahumanities.fas.harvard.edu/). One of the three or four most cited postcolonial theorists in the world — known for the concepts of **hybridity**, **mimicry**, **third space**, and **ambivalence** in colonial discourse. Core figures in the postcolonial canon alongside Edward Said and Gayatri Chakravorty Spivak. Significant intellectual interlocutor of [[Premesh Lalu]]; they co-authored "Temporalities of Race and Translational Memory" (*South African Journal of Science*, 2022) — a rare Bhabha co-authorship that signals mutual intellectual respect.

## Domains
- Colonial discourse analysis — the language and logic of colonial power
- Hybridity, ambivalence, mimicry — the postcolonial subject's complex relationship to colonial culture
- Third space — the site where cultural meaning is negotiated and contested; "in-between" as productive space
- Cultural time — the temporalities of colonial and postcolonial experience
- Translational memory — how memory crosses cultural and temporal boundaries

## Key Works
- *[The Location of Culture](https://www.routledge.com/The-Location-of-Culture/Bhabha/p/book/9780415336390)* (Routledge, 1994) — the foundational text; essays on hybridity, mimicry, ambivalence, third space. One of the most cited books in cultural studies.
- *[Nation and Narration](https://www.routledge.com/Nation-and-Narration/Bhabha/p/book/9780415012560)* (ed., Routledge, 1990) — foundational edited volume on nation and its cultural construction
- *[A Measure of Dwelling](https://www.hup.harvard.edu/catalog.php?isbn=9780674265660)* (Harvard UP, 2024) — his most recent book; on dwelling, temporality, and modern life
- *["Temporalities of Race and Translational Memory"](https://doi.org/10.17159/sajs.2022/14937)* — with [[Premesh Lalu]]. *South African Journal of Science* 118(9/10), 2022.

## Why Interesting
Bhabha's concept of the "third space" — where cultures meet, negotiate, and produce new hybrid forms — is directly relevant to Amber's interest in human-AI cohabitation and more-than-human design. If human-AI encounters are a kind of "third space" where neither human nor machine norms fully apply, Bhabha's framework is a theoretical resource. His co-authorship with Lalu (2022) signals that he is in intellectual dialogue with the CHR orbit — and his presence at Harvard makes him London-adjacent via transatlantic networks.
