# Simon Colton

Creator of [The Painting Fool](http://www.thepaintingfool.com/) — a computational painter designed to be taken seriously as an artist, not just a tool. Co-author of "Computational Creativity: The Final Frontier?" (ECAI 2012), one of the field's most-cited papers. Pioneer of the idea that creative systems must be *motivated*, not just programmed.

## Key Works

- [Colton & Wiggins, "Computational Creativity: The Final Frontier?"](https://doi.org/10.3233/978-1-61499-098-7-21) (ECAI 2012) — one of the most-cited CC papers; defines scope and aspirations of the field
- HR system — automated mathematical theory formation and discovery
- *[Computational Creativity: The Philosophy and Engineering of Autonomously Creative Systems](https://doi.org/10.1007/978-3-319-43610-4)* (Veale & Cardoso eds., 2019) — contributing author

## Why Interesting
The Painting Fool explicitly operationalises the question "what makes a machine creative?" by designing a system with motivation, imagination, and emotional response — not just output generation. Colton's insistence that creative systems must have *reasons* for their choices, and must be able to reflect on their work, connects directly to Amber's interest in agent intentionality and machine behavior.

Key question Colton asks: can the system be said to be *expressing* something? This is the aesthetic autonomy question.
