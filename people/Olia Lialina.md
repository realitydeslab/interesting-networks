# Olia Lialina

Pioneering net artist (Moscow/Stuttgart) and theorist; created *My Boyfriend Came Back from the War* (1996), one of the first canonical internet artworks; professor at Merz Akademie Stuttgart; writes theory on vernacular web aesthetics and user agency.

## Domains
Net art, internet theory, vernacular web aesthetics, user agency, digital preservation

## Key Works
- [*My Boyfriend Came Back from the War*](http://www.teleportacia.org/war/) (1996) — canonical hypertext narrative in HTML frames; first work in Rhizome Net Art Anthology
- [*Agatha Appears*](http://www.teleportacia.org/agatha/) (1997) — story told through hyperlink titles
- [*Turing Complete User*](http://contemporary-home-computing.org/turing-complete-user/) (2012) — theory essay on user as active computational agent
- [*A Vernacular Web*](http://art.teleportacia.org/observation/vernacular/) (2005) — essay on folk aesthetics of early web

## Why Interesting
Her "Turing Complete User" directly addresses Amber's interests: what kind of agency does a user/agent have in a computational system? Her work on vernacular web aesthetics connects to more-than-human design — the idea that folk/amateur web aesthetics were a form of human expression being systematically erased by platform design. Her work is also about machine death: *My Boyfriend* now requires emulation to run properly.
