# Liu Cixin 刘慈欣

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Stub created during Chinese Art-Tech Scene deep research.

China's most celebrated science fiction writer. First Asian author to win the Hugo Award (for *The Three-Body Problem*, 2015). Described by Barack Obama as having "blew my mind." Former software engineer who spent decades writing sci-fi while working at a power plant in Shanxi Province. Considered the key figure in the global explosion of Chinese science fiction.

## Background
Born 1963 in Beijing; raised in Shanxi Province after parents sent to coal-mining area. Studied computer science. Worked as software engineer at Niangziguan Power Plant (娘子关发电厂) for over a decade while secretly writing science fiction — a deeply double life. The power plant context is significant: his work is pervaded by energy, infrastructure, industrial scale, and planetary systems.

## Key Works

### Three-Body Problem Trilogy (地球往事三部曲)
- ***The Three-Body Problem*** (三体, 2008 / English 2014, trans. Ken Liu) — Hugo Award 2015. Cultural Revolution physicist receives signal from alien civilization. Explores civilizational strategy, cosmological violence, and the "dark forest" theory: every civilization must destroy others to survive.
- ***The Dark Forest*** (黑暗森林, 2008 / English 2015, trans. Joel Martinsen) — The dark forest hypothesis as cosmological axiom.
- ***Death's End*** (死神永生, 2010 / English 2016, trans. Ken Liu) — Hugo Award finalist. Civilizational extinction at cosmic scale.

The trilogy has been adapted for Chinese TV series by Tencent (2023) and Netflix (3 Body Problem, 2024).

### Other Key Works
- ***Ball Lightning*** (球状闪电, 2004) — Quantum scale military technology
- ***The Wandering Earth*** (流浪地球, 2000) — The sun dies; Earth fitted with engines and moved to new star system. Adapted into hugely successful Chinese film (2019).
- ***Supernova Era*** (超新星纪元) — All adults die from cosmic radiation; children inherit Earth
- Short story collection: *The Dark Forest*, *Remembrance of Earth's Past*

## For Amber
*The Three-Body Problem*'s "dark forest" hypothesis has become one of the most cited frameworks for thinking about AI alignment and civilizational risk. The trilogy is widely read in tech and AI safety communities globally (including at companies like OpenAI). Amber's engagement with AI ethics, machine behavior, and planetary futures makes this essential background. The *Wandering Earth* concept — Earth itself as spaceship, engineered exodus — is a distinctive form of planetary-scale speculative engineering that resonates with Anthropocene thinking.

## Why Interesting
Liu Cixin represents something unprecedented: a science fiction writer from outside the Anglo-American tradition who has reshaped global science fiction discourse. His civilizational-scale thinking (every civilization must hide or be destroyed) has entered AI alignment debates as a serious philosophical position. His work operates at cosmic scales that dwarf most Western sci-fi's assumptions — not just technological change but the death of stars, the dimensional folding of space, civilizational extinction across geological time.

His background as a power plant engineer gives his work a distinctive materialist grounding: physics is always physical infrastructure, energy is always an engineering problem, the universe is always a system with engineering constraints.
