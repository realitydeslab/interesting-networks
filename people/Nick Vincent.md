# Nick Vincent

Assistant Professor of Communication at SFU; affiliated with the [[Digital Democracies Institute]]. Works on human-centered AI with a focus on **data leverage** — the idea that human activities are *upstream* of AI systems, and that organized groups can use this to bargain for better outcomes from AI.

## Core Concepts
- **[Data leverage](https://www.nickmvincent.com/static/data_leverage.pdf)**: Organized groups can withhold or redirect their data contributions to AI systems as a form of bargaining power — analogous to labor organizing.
- **[Data dignity](https://www.radicalxchange.org/concepts/data-dignity/)**: Framework (via RadicalxChange) for ensuring people retain rights and rewards from their data contributions to AI.

Research goal: frameworks, tools, and empirical results toward "an ecosystem of highly capable and widely beneficial AI technologies that mitigate — rather than exacerbate — inequalities in wealth and power."

## Why Interesting
His framing reposisions the AI governance debate from "alignment" (technical) to "data labor" (political-economic). The question isn't just whether AI is safe — it's who owns the data that trained it and who benefits. This is directly adjacent to Amber's interests in decentralized AI and sovereign agents — Vincent asks whether humans can maintain leverage over the systems they've fed. A key bridge between STS/critical theory and practical AI governance strategy.
