# Sylvia Wynter

Jamaican-born philosopher and scholar emerita at Stanford. Formulated a decolonial critique of humanism that predates and challenges posthumanism: "Man" as a genre of being is a colonial invention, not a universal. Her 2003 essay "Unsettling the Coloniality of Being/Power/Truth/Freedom" is essential.

## Why Interesting
Wynter's critique: Western humanism invented "Man" as a particular (white, European, rational) figure, and then claimed it as the universal human. Any posthumanism that doesn't address this just invents a new exclusionary universal. Directly relevant to Amber's work — cosmotechnics (Yuk Hui) makes a similar move for Chinese techné.
