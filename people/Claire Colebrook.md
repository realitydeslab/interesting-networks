# Claire Colebrook

Distinguished Professor at **Penn State University**, one of the most important philosophers of **posthumanism, extinction, and feminist theory** working today. Former student and major interpreter of Deleuze. Her work tracks what happens when "life" becomes uncoupled from the human — and how extinction becomes not just a threat but a philosophical category.

Co-edits **Critical Climate Change** and **CCC2 Irreversibility** series at **[[Open Humanities Press]]** (with Tom Cohen). The series is the premier academic publishing home for extinction thinking and Anthropocene theory.

Her trajectory: from careful Deleuzian scholarship (*Deleuze and the Meaning of Life*, *Understanding Deleuze*) to increasingly urgent engagement with the logic of extinction — *Death of the PostHuman* (OHP, 2014) argues posthumanism has its own logic of exhaustion; *Sex After Life* (OHP, 2014) thinks sexuality beyond the life-drive.

## Why Interesting
Colebrook is doing something philosophically rare: she insists that posthumanism must be thought through to its conclusion — which is not some warm cyborg fusion but the possibility of extinction of the human as a category. She refuses consoling posthumanism (Haraway's warm "make-kin") in favour of rigorous confrontation with what it means for the human to become literally obsolete. This is philosophically more honest and more difficult. Her OHP books are free and essential.
