# Mark Coeckelbergh

Belgian-Austrian philosopher of technology and AI ethics. Professor at the [University of Vienna](https://coeckelbergh.net/), Department of Philosophy. Among the most prolific contemporary philosophers working on **robot ethics**, **AI governance**, and the phenomenological dimensions of artificial intelligence. Author of a major monograph-length *AI Ethics* textbook (MIT Press, 2020) and a series of books on the social and political dimensions of AI. Speaker at the [[Research Network for Philosophy and Technology]] Dialogues (Nov 3, 2025).

## Background
Trained at KU Leuven and Oxford. Has held positions at De Montfort University (Leicester, UK), University of Twente (Netherlands), and since ~2018 at University of Vienna. Works at the intersection of continental philosophy of technology (phenomenology, hermeneutics) and applied ethics of AI and robotics. Known for accessible, policy-relevant writing alongside more theoretical work.

## Why Interesting
Coeckelbergh bridges the rigor of continental philosophy of technology with the practical urgency of AI governance. His *AI Ethics* is now the standard graduate textbook. For Amber: his political philosophy of AI angle is useful for framing design research in terms of power and governance, not just usability. Less theoretically exotic than Hui or Hörl, but useful as a more accessible bridge to policy/governance conversations.
