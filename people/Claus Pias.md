# Claus Pias

German media theorist specializing in the history of **cybernetics, game theory, and digital computing**. Professor at Leuphana University Lüneburg (MECS — Media, Culture and Society in the Computer Age). One of the key historians of mid-20th-century feedback systems, simulations, and their intellectual history.

## Domains
- History of cybernetics (Norbert Wiener, Macy Conferences)
- Game theory as media and cultural phenomenon
- History of digital computing
- Media archaeology

## Key Works
- *Computer Spiel Welten* (2002) — media theory of computer games; one of early scholarly treatments
- Editor: *Cybernetics: The Macy Conferences 1946–1953. The Complete Transactions* (Diaphanes, 2016) — essential archive edition ⭐⭐⭐
- Works on simulation, control, and feedback in media history

## Key Communities
- [[IKKM Weimar]] — Senior Fellow, Oct 2008 – Mar 2009
- [[ICAM Leuphana]] / MECS — long-term faculty

## Why Interesting
Pias is the best German-language historian of cybernetics and its relation to media and society. The Macy Conferences edition he edited is an essential archival resource for understanding the origins of information/communication paradigms. Relevant to Amber's interest in cybernetic lineages, agent-based systems, and the pre-history of AI.
