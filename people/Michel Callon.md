# Michel Callon

Co-founder of Actor-Network Theory. Economist and sociologist of science and technology at Mines Paris. Author of the landmark 1984 "scallops paper" defining the four moments of translation. Also developed the sociology of markets as sociotechnical devices.

## Domains
- Actor-Network Theory (co-founder)
- Sociology of translation
- Economics of scientific knowledge
- Markets as sociotechnical assemblages

## Key Works
- [Some Elements of a Sociology of Translation](https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1467-954X.1984.tb00113.x) (1984) — the scallop paper. Foundational ANT text.
- *The Laws of the Markets* (ed., 1998) — markets as performative, sociotechnical constructions

## Why Interesting
The scallops paper is the methodological template for studying how heterogeneous actors get enrolled into networks. Directly applicable to AI deployment studies — how AI systems build their networks of users, data, institutions.
