# Keeanga-Yamahtta Taylor

Professor of African American Studies at Princeton University. Scholar and activist working on race, housing, politics, and the Black freedom movement. Wrote the **foreword to the 2022 revised 25th anniversary edition** of **[[Saidiya Hartman]]**'s *Scenes of Subjection*.

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-27:** Stub created during Saidiya Hartman deep research.

## Key Works
- **[*From #BlackLivesMatter to Black Liberation*](https://www.haymarketbooks.org/books/875-from-blacklivesmatter-to-black-liberation)** (Haymarket Books, 2016; expanded 2nd ed. 2024) — major political history connecting BLM to the longer Black liberation tradition
- **[*Race for Profit: How Banks and the Real Estate Industry Undermined Black Homeownership*](https://uncpress.org/book/9781469653525/race-for-profit/)** (UNC Press, 2019) — winner of several awards; on predatory inclusion in housing policy
- Foreword to *Scenes of Subjection* (2022 edition)

## Key Connections
- **[[Saidiya Hartman]]** — contributed foreword to *Scenes* 2022 edition
- **[[Mariame Kaba]]** — intellectual peer in the Black radical movement

## Why Interesting
Taylor bridges academic historical scholarship and political analysis in an accessible way. Her work on housing and race is directly relevant to design/technology studies focused on algorithmic discrimination, redlining algorithms, and urban tech.
