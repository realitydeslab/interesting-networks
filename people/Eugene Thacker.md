# Eugene Thacker

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-03-03:** Stub created during Speculative Realism deep research.

Philosopher and media theorist at The New School for Social Research. Known for the *Horror of Philosophy* trilogy (*In the Dust of This Planet*, *Starry Speculative Corpse*, *Tentacles Longer Than Night*) — philosophical horror as a genre that takes seriously the thought of a world without humans. Participates in the SR orbit via his work on "the world-without-us" — a radical anti-anthropocentrism that extends from continental philosophy into horror aesthetics, biopolitics, and dark ecology. Also known for biopolitics and post-biopolitical thought. Appeared at the OOO symposium at Georgia Tech (2010).

## Why Interesting
The "world without us" concept connects to machine death / machine consciousness themes (Amber's mortality interest). Thacker thinks philosophically about the limits of human cognition and the unthinkable excess of world beyond human access — which maps onto AI as radical otherness. The horror philosophy framing is unusual and productive — it approaches SR's core question (what is the world independent of humans?) through aesthetic-existential rather than dry metaphysical means.
