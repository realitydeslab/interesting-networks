# Fiona Raby

British designer, researcher, and educator. Co-creator (with [[Anthony Dunne]]) of **speculative design** and **critical design** as disciplines. Fiona's distinct trajectory — trained in architecture and computation before partnering with Dunne — grounds the practice in spatial thinking, systems logic, and a sensitivity to how invisible technologies reshape lived environments. She is not simply "Dunne's partner"; her architectural and computational background is what gave critical design its rigor beyond industrial design, connecting it to urbanism, information space, and the politics of the electromagnetic spectrum.

## Background

### Education & Early Career
- Studied **Architecture at the Royal College of Art** — this is the crucial distinction. While Dunne came through Industrial Design and Sony Tokyo, Fiona came through architecture: spatial thinking, environmental sensitivity, the politics of the built environment.
- Completed an **MPhil in Computer Related Design (CRD)** at the RCA — one of the earliest programmes to treat computing as a design material rather than an engineering tool. The CRD programme, founded by [[Gillian Crampton Smith]], was a radical experiment in the early 1990s.
- **Co-founding member of the CRD Research Studio at RCA** — alongside Dunne, this was where "critical design" was born as a research practice. The studio operated as Senior Research Fellows, producing foundational work on how electronic objects inhabit domestic space.

### The Architecture + Computation Synthesis
Fiona's architecture training is visible throughout the practice: the concern with *space* (electromagnetic space, information space, cellular networks as urban territory), the attention to *inhabitation* (how people live with objects, not just use them), and the use of *spatial narrative* (scenarios that unfold in rooms, homes, landscapes). Her early writing — "Fields and Thresholds" (1995) in *Architects in Cyberspace*, "Tuneable Cities" (1998) — shows someone thinking about computation as an architectural material.

The FLIRT project (1998–2000), an EU-funded research project she led at CRD, was explicitly about "the genuine fusion between information space and urban territory" created by mobile cellular networks. This is architectural thinking applied to the invisible: how data shapes the experience of place.

### Academic Trajectory
- **CRD Research Studio, RCA** (1994–2005) — Senior Research Fellow and co-founding member
- **Reader in Design Interactions, RCA** (2005–2015) — while Dunne was Professor and Head of Department, Fiona held the Reader position (equivalent to Associate Professor), teaching and supervising research
- **Professor of Industrial Design, University of Applied Arts Vienna** — concurrent appointment
- **University Professor of Design and Social Inquiry, Parsons / The New School** (2016–2025) — co-directed the **Designed Realities Studio** with Dunne, developing practice-led research bringing design, humanities, and social sciences into conversation
- **Distinguished Visiting Professor, China Academy of Art** (2025–) — Hangzhou, marking a pivot toward Asian design education

## Key Works

### Books
- [*Hertzian Tales: Electronic Products, Aesthetic Experience and Critical Design*](https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262541992/) (1999 CRD Research / 2005 MIT Press, with Anthony Dunne) — the founding text of critical design. Argues for design that engages with the electromagnetic spectrum and its cultural implications.
- [*Design Noir: The Secret Life of Electronic Objects*](https://www.bloomsbury.com/uk/design-noir-9781350070417/) (2001 August/Birkhäuser / 2021 Bloomsbury reissue, with Dunne) — extends critical design into "dark" psychological territory. Republished 2021 as part of the *Designing in Dark Times* series.
- [*Speculative Everything: Design, Fiction, and Social Dreaming*](https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262019842/) (2013 MIT Press, with Dunne) — the canonical text that codified speculative design as a discipline. Translated into Chinese (2017, Jiangsu Fine Art Publishing), Russian (2017, Strelka), Korean (2024, agbook).
- *Not Here, Not Now: Speculative Thought, Impossibility, and the Design Imagination* (2025 MIT Press, with Dunne) — their latest, moving beyond futures toward impossibility and expanded imagining.

### Design Projects (chronological)
- **Hertzian Tales** (1994–97) — the original series of electronic objects exploring the invisible electromagnetic landscape of domestic space
- **Weeds, Aliens and Other Stories** (1994–98, with [[Michael Anastassiades]]) — psychological furniture exploring human-plant relationships. In the **permanent collection of the V&A**.
- **[Project #26765: FLIRT](https://dunneandraby.co.uk/content/projects/72/0)** (1998–2000) — EU-funded research on location-specific information as medium for social interaction. Fiona Raby led the CRD team. Collaboration with Philips, Helsinki Telephone Corporation, Infogrames. Team included [[Ben Hooker]], Shona Kitchen, Brendan Walker.
- **MSET** (2000–01) — Science Museum exhibition
- **[Placebo Project](https://dunneandraby.co.uk/content/projects/70/0)** (2001) — eight prototype objects placed in volunteers' homes to investigate attitudes toward electromagnetic fields. Photography by Jason Evans. A landmark in taking conceptual design into everyday life.
- **BioLand** (2002–03) — exploring biotechnology futures
- **Is This Your Future?** (2004) — Science Museum, London
- **[Designs for Fragile Personalities in Anxious Times](https://dunneandraby.co.uk/content/projects/71/0)** (2004–05, with Michael Anastassiades) — objects for irrational but real anxieties (fear of alien abduction, nuclear annihilation). **Huggable Atomic Mushroom in permanent collection of MoMA**. Hideaway Type 2 in Frac Ile-de-France.
- **[Evidence Dolls](https://dunneandraby.co.uk/content/projects/69/0)** (2005) — commissioned by Centre Pompidou, Paris. 100 dolls exploring genetics, designer babies, DNA theft through interviews with young women. Illustrations by [[Åbäke]].
- **Spymaker** (2006–07) — commissioned by the Science Museum for Spymaker exhibition. Em-muzzle: a dog with its smell-brain remapped to sense electromagnetic fields.
- **[Technological Dreams Series: No.1, Robots](https://dunneandraby.co.uk/content/projects/10/0)** (2007) — four robots exploring future human-robot interdependencies. Curated by Jan Bolen for [[Z33]]. Video by [[Noam Toran]], sound by [[Scanner]]. **In permanent collections of MoMA and Fnac (Fond national d'art contemporain), Paris.**
- **[Do you want to replace the existing normal?](https://dunneandraby.co.uk/content/projects/75/0)** (2007–08, with Michael Anastassiades) — speculative objects including Statistical Clock, S.O.C.D., Risk Watch, Alignment. Funded by Arts Council England.
- **[Designs for an Overpopulated Planet: Foragers](https://dunneandraby.co.uk/content/projects/510/0)** (2009) — DIY digestive devices using synthetic biology. Commissioned by [[Design Indaba]] for Protofarm 2050.
- **United Micro Kingdoms (UMK)** (2012–13) — four alternative political systems for the UK. Major exhibition project.
- **Not Here, Not Now** (2014) — exhibition/project exploring impossibility
- **Meinong's Jungle (Theory of Objects)** (2015) — objects that don't exist but have properties
- **Many Worlds Working Group (MWWG)** (2017–) — ongoing collaborative research
- **In Search of an Impossible Object** (2018, with Philipp Schmitt) — Designed Realities Studio, The New School
- **Archive of Impossible Objects: Globes** (2019) — continuing the impossibility thread

### Fiona's Independent/Lead Work
- **FLIRT** (1998–2000) — Fiona Raby is credited as lead researcher, not just co-author. This is her most clearly independent major project, exploring urban-digital fusion through mobile networks.
- **"Fields and Thresholds"** (1995) — early architectural writing on cyberspace
- **"Tuneable Cities"** (1998) — spatial/urban computing essay

## Key Communities
- **[[Computer Related Design (CRD) Research Studio, RCA]]** — the institutional birthplace of critical design. Founded in the early 1990s by [[Gillian Crampton Smith]]. Fiona was a founding member alongside Dunne. This studio was where design research met computing before "interaction design" was a field.
- **[[Design Interactions, RCA]]** — the MA programme Dunne headed (2005–2015) with Fiona as Reader. Produced a generation of speculative/critical designers: [[James Auger]], [[Noam Toran]], [[Elio Caccavale]], [[Onkar Kular]], [[Revital Cohen]], [[Bernd Hopfengärtner]], [[Alice Wang]], [[Gunnar Green]], [[Ai Hasegawa]], and many more.

- **[[RSA Royal Designers for Industry]]** — the Faculty of RDIs, UK's highest design honour community. Only 200 can hold the title.

## Research Taste Analysis
**For Amber's practice, Fiona Raby matters in several specific ways:**

**Speculative & Design Futures** (core thread): Fiona is literally the co-creator of this field. But what's most relevant for Amber isn't the canonical "speculative design" — it's the *evolution* of the practice. The trajectory from "Hertzian Tales" (1994) → "Speculative Everything" (2013) → "Not Here, Not Now" (2025) shows a movement from electromagnetic invisibility → design fiction → impossibility/expanded imagining. The latest work on impossibility and "worlds of many worlds" connects directly to Amber's interest in posthuman multiplicity and non-Western cosmologies.

**Critical Computing** (core thread): The CRD Research Studio is a *direct ancestor* of the Aarhus critical computing tradition that Amber operates in. Fiona's MPhil in "Computer Related Design" was asking the same question in 1990s London that critical computing asks now: what does it mean to treat computing as a cultural/political material rather than an engineering tool?

**More-than-human** (core thread): The "Foragers" project (2009) — DIY synthetic biology digestive devices inspired by non-human digestive systems — is explicitly posthuman. The "Technological Dreams: Robots" (2007) asks about cohabitation with non-human technological entities. The "Many Worlds Working Group" explores plural ontologies.

**Practice-based research**: D&R are perhaps the strongest living example of design-as-research. Their entire career demonstrates that making objects and scenarios IS a form of knowledge production — exactly the principle Amber's practice embodies.

**The China pivot**: Fiona's move to CAA Hangzhou is directly relevant. If Amber is interested in non-Western techno-philosophical traditions and Chinese design education, D&R's engagement with CAA, CAFA Beijing, and the Chinese translation of their books creates a bridge.

**Actionable for Amber**:
- The "Designed Realities Studio" model at Parsons — interdisciplinary design + humanities + social science — could inform Amber's own pedagogical approach
- D&R's relationship with Panasonic shows how to maintain critical practice while engaging with industry
- The FLIRT project model (EU-funded, multi-partner, research-through-design) is a template for the kind of funded research Amber might pursue
- The "Not Here, Not Now" framework of impossibility connects to Amber's interest in speculative computation via [[Antikythera]] and [[Benjamin Bratton]]

**Interest level**: ⭐⭐⭐ — foundational figure for Amber's field. The new China connection and evolving intellectual trajectory (toward impossibility, plural worlds) keep this alive and relevant rather than merely historical.
