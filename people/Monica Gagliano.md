# Monica Gagliano

Research scientist pioneering the study of **plant communication, cognition, and learning**. Known for bridging rigorous Western scientific methodology with Indigenous knowledge and direct plant-communication experiences (visions, dreams, sensations). Author of *Thus Spoke the Plant* (2018). Has appeared on [[Emergence Magazine]] podcast: "Learning to Listen to Plants."

## Why Interesting
Gagliano is at the radical edge of more-than-human science: she claims plants literally communicate with her through visions and guided her research. This is not mysticism-replacing-science but science-alongside-indigenous-knowing. Direct resonance with Amber's more-than-human design, plant intelligence, and posthuman epistemology interests.

Her research on plant learning and memory challenges the human-centric definition of intelligence — feeds directly into the "Ecology of Intelligence" frame.
