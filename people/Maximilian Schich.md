# Maximilian Schich

Founder and ERA Chair holder of [[CUDAN Open Lab]]. Multidisciplinary scientist and art historian who published the landmark ["A Network Framework of Cultural History"](https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/science.1240064) in *Science* (2014) and built the field of Cultural Data Analytics as a discipline.

## Background
Art history PhD (LMU Munich / HU Berlin / Bibliotheca Hertziana Rome). Postdoc at [[BarabásiLab]] (Boston) and Dirk Helbing's group (ETH Zurich) — network science and computational social science. Associate Professor at UT Dallas (Arts & Technology), founding member of the Edith O'Donnell Institute of Art History. Moved to Estonia June 2020 to build and lead CUDAN Open Lab via EU Horizon 2020 ERA Chair grant.

## Key Works
- [*A Network Framework of Cultural History*](https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/science.1240064) — Schich et al. (2014, *Science*). Maps 150K births/deaths of historical notables to reveal cultural flows across space and time. Accompanied by viral Nature video *Charting Culture* (600K+ views). Landmark paper that inaugurated computational cultural history.
- *Cultural Interaction* (forthcoming book) — two-decade synthesis of systematic science of art and culture
- Ongoing: [CUDAN ERA Chair Final Report](https://doi.org/10.3030/810961) as comprehensive field-definition document

## Key Communities
- **[[CUDAN Open Lab]]** — founder and ERA Chair holder (2020–2024); still leading (2024–2027)
- **BarabásiLab** (postdoc) — network science formation
- Edith O'Donnell Institute of Art History, UT Dallas — co-founding member

## Why Interesting
Schich is one of the rare figures who successfully built a new *field* rather than just doing research. The ERA Chair model — assembling a multidisciplinary team around a big cultural question with deliberate academic mixing — is an institutional innovation as significant as his scientific contributions. His 2014 *Science* paper proved that network science could genuinely illuminate art history, opening a space that many now inhabit.

For Amber: Schich's career arc (art history → network science postdoc → field-defining paper → ERA Chair → book) is a model for how to build discipline-bridging research to institutional scale. The *Cultural Interaction* book, when published, will be essential reading.
