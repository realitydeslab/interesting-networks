# Nanne van Noord

Assistant Professor at the **University of Amsterdam** (intersection of Informatics/AI and Film Studies/Cultural Analytics). Works on AI and cultural heritage, particularly computational film analysis and the application of computer vision to historical film collections.

## Collaboration with [[Daniel Chávez Heras]]
Key collaborator on the **Sculpting Time with Computers** workshop series (KCL, 2023–2024). Co-presented at the **EYE Museum International Film Conference** (Amsterdam, May 2024): "Sculpting time with Computers: Identifying temporal dynamics in large audiovisual collections" — with Daniel and Mila Oiva. Participant in the Sculpting Time workshops that analyzed Soviet newsreel collections.

## Why Interesting
Represents the European computational film studies scene, working at the University of Amsterdam where there's strong tradition of both film studies (Patricia Pisters, etc.) and AI research. His collaboration with Daniel bridges KCL (London) and UvA (Amsterdam), creating a UK–Netherlands axis in computational film studies.
