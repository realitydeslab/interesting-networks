# Jasbir Puar

Queer theorist and political geographer at Rutgers University. Known for *Terrorist Assemblages: Homonationalism in Queer Times* (Duke UP, 2007) and *The Right to Maim: Debility, Capacity, Disability* (Duke UP, 2017). Works at the intersection of queer theory, biopolitics, assemblage theory, and disability/crip theory.

## Key Works
- [*Terrorist Assemblages: Homonationalism in Queer Times*](https://www.dukeupress.edu/terrorist-assemblages) (Duke UP, 2007) — coined "homonationalism"; critique of how LGBT rights become entangled with US imperialism and anti-Muslim racism
- [*The Right to Maim: Debility, Capacity, Disability*](https://www.dukeupress.edu/the-right-to-maim) (Duke UP, 2017) — biopolitics of disability; Israel-Palestine; the concept of "debility" as a political tool
- **"Contagion, Information, Territory"** (2025) — conference paper co-authored with [[Ramon Amaro]], examining data infrastructures and the Black technical object

## Key Relationships
- [[Ramon Amaro]] — co-author (2025)
- Influenced by Gilles Deleuze & Félix Guattari (assemblage theory), Michel Foucault (biopolitics), Donna Haraway

## Why Interesting
Puar's work on debility, biopolitics, and queer assemblage intersects with Amaro's Black technical object through the question of which bodies are rendered as data, which are marked as machinable. The 2025 collaboration signals that these two fields (Black studies/ML critique + queer/crip biopolitics) are being explicitly connected.
