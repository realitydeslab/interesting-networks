# Frida Orupabo

Norwegian-Nigerian visual artist. Works primarily through digital photo collages disseminated via Instagram (@nemiepeba). Trained as a sociologist. Assembles collages from digital archives — historical and contemporary images — to challenge and deconstruct the white gaze. Works express pain, powerlessness, power, and resistance to stereotypes. Has exhibited work as large-scale cut-out figures (three-dimensional photographs).

Exhibited with [[Arthur Jafa]] at Kunstnernes Hus Oslo (2019) and Moderna Museet Stockholm (2019). Jafa incorporated her Instagram feed material into his video work *Mix 1–4_constantly evolving*. Also exhibited at 58th [[Venice Biennale]] (2019).

## Why Interesting
Her practice — disseminating art via Instagram as the primary platform, treating social media feeds as artwork — is a model of using existing digital infrastructure as exhibition space. The sociological background gives her collage practice an analytical rigor that goes beyond aesthetics. The white gaze deconstruction project aligns with Jafa's camera-as-white-gaze analysis from a different medium and cultural position (Norwegian-Nigerian vs. African-American).
