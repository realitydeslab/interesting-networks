# Francis Alÿs

Belgian conceptual artist based in Mexico City since 1987. Trained as an architect; turned to art after relocating to Mexico for humanitarian work. One of the most internationally celebrated artists associated with the Mexico City contemporary scene. Known for durational walks, political allegories, and works that translate global geopolitical tensions into poetic physical actions. His work ranges from walking a melting ice block through Mexico City to "moving" a sand dune in Peru with 500 volunteers.

## Key Works
- *When Faith Moves Mountains* (2002, Lima) — 500 volunteers moved a sand dune 10cm. One of the most cited works in contemporary art.
- *Fabiola* (1994–ongoing) — collection of amateur reproductions of a medieval painting
- *The Loop* (1997) — avoided crossing the US-Mexico border by flying around the entire world instead
- *Sleepers* (1999) — photographs of people and animals sleeping in Mexico City streets
- *Tornado* (2000–2010) — entering tornado funnels in Tlaxcala, Mexico
- *Children's Games* series (1999–ongoing) — documentation of children's games worldwide

## Why Interesting
Central figure in the "post-Mexican" art scene that [[Cuauhtémoc Medina]] theorized. His work occupies the intersection of conceptual art, political allegory, and Latin American urbanism — a model for how art can translate structural political conditions into poetic actions.
