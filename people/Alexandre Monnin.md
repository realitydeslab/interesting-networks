# Alexandre Monnin

French philosopher focused on the ontology of the Web, and latterly "Closing Worlds Design" — responsible dismantling of digital and industrial systems in the Anthropocene. Key figure at [[IRI (Institut de recherche et d'innovation)]] in 2012–2013, where he ran Digital Studies / Metadata Studies seminars that formed the core program of the [[Digital Studies Network]]. Now working at the intersection of Web philosophy, pharmacological critique ([[Bernard Stiegler]]'s framework), and degrowth design.

## Domains
- Philosophy of the Web: what are URLs? What kind of objects are hyperlinks? The ontology of web resources.
- Metadata studies: how data schemas determine what knowledge is possible (epistemological dimension of metadata)
- "Closing Worlds Design" (*Design des Mondes qui se ferment*) — design methodology for responsibly reducing/dismantling digital and industrial systems; response to climate crisis
- Pharmacological critique of digital capitalism (Stieglerian framework)
- Degrowth and digital infrastructure responsibility

## Key Works
- *Héritage et Fermeture: une écologie du démantèlement* — with Diego Landivar and Emmanuel Bonnet (2021). Extension of pharmacological thinking into responsible closure of worlds/systems. Key text for degrowth design.
- Numerous papers on the philosophy of the hyperlink, URL ontology, and web resources — the philosophical foundation for thinking about what "the Web" is.

## Why Interesting
Rare philosopher who went deep into both (a) the technical ontology of the Web — what kind of things URLs, hyperlinks, and web resources actually *are* — and (b) the pharmacological critique of those systems. His "Closing Worlds Design" concept is original: instead of asking "how do we build better systems?" asking "how do we responsibly dismantle systems that are harming us?"
