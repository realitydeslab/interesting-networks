# Mark Tribe

American artist and curator. Founder of [[Rhizome]] (1996). Associate Professor of Literary Arts at Brown University. Founded Rhizome as an email list while living in Berlin; vision: "Artforum meets AltaVista — a bottom-up alternative to the top-down art world." Participated in the inaugural Rhizome/Net Art Anthology panel at New Museum (2016) alongside Olia Lialina, Martha Wilson, Ricardo Dominguez.

## Background
- Founded Rhizome February 1, 1996 while living in Berlin
- First subscribers included people met at Ars Electronica
- Email list hosted by Desk.nl (Amsterdam)
- Built Rhizome from email list to website (August 1996) to nonprofit (1998)
- Interview with Laurel Ptak for CCS Bard: "I thought of it as Artforum meets AltaVista, as a kind of bottom-up alternative to the top-down hierarchies of the art world"
- Now Associate Professor at Brown University; teaches literary arts
- Co-authored with Reena Jana: *New Media Art* (Taschen, 2006)

## Key Works / Writing
- *[New Media Art](https://www.taschen.com/)* (with Reena Jana, Taschen, 2006) — survey of new media art practices

## Why Interesting
The founding story of Rhizome is essentially the founding story of institutional net art — what it means to build a community infrastructure around internet-based art practices. Tribe's vision (bottom-up, community-driven, alternative to art world hierarchies) shaped how the net art field understood itself. Now at Brown teaching; represents the academicization of this history. Less active in current scene but important as a historical figure.
