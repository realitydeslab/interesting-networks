# Blaise Agüera y Arcas

VP & Fellow at Google, CTO of Technology & Society, founder of Paradigms of Intelligence (Pi) — the closest thing to an ALife lab inside big tech. Bridges AI research with philosophical questions about consciousness, evolution, and the nature of intelligence. Invented federated learning, founded Google's Artists + Machine Intelligence program, and writes prolifically about AGI, consciousness, and computational approaches to life.

## Background
Born 1975 in Providence, RI (Spanish father, American mother), grew up in Mexico City. Early technical prodigy — as a teenager, interned with US Navy research center in Bethesda, Maryland, reprogramming guidance software for aircraft carriers to reduce seasickness. 

**BA Physics, Princeton University (1998)** — where he worked on computational analysis of historical printing techniques, publishing findings that challenged traditional attributions of Gutenberg's printing methods (2001). This early computational humanities work presaged his later art-science bridging.

**Career trajectory:** Founded Seadragon Software (2003) → sold to Microsoft Live Labs (2006) → Microsoft Distinguished Engineer (2011), architect of Bing Maps/Bing Mobile → Google (2013) to lead machine intelligence efforts.
