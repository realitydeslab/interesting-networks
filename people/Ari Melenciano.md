# Ari Melenciano

Artist, technologist, researcher, and cultural theorist. b. 1993, Miami, FL. Based in New York. Founder and Executive Director of [[Afrotectopia]]. Previously at Google's Creative Lab. [Eyebeam](https://eyebeam.org/) Rapid Response Fellow (2020). Teaches at NYU ITP, NYU DPI, Pratt Institute, Hunter College, The New School.

## Background
Miami-born (1993), now based in NYC. Studied and taught at NYU ITP — the primary node for her art-tech practice. Previously a technologist at Google's Creative Lab. Founded Afrotectopia in 2018, which grew from a NYC festival to an international fellowship platform.

## Domains

- Speculative design

- Radical Technoculture for Racial Equity
- Omni-Specialized Design Practices

## Key Works
- **[*Black Metal*](https://www.afrotectopia.org/)** (2024) — Limited edition art book (500 copies). Space travel, sci-fi, speculative design, Black culture. Co-created via NYU ITP × MIT Space Exploration Initiative incubator.
- **üT¨u** — 3D rendered interactive online environment (webex). Experimental pedagogy guided by biomimicry philosophy, indigenous ancestral intelligence, speculative design. Simulates utopian society rooted in natural intelligence. (Rapid Response, Eyebeam 2020)
- **Imagineer Fellowship** — Inaugural 2020: 10 interdisciplinary Black innovators globally, 6-week program with open Imaginariums.

## Why Interesting
Ari bridges radical Black imagination and critical technology — from a more-than-human angle (she frames technology as "extension of sentient capability," not just human capability). Her omni-specialized, community-first approach to futures work is directly resonant with Amber's interests in more-than-human design, speculative design, and community infrastructure building.
