# Ryan Trecartin

Video artist whose work absorbs internet vernacular, consumer culture, and networked identity into cacophonous, high-speed narrative environments. One of the defining artists of the post-internet generation. Co-curator (with [[Lauren Cornell]]) of the [New Museum Triennial: *Surround Audience*](https://www.newmuseum.org/exhibitions/view/surround-audience) (2015).

## Domains
- Video art and installation
- Post-internet aesthetics
- Queer performance and identity
- Consumer culture critique
- Networked sociality as art material

## Background
Trecartin came to prominence in the 2000s with video works that used YouTube, social media language, and consumer culture as both form and content. His collaborations with artist Lizzie Fitch are foundational to his practice. He was included in the 2009 New Museum Triennial *Younger Than Jesus* and went on to co-curate the 2015 edition, *Surround Audience*, with curator [[Lauren Cornell]] — an unusual instance of an artist co-organizing a major institutional triennial.

## Key Works
- ***A Family Finds Entertainment*** (2004) — breakthrough video work
- ***i-be area*** (2007)
- ***Re'Search Wait'S*** (2009–10)
- ***Item Falls*** (Lizzie Fitch / Ryan Trecartin, 2013)
- **[New Museum Triennial: *Surround Audience*](https://www.newmuseum.org/exhibitions/view/surround-audience)** (co-curated with Lauren Cornell, New Museum, 2015) — named for the condition of total mediated immersion

## Key Communities

## Key Relationships

## Why Interesting
Trecartin is the artist-as-curator figure in Cornell's orbit. The trust she placed in him as co-curator signals her belief in artist intelligence in institutional settings. His work is the aesthetic-critical practice that the *Surround Audience* exhibition theorized.
