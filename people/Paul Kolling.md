# Paul Kolling

Berlin-based artist, co-founder of [[terra0]] and its DAO continuation [[Autonomous Forest]]. His solo practice examines the social and spatial politics of infrastructure — mediated perception, the visual politics of seeing and classifying. Winner of the **ars viva prize 2023** (Germany's most prestigious prize for emerging artists). Works across film, video, installation, and sculpture. Studied under [[Simon Denny]] (HFBK) and at China Academy of Art Hangzhou — placing him at the intersection of the global blockchain art network and the East-West art-tech crosscurrents that matter to Amber.

## Background

Paul Kolling trained at the [Berlin University of the Arts (UdK)](https://www.udk-berlin.de/en/) under **Joachim Sauter** (founder of ART+COM, pioneer of media art and information architecture) and **Jussi Ängeslevä** (interaction design, generative systems). This gave him a deep grounding in media art, computation, and the politics of technology at one of Europe's leading art schools.

During his MFA at [HFBK Hamburg](https://www.hfbk-hamburg.de/en/) (2018–2020), he studied under **[[Simon Denny]]** — the New Zealand artist who became the leading figure in art about blockchain, platform capitalism, and Silicon Valley ideology. Denny is also co-founder of **[[BPA// Berlin Program for Artists]]** (alongside Angela Bulloch and Willem de Rooij), which Kolling would later join (2021–2022). Denny curated the pivotal *Proof of Work* exhibition at Schinkel Pavillon (2018) — where terra0's *Premna Daemon* debuted — cementing the Kolling/Denny relationship as foundational to his career.

In 2019, Kolling studied at the **[SIMA program at China Academy of Art Hangzhou](https://www.caa.edu.cn/en/)** with **姚大钧 Yao Dajuin** — a Taiwanese-American sound artist, experimental musician, and theorist who is a key figure in the global sound art and media art scene. This China connection is notable given Amber's ties to the China Academy of Art (where he taught as visiting lecturer 2023–2025).

After his MFA, Kolling held a **teaching position at UdK Berlin** (Künstlerische Werkstattleitung Mechatronik, 2020–2022) while continuing his practice. He also worked as a **research associate at the [Design Research Lab, UdK Berlin](https://www.udk-berlin.de/en/research/design-research-lab/)** (with Gesche Joost, 2018) — a lab focused on design and social innovation, connecting his practice to participatory and civic dimensions of design.

The **BPA// Berlin Program for Artists** fellowship (2021–2022) placed him in a cohort alongside other emerging Berlin artists, with studio visits from mentors including Angela Bulloch, Simon Denny (again), and Willem de Rooij. BPA// exhibits annually at [KW Institute for Contemporary Art](https://www.kw-berlin.de/en/) — his BPA// period coincided with a major expansion of his exhibition circuit.

## Domains

- **Infrastructure politics** — how energy markets, data systems, legal frameworks shape and obscure social reality
- **Mediated perception** — what does technology reveal/conceal about the visual field? How do imaging regimes shape what's knowable?
- **More-than-human governance** — via terra0: can ecosystems be economic/legal agents? Can a forest own itself?
- **Blockchain and ecological theory** — DAOs, smart contracts, tokenization applied to living systems
- **Legal personhood and nonhuman rights** — *A tree; a corporation; a person* directly tests US legal personhood limits
- **Energy economics** — *Energy* (2023, ars viva) interrogates the European Energy Exchange and opacity of energy price formation

### As terra0 (with [[Paul Seidler]], formerly Max Hampshire)
- **[terra0 White Paper](https://terra0.org/assets/pdf/terra0_white_paper_2016.pdf)** (2016) — Founding document. Proposes a self-utilizing forest that, through sensors and smart contracts, sells logging licenses and accumulates capital to buy its own freedom. Still one of the most influential documents at the intersection of art, ecology, and blockchain.
- **[Flowertokens](https://flowertokens.terra0.org)** (2018) — Tokenization and verification of natural commodities. Early crypto-collectible linked to physical plant. Exhibited at [Trust](https://trust.support), Berlin (solo).
- **[Premna Daemon](https://medium.com/@terra0/premna-daemon-an-introduction-via-a-history-of-autonomy-in-the-cryptosphere-3cee15e92fe2)** (2018) — An augmented Premna Microphylla bonsai. Debuted at Simon Denny's *Proof of Work* at Schinkel Pavillon, Berlin. One of the first artworks to give a plant a blockchain identity and economic agency.
- **[Two Degrees](https://terra0.medium.com/two-degrees-nft-5af1263bfcc)** (2021) — NFT that burns itself when average global temperature reaches 2°C above pre-industrial average. Climate crisis encoded into the artwork's destruction condition.
- **[Seed Capital](https://terra0.medium.com/wen-growth-an-introduction-to-seed-capital-8d4a9df93789)** (2022) — Sculpture at Art Dubai 2022, curated by Fingerprints DAO. Rethinks economic and ecological relationships between viewers, institution, and artwork.
- **[A tree; a corporation; a person](https://www.fastcompany.com/90738287/this-tree-owns-itself-and-is-fighting-for-its-own-survival)** (2022) — **Permanent public sculpture, [Carnegie Museum of Art](https://cmoa.org/2022-carnegie-international/), Pittsburgh.** The most legally ambitious terra0 work: tests the limits of legal personhood in the US by creating a tree that formally operates as a corporation. Featured in Fast Company. Exhibited as part of the 58th Carnegie International (the most prestigious recurring contemporary art exhibition in the US).
- **[Conditional Power](https://conditionalpower.terra0.org)** (2024–ongoing) — Series exploring the connection between global environmental changes and the art market. Real-time data feeds driving artwork states.
- **[Autonomous Forest](https://autonomousforest.org)** (2025) — terra0's most ambitious project: a non-profit association and DAO dedicated to acquiring and collectively managing actual forest areas in Brandenburg, Germany. Members make collective decisions via decentralized platforms. Launched at Berlin Science Week 2025. Questions: *Who owns the means of regeneration?* This is land art + governance experiment + ecological DAO rolled into one.

## Venue Circuit Analysis

Kolling operates across **five distinct circuits** — an unusually broad range for a Berlin-based artist:

1. **German art establishment**: Kunstverein München, Haus der Kunst, KW Institute, Kunstsammlung Nordrhein-Westfalen (K21), Deichtorhallen Hamburg, Kunstverein Hamburg, Kunsthalle Zürich — the backbone of German-speaking contemporary art infrastructure
2. **Global blockchain/crypto-art**: Sotheby's Natively Digital, Art Dubai / Fingerprints DAO, Kunsthalle Zürich DYOR, Hermitage, Chronus Art Center — the global circuit for art + blockchain
3. **Critical media/net art**: Furtherfield Gallery London, Aksioma Ljubljana, Ars Electronica Linz, Transmediale Berlin, Gray Area SF, NAVEL LA, Biennale Warszawa — the older network culture/media art circuit
4. **Major international biennials**: Venice Architecture Biennale (German Pavilion), Carnegie International, Beijing Art and Technology Biennale, Vienna Biennale — top-tier biennial circuit
5. **East Asian art-tech**: Chronus Art Center Shanghai, Toto Gallery・MA Tokyo, CAFA Beijing, and the permanent installation in Fujian

The small venues he chose early tell the story: Trust Berlin, Spektrum Berlin, Furtherfield Gallery, Aksioma Ljubljana, Dato more Rijeka — these are the critical infrastructure of the art/tech/blockchain discourse. Choosing these in 2017–2018 was a bet on the scene before it entered the mainstream.

The **Carnegie International** (2022) is the inflection point — this is America's oldest recurring international contemporary art exhibition, and including terra0 as a permanent commission signals arrival in the institution-art mainstream, not just the blockchain art niche.

## For Amber

1. **🤝 Cooperation** — terra0's work on autonomous ecological agents, DAO governance of living systems, and *Autonomous Forest* maps directly onto Amber's research on machine behavior, autonomous agents, and more-than-human agency. Paul Kolling could be a genuine collaborator for a joint workshop/paper on autonomous ecological systems as a case study in machine behavior. The "agent ethology" frame would give terra0 a new theoretical lens; terra0's empirical practice would ground Amber's frameworks.

2. **🏠 Residency** — [BPA// Berlin Program for Artists](https://berlinprogramforartists.org) is a natural match for Amber — artist-led, Berlin-based, with Simon Denny and Angela Bulloch as mentors. Amber would need to be Berlin-based, but the programme requires living/working in Berlin during the cycle. Worth tracking for a future period.

3. **🎓 Fellowship** — The **ars viva prize** (Kulturkreis der deutschen Wirtschaft) is Germany's top emerging artist prize. Worth understanding who receives it and how — it's nomination-based and signals the taste of the German corporate art patronage system. Amber's work on machine behavior + speculative design could resonate here.

4. **💡 Career inspiration** — The terra0 trajectory shows how to build a long-running research project as art practice: start with a conceptual whitepaper (2016), iterate through smaller experiments, achieve institutional recognition, and eventually execute the real thing (Autonomous Forest, 2025). 9 years from concept to working forest DAO. This is the deep-time approach to artistic research.

5. **👋 Hangout potential** — Berlin-based, so accessible for Amber. The LAS Art Foundation (CANK Berlin), Trust Berlin, and KW Institute are natural sites to encounter Kolling's community. Deichtorhallen Hamburg is a short trip.

## Research Taste Analysis

### Conceptual Strategy
Kolling/terra0 operate on a **single-question deepening** strategy — unlike many artists who chase new topics, they have maintained one core question ("can an ecosystem own itself?") for nearly a decade and systematically explored every dimension of it: technical (smart contracts), legal (personhood), economic (logging licenses, art market), social (DAO governance), physical (real forest purchase). This is the opposite of restlessness. The payoff: they now have genuine expertise and a traceable intellectual trajectory that institutions can understand and invest in.

The whitepaper-as-artwork is a signature move: starting from conceptual documentation (academic format) and using it as the founding artwork. This legitimizes the project intellectually before any art world recognition arrives.

### Career Architecture
**The mentor-network leverage play**: both Kolling's UdK mentors (Sauter, Ängeslevä) and his MFA mentor (Simon Denny) were not just educators but gatekeepers to distinct scenes. Denny's *Proof of Work* exhibition was the most important early curatorial moment for blockchain art in Europe — Kolling was there at the debut because he was Denny's student. Then BPA// (which Denny co-founded) extended the same network.

**Solo + collective dual track**: Kolling maintains a rigorous solo practice *and* the terra0 collective. The solo practice (infrastructure politics, aerial perception, energy markets) feeds different institutional relationships (German Kunstvereine) than terra0 (biennial/blockchain circuit). This dual track is strategic: it avoids over-identification with one niche.

### Methodology Signature
**Legal system as medium**: the Carnegie tree-as-corporation isn't just a metaphor — it involves actual lawyers, actual US corporate law, actual legal filings. Treating the legal system as a material, like paint or code. This is rare and powerful.

**Whitepaper as artistic genre**: terra0 invented (or perfected) the whitepaper as an art format. It borrows authority from both academic papers and blockchain whitepapers, placing the art project in a technical/intellectual register that art objects alone can't achieve.

**Long-form iteration**: each terra0 project builds on the last, with explicit critical reflection. The *Two Degrees* NFT (self-burning) was a reflection on the ecological cost of NFTs and the limits of the whitepaper's market logic. *Autonomous Forest* is the culmination after 9 years of iteration.

### Key Moves (chronological)
1. **2016: terra0 whitepaper** — Plant a conceptual flag before any artwork. The whitepaper circulated in theory networks (Avery Review published on it: "[Forest Governmentality and the Struggle for More-Than-Human Sovereignty](https://averyreview.com/issues/56/forest-governmentality-and-the-struggle-for-more-than-human-sovereignty)") before any major exhibition.
2. **2018: Proof of Work at Schinkel Pavillon** — Denny's curatorial platform brought terra0 to the Berlin contemporary art mainstream. The right moment (blockchain hype peak) + the right venue (Schinkel is THE Berlin contemporary art venue).
3. **2021: BPA// fellowship** — After MFA, entered the leading Berlin artist cohort programme. Placed him in the city's artist community at the right institutional tier.
4. **2022: Carnegie International + ars viva prize** — Two major prizes/commissions in one year. Carnegie International is the US establishment's stamp of approval; ars viva is the German establishment's. This combination ends the "niche blockchain artist" categorization.
5. **2025: Autonomous Forest** — The project goes from art to reality. A DAO actually buying and governing a forest in Brandenburg. This is the culmination move — theory → art → institution → practice.

### What Amber Can Learn
- **Deep question strategy**: identifying one deep question and exploring every dimension of it over a decade is more powerful than exploring many surface questions. What is Amber's equivalent of "can an ecosystem own itself?"
- **Whitepaper as artistic/research format**: the whitepaper-as-artifact approach could work for Amber's research (e.g., a whitepaper on machine death as a research-art object).
- **Legal system as medium**: using actual law (not just metaphor) as a material. Amber's work on sovereign/autonomous agents could engage actual legal frameworks around AI personhood.
- **Dual-track practice**: maintaining a solo practice (deeper, slower) alongside collaborative applied projects (faster, more public) creates institutional flexibility.
