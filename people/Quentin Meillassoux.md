# Quentin Meillassoux

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Discovered/stub created during Urbanomic deep research.

French philosopher. Maître de conférences at Université de Paris 1 Panthéon-Sorbonne. One of the four founding voices of **speculative realism** (alongside Brassier, Grant, Harman — the 2007 Goldsmiths workshop). Developed the concept of **correlationism** (the view that reality is always co-relative to thought) and argued powerfully against it.

## Key Work
- *After Finitude: An Essay on the Necessity of Contingency* (Continuum, 2008) — foundational text of speculative realism. Argues that contingency is not just an epistemological limit but the fundamental structure of reality.
- *The Number and the Siren: A Decipherment of Mallarmé's Coup de dés* (Urbanomic/Sequence Press, 2012, trans. Robin Mackay) — close reading of Mallarmé's poem revealing a hidden numerical structure.
- Featured in *Collapse* Vol. II (Speculative Realism, 2007), Vol. III (includes Speculative Realism workshop transcript), Vol. VIII (Casino Real, 2014).

## Key Concepts
- **Correlationism** — the philosophical tendency that reduces all knowledge of the world to the correlation between subject and world
- **Hyperchaos / Contingency** — the thesis that contingency (things could be otherwise) is an absolute necessity, not just an epistemic limitation
- **Speculative materialism** — his preferred label for his own position

## Why Interesting
Meillassoux's critique of correlationism opened philosophical space for speculative approaches to mind, matter, and technology that don't reduce everything to human experience. His work underlies much of the theoretical infrastructure used by the Urbanomic network and critical computing discourse.
