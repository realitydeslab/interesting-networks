# Martin Warnke

Professor at [[ICAM Leuphana]], trained as mathematician/physicist, turned media theorist and digital humanist. Co-PI of the DFG MECS (Medienkulturen der Computersimulation) group. Organizer of the long-running **HyperKult** workshop on history and theory of digital media. Recently published on quantum computing and the history of computer simulation of living beings.

## Domains
- Computer simulation as media culture and epistemology
- History of digital computing and digital knowledge
- Quantum computing (theory and media studies angle)
- Digital image annotation (HyperImage, Meta-Image)
- Chinese social credit system / scoring cultures
- Artificial life history

## Key Works
- [*The Depths of Illusion: Knowing Reality Through Computer Simulation*](https://fis.leuphana.de/de/publications/d2c45f85-5dd8-446b-9c1b-d60f040e30e3) (with Anne Dippel, 2024, Berlin)
- [*Arrival of a Kitty: history and theory of the first computer simulation of a living being*](https://fis.leuphana.de/de/publications/e1abccd2-229c-4ae8-b036-13b4fac856f9) (ed., 2022, Berlin) — on Konstantinow's early cat simulation
- [*Sozialkybernetik in statu nascendi*](https://fis.leuphana.de/de/publications/fb5a0292-1ac7-40d8-b8ef-7a26b59ae4e6) (with Woesler, 2024) — social cybernetics and China's social credit system
- "Quantum Computing and the Analog/Digital Distinction" (with Schröter & Ernst, *Grey Room* 86, 2022)
