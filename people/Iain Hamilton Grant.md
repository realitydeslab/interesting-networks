# Iain Hamilton Grant

British philosopher. Key [[CCRU]] member and early speculative realist. Produced the first English translation of Jean-François Lyotard's *Libidinal Economy* as a Master's student at Warwick — a foundational text for CCRU accelerationist theory. Now teaches at University of the West of England, Bristol.

## Background
- MA student at Warwick in the early 1990s. Encountered Lyotard's *Libidinal Economy* — "I couldn't believe it! For a book by a Marxist to say, 'There's no way out of this', meaning capitalism... that's a first, as far as I know." Spent an obsessive six months producing the first English translation instead of writing his dissertation.
- [[Nick Land]] student. On Land: "Lots of people are clever, but I've never witnessed anyone who could so forensically destroy a thesis."
- After CCRU became extreme: "The later excesses drove several members into mental breakdown. I left before it descended into sheer madness."

## Key Works
- *Philosophies of Nature After Schelling* (Continuum, 2006) — Major contribution to speculative realist thinking
- Translation: Lyotard, *[Libidinal Economy](https://www.bloomsbury.com/uk/libidinal-economy-9781474281041/)* (1993, first English trans.) — foundational CCRU source text
- Contributed to *Collapse* journal and CCRU publications

## Key Communities
- [[CCRU]] — Early core member
- Speculative Realism network (with Ray Brassier, Quentin Meillassoux, Graham Harman)

## Key Relationships
- [[Nick Land]] — Teacher/mentor at Warwick CCRU
- [[Ray Brassier]] — Fellow speculative realist, CCRU contemporary

## Why Interesting
Bridge between CCRU and speculative realism. His translation of Lyotard is a genealogically important act — he materially enabled the CCRU's key theoretical source to reach English readers. His later Schelling-based naturalism represents a more rigorous post-CCRU trajectory than Land's.
