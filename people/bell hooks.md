# bell hooks

(Gloria Jean Watkins, pen name always lowercase) — one of the most influential feminist cultural theorists of the 20th century. Author of *Ain't I a Woman: Black Women and Feminism* (1981), *Feminist Theory: From Margin to Center* (1984), *Yearning: Race, Gender, and Cultural Politics* (1990), *Teaching to Transgress* (1994), and many others. Distinguished Professor at Berea College. Critical of both mainstream feminism (for ignoring race/class) and mainstream anti-racism (for ignoring gender/sexuality). 

Co-authored *Daughters of the Dust: The Making of an African American Woman's Film* (1992) with [[Julie Dash]] and [[Toni Cade Bambara]] — her presence signals the film's deep grounding in Black feminist critical theory.

- [[Julie Dash]] — co-authored the *Daughters of the Dust* making-of book
- [[Toni Cade Bambara]] — co-author, peer in Black feminist intellectual community
