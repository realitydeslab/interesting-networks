## Key Works

### Foundational Papers
- **[Generative Agents: Interactive Simulacra of Human Behavior](https://arxiv.org/abs/2304.03442)** (2023) — The breakthrough paper that launched the current wave of LLM agent simulation research. Introduced agents that "wake up, cook breakfast, and head to work; artists paint, while authors write; they form opinions, notice each other, and initiate conversations; they remember and reflect on days past as they plan the next day." Demonstrated emergent social behaviors like autonomous party planning and social coordination. **Best Paper Award at UIST and CHI**. Interactive demo available at [Reverie](https://reverie.herokuapp.com/arXiv_Demo/).

- **[Generative Agent Simulations of 1,000 People](https://arxiv.org/abs/2411.10109)** (2024) — Scaled up to population-scale simulations with 1,052 real individuals. Agents replicate participants' responses on the General Social Survey 85% as accurately as participants replicate their own answers two weeks later. Reduces accuracy biases across racial and ideological groups compared to demographic-based agents. Demonstrates validated behavioral replication at scale.

### Research Methods & Validation

- Pioneered approaches for testing agent architectures through ablation studies (observation, planning, reflection components)

## Research Taste Analysis

### Conceptual Strategy — Foundation Setting
**Two-word concept coining:** "Generative Agents" became the definitive term for an entire field, now cited thousands of times. Joon didn't just build a system — he named and defined a new research area.

**Seed paper strategy:** The initial Generative Agents paper laid out a complete conceptual map (architecture with memory/reflection/planning), then subsequent work fills in empirical validation and scaling. Classic approach of establishing the framework first, then proving it works.

### Methodology Signature — Validation at Scale  
**Scale play:** Moved from 25 agents (impressive demo) to 1,052 agents (validated science). The progression shows understanding that toy examples don't establish scientific credibility — you need population-scale validation.

**Cross-discipline poaching:** Borrowed from psychology (personality assessment), sociology (General Social Survey), and gaming (The Sims-style interaction) to create something entirely new in computer science.

**Ground truth validation:** Most AI demos are impressive but unverified. Joon's approach of validating agent responses against real human surveys (85% accuracy) transforms speculation into empirical science.

### Career Architecture — Strategic Positioning
**Field declaration:** Instead of incremental advances in existing areas, declared an entirely new field at the intersection of AI, HCI, and social science. This gives him definitional authority over the domain.

**Coalition papers:** Co-authorship with Percy Liang (foundation models leader) and Michael Bernstein (social computing expert) positions him at the center of the most important technical and social networks at Stanford.

**Platform building:** The Reverie demo and open research approach create infrastructure others can build upon, establishing him as the field's foundational figure.
