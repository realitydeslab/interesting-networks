# Dipesh Chakravarty

Historian at the University of Chicago. Founding member of the **Subaltern Studies Collective**. Author of *Provincializing Europe: Postcolonial Thought and Historical Difference* (Princeton, 2000) — one of the most cited works in postcolonial theory, arguing that European thought is both indispensable and inadequate for understanding non-Western histories.

Also known for work on climate and the Anthropocene: *The Climate of History in a Planetary Age* (Chicago, 2021) — argues that climate change requires thinking across the species/geological scale, not just the political/social.

**Raqs connection**: participated in the artist talk and discussion for [[Raqs Media Collective]]'s *Cavalcade* at the Neubauer Collegium, University of Chicago (May 8, 2025), alongside [[Prathama Banerjee]], moderated by [[Dieter Roelstraete]]. His presence at a Raqs AI film event signals deep intellectual kinship — both are working to expand the frame of political thought beyond Western modernity.

**Key concepts**: provincialization, the limits of historicism, multiple modernities, the Anthropocene as category, non-human history.
