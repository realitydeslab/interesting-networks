# Tom Rodden

FRS. Professor at University of Nottingham, formerly Chief Scientist at Microsoft Research Cambridge. Co-lead of the UK Equator Project (2000–2007) — the landmark multidisciplinary research programme exploring physical/digital boundaries that [[Yvonne Rogers]] participated in. CSCW, ubiquitous computing, domestic technology, the "internet of things" before the phrase existed. Also FRS — he and Rogers are both Fellows, one of the few computer scientist-FRS pairs who also collaborate.

## Key Works
- UK Equator Project (PI, 2000–2007)
- "Human-Computer Interaction in the Year 2020" (with Harper, Rogers, Sellen, Microsoft Research, 2008)
- Foundational CSCW papers on shared workspaces, physical/digital boundaries, domestic computing

## Why Interesting
The Nottingham Mixed Reality Lab (Steve Benford) + Rodden is the other major UK "in the wild" HCI programme alongside UCLIC. Their work prefigured the smart home, IoT, and ambient computing movements by a decade. Rodden's elevation to FRS (alongside Rogers) marks HCI as a field the scientific establishment now takes seriously.
