# Thomas Lamarre

Media theorist specializing in Japanese animation (anime), manga, and media technology. Major figure bridging Japanese studies, media theory, and Deleuzian/Simondonian philosophy. Author of *The Anime Machine* (2009, Minnesota UP) — the definitive theoretical account of anime as a media technology. Published "Humans and Machines" in *Inflexions* No. 5 (Gilbert Simondon issue, 2012) — a key text connecting Simondon's individuation theory to media technology.

## Key Works
- [*The Anime Machine: A Media Theory of Animation*](https://www.upress.umn.edu/book-division/books/the-anime-machine) (2009, Minnesota UP) — anime as technical-philosophical object
- "Humans and Machines" — *Inflexions* No. 5 (2012) — Simondon + media technology
- [*The Anime Ecology*](https://www.upress.umn.edu/book-division/books/the-anime-ecology) (2018, Minnesota UP) — follow-up, ecology framing

## Domains
- Japanese media theory
- Simondon + technical objects + individuation
- Animation and moving image theory
- Media ecology

## Why Interesting
The Japan-to-Western-theory pipeline he embodies is rare. His Simondon work provides conceptual tools for thinking about AI/machine agency (individuation, technical objects). The anime ecology framing has potential connections to ALife and computational creativity thinking.
