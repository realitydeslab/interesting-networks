# Daniel Birnbaum

Curator and art critic who made the unexpected leap from directing the [53rd Venice Biennale](https://www.labiennale.org/) (2009) and [Moderna Museet](https://www.modernamuseet.se/stockholm/en/) Stockholm to becoming Creative Director of [[Acute Art]], the VR/AR art production company. His presence gives Acute Art credibility in the contemporary art world that no amount of technology can buy.

## Domains
Contemporary art curation, VR/AR art, institutional leadership, philosophy of time (Husserl/phenomenology).

## Background
Long career as curator and art critic. Wrote on phenomenology and Husserl. Directed Moderna Museet (Sweden's national museum of modern art) for over a decade. Directed the 53rd Venice Biennale (2009) — "Fare Mondi / Making Worlds." Joined Acute Art in January 2019, signaling that top curators see VR/AR as a serious art medium.

## Key Works
- **53rd Venice Biennale** (2009) — "Fare Mondi / Making Worlds" — explored contemporary artistic practice as world-making
- **The Looking Glass** (2021) — curated major free AR exhibition at The Shed + High Line, NYC
- Multiple Acute Art exhibitions globally

## Why Interesting
His move to Acute Art is the strongest possible signal that VR/AR is being taken seriously by art world insiders — not just tech enthusiasts. The institutional legitimacy he brings is the reason Abramović, Eliasson, Kapoor trust Acute Art with their first immersive works. Understanding Birnbaum's curatorial logic (world-making, phenomenology) explains Acute Art's approach.
