# Andres Karjus

Computational linguist and Senior Research Fellow at [[CUDAN Open Lab]]. Studies language and culture change at the intersection of corpus analysis, computational simulation, and human experiments. Recently pivoting toward LLMs in humanities research. Technically the most versatile member of CUDAN.

## Background
PhD from the Centre for Language Evolution, University of Edinburgh — lexical dynamics. Computational approaches to language change: how and why languages evolve, using text corpora, ML, and human experiments. Now at CUDAN as Senior Research Fellow, extending methods from language to broader cultural dynamics.

## Key Works
- ["Compression ensembles quantify aesthetic complexity and the evolution of visual art"](https://doi.org/10.1140/epjds/s13688-023-00397-3) — Karjus, Canet Solà, Ohm, Ahnert, Schich (2023, *EPJ Data Science*). Uses image compression algorithms as a proxy for aesthetic complexity; maps trajectories of art movements.
- ["Large language models to supercharge humanities and cultural analytics research"](https://cudan.tlu.ee/conference/CUDAN2023-abstract-233.pdf) — Karjus (CUDAN 2023 talk). Forward-looking: LLMs as research infrastructure for humanities.
- ["Evolving linguistic divergence on polarizing social media"](https://doi.org/10.1057/s41599-024-02922-9) — Karjus & Cuskley (2024, *HSSCO*). Language change as signal of social fragmentation.
- ["Quantifying the global film festival circuit"](https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pone.0297404) — Zemaityte, Karjus et al. (2024, *PLOS ONE*). Network analysis of cultural value creation in global film.
- ["Reliable detection and quantification of selective forces in language change"](https://doi.org/10.1515/cllt-2023-0064) — Guerrero Montero, Karjus et al. (2023, *Corpus Linguistics*).

## Why Interesting
Karjus is doing what most humanities-facing computation researchers talk about but rarely achieve: methodological import from physics and information theory (compression ensembles) that yields genuinely new cultural insight rather than dressed-up metrics. His LLMs-in-humanities work is pragmatic and forward-looking. For Amber: his work on linguistic divergence on polarizing social media is directly relevant to machine behavior research; his compression ensemble method could be adapted to analyze AI-generated image datasets.
