# Andres Karjus

Computational linguist / cultural data analyst. Senior Research Fellow at CUDAN Open Lab (2020-2024), now Research Fellow at School of Humanities, Tallinn University. Works at the intersection of language evolution, cultural dynamics, aesthetic complexity measurement, and film industry networks.

## Key Works
- ["Compression ensembles quantify aesthetic complexity and the evolution of visual art"](https://doi.org/10.1140/epjds/s13688-023-00397-3) (*EPJ Data Science* 2023) — novel method using compression algorithms to measure aesthetic complexity in art history
- ["Evolving linguistic divergence on polarizing social media"](https://doi.org/10.1057/s41599-024-02922-9) (*HASS Comms* 2024)
- ["Quantifying the global film festival circuit"](https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pone.0297404) (*PLOS ONE* 2024, with Zemaityte et al.)
- ["Machine-assisted mixed methods"](https://doi.org/10.48550/arXiv.2309.14379) (arXiv 2023) — methodological manifesto
- ["Conceptual Similarity and Communicative Need Shape Colexification"](https://doi.org/10.1111/cogs.13035) (*Cognitive Science* 2021)

## Why Interesting
Rare figure who does rigorous quantitative work on cultural/linguistic dynamics while maintaining critical awareness. The compression ensemble method for aesthetic complexity is genuinely novel — applying algorithmic information theory to art history.
