# Toni Cade Bambara

African-American writer, social activist, and filmmaker. Key figure in Black feminist thought. Author of *The Salt Eaters* (1980) and the short story collections *Gorilla, My Love* (1972) and *The Sea Birds Are Still Alive* (1977). Co-wrote the making-of book for *Daughters of the Dust* (1992) with [[Julie Dash]] and [[bell hooks]]. Also worked in documentary film later in life. One of the literary figures (alongside Toni Morrison and Alice Walker) who turned Dash toward narrative filmmaking.

## Key Connections
- [[Julie Dash]] — co-authored *Daughters of the Dust: The Making of an African American Woman's Film* (1992)
- [[bell hooks]] — co-author on same project
- Toni Morrison — close peer in Black feminist literary circles
