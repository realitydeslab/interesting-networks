# James Lovelock

Chemist and independent scientist who formulated the Gaia hypothesis — the idea that Earth's biosphere acts as a self-regulating system to maintain conditions for life. Inventor of the electron capture detector (used to detect CFCs in the atmosphere). Worked with Lynn Margulis from the early 1970s to develop the scientific basis of Gaia. Died July 26, 2022 at age 103.

## Why Interesting
Co-developer of Gaia hypothesis with Lynn Margulis. The Gaia hypothesis is foundational to ecological systems thinking, Earth systems science, and more-than-human environmental philosophy. Lovelock was the chemist; Margulis provided the microbial biology mechanism. His work inspired deep ecology and continues to be debated in climate/Earth systems science.
