# Etienne Turpin

Research director, artist, and architect. Based between Indonesia and Berlin. Founder of [ANNA (Art, Nature, Natures, Architectures)](https://www.annamiasma.com/) — a research office for long-term projects at the intersection of architecture, art, and ecological systems. His work focuses on **urban ecologies, Anthropocene architectures, and the politics of environmental visualization**.

Co-edited two major Open Humanities Press volumes in the Critical Climate Change series:
- [*Architecture in the Anthropocene: Encounters Among Design, Deep Time, Science and Philosophy*](https://openhumanitiespress.org/books/titles/architecture-in-the-anthropocene/) (OHP, 2013) — **FREE OA** — edited by Turpin; brings architecture into dialogue with stratigraphy, deep time, ecological philosophy
- [*Art in the Anthropocene*](https://openhumanitiespress.org/books/titles/art-in-the-anthropocene/) (OHP, 2015) — **FREE OA** — co-edited with Heather Davis; essays by Anna Tsing, Timothy Morton, Joanna Zylinska, many others

Also edited *Art and the City* and worked extensively in Jakarta, Indonesia on urban environmental projects — his work bridges theory with on-the-ground environmental action in cities of the Global South.

## Why Interesting

*Architecture in the Anthropocene* is one of the most interesting edited volumes for thinking design beyond anthropocentrism — specifically from within the architecture/design tradition. Contributors include architects, philosophers, and scientists asking how the architectural imagination must change when dealing with deep time, mass extinction, and geological scale. For Amber: this is the architecture version of what she's doing in more-than-human design. The methodology (how do you design *for* a 100,000-year timescale?) is transferable to computing/interface design.

The ANNA research office model — running long-term speculative projects at the intersection of design, art, and ecology — is also a potential model for Amber's own practice structure.
