# Ulises A. Mejias

Professor of Communication Studies at SUNY Oswego. Co-founder of the [Non-Aligned Technologies Movement (NATM)](https://nonalignedtech.net/) and the [Tierra Común](https://tierracomun.net) network. Recipient of the SUNY Chancellor's Award for Excellence in Scholarship.

Key theoretical contribution: **Data Colonialism** — the argument that Big Data represents a new colonial frontier, this time extracting human life and experience rather than land and natural resources. Co-developed with Nick Couldry (LSE).

## Key Publications
- **"Data Colonialism: Rethinking Big Data's Relation to the Contemporary Subject"** (Television & New Media, 2018) — ~50,000 views, ~1,670 citations. The foundational paper.
- **"The Decolonial Turn in Data and Technology Research"** (Information, Communication & Society, 2021) — ~27,000 views
- **_A Billion Black Anthropocenes or None_** — book-length development of data colonialism framework (with Couldry)
- **"The Museum as a Cultural Response to Data Colonialism"** — chapter in "Museo digital. Inteligencias y artificios" (UNAM/MUAC + Fundación Telefónica, 2024)

## Key Projects
- **Non-Aligned Technologies Movement (NATM)** — coalition for tech autonomy from US/China Big Tech duopoly. Inspired by Cold War Non-Aligned Movement. Ulises leads the Civil Society working group.
- **Tierra Común** — Latin American activist/researcher network for data decolonization. Based in CDMX ecosystem with collectives like Rancho Electrónico.
