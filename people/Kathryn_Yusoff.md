# Kathryn Yusoff

Professor of Inhuman Geography at Queen Mary University of London. Author of *A Billion Black Anthropocenes or None* (2018) — the foundational text connecting the Anthropocene to slavery and colonialism. Co-curator of the British Pavilion, Venice Architecture Biennale 2025 alongside [[Cave_bureau]] and Owen Hopkins.

## Why Interesting
Yusoff's "inhuman geography" and her argument that the Anthropocene begins with slavery (not the steam engine) is essential theoretical framing for more-than-human and decolonial design work. Her book *A Billion Black Anthropocenes or None* is cited constantly in critical climate discourse. Based in London — proximate to Amber. Queen Mary is in East London.

## Key Works
- [*A Billion Black Anthropocenes or None*](https://www.upress.umn.edu/book-division/books/a-billion-black-anthropocenes-or-none) (University of Minnesota Press, 2018) — argues that the Anthropocene is not a new epoch but a continuation of colonial extraction that began with slavery. Essential.
