# Max Mathews

Father of computer music. Wrote MUSIC (1957) at Bell Labs — the first widely used program for computer sound generation. Founded the entire field of computational creativity in music. Later at Stanford CCRMA. His work directly precedes ALife-based generative music and computational creativity as a research field.

## Why Interesting
Mathews showed that algorithmic/computational systems could be serious vehicles for musical creativity — inside an industrial research lab, no less. This is a direct predecessor to Amber's interests in ALife, generative systems, and computational creativity. MUSIC became the ancestor of SuperCollider, Max/MSP, and all modern computer music platforms.
