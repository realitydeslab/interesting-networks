# Alexander G. Weheliye

Professor of African American Studies at Northwestern University. Member of [[Practicing Refusal Collective]]. Author of *Habeas Viscus* — a pivotal critique that shows how Foucault and Agamben's biopolitics theories systematically exclude Black and racialized lives, and proposes Black feminist frameworks (Hortense Spillers, Sylvia Wynter) as more adequate theories of the human.

## Key Works
- *[Habeas Viscus: Racializing Assemblages, Biopolitics, and Black Feminist Theories of the Human](https://www.dukeupress.edu/habeas-viscus)* (Duke UP, 2014) — argues that Foucault's biopolitics and Agamben's bare life are incomplete because they ignore racial formation; proposes "racializing assemblages" (after Hortense Spillers and Sylvia Wynter) as a corrective
- *[Phonographies: Grooves in Sonic Afro-Modernity](https://www.dukeupress.edu/phonographies)* (Duke UP, 2005) — Black sonic cultures and technologies of modernity

## Why Interesting
*Habeas Viscus* is essential reading for anyone working on AI and biopolitics, because it shows exactly where standard biopolitical frameworks (Foucault/Agamben) break down when race enters. This is directly relevant to Amber's thinking about AI governance, agent sovereignty, and who is included in theories of "the human" in AI systems.
