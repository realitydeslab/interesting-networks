# Lucia Pietroiusti

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-27:** Stub created during General Ecology deep research. Lucia left Serpentine (~2024–2025), now independent.
- **2024:** Head of Ecologies for Serpentine's Infinite Ecologies Marathon (last major event under her leadership, July–Sept 2024). No longer on Serpentine team page as of early 2026.

Italian-born curator and ecological thinker. Founded [[General Ecology]] at Serpentine Galleries in 2018 — the most rigorous ecology programme in the UK art world. Ran it as Curator (2018–2023), then Head of Ecologies (2023–~2025). Creator of *The Shape of a Circle in the Mind of a Fish* symposia series and major force behind Back to Earth and the Infinite Ecologies Marathon. Left Serpentine and is now independent — likely developing next project.

## Background
Founded General Ecology at Serpentine in 2018. Prior role: curator of public programmes at Serpentine (including co-curating the Extinction Marathon with Gustav Metzger in 2014). Her work consistently targets the institutional — not just making ecological art, but restructuring how art institutions think about their purpose and operations in an ecological crisis.

## Key Works / Programmes
- **[[General Ecology]]** (2018–2023) — Founded and led. Serpentine's cross-institutional ecology programme.
- **The Shape of a Circle in the Mind of a Fish** (2018–2022) — 5-edition interspecies symposium series, co-curated with [[Filipa Ramos]]. Editions: Language (ZSL, 2018), We Have Never Been One (Ambika P3, 2018), PLANTSEX and With Plants (EartH Hackney, 2019), The Understory of the Understory (online/themind.fish, 2020), The Shape of a Circle in the Dream of a Fish (Porto, 2022).
- **Back to Earth** (2020–2022) — Co-curated and produced Serpentine's multi-year climate emergency programme with 100+ artists.
- **Infinite Ecologies Marathon** (2023–2024) — Head of Ecologies. Prelude (Oct 2023), Marathon (July 2024), Seeds (Sept 2024).
- **Extinction Marathon** (2014) — Co-curated with Gustav Metzger — precursor programme.
