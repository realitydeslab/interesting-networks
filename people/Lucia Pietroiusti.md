# Lucia Pietroiusti

Italian curator, Head of Ecologies (former) at **[[Serpentine Galleries]]**. Founded and led the [General Ecology](https://www.serpentinegalleries.org/ecologies/) project from 2018 to 2024 — Serpentine's flagship research programme exploring ecological thinking, more-than-human consciousness, and artistic responses to the climate emergency. Creator of the "The Shape of a Circle in the Mind of a Fish" symposium series (5 editions, 2018–2022) with [[Filipa Ramos]] — a landmark interspecies consciousness inquiry bringing together ecologists, computer scientists, anthropologists, musicians and artists. Co-curated the **13th Shanghai Biennale "Wetware"** (2021) with Andrés Jaque and Marina Otero Verzier at [[Power Station of Art]]. Left Serpentine after the Infinite Ecologies Marathon (2024). One of the most consequential ecology-art curators of her generation — her work *institutionalized* ecological thinking at a major public gallery and turned it into a long-term research programme.

## Key Works & Projects

### General Ecology (2018–2024)
[Serpentine General Ecology](https://www.serpentinegalleries.org/ecologies/) — founded by Lucia Pietroiusti. Multi-year programme exploring ecological thinking across art, science, politics, and philosophy. Spawned the Fish series, Back to Earth, the Extinction Marathon legacy, and ultimately the Ecologies department. Arguably the most sustained institutional experiment in ecology-at-an-art-institution in the UK.

### The Shape of a Circle in the Mind of a Fish (2018–2022)
[Serpentine archive](https://www.serpentinegalleries.org/) — co-curated by Lucia Pietroiusti and [[Filipa Ramos]]. Multi-year interdisciplinary symposium and research project on interspecies consciousness. Five editions:

1. **Language** — [ZSL London Zoological Society](https://www.zsl.org/), May 2018. Animal and artificial consciousness, language and interspecies communication. Participants included computer scientist Andrew Adamatzky, internet pioneer Vint Cerf, writer Ted Chiang.
2. **We Have Never Been One** — [Ambika P3](https://www.ambikap3.com/), University of Westminster, December 2018. Interior multitude, swarming organisms, symbiosis, entanglements. Participants included anthropologist Anna L. Tsing.
3. **PLANTSEX / With Plants** — [Cinéma Lumière](https://www.institut-francais.org.uk/) (Institut français du Royaume-Uni) and EartH Hackney, April/May 2019. Plant sentience, communication, eroticism, mysticism. In partnership with Institut français du Royaume-Uni and Fluxus Art Projects.
4. **The Understory of the Understory** — Online at [themind.fish](https://themind.fish/) (purpose-built website designed by Giles Round), December 2020. Ground, land, soil, Earth teeming life. Participants included musician Peter Gabriel, sound recordist Chris Watson, artists Karrabing Film Collective, Kapwani Kiwanga, Tabita Rezaire; theologians Amy Hollywood, Simone Kotva; philosopher Michael Marder.
5. **The Shape of a Circle in the Dream of a Fish** — [Galeria Municipal do Porto](https://www.cm-porto.pt/gmp), November 2022. Dream life across species boundaries; animal/plant consciousness; shamanic and mystical encounters. Participants: Yussef Agbo-Ola (Olaniyi Studio), Federico Campagna, Nicola S. Clayton, Onome Ekeh, Cru Encarnação, Alex Jordan, Sophie Lunn-Rockliffe, Nahum Mantra, Hatis Noit, Giles Round, Rain Wu.

### PLANTSEX (April 2019)
[Serpentine archive](https://www.serpentinegalleries.org/whats-on/plantsex/) — co-curated with Filipa Ramos and Kostas Stasinopoulos. Evening of talks, screenings, performances at Cinéma Lumière on botany and eroticism. Accompanied by PLANTSEX special issue of [MAL Journal](https://maljournal.com/), co-edited by Maria Dimitrova, Lucia Pietroiusti, Filipa Ramos, and Kostas Stasinopoulos. Participants: Chloe Aridjis, Alex Cecchetti, Emanuele Coccia, Jenna Sutela, Laurence Totelin; films by Melanie Bonajo, Dineo Seshee Bopape, Victoria Sin.

### Extinction Marathon (2014)
[Serpentine archive](https://www.serpentinegalleries.org/whats-on/extinction-marathon/) — co-curated with Hans Ulrich Obrist, Gustav Metzger, Jochen Volz, Ben Vickers, Claude Adjil. Precursor to General Ecology. Foundational event establishing Serpentine's ecology programme.

### Back to Earth (2020–2022)
[Serpentine](https://www.serpentinegalleries.org/whats-on/back-to-earth/) — climate emergency art project. Exhibition (June–September 2022) and long-running programme. Curated and produced by Rebecca Lewin, Hans Ulrich Obrist, Jo Paton, Lucia Pietroiusti, Holly Shuttleworth, and Kostas Stasinopoulos. Included work by Agnes Denes, Alexandra Daisy Ginsberg, Brian Eno, Carolina Caycedo, Cooking Sections, Dineo Seshee Bopape, Formafantasma, Sissel Tolaas, Superflux, Tabita Rezaire.

### 140 Artists' Ideas for Planet Earth (2022)
[Penguin Press](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/) — part of the Back to Earth project. Co-produced with 140 artists, scientists, architects, filmmakers. Edited by Hans Ulrich Obrist and Kostas Stasinopoulos.

### 13th Shanghai Biennale "Wetware" (2021)
[Power Station of Art](https://www.powerstationofart.com/) — co-curated with Andrés Jaque and Marina Otero Verzier. Brought the Serpentine's ecology discourse into PSA. A key moment connecting Western ecology-art discourse with China's contemporary art scene.

### Infinite Ecologies Marathon: The Prelude (October 2023)
[Serpentine](https://www.serpentinegalleries.org/whats-on/infinite-ecologies-marathon-the-prelude/) — curated by Hans Ulrich Obrist, Lucia Pietroiusti (Head of Ecologies), and Kostas Stasinopoulos. Participants included Marina Abramović, Brian Eno, James Bridle, Cooking Sections, Mariana Mazzucato, Tomás Saraceno, Nikolaj Schultz, Yinka Shonibare, Adrián Villar Rojas, Johannes Vogel.

### Infinite Ecologies Marathon (July–September 2024)
[Serpentine](https://www.serpentinegalleries.org/whats-on/infinite-ecologies-marathon/) — curated by Hans Ulrich Obrist, Lucia Pietroiusti (Head of Ecologies), Kostas Stasinopoulos, Daisy Gould, Isobel Peyton-Jones. Her final major project at Serpentine. Included "A Gathering on Art and Land" (July 13, 2024) with Marcus Coates, Sandra Knecht, Annalee Davis, Caroline A. Jones, Yinka Shonibare, G.A.S. Foundation, Sky Hopinka. Programme examined soil, farming, gardening, burial as ecological practices.

## Research Taste Analysis

### Conceptual Strategy
**Long series over one-off** — Lucia builds conceptual momentum through repeat editions, each one deepening and complicating the previous. The Fish series doesn't deliver a conclusion — it expands the question. Each edition added a new dimension: Language → multiplicity → plants/eroticism → soil → dreams. The series *grows* the question rather than answering it.

**Discipline-crossing as method** — She consistently assembles rooms where the disciplines are maximally different: a sound recordist (Chris Watson), an internet pioneer (Vint Cerf), a sci-fi writer (Ted Chiang), a cognition researcher — in the same room about animal language. The productive friction between their frameworks IS the research.

**Naming as infrastructure** — "General Ecology" is a brilliant title: it's expansive enough to include climate, biology, anthropology, philosophy and art, while being specific enough to have a clear centre of gravity. "The Shape of a Circle in the Mind of a Fish" is an unanswerable question posed as a title — it invites the enquiry without closing it.

### Career Architecture
**Programme building over shows** — Rather than curating individual exhibitions, she built a *programme* (General Ecology) that lasted a decade and generated multiple sub-projects. This is infrastructure building, not curation-as-taste-making.

**Scientific partnerships as legitimation** — By hosting events at ZSL (London Zoo) and partnering with Cambridge cognition researchers, she anchored the more-than-human discourse in genuine science. This made it harder to dismiss as art-world navel-gazing.

**Title evolution as power-building** — Curator → Founder, General Ecology → Strategic Consultant → Head of Ecologies. Each title gave her more structural power within Serpentine while also signalling a different relationship to the work (from producer to architect).

### Methodology Signature
**Community accumulation** — Each Fish edition built a slightly different community but with significant overlap. Over 5 editions, she built a stable network of ecology-art thinkers who trust each other and speak a common language.

**Infrastructure for encounter** — Themind.fish (purpose-built website for online edition), the physical venue choices (zoo, university, arts cinema), the accompanying journal issues — she thinks about the container of the encounter, not just the content.

**Publication as extension** — The MAL Journal PLANTSEX issue is a clever move: it extends the event into a lasting artefact while also pulling in an existing publication community (the journal's readership) into the ecology-art sphere.

### Network Strategy
**Small team, long-term** — Her core team (Filipa Ramos, Kostas Stasinopoulos, Holly Shuttleworth) worked together across multiple projects over years. Trust and shared vocabulary are the result.

**Global south/east bridging** — The Shanghai Biennale co-curation, and the presence of artists like Karrabing Film Collective, Rain Wu, and Cru Encarnação in the Fish series, shows she consistently works to decenter Western ecology discourse.

### Key Moves
1. **2014: Extinction Marathon** — Positioned herself at the Serpentine/HUO/Metzger intersection at the exact moment climate was becoming a serious art-world topic. Established ecology credentials early.
2. **2018: Founded General Ecology** — Rather than just curating shows, she declared a programme and gave it a name. This was a territorial claim on "ecology as curatorial methodology" at a major institution.
3. **2018–2022: Fish series over 5 editions** — Refused to reduce the series to a single event. Patience and sustained inquiry built a genuine research community, not just an event audience.
4. **2019: PLANTSEX + MAL Journal** — Extended ecology into sexuality and eroticism (unexpected territory). This kept the programme from becoming predictable; it showed ecology could be weird and provocative, not just earnest.
5. **2021: 13th Shanghai Biennale** — Took the General Ecology frame to a mega-institutional context (PSA) in China. International positioning; showed the framework travels.
6. **2023: Head of Ecologies** — The creation of a formal Ecologies department at Serpentine was the institutional legacy of her decade of work. Her title changed from "Founder of a project" to "Head of a department."
7. **2024: Departure** — After the Infinite Ecologies Marathon, she left. This suggests she had completed what she set out to do institutionally and is moving to the next phase. Worth watching.

### What Amber Can Learn
- **The series model** — Run the same question across 5 editions rather than 5 different topics. Community accumulates; the question deepens.
- **Science partnerships legitimate radical ideas** — Partnering with ZSL, Cambridge cognition researchers, and computer scientists makes the interspecies consciousness work harder to dismiss. If Amber's work involves wild claims, find scientific collaborators.
- **Name your programme** — "General Ecology" is a programme name, not a project title. Declaring a programme gives you years of coherent activity to build.
- **Infrastructure of encounter matters** — Don't just design the content; design the container. Themind.fish vs. a Zoom call produces different kinds of thought.
- **Exit before you become the institution** — Lucia left while Serpentine's Ecologies department was at peak legitimacy, not after it calcified. Timing departures for maximum leverage is a career move worth studying.
