# Takashi Ikegami

Professor at the University of Tokyo. One of Japan's leading ALife researchers and a pioneer of ALife art. Art keynote panelist at ALIFE 2025.

## Domains
Artificial life, self-organization, consciousness, art-science, emergent behavior, virtual ecosystems.

## Background
Long career at the University of Tokyo studying artificial life from both scientific and artistic perspectives. Advisor to ALIFE 2025 (Kyoto). Part of the Japanese ALife tradition that includes connections to robotics, interactive art, and complex systems.

## Key Works
- Research on self-organization, emergence, and the boundary between life and non-life
- ALife art installations — exploring artificial life as aesthetic experience
- Advisory role at ALIFE 2025, art keynote panelist
- Mentor to the Japanese ALife community

## Why Interesting
Rare figure who bridges ALife science and art practice in Japan. His work treats artificial life as an aesthetic and philosophical project, not just a technical one. The art keynote at ALIFE 2025 signals this integration. For Amber (who values art-science bridges), Ikegami represents a model for how to work at the intersection of ALife and creative practice.
