Professor at University of Tokyo's Graduate School of Arts and Sciences. Pioneer of artificial life research with deep integration into art practice. Creates humanoid robots that bridge AI consciousness research and artistic performance. Leads Japan's ALife art movement through 20+ years of interdisciplinary work spanning cellular automata, neural networks, LLM agents, and robotic embodiment.
