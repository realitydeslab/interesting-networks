# Philip Tinari

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Stub created during Chinese Art-Tech Scene deep research.

Director and CEO of [[UCCA Center for Contemporary Art]], the premier independent contemporary art institution in mainland China. American-born art historian, critic, and curator based in Beijing. Has been the key figure in connecting Chinese contemporary art to global institutional circuits.

## Background
American. Based in Beijing since early 2000s. Director/CEO of UCCA. Co-led UCCA's transformation from Belgian-funded institution to Chinese/international patron-owned nonprofit (2017). Art historian and critic; writes for ArtForum, Art in America, and other publications. Also a translator of Chinese literary and critical texts.

Fluent Mandarin speaker. Among the small group of Western figures who have genuinely embedded in the Chinese art world over decades rather than passing through.

## Key Projects
- **UCCA Director** — since 2012 (jointly with May Xue until her departure 2017)
- **Co-curator: "Art and China after 1989: Theater of the World"** — Guggenheim Museum, New York (2017); with Alexandra Munroe and Hou Hanru. Most comprehensive institutional survey of Chinese art in the United States. Also toured to Guggenheim Bilbao and SFMOMA.
- **Diriyah Contemporary Art Biennale** — UCCA collaboration (2022)
- **"Bentu: Chinese Artists in a Time of Turbulence and Transformation"** — Fondation Louis Vuitton, Paris (2016)

## Why Interesting
Rare figure who has genuinely bridged American art criticism and Chinese contemporary art over multiple decades. The Guggenheim "Art and China after 1989" co-curatorship is the most significant Western institutional engagement with Chinese art history in a generation.
