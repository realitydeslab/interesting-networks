# Lee Cronin

Regius Professor of Chemistry at the University of Glasgow. Building chemical computers and chemputers. Co-developed assembly theory with [[Sara Imari Walker]].

## Domains
Chemical computation, chemputers (robot chemists), assembly theory, origin of life, inorganic biology.

## Key Works
- **Assembly theory** — a framework for measuring complexity and detecting life signatures (with [[Sara Imari Walker]])
- [Assembly theory paper in Nature](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-023-06600-9) (2023)
- **Chemputer** — a programmable chemical robot that can synthesize molecules from code
- Research on inorganic life — can non-carbon chemistry produce life-like systems?

## Key Communities
- University of Glasgow Cronin Group
- [[Santa Fe Institute]] — connected
- Origin of life research community

## Key Relationships
- [[Sara Imari Walker]] — assembly theory collaborator
- Origin of life research community

## Why Interesting
Assembly theory is a genuinely new way to think about life — measuring the complexity of objects to determine if they were produced by evolution. His chemputer work is "wet ALife" at its most ambitious: programming chemistry to create new molecules. Controversial but generative.
