# Koyo Kouoh (1967–2025)

Cameroonian-Swiss curator; Executive Director and Chief Curator of Zeitz MOCAA Cape Town (from 2019). Appointed 2026 Venice Biennale Artistic Director; died May 10, 2025. The 61st Biennale "In Minor Keys" proceeds posthumously per her vision.

## Background
Born Douala, Cameroon; moved to Zurich age 13. Studied business administration + banking in Zurich; cultural management in France. Fluent in French, German, English, Italian. Moved to Dakar 1995 after encountering its art scene. Founded RAW Material Company (artist residency + exhibition space, Dakar). Named one of Africa's pre-eminent art curators by NYT (2015). ArtReview Power 100 for 8 consecutive years (2014–2022). Appointed Zeitz MOCAA director 2019 — positioned it "at the cutting edge of contemporary art by championing Pan-Africanism."

## Key Works & Contributions
- Founded [RAW Material Company](https://rawmaterialcompany.org/), Dakar — artist residency + exhibition space
- Led Zeitz MOCAA Cape Town from 2019
- Co-edited *Töchter Afrikas* (1994) — German-language companion to Margaret Busby's *Daughters of Africa*
- Appointed 2026 Venice Biennale Artistic Director — "In Minor Keys"

## Awards
- Swiss Grand Award for Art
- Prix Meret Oppenheim

## Why Interesting
One of the most important African curators of the 21st century. Her approach to Pan-Africanism and diaspora art connects to the broader decolonial turn in contemporary curating. "In Minor Keys" as a curatorial thesis — foregrounding subalternity — is directly adjacent to Amber's interest in non-Western perspectives and cosmotechnics.
