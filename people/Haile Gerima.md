# Haile Gerima

Ethiopian filmmaker and professor. One of the founding figures of the L.A. Rebellion — the movement of UCLA-trained filmmakers in the 1960s–70s who created a politically radical, formally innovative Black cinema outside of Hollywood. Professor of Film at [Howard University](https://howard.edu/) in Washington D.C. since the 1970s. Primary mentor of [[Arthur Jafa]].

## Key Works
- *Harvest: 3000 Years* (1976) — Ethiopian village life under feudalism. Made in Ethiopia.
- *Bush Mama* (1975) — LA Watts, Black woman's survival
- [*Sankofa*](https://sankofa.com/) (1993) — Self-distributed film about the Middle Passage and slavery. Gerima toured it personally across HBCUs and Black community venues after failing to get distribution. Commercially successful through self-distribution. Founded Mypheduh Films and Sankofa bookstore/video store in DC as infrastructure for Black cinema distribution.
- *Teza* (2008) — Ethiopian diaspora intellectual returning home. Won 2 prizes at Venice Film Festival.

## Why Interesting
Gerima represents the intellectual and institutional backbone of a specifically Black film practice that operates entirely outside Hollywood. His pedagogy at Howard University produced multiple generations of Black filmmakers who think of cinema as a political and cultural tool, not just entertainment. His self-distribution model for *Sankofa* is a case study in artist-controlled cultural infrastructure. His mentorship of [[Arthur Jafa]] is the origin of Jafa's conceptual framework.
