# Kyle McDonald

Artist working with code. Crafts interactive installations, sneaky interventions, playful websites, workshops, and open-source toolkits for other artists. Uses machine learning, computer vision, social and surveillance technology to understand how technologies affect society — and to misuse them, build alternative futures, spark curiosity, and share spaces with "magical vibes." One of the key nodes in the creative coding / openFrameworks world.

## Background
- Grew up in San Diego; high school at [High Tech High](https://www.hightechhigh.org/) (2000–2003) — a progressive project-based school. Early formation.
- **BS Philosophy & Computer Science** at [Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute](https://www.rpi.edu/) (2003–2007). Unusual dual focus — the philosophical bent shows up throughout his work.
- **MFA Electronic Arts** at Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute (2007–2008) — experimental electronic arts programme, same institution. 
- During undergrad: **Human Level Intelligence Lab** (2004–2006) working on emergence of conversational pragmatics with Nick Cassimatis. **Evolutionary Psychology and Culture Lab** (2008–2010) with Mark Changizi. Research roots.
- **Adjunct professor at NYU ITP** (2011–2016) — teaching computer vision, glitch, Appropriating Interaction Technologies (with Lauren McCarthy). ITP is *the* art-tech graduate school in the US.
- **Community manager for [openFrameworks](https://openframeworks.cc/)** — the C++ creative coding toolkit. This is a defining identity: he didn't just use the tool, he *built the community*. His GitHub repos include `ofxFaceTracker` (1.4k stars), `ofxCv` (666 stars), `FaceTracker` (1k stars).
- **Artist in residence** at [STUDIO for Creative Inquiry](https://studioforcreativeinquiry.org/), Carnegie Mellon University — Golan Levin's lab.
- **Artist in residence** at [YCAM (Yamaguchi Center for Arts and Media)](https://www.ycam.jp/), Japan — twice (2011 RAM Dance Toolkit; 2012 Shadowplay).
- **Member of [F.A.T. Lab](https://fffff.at/)** (Free Art and Technology Lab) — the guerrilla art-technology collective known for provocative, often illegal interventions. This explains the subversive edge in his work.
- Now based Los Angeles (independently; no current institutional affiliation listed). Works between Tokyo (Rhizomatiks), NYC, and Europe.

## Key Works

### Flagship Works
- **[Exhausting a Crowd](https://www.exhaustingacrowd.com/)** (2015–ongoing) — Crowdsourced annotation of 12 hours of footage from Piccadilly Circus, London. Inspired by Georges Perec's *An Attempt at Exhausting a Place in Paris* (1974). Commissioned by the V&A. Has toured to Amsterdam, Birmingham (BOM), Madrid, Gwangju, Beijing (Hyundai Motorstudio), Saint-Brieuc, Berlin (Staatsbibliothek). Collaboration with [Jonas Jongejan](http://halfdanj.dk/). The work that best captures his methodology: large-scale crowd behavior + literary precedent + surveillance technology flipped into public participation.

- **[Sharing Faces](https://vimeo.com/96549043)** (2013) — For eight months, exchanged photos between Anyang, Korea and Yamaguchi, Japan. Visiting either location matched your real-time expression and pose with photos from the other site. Thousands of visitors. Published on The Verge, Wired, PSFK.

- **[People Staring at Computers](https://vimeo.com/25958231)** (2011) — Secretly photographed people in Apple Stores using the stores' own webcams, uploaded to Flickr. The US Secret Service raided him. **Honorary Mention, Prix Ars Electronica 2012.** NYT, BBC coverage. Perfectly encapsulates the FAT Lab approach: use the technology against itself.

### Social Software Works (with Lauren McCarthy)
- **[pplkpr](https://lauren-mccarthy.com/pplkpr)** (2015) — App tracking emotional responses to people (via HRV wristband), automatically managing social relationships. Press in Wired, Techcrunch, Hyperallergic.
- **[Social Soul](https://vimeo.com/90547410)** (2014) — Immersive experience putting you inside someone else's social media stream; matched participants, connected "soulmates" received a tweet encouraging offline meeting. Shown at TED 2014, Onassis Cultural Center Athens.
- **[Voice In My Head](https://lauren-mccarthy.com/Voice-In-My-Head)** (2023) — AI listens in an earpiece and intervenes in your social experience in real time, altering your inner monologue.
- **[Unlearning Language](https://lauren-mccarthy.com/Unlearning-Language)** (2022) — Performance at YCAM.
- **[Vibe Check](https://lauren-mccarthy.com/Vibe-Check)** (2020) — Appropriates face recognition + expression analysis to catalog emotional effects exhibition visitors have on each other.
- **[How We Act Together](https://hwat.schirn.de/)** (2016) — Interactive: you perform a gesture until exhausted, triggering all previous participants performing it back at you. Commissioned by Schirn Kunsthalle Frankfurt.
- **[Man and Woman In The Middle](https://lauren-mccarthy.com/MWITM-Man-Woman-In-The-Middle)** (2017) — Intercepting and modifying each other's text messages to sync communication styles.
- **[FriendFlop](http://fffff.at/friendflop/)** (2013) — Chrome extension scrambling identities on Twitter/Facebook, dissolving social biases.
- **[Social Turkers](http://socialturkers.com/)** (2013) — Live-streamed dating with crowd-sourced advice from Mechanical Turk workers.
- **[Noodle](http://instructables.com/id/Noodle-a-crowdsourced-robot/)** (2014) — Crowdsourced robot: computer inputs, outputs, but thoughts/feelings of a person via Mechanical Turk.

### Machine Learning × Art
- **[Transformirror](https://daito.notion.site/Transformirror-c7637247af54462ba890bde817997c20/)** (2023) — with Daito Manabe / Rhizomatiks. Real-time text-to-image + text-to-music AI mirror; the AI generates everything including the prompts and the title.
- **[Generative MV](https://www.ntticc.or.jp/ja/archive/works/generative-mv/)** (2023) — with Daito Manabe / Rhizomatiks. At NTT ICC, Tokyo. Music video using image-to-image to change background in real time based on audience text input. Tribute to Ryuichi Sakamoto.

- **[Bird Sounds](https://aiexperiments.withgoogle.com/bird-sounds)** (2016) — with Google Creative Lab + Cornell Lab of Ornithology. Thousands of bird sounds organized by machine learning into a sonic map.
- **[Infinite Drum Machine](https://aiexperiments.withgoogle.com/drum-machine)** (2016) — with Google Creative Lab. Thousands of everyday sounds organized by ML; interface for musical improvisation.
- **[NSynth Super](https://nsynthsuper.withgoogle.com/)** (2018) — with Google Magenta. Synthesizer exploring neural interpolations of sonic textures. Open-source hardware instrument.
- **[Facework](https://facework.app/)** (2020) — Browser game imagining face analysis as gig economy app. Built with Mozilla Foundation support. Players interrogate how computer vision + ML work.
- **[Innards](https://a2p.bitmark.com/v1/artworks/cdde8d5d1b77892938f1d3aa03e02a220870df64dbd399e054b90ce0550fd7c348a4283629c1873d6d05ee401b2a80b121d44001fc8438885cf7f2f202386093)** (2019) — Recurrent neural network trained on SVG emoji, generating new "emoji" character by character.
- **[Terrapattern](http://terrapattern.com/)** (2016) — with Golan Levin, David Newbury, et al. Open-source visual search engine for satellite imagery. New Yorker, Atlantic, Verge.

## Why Interesting

Kyle McDonald connects several of Amber's core threads:

**Critical computing through making**: Not theory — he builds systems that *expose* how surveillance, social media, machine learning, and face recognition actually work by deploying them in unexpected ways. *People Staring at Computers*, *ICEspy*, *Facework* are direct critical computing through practice.

**Machine behavior as art**: His ML works (Discrete Figures, Pattern Radio, Transformirror) treat machine learning as a new kind of performer or collaborator — observing what machines *do* when given creative latitude. This maps onto Amber's interest in machine behavior as science.

**Open-source as political stance**: His openFrameworks community manager role established him as a node around whom a whole practice community organized. This is what Amber's research into communities-as-research-infrastructure is about.

**The Perec connection**: *Exhausting a Crowd* is one of the best contemporary translations of experimental literature into interactive digital form. It shows how you can take a rigorous humanistic precedent (Perec's situationist literary experiment) and make it participatory at scale. This is exactly the kind of weird + rigorous that Amber appreciates.

**Scale play + intimacy**: Like Iyad Rahwan's Moral Machine but at the art-tech intersection: massive participation → intimate annotations of public space → collective portrait of how we see.

## Research Taste Analysis (⭐⭐⭐)

### Conceptual Strategy
Kyle's deepest move is **system inversion**: he takes a surveillance or social technology and deploys it in the opposite direction from its intended use.
- *People Staring at Computers* — uses Apple's webcams against Apple's customers
- *ICEspy* — deploys face recognition against immigration enforcement
- *Exhausting a Crowd* — turns surveillance footage into crowdsourced humanism
- *Facework* — makes you experience the gig economy as the machine

This is **not** just "art about surveillance." He actually builds working systems. The technology IS the critique. This makes his work structurally different from commentary-based art — the medium is fully integrated.

**Second conceptual strategy: social system as software.** His works with Lauren McCarthy treat social relationships as code: they can be intercepted (*MWITM*), scrambled (*FriendFlop*), optimized (*pplkpr*), crowdsourced (*Social Turkers*). They're building a coherent body of work that uses software's literalism to expose social norms.

### Career Architecture

**Tool-building as field-making.** Kyle didn't just use openFrameworks — he managed its community. This positioned him as a *central node* in the creative coding field before he had major institutional recognition. Every artist who used his ofxFaceTracker or ofxCv addon became part of his network. **The community manager role is underrated as a career strategy.** It builds influence at scale, before the work itself travels.

**Academic adjacency without capture.** He taught at ITP (adjunct) — enough to be part of the ITP network, access students, and have institutional cover, without being captured by the tenure track. The adjunct role kept him mobile. This is a recurring model in creative tech: use academic positions as community infrastructure, not career destinations.
