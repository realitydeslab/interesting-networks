# Sam Lavigne

New York-based artist and programmer. Builds open-source tools at the intersection of computation and political critique. Best known for **[Videogrep](https://antiboredom.github.io/videogrep/)** — a Python library for automatically searching for and cutting together video clips based on their transcribed speech. Used extensively for computational supercuts and video essays.

Other notable projects: *Scrapism* (web scraping as artistic practice), *Conductor* (conductor AI), work on ICE database scraping (which got significant attention), *Simple Saver* (text editor for radical simplicity).

## Commissioned by [[Daniel Chávez Heras]]
Daniel commissioned Sam to develop Videogrep as part of the **Creanalytics** project (with [[Jonathan Gray]] and [[Mercedes Bunz]]) — using the supercut as both creative output and analytic method in computational humanities.

## Why Interesting
Sam is an example of a "critical tool-maker" — someone whose primary artistic output is tools that others can use for critical/political purposes. Videogrep is widely used in media studies and digital humanities. His work sits at the boundary of art, activism, and software engineering.
