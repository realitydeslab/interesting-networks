# Ken Liu 刘宇昆

Writer and translator who bridged Chinese science fiction to the Anglophone world. Translated [[Liu Cixin]]'s *The Three-Body Problem* (2014) and *Death's End* (2016); both won or were nominated for the Hugo Award. His own short story *The Paper Menagerie* (2012) is the first story to win the Hugo, Nebula, and World Fantasy Award simultaneously. Also edited the *Invisible Planets* (2016) and *Invisible Planets 2* anthologies, introducing [[Chen Qiufan]], [[Xia Jia]], and others to Western readership.

## Background
Born in Lanzhou, China; grew up in the US from age 11. BA in English and Computer Science from Harvard; JD from Harvard Law. Practiced corporate law briefly, then moved into software engineering (Microsoft) and eventually full-time writing and translation. His position bridging Chinese and American culture — linguistically, professionally, and imaginatively — is what made him uniquely suited to translate [[Liu Cixin]].

## Why Interesting
Ken Liu's translation philosophy — preserving rather than domesticating cultural specificity — is partly responsible for why *The Three-Body Problem* became a genuinely cross-cultural phenomenon rather than just another Western SF novel. His own fiction (collected in [*The Paper Menagerie and Other Stories*](https://us.macmillan.com/books/9781481442541/thepapermenagerieandotherstories)) blends SF, fantasy, and Chinese-American historical fiction into something distinctive. His editorial anthologies are the best entry point into contemporary Chinese SF.
