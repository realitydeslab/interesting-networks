# Emanuele Arielli

Philosopher of art and aesthetics; co-author with [[Lev Manovich]] of *Artificial Aesthetics: Generative AI, Art and Visual Media* (2024). Venice-based theorist who brings philosophy of art, aesthetics, and psychology of art to AI image analysis. Organizer of the **EA-AI (Ethics and Aesthetics of Artificial Images)** conference in Venice.

## Domains
- Philosophy of art and aesthetics
- AI aesthetics
- Psychology of art and perception
- Ethics of AI-generated images

## Key Works
- [*Artificial Aesthetics: Generative AI, Art and Visual Media*](https://manovich.net/index.php/projects/artificial-aesthetics) (with Lev Manovich, 2024) — free PDF. 9 chapters released 2021-2024. Arielli authored: ch. 1 (Even an AI Could Do That), ch. 3 (Techno-animism and the Pygmalion Effect), ch. 6 (Human Perception and The Artificial Gaze), ch. 8 (From Tools to Authors), ch. 9 (Made by and for Humans? The Issue of Aesthetic Alignment)

Other keynotes: Neta Alexander (Yale), Neil Leach (Harvard), Matteo Pasquinelli (Ca' Foscari), Joanna Zylinska (King's College), Lev Manovich (CUNY), Antonio Somaini (Sorbonne Nouvelle). Scientific committee: Arielli, Alberto Attilio Bassi, Marco Bertozzi, Paolo Garbolino, Fabrizio Gay, Angela Mengoni, Michele Sinico, Simone Spagnol.

## Why Interesting
Arielli is the philosopher side of the Manovich-Arielli collaboration. His contribution to AI aesthetics is grounded in traditional aesthetics philosophy and psychology of art — a complement to Manovich's computational/media theory approach. The EA-AI Venice conference he organizes is a key emerging venue for exactly the kind of work Amber does.
