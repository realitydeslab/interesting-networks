# Ruha Benjamin

Sociologist and author of *Race After Technology* (2019) — the book that coined the term **"New Jim Code"** to describe how algorithmic systems embed and amplify racial discrimination under the guise of objectivity. Also wrote *Viral Justice* (2022) and *Imagination: A Manifesto* (2024). One of the defining theoretical voices in the critical AI space. [[Stephanie Dinkins]] quotes her directly in her artist statement.

## Why Interesting
Benjamin provides the intellectual framework for exactly what Dinkins builds: she theorizes the "New Jim Code" (racial bias embedded in code), Dinkins builds the counter-code. Together they represent theory + practice in the same field.

Directly relevant to Amber's machine behavior + critical computing interests. Benjamin's concept of "imagination as contested terrain" maps to Amber's speculative design work.
