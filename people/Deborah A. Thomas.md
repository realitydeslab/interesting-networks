# Deborah A. Thomas

Thomas Professor of Africana Studies and Anthropology at University of Pennsylvania. Member of [[Practicing Refusal Collective]]. MacArthur Fellow (2018). Anthropologist, documentary filmmaker, and theorist of Caribbean Black life, political violence, and colonial aftermath.

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-27:** Stub created during Practicing Refusal Collective deep research.
- **2018:** Named MacArthur Fellow.

## Key Works
- *[Political Life in the Wake of the Plantation: Sovereignty, Witnessing, Repair](https://www.dukeupress.edu/political-life-in-the-wake-of-the-plantation)* (Duke UP, 2019) — on Jamaica and the plantation's aftermath; examines reparative practices
- *[Exceptional Violence: Embodied Citizenship in Transnational Jamaica](https://www.dukeupress.edu/exceptional-violence)* (Duke UP, 2011) — on security, violence, and citizenship in post-colonial Jamaica
- *[Modern Blackness: Nationalism, Globalization, and the Politics of Culture in Jamaica](https://www.dukeupress.edu/modern-blackness)* (Duke UP, 2004) — Jamaica's competing cultural nationalisms
- Documentary films including *[Bad Friday: Rastafari After Coral Gardens](https://vimeo.com/)* (2011)

## Key Connections

## Why Interesting
Thomas bridges ethnographic fieldwork, documentary film, and high theory — practice-based in the same way Amber values. Her attention to "political life" and reparative practices connects to questions of collective agency and what living otherwise looks like.
