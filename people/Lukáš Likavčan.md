# Lukáš Likavčan

Czech-Slovak philosopher of technology and environmental humanities. Author of *[Introduction to Comparative Planetology](https://strelka.com/en/press/books/introduction-to-comparative-planetology)* (Strelka Press, 2019) — free book that became foundational text in the planetary computation scene. Works at the intersection of philosophy of science, media theory, and astrobiology to develop new conceptual tools for thinking about planetary-scale processes. Teaches at MA Information Design, [[Design Academy Eindhoven]] and MA Narrative Environments, UAL Central Saint Martins. Researcher at the Institute of Philosophy, Slovak Academy of Sciences. Previously a key researcher and project manager at [[Antikythera]] (Berggruen Institute). Co-curates the [[Earthsuits]] R&D platform with [[Stephanie Sherman]].

## Key Works

### Books
- **[*Introduction to Comparative Planetology*](https://strelka.com/en/press/books/introduction-to-comparative-planetology)** (Strelka Press, 2019) — free PDF book, the foundational text. Develops the concept of "comparative planetology" as a humanistic practice: using the astronomical study of planets (including exoplanets) as a framework for thinking about Earth's particularity and genericness. Deeply influenced by Bratton's planetary computation framework but extends it into astronomical and ecological registers. Essential reading.

### Preprints & Articles (Selected)
- **["Is the Solar System a Wilderness or a Construction Site?"](https://www.likavcan.com/articles/preprint-is-the-solar-system-a-wilderness-or-a-construction-site)** (2025, preprint, with Jens Soentgen) — frames the asteroid belt, comets, and gas giants as either natural wilderness to be preserved or raw material for industrial extraction. Thinks through space ethics via environmental philosophy.
- **["The Planetary and Its Uses: Grounding Political Economy in an Astrobiological Perspective"](https://www.likavcan.com/articles/preprint-the-planetary-and-its-uses-grounding-political-economy-in-an-astrobiological-perspective)** (2025, preprint) — connects astrobiological concepts (habitability zones, biosignatures) to political economy. An unusual cross-disciplinary move.
- **["Another Earth: An Astronomical Concept of the Planet for the Environmental Humanities"](https://www.likavcan.com/articles/another-earth-an-astronomical-concept-of-the-planet-for-the-environmental-humanities)** (2024, *Environmental Humanities*) — peer-reviewed journal article. Uses the search for exoplanets and Earth-like worlds to theorize how "Earth" should function as a concept in environmental humanities. Uses exoplanet astronomy as defamiliarization technique.
- **["[preprint] The Grass of the Universe: Rethinking Technosphere, Planetary History, and Sustainability with Fermi Paradox"](https://arxiv.org/abs/2411.08057)** (2024, arXiv) — SETI as tool for environmental humanities. Argues that (1) the technosphere is a transitory layer that should fold back into the biosphere; (2) planetary history must be understood generically, not terrestrially; (3) sustainability is insufficient — proposes "habitability" and "genesity" as better concepts. On arXiv, which means he's comfortable in interdisciplinary scientific spaces.
- **"Light, Metabolisms, Elemental Media: Theorising Human Mediality in the Anthropocene"** (2023, *Visual Resources*, [DOI](https://doi.org/10.1080/01973762.2023.2253099)) — applies elemental media theory and metabolisms to cosmic/planetary ecologies emerging from Earth-Sun interaction. Connects photosynthesis and carbon isotope chemistry to media theory and develops the concept of "metabolic self."
- **["Can Artificial Neural Networks Be Normative Models of Reason?"](https://www.transcript-verlag.de/978-3-8376-6660-1/ki-realitaeten/)** (2023, in *KI-Realitäten*, Transcript Verlag, with Carl Christian Olsson) — philosophical analysis of whether ANNs can serve as topological models of thinking that escape Kant's anthropomorphic body-based orientation. Free full text from publisher.
- **["How Am I Called Upon by the Planet?"](https://www.sum.si/journal-articles/how-am-i-called-upon-by-the-planet)** (*ŠUM #23*) — applies Althusser's interpellation concept to the planetary scale. How does the planet call us as biosphere-members, as organisms defined by metabolic exchange?
- **"Deep Politics"** (in *Vertical Atlas*, [verticalatlas.net](https://verticalatlas.net/#lukas-likavcan)) — essay on verticality in politics; argues for a geological, stratigraphic notion of verticality against purely social/hierarchical interpretations.
- **"Earthlings"** — curatorial statement for the [11th Fotograf Festival](https://fotografestival.cz/), Prague (2021). Philosophical essay on photography, nature, automation, and what it means to be "impressed by Nature's hands."
- **"Cosmo-engineers"** (in *Liquid Dogmas*, curated by Martin Piaček, [PDF](http://liquiddogmas.org/photo/LikavcanEn_5fec5fc18b160.pdf)) — engages with Federico Campagna's *Technic and Magic*, thinking through re-enchantment of the world against instrumental rationality.
- **"Automation with a Socialist Face"** (with Ben Peters, [Public Seminar](https://publicseminar.org/essays/automation-with-a-socialist-face/)) — case study of Radovan Richta's *Civilization at the Crossroads* (1966), a collective Czechoslovak work on socialism and scientific-technical revolution. Shows that automation could be Marxist without being Soviet.
- **"Tickling the Object Until It Starts to Laugh"** (in-studio text, Karlin Studios) — phenomenological investigation of objects and their surfaces; Leibnizian/archaeological approach to objecthood.

## Research Taste Analysis

### Conceptual Strategy
**Scientific concept appropriation for humanistic ends** — Likavčan consistently takes concepts from actual sciences (astrobiology, SETI, comparative planetology, carbon isotope chemistry) and uses them as defamiliarizing lenses on familiar humanistic questions (sustainability, political economy, mediality). This is the opposite of most philosophy of science, which studies scientific concepts from outside; he absorbs them and redeploys.

**Fermi paradox as generative engine** — the Fermi paradox (why don't we see other civilizations if they should exist?) becomes a thought experiment machine: if civilizations that grow exponentially destroy themselves, what does that tell us about sustainable earthly inhabitation? This is philosophy-by-counterfactual, using speculative scenarios to generate normative conclusions.

**Two-concept synthesis:** "Habitability" + "Genesity" (his new terms from the Grass of the Universe paper) — inventing vocabulary is a mark of a philosopher who is generating, not just analyzing. Amber should note this as a strategy: propose new concepts as alternatives to existing unsatisfying ones.

**The reframe strategy:** Instead of "Earth" as home, "Earth as earthsuit"; instead of "sustainability," "habitability" and "genesity"; instead of "environmental humanities," "comparative planetology." Each reframe opens up a different set of questions and possibilities.

### Career Architecture
**Progressive platform-building through residencies:** The Terraforming at Strelka → Antikythera researcher → Substrate consultancy + Earthsuits platform. Each step gave new institutional support while building toward independence. He's now in the most autonomous phase.

**Two-country dual academic base:** Slovak Academy of Sciences (academic legitimacy) + Design Academy Eindhoven and CSM (design world credibility). The combination positions him in both philosophy and design — neither purely academic nor purely practitioner.

**Visiting scientist strategy:** Embedding at Leiden Observatory, University of Vienna, etc. is not just CV-building — it's how he absorbs scientific knowledge that makes his philosophy credible to natural scientists. Most philosophers don't do this.

### What Amber Can Learn

Amber could pursue a visiting position in an actual AI lab, biology lab, or ecology institute. The work produced from inside looks different.

3. **Concept coining as research output.** "Genesity" and "habitability" as replacements for "sustainability." The short preprint on arXiv proposes new concepts, argues for them, and moves on. This is faster than building toward grand monographs and more agile.

4. **Exhibition curation as parallel thinking mode.** Curating "Civilization at the Crossroads" (2018–2019) alongside philosophical writing produced insights that pure writing couldn't. Amber could look for curatorial opportunities as a mode of research rather than just dissemination.

5. **Maintain two simultaneous institutional homes.** Slovak Academy + DAE/CSM means he's not fully dependent on either. The design schools give him access to students and practice; the Academy gives him research time and publication credentials. Amber could think about a similar dual structure.

6. **Free, short, designed books as calling cards.** *Introduction to Comparative Planetology* works because it's short enough to read in two hours, free to share, and designed attractively. In a crowded academic field, a well-designed free book often outperforms a paywalled journal article in terms of actual impact.
