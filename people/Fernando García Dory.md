# Fernando García Dory

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-27:** Stub created during Lucia Pietroiusti deep research. Co-edited *Microhabitable* (Koenig Books, 2023) with [[Lucia Pietroiusti]].

Spanish artist and founder of **INLAND-Campo Adentro**, an art-agricultural project based in Spain that operates at the intersection of rural practice, food sovereignty, land politics, and contemporary art. Co-edited **[*Microhabitable*](https://www.serpentinegalleries.org/whats-on/microhabitable/)** (Koenig Books, 2023) with [[Lucia Pietroiusti]], in collaboration with [[General Ecology]], INLAND-Campo Adentro, and Matadero Madrid. INLAND works with shepherds, farmers, and rural communities as cultural and political actors — radical in its refusal of the urban/rural split that most contemporary art reproduces.

## For Amber
- **Land-based practice as critique of computational extraction.** García Dory's work with agricultural communities is a counterpoint to tech/urban assumptions about where the future is. Relevant if Amber's work explores land, resource extraction, or the politics of computation's physical infrastructure.
- *Microhabitable* as a reference — it's a model for a publication that bridges institutional art and practical ecology.

## Why Interesting
The specific move of organising *with* rural communities (not *about* them, not *for* them) is rare in contemporary art. INLAND is one of the most coherent long-running examples of community-based ecological art practice in Europe.
