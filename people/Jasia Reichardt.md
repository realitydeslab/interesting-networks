# Jasia Reichardt

Polish-British curator and writer. Curated **[Cybernetic Serendipity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cybernetic_Serendipity)** at ICA London in 1968 — the founding exhibition of computational and generative art. The show featured computer-generated art, music, and poetry; Gordon Pask's Colloquy of Mobiles; Nam June Paik's Robot K-456; Edward Ihnatowicz's Sound Activated Mobile; Jean Tinguely's painting machines. It toured to Washington D.C. and San Francisco. Also curated pioneering shows on cybernetics and art at ICA through the late 1960s-70s.

## Why Interesting
Cybernetic Serendipity is one of the direct historical ancestors of all AI art, generative art, and machine behavior art. Reichardt's curatorial act in 1968 — bringing together artists, cyberneticists, and computer scientists — is a direct precedent for what Amber does intellectually. Understanding her approach to framing "machines and creativity" in 1968 is historically important. If she's still active, she'd be a remarkable figure for Amber to encounter.
