# Sally Potter

(born 1949, London) English film director and screenwriter; major international filmmaker (*Orlando*, 1992). Her origin is in the [[London Film-Makers' Co-operative (LFMC)]], where she made her first experimental films as a teenager. A rare figure who moved from avant-garde LFMC practice to internationally distributed feature filmmaking while maintaining a formally experimental sensibility.

## Domains
Feature film direction; experimental short film; choreography/dance; feminist film; queer cinema; music and songwriting.

## Background
Born Charlotte Sally Potter, 19 September 1949, London. Began making amateur films at 14 with an 8mm camera. Dropped out of school at 16. Joined the LFMC in the late 1960s; made early experimental shorts *Jerk* (1969) and *Play* (1970). "If I waited for somebody to give me money to do something, I'd never do anything." Trained as dancer and choreographer at London School of Contemporary Dance. Founded Limited Dance Company with Jacky Lansley. Member of the **Feminist Improvising Group** and The Film Music Orchestra. Collaborated with composer Lindsay Cooper on song cycle *Oh Moscow*, performed throughout Europe, Russia, and North America in the late 1980s. Returned to filmmaking with *Thriller* (1979), an international festival hit; then *The Gold Diggers* (1983), her first feature. *Orlando* (1992) with Tilda Swinton became internationally acclaimed, winning 25+ awards. Received OBE.

## Key Works
- *Jerk* (1969), *Play* (1970) — early LFMC experimental shorts
- *Combines* (1972) — film and dance piece
- *Thriller* (1979) — deconstruction of La Bohème through a feminist/structural lens; international festival hit
- *The Gold Diggers* (1983) — first feature; stars Julie Christie; feminist experimental feature
- *Orlando* (1992) — based on Virginia Woolf novel; stars Tilda Swinton; Academy Award nominations; Venice Audience Prize; Felix Award
- *The Tango Lesson* (1996) — semi-autobiographical; Potter also performs
- *Yes* (2004), *Rage* (2009)
- *The Roads Not Taken* (2020) — Berlinale premiere

## Key Communities
- [[London Film-Makers' Co-operative (LFMC)]] — early career foundation
- **Feminist Improvising Group** — music ensemble; feminist avant-garde music scene

## Why Interesting
Potter is proof that LFMC experimental film practice could lead somewhere. The formal rigor she developed in the early 1970s at the Co-op is visible in Orlando's costume design / gender-fluid protagonist — a film that takes on an "unfilmable" novel and solves it through thinking about how cinema represents time and embodied transformation. The trajectory from LFMC shorts to international festival films is instructive: she learnt direction from choreography, not from film school.
