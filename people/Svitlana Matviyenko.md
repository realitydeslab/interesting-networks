# Svitlana Matviyenko

Associate Professor at SFU School of Communication; leads the **Cyberwar Topologies** research stream at the [[Digital Democracies Institute]]. Ukraine-born scholar of cyberwar, political economy of information, and critical infrastructure studies. Uniquely positioned at the intersection of post-Soviet media history, decolonial theory, and contemporary warfare.

## Background
Ukrainian scholar at SFU. Her work spans: cyberwar as political-economic practice; how infrastructure is weaponized; post-Soviet media histories; colonial technologies of Russia; elemental media theory. Co-authored *Cyberworlds* (with Nick Dyer-Witheford). Ran working groups on fascism (with Alberto Toscano), elemental media and pollution (with Rahul Mukherjee), and Soviet network histories.

## Why Interesting
Her framework for understanding "filtration camps" as data-production systems — where the process of creating a deportation subject is itself a computational process of classification and sorting — is a chilling and precise articulation of what agentic systems do when they encounter bodies. This speaks directly to questions of machine behavior in state-controlled contexts. Her post-Soviet internet history work also provides rare historical texture to questions about alternative computing futures.
