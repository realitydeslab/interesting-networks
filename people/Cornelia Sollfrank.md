# Cornelia Sollfrank

Feminist net artist and scholar working at the intersection of hacker culture, feminist theory, and art practice. Pioneer of net art in the 1990s (net.art). Her *Female Extension* (1997) — submitting AI-generated works by 289 fake female artists to a net art competition — is a seminal work of both feminist art and early generative/bot art. Currently at Leuphana University. On the advisory board of [[Joanna Zylinska]]'s MEDIA:ART:WRITE:NOW series (Open Humanities Press).

## Key Work
- *Female Extension* (1997) — bot-submitted feminist intervention in net art competitions
- Research on commons, copyleft, free culture, cyberfeminism

## Communities
- European net art / media art scene
- Feminist hacker / cyberfeminist lineage
- [[Open Humanities Press]] ecosystem

## Why Interesting
Rare combination of rigorous feminist theory + active hacker practice. Her work on gender and computational creativity is highly relevant to AI art criticism. Long-term node in the critical European media art network.
