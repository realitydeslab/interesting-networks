# Moor Mother (Camae Ayewa)

Poet, musician, and activist from Philadelphia. One half of [[Black Quantum Futurism]] (with [[Rasheedah Phillips]]); co-leads free jazz collective [[Irreversible Entanglements]] and hip-hop/noise duo 700 Bliss (with DJ Haram). Born 1981, Aberdeen, Maryland; grew up in public housing; moved to Philadelphia to study photography at the Art Institute. Now faculty at USC Thornton School of Music (since Fall 2021).

## Why Interesting
Camae's music is the sonic practice of the [[Black Quantum Futurism]] theoretical framework — noise, free jazz, experimental hip-hop as temporal disruption. Her work spans the full circuit from community activism to international festival stages. As faculty at USC Thornton, she's institutionalising Afrofuturist music practice.
