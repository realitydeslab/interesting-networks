# Angela Bulloch

Canadian artist based in Berlin. YBA-adjacent generation — studied at Goldsmiths with Damien Hirst, Sarah Lucas, et al. Known for large-scale pixel-based light installations using DMX modules (custom "pixel boxes"), kinetic sculpture, and rule-based systems. Her work investigates systems, rules, and how behavior emerges from simple instruction sets — a proto-computational aesthetic developed long before "computational art" was a category.

Co-founded [[BPA// Berlin Program for Artists]] (2016) with [[Simon Denny]] and [[Willem de Rooij]]. This institutional contribution to Berlin's art ecosystem is significant.

Represented by Galerie Buchholz — the same gallery as de Rooij and (historically) Denny, cementing the Buchholz orbit as the institutional backbone of their shared project.

## Key Works
- DMX pixel modules (colored light cubes responding to sound/data inputs)
- Various rule-based and behavioral installation works

## Why Interesting
The Goldsmiths connection, the systems/rules aesthetic, the Buchholz orbit, and the BPA// co-founding make her a key figure in the Berlin contemporary art infrastructure. Her interest in rule-based emergence has deep resonance with computational creativity and agent behavior.
