# Sohrab Hura

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-27:** Stub created during EYE Filmmuseum deep research. EYE Art & Film Prize winner 2025 (11th edition).

Indian photographer and filmmaker (New Delhi). Member of [Magnum Photos](https://www.magnumphotos.com/). Works across photography, film, drawing, painting, sound, and text — describes his oeuvre as "a tree whose branches reach toward the light, while the roots intertwine with those of other trees in a larger underground network." Received EYE Art & Film Prize 2025 for "the powerful way in which he captures today's surreal reality in images" — moving fluidly between photography, film, and painting to explore violence, inequality, religion, mental health, family, and the distortion of reality through digital image flows.

## Domains

- **Photography × film × painting × drawing** — hybrid medium practice
- **South Asian social/political life** — caste, inequality, religious tension, Kashmir conflict
- **Mental health and family** — intimate documentary of his mother's illness (early work)
- **Digital distortion of reality** — how the digital image flow changes perception and truth
- **Essay film and encounter** — "somewhere between essay and encounter, between documentary and dream"
- **Disinformation and image politics** — raw and absurd portrayals of how images circulate and deceive

## Background

Started taking photographs at age 21 to cope with his mother's illness. Early work: documentary, rural India, livelihood security. Over time expanded into a "varied interplay of film, drawings, photography, sound and text." Member of Magnum Photos. Based in New Delhi.

## Key Works

- **[Bittersweet](https://sohrab-hura.com/)** (2019) — film. Won Main Prize, Internationale Kurzfilmtage Oberhausen (2020)
- **[The Lost Head & The Bird](https://sohrab-hura.com/)** (2017) — film. Won NRW Award (2018), Videonale Award of the Fluentum Collection (2019)
- **[The Coast](https://sohrab-hura.com/)** (2020) — film. Premiered at Berlinale 2021
