# Nicholas de Monchaux

Professor and Head of Architecture at MIT; STS affiliate faculty. Partner in architecture practice [modem](https://modem.work/) and founder of design technology company [Local Software](https://uselocalco.de/). Author of [*Spacesuit: Fashioning Apollo*](https://mitpress.mit.edu/books/spacesuit) (MIT Press, 2011) — architectural/cultural history of the Apollo spacesuit. Fellow of the American Academy in Rome.

## Key Works
- [*Spacesuit: Fashioning Apollo*](https://mitpress.mit.edu/books/spacesuit) (MIT Press, 2011) — winner of Eugene Emme Award from American Astronautical Society; shortlisted for Art Book Prize. History of how the Apollo spacesuit was designed by women from the Playtex underwear company — a triumph of intimacy over engineering.
- [*Local Code: 3,659 Proposals about Data, Design, and the Nature of Cities*](https://www.papress.com/html/product.details.dna?isbn=9781616892272) (Princeton Architectural Press, 2016)

## Why Interesting
The *Spacesuit* book is a key reference for [[Earthsuits]]. Its insight — that the most sophisticated life support system ever built emerged from soft materials and women's underwear expertise, NOT from hard engineering — is a founding argument for Earthsuits' entire framework.
