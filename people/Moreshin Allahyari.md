# Moreshin Allahyari

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Created stub note. Discovered as [[Eyeo Festival]] speaker — one of the clearest examples of decolonial digital art in the creative coding community.

Iranian-American artist working at intersection of 3D printing, digital humanities, decolonial theory, Afrofuturism, and speculative fiction. Known for **Material Speculation** series (2014–2016): 3D-printed reconstructions of IS-destroyed ancient artifacts, each embedded with a flash drive containing historical documentation. Bridges ancient material culture and digital preservation with political urgency.

Co-authored (with Daniel Rourke) *[The 3D Additivist Manifesto](http://additivism.org/manifesto)* (2015) — a speculative/provocative call to use 3D printing as tool of radical change.

Also works on "She Who Sees the Unknown" series — reconstructing pre-Islamic Middle Eastern female djinn figures as a feminist critique of colonial erasure.

## Why Interesting
One of the rare figures who uses creative coding tools for explicitly decolonial, feminist, and anti-colonial ends. Not just "art-tech" but materially political. The connection between 3D printing, ancient artifacts, and digital preservation is genuinely sui generis. Eyeo invited her specifically when the festival was deepening its critical lens — a strong curatorial signal.

For Amber: the crossover of non-Western epistemologies + computational tools is exactly the territory Amber's cosmotechnics interests approach from a different angle.
