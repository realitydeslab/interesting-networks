# Christine Sun Kim

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Stub created during Wellcome Trust deep research. Currently showing *1880 THAT* at Wellcome Collection (17 Apr 2025 – 6 Apr 2026) with Thomas Mader.

Deaf American artist working with sound, sign language, language as home, and the politics of communication. Known for humorous yet rigorous work exploring what it means to live under the threat of language loss. Currently showing at Wellcome Collection London.

## Why Interesting
**Sound art from inside Deaf experience.** Kim uses sound — the thing she supposedly doesn't have access to — as primary artistic medium. Deeply political: her work addresses the 1880 Milan Congress that banned sign language in Deaf schools. Connects to: embodiment, communication, language politics, disability justice, access. Her work at Wellcome Collection (*1880 THAT*, with Thomas Mader) uses humour, film, and sculpture to explore communication rights.

Connects to Amber's interests: embodiment, participatory approaches, non-normative bodies + technologies, language and identity.
