# Yuk Hui

Hong Kong philosopher of technology. One of the most influential contemporary philosophers of technology. Doctoral student of [[Bernard Stiegler]] at Goldsmiths.

## Key Works
- *On the Existence of Digital Objects* (2016) — homage to Simondon
- *The Question Concerning Technology in China: An Essay in Cosmotechnics* (2016) — response to Heidegger
- *Recursivity and Contingency* (2019) — philosophical treatise on cybernetics
- *Art and Cosmotechnics* (2021)

## Key Concepts
- **Cosmotechnics** — unification of cosmic and moral orders through technical activities; overcomes technics/nature dualism
- **Technodiversity** — against technological monoculture, for multiple technological traditions
- Contributed to discourse on sinofuturism

## Connections
- [[Benjamin Bratton]] — in dialogue (planetary computation)
- [[Antikythera]] — intellectual orbit (Berggruen Prize juror since 2020)
- [[Bernard Stiegler]] — doctoral advisor
- [[Gilbert Simondon]] — key influence
- Taught at China Academy of Art (where Amber teaches!)
- Research Network for Philosophy and Technology — convenor since 2014
- Previously at Leuphana University, Bauhaus University

## Why Interesting
Essential figure for non-Western tech philosophy. Cosmotechnics directly challenges the universality of Western technology concepts. Connection to China Academy of Art is a direct institutional link to Amber. Bridges continental philosophy, Chinese thought, and cybernetics.
