# Yuk Hui 許煜

Hong Kong philosopher of technology. Professor of Philosophy at Erasmus University Rotterdam (Chair of Human Conditions), directing the Erasmus Institute for Philosophy and Technology. One of the most influential contemporary philosophers of technology. Originator of **cosmotechnics** — the thesis that technology is not universal but culturally constituted, that different civilizations produce different unifications of the cosmic and moral through technical activity. Described by *El Mundo* as a "new superstar of thought."

## Background
Yuk Hui's trajectory is unusual: he started as a **computer engineer** (BEng, University of Hong Kong), then pivoted entirely to philosophy. He wrote his doctoral thesis at **Goldsmiths, University of London** under **[[Bernard Stiegler]]** (1952–2020), the French philosopher of technology and pharmacology. After his PhD, he worked at Stiegler's **Institut de Recherche et d'Innovation** (IRI) in Paris — the Centre Pompidou's tech-philosophy lab. He then moved to Germany, teaching at **[Leuphana University Lüneburg](https://www.leuphana.de/)** where he obtained his **Habilitation** (the highest German academic qualification) in philosophy of technology. He also taught at **[Bauhaus University Weimar](https://www.uni-weimar.de/)** and **[Strelka Institute](https://strelka.com/)** in Moscow.

His intellectual trajectory traces a distinctive line: from engineering → Stiegler's pharmacology → Simondon's individuation → Heidegger's question concerning technology → Chinese philosophy (Mou Zongsan, Qi/Dao) → **cosmotechnics** as synthesis. The engineering background is not incidental — it gives him technical fluency unusual among continental philosophers. He is multilingual (English, French, German, Mandarin, Cantonese) and has translated major works between these languages, including Simondon's *Du mode d'existence des objets techniques* into Chinese and Graeber's *Fragments of an Anarchist Anthropology* into Chinese.

Currently holds professorships at **[Erasmus University Rotterdam](https://www.eur.nl/en/people/yuk-hui)** and **[City University of Hong Kong](https://scholars.cityu.edu.hk/en/persons/yukhui/)**. Has been a visiting professor at the **[China Academy of Art](http://www.caa.edu.cn/)** (Hangzhou — where Amber teaches!) and the **[University of Tokyo](https://www.u-tokyo.ac.jp/en/)**.

## Key Concepts
- **Cosmotechnics** — the unification of cosmic and moral orders through technical activities. Different civilizations have different cosmotechnics; technology is not universal but culturally constituted. Overcomes the dualism of technics/nature. Proposes the "antinomy of the universality of technology."
- **Technodiversity** — against the monoculture of Western technological modernity; argues for recovering and cultivating multiple technological traditions. A political and philosophical programme.
- **Technophany** — the appearance/manifestation of technology as a philosophical event.
- **Planetary thinking** — rethinking technology and ecology at a planetary scale, beyond the nation-state and beyond Europe.

## Key Works

### Monographs
1. [*On the Existence of Digital Objects*](https://www.upress.umn.edu/book-division/books/on-the-existence-of-digital-objects) (University of Minnesota Press, 2016) — Prefaced by [[Bernard Stiegler]]. An ontology of digital objects via [[Gilbert Simondon]]'s individuation theory. *Jahrbuch Technikphilosophie* said it has "all the qualities of becoming a genuine classic." Translated into Chinese, Korean, Spanish.
2. [*The Question Concerning Technology in China: An Essay in Cosmotechnics*](https://www.urbanomic.com/book/question-concerning-technology-china/) ([[Urbanomic]]/MIT Press, 2016; 2nd ed. 2019; 3rd print 2023) — The founding text of cosmotechnics. A response to Heidegger's 1953 essay, arguing that the concept of technology in Western philosophy does not coincide with that of China. Carl Mitcham called it the most challenging work for understanding both Western technology philosophy and China's rise. Translated into 9 languages (German, French, Italian, Korean, Japanese, Russian, Chinese, Spanish forthcoming). Chinese edition published by **China Academy of Art Press** (2021).
3. [*Recursivity and Contingency*](https://rowman.com/ISBN/9781786600523/Recursivity-and-Contingency) (Rowman & Littlefield, 2019) — Prefaced by Howard Caygill. A philosophical genealogy of cybernetics from Kant through German Idealism to 20th-century cybernetics. Reviewed in *The Philosophical Quarterly*, *American Book Review*. Translated into 6 languages (Russian, Japanese, Chinese, Polish, Spanish, Korean).
4. [*Art and Cosmotechnics*](https://www.upress.umn.edu/book-division/books/art-and-cosmotechnics) (University of Minnesota Press/[[e-flux]], 2021) — Extends cosmotechnics into aesthetics. Explores the logic of Chinese landscape painting (*shanshui*) as a different technical-aesthetic paradigm. Translated into Spanish, Japanese, Korean, Chinese, Russian.
5. [*Post-Europe*](https://mitpress.mit.edu/9798985423518/post-europe/) (Sequence Press/[[Urbanomic]], 2024) — A philosophical manifesto on thinking beyond the European framework. Translations forthcoming in Spanish, Russian, Japanese, Chinese.
6. [*Machine and Sovereignty*](https://www.upress.umn.edu/9781517917418/machine-and-sovereignty/) (University of Minnesota Press, 2024) — Concludes his cosmotechnics project: on planetary thinking, technological sovereignty, and the future of the machine. Translations forthcoming in Spanish, Russian, Chinese.
7. [*Kant Machine*](https://www.bloomsbury.com/uk/kant-machine-9781350602502/) (Bloomsbury, 2026) — On the origins and futures of AI through the lens of Kant. His most recent monograph.

## Venue Circuit Analysis
Hui moves across **five distinct circuits**:

1. **European continental philosophy**: Collège International de Philosophie, ENS Paris, Centre Pompidou, Goldsmiths, Leuphana, Bauhaus, various German/Dutch/Swiss universities. The Heidegger-Simondon-Stiegler heartland.
2. **Global art institutions**: Guggenheim, TaiKwun, CIMAM, National Art Museum of China, Ural Industrial Biennale, STRP, Museum Fridericianum (Kassel). He functions as the philosopher that art institutions invite when they want serious tech-philosophy.
3. **Chinese intellectual circuit**: Tsinghua, Fudan, Nanjing, Renmin, China Academy of Art, HKUST, CityU HK. He is one of very few philosophers who operates at the highest level in both Western and Chinese academia simultaneously.
4. **Speculative theory circuit**: e-flux, [[Urbanomic]], [[Strelka Institute]], Mapping Festival. The [[CCRU]]-adjacent, accelerationist-adjacent, but distinct — Hui's project is explicitly a critique of acceleration/universalism.
5. **Latin American reception**: Caja Negra (Buenos Aires), reviews in *El País*, *Folha de São Paulo*, *El Mundo*. Unusual for a philosopher — his technodiversity argument resonates strongly in the Global South.

**Pattern**: Unlike many philosophers, Hui doesn't just do academic conferences. He regularly speaks at **art museums, biennales, and cultural institutions** — his philosophy has aesthetic and curatorial dimensions. The Guggenheim catalogue essay, the TaiKwun dialogue with Obrist, the Fridericianum "Inhuman" show — these are not peripheral. Art is a site of cosmotechnical investigation for him.
