# Wendy Hui Kyong Chun

Korean-American media theorist and critical digital studies scholar. Professor at [Simon Fraser University](https://www.sfu.ca/communication/people/faculty/wendy-hui-kyong-chun.html), School of Communication. Director of the [Digital Democracies Institute](https://www.sfu.ca/digital-democracies-institute.html). One of the most important voices in **critical digital studies** — particularly on the intersection of race, algorithms, data, and governance. Author of *Discriminating Data* (MIT Press, 2021), *Programmed Visions* (MIT Press, 2011), and *Control and Freedom* (MIT Press, 2006). Upcoming speaker at the [[Research Network for Philosophy and Technology]] Dialogues (May 4, 2026).

## Background
Trained in Systems Design Engineering (University of Waterloo) then switched to English and Film Studies (Princeton). This engineering → humanities trajectory mirrors [[Yuk Hui]]'s (engineering → philosophy). Developed "new media studies" that take technical systems seriously as cultural/political objects. Former Professor at Brown University (Media Studies); moved to SFU ~2019 to found the Digital Democracies Institute.

Her core contribution: showing how **homophily** (the tendency to associate with similar others) is built into algorithmic systems and reinforces segregation, discrimination, and epistemic bubbles — not as a bug but as a structural feature. *Discriminating Data* (2021) is the most rigorous account of how machine learning perpetuates racial discrimination through seemingly neutral technical design choices.
