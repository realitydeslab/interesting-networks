# Joanne McNeil

American writer, editor, and art critic. Former Editor at [[Rhizome]] (2011–2012). Author of *Lurking: How a Person Became a User* (MCD/FSG, 2020) — a critical history of the internet from the perspective of its users. Novel *Wrong Way* (2023) — gig worker employed by autonomous vehicle company. Inaugural recipient of the Thoma Foundation Digital Arts Writing Award (2015). Writes Speculations column for Filmmaker Magazine.

## Key Works
- *[Lurking: How a Person Became a User](https://www.mcdbooks.com/books/lurking)* (MCD/FSG, 2020) — critical history of internet culture from user perspective; covers surveillance, search, lurking, shopping, tracking. Written as clear-eyed cultural criticism, not tech journalism.
- *[Wrong Way](https://us.macmillan.com/books/9780374610661/wrongway)* (FSG, 2023) — first novel; centers a gig worker employed by autonomous vehicle company. Fiction that embeds serious thinking about automation, labor, and technology.

## Why Interesting
McNeil represents the critical writer track out of the net art world — from Rhizome editorship to serious book authorship. *Lurking* is an essential text for anyone thinking about how people relate to digital systems. The move to fiction (*Wrong Way*) is interesting: using the novel form to process questions about automation, gig labor, and autonomous systems that are directly in Amber's territory. The combination of critical cultural history and fiction is a model worth studying.
