# Patricia Pisters

Professor of Film Studies at University of Amsterdam. A leading Deleuzian film theorist who has extended her approach to neuroscience and AI. Author of *[The Neuro-Image: A Deleuzian Film-Philosophy of Digital Screen Culture](https://sup.org/books/title/?id=20093)* (Stanford UP, 2012) — applied Deleuze's time-image to contemporary digital screen culture through neuroscience. More recently working on AI and cinematic perception in relation to ecology and nature.

## Why Interesting
Pisters brings Deleuzian philosophy of cinema into dialogue with AI and neuroscience — which means she's thinking about machine perception and animal/plant/ecological perception as part of the same theoretical terrain. Her AIC talk "Re-enchanting Nature and AI through Cinematic Perception" suggests she's now extending her neuro-image framework to ask how AI changes our relationship to nature through visual media. This is adjacent to Amber's machine behavior and more-than-human interests — especially around questions of perception, embodiment, and ecological entanglement.
