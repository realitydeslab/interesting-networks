# Gilbert Simondon

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Created during cosmotechnics research. Foundational figure in technics tradition leading to Yuk Hui.

French philosopher (1924-1989) foundational to continental philosophy of technology. His work on technical objects and individuation directly influenced Bernard Stiegler and through him, Yuk Hui's cosmotechnics.

## Background
Studied at École Normale Supérieure. Developed groundbreaking philosophy of technology and individuation that challenged hylomorphic thinking. Work largely overlooked during his lifetime but gained massive influence from 1990s onward.

## Domains
- Philosophy of technology and technical objects
- Theory of individuation (physical, biological, psychic, collective)
- Cybernetics and information theory
- Aesthetics and perception

## Key Works
- **On the Mode of Existence of Technical Objects** (1958) — foundational work on technical philosophy
- **Individuation in Light of Notions of Form and Information** (1964) — theory of individuation
- **Imagination and Invention** (1965-66) — aesthetics and creativity

## Why Interesting
Simondon's philosophy of technical objects provides the foundational concepts that Stiegler and then Hui build upon. His notion that technical objects have their own mode of existence and development (concretization) challenges instrumental views of technology. Essential reading for understanding the genealogy of cosmotechnics.

### Conceptual Strategy
- **Ontological reframing** — argued technical objects have genuine ontological status, not just instrumental
- **Process philosophy** — focused on individuation as ongoing process rather than fixed individuals
- **Cybernetic integration** — early adoption of cybernetics and information theory in humanistic philosophy

### Key Moves
1. **1958:** *Technical Objects* — revolutionary argument that technology has its own evolutionary logic
2. **1960s:** Individuation theory — developed comprehensive theory linking physical, biological, and social processes
3. **Posthumous recognition** — work gained massive influence after death through Stiegler and others

### What Amber Can Learn
- **Ontological boldness** — willingness to grant genuine reality to non-human entities (technical objects)
- **Process thinking** — focus on becoming rather than being
- **Interdisciplinary synthesis** — integration of technical and humanistic thinking
