# Gilbert Simondon

French philosopher. Theory of individuation and the mode of existence of technical objects. Rediscovered and enormously influential in 21st century.

## Key Works
- *On the Mode of Existence of Technical Objects* (1958/2017 English)
- *Individuation in Light of Notions of Form and Information* (1958/2020 English)

## Key Concepts
- **Individuation** — becoming rather than being; process rather than substance
- **Transduction** — how information transforms systems
- Technical objects have their own mode of existence — not mere tools

## Connections
- [[Bernard Stiegler]] — deeply influenced by Simondon
- [[Yuk Hui]] — *On the Existence of Digital Objects* is an homage
- [[Donna Haraway]] — parallel trajectory (nonhuman agency)
- Deleuze — influential on Deleuze's philosophy of difference

## Why Interesting
Simondon argued in 1958 that technical objects have their own mode of existence — they are not just human tools. This prefigures by 60+ years the current debates about machine agency and more-than-human design. Essential genealogy.
