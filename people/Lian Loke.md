# Lian Loke

Associate Professor at University of Sydney. Co-named "the somatic turn in HCI." Leading theorist of movement, soma, and felt-sense as design materials — with deep roots in dance studies, Feldenkrais, and Gendlin's Focusing. Her 2018 *Interactions* paper with [[Thecla Schiphorst]] is the flag-planting manifesto for first-person somatic HCI. Recent pivot into human-robot embodied interaction.

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-27:** Initial deep research. Full ⭐⭐⭐ profile created.
- **2025:** Co-authored book with Thecla Schiphorst, *[Experience as Skill: The Somatic Turn in Human-Computer Interaction](https://link.springer.com/book/9783030061975)* (Springer) — the definitive text.
- **2025:** New wave of human-robot co-embodiment work: workshops, empathic entanglements, design toolkits for HRC.

## Research Taste Analysis (⭐⭐⭐)

### Conceptual Strategy
**Framework multiplication:** Loke repeatedly imports from adjacent disciplines and coins connective frameworks:
- Labanotation (dance notation) → HCI design representation
- Phenomenology (Merleau-Ponty) + Interaction Design → "embodied approach"
- Shklovsky's defamiliarisation + Merleau-Ponty → "moving and making strange"
- Gendlin's Focusing (psychotherapy) + Design → "felt sense in design"
- Shusterman's somaesthetics (pragmatist aesthetics) + HCI → "somatic turn"
- Buddhist contemplative practice + interaction → contemplative computing

**Seed paper strategy:** The 2007 *PUC* movement frameworks paper → 2009 *IJHCS* design representations paper → 2013 *TCHI* embodied approach → 2018 *Interactions* "somatic turn." Each paper maps territory then the next empirically fills a slot.

**Two-word concept coining:** "somatic turn," "somaesthetic design," "choreographic inscriptions," "felt sense," "tacit narratives." She has a talent for naming that makes concepts citeable and tractable.

### Career Architecture
**Field declaration (later stage):** The 2018 *Interactions* paper with Schiphorst is a textbook field declaration: survey the landscape, name it, give it a flag. It was followed by the Springer book (2025) — the monograph that establishes the field properly. This is a classic decade-long trajectory: empirical work (2004–2013) → conceptual synthesis (2013–2018) → field codification (2018–2025).

**Coalition papers:** "Move to be Moved" (CHI 2016, 14 co-authors) is a coalition paper — gathering all the major somatic HCI voices under one banner. Loke's role as one of 14 co-authors with Höök, Schiphorst, Isbister, Forlizzi signals her membership in the inner circle.

**Platform building:** TEI 2020 Program Chair and OzCHI 2014 co-chair are platform moves — she's not just presenting at these venues, she's shaping them.

**Pacific-rim anchoring:** Smart strategic choice to be THE somatic HCI voice in Australia/Pacific-rim. Less competition than Europe/North America; allows her to dominate OzCHI while having full CHI/TEI credibility.

### Methodology Signature
**First-person + third-person integration:** Her most sophisticated methodological move is integrating first-person (Focusing, autoethnography, felt-sense introspection) with third-person design research (user studies, toolkits). This is rare — most HCI researchers do one or the other.

**Art-science bridging:** Long collaboration with artists (George Khut, Keith Armstrong) feeds back into academic research. The Feldenkrais re-sensitization paper (IJART 2013) is pure art-research.

**Toolkit formalization:** Tendency to formalize embodied knowledge into transferable tools: Touch Toolkit (TEI 2014), Body Maps (TEI 2022), Focusing method (CHI 2022). This is smart — it makes tacit somatic knowledge transmittable, which broadens impact.

### Network Strategy
**Deep dyadic collaborations:** The Claudia Núñez-Pacheco axis (2014–2022) produced ~8 papers across all the key journals. This depth-focused collaboration model produces high-quality theoretical work vs. spreading thin across many co-authors.

**International node integration:** She connects to the Stockholm soma design network (Höök, Balaam, Ståhl) through the "Move to be Moved" workshop and Body Maps (TEI 2022), anchoring her globally while staying Sydney-based.

**Student-as-collaborator model:** Most of her depth publications (Núñez-Pacheco, Mah, Cochrane, Zhao) are co-authored with PhD students. She invests deeply in a few students and produces journal papers with them — good model for building a school of thought.
