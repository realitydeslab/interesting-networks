## Background

Lian Loke is an Associate Professor at the [University of Sydney Design Lab](https://www.sydney.edu.au/arts/schools/sydney-college-of-the-arts/design-lab.html), located within the Faculty of Arts and Social Sciences (previously Architecture). She has been based there for over a decade.

Her intellectual lineage runs through dance, movement, and somatic practices — she approaches computing not through the screen but through the body. Two formative collaborations define her trajectory:

1. **Thecla Schiphorst** (SFU, dance + computing) — together they named "the somatic turn in HCI" in 2018, building on Schiphorst's decades of work on body-centered computing. This partnership gives Loke's work transnational reach and historical depth.

2. **Kristina Höök** (KTH) — in 2018 Loke joined the broader soma design community for a landmark multi-author manifesto: "Embracing First-Person Perspectives in Soma-Based Design" (21 co-authors including Höök, Forlizzi, Schiphorst, Isbister, Mueller). This positioned her as a core member of the European soma design school within an Australian context.

Her core methodological contribution is **Gendlin Focusing** in interaction design — she has spent years (2016–2022) with collaborator Claudia Núñez-Pacheco developing this psychotherapy technique for accessing bodily "felt sense" as a formal design research method. This is genuinely distinctive: most soma design work uses movement exercises or body scanning; Focusing goes further into the pre-verbal, implicit body knowledge.

**Recent pivot (2019–2025):** A new research stream in **human-robot interaction** at close-proximity fabrication/construction scenarios, led by PhD student Yi Zhao and architectural collaborator Dagmar Reinhardt. This HRI work is more applied/technical but still grounded in embodied, somatic perspectives on how people move with robots.

## Key Works

### Landmark / Field-Defining
- [**The somatic turn in human-computer interaction**](https://doi.org/10.1145/3236675) (ACM *Interactions*, 2018) — co-authored with Thecla Schiphorst. The paper that named and defined the somatic turn in HCI. Argues that the field has moved from Merleau-Ponty's philosophy toward actual practice — researchers bringing body awareness, movement, and somatic methods into their design processes. A must-read for anyone in this space.

- [**Embracing First-Person Perspectives in Soma-Based Design**](https://doi.org/10.3390/informatics5010008) (*Informatics*, 2018) — 21 co-authors: Kristina Höök, Baptiste Caramiaux, Cumhur Erkut, Jodi Forlizzi, Michael Haller, Caroline Hummels, Katherine Isbister, Lian Loke, Florian 'Floyd' Mueller, Marianne Graves Petersen, Thecla Schiphorst, Elena Márquez Segura, Anna Ståhl, Dag Svanæs, Jakob Tholander, and more. The soma design community's manifesto for first-person methods. Argues for introspective data collection and body-based inquiry as legitimate research.

### Focusing / Felt Sense Methods
- [**Focusing for Interaction Design: An Introspective Somatic Method**](https://doi.org/10.1145/3491102.3501978) (CHI 2022) — with Claudia Núñez-Pacheco. The fullest articulation of using Gendlin's Focusing (a body-oriented psychotherapy technique) as a design research method. Focusing involves attending to the pre-verbal "felt sense" — the bodily knowing before it becomes words.

- **Towards a technique for articulating aesthetic experiences in design using Focusing and the Felt Sense** (*The Design Journal*, 2018) — with Claudia Núñez-Pacheco. Early methodological paper developing the Focusing technique for design.

- **Developing somatic sensibilities for practices of discernment in interaction design** (2018) — companion paper on training somatic awareness in designers.

- [**Tacit Narratives: Surfacing Aesthetic Meaning by Using Wearable Props and Focusing**](https://doi.org/10.1145/3024969.3024979) (TEI 2017) — with Núñez-Pacheco. Using props + Focusing to access tacit aesthetic knowledge.

- [**Getting into someone else's soul: communicating embodied experience**](https://doi.org/10.1080/14626268.2020.1835987) (*Digital Creativity*, 2020) — with Claudia Núñez-Pacheco. How to communicate first-person embodied experience across the research/design boundary.

### Soma and Body Methods
- [**Body Maps: A Generative Tool for Soma-based Design**](https://doi.org/10.1145/3490149.3502262) (TEI 2022) — with Karen Anne Cochrane, Kristina Mah, Anna Ståhl, Claudia Núñez-Pacheco, Madeline Balaam, Naseem Ahmadpour. Body mapping as a generative design tool — participants draw/map felt sensations on body silhouettes.

- [**Re-sensitising the body: interactive art and the Feldenkrais method**](https://doi.org/10.1504/IJART.2013.058283) (*Int. J. Arts and Technology*, 2013) — with George Poonkhin Khut, Maggie Slattery, Catherine Truman, Lizzie Muller, Jonathan Duckworth. Early work on Feldenkrais (movement reeducation method) and interactive art — foundational for her movement-methods trajectory.

- **Intimate Aesthetics and Facilitated Interaction** (book chapter, 2014) — with George Poonkhin Khut. In *Interactive Experience in the Digital Age* (Springer, 2014).

### Contemplative Design
- [**Towards a Contemplative Research Framework for Training Self-Observation in HCI: A Study of Compassion Cultivation**](https://doi.org/10.1145/3471932) (ACM *TOCHI*, 2021) — with Kristina Mah, Luke Hespanhol. Major journal paper on autoethnography of Tonglen Buddhist meditation practice as a design research approach. Argues for contemplative frameworks for HCI self-observation training.

- [**Designing With Ritual Interaction: A Novel Approach to Compassion Cultivation Through a Buddhist-Inspired Interactive Artwork**](https://doi.org/10.1145/3374920.3374947) (TEI 2020) — with Kristina Mah, Luke Hespanhol.

- [**Breathing Scarf: Using a First-Person Research Method to Design a Wearable for Emotional Regulation**](https://doi.org/10.1145/3490149.3501330) (TEI 2022) — with Karen Cochrane, Yidan Cao, Audrey Girouard.

- [**Understanding the First Person Experience of Walking Mindfulness Meditation Facilitated by EEG Modulated Interactive Soundscape**](https://doi.org/10.1145/3430524.3440637) (TEI 2021) — with Karen Anne Cochrane, Matthew Leete, Andrew J. Campbell, Naseem Ahmadpour.

### Dance/Movement/Sonification
- [**Performer-machine scores for choreographing bodies, interaction and kinetic materials**](https://doi.org/10.1145/2790994.2790999) (MOCO 2015) — with Dagmar Reinhardt, Jodie McNeilly. Choreographic scores for human-machine-material interaction. The seed paper for her later HRI work.

- **Felt Sense through Auditory Display: A Design Case Study into Sound for Somatic Awareness while Walking** (C&C 2017) — with Frank Feltham. Sonification for somatic awareness while walking.

- [**The Prefix/Suffix Model: Data Extraction to Encourage Expressive Walking Movements through Sonification**](https://doi.org/10.1145/3374920.3374985) (TEI 2020) — with Frank Feltham, James Curtis.

- [**Enriching teachers' assessments of rhythmic Forró dance skills by modelling motion sensor data**](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ijhcs.2022.102776) (*Int. J. Human-Computer Studies*, 2022) — with Augusto Dias Pereira dos Santos, Kalina Yacef, Roberto Martínez Maldonado.

### Urban / Playful Interaction
- [**To Play and To Be Played: Exploring the Design of Urban Machines for Playful Placemaking**](https://doi.org/10.3389/fcomp.2021.635949) (*Frontiers in Computer Science*, 2021) — with Louis Chew, Luke Hespanhol.

## Research Taste Analysis

### Conceptual Strategy
**Import and formalize** — her signature move is finding a technique from an adjacent field (Feldenkrais movement reeducation, Gendlin Focusing therapy, Buddhist Tonglen meditation) and formalizing it as an interaction design research method. She doesn't invent new phenomena; she imports existing somatic practices and asks "how does this become a design method?"

**Coalition papers** — the 2018 Informatics paper (21 co-authors) and "Move to be Moved" CHI SIG (15+ co-authors) are classic community-building moves. The manifesto format as territory-marking.

**Two-word concept coining** — "somatic turn" (with Schiphorst) is a deliberate act of naming. A named discourse is a citeable, teachable, institutionalizable thing.

### Career Architecture
**Community building over solo fame** — her citation metrics are solid but not stellar. What distinguishes her career is *institutional investment*: chairing major conferences (TEI 2014, TEI 2020, OzCHI 2020), editing proceedings, building the Australian soma design scene.

**Geographic positioning** — she's the soma design community's Southern Hemisphere node. This is a strategic niche. She's not competing with Höök at KTH; she's the Höök-school's presence in Australia, with local relevance and connection to OzCHI.

**PhD student pipeline** — her most consistent collaborators are PhD students (Núñez-Pacheco, Mah, Cochrane, Zhao) who she shapes and publishes with. This is how she scales her research agenda.

### Methodology Signature
**First-person methods as a sustained program** — where most HCI researchers do one or two papers on methods, she has spent 10+ years systematically developing Focusing as a design research methodology, iterating from early workshops through full CHI papers. Methodological depth is her signature.

**Practice-import** — Feldenkrais, Gendlin, Tonglen Buddhism, Forró dance rhythm sensors. She finds practices that already know something about body experience and asks how they become design tools.

### What Amber Can Learn

- **Name the turn**: When you have enough work, write the "somatic turn"-style paper — not a case study but a field declaration. Costs one paper's worth of effort, earns citation landmark status.
- **Conference co-chair as field-building**: Amber could build the Machine Behavior / ALife / More-than-Human Design conference circuit the way Loke built the somatic HCI circuit in Australia. Think about which venue needs Amber as a co-chair in 5 years.
