# Jussi Parikka

Finnish media theorist, curator, and Professor in Digital Aesthetics and Culture at Aarhus University. Pioneer of media archaeology, environmental media studies, and "geology of media" thinking. Founding co-director of the Environmental Media and Aesthetics research program. Member of Academia Europaea (2021). Bridges critical theory, environmental humanities, and curatorial practice.

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Deep research completed. Full profile with curatorial work, recent publications, and collaborative networks.

## Background
- Born in Finland, completed PhD in Cultural History at University of Turku (2007) with dissertation "Digital Contagions: A Media Archaeology of Computer Worms and Viruses"
- Pioneered media archaeology approach in English-speaking academia, translating and expanding German media archaeology tradition
- Moved from singular media object analysis to infrastructure focus — cables, environmental impacts, "geology of media"
- Academic trajectory: University of Turku → Aarhus University (current)
- **Teaching experience:** 20+ years across five countries
