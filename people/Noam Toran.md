# Noam Toran

British-Israeli filmmaker and designer. **Died in 2018**, aged approximately 42. One of the central figures in speculative design's intersection with cinema. Graduated from [[Design Interactions (RCA)]] and later returned as Senior Tutor (2005–2015) under [[Anthony Dunne]] and [[Fiona Raby]]. His practice treated design as film and film as design — objects as narrative props, scenarios as screenplays, exhibitions as film sets.

Most known for *Accessories for Lonely Men* (2001) — speculative accessories for solitary male fantasy life — and *Orders of Magnitude* (2010) — a 10-hour film exploring the history of the computer as a social machine. Made the video component for D&R's *Technological Dreams Series: No.1, Robots* (2007). Co-founded the design-film collaboration **[Toran + Shapiro](http://www.noamtoran.com)** with Onkar Kular and Chris O'Shea.

## Why Interesting
A crucial figure in the history of design-as-filmmaking and the DI legacy — now a historical figure given his early death. His work represents the most cinematic direction within the DI tradition; the influence lives on through his students. Understanding his practice helps map the "film" pole of the DI approach versus the "biology" or "ethics" poles.

## Key Works
- *Accessories for Lonely Men* (2001) — speculative accessories for male fantasy and loneliness
- *The MacGuffin Library* (2007–08) — a library of fictional objects from cinema history
- *Orders of Magnitude* (2010) — 10-hour documentary-design on the history of computing as social machine
- *Technological Dreams: Robots* video component (2007, with Dunne & Raby)
