# Cao Fei 曹斐

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Deep research completed. Major solo exhibition "Testimonies to the Near Future" at Kunstmuseum Basel opening May 30, 2026. Recent work includes homage to late sister Cao Xiaoyun at Art Gallery of New South Wales retrospective. Ranked #29 in ArtReview Power 100 (2025).

Multi-media artist and filmmaker exploring the intersection of virtuality, technology, and rapid social transformation. Pioneer of Chinese digital art who examines the "liquid society" where boundaries between virtual and material worlds become porous. For 25 years, has used video, installation, VR, and digital media to portray the politics of contemporary China while addressing universal questions about technology's impact on humanity.

## Background

Born in Guangzhou in 1978, Cao Fei emerged during China's period of rapid economic transformation. Studied at the China Academy of Art (formerly Zhejiang Academy of Fine Arts) in Hangzhou, where she worked under [[Zhang Peili]] — considered the father of Chinese video art and maker of China's first video artwork "30x30" (1988). Currently based in Beijing, where she has conducted extensive research in the Jiuxianqiao ('Hong Xia') district where she lives and works.

Her sister Cao Xiaoyun was also an artist who immigrated to Sydney, Australia before her death two years ago — an experience that deeply influences Cao Fei's current work examining migration, identity, and cultural displacement.

## Domains

**Virtual Worlds & Digital Culture:** Pioneering exploration of virtual environments, particularly Second Life and digital gaming cultures, to examine urbanization, labor, and social transformation.

**Technology & Alienation:** Investigation of how rapid technological advancement affects human relationships, labor conditions, and social structures, particularly in the context of China's economic transformation.

**Video & Film:** Cinematic explorations of post-apocalyptic scenarios, automation's impact on bodies and labor, and the intersection of reality and fiction.

**Immersive Installation:** Site-specific works that blend archival material, furniture, and digital media to create total environments examining urban transformation and social history.

## Key Works

- **[RMB City](https://www.serpentinegalleries.org/whats-on/cao-fei/)** (2007-2011) — Virtual city constructed in Second Life exploring Chinese urbanization, labor, and digital existence. Visionary work examining the meeting of virtual and physical worlds.
- **[Whose Utopia?](https://www.serpentinegalleries.org/whats-on/cao-fei/)** (2006) — 20-minute video examining automation and factory labor. Screened at major international venues.
- **[Nova](https://www.serpentinegalleries.org/whats-on/cao-fei/)** (2019) — Film exploring urban transformation and social history of Beijing's Jiuxianqiao district.
- **[La Town](https://www.serpentinegalleries.org/whats-on/cao-fei/)** (2014) — 41'58" stop-motion animation presenting post-apocalyptic cityscape through cinematic lens.
- **[Asia One](https://www.serpentinegalleries.org/whats-on/cao-fei/)** (2018) — 63'20" video examining significance of automation on human body and laboring self.
- **[The Eternal Wave](https://www.serpentinegalleries.org/whats-on/cao-fei/)** (2019) — VR work produced with [[Acute Art]], exploring virtual possibilities and domestic spaces.
- **Blueprints** (2020) — Major Serpentine Galleries installation bringing together archival material and furniture from Beijing studio.

## Research Taste Analysis

### Conceptual Strategy
**Virtual World Ethnography** — Cao Fei doesn't just use digital tools; she treats virtual spaces like Second Life as genuine social environments worthy of anthropological study. RMB City wasn't a digital artwork but a functioning virtual city examining real urbanization questions.

**Present-Future Telescoping** — Her work consistently examines current technology through post-apocalyptic lens (La Town) or utopian speculation (RMB City), using science fiction methodology to understand present transformation.

### Career Architecture  
**Cross-Platform Validation** — Moves fluidly between art world (Venice Biennale, MoMA) and technology discourse, using each context to legitimate the other. Her work gains art world credibility through museum shows while bringing artistic critique to technology spaces.

**Geographic Bridge-Building** — Deliberately positions between Chinese art scene and international institutions, translating local Chinese digital experience for global audience while maintaining roots in Beijing art community.

### Methodology Signature
**Virtual Reality as Research Tool** — Uses VR and virtual worlds not as entertainment but as investigative medium. The Eternal Wave and RMB City demonstrate how virtual environments can reveal social and political structures.

**Archive-Fiction Hybrid** — Combines rigorous archival research (5 years studying Jiuxianqiao district) with speculative worldbuilding, creating works that are both documentary and imaginative.

### Key Moves (chronological)
1. **Early 2000s: Video Art Foundation** — Studied under Zhang Peili, connected to foundational moment of Chinese video art
2. **2007-2011: RMB City** — Created functioning virtual city in Second Life, establishing virtual worlds as legitimate art medium before it became mainstream
3. **2010: Hugo Boss Prize nomination** — Gained international recognition, legitimizing Chinese digital art in Western institutions  
4. **2015-2020: Museum Integration** — Major shows at MoMA, Guggenheim, Pompidou established her as essential voice in contemporary art canon
