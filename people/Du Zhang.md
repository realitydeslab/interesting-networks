Artist and design researcher at the intersection of XR practice, phenomenology, technofeminism, and posthumanism. Explores how humans and machines co-sense and co-create realities.

## Background
Self-described: "As an artist and design researcher I explore how humans and machines co-sense and co-create realities. Rooted in phenomenology, technofeminism, and XR practice, my work investigates embodiment, perception, and the porous boundaries between digital and physical space. Through my practices I am developing sensing methods that convey embodied knowledge — inviting beings to experience how the body, technology, and environment think and feel together, in the attempt to reclaim agency through poetic, sensory forms of design research and world-making."
