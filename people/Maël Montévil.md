# Maël Montévil

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Created stub. Discovered via [[IRI (Institut de recherche et d'innovation)]] Scientific College. Needs full deep research.

Theoretical biologist at CNRS/ENS Paris. Works at the crossroads of experimental biology, mathematics, and philosophy. Develops rigorous alternatives to computational and reductionist models of life. Key concepts: **historicity** of organisms, **organization** as closure of constraints, **anti-entropy**, **symmetry changes**, **biological measurement**, **disruption**. Member of IRI's Scientific College — connects technical biology to Stiegler's pharmacological framework and the organology of knowledge.

## Background
Trained in physics and mathematics, turned to theoretical biology and philosophy of biology. Researcher (Chargé de recherche) at Centre Cavaillès (CNRS), housed at ENS Paris. Works with Ana Soto and Carlos Sonnenschein (Tufts) on biological theory.

## Domains
- Theoretical biology: what makes organisms irreducible to physics or computation
- Anti-entropy: organisms as historical beings that create new possibilities
- Philosophy of biology: critique of computational/informational models of life
- Disruption biology: how industrial/technological disruption affects biological processes
- Mathematical foundations of biological reasoning
- Organology (Stieglerian framework applied to biological knowledge)

## Key Works
- Work on anti-entropy in organisms — biological systems don't obey statistical mechanics the way physical systems do; they generate new configurations (anti-entropy)
- *"Measurement in biology is methodized by theory"* — on how biological measurement presupposes theoretical frameworks
- Collaboration with IRI on organology of biological knowledge
- 2026 event: *Théoriser le vivant* (Théorize the Living) — conference on new theoretical synthesis in biology
