# Robin Wall Kimmerer

Botanist, author, and member of the Citizen Potawatomi Nation. Author of *[Braiding Sweetgrass: Indigenous Wisdom, Scientific Knowledge, and the Teachings of Plants](https://milkweed.org/book/braiding-sweetgrass/)* (Milkweed Editions, 2013) — one of the most influential books on more-than-human thinking. Founder of the [Center for Native Peoples and the Environment](https://www.esf.edu/cnpe/) at SUNY ESF. Argues for plants as teachers, for the grammar of animacy (using "ki" instead of "it" for non-human beings), and for Indigenous science as rigorous epistemology.

Conversation with [[Jenny Odell]] published in [Orion Magazine](https://orionmagazine.org/article/gift-thinking/): "Gift Thinking" (2025).

## Why Interesting
Central figure in more-than-human design and ecological humanities. Her argument that Western grammar — using "it" for non-human beings — enforces objectification is directly relevant to Amber's more-than-human research. If we develop AI "ki" — entities with presence — how does language shape our relation to them? Her bridge between Indigenous knowledge systems and scientific botany is also a model for non-Western techno-philosophical traditions.
