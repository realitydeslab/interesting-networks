# Federico Campagna

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-27:** Stub created during Lucia Pietroiusti deep research. Multiple appearances in the Fish series (including Part 5, Porto 2022) and Extinction Marathon (2014).

Italian philosopher and writer based in London. Published by **Fitzcarraldo Editions** — the most intellectually prestigious independent publisher in the UK. Appeared in multiple editions of *The Shape of a Circle in the Mind of a Fish* series (curated by [[Lucia Pietroiusti]] and [[Filipa Ramos]]) and the Extinction Marathon (2014). His work develops a **philosophy of existence** that draws on mysticism, cosmology, anarchism, and metaphysics to address questions of reality, technology, and the possible. Key books: *Technic and Magic* (Bloomsbury, 2018) and *Prophetic Culture* (Bloomsbury, 2020).

## Key Works
- *Technic and Magic: The Reconstruction of Reality* (Bloomsbury, 2018) — proposes "magic" as an alternative cosmological framework to "technic" (which structures capitalist/scientific modernity)
- *Prophetic Culture: Recreation for Adolescents* (Bloomsbury, 2020) — imagination and creativity as modes of prophetic knowledge
- Multiple essays in Fitzcarraldo Editions contexts and e-flux

## For Amber
- **Philosophy of technology from the outside.** Campagna's critique of "technic" as the dominant cosmology of modernity is directly relevant to Amber's work on AI and computational culture. *Technic and Magic* is the book to read.
- His repeated presence in the Fish series suggests he bridges ecology and philosophy in a way that's rare and useful.
- Fitzcarraldo connection — useful venue for writing at the theory/fiction/philosophy intersection.

## Why Interesting
His move is to take mysticism *seriously* as a philosophical method — not as irrationalism but as an alternative cosmological framework. In a moment where AI/tech discourse is saturated with "technic" thinking (efficiency, optimization, computation), a philosopher of magic is genuinely contrarian in a useful way.
