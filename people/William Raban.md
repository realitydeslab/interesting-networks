# William Raban

(born 1948) British structural filmmaker and video artist; Manager of [[London Film-Makers' Co-operative (LFMC)]] Workshop 1972–76; key figure in the institutional infrastructure of British experimental film.

## Background
Born 1948, Fakenham. BA painting, Saint Martins School of Art 1971; MA (Fine Art), Reading University 1974. Became Manager of the LFMC Workshop 1972–76 — responsible for the production facility that enabled a generation of structural-materialist filmmakers. Published bi-monthly journal *Filmmakers' Europe* 1977–81. Part-time senior lecturer in Film at Saint Martin's School of Art 1976–89. Reader in Film, University of the Arts London. Member of editorial board, *Vertigo* film magazine.

## Quote
"The films mirror the processes of production constructing the reflexive space in which the audience can be directly engaged."

## Key Communities
- [[London Film-Makers' Co-operative (LFMC)]] — Workshop Manager 1972-76
- [[LUX]] — films distributed by LUX

## Why Interesting
Raban represents the maker-technician hybridity central to the LFMC model: he managed the workshop (enabling others to make films) while also making his own structural films. His later video work demonstrates the 1970s-to-digital trajectory of the structural-materialist tradition.
