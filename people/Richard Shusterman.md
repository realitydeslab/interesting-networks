# Richard Shusterman

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Stub created during Soma Design Research Community deep research.

American pragmatist philosopher, Dorothy F. Schmidt Eminent Scholar Chair at Florida Atlantic University. Founder of somaesthetics — the interdisciplinary field devoted to the critical study and meliorative cultivation of the experience and use of the living body. His philosophy is the direct theoretical foundation for [[Kia Höök]]'s soma design methodology and, by extension, the entire [[Soma Design Research Community]].

## Why Interesting
Shusterman created the philosophical vocabulary that Höök operationalized into design methodology. Without his concept of somaesthetics — the body as site of sensory appreciation and creative self-cultivation, not just physiological mechanism — soma design as a field would not exist. He also connects American pragmatism (Dewey) to European phenomenology (Merleau-Ponty), exactly the philosophical synthesis that soma design needs.

## Key Works
- [**Pragmatist Aesthetics**](https://www.cambridge.org/core/books/pragmatist-aesthetics/A7F58EB4B4D2D1ACA8E19FAF1BBBAB61) (1992) — first major articulation of somaesthetics as a philosophical concept
- [**Practicing Philosophy: Pragmatism and the Philosophical Life**](https://routledge.com) (1997) — pragmatist philosophy as lived practice
- [**Body Consciousness: A Philosophy of Mindfulness and Somaesthetics**](https://www.cambridge.org/core/books/body-consciousness/0B5FA1B4B39E59C2D940E5C7484F1F37) (Cambridge University Press, 2008) — the definitive text for soma design: theory of bodily experience applied to mindfulness and somatic disciplines
- **Somaesthetics entry in Encyclopedia of Human-Computer Interaction** — directly addresses soma design and HCI connection
