# Anna Longo

Italian-French philosopher of mathematics, logic, and AI. Affiliated with Université Paris 1 Panthéon-Sorbonne. Works at the intersection of philosophy of mathematics, continental philosophy, and the theory of artificial intelligence — particularly focused on machine creativity, generative systems, and what computation means philosophically. Guest editor of *Technophany* Vol. 3 No. 2 (2025): *Computational Creativity*. Active in the [[Research Network for Philosophy and Technology]].

## Background
Works on the philosophical foundations of mathematical formalization, computation, and AI — drawing on Deleuze, Badiou, and the philosophy of mathematics. Her approach is distinctive: rather than asking "can AI be creative?", she asks what the philosophical conditions of creativity even are, and whether AI changes those conditions. This makes her work theoretical and foundational rather than descriptive.
