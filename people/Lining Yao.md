# Lining Yao 姚林鋆

Associate Professor at CMU HCI Institute. Leads the [Morphing Matter Lab](https://morphingmatter.cs.cmu.edu/). Invented bioLogic (living bacterial skin as actuators) and Aero-Anima (shape-changing textiles). Former MIT Tangible Media Group (Ph.D. 2017 under Hiroshi Ishii). One of the most cited next-generation TUI researchers.

## Domains
Shape-changing interfaces, bio-hybrid computing, morphing materials, 4D fabrication, wearable electronics, human-material interaction.

## Background
Ph.D. 2017, MIT Media Lab (Tangible Media Group, under [[Hiroshi Ishii]]). Now tenured faculty at CMU. Created some of the most visually compelling work in tangible/material computing — bacterial skin clothing, self-morphing structures.

## Key Works
- **[bioLogic](https://tangible.media.mit.edu/project/biologic/)** (2015) — living natto bacteria as actuators for self-ventilating sportswear. CHI 2015 Best Paper Honorable Mention + Best Talk. Collaboration with New Balance.
- **[Morphing Matter Lab projects](https://morphingmatter.cs.cmu.edu/)** — ongoing research on self-shaping, programmable materials

## Why Interesting
Lining Yao's work directly operationalizes "more-than-human" design — using living organisms as components. The bioLogic work is remarkable because it treats bacteria not as a danger to be sterilized but as an actuator to be cultivated. This is a different ontological relationship to materials. Her work at CMU continues to push at the bio-digital interface.
