# VALIE EXPORT

(Born Waltraud Lehner, 17 May 1940, Linz) Austrian avant-garde artist. Pioneer of feminist performance and Expanded Cinema. One of the defining artists of the Viennese avant-garde of the 1960s–70s. Changed her name from Waltraud Hollinger to VALIE EXPORT in 1967 — a statement of self-determination within the Viennese art scene dominated by the male Actionists.

Best known for **Tapp-und-Tast-Kino** (Tap and Touch Cinema, performed in 10 European cities) and **Aktionshose: Genitalpanik** (Action Pants: Genital Panic, 1968). Co-created **Expanded Cinema** with [[Peter Weibel]], Ernst Schmidt Jr., and Hans Scheugl from 1967 onward — a Vienna-based response to American expanded cinema theory, interrogating the ideological and technological conditions of cinematic representation from a feminist perspective.

Her work includes video installations, computer animations, photography, sculpture, performance, and theoretical publications.

## Why Interesting
Pioneer of feminist media art and performance — a lineage that runs directly from her body-as-medium work in the late 1960s through to contemporary posthuman and cyborg feminism. The Expanded Cinema collaboration with Peter Weibel is a founding document of European media art theory. She was working on technology, embodiment, and power in media at a moment (1967) when none of those concepts had their current names.

## Key Works (selected)
- **Tapp-und-Tast-Kino** (Tap and Touch Cinema, 1968–1971) — audience reaches into a box strapped to her chest to touch her bare breasts; subverts passive cinema spectatorship
- **Aktionshose: Genitalpanik** (Action Pants: Genital Panic, 1968–1969) — crotchless pants performance in art cinema, Munich; confronts gendered spectatorship
- **Expanded Cinema performances** (1967+, with Weibel, Ernst Schmidt Jr., Hans Scheugl) — dissolving film into live performance
- **Syntagma** (1983) — video installation
- **Remote, Remote** (1973) — video work
- Multiple film works, video installations, photography series
- **i=EXPORT** (1968) — performance documentation series

## Awards & Honors
