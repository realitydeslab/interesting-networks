# Antoinette Rouvroy

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Created stub. Discovered via [[IRI (Institut de recherche et d'innovation)]] Scientific College. Needs full deep research.

Belgian legal scholar and philosopher. Senior researcher at CRIDS (Centre de Recherche en Information, Droit et Société), University of Namur. Best known for developing the concept of **"algorithmic governmentality"** (with Thomas Berns) — a Foucauldian analysis of how data-driven systems produce new forms of power that bypass subjectivity itself. One of the most rigorous thinkers connecting continental philosophy (Foucault, Deleuze, Stiegler) to contemporary AI governance.

## Background
Legal scholar turned philosopher. PhD-level work at intersection of law, philosophy, and science & technology studies. Member of IRI's Scientific College, connecting her work directly to Stiegler's pharmacological framework.

## Domains
- Algorithmic governmentality (power through data rather than discipline)
- Data politics and pre-emption
- Philosophy of technology (Foucauldian approach)
- Legal theory of digital subjects
- Critique of "big data" epistemologies
- Biopolitics and digital governance

## Key Works
- *"Algorithmic Governmentality and Prospects of Emancipation"* (2013, with Thomas Berns) — foundational paper on how statistical/algorithmic prediction creates new power without individual address
- *"The Digital Disruption of the Self"* — on how profiling bypasses subjectivity
- Research on "data-driven" governance regimes

## Key Relationships & Collaborators
- **Thomas Berns** — co-developer of algorithmic governmentality concept (Université Libre de Bruxelles)
- **[[Bernard Stiegler]]** — IRI Scientific College connection; Stieglerian framework intersects with her Foucauldian analysis
- **[[IRI (Institut de recherche et d'innovation)]]** — Scientific College member
