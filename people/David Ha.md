## Background
David Ha represents a rare breed in AI research — someone who bridges the conceptual sophistication of artificial life with the computational power of modern deep learning. His career trajectory from Google Brain researcher to founding Sakana AI in Tokyo demonstrates a consistent vision: intelligence emerges not just from scale and data, but from fundamental architectural principles that encode behaviors.

His work consistently challenges the orthodoxy of gradient-based learning, asking instead: what can network architectures accomplish with minimal or random weights? This question connects deeply to evolutionary and biological principles, where structure often precedes learning. Based in Tokyo, he's building Sakana AI as an explicitly nature-inspired alternative to the scaling paradigm dominating Western AI.

## Key Works
- **[World Models](https://worldmodels.github.io/)** (2018, with Jürgen Schmidhuber) — NeurIPS oral presentation. Generative RNN learns compressed spatio-temporal representations of RL environments. Agent trained entirely inside its own world model, then transferred to real environment. Foundational work in model-based RL.
- **[Weight Agnostic Neural Networks](https://weightagnostic.github.io/)** (2019, with Adam Gaier) — NeurIPS. Search for architectures that encode solutions without weight training. Single shared weight parameter across entire network. Challenges the assumption that weights contain intelligence.
- **[Sketch-RNN: A Neural Representation of Sketch Drawings](https://arxiv.org/abs/1704.03477)** (2017) — Recurrent VAE that learns to generate stroke-based drawings from Quick, Draw! dataset. [Interactive demos](https://magenta.tensorflow.org/sketch-rnn-demo) became viral, showing AI drawing completion and style transfer.
- **[Hypernetworks](https://arxiv.org/abs/1609.09106)** (2016, with Andrew Dai, Quoc Le) — ICLR. One network generates weights for another network. Relaxed form of weight-sharing enabling adaptive computation.
- **[EvoJAX: Hardware-Accelerated Neuroevolution](https://github.com/google/evojax)** (2022, with Yujin Tang, Yingtao Tian) — GECCO. Scalable neuroevolution toolkit running on TPU/GPU. Entire evolution pipeline implemented in JAX for massive parallelization.
- **[Pommerman: A Multi-Agent Playground](https://www.pommerman.com/)** (2018, with Cinjon Resnick, Wes Eldridge, Denny Britz, Jakob Foerster, Julian Togelius, Kyunghyun Cho, Joan Bruna) — Multi-agent environment based on Bomberman. NeurIPS competitions 2018-2019.
