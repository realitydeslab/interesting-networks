# Mary Catherine Bateson (1939–2021)

Cultural anthropologist. Daughter of [[Margaret Mead]] and [[Gregory Bateson]]. Grew up at the center of the cybernetics/anthropology world — her parents brought her to the Macy Conferences as a child. Wrote *With a Daughter's Eye* (1984), a memoir of her upbringing by two extraordinary intellectual parents. Her major independent contribution: *Composing a Life* (1989), which applied anthropological life-narrative methods to women's lives, arguing that women's lives are improvisational compositions rather than linear career trajectories. Taught at Harvard, Amherst, and George Mason. President of the [[Institute for Intercultural Studies]] until 2010.
