# Anton Vidokle

Founder and director of [[e-flux]], the most influential platform for contemporary art theory. Artist working in film, video, installation, and curating. The person who turned an email announcement service into the art world's most important theory journal.

## Background
Born 1965, Moscow. Lives and works in New York and Berlin. Co-organized the landmark 1998 group exhibition *The Best Surprise is No Surprise* at Holiday Inn, Chinatown NYC, using email to draw hundreds of guests — the experiment that inspired e-flux. Founded e-flux one month later. In 2008 co-founded e-flux journal with [[Julieta Aranda]] and Brian Kuan Wood.

Artist practice focused on film and video, particularly on Russian Cosmism. Has directed multiple film trilogies. Curated 14th Shanghai Biennale "Cosmos Cinema" (2023). Resident Professor at Ashkal Alwan's Home Workspace Program, Beirut (2013–14). Lectures internationally; contributes to October, Frieze, A Prior.

## Why Interesting
Vidokle is the institutional architect of contemporary art theory publishing. Understanding how e-flux was built — from an email list to a global institution — is a masterclass in platform-building as artistic/intellectual practice. He is also the central node connecting the art world with Russian Cosmism, which has become an important current in critical theory engaging with immortality, space, and collective futures.
