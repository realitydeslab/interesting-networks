# Tomás Saraceno

Argentinian-German artist working at the intersection of art, science, ecology, and multispecies coexistence. Known for spider web research, aerosolar sculptures, and radical reimaginings of human-nonhuman relations. One of the most serious "more-than-human" artists working today. Collaborated with [[Acute Art]] on *Webs of Life* (2020, AR) — an experiment in biodiversity and technodiversity that uses AR to promote arachnophilia and resist the Sixth Mass Extinction.

## Domains
More-than-human design, multispecies coexistence, aerosolar art/travel, spider web research, ecological activism, speculative architecture.

## Background
Trained as architect and artist. Founded [Studio Tomás Saraceno](https://studiotomassaraceno.org/) in Berlin. Developed [Arachnophilia](http://arachnophilia.net/) — a long-term research platform studying spider webs and advocating for spider-human coexistence. Solar-powered aerosolar works explore non-fossil fuel collective travel. Collaborates with scientists, climate activists, and Indigenous communities.

## Key Works
- *Webs of Life* (2020, AR) — with [[Acute Art]] + Arachnophilia. AR spiders (*Bagheera kiplingi*, *Maratus speciosus*) placed in user's environment using phone camera. Users must photograph real spiders to unlock the work. Part of Serpentine *Back to Earth* (2021).
- [*On Air*](https://palaisdetokyo.com/en/exposition/on-air/) — Palais de Tokyo (2018)
- *Aerocene* — aerosolar sculptures floating purely on sun and air
- [*Cosmic Jive with the Pacha, Suns, Spiders, and Friends*](https://studiotomassaraceno.org/) — ongoing spider web research

## Exhibitions (recent)
- Serpentine Gallery, London (2021, *Webs of Life*)
- National Gallery of Singapore (2022, *Visions*)
- Leeum Museum of Art, Seoul (2022, *Kaleidoscope Eyes*)
- PROA, Buenos Aires (2022)

## Why Interesting
Saraceno is the clearest example of "more-than-human" art practice that uses advanced technology (including AR) not to showcase tech but to deepen human-nonhuman relations. His *Webs of Life* AR work literally asks users to look for and photograph real spiders — subverting the digital to reconnect with the physical. This is the kind of more-than-human design Amber is theorizing. Very worth deep-researching.
