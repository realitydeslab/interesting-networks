# Elisa Giaccardi

Professor of Interactive Media Design at TU Delft, founder and leader of the Connected Everyday Lab. Leading theorist and practitioner of more-than-human design approaches, posthuman design research, and biological-HCI. Pioneer in exploring how design can account for multispecies worlds and non-anthropocentric perspectives.

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Initial deep research. Major publications in DIS 2025, CHI 2024, CoDesign 2024.

## Background
**Academic trajectory:** Started in Heritage Studies and Digital Media before evolving into posthuman and more-than-human design research. Her work represents a paradigmatic shift from human-centered design toward approaches that account for multispecies, ecological, and non-human agencies. Based at TU Delft's faculty of Industrial Design Engineering.

**Theoretical grounding:** Draws extensively on feminist new materialism (particularly Karen Barad's agential realism), posthuman theory, and decolonial perspectives. Bridges critical theory with practical design research methodologies.

## Domains
- **More-than-human design** — design methodologies that account for non-human agencies

## Key Works
- **[Diffractive Interfaces: Facilitating Agential Cuts in Forest Data Across More-than-human Scales](https://doi.org/10.1145/3715336.3735404)** (DIS 2025) — with Seowoo Nam, Iohanna Nicenboim. Applies Karen Barad's concepts of diffraction and agential cuts to forest ecosystem data visualization, challenging anthropocentric urban forest management.
- **[Living with Cyanobacteria: Exploring Materiality in Caring for Microbes in Everyday Life](https://doi.org/10.1145/3613904.3642039)** (CHI 2024) — with Jiwei Zhou, Zjenja Doubrovski, Elvin Karana. Designed living cyanobacterial artifacts for domestic air purification, exploring human-microbe care relationships.
- **[In conversation with ghosts: Towards a hauntological approach to decolonial design for/with AI practices](https://doi.org/10.1080/15710882.2024.2320269)** (CoDesign 2024) — with Mugdha Patil, Nazli Cila, Johan Redström. Critiques colonial temporalities in speculative design, proposing hauntology as framework for pluriversal futures.
- **["Try this and see if it works for you": A new perspective on household improvisation and responses from heat pump supply-side actors](https://research.tudelft.nl/en/publications/17cbf376-b4f8-4aa8-b7b3-922eab75029a)** (2025) — with Evert van Beek, Stella Boess, Alessandro Bozzon. Infrastructure and everyday practice.
- **[Does AI need designing? Exploring Design in Clinical AI](https://research.tudelft.nl/en/publications/a523f9f9-a543-430a-a0e1-16c9f8b8beb4)** (2024) — with Somayeh Ranjbar, Jeroen Raijmakers, Peter Lloyd, Somaya Ben Allouch. Critical perspectives on AI in healthcare.
- **"Things as Co-ethnographers"** — influential concept proposing objects as active research participants rather than passive subjects of study
- **"Foundations of Metadesign"** — foundational work on metadesign theory and participatory design approaches

Elisa represents a crucial bridge between critical posthuman theory and practical design research. This theoretical innovation has practical implications for how we design with/for non-human entities.

Her recent work demonstrates sophisticated engagement with feminist new materialism (Karen Barad's agential realism) applied to concrete design problems - from forest data visualization to living microbial interfaces. This combination of theoretical rigor and practical application is rare in the design research field.

The biological-HCI work is particularly significant - designing interfaces with living cyanobacteria represents a genuine more-than-human design practice, not just theory. Her decolonial design work challenges temporal assumptions in speculative design, offering hauntology as a framework for non-colonial futures.
