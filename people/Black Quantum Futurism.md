# Black Quantum Futurism

Multidisciplinary collective (Camae Ayewa + Rasheedah Phillips, Philadelphia) deploying quantum physics as a framework for understanding how marginalised communities experience time. Theory-practice spans: sound/music, installation, community oral history, housing justice advocacy, film, writing, and large-scale public commissions. 2021 CERN Collide Award winners.

## Background

**Rasheedah Phillips** founded [The AfroFuturist Affair](https://www.afrofuturistaffair.com) in 2011 — a web platform and community for writers of colour working in speculative fiction. She graduated from Temple University's Beasley School of Law in 2008 (while working and raising a child), then joined Community Legal Services in Philadelphia, becoming Managing Attorney of the Housing Unit. The AfroFuturist Affair grew into the founding of the Black Quantum Futurism collective in **2014**, with artist and musician **Camae Ayewa** (born November 19, 1981, Aberdeen, Maryland; grew up in public housing; moved to Philadelphia to study photography at the Art Institute).

The collective is formed by two queer Black women in North Philadelphia. Their work insists the philosophical and the material are inseparable: Rasheedah litigates housing cases while theorising temporal dispossession; Camae makes free jazz while composing collective soundscapes. In **June 2016**, they opened the [Community Futures Lab](https://futureslab.community/) in the Sharswood-Blumberg neighbourhood of North Philadelphia — an "Afrofuturist community centre" hosting workshops, a library, oral history documentation, and teach-ins, designed as active resistance to the neighbourhood's rapid gentrification.

The name "Black Quantum Futurism" is both the collective's name and their theoretical framework: a claim that quantum physics — with its non-linear time, superposition, entanglement, and observer-dependent reality — maps onto Afrocentric and indigenous African temporal frameworks better than Western linear time does. Their 2015 book *Black Quantum Futurism: Theory & Practice, Vol. 1* lays out this framework systematically.

- **Quantum temporalities** — non-linear time, temporal sovereignty, dismantling colonial time structures (GMT, legal time, eviction law as time manipulation)

- **Sound / experimental music** — [[Moor Mother]]'s free jazz, noise, experimental hip-hop; BQF collective sound works
- **Housing justice** — Rasheedah's legal practice and writing on how racist housing policy denies Black people their temporal futures

## Ideas & Theory

### Quantum Temporalities — Core Framework
BQF's central claim: quantum mechanics (superposition, entanglement, observer-dependency, non-linear causality) better describes the temporal experience of marginalised communities than Western linear clock-time does.

Key moves:
1. **Slave ships as temporal rupture** — the Middle Passage didn't just displace people in space but collapsed and fractured time. Generational trauma is literally temporal entanglement across past/present/future.
2. **Gentrification as temporal dispossession** — eviction removes you from your neighbourhood's *future*, not just its present. In Philadelphia, 70% of evictees are Black women: this is temporal violence.
3. **GMT / colonial time zones as power** — Greenwich Mean Time and industrial clock-time were tools of colonial control. "Dismantling the Master's Clock" (essay title) echoes Audre Lorde's "master's tools."
4. **African temporal frameworks as quantum** — many indigenous African systems treat time as cyclical, non-linear, ancestral (ancestors present in the now). BQF argues this is structurally closer to quantum mechanics than Newton.

### CPT Symmetry and Violations
In physics: CPT symmetry = laws of nature are unchanged if you simultaneously reverse **Charge** (matter ↔ antimatter), **Parity** (spatial reflection), and **Time** (time reversal). The universe *should* be CPT symmetric, but violations have been observed.

BQF's project: what are the CPT *violations* in human experience? For Black communities:
- **Time violation** — historical trauma echoes forward; futures are foreclosed by past injustice
- **Parity violation** — spatial segregation = unequal spatial reflection
- **Charge violation** — the social "charge" of Blackness as a category creates asymmetries

This is both metaphor and genuine inquiry — they engaged actual CERN physicists studying CPT violation experiments.

### "Time Travel as Liberation"
BQF runs DIY Time Travel workshops and Oral Futures Booths where community members record future visions. The proposition: marginalised communities have been denied their futures (by enslavement, redlining, eviction). Time travel — as imagination, practice, ritual — is a method for reclaiming temporal sovereignty. Not escapism; practical: if you can imagine a future for your neighbourhood, you can organise toward it.

### Quantum Event Mapping
A cartographic practice: mapping communities not in space (traditional neighbourhood map) but in **event-time** — significant temporal events in a community's memory, layered with projected futures. The map becomes a temporal artifact rather than a spatial one. Deployed in Community Futures Lab's [futureslab.community](https://futureslab.community/) interactive map.

## For Amber

1. **🤝 Cooperation** — BQF's Community Futures Lab is a rare example of **participatory temporal design**: asking communities to document their futures, not just their histories. Direct overlap with Amber's participatory/democratic design interest. Potential framing: temporal sovereignty as a design question. Also: the Quantum Temporalities framework is adjacent to Amber's "more-than-human" thread — quantum physics as de-centering the human linear subject.

2. **🏠 Residency** — [Arts at CERN Collide Award](https://arts.cern/programme/collide) is an annual open call. Given Amber's art-science work, this is a strong fit. Next call typically opens autumn.

3. **🎓 Fellowship** — The **Vera List Center Fellowship** (New School, NYC) is worth tracking. The **Creative Capital Award** model (fellowships for project-based artists) is useful to understand the US arts funding landscape. The **Knight Arts + Tech Fellowship** (US-based but networked) is another model.

## Research Taste Analysis

### Conceptual Strategy
**Framework multiplication** at its finest: took quantum physics (technical domain) + Afrocentric temporalities (indigenous philosophy) + housing justice (material politics) and fused them into a framework simultaneously art theory, legal theory, and speculative physics. The result is unciteable in any single discipline and therefore circulates freely across all of them.

**Provocation-first naming**: "Black Quantum Futurism" forces anyone who reads it to confront the juxtaposition of "Blackness" and "quantum physics" — two domains that *shouldn't* be in dialogue according to Western academic silos.

### Career Architecture
**Community infrastructure as platform**: Instead of building a lab or journal, BQF built a *community space* (Futures Lab). A different kind of platform — not institutional but participatory. The community space generates the material (oral futures, memories) that feeds the art, which generates institutional recognition, which funds more community work.

**Dual legitimacy**: Rasheedah maintains her law career (Managing Attorney, Community Legal Services) while building an international art career. She can speak in art *and* legal contexts — cross-pollinating audiences that don't normally meet.

### Methodology Signature
**Scale inversion**: Most artists scale from small to big (community → institution, sequentially). BQF runs both simultaneously. The Community Futures Lab is hyper-local (one neighbourhood in North Philadelphia); documenta fifteen is maximally global. They reinforce each other in real time.

**Speculative documentation**: The Oral Futures Booth methodology is genuinely novel — documenting imagined futures as seriously as documented pasts. Makes the archive speculative and the speculation archival.

### Key Moves
1. **2011**: Found The AfroFuturist Affair → builds community of writers of colour → establishes legitimacy as organiser before making art
2. **2014**: Collective formation with Camae → theory (Rasheedah) + sound/performance (Camae) = the collective form as epistemic claim
3. **2015**: Publish *BQF Theory & Practice Vol. 1* → theoretical foundation published first → gives subsequent art a citeable anchor
4. **2016**: Community Futures Lab opens → plants permanent flag in North Philadelphia → moves from "artist about community" to "artist embedded in community"
5. **2017**: Pew Fellowship + Serpentine Gallery Park Nights → simultaneously Philadelphia local and London international → legitimacy at both extremes simultaneously
6. **2021**: CERN Collide Award → science establishment validates their quantum claims → transforms "quantum as metaphor" into "quantum as serious inquiry"
7. **2022**: documenta fifteen (lumbung model) → the most prestigious contemporary art context validates their community methodology → the lumbung's horizontalist values align structurally with BQF's practice

### What Amber Can Learn
1. **Community infrastructure as research methodology**: The Futures Lab is not just an art space — it's a data collection infrastructure for temporal experience. Amber could build participatory infrastructure where her research questions get *lived*.
2. **Theory first, then community, then institution**: BQF published their theoretical framework before applying for major grants. The theory gave coherence and made subsequent work legible to multiple fields simultaneously.
3. **The collective as epistemic claim**: By working as a duo with genuinely different skill sets, BQF argues (through practice) that the questions they're asking require multiple kinds of expertise. Relevant to Amber's collective agency / distributed systems research.
4. **Science as material, not just metaphor**: The CERN residency is not metaphorical engagement with quantum physics — they read papers, met physicists, engaged actual CPT symmetry experiments. Using science as genuine research material (not just aesthetic inspiration) is a model for Amber's art-science work.
