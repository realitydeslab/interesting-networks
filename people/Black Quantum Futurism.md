# Black Quantum Futurism

Multidisciplinary collective founded by **Camae Ayewa** (musician/artist) and **Rasheedah Phillips** (attorney/writer/artist), Philadelphia-based. Explores intersections of futurism, creative media, DIY aesthetics, and activism in marginalised communities through an **alternative temporal lens** — personal, cultural, familial, and communal cycles of experience. Methods: writing, music, film, visual art, creative research.

**2020 Collide Award** (Arts at CERN × Barcelona): Project *"CPT Symmetry and Violations"* — engaging CERN's research into charge, parity, and time-reversal symmetry to rethink how quantum physics can transform lived experiences of time in marginalised communities. Residency at CERN + Hangar Barcelona.

Rasheedah Phillips also contributed to *In the Spaces Between* (2025, dpr-barcelona), the first Arts at CERN publication.

Camae Ayewa performs as Moor Mother — prominent Philadelphia noise/free jazz/noise/hip-hop artist. Phillips runs the Community Futures Lab (Philadelphia).

**For Amber:** Directly relevant — uses quantum physics to think about collective temporal experience and community futures. Overlap with Amber's work on collective agency and distributed/sovereign AI.
