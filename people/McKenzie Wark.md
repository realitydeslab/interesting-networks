# McKenzie Wark

Media theorist, Situationist scholar, queer theorist, and Marxist cultural critic. One of the most prolific and electric writers in contemporary critical theory. Best known for *A Hacker Manifesto* (Harvard UP, 2004) and extensive work on the Situationist International (*The Beach Beneath the Street*, *The Spectacle of Disintegration*). Has also written intensely personal work on trans experience (*Reverse Cowgirl*). Professor at [The New School for Social Research](https://www.newschool.edu/nssr/). Co-author of *[Excommunication](http://press.uchicago.edu/ucp/books/book/chicago/E/bo14413838.html)* (U Chicago Press, 2013) with [[Alexander Galloway]] and [[Eugene Thacker]]. Part of the "New York school of media theory."

## Key Works
- *[A Hacker Manifesto](https://www.hup.harvard.edu/catalog.php?isbn=9780674015432)* (Harvard UP, 2004) — the "vectoralist class" and information as a mode of production; hackers as a new class producing new kinds of value
- *[Gamer Theory](https://www.hup.harvard.edu/catalog.php?isbn=9780674025196)* (Harvard UP, 2007) — gamespace as the allegory of contemporary life under capitalism
- *[The Beach Beneath the Street](https://www.versobooks.com/books/648-the-beach-beneath-the-street)* (Verso, 2011) — recovery of the Situationist International as radical theory
- *[The Spectacle of Disintegration](https://www.versobooks.com/books/1322-the-spectacle-of-disintegration)* (Verso, 2013) — continuation of Situationist rereading
- *[Excommunication: Three Inquiries in Media and Mediation](http://press.uchicago.edu/ucp/books/book/chicago/E/bo14413838.html)* (U Chicago Press, 2013) — co-authored with [[Alexander Galloway]] and [[Eugene Thacker]]
- *[Molecular Red](https://www.versobooks.com/books/1912-molecular-red)* (Verso, 2015) — Soviet ecology, Bogdanov, unexpected eco-Marxism
- *General Intellects* (Verso, 2017) — twenty-one thinkers for the 21st century
- *Capital Is Dead* (Verso, 2019) — is there something worse than capitalism?
- *Reverse Cowgirl* (Semiotext[e], 2020) — personal/queer theory memoir

## Why Interesting
Part of the Galloway/Thacker/Wark triangle that constitutes New York media theory. His Situationist sequence and Bogdanov work offer a radical historical grounding for thinking about contemporary tech culture. The *A Hacker Manifesto* "vectoralist class" framework is relevant to understanding the class politics of AI platforms.
