# McKenzie Wark

Media theorist, Situationist scholar, queer theorist, and Marxist cultural critic. One of the most prolific and electric writers in contemporary critical theory. Best known for *A Hacker Manifesto* (2004) and extensive work on the Situationist International (*The Beach Beneath the Street*, *The Spectacle of Disintegration*). Has also written intensely personal work on trans experience (*Reverse Cowgirl*). Teaches at The New School.

## Key Works
- *A Hacker Manifesto* (Harvard, 2004) — class analysis of information capitalism; "hackers" as a new class producing new kinds of value
- *Gamer Theory* (Harvard, 2007) — video games as allegories for social conditions under capitalism
- *The Beach Beneath the Street* (Verso, 2011) — recovery of the Situationist International as radical theory not just art
- *The Spectacle of Disintegration* (Verso, 2013) — continuation of Situationist rereading
- *Molecular Red* (Verso, 2015) — on Soviet ecology and Platonov; unexpected eco-Marxism
- *General Intellects* (Verso, 2017) — twenty-one thinkers for the 21st century
- *Capital Is Dead* (Verso, 2019) — is there something worse than capitalism?
- *Reverse Cowgirl* (Semiotext[e], 2020) — personal/queer theory
