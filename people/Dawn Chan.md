# Dawn Chan

Curator and writer working at the art-technology intersection. Co-curator (with [[Lauren Cornell]], [[Xue Tan]], Tobias Berger, and Jeppe Ugelvig) of *[Phantom Plane: Cyberpunk in the Year of the Future](https://www.taikwun.hk/en/programme/detail/phantom-plane-cyberpunk-in-the-year-of-the-future/467)* (Tai Kwun Contemporary, Hong Kong, 2019). Former staff critic/writer at Artforum.

## Domains
- Contemporary art and technology criticism
- Internet and post-internet art
- Curatorial practice

## Background
Chan worked as a critic and editor at Artforum before moving into curatorial practice. Her writing addresses art's relationship to digital culture, technology, and media.

## Key Works
- ***[Phantom Plane: Cyberpunk in the Year of the Future](https://www.taikwun.hk/en/programme/detail/phantom-plane-cyberpunk-in-the-year-of-the-future/467)*** (co-curated with Lauren Cornell, Xue Tan, Tobias Berger, Jeppe Ugelvig; Tai Kwun Contemporary, Hong Kong, 2019) — group exhibition examining cyberpunk's aesthetics as an index of the present.

## Key Relationships

## Why Interesting
Chan bridges critical writing (Artforum) and curatorial practice, operating in the same art-technology space as Cornell. Her involvement in *Phantom Plane* suggests an ongoing collaborative relationship around technology and visual culture.
