# Holly Herndon

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Deep research conducted. Expanded from stub to comprehensive profile.

Pioneer of AI-human musical collaboration and AI ethics advocate. Composer-researcher who treats AI as creative collaborator rather than tool. Co-founder of Spawning, advancing artist consent in AI training.

## Background
Born in Tennessee. Started with traditional German studies before pivoting to experimental electronic music. **MFA Music, Mills College** — studied under experimental electronic music pioneers. **PhD Music Composition (Computer Music), Stanford University** — doctorate focused on the intersection of voice, technology, and live performance. This academic foundation is crucial: her AI work has theoretical rigor because she's trained as a computer music researcher, not just an artist using AI tools.

Moved to Berlin in early 2010s, immersing in the city's experimental electronic music scene. The geographical shift from US academic contexts to Berlin underground was key to developing her aesthetic.

## Key Works
- **[PROTO](https://hollyherndon.bandcamp.com/album/proto)** (2019, 4AD) — Album created with "Spawn," an AI trained on her voice and that of her collaborators. Spawn is credited as a band member. Features choir of human and AI voices. Breakthrough work in AI-human creative collaboration.
- **[Platform](https://hollyherndon.bandcamp.com/album/platform)** (2015, 4AD) — Album exploring digital intimacy, surveillance, and online presence
- **[Movement](https://hollyherndon.bandcamp.com/album/movement)** (2012, RVNG Intl) — Debut album combining laptop composition with processed vocals
- **[Holly+](https://holly.plus/)** — AI deepfake of her voice released as open-source tool. Anyone can use it but must pay to her DAO. Radical experiment in posthuman identity and digital consent.
- **[Spawning](https://spawning.ai/)** — Co-founded AI consent platform. [Have I Been Trained](https://haveibeentrained.com/) lets artists check if their work was used in AI training datasets.
- **Infinite Scroll** (2021) — Installation piece about digital attention
