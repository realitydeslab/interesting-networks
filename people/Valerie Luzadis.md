# Valerie Luzadis

Ecological economist at SUNY-ESF; past president of the US Society for Ecological Economics. Key intellectual interlocutor for [[Robin Wall Kimmerer]] in developing the gift economy / serviceberry economy argument. Appears centrally in Kimmerer's essay "The Serviceberry" (Emergence Magazine, 2022) validating botanical gift-economy intuitions from an economic theory perspective.

Ecological economics = integrates Earth's natural systems and human values into economic theory, as opposed to environmental economics (which just tallies trade-offs of wrecking vs. restoring ecosystems). Framing: "economics is how we organize ourselves to sustain life and enhance its quality."

## Why Interesting
Bridge between ecological economics and Indigenous gift economy theory. The Kimmerer/Luzadis collaboration shows how TEK and ecological economics converge — both critique scarcity-as-primary-principle and propose reciprocity as the economic logic of living systems.
