# Sally Engle Merry

Feminist legal anthropologist at NYU. Co-editor with [[Richard Rottenburg]] of *A World of Indicators* (Cambridge UP, 2015) — the key theoretical intervention on how quantitative metrics become governing technologies. Pioneer of indicator studies and human rights quantification.

## Domains
- Legal anthropology
- Human rights and quantification
- Gender violence and indicators
- STS and law

## Key Works
- *A World of Indicators* (co-ed. with Rottenburg, Park, Mugler; Cambridge UP, 2015)
- *The Seductions of Quantification* (U Chicago Press, 2016)
- *Human Rights and Gender Violence* (U Chicago Press, 2006)

## Why Interesting
Merry's work on "the seductions of quantification" — how metrics seduce by appearing neutral while encoding values — is directly applicable to AI/ML governance. She provides the feminist and legal anthropology lens on the indicator/quantification world that Rottenburg studies from the STS side.
