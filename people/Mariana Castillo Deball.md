# Mariana Castillo Deball

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-27:** Stub created during MUAC deep research.
- **2021–22:** Major exhibition at [[MUAC]] (Oct 16, 2021 – May 1, 2022).
- **2021:** Sala10 show at MUAC.
- **2020–21:** Featured in MUAC's "Palabras Clave" digital series — episode on *ixiptla*.

Mexican-German artist (based Mexico City/Berlin) working with **pre-Columbian concepts, archival documentary practices, and the politics of representation and historical inheritance**. Her MUAC work explored the Nahua concept of ***ixiptla*** (from *xip*: skin/covering + *ixtli*: eyes/face/surface of a conscious being) — a representation, replica, or image that is also a presence and a skin.

Her practice reappropriates history through the study of *ixiptlas* (copies/replicas of pre-Columbian sculptures, documents, and historical monuments), resignifying their new identities. The question: what happens when a copy becomes a new original? How do historical objects get reactivated in contemporary contexts?

Key approach: documentary practice + archaeological knowledge + speculative reimagination. She makes artist's books, installations, and works that function simultaneously as art objects and archival propositions.

## Why Interesting
For Amber: Castillo Deball's work on representation, copies, and identity is adjacent to questions of AI simulation, training data, and what it means for a copy to carry "presence." The *ixiptla* concept — a representation that is also a skin, an image that is also a being — is philosophically interesting for thinking about LLMs and synthetic media. Her archival-documentary practice is a model for how to do rigorous research-based art from a non-Western conceptual framework.
