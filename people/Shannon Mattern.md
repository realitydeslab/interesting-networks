# Shannon Mattern

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Created stub note. Discovered as [[Eyeo Festival]] speaker — critical media theorist deeply relevant to Amber's infrastructure/computation intersection.

Media theorist, urban intelligence researcher, essayist. Professor at The New School. Writes about the intelligence embedded in cities, buildings, libraries, and material infrastructures. Best known for placing computation and data within their physical, historical, and political context — treating "smart cities" as historical artifacts, not innovations.

Key books:
- *[Code and Clay, Data and Dirt: Five Thousand Years of Urban Media](https://www.upress.umn.edu/book-division/books/code-and-clay-data-and-dirt)* (2017) — Ancient cities as media infrastructure.
- *[A City Is Not a Computer](https://placesjournal.org/article/a-city-is-not-a-computer/)* (Places Journal, 2017) — Widely read essay against smart city techno-optimism.
- *[The Library as Infrastructure](https://placesjournal.org/article/library-as-infrastructure/)* (Places Journal, 2014)
- *[New York, Smart City](https://mitpress.mit.edu/books/new-york-smart-city)* — MIT Press.

Regular contributor to *Places Journal* and *e-flux Architecture*.

## Why Interesting
Mattern's work is an elegant bridge between media theory and computation — she asks what it means for a city to "know" something, what counts as "intelligence" at an infrastructure scale. This is adjacent to Amber's interest in non-human computation, agent behaviors at scale, and cosmotechnics. The framing of "urban intelligence" resonates with questions about what computation looks like outside of individual AI agents.
