# Mar Canet Solà

Artist-researcher and PhD student at [[CUDAN Open Lab]] (Baltic Film, Media and Arts School, Tallinn University). Explores new methods and interfaces for navigating the latent space of AI models through artistic practice. Works across generative AI art, biometric interactive systems, and physical-digital materiality.

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-27:** Stub created from CUDAN deep research.
- **2024:** Publications on gaze-controlled latent space navigation, interactive knitting with brain data, urban media art.

## Domains

- Generative AI in interactive systems

## Background
MA from Interface Cultures, University of Art and Design Linz (Austria) — media art tradition with technical rigor. BA in Art and Design (ESDI Barcelona) + BSc Computer Game Development (University of Central Lancashire, UK). This unusual combination — European media art tradition + UK game development + Austrian interface design — produces genuinely hybrid practice. PhD at CUDAN under BFM school.

## Key Works
- **"Visions of Destruction"** — Canet Solà & Guljajeva (TEI 2024, VINCI 2024, SIGGRAPH Asia 2023). Gaze-controlled navigation of a diffusion model's latent space to explore human impact on nature. Interactive art installation.
- **"Neuroknitting Beethoven"** — Canet Solà, Guljajeva, Kuzmin (2023, *International Journal of Performance Arts and Digital Media*). Telematic knitting performance using EEG data during pandemic.
- **"We Are The Clouds"** — Guljajeva & Canet Solà (arXiv, 2024). Urban media art + participatory interaction.
- **AI-Aided Ceramic Sculptures** — Canet Solà & Guljajeva (EvoMUSART 2023). Bridging deep learning with physical ceramic materiality.
- **Collection Space Navigator** — co-creator with Tillmann Ohm; see [[Tillmann Ohm]].

## Key Relationships

- **Varvara Guljajeva** — sustained artistic collaboration on interactive/generative works

## Why Interesting
Canet Solà is doing exactly the work at the art-AI interface that Amber is interested in: not AI as a tool to make images, but AI latent spaces as navigable territories, and physical/biological interfaces (gaze, EEG) as the navigation method. The ceramic work is particularly interesting — using deep learning to design physical objects that carry the AI's aesthetic logic into material form. For Amber: potential direct collaboration; the latent space navigation work overlaps significantly with questions about AI aesthetics and machine creativity.
