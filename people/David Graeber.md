# David Graeber

American-British anthropologist and anarchist organizer. Died September 2020, age 59. One of the most influential radical intellectuals of the 21st century. Best known for *Debt: The First 5,000 Years* (Melville House, 2011) — a 5,000-year history of debt that argues money and markets did not precede or enable states, but were products of them; debt is fundamentally a *moral* and *political* category before it is an economic one. Co-organized Occupy Wall Street's Strike Debt campaign with [[Astra Taylor]] and others. Reportedly coined "We are the 99%."

Also wrote *Bullshit Jobs: A Theory* (Simon & Schuster, 2018) — argues that a huge proportion of jobs in modern economies are meaningless even to the people doing them, deliberately maintained because egalitarian work-sharing would undermine the social order.

## Why Interesting
Graeber's *Debt* provides the deep historical framework for understanding why [[Astra Taylor]]'s Debt Collective works politically: debt is not a neutral financial instrument but a power relation. His anthropological analysis shows debt has always been a mechanism of subjugation — which is why collective refusal (debt strikes) is genuinely revolutionary.

His *Bullshit Jobs* argument is relevant to AI automation debates: if the economy already maintains meaningless jobs for social control reasons, "AI replacing jobs" may produce more bullshit jobs, not fewer.
