# meson press

**[meson press](https://meson.press)** is an open-access academic press publishing research on digital cultures and networked media. Co-founded by **[[Mercedes Bunz]]**, Marcus Burkhardt, and Andreas Kirchner. Grew out of the **Hybrid Publishing Lab at Leuphana University of Lüneburg** (2012–2014). Organized as a cooperative.

91 titles to date. Books are published under Creative Commons CC-BY-SA license where possible; print-on-demand distributed worldwide, digital editions free to download.

Member of **[ScholarLed](https://scholarled.org/)** — a consortium of Open Access presses — and the **[Radical Open Access Collective](http://radicaloa.disruptivemedia.org.uk/)**.

Co-published *Communication* (Paula Bialski, Finn Brunton, Mercedes Bunz, 2019) with University of Minnesota Press — demonstrating hybrid North American / European distribution.

## Why Interesting
meson press is a model for scholar-led infrastructure: academics who, frustrated with the academic publishing industry, built their own press rather than waiting for commercial publishers to do it right. The cooperative structure + CC licensing + print-on-demand is a replicable model. For digital humanities scholars wanting to reach global audiences without APC fees, it's worth knowing. Also: as a Leuphana spinoff, it carries traces of the Lüneburg media theory scene (Norbert Bolz, Friedrich Kittler connections to that milieu).
