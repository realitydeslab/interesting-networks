# Harold Cohen

Creator of AARON (1973–2016) — the longest-running generative art system in history. A painter who turned to computing in the 1960s, Cohen spent four decades developing AARON: a rule-based system that evolved from simple line drawings to complex figure compositions with colour and texture. Exhibited globally including at the Tate, SFMOMA, and major international galleries.

## Key Works

## Key Insight: Cohen resisted calling AARON "creative"
Cohen repeatedly refused to call AARON creative — he saw himself as the creative agent, with AARON as an incredibly sophisticated tool/collaborator. This makes AARON the canonical case study for debates about **where creativity resides**: in the programmer, the system, or the system's outputs.

## Why Interesting
AARON is the historical anchor for all CC discourse. Every discussion of machine creativity references it. Cohen's philosophical position (refusing to ascribe creativity to AARON) is a useful counterpoint — and raises questions directly relevant to Amber's work: if a machine does something creative, but doesn't know it's doing it, is it creative?
