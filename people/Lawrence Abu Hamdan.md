# Lawrence Abu Hamdan لورانس أبو حمدان

Contemporary artist, researcher, filmmaker, and audio investigator. Describes himself as a "private ear." Works on the **politics of listening** — sound as legal evidence, voice as testimony, architecture as acoustic witness. Founded [Earshot](https://earshot.io/), the first agency for sound and acoustic analysis dedicated to open-source investigation and human rights. **2019 Turner Prize co-winner.** Work in collections of MoMA, Guggenheim, Tate, Centre Pompidou, Museo Reina Sofía.

## Background

Born 1985 in Amman, Jordan, to a Lebanese Druze father and an English mother. The Abu Hamdan family is from the Chouf in Lebanon — third generation to live in Jordan due to armed conflict. Great-grandparents were resistance fighters against French colonialism. The family is Druze, a minority that believes in reincarnation — a theme that recurs in his art (the story of Bassel Abi Chahine in *Natq*).

When he was seven, parents divorced. Moved with his mother and brother Shakeeb to Yorkshire, UK. Attended [Bootham School](https://www.boothamschool.com/) (Quaker boarding school in York). His first experience of linguistic displacement — answering every question with "What?" — led his mother to take him for a hearing test: "my first time in an audio laboratory."

**Punk years:** In the 2000s, played in DIY bands in Yorkshire — Isambard Kingston Brunel, Poltergroom, and **Cleckhuddersfax** (released on [Upset The Rhythm](https://utr.london/)). Abu Hamdan was lead vocalist. The punk/DIY ethos of anti-industry, anti-capitalism, self-organization runs through everything he does.

**Education:** Moved to Leeds, organized noise-music shows. Enrolled at **Middlesex University** for **Sonic Arts** — combining acoustic phenomena and artistic experimentation. Then encountered [[Eyal Weizman]]'s work at Manifesta 7 in Italy. Told Weizman he was interested in sound and architecture. "What sound? What architecture?" Weizman countered. Followed Weizman to **[Goldsmiths, University of London](https://www.gold.ac.uk/)**, Centre for Research Architecture. Part of the **first cohort** (2009) that became [[Forensic Architecture]] — alongside Lorenzo Pezzani (ocean/borders), Nabil Ahmed and Paulo Tavares (environmental violence). PhD completed 2017 in forensic sound analysis. Dissertation still circulated among students — Weizman calls it "Lawrence Abu Hamdan Studies."

**Professorship:** J. Gutenberg University, Mainz.

**Married to Nora** (curator), two daughters. Family splits time between Dubai/Beirut and London.

Represented by **[Galerie Mor Charpentier](https://www.mor-charpentier.com/artist/lawrence-abu-hamdan/)**, Paris.

## Key Works

### Investigative Works
- **[Saydnaya (The Missing 19dB)](https://forensic-architecture.org/investigation/saydnaya)** (2017) — Sound piece examining how whispers of Syrian prison detainees dropped 19 decibels after 2011, marking transformation from prison to death camp. Commissioned by [Sharjah Art Foundation](https://www.sharjahart.org/). Core collaboration with [[Forensic Architecture]] for [Amnesty International](https://www.amnesty.org/).
- **[Rubber Coated Steel](https://lawrenceabuhamdan.com)** (2016) — Film about killings of Nadeem Nawara and Mohammad Abu Daher, two Palestinian teenagers shot by Israeli border police. Abu Hamdan's audio analysis proved live ammunition was fired from rubber-bullet extension. Led to first-ever indictment of an Israeli officer for killing a Palestinian child. **Won short film award at [Rotterdam International Film Festival](https://iffr.com/) 2017.**
- **[Earshot (Portikus installation)](http://www.portikus.de/en/exhibitions/199_earshot)** (2016) — Reflects on the Nawara/Abu Daher case. Exhibited at [Portikus](http://www.portikus.de/), Frankfurt. Won the **Nam June Paik Award**.
- **Hind Rajab investigation** (2024) — Earshot commissioned by Washington Post to investigate killing of 6-year-old Hind Rajab in Gaza. Audio ballistic analysis suggested IDF tanks were present and likely fired at the family, contradicting IDF claims. Featured in Al Jazeera documentary *The Night Won't End*.
- **Al-Ahli Hospital investigation** (2023-2024) — Earshot's Doppler analysis cast doubt on Israel's claim that a misfired Palestinian rocket caused the hospital explosion. [[Forensic Architecture]] published full investigation.
- **Nahel Merzouk investigation** (2023) — Audio analysis of the killing of a 17-year-old in Nanterre, France by police. Earshot re-created the incident acoustically.
- **Reuters journalists attack** (2023) — Earshot investigated attack on journalists in southern Lebanon. Drone analysis showed the aircraft circled journalists at least 11 times in 46 minutes before the attack.

### Art Works
- **[Walled Unwalled](https://www.tate.org.uk/whats-on/turner-contemporary/turner-prize-2019/lawrence-abu-hamdan)** (2018) — Short film. Abu Hamdan presents cases where auditory evidence was collected through walls (Oscar Pistorius case, etc.). Installed at Tate Modern. Possibly his most acclaimed work.
- **[After SFX](https://lawrenceabuhamdan.com)** (2018) — Live performance first at Tate Modern. Uses Foley objects to reconstruct sounds from investigations — pita bread hitting floor (Saydnaya hunger), popping lice (loudest sound in prison silence).
- **[Earwitness Theatre](https://chisenhale.org.uk/exhibition/lawrence-abu-hamdan/)** (2018) — Installation of ~100 objects used to describe sounds in legal cases. Co-commissioned by [Chisenhale Gallery](https://chisenhale.org.uk/), London.
- **[Air Pressure](https://lawrenceabuhamdan.com)** (2021) — Hour-long live audio essay analyzing Israeli violations of Lebanese airspace (22,000+ incursions 2007-2022). "Not loud enough to be terrifying, but frequent enough to fuel a near-constant dread."
- **[Natq](https://lawrenceabuhamdan.com)** (2019) — Monologue about Bassel Abi Chahine, a Druze man who believes he is a reincarnated child soldier — using reincarnation testimony to traverse generational silence about Lebanon's civil war.
- **The Whole Truth** (2012) — Radio documentary exploring voice-based lie-detection technologies.
- **Aural Contract: The Freedom of Speech Itself** (2012) — Solo show at [The Showroom](https://www.theshowroom.org/), London.
- **[Taqiyya — The Right to Duplicity](https://kunsthallesanktgallen.ch/)** (2015) — Solo at Kunsthalle St. Gallen. Explores the Druze/Shia concept of permissible dissimulation.
- **[Earwitness Inventory](https://brooklynrail.org/2021/12/criticspage/Ear-Witness)** — Ongoing archive/sound-effects library designed to solicit earwitness testimony for legal cases. "Both a sound-effects library and a repository for acoustic experience."

## Venue Circuit Analysis

Abu Hamdan moves across **five distinct circuits**, each serving a different function:

### 1. UK Institutional (home base)
Tate Modern, Chisenhale Gallery, The Showroom, Turner Contemporary, Whitechapel Gallery, Towner Eastbourne, Spike Island Bristol, British Art Show. This is where he built his career — the Chisenhale→Tate→Turner trajectory is a classic UK art-world ascent.

### 2. European Institutional
Hamburger Bahnhof (Berlin), Portikus (Frankfurt), Secession (Vienna), Bonniers Konsthall (Stockholm), Witte de With (Rotterdam), MUDAM (Luxembourg), Moderna Museet (Stockholm), MACBA (Barcelona), Centre Pompidou (Paris), Kunsthalle St. Gallen, Van Abbemuseum, Frac Franche-Comté. The DAAD residency and Nam June Paik Award anchored his German circuit. Represented by Galerie Mor Charpentier (Paris).

### 3. Biennial Circuit (global visibility)
Venice, Berlin, São Paulo, Sydney, Gwangju, Liverpool, Shanghai, Toronto, Manifesta, Sharjah, Contour. He participates in the most prestigious biennials worldwide — this is where the art world encounters his investigative work.

### 4. Gulf/MENA Circuit
Sharjah Art Foundation (multiple solo shows + biennials), Jameel Arts Centre (Dubai), Sfeir-Semler Gallery (Beirut/Hamburg), Beirut Art Center. Based in Dubai. His Lebanese/Jordanian heritage gives him deep roots here. Sharjah is particularly important — they commissioned *Saydnaya*.

### 5. US Institutional
MoMA (New York), Hammer Museum (LA), Contemporary Art Museum St. Louis, New Museum Triennial, Vera List Center (New School), Gray Center (UChicago). His US presence is institutional-academic rather than commercial.

**Pattern analysis:** Unlike artists who stick to one circuit, Abu Hamdan's **dual practice** (investigation + art) gives him access to both art-world and human-rights/academic circuits simultaneously. The small/niche venues he chooses — Chisenhale, The Showroom, Portikus, Casco — are all known for commissioning ambitious, research-heavy work. His gallery (Mor Charpentier) is a mid-size Paris gallery with a political bent, not a mega-gallery — consistent with his anti-commercial ethos rooted in punk DIY culture.

## Why Interesting

Abu Hamdan embodies the **ultimate art-science-politics convergence**: his artworks are literally legal evidence, his investigations literally become art, and neither practice dilutes the other. This is rare — most "research-based art" is either watered-down research or didactic art. Abu Hamdan's work succeeds in both registers because he takes **sound as a genuinely independent epistemological domain**, not a metaphor for something else.

For Amber's interest in **more-than-human** sensing: Abu Hamdan's work treats buildings, walls, air, and objects as witnesses — the architecture itself testifies through its acoustic properties. Saydnaya prison "speaks" through the sound memories of its former inmates. This is more-than-human witnessing in the most literal and urgent sense.

For **critical computing**: Earshot uses computational tools (spectral analysis, Doppler effect modeling, electromagnetic analysis) in service of human rights — turning the surveillance apparatus against the state. His Vera List project on ShotSpotter directly critiques algorithmic surveillance.

For **edge of chaos**: Sound investigation is exploding as a field right now — deepfakes, AI-generated audio, conflict documentation via cell phones. Abu Hamdan is at the exact center of this emerging domain.

## Research Taste Analysis

### Conceptual Strategy
- **Sensory monopoly**: Abu Hamdan claimed an entire sensory domain — sound/listening — as his territory within counter-forensics. While [[Eyal Weizman]] took architecture/space and others took ocean/environment, Abu Hamdan took the ear. This is **field declaration through sensory specificity**: narrower than "forensic architecture" but inexhaustible in application.
- **Dual-register practice**: Every investigation produces both legal evidence AND art. This isn't opportunism — it's structural. The art explores what the investigation can't say (the excess, the political context, the structural violence), while the investigation grounds the art in verifiable claims. Neither is secondary.
- **Concept coinage**: "Private ear," "earwitness," "politics of listening," "counter-forensics" — each becomes a citeable, memorable label.

### Career Architecture
- **Apprenticeship→Agency pipeline**: 2009-2023 inside [[Forensic Architecture]] → 2023 founded Earshot. Fourteen years of proving the methodology before institutionalizing it. This patience is strategic — he had the Turner Prize, the MoMA shows, the legal precedents before going independent.
- **Prize accumulation as legitimacy**: Nam June Paik (new media credibility) → Abraaj (Gulf credibility) → Turner (UK institutional peak) → Edvard Munch (Scandinavian circuit) → Future Fields Commission (US institutional). Each prize opens a different geographic/institutional door.
- **Strategic solidarity**: Sharing the Turner Prize was simultaneously an anti-competitive political gesture AND a massive media story. It made Abu Hamdan more visible than winning alone would have.

### Methodology Signature
- **Case-based practice**: Every artwork starts from a specific investigation — not abstract concepts but real events with real stakes (Saydnaya, Nawara/Abu Daher, Hind Rajab). This grounds the work.
- **Foley as epistemology**: Using everyday objects to re-create sounds from testimony is both an investigative technique and a performance methodology. The Earwitness Inventory is simultaneously a forensic tool, an archive, and an art installation.
- **Live audio essay**: Abu Hamdan invented a new performance form — the monologue-investigation-sound-piece hybrid. Published as *Live Audio Essays* (Primary Information, 2023).

### Network Strategy
- **Institutional hedging**: Based in Dubai but operating from London (FA offices), showing in Paris (gallery), teaching in Chicago, performing at MoMA. No single institutional dependency.
- **NGO partnerships**: Amnesty International, Human Rights Watch, B'Tselem, Defense for Children International. These aren't just clients — they validate the investigative work and connect to policy/legal circuits outside the art world.
- **Media partnerships**: Washington Post, Al Jazeera, ITV. Mainstream media commissions extend reach far beyond art audiences.

### Key Moves (chronological)
1. **2009: Joined Goldsmiths CRA first cohort** — found the intellectual home and the collaborative structure (FA) that would define his career. Timing: Weizman was just formalizing counter-forensics.

### What Amber Can Learn
- **Claim a sensory/methodological territory early**: Abu Hamdan didn't do "forensics broadly" — he did *sound* forensics. Amber should similarly claim a specific intersection (e.g., "machine ethology" or "computational more-than-human sensing") and own it.
- **Build the dual-register practice**: Make every research project function in two domains — academic AND artistic, or academic AND public. The second platform multiplies impact without extra work.
- **13-year incubation before institutionalization**: Abu Hamdan spent over a decade inside FA before founding Earshot. Don't rush to build infrastructure — prove the methodology first through visible casework.
- **Use prizes/awards as geographic expansion tools**: Each prize opened a new circuit. Be strategic about which awards to pursue based on which networks they unlock.
- **The solidarity move**: Abu Hamdan's Turner Prize sharing was genius positioning — it made him more famous AND more politically credible. Look for moments where generosity IS strategy.
- **Case-based, not concept-based**: Start from specific, real situations. The conceptual framework emerges from the cases, not the other way around. This makes the work unimpeachable.
- **Punk→PhD→Agency is a viable trajectory**: Abu Hamdan went from DIY music scenes to PhD to independent agency without losing the anti-institutional ethos. The DIY spirit survived institutional contact.
