# Lawrence Abu Hamdan

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Created stub during Forensic Architecture deep research. Flagged as ⭐⭐⭐ candidate — queued for full deep research.

Contemporary artist and researcher based in Dubai. Describes himself as a "private ear." Works on the **political effects of listening** — sound as legal evidence, voice as testimony, architecture as acoustic witness. PhD at the Centre for Research Architecture, Goldsmiths — part of the [[Forensic Architecture]] orbit.

**2019 Turner Prize winner** (jointly with Helen Cammock, Oscar Murillo, Tai Shani) — for *Walled Unwalled* (2018, Tate Modern) and the Saydnaya investigation. The jury gave the prize to all four nominees jointly in response to the political moment.

Founded **[Earshot](https://earshot.io/)** — "the first agency for sound and acoustic analysis dedicated to open-source investigators and the field of human rights." Earshot is essentially the sonic arm of counter-forensics. Launched 2023.

Fellow at University of Chicago. Work in collections of MoMA, Guggenheim, Tate, Centre Pompidou, Museo Reina Sofía.

## Why Interesting
Abu Hamdan is extraordinary because he treats **sound as architecture** and **listening as a political act**. His Saydnaya work with FA — reconstructing a secret Syrian prison from the sonic memories of former detainees — is one of the most remarkable examples of non-human witnessing: the building itself becomes the witness, mediated through acoustic memory.

For Amber: he sits exactly at the intersection of more-than-human sensing (sounds, acoustics, non-visual environments), critical computing (open-source investigation tools for human rights), and art-science (exhibitions that are also legal evidence). His Earshot project is a direct institutional model for how to build a research agency around a specific sensory/computational methodology.
