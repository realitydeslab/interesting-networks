# Casey Reas

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Created stub note. Discovered through **[[Golan Levin]]** research as fellow Maeda ACG alumnus and co-creator of Processing.

Artist and educator. Co-creator (with Ben Fry) of **[Processing](https://processing.org/)** — the open-source creative coding environment that has transformed arts education globally. Professor at UCLA Design Media Arts. Fellow alumnus of John Maeda's Aesthetics and Computation Group at MIT Media Lab alongside [[Golan Levin]]. His generative/algorithmic art practice has been exhibited at major institutions worldwide.

## Key Works
- **[Processing](https://processing.org/)** (co-created with Ben Fry, 2001–present) — The foundational open-source creative coding environment. Free, Java-based. Used by millions globally. Stewarded by the [[Processing Foundation]].
- Generative paintings and software works — exhibited at MoMA, Whitney, ZKM, Ars Electronica
- **[Form + Code in Design, Art, and Architecture](https://formandcode.com/)** (book, with Chandler McWilliams) — Princeton Architectural Press, 2010

## Key Communities
- **[[Processing Foundation]]** — Co-founder
- **[[MIT Media Lab]]** — ACG alum (Maeda group)
- **[[UCLA]]** — Faculty, Design Media Arts

## Why Interesting
Processing is the most important piece of infrastructure in computational art education. As its co-creator, Reas has shaped how an entire generation of artists thinks about code. This makes him a field-defining figure — not just an artist but an infrastructure builder.
