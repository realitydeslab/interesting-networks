# Safiya Umoja Noble

Information studies scholar and author of *Algorithms of Oppression* (2018), which documented how Google's search algorithm surfaces racist and sexist content when searching for Black women. Co-founder of the UCLA Center on Race and Digital Space. MacArthur Fellow (2021). One of the most widely read critics of commercial algorithmic systems.

## Domains

- Black feminist technology criticism

## Key Works
- [*Algorithms of Oppression: How Search Engines Reinforce Racism*](https://nyupress.org/9781479837243/algorithms-of-oppression/) (NYU Press, 2018) — essential. Specific, empirically grounded, devastating critique of commercial search.
- MacArthur Fellowship 2021 — URL: https://www.macfound.org/fellows/class-of-2021/safiya-umoja-noble/

## Why Interesting
Noble's work grounds abstract critique in specific, documented harms. Her intersection of feminist, critical race, and STS approaches models how to bring disciplinary rigor to technology criticism. Connects to [[Algorithmic Governance]], [[Data Feminism]], [[Ruha Benjamin]].
