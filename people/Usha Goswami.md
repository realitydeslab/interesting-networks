# Usha Goswami

Director, Centre for Neuroscience in Education, University of Cambridge. Leading researcher on rhythm processing in language acquisition, amplitude modulation in speech, and reading/dyslexia. Developed the **S-AMPH (Spectral-Amplitude Modulation Phase Hierarchy)** computational model that reveals hierarchically nested rhythm structures in speech, music, babytalk, and poetry. Close collaborator with [[Tatsuya Daikoku]] on cross-domain amplitude modulation studies.

## Why Interesting
S-AMPH model provides a language-blind computational tool for analyzing rhythm across human communicative genres. Her work grounds musical/linguistic rhythm in shared neurophysiological constraints — relevant to ALife and generative music questions about universal structures in human communication.

## Key Relationships
- [[Tatsuya Daikoku]] — several co-authored papers on rhythm, poetry, music structure
