# Carlos Casas

Spanish artist and filmmaker working at the intersection of documentary, visual art and sound. Known for long-form immersive films about extreme environments — the desiccating Aral Sea, melting glaciers, the last nomads. His work explores ecological collapse, non-human temporalities, and the documentary form pushed to its limits.

## Key Works
- **[*Aral: Fishing in an Invisible Sea*](https://www.carloscasas.net/)** (2004, with [[Saodat Ismailova]]) — Best Documentary, Turin Film Festival. The Aral Sea catastrophe.
- **[*Burial*](https://www.carloscasas.net/)** — about glacial retreat and geological time
- *Hunter* (2010) — the last Siberian Yakut hunters
- *Avalanche* — about the Pamir Mountains

## Why Interesting
Discovered through Ismailova collaboration. His practice is genuinely ecological in a non-sentimental way — slow, durational, non-dramatic. The Aral film is a landmark ecological documentary. Worth deeper research to understand the full practice.
