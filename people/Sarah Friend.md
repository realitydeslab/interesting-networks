# Sarah Friend

Berlin-based artist and developer. Makes software, games, and blockchain-based artworks. Her work *Lifeforms* (2021) — NFTs that die (burn) if not transferred within 90 days — is a key example of using ERC-721 mechanics as artistic medium. Described by Paul Seidler in his 2025 Navigationen essay as a practitioner of "market-refusal or market-nonconformity" via the programmability of NFTs. Adjacent to terra0's approach: technical affordances of smart contracts used to complicate rather than celebrate the commodity form.

## Key Works
- **Lifeforms** (2021) — NFTs require transfer within 90 days or they die (burn function). Creates care obligations; critique of NFT as mere speculation vehicle. ("I found myself the accidental creator of a digital pet graveyard" — Friend)
- Various game/software works

## Why Interesting
Friend and Seidler represent a cluster of Berlin-based artist-programmers who engage with blockchain as a cultural medium (not commodity). Her "market-refusal" aesthetic and the use of transfer/burn functions for narrative/relational ends is directly parallel to terra0's practice. Worth researching for her full body of work.
