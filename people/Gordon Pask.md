# Gordon Pask

British cybernetician, architect of Conversation Theory and one of the foundational figures of second-order cybernetics. Close intellectual ally of [[Roy Ascott]] — their friendship was central to the development of cybernetic aesthetics. Proposed Ascott for Associate Membership of the Institution of Computer Science (1968). Pask's Conversation Theory (learning as conversation between participant and environment) directly shaped Ascott's thinking about interactive art as a feedback system between artist, viewer, and environment. Also collaborated with [[Cedric Price]] and [[Joan Littlewood]] on the Fun Palace project. His "musicolour" machine (1953) and "SAKI" (Self-Adaptive Keyboard Instructor) were early cybernetic artworks/systems.
