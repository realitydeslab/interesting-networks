# Eyal Weizman

British-Israeli architect. Professor of Spatial and Visual Cultures at [[Goldsmiths Visual Cultures]], University of London. Founding director of [Forensic Architecture](https://forensic-architecture.org/) (2010) — the research agency that uses architectural analysis, computation, and media evidence to investigate state violence and human rights violations. MBE (2020), FBA (2019). Prix Ars Electronica 2017, Turner Prize nomination 2018, Peabody Award 2021.

Co-author (with Matthew Fuller) of *[Investigative Aesthetics: Conflicts and Commons in the Politics of Truth](https://www.versobooks.com/books/3832-investigative-aesthetics)* (Verso, 2021) — key text for the intersection of art, evidence, and political truth.

## Why Interesting
Weizman demonstrates that rigorous aesthetic analysis can serve as political intervention and legal evidence. His concept of "investigative aesthetics" — using visual/spatial analysis as a form of truth-production — is directly relevant to Amber's interests in critical computing and more-than-human systems. Forensic Architecture represents the most politically consequential art-research agency of the 2010s.
