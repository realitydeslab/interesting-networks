# Dennis Bray

Cambridge computational cell biologist. Author of *Wetware: A Computer in Every Living Cell* (2009, Yale UP), which is the foundational philosophical-scientific argument that cells are computers — not metaphorically but computationally literally. Emeritus, so not currently active on new research, but his book is the starting point for the field.

## Domains
- Cellular computation and signalling
- Biological information processing
- Philosophy of biological systems

## Key Works
- [*Wetware: A Computer in Every Living Cell*](https://yalebooks.yale.edu/book/9780300167849/wetware/) (2009, Yale UP) — foundational text; argues cell architecture IS hardware+software unified.

## Why Interesting
Theoretically important for understanding what "computation" means in biological substrates. The book is accessible and builds philosophical ground for the whole field. Cambridge proximity = relevant for Amber.
