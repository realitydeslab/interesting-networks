# Warren McCulloch (1898–1969)

American neurophysiologist, philosopher, and cybernetician. Chair of the Macy Cybernetics Conferences. With Walter Pitts, authored **"A Logical Calculus of Ideas Immanent in Nervous Activity"** (1943) — the foundational paper of artificial neural networks, proving that neural nets can compute any boolean logic function. One of the most important papers in the intellectual history of AI.

## Key Works
- **McCulloch & Pitts, "A Logical Calculus of Ideas Immanent in Nervous Activity"** (1943, *Bulletin of Mathematical Biophysics*) — [DOI: 10.1007/BF02478259](https://doi.org/10.1007/BF02478259) — THE founding paper; showed how formal neurons can compute logical propositions; direct ancestor of deep learning
- **[*Embodiments of Mind*](https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262130875/)** (1965, MIT Press) — collected papers; his mature thinking on brain, mind, logic

## Why Interesting
The McCulloch-Pitts neuron (1943) is the beginning of the conceptual chain that leads to deep learning. McCulloch was also the key organizer of the Macy cybernetics conferences — his role in creating the *community* that invented AI is as important as his technical contributions. His "reverberating circuits" model of hallucinations and phantom limbs shows the cybernetics insight applied to consciousness.
