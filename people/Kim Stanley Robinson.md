# Kim Stanley Robinson

Science fiction writer and environmental advocate. Long Now Foundation Council member. Best known for the Mars trilogy (*Red Mars*, *Green Mars*, *Blue Mars*) and *The Ministry for the Future* (2020) — perhaps the most serious fictional treatment of climate governance and economic transformation. Robinson is unusual in SF: he does rigorous futures research, consults scientists, and writes speculative fiction as policy thought experiment. Appeared on Long Now Talks with Stephen Heintz (featured in Pace Layers journal #2, 2025).

**Source:** Discovered via Long Now Foundation deep research (2026-03-02).

## Why Interesting
*The Ministry for the Future* is the most discussed serious climate-futures novel of the decade — referenced by policymakers, economists, environmentalists. His ecological sci-fi operates at civilizational timescale, which is Long Now territory. He appeared on Science Friday NPR with Sara Imari Walker ("Meet the Dune Readers"), suggesting overlap with ALife/astrobiology community. For Amber: his work connects more-than-human futures, ecological speculative design, and civilizational-scale thinking.

## Key Works
- **[*The Ministry for the Future*](https://www.hachettebookgroup.com/titles/kim-stanley-robinson/the-ministry-for-the-future/9780316300162/)** (2020, Orbit) — near-future novel about a UN body (the eponymous ministry) tasked with advocating for future generations. Carbon coins, geoengineering, climate refugees. Serious policy speculation in fiction form.
- **[Mars Trilogy](https://www.kimstanleyrobinson.info/content/mars-trilogy)** — *Red Mars* (1992), *Green Mars* (1993), *Blue Mars* (1996). Hugo and Nebula winners. Terraforming as ecology, politics, and moral philosophy.
- **[*The Years of Rice and Salt*](https://www.kimstanleyrobinson.info/content/years-rice-and-salt)** (2002) — alternate history where the Black Death killed 99% of Europeans; told through Buddhist reincarnation cycles over 700 years.
- **[*New York 2140*](https://www.kimstanleyrobinson.info/content/new-york-2140)** (2017) — sea-level rise future of New York as archipelago, with economic analysis of financial systems.
