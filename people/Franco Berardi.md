# Franco Berardi ("Bifo")

Italian Marxist philosopher and activist (born 2 November 1949, Bologna), known by the nickname **"Bifo"**. A founding member of Italy's *autonomia* movement who met [[Antonio Negri]] through **Potere Operaio**. Founded **Radio Alice** (1976), Italy's first free pirate radio station. Fled to France after 1977, where he worked with **Félix Guattari** on schizoanalysis. 

Author of over two dozen books. His work focuses on **cognitive capitalism, media theory, and the subjective effects of digitization on labor and desire**. He uses the concept of "cognitariat" (cognitive proletariat) and analyzes how psycho-social life is caught in capitalist production circuits.

Notable for his pessimistic turn in recent years — where Negri/Hardt maintained an affirmative politics of the multitude, Berardi has increasingly described contemporary capitalism as producing exhaustion, depression, and psychopathology ("The Soul at Work"), and has become more critical of accelerationist optimism.

## Why Interesting
A bridge between Italian autonomism and media/communication theory. His analysis of how digital capitalism colonizes subjective life (attention, affect, desire) is relevant to AI's psychic effects. His founding of Radio Alice in 1976 is a fascinating precursor to contemporary DIY media, open broadcasting, and platform alternatives. His pessimism vs. Negri/Hardt's optimism is a productive tension worth understanding.

## Key Works

- *[After the Future](https://www.akpress.org/after-the-future.html)* (AK Press, 2011) — on the end of the future as concept in Western thought
- *[The Uprising: On Poetry and Finance](https://www.semiotexte.com/shop/books/intervention/the-uprising.html)* (Semiotext(e), 2012) — poetry and revolt
- *[Heroes: Mass Murder and Suicide](https://www.versobooks.com/products/172-heroes)* (Verso, 2015) — on violence and media
- *[Futurability: The Age of Impotence and the Horizon of Possibility](https://www.versobooks.com/products/218-futurability)* (Verso, 2017) — political impotence in the present age
