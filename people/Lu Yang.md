# Lu Yang 陆扬

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Initial deep research. Full profile created from Wikipedia, Société gallery, and institutional sources.

Chinese multimedia artist creating 3D animations, video installations, and interactive games that fuse Buddhist cosmology, neuroscience, gaming aesthetics, anime, and gender fluidity. Best known for the ongoing DOKU avatar project — a genderless digital reincarnation exploring consciousness, identity, and the six realms of samsara. One of the most internationally visible Chinese new media artists of this generation.

## Background
- Born 1984, Shanghai. Raised by a Buddhist family; early interest in religion and belief systems later merged with biology and neuroscience. Childhood heavily influenced by anime, J-pop, and otaku subculture.
- Parents worked for a medical company; spent significant childhood time being treated for asthma — medical environments became formative.
- Originally admitted to painting programme, switched to **MFA in New Media at [China Academy of Art](https://www.caa.edu.cn/)**, Hangzhou, studying under **[[Zhang Peili]]** (father of Chinese video art). This is Amber's institution — direct connection.
- Gender-neutral; uses she/her pronouns. Has stated: "By living on the Internet, you can abandon your identity, nationality, gender, even your existence as a human being."
- Lives and works between **Shanghai** and **Tokyo**.

## Key Works

### DOKU Series (2018–ongoing)
The central project of Lu Yang's recent career. DOKU (from "Dokusho Dokushi" — "We are born alone, and we die alone") is a genderless digital avatar created using motion capture technology, 3D animation, and face scanning. Six versions correspond to the six realms of Buddhist reincarnation: Human, Heaven, Asura, Animal, Hungry Ghost, Hell.

- **[DOKU the Creator – Bhavachakra](https://societeberlin.com/artists/lu-yang/)** (2025) — immersive video + prints. DOKU becomes the Creator, a meditative presence conjuring phenomena through contemplative stillness. Digital sublime meets karmic recursion. Solo exhibition at Société, Berlin.
- **[DOKU The Flow](https://www.fondationlouisvuitton.fr/)** (2024) — film drawing on Madhyamika Buddhist philosophy (all phenomena devoid of nature/substance/essence). Produced with support of Fondation Louis Vuitton. Shown at Open Space #14, Fondation Louis Vuitton, Paris.
- **[Doku: Digital Reincarnation](https://arts.timessquarenyc.org/)** (2023) — five-channel video installation taking over Times Square every night in December 2023. Post-human dance party.
- **[DOKU the Self](https://societeberlin.com/artists/lu-yang/)** (2022) — first narrative DOKU film, 34 min. Inspired by "overview effect" experienced during a lightning storm flight. Shown at 59th Venice Biennale: The Milk of Dreams.
- **DOKU – Binary Conflicts Invert Illusions** (2022) — commissioned by Zabludowicz Collection. Heaven and Hell avatars dance on yin yang symbol, forming hybrid "binary god."
- **DOKU – 6 Realms of Reincarnation** (2021) — hyper-realistic avatar based on face scan of artist. Shown at ARoS, Aarhus.

### Earlier Key Works
- **[Material World Knight](https://societeberlin.com/artists/lu-yang/)** — interactive game installation. Philosophical quest through techno-psychedelic realms featuring characters from earlier works.
- **[Lu Yang NetiNeti Arcade](https://www.zabludowiczcollection.com/)** (2022–ongoing) — immersive game installation. "Neti neti" = Sanskrit for "neither this, nor that." Commissioned by Zabludowicz Collection.
- **Great Adventure of Material World** — neuroscience × Buddhism exploration
- **Uterus Man** (2013) — early breakthrough work. Anime-inspired character with uterus-shaped weapons fighting disease. Provocative merger of medical science, gender, and pop culture. Featured in NY Times profile.
- **Electromagnetic Brainology** — installation exploring neuroscience through stimulation technologies
- **Delusional Mandala** (2015) — shown at 56th Venice Biennale, China Pavilion

## Research Taste Analysis

### Conceptual Strategy
- **Framework multiplication**: Buddhist cosmology × neuroscience × gaming × anime → unique aesthetic-philosophical position that no one else occupies
- **Avatar as conceptual vehicle**: DOKU isn't just a character — it's a philosophical argument about consciousness, identity, and reincarnation made flesh (digitally)
- **Maximal aesthetics as philosophical tool**: The overwhelming visual intensity (anime, gaming, psychedelia) isn't decoration — it enacts the Buddhist concept of maya (illusion)

### Career Architecture
- **Mentor lineage**: Studying under Zhang Peili (father of Chinese video art) gives institutional legitimacy and art-historical positioning
- **Prize escalation**: Venice China Pavilion (2015) → Venice main exhibition (2022) → Deutsche Bank Artist of the Year (2022) → Fondation Louis Vuitton (2024). Each prize unlocks the next tier.
- **Single concept, infinite scaling**: DOKU has been the vehicle for 5+ years of work across every conceivable format and venue. This is the "conceptual franchise" model.

### Methodology Signature
- **Technical ambition as moat**: Motion capture, face scanning, 3D animation, game engines — the production values create a barrier to entry that distinguishes the work from simpler digital art
- **Pop culture as trojan horse**: Anime/gaming aesthetics make the Buddhist philosophy accessible and attention-grabbing. The "fun" surface delivers "deep" content.
- **Collaborative production**: Unlike the lone-genius model, Lu Yang works with teams of scientists, dancers, animators, musicians. The avatar is a collective creation.

### Network Strategy
- **Dual-base strategy**: Shanghai + Tokyo bridges Chinese and Japanese art/tech scenes
- **Gallery choice**: Société Berlin is experimental, not mega-commercial — maintains credibility while accessing European market
- **Institutional collector focus**: Collections (Fondation Louis Vuitton, M+, Deutsche Bank, Hirshhorn) > commercial sales. This builds long-term art-historical presence.

### Key Moves (chronological)
1. **Studied under Zhang Peili at CAA** — inherited legitimacy from the founder of Chinese video art. Gave access to the Chinese art establishment.
2. **Venice Biennale China Pavilion (2015)** — early career Venice appearance established international visibility. Showed *Delusional Mandala*.
3. **Created DOKU avatar (2018)** — the conceptual pivot that unified all previous interests (Buddhism, neuroscience, gaming, identity) into one scalable project.
4. **Venice Biennale main show (2022)** — Cecilia Alemani's *The Milk of Dreams* included DOKU. Moving from national pavilion to main exhibition = major legitimation upgrade.
5. **Deutsche Bank Artist of the Year (2022)** — unlocked European institutional circuit: PalaisPopulaire Berlin, MUDEC Milan, Kunsthalle Basel, and broader visibility.
6. **Fondation Louis Vuitton commission (2024)** — *DOKU The Flow* produced with FLV support. Top-tier institutional patronage.
7. **Société Berlin gallery (ongoing)** — chose experimental gallery over mega-gallery, maintaining artistic credibility while scaling commercially.

### What Amber Can Learn

- **Pop culture as philosophical delivery system**: Gaming/anime aesthetics aren't frivolous — they're strategic. They make complex ideas (Buddhist emptiness, neuroscience of consciousness) accessible to broad audiences including institutional curators.
- **Prize-to-institution pipeline**: Each prize unlocks the next tier of visibility. Map the chain and plan accordingly.
- **Technical production as differentiator**: Investing in motion capture, face scanning, and game engine production creates work that is visually undeniable and hard to replicate.
- **The China Academy of Art → international circuit pathway**: Lu Yang's trajectory is a map Amber can study for navigating the same institutional ecosystem.
