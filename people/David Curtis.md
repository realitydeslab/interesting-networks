# David Curtis

Key curator and historian of British experimental and artists' film; long-term programmer at the BFI; the most important single institutional figure in preserving and contextualising the legacy of the [[London Film-Makers' Co-operative (LFMC)]] and British avant-garde film more broadly.

## Domains
Film curatorship; British experimental film history; artists' moving image; archival practice.

## Background
Long career at the British Film Institute, where he programmed experimental and artists' film and built institutional relationships with the LFMC and its successor organisations. Close relationship with [[Malcolm Le Grice]] and the structural-materialist tendency. Author of the key historical overview of the field.

## Key Publications
- *A History of Artists' Film and Video in Britain* (BFI, 2007) — the definitive historical survey; essential reference for understanding the LFMC, structural/materialist film, and everything that followed

## Key Relationships
- [[Malcolm Le Grice]] — close curatorial/institutional relationship; Curtis was central to BFI support for structural-materialist practice
- [[London Film-Makers' Co-operative (LFMC)]] — the institution whose legacy he most shaped archivally
- [[LUX]] — relationship continues through LUX's archival work

## Why Interesting
Curtis is the institutional memory of British experimental film. Any research into the LFMC or British structural-materialist tradition will encounter his work as a curator and historian. *A History of Artists' Film and Video in Britain* (2007) is the necessary reading for the context Le Grice operated in.
