# Shaila Agha

Kenya-based co-author of *Ecological Institutions: Law, Economics, and Technology in a More-Than-Human World* ([[Regen Foundation]], 2025). Brings East African mutualism lens to the project — chamas (rotating savings groups), mutual aid traditions, and kinship-based governance as ancestral precedents for modern ecological institutions.

## Domains

- **Ancestral governance protocols** — consent, consensus, witnessing as institutional design

- **Ecological institutions** — building governance systems for non-human participants

## Key Contribution
Research connecting Kĩrĩnyaga (Mount Kenya) to governance: not just as a mountain but as a seed bank, water tower, and living reference point that shaped rituals, protocols, and ways of governing life together long before formal institutions existed. These practices treated land and ecology not as objects to manage, but as participants in governance. Gave a talk on this "through an East African mutualism lens" (recording available via Regen Foundation LinkedIn).
