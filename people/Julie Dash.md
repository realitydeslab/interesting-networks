# Julie Dash

Filmmaker and director. Made *Daughters of the Dust* (1991) — the first feature film by an African-American woman to receive wide theatrical distribution in the US. An L.A. Rebellion-adjacent filmmaker who developed a distinctly Black feminist visual language. Former wife of [[Arthur Jafa]], who was DP on *Daughters of the Dust*. Central figure in independent Black American cinema.

## Why Interesting
*Daughters of the Dust* is one of the most formally innovative films in American cinema — a non-linear narrative of Gullah families on the Sea Islands of South Carolina at the turn of the 20th century. Shot by [[Arthur Jafa]], the film's visual language (bleached-out light, slow motion, layered sound, ancestor spirits) is the closest embodiment of the BVI concept. The film was released in 1991, influenced Beyoncé's *Lemonade* (2016), and has never stopped being relevant. Dash's career after *Daughters* is worth examining — she moved primarily into TV (including an episode of *Incognito*) while the film grew into a landmark.
