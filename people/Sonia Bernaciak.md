Researcher, philosopher of science, and artist. PhD from Royal College of Art. Works at the intersection of non-human aesthetics, synthetic recognition, and self-assembling physical AI systems. Philosophically grounded in complexity science, she investigates how synthetic intelligence operates at a planetary scale and what new materialisms are needed to grasp it. ## Background

Sonia Bernaciak publishes and exhibits under the name **Sonia Bernac**. Her full surname Bernaciak appears in institutional contexts.

She holds a **PhD from the [Royal College of Art](https://www.rca.ac.uk/)** (AiDesign Lab), where she remains as a Researcher in Artificial and Distributed Intelligence. She also holds a concurrent research position at **[Hong Kong Polytechnic University](https://www.polyu.edu.hk/)** — the basis of the Hong Kong connection noted in the stub.

Her intellectual formation is philosophical: she grounds her practice in complexity science, old and new materialisms, and the epistemology of synthetic intelligence. Her research explicitly questions what inherited materialist frameworks miss about synthetic intelligence operating at planetary scale. This makes her simultaneously a practitioner (installations, sculptures, AI systems) and a theorist (philosophy of science, epistemology of computation).

Based in **London**, UK.

## Domains

- **Non-human aesthetics and synthetic recognition** — how machines sense, and how sensing conditions sense-making in multisensory generative environments
- **Self-assembling physical AI systems** — work developing AI systems that evolve their own dynamics and material aesthetics through sensors, code, and material experimentation; physical instantiations of open-ended evolution
- **Complexity science and epistemology** — tracing the epistemological burdens of old and new materialisms that prevent understanding synthetic intelligence at planetary scale
- **Latent spaces, AI hallucinations, semi-autonomous simulacra** — theoretical work on how AI systems generate images of thought; the strange agency of semi-autonomous synthetic entities
- **Synthetic teratologies** — her conceptual original contribution: pathological systemic formations shaped by exclusion, compulsion, or sadistic imaginaries in AI systems
- **AI alignment as generative misalignment** — co-developed the "Uncanny Ridge" concept: productive intersystemic misrecognition as a source of novelty (rather than alignment/coherence as the design goal)
- **Deep time and cognitive preservation** — speculative work on how AI systems (LLMs) could serve as cognitive time capsules, preserving human intelligence across civilizational collapse scenarios

## Key Works

### Publications (Antikythera Journal, MIT Press)

- **[Traversing the Uncanny Ridge: Searching for Novelty in Intersystemic Communication](https://doi.org/10.1162/anti.5czi)** (2025, *Antikythera: Journal for the Philosophy of Planetary Computation*) — Lead author. With Tyler Farghly (Oxford, PhD ML) and Gary Zhexi Zhang. Introduces the "Uncanny Ridge" concept: an optimal zone of novelty and complexity where generative AI outputs evoke productive misrecognition — calibrated between complete predictability and unrecognizable randomness. Inverts Mori's Uncanny Valley: instead of human discomfort toward near-human simulations, focuses on when AI system misrecognition *produces* novelty. Proposes the "uncanny index" — a metric for quantifying novelty as a loss function in generative AI. Draws on psychology of creativity: sustained novelty from dynamic tensions, not simplistic divergence. [Download PDF](https://cdn.sanity.io/files/y9ih15q4/production/1b6061320f0820591647017dc276cc06c5776c5f.pdf)

- **[Chronoseed: A Generative AI Time Capsule](https://doi.org/10.1162/anti.5czo)** (2025, *Antikythera: Journal for the Philosophy of Planetary Computation*) — With Jackie Kay (Google DeepMind / UCL) and Winnie Street (Google DeepMind). Proposes "Chronoseed" — a speculative thought experiment: an AI time capsule that preserves not just an archive of human knowledge but the *cognitive function* of human thought within a neural network. Must persist across civilizational collapse scenarios. Constructs a three-dimensional design space: durability, accessibility, completeness. Engages historical time capsules, long-term nuclear waste warning systems, and science fiction. Identifies **DNA embedding** as the most viable option for long-term durability and recoverability. [Download PDF](https://cdn.sanity.io/files/y9ih15q4/production/6a776b5152b9713b21202c4b8744dba52a023ba6.pdf)

- **[Cognitive Infrastructures](https://doi.org/10.1162/anti.5czg)** (2025, *Antikythera: Journal for the Philosophy of Planetary Computation*) — Collective/composite article (17 co-authors) emerging from the Antikythera 2024 Studio. Bernaciak contributed as studio researcher. Explores AI as a real, material force embedded within physical infrastructures — not just disembodied virtual brain. The composite speculation: executable embedding of digital twins of brain organoids driving artificial epidermal mediated distributed organs, part of xenomorphic biohybrid robotic phenotypes. [DOI: 10.1162/anti.5czg](https://doi.org/10.1162/anti.5czg)

## Research Taste Analysis

### Conceptual Strategy

**Double negative move:** Her conceptual innovation repeatedly inverts existing frameworks rather than creating from scratch. The Uncanny Ridge inverts Mori's Uncanny Valley. The "non-human ≠ inhumane" formulation inverts the common slippage. "Synthetic teratologies" inverts the default assumption that pathological formation is a bug rather than a structuring force. This pattern — taking a received concept and inverting the valuation — is a highly efficient conceptual generator.

**Seed paper strategy:** The Antikythera papers (2025) function as conceptual seeds: the Uncanny Ridge concept, the Chronoseed concept, the teratologies framing are all introduced as thought experiments with clear empirical/design implications. The papers don't exhaust the ideas — they plant them. Follow-on empirical work (building the systems, testing the uncanny index, designing actual Chronoseeds) is implicit.

**Two-word concept coining:** "Uncanny Ridge," "Synthetic Teratologies," "Chronoseed" — all memorable, citable, two-word constructions that function as conceptual anchors. This is a signature of researchers who understand that naming is half the battle in establishing a research programme.

### Career Architecture

**Dual-institution positioning:** RCA (London art-tech credibility) + PolyU (Asia research credibility) creates a hinge position between two important ecosystems. Neither is center-periphery to the other — they're complementary circuits. This is smarter than single-institution concentration.

**Antikythera as credentialing accelerator:** Getting into the Antikythera studio early (when the journal was just launching) + having papers in Volume 1 of their MIT Press journal positions her at the founding moment of an institution that will become more important over time. Early membership in a fast-rising institution compounds.

**Practice + theory combination:** Maintaining an active artistic practice (immersive installation, sculpture) while publishing in a peer-reviewed MIT Press journal is a strategic position that very few people occupy. It gives access to both art exhibition networks and academic citation networks simultaneously.

### Methodology Signature

**Material-conceptual bridging:** Her methodology starts with physical AI systems (sensors, code, material experimentation) and ascends to philosophical concepts (synthetic recognition, teratologies, complexity science). This upward movement — material → conceptual — is the opposite of most academic AI philosophers who start with concepts and descend (sometimes) to applications. Her approach gives the concepts material weight.

**Thought experiment methodology:** The Chronoseed paper explicitly describes itself as "a speculative thought experiment." This is increasingly legitimate methodology in philosophy of AI — using speculation rigorously to identify design spaces and evaluate possibilities systematically. The three-dimensional design space (durability × accessibility × completeness) imposed on the thought experiment is rigorous structure applied to speculative content.

### Network Strategy

**Quality over quantity:** Her collaborator list (Tyler Farghly at Oxford ML; Jackie Kay and Winnie Street at Google DeepMind; Gary Zhexi Zhang) are all high-quality, strategically positioned people. Small collaborator network, each connection high-signal. This concentrates credibility rather than diluting it.

**Antikythera cohort building:** The 2024 studio group (17 people) becomes a mutual citation and recommendation network. Being part of the Antikythera cohort gives access to everyone else in that cohort — Blaise Agüera y Arcas, Sara Imari Walker, Joel Z Leibo. These affiliate relationships activate longitudinally.

### What Amber Can Learn

The inversion simultaneously demonstrates familiarity with the prior art and creates immediate conceptual novelty.

2. **Material first, theory second:** Starting from physical AI systems gives the philosophical work empirical weight and publication credibility in both art and science-adjacent venues. Don't publish theory alone; demonstrate it with systems.

3. **Naming matters:** "Synthetic Teratologies," "Uncanny Ridge," "Chronoseed" — these are concepts that will be cited because they're memorable. Amber should cultivate the discipline of naming concepts at the level of naming artworks.

4. **Studio-as-paper-generator:** The Antikythera studio format (one month, intensive, led by design direction) produced two MIT Press papers. This is a model for treating residencies and studios as publication opportunities rather than just experiential ones.
