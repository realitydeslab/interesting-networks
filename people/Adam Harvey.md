# Adam Harvey

Artist, researcher, and technologist (US/DE). Pioneer of **anti-surveillance aesthetics** — uses deep technical understanding of computer vision to build artistic countermeasures. Creator of CV Dazzle, HyperFace, Stealth Wear, MegaPixels/Exposing.ai, and VFRAME. The defining figure at the intersection of critical computer vision, privacy art, and humanitarian applications of AI. Based in Berlin since roughly 2013.

## Background
Born 1981 in the US. Did a B.A. at Penn State in Mechanical Engineering but spent his time as a photojournalist at the student newspaper — the first signal of his later fusion of technical and image-based thinking. Moved to NYC, worked as a freelance photographer, then did his [M.S. at ITP NYU](https://tisch.nyu.edu/itp) (2010) — the famous program at Tisch School of the Arts that's produced a generation of artist-technologists (Lauren McCarthy, Kyle McDonald, Tega Brain all ITP). His thesis there was CV Dazzle.

Moved to Berlin sometime between 2012-2013. Has been based there ever since. The Berlin location connects him to the German media art / tech-politics / digital rights milieu — Transmediale, re:publica, Eigen Art Lab, Disruption Network Lab, Weizenbaum Institut.

His intellectual trajectory: ITP thesis → fashion/surveillance art (CV Dazzle, Stealth Wear, HyperFace) → investigative research on biometric datasets (MegaPixels, Exposing.ai, Creative Commons report) → computer vision for human rights (VFRAME) → now applied humanitarian tech (Tech 4 Tracing). A consistent thread: using technical mastery to expose and subvert surveillance infrastructures.

## Key Works

### Anti-Surveillance Art
- **[CV Dazzle](https://adam.harvey.studio/cvdazzle/)** (2010–ongoing) — Master's thesis at ITP NYU. Camouflage designs using makeup and hair styling that defeat face detection algorithms. Used genetic algorithms to reverse-engineer the Viola-Jones haarcascade face detector's "ideal face" — then inverted it. Collaborators: DIS Magazine (Looks 2-4), New York Times (Look 5 commission). Updated 2020 for Walker Art Center's "Designs for Different Futures." **The first documented camouflage technique to successfully attack a CV algorithm.**

- **[Stealth Wear](https://adam.harvey.studio/stealth-wear/)** (2012–2013) — Anti-drone thermal camouflage. Silver-plated fabric reflects LWIR infrared, reducing the wearer's thermal signature to aerial surveillance. Includes Anti-Drone Hijab, Burqa, Hoodie. Inspired by Islamic dress reimagined as drone-age protection garment. First presented at Tank Magazine HQ, London, Jan 17, 2013. Designed with Johanna Bloomfield. Exhibited at Marta Herford, Utah MOCA, Boghossian Gallery Brussels, SMFA Boston.

- **[HyperFace](https://adam.harvey.studio/hyperface/)** (2015–2017) — False-face decoy camouflage. Prints maximally-activated false faces to overwhelm face detection algorithms. Opposite strategy to CV Dazzle: instead of hiding a face, creates so many false-face candidates that the algorithm's attention is misdirected. Developed in collaboration with **[[Hyphen-Labs]]** (Ashley Baccus-Clark, Carmen Aguilar y Wedge, Ece Tankal, Nitzan Bartov, JB Rubinovitz) for their **NeuroSpeculative AfroFeminism** project. Debuted at Sundance Film Festival 2017. First presented at 33C3 Hamburg (Dec 30, 2016). Exhibited at Vitra Design Museum (Politics of Design), HMKV.

- **[Camoflash](https://ahprojects.com/)** — LED anti-paparazzi device. Blinds camera sensors with targeted infrared LEDs.

- **[Off Pocket](https://ahprojects.com/)** — Signal-blocking pants pocket. Severs all wireless signal to a phone.

### Investigative Research
- **[MegaPixels](https://exposing.ai/)** (2018–2021, now exposing.ai) — Investigative research project exposing how photos uploaded to Flickr and other platforms ended up as non-consensual biometric training data for commercial facial recognition systems. Created a searchable interface allowing people to check if their Flickr photos were used in recognition datasets. Resulted in Microsoft, IBM, and others taking down datasets. Front-page NYT coverage (2019). Featured in Financial Times, Nature, BBC. Now hosted at exposing.ai.

- **[Researchers Gone Wild](https://adam.harvey.studio/researchers-gone-wild/)** (2018–2021) — Exhibition version of the Exposing.ai research. Showed at Eigen Art Lab, Berlin (2020). Included visualizations of datasets like Duke MTMC, UCF Selfie, LFW. Documented photography: Eike Walkenhorst.

- **[Face Recognition Datasets and the Creative Commons License Problem](https://adam.harvey.studio/creative-commons/)** (2021–2022) — Commissioned research for OpenFuture.eu's #AICommons project. Investigates how CC licenses became a backdoor enabling biometric mass surveillance — tracing the pipeline from Flickr → YFCC100M → MegaFace → commercial FRT systems.

- **[Today's Selfie Is Tomorrow's Biometric Profile](https://adam.harvey.studio/todays-selfie-essay/)** (2022) — Essay commissioned by HMKV for "House of Mirrors: Artificial Intelligence as Phantasm" exhibition.

### Privacy Campaigns & Products
- **[Think Privacy](https://adam.harvey.studio/think-privacy/)** (2016–ongoing) — Privacy propaganda campaign. Posters and objects inspired by Facebook's motivational office slogans but subverted: "Data Never Dies," "Anything You Say Can and Will Be Used Against You in a Targeted Advertisement," "Today's Selfie Is Tomorrow's Biometric Profile." Launched at New Museum Store (2016); commissioned by V&A Museum London (mirror, 2017), Whitney Museum Store (Laura Poitras exhibition), Seoul Mediacity Biennale (2018), Aksioma Ljubljana.

- **[DataPools / SkyLift](https://adam.harvey.studio/datapools/)** (2016–2020) — Wi-Fi geolocation spoofing project. A small device that tricks your phone into thinking it's at Jeff Bezos's pool or Peter Thiel's Hawaii house. Collaboration with **[[Anastasia Kubrak]]**. Exhibited at Tropez Berlin, Istanbul Design Biennial (2018), Bureau Europa Maastricht (2019), Science Gallery Dublin (2019), ZKM Open Codes/Schloss Web (2018). Source code open at github.com/adamhrv/skylift.

### Humanitarian Tech / Computer Vision for Human Rights
- **[VFRAME](https://vframe.io/)** (2017–ongoing) — Visual Forensics and Metadata Extraction. Computer vision toolkit for human rights researchers analyzing conflict-zone video archives. Built initially with Syrian Archive (now Mnemonic.org) in Berlin. Detects illegal munitions (RBK-250, 9N235, AO-2.5RT) with up to 99% accuracy at 400+ FPS using synthetic training data generated through photogrammetry and 3D printing. Won **Ars Electronica Award of Distinction** (2019). Nominated for EU STARTS Prize (2018) and Beazley Design of the Year Award (2019). Peer-reviewed paper in Journal of Conventional Weapons Destruction (2023). Currently partnered with Tech 4 Tracing; presented at GICHD Mine Action Technology Workshop 2021, 2023.

- **[DFACE.app](https://adam.harvey.studio/dface/)** (2021–2022) — Automatic, private, open-source face redaction web app.

## Research Taste Analysis (⭐⭐⭐)

### Conceptual Strategy
**Single-concept expansion** — Harvey's entire practice is built on one fundamental idea: *if a technical system can be analyzed, its vulnerabilities can be exploited*. This is CV Dazzle. He then runs this same logic through every new domain he enters: face detection → face recognition → drone thermal → biometric datasets → conflict zone weapons. He never needs a new concept because the original concept is deep enough to sustain 15+ years of development.

**Technical inversion as artistic method** — every Harvey project is an inversion: face detection → anti-face; surveillance datasets → exposure of the dataset; privacy-erasing technology → privacy-restoring countermeasure. This inversion structure is immediately legible and immediately political.

**Provocation-first** — CV Dazzle as makeup is a provocation: is it fashion? is it protest? is it research? It works precisely because it sits in the intersection and refuses to be categorized. This forces media coverage and academic citation simultaneously.

### Career Architecture
**Thesis-as-career-foundation** — CV Dazzle was his 2010 master's thesis. He has never escaped it — and that's strategic, not accidental. He continually updates, recontextualizes, and extends the concept. The thesis became the seed for everything.

**Research-as-art-as-advocacy** — Harvey operates simultaneously as artist (exhibitions), researcher (papers, datasets), and policy advocate (presenting to UN, EU institutions). This multiplicity of roles means he can appear in very different contexts, building influence across domains without being pigeonholed.

**Berlin as base** — Choosing Berlin over NYC or London was a deliberate choice that placed him adjacent to German digital rights culture, EU policy discourse, and the media art institutional circuit. The EU has much stronger data protection politics than the US; being Berlin-based made his surveillance critique more institutionally credible and gave him access to EU funding mechanisms.

**Pivot to humanitarian** — The VFRAME/Tech4Tracing move is fascinating: when the artistic/research phase of a practice reaches saturation (everyone knows facial recognition datasets are problematic now), pivot the technical skills to applied humanitarian work. This is career extension through application, not abandonment.

### Methodology Signature
**Technical depth as credibility** — Harvey writes code. He trains models. He publishes peer-reviewed papers. This technical depth is unusual among artist-researchers and gives him authority to criticize the technology he's engaging with. He's not just critiquing; he's demonstrating.

**Reverse engineering as art practice** — The core methodology: take a black-box technical system, reverse-engineer it, expose its vulnerabilities, build something from those vulnerabilities. This is fundamentally an engineering methodology deployed as art.

**Synthetic data approach** — VFRAME's innovation: use photogrammetry + 3D rendering + 3D printing to generate synthetic training data for weapons detection, avoiding the need to use real conflict zone footage (which has ethical problems). This is a methodological contribution to human rights investigation tech.

### Key Moves
1. **2010: CV Dazzle as ITP thesis** — Chose a thesis topic that was simultaneously fashion, political art, and technical research. This tripling of registers meant it was immediately publishable in art, tech, and policy contexts.

2. **2012-2013: Stealth Wear in London** — Moved from face detection to drone surveillance, from makeup to fabric engineering, launched at Tank Magazine (fashion, not tech). Showed the concept could migrate across domains.

3. **2015-2017: HyperFace + Hyphen-Labs collaboration** — Deliberately partnered with Afrofuturist collective to give the work racial/political grounding. NeuroSpeculative AfroFeminism at Sundance gave him both an art-world debut and a political framing.

4. **2017-2019: Pivot to investigative research (MegaPixels/Exposing.ai)** — Shifted from making camouflage to exposing the infrastructure. This was an investigative journalism move: do the work that WIRED and NYT should be doing, but faster and deeper. Generated major media coverage and actual policy consequences (companies took down datasets).

5. **2017-present: VFRAME** — Applied the same technical skills to humanitarian work. This pivot gave him access to institutional funding (EU, GICHD) and genuine urgency. The Syria/Ukraine connection makes the work undeniably important.

6. **2025: Chief Technologist at Tech4Tracing** — Full institutional embedding. The independent artist/researcher has become a humanitarian NGO technical lead. Highest-stakes application of his skills.

### What Amber Can Learn
- **The thesis-as-seed strategy** — One deep foundational concept, extended for a career. What is Amber's equivalent? Identify it early and protect it.
- **Technical depth = political authority** — Harvey's most effective critique is that it's technically irrefutable. Amber should think about which technical systems she can master deeply enough to make her critique unanswerable.
- **Inversion as method** — Every Harvey project inverts the dominant logic of a technology. What technologies in Amber's domain can be inverted?
- **Multiple readership simultaneously** — Harvey publishes art, research, and policy advocacy simultaneously. Amber should look for opportunities to publish the same underlying work across multiple formats and audiences.
- **Choosing the right city** — Berlin gave Harvey a specific institutional ecology. Amber is in London/Oxford/Cambridge — this gives her the UK tech-policy scene, AI Safety ecosystem, and British contemporary art world. Which of these ecologies is most aligned with her practice?
