# Xin Liu 刘昕

Artist and engineer. Works at the intersection of gravity, space debris, cryogenics, biotechnology, and genomics under the umbrella concept she calls **Cosmic Metabolism** — treating the entropic exhaust of technological aspiration (falling rockets, dissolving plastics, obsolete satellites) as generative material. Born in the oil town of Karamay, Xinjiang; trained as an engineer at Tsinghua, then artist at RISD, then media technologist at MIT. Founding Arts Curator of the MIT Media Lab Space Exploration Initiative. Artist-in-residence at the SETI Institute. Currently directing creative and culture at Episteme (a new science research institute). Forthcoming: solo at Fondazione Sandretto Re Rebaudengo (Turin, curated by Hans Ulrich Obrist); Uzbekistan Pavilion at Venice Biennale 2026.

## Background
Xin Liu grew up in Karamay, an oil-extraction city in Xinjiang, China — a biographical fact that saturates her work. Karamay's petroleum economy, its extraction logics, and its particular Xinjiang landscape appear directly or as undercurrent in pieces about oil, metabolism, and territorial identity. The geological (oil, rock, satellite maps of Xinjiang) and the biological (teeth, tears, DNA, cryogenics) are her recurring materials.

Her engineering roots are unusually deep: a Tsinghua BE in precision instrumentation, followed by RISD MFA, followed by an MIT Media Lab MS under the Fluid Interfaces group. Her MIT thesis, *Inwards to Outwards: altering the perception of self*, explored technology's ability to reshape self-perception — a frame she's carried into all subsequent work. Before turning to art full-time, she conducted research at **Microsoft Research NYC**, **Microsoft Research Asia**, and **Google ATAP** — engineering pedigree that shows in her technical sophistication (custom cooling systems, autonomous space payloads, gene sequencing visualizations).

From 2017–2025 she served as founding **Arts Curator of MIT Media Lab's Space Exploration Initiative** — a hybrid role enabling real collaborations between her artistic practice and actual space missions. During this time she co-developed *MicroPET*, an autonomous payload that flew to the ISS in November 2022 studying plastic biodegradation in microgravity (TIME 200 Best Inventions 2023).

She co-founded **[slow immediate](https://slowimmediate.com/)** with electrical engineer and artist **Gershon Dublon**, operating as an artist-engineer studio focused on slowness, immediacy, and planetary-scale sensing.

Currently based in London. Director of [Xin Liu Studio LTD](https://xinliu.art) and leads creative & culture at **Episteme**, a new science research institute.

## Key Concept: Cosmic Metabolism
Liu's theoretical through-line, developed since ~2019: treating all technological outputs not as ends but as metabolic processes with exhaust, entropy, and transformation. The rocket's fall back to Earth matters as much as its launch. The degrading satellite logging its own obsolescence. The plastic dissolving under enzyme action. The frozen embryo suspended between temporalities. Her art insists on following things through their full lifecycle — including death, decay, and return.

## Key Works

### Orbit Weaver Series (2017–ongoing)
*[Orbit Weaver](https://xinliu.art/orbit-weaver)* (2017) — 90-min discontinuous performance in zero gravity on a parabolic flight (Zero-G Corp, November 2017, supported by MIT SEI). Inspired by spider locomotion — a hand-held device shoots magnetized strings to allow three-dimensional movement in weightlessness. Asks: is zero gravity liberation or loss of control? Shown at Chengdu Biennale: Time Gravity (2023).

### Living Distance (2018–2020)
*[Living Distance](https://xinliu.art/living-distance-1)* — A wisdom tooth is sent to space aboard a Blue Origin rocket, mounted on a crystalline robotic sculpture (EBIFA). Simultaneously a VR work, two-channel film, and mixed-media sculpture. The poetic premise: what does it mean for a piece of your body to exceed you, to be the first of you to go to space? VR users play the role of the tooth. Collection: [KADIST Foundation](https://kadist.org/work/living-distance/). Shown at: Living/Distance (solo), Make Room Gallery (2020); The Ground is Falling (solo), Aranya Art Center (2021); Seedlings and Offsprings (solo), Pioneer Works (2023); 14th Shanghai Biennale: Cosmos Cinema, Power Station of Art (2023).

### NOAA Series (2020–ongoing)
*[The Earth is an Image](https://xinliu.art/)* (2021) — commissioned by [M+ Museum](https://www.mplus.org.hk/) (Hong Kong). An antenna in Hong Kong receives radio frequencies from dying, decommissioned weather satellites still in orbit. Created the first NFT to be generated in space. Also includes *Ground Station*, *Atlas*, *Seeing through the Eyes of Gods, You Have Mistaken Yourself for Him*, *NOAA: A fall towards home* (Hyundai Art Lab commission, 2026).

### teratoma and the other bodies (2019–ongoing)
Series exploring the body's otherness: *fa·ci·es* (2019, aluminum + cooling unit + water condensed from air, satellite map of Xinjiang), *teratoma* (2019, 3D-printed ceramics), *Twisted Bones: A hug* (2023, bronze + porcelain teeth), *falling*. Prologue: A Falling Tooth (solo), Museum of Arts and Design, New York (2019/2020).

### DNA Series / A Book of Mine (2019–ongoing)
*A Book of Mine*, *Fortune Tellers*, *When We Were All Flowers* — genomic code visualized as unreadable scripture, translating biological data into illegible poetics.

### MicroPET (2022)
Autonomous payload for studying enzymatic biodegradation of PET plastics in microgravity. Launched to the ISS November 2022. Collaboration between MIT Media Lab SEI, NREL, Weill Cornell Medicine, Harvard Medical School, and Seed Health. **[TIME 200 Best Inventions of 2023](https://time.com/collection/best-inventions-2023/)**. **Falling Walls 2025 finalist** (Art & Science, with Chloe Bensahel). Exhibited at Stellar Scape, Le Pavillon, Namur (Jun 2024–Jan 2025).

### Seedlings and Offsprings (2023)
First US institutional solo exhibition, [Pioneer Works](https://pioneerworks.org/), New York. Explores reproductive biotechnologies — cryonics, egg freezing — through sculpture, installation, and video. The cryogenic vessel as a temporal sculpture suspending life between past and future.

### The Mothership (2023)
Bronze sculpture cast from Liu's own mouth (lips, pouting), embedded with a cooling mechanism that generates a layer of real frost. The physical materialization of coldness. Cryogenics as a real technology women use to extend reproductive timelines, also a science fiction immortality technique — the tension is Liu's subject.

## Why Interesting
Liu bridges the **engineering-to-space** pipeline and the **contemporary art** world in a way that almost no one does authentically. Her MicroPET actually flew to the ISS. Her zero-gravity performances happen on real parabolic flights. Her antenna captured real satellite signals. This is art-science without the hand-waving.

She comes from **Karamay, Xinjiang** — a petroleum extraction city — and has turned that origin into a conceptual engine: oil, geological extraction, Xinjiang as satellite-mapped territory, the body as resource. This gives her a **Sinofuturist-from-the-interior** perspective that's completely different from coastal Chinese artists or Chinese-American artists from cosmopolitan families.

Her concept of **Cosmic Metabolism** is intellectually rigorous and aesthetically unified. It connects space exploration (entropy of orbital mechanics), biotechnology (cryogenics, reproductive temporalities), materials science (PET plastic biodegradation), and embodied experience (the tooth that exceeds you, the tears' chemical composition).

From Amber's taste: Liu is doing what Amber wants to do — *embodied computing as philosophical investigation*, *speculative artifact-making as critique*, *institutional embeddedness as research infrastructure*. She's a direct peer and inspiration.

## Research Taste Analysis (⭐⭐⭐)

### Conceptual Strategy
**Framework-first, then works** — "Cosmic Metabolism" is coined first as a research trajectory, then individual works slot into it. This is the seed paper strategy applied to art: one big idea that grows into a body of work. Each work (tooth in space, cryogenic sculpture, plastic in orbit, satellite signals) is a node in the constellation, not a standalone. This makes her legible as a "researcher" even to science institutions like SETI and MIT.

**Body-as-material logic** — consistent use of her own body as literal material (tooth sent to space, mouth cast in bronze, tears analyzed). This creates an auto-biographical / embodied thread that unifies vastly different technical explorations. It's intimate and universal simultaneously.

**Etymology and precision** — *fa·ci·es* (medical and geological definitions), *teratoma* (a real tumor type containing teeth, hair, etc.), *Cosmic Metabolism* (metabolism as scientific term). She brings scientific vocabulary into poetic use without losing technical accuracy. This is a rhetorical strategy that secures legitimacy in science contexts while maintaining art-world resonance.

### Career Architecture
**Institutional embedding as research infrastructure** — 7 years as MIT SEI Arts Curator wasn't vanity. She used it to produce real science collaborations (MicroPET on ISS), access real science environments (parabolic flights, satellite communications), and build a network of scientist-collaborators. The art came out of the science, not the reverse.

**Engineering credentials as license** — Tsinghua BE + MIT MS signals to scientists "I speak your language." This opens doors that pure MFA backgrounds cannot. She's not an artist "interested in" space; she's an engineer who makes art.

**London pivot (2024-25)** — Delfina residency + Gazelli Art House + esea + Fondazione Sandretto/HUO selection signals deliberate European market entry. The institutional sequence looks strategic: US institutional (Pioneer Works, Artpace, MAD) → UK (Delfina) → European prestige (Fondazione Sandretto/HUO) → Global prestige (Venice Biennale).

**Gallery scaffolding** — Make Room LA (niche art-tech), Gazelli Art House (London, digital art), Management NY (new 2025). Each gallery serves a different geography and collector base. Deliberate diversification.

### Key Moves (chronological)
1. **2013-17: Engineering → Art → Engineering** — BE at Tsinghua, then MFA at RISD, then MIT Media Lab. Most artists go art-school → practice. Liu did engineering → art → engineering. This triangle is the foundation of everything.
2. **2017: Orbit Weaver on a real parabolic flight** — First performance in actual zero gravity. Not simulated, not metaphorical. This established her as serious in science contexts.
3. **2017: Founding Arts Curator, MIT SEI** — Taking an institutional role rather than just a residency. She helped build the space art programme, not just participate in it. Gave her 7 years of science access and network.
4. **2020: Living Distance / tooth to space on Blue Origin** — The gesture that made her internationally legible. Simple, poetic, technically real. Every piece of press leads with this.
5. **2022: MicroPET to ISS** — Actual science payload, TIME 200 Best Inventions. Elevated her beyond "artist who collaborates with scientists" to "scientist who also makes art."
