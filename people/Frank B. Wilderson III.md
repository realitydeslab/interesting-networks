# Frank B. Wilderson III

Scholar, memoirist, and activist. Former student of **[[Saidiya Hartman]]** at UC Berkeley. Coined the term **"Afropessimism"** — a theoretical framework holding that anti-Blackness is not a form of racism among others but the foundational antagonism of Western modernity. Chair of African American Studies at UC Irvine.

## Key Works
- **[*Afropessimism*](https://us.macmillan.com/books/9781324091639/afropessimism)** (Liveright/W.W. Norton, 2020) — memoir and theoretical treatise; charts the intellectual genealogy of Afropessimism and Wilderson's own biographical arc (including his time in the ANC in South Africa)
- **[*Red, White & Black: Cinema and the Structure of U.S. Antagonisms*](https://www.dukeupress.edu/red-white-and-black)** (Duke University Press, 2010) — the academic theoretical foundation

## Key Connections
- **[[Saidiya Hartman]]** — former mentor; her work provided crucial theoretical groundwork for Afropessimism (though she doesn't identify with the label)
- Orlando Patterson (*Slavery and Social Death*) — key theoretical source

## Why Interesting
Afropessimism is one of the most contested and generative theoretical frameworks in contemporary Black studies and political theory. Understanding its genealogy (through Hartman) is important for understanding debates about race, capitalism, and political possibility.
