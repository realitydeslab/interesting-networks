# Benjamin Bratton

Philosopher of technology. Professor of Philosophy of Technology and Speculative Design at UC San Diego. Director of Antikythera, a think-tank researching the future of planetary computation at the Berggruen Institute. Through the lens of planetary computation, establishes new philosophical frameworks for interpreting the past, present and future co-evolution of life, culture, and technology.

## Key Works
### Books
- *[The Stack: On Software and Sovereignty](https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262029575/the-stack/)* (MIT Press, 2015) — 500+ page foundational text introducing "planetary computation" as concept. Six-layer analysis: Earth, Cloud, City, Address, Interface, User layers. Essential reading for understanding computation as geological and political force.
- *[The Revenge of the Real: Politics for a Post-Pandemic World](https://www.versobooks.com/products/2749-the-revenge-of-the-real)* (Verso, 2021) — short polemic examining tension between populism and scientific governance through COVID-19 lens. Critiques biopolitical theory's inadequacy for planetary governance.
- *[The Terraforming](https://www.amazon.com/Terraforming-Benjamin-Bratton-ebook/dp/B07Y7HD3GR)* (Strelka, 2019) — manifesto arguing for deliberate anthropogenic artificiality rather than retreat from Anthropocene. Terraforming Earth, not Mars.
- *[Accept All Cookies](https://berggruen.org/library/accept-all-cookies)* (Berggruen Press, 2025) — collection of key Antikythera concepts as provocative aphorisms. Written in conjunction with Venice Architecture Biennale 2025 exhibition.
- *[Dispute Plan to Prevent Future Luxury Constitution](https://mitpress.mit.edu/9783956791956/dispute-plan-to-prevent-future-luxury-constitution/)* (E-Flux, 2007) — early collection of short fictions examining architectural and political utopianism.
- *[The New Normal](https://www.park-books.com/en/product/the-new-normal/313)* (Park Books, 2020) — co-edited with [[Nicolay Boyadjiev]] and Nick Axel. Features work from Strelka Institute program.

### Edited Collections
- *[Machine Decision is Not Final: China and the History and Future of Artificial Intelligence](https://www.urbanomic.com/book/machine-decision-is-not-final/)* (Urbanomic, 2024) — co-edited with [[Anna Greenspan]] and [[Bogna Konior]]. ## Research Taste Analysis

### Conceptual Strategy
**Planetary-scale concept multiplication** — Bratton consistently takes familiar concepts (sovereignty, computation, intelligence) and scales them to planetary level, revealing new implications. The Stack methodology: identify a complex system, decompose it into functional layers, analyze each layer's contested affordances.

**Infrastructure philosophy** — treats abstract concepts as operational infrastructure. Not "what is computation?" but "how does planetary computation reorganize sovereignty, territory, governance?" Philosophy becomes engineering at civilizational scale.

**Cross-domain synthesis** — combines Continental philosophy (especially German media theory), Silicon Valley technological analysis, geopolitical theory, and speculative design. Each domain provides conceptual tools the others lack.

### Career Architecture
**Institution building as theory practice** — Antikythera isn't just a research project, it's infrastructure for a new kind of philosophy. Think tank model allows philosophy to operate at policy/industry scale rather than purely academic scale.

**Strategic positioning between domains** — academic (UCSD), industry (Google), think tank (Berggruen), design (SCI-Arc, Venice). Each position enables work the others couldn't support.

**Publishing as platform building** — MIT Press partnership creates publishing infrastructure for entire school of thought, not just individual books.

### Methodology Signature
**Speculative engineering** — thought experiments grounded in technical infrastructure analysis. Not pure speculation but speculation constrained by technological affordances.

**Scale jumping** — constantly moves between micro-technical details and planetary-civilizational implications. Chip architecture → geopolitics → philosophical anthropology.

**Design as philosophical practice** — uses design studios, workshops, prototyping as ways of thinking. Philosophy becomes making.

### Network Strategy
**Cross-disciplinary coalition building** — Antikythera explicitly brings together computer scientists, philosophers, architects, artists, policy people. Creates intellectual infrastructure that transcends disciplinary boundaries.

**Strategic collaboration** — collaborations with [[Bogna Konior]], [[Anna Greenspan]] provide access to different philosophical traditions (accelerationism, China studies, feminist theory).

**Institutional leverage** — Berggruen Institute backing provides resources and platform that pure academic position wouldn't allow.
