# Giorgia Lupi

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Created stub note. Discovered as [[Eyeo Festival]] speaker (2022 edition), key figure in "Data Humanism" movement.

Italian-American information designer and partner at [Pentagram](https://www.pentagram.com/) in New York. Pioneer of **"Data Humanism"** — the idea that data visualization should center human complexity and imperfection rather than false precision. Best known for *[Dear Data](http://www.dear-data.com/)* (with [[Stefanie Posavec]]) — a year-long postcard project where two designers sent each other hand-drawn data visualizations every week. The project became a book and a permanent collection at MoMA.

Also co-authored *[Observe, Collect, Draw](https://www.observecollectdraw.com/)* (interactive journal). Collaborates with institutional clients (Google, Microsoft, UN) on large-scale data-driven narratives.

## Key Works
- *[Dear Data](http://www.dear-data.com/)* (with [[Stefanie Posavec]], 2015–2016) — Year of hand-drawn data postcards. Book: Princeton Architectural Press. MoMA collection.
- *[Observe, Collect, Draw](https://www.observecollectdraw.com/)* — Interactive visual journal.

## Why Interesting
The "Data Humanism" framing is a genuine intellectual position against quantitative reductionism — resonates with Amber's interest in non-reductive computational approaches. The *Dear Data* project is a rare example of analog+computational synthesis that achieved both critical and institutional success. Lupi's position at Pentagram gives her unusual power to shape how data visualization enters mainstream design culture.
