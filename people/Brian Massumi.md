# Brian Massumi

Professor at the **Université de Montréal**, and one of the founding figures of **affect theory** in humanities and cultural studies. His translation of Deleuze & Guattari's *A Thousand Plateaus* (Minnesota UP, 1987) is the canonical English version — making him responsible for how an entire generation of Anglophone scholars accessed D&G. His own work develops a Deleuzian theory of affect, perception, and political action.

Co-founded the **SenseLab** with [[Erin Manning]]. They've run it for 20+ years as a research-creation collective working through movement, sensation, and collective event-making.

Key claim: **affect is prior to cognition** — bodily intensity precedes and exceeds conscious perception and meaning-making. This has become foundational to a huge swathe of media theory, cultural studies, and art practice over the past two decades.

## Key Works
- *A User's Guide to Capitalism and Schizophrenia: Deviations from Deleuze and Guattari* (MIT Press, 1992) — introduces D&G to critical theory anglophone readers
- *Parables for the Virtual: Movement, Affect, Sensation* (Duke UP, 2002) — his most cited work; affect theory and media
- *The Power at the End of the Economy* (Duke UP, 2014) — neoliberalism and affective labour
- *Ontopower: War, Powers, and the State of Perception* (Duke UP, 2015) — security state and pre-emption
- *The Principle of Unrest: Activist Philosophy in the Expanded Field* (OHP, 2017) — short essays; in Immediations series
- *99 Theses on the Revaluation of Value: A Postcapitalist Politics* (Minnesota UP, 2018)

## Why Interesting
Massumi is one of those rare theorists whose conceptual apparatus has escaped the academic journal and become a working tool for practitioners: choreographers, media artists, game designers, architects. The concept of "affect" as he defines it — autonomic bodily intensity that arrives before conscious labelling — has changed how people in art and design think about perception and response. *Parables for the Virtual* remains essential reading for anyone thinking about movement, sensation, and mediated experience.
