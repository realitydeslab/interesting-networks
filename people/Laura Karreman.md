# Laura Karreman

Researcher at Utrecht University, Department of Media and Culture Studies. Chaired **MOCO'24** (Utrecht, "Beyond Control"). Steering committee member of **[[MOCO (Movement and Computing)]]**.

PhD thesis: "The Motion Capture Imaginary: Digital Renderings of Dance Knowledge" (Ghent University, 2017) — a foundational work asking how motion capture shapes what we can know about dance. Humanities perspective on dance digitization.

Research: dance and digital culture, motion capture, dance archives, embodied knowledge, digital humanities.

## Key Works
- "The Motion Capture Imaginary" (PhD thesis, 2017) — Ghent University
- Co-authored with Nanna Verhoeff: *Situating Data: Inquiries in Algorithmic Culture* (Amsterdam University Press, 2023)

## Key Communities
- [[MOCO (Movement and Computing)]] — steering committee, chaired MOCO'24
- Utrecht University — Faculty of Humanities (unusual for MOCO — most hosts are science/engineering)
- [SPRING Performing Arts Festival](https://spring-festival.nl/) — collaborating partner for MOCO'24

## Why Interesting
Rare humanities scholar doing movement computing research — not building systems, but asking what systems do to dance knowledge. The motion capture imaginary question is genuinely provocative. MOCO'24's "Beyond Control" theme under her leadership was one of the most theoretically sophisticated editions.
