# Okwui Enwezor

Nigerian-born curator and critic. The most important curatorial figure of the postcolonial turn in contemporary art. First non-European to head documenta — **Documenta 11** (2002) is universally regarded as the first truly global, postcolonial documenta. Co-founder of *NKA: Journal of Contemporary African Art* (1993). Director of Haus der Kunst, Munich (2011–2019). Artistic Director of Venice Biennale 56 (2015). Died 2019.

**Raqs connection**: Invited [[Raqs Media Collective]] to Documenta 11 (2002) — a foundational early validation for Raqs internationally. Enwezor's postcolonial curatorial vision shares deep intellectual ground with Raqs: the critique of Western-centric art institutions, the importance of archive-based practice, the politics of knowledge production.

**Documenta 11** was structured around five "platforms" across four continents before its Kassel exhibition — treating the exhibition as only one part of a transdisciplinary, global knowledge project. This model influenced how Raqs later conceived the 11th Shanghai Biennale.

**Key exhibitions**: In/Sight: African Photographers 1940-Present (Guggenheim 1996); documenta 11 (2002); Archive Fever (ICP 2008); The Rise and Fall of Apartheid (Haus der Kunst 2013); Venice Biennale 56 (2015).
