# Paulo Tavares

Architect and researcher. Key contributor to [[Forensic Architecture]]'s environmental justice investigations, especially work on Amazonian Indigenous territories and colonial histories. Trained at Goldsmiths' Centre for Research Architecture.

## Domains
- Environmental forensics
- Indigenous rights and territory
- Colonial violence and land
- Architecture as political ecology

## Key Work
- **Yanomami Territory investigations** — documenting illegal gold mining and violence in Yanomami Indigenous territories in the Amazon Rainforest
- **Namibia colonial genocide** (with Forensis) — reconstructing sites of German colonial violence (1904–1908), including concentration camp on Shark Island
- Research on how forests, rivers, and landscapes function as witnesses to violence — extending Susan Schuppli's "matter as witness" into ecological space

## Why Interesting
Extends FA's counter-forensic methodology into the domain of environmental and colonial violence. His work asks: what does the land remember? How do forests testify? Deeply resonant with more-than-human design and multispecies thinking.
