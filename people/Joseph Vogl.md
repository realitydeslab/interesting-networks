# Joseph Vogl

German media theorist and literary scholar at Humboldt University Berlin and Princeton University. Best known for his media-historical analysis of **financial capitalism, the state, and sovereignty** — tracing how markets, credit, and debt operate as media systems. Vogl occupies the intersection of German media theory, Foucauldian governmentality studies, and political economy.

## Domains
- Media history of financial capitalism, credit, and sovereign debt
- Governmentality, state/market assemblages
- Literary theory and media aesthetics
- German Idealism and its relation to media

## Key Works
- [*The Specter of Capital*](https://www.sup.org/books/title/?id=22745) (Stanford UP, 2015; orig. *Das Gespenst des Kapitals*, 2010) — media history of financial capitalism; how finance constructs its own "oikodicy" (secular theodicy of market rationality) ⭐⭐⭐
- [*The Ascendancy of Finance*](https://www.wiley.com/en-us/The+Ascendancy+of+Finance-p-9781509510078) (Polity, 2017) — continuation of the financialization analysis
- *Kalkül und Leidenschaft* (Diaphanes, 2002) — on 18th-century political economy and passions
- Works on Kleist, Kafka, and literary materiality

## Key Communities
- [[IKKM Weimar]] — Senior Fellow, Apr–Sep 2011
- Humboldt University Berlin — long-term position
- Princeton University — visiting engagements

## Why Interesting
Vogl applies the media-historical method to financial systems — showing how credit, debt, futures contracts, and derivatives are *media* in the full technical sense: they process information, produce representations, and constitute subjects. This is directly relevant to Amber's interest in agentic systems operating within economic/protocol infrastructures. The *Specter of Capital* is a model for how media analysis can illuminate political economy without resorting to simple ideology critique.
