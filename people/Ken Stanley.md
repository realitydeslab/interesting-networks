# Ken Stanley

Computer scientist and philosopher of open-endedness. Co-inventor of NEAT neuroevolution and novelty search. Leading thinker on why objective-driven search fails and how non-objective discovery enables breakthrough innovation. His "Why Greatness Cannot Be Planned" fundamentally challenges how we approach AI research, creativity, and discovery.

## Background

Kenneth O. Stanley earned his PhD in Computer Science from the University of Texas at Austin under **[[Risto Miikkulainen]]**, developing the foundational work on **NeuroEvolution of Augmenting Topologies (NEAT)**. His doctoral research established him as a pioneer in evolutionary computation, specifically in evolving both neural network weights and topologies simultaneously.

After his PhD, he became Charles Millican Professor in Computer Science at the **University of Central Florida**, where he built one of the world's leading research groups in artificial life, evolutionary computation, and open-endedness. His lab, EPlex (Evolutionary Complexity Research Group), became a major force in computational creativity.

His career trajectory represents a unique path: from academic research to industry leadership at major AI companies, then to independent work focused on scientific superintelligence.
