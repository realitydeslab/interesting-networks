# Ken Stanley

Computer scientist. Co-inventor of NEAT (neuroevolution) and novelty search. Key thinker on open-endedness and serendipity in AI.

## Key Works
- *Why Greatness Cannot Be Planned: The Myth of the Objective* (2015) — with Joel Lehman
- NEAT — NeuroEvolution of Augmenting Topologies
- Novelty search — searching for novelty rather than objectives
- Picbreeder, DALL-E precursor concepts

## Connections
- [[Joel Lehman]] — close collaborator, co-author
- [[David Ha]] — open-endedness community
- [[Sakana AI]] — intellectual influence
- [[ALIFE Conference]] — regular contributor
- [[Santa Fe Institute]] — intellectual orbit

## Why Interesting
"Why Greatness Cannot Be Planned" is a paradigm-shifting book about the failure of objective-driven search. Novelty search is foundational for open-endedness. His thinking on serendipity and non-objective discovery connects to computational creativity and speculative design.
