# Sara Imari Walker

Theoretical physicist and astrobiologist developing new physics to explain what life is, how it emerged, and how to detect it. Co-developer of assembly theory, a groundbreaking framework for quantifying the emergence of complexity and life. Deputy Director of the Beyond Center for Fundamental Concepts in Science at Arizona State University.

## Background
Born and raised in Connecticut. Graduated cum laude from Florida Institute of Technology with B.S. in Physics (2005), then earned PhD in Physics and Astronomy from Dartmouth College (2010) under doctoral advisor **[[Marcelo Gleiser]]**. Her thesis "Theoretical Models for the Emergence of Biomolecular Homochirality" established her early focus on life's origins.

After Dartmouth, began postdoctoral fellowship at **[[Georgia Institute of Technology]]** working at the NSF/NASA Center for Chemical Evolution. In 2011, accepted NASA Postdoctoral Program Fellowship with the **[[NASA Astrobiology Institute]]** at ASU. Became assistant professor at ASU's School of Earth and Space Exploration and Beyond Center in 2013, where she has been building her emergence lab focused on the fundamental question: "What is Life?"

## Domains
