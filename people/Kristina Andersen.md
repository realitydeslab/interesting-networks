# Kristina Andersen

Associate professor at TU Eindhoven's Future Everyday cluster. Works on imagining technological futures through digital craftsmanship and collaborations with semi-intelligent machines in the context of material practices and soft fiber-based things. Co-chair of [[ACM DIS]] Steering Committee alongside [[Ron Wakkary]]. Previously based at STEIM (Amsterdam) for 14 years.

## Background
- Based at STEIM (Studio for Electro-Instrumental Music, Amsterdam) for 14 years
- Part of the Making Things Public art research program at Gerrit Rietveld Academie

- Longstanding advisor of Stimuleringsfonds Creatieve Industrie (Dutch Creative Industries Fund)

## Why Interesting
The STEIM connection is significant — 14 years at one of the most important experimental electronic music studios in the world gives her a perspective on technology-as-instrument that most HCI researchers lack. The "how can we allow each other to imagine our possible technological futures?" framing is distinctly feminist and speculative. Her position co-chairing DIS alongside Wakkary means she's co-shaping the field's direction.
