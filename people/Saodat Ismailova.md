# Saodat Ismailova

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-27:** Stub created during EYE Filmmuseum deep research. EYE Art & Film Prize winner 2022.

Uzbek filmmaker and video artist working at the intersection of cinema and visual art. First generation of Central Asian artists to come of age in the post-Soviet era. Her work investigates the rich, layered culture of Central Asia — connecting myths, rituals, dreams, and spiritual heritage to contemporary ecological and political crises. Works between Tashkent and Paris; studied at Le Fresnoy (France).

## Domains

- **Central Asian spiritual heritage** — Sufi traditions, oral knowledge, women's spiritual roles, ritual practice
- **Post-Soviet ecology** — the Aral Sea disaster, the destruction of landscapes, language loss under Soviet regimes
- **Women's identity and emancipation** — women as keepers of cultural/spiritual heritage, from mother to daughter
- **Archival practice** — Soviet cinema archive, early Uzbek films, found footage
- **Multi-channel video installation** — immersive, multi-sensory, music-forward
- **Film + visual art crossover** — documentary films that become installations

## Background

Studied film at Tashkent State Art Institute. Won Grand Prix at Tashkent Student Film Festival (1999). Continued at Fabbrica, Centre for the Study of Arts, Treviso, Italy (2002). Documentary *Aral: Fishing in an Invisible Sea* — Best Documentary, Turin Film Festival (2004). Trained in directing/scriptwriting at Robert Redford's Sundance Institute. Feature debut *40 Days of Silence* — nominated Best Debut Film, Berlin Film Festival (2014). Studied at [Le Fresnoy](https://www.lefresnoy.net/) (2015–2017).

Since 2017: major institutional exhibitions including Centre Pompidou, Stedelijk Museum, Musée du quai Branly.

## Key Works

- **[Zukhra](https://www.saodatismailova.com/)** (2013) — first video installation, Venice Biennale (Central Asian Pavilion). About Zukhra, the Uzbek name for Venus/the Evening Star.
- **[The Haunted](https://www.saodatismailova.com/)** (2017) — Caspian tiger, threatened with extinction. At Tromsø Centre for Contemporary Art.
- **[Two Horizons](https://www.saodatismailova.com/)** (2017) — two-channel HD video, 24 min, 4.1 surround.
- **[Stains of Oxus](https://www.saodatismailova.com/)** (2016) — three-channel installation about the Amu Darya river and Soviet-era destruction.
- **[Qyrg Qyz](https://www.saodatismailova.com/)** (2018) — interdisciplinary project. Musée du quai Branly, Paris; Brooklyn Academy of Music, New York.
- **[Her Right](https://www.saodatismailova.com/)** (2020) — video collage on women's religious autonomy.
- **[18,000 Worlds](https://www.eyefilm.nl/en/programme/saodat-ismailova/841392)** — first major retrospective at [[EYE Filmmuseum]] (Jan–June 2023). Title refers to Islamic cosmology: 18,000 worlds in the universe.
