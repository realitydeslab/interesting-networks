# Qiu Zhijie 邱志杰

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Stub created during Chinese Art-Tech Scene deep research.

Chinese artist, curator, and theorist. Professor at [[China Academy of Art]] (CAA). One of the key Chinese artist-curators who bridges art practice with critical theory. Known for a "total art" approach encompassing video, calligraphy, performance, installation, map-making, and writing. Important figure in connecting Chinese contemporary art discourse to both Chinese tradition and international critical theory.

## Background
Born 1969, Zhangzhou, Fujian. Studied at China Academy of Art (then Zhejiang Academy of Fine Arts) in Hangzhou. Has been a professor at CAA for many years and is a central figure in the School of Intermedia Art's intellectual culture. Also teaches and works in Beijing.

Has curated major Chinese biennials and international exhibitions, including serving as curatorial commissioner for China's Venice Biennale pavilion. Part of a generation (alongside Cao Fei, Lu Yang, Zhang Peili's students) that developed Chinese media art in the 2000s–2010s.

## Domains
- **Calligraphy and text** — His practice grew out of Chinese calligraphic tradition; his early performance works included writing *Heart Sutra* repeatedly.
- **Video and installation** — Medium-length and feature-length works exploring memory, history, power.
- **Map-making** — Large-scale diagrammatic works mapping relationships between concepts, histories, and futures. "Total art" methodology.
- **Curating and theory** — Major Chinese curator. Has curated China's pavilion at Venice Biennale multiple times. Major thematic exhibition curator.

## Key Works
- ***Copying the Orchid Pavilion Preface One Thousand Times*** (临摹兰亭序一千次, 1990–1995) — Performance: repeatedly copying Wang Xizhi's 4th-century calligraphic masterpiece until the paper is illegible. Questions repetition, mastery, and tradition.
- ***Total Art Map*** series — Large-scale diagrammatic maps connecting art history, theory, practice. A kind of visual encyclopedia.
- Solo exhibitions at UCCA Beijing, international institutions.
