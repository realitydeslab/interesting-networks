## Key Works
- **[AI-generating algorithms, an alternate paradigm for producing general artificial intelligence](https://arxiv.org/abs/1905.10985)** (2019) — his most ambitious paper. Proposes that instead of manually designing AI piece by piece, we should create algorithms that automatically generate AI. Identifies three pillars: meta-learning architectures, meta-learning learning algorithms, and generating effective learning environments. Argues this could be faster path to AGI than manual approach.
- **[Illuminating search spaces by mapping elites](https://arxiv.org/abs/1504.04909)** (2015) — introduces MAP-Elites algorithm with Jean-Baptiste Mouret. Rather than finding single optimal solution, creates map of high-performing solutions across different behavioral dimensions. Revolutionized evolutionary computation by focusing on diversity and illumination rather than just optimization.
- **[Go-Explore: a New Approach for Hard-Exploration Problems](https://arxiv.org/abs/1901.10995)** (2019) — with Adrien Ecoffet et al. at Uber AI Labs. Revolutionary approach to exploration in reinforcement learning using "remember, return, explore" strategy. Achieved superhuman performance on Montezuma's Revenge (18 million points, beating human world record) and first algorithm to score above zero on Pitfall.
- **[OMNI: Open-endedness via Models of human Notions of Interestingness](https://arxiv.org/abs/2306.01711)** (2023) — with Jenny Zhang et al. Uses foundation models as "models of interestingness" to guide open-ended learning toward tasks that are both learnable and interesting, based on humans' implicit understanding of what's worthwhile.
- **[Managing extreme AI risks amid rapid progress](https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/science.adn0117)** (2024) — co-author with Yoshua Bengio, Geoffrey Hinton, Stuart Russell and other AI safety luminaries. High-profile Science paper on AI existential risks and governance.
- **[The Surprising Creativity of Digital Evolution](https://www.nature.com/articles/s42256-020-0163-1)** (Nature Machine Intelligence, 2020) — with Joel Lehman et al. Demonstrates unexpected creative solutions found by evolutionary algorithms.
- **[Biological underpinnings for lifelong learning machines](https://www.nature.com/articles/s42256-022-00452-0)** (2022) — with 40+ co-authors including major figures across AI/neuroscience. Comprehensive review connecting biological learning to machine learning.
