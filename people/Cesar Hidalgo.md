## Why Interesting for Amber
His EAAMO colloquium: **"Why do people judge humans differently from machines? The role of agency and experience"** — found that people apply utilitarian moral philosophy to machines but deontological philosophy to humans. Key finding: judging machines becomes more similar to humans when machines are perceived as having more **agency** (ability to plan/act) but NOT more **experience** (ability to feel). This directly intersects with Amber's machine behavior and machine death research — questions of moral status, agency perception, care ethics for machines.
