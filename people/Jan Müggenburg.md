# Jan Müggenburg

Professor for Digital Cultures at [[ICAM Leuphana]] and Speaker of the Leuphana Centre for Digital Cultures (CDC). Historian of cybernetics and bionics, media archaeologist, expert on Heinz von Foerster and the Biological Computer Laboratory (BCL). Currently working on a media archaeology of assistive interfaces and augmentative communication.

## Domains
- History of cybernetics (Heinz von Foerster, BCL, second-order cybernetics)
- Media archaeology of science
- History of bionics and biological computing
- Assistive interfaces and augmentative/alternative communication (AAC)
- Media history of batteries and energy storage

## Key Works
- [*Lebhafte Artefakte: Heinz von Foerster und die Maschinen des Biological Computer Laboratory*](https://fis.leuphana.de/de/publications/2c0d9778-f177-4321-9e0f-a55f503c7d21) (2018, Göttingen) — definitive study of von Foerster's BCL and the life-like machines it produced
- [*Reichweitenangst: Batterien und Akkus als Medien des Digitalen Zeitalters*](https://fis.leuphana.de/de/publications/96659a16-877f-43af-8103-0ce424a1ec18) (2022, Bielefeld, ed.) — batteries as media of the digital age
- "From Learning Machines to Learning Humans" (*History of Education*, 2021) — how cybernetic machine models inspired pedagogies
- "Me and AAC — Alternative and Augmented Communication in West Germany" (*TMG Journal of Media History*, 2024)
- "Perverse Bienen: Artificial Life und der Apfel der Erkenntnis" (with Warnke, *ZfM* 10/18, 2018) — artificial life and the apple of knowledge

## Why Interesting for Amber
Müggenburg's BCL work is the deepest historical account of the ALife genealogy in Germany. His work on von Foerster — who coined the term "second-order cybernetics" and whose BCL produced self-organizing machines — is essential context for Amber's own ALife and machine behavior research. The paper on "Perverse Bienen" (perverse bees/artificial life) with Warnke is directly in Amber's territory.
