# Elizabeth A. Povinelli

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-03-01:** Stub created during Karrabing Film Collective deep research.

Anthropologist and critical theorist at Duke University. Key theorist of "geontopower" — the power exercised through distinctions between Life and Nonlife. Long-term collaborator with [[Karrabing Film Collective]]. Her concept of "geontologies" is a foundational framework for more-than-human theory from an Indigenous/settler colonial perspective.

## Key Works
- *[Geontologies: A Requiem to Late Liberalism](https://www.dukeupress.edu/geontologies)* (Duke UP, 2016) — key text. Geontopower, Life/Nonlife, late liberalism.
- *Economies of Abandonment: Social Belonging and Endurance in Late Liberalism* (Duke UP, 2011)
- *Between Gaia and Ground* (2021, with Karrabing Film Collective)
- Essay in e-flux Journal on Karrabing's work

## Why Interesting
Direct intellectual bridge between Indigenous ontology and more-than-human theory. Geontopower is one of the most rigorous theoretical tools for thinking about non-human agency in political terms.
