# Anthony Dunne

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Full deep research completed. Comprehensive profile written covering career trajectory, all projects, exhibitions, publications, awards, collaborators, venue circuit analysis, and Research Taste Analysis.

Co-creator (with [[Fiona Raby]]) of **speculative design** and **critical design** as academic disciplines. Through [[Dunne & Raby]] (est. 1994) and his teaching at the [[Royal College of Art]], [[Parsons School of Design|Parsons/The New School]], and now the [[China Academy of Art]], Dunne has shaped how an entire generation thinks about design's relationship to technology, society, and the imagination. His books — *Hertzian Tales* (1999), *Design Noir* (2001), *Speculative Everything* (2013), and *Not Here, Not Now* (2025) — are foundational texts. Work in permanent collections at [[MoMA]], [[V&A]], [[MAK Vienna]], and [[FRAC|Fnac/FRAC]].

## Background
- Born in Ireland. Studied Industrial Design at the [[Royal College of Art]], then worked at **Sony Design in Tokyo** — a formative experience with Japanese corporate design culture.
- Returned to London and completed a **PhD in Computer Related Design (CRD)** at the RCA under [[Gillian Crampton Smith]]. The CRD Research Studio became the institutional seedbed for critical design thinking.
- **Founding member of the CRD Research Studio** at RCA with Fiona Raby, where they were Senior Research Fellows.
- **Professor and Head of Design Interactions, RCA** (2005–2015) — the programme that, in [[Paola Antonelli]]'s words, "changed the course of design." Built a unique MA that brought synthetic biology, speculative fiction, electronic arts, and critical theory into design education. The programme's closure/suspension after his departure triggered a crisis at the RCA.
- **University Professor of Design and Social Inquiry, Parsons/The New School, New York** (2016–2025) — co-directed the **Designed Realities Studio** with Fiona Raby. Worked across Parsons' School of Design Strategies and the **Graduate Institute for Design, Ethnography and Social Thought (GIDEST)**. Developed practice-led research bringing design, humanities, and social sciences into conversation.
- **Distinguished Visiting Professor, [[China Academy of Art]]** (2025–present) — the latest pivot, significant for the East/West intellectual axis.
- Life partner and collaborator with [[Fiona Raby]].

## Key Works

### Books
- [*Not Here, Not Now: Speculative Thought, Impossibility, and the Design Imagination*](https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262049665/not-here-not-now/) (Dunne & Raby, MIT Press, 2025) — Explores designing when the future seems impossible. A "conceptual travelogue" through science, philosophy, and literature. Includes imagined archives, libraries, glossaries, taxonomies, and speculative objects (stone rafts, vegetable lambs, pocket universes, objects undergoing space-time collapse).
- [*Speculative Everything: Design, Fiction, and Social Dreaming*](https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262019842/) (Dunne & Raby, MIT Press, 2013) — The canonical speculative design text. Translated into Chinese (2017, Jiangsu Fine Art Publishing House; 2024, agbook Seoul), Russian (2017, Strelka Institute), Korean (2024). Paperback reissued 2024 with new foreword.

### Major Design Projects (chronological)
- **Hertzian Tales** (1994–97) — The original PhD research project. Electronic products reimagined as cultural and aesthetic objects rather than functional tools. Coined "critical design."
- **Weeds, Aliens and Other Stories** (1994–98) — with [[Michael Anastassiades]]. Psychological furniture exploring English garden obsessions. In the permanent collection of the [[V&A]].
- **Project #26765: FLIRT** (1998–2000) — EU-funded research on location-specific mobile interaction. Collaboration between Philips Research, Helsinki Telephone Corporation, Infogrames, and RCA. Included "Pixel Kissing" social experiments and "Lost Cat" virtual creatures roaming Helsinki's cellular network. Research team: Neil Clavin, Paul Farrington, Marcus Gosling, Nick Durrant, Shona Kitchen, [[Ben Hooker]], Arita Patel, Fiona Raby, Niell Sweeny, Brendan Walker.
- **Placebo Project** (2001) — Eight prototype objects placed with volunteers to investigate attitudes toward electromagnetic fields in the home. Objects designed to elicit stories about the "secret life" of electronic objects. Electronics: Jon Rogers. Photography: [Jason Evans](http://www.jasonevans.info/).
- **Designs for Fragile Personalities in Anxious Times** (2004/05) — with [[Michael Anastassiades]]. Objects designed for irrational but real anxieties (alien abduction, nuclear annihilation). "Huggable Atomic Mushroom" in MoMA permanent collection. "Hideaway Type 2" in FRAC Île-de-France.
- **Evidence Dolls** (2005)
- **Spymaker** (2006/07)
- **Technological Dreams Series: No.1, Robots** (2007) — Four robots exploring different human-robot relationships (subservient, intimate, dependent, equal). Video by [[Noam Toran]], sound design by [[Scanner]]. Commissioned by [[Z33]] (curated by Jan Bolen). In permanent collections of **MoMA, New York** and **Fnac (Fond national d'art contemporain), Paris**.
- **Do You Want to Replace the Existing Normal?** (2007/08) — with [[Michael Anastassiades]]. "Designs for an Over-Anxious World." Includes Statistical Clock, S.O.C.D., Risk Watch, Alignment. Electronics: Chris Hand, Erik Kearney. Software: David Muth. Funded by Arts Council England.
- **Designs for an Overpopulated Planet: Foragers** (2009) — Speculative devices for a future of food scarcity.
- **What If...** (2009) — Exhibition at [[Science Gallery Dublin]].
- **United Micro Kingdoms (UMK)** (2012/13) — Speculative design of four alternative political systems for England. Major exhibition project.
- **Not Here, Not Now** (2014–ongoing) — Long-running research project exploring impossibility and the design imagination, culminating in the 2025 book.
- **Meinong's Jungle (Theory of Objects)** (2015) — Philosophical design project.
- **Many Worlds Working Group (MWWG)** (2017–) — Ongoing research group.
- **In Search of an Impossible Object** (2018) — with Philipp Schmitt, Designed Realities Studio, The New School.
- **Archive of Impossible Objects: Globes** (2019)

## Key Concepts Developed
1. **Critical Design** — "Uses speculative design proposals to challenge narrow assumptions, preconceptions and givens about the role products play in everyday life. More of an attitude than anything else." First used in *Hertzian Tales* (1999). Its opposite is "affirmative design."
2. **Speculative Design** — Design that uses fiction, scenarios, and imagined futures not to predict but to provoke discussion about preferable futures.
3. **Design Noir** — The "secret life" of electronic objects. Dark, complex emotions as legitimate territory for design.
4. **Hertzian Space** — The invisible electromagnetic landscapes created by electronic objects in domestic environments.
5. **Design Interactions** — Design as a vehicle for exploring technology's social/cultural/ethical implications, not just solving problems.
6. **Impossibility as Design Material** — In *Not Here, Not Now*, impossibility is treated not as a failure but as generative: "When reality fails us, what can design do?"

## Venue Circuit Analysis
Dunne operates across **four distinct circuits**:

### 1. Elite Design Education (global)
The lecture list reads like a directory of the world's top design schools: RCA, Parsons, RISD, Yale, Harvard, Columbia, Pratt, Cranbrook, SAIC, Aalto, Eindhoven, Konstfack, ECAL, ENSCI, Sandberg, UdK Berlin, Kolding. He has personally lectured at essentially every significant design school in Europe, North America, and increasingly Asia. This is the **institutional backbone** of speculative design's spread.

### 2. European Design Institutions
Strelka Moscow, Vitra Design Museum, Design Museum Gent, MAK Vienna, Z33 Hasselt, Whitechapel Gallery London, Architectural Association, Centre Pompidou. The European circuit emphasises cultural institutions and biennales over commercial design events.

### 3. Asian Art-Tech (emerging)
China Academy of Art (Hangzhou), CAFA (Beijing), Shih Chien University (Taipei), KYOTO Design Lab, IAMAS, Musashino Art University, Samsung Research Seoul, MMCA Seoul, HKDI. The China pivot (2019 first lecture → 2025 Distinguished Visiting Professor) is a deliberate trajectory.

### 4. Industry Research Labs
Sony Design Tokyo (early career), Intel Research, Microsoft Research (Cambridge + Asia), Nokia, Panasonic (multiple offices), Spotify, IDEO, Modern Meadows. These are not marketing engagements — they're research conversations. Dunne positions speculative design as a tool for corporate R&D imagination.

**What the circuit reveals:** Dunne has bypassed the art world's gallery/biennial system almost entirely. His circuit is **design education + cultural institutions + corporate research labs**. This is strategic: by operating through education, he shaped practitioners rather than collectors. The China Academy of Art appointment suggests a new phase focused on non-Western design imagination.

## Research Taste Analysis

### Why Amber should care deeply
Anthony Dunne is the **intellectual godfather** of the field Amber works in. But beyond the obvious genealogy, there are specific strategic insights:

**1. The meta-strategy is institutional, not aesthetic.** Dunne didn't just make speculative objects — he built an *educational programme* (Design Interactions) that trained a generation. The lesson: to change a field, change how it's taught. Amber's own position at the intersection of art, technology, and critical theory could learn from this institutional strategy.

**2. The China pivot is the most interesting current move.** After building critical design in London (RCA) and trying to transplant it to New York (Parsons), Dunne is now taking it to Hangzhou. This isn't retirement — it's an attempt to see what speculative design becomes when filtered through Chinese intellectual traditions. The "Many Worlds Working Group" and "Not Here, Not Now" project both gesture toward non-Western ontologies (multiple worlds, impossibility as productive). This resonates directly with Amber's interest in [[Sinofuturism]] and non-Western tech theory.

**3. The corporate research lab circuit is underrated.** Dunne's repeated engagement with Sony, Intel, Microsoft, Panasonic, and Spotify as *research* interlocutors (not just clients) shows a model where speculative/critical design operates as applied philosophy for technology companies. This is a viable career path Amber could explore.

**4. "Not Here, Not Now" marks a conceptual shift.** The early work was about *electronic objects* (Hertzian Tales) and *everyday technology* (Design Noir). The middle period was about *futures* (Speculative Everything). The latest work is about *impossibility* — designing when the future itself seems foreclosed. This trajectory mirrors the broader shift in critical design from tech-critique to ontological speculation, which connects to Amber's interests in posthumanism and more-than-human design.

**5. Key connections for Amber's network:**
- [[Paola Antonelli]] (MoMA) — Dunne's most powerful institutional ally
- [[James Auger]] — DI alumnus, now working on critical design in France
- [[Superflux]] (Anab Jain) — DI-adjacent, one of the most successful speculative design studios
- [[Julian Bleecker]] / Near Future Laboratory — design fiction practitioner
- [[Ai Hasegawa]] — Japanese speculative designer
- The China Academy of Art connection links directly to Chinese art-tech scene

**6. The "Not Art" insistence is ideological.** Dunne has consistently and deliberately refused to call his work art ("If it is regarded as art it is easier to deal with, but if it remains as design it is more disturbing"). This is a strategic choice about where critique has more power. Worth thinking about for Amber's own positioning.

## Why Interesting
The founder of the field. But more than that: Dunne's career is a **case study in how to institutionalize a radical idea**. From PhD thesis (Hertzian Tales) → to named discipline (critical design) → to educational programme (Design Interactions, RCA) → to canonical text (Speculative Everything) → to global circulation (Parsons, Strelka, China Academy of Art). Every step was deliberate. The China pivot signals that the next chapter of speculative design may be written in Hangzhou, not London or New York.
