# Pablo Wendel

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-03-04:** Stub created during E-WERK Luckenwalde deep research.

German artist who acquired and reanimated a former brown-coal power station in Luckenwalde as **E-WERK Luckenwalde** (launched 2019) — the world's first contemporary art centre powered by its own renewable energy production. Founded **Performance Electrics gGmbH**, the company managing the energy infrastructure. Co-directs E-WERK with curator [[Helen Turner]].

His practice sits at the intersection of electricity, machines, and live performance. The central conceptual move of his life's work: a dead industrial machine (coal plant) reanimated as a living art institution powered by different energy. Machine death and resurrection as practice.

## Key Work
- **E-WERK Luckenwalde** — founded 2017; launched 2019; co-directed with Helen Turner
- **Performance Electrics gGmbH** — the energy company through which the art is funded
- **E-PAVILION** — yearly commissioning programme for permanent functional sculptures on E-WERK grounds
- **Kunststrom / switch.kunststrom.com** — allows households to switch their electricity provider to E-WERK's renewable supply

## For Amber
The artist whose career *is* the question of machine death, institutional autonomy, and energy-funded cultural production. The coal plant IS the artwork — every other work flows from that founding gesture. Worth studying as a model for how an artist can build infrastructure rather than objects.
