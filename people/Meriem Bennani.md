# Meriem Bennani

Moroccan-American video artist and filmmaker based in Brooklyn, NY. Makes groundbreaking video installations and sculptures that use 3D animation, augmented reality, projection mapping, and documentary hybrid forms to explore migration, identity, postcolonial history, biotechnology, and queerness — always through an unexpectedly absurdist, campy, humorous lens. Best known for the CAPS Trilogy (2018–2022) and the viral pandemic series *2 Lizards* (2020, with [[Orian Barki]]). In 2025, co-directed her debut animated feature film *Bouchra* with Barki, screening at TIFF and NYFF. December 2025: won the inaugural BOSS Award for Outstanding Achievement at Art Basel Miami Beach ($50,000).

## Domains

- **3D animation + projection mapping** — uses AR and 3D-animated characters as narrative vehicles; maps video to custom sculptural projection structures
- **Migration and diaspora** — North African immigrant experience, border politics, teleportation as metaphor for displaced populations
- **Postcolonial Morocco** — French colonial education system, Moroccan family structures, Islamic fashion, vernacular culture
- **Biotechnology + speculative fiction** — imagines futures where biotech (teleportation, genetic modification) restructures society
- **Gender, queerness, sexuality** — explored through camp, comedy, and indirect approach to taboo subjects
- **Reality TV / documentary hybrid** — "hyperactivity of genre" — appropriates TV formats, phone footage, advertising, music video, sci-fi
- **Instagram as exhibition medium** — has used social media as primary distribution for works (2 Lizards first distributed on Instagram; Snapchat following)
- **Animated feature film** — expanded practice into feature-length arthouse animation (*Bouchra*, 2024)

## Background

Born 1988 in Rabat, Morocco. Grew up between Rabat and Paris. Earned her **MFA in Animation** from the **École Nationale Supérieure des Arts Décoratifs** (ENSAD), Paris (2011) and her **BFA** from **The Cooper Union**, New York (2012). The combination matters: French art-school rigor + NY avant-garde context + Moroccan cultural DNA = an artist who can move between institutional legitimacy and radical informality.

Her work consistently fuses the specificities of Moroccan life — family dynamics, vernacular music, class markers, religious identity, post-French colonial education — with the global circulation of digital media. The absurdist humor isn't decoration: it's strategy. Bennani has said she wants to approach "sensitive subjects with unexpected freshness" and reach the rawness of Moroccan cultural humor she witnessed as a child but couldn't speak in herself. Art becomes the medium for that frankness.

Runs **JNOUN**, a creative studio she founded with her sister **Zahra Bennani** (design work, parallel to art practice).

## Key Works

### The CAPS Trilogy (2018–2022)
A three-part speculative fiction set on CAPS — a fictional island in the Atlantic Ocean where teleportation has replaced air travel and displaced migrants caught teleporting illegally are interned inside a magnetic shield. The world of the CAPS is built out across three works:

- **[*Party on the CAPS*](https://www.c-l-e-a-r-i-n-g.com/exhibitions/party-on-the-caps/)** (2018) — 8-channel video installation, 30 minutes. Fiona, an eccentric 3D-animated crocodile, narrates life on the island. The installation uses a wrap-around screen, custom sculpture (croco-pleather stools with LED lights, metal ladders), and rear projection. World premiere: **Biennale de l'Image en Mouvement (BIM)**, Geneva, 2018. Subsequent solo at CLEARING New York, 2019; Julia Stoschek Collection, Berlin, 2020.

- **[*2 Lizards*](https://www.moma.org/magazine/articles/505)** (2020, with [[Orian Barki]]) — 8-episode animated web series made during COVID-19 lockdown in Brooklyn. Bennani and Barki voice unnamed lizard versions of themselves navigating a NYC populated by anthropomorphic animals. Made 2–7 days per episode using iPhone-shot live-action backgrounds + 3D animation. First distributed on Instagram (viral hit, millions of views), then on Adult Swim's *Off the Air*. Acquired by **MoMA** (2021, through generosity of Elie Khouri) and **Whitney Museum**. Later given solo presentation at Whitney (2022) and Nassauischer Kunstverein Wiesbaden (2021). Cited as one of the earliest artworks to directly address the pandemic.

- **[*Life on the CAPS*](https://www.thepowerplant.org/whats-on/exhibitions/life-on-the-caps)** (2022) — final chapter of the trilogy. Rooted in research on island societies, biotechnology, and vernacular Moroccan music. Layered live-action + CGI. Toured widely: Renaissance Society (Chicago), Nottingham Contemporary (UK), Power Plant (Toronto, paired with TIFF Wavelengths program), Hartwig Art Foundation (Amsterdam), Fondation Kamel Lazaar (Tunis), and more.

### Other Major Works

- **[*Fardaous Funjab*](https://www.meriembennani.com/)** (2014) — Fake reality TV show about a hijab designer. Camp exploration of Muslim fashion and identity politics. Early signal of her "hyperactivity of genre" approach.

- **[*FLY*](https://www.meriembennani.com/)** (2017) — Multi-layered video; a 3D-animated fly navigates Bennani's home city of Rabat, talks with family members, bursts into Rihanna. About digital presence and family in diaspora.

- **[*Ghariba/Stranger*](https://www.artdubai.ae/portraits-meriem-bennani/)** (2017) — Commissioned by Art Dubai Projects. Interactive installation featuring video portraits of Moroccan women in a series of viewing stations; visitors journey through a game-like narrative. Produced in collaboration with Forward James Filmmakers.

- **[*Mission Teens: French School in Morocco*](https://whitney.org/exhibitions/2019-biennial/)** (2019) — 36-minute video commissioned for the **Whitney Biennial 2019**. A fictional documentary following students at Descartes High School (the French school in Rabat), narrated by a donkey avatar. Interrogates French colonial education inherited by Moroccan elite. Accompanied by sculptural "Pony Tail" viewing station; shown in CLEARING's Brussels gallery (2021). Also: expanded as solo exhibition at **Fondation Louis Vuitton**, Paris (Oct 2019–Jan 2020).

- **[*Guided Tour of a Spill (CAPS Interlude)*](https://www.thepowerplant.org/)** (2021) — Solo at François Ghebaly, LA.

- **[*Windy*](https://www.thehighline.org/)** (2022) — Public art commission co-commissioned by High Line Art and Audemars Piguet Contemporary. On the High Line, New York.

- **[*Cursed Objects*](https://www.c-l-e-a-r-i-n-g.com/)** (2022) — Solo at CLEARING, Brussels.

- **[*Bouchra*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bouchra)** (2024, with [[Orian Barki]]) — Debut animated feature film (83 min festival version). A queer Moroccan coyote filmmaker (Bouchra) navigates her relationship with her mother after coming out. Shot in 3D animation with anthropomorphic animal characters against live-action backgrounds. Co-written with Ayla Mrabet. Produced by Fondazione Prada, 2 Lizards Production, Hi Production, SB Films. Features voice work from [[Yto Barrada]]. Music by Flavien Berger. World premiere as *For Aicha*: Fondazione Prada, Milan, October 2024. Festival: **TIFF Platform** (Sept 2025), **New York Film Festival** (Currents section, Sept 2025), Rome (Oct 2025), Bordeaux (Oct 2025). North American distribution: Film Movement (Dec 2025). Hailed as "one of the most intimate and political animations of the year."

- **[*Sole Crushing*](https://www.lafayetteanticipations.com/)** (2025) — Solo at Lafayette Anticipations, Paris.

## Venue Circuit Analysis

Bennani moves through **four distinct circuits** that rarely overlap — and she connects them:

**1. New York institutional/prestige:**
Whitney Museum, MoMA, High Line, Julia Stoschek, CLEARING NY, Whitney Biennial. She's now fully canonized in the NYC art world.

**2. European film-art crossover:**
EYE Filmmuseum (Amsterdam), Biennale de l'Image en Mouvement (Geneva), Nottingham Contemporary (UK), Nassauischer Kunstverein (Germany), Fondation Louis Vuitton (Paris), Lafayette Anticipations (Paris), Sadie Coles (London). The EYE Prize was the entry point; the BIM premiere cemented her in this circuit.

**3. MENA circuit:**
Art Dubai Projects, Fondation Kamel Lazaar (Tunis), NG Essaouira (Morocco). She explicitly returns to her Moroccan roots — not just thematically but institutionally. This distinguishes her from artists who only point back to origin as subject matter.

**4. Film festival circuit (emerging):**
TIFF Wavelengths/Platform, NYFF Currents, Rome, Bordeaux. The *Bouchra* feature marks a genuine expansion into cinema — not just "video art at film festivals" but a proper animated feature with distribution (Film Movement).

The key insight: **she builds in North Africa what others only reference.** Solo shows in Tunis, Essaouira, and Art Dubai aren't tokenism — they're institutional commitments to MENA audiences. Her gallery choices (CLEARING, François Ghebaly, Sadie Coles) give her three geographic bases (NY, LA, London) while Fondazione Prada gives her European film prestige.

Small venues that matter: **Nassauischer Kunstverein Wiesbaden** (German public for experimental video), **Renaissance Society Chicago** (the key midwestern platform for serious contemporary work), **Stanley Picker Gallery** (Kingston — small UK art school gallery, where she started).

## Why Interesting

**The core move:** Bennani takes the most advanced digital tools available (3D animation, motion capture, projection mapping, AR) and uses them to tell stories about the most politically urgent subjects (immigration, postcolonialism, queerness, family) — but always through absurdism, not earnestness. This is the same move Amber's research tracks: computational power as *narrative and political* medium, not just technical spectacle.

**The CAPS world** is gold-standard speculative design: a fully imagined society with its own rules (teleportation replaces airplanes, magnetic shields, displaced populations), used to analyze real border politics without being didactic. The crocodile narrator is design thinking applied to politics — make it strange enough to see clearly.

**The viral hit question:** *2 Lizards* went viral during COVID because it was distributed on Instagram — not in a gallery, not at a festival, but on the phone. Bennani has consistently used social media as a distribution medium (Instagram, Snapchat), treating the scroll as an exhibition context. This is ahead of the curve on questions that are now central to digital art: what does exhibition mean when the screen is in everyone's pocket?

**The feature film move:** *Bouchra* is significant — she's taken the technique developed over a decade of installation (3D animals, live-action backgrounds, hybrid documentary) and pushed it into arthouse cinema. The film is openly queer, explicitly Moroccan, and uses animation to approach intimacy that live-action would make unbearable. TIFF Platform + NYFF Currents + Film Movement distribution = she's now a filmmaker, not just a video artist.

**Connection to Amber's research:** 
- Computational creativity: using 3D animation as epistemological tool, not just aesthetic
- More-than-human: the anthropomorphic animal characters (lizards, coyotes, crocodiles) trouble the boundary between human and animal experience
- Speculative design: CAPS world is a fully built speculative society with coherent rules and political analysis
- Feminist/queer: *Bouchra* is a queer coming-out story told through animation — the form enables the vulnerability
- The "high-tech idiom" concept: advanced tools + political urgency + humor = Amber's Venn diagram
