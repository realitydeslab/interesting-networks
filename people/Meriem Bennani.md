# Meriem Bennani

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-27:** Stub created during EYE Filmmuseum deep research. EYE Art & Film Prize winner 2019.

Moroccan-American video artist and filmmaker based in New York. Works at the intersection of augmented reality, 3D animation, installation, and documentary. Best known for immersive multimedia environments that explore migration, identity, biotechnology, gender, and the digital distortion of reality — through an unexpectedly light, absurdist, and humorous lens.

## Domains

- **Augmented reality and 3D animation** — uses AR and 3D-animated characters as narrative vehicles
- **Migration and identity** — North African diaspora, immigrant experience, digital displacement
- **Biotechnology art** — speculative biological futures (teleportation, genetic modification)
- **Gender and religion** — taboo subjects explored through camp and comedy
- **Reality television / documentary hybrid** — appropriates commercial media formats
- **Immersive installation** — room-filling environments with sculptural elements, precise projection mapping

## Background

Studied in Paris and New York. Trained in fine art with strong roots in video and performance. Her work is distinctive for its "high-tech idiom" — deploying cutting-edge digital tools (AR, mapping, 3D animation) to discuss political/social issues with surprising lightness. The absurdist comic tone is deliberate strategy to approach subjects (immigration, religious taboo, biotech ethics) without moralizing.

Selected exhibitions: MoMA PS1, Art Dubai, Centre d'Art Contemporain Genève (Biennale de l'Image en Mouvement).

## Key Works

- **[Fardaous Funjab](https://www.meriembennani.com/)** (2014) — Fake reality TV show featuring a hijab designer. Camp exploration of Muslim fashion, identity politics.
- **[FLY](https://www.meriembennani.com/)** (2017) — Multi-layered choreography; 3D-animated fly buzzes through her home city, talks to family, bursts into Rihanna. About digital presence and family diaspora.
- **[Party on the CAPS](https://www.meriembennani.com/)** (2018) — Immersive installation. Fiona, an eccentric crocodile, narrates life on CAPS, an imaginary island/refugee camp where teleportation has replaced airplanes. Speculative biotech + immigration politics.

## Awards & Recognition

- **[EYE Art & Film Prize 2019](https://www.eyefilm.nl/en/about-eye/eye-prize/meriem-bennani)** — €/£25,000 + EYE exhibition. Jury: "pioneering video artist... reprogrammes original material and lends it new meaning through playful digital distortion and surrealist disruption."
- Joint exhibition at [[EYE Filmmuseum]] (2022) with Kahlil Joseph and Karrabing Film Collective.
