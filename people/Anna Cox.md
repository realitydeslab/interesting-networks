# Anna Cox

Professor of HCI at the **UCL Interaction Centre (UCLIC)**, in the Division of Psychology and Language Sciences. Her research focuses on understanding the relationships between the design of information and communications technologies (ICTs) and **behavioural outcomes**, and leveraging these relationships in the design of novel interfaces and systems to support people in work.

## Why Interesting

- Co-organizer of **CHI 2026 W57: The Future of Cognitive Personal Informatics** — directly relevant to the ClearMind metacognition coach concept
- Working at the intersection of **consumer neurotechnology** and **personal informatics** — how people will live with cognitive tracking technology
- UCL is Amber's institution network (London connection)
- The "Cognitive Personal Informatics" framing maps directly onto what ClearMind does: making cognitive patterns visible and actionable

## Connection to Amber's Work

- **W57 workshop** explores how consumer neurotechnology will become cognitive personal informatics — the same vision as ClearMind but from a wearable/physiological sensing angle
- ClearMind approaches cognitive personal informatics through **language analysis + AI** rather than EEG/fNIRS — complementary approach
- Potential collaborator or advisor for the metacognition product's academic grounding
- Workshop is contributing to a **Springer edited book** — possible publication venue

## Key Research Areas

- Behavioral outcomes of technology design
- Cognitive personal informatics
- Consumer neurotechnology interaction design
- Digital wellbeing and work-life balance
- Neuroergonomics and future of work
