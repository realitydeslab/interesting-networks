# Norbert Wiener (1894–1964)

American mathematician, computer scientist, philosopher. MIT professor. Coined and founded **cybernetics** — the science of circular causation, feedback, and control in animals and machines. One of the most consequential intellectuals of the 20th century for the fields of AI, cognitive science, robotics, and complex systems.

Cybernetics, stochastic processes, information theory, philosophy of mind, ethics of automation.

## Key Works
- **[*Cybernetics: Or Control and Communication in the Animal and the Machine*](https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262730099/)** (1948) — the founding text; coined "cybernetics"
- **[*The Human Use of Human Beings: Cybernetics and Society*](https://archive.org/details/humanuseofrhumanbeings)** (1950) — popular version; early critical ethics of automation; remarkably prescient on AI and labor
- **"Behavior, Purpose and Teleology"** with Rosenblueth & Bigelow (1943, *Philosophy of Science*) — [DOI: 10.1086/286878](https://doi.org/10.1086/286878) — the proto-cybernetics paper; first scientific treatment of goal-directed behavior via feedback

## Why Interesting
Wiener was the first to argue rigorously that **all intelligent behavior is the result of feedback mechanisms that could be simulated by machines**. This is the foundational claim behind machine behavior research, ALife, and agent ethology. Also remarkable: Wiener was *worried* about automation eliminating jobs and undermining human dignity — writing a progressive critique of AI in 1950 that reads as current.
