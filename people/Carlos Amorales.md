# Carlos Amorales

Mexican artist whose practice spans graphic design systems, video, animation, performance, and installation. Known for "Amorales & Co.," a company whose logo is a black silhouette wrestling mask (luchador), and for creating complex visual lexicons — "Liquid Archive" (2004–ongoing), a database of drawn silhouettes that can be recombined. Works with narrative instability, the aesthetics of violence and spectacle, and how visual systems encode power.

## Why Interesting
Part of the "post-Mexican art" formation theorized by [[Cuauhtémoc Medina]]. His "Liquid Archive" concept — a visual database that can be recombined to generate new meanings — has interesting resonances with questions about how generative AI systems create and distribute visual vocabularies.
