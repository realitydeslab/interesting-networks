# Nadia Yala Kisukidi

French-Congolese philosopher at University of Paris 8. Regular participant in [[Les Ateliers de la Pensée de Dakar]]. Works on African philosophy, decolonial thought, and Black feminism. Rising major figure in African-diasporic intellectual circles. Works on the relationship between diaspora and homeland, and on African philosophical traditions.

## Domains
- African philosophy (ubuntu, traditions)
- Decolonial thought
- Black feminism
- Philosophy of the diaspora

## Why Interesting
She's at the intersection of African philosophical tradition and continental European philosophy (Paris 8 is Deleuze/Badiou territory). This cross-pollination is exactly the kind of bridging work that produces new conceptual frameworks. Closer to London/Oxford than Sarr.
