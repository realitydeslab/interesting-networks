# Georges Canguilhem

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Stub created during Simondon deep research. Key figure in French philosophy of science and medicine.

French philosopher and physician (1904–1995). One of the most important figures in French philosophy of science and medicine. Director of the Institut d'histoire des sciences et des techniques (Sorbonne). Key thesis advisor and friend of [[Gilbert Simondon]] (directed Simondon's complementary thesis on technical objects). His concept of *the normal and the pathological* influenced Foucault, Althusser, Bourdieu, and an entire generation. A student of Alain; a teacher of Foucault (who dedicated his major works to Canguilhem).

- *Études d'histoire et de philosophie des sciences* (1968)

## Why Interesting
Canguilhem connects Simondon's framework to the broader tradition of French philosophy of science (Bachelard, Koyré). His concept of normativity — that living beings establish their own norms rather than conforming to external standards — resonates with Simondon's individuation and with more-than-human design. Supervised Simondon's complementary thesis directly.
