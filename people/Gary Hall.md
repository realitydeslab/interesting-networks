# Gary Hall

Professor of Media at **Coventry University**, where he directed the **Centre for Postdigital Cultures** (founded 2017; he ran it until 2025). Co-founder and Director of **[[Open Humanities Press]]**. Key figure in the **[[Radical Open Access Collective]]** — for Hall, open access is not a publishing decision but a political act challenging the commodification of knowledge.

Critical theorist and media philosopher working on digital culture, politics, technology, and the question of what scholarship should be in an age of AI and neoliberal academic capitalism. His recent work (*Masked Media*, 2025) has taken a radical step: co-writing with AI and collective collaborators under a **Collective Conditions for Re-Use** licence, refusing the category of "human-authored work" entirely.

## Key Works
- *Digitize This Book! The Politics of New Media, or Why We Need Open Access Now* (Minnesota UP, 2008) — critique of digital humanities orthodoxies; argument for radical OA as political necessity
- *Pirate Philosophy: For a Digital Posthumanities* (MIT Press, 2016) — piracy as philosophical practice; hacking academic culture; what would it mean to genuinely unsettle academic convention?
- *The Uberfication of the University* (Minnesota UP, 2016) — short provocation; the university as platform; academic labour and the gig economy
- *A Stubborn Fury: How Writing Works in Elitist Britain* (OHP, 2021) — class, culture, and the English literary novel; playfully pirates Tom McCarthy, Eribon, Édouard Louis; challenges the grip of realist humanist writing on British culture
- *Masked Media: What It Means to Be Human in the Age of Artificial Creative Intelligence* (OHP, 2025) — follow-up to *A Stubborn Fury*; co-written with AI + collective collaborators; challenges liberal humanist authorship; published under Collective Conditions for Re-Use licence; argues there is "no such thing as the human" — the nonhuman is already in(the)human

## OHP Leadership
Co-founded OHP in 2006 with Sigi Jöttkandt, Paul Ashton, and David Ottina. Edits the **Liquid and Living Books** cluster: Liquid Books (open editing/remixing), Living Books About Life (JISC-funded, humanities-science bridge), and Combinatorial Books: Gathering Flowers (with Janneke Adema — creative remix of OHP back catalogue).

## Why Interesting
Hall is the most persistent and principled radical open access advocate in UK academia. Unlike those who "support OA" while still publishing in prestige journals, Hall has organized his entire career around demonstrating that serious scholarship can be done outside commercial publishing logic. *Masked Media* (2025) is genuinely risky as an academic book — co-written with AI, published under a non-standard licence, explicitly rejecting authorship as a legal-ontological category. This is not posturing: it is a philosophical position enacted through the form of the book itself. His Centre for Postdigital Cultures has become one of the few academic centres genuinely doing "postdigital" work (not just celebrating the digital, but critically inhabiting it).
