# Gary Hall

Professor of Media at Coventry University. Co-founder of **[[Open Humanities Press]]** and a key figure in the **Radical Open Access Collective**. Philosopher and media theorist working on digital culture, open access, and the politics of knowledge production.

Co-author (with Joanna Zylinska and Clare Birchall) of the Jisc-funded **Living Books about Life** project — open-access books bridging humanities and sciences. Has published in Zylinska's MEDIA : ART : WRITE : NOW series at Open Humanities Press (*A Stubborn Fury*, *Masked Media*).

Known for challenging academic publishing orthodoxies and advocating for radical open access as a political act, not just a technical solution. Key interlocutor of Joanna Zylinska's open-access publishing world.

## Key Works
- *Digitize This Book!* (2008) — critique of digital humanities and open access
- *Pirate Philosophy* (2016, MIT Press) — hacking academic culture
- *A Stubborn Fury* (Open Humanities Press) — part of MEDIA:ART:WRITE:NOW series
- *Masked Media* (Open Humanities Press) — second in the series
