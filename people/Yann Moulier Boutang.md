# Yann Moulier Boutang

French economist, philosopher, and political theorist. Co-founder with [[Antonio Negri]] and [[Maurizio Lazzarato]] of both *Futur Antérieur* (1989–1998) and *Multitudes* (2000–present). Primary theorist of "cognitive capitalism" — the thesis that contemporary capitalism is driven by the extraction of value from cognitive/creative/knowledge work rather than physical labor. Has written on migration, digital labor, and the political economy of the internet.

## Domains
- Cognitive capitalism (his central framework)
- Political economy of knowledge and creativity
- Migration and labor
- Digital economics
- Post-operaismo political theory

## Key Work
- *Cognitive Capitalism* (2011, Polity) — the English translation of his main theoretical work. Argues that knowledge, creativity, and cognitive labor have become the primary source of capitalist value — extending the immaterial labor thesis into economics.
- *Le capitalisme cognitif* (2007, French original)
- Co-editor of *Futur Antérieur* and *Multitudes*

## Why Interesting
"Cognitive capitalism" is one of the better frameworks for understanding the political economy of AI — if cognitive labor is the new frontier of value extraction, then AI systems that automate cognitive work are the most significant development in capitalist history since industrialization. This framework is more rigorous than "AI will take our jobs" and more specific than general Marxist analysis.
