## The Digital Consciousness Model (DCM) — Key Technical Contribution

The DCM is Shiller's most significant current project. Structure:
- **13 consciousness stances** incorporated (leading scientific theories + informal perspectives)
- **200+ indicators** (behavioral, architectural, biological) assessed by experts
- **Hierarchical Bayesian model**: indicators → consciousness-relevant features → stance verdicts → aggregated probability
- **Systems compared:** 2024 state-of-the-art LLMs (GPT-4, Claude 3 Opus), humans, chickens, ELIZA
- **Starting prior:** ⅙ probability of consciousness across all systems
- **Key finding:** Evidence leans against 2024 LLMs being conscious, but not overwhelmingly; perspectives that focus on biological similarity/embodiment push probability down strongly; perspectives emphasizing cognitive complexity push it up.
- Webinar held February 10, 2026 to discuss findings.

The project is explicitly modeled on Rethink Priorities' **Moral Weight Project** (which estimated welfare ranges across animal species) — same team, same Monte Carlo methodology, applied to AI.

## Research Taste Analysis

### Conceptual Strategy
**Theory aggregation over theory commitment**: Most consciousness researchers pick a theory and defend it. Shiller's signature move is to explicitly refuse this — to instead build models that weight all credible theories by expert assessment. This is not epistemic timidity; it's a deliberate strategy for producing *actionable* conclusions despite intractable disagreement. The DCM's 13 stances approach produces something no single-theory argument can: a result that remains useful even if you're uncertain which theory is correct.

**Operationalize the hardest question**: The hard problem of consciousness is literally called hard. Shiller's move is to ask: given that we can't solve it, what can we do? His answer: build probabilistic scaffolding around it. Don't wait for certainty; use Bayesian reasoning to make uncertainty itself legible and actionable.

**Skepticism as contribution**: The "Skepticism about Introspection in LLMs" paper is a model of using rigorous argument to resist over-confident claims in a field prone to anthropomorphism. He's not saying "definitely not conscious" — he's saying "here are specific reasons to doubt specific experimental interpretations." This precision about what the evidence does and doesn't show is itself a research contribution.

### Career Architecture
**Institutional platform building**: Rather than staying in traditional academic philosophy (tenure track), Shiller has built research infrastructure — Rethink Priorities (existing institution), AI Cognition Initiative (new institution he helped create), Cambridge Digital Minds (fellowship he's teaching at). Each institution creates leverage for research that journals can't.

**EA community as academic bypass**: Publishing on LessWrong and EA Forum alongside peer-reviewed journals lets Shiller reach practitioners and funders directly. The DCM paper was released to LW/EA audiences before going through traditional peer review. This is a deliberate acceleration strategy: get feedback from smart people who care about impact, not just credentialed academics.

**Moral Weight Project as proof of concept**: By joining RP after the animal welfare Moral Weight Project had already proven the methodology works for cross-species welfare estimation, Shiller positioned himself to extend that methodology to AI — the most impactful possible application. Strategic adoption of proven methods from adjacent domain.

**Web development → Research**: The Humane League web developer background is interesting. Shiller has technical credibility that pure philosophers lack, and philanthropic/EA community immersion that pure AI researchers lack. The career arc deliberately constructed the rarest possible combination.

### Methodology Signature
**Bayesian consensus building**: His explicit approach to priors in the DCM — choosing a ⅙ prior and acknowledging it's somewhat arbitrary, then checking it against intuitive outcomes for humans/chickens/ELIZA — is philosophically honest and practically useful. He wants results that could be consensus outputs even from deeply disagreeing experts.

**Technical precision about architecture**: The "Skepticism about Introspection" paper shows detailed knowledge of LLM attention mechanisms, tokenization, RLHF vs RLVF training phases — level of technical detail unusual for philosophers. This lets him make claims about what architectures could and couldn't plausibly support.
