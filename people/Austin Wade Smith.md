# Austin Wade Smith

Executive Director of [[Regen Foundation]]. Visionary of "living covenants" — DAOs enacted as public infrastructure that inherently incentivize ecological stewardship. Key voice in the bioregional governance movement and ReFi (Regenerative Finance) space. Uses pronouns they/them.

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-27:** Created stub from Regen Foundation deep research.

## Domains
- **Ecological institutions** — governance frameworks where living systems participate directly
- **Bioregional governance** — organizing social and economic life around ecosystem boundaries
- **DAO design as commons** — blockchains as "addressing" technologies giving non-humans rights and economic presence
- **Regenerative finance (ReFi)** — financial instruments for ecosystem regeneration

## Key Idea
*"If I say a Douglas fir has a treasury and has an endpoint in a digital addressing system, that's possible, and we don't have to inherit whether or not Chase Bank allows you to open an account for nonhuman entities."*

**On legibility:** *"We're so habituated into auditing things into discrete boundaries that we lack resources to understand from an economic perspective how to define value as anything other than a commodity."*

The key move: recognizing that "many systems people have built are also inherently ecological and living systems too — our infrastructure can also be recast and understood as living systems if we understand that there isn't a separation between nature and people."

## Why Interesting
Austin is building one of the most practically-grounded more-than-human governance projects anywhere. Unlike many theorists, they're deploying actual systems — blockchains that encode ecological stewardship, forests with crypto wallets, rivers holding communal funds. Their framing (infrastructure as living system, digital/natural as never-separate) is philosophically rigorous and practically implemented. Featured in [Noema Magazine's bioregionalism feature](https://www.noemamag.com/inside-the-push-to-rebuild-society-around-ecosystems/) as a leading voice in the BioFi movement.
