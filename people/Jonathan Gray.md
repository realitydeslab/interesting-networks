# Jonathan Gray

Professor at the **Department of Digital Humanities, King's College London**. Works on **data cultures**, **knowledge politics**, and **public data infrastructures** — studying how data is produced, circulated, and put to work in public life, journalism, science, and policy.

Recently published ***Public Data Cultures*** — launched alongside books by [[Joanna Zylinska]] and Maya Indira Ganesh at a joint KCL event (late 2025).

## Collaborations with [[Daniel Chávez Heras]]
Co-developed the **Creanalytics** project: using video editing techniques (computational supercuts via Videogrep) as data analysis. Also co-developed work on Creanalytics with [[Mercedes Bunz]].

## Why Interesting
Occupies a distinctive position in digital humanities — critically rigorous about data while also technically engaged. The Creanalytics collaboration with Daniel shows an interesting convergence between Gray's data cultures perspective and Daniel's computational cinema work.
