# Kerry James Marshall

Painter and artist. One of the most significant American painters working today. Known for large-scale figurative paintings of Black subjects — celebrations of Black everyday life, joy, and community, rendered with monumental historical ambition. Deeply connected to the Black LA arts community. Production designer on [[Julie Dash]]'s *Daughters of the Dust* (1991). Long-term creative relationship with [[Arthur Jafa]]. Represented by [Jack Shainman Gallery](https://www.jackshainman.com/) and [David Zwirner](https://www.davidzwirner.com/).

## Why Interesting
Marshall's painting practice is a formal intervention — by painting Black subjects at monumental scale, in the tradition of Western history painting, he asks what it means to make Black people visible in that tradition. The *School of Beauty and Culture* (1993), *Better Homes, Better Gardens* (1994–97), and the *Mastry* retrospective (MCA Chicago, 2016; MoMA PS1, 2017) are the landmarks. His work is the painting equivalent of what Jafa does in video: formal rigor + cultural urgency + historical depth.

The production designer role on *Daughters of the Dust* is significant — the visual world of that film (the color palette, the costuming, the objects) is partly Marshall's contribution.
