# Steve Woolgar

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-03-03:** Stub created during EASST deep research. Oxford-based STS founding figure — proximity flag for Amber.

STS founding figure. Pioneer of laboratory studies and reflexivity in science studies. Co-author (with Bruno Latour) of *Laboratory Life: The Construction of Scientific Facts* (1979/1986) — one of the foundational texts of STS. More recent work on "ontological choreography," digital infrastructures, and accountability in technology.

## Background
Professor at Oxford's Saïd Business School. Emeritus standing in UK STS. Long career at Brunel, then Oxford. Key figure in establishing STS as a discipline. Distinctive approach: radical reflexivity — applying STS methods to STS itself.

## Domains
- Laboratory studies, ethnomethodology of science
- Reflexivity in STS
- Technology, accountability, ontological choreography
- Digital infrastructures and "accountability" in digital systems

## Key Works
- [*Laboratory Life*](https://www.press.princeton.edu/books/paperback/9780691028323/laboratory-life) (with Latour, 1979/1986) — *the* STS laboratory study classic
- [*Science: The Very Idea*](https://www.routledge.com/Science-The-Very-Idea/Woolgar/p/book/9780415046619) (1988) — accessible intro to STS epistemology
- *Virtual Society? Technology, Cyberbole, Reality* (ed., 2002)
- Various essays on ontological choreography and "ontological politics" (with Annemarie Mol)
