# Jenny Odell

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Created stub note. Discovered as [[Eyeo Festival]] speaker and Eyeo's most-cited endorser. Key figure at intersection of attention, technology critique, and slow observation.

Multi-disciplinary artist and writer. Best known for *[How to Do Nothing: Resisting the Attention Economy](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/603652/how-to-do-nothing-by-jenny-odell/)* (Melville House, 2019) — a book arguing for the radical act of withdrawal, attention, and refusal of productivity culture. Started as a talk at Eyeo 2019 (or earlier editions) and became a New York Times bestseller.

Also author of *[Saving Time: Discovering a Life Beyond the Clock](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/621717/saving-time-by-jenny-odell/)* (2023) — on the political economy of time.

Art practice: collage work using satellite imagery, screenshots, e-commerce products. Works that use computational tools to reveal the strangeness of digital infrastructure.

Her Eyeo talk was so celebrated that Dave Schroeder quoted her endorsement of the festival on the homepage: *"Thanks for gathering a community whose perspective on technology is refreshingly critical and deeply human."*

## Why Interesting
*How to Do Nothing* is one of the clearest articulations of why non-productivity, slowness, and attention matter — and it emerges from within the tech world (Odell has been embedded in Silicon Valley culture while critiquing it). Amber's work on non-human computation and more-than-human thinking shares the impulse to slow down and notice what isn't captured by efficiency metrics. Odell is also a birdwatcher — the "close observation" practice is a method, not a hobby.
