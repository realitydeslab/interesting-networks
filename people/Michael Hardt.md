# Michael Hardt

American political philosopher and literary theorist at **Duke University**. Best known for the *Empire* trilogy co-authored with **[Antonio Negri](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antonio_Negri)**: *Empire* (2000), *Multitude: War and Democracy in the Age of Empire* (2004), *Commonwealth* (2009). These books proposed that global capitalism had produced a new form of sovereignty ("Empire") no longer organized around nation-states, and that resistance would emerge from the "multitude" — the transversal class of immaterial laborers, migrants, queer people, etc.

## Why Interesting

Hardt and Massumi are interconnected institutional presences: Hardt co-edited the **Theory Out of Bounds** series at University of Minnesota Press (1993–2007) with Massumi and Sandra Buckley. This series shaped what "critical theory" meant in English for 15 years, publishing Agamben, Guattari, Virilio, Lyotard, and many others. Both Hardt and Massumi come from D&G-inflected political philosophy but developed differently: Hardt moved toward the Negri/autonomism orbit; Massumi stayed with affect and process philosophy.

Hardt's blurb for Massumi's *The Personality of Power* (2025): *"By mobilizing and building creatively on Deleuze and Guattari's conceptual arsenal, Brian Massumi provides a new and convincing analysis of the political and affective logics of Trump and his followers."*

## Key Works
- *Empire* (Harvard UP, 2000) — with Antonio Negri
- *Multitude: War and Democracy in the Age of Empire* (Penguin, 2004) — with Negri
- *Commonwealth* (Harvard UP, 2009) — with Negri; the conclusion of the trilogy
- *Assembly* (Oxford UP, 2017) — with Negri
- *Marx in Motion: A New Materialist Marxism* (Oxford UP, 2023)
- *Gilles Deleuze: An Apprenticeship in Philosophy* (Minnesota UP, 1993) — his dissertation, published; reading of Deleuze through Spinoza and Bergson
