# Lynn Margulis

Evolutionary biologist who overturned the field with the theory of endosymbiosis — the idea that eukaryotic cells (all complex life) originated from symbiotic mergers between bacteria. She also developed, with James Lovelock, the scientific basis of the Gaia hypothesis. First wife of Carl Sagan. Member of the [[Lindisfarne Association]]. One of the most important biologists of the 20th century; systematically marginalized by mainstream neo-Darwinism and vindicated by subsequent research.

## Why Interesting

Margulis's endosymbiosis theory demolished the individualist picture of the organism — life is not a collection of competing individuals but a history of symbiotic unions. A bacterium became a mitochondrion; another became a chloroplast; *you* are a colony. This is the empirical foundation for more-than-human thinking: organisms are not bounded individuals but relational assemblages. Her work is directly relevant to Amber's more-than-human design and machine behavior interests — she demonstrated that "identity" in life is inherently porous and symbiotic.

## Key Works
- [*Symbiosis in Cell Evolution*](https://archive.org/details/symbiosisincelle0000marg) (1981, W.H. Freeman) — the major scientific monograph; endosymbiosis theory in full
- [*Microcosmos: Four Billion Years of Microbial Evolution*](https://archive.org/details/microcosmosfourl00marg) (with Dorion Sagan, 1986, Summit Books) — accessible account of bacterial evolution and symbiosis
- [*What is Life?*](https://archive.org/details/whatislife00marg) (with Dorion Sagan, 1995, Simon & Schuster) — philosophical questions about life's nature
- [*Acquiring Genomes: A Theory of the Origins of Species*](https://www.basicbooks.com/titles/lynn-margulis/acquiring-genomes/9780465043927/) (with Dorion Sagan, 2002, Basic Books) — major challenge to standard evolutionary theory; speciation by symbiosis not just mutation+selection
- *Slanted Truths* (with Dorion Sagan, 1997) — essays on science and culture

## Key Concepts
- **Endosymbiosis (serial endosymbiosis theory)** — eukaryotic organelles (mitochondria, chloroplasts) were once free-living bacteria incorporated into ancestral cells by symbiosis, not competition; confirmed by molecular biology
- **Symbiogenesis** — new species arise primarily through symbiosis and horizontal gene transfer, not just gradual mutation and selection
- **Gaia hypothesis** — co-developed with James Lovelock; Earth's biosphere is a self-regulating system that maintains conditions for life
- **Holobiont** — the organism as assemblage of multiple species (host + microbiome); now central to contemporary biology
