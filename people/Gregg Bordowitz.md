# Gregg Bordowitz

Writer, video artist, AIDS activist. ISP alum (1985–86) who became ISP Director in 2023. Central figure in AIDS activist art — co-founded Testing the Limits collective, DIVA (with ACT UP). Tested HIV+ in 1988. Key bridge between video activism, queer theory, and art education.

## Domains
Video art, AIDS activism, writing, institutional art education, queer theory.

## Key Works
- *Fast Trip, Long Drop* (1994) — landmark AIDS activist documentary
- *Picturing a Coalition* (1994 essay) — video as political tool

## Key Communities
- [[Whitney Independent Study Program]] — Director (2023–present); alum (1985–86)
- ACT UP — direct action advocacy, AIDS activism
- Testing the Limits — video activist collective
- DIVA (Damned Interfering Video Activists)
- School of the Art Institute of Chicago — former professor (Video/New Media/Animation)

## Writing
- "AIDS: Cultural Analysis/Cultural Activism" — *October* journal (1987)

## Why Interesting
Bordowitz embodies the ISP tradition: rigorous theory + activist practice + institutional critique. His appointment as ISP Director signals the program's recommitment to activist/video art lineage. The bridge between critical theory, video, and political action is directly relevant to Amber's interest in art as political practice.
