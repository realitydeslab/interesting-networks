# Samia Henni

Historian and exhibition-maker working at the intersection of colonial history, architecture, and spatial justice. Her primary focus is France's colonial and military violence in Algeria — specifically how architecture was weaponized as counterinsurgency, and how France's nuclear weapons program was tested in the Algerian Sahara. Currently teaching at McGill University. One of the most important voices in architectural decoloniality.

## Why Interesting
Henni is doing something rare: treating colonial architecture not as style or history but as weaponized infrastructure — and making that argument through exhibitions as much as books. The traveling *Discreet Violence* exhibition was a remarkable piece of anti-institutional cultural diplomacy. Her work on nuclear colonialism (France testing atomic bombs in Algeria through 1966) is a largely unknown story with massive ongoing implications. For Amber: this is someone working at the intersection of spatial justice, decoloniality, and practice-based knowledge — the kind of rigorous engagement between making and thinking that resonates with more-than-human design perspectives.
