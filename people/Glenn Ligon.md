# Glenn Ligon

Conceptual artist (born 1960, Bronx). ISP alum (1985). Work explores race, language, desire, sexuality, identity using text. One of the originators of the term "Post-Blackness." Draws on James Baldwin, Zora Neale Hurston, Richard Pryor. Works in painting, neon, video, photography. Now also ISP faculty/mentor.

## Domains
Conceptual art, text-based art, race, language, identity, queer theory, Black American cultural history.

## Key Works
- *Untitled (I Am Not Tragically Colored)* (1990) — text painting drawing on Hurston
- Neon text works drawing on Baldwin and Pryor

## Awards
- MacArthur Fellowship
- Numerous major museum acquisitions

## Key Communities
- [[Whitney Independent Study Program]] — alum (1985) + current faculty
- Foundation for Contemporary Arts (board)
- Robert Rauschenberg Foundation (board)

## Why Interesting
Ligon's method — using language as both subject and medium, grounding aesthetics in Black literary theory — is a model for art that is simultaneously theoretically rigorous and politically engaged. The connection between text, race, and material practice is relevant to Amber's interest in critical computing and the politics of representation.
