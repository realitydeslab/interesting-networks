# Brian Larkin

Anthropologist at Columbia University; one of the founders of **infrastructure studies** as a subfield of media anthropology. Known for his ethnography of Nigerian media infrastructure (film, radio, cellular networks) and for theorizing infrastructure as both material system and poetic/political form.

## Domains
- Infrastructure studies (political, poetic, and technical dimensions)
- Media anthropology in Nigeria/West Africa
- Colonial and postcolonial technology transfer
- Film and cinema in non-Western contexts

## Key Works
- [*Signal and Noise: Media, Infrastructure, and Urban Culture in Nigeria*](https://www.dukeupress.edu/Signal-and-Noise) (Duke UP, 2008) — ethnography of Nigerian media; cinema, radio, urban infrastructure; essential text for non-Western media studies ⭐⭐⭐
- ["The Politics and Poetics of Infrastructure"](https://www.annualreviews.org/doi/10.1146/annurev-anthro-092412-155522) (Annual Review of Anthropology, 2013) — widely cited theoretical statement on infrastructure's dual nature ⭐⭐⭐

## Why Interesting
Larkin's "politics and poetics of infrastructure" framing is essential for thinking about how technical systems operate simultaneously as functional networks AND as cultural/political statements. His work on Nigerian cinema infrastructure shows how media travels, adapts, and transforms in non-Western settings — critical counterpoint to Eurocentric media theory. Highly relevant to Amber's interest in decentralized/distributed technical systems and their cultural dimensions, and to how "protocols" structure social life.
