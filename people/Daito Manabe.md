# Daito Manabe 真鍋 大度

Artist, programmer, composer, DJ. Co-founder and principal of [Rhizomatiks](https://rhizomatiks.com/) — Tokyo's premier creative technology collective. Works at the intersection of data, body, technology, and live performance. Trained in mathematics (Tokyo University of Science) and media arts (IAMAS), his practice fuses rigorous engineering with aesthetic ambition. Known for: viral face-hacking experiments (2008), transformative live performance technology for Perfume, the AR presentation at Rio 2016 Olympics closing ceremony, and a long catalog of Ars Electronica distinctions. Primary bridge between the Euro-American art-tech world and Japan's uniquely technically sophisticated scene.

## Background
- Born 1976, Tokyo, into a musical family. Fascinated by video game music and programming from age 10 (NEC computer, DX-7 synthesizer, wanted to make a game like *Gradius*).
- **Mathematics, [Tokyo University of Science](https://www.tus.ac.jp/en/)** — encounter with composer Iannis Xenakis's ideas on mathematical music generation became the conceptual foundation of his creative work.
- **[IAMAS (Institute of Advanced Media Arts and Sciences)](https://www.iamas.ac.jp/en/)**, Gifu — Japan's pioneering graduate program for art × technology. Studied experimental media arts, programming as creative medium. Met **Motoi Ishibashi** (future Rhizomatiks CTO) here.
- During and after university: worked as DJ, opened for Flying Lotus and Squarepusher in Tokyo; played in a jazz band.
- **2004–2009:** Collaborated on productions with Dumb Type artist collective ([Fujimoto Takayuki](https://www.dumbtype.com/)) — where he also connected with choreographer MIKIKO.
- **2006:** Co-founded Rhizomatiks (now organized under Abstract Engine Co., Ltd., Shibuya-ku, Tokyo) with Motoi Ishibashi, Seiichi Saito, Hidenori Chiba. Company framing: "like a music label" — values-aligned people pursuing independent projects under one roof.
- **2025:** Co-founded [sonicPlanet LTD](https://sonicplanet.io/) in London with Sinan Bokesoy — developing experimental software and plugins for generative audio.
- Also leads **Studio Daito Manabe** — personal research/production studio within the Abstract Engine ecosystem.

## Venue Circuit Analysis

Daito Manabe operates across **five distinct circuits** simultaneously:

1. **Japanese pop culture / mass market**: NHK Kohaku, Perfume concerts, Tokyo Dome, TV Asahi. This circuit is unique — almost no Western media artists operate at this level of mainstream entertainment. It funds the experimental R&D.

2. **Japanese institutions / serious art**: NTT ICC (Tokyo), YCAM (Yamaguchi), Japan Media Arts Festival, Kyoto University. The institutional legitimacy circuit in Japan.

3. **European electronic music festivals**: Sónar Barcelona, MUTEK (multiple cities), TodaysArt (The Hague), Dekmantel (Amsterdam), Resonate (Serbia), STRP (Netherlands). These are nightlife-adjacent venues that validate experimental AV performance — not gallery art.

4. **European media art / Prix Ars Electronica**: Ars Electronica (Linz), S+T+ARTS Prize (EU). The "art world" validation circuit.

5. **International spectacle / ceremony**: Olympic ceremonies, World Expo. The largest possible stages. Demonstrates that experimental media art methods can scale to global broadcast.

What's distinctive: he is **the** canonical bridge between Japanese pop culture production and the global experimental art-tech circuit. The Perfume collaborations are not a compromise — they fund and enable the experimental work, and the experimental work keeps the commercial work technically ambitious. He has operated this dual track deliberately for 15+ years.

## Why Interesting

Rhizomatiks is arguably the world's most technically sophisticated art-tech collective. The **Japanese specificity** matters: their work is embedded in pop culture (Perfume, major TV broadcasts, national ceremonies) in ways the European media art world never is. This gives them resources, scale, and cultural embeddedness that Western equivalents lack.

Manabe's **face-hacking** origin story is important — he found an aesthetic in the involuntary, in the body as instrument, in the gap between internal intention and external expression. This concern with embodiment and the body-machine interface runs through everything: the faces, the dancers with sensors, the neurostimulation research, the drone-body choreographies.

His **mathematics + IAMAS** background is unusual: he has genuine scientific training (Iannis Xenakis as inspiration shows this runs deep), not just "art with tech." The mathematical approach to music generation became a computational approach to all expression.

**YCAM connection**: His 2010 workshop and 2016 *border* performance at YCAM show that he's deeply embedded in Japan's most serious art-technology institution, not just the commercial sector.

His **neuroscience turn** (cultured neural cells as computing substrate, TMS experiments, Brain Processing Unit) shows continued interest in what machines can learn from biological computation — positioning him at the art-science frontier, not just art-tech.

Connects to **[[Ars Electronica]]**, **[[Japan Media Arts Festival]]**, **[[YCAM]]**, [[Kyle McDonald]], and the broader computational performance art world.

The *Phase Forms* model (tight generative sound-visual systems in performance) is technically and conceptually adjacent. A collaboration could be imagined around machine behavior as performance.

4. **💡 Career inspiration** — The **dual track** (pop culture / experimental art) is the master lesson: Manabe uses commercial productions to fund experimental R&D, and vice versa. The Perfume × technology collaborations generate resources and technical demand that pushes Rhizomatiks to solve problems no pure art project would fund. This is not selling out — it's a specific institutional model.

5. **👋 Hangout potential** — Tokyo-based primarily. However, with sonicPlanet LTD now in London, there may be London proximity. Sónar Barcelona, MUTEK festivals, Ars Electronica are likely encounter points.

6. **🔧 Professional inspiration** — His **iterative trial-and-error** methodology: "designing the path to the goal." He doesn't narrow down ideas at the start — he tries things until they work. Also: using **off-the-shelf / cheap technology** (the laser fade-out used cheap auction-site lasers) and finding new aesthetic territory in simple constraints.

7. **🌍 Areas to know** — The **Japanese tech-art scene** (YCAM, NTT ICC, Rhizomatiks) is genuinely distinct and technically more sophisticated than the European media art scene in certain domains. Worth understanding as its own territory with its own logic and values.

## Research Taste Analysis (⭐⭐⭐)

### Conceptual Strategy

**Bodies as data, data as bodies.** Manabe's core move is to take invisible signals — electric muscle impulses, electromagnetic waves, motion capture data, ML latent space — and make them visible/audible/embodied. The face-hacking works: he didn't just do face tracking, he made the face move involuntarily, exposing the gap between voluntary and involuntary movement. The Sensing Streams work: electromagnetic waves you're always bathed in become audible. The neural cell computing: biological computation made perceptible. This is a consistent aesthetic logic across 15 years: **render the invisible structure of bodies and machines**.

**The live constraint as artistic principle.** At the Rio Olympics: "Just pressing play on pre-made CG didn't interest me. People worldwide were watching live — if the visuals weren't live, it seemed pointless." This is a philosophical stance, not a technical preference. Liveness as what separates performance from cinema. All his major works are real-time — drones tracking live, AR responding to live body positions, ML generating in real time from live input. This commitment to real-time is both an aesthetic and an ethical stance on what constitutes genuine performance.

**Commercial infrastructure as experimental R&D.** The Sound of Honda campaign (Cannes Grand Prix, D&AD Black Pencil) is a *better* art project for having been funded as advertising. The technical problem (visualizing Ayrton Senna's telemetry data as live sound and light) required solving problems no art grant would have funded. Manabe has understood from early on that the separation between "art" and "commercial" is a false economy — commercial projects force you to solve problems at scale, under pressure, with real deadlines.

### Career Architecture

**Founding a collective, not a studio.** "Rhizomatiks is like a music label." This framing is crucial: he didn't build a hierarchical studio where he's the auteur — he built an ecosystem where values-aligned people pursue independent projects under shared infrastructure. This is why Rhizomatiks produces such diverse work: each person has artistic agency, and the collective provides resources, reputation, and technical infrastructure. Compare to: Superflex, Dumb Type, other collective models.

**The pop culture anchor.** Perfume collaboration from 2007 gave Rhizomatiks a continuous, well-resourced platform to develop and field-test technology at scale. Most experimental media artists work project-to-project, with long fallow periods between commissions. Rhizomatiks had a permanent "live performance laboratory" in the form of Perfume's ongoing touring and productions. This is genuinely unusual and probably explains why their technology is more mature than peers.

**The IAMAS → Rhizomatiks pipeline.** IAMAS (Gifu) is Japan's equivalent of ITP or IAMAS-type programs — serious graduate education in media arts. Meeting Ishibashi there, then co-founding together, is the "find your technical co-founder through graduate school" model. Worth noting: IAMAS is not in Tokyo — it's in Gifu. Choosing a regional institution signals seriousness over prestige.

**Bilateral bridging (Japan ↔ Euro-American).** Manabe is one of a small number of figures who genuinely operates with credibility in both the Japanese scene and the European/American media art world. His Prix Ars Electronica distinctions validate him in Europe; his Perfume and NHK work validates him in Japan. These circuits rarely overlap; he is one of their few connections. This bridging position is structurally undervalued by each side.

### Key Moves (chronological)

1. **2006: Co-founding Rhizomatiks as collective, not studio.** Set up the institutional infrastructure (values-aligned people, shared resources, artistic independence) before having the projects to justify it. This is the move that enables everything else.

2. **2007: First Perfume collaboration (Polyrhythm).** The pop culture anchor that funds continuous R&D. Recognized early that commercial pop performance could be a laboratory for experimental technology. Most experimental artists see this as compromise; Manabe saw it as infrastructure.

3. **2008: Electric Stimulus to Face on YouTube.** The viral moment. But what made it the right viral moment: he wasn't trying to go viral (YouTube was new), he was documenting an experiment. The authenticity of genuine R&D, shared publicly, traveled further than designed content. A model for how to get international attention as an experimental artist.

4. **2013–2014: Sound of Honda.** The advertising project that won Cannes Grand Prix AND D&AD Black Pencil AND Japan Media Arts Festival Grand Prize — simultaneously validated in advertising, design, and media art. Demonstrated that a single work could win the highest awards in three different fields at once. This cross-validation is extremely rare and positioned Rhizomatiks as category-spanning rather than category-specific.

5. **2016: Rio Olympics AR ceremony.** The largest possible public platform. Demonstrated that experimental AR/real-time CG techniques developed through art works could be deployed in a broadcast viewed by a billion people. Justified the entire methodology at global scale.

6. **2018: discrete figures (with Kyle McDonald).** The intentional US creative coding ↔ Tokyo bridge. Bringing Kyle McDonald into Rhizomatiks as a collaborator was a deliberate bridging move — it connected the openFrameworks/ITP community with the Rhizomatiks ecosystem.

7. **2025: Phase Forms + sonicPlanet.** The shift toward solo AV performance and proprietary tool development. Phase Forms is a solo work — separating himself artistically from the collective. sonicPlanet (London) is a new institutional form: reconstructing decades of experimental tools, "rethinking foundations of creativity in the AI era." This looks like a late-career infrastructural move.

### What Amber Can Learn

- **Build the commercial anchor.** Amber needs a version of the Perfume relationship: a resource-generating partnership that also functions as a creative lab. This doesn't have to be pop music — it could be an ongoing institutional commission, a residency that provides infrastructure, a consultancy that funds artistic R&D. The lesson is: identify your Perfume early.

- **The live constraint as philosophical stance.** Manabe's commitment to real-time is not just technical — it's philosophical. Having a strong stance on *what kind of work you make and why* is career infrastructure. It shapes which commissions you accept, which collaborators you find, which venues welcome you.

- **Collective vs. studio.** If Amber ever builds an organization, the collective model ("music label") rather than the studio model ("auteur + staff") creates more artistic diversity, more talent retention, and more institutional resilience. Each member of Rhizomatiks has an artistic identity; they're not just implementing Manabe's vision.

- **Cross-validate in multiple fields simultaneously.** Sound of Honda winning advertising, design, AND media art awards at the same time is unusual and powerful. Amber could aim for works that are simultaneously publishable as research, exhibitable as art, and deployable as tools — maximizing her presence across multiple fields at once.

- **Render the invisible.** The Manabe conceptual move (make invisible signals visible, inaudible signals audible) is directly applicable to Amber's interests in machine behavior and computational creativity. What are the invisible structures of machine behavior that could be rendered perceptible? This is a generative question.
