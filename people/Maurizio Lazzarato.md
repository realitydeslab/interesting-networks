# Maurizio Lazzarato

Italian sociologist and philosopher living in Paris. Key theorist of **immaterial labor** — work that produces immaterial products: information, knowledge, communication, relationships. Concept developed in the autonomist (*operaismo*) tradition, now foundational to political economy of digital/cognitive capitalism. Author of *Signs and Machines: Capitalism and the Production of Subjectivity* (2014) and *The Making of the Indebted Man* (2012).

## Why Interesting

Lazzarato coined "immaterial labor" in a 1996 essay that shaped how we understand digital platform work, data labor, care work, and affective production. His work on **debt** as a form of social control (*The Making of the Indebted Man*, 2012) is directly relevant to AI's political economy. A key figure in the intellectual tradition that produced [[Michael Hardt]] and [[Antonio Negri]]'s *Empire*.

## Key Works
- "Immaterial Labor" (1996) — in *Radical Thought in Italy* (ed. Hardt, Minnesota UP, 1996). The foundational essay.
- [*The Making of the Indebted Man*](https://semiotexte.com/?p=706) (Semiotext(e), 2012) — debt as a disciplinary mechanism across capitalist society.
- [*Signs and Machines: Capitalism and the Production of Subjectivity*](https://semiotexte.com/?p=700) (Semiotext(e), 2014) — Guattari-inflected analysis of semiotic capitalism.
- [*Capital Hates Everyone: Fascism or Revolution*](https://semiotexte.com/?p=1244) (Semiotext(e), 2022)
