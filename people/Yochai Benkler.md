# Yochai Benkler

Israeli-American legal scholar and author. Berkman Professor of Entrepreneurial Legal Studies at Harvard Law School; faculty co-director, Berkman Klein Center. Coined "commons-based peer production" (2001) and wrote *The Wealth of Networks* (2006) — the theoretical foundation for digital commons and decentralized social production.

## Domains
Commons-based peer production, internet law, networked information economy, digital commons, open source, network neutrality.

## Key Works
- [*The Wealth of Networks: How Social Production Transforms Markets and Freedom*](https://cyber.harvard.edu/wealth_of_networks/) (2006, Yale University Press) — foundational. Free PDF under CC license.
- ["Coase's Penguin, or Linux and the Nature of the Firm"](https://www.jstor.org/stable/1562247) (2002, Yale Law Journal) — coined CBPP

## Why Interesting
Theoretical architect of the digital commons. *Wealth of Networks* is required background for Amber's [[Agentive Commoning]] concept. His CBPP framework needs extending to non-human agents.
