# Nick Couldry

British media scholar and sociologist at LSE. Co-developed the data colonialism framework with Ulises Mejias; one of the most important critics extending Zuboff's surveillance capitalism toward decolonial and structural theory.

## Domains
- Media studies and critical data studies
- Data colonialism — framing data extraction as colonial appropriation
- Social theory of media and technology
- Human data futures; data governance alternatives

## Key Works
- [*The Costs of Connection: How Data Is Colonizing Human Life and Appropriating It for Capitalism*](https://www.sup.org/books/title/?id=28816) (with Ulises Mejias, 2019, Stanford UP) — foundational data colonialism text
- [Data Colonialism: Rethinking Big Data's Relation to the Contemporary Subject](https://doi.org/10.1177/1527476418796632) (with Mejias, 2018, *Television & New Media*) — condensed version of the argument
- *Media Society World* (2012) — social theory of media
- *Why Voice Matters* (2010) — democratic voice and media

## Why Interesting
Couldry's move from "surveillance capitalism" to "data colonialism" shows how the framework can be decolonized and made structurally more powerful. The colonial appropriation framing — data as equivalent to land, bodies, and labor extracted by historical colonialism — is philosophically richer and more globally applicable. Amber's work in Oxford with a critical computing lens could benefit from engaging this framework seriously.
