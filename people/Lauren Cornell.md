# Lauren Cornell

Director of the Graduate Program AND Chief Curator of Hessel Museum, Center for Curatorial Studies (CCS), Bard College. Former Executive Director of Rhizome (2005–2012). One of the most sophisticated thinkers about art and technology operating within a major curatorial institution. Co-editor of *Mass Effect: Art and the Internet in the 21st Century* (MIT Press, 2016). First person to simultaneously hold the director of graduate program and chief curator roles at CCS Bard.

## Background
Cornell moved from net art / internet-focused practice (Rhizome) to institutional curatorial power (New Museum, then CCS Bard). This trajectory makes her unusual: she is not a traditional art historian who stumbled into technology, but someone who built her entire career at the art-internet intersection and is now running a prestigious curatorial training program. Her position at CCS (since 2017) is the first to combine academic program direction with chief curation of the museum.

## Key Works
- *[Mass Effect: Art and the Internet in the 21st Century](https://mitpress.mit.edu/books/mass-effect)* (co-edited with Ed Halter; New Museum / MIT Press, 2016) — essential anthology on art and the internet
- *Phantom Plane: Cyberpunk in the Year of the Future* (2019, Tai Kwun Contemporary, Hong Kong) — co-curated with Xue Tan and Dawn Chan; cyberpunk + technology + contemporary art
- *Dara Birnbaum: Reaction* (Hessel Museum, CCS Bard, 2022) — first US retrospective of video art pioneer
- *Sky Hopinka: Centers of Somewhere* (Hessel Museum, 2020) — commissioned multi-channel video work
- *Nil Yalter: Exile is a Hard Job* (Hessel Museum + Museum Ludwig, Cologne) — co-organized retrospective
- *Invisible Adversaries* (2016, Hessel Museum, co-curated with Tom Eccles) — 10th anniversary Hessel Collection exhibition with cyberfeminist themes
- *Seven on Seven* (annual, New Museum/Rhizome) — co-founded conference pairing artists with technologists
