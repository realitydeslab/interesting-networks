# Long Pan (龙盼)

Social practice artist working across art, research, technology, and community. Collaborates on issues of bioremediation and biopolitics using digital and biological materials — fungi, plants, pollutants, electronics, minerals. Based in China; focuses on the human footprint in the environment and biological responses to environmental change, especially in China's context of ecological crisis and technological exuberance.

## Key Works
- *Flux in Dust* (2023, [[Chronus Art Center]]) — CAC Projects #1. Fieldwork in Guiyu, Guangdong (world's largest e-waste dump). *Leaf*: plants accumulating heavy metals from e-waste visualized as ceramic glaze. *Fire Works*: copper liquefied and returned to earth via traditional Chinese ritual *Datiehua* (打铁花).
- Practice involves **phytometallurgy** and **fungal degradation** as artistic methods

## Why Note-Worthy
Represents a distinctive Chinese ecological art practice: not performative "green art" but rigorous fieldwork in the specific geographies of China's tech economy. The Guiyu work is irreplaceable documentation of e-waste infrastructure as aesthetic subject.
