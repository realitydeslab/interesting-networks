# Malcolm Le Grice

(1940–2024) Central architect of British structural-materialist film. Co-founder of the [[London Film-Makers' Co-operative (LFMC)]] workshop; BFI calls him "probably the most influential modernist filmmaker in British cinema." Started as a painter; from mid-1960s made film and later digital/computer works that expose the material properties of the medium. Died December 2024.

## Domains
Structural-materialist film; experimental cinema; computer-generated film (pioneer); expanded cinema; film theory; digital installation.

## Background
Born Plymouth, Devon, May 1940. Studied painting at Arts University Plymouth, then Slade School of Fine Art. Started making film in mid-1960s alongside painting career. Joined LFMC committee 1969, Chair 1970–73. Physically built the Co-op's workshop at Robert Street Arts Lab with Fred Drummond. Established film at Saint Martin's School of Art and Goldsmiths. Balanced filmmaking practice with institutional activism (BFI and Arts Council committees). Later Dean of Media Arts, University of Westminster; Professor Emeritus, University of the Arts London. Died 3 December 2024.

## Key Works
- *Berlin Horse* (1970) — dual-screen; rephotographed found footage, colour-shifted optical printing. Defines British structural-materialist practice.
- *Horror Film 1* (1971) — shadow-play performance film; projected film interacts with live shadows
- *After Lumière – l'arroseur arrosé* (1974) — structural deconstruction of early cinema
- *Blackbird Descending – tense alignment* (1977)
- *Finnegans Chin* (1981) — later career major work; broadcast on British TV
- *Chronos Fragmented* (1995)
- *Experimental Cinema in the Digital Age* (collected essays, BFI 2001/2011)

## Key Publications
- [*Abstract Film and Beyond*](https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262620208/) (Studio Vista / MIT, 1977) — first book-length history of experimental cinema by a British practitioner
- *Experimental Cinema in the Digital Age* (BFI, 2001/2011) — collected essays

## Key Communities
- [[London Film-Makers' Co-operative (LFMC)]] — co-founder, committee chair 1970-73, workshop builder
- [[LUX]] — successor org; holds his films in distribution

## Why Interesting
Le Grice is the historical anchor of British experimental film. Understanding his work — both the films and the theoretical writing — is necessary context for understanding the LFMC, the structural-materialist tradition, and by extension all contemporary British artists' film practice. His death in December 2024 means a major moment of institutional reflection is happening right now at LUX and in the wider field.
