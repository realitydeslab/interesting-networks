# Genevieve Bell

Cultural anthropologist, futurist, and institutional builder. Founder of the ANU School of Cybernetics (2021), now ANU Vice-Chancellor (January 2024). Distinguished Professor, Florence Violet McKenzie Chair. Also VP and Senior Fellow at Intel Corporation. PhD in cultural anthropology from Stanford (1998). 18 years at Intel guiding product development via social science. Founded 3Ai in 2017 to build a new branch of engineering for AI ethics. First Engelbart Distinguished Fellow (SRI International, 2020). Presented ABC Boyer Lectures 2017. Officer of the Order of Australia. Member of PM's National Science and Technology Council.

Key achievement: built an entire institutional apparatus (school, degree program, residencies, partnerships) around cybernetics as a living framework for AI governance and sociotechnical futures.
