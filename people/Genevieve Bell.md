# Genevieve Bell

Cultural anthropologist and technologist. Co-author with [[Paul Dourish]] of *[Divining a Digital Future: Mess and Mythology in Ubiquitous Computing](https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262528955/)* (MIT Press, 2011) — a cultural/anthropological critique of ubicomp's myths about "the home," "the city," and "the future." Formerly VP/Director at Intel Labs (where she led Intel's People and Practices Research for 18 years); now Distinguished Professor at ANU and founding Director of the [3A Institute](https://3ainstitute.org/) focused on AI and data governance.

## Why Interesting
The Dourish × Bell collaboration is a model for how anthropology and CS can work together — not applied anthropology, but genuine co-theorization. Her Intel work shows what critical thinking looks like inside a tech corporation. The 3A Institute is building frameworks for "responsible AI" that are empirically grounded rather than principles-only.
