# Philip Galanter

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-03-03:** Stub created from Generative Art deep research.

The theorist of generative art. Professor at Texas A&M. Wrote the canonical definition paper "What is Generative Art? Complexity Theory as a Context for Art Theory" (2003), which remains the primary theoretical reference for the field. Uses complexity theory (order/chaos continuum) to position generative art as systems that exploit the "edge of chaos" — productive instability between pure order and pure randomness. Also writes on generative art theory, computational aesthetics, and complexity aesthetics.

## Key Works
- ["What is Generative Art? Complexity Theory as a Context for Art Theory"](https://www.philipgalanter.com/downloads/ga2003_paper.pdf) (Proc. Generative Art Conference, Milan, 2003) — canonical definition
- "Generative Art Theory." In *A Companion to Digital Art* (ed. Paul), 2016, Wiley-Blackwell. DOI: [10.1002/9781118475249.ch9](https://doi.org/10.1002/9781118475249.ch9)
- Various papers on complexity aesthetics

## Why Interesting
- Provides the theoretical framework that names and locates generative art intellectually
- The "edge of chaos" framing connects directly to ALife theory (complexity, emergence)
