# Maurice Benayoun

Pioneering French-Algerian new media artist and theorist. One of the earliest artists working with virtual reality, networked telematic art, and AI art. Professor at City University of Hong Kong School of Creative Media. Shown at [[Art Center Nabi]] (Seoul), [[ZKM Center for Art and Media]] (Karlsruhe), and global circuit. His West-East positioning (French career + Hong Kong academic base) is particularly interesting for Amber.

## Domains
- Virtual reality and immersive art (from early 1990s)
- Networked/telematic art
- AI and emotional/social machine intelligence
- Critical media theory
- Cross-cultural bridging (French art world + Asian academic base)

## Background
Born Algeria. Based in Paris and Hong Kong. Professor at [City University of Hong Kong School of Creative Media](https://www.scm.cityu.edu.hk/). One of the founding figures of VR art in the 1990s (World Skin, 1997). Developed concept of "Emotional Intelligence" in art machines. Exhibited widely in Europe and Asia. Featured at Art Center Nabi's "Why Future Still needs Us – AI and Humanity" (2016).

## Key Works
- **World Skin** (1997) — VR installation about media and war; Grand Prix Interactive Art, Ars Electronica 1998
- **The Tunnel Under the Atlantic** (1995) — real-time transatlantic virtual connection between Paris and Montreal
- **Emotional Vortex** — AI + emotion
- Various AI art installations at ZKM, Art Center Nabi, etc.

## Why Interesting
West-East media artist with deep credentials in VR, telematic, AI art. His Hong Kong position makes him a bridge to Asia-Pacific media art. The emotional intelligence / AI theme connects to Nabi's E.I.Lab and directly to Amber's interests in machine behavior and computational creativity. Worth deep-researching.
