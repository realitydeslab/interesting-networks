# Chanika Svetvilas

Multidisciplinary artist. Artist-in-Residence at the [[Ida B. Wells Just Data Lab]], Princeton (2022–23). Project: [**"Anonymous Was the Data"**](https://chanikasvetvilas.com/anonymous-was-the-data/) — a voluntary survey of AAPI individuals about mental health differences and healthcare access, with data mapped onto 3D-printed distorted prescription bottles forming a collective installation. The project addresses the mental health crisis among AAPI communities (Asian Americans have the highest suicide rates in certain demographics) by treating individual data as dignified rather than aggregated into clinical statistics.

The installation culminated at Lewis Center for the Arts, Princeton (September 2023), with a workshop, artist talk, and panel discussion.

## Why Interesting
Uses participatory data collection + material fabrication (3D printing) as a form of data justice art. The distorted prescription bottle form is a strong formal move — medicalization and data quantification critiqued through the same aesthetic object. Working at the intersection of AAPI community organizing, critical data, and art — an underrepresented combination.
