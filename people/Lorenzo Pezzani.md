# Lorenzo Pezzani

Architect and researcher. Co-founder of [[Forensic Oceanography]], the sub-project of [[Forensic Architecture]] investigating deaths at sea in the Mediterranean. Uses spatial analysis, satellite data, and ship tracking to document the conditions under which migrants die crossing from North Africa to Europe — and to hold European states and NATO accountable for failures to respond.

## Domains
- Forensic investigation of migration and border regimes
- Spatial analysis of maritime deaths
- Counter-cartography and the politics of territory
- Legal accountability in humanitarian law

## Key Works
- **[The Left-to-Die Boat](https://forensic-architecture.org/investigation/the-left-to-die-boat)** (2012) — investigation into 73 migrants who died while drifting for two weeks within NATO's maritime surveillance area
- **[Forensic Oceanography](https://forensic-oceanography.org/)** — ongoing research programme

## Key Communities
- [[Forensic Architecture]] — co-founder of Forensic Oceanography sub-project
- [[Goldsmiths Visual Cultures]] — institutional home

## Why Interesting
Forensic Oceanography extends FA's methodology from terrestrial to oceanic space — using the same counter-forensic logic to map how border regimes produce death. The work treats the sea as a crime scene and ships' AIS tracking data as testimony.
