# Anicka Yi

Korean-American artist working at the intersection of biology, technology, and olfaction. Known for creating environments that explore machine consciousness, posthuman ecology, and interspecies relations. Work ranges from bio-art installations to AI agents. Represented by Gladstone Gallery and 47 Canal (NYC). Appeared in Simon Denny's MUD group show at Petzel (2024).

## Why Interesting
Yi's work is directly at the edge of more-than-human design and machine consciousness. Her "agentic" approach to materials — training AI on olfactory data, creating environments where microbiomes generate decisions — is one of the most rigorous art-science engagements with non-human agency. Her Tate Turbine Hall commission (2021) used "xenojellyfishes" — floating AI agents modeled on biological organisms. This is ALife meeting contemporary art at the highest institutional level.

## Key Works
- **In Love With The World** (2021, Tate Modern Turbine Hall) — floating AI agents as synthetic lifeforms
- **Metaspore** (2019) — biological + machine environment
- Various olfactory/microbiome installations
