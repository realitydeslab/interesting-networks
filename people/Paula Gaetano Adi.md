Argentinian artist and researcher at Arizona State University. Works at the intersection of robotics, postcolonial theory, feminist science studies, and ALife. 2025 AL&I Golden Nica winner.

## Why Interesting

**Guanaquerx** (2025 Golden Nica, AL&I): envisions robotics as a technology of liberation, inviting pluriversal engagement with the past. The jury called it "an extraordinary scope and ambition" — "robotics as liberation" is a direct challenge to the dominant Western techno-futurist framing of AI/robotics. This is precisely the kind of non-Western + critical + rigorous work that Amber's research taste prizes.

Intersections with Amber: more-than-human robotics, ALife as political practice, postcolonial technology critique, cosmotechnics resonance.
