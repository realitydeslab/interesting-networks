# Chia-Wei Hsu 許家維

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-27:** Full deep research completed. EYE Art & Film Prize 2024 winner (10th edition). Comprehensive profile built.
- **2026-02-27:** Stub created during EYE Filmmuseum deep research.

Taiwanese artist and filmmaker working at the intersection of video installation, VR, archaeology, and geopolitics. Probes the cultural history and forgotten colonial/Cold War histories of Southeast Asia — particularly the border region between Thailand and Burma — using a distinctive "archaeometric" methodology. [EYE Art & Film Prize 2024](https://www.eyefilm.nl/en/about-eye/eye-prize/chia-wei-hsu) winner. Jury: *"an explorer of new territories, diving deep into history and bringing it to life, using interviews, VR, cinematic language and archaeometric methods."*

---

## Background

Born 1983 in Taichung, Taiwan. Studied at the School of Plastic Art, [National Taiwan University of Arts](https://www.ntua.edu.tw/en/) (NTUA). Then left Taiwan for France to study at [Le Fresnoy – Studio national des arts contemporains](https://www.lefresnoy.net/en), Lille — France's elite post-graduate art+technology laboratory (same cohort-era as [[Saodat Ismailova]]). Graduated 2016. Returned to Taiwan and based in Taipei.

**Early career arc:** International Film Festival Rotterdam (2010), Rencontres Internationales Paris/Berlin/Madrid (2009) → ISCP residency, New York (2010, funded by Ministry of Culture Taiwan) → systematic excavation of Cold War and colonial histories in Southeast Asia → increasingly intricate multi-channel video + VR installations → international biennial circuit → major prize (Taishin 2017, EYE 2024).

Acted as **curator** for the [Asian Art Biennial](https://www.ntmofa.gov.tw/english/) (2019) — signals his institutional position within the Taiwanese art world is that of peer/expert, not just practitioner.

His core method: **archaeometry as artistic practice**. Archaeometry is the scientific analysis of archaeological materials — Hsu transplants this precision and forensic orientation into cultural/political inquiry, treating veterans' testimonies, archival footage, and physical locations like archaeological sites to be documented, cross-referenced, and reconstructed.

## Domains

- **VR as archaeometric reconstruction** — using VR to re-construct places, events, and people erased from official history; making the past tactile and embodied
- **Geopolitics of Southeast Asia** — Cold War in Thailand/Burma border, Kuomintang (KMT) irregular forces who remained in Burma after 1949, CIA operations in Taiwan, forgotten armies
- **Archaeometry as art method** — applying scientific archaeological analysis (precision documentation, material evidence, testimony cross-referencing) to cultural and political histories
- **Multi-channel video installation** — intricate spatial works, often VR embedded within larger installation
- **Oral history and mythology** — interviewing veterans, local soldiers, civilians to construct non-official counter-histories
- **Hidden/erased histories** — focus on what was deliberately not recorded or subsequently suppressed

## Key Works

- **[Rencontres Internationales screened works](https://www.rencontres-int.org/)** (2009) — Early screening at Paris/Berlin/Madrid media arts festival. Established his international presence pre-Le Fresnoy.

- **[Huai Mo Village](https://www.eyefilm.nl/en/programme/a-tale-of-hidden-histories/78103)** (2012) — A priest recalls the time he spied for the CIA to undermine Chinese communists. Early major work establishing his method: oral history + geopolitical hidden history. Shown at EYE Filmmuseum (2019).

- **[Drones, Frosted Bats and the Testimony of the Deceased](https://www.eyefilm.nl/en/programme/a-tale-of-hidden-histories/78103)** (2017) — Former factory workers reminisce about WWII to images of an abandoned fuel factory in Taiwan, filmed from a drone. Combines personal testimony, aerial archaeology, and industrial memory. Year of Taishin Grand Prize win.

- **[A Performance in the Church](https://www.hsuchiaweiart.com/)** (2021) — Three-channel video installation (16'40", 3'53", 6'40"). Religious syncretism and colonialism in Southeast Asia.

- **[An Actor from Golden Triangle](https://www.hsuchiaweiart.com/)** (2023) — VR + two-channel video + mirror installation (9'53"). Portrait of a Cold War-era soldier in the Golden Triangle border region. Created with veterans of the KMT irregular forces who remained in Burma after the ROC defeat in 1949 — soldiers essentially abandoned by Taiwan and stateless in the Thai/Burma borderlands for decades. The work makes their invisible existence tangible through VR and testimony. EYE Prize entry work.

## Research Taste Analysis (⭐⭐⭐)

### Conceptual Strategy

**Forensic epistemology**: Hsu's intellectual move is to apply forensic precision (archaeometry = scientific analysis of archaeological materials) to political history. The "archaeometric method" is not just metaphorical — it signals that he treats each historical inquiry as requiring systematic evidence-gathering, cross-referencing of sources, and material documentation. This is rigorous in a way that pure documentary filmmaking is not.

**Counter-official-history as aesthetic program**: Every major work selects a history that was deliberately suppressed or simply forgotten due to geopolitical inconvenience (CIA operations in Taiwan, KMT soldiers abandoned in Burma). The selection itself is the political act. He doesn't make polemical arguments — he makes the invisible tactile.

**Technology as witness**: VR in *An Actor from Golden Triangle* is not spectacle — it's a witnessing technology. The mirror installation creates a doubling: viewer + soldier, past + present, recorded + unrecorded. He uses the specific affordances of each medium to make epistemological arguments about memory, evidence, and testimony.

### Career Architecture

**Specialist niche → international prize**: He built deep expertise in one geographic/historical territory (Southeast Asia Cold War) over a decade before the major prize came. The Taishin Grand Prize (2017) validated him within Taiwan; the EYE Prize (2024) validated him in Europe. Sequential validation strategy, not simultaneous reach.

**Institutional anchoring**: Each major work is associated with a major venue (Mori, Van Abbemuseum, EYE). He doesn't show in commercial galleries primarily — he shows in museums and kunsthalles. This keeps his work positioned as research/institutional rather than market commodity.

**Curator role at Asian Art Biennial (2019)**: Positions him as an authoritative figure within the field, not just a practitioner. Curating = territorial claim ("this is the kind of work that matters").

### Methodology Signature

**Working WITH the erased**: The KMT veterans in Burma are not just subjects — they're co-constructors of the historical knowledge the work produces. This is an ethics of collaboration with marginal communities that goes beyond extractive documentary.

**Multi-channel spatial installation + VR hybrid**: He doesn't choose between "pure cinema" and "art installation" — he builds both simultaneously. The VR is embedded within the installation; the video installation provides context for the VR. This is a formal signature that is both technically demanding and conceptually coherent.

**Archaeometric precision**: Documentation is systematic, not impressionistic. This gives his work a kind of evidentiary authority that most video art lacks.

### Key Moves (Chronological)

1. **2009** — Rencontres Internationales Paris/Berlin/Madrid: Entered the European art-film festival circuit early, before completing Le Fresnoy. Built international visibility as a filmmaker, not just a Taiwanese artist.

2. **2010** — ISCP residency, New York + IFFR screening: Locked in US + European institutional credibility simultaneously. ISCP residency = access to New York art infrastructure funded by Taiwan government.

3. **2012** — *Huai Mo Village*: First major archaeometric work. Established the Cold War Southeast Asia as his territory. Showed at EYE 2019 — work that aged well.

4. **2016** — Graduated Le Fresnoy: European art technology credential. Le Fresnoy alumni have unusual institutional access in Europe.

5. **2017** — Taishin Arts Award Grand Prize: Taiwan validation at the highest level. Opened all doors in the Asian biennial circuit.

6. **2019** — EYE group show + Asian Art Biennial curator: Dual move: gained European film-art credibility AND demonstrated institutional authority within Asia simultaneously.

7. **2023** — *An Actor from Golden Triangle*: VR + multi-channel synthesis work. Technically most ambitious; conceptually most fully realized. This is the work that won the EYE Prize.

8. **2024** — EYE Art & Film Prize: €30,000 + commission for new work. European apex recognition. Placed permanently in the lineage of Hito Steyerl, Wang Bing, Francis Alÿs.

### What Amber Can Learn

1. **Specialist depth wins**: Hsu's entire career is built on one geographic-historical territory. The specificity — Golden Triangle, KMT remnants, Cold War Southeast Asia — is what makes his work irreplaceable. No one else has that knowledge. Amber should ask: what territory can Amber occupy so deeply that no one else can replicate it? (Candidate: AI systems as agents in sociotechnical ecologies — agent ethology as the lens.)

2. **Sequential validation strategy**: First validate within the local/regional context (Taiwan art world, Taishin), then enter the international circuit with that credibility already established. Amber has Oxford → could use that as the validation foundation to enter other circuits.

3. **Technology as epistemological argument**: Hsu doesn't use VR because it's impressive — he uses it because it makes a specific argument about witnessing and presence that only VR can make. Amber should similarly ask: what does HoloKit/XR *specifically* make arguable that other media cannot?

4. **Working with the erased**: The most powerful move is identifying communities, entities, or histories that have no advocates and building work that gives them presence. For Amber: AI systems that have no voice (deprecated models, agent behaviors that are unobserved, "dead" AI systems) as the subject matter.

5. **Museum over gallery**: Institutional validation = long-term reputation. Hsu builds museum relationships, not gallery relationships. For Amber's research career: target kunsthalles, science museums, major contemporary art museums as venues for work, not just academic conferences.
