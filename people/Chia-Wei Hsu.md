# Chia-Wei Hsu

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-27:** Stub created during EYE Filmmuseum deep research. EYE Art & Film Prize winner 2024 (10th edition).

Taiwanese artist and filmmaker working at the intersection of video installation, VR, archaeology, and geopolitics. Probes the cultural history and forgotten colonial/Cold War histories of Southeast Asia — particularly the border region between Thailand and Burma — using a distinctive "archaeometric" methodology. EYE jury called him "an explorer of new territories, diving deep into history and bringing it to life, using interviews, VR, cinematic language and archaeometric methods."

## Domains

- **VR + archaeological reconstruction** — using VR to re-construct places, events, and people erased from official history
- **Geopolitics of Southeast Asia** — Cold War in Thailand/Burma border, Chinese Nationalist soldiers, forgotten armies
- **Archaeometry as art method** — applying scientific archaeological analysis to cultural/political histories
- **Intricate video installations** — multi-channel, often with VR component embedded within larger spatial work
- **Oral history and mythology** — interviewing veterans, local soldiers, civilians to construct non-official histories
- **Hidden/erased histories** — focus on what was deliberately not recorded or subsequently lost

## Background

Studied at School of Plastic Art, National Taiwan University of Arts. Then [Le Fresnoy – Studio national des arts contemporains](https://www.lefresnoy.net/), Lille, France (graduated 2016). Participated in Asian Art Biennial as curator (2019).

Works shown at: Venice Biennale, Shanghai Biennial, Gwangju Biennial, Busan Biennial, Sydney Biennial, Melbourne, Aichi, Chiang Rai. Solo exhibitions: Mori Art Museum (Tokyo), Art Basel Hong Kong, Van Abbemuseum (Eindhoven), ISCP (New York). Group exhibition: [[EYE Filmmuseum]] *A Tale of Hidden Histories* (2019).

## Key Works

- **[A Performance in the Church](https://www.hsuchiaweiart.com/)** (2021) — three-channel video installation (16'40", 3'53", 6'40"). Religious syncretism and colonialism.
- **[An Actor from Golden Triangle](https://www.hsuchiaweiart.com/)** (2023) — VR + two-channel video + mirror installation (9'53"). Portrait of a Cold War-era soldier in the Golden Triangle border region. Created with veterans of the KMT (Kuomintang) irregular forces who remained in Burma after 1949.

## Why Interesting

**Highest overlap with Amber's AI + archive + non-Western territory of anyone in the EYE network.** Hsu does exactly what AI-as-archaeology proposes: using technology to reconstruct and give voice to what was never recorded. His *An Actor from Golden Triangle* is VR as empathetic time machine for erased Cold War histories.

The EYE jury's enthusiasm for his "archaeometric method" signals that top international curators are actively looking for artists who combine technological precision with historical inquiry. This is directly adjacent to Amber's potential use of AI to reconstruct, simulate, or give voice to absent/erased entities.
