# Elio Caccavale

Italian-British designer and researcher. One of the early (pre-2005 cohort) [[Design Interactions (RCA)]] graduates. Pioneer of **bioethics-as-design-material** — using design to make visible and contestable the ethical dimensions of biotechnology, transplantation medicine, and genetic engineering.

Most known for *Utility Pet* (2003–04) — a project exploring the ethics of transgenic organ transplantation through the figure of a pig designed to produce human-compatible kidneys. The work combines domestic product design with medical ethics and biotechnology in a way that prefigures all subsequent DI biodesign work. Has also collaborated with bioethicists, scientists, and hospitals. Likely early DI alumnus (pre-Dunne era or early Dunne era).

## Why Interesting
A historically important DI figure who pioneered biodesign as critical practice *before* it became fashionable. His approach — working with actual scientists and bioethicists, not just speculating — gives his work more traction than typical speculative design. Understanding his trajectory shows how DI's biodesign thread (Cohen, Ginsberg, Hasegawa) has roots.

## Key Works
- *Utility Pet* (2003–04) — pig designed as organ donor for humans; explores transgenic organ transplantation ethics
- Various bioethics design projects at RCA and UCL
