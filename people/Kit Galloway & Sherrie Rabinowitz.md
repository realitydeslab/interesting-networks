# Kit Galloway & Sherrie Rabinowitz

Pioneering duo in satellite-based telematic art and co-founders of Electronic Café International (ECI), Santa Monica. Worked collaboratively under the name **Mobile Image**. Kit Galloway (born 1948); Sherrie Rabinowitz (1950–2013; died 2013). Met in 1975; collaborated until Rabinowitz's death.

## Key Works
- **Satellite Arts Project** (1977) — With NASA support; connected East and West coast US artists via satellite for live composited dance performance. First satellite telematic artwork.
- **Hole-in-Space** (1980) — Unannounced public installation creating real-time video link between pedestrians in New York City and Los Angeles. Seminal public telematic encounter.
- **Electronic Café** (1984, LA Olympics) — Networked five culturally diverse restaurants in LA via slow-scan video, fax, and computer; pioneered community-based networked art spaces.
- **Electronic Café International** (1988–ongoing) — Performance, workshop, networking center in Santa Monica; art hub for networked culture.

## Why Interesting
The first practitioners of satellite-mediated telematic art; their work proves that the idea of "location" in art was already contested before the internet. *Hole-in-Space* is one of the most elegant dematerializations of geographic distance in art history. Relevant for thinking about how agents (human or AI) copresence across distance.
