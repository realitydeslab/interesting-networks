# Nils Bubandt

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Full deep research completed. Note rewritten from stub to gold standard.

Professor of Anthropology at Aarhus University, Denmark. One of the architects of European multispecies ethnography and Anthropocene anthropology. Co-director of AURA (Aarhus University Research on the Anthropocene) with Anna Tsing. Conducts intensive fieldwork in Indonesia on spirit politics, witchcraft, environmental disaster, and the intersections of human and non-human worlds. Major co-author on *Arts of Living on a Damaged Planet* (2017) and contributor to *Feral Atlas* (2020). The deepest "multispecies + more-than-human + spirit world" synthesizer in European anthropology today.

---

## Background

Nils Ole Bubandt has conducted intensive anthropological fieldwork in Indonesia for over two decades, based primarily in the Maluku (North Maluku/Halmahera) region — one of the most contested and spiritually rich areas of Eastern Indonesia. His earliest fieldwork (his PhD research, completed c. 1995) was among the Buli people of Central Halmahera, documenting embodied morality, headhunting, and political violence.

**PhD:** Australian National University, mid-1990s — "Warriors of the Hornbill, victims of the Mantis: history and embodied morality among the Buli of Central Halmahera" (1995). This established his long-term commitment to deep fieldwork in Eastern Indonesia.

He joined **Aarhus University** in Denmark where he rose to Full Professor in the Department of Anthropology (School of Culture and Society), eventually becoming **local researcher-in-charge** of the AURA project — making him the principal Danish institutional anchor for a major Danish National Research Foundation grant (funded 2013–2018).

His trajectory is remarkable for bridging two worlds: (1) Southeast Asian ethnographic anthropology, with its focus on spirits, sorcery, and political violence; and (2) multispecies / Anthropocene studies. He is one of the few people who has done this transition seriously, bringing Indonesian spirit worlds into conversation with global climate debates.

## Key Works

### Books (authored)

- [*Democracy, Corruption and the Politics of Spirits in Contemporary Indonesia*](https://www.routledge.com/Democracy-Corruption-and-the-Politics-of-Spirits-in-Contemporary-Indonesia/Bubandt/p/book/9781138795181) (Routledge, 2014) — capstone of his Indonesian fieldwork phase. Spirited account of how sorcery and witchcraft structure local democratic politics. Key contribution: spirits are not epiphenomenal to politics but constitutive of it.
- [*The Empty Seashell: Witchcraft and Doubt on an Indonesian Island*](https://www.cornellpress.cornell.edu/book/9780801453076/the-empty-seashell/) (Cornell University Press, 2014) — parallel volume on witchcraft, doubt, and epistemology on Buli. Examines how people maintain belief alongside genuine skepticism. Highly influential in the anthropology of witchcraft.

- [**"The Dark Side of Empathy: Mimesis, Deception, and the Magic of Alterity"**](https://doi.org/10.1017/S0010417514000589) (with Rane Willerslev, *Comparative Studies in Society and History*, 2015) — 93 citations. Philosophical anthropology of deception and alterity.

- [**"Sorcery, Corruption, and the Dangers of Democracy in Indonesia"**](https://doi.org/10.1111/J.1467-9655.2006.00298.X) (*JRAI*, 2006) — 53 citations. Foundational for the spirits/politics nexus.
- [**"Rumors, Pamphlets, and the Politics of Paranoia in Indonesia"**](https://doi.org/10.1017/S0021911808001162) (*Journal of Asian Studies*, 2008) — 49 citations.
- [**"Interview with an Ancestor: Spirits as Informants and the Politics of Possession in North Maluku"**](https://doi.org/10.1177/1466138109339044) (*Ethnography*, 2009) — 33 citations. Methodological tour-de-force: treating spirits as informants.
- [**"Less Than One But More Than Many: Anthropocene as Science Fiction and Scholarship-in-the-Making"**](https://doi.org/10.3167/ARES.2015.060109) (with Heather Swanson and Anna Tsing, 2015) — 63 citations.

- **"Wasteland Ecologies: Undomestication and Multispecies Gains on an Anthropocene Dumping Ground"** (with Colin Hoag and Filippo Bertoni, *Journal of Ethnobiology*, 2018) — 20 citations.

- [**"Spirits as Technology: Tech-Gnosis and the Ambivalent Politics of the Invisible in Indonesia"**](https://doi.org/10.1007/S11562-017-0391-9) (*Contemporary Islam*, 2019) — Bridges tech studies and Islamic invisible worlds.
- [**"A Second Look at Invisibility: Al-Ghayb, Islam, Ethnography"**](https://doi.org/10.1007/S11562-017-0395-5) (with Mikkel Rytter and Christian Suhr, *Contemporary Islam*, 2019) — 26 citations.

## AURA Research Projects

Within AURA (2013–2018), Bubandt led two major fieldwork projects:

1. **Lusi Mud Volcano (East Java)** — A mud volcano that erupted in 2006, flooding 9 villages and displacing 75,000 people, with an estimated $2.2 billion in damage. Disputed between natural event and industrial blowout (the Lapindo Brantas oil company). Bubandt studied this disaster as an Anthropocene example where nature/politics/spirits collapse together. Contributes to *Feral Atlas* with the poster "Mud overflows boreholes, politics, and reason."

2. **Raja Ampat (Papua, Indonesia)** — An archipelago with possibly the world's highest marine biodiversity. Marine protected areas set up 2006 for dive tourism. Bubandt studied the overlapping conservation regimes, local millenarianisms, and bio-social dynamics in this "Last Paradise on Earth."

## Research Taste Analysis (⭐⭐⭐)

### Conceptual Strategy

**Concept transplantation across registers:** Bubandt's core move is taking concepts from one register and transplanting them into another:
- "Spirits" → from religious epiphenomenon → to constitutive political actors
- "Feral" → from animal behavior → to post-industrial ecological dynamics
- "Patchy" → from landscape ecology → to Anthropocene critique
- "Witchcraft" → from primitive belief → to epistemology of doubt

This is the **"strange concept in a new field"** strategy — he takes his Indonesian spirit knowledge and makes it legible to Anthropocene/multispecies studies without losing its strangeness.

**Framework multiplication:** He rarely works alone conceptually. His best papers emerge from multi-author workshops where different disciplinary framings collide (Haraway + Tsing + Latour + Stengers roundtables are his most-cited work). He is a **coalition builder** who knows that the collision of perspectives produces the best concepts.

### Career Architecture

**Platform building through collective volumes:** His strategy has been consistently to build **edited volumes** that create platforms for multiple scholars rather than solo monographs that stake individual claims. *Arts of Living on a Damaged Planet*, *Experiments in Holism*, *Philosophy on Fieldwork* — these are coalition papers that establish territory for a field.

**Institutional anchoring:** He anchored AURA from the Danish side, giving Anna Tsing's international prestige a local institutional home. This "local anchor" role is strategically underrated — it made Aarhus a significant European node for multispecies studies.

**Long fieldwork as authority:** 25+ years in North Maluku gives him a kind of authority that cannot be replicated by shorter fieldwork. He is the person you go to for Eastern Indonesia spirit politics. This depth becomes a platform from which he can launch into broader theoretical debates.

### Methodology Signature

**Multi-species informants:** His methodological intervention is treating non-humans (spirits, volcanoes, coral reefs, mud) as informants in their own right. His paper "Interview with an Ancestor" (2009) literally interviews a spirit — this is methodological provocation as philosophical argument.

**Scale bridging:** He moves between intimate fieldwork encounters (smells of disgust in Halmahera, 1998) and global Anthropocene debates (Patchy Anthropocene, 2019). The fieldwork always grounds the theory.

**The "weird" paper:** Alongside theoretical work, he publishes genuinely strange papers — cryptozoology monsters at the Anthropocene cusp (2019), the odour of things (1998). These papers are his way of staying methodologically alive; they signal that he hasn't been domesticated by theory.

### Key Moves (Chronological)

1. **1995:** PhD on Buli of Halmahera — established Eastern Indonesia as his territory. Crucial foundation: nobody else owns this field.
2. **1998:** "Odour of Things" — sensory anthropology; signals that he won't be confined to standard political anthropology.
3. **2005:** "Vernacular Security" (187 citations) — breaks into security studies from anthropology. Shows the breadth move.
4. **2006-2009:** Indonesia trilogy on sorcery, democracy, and violence — establishes the "spirits/politics" nexus as scholarly territory.
5. **2010:** *Experiments in Holism* (co-edited with Ton Otto) — theoretical infrastructure building for the discipline.
6. **2013-2018:** AURA directorship — major institutional move; turns from Indonesian specialist to Anthropocene scholar without abandoning Indonesia.
7. **2014:** Double book year — *Democracy, Corruption* and *The Empty Seashell* simultaneously. Peak Indonesian ethnography.
8. **2016-2017:** Roundtables + *Arts of Living* — the multispecies transition is complete. He's now a core voice in global Anthropocene debates.
9. **2019:** "Patchy Anthropocene" (284 citations) — lands in *Current Anthropology*, the top disciplinary journal. This is the consolidation moment.
10. **2020:** Feral Atlas — takes multispecies work into digital humanities and art world. New circuit.

### What Amber Can Learn

1. **The "depth becomes breadth" move:** Bubandt's 25-year Indonesia expertise is what makes his multispecies theory credible. Deep expertise in a specific domain (spirits / Indonesia) becomes the platform for broader theoretical claims. For Amber: depth in a specific empirical domain (a specific ALife system, a specific more-than-human community) creates theory authority.

2. **Coalition scholarship is not a compromise, it's a strategy:** His most-cited works are roundtables and edited volumes. The solo genius model is less influential than building intellectual coalitions. For Amber: find 3-4 key interlocutors across disciplines and build projects together.

3. **The "weird paper" as methodological claim:** Publishing genuinely strange work (cryptozoology, spirit interviews) signals methodological freedom and prevents theoretical domestication. For Amber: don't only publish the safe papers.

4. **Concept coining as field declaration:** "Feral politics," "patchy Anthropocene" — these terms are citable handles that help others affiliate with the project. For Amber: invest in naming concepts, not just describing them.

5. **Institutional anchoring creates compound interest:** Being the Danish anchor for AURA made Aarhus matter internationally. For Amber: being the institutional anchor for a network (even a small one) creates disproportionate returns.
