# Astra Taylor

Filmmaker, writer, and activist. Director of *Examined Life* (2008) — philosophical documentary featuring Žižek, Butler, West, Hardt, Nussbaum — and *What Is Democracy?* (2018). Co-founder of the **Debt Collective** (debtor organizing). Author of *The People's Platform: Taking Back Power and Culture in the Digital Age* (2014) and *Democracy May Not Exist, but We'll Miss It When It's Gone* (2019). One of the clearest voices on the political economy of digital technology and on democracy as a practice, not a system.

## Why Interesting

Bridges radical philosophy (she studied with Wendy Brown at Berkeley, featured Judith Butler, Michael Hardt, Slavoj Žižek in *Examined Life*) with grassroots organizing (Debt Collective) and documentary filmmaking. Her critique of digital technology is not technophobic — it's political: who controls the platforms, who benefits, who's dispossessed. *What Is Democracy?* directly connects ancient Athenian democracy debates to contemporary crisis. Exactly the kind of figure who bridges Amber's domains: critical computing, participatory design, and political philosophy.

## Key Works
- [*Examined Life*](https://www.sphinx-productions.com/films/examined-life/) (2008, dir.) — documentary film, 87 min. Features walking conversations with Judith Butler, Cornel West, Michael Hardt, Slavoj Žižek, Martha Nussbaum, Avital Ronell, Sunaura Taylor, Peter Singer. Theatrical release.
- [*Examined Life*](https://www.sphinx-productions.com/films/examined-life/) companion book (2009)
- [*Žižek!*](https://www.sphinx-productions.com/films/zizek/) (2005, dir.) — documentary on Slavoj Žižek
- [*The People's Platform: Taking Back Power and Culture in the Digital Age*](https://henryholtholt.com/the-peoples-platform/) (Henry Holt, 2014) — critique of digital platform capitalism
- [*What Is Democracy?*](https://whatisdemocracy.net/) (2018, dir.) — documentary film. Explores democracy philosophically and politically across Greece, Italy, and US.
- [*Democracy May Not Exist, but We'll Miss It When It's Gone*](https://us.macmillan.com/books/9781250179777/democracymaynotexist) (Metropolitan Books, 2019) — companion book to *What Is Democracy?*
