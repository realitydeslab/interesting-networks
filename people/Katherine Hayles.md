# N. Katherine Hayles

Distinguished literary critic, theorist, and pioneer of posthumanism and digital humanities. Originally trained as a research chemist, she transitioned to English literature in the 1970s and became one of the most influential scholars of literature, science, and technology. Her work bridges cybernetics, cognitive science, electronic literature, and computational media.

## Background

Born 1943 in St. Louis, Missouri. Originally trained as a chemist — BS Chemistry from Rochester Institute of Technology (1966), MS Chemistry from California Institute of Technology (1969). Worked as research chemist at Xerox Corporation (1966) and Beckman Instrument Company (1968-1970) before pivoting to literature.

Made groundbreaking career transition in early 1970s: MA English Literature from Michigan State University (1970), PhD English Literature from University of Rochester (1977). This dual background in hard sciences and humanities became central to her intellectual approach — she brings genuine scientific literacy to literary and cultural criticism.

Career trajectory shows strategic institutional moves: Dartmouth (1975-82), University of Missouri-Rolla (1982-85), University of Iowa (1985-92), UCLA as Hillis Professor (1992-2008), Duke as James B. Duke Distinguished Professor (2008-2018), returned to UCLA as Distinguished Research Professor (2018-present).

## Key Works

**[*How We Became Posthuman: Virtual Bodies in Cybernetics, Literature and Informatics*](https://press.uchicago.edu/ucp/books/book/chicago/H/bo3773600.html)** (University of Chicago Press, 1999) — Seminal work tracing the history of cybernetics and defining posthumanism. René Wellek Prize winner. Essential reading for understanding human-machine relationships.

**[*Writing Machines*](https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262582155/writing-machines/)** (MIT Press, 2002) — Explores literary dimensions of new media. The book itself is designed as a "technotext." Won Susanne Langer Award.

**[*Electronic Literature: New Horizons for the Literary*](https://www.ndpress.nd.edu/9780268030858/electronic-literature/)** (University of Notre Dame Press, 2008) — Comprehensive survey establishing electronic literature as legitimate field. Accompanying website at newhorizons.eliterature.org.

**[*How We Think: Digital Media and Contemporary Technogenesis*](https://press.uchicago.edu/ucp/books/book/chicago/H/bo13141979.html)** (University of Chicago Press, 2012) — Examines co-evolution of humans and technology, introduces concept of "technogenesis."

**[*Unthought: The Power of the Cognitive Nonconscious*](https://press.uchicago.edu/ucp/books/book/chicago/U/bo26181184.html)** (University of Chicago Press, 2017) — Develops theory of cognitive nonconscious bridging human and machine cognition.

**[*Postprint: Books and Becoming Computational*](https://cup.columbia.edu/book/postprint/9780231198240)** (Columbia University Press, 2021) — Latest major work on how print culture transforms in computational age.

**[*Bacteria to AI: Human Futures with our Nonhuman Symbionts*](https://press.uchicago.edu/ucp/books/book/chicago/B/bo206654063.html)** (University of Chicago Press, 2025) — Most recent book extending cognitive framework from bacterial to artificial intelligence.

## Research Taste Analysis

**This section analyzes HOW Hayles builds her research, not just WHAT she researches.**

### Conceptual Strategy — Disciplinary Bridge-Building
Hayles operates by creating conceptual bridges between seemingly disparate fields. Her core innovation is bringing genuine scientific literacy to literary criticism. Unlike humanists who gesture toward science metaphorically, she reads primary scientific literature and engages with actual scientific concepts. This dual competence allows her to identify genuine correspondences between scientific and literary discourse rather than superficial analogies.

### Career Architecture — Field Declaration Strategy  
Hayles doesn't just work in existing fields — she declares new ones. *How We Became Posthuman* essentially founded posthuman studies as a field. Her work on electronic literature helped establish digital literary studies as legitimate academic inquiry. She builds intellectual infrastructure: terminology, canonical texts, institutional support, and training programs.

### Methodology Signature — Historical Genealogy + Theoretical Synthesis
Her signature method combines historical analysis with theoretical synthesis. She traces genealogies (cybernetics, information theory, AI development) while building overarching theoretical frameworks. Each major work excavates a historical trajectory while proposing new conceptual tools.

### Network Strategy — Institution Building
Rather than just publishing, Hayles builds institutions. She directed the Electronic Literature Organization, established prize committees, and created academic programs. She understands that lasting intellectual impact requires institutional infrastructure, not just ideas.

### Key Career Moves (Chronological)

1. **1970s Career Pivot** — Chemistry to Literature PhD. Timing was perfect: science studies and interdisciplinary work were emerging. Her scientific background became an asset rather than abandonment.

2. **1991 Guggenheim + Society for Literature and Science Presidency** — Used fellowship to write cybernetics work while building institutional authority in science-literature studies.

3. **1999 *How We Became Posthuman*** — Published at perfect moment when internet culture and AI were becoming mainstream concerns. Provided theoretical framework just as cultural moment demanded it.

4. **2001-2006 Electronic Literature Organization Leadership** — Recognized that digital literature needed institutional support to become legitimate field. Built organization, conferences, and collection archives.

5. **2008 Move to Duke** — Strategic relocation to top-tier literature program where she could develop graduate students and build scholarly networks in prestigious environment.

6. **2018 Return to UCLA** — Positioned herself in major West Coast digital humanities hub while maintaining emerita status at Duke. Access to tech industry and digital culture.
