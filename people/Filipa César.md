# Filipa César

Portuguese artist and filmmaker. Currently Professor for Time-Based Media & Performance at [[HfG Karlsruhe]] (with Diana McCarty). Explores boundaries between cinema and its reception, politics and poetics of moving image, environmental justice, archival practices.

Since 2011: collectively exploring militant cinema of the African liberation movement in Guinea-Bissau — workshops, archives, films, performances, publications, community events. Co-founded **Mediateca Onshore in Malafo** with cine-kins.

First feature essay film: **SPELL REEL** (premiered Berlinale 2017).

Shown at: Gasworks London, Flaherty Seminar, MoMA New York, Third Text, L'Internationale Online, Harvard Art Museum, HKW Berlin, Gulbenkian Foundation Lisbon, GfZK Leipzig.

## Why Interesting
Rare combination: decolonial archival practice + cinematic aesthetics + institutional critique + collective methodology. The Guinea-Bissau work is a long-term commitment (since 2011) to a specific archival politics — serious and unusual. Connects to HKW Berlin circuit which overlaps with Amber's European network.
