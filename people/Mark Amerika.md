# Mark Amerika

Artist, writer, professor at the **University of Colorado Boulder** and director of the **[Techne: Art+Research Lab](http://art.colorado.edu/art+research.html)** there. Pioneer of net art (*[GRAMMATRON](https://grammatron.com/)*, 1997 — one of the first major web-based narrative art works); now deeply engaged with AI art-making, remix theory, and experimental publishing. Long-term intellectual partner of Gary Hall — organized radical open access symposia at CU Boulder, published Hall's *Inhumanist Manifesto: Extended Play* (2017), connected to Hall through OHP's *remixthebook* project.

## Domains
- Net art and digital literature (GRAMMATRON, PHON:E:ME, etc.)
- Remix theory — *remixthebook* (Minnesota UP, 2011) — theory of culture as perpetual remix
- Experimental publishing and the digital humanities
- AI art-making — artist as "postproduction engine"
- Electronic literature
- Cultural theory of digital media

## Why Interesting
Amerika bridges the older net-art/electronic-literature world with current AI art discourse, and does so with theoretical sophistication (he has proper art theory chops, not just tech enthusiasm). The Techne Lab publishes short theoretical pamphlets (the E-Pamphlet series) which are a model for experimental scholarly publishing. His long relationship with Hall means he's a node connecting the critical OA world to the creative/AI art world.

*remixthebook* is a genuinely interesting form: a book that is also a website, also a set of remixes by other artists/theorists, published by Minnesota UP — making the form the argument. This is very much in Hall's register.
