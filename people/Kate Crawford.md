# Kate Crawford

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Deep research completed. Comprehensive profile with art collaborations, academic positions, and key works.

Leading scholar of artificial intelligence and its material impacts. Research Professor at USC Annenberg, Senior Principal Researcher at Microsoft Research New York, and inaugural visiting chair of AI and Justice at École Normale Supérieure in Paris. Bridges critical theory with technical analysis through both academic research and award-winning art practice.

## Background

Research Professor at **[University of Southern California Annenberg](https://annenberg.usc.edu/)** School for Communication and Journalism. Senior Principal Researcher at **[Microsoft Research New York](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/research/)** where she co-founded the FATE group (Fairness, Accountability, Transparency and Ethics in AI). Currently inaugural visiting chair of AI and Justice at **[École Normale Supérieure](https://www.ens.psl.eu/)** in Paris.

Distinguished for her unique approach that combines rigorous technical research with critical theory and artistic practice to make visible the hidden costs and infrastructures of AI systems.

## Domains

AI systems and their material impacts, algorithmic bias and fairness, data ethics, environmental costs of computation, AI governance and policy, critical data studies. Particular focus on the planetary-scale extraction processes that underlie AI systems - from mining rare earth minerals to exploitative labor practices.

## Research Taste Analysis

Crawford operates on multiple complementary strategies simultaneously:

### Conceptual Strategy — Infrastructure Exposure
Crawford's signature move is **making the invisible visible** — taking systems that appear ethereal or abstract (AI, algorithms, clouds) and revealing their material foundations. This isn't just description but a form of **conceptual archaeology**, excavating the hidden layers that make AI possible.

### Art-Science Methodology — Visualization as Research 
Rather than treating art as illustration of research findings, Crawford uses **art practice as a research method**. Projects like "Anatomy of an AI System" generate insights that couldn't emerge from traditional academic work. The visualization process itself becomes a way of thinking through complex systems.

### Career Architecture — Institution Bridging
Crawford doesn't just work across disciplines — she **strategically occupies positions** that give her legitimacy in multiple worlds: Microsoft Research (industry credibility), USC (academic standing), ENS Paris (critical theory credentials), major art institutions (cultural capital). This positioning allows her to speak authoritatively across domains.

### Scale Strategy — Planetary Thinking
Crawford consistently operates at **planetary scale** — not just looking at local effects of technology but tracing global supply chains, environmental impacts, and geopolitical implications. This scale allows her to connect seemingly distant phenomena (cobalt mining in Congo, AI training in Silicon Valley).

### Network Amplification — Collaboration as Research
Her collaborations aren't just partnerships but **strategic alliances** that amplify reach: Joler brings Eastern European critical tech perspective and design expertise; Whittaker brings tech industry insider knowledge and organizing experience; art institutions provide platforms for public engagement.
