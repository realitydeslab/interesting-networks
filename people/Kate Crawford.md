# Kate Crawford

Australian researcher, writer, artist, and academic. Leads the field of critical AI studies — the only person who has built major research institutes, written the definitive popular book on AI's material costs, AND produced award-winning art that now sits in MoMA and V&A. Co-founded the AI Now Institute with [[Meredith Whittaker]]. Long-term collaboration with [[Vladan Joler]] produces the most intellectually ambitious art-research of the critical tech field.

## Background
Born 1974 in Australia. **PhD, [University of Sydney](https://www.sydney.edu.au/)** — doctoral work on communication and social media, forming the foundation of her interdisciplinary research practice. Her 2006 book based on her PhD (*Adult Themes – Rewriting the Rules of Adulthood*, Pan Macmillan) won the individual category of the **Manning Clark National Cultural Award**. In 2008 she received the **Max Crawford Medal** for outstanding scholarship from the Australian Academy of the Humanities.

Early career in Australia: associate professor at Journalism and Media Research Centre, UNSW; contributing writer for Sydney Morning Herald and Foreign Policy; member of Clan Analogue music collective; one half of Canberra electronic music duo **[B(if)tek](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/B(if)tek)** (with Nicole Skeltys), releasing three albums (1998–2003) and co-founding Deluxe Mood Recordings record label. Feminist collective **[Deep Lab](http://www.deeplab.net/)** member.

Moved to the US and joined **Microsoft Research** (Social Media Collective) in New York. Senior Fellow at NYU Information Law Institute. Visiting Professor at MIT Center for Civic Media. From 2017, co-founded the **[AI Now Institute](https://ainowinstitute.org/)** at NYU with Meredith Whittaker, establishing the dominant institutional voice for critical AI research in the US.

Currently: **Research Professor of Communication and STS at [USC Annenberg](https://annenberg.usc.edu/faculty/kate-crawford)**; **Senior Principal Researcher at [Microsoft Research New York](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/research/lab/microsoft-research-new-york/)**; inaugural **Visiting Chair for AI and Justice at [École Normale Supérieure](https://www.ens.psl.eu/en)**, Paris (in partnership with Fondation Abeona); AI Council of President Sánchez of Spain; **WEF Global Agenda Council on Data-Driven Development**.

**TIME100** named Crawford as one of the world's most influential people in AI.

## Key Works

### Books
- **[Atlas of AI: Power, Politics, and the Planetary Costs of Artificial Intelligence](https://yalebooks.yale.edu/book/9780300209570/atlas-ai)** (Yale University Press, 2021) — The defining popular work on AI's hidden material costs. Named best book of 2021 by the *Financial Times*. Won three international prizes. Translated into twelve languages. Covers lithium mining, labor exploitation, surveillance, ecological costs of computing infrastructure. The book that reshaped public conversation on AI.

- **[Adult Themes – Rewriting the Rules of Adulthood](http://www.panmacmillan.com.au/9781742625188/)** (Pan Macmillan, 2006) — doctoral work; Manning Clark National Cultural Award winner.

- **Understanding the Internet: Language, Technology, Media, and Power** (with Chris Chesher and Anne Dunn, Palgrave Macmillan, 2014)

### Art & Research Projects
- **[Calculating Empires: A Genealogy of Technology and Power Since 1500](https://calculatingempires.net/)** (2023, with [[Vladan Joler]]) — Massive research visualization mapping the co-evolution of technological and social structures across five centuries, organized around communication, computation, classification, and control. Commissioned by Fondazione Prada Milan (2023). **Silver Lion, Venice Architecture Biennale 2025** (Intelligens. Natural. Artificial. Collective). **Grand Prize, European Commission S+T+ARTS 2024**. **Boghossian Foundation Art Prize 2024**. Toured: Fondazione Prada Milan → Mori Museum Tokyo → Jeu de Paume Paris → Design Museum Barcelona → Rijksmuseum Twenthe → KW Institute Berlin.

- **[Anatomy of an AI System](https://anatomyof.ai/)** (2018, with [[Vladan Joler]]) — Large-scale map and long-form essay mapping the human labor, data, and planetary resources required to produce a single Amazon Echo interaction. **Beazley Design of the Year Award, Design Museum London (2019)**. In permanent collections of **MoMA New York**, **V&A London**, **Ars Electronica Center Linz**, and **Design Museum London**. Shown in over 100 exhibitions worldwide.

- **[Excavating AI](https://excavating.ai/)** (2019, with [[Trevor Paglen]]) — Investigation of training datasets used in AI, tracing how values and biases are embedded in labeled image databases including ImageNet. **Ayrton Prize, British Society for the History of Science**. Revealed how AI systems encode social hierarchies and racist/sexist classifications.

- **"Training Humans"** (2019, with [[Trevor Paglen]]) — Exhibition at **Fondazione Prada, Milan** — a collection of photographs used to train facial recognition and emotion detection AI systems, made visible as images in an art context. First major public reckoning with the hidden assumptions in AI training data.

## Venue Circuit Analysis
Crawford operates across **three distinct circuits** simultaneously:

1. **Critical Art Institutions**: MoMA, V&A, Fondazione Prada, Mori Museum, Jeu de Paume, KW Berlin, Design Museum London, Venice Biennale — highest legitimacy as artistic practice. The "Anatomy of AI" reaching MoMA and V&A means her research-art has museum validation usually reserved for pure art.

2. **Policy and Governance**: UN, White House, EU Parliament, Spain AI Council, ENS Paris — direct policy influence. Rare for an academic to operate at this level across multiple jurisdictions.

3. **Academic Research**: USC Annenberg, NYU (AI Now), Microsoft Research, Knowing Machines (Sloan) — three institutional positions simultaneously. Each validates the others: MSR gives industry access; USC gives teaching platform; AI Now gives policy independence.

**Key pattern**: Never fully committed to any one institutional logic. Uses the tension between art world, policy world, and academia to maintain independence and credibility across all three. The Venice Silver Lion validates the research; the UN advisory validates the politics; the Yale book validates the scholarship.

## Research Taste Analysis

### Conceptual Strategy — The Materialist Inversion:
Crawford's core intellectual move is **radical inversion**: take the supposedly immaterial (AI, data, algorithms, "the cloud") and reveal its brutal materiality (lithium mines, labor camps, server farms, colonial histories). This inversion works as both intellectual argument AND artistic method — the Anatomy map literally shows you what's behind the smooth cylinder of the Amazon Echo.

**Two-word concept strategy**: "Atlas of AI" — the word "atlas" does enormous work. It positions AI as something requiring geographic investigation: territory, resources, exploration, conquest. It imports the whole history of colonial mapping. This is how she makes a new concept stick — by activating an old word's full historical weight.

**Genealogy method**: *Calculating Empires* deploys Foucauldian genealogy at civilizational scale — 500 years, four themes, showing how current AI is the latest in a long line of calculation-for-domination technologies. This historicizes what tech companies present as unprecedented, making their power structures legible through past patterns.

### Career Architecture — Multi-Institutional Platform Building:
**Simultaneous legitimacy strategy**: Crawford holds positions at Microsoft Research, USC Annenberg, AND the AI Now Institute simultaneously. Each position gives different kind of credibility (industry access, academic standing, policy influence) and each protects her from being captured by the others.

**Institute founding as field declaration**: Co-founding FATE and AI Now Institute weren't just organizational moves — they were **declarations that a field exists**. Before AI Now, there was no institutional home for critical AI research as a discipline. Creating the institution created the field. This is the highest-leverage academic move possible.

**Book as manifesto timing**: *Atlas of AI* (2021) appeared exactly when AI was going mainstream (GPT-3 moment), positioning Crawford as the essential critic before the hype peaked. Timing the "big synthesis book" correctly is a key career move.

### Methodology Signature — Visualization as Investigation:
**The map as argument**: *Anatomy of an AI System* and *Calculating Empires* aren't illustrations of arguments — they ARE the arguments. The visual form does analytical work that prose can't. This is Crawford and Joler's key methodological innovation: using visualization as a primary research output rather than a supplement.

**Art-exhibition as research dissemination**: Showing work at MoMA and Fondazione Prada reaches audiences that academic journals never will. The exhibition circuit functions as research dissemination to different publics. Reaching policymakers through Venice Biennale rather than white papers is a genuine methodological choice.

**The long-form essay + map format**: *Anatomy of AI* paired a massive visual map with a 10,000-word essay — each illuminates the other. This format (visualization + long-form text) is a replicable template for research that wants to be both rigorous AND visually compelling.

### Network Strategy — The Triangle of Validation:
Crawford has built a **validation triangle** between: (1) art institutions (MoMA, Venice, Serpentine), (2) policy spaces (UN, White House, EU), (3) academic journals (Nature, ST&HV). Each validates work for audiences who wouldn't accept the others. The same research project that gets a Venice Silver Lion also gets cited in Congressional testimonies also appears in peer-reviewed journals.

**Collaborator selection as amplification**: Both major collaborators (Joler, Paglen) have their own strong institutional presences and distinct audience bases. Collaborating with Joler adds European critical media art circuit. Collaborating with Paglen adds the surveillance art world and MacArthur network. Each collaboration opens different institutional doors.

**Policy as research platform**: Advisory roles with UN, White House, EU Parliament, Spain's AI Council aren't just service — they're data access, legitimacy signals, and platforms for introducing research into policy discourse. The feedback loop between policy work and academic research is a deliberate strategy.

### Key Moves (chronological)
1. **Electronic music → media studies (1998–2010)**: Started as artist (B(if)tek). PhD gave academic legitimacy. Manning Clark and Max Crawford medals established Australian reputation. Established the pattern: art + scholarship, neither diminishing the other.

2. **Microsoft Research hire (c.2010)**: Joining MSR New York put her inside the most important industrial research lab. Access to industry data, industry networks, credibility with tech companies AND leverage to critique them. "Senior Principal Researcher" title is high enough to speak credibly to industry.

3. **FATE co-founding (c.2014)**: With FAccT conference now a major venue for AI accountability research, this move essentially created the field's primary academic home. First-mover advantage in defining what "AI ethics" means as a scholarly discipline.

4. **AI Now Institute co-founding (2017)**: With Meredith Whittaker, created the independent policy research institute that would define critical AI discourse. Strategic timing — 2017 was when AI hype was accelerating, AI Now became the essential counter-narrative institution.

5. **Anatomy of an AI System (2018)**: First major art collaboration with Joler. Established the methodology (visualization + long-form essay as research output). MoMA acquisition and Design of the Year award proved art institutions would take this seriously.

6. **Atlas of AI (2021)**: Synthesized a decade of research into the definitive popular text. Twelve languages, FT best book, international prizes. This was the moment Crawford became the leading public intellectual on AI critique — not just a researcher but a voice.

7. **Calculating Empires (2023–2025)**: Expanded the methodology to civilizational scale. Venice Silver Lion confirmed art world status at the highest level. European Commission prize confirmed policy relevance. This project represents the pinnacle of the Crawford-Joler method so far.

### What Amber Can Learn
**The atlas format**: Making a large-scale map + long essay as a primary research output — not as illustration but as argument. This is a template for how to make complex system analysis both rigorous and visually accessible.

**Two-word concept coining**: "Atlas of AI" is a masterclass in naming. The word "atlas" does so much work: colonial geography, exploration, resource mapping, the myth of comprehensiveness. Find the single word that activates an entire conceptual history when applied to a new domain.

**Institute-founding as field-shaping**: Don't just publish in the field — create the institutional home that defines what the field is. Even small institutes (AI Now started tiny) can punch far above their weight if they arrive at the right moment.

**The multi-institutional strategy**: Don't choose between industry, academia, and independent nonprofit. Each position gives you something the others can't. Microsoft Research gives data access and industry credibility. USC gives teaching and academic legitimacy. AI Now gives policy independence and public voice. This combination is rare and powerful.

**Art exhibitions as research platforms**: Major art institutions (Fondazione Prada, Mori Museum, Jeu de Paume) reach policymakers, wealthy collectors, and cultural influencers who never read academic journals. The exhibition as research dissemination channel is underused in critical computing.

**Timing the synthesis**: The "big book" matters as much as the timing. *Atlas of AI* appeared exactly when AI went mainstream. Watch for moments when a field is about to tip into public consciousness — the synthesis book/project written just before the tip gets disproportionate attention.
