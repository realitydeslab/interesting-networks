# Stephen Dwoskin

(1939–2012) Brooklyn-born, disabled avant-garde filmmaker; co-founder of the [[London Film-Makers' Co-operative (LFMC)]]; one of the most significant figures in the transition of American underground film culture to Britain. His work is closely connected to gaze theory (associated with [[Laura Mulvey]]); he also rejected the "disabled filmmaker" framing while becoming an important figure in the politics of disability representation.

## Domains
Avant-garde/experimental film; gaze and the politics of looking; embodiment and disability; body; desire; documentary.

## Background
Born Brooklyn, January 15, 1939. Contracted polio at age nine; gruelling rehabilitation; walked with crutches for much of his life, later used wheelchair. Studied at Parsons The New School for Design (teachers included Willem de Kooning and Josef Albers), then New York University. Worked as a graphic designer/art director at CBS and Epic Records. Began making experimental films 1961; became part of the bohemian NY underground film world around [[Jonas Mekas]]. Received Fulbright Scholarship to London in 1964; stayed permanently. Co-founded LFMC 1966 alongside Malcom Le Grice and others. Key figure in British avant-garde film through early 2000s; died June 28, 2012 in London. Archive at University of Reading.

## Key Works
- *Times For* (1970) — cast includes [[Carolee Schneemann]]; inaugurated film series with soundtracks by Gavin Bryars
- *Dyn Amo* (1972) — shot in a Soho strip club; cast includes Jenny Runacre; praised in nascent second-wave feminist press
- *Behindert* (1974) — first film to explicitly address his life as a disabled man; made for German broadcaster ZDF
- *Silent Cry* (1977)
- *Trying to Kiss the Moon* (1994) — autobiographical film using home movies his father shot before he contracted polio
- *Intoxicated By My Illness* (2001) — return to underground with digital technology; praised by French magazine *Trafic*
- *The Sun and the Moon* (2007)
- Book: *Film Is...* (Peter Owen / Overlook Press, 1975) — about underground/experimental/avant-garde cinema

## Key Communities
- [[London Film-Makers' Co-operative (LFMC)]] — co-founder
- [[The Other Cinema]] — associated with this radical distribution collective
- **Spectre** — co-founded film collective (c. 1980) intended to produce films for Channel 4; included Vera Neubauer, Simon Hartog, Phil Mulloy, John Ellis, Keith Griffiths

## Why Interesting
Dwoskin is the transatlantic hinge — the person who physically brought the Mekas cooperative model to Britain. His films' engagement with the gaze, the body, and desire was more phenomenological and more personally inflected than the hard-structural filmmakers around him at the LFMC. The tension between his work and the structural-materialist position (especially Gidal's) is productive: he belonged to the Co-op without entirely belonging to its dominant theoretical tendency. His disability films are also an underexplored chapter of the politics of representation.
