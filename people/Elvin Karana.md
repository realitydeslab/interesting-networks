# Elvin Karana

Associate Professor at TU Delft, Faculty of Industrial Design Engineering. Leads research on Materials Experience — how people experience and relate to materials in products. Collaborates with [[TU Delft Connected Everyday Lab]] on biological HCI (co-author on CHI 2024 cyanobacteria paper with Jiwei Zhou and [[Elisa Giaccardi]]).

## Domains
- Materials experience and materiality in design
- Biological/living materials as design medium
- Human-material relations
- Biofabrication and living artefacts

## Key Works
- [Living with Cyanobacteria](https://research.tudelft.nl/en/publications/5898b8ef-e796-4f58-bc96-5a3bb86eee0f) (CHI 2024, with Zhou, Doubrovski, Giaccardi) — cyanobacterial living artefact for domestic air purification
- [Regenerative Material Ecologies in HCI](https://research.tudelft.nl/en/publications/c14d47d3-fc65-4cfe-b72c-dca176b8ba2c) (2025, with Nicenboim + large coalition) — regenerative material perspectives in HCI

## Key Communities
- [[TU Delft Connected Everyday Lab]] — close collaborator, biological HCI
- [[TEI Community]] — tangible embedded interaction

## Why Interesting
Bridge figure between materials design and biological HCI. As living materials become interface materials, Karana's materials experience framework becomes essential. Co-supervises PhDs at intersection of biology and everyday product design.
