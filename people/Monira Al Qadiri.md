# Monira Al Qadiri

Kuwaiti visual artist (born Dakar, Senegal, 1983); sister of [[Fatima Al Qadiri]]. Founded the GCC art collective (2013). PhD in Intermedia Art from Tokyo University of the Arts (dissertation: "The Aesthetics of Sadness in the Middle East"). Work focuses on petrostate aesthetics, oil culture, gender identity, and the memory of pre-oil Gulf culture (pearl diving). Based in Berlin.

## Key Works
- *Spectrum I* — iridescent sculptures connecting pearls and oil; in Van Abbemuseum permanent collection
- *Empire Dye* (2018) — enlarged Murex snail shell in violet (oil rig alarm color); climate warning
- *Abu Athiyya (Father of Pain)* (performance) — portrays southern Iraqi singer Yas Khodhor; drag as vehicle for transformation
- *Behind the Sun* (2013) — video art
- *Rumours of Affluence* (2012) — video art

## Why Interesting
Her sustained practice on petrostate aesthetics is the most developed visual art treatment of Gulf Futurism's themes. The oil/pearl iridescence as conceptual bridge is elegant. Her Tokyo art education creates an interesting Japan-Gulf-Europe axis that parallels Amber's China-Gulf-UK connections.
