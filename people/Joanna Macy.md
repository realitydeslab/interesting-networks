# Joanna Macy

Buddhist scholar, environmental activist, and systems theorist. Creator of the "Work That Reconnects" — a framework for processing ecological grief and despair and turning it into engaged action. Author of *Mutual Causality in Buddhism and General Systems Theory* (1991), *World as Lover, World as Self* (2007), *Active Hope* (2012, with Chris Johnstone).

[[David Abram]] describes a formative early encounter: driving in the early 1980s, he caught Macy's voice on the radio — "breathy, with a slightly nasal twang, saying something astonishingly simple" — and it was a revelatory moment. Her Buddhist-ecological worldview (interdependence, impermanence, grief as love) runs parallel to Abram's ecological phenomenology.

Deep ecology adjacent; connected to Arne Naess, Fritjof Capra, Vandana Shiva circles.
