# Daniel Shiffman

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Created stub note. Discovered through **[[Processing Foundation]]** deep research as third project lead and Board Director.
- **2024:** Updated *The Nature of Code* for p5.js (JavaScript edition), published by No Starch Press.

Programmer, educator, and one of the most effective creative coding teachers alive. Associate Arts Professor at NYU Tisch ITP. Board of Directors, [[Processing Foundation]]. Third project lead at Processing (joined 2012, alongside co-creators Casey Reas and Ben Fry). Creator of **[The Coding Train](https://thecodingtrain.com/)** — a hugely popular YouTube channel teaching creative coding, computational art, and simulated natural systems. Author of *The Nature of Code* (the definitive book on simulating natural systems with code).

## Key Works
- **[The Coding Train](https://thecodingtrain.com/)** — YouTube channel with millions of views. Playful, accessible tutorials on Processing, p5.js, ml5.js, and natural systems simulation. Made computational thinking accessible to artists with no prior coding background.
- **[The Nature of Code](https://natureofcode.com/)** — 2012 (Processing), 2024 (p5.js, No Starch Press). Simulating natural systems with programming: physics, agents, fractals, neural networks, evolution, swarms. A landmark creative coding pedagogy text.
- **[Learning Processing](http://learningprocessing.com/)** — 2008; 2nd edition 2015. Beginner's guide to Processing.
- **ml5.js contributions** — Tutorials for the machine learning for artists library (p5.js-based, NYU ITP-developed).
- **Swarm #1, #2, #3 (2002, 2004)** — Early artworks. Algorithms creating virtual flocking birds from live video input; Craig Reynolds' Boids model as real-time digital brushstrokes.

## Why Interesting
Shiffman is the **educator-multiplier** of the creative coding world. The Nature of Code and The Coding Train have reached people who would never attend a workshop or read an academic paper. His pedagogy is strikingly aligned with ALIFE and machine behavior research: flocking, emergence, evolutionary systems, neural networks — all explained through code, all oriented toward understanding natural systems computationally. The 2024 p5.js edition of *The Nature of Code* is one of the clearest bridges between creative coding practice and computational biology/ALIFE concepts that exists in accessible form.

For Amber's research interests, Shiffman is interesting not as a practitioner but as a **pedagogy model**: how do you teach computational thinking about natural systems to people who approach through art? His answer is: through making, through play, through visual feedback loops.
