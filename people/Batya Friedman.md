# Batya Friedman

Professor at the UW Information School and founding pioneer of **Value Sensitive Design** (VSD) — a methodology for accounting for human values in a principled, systematic manner throughout the design process. Pioneered VSD in the 1990s. Her lab ([vsdesign.org](https://vsdesign.org/)) continues developing theory, method, and practice for values-centered technology design — explicitly for "all stakeholders — human and beyond human — to flourish."

Co-created **Data Statements** with [[Emily Bender]] (first conceptualized 2017, published 2018 in TACL) — the framework for documenting NLP datasets to surface bias and enable transparency.

## Why Interesting
VSD is one of the few frameworks that tries to operationalize ethics in design systematically — not as an add-on checklist but as a design methodology woven through the entire process. Connects to participatory design, responsible AI, and more-than-human design traditions.
