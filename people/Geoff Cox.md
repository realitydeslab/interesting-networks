# Geoff Cox

Professor at **Aarhus University** (SHAPE — Digital Citizenship & Digital Aesthetics Research Centre) and **London South Bank University** (Centre for the Study of the Networked Image). Key figure in critical net aesthetics, and one of the main organizers of the **Toward Minor Tech** workshop series at **[[transmediale]]** (Berlin) — held annually, co-organized with **Christian Ulrik Andersen**.

Author of *Speaking Code: Coding as Aesthetic and Political Expression* (MIT Press, 2013, with Alex McLean). Associated with the *A Peer-Reviewed Journal About* (APRJA) — the publication that carried Daniel Chávez Heras's 2019 paper on "Spectacular machinery and encrypted spectatorship."

## Toward Minor Tech
The annual workshop interrogates what "minor tech" could mean — drawing on Deleuze and Guattari's concept of "minor literature" to think about technologies of resistance, refusal, and small-scale alternatives to big tech. [[Daniel Chávez Heras]] has been a regular contributor and has supported PhD students as they develop contributions — a relationship of intellectual mentorship/community within this workshop.
