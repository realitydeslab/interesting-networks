# Erich Hörl

German media philosopher and philosopher of technology. Professor at [Leuphana University Lüneburg](https://www.leuphana.de/), Institute for Culture and Aesthetics of Digital Media (ICAM). Originator of the concept of **general ecology** — the environmental turn in media and technology philosophy that takes ecological thought beyond biology into a philosophy of the technosphere, sense, and milieu. One of the most important thinkers in contemporary philosophy of technology and media at the intersection of posthumanism and continental philosophy. Key node in the [[Research Network for Philosophy and Technology]].

## Background
Trained in philosophy in Germany. Developed the concept of "general ecology" as a framework for thinking the technological condition of contemporary existence — not ecology in the biological sense, but a broader ecology of sense, milieu, and technological environment. Edited the foundational volume *General Ecology: The New Ecological Paradigm* (Bloomsbury, 2017), bringing together Bernard Stiegler, Gilbert Simondon, Félix Guattari, and others into a coherent framework. Formerly associated with Ruhr-Universität Bochum.

His work is at the intersection of: French continental philosophy of technology (Simondon, Stiegler), German media theory (Kittler, Siemens), posthumanism, and ecological thought. Focuses on what he calls the "age of general ecology" — the epoch in which technology becomes the total milieu of human existence.
