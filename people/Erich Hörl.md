# Erich Hörl

German-Austrian media philosopher and philosopher of technology. W3-Professor for Media Culture and Media Philosophy at [Leuphana University Lüneburg](https://www.leuphana.de/en/), Director of the [Leuphana Institute for Advanced Studies in Culture and Society (LIAS)](https://www.leuphana.de/en/), and Vice-President for Research. Originator of the concept of **general ecology** — the environmental/ecological turn in media and technology philosophy that reframes ecological thought as a philosophy of the technosphere, sense, milieu, and technological condition. One of the most important thinkers in contemporary philosophy of technology, bridging French continental philosophy of technology (Simondon, Stiegler, Nancy), German media theory (Kittler, cybernetics), and contemporary ecological thought. Key node in the [[Research Network for Philosophy and Technology]] and intellectual inheritor of Bernard Stiegler's organological programme.

## Background

Born in Vienna, studied philosophy and communication theory in **Vienna and Paris** — a classical Central European intellectual formation that gave him fluency in both analytic media theory and French continental philosophy. His Paris sojourn oriented him firmly toward the Simondon-Stiegler lineage.

**PhD 2003, Humboldt-Universität zu Berlin** (Kulturwissenschaft): *"Die heiligen Kanäle. Über die archaische Illusion der Kommunikation"* (published as *Sacred Channels*, Amsterdam: De Gruyter, 2018 in English). The dissertation's premise — that communication is a mythological/archaic construct rather than transparent exchange — established his lifelong concern with the technological constitution of sense and meaning.

**2003-2005:** Research associate, DFG research group ["Bild, Schrift, Zahl" (Image, Script, Number)](https://www.hu-berlin.de/) at the Hermann von Helmholtz-Zentrum für Kulturtechnik, Humboldt-Universität zu Berlin — the seedbed of German Kulturtechnik (cultural techniques) research. This positioned him at the heart of the German post-Kittler media theory scene.

**2004-2006:** Assistant for Philosophy of Technology at ETH Zürich — the Swiss encounter between philosophy and engineering that sharpened his techno-philosophical vocabulary.

**2006-2012:** Junior Professor (then 2012-2014 W2-Professor) for Media Technology and Media Philosophy at **Ruhr-Universität Bochum**. Founded and directed the [Bochumer Kolloquium Medienwissenschaft (bkm)](https://www.ruhr-uni-bochum.de/medienwissenschaft/) from 2007, a major annual media studies conference in the German-speaking world.

**2010/11:** Senior Fellow at the [IKKM (Internationales Kolleg für Kulturtechnikforschung und Medienphilosophie)](https://www.ikkm-weimar.de/), Bauhaus-Universität Weimar — the premier German interdisciplinary research institute for cultural techniques and media philosophy, where he consolidated his conceptual framework.

**2013/14:** Senior Fellow at the DFG-Kollegforschergruppe [Medienkulturen der Computersimulation (mecs)](https://www.leuphana.de/en/), Leuphana University.

**Since 2014:** W3-Professor for Media Culture and Media Philosophy, [Leuphana University Lüneburg](https://www.leuphana.de/en/). The Leuphana move marked a decisive shift: from Bochum's more classical media-technical approach to the more interdisciplinary, theory-inflected environment of Lüneburg. It was at Leuphana that the *General Ecology* project crystallized.

**2016:** Senior Fellow, [Kulturwissenschaftliches Kolleg Konstanz](https://www.exc.uni-konstanz.de/inequality/kolleg-konstanz/) — the "Cultural Sciences College" at Konstanz, part of the German Excellence Initiative.

**Since 2022:** Dual role as Director of [LIAS (Leuphana Institute for Advanced Studies in Culture and Society)](https://www.leuphana.de/en/) and Vice-President for Research, making him one of the most institutionally powerful figures in German media studies / philosophy of technology.

**Since 11/2024:** Extraordinary Professor at the [Center for Humanities Research (CHR)](https://www.uwc.ac.za/), University of the Western Cape, South Africa — a striking South-South intellectual axis, developed through collaboration with Premesh Lalu (decolonial theory, the postcolony, university transformation).

His current work focuses on: (1) **theory and history of disruption** — disruptivity as the key problem of understanding the contemporary world; (2) **general ecology** and critique of **environmentality** — the becoming-environmental of thinking, power, and capital.

## Key Works

### Books (monographs)
- *[Sacred Channels: The Archaic Illusion of Communication](https://www.degruyter.com/document/doi/10.1515/9780226524528/html)* (Amsterdam: De Gruyter, 2018; trans. Nils F. Argues that communication is not transparent transmission but a construction rooted in archaic, theological, and mythological genealogies. ## Research Taste Analysis

### Conceptual Strategy

**Framework multiplication + field declaration via anthology:**
Hörl's most important strategic move was not writing a single-authored treatise but *curating the field* through *General Ecology* (2017). By assembling Stiegler, Simondon, Guattari, Ingold, Fuller, Hansen, and Weizman under one cover with his own programmatic introduction, he simultaneously:
1. Defined "general ecology" as a new paradigm (by naming it as such in the subtitle)
2. Claimed the field before it crystallized
3. Made himself *necessary* to subsequent work — any scholar working on technological ecology must engage this volume
4. Created an intellectual coalition rather than a solo claim

This is the **seed anthology strategy**: write the introduction that names the paradigm, curate the contributors who will be associated with it, and publish it at the moment when enough disparate work exists to be coherent but before anyone else has synthesized it.

**Two-word concept coining:**
- "General ecology" — simple, memorable, expandable, cites Guattari (Trois écologies) while moving beyond it
- "Environmentality" — Foucault + environment; immediately understandable to anyone trained in biopolitics
- "The technological condition" — clean, Heideggerian resonance, programmatic
- "Cybernetization" — nominalization of a process, allows historical narrative

Each of these moves quickly from Hörl's essay into the discourse of adjacent scholars. The concepts are *designed to travel*.

**Framework escalation:**
Each concept Hörl introduces is more expansive than the last:
- "Technological condition" (2015) — about technology
- "General ecology" (2017) — about all ecology/milieu/sense
- "Environmentality" (2018) — about power in the ecological epoch
- "Disruption" (current) — about historical temporality itself

Each step raises the stakes: from media philosophy → ecology → power → history. This is framework escalation as a career strategy.

### Career Architecture

**Platform-building at every stage:**
1. **Bochum (2007-2014):** Built the bkm conference — not the most prestigious but *his own*. Gives him curatorial authority, builds network, attracts speakers who become collaborators.

### What Amber Can Learn

1. **The Seed Anthology Strategy:** Don't wait until famous to edit a major volume. Instead: identify a field that is *emerging but unnamed*, write an introduction that names it, curate contributors who are doing the work, publish it. The curation authority becomes your reputation.

2. **Concepts designed to travel:** "General ecology," "environmentality," "technological condition" are all phrases that work in casual speech, in abstracts, in grant applications, and in peer-reviewed papers. Design concepts that are *portable across registers*. Two-word combinations that cross two existing fields are particularly powerful.

3. **Platform before prestige:** Build your own venue (conference, workshop series, annual event) before you're invited to others' platforms. The convening role teaches you who the relevant people are and gives you a reason to contact anyone in the field.

4. **Framework escalation over time:** Start with a specific domain claim, then gradually show that your framework applies to a wider domain. Each paper should show the *expandability* of the framework, not just add more evidence for the original claim.

5. **Strategic Global South moves:** As a European theory person, connecting to African, Latin American, or Asian institutional partners creates a distinctive intellectual positioning that is increasingly valued and is genuinely rare in the German humanities tradition. It also opens access to funding streams and audiences that Europe-only scholars miss.
