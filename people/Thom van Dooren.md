# Thom van Dooren

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-03-02:** Stub created during Multispecies Design deep research.

Professor of Environmental Humanities at the University of Sydney (School of Humanities + Sydney Environment Institute). From 2024, Humboldt Research Award Fellow at [MESH (Multidisciplinary Environmental Studies in the Humanities)](https://mesh.uni-koeln.de/) at the University of Cologne. Elected Fellow of the Australian Academy of the Humanities (2022).

Co-founded **extinction studies** with [[Deborah Bird Rose]]. His approach: rigorous philosophy and ethics through deep ethnographic engagement with specific threatened species (albatrosses, cranes, honeybees, penguins, crows, snails). The question that drives him: what does it mean to lose a way of life? Not just biodiversity statistics, but the texture of lives lived in multispecies entanglements.

## Domains
- Extinction studies (field co-founder)
- Environmental humanities — philosophy, ethics, and cultural politics of extinction
- Multispecies ethnography — studying human entanglements with threatened species
- Science and technology studies (STS) adjacent
- More-than-human storytelling and communication

## Key Works
- [*Flight Ways: Life and Loss at the Edge of Extinction*](http://thomvandooren.org/publications/flight-ways/) (Columbia University Press, 2014) — albatrosses, cranes, penguins, vultures, honeybees. Finalist, Ludwik Fleck Prize (4S).
- [*The Wake of Crows: Living and Dying in Shared Worlds*](http://thomvandooren.org/wake) (Columbia University Press, 2019) — crow-human entanglements and multispecies ethics.
- [*A World in a Shell: Snail Stories for a Time of Extinctions*](https://www.thomvandooren.org/a-world-in-a-shell/) (MIT Press, 2022) — Hawaiian tree snails, extinction, and storytelling. Gold Nautilus Book Award; shortlisted Prime Minister's Literary Award (non-fiction). Translated into French, Japanese, Italian, Chinese.
- *Multispecies Storytelling in Catastrophic Times* (2025 ongoing talk series) — drawing on *A World in a Shell*, exploring narrative methods for multispecies research.
- *The Craft of Poisoning* (Cambridge Elements: Environmental Humanities, forthcoming 2026/2027) — short book in progress.

## Why Interesting
Van Dooren is one of the few scholars in the multispecies tradition who actively collaborates with designers (multimedia essay, 2024, with Zoë Sadokierski, Myles Oakey, Timo Rissanen). His extinction studies framework — taking seriously the loss of specific ways of life — is philosophically rich and could inform how Amber thinks about machine behavior and the death/discontinuation of AI systems (machine death, machine mortality themes). His emphasis on *storytelling* as a research method connects with speculative design practice.
