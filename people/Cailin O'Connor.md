# Cailin O'Connor

Philosopher of science at UC Irvine working on game theory, social epistemology, evolution of social norms, and misinformation. Co-author (with James Weatherall) of [*The Misinformation Age: How False Beliefs Spread*](https://yalebooks.yale.edu/book/9780300251517/the-misinformation-age/) (2019, Yale UP) — models how false beliefs propagate through social networks using evolutionary game theory. Faculty affiliate at [[CREATE — Steckler Center for Responsible, Ethical, and Accessible Technology (UCI)]].

## Domains
- Philosophy of science, social epistemology
- Game theory, evolutionary game theory
- Misinformation, belief propagation in networks
- Evolution of gender norms and inequality (*The Origins of Unfairness*, 2019)

## Why Interesting
Her use of game theory and evolutionary models to explain how false beliefs and unfair norms spread connects to Amber's interests in agent behavior, machine behavior, and how AI systems might change societal epistemic norms. She models societies as evolving systems — close to ALife / social simulation territory. Discovery via [[CREATE — Steckler Center for Responsible, Ethical, and Accessible Technology (UCI)]].
