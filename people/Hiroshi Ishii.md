# Hiroshi Ishii

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-27:** Stub created during TEI Community deep research.

Jerome B. Wiesner Professor of Media Arts and Sciences at MIT Media Lab. Co-founded the ACM TEI conference series (with Brygg Ullmer). Creator of the Tangible User Interface (TUI) concept. Vision trajectory: **Tangible Bits** (1997, CHI paper with Ullmer) → **Radical Atoms** (2012, shape-changing materials as programmable matter) → **TeleAbsence** (2024+, connecting across time including to deceased). Won SIGCHI Lifetime Research Award 2019. ACM Fellow 2022. Motto: "Be Artistic and Analytic. Be Poetic and Pragmatic." Founded Tangible Media Group at MIT.

## Key Works
- "Tangible Bits: Towards Seamless Interfaces between People, Bits and Atoms" CHI 1997 — founding paper of TUI
- Radical Atoms vision (2012) — hypothetical programmable materials
- TeamWorkStation (1990), ClearBoard (1992) — CSCW works at NTT Japan

## Why Interesting
Patriarch of the tangible interaction community. His vision of computing that transcends GUIs — through physical form, materiality, and the body — resonates deeply with Amber's more-than-human design and HoloKit work. The "TeleAbsence" vision (connecting to past selves, deceased loved ones) connects to Amber's interest in mortality and machine death.
