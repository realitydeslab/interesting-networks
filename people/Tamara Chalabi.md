# Tamara Chalabi

Iraqi-British historian, cultural entrepreneur, and founder/chair of the [[Ruya Foundation]], Baghdad. Trained at Harvard (PhD candidate in history); daughter of Iraqi politician Ahmed Chalabi. She bridges the Iraqi art world and international contemporary art through cultural programming, artistic facilitation, and the Venice Biennale Iraqi Pavilion.

## Background
- Harvard-educated historian; specialist in Iraqi and Middle Eastern history
- Founded the [Ruya Foundation](https://ruyafoundation.org/) — the primary contemporary art organization in Iraq
- Author of *The Shia of Samarra: The Living Fabric of an Iraqi City* (I.B. Tauris, 2011)
- Commutes between London and Baghdad
- Facilitated [[Francis Alÿs]]'s Iraq residencies (2016 Baghdad workshop; November 2016 Mosul front line trip with Kurdish battalion)
- Co-produced *Sandlines, the Story of History* (2018–2020) with [[Francis Alÿs]] and [[Julien Devaux]]
- Curated/organized the Iraqi Pavilion at the 57th Venice Biennale (2017)

## Key Works
- **Iraqi Pavilion, 57th Venice Biennale** (2017) — presented [[Francis Alÿs]]'s *as If* (Mosul)
- *Sandlines, the Story of History* (2018–2020) — co-production
- Multiple editions of annual Ruya cultural programming in Baghdad and internationally

## Key Relationships
- **[[Francis Alÿs]]** — deep collaboration and friendship; published conversation (2017) about art and reconciliation
- **Ruya Foundation team** — builds an organization bridging Baghdad and international art world

## Why Interesting
Chalabi represents the possibility of building serious cultural infrastructure in the most difficult contexts. Her position — historian with deep family connections to Iraqi politics (Ahmed Chalabi was a controversial figure in the Iraq War), using art rather than politics as her tool — is both unusual and productive. The 2017 Venice conversation with [[Francis Alÿs]] is one of the most honest and thoughtful documents about what art can and cannot do in conflict contexts.
