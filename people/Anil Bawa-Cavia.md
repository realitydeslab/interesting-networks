# Anil Bawa-Cavia

Theorist of computational reason and inhuman intelligence. Author of *Logiciel: Six Seminars on Computational Reason*. Piece "The Inclosure of Reason" published in [HKW Technosphere Magazine](https://technosphere-magazine.hkw.de/). Guest on [[Interdependence Podcast]] (2022) — episode titled "Inhuman Intelligence."

## Domains
- Philosophy of computation
- Computational reason as political form
- Inhuman intelligence (non-human machine cognition)
- Critical theory of software

## Why Interesting
Thinking about computation not as tool but as a form of reason with its own politics — directly relevant to Amber's machine behavior / agent ethology framing. The "inclosure of reason" concept (computation as structurally enclosing the human) is a key theoretical reference for post-humanism in AI.
