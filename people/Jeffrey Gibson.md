# Jeffrey Gibson

Choctaw and Cherokee artist. Represented the United States at the 58th Venice Biennale (2024) — the first Indigenous artist to do so solo. Member of [[Forge Project]]'s Indigenous Steering Council. Major figure in contemporary Indigenous art: large-scale paintings and sculptures fusing Indigenous geometric patterns with queer subjectivity, pop color, and protest text. MacArthur Fellow 2019.

## Background
Choctaw Nation of Oklahoma and Mississippi Band of Choctaw Indians / Cherokee descent. Born in Colorado Springs, raised internationally (parents in US military). Studied at The School of the Art Institute of Chicago, The Glasgow School of Art, Goldsmiths (London). Based in Hudson Valley, NY.

## Key Works
- US Pavilion, 58th Venice Biennale (2024) — *The Space Between Us*
- Large-scale works fusing Indigenous geometric patterns, queer identity, pop text
- Collaboration with [[Forge Project]] (ISC member)

## Awards & Fellowships
- MacArthur Fellow (2019)
- 58th Venice Biennale, US Pavilion (2024) — first Indigenous solo representation

## Key Communities
- [[Forge Project]] — Indigenous Steering Council member
- Jack Shainman Gallery — represented

## Key Relationships
- [[Candice Hopkins]] — Forge Project Executive Director; close colleague

## Why Interesting
The first Indigenous artist to solo-represent the US at Venice. His work fuses Indigenous geometric patterns with queer politics and protest culture — a political aesthetics that refuses both "authentic" Indigeneity and assimilationist mainstream art. The MacArthur + Venice combination is a signal of maximum institutional recognition. The fact that he serves on the Forge ISC (alongside Hopkins) is a sign of the emerging institutional infrastructure for Indigenous contemporary art.
