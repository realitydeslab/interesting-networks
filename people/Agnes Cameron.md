# Agnes Cameron

Artist, researcher, and specialist technician at UAL's Creative Computing Institute. Co-founder of [[Foreign Objects]] design studio (with [[Gary Zhexi Zhang]]). Makes ecological simulations, open-source investigation tools, knitting machines, synthesisers, and critical writing. Wants an "unalienated relationship to technology." Will teach anyone anything.

## Key Works

### Simulation and Political Ecology
- **[Permaculture Network](https://rokeby.github.io/permaculture/)** (2019/2020) — agent-based simulation of Sakiya's land in Palestine; models goats, plants, and ecological flows. Built with Gary Zhexi Zhang during Sakiya residency. Commissioned as Schloss Solitude / ZKM web residency. "On goats and real/virtual ecologies." The politics: using simulation to represent Palestinian land sovereignty rather than surveillance or extraction. GitHub: [rokeby/permaculture](https://github.com/rokeby/permaculture).
- **[The First 10,000 Years](https://fud.global/)** (2020/2021) — live catastrophe insurance marketplace simulation. Part of Gary Zhexi Zhang's *fud* project, commissioned by Arts Catalyst and Bloc Projects Sheffield in partnership with Medialab Prado. "A hurricane observatory, financial exchange and chatroom in which human and non-human players place their wagers in a marketplace for systemic uncertainties." Cameron co-created the technical architecture. Based on how insurance industry simulates 10,000 years of future weather to price risk.

### Investigation and Journalism Tools
- **[Multispectral Satellite Imagery Explorer](https://soup.agnescameron.info/2025/01/10/satellite.html)** (Jan 2025) — Bellingcat Technical Writing Fellowship project. Google Earth Engine tool for open-source investigators. Accompanying [Bellingcat guide](https://www.bellingcat.com/resources/2025/01/10/satellite-imagery-bands-guide/) explains multispectral imaging to non-specialists, with case studies on mining and deforestation. Technically sophisticated but accessible.
- **[Arms Industry Wikithon](https://n-space-shs.github.io/arms-industry-wikithon/)** (2025/2026) — monthly sessions at Newspeak House, expanding Wikipedia coverage of companies, entities and individuals in the global arms industry. Hybrid (in-person + online). No prior knowledge required. Very direct political tech practice.

## Venue Choice Analysis
Cameron moves through a very specific circuit: **critical technology pedagogy** (MIT, Parsons, Hunter, UAL CCI) × **art-science residencies with political commitments** (Sakiya, Signal Culture) × **open-source journalism/investigation** (Bellingcat) × **political technology** (Newspeak House) × **art infrastructure** (Somerset House Studios, Arts Catalyst). 

She doesn't play the gallery circuit at all — almost none of her work shows in white cube spaces. Instead she works through: commissions (Arts Catalyst), residencies (Sakiya, Schloss Solitude, Signal Culture), fellowships (Bellingcat), and institutions (MIT, UAL). This is a practitioner who runs towards institutional infrastructure and technical tools rather than exhibition-making.

Her webring (Moving Castles, Dan Taeyoung, Laurel Schwulst, Mindy Seu, Trust, Omar Rizwan, Toby Shorin, Knowledge Futures Group, are.na) reveals her taste: independent web culture, critical computing, care-ful design, distributed learning.

## Why Interesting
Three things make Agnes Cameron unusually compelling for Amber's network:

**1. The pedagogical-technical-political triangle.** She doesn't separate making tools, teaching them, and taking political positions. The satellite imagery guide is Bellingcat journalism infrastructure. The permaculture simulation is Palestinian land sovereignty software. The Arms Industry Wikithon is direct democracy infrastructure. This is "agentive commoning" before the term existed.

**2. Simulation as political form.** Her work with Gary Zhexi Zhang on ecological and financial simulations treats modelling itself as a political act — who gets to simulate what land, what futures, what catastrophes. "Who models the modellers?" is not rhetorical for her. This resonates directly with Amber's interests in agent ethology, open-endedness, and more-than-human design.

**3. Unalienated materiality.** She builds synthesisers, hacks knitting machines, teaches embroidery alongside blockchain. This is hands-on materialist practice that refuses to separate digital from physical, cute from critical. The blog ("things from the room in the back") is a window into this — food texture, bread, knitting as much as simulation theory.

Connection to Amber's work: she's working on what agent-based simulation *is for* politically, how land gets modelled, and who controls that modelling — questions very close to Amber's HoloKit/agentic systems research but from an arts/OSINT/ecology direction. The Gary Zhexi Zhang connection already makes her an adjacent node; she deserves her own relationship.
