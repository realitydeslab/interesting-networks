# Kite (Suzanne Kite)

Oglála Lakȟóta artist, composer, researcher, and educator. One of the very few Indigenous artists working with AI and machine learning from Indigenous ontological frameworks. Heads an **Indigenous AI Lab at Bard College**. PhD from Concordia University (Montreal) on **Lakota ontology, AI, and contemporary art practice** — a landmark at the intersection of Indigenous epistemology and machine intelligence. 2023 Forge Project Fellow.

## Background
Oglála Lakȟóta. MFA from Bard College. PhD from Concordia University (Montreal) — dissertation on intersections of Lakȟóta ontology, AI, and contemporary art. Working with AI/ML since 2018. Runs Indigenous AI Lab at Bard. Published in The Funambulist ("Dreaming a Sovereign Indigenous Future," 2019) — establishing Indigenous protocols for AI.

## Key Works
- *Listener* (2018–ongoing) — hair-braid interface with electronics, sweetgrass, recycled materials; performance work with biometric listening
- *Pȟehíŋ kiŋ líla akhíšoke (Her hair was heavy.)* (2019) — performance, REDCAT Los Angeles
- *Cosmologyscape* (2024, with Alisha B Wormsley) — Creative Time commission; dream collection site → sculptures (Artforum, EMPAC)
- Dream scores — graphic scores from Lakota-language-derived wooden block notation; performed with Silk Road Ensemble, Third Coast Percussion, and others
- *Waniyetu Wowapi* (winter count works) — Lakota time-recording system as artistic/research methodology
- ["Dreaming a Sovereign Indigenous Future"](https://thefunambulist.net/magazine/24-futurisms/dreaming-sovereign-indigenous-future-suzanne-kite) — The Funambulist Magazine, 2019. Framework for Indigenous protocols for AI.

## Why Interesting
**Kite is one of the most important emerging figures at the intersection of Indigenous sovereignty and AI.** She is doing what almost no one else is: building an actual Indigenous AI Lab, grounding machine learning research in Lakota ontology rather than Western epistemologies, and creating art that enacts rather than just theorizes Indigenous technological sovereignty.
