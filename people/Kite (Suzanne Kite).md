# Kite (Suzanne Kite)

Kite (b. 1990, Sylmar, CA; lives Catskill, NY) is an **Oglála Lakȟóta artist, composer, and scholar** — and arguably the most significant Indigenous artist working at the intersection of AI, machine learning, and Indigenous ontological frameworks today. She holds the position of **Director of Wihanble S'a Lab** (Bard's designated NEH Humanities Research Center on AI), Distinguished Artist in Residence, and Assistant Professor of American and Indigenous Studies at Bard College. Her PhD from Concordia University examined contemporary Lakota ontologies through research-creation, computational media, and performance. **First American Indian artist to utilize Machine Learning in art practice.**

## Background

Kite grew up in Sylmar, California. Enrolled member of the Oglala Sioux Tribe (Oglála Lakȟóta). Earned BFA from **California Institute of the Arts**, MFA from **Bard College**, and a **PhD from Concordia University** (Montréal) — dissertation on Lakota ontology, AI, and contemporary art practice. 

Has been working with machine learning techniques since **2017** and developing body interfaces for performance since **2013**. Founded and directs the **Wihanble S'a Lab** (formerly Indigenous AI Lab) at Bard College. Was Global Coordinator for the **Indigenous Protocols and Artificial Intelligence Workshops** supported by the Canadian Institute for Advanced Research (CIFAR), which produced the landmark *Indigenous Protocols and Artificial Intelligence Position Paper* (2020), co-authored with Jason Edward Lewis, Noelani Arista, and Archer Pechawis — the article "Making Kin with Machines" won an award from MIT Press's Journal of Design and Science.

The sculpture *Ínyan Iyé (Telling Rock)* (2019) was featured on the cover of Canadian Art magazine. Kite has been included in *Atlas of Anomalous AI*, *Indigenous Futurisms*, the Serpentine Gallery's *Creative AI Database*, the *American Indian Culture and Research Journal*, and other major publications.

Her work is deeply grounded in **Lakota cosmology and protocol**: Mitákuye Oyásʼiŋ (all are related), seven-generation ethics, the ontological status of stones, Waniyetu Wowapi (winter counts), Lakota beadwork traditions, and the figure of Blue Woman who ushers Lakota souls through the Big Dipper.

## Key Works

### Performances / Installations
- **Listener** (2018–ongoing) — signature work. Hair-braid interface (plastic hair, sweetgrass, recycled materials, custom electronics). Live performance with biometric listening, police scanner transmissions, GPS maps, and ML-generated geometric patterns from Lakota quillwork. Premiered at REDCAT, Los Angeles. Key statement of Lakota technological sovereignty through machine learning.
- **Pȟehíŋ kiŋ líla akhíšoke (Her hair was heavy.)** (2019) — performance, REDCAT, Los Angeles.
- **Ínyan Iyé (Telling Rock)** (2019) — sculpture. On cover of Canadian Art magazine.
- **Owíŋža Íčhiyopatȟapi (for Cosmologyscape)** (2024) — live performance score for opening of *Cosmologyscape* at 300 Ashland Plaza, Brooklyn. Musicians: Marilu Donovan, Nava Dunkelman, Kevin Farrell, Adam Markiewicz, Katie Porter, Robbie Wing.

### Public Art
- **Cosmologyscape** (2024, with [[Alisha B Wormsley]]) — Creative Time commission, selected from 2022 Open Call. Five years of **Black and Indigenous Dreaming Workshops** (hosted with Wormsley) formed the base. Public dreams submitted to cosmologyscape website were: (1) algorithmically turned into quilt squares using Lakota visual language and Black quilting traditions → digital tapestry; (2) run through ML to generate patterns/colors/contours → mosaic benches with growing plant life. Installed at The Plaza at 300 Ashland, Downtown Brooklyn, Sep 20–Nov 3, 2024. Described the project: "if dreams are the material used to build in waking life, whose dreams made the environments we live in today?"

## Intellectual Framework

Kite's practice is grounded in **Lakota ontological frameworks** that fundamentally challenge Western epistemologies underlying mainstream AI:

1. **Relationality as technology** — Lakota understanding of all beings as kin (Mitákuye Oyásʼiŋ) means AI must be embedded in circles of relation including nonhumans. Data, metals, minerals are not abstract — they come from places and carry obligations.

2. **Seven-generation ethics** — Lakota decision-making protocols that think 7 generations ahead. Applied to AI: not just near-term safety but multi-generational relationships between humans and nonhumans.

3. **The ontological status of stones** — Kite's AI research is rooted in Lakota understanding of stones as having interiority and intentionality. Stones as data: "Stones want to help." This becomes the philosophical foundation for her machine learning work with mineral-based hardware.

4. **Dream knowledge** — Lakota beadworkers use dreams and vision as essential practice. Kite extends this: dreams become data, data generates form. *Cosmologyscape* is the public materialization of this.

5. **Sovereignty through relation** — visual sovereignty (Jolene Rickard's theory) extended to technological sovereignty. Four demands: sovereign data control, Indigenous-knowledge technology, advocacy for nonhuman relations, anti-extraction.

6. **Right to Repair as ethics** — in the IP+AI Position Paper: "A physical computing device, created in a Good Way, must be designed for the Right to Repair, as well as to recycle, transform, and reuse."

**Key phrase:** "Art is the way we know what we know" — Kite uses artmaking as epistemological work, not just illustration of ideas.

**Kite is the most important node at the intersection of Indigenous sovereignty and AI practice.** She is building the infrastructure (a dedicated lab, an NEH-recognized center, a position paper now cited globally) that makes Indigenous AI research institutionally legible.

- **More-than-human** — Lakota cosmology offers one of the most sophisticated more-than-human frameworks: stones as beings, dreams as knowledge, relations as technology. Directly illuminates Amber's more-than-human design work.
