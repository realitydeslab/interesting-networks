# Lauren Lee McCarthy

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Initial deep research. Created comprehensive profile with full background, works, and network analysis.

Artist, programmer, and educator exploring social relationships in the context of surveillance, automation, and AI. Creator of p5.js, one of the most influential creative coding frameworks. UCLA faculty member investigating how technology mediates human intimacy and control.

## Background
Born with dual degrees from MIT in Art and Design and Computer Science (2008), followed by MFA in Design Media Arts from UCLA (2011). Career trajectory combines rigorous technical education with critical art practice.

**Professional path:** Started as Designer/Software Developer at Sosolimited (2011-2013) working on large-scale interactive installations, then Full-Time Faculty at NYU ITP (2013-2016) before current position as Assistant Professor at UCLA Design Media Arts (2016-Present).

**Technical foundation:** Deep programming background enables her to build complete technical systems rather than relying on collaborators — from p5.js framework development to real-time surveillance infrastructure for performances.

## Domains
- **Human-AI relations:** Performance art examining power dynamics between humans and algorithmic systems
- **Surveillance culture:** Interactive installations critiquing normalization of constant monitoring  
- **Creative coding infrastructure:** Building accessible tools for artists and educators
- **Social automation:** Investigating how technology mediates intimacy, dating, relationships
- **Critical design:** Using speculative scenarios to examine technological futures

## Key Works

### Major Performance Cycle: Human-AI Relations
- **[LAUREN](https://get-lauren.net/LAUREN)** (2017-ongoing) — Performance where she becomes a human Alexa, installed in participants' homes via custom smart devices. Participants control her through app interface as she watches and responds 24/7. **Prix Ars Electronica Golden Nica** (2019), exhibited globally including **[[Ars Electronica]]**, **[[Ford Foundation Gallery]]**.
- **[SOMEONE](https://get-lauren.net/SOMEONE)** (2019) — Crowdsourced surveillance performance where remote workers watch participants' homes via connected cameras, responding to requests and observations. 2-month duration with 4 participants.
- **[LAUREN Portable](https://get-lauren.net/LAUREN-Portable)** (ongoing) — Mobile version allowing Lauren to be "installed" in any space temporarily.

### Surrogate Performance Series  
- **[Surrogate](https://get-lauren.net/Surrogate)** (2022-2024) — 9-month performance where she carried someone else's baby while they monitored and controlled her via app. **Book published by TTTelematic** (2024). Major exhibitions at **[[MU Hybrid Art House]]**, **Mandeville Gallery UCSD**.
- **[Surrogate Performance](https://get-lauren.net/Surrogate-Performance)** — Live performances exploring bodily autonomy and technological mediation of pregnancy.

### Saliva/Bodily Data Works

## Why Interesting

**Triple impact:** Lauren is rare in having massive technical impact (p5.js), prestigious art world recognition (Ars Electronica Golden Nica), and institutional academic platform (UCLA faculty). Most people have one; few have all three.

**Uncomfortable surveillance art:** Her work isn't just critical of surveillance culture — it's participatory and uncomfortable. LAUREN involves real surveillance relationships; SOMEONE creates actual labor relationships around watching. This is embodied critique, not conceptual.

**Human-AI relations:** LAUREN and related works directly anticipate and examine the current moment of AI assistants, smart homes, and human-AI interaction. She was performing as human Alexa years before ChatGPT.

**Infrastructure builder:** p5.js is real infrastructure used by millions. Most critical artists critique existing systems; Lauren builds alternative systems and then critiques through them.

**Institutional positioning:** UCLA faculty position gives her platform to shape next generation of media artists while maintaining critical practice. Rare combination of institutional power and critical edge.

## Research Taste Analysis

**Conceptual Strategy — Participatory Critique:**
Lauren doesn't just represent surveillance or AI relationships — she creates real ones. LAUREN involves actual participants inviting her into their homes as human Alexa. Social Turkers livestreamed real dates. This is methodology: instead of representing power relationships, instantiate them and examine the experience.

**Career Architecture — Infrastructure + Critique:**  
Unique strategy of building widely-adopted technical infrastructure (p5.js) while maintaining critical art practice. The infrastructure gives her credibility and platform; the art work gives her edge and relevance. Most people choose one or the other.

**Methodology Signature — Systems Building:**
Technical background enables her to build complete functional systems rather than mock-ups or prototypes. LAUREN requires custom smart home devices, apps, real-time communication. pplkpr needed actual heart rate monitoring, relationship management algorithms. She can implement her speculations fully.

**Network Strategy — Multi-Institutional:**
Operates simultaneously across art world, tech education, and research institutions. Board position at Processing Foundation gives technical community influence. UCLA faculty position provides academic platform. Exhibition at venues like Ars Electronica and Ford Foundation gives art world legitimacy.

### What Amber Can Learn

**Infrastructure as Art Practice:** Build functional technical systems that serve real communities, then use them as platforms for critical work. p5.js serves millions but also enables Lauren's critical positioning.

**Institutional Multi-Positioning:** Don't choose between academic, art world, or tech communities — operate across all three simultaneously for maximum impact and platform.

**Embodied Critique Over Representation:** Instead of making art about surveillance, create actual surveillance relationships and examine them. Instead of critiquing social apps, build better social apps.

**Technical Depth Enables Conceptual Freedom:** Deep programming skills allow complete implementation of speculative ideas. Don't just prototype — build working systems that people can actually use.

**Education as Platform:** Faculty positions provide stable platform for experimental work while building network of collaborators (students/alumni) and institutional legitimacy.
