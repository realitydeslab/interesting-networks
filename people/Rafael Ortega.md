# Rafael Ortega

Mexican filmmaker and long-term collaborator of [[Francis Alÿs]]. Has co-directed and filmed many of Alÿs's most important action-films and political works over 20+ years. Ortega is the cinematographic eye behind some of contemporary Latin American art's most significant moving image works.

## Background
Based in Mexico City. Works primarily in documentary and art film at the intersection of performance and political action. Long-standing collaboration with [[Francis Alÿs]] and [[Cuauhtémoc Medina]].

## Key Collaborations with Francis Alÿs
- *When Faith Moves Mountains* (2002, Lima, Peru) — co-director/cinematographer. 500 volunteers, one dune, 4 inches.
- *Patriotic Tales* (1997, Mexico City) — co-director/cinematographer. Sheep circling the Zócalo flagpole.
- *Rehearsal I (Tijuana)* (1999–2001) — co-director/cinematographer. VW Beetle repeatedly attempting to climb a hill.
- *Francis Alÿs: Ricochets* (Barbican, 2024) — "A Conversation with Children" (with Alÿs).

## Key Relationships
- **[[Francis Alÿs]]** — 20+ year collaboration; the most sustained cinematographic relationship in Alÿs's practice
- **[[Cuauhtémoc Medina]]** — critical interlocutor; co-appears in the Alÿs network

## Why Interesting
Ortega is the invisible technical collaborator who enables Alÿs's action-films. The question of what happens to the image when the work is primarily the action — how do you film something poetic without making it merely picturesque — is his problem to solve. Worth researching more deeply to understand his independent practice.
