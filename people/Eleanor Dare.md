# Eleanor Dare

Dr Eleanor Dare is the Convenor of the Cambridge Data Schools at the University of Cambridge, Postdoctoral Research Associate at CDH, and former Methods Fellow (2023–24). Artist, researcher, developer, educator.

## Background
- Former Reader in Digital Media and Head of Programme for MA Digital Direction at the **Royal College of Art**
- MSc and PhD in Arts and Computational Technology (specialising in AI and automated literature/artists' books)
- Senior Teaching Associate at Faculty of Education, University of Cambridge (Educational Technologies, Arts and Creativity)
- Co-founded [xordinary Stories](https://xordinarystories.com/) with Professor Dylan Yamada-Rice

## Research Interests
- Critical AI
- Creative coding
- AI and human rights/ethics
- Worldbuilding
- Non-human photography
- Drawing machines
- Games and interactive storytelling
- Digital media arts
- VR content development

Published in MIT Leonardo, ACM Interactions, Routledge, Sage, Intellect Books. AHRC Peer Review College Member.

## Key Projects at CDH
- **Cambridge Data Schools** — Convenor. Designed and delivers Cultural Heritage Data School, Critical AI Data School, Social Data School, Constituent Data School.
- **AI Forensics** — Research project on AI and human rights
- **ESRC Digital Good project**
- **Rear Windows** and **Monstrous** — AI and ethics public engagement
- **Digital and New Media Arts** module — MPhil course at CDH exploring web art, cybernetic serendipity, live coding, AI and generative art
