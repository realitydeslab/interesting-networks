# Tarsh Bates

Artist and researcher at UWA / SymbioticA. Works on human-microbiome relations, multi-species bodies, and biological identities. Collaborated with TC&A on **Crossing Kingdoms** (2018) — the yeast-mammalian cell fusion project exhibited at Edinburgh International Science Festival. Research focuses on the relationship between Candida albicans (common fungal symbiont) and human bodies as a lens for thinking about the boundaries of the self.

## Key Works
- **Crossing Kingdoms** (2018) — collaboration with [[Ionat Zurr]], [[Oron Catts]], and SynthSys scientists at University of Edinburgh. Cross-kingdom cell fusions. Exhibited at Edinburgh International Science Festival.

## Why Interesting
Microbiome art occupies an interesting niche: the body is already a multispecies assemblage; Bates makes that visible. Connects to Donna Haraway's holobiont thinking, STS, and feminist more-than-human scholarship.
