# Paul Seidler

Artist, researcher, and programmer based in Berlin. Co-founder and lead programmer of [[terra0]] (since 2015). Builds smart contracts, zero-knowledge circuits, and token-based protocols as artistic medium — works that investigate value, ownership, and encryption at the level of code rather than image. Situates his practice within the lineage of cypherpunk experimentation, software art, demoscene culture, systems aesthetics, and institutional critique. One of the most technically rigorous artist-programmers working on autonomous non-human systems.

## Background

Seidler graduated from the **[Universität der Künste Berlin (UdK)](https://www.udk-berlin.de)** in 2020, in the class of **Prof. Joachim Sauter** — the founder of ART+COM, one of the pioneering computational and interaction design firms globally, and a foundational figure in German new media art. Sauter's class at UdK was notable for its emphasis on critical engagement with technology as artistic material. Paul Kolling (terra0 co-founder) also studied in the same class (alongside Jussi Ängeslevä), suggesting terra0 partially emerged from this shared educational formation.

Since 2015 — notably before graduating — Seidler has been engaged with blockchain-based technologies as a primary artistic medium. This is significant: terra0 predates the NFT boom by years, situating Seidler within the early Ethereum experimental community rather than the later commercial wave.

His intellectual lineage runs through:
- **Friedrich Kittler** — German media theorist, "the technical a priori," materiality of inscription systems. Kittler's idea that culture is contingent on its technical conditions of mediation is Seidler's philosophical bedrock.
- **The demoscene** — 1980s/90s subculture of real-time computer art on constrained hardware. Seidler invokes this as the most "materialist" practice in computing: rather than using technology as a transparent medium for ideas, demosceners ask "what can I achieve with the hardware in my hands?" This stance directly informs his approach to smart contracts as a self-referential medium.
- **JODI** (Joan Heemskerk + Dirk Paesmans) — net art collective. Alexander Galloway's concept of their practice as "infrastructural modernism" — art that exposes and inhabits the grammar of its infrastructure rather than using it as neutral ground — is directly cited by Seidler as a model.
- **Cypherpunk tradition** — the pre-Bitcoin movement using cryptography for political ends (anonymous remailers, DigiCash, PGP). Seidler explicitly places terra0 within this genealogy.
- **Anna Greenspan** — philosopher of capitalism and time. Her PhD thesis *Capitalism's Transcendental Time Machine* (on Kantian time + capitalist temporality) was the intellectual center of Seidler and Max Hampshire's "Temporal Secessionism" research, which explored blockchains as a new temporal regime.

## Key Works

### terra0 Projects (collective, as lead programmer)
- **[terra0 white paper](https://terra0.org/assets/pdf/terra0_white_paper_2016.pdf)** (2016) — the founding document. Can an augmented forest own and utilize itself? Proposed a self-utilizing forest that sells logging licenses via smart contracts and accumulates capital. The conceptual seed for everything that followed.
- **[Flowertokens](https://flowertokens.terra0.org)** (2018) — early experiment in tokenization of natural commodities. Combined crypto-collectible with physical asset. First shown at Trust Berlin.
- **[Premna Daemon](https://medium.com/@terra0/premna-daemon-an-introduction-via-a-history-of-autonomy-in-the-cryptosphere-3cee15e92fe2)** (2018) — augmented Premna Microphylla (bonsai) given autonomous economic agency. Developed for *Proof of Work* at Schinkel Pavillon, curated by Simon Denny. Comes with an important essay tracing cypherpunk history of autonomous systems.
- **[Two Degrees](https://terra0.medium.com/two-degrees-nft-5af1263bfcc)** (2021) — NFT of a German forest LiDAR scan. Oracle smart contract checks NASA global temperature data. If temperature exceeds 2°C above average, anyone can call the burn function and delete the token. Art staked on climate outcomes; uses DeFi oracle logic as ecological contingency.
- **[Seed Capital](https://terra0.medium.com/wen-growth-an-introduction-to-seed-capital-8d4a9df93789)** (2022) — sculpture rethinking economic and ecological relationships between viewers, institution, and artwork. Art Dubai 2022.
- **[A tree; a corporation; a person](https://www.fastcompany.com/90738287/this-tree-owns-itself-and-is-fighting-for-its-own-survival)** (2022, permanent) — a tree incorporated as a legal US entity, installed as permanent sculpture at Carnegie Museum of Art, Pittsburgh. Tests limits of non-human legal personhood in practice (not just theory).
- **[Conditional Power](https://conditionalpower.terra0.org)** (2024–ongoing) — series connecting global environmental changes to art market dynamics.
- **[Autonomous Forest](https://autonomousforest.org)** (2025–) — terra0's culminating work. A real German forest owned by a Verein (legal non-profit) + Ethereum DAO. NFT holders (0.08–0.1 ETH) vote in forest governance. Custom geohash grid maps physical land to blockchain tokens. Not a simulation — actual land, actual governance.
- **World Computer Sculpture Garden** (Nov 2024, on Ethereum, permanent) — on-chain sculpture garden.

### Solo/Independent Works
- **Temporal Secessionism: Timezones 1–3** (2021, with Max Hampshire) — sculptural objects proposing new timezones based on techno-social consensus processes. Shown at 34th Ljubljana Biennale of Graphic Arts ("ISKRA DELTA") and 7th Athens Biennale ("ECLIPSE").
- **Timezone 4** (2021) — digital-only work, part of Projekt Atol's Plaza Protocol, Ljubljana.
- **[Smol Dollar](https://smol.dollar.supply/)** (2024) — NFT with autonomous price floor mechanism. Mint price partially goes to a reserve held in STETH (a yield-bearing ETH derivative) — the reserve grows via staking rewards, and secondary sale fees are added automatically. Smart contract acts like a sovereign price floor, not unlike a central bank's reserve operations. Solo work.

## Intellectual Connections: Key Concepts

### 1. Technical Materiality (Kittler)
Seidler's core epistemological move: cultural and aesthetic possibilities are determined by the technical infrastructure that produces them. A smart contract's aesthetic possibilities are determined by Solidity's data structures, the EVM's gas model, and Ethereum's consensus mechanism — not by the artist's "intentions." The art is in the code, not in the image the code points to.

### 2. Infrastructural Modernism (Galloway via JODI)
From Galloway's essay on JODI: art that exposes, inhabits, and is formally determined by its technical infrastructure. Seidler extends this to blockchain: an NFT artwork that doesn't write its own smart contract is, in this view, not really engaging with its medium at all.

### 3. Demoscene as Precedent
The 1980s demoscene — making real-time art on constrained hardware (Atari 800, C64) — is Seidler's historical model for technically serious blockchain art. The demoscene asked "what can this specific machine do?" rather than "what technology would I need for my concept?" This anti-Platonism about technology is fundamental.

### 4. The Autonomy Paradox (terra0)
To escape the property regime, a non-human entity must first participate in it. The self-utilizing forest must sell logging rights to accumulate capital to buy its own autonomy. This is not a failure of radicalism — it's the deliberate exposure of how market logic constrains all available paths to autonomy.

### 5. Consensus as Temporal Regime
Blockchains as novel timekeeping systems — not just financial infrastructure. Consensus time (ordinal, distributed, non-spatialised) vs. Unix/NTP time (cardinal, hierarchical, GMT-anchored). The blockchain doesn't just record transactions; it produces time.

### 6. Commodity Form and NFTs
His 2025 essay in *Navigationen* is the most rigorous academic treatment of NFTs as commodity form: deriving their market dynamics from ERC-721's technical specifications, then reading them through Marx, Graw, and Beech. Result: artworks that are produced as smart contracts are historically singular in that the commodity form is not superimposed on the artwork but constitutive of it.

## Why Interesting

Seidler sits at the intersection of three of Amber's core research interests:

**More-than-human governance** — terra0's Autonomous Forest is the most sophisticated existing implementation of a non-human governance system: real land, real DAO, real legal structure. This is ALife-as-governance in practice, not theory.

**Machine behavior** — the question terra0 keeps asking is fundamentally behavioral: what does an autonomous non-human system *do* when given economic agency? How does a smart-contract-governed forest *behave* in a market? These are agent behavior questions.

**Decentralized AI / sovereign agents** — Seidler's long engagement with cypherpunk history, Ethereum DAOs, and the Premna Daemon tradition is directly relevant to Amber's thinking on sovereign agents. terra0 is the art-side of the same conversation about non-human agents with economic and legal agency.

Beyond the intellectual alignment, Seidler is one of the rare artist-programmers who writes at the level of both technical rigor (he writes the actual smart contracts and ZK circuits) and cultural/philosophical sophistication (he engages Marx, Kittler, Greenspan, Galloway seriously). This combination is exactly what Amber's research area needs — practitioners who can do both.

## Research Taste Analysis (⭐⭐⭐)

### Conceptual Strategy

**Technical a priori as generative constraint**: Seidler's key conceptual move is Kittlerian — the technical medium doesn't just carry the message, it determines what messages are possible. His "On Smart Contracts as a medium" series systematically derives artistic possibilities from EVM specifications, gas costs, and consensus mechanisms. This is the opposite of "I have an idea, what technology would realize it?" — it's "what ideas does this technology make possible?" This is productively constraining: it means Seidler's work is always legible as *about* its medium, not just *using* it.

**Provocation-first then infrastructure**: The terra0 whitepaper (2016) was a provocation. "Can a forest own itself?" Absurdist question. But then: 9 years of systematic implementation, culminating in a real DAO-governed forest (2025). The provocation buys time and attention; the technical execution earns respect.

### Career Architecture

**Collective identity as institutional buffer**: By routing institutional relationships through terra0 (rather than as "Paul Seidler solo"), Seidler gains a kind of institutional continuity and shared identity that individual artists can't sustain alone. When Paul Kolling wins ars viva and gets a Kunsthalle München show, terra0's institutional reputation benefits. When Seidler writes the smart contracts for Carnegie International, terra0 gains credibility. The collective functions as a holding company for distributed capabilities.

**Staying in the background**: Unlike some blockchain artists who have become celebrities (Beeple, Kevin Abosch), Seidler operates without personal brand inflation. His Twitter/X is @plsdlr — minimal persona. This is a deliberate choice that keeps the work primary and the person secondary, which is the opposite of crypto influencer culture. Ironically, it builds more durable credibility.

**Theory-practice integration**: His 2025 essay "Artwork, Market and Commodity Form" was published in an academic media studies journal (Universität Siegen's Navigationen) — not an art magazine. This is unusual for an artist; it signals a deliberate investment in academic humanities reception, not just art world reception. This dual positioning (art world + academic humanities) is valuable and hard to achieve.

### Key Moves (Chronological)

1. **terra0 whitepaper (2016) — before Ethereum was mainstream**: Starting a blockchain-based art project before the NFT boom (2021) and even before most artists had heard of Ethereum placed Seidler in a position of pre-existing credibility when the hype arrived. He didn't need to ride the wave — the wave came to him.

2. **Proof of Work (2018) with Simon Denny**: Getting curated by one of the most important figures in blockchain art (and a rigorous institutional critic) was a crucial legitimating moment. Denny's curatorial framing placed terra0 squarely in the tradition of institutional critique, not crypto boosterism.

3. **Temporal Secessionism (2019) — expanding from ecology to time**: Moving from "forests and blockchains" to "blockchains as temporal regimes" showed Seidler's intellectual range. The collaboration with Max Hampshire and the Urbit reading group placed him in a more speculative/philosophical register.

4. **Two Degrees (2021) + Sotheby's Natively Digital**: Getting terra0's work into Sotheby's first major NFT sale (before the crash) was an institutional endorsement that came without compromising the critical position. The work (burning itself if temperatures exceed 2°C) was the most rigorous piece in the sale.

5. **Carnegie International (2022) — permanent sculpture**: "A tree; a corporation; a person" becoming a permanent public work at a major American museum sealed terra0's status as serious institutional art, not blockchain-art-world work. Carnegie is not Sotheby's — different legitimacy entirely.

6. **Academic paper in Navigationen (2025)**: The shift to academic humanities publication is the latest move — positioning terra0's intellectual framework within media studies / cultural theory, where it can be taught and cited as scholarship, not just exhibited as art.

### What Amber Can Learn

1. **Start the project before you have institutional support**: terra0 started in 2015, exhibited at Trust Berlin and small venues, built the conceptual framework for years before arriving at Carnegie International (2022) and La Biennale di Venezia (2021). Amber doesn't need to wait for Oxford to legitimate her more-than-human governance ideas — start building the infrastructure now.

2. **Write the theory in parallel**: Seidler's "On Smart Contracts as a medium" trilogy and the 2025 Navigationen essay create a theoretical framework that outlasts any single artwork. Writing essays that theorize your practice, published in different venues (Mirror.xyz + academic journal + exhibition catalogue), builds cumulative intellectual authority. Amber should do the same for agent ethology and machine behavior.

3. **Collective + solo dual track is sustainable**: The collective (terra0) handles institutional relationships and large projects; the solo practice (plsdlr.net) handles theoretical writing and smaller experiments. This division of labor is worth adopting.

4. **Choose contexts by intellectual fit, not prestige**: The small-venue choices (Maribor, Osnabrück, NAVEL LA, Befriending Hyperobjects) signal that Seidler selects based on thematic fit, not venue rankings. These choices build depth and credibility with the intellectually serious part of the audience that matters most for long-term influence.
