# Liam Gillick

British artist, writer, and theorist based in New York and London. One of the central figures in the relational aesthetics and post-conceptual art generation of the 1990s. Works with text, architecture, modular structures (colorful screens, platforms, industrial materials), and social relations as artistic medium. His work critically examines how social, economic, and political systems are organized and visualized. Part of the Goldsmiths YBA generation (studied 1984–87, overlapping with [[Angela Bulloch]]). Collaborated with [[Angela Bulloch]] on *We Are Medi(eval)* at Cubitt Artists, London (2006). Nominated for the Turner Prize 2002.

Professor at Columbia University (MFA programme). Major retrospectives at mumok Vienna, Witte de With Rotterdam, Kunsthalle Zurich.
