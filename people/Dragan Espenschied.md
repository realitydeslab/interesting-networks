# Dragan Espenschied

German-American digital art preservationist and artist. Leads the Digital Art Conservation programme at [[Rhizome]] (since ~2014). Specialist in software emulation, web archiving, and digital preservation. Co-developer of Colloq (with Ilya Kreymer). Key figure in Software Preservation Network. Previously member of early German internet art collective BETATEST.

## Background
- Part of BETATEST — early German internet art collective/community
- Joined Rhizome as Digital Conservator / Preservation lead (~2014)
- Co-developed Colloq social media archiving tool with Ilya Kreymer
- Leads Rhizome's approach to emulation as a preservation strategy

## Key Work
- **Colloq** (2014): Social media archiving tool; replicates visual interface at time of capture; first used for Amalia Ulman's "Excellences and Perfections" Instagram project
- Digital preservation methodology work cited in library/archive contexts
- Speaks at archives/preservation conferences internationally

## Why Interesting
Espenschied is one of the few people who has thought deeply about what it means to preserve interactive, software-dependent art. His methodology (emulation over migration; symmetrical web capture; interface fidelity) has broader implications for anyone working with software-based creative work. Relevant to Amber when thinking about how to document and preserve AI/agent-based artworks — what survives, what disappears, what can be replayed.
