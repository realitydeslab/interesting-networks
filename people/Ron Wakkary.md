# Ron Wakkary

Full professor of Interaction Design at TU Eindhoven's Future Everyday cluster and full professor at Simon Fraser University's School of Interactive Arts and Technology (SIAT) in Canada. Founder of the **Everyday Design Studio** at SFU. Pioneer of **more-than-human design** — a framework that takes seriously the agency of non-human entities (tools, environments, materials, plants, animals) in design processes. Author of *Things We Could Design: For More Than Human-Centered Worlds* (MIT Press, 2021). One of the most influential voices pushing HCI and interaction design beyond anthropocentrism. Co-chair of ACM [[ACM DIS|DIS]] Steering Committee. Former co-Editor-in-Chief of *ACM interactions* magazine (2010–2017).

## Background
Ron Wakkary's trajectory is unusual and revealing: he started as a visual artist, not a computer scientist. His BFA from NSCAD (Nova Scotia College of Art and Design) — one of Canada's most conceptually rigorous art schools — and MFA from SUNY ground him in making, craft, and critical visual practice. His PhD in Human-Computer Interaction at the University of Plymouth then brought him into computing, but always with an artist's sensibility for the materiality and lived experience of technology.

This art-to-HCI trajectory is the key to understanding his work. While most HCI researchers start from computation and try to add "the human," Wakkary started from making and asks: what happens when we stop centering the human entirely? The result is a body of work that treats designed things not as tools-for-humans but as participants in a shared world — entities that co-create meaning alongside humans, animals, plants, and materials.

He founded the **[Everyday Design Studio (EDS)](http://eds.siat.sfu.ca/)** at SFU's SIAT, which has been his primary research home. The studio's ethos is explicitly collaborative and design-through-making: computational things are designed and deployed in the world to generate questions, not answer them.

His dual appointment at TU Eindhoven (since ~2018) positions him at the heart of European design research — the Future Everyday cluster in Industrial Design is one of the strongest concentrations of more-than-human and posthuman design researchers in the world, alongside colleagues like [[Kristina Andersen]], [[Doenja Oogjes]], [[Oscar Tomico]], and [[Daisy Yoo]].

His signature quote captures his ethos: *"Often, design research draws on design's capacity to solve problems. However, using design to create problems, rather than solutions, may be the stranger but wiser choice."*

## Domains

- **Philosophy of technology through design** — using design practice to engage with Heidegger, postphenomenology, posthumanism
- **Research through design (RtD)** — design artifacts as a mode of inquiry and knowledge generation
- **Tangible and embodied interaction** — physical-digital computing, material practice
- **Everyday design** — how people improvise, appropriate, and redesign the computational things in their lives
- **Unmaking and sustainability** — designing for biodegradation, decay, and disassembly

## Key Works

### Book
- [*Things We Could Design: For More Than Human-Centered Worlds*](https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262542876/things-we-could-design/) (MIT Press, 2021) — The foundational text of more-than-human design. Argues that design must move beyond human-centered frameworks to account for the agency of things, materials, and environments. Draws on postphenomenology and philosophy of technology to reimagine what design is *for*. Winner of the **Kyoto Design Award 2021** for design theory.

## Venue Circuit Analysis
Wakkary operates across three interconnected circuits:

1. **ACM HCI Circuit (primary home):** CHI, DIS, CSCW, TEI, C&C. His 113+ DBLP publications are overwhelmingly in ACM venues. DIS is his spiritual home — he co-chairs its steering committee and has shaped its direction for 15+ years. CHI is his reach venue — where he brings more-than-human thinking to the broader HCI community.

2. **Scandinavian/European Design Research:** NordiCHI, Aarhus Conference, RTD, DRS. These venues share his values of participatory design, democracy, and critical engagement with technology. The Aarhus tradition of computing-and-democracy since 1975 aligns perfectly with his decentering agenda.

3. **Design Theory/Journals:** ACM ToCHI, International Journal of Design, CoDesign, Archives of Design Research, Temes de Disseny. His editorial board memberships across multiple journals signal his field-shaping ambition — he's not just publishing in these venues, he's deciding what gets published.

**Pattern:** Wakkary never appears in purely engineering or industry venues. Every venue he chooses has a critical, humanistic, or design-theoretical orientation. He doesn't chase impact factor — he builds the venues that matter. DIS's current identity as the leading critical design research venue is significantly shaped by his 15+ years of leadership.

## Research Taste Analysis

### Conceptual Strategy
- **Field declaration:** Wakkary didn't discover more-than-human design — he *declared* it. The 2021 MIT Press book is essentially a manifesto: here is what this field is, here are its foundations, here is where it goes. This is the "zoom out from results → declare a discipline" strategy at its most effective.
- **Framework multiplication:** More-than-human × everyday design × postphenomenology = a unique intellectual position. By combining philosophy of technology (Verbeek, Ihde) with domestic design practice and posthumanism, he created a space no one else occupied.
- **Two-word concept:** "More-than-human design" as a phrase is perfectly engineered — it positions itself as an expansion of "human-centered design," implying that HCD is insufficient without explicitly attacking it. Diplomatic radicalism.
- **Provocation-first:** "Designing problems, not solutions" — the signature quote is itself a provocation that forces engagement.

### Career Architecture
- **Strategic dual appointment:** SFU (Canada) for the established lab, students, and North American network. TU Eindhoven (Netherlands) for the European design research ecosystem and critical mass of like-minded researchers. Two continents, double the institutional leverage.
- **Platform building (DIS):** Co-chairing the DIS Steering Committee = controlling the premier venue for critical design research. 15+ years of shaping what gets published, who keynotes, what topics are centered. This isn't just leadership — it's infrastructure.
- **Journal gatekeeping:** Co-Editor-in-Chief of *ACM interactions* (2010–2017) + editorial boards of ToCHI, IJD, ADR, JHTR, TdD. Six journal positions = significant influence over what counts as "good" design research.
- **The book as capstone:** *Things We Could Design* (MIT Press, 2021) arrived after 20+ years of empirical work. By the time the book came out, the community was already there to receive it. Timing is everything.

### Methodology Signature
- **Design-through-making as inquiry:** Every project starts with making a thing — Morse Things, plant studios, compost kits, backyard ecologies — and the theoretical insights emerge from living with those things over time. This is research through design at its most rigorous.
- **First-person, long-duration:** Many projects involve living with designed things for months or years, observing how they change, accumulate meaning, and develop "character." Not surveys, not experiments — slow observation.
- **Conceptual provocations as artifacts:** Things like "Morse Things" (objects that communicate in inscrutable ways) are designed to create conceptual disturbances, not solve user problems. The artifact IS the argument.

### Network Strategy
- **Coalition manifestos:** The Aarhus 2025 paper "The Future of More-Than-Human Design" has 19 co-authors — Barendregt, Bekker, Berger, Dalsgaard, Eriksson, Frauenberger, Friedman, Giaccardi, Hansen, Jensen, Light, Lindley, Nicenboim, Nilsson, Redström, Sontopski, Wiberg, Yoo. This is a territorial claim: "these 19 people are the field."
- **Mentorship as multiplication:** Students from the Everyday Design Studio (Doenja Oogjes, Tiffany Wun, Lauren Thu, Nazmus Sakib) extend his framework into new domains. Each PhD becomes another node in the network.
- **The TU/e cluster:** By establishing a critical mass at Eindhoven (Andersen, Oogjes, Tomico, Yoo + himself), Wakkary created a gravitational center for more-than-human design in Europe. Researchers come to TU/e because the cluster is there.
