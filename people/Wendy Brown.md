# Wendy Brown

Political theorist at UC Berkeley. One of the most important theorists of **neoliberalism as a political rationality** — not just an economic policy but a mode of governing that colonizes all spheres of life with market logic. Author of *Undoing the Demos: Neoliberalism's Stealth Revolution* (Zone Books, 2015) — seminal analysis of how neoliberalism hollows out democratic citizenship by turning every person and institution into human capital. Also wrote *In the Ruins of Neoliberalism* (Columbia UP, 2019) on how neoliberalism gives rise to right-wing authoritarianism.

Featured in [[Astra Taylor]]'s *What Is Democracy?* (2018) — one of the key intellectual voices in the film.

## Key Works (Selected)
- [*Undoing the Demos: Neoliberalism's Stealth Revolution*](https://www.zonebooks.org/books/1-undoing-the-demos) (Zone Books, 2015) — thesis: neoliberalism doesn't just deregulate markets, it transforms citizenship itself into human capital management, killing the political subject democracy requires
- [*In the Ruins of Neoliberalism: The Rise of Antidemocratic Politics in the West*](https://cup.columbia.edu/book/in-the-ruins-of-neoliberalism/9780231193856) (Columbia UP, 2019) — how neoliberalism creates the conditions for fascism
- *States of Injury: Power and Freedom in Late Modernity* (Princeton UP, 1995)

## Why Interesting
Her analysis of neoliberalism as a **political rationality** (not just economic policy) is directly relevant to critical computing: platforms and algorithms operate as exactly the kind of human-capital-optimization machines Brown describes. *Undoing the Demos* is the theoretical foundation for many HCI/STS critiques of tech platforms.
