# Kader Attia

Franco-Algerian visual artist. One of the most important artists working on the concept of **repair** (*réparation*) — both as formal aesthetic practice and as political/philosophical concept. His work meditates on colonial wounds, restitution, and the way cultures and bodies survive destruction by being transformed through repair rather than restored to an original state. Participated in [Les Ateliers de la Pensée de Dakar](https://ateliersdlapensee.com/) 2019 edition. Founded **La Colonie** (Paris, 2016–2020), a cultural space dedicated to decolonial thinking and practice.

## Key Works & Ideas
- **Repair**: the central concept — repair as difference, not restoration to an original. A repaired vase is not the same vase; repair produces something new from destruction. This has profound implications for thinking colonialism's aftermath.
- **Ghost (2007)** — sculpture of crouching figures made of aluminum foil; comments on absence, trauma, invisibility.
- **Open Your Eyes (2010)** — monumental photo installation comparing colonial-era "orthopedic" surgery on wounded soldiers (WWI) with West African art's negotiation of bodily damage.
- **The Repair from Occident to Extra-Occidental Cultures (2012)** — major conceptual installation at dOCUMENTA(13).
- **La Colonie (2016–2020)** — Paris cultural space he founded; interdisciplinary space for decolonial art, philosophy, and practice.

## Why Interesting
His concept of **repair** is directly relevant to:
- AI ethics discussions on repairing colonial damage through technology (not restoring but transforming)
- Heritage restitution debates (connected to Sarr-Savoy Report)
- Machine behavior and care ethics — what does "repair" mean when machines are agents?
