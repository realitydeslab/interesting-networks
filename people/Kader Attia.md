# Kader Attia

Franco-Algerian artist whose work is organized around the concept of **repair** (*réparation*) — both as a formal aesthetic practice and as a political/philosophical framework for understanding colonialism's aftermath. One of the most conceptually rigorous decolonial artists working today. Founded [[La Colonie]] (Paris, 2016–2020), a key space for decolonial thought and practice. Curated the 12th Berlin Biennale (2022). Works are in permanent collections at MoMA, Tate, Centre Pompidou, Guggenheim, and ICA Boston.

## Background
Born December 30, 1970 in **Dugny**, a working-class commune in Seine-Saint-Denis (the banlieues north of Paris). His parents were Algerian. He grew up between **Paris and Algeria** — the experience of inhabiting the margins of both, belonging fully to neither, is foundational to his work.

Trained in applied arts at the **[École Duperré](https://www.duperre.org/)** in Paris, then at the **[École des Arts Appliqués La Massana](https://www.lamassana.cat/)** in Barcelona, before graduating from the prestigious **[École Nationale Supérieure des Arts Décoratifs (ENSAD)](https://www.ensad.fr/)** in Paris in 1998. His training in applied/decorative arts — not fine arts — has shaped his investment in craft, material culture, and objects as carriers of meaning.

After school he spent time in **Congo (DRC)** and in the Paris banlieues — both experiences feeding his investigation of colonial injury and survival. His identity as a Maghrebi Frenchman, and his lived proximity to postcolonial communities both in France and in Africa, grounds his theoretical work in lived experience rather than academic distance.

He lives and works between **Paris and Berlin**.

## Domains
- **Repair as philosophy:** the concept of repair (*réparation*) as central political and aesthetic paradigm — repair that transforms rather than restores
- **Postcolonial / decolonial aesthetics:** colonial wound, cultural destruction, restitution, memory, trauma
- **Cross-cultural material culture:** African masks, indigenous artifacts, modernist architecture, medical prosthetics read together
- **Installation and sculpture:** physical, spatial art that envelops the viewer in historical argument
- **Film and photography:** documentary and essayistic moving image; oral history practices
- **Institutional practice:** founded La Colonie (Paris); curated Berlin Biennale 12; participates in major biennials

## Key Works
- **[La Piste d'Atterrissage (The Landing Strip)](https://www.kaderattia.de/)** (2000) — photographic series of Algerian transgender sex workers in Paris's banlieues in the late 1990s. First major work. Combines documentary tenderness with critique of French postcolonial marginalisation.
- **[Oil and Sugar #2](https://www.icaboston.org/art/kader-attia/oil-and-sugar-2/)** (2007) — video: black oil dissolves a mountain of sugar cubes. Modernist architecture (white cube), the Kaaba, global trade, colonial extraction — all converge. In the ICA Boston permanent collection.
- **[Ghost](https://www.centrepompidou.fr/id/c4rbrjA/rngAML/fr)** (2007) — sculpture of crouching figures made from aluminum foil. Haunting presences of colonial subjects; absence and trauma as form. In Centre Pompidou's permanent collection.
- **[Untitled (Ghardaïa)](https://www.tate.org.uk/art/artworks/attia-untitled-ghardaia-t13179)** (2009) — cooked couscous and photocopies of portraits of Fernand Pouillon and Le Corbusier. Architecture, colonial urbanism, North Africa. In Tate's permanent collection.
- **[Open Your Eyes](https://www.moma.org/collection/works/156765)** (2010) — monumental photo installation juxtaposing colonial-era photographs of WWI soldiers' facial reconstruction surgery with African tribal masks bearing intentional facial scarring. Shows how the West treats repair as erasure while non-Western cultures value the trace. In MoMA's permanent collection.
- **[The Repair from Occident to Extra-Occidental Cultures](https://www.thegreenbox.net/en/books/repair-occident-extra-occidental-cultures)** (2012) — major installation at **dOCUMENTA(13)** (Kassel). Full-room archive on industrial shelves: photographs of WWI facial surgery alongside African masks with visible repair, vintage books (including Lévi-Strauss's *La Pensée Sauvage*). A museum display about museum displays.
- **Reason's Oxymorons** (2015) — 18 films in cubicles. Explores psychiatric history, colonialism, and the Enlightenment's dark side.
- **Mirrors and Masks** (2013–2023) — ongoing series: wooden African masks with mirrors, pigment, glue. Reflectivity as doubling, identity, the colonial gaze.
- **Shifting Borders** (2018) — video triptych with oral histories of postcolonial trauma: 1980 Gwangju Uprising (South Korea), and other events. Prosthetic legs among the screens as material scars.
- **Some Modernity's Footprints** (2018) — railway ties and metal staples. Colonial infrastructure as wound.
- **On Silence** (2021) — vintage prostheses, cables, dimensions variable. At Mathaf, Doha.
- **[Untitled (Big Bang)](https://www.lehmannmaupin.com/artists/kader-attia)** (2024) — mirror-polished stainless steel sculpture, 176 × 176 × 14 cm.

### Key Publication
- *[The Repair from Occident to Extra-Occidental Cultures](https://www.thegreenbox.net/en/books/repair-occident-extra-occidental-cultures)* (The Green Box Kunstedition, 2013) — the book-length companion to his major installation; essential reading on repair as concept.
- *[The White West: Fascism, Unreason, and the Paradox of Modernity](https://mitpress.mit.edu/9783956795336/the-white-west/)* (MIT Press, 2024) — edited anthology he compiled, examining Western fascism and colonial modernity. Major theoretical intervention.

## Venue Circuit Analysis (⭐⭐⭐)
Attia moves in the **European institutional art circuit** at its highest level:

1. **Tier-1 European institutions**: Hayward Gallery (London), Centre Pompidou (Paris), Tate (London), Hamburger Bahnhof (Berlin), Mathaf (Doha). He's deeply embedded in Western institutional infrastructure while critiquing it from within.

2. **The biennial circuit**: dOCUMENTA (Kassel), Venice Biennale, Berlin Biennale (which he curated). These are the key legitimating venues for international contemporary art. He's moved from participant to curator — a significant institutional promotion.

3. **North American museum circuit**: MoMA (permanent collection), Guggenheim (exhibition), ICA Boston (permanent collection), Lehmann Maupin (New York gallery). Strong US footprint despite being Europe-based.

4. **Global South adjacency**: Sharjah Art Foundation, Mathaf Doha, Les Ateliers de la Pensée de Dakar. Not just tokenistically — these are substantive relationships.

5. **Self-created infrastructure**: La Colonie (Paris, 2016–2020) — shows his commitment to institution-building outside established channels.

**Pattern:** He operates simultaneously inside and against Western art institutions. The Prix Marcel Duchamp gave him institutional legitimacy; he then used that legitimacy to open La Colonie and curate the Berlin Biennale — turning establishment recognition into decolonial platform-building.

## Why Interesting
Attia's **repair** concept is genuinely relevant to Amber's research interests in several ways:
- **Machine behavior / more-than-human design:** What does repair mean when the agent doing the repairing is non-human? Machines that "fix" things transform them — they don't restore originals. This is Attia's central insight applied to AI systems.
- **Care ethics:** Repair is inherently a care practice. The prosthetics, the scarring, the stitched mirrors — all of these are about care that acknowledges damage rather than hiding it.
- **Decolonial computing:** Attia's interrogation of Western modernity and its relationship to non-Western knowledge systems speaks directly to debates about AI training data, algorithmic bias, and whose knowledge gets encoded.
- **La Colonie as model:** A decolonial cultural infrastructure that was artist-founded, interdisciplinary, and community-oriented. A model for what alternative knowledge institutions can look like.

From research-taste.md: Attia sits squarely in the **non-Western + Western perspectives bridged** and **creative practice asking philosophical questions** categories. He's also deeply invested in the colonial wounds that shape contemporary machine/body/city relationships.

## Research Taste Analysis (⭐⭐⭐)

### Conceptual Strategy
**Framework duplication across domains:** Attia's core move is to hold two things together that seem disparate and reveal the hidden similarity. WWI facial surgery + African tribal masks = Western repair erases trace / non-Western repair celebrates scar. Sugar cubes dissolving in oil = modernist white cube + colonial extraction + Kaaba. This is not juxtaposition for shock — it's a rigorous epistemological argument that works through visual thinking.

**Concept coining:** *Réparation* as a concept is doing enormous philosophical work in his oeuvre. By staying with one word across decades of work, Attia builds conceptual depth — the concept accumulates meaning as each new work adds a layer. This is unusual disciplinary patience.

### Career Architecture
**Field declaration via curatorial power:** Curating the 12th Berlin Biennale is a declaration: "I am not just an artist responding to colonialism — I am an intellectual shaping how the art world thinks about it." Moving from artist to curator is the field-declaration move.

**Institution founding:** La Colonie was a territorial claim on Paris's intellectual scene. It said: "The conversation about decolonial thought in Paris will happen here, in a textile factory near Gare du Nord, not in a white-cube gallery in the Marais." The location was the argument.

**Strategic timing:** Prix Marcel Duchamp (2016) → La Colonie founding (2016) → Joan Miró Prize (2018) → Berlin Biennale curator (2022). Each step built on the previous. The prizes didn't change his practice; they gave him the platform to scale his politics.

### Methodology Signature
**Archive-as-artwork:** His major installations are functioning archives. "The Repair from Occident to Extra-Occidental Cultures" looks like a museum storage room. This is a critique embedded in the form: the same violent act of collecting, classifying, and shelving non-Western culture that produced colonial museums is re-enacted — but now directed by a postcolonial subject. The archive IS the critique.

**Oral history as form:** "Shifting Borders," "The Body's Legacies" — he conducts interviews with survivors, academics, healers, and presents the footage alongside objects. This is ethnographic methodology imported into contemporary art practice.

### Key Moves (chronological)
1. **2000 — La Piste d'Atterrissage:** Documenting marginalized Algerian trans women in Paris. Establishes his method: close ethnographic attention to those on the edge of colonial society.
2. **2007 — Ghost:** Monumental aluminum foil sculpture. Establishes his scale and material ambition. Centre Pompidou notices.
3. **2012 — documenta 13:** "The Repair from Occident to Extra-Occidental Cultures" — the work that names his project and announces his thesis. The international art world pays attention.
4. **2013 — Book publication:** *The Repair from Occident to Extra-Occidental Cultures* (Green Box). Solidifies the conceptual claim as text, not just image.
5. **2016 — Prix Marcel Duchamp + La Colonie:** Uses the prize's credibility to open a space. Simultaneous institutional recognition + grassroots institution-building.
6. **2019 — Hayward retrospective:** Career-defining survey in a major London institution. NYT profile. European media art mainstream.
7. **2022 — Berlin Biennale curatorial role:** The full transformation from artist to field shaper.
8. **2024 — MIT Press book:** *The White West* — edited volume. Now operating as a theoretical editor, not just practitioner.

### What Amber Can Learn
- **Stay with one concept for decades.** Attia has spent 25+ years on *repair*. The depth this produces is only possible through sustained commitment. Amber's equivalents: machine death, agent ethology. Don't dilute them.
- **The institution you build IS the argument.** La Colonie was located near Gare du Nord deliberately — that's where Algerian migrant workers were historically concentrated. The venue made the conceptual claim before any artist showed work there.
- **Win institutional prizes, then use them to make your own institutions.** The Prix Marcel Duchamp gave Attia credibility; he used it to open La Colonie. Reality Design Lab is Amber's La Colonie — the prize would help sustain it.
- **Archive-as-critique is a powerful form.** Amber's research into agent behavior could take the form of installations that look like scientific archives but are actually critical arguments about what gets measured, what gets ignored.
