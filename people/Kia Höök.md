## Background
Professor and researcher at KTH Royal Institute of Technology, Stockholm, where she leads groundbreaking work in somaesthetic interaction design. [Need to research: educational background, career trajectory, origin story of interest in embodied computing].

Höök's work represents a fundamental shift from traditional HCI's cognitive focus to a body-centered approach that acknowledges the felt, sensory, and emotional dimensions of human-computer interaction. Her theoretical foundation draws from Richard Shusterman's somaesthetics - the philosophical study of bodily experience as a locus of knowledge.
