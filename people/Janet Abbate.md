# Janet Abbate

Historian of computing and the internet at Virginia Tech's Science and Technology Studies program. Pioneer of feminist history of computing.

## Domains

- History of the internet and networking
- Gender, women, and computing history
- Feminist science and technology studies

## Key Works

- *[Inventing the Internet](https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262511117/inventing-the-internet/)* (MIT Press, 1999) — definitive early history of the internet; how military and academic networks became the public internet
- *[Recoding Gender: Women's Changing Participation in Computing](https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262534536/recoding-gender/)* (MIT Press, 2012) — feminist history of women in computing from early human computers to the present
- *[Abstractions and Embodiments: New Histories of Computing and Society](https://www.press.jhu.edu/books/title/12637/abstractions-and-embodiments)* (JHU Press, 2022) — co-edited with [[Stephanie Dick]]

## Key Relationships

- [[Stephanie Dick]] — co-editor, *Abstractions and Embodiments* (JHU Press, 2022)

## Why Interesting

One of the founding figures of feminist computing history. *Recoding Gender* is required reading for understanding how gender shaped the computing profession. Her collaboration with Stephanie Dick on *Abstractions and Embodiments* extends this tradition to a new generation of critical computing historians.
