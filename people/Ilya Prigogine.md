# Ilya Prigogine

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Stub created during Isabelle Stengers deep research.

Russian-Belgian physical chemist and Nobel laureate (Chemistry, 1977). Spent most of his career at the [Université libre de Bruxelles (ULB)](https://www.ulb.be/) and the [University of Texas at Austin](https://www.utexas.edu/). His work on **dissipative structures** — complex systems far from thermodynamic equilibrium that spontaneously self-organize — transformed how we understand complexity, time, and emergence. The philosophical implications of this work were developed by his collaborator [[Isabelle Stengers]] in *Order Out of Chaos* (1984) and subsequent works. Prigogine's insight: **irreversibility and complexity are not exceptions to physical law — they are the rule**. Classical determinism (Newton, Laplace) described only limiting, artificial cases.

## Domains
- Non-equilibrium thermodynamics
- Dissipative structures (self-organization far from equilibrium)
- Irreversibility and the arrow of time
- Complexity science and emergence
- Philosophy of time in physics

## Key Works
- *La Nouvelle Alliance* (with [[Isabelle Stengers]], Gallimard, 1979) — French original of *Order Out of Chaos*
- *Order Out of Chaos: Man's New Dialogue with Nature* (with Stengers, Bantam, 1984) — major international impact
- *The End of Certainty: Time, Chaos and the New Laws of Nature* (with Stengers, Free Press, 1997)
- *From Being to Becoming: Time and Complexity in the Physical Sciences* (Freeman, 1980) — technical summary

## Awards
- **Nobel Prize in Chemistry (1977)** — for his contributions to non-equilibrium thermodynamics, particularly the theory of dissipative structures
- Solvay Conferences organizer / director at ULB

## Key Relationships
- [[Isabelle Stengers]] — close collaborator and philosophical interpreter

## Why Interesting
Prigogine's complexity science is the empirical foundation for much of what Stengers, Haraway, and Latour developed philosophically. Dissipative structures are the prototype for understanding life, society, and AI systems as **irreversible, creative, open-ended processes rather than deterministic machines**. His work is the scientific basis for process philosophy's relevance to contemporary biology, ecology, and AI. Directly relevant to [[Artificial Life]] and emergence research in Amber's territory.
