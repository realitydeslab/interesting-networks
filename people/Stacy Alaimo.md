# Stacy Alaimo

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Stub created — discovered as DIS 2025 opening keynote speaker.

Barbara and Carlisle Moore Professor of English and Core Faculty in Environmental Studies at University of Oregon. Key voice in posthumanism, material feminism, and blue (oceanic) humanities. Her DIS 2025 keynote "Designing Encounters with Deep Sea Life: Creaturely Aesthetics in the Anthropocene" brought posthumanist theory directly into the design research community.

## Why Interesting
Bridges feminist theory, environmental humanities, and posthumanism in ways directly relevant to more-than-human design. Her concept of "trans-corporeality" (bodies as porous, entangled with environment) connects to soma design and ecological thinking in HCI. The DIS community's choice of her as keynote signals how seriously they take posthumanist theory.

## Key Works
- *Undomesticated Ground: Recasting Nature as Feminist Space* (2000)
- *Bodily Natures: Science, Environment, and the Material Self* (2010) — ASLE book award; trans-corporeality
- *Exposed: Environmental Politics and Pleasures in Posthuman Times* (2016)
- *The Abyss Stares Back: Encounters with Deep-Sea Life* (2025)
- Co-edited *Material Feminisms* (2008, with Susan Hekman)
- Co-edits the Elements series at Duke University Press
