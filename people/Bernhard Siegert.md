# Bernhard Siegert

German media theorist and historian; co-founder and co-director of [[IKKM Weimar]]; chair for Theory and History of Cultural Techniques at [Bauhaus-Universität Weimar](https://www.uni-weimar.de/). The principal architect of **Kulturtechnik** (cultural techniques) as a rigorous research paradigm. Student of [[Friedrich Kittler]].

## Domains
- Cultural techniques (Kulturtechniken): writing, counting, drawing, coding, mapping as ontologically constitutive operations
- Media history and philosophy (postal systems, passports, navigation, doors, grids)
- Archaeology of technical artifacts as cultural operators

## Background

Born 1959, Bremen. Studied Germanic Studies, Philosophy, and History at Albert-Ludwigs-Universität Freiburg (1987 graduation). Followed Friedrich Kittler to Ruhr University Bochum, receiving doctorate 1991. Habilitation at Humboldt University Berlin 2001. Same year, appointed to Bauhaus-Universität Weimar's chair for Theory and History of Cultural Techniques.

Co-founded [[IKKM Weimar]] with Lorenz Engell in 2008; co-directed until its closure in 2020. Regarded (alongside Kittler, Norbert Bolz, Wolfgang Coy) as a pioneer of German media theory.

## Key Works
- [*Cultural Techniques: Grids, Filters, Doors, and Other Articulations of the Real*](https://www.fordhampress.com/9780823254873/) (Fordham University Press, 2015) — the canonical English-language statement of the cultural techniques paradigm; essays on doors, grids, ships' logs, and other technical operators as cultural constitutors ⭐⭐⭐
- *Final Frontiers: Eine Medienarchäologie des Meeres* (Brill/Fink, 2024) — media archaeology of the ocean; navigation, charting, the sea as medium
- *Passagiere und Papiere* (Fink, 2006) — history of the passport as cultural technique of identity verification
- *Relays: Literature as an Epoch of the Postal System* (Stanford UP, 1999) — media history of postal communication as literary epoch
- *Passage des Digitalen* (Brinkmann und Bose, 2003) — digital media as historical passage

## Key Communities
- [[IKKM Weimar]] — co-founder and co-director 2008–2020
- Bauhaus-Universität Weimar — chair since 2001

## Key Relationships
- [[Friedrich Kittler]] — doctoral supervisor and intellectual predecessor; Siegert continues/extends Kittler's media materialism toward cultural techniques
- Lorenz Engell — co-director at IKKM, complementary approaches (Engell: aesthetics; Siegert: history/ontology)
- Sybille Krämer — parallel cultural techniques research at Humboldt/Leuphana (image, writing, number)
- [[Erich Hörl]] — IKKM Senior Fellow; extended cultural techniques toward "general ecology"

## Why Interesting
Siegert is the linchpin figure connecting post-Kittler German media materialism to the broader cultural techniques research programme that now influences STS, design theory, and media archaeology globally. His work on cultural techniques directly informs the theoretical vocabulary for thinking about technical artifacts as not merely tools but as constitutors of social/cultural distinctions — highly relevant to Amber's work on agent behavior, protocol studies, and more-than-human design. The *Cultural Techniques* book (2015) is essential reading for anyone working on how technical practices shape what counts as culture, human, or agent.
