# Wu Tsang

Filmmaker, artist, and performer whose work explores hidden histories, marginalized narratives, and the act of performing itself. Described as concerned with "in-betweenness" — refusing binary categorization. Films are hybrids of documentary and narrative. **MacArthur Fellow (2018).**

Collaborator of [[Fred Moten]], whose writing and voice appear in her work.

## Background
- Born 1982, Worcester, Massachusetts
- BFA (2004) School of the Art Institute of Chicago; MFA (2010) UCLA
- Based in New York and Berlin
- Received MacArthur Fellowship in 2018 (two years before Moten's)
- Work presented at Tate Modern (London), Stedelijk Museum (Amsterdam), Migros Museum (Zurich), Whitney Museum, New Museum (NYC), MCA Chicago, MOCA Los Angeles, SFMOMA
- Participated in Whitney Biennial, Liverpool Biennial, and Gwangju Biennial (all 2012)

## Key Works
- *Wildness* (2012) — best-known documentary. Documents LA trans bar "Silver Platter" (serving Latin LGBT community since 1963) when a group of young artists hosts a weekly performance night there. Questions of community, space, ownership. Premiered at MoMA Documentary Fortnight. Moten is a key collaborator.
- *Duilian* (2015) — film on Chinese feminist revolutionary Qiu Jin and her queer relationship with calligrapher Wu Zhuying
- *Under Cinema* (2017) — follows R&B singer Kelela through her life as a Black artist; hand-held, intimate
- *A Day in the Life of Bliss* — performance work
- Multiple gallery installations at Tate, Stedelijk, Whitney, New Museum

## Key Connections
- **[[Fred Moten]]** — key collaborator; Moten's writing/voice features in her work
- **boychild** — performance art collaborator
- **Kelela** — subject of *Under Cinema*

## Why Interesting
Bridges Black performance theory, trans/queer performance, documentary filmmaking, and the visual art world. Her Moten collaboration is a key node in the Black-studies ↔ contemporary art bridge. The double MacArthur (2018 + Moten 2020) from the same intellectual orbit is notable.
