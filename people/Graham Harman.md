# Graham Harman

American philosopher and founding figure of **Object-Oriented Ontology (OOO)** — one of the dominant speculative realist currents since the mid-2000s. Currently Distinguished Professor of Philosophy at **UC Santa Cruz** and a core faculty member at the **European Graduate School (EGS)** in Switzerland.

Core OOO thesis: objects at every scale (quarks to galaxies, fictional characters to tools) are fundamentally withdrawn — they exceed every relation they enter into. Reality cannot be exhausted by human access to it (anti-correlationism). Objects have a "fourfold" structure (real object, sensual object, real qualities, sensual qualities). Philosophy should be speculative, gamble on ideas, not procedurally cautious.

Co-edits the **[[Open Humanities Press]]** **New Metaphysics** series (with Bruno Latour until his death). The series manifesto: against "nail-filing analytic critique and continental reverence for dusty textual monuments." Published *Realist Magic* (Timothy Morton), *The Democracy of Objects* (Levi Bryant), *New Materialism* interviews, and many more.

## Key Works
- *Tool-Being: Heidegger and the Metaphysics of Objects* (Open Court, 2002) — debut; OOO derived from Heidegger's tool-analysis
- *Guerrilla Metaphysics: Phenomenology and the Carpentry of Things* (2005)
- *Prince of Networks: Bruno Latour and Metaphysics* (re.press, 2009)
- *The Quadruple Object* (Zero Books, 2011) — systematic OOO ontology
- *Weird Realism: Lovecraft and Philosophy* (Zero Books, 2012)
- *Speculative Realism: An Introduction* (Polity, 2018)
- *Art and Objects* (Polity, 2019) — OOO aesthetics

## Connections
- [[Bruno Latour]] — co-edited New Metaphysics; Harman wrote *Prince of Networks* about Latour's actor-network theory
- [[Open Humanities Press]] — New Metaphysics series editor
- [[Timothy Morton]] — published *Realist Magic* in New Metaphysics; major OOO fellow traveller
- [[Quentin Meillassoux]] — both respond to Meillassoux's critique of correlationism
- [[Ray Brassier]] — fellow speculative realist (but diverged sharply from OOO)
- [[Yuk Hui]] — Hui's cosmotechnics engages and critiques Harman's OOO

## Why Interesting
OOO is one of the dominant philosophical moves in contemporary art and design theory. It licensed an entire generation of theorists to take objects seriously, opened new materialist approaches to media and technology, and has been taken up by artists and architects worldwide. Its flatness (equal ontological status to all objects) is both its appeal and its most contested claim. Harman's prolific output (20+ books) and institutional positions at EGS give him enormous pedagogical influence on global theory networks.
