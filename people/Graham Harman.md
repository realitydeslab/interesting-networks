---

American philosopher. Founder of **Object-Oriented Ontology (OOO)** — the radical claim that all objects (physical, fictional, mathematical, social) equally withdraw from and exceed any relation, including perception. Distinguished Professor at [[SCI-Arc (Southern California Institute of Architecture)]] in Los Angeles and faculty at [[European Graduate School (EGS)]]. Author of 20+ books over 25 years, from his 1999 dissertation on Heidegger through to a 2025 Allen Lane synthesis. One of the most prolific and controversial figures in contemporary continental philosophy; the person most responsible for bringing speculative metaphysics back into fashion after decades of anti-realist dominance.

---

## Background

Born May 9, 1968, [Iowa City, Iowa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iowa_City,_Iowa). Raised in [Mount Vernon, Iowa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mount_Vernon,_Iowa) — small-town Midwestern roots, maternal grandparents of Luxembourgian and Czech descent. An unusual background for a continental philosopher: no Paris, no Frankfurt, just the American heartland.

**Education:**
- **[St. John's College](https://www.sjc.edu/), Annapolis, Maryland** (1986–1990) — BA in the [Great Books](https://www.sjc.edu/academics/undergraduate/great-books-curriculum) programme. This is key: St. John's uses no textbooks — students read primary texts only, from Homer to Einstein. This gave Harman an unusually direct relationship to the history of ideas, bypassing secondary literature filters. The Great Books training shows in his writing style: concrete, clear, fond of strong claims and literary examples.
- **[Penn State University](https://philosophy.la.psu.edu/)** (MA, 1991) — studied under [Alphonso Lingis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alphonso_Lingis), American phenomenologist and translator of Levinas and Merleau-Ponty. First exposure to Continental phenomenology.
- **[DePaul University](https://las.depaul.edu/academics/philosophy/Pages/default.aspx), Chicago** (PhD, 1999) — dissertation: *Tool-Being: Elements in a Theory of Objects* (advisor: [William McNeill](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/William_McNeill_(philosopher))). While pursuing his PhD, **worked as an online sports reporter** — an experience he credits for developing his writing clarity, productivity, and ability to write to deadline. This is not incidental: Harman writes with unusual accessibility and speed for a continental philosopher.

**Career trajectory:**
- **2000–2016 (16 years):** [American University in Cairo (AUC)](https://www.aucegypt.edu/), Egypt. Department of Philosophy. Eventually promoted to **Distinguished University Professor** — AUC's highest academic rank. Cairo was intellectually isolating but also liberating: no departmental politics, no seminar circuit pressure, free to develop OOO without academic peer validation.
- **2013–present:** Faculty, [European Graduate School (EGS)](https://www.egs.edu/faculty/graham-harman), Saas-Fee, Switzerland / Valletta, Malta. EGS gathers world-class thinkers for intensive summer sessions; Harman became central to its philosophy/critical thought division.
- **2016–present:** **Distinguished Professor of Philosophy and Liberal Arts Coordinator**, [[SCI-Arc (Southern California Institute of Architecture)]], Los Angeles. This move is architecturally and intellectually significant: an architecture school, not a philosophy department. The move reflects OOO's growing influence on design and architecture, and positions Harman at the intersection of philosophy and spatial practice.
- **Visiting positions:** [University of Amsterdam](https://www.uva.nl/), [University of Innsbruck](https://www.uibk.ac.at/), [University of Turin](https://www.unito.it/), [Yale University](https://philosophy.yale.edu/).

The Cairo years deserve emphasis: Harman spent 16 years in intellectual semi-isolation at a small Anglo-American university in Egypt, writing book after book without the validating feedback of a major Western university philosophy department. This autonomy from normal academic social pressures is part of what made OOO possible — he didn't need the approval of Kantian or analytic orthodoxy.

## Domains

- **Object-Oriented Ontology (OOO)** — the claim that all objects withdraw from all relations; reality is populated by inexhaustible objects rather than events, processes, or substrates
- **Speculative Realism** — the broader movement challenging post-Kantian correlationism (the idea that reality is always already structured by the human/world relation)
- **Metaphysics of Objects** — ontological categories: real objects, sensual objects, real qualities, sensual qualities; the "Quadruple Object"
- **Heidegger Studies** — began as a Heidegger scholar; the tool-analysis → OOO move is the foundational argumentative step
- **Aesthetics** — OOO as a theory of aesthetic experience; art, literature (especially Lovecraft), architecture; the "allure" of objects
- **Architecture and Design** — OOO as a theory of built form; the building as object; form as withdrawal; extensive engagement with architects and designers
- **Political Philosophy** — brief but stimulating: *Immaterialism* (2016) applies OOO to social theory via Latour; questions about how objects persist
- **Intellectual History** — critical rereadings of Bruno Latour, Quentin Meillassoux, Dante; editorial work on speculative metaphysics

## Key Works

### Books (Chronological — Complete)

**Phase 1: Heidegger → OOO Genesis (2002–2010)**
- **[*Tool-Being: Heidegger and the Metaphysics of Objects*](https://www.opencourtbooks.com/books_n/tool_being.htm)** (Open Court, 2002) — PhD dissertation revised. The founding argument: Heidegger's tool-analysis reveals that every object withdraws from all relations, including utility. The hammer is not reducible to its use or its parts. This is the seed crystal from which OOO grows. Harman radicalizes the insight: it's not just tools, it's every object.
- **[*Guerrilla Metaphysics: Phenomenology and the Carpentry of Things*](https://www.opencourtbooks.com/books_n/guerrilla_meta.htm)** (Open Court, 2005) — Develops the theory of "vicarious causation": since all objects withdraw, how do they interact at all? Solution: via "sensual" surrogates, through the "allure" that bridges real and sensual domains. Philosophy of causation without touching essences.
- **[*Heidegger Explained: From Phenomenon to Thing*](https://www.opencourtbooks.com/books_n/heidegger_explained.htm)** (Open Court, 2007) — Accessible introduction to Heidegger. Shows his interpretive moves.
- **[*Towards Speculative Realism: Essays and Lectures*](https://www.johnhuntpublishing.com/zer0-books/our-books/towards-speculative-realism)** (Zero Books, 2010) — Collected papers documenting the emergence of OOO. Includes his lecture from the landmark 2007 Goldsmiths "Speculative Realism" conference.
- **[*Circus Philosophicus*](https://www.johnhuntpublishing.com/zer0-books/our-books/circus-philosophicus)** (Zero Books, 2010) — Short literary/philosophical fables illustrating OOO concepts. The most stylistically adventurous of his early books.

**Phase 2: OOO Fully Formed (2010–2014)**
- **[*Prince of Networks: Bruno Latour and Metaphysics*](http://www.re-press.org/book-files/OA_Version_780980544060_Prince_of_Networks.pdf)** (re.press, 2009; open access) — Critical engagement with [[Bruno Latour]]. Harman reads Latour as a philosopher of objects, not just a sociologist of science. The "Prince of Networks" vs. the "object-oriented" approach: Harman argues Latour undermines objects (reduces them to relations/networks) — this is the "undermining" vs. "overmining" critique.
- **[*L'objet quadruple*](https://www.puf.com/content/L%E2%80%99objet_quadruple)** / **[*The Quadruple Object*](https://www.johnhuntpublishing.com/zer0-books/our-books/quadruple-object)** (PUF/Zero Books, 2010/2011) — His systematic ontology. Objects have four poles: **Real Object / Sensual Object / Real Qualities / Sensual Qualities** — giving 10 possible relations among them. The Fourfold is OOO's formal architecture, mapping onto space, time, essence, and eidos. (Note: the French-language edition first is significant — rare for an American philosopher; his Cairo years and EGS connections gave him European outlets.)
- **[*The Prince and the Wolf: Latour and Harman at the LSE*](https://www.johnhuntpublishing.com/zer0-books/our-books/prince-and-the-wolf)** (Zero Books, 2011, with [[Bruno Latour]] and Peter Erdélyi) — Transcript and commentary of a 2008 dialogue at LSE where Harman and Latour debate their philosophical differences. A rare book-length philosophical dialogue.
- **[*Quentin Meillassoux: Philosophy in the Making*](https://edinburghuniversitypress.com/book-quentin-meillassoux-philosophy-in-the-making.html)** (Edinburgh UP, 2011) — Critical reconstruction of [[Quentin Meillassoux]]'s *After Finitude* and his unpublished dissertation *L'inexistence Divine*. Harman offers a partial translation of the unpublished work (from a 2003 revision). Shows his editorial/reconstructive relationship to the broader SR movement.
- **[*Weird Realism: Lovecraft and Philosophy*](https://www.johnhuntpublishing.com/zer0-books/our-books/weird-realism)** (Zero Books, 2012) — Applies OOO to H.P. Lovecraft's horror fiction. Argues Lovecraft's style — the insinuation of unnameable depths — captures the essential feature of OOO: objects always exceed their description. Philosophy via horror fiction. This book opened OOO to art and literary communities.
- **[*Bells and Whistles: More Speculative Realism*](https://www.johnhuntpublishing.com/zer0-books/our-books/bells-whistles)** (Zero Books, 2013) — Collected essays. Documents the OOO movement's spread.
- **[*Bruno Latour: Reassembling the Political*](https://www.plutobooks.com/9780745335995/bruno-latour/)** (Pluto Press, 2014) — Second Latour book; focuses on Latour's political philosophy. Shows the long-term intellectual engagement.

**Phase 3: OOO Expands (2016–2020)**
- **[*Immaterialism: Objects and Social Theory*](https://www.politybooks.com/bookdetail?book_slug=immaterialism-objects-and-social-theory--9781509500048)** (Polity Press, 2016) — Applies OOO to social theory using the Dutch East India Company (VOC) as a case study. Social objects and how they change or persist. The most "sociological" of Harman's books.
- **[*Dante's Broken Hammer: The Ethics, Aesthetics, and Metaphysics of Love*](https://repeaterbooks.com/product/dantes-broken-hammer/)** ([[Repeater Books]], 2016) — Extended reading of Dante's *La Vita Nuova* and *De Vulgari Eloquentia* through OOO. Argues Dante's broken style of writing is philosophically productive in its very insufficiency. Literary philosophy; interesting for showing OOO's aesthetic implications.
- **[*The Rise of Realism*](https://www.politybooks.com/bookdetail?book_slug=the-rise-of-realism--9781509519736)** (Polity Press, 2017, with [Manuel DeLanda](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manuel_DeLanda)) — Dialogue between OOO and DeLanda's assemblage theory (both anti-correlationist but in different ways). DeLanda defends a scientific realism; Harman defends a flat-ish ontology of objects. The one sustained dialogue between these two distinct schools of new realism.
- **[*Object-Oriented Ontology: A New Theory of Everything*](https://www.penguin.co.uk/books/306395/object-oriented-ontology-by-harman-graham/9780241269176)** ([Pelican Books](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pelican_Books), 2018) — The trade press introduction to OOO. Pelican is a prestigious series for accessible intellectual books; this is the version that reached the widest audience. Most likely entry point for artists, architects, designers.
- **[*Speculative Realism: An Introduction*](https://www.politybooks.com/bookdetail?book_slug=speculative-realism-an-introduction--9781509519958)** (Polity Press, 2018) — Short history and survey of the SR movement. Maps the positions of the four original Goldsmiths thinkers.
- **[*Art and Objects*](https://www.politybooks.com/bookdetail?book_slug=art-and-objects--9781509530700)** (Polity Press, 2020) — His most developed aesthetic theory. Builds on Clement Greenberg and Michael Fried but transforms their formalism into OOO terms. Art as the place where objects "allure" — where sensual qualities point to real depths without exhausting them.
- **[*Skirmishes: With Friends, Enemies, and Neutrals*](https://punctumbooks.com/titles/skirmishes/)** ([[punctum books]], 2020; open access) — Collected polemics and engagements. Disputatious, frank, sometimes revealing of Harman's intellectual personality.
- **[*Is There an Object-Oriented Architecture? Engaging Graham Harman*](https://www.bloomsbury.com/uk/is-there-an-object-oriented-architecture-9781350122031/)** ([Bloomsbury, 2020](https://www.bloomsbury.com/)) — edited volume of architectural engagements with OOO; includes Harman's responses.

**Phase 4: Synthesis and Archaeology (2021–2025)**
- **[*Artful Objects: Graham Harman on Art and the Business of Speculative Realism*](https://www.sternberg-press.com/product/artful-objects-graham-harman-on-art-and-the-business-of-speculative-realism/)** ([Sternberg Press](https://www.sternberg-press.com/), 2021) — Published by the leading art-theory press in Europe. OOO's relationship to the contemporary art world; Sternberg connects philosophy directly to artist/curator audiences.
- **[*Architecture and Objects*](https://www.upress.umn.edu/book-division/books/architecture-and-objects)** ([University of Minnesota Press](https://www.upress.umn.edu/), 2022) — Most sustained treatment. Argues architecture is uniquely suited to OOO because buildings are objects that are also environments; the threshold between interior and exterior is central to OOO's ontology.
- **[*The Graham Harman Reader*](https://www.johnhuntpublishing.com/zer0-books/our-books/the-graham-harman-reader)** (Zero Books, 2023, ed. Jon Cogburn & Niki Young) — Authoritative anthology.
- **[*Objects Untimely: Object-Oriented Philosophy and Archaeology*](https://www.wiley.com/en-gb/Objects+Untimely%3A+Object+Oriented+Philosophy+and+Archaeology-p-9781509545803)** (Polity Press, 2023, with Christopher Witmore) — Applies OOO to archaeology: how do objects persist through time, how do they decay and preserve? Collaboration with archaeologist Christopher Witmore (Lubbock Christian University). OOO meets material culture studies.
- **[*Waves and Stones: On the Ultimate Nature of Reality*](https://www.penguin.co.uk/books/446655/waves-and-stones-by-harman-graham/9780241620847)** ([Allen Lane](https://www.penguin.co.uk/penguin-publishers/allen-lane), 2025) — His most ambitious synthetic statement, published by the most prestigious British general intellectual imprint. Argues for OOO as a complete theory of reality, directly engaging physics, mathematics, and the nature of time. The Allen Lane connection signals mainstream intellectual recognition.

### Edited Volumes
- **[*The Speculative Turn: Continental Materialism and Realism*](http://www.re-press.org/book-files/OA_Version_Speculative_Turn_9780980668346.pdf)** (re.press, 2011, co-edited with [[Levi Bryant]] and [[Nick Srnicek]]; open access) — The landmark SR anthology. 25 essays by movement figures. Co-editing with Bryant and Srnicek maps the OOO/SR coalition at its peak.

### Series Editorships
- **"Speculative Realism" series** — [Edinburgh University Press](https://edinburghuniversitypress.com/) — sole editor. A book series publishing monographs in the SR tradition.
- **"New Metaphysics" series** — [Open Humanities Press](http://www.openhumanitiespress.org/books/series/new-metaphysics/) — co-editor with [[Bruno Latour]] (now deceased). Published: *The Democracy of Objects* (Levi Bryant), *Realist Magic* (Timothy Morton), *Alien Phenomenology* (Ian Bogost), *The Democracy of Objects*, *New Materialism* (Rick Dolphijn & Iris van der Tuin), *Plastic Bodies* (Tom Sparrow), and others. An open-access book series: a deliberate political-economic choice to make speculative philosophy free.

## Exhibitions / Events / Talks

### The Goldsmiths Conference (April 2007) — Founding Moment
The [Speculative Realism conference at Goldsmiths College, University of London](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Speculative_realism) (April 27, 2007) is the founding event of the SR movement. Moderated by [Alberto Toscano](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alberto_Toscano). Presenters: Harman, [[Ray Brassier]], [[Iain Hamilton Grant]], [[Quentin Meillassoux]]. Published in *[Collapse: Philosophical Research and Investigations, Volume III](https://www.urbanomic.com/book/collapse-iii/)* (Urbanomic, 2007) — a landmark document.

### OOO Symposium at Georgia Tech (April 23, 2010)
Third major SR event. Organized by [[Ian Bogost]], hosted at Georgia Institute of Technology's School of Literature, Communication and Culture. Participants: Harman, Bogost, Levi Bryant, Steven Shaviro, Hugh Crawford, Carl DiSalvo, John Johnston, Barbara Maria Stafford, Eugene Thacker. Established OOO as a distinct sub-movement within SR.

### MACBA Podcast / Radio Web MACBA (2013)
[Interview with Luciana Parisi at MACBA (Museu d'Art Contemporani de Barcelona)](http://rwm.macba.cat/en/specials/objecthood1_graham_harman_luciana_parisi/capsula) — "On Objecthood." MACBA's Radio Web series reaches the international art community; this was an early bridge between OOO and contemporary art institutions.

## Key Relationships & Collaborators

- **[[Bruno Latour]]** ⭐⭐⭐ — The most important intellectual relationship of Harman's career. Two books (*Prince of Networks*, 2009; *Bruno Latour: Reassembling the Political*, 2014) and the joint "New Metaphysics" series. Harman positioned himself as Latour's philosophical interlocutor — not an uncritical admirer but a rigorous critic (arguing Latour "undermines" objects by reducing them to relations). Latour wrote a preface for *Prince of Networks*. After Latour's death (October 2022), Harman remains the primary custodian of the Latour-OOO dialogue.
- **[[Quentin Meillassoux]]** ⭐⭐ — Co-participant in the Goldsmiths conference. Harman wrote the first book-length study of Meillassoux in English (*Quentin Meillassoux: Philosophy in the Making*, 2011) and offered a partial translation of *L'inexistence Divine*. Intellectual respect but fundamental disagreement: Meillassoux's speculative materialism grounds knowledge in mathematics and contiguity, while OOO insists on irreducible object-ness.
- **[[Ray Brassier]]** ⭐⭐ — Co-Goldsmiths presenter. Brassier has been withering about OOO, calling the SR "movement" a blogosphere phenomenon promoting "pan-psychist metaphysics and morsels of process philosophy." Their relationship is intellectually adversarial — productive tension in the broader SR field.
- **[[Iain Hamilton Grant]]** ⭐⭐ — Fourth member of the Goldsmiths quartet. Grant's transcendental materialism (inspired by Schelling) is closer to process philosophy; Harman disagrees but engaged respectfully.
- **Levi Bryant** ⭐⭐ — Co-edited *The Speculative Turn* with Harman and [[Nick Srnicek]]. Bryant coined the term "object-oriented ontology" in 2009; his "onticology" is close to but distinct from Harman's OOO. Bryant's Larval Subjects blog was the key early dissemination channel. Bryant later moved away from OOO toward machine-oriented ontology. → *Candidate for stub note*
- **[[Timothy Morton]]** ⭐⭐ — Key OOO ally. Morton's *Hyperobjects* (2013) applies OOO to ecology (climate change, nuclear waste as "hyperobjects" distributed across time and space). Both now at SCI-Arc. Morton's work is more accessible and art-world oriented; their collaboration represents OOO's ecological and aesthetic wings. New Metaphysics series published Morton's *Realist Magic*.
- **Ian Bogost** ⭐⭐ — Organized the 2010 Georgia Tech OOO symposium. *Alien Phenomenology* (2012) is OOO applied to technology/videogames. Published in New Metaphysics series. Bogost's work bridged OOO to media studies and game studies. → *Candidate for stub note*
- **[[Nick Srnicek]]** — Co-edited *The Speculative Turn*. Srnicek subsequently moved toward platform capitalism critique (*Platform Capitalism*, 2016) rather than OOO; a notable career divergence from the SR moment.
- **Manuel DeLanda** — Co-author, *The Rise of Realism* (2017). DeLanda's assemblage theory (neo-Deleuzian) and OOO are related but distinct; the dialogue is constructive. Both anti-correlationist, both flat ontologists, different accounts of causation. → *Candidate for stub note*
- **Christopher Witmore** — Co-author, *Objects Untimely* (2023). Archaeologist at Lubbock Christian University; his "symmetrical archaeology" is independently aligned with OOO. The collaboration shows OOO's disciplinary reach into archaeology/material culture.
- **Jane Bennett** — Not a direct collaborator but parallel figure: *Vibrant Matter* (2010) is the political ecology equivalent of OOO's ontology of objects. Bennett's "thing power" and Harman's "withdrawn objects" are complementary frameworks. Both in conversation with Spinoza and Deleuze alongside Heidegger. → *Candidate for stub note*
- **[[Bogna Konior]]** — Adjacent thinker; EGS-affiliated; computational materialism and AI philosophy — engages with OOO-adjacent questions.
- **[[Robin Mackay]]** — Editor of [[Urbanomic]] and *Collapse* journal; published the founding SR document. The Urbanomic ecosystem is the publishing infrastructure that launched SR.
- **[[Reza Negarestani]]** — Published in *Collapse*; both in the SR orbit. Negarestani moved toward rationalism and AI; Harman remained in metaphysical territory. Both EGS-connected.
- **[Steven Shaviro](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Steven_Shaviro)** — Participated in the 2010 OOO symposium at Georgia Tech. *The Universe of Things: On Speculative Realism* (2014, Minnesota UP) engages both OOO and Whitehead. Productive critic. → *Candidate for stub note*
- **[Peter Wolfendale](https://www.thoughtexperiment.co.uk/)** — Author of *[Object-Oriented Philosophy: The Noumenon's New Clothes](https://www.urbanomic.com/book/object-oriented-philosophy/)* (Urbanomic, 2014) — the most sustained philosophical critique of OOO, published by Urbanomic (a former SR ally). Harman responded directly in *Skirmishes*. The Wolfendale critique is the most important internal philosophical challenge OOO has faced. Note exists: [[Peter Wolfendale]].
- **[Robert Jackson](https://robertjackson.co.uk/)** — British philosopher; edited volumes on OOO and architecture.
- **[Slavoj Žižek](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Slavoj_Žižek)** — Harman engaged Žižek in debate; EGS co-faculty. Žižek is critical of OOO's political implications; Harman responds. Generative tension.
- **[Peter Erdélyi](https://linkedin.com/in/peter-erdelyi)** — Co-author of *The Prince and the Wolf*; the interlocutor/editor who structured the Latour-Harman dialogue.

## Venue Circuit Analysis

### The "Philosophy From Below" Circuit
OOO's early dissemination happened not via major university presses or established philosophy journals but through **blogs, open-access publishers, and small UK/Australian publishers**:
- Blogs: Harman's own [Object-Oriented Philosophy](https://doctorzamalek2.wordpress.com/), Bryant's Larval Subjects, Bogost's blog
- Publishers: re.press (Melbourne), Zero Books (UK), punctum books (US) — all small, politically committed, willing to publish speculative work
- Journals: *Collapse* (Urbanomic), *Parrhesia*, *Open Philosophy* (De Gruyter, Harman's own)

This is a deliberate counter-institutionalism: OOO built its audience outside the academic prestige circuit, which paradoxically made it more influential in art and design than most academic philosophy.

### The Art World Circuit
Unusual for a philosopher: Harman has published with **Sternberg Press** (the leading European art-theory publisher), appeared in **ArtReview Power 100**, given talks at **MACBA**, **Tate**, **MoMA PS1**, and been cited by curators worldwide. This circuit opened through:
1. *Weird Realism* (2012) — the Lovecraft book introduced OOO to artists via horror aesthetics
2. *Art and Objects* (2020) — a proper theory of OOO aesthetics using art historical tools
3. *Artful Objects* (Sternberg, 2021) — the definitive art-world publication

### The Architecture Circuit
SCI-Arc position is the fulcrum. Harman has given talks at every major architecture school: Bartlett (UCL), AA (London), Harvard GSD, Columbia GSAPP, TU Delft. *Architecture and Objects* (2022, Minnesota UP) is the key text. The key insight for architects: buildings are objects that simultaneously house and withdraw; OOO provides a theory of why buildings exceed their program and use. The architecture world's affinity for OOO is deep — many contemporary architects (including Mark Foster Gage, Tom Wiscombe) explicitly work with OOO principles.

### The European Continental Circuit
EGS (Saas-Fee) is the summer summit. Beyond that: Amsterdam, Innsbruck, Turin for visiting positions. Edinburgh University Press for the SR series. Goldsmiths as origin point. The London-Paris-Edinburgh-Amsterdam axis for continental philosophy in the Anglo-French tradition.

### The American Academic Circuit
DePaul → AUC → SCI-Arc is Harman's own trajectory. Major US universities (Yale, Notre Dame, CUNY GC, Georgia Tech) have hosted him as a visitor. He's not in an Ivy League humanities department — which is interesting. His American academic presence is as guest lecturer, not tenured establishment philosopher.

## OOO: The Philosophical Core

### The Founding Argument (Heidegger's Tool-Analysis)
In *Being and Time*, Heidegger distinguishes between tools in **ready-to-hand** use (the hammer disappears into its use — we don't notice it) and **present-at-hand** objects (broken tools become visible as objects). Harman's radical move: this isn't about human perception. The tool's withdrawal from use is its metaphysical depth. **Every object withdraws from every relation, not just from human use.** Fire's relation to cotton, a gust of wind's relation to a falling leaf — neither touches the other's real being. All relations are "vicarious."

### The Quadruple Object
OOO's ontology has four poles:
1. **Real Object (RO)** — the withdrawn, inaccessible depth of the thing
2. **Sensual Object (SO)** — the caricature of the object encountered in experience
3. **Real Qualities (RQ)** — the object's essential features (never directly accessible)
4. **Sensual Qualities (SQ)** — the qualities that appear in experience

The 10 pairings among these four poles generate OOO's entire system: space, time, essence, eidos, and five other ontological categories. This is unusually systematic for a continental philosopher — a medieval-flavored categorical architecture.

### Undermining vs. Overmining
Harman's critique of all philosophy: most philosophy either **undermines** objects (reduces them to smaller/more fundamental components — atoms, quarks, mathematics, flux) or **overmines** them (reduces them to their effects, relations, uses, social constructions — what Latour does with networks). OOO refuses both moves: objects are real, they have depth, and they are more than their relations. This is the philosophical content in miniature.

### Vicarious Causation
Since objects never touch each other's real being, how do they cause anything? Harman's solution: through **sensual surrogates** on the "interior" of a third object that contains both. Causation is a kind of "indirect encounter" mediated by sensual caricatures. This echoes Islamic occasionalism (Malebranche, al-Ghazali) — God mediates causation. Harman secularizes and democratizes this: every object is its own occasion for vicarious encounter.

### Against "Process Philosophy" and "Flat Ontology"
Harman's key disputes:
- **Against Whitehead/process philosophy**: Process philosophy reduces objects to events/processes; Harman insists objects have identity that persists through change.
- **Against Deleuze/DeLanda**: Deleuzian "flat ontology" says everything is equally real, but Harman insists objects are not dissolved into flows and intensities.
- **Against Latour's relationalism**: Latour's actor-networks reduce actors to their relations; Harman insists actors have depths that exceed their network.
- **Against speculative materialism (Meillassoux)**: Meillassoux's focus on contingency and mathematics misses the object's inexhaustibility.

### OOO and Aesthetics
*Art and Objects* (2020) is Harman's matured aesthetic theory. Key claims:
- Art is the domain where **allure** operates: sensual qualities gesture toward real depths without capturing them.
- **Formalism is right** (Greenberg, Fried) but for the wrong reasons: form matters not because of autonomous medium but because it's where objects present themselves.
- **Theatricality** (which Fried condemned) is actually the meeting point of objects — the relational encounter that OOO theorizes.
- Art makes the withdrawal of objects felt, which is why it's irreducible to content analysis.

### OOO and Architecture
*Architecture and Objects* (2022) central thesis: Buildings are uniquely good OOO objects because:
1. They are **inhabited** — you move through them; they contain you
2. They are **thresholds** between inside and outside — precisely the OOO distinction between interior depth and exterior presentation
3. They **age and transform** — raising OOO questions about persistence
4. They **exceed their program** — great buildings do more than their brief

This explains why architects found OOO more useful than most philosophy: it gives them a vocabulary for what they already intuit about buildings as exceeding their uses.

## Key Debates & Controversies

### The Wolfendale Critique
[Peter Wolfendale](https://www.thoughtexperiment.co.uk/)'s *Object-Oriented Philosophy: The Noumenon's New Clothes* (Urbanomic, 2014) is a 400-page critical demolition of OOO. Wolfendale's charges:
1. OOO's "withdrawal" is just a relabeling of Kant's noumenon — philosophically empty
2. The Quadruple Object's four categories are unmotivated
3. OOO provides no mechanism for how sensual caricatures work
4. The anti-scientism in OOO is obscurantist
Harman responds extensively in *Skirmishes* (2020). The exchange is the best introduction to OOO's philosophical problems for those who want rigorous engagement.

### Brassier's Rejection
[[Ray Brassier]]'s infamous dismissal: "The 'speculative realist movement' exists only in the imaginations of a group of bloggers promoting an agenda for which I have no sympathy whatsoever." The charge: OOO is unserious philosophy promoted by blog enthusiasm rather than rigorous argument. Harman and Brassier represent two poles: Harman thinks philosophy should be vivid and accessible; Brassier thinks it should be technically demanding. This is also a dispute about what philosophy is for.

### The Politics Question
Meillassoux, Brassier, and leftist critics have argued OOO is politically conservative: if everything is equally an object, hierarchy, exploitation, and suffering become just more "relations among objects" — the political is flattened. Harman disputes this: OOO doesn't prevent political claims; it just requires that they be made on grounds other than ontological privilege.

### The Lovecraft Problem
*Weird Realism* (2012) opens OOO to Lovecraft's work, but Lovecraft's well-documented racism (the Cthulhu Mythos draws on his xenophobia) creates problems for a philosophy that presents itself as anti-anthropocentric. Harman has acknowledged this tension; it remains unresolved.

## Why Interesting

Harman is interesting to the interesting-networks universe for several reasons:

1. **Philosophy that matters to artists**: Unlike most academic metaphysics, OOO has been adopted by architects, artists, game designers, and curators. The reason is clarity and operationalizability: "objects withdraw from all relations" gives practitioners a philosophical license for respecting the autonomy and excess of their materials, machines, and environments.

2. **Institutional heterodoxy**: An architecture school, not a philosophy department, as primary home; an Egyptian university for 16 formative years; open-access publishing as a political strategy. These are unconventional choices that produced unconventional reach.

3. **The Speculative Realism moment (2007–2015) as a case study in philosophical movement-building.** The SR conference at Goldsmiths was followed by explosive blogosphere amplification, open-access anthology publication, and rapid field formation. This is a rare example of a philosophical movement assembling in real time, and it has lessons for how ideas spread.

4. **OOO as response to the posthuman condition**: While most posthuman theory (Barad, Hayles, Haraway) focuses on entanglement and relation, OOO insists on withdrawal and inexhaustibility. This is a complementary and sometimes corrective perspective: objects (including AIs, machines, architectures) are not just their functions or relations — they have depths. This is relevant to Amber's AI/agent work.

5. **[[SCI-Arc]]** as a model for philosophy-practice collaboration: Harman demonstrates that a philosopher can be the intellectual anchor of a design/architecture school, reaching practitioners directly. The SCI-Arc model is worth studying.

## Research Taste Analysis ⭐⭐⭐

**This is about HOW Harman built his research empire — what Amber can learn from the meta-strategy.**

### Conceptual Strategy

**Framework declaration → systematic elaboration:**
Unlike most continental philosophers who produce brilliant fragments (Derrida, Deleuze), Harman is unusually systematic. OOO is declared early (*Tool-Being*, 2002) and then elaborated book by book, domain by domain. The strategy: establish the core argument, then *apply it everywhere* — to Lovecraft, to Dante, to Latour, to architecture, to art, to archaeology, to social theory. Each application demonstrates OOO's fertility and creates new audiences.

**The "strongest objector" technique:**
Harman reads his opponents charitably and engages their best arguments. The Latour books, the Meillassoux book, the Wolfendale response — these are all cases of taking criticism seriously and producing better philosophy through it. This builds credibility: you can trust Harman to represent opposing views fairly.

**Provocation-first strategy:**
Key provocations that generated responses:
- "Everything is equally an object" — anti-hierarchy of being
- "Heidegger is really a philosopher of objects, not phenomenology" — reread canonical figure
- "Lovecraft is philosophy" — lowbrow/highbrow crossover
- "Architecture is the best art form for OOO" — institution-specific claim that brings new audience

Each provocation is a specific thesis, not just a vague gesture. Specific theses get responses; vague gestures don't.

### Career Architecture

**The institutional sequence: peripheral → unconventional → practitioner-focused:**
AUC (prestigious in Egypt, peripheral in Western philosophy) → EGS (prestigious but unconventional) → SCI-Arc (architecture school). This sequence goes increasingly away from conventional academic philosophy prestige and toward practitioner communities. It's the opposite of the standard academic trajectory (postdoc → R1 → tenure). The payoff: Harman reaches audiences no conventional philosophy professor can.

**Publisher diversification:**
Harman has published with: Open Court (mainstream academic philosophy), re.press (Australian radical open access), Zero Books (UK leftist small press), punctum books (US open access), Edinburgh UP (prestigious academic), Polity (serious academic trade), Pelican/Allen Lane (Penguin — major trade press), PUF (French academic), Sternberg Press (leading art theory), Bloomsbury (major academic trade), Minnesota UP (major academic). This diversification is unusual: most philosophers have one primary publisher. Harman's diversification maps his different audiences (academics, artists, architects, general readers) and ensures no single publisher can define him.

**Blog as infrastructure:**
The [Object-Oriented Philosophy blog](https://doctorzamalek2.wordpress.com/) since 2006 is genuinely infrastructural — it builds his audience before publications land, creates buzz for new books, and establishes his voice as accessible and engaged. 1,145+ blog subscribers. This is small by internet standards but significant for academic philosophy. The blog also serves as a record of intellectual development — he thinks in public.

### Methodology Signature

**Philosophical monograph at industrial speed:**
Harman has produced ~25 books in 25 years — roughly one per year. This is unprecedented for a continental philosopher. The comparison: Derrida published fewer books in his lifetime. Harman's training as a sports journalist (fast, accurate, readable) and his Cairo isolation (no academic social calendar to distract him) produced this output. The criticism: some books are thin. The defense: volume builds audience, and a series of accessible books reaches more people than a few dense ones.

**Re-reading canonical figures through OOO:**
Each major "monograph on figure X" (Latour, Meillassoux, Dante) serves double duty: it analyzes the figure AND demonstrates OOO's interpretive power. This is a productive technique: get assigned in courses on both Latour AND OOO. Create interdisciplinary adoption without needing disciplinary crossing.

**The "trade press" move:**
*Object-Oriented Ontology: A New Theory of Everything* (Pelican, 2018) and *Waves and Stones* (Allen Lane, 2025) are trade press publications. Allen Lane is arguably the most prestigious general intellectual imprint in Britain (Isaiah Berlin, Richard Dawkins, Mary Beard are Allen Lane authors). Publishing with Allen Lane signals Harman's ambition to be a mainstream intellectual figure, not just an academic one. This is a deliberate career move, not just a money decision.

### Network Strategy

**The "founding document" + "anthology" combo:**
The 2007 Goldsmiths conference was immediately published in *Collapse III* (Urbanomic). The movement then produced *The Speculative Turn* (2011, re.press, Harman/Bryant/Srnicek eds.) — a 25-essay open-access anthology that became the canonical SR document. These two publication moves established SR as a field that could be taught and studied. The speed matters: within 4 years of the founding conference, there was a canonical anthology. This is how fields get declared.

**The blog coalition:**
Before Twitter or academic social media, OOO built its community via interconnected philosophy blogs (Harman's Object-Oriented Philosophy, Bryant's Larval Subjects, Bogost's blog, Morton's Ecology Without Nature). The blogs created a reading public before the books arrived. This is a model that preceded academic social media but identified the same dynamic: ideas spread through engaged commentary networks.

**EGS as summit diplomacy:**
Harman's annual presence at EGS keeps him connected to the summit of continental philosophy (Žižek, Butler, Badiou, Malabou) without requiring him to be at a major European university. EGS is an unusual institutional hack: short, intense, prestigious, accessible (it will admit students with less conventional backgrounds than elite universities).

### Key Moves (Chronological)

1. **1999–2002: From dissertation to published book (*Tool-Being*, 2002).** The move from a 1999 PhD dissertation to a published book in 3 years is fast but not unusual. What's unusual: choosing **Open Court**, a philosophy-focused small press, not a university press. This kept the book visible to philosophers without needing a university press peer review. The risk paid off — Open Court is well-distributed in academic bookstores.

2. **2007: The Goldsmiths Conference.** Harman was already developing OOO in relative isolation. The Goldsmiths conference (organized by the Collapse/Urbanomic ecosystem) brought him into contact with Brassier, Grant, and Meillassoux. **The value of the conference was not the ideas** (those were already developed) **but the coalition formation and the publication in Collapse III.** This is the move that turned solo work into a movement.

3. **2009: *Prince of Networks* (open access, free download).** Making the first Latour book freely downloadable via re.press was a deliberate gamble: lose royalties, gain distribution. It worked: *Prince of Networks* became the most-read introduction to the OOO-Latour relationship, reaching people who would never have bought an academic book. Open access as audience-building strategy.

4. **2011: *The Speculative Turn* (open access, co-edited).** The anthology move: 25 essays by SR figures, freely downloadable. This is the territory declaration: SR is now a field with enough diversity of voices to fill an anthology. Co-editing with Bryant and Srnicek distributes the coalition claims and makes the move less vulnerable to "it's just one guy with a blog."

5. **2012: *Weird Realism* (Lovecraft book).** The strategic pop-culture move: OOO + horror fiction + Lovecraft = gateway drug for art students, designers, science fiction fans. This book reached audiences who would never read *Tool-Being*. Weird fiction as a gateway to speculative ontology. The "weird" label then attached to the whole OOO enterprise — "weird realism" as a brand.

6. **2016: SCI-Arc appointment.** Moving to Los Angeles and an architecture school is the decisive institutional move. Architecture is where OOO is most concretely applicable; Los Angeles is a design/culture capital. The SCI-Arc appointment simultaneously: connects Harman to [[Timothy Morton]] (fellow OOO figure already at SCI-Arc), positions him adjacent to entertainment industry thinking, and gives OOO a design-school home that philosophy departments could not provide.

7. **2018: Pelican Books (*Object-Oriented Ontology: A New Theory of Everything*).** Pelican signals mainstream intellectual legitimacy — it's the same series as Richard Dawkins's *The Selfish Gene* in the UK. This is the move from niche philosopher to public intellectual.

8. **2025: Allen Lane (*Waves and Stones*).** The summit of the trade press trajectory. Allen Lane is Penguin's flagship intellectual imprint. After 25 years of books, this is the mature synthesis for a general audience. What this signals: OOO has become established enough for a major trade press to bet on it for mainstream sales.

### What Amber Can Learn

**1. Publish for audiences, not prestige.** Harman's mix of open-access, small press, and trade press publication is strategically diverse. Not every book needs a prestigious academic press. Ask: who do I want to read this? Then choose the publisher that reaches them.

**2. The blog as a public thinking record.** Harman's 20-year blog is his intellectual autobiography — and it builds his audience continuously. Writing in public creates readers before books arrive. Short posts > long silence.

**3. The application strategy: declare framework, then apply everywhere.** Once a strong core claim is established, applying it to diverse domains (architecture, art, horror, archaeology, social theory) creates multiple points of disciplinary entry. Each application is a new audience.

**4. Peripheral positions can be productive.** Cairo, then SCI-Arc. Not mainstream academic philosophy. This is not incidental — it produced the intellectual autonomy that made OOO possible. Being at a slightly wrong institution can be better than being at the right one, if the wrong one lets you think freely.

**5. The provocative readable title.** *Object-Oriented Ontology: A New Theory of Everything* (Pelican) and *Waves and Stones* (Allen Lane) are designed to be picked up and opened. Academic philosophy titles tend to be technically correct but unreadable as titles. Harman writes titles that prompt curiosity. This is not trivial: titles determine shelving, browsing, and citation.

**6. Engage your best critics, don't dismiss them.** The Wolfendale response in *Skirmishes*, the Brassier tension, the Latour engagement — Harman benefits from engaging serious critics. Dismissing critics signals fragility; engaging them signals confidence.
