# Adriano Pedrosa

Brazilian curator. Artistic Director of [Museu de Arte de São Paulo (MASP)](https://masp.org.br/en). Curated the 60th Venice Biennale (2024) — "Stranieri Ovunque / Foreigners Everywhere" — first Latin American director and first director based in the Southern Hemisphere.

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-27:** Stub created during Venice Biennale deep research.
- **2024:** 60th Venice Biennale "Foreigners Everywhere" — centered Global South, indigenous, queer, diaspora art. Golden Lion for National Pavilion: Australia (Archie Moore). Lifetime: Anna Maria Maiolino.

## Background
At MASP since the 2000s. Known for his "Histórias" series at MASP — deep thematic exhibitions: Histórias da Sexualidade (2017), Afro-Atlantic Histories (2018), Histórias Brasileiras (2022), Histórias da Ecologia (2025). Former co-organizer: Bienal de São Paulo 2006, biennials in Tijuana, San Juan, Istanbul (2011 with Jens Hoffmann). Recipient of Audrey Irmas Award for Curatorial Excellence (2023), Bard Center for Curatorial Studies.

## Why Interesting
His curatorial practice is a systematic redrawing of art history from the Global South. The Venice 2024 thesis — art by outsiders, foreigners, diaspora — is where curating meets political theory. For Amber: the "global art history" frame Pedrosa pioneered at MASP (and tested at Venice) is the institutional counterpart to cosmotechnics / non-Western philosophy in academic discourse.
