# Geof Bowker

STS scholar and information scientist. Co-author (with Susan Leigh Star) of [*Sorting Things Out: Classification and its Consequences*](https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262522953/) (1999, MIT Press) — one of the most cited STS texts, foundational for understanding how classification systems encode power and shape what is visible/invisible. Runs the **Values in Design Laboratory** at UC Irvine's ICS school, adjacent to [[CREATE — Steckler Center for Responsible, Ethical, and Accessible Technology (UCI)]]. PhD from University of Melbourne in History and Philosophy of Science; postdoc at Ecole des Mines, Paris.

## Domains
- Science and Technology Studies (STS)
- Information infrastructure and classification
- Biodiversity informatics, environmental data sharing
- Values in design, sociotechnical systems

## Why Interesting
*Sorting Things Out* directly relevant to Amber's interest in how AI agents and computational systems categorize the world — hidden infrastructures, invisible standards, the politics of classification. The Values in Design Lab extends this into active design research. Discovery via [[CREATE — Steckler Center for Responsible, Ethical, and Accessible Technology (UCI)]].
