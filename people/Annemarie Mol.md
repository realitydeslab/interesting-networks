# Annemarie Mol

Philosopher-anthropologist and post-ANT theorist. Developed "ontological politics" — the idea that realities are multiple, enacted differently across practices. Her ethnography of atherosclerosis (*The Body Multiple*) showed how a single disease is performed differently in clinical settings, producing multiple, partially connected objects. Major influence on feminist STS and care studies.

## Domains
- Post-ANT / ontological politics
- Medical anthropology
- Care and eating practices
- Feminist STS

## Key Works
- [*The Body Multiple: Ontology in Medical Practice*](https://www.dukeupress.edu/the-body-multiple) (2002, Duke UP) — ANT meets ontological turn; reality as multiple
- *The Logic of Care: Health and the Problem of Patient Choice* (2008) — care as practice, not rights

## Why Interesting
Her move from ANT to "ontological politics" matters for AI: when we say an AI system "behaves," which enactment of that behavior are we measuring? AI systems are performed differently in different use contexts — Mol's framework captures this multiplicity. Relevant to agent ethology: are we studying the same "AI" across contexts?
