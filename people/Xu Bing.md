# Xu Bing 徐冰

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Stub created during Chinese Art-Tech Scene deep research.

Major Chinese conceptual artist working at the intersection of language, text, and technology. Created some of the most important Chinese artworks of the 20th-21st centuries. Recently transformed into one of the most significant Chinese artists engaging directly with digital surveillance, AI, and technology critique.

## Background
Born 1955, Chongqing. BA and MFA in printmaking, Central Academy of Fine Arts (CAFA), Beijing. Sent to rural Yunnan province during Cultural Revolution for "re-education" — experience that profoundly shaped his thinking about text, language, and political power. Moved to New York 1990 on MacArthur Fellowship. Former Vice President of CAFA (2010–2014, returned to China). MacArthur "Genius" Fellow. Works across printmaking, installation, video, and public art.

## Key Works

### Language / Text
- **[*Book from the Sky*](https://www.xubing.com/en/work/details/188)** (天书, 1988–1991) — Monumental installation: woodblock-printed books and scrolls covered in thousands of invented Chinese characters that look authentic but are completely meaningless. Took years to carve. Critiques the ideology embedded in written language and the cultural revolution's claims about meaning. One of the most important Chinese artworks of the 20th century.
- ***Square Word Calligraphy*** (英文方块字书法, 1994–ongoing) — System that writes English words in the visual structure of Chinese characters. Transforms how both Chinese and Western viewers experience each language.

### Surveillance / Technology
- ***Dragonfly Eyes*** (蜻蜓之眼, 2017) — Feature-length narrative film composed entirely of CCTV and surveillance footage scraped from the internet. No actors, no crew — only found surveillance footage. A love story assembled from China's ubiquitous surveillance apparatus. One of the most powerful artworks about technological surveillance ever made. Premiered at Toronto International Film Festival (TIFF) 2017.

### Other Key Works
- ***Background Story*** (背后的故事, 2004–ongoing) — Chinese landscape paintings reproduced on the back of glass panels using accumulated plant debris. The landscapes appear as art on the front but are actually mundane materials from behind.
- ***Phoenix*** (凤凰, 2008–2010) — Two enormous phoenix sculptures made from construction debris and discarded workers' tools from Beijing's Olympic building boom. Commissioned for CITIC Plaza atrium; shown at Cathedral of St. John the Divine, NY.

## Awards & Recognition
- **MacArthur Fellowship** (1999) — "Genius" grant
- **Honorary degrees:** Harvard, Columbia, Maryland Institute College of Art
- **First Emperor Award** — British Museum
- Museum collections: MoMA, Guggenheim, British Museum, V&A, Brooklyn Museum, Tate Modern

## For Amber
*Dragonfly Eyes* is directly relevant to Amber's interest in critical computing and surveillance — possibly the most important Chinese artwork about how AI/algorithmic surveillance reshapes narrative and subjectivity. Square Word Calligraphy also raises questions about language-technology interfaces directly relevant to HCI.

## Why Interesting
**Scale of linguistic critique:** Works that expose how writing systems carry power, ideology, and meaning — and what happens when that meaning is destabilized. *Book from the Sky* is foundational for anyone thinking about how systems of representation shape cognition and social organization.

**Surveillance art:** *Dragonfly Eyes* is arguably the most important recent Chinese work engaging with AI/surveillance as artistic subject matter. A narrative film about surveillance cameras, assembled from surveillance cameras — the medium is the message at maximum intensity.
