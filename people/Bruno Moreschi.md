# Bruno Moreschi

Brazilian artist and researcher working at the intersection of artistic research and critical AI/ML studies. His practice investigates Large Scale Vision Datasets (LSVDs) — the millions of scraped images used to train computer vision systems — exposing their postcolonial, extractive, and ideologically laden nature. He transforms research into artworks, exhibitions, didactic formats, and publications, positioning himself at the intersection of institutional critique and computational culture studies.

## Background
- PhD in Arts, State University of Campinas (Unicamp), São Paulo (2019)
- MSc in Arts, Unicamp (2014)
- BA in Communication, Federal University of Santa Catarina (2006)
- Previously: Early-career Fellow, Collegium Helveticum (joint IAS of ETH Zurich, University of Zurich, Zurich University of the Arts)
- LIAS Fellow 2024/25: researching role of digital images in Large Scale Vision Datasets

## Key Work
- **"Beyond First Glance: A Dive into AI/Computer Vision Through the Images in Large Scale Vision Datasets"** — LIAS research project. Analyses training datasets as repositories of dominant ideology and postcolonial extractive logic — millions of images taken without consent from social networks, labelled by microworkers.
- **ZKM "After Memory"** symposium (Oct 2024, Karlsruhe) — presented at this major media art institution
- Co-authored with Gabriel Pereira: "Living with Images from Large-Scale Data Sets: A Critical Pedagogy for Scaling Down" (*photographies*, 2023)
- Co-authored with Pereira, Mintz, Beiguelman: "We've Always Been Antagonistic: Algorithmic Resistances and Dissidences beyond the Global North" (*Media International Australia*, 2022)

## Why Interesting
Moreschi's approach — using artistic and experimental methodologies to crack open the opacity of AI systems, specifically through their training data — is exactly the kind of practice-based AI critique that bridges Amber's computational background with his more-than-human/critical design interests. His emphasis on the Global South perspective on AI training data aligns with LIAS's decolonial turn. The ZKM connection is also notable.
