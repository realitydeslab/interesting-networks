# Kay Gabriel

Trans poet and theorist. Part of the Brooklyn trans bohemia documented by [[McKenzie Wark]] in her 2022 *Document Journal* essay. Author of poetry collections and critical writing that sits at the intersection of trans experience, Marxism, and lyric form. Associated with the milieu of trans writers and artists that emerged in Brooklyn in the 2018–2022 period.

## Key Works
- *Kissing Other People or the House of Fame* (poetry collection) — lyric poems about trans experience, love, and the social body
- *A Queen in Bucks County* (poetry collection)
- Critical essays on trans literature, Marxism, and poetry

## Why Interesting
Gabriel represents the poetic/theoretical wing of the Brooklyn trans bohemia: where [[Torrey Peters]] is primarily a fiction writer and [[McKenzie Wark]] is primarily a theorist, Gabriel bridges poetry and critical theory in a way that's genuinely unusual. The Marxist orientation — theorizing trans experience through class and labor — is a distinctive move that places her in productive tension with queer theory's more idealist tendencies.
