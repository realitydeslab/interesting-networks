# Timothy Morton

Distinguished Professor of English at **UC Davis** (previously Rice University). One of the most influential and controversial philosophers of the contemporary moment — a central figure in **Object-Oriented Ontology (OOO)** and the originator of **Dark Ecology**. Known for coining the term **"hyperobjects"** (entities massively distributed in time and space relative to humans — like climate change, the biosphere, nuclear material) which has become one of the most used concepts in contemporary art and theory.

Their work performs an unusual synthesis: Keats scholarship → Romantic ecology → Deleuze → OOO → Buddhism → queer theory. The result is a philosophy of the uncanny intimacy between humans and their environments — **"dark ecology"** names the fact that ecology is not comfortable coexistence but threatening strangeness. They often write in a register that is deliberately weird, funny, and unsettling.

Identifies as nonbinary (they/them). Their public profile — mixing theory with popular culture references, writing for non-academic audiences, social media presence — makes them one of the few academic philosophers with genuine cultural reach beyond universities.

## Key Works
- *Ecology Without Nature: Rethinking Environmental Aesthetics* (Harvard UP, 2007)
- *The Ecological Thought* (Harvard UP, 2010) — introduces "the mesh" and "dark ecology"
- *Realist Magic: Objects, Ontology, Causality* (Open Humanities Press, 2013) — OOO theory of causality; free OA; in New Metaphysics series
- *Hyperobjects: Philosophy and Ecology after the End of the World* (Minnesota UP, 2013) — coined "hyperobject"; the most widely cited OOO concept
- *Dark Ecology: For a Logic of Future Coexistence* (Columbia UP, 2016) — the philosophy of dark ecology fully developed
- *Humankind: Solidarity with Non-Human People* (Verso, 2017) — Marxist/Buddhist synthesis; solidarity beyond species
- *Being Ecological* (MIT Press / Pelican, 2018) — popular science version
- *All Art Is Ecological* (Penguin, 2021) — art and climate
- *Hyposubjects: On Becoming Human* (OHP, 2021) — co-authored with **Dominic Boyer** (Rice); in CCC2 series; theory of the "hyposubject" as the modest, below-the-surface subject needed after hyperobjects
- *Hell: In Search of a Christian Ecology* (Columbia UP, 2021)
- *Percolate: Lectures on Environmental Aesthetics* (Minnesota UP, 2022)

## Why Interesting
Morton is proof that the weirdest speculative philosophy can reach the broadest cultural audience — *Hyperobjects* is cited everywhere from architecture studios to climate science papers to contemporary art criticism. They make philosophy feel urgent, fun, and slightly terrifying simultaneously. "Hyperobjects" is perhaps the single most influential concept from humanities into contemporary art practice in the past decade. Their *Realist Magic* (free OA from OHP) is the best technical introduction to OOO causality and is directly relevant to any thinking about nonhuman agency in AI art contexts.
