# Julius Eastman (1940–1990)

Queer Black American composer and musician. Pioneer of "militant minimalism" who revolutionised the East Coast new music scene of the 1970s and 1980s. Incorporated racial and homophobic slurs into composition titles as defiant political acts. Subject of [[The Otolith Group]]'s *The Third Part of the Third Measure* (2017). Increasingly recognised posthumously as one of the most radical composers of the 20th century.

## Why Interesting
Radical Black queer compositional practice that anticipated current conversations about identity, sound, and the political in music. Connection to [[The Otolith Group]]'s restaging of his 1980 performance. His phrase about his compositions — "Either I glorify them or they glorify me" — is a model for how artists reclaim hostile language.

## Key Works
- *Evil Nigger* (1979), *Gay Guerrilla* (1979), *Crazy Nigger* (1979) — compositions whose titles were deliberately provocative
- *The Third Part of the Third Measure* speech (1980, Northwestern University) — explained his compositional logic and the political use of slurs
