# Matt Colquhoun

British writer and theorist. Was [[Mark Fisher]]'s student at [[Goldsmiths Visual Cultures]] in the Visual Cultures department. Maintains the k-punk tradition through his blog [Xenogothic](https://xenogothic.com/). A key figure in the ongoing elaboration of Fisher's intellectual legacy. Publishes at [[Repeater Books]].

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Created stub during Repeater Books deep research.

## Background
Studied under Mark Fisher at Goldsmiths. Fisher's death in January 2017 became the subject of Colquhoun's book *Egress* — a theoretical memoir that is simultaneously an act of mourning and a rigorous extension of Fisher's thought. Has since become one of the clearest inheritors of the k-punk tradition.

Blogs at [xenogothic.com](https://xenogothic.com/) in a style directly continuing Fisher's k-punk approach: theory-inflected writing on music, film, politics, and affect.

## Key Works
- **[Egress: On Mourning, Melancholy and Mark Fisher](https://repeaterbooks.com/product/egress-on-mourning-melancholy-and-mark-fisher/)** (Repeater, 2020) — Theoretical memoir on Fisher's death; situates mourning within political and philosophical frameworks
- **[Postcapitalist Desire: The Final Lectures](https://repeaterbooks.com/product/postcapitalist-desire-the-final-lectures/)** (Repeater, 2021) — Editor; transcripts of Fisher's final Goldsmiths lecture series on desire and capitalism
- **[Narcissus in Bloom: An Alternative History of the Selfie](https://repeaterbooks.com/product/narcissus-in-bloom/)** (Repeater) — On photography, self-image, and digital culture
- Xenogothic blog (ongoing) — key primary source for k-punk continuation
