# Alexander Kluge

Germany's most significant living filmmaker, author, and TV producer/broadcaster. One of the founders of New German Cinema (alongside Fassbinder, Herzog, Wenders). Student and intellectual heir of Theodor W. Adorno and the Frankfurt School. Author of extraordinary philosophical-literary prose (collected in thousands of "stories"). Producer/host of late-night cultural TV programming on German commercial channels through DCTP (Development Company for Television Program) — a media theory in practice. Has had extended dialogue with Joseph Vogl on economics and media (*Soll und Haben: Fernsehgespräche*, 2009).

## Domains
- Experimental film (New German Cinema)
- Frankfurt School critical theory
- Philosophical literary prose
- Television as cultural technique
- Political economy critique
- Aesthetics of resistance
- History, trauma, memory (especially WWII and German catastrophe)

## Key Works
- *Abschied von gestern* (Yesterday Girl, 1966) — founding New German Cinema film
- *In Gefahr und größter Not bringt der Mittelweg den Tod* (1974) — co-dir. with Volker Schlöndorff
- *Die Patriotin* (The Patriot, 1979) — collage film on German history
- *Geschichte und Eigensinn* (1981) with **Oskar Negt** — monumental 1,300-page critique of political economy and history
- *Chronik der Gefühle* (2000) — two-volume prose collection
- *Soll und Haben: Fernsehgespräche* (diaphanes, 2009) with **[[Joseph Vogl]]** — TV dialogues on economics, media, sovereignty
- Hundreds of late-night TV programs via DCTP featuring conversations with philosophers, scientists, artists

## Key Relationships
- **[[Joseph Vogl]]** — intellectual collaborator; *Soll und Haben* dialogues
- **Oskar Negt** — long-term collaborator; *Geschichte und Eigensinn*
- **Theodor W. Adorno** — mentor; studied law and music with him in Frankfurt
- **Rainer Werner Fassbinder, Werner Herzog, Wim Wenders** — New German Cinema generation

## Key Communities
- **Frankfurt School** — intellectual formation
- **DCTP** — his own TV production company, pioneering "editorial windows" on commercial German TV
- **New German Cinema** — founding figure with the "Oberhausen Manifesto" (1962)
- **[[diaphanes]]** — his German publisher for theory texts

## Why Interesting
The most important model for how a theorist can operate across multiple media simultaneously: film, prose, television, philosophical dialogue. His DCTP operation is a kind of slow-media institution — 20-minute cultural segments broadcast after midnight on RTL and Sat.1. His practice of philosophical conversation-as-media (with Vogl, Habermas, Badiou, Sloterdijk, and many others) is itself a theoretical gesture. For Amber: Kluge demonstrates how to make radical intellectual work with/in mass media rather than against it.
