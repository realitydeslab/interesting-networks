# Jacob Steinhardt

PhD from Percy Liang's lab at Stanford (2018, co-advised with John Duchi). Now Assistant Professor of Statistics at UC Berkeley and founder of [Transluce](https://transluce.org/) — an AI interpretability and safety startup. One of the most rigorous AI safety researchers working today. Works on the interface of statistical learning theory and AI safety: robustness, distributional shift, forecasting AI capabilities. Famous for his ML progress forecasting ("AI and Compute" work). His "Eliciting Latent Knowledge" work on how to extract what models actually know (vs. what they say) is influential in alignment circles.

## Background
- PhD Statistics/Computer Science, Stanford (2018) — co-advised by [[Percy Liang]] and John Duchi
- Assistant Professor, Statistics, UC Berkeley (2019–present)
- Founder, [Transluce](https://transluce.org/) — AI safety/interpretability

## Key Works
- Distributional robustness / worst-case risk
- ML forecasting: documented the scaling laws of AI capabilities before they became widely known
- "Eliciting Latent Knowledge" — how to ask models questions they can't deceive you on
- "Stronger data poisoning attacks break data sanitization" (with Liang)
- Recent: eliciting language model behaviors with investigator agents (ICML 2025, with Liang, Tatsunori Hashimoto)

## Why Interesting
The intellectual lineage from Liang → Steinhardt → AI safety community is direct. He's the serious theory person doing safety work — not policy-focused, but deeply technical. Transluce is a rare startup doing interpretability research at depth. His forecasting work was predictively accurate about AI timelines in ways that matter.
