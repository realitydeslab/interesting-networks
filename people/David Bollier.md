# David Bollier

American activist, writer, and policy strategist focused on the commons as a paradigm for economics, politics, and culture. Director of the Reinventing the Commons Program at the Schumacher Center for a New Economics. Co-founder of Commons Strategies Group. Berlin Prize 2012. Key collaborator with [[Silke Helfrich]].

## Domains
Commons theory, digital commons, political economy, free culture, open source, commoning as practice.

## Key Works
- [*Free, Fair and Alive: The Insurgent Power of the Commons*](https://www.freefairandalive.org/) (with Silke Helfrich, 2019) — ontological reframe: commoning as verb/practice
- [*Think Like a Commoner*](https://thinklikeacommoner.com/) (2014) — accessible primer
- [*Viral Spiral*](https://archive.org/details/viralspiral_bollier) (2009) — history of Creative Commons and free culture movement
- Podcast: [Frontiers of Commoning](https://podcasts.apple.com/us/podcast/frontiers-of-commoning-with-david-bollier/id1501085005)

## Why Interesting
With Helfrich, reframed commons from economics to ontology ("commoning"). Relevant to Amber's more-than-human design philosophy — both resist enclosure of life into commodities. His "Ontoshift" parallels Amber's interest in relations-over-entities.
