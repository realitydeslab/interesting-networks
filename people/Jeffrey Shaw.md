# Jeffrey Shaw

(Born 1944, Melbourne) Visual artist and pioneer of new media art. Leading figure in expanded cinema, interactive art, virtual reality, augmented reality, and navigable cinematic systems. Born in Melbourne to Polish immigrants. Left Australia in 1965, lived in Milan, London, Amsterdam for 25 years. In 1991 moved to Germany at [[Heinrich Klotz]]'s invitation to become the **founding director of the ZKM Institute for Visual Media** — where he spent 11 years producing some of the most important interactive and VR artworks of the 1990s. Close long-term collaborator of [[Peter Weibel]].

Co-designed the inflatable pig Algie photographed above Battersea Power Station for Pink Floyd's *Animals* album (1977). Professor at HfG Karlsruhe from 1995.

## Why Interesting
Shaw bridged the EARLY history of interactive art (inflatable environments in the late 1960s, Eventstructure Research Group Amsterdam) through to high-tech VR in the 1990s and beyond. His ZKM Institute for Visual Media produced some of the most important interactive art of the period. His collaboration with Peter Weibel (*Future Cinema*, *Net_condition*) is foundational for understanding how ZKM shaped media art theory.

## Key Works (selected)
- **Legible City** (1989) — cycling through a city of text; pioneering interactive VR installation
- **EVE (Extended Virtual Environment)** (1993) — panoramic VR installation
- **The Configuring Eye** (1990)
- **Place: A User's Manual** (1995)
- **conFIGURING the CAVE** (1996–1997, with Agnes Hegedüs, Bernd Lintermann)
- Algie (inflatable pig) — for Pink Floyd *Animals* (1977, with Eventstructure Research Group)

## Key Exhibitions Co-curated
- **Net_condition** (ZKM, 2000) — with Peter Weibel
- **[Future Cinema](https://zkm.de/en)** (ZKM, 2002) — with Peter Weibel; anthology of cinematic imagination after film
