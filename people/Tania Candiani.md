# Tania Candiani

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-27:** Stub created during MUAC deep research.
- **2022:** Major retrospective at [[MUAC]] (Jun 18 – Nov 27, 2022) — sound, tradition, community acoustics.

Mexican artist working at the intersection of **sound, technology, cultural tradition, and community**. Major MUAC retrospective in 2022. Her practice investigates sound as social inscription: how traditions encode community identity, how acoustic practices preserve and transform cultural memory, and how technology mediates these processes.

Key works from MUAC show:
- **Reverencia** (2019) — tradition as code, community as acoustic practice
- **Cónica** (2016–19) — resonance, percussion, community bodies
- **Nombrar el agua** (2019) — global water crisis, drought, community naming practices
- **Cuenca** (2022) — watershed as acoustic/cultural space

Her research-based practice examines sonority's limits and "writings" — what constitutes a sound? How does tradition operate as a system of shared codes that addresses societal interests? Works address environmental concerns (water scarcity) alongside cultural/community questions.
