# Walter Kitundu

Bay Area artist, musician, and instrument inventor. Best known for building **phonoharp bird feeders** — hybrid phonograph/instrument sculptures that birds interact with while feeding, creating music. The work literally makes birds into musicians and birds into collaborators.

Paneled with [[Jenny Odell]] at [[Eyeo Festival]] 2017 ("For The Birds"), moderated by [[Jer Thorp]]. MacArthur Fellow (2008).

## Why Interesting
His practice is one of the most literal explorations of more-than-human creative agency: birds as co-composers, non-human agents participating in aesthetic production. Directly relevant to Amber's interests in agent ethology and more-than-human design. The phonoharp is an interface between biological and mechanical systems — exactly the kind of hybrid that interests Amber. MacArthur genius grant indicates serious institutional recognition.
