# Cameron Rowland

Conceptual artist whose work traces the material afterlives of slavery through objects, legal contracts, and institutional critique. Collaborated with **[[Saidiya Hartman]]** on the 2022 revised edition of *Scenes of Subjection*, contributing notations and compositions that function as "crib sheets" for the text.

## ⚡ Recent Updates

## Background
Based in New York. Shows primarily at **Miguel Abreu Gallery**, New York.

## Key Works
- **"91020000"** (MoMA PS1, 2016) — debut solo show; traced the use of convict labor in manufacturing, presenting objects made by incarcerated people in New York State alongside legal documents. Extraordinary.
- **Notations and compositions in *Scenes of Subjection*** (25th Anniversary edition, W.W. Norton, 2022) — visual-conceptual annotations to Hartman's text

## Key Connections
- **[[Saidiya Hartman]]** — collaborator on *Scenes* 2022 edition
- **[[Torkwase Dyson]]** — fellow visual contributor to *Scenes* 2022
- Miguel Abreu Gallery; MoMA PS1

## Why Interesting
Rowland's work is a perfect example of how contemporary art can be a form of legal/historical research. His approach to objects and contracts parallels Hartman's approach to the archive. The collaboration itself is a model for scholar-artist collaboration.
