# Michael Marder

Ikerbasque Research Professor of Philosophy at the University of the Basque Country (UPV/EHU). The originator of **plant philosophy** in contemporary continental thought — his *Plant-Thinking* (2013) argued for vegetal being as a philosophical problem that challenges animocentric and anthropocentric assumptions in phenomenology, ethics, and political philosophy.

His work sits at the intersection of Heidegger, phenomenology, ecology, and Derrida — but applied to plants as philosophical subjects. Not "plants have rights" (utilitarian) but "plant-being restructures our understanding of life, time, and subjectivity."

## Key Works (Selected)

- [*Plant-Thinking: A Philosophy of Vegetal Life*](https://cup.columbia.edu/book/plant-thinking/9780231161329) (Columbia University Press, 2013) — the foundational text; Heideggerian analysis of vegetal existence; reframes temporality, intentionality, and life through the lens of plants
- [*The Philosopher's Plant: An Intellectual Herbarium*](https://cup.columbia.edu/book/the-philosophers-plant/9780231168090) (Columbia University Press, 2014) — 12 philosophers, 12 plants; philosophy's repressed vegetal figures
- [*Through Vegetal Being: Two Philosophical Perspectives*](https://cup.columbia.edu/book/through-vegetal-being/9780231172677) (Columbia University Press, 2016) — co-authored with Luce Irigaray; plant philosophy meets feminist phenomenology
- [*The Chernobyl Herbarium: Fragments of an Exploded Consciousness*](https://openhumanitiespress.org/books/titles/the-chernobyl-herbarium/) (Open Humanities Press, 2016) — **FREE OA** — with photographs by Anaïs Tondeur; plants growing in the Chernobyl exclusion zone as philosophical figures; nuclear aftermath + vegetal persistence
- *Dust* (Bloomsbury, 2016) — philosophy of dust as elemental
- [*The Philosophical Salon*](https://openhumanitiespress.org/books/titles/the-philosophical-salon/) (Open Humanities Press, 2016) — with Patricia Vieira; philosophical essays in accessible form
- *Pyropolitics: When the World Is Ablaze* (Rowman & Littlefield, 2015)
- *General Ecology: The New Ecological Paradigm* (Bloomsbury, 2021) — edited volume; ecological philosophy
- *Plants in Place: A Phenomenology of the Vegetal* (Columbia University Press, 2023) — with Edward S. Casey

## Why Interesting

*Plant-Thinking* offers the most rigorous philosophical account of **vegetal being** available — and it does so without anthropomorphizing (plants don't "feel" or "decide" in human ways; that's the point). For Amber: plant philosophy opens onto questions about life without consciousness, agency without will, behavior without intention — all directly relevant for thinking about machine behavior and more-than-human design. The Chernobyl Herbarium is also a beautiful art-theory collaboration (photographs + philosophy) that models a different kind of art-science hybrid practice.

Also: Marder's work is widely cited in the Critical Animal Studies, vegan theory, and ecological humanities communities — these are important adjacent communities for more-than-human design that Amber might not otherwise encounter.
