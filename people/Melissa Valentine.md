# Melissa Valentine

Organizational behavior researcher and co-author with [[Michael Bernstein]] on the Flash Teams research program. Studies how technology — especially AI — reshapes organizational structures, teamwork, and labor. Professor at Darden School of Business (UVA) after Stanford.

## Background
- PhD from Stanford HCI Group under Michael Bernstein
- Research interest: AI and the future of organizational forms — specifically how AI enables new kinds of on-demand, flexible work structures
- Now at Darden School of Business, University of Virginia

## Key Works
- **[*Flash Teams: Leading the Future of AI-Enhanced, On-Demand Work*](https://www.amazon.com/Flash-Teams-Leading-AI-Enhanced-Demand/dp/0262049848)** (MIT Press 2025, **national bestseller**) — Co-authored with Bernstein. The definitive book on AI and the future of work.
- **[Flash Teams](https://hci.stanford.edu/publications/2014/flashteams/flashteams-uist2014.pdf)** (UIST 2014, **Best Paper**) — Original flash teams paper with Bernstein.
- **[Flash Organizations](https://hci.stanford.edu/publications/2017/flashorgs/flash-orgs-chi-2017.pdf)** (CHI 2017, **Best Paper**) — Successor work on on-demand organizational scaling.
- Multiple CSCW 2024 papers on algorithmic management and classification schemes in healthcare.

## Why Interesting
The Flash Teams book is a national bestseller about AI reshaping work — a rare case of HCI/organizational research crossing over to a general audience. Her work on how algorithmic systems change labor conditions connects to Amber's interest in the political economy of AI. Worth tracking for her perspective on organizational behavior + AI systems.
