# Eduardo Kac

Brazilian-American artist and theoretical pioneer who coined the term "bio art" and established transgenic art as a field. Professor of Art at SAIC. Created landmark works including GFP Bunny (Alba, 2000), Genesis (1999), Natural History of the Enigma (2003-2008), and space artworks. His practice spans telepresence, robotics, biotechnology, genetics, and space exploration — bridging art, science, and ethics in ways that define the field.

## Background
- Born 1962 in Rio de Janeiro, became fluent in English as a child. Started as performance artist in Rio (1980), participating in the radical **Movimento de Arte Pornô** under Brazil's military dictatorship.
- **Education trajectory:** BA Communications (Rio, 1985) → MFA SAIC (1990) → PhD Planetary Collegium/University of Wales (2003). This interdisciplinary path explains his unique theoretical sophistication.
- **Career arc:** Performance (1980s Rio) → Digital/Telecommunications art (1980s-90s) → Telepresence/Robotics (1990s) → Bio Art (late 1990s-present) → Space Art (ongoing). Each phase built foundational techniques for the next.
- Moved from Rio to Chicago (1989) where he's remained, building SAIC into a major bio art center.

## Domains
**Transgenic Art** — genetic engineering as artistic medium (GFP Bunny, Natural History of the Enigma)
**Telepresence Art** — robotic systems bridging physical locations (Ornitorrinco, 1989)
**Space Art** — artworks designed for zero gravity and deep space (Inner Telescope, Adsum)
**Holopoetry** — holographic interactive poetry (invented 1983, 23 works)
**Bio Robotics** — functional merger of robotics and biotechnology
**Interspecies Communication** — art systems including plants and animals as participants
**Telecommunications Art** — fax, slow-scan TV, early internet (1980s-90s)

## Key Works
**[Genesis](https://www.ekac.org/genesis.html)** (1999) — Biblical verse translated to Morse code then DNA, introduced into bacteria. Participants use UV light to mutate the DNA, changing the text when translated back. Won [[Ars Electronica]] Golden Nica 2008. The work that established transgenic art.

**[GFP Bunny](https://www.ekac.org/gfpbunny.html)** (2000) — Alba, an albino rabbit with jellyfish green fluorescent protein gene, glows green under blue light. Created with French laboratory. Sparked global ethical debate, never released to Kac's family as planned. Referenced in *Big Bang Theory*, *Sherlock*, *The Simpsons*, Margaret Atwood's *Oryx and Crake*, Michael Crichton's *Next*.

**[Natural History of the Enigma](https://www.ekac.org/enigma.html)** (2003-2008) — "Plantimal" hybrid of Kac's DNA with petunia, called Edunia. Red veins mimic human blood flow. Golden Nica winner, permanent collections worldwide.

**[Inner Telescope](https://www.ekac.org/inner.html)** (2017) — Paper sculpture created by French astronaut [[Thomas Pesquet]] on International Space Station. Reads as "M-O-I" (French for "me") or stylized human figure with umbilical cord cut.

**[Adsum](https://www.ekac.org/adsum-new.html)** (2019-present) — Five-phase lunar artwork project. Glass with laser-engraved symbols (hourglass, Earth/Moon circles, infinity). Prepared for Moon mission.

**[Time Capsule](https://www.ekac.org/timecaps.html)** (1997) — Performance where Kac implanted RFID chip (pet tracker) in himself, registered on pet database as first human. Live on TV and internet. Coined "bio art" term.

**Ornitorrinco** (1989) — Early telepresence robot allowing remote control of camera/motion across locations. Collaboration with [[Ed Bennett]].

**Teleporting An Unknown State** (1994) — Plant in gallery survives via webcam light from distant cities, transmitted by participants. Early "internet of things" art.

**Monogram** (1996) — Space artwork included on DVD attached to Cassini spacecraft mission to Saturn.

## Research Taste Analysis

**Conceptual Strategy — Field Declaration:**
Kac operates via **terminological empire building** — he doesn't just make works, he coins the terms that define entire fields ("bio art," "transgenic art," "plantimal," "biorobotics"). Each new term creates intellectual territory he can occupy and expand.

**Career Architecture — Progressive Complexity:**
Unlike artists who find a medium and stick to it, Kac uses each phase to enable more ambitious next phases:
- Performance (1980s) → learned body/ethics questions
- Digital/telecommunications (1980s-90s) → learned networked systems  
- Telepresence/robotics (1990s) → learned remote embodiment
- Bio art (1990s-2000s) → learned genetic/biological systems
- Space art (2000s-present) → learned institutional collaboration at highest levels

Each phase builds technical and institutional capacity for the next. By 2017, he can casually collaborate with ESA astronauts because he spent decades building credibility.

**Methodology Signature — Ethics as Spectacle:**
Kac's breakthrough insight: **make the ethics the artwork**. GFP Bunny's power isn't the glowing rabbit — it's forcing global debate about genetic modification ethics. Genesis makes participants complicit in genetic mutation. The controversy **is** the artistic material.

**Network Strategy — Institution Building:**
Rather than remaining in art contexts, Kac deliberately builds relationships with science institutions (NASA, French Space Agency, INRA labs). This gives him access to technologies and platforms impossible for artists operating only in art institutions.
