# Sougwen Chung (鍾愫君)

Canadian-Chinese artist. Pioneer of human-machine collaborative drawing. Works with robotic arms trained on their drawing style.

## Key Books/Papers
- D.O.U.G. (Drawing Operations Unit: Generation) series — robots that draw with/alongside her
- "Omnia per Omnia" — multi-robot collaboration
- Former researcher at MIT Media Lab

## Key Communities
- MIT Media Lab — former researcher
- London art-tech scene

## Key Venues

- Barbican, V&A
- MoMA
- TED — speaker

## Key Relationships
- MIT Media Lab alumni network
- [[Holly Herndon]] — human-AI collaboration peer (music vs. visual)
- → Explore: Patrick Tresset (robotic drawing, RIP)

## Why Interesting
The most compelling artist doing human-robot collaborative creation. D.O.U.G. is not a tool — it's a collaborator that has been trained on Chung's own gestures. This is genuinely more-than-human art practice. Canadian-Chinese identity connects to the Asian diaspora tech-art scene.
