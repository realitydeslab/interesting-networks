# Sougwen Chung (鍾愫君)

Canadian-Chinese artist and pioneer of human-machine collaboration. Creates performative drawing installations with robots trained on decades of their own gestural data. Founder of SCILICET. 38 years old.

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Initial deep research. Created comprehensive note with full profile.

## Background
- Born 1986 in Toronto, Canada. Canadian-Chinese heritage, raised between Toronto and Hong Kong.
- Father was an opera singer who ensured musical training from young age — grew up playing violin and piano. This musical performance background fundamentally shapes their approach to drawing as gesture and embodied action.
- Moved to US as teenager for education.
- **BFA, [Indiana University Bloomington](https://www.indiana.edu/)** — traditional fine arts foundation
- **Masters Diploma in Interactive Art, [Hyper Island](https://www.hyperisland.com/), Sweden** — experimental digital arts education
- Now based in London, runs company **SCILICET**
- Recently discovered one of their ancestors in the first generation of Chungs from Hunan was a prominent calligrapher to the emperor — generational connection to mark-making traditions

## Domains
**Human-Machine Collaborative Art:** Develops robotic systems (D.O.U.G. series) that draw with them in real-time performances. Not automation but genuine collaboration.

**Drawing as Performance:** Live drawing sessions where human and robot co-create, exploring gesture, memory, and mark-making.

**Computational Memory Research:** Uses machine learning trained on personal artistic archive to explore questions of human/machine memory and temporality.

**Biofeedback Art:** Recent work incorporates EEG headsets to translate meditative states and brain activity into robotic movement.

**Research Practice:** Former MIT Media Lab researcher, current Bell Labs Artist-in-Residence.

## Why Interesting
Sougwen Chung represents the most sophisticated approach to human-machine collaboration in contemporary art. Unlike artists who use AI as a tool, Chung develops genuine co-creative partnerships where agency is distributed between human and machine. The D.O.U.G. series evolves from simple mimicry to complex forms of shared consciousness via biofeedback and computational memory.

Their work directly addresses questions central to Amber's research: What constitutes agency? How do different forms of intelligence collaborate? What does it mean to be more-than-human? The rigorous research approach (MIT Media Lab, Bell Labs) combined with deep performance practice creates art that is both technically sophisticated and philosophically profound.

The Canadian-Chinese identity adds crucial perspective on how non-Western approaches to consciousness and embodiment inform technological development. Their discovery of ancestral connections to imperial Chinese calligraphy suggests deep cultural continuity in understanding mark-making as embodied practice.

## Research Taste Analysis

### Conceptual Strategy
**Embodied Materialism** — Chung grounds abstract concepts (AI, consciousness, memory) in physical gesture and mark-making. Rather than purely digital art, they insist on materiality: ink on paper, robot arms in space, human bodies in performance.

**Recursive Training** — Uses their own artistic archive as training data, creating feedback loops where the machine learns from human, human learns from machine, and new collaborative works become new training data. This recursive approach generates genuinely emergent behaviors.

**Performance as Research** — Treats live drawing performances as research methodology. The unpredictability, timing, and audience presence become essential elements of the investigation rather than just documentation of pre-existing ideas.

### Career Architecture
**Institution Bridge-Building** — Simultaneously embedded in tech research (MIT Media Lab, Bell Labs) and art institutions (V&A, New Museum). This dual positioning allows genuine two-way translation between fields.

**Platform Creation** — Founded SCILICET as creative studio/research platform. This institutional independence allows experimental work without academic or corporate constraints.

**Recognition Diversification** — Builds credibility across multiple recognition systems: art world (museum acquisitions), tech world (TIME AI lists), academic world (research fellowships), media world (TED talks).

### Methodology Signature
**Multi-Generational Systems** — Doesn't just create one robotic collaborator but develops generations (D.O.U.G._1 through 6) that each explore different aspects of human-machine relationships. This longitudinal approach allows deep investigation of evolving partnerships.

**Biometric Integration** — Incorporates EEG headsets, meditation, and biofeedback to explore non-conscious forms of collaboration. This pushes beyond intentional gesture into autonomic and meditative states as creative input.

**Memory as Medium** — Uses personal artistic archive as both material and subject matter. The 20-year dataset becomes a way to collaborate with their own past selves through machine mediation.

### Network Strategy
**Cross-Cultural Positioning** — Canadian-Chinese identity allows movement between North American tech scene and Asian art-tech communities. Recent discovery of calligrapher ancestor provides deep cultural grounding for contemporary practice.

**Industrial Embedding** — Artist-in-residence positions at actual technology development sites (Bell Labs, MIT Media Lab) rather than just art institutions. This provides access to cutting-edge tools and genuine collaboration with researchers.

**Performance Circuit** — Uses live drawing performances at major conferences (TED, SXSW) as both artistic practice and network building. Combines intimate artistic process with public engagement.
