# Carl DiSalvo

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Stub created — prominent DIS Critical Computing contributor.

Professor at Georgia Tech's Digital Media program. Pioneer of "adversarial design" and "agonistic design" — making things that create friction, controversy, and political conflict rather than consensus. Key voice in DIS Critical Computing community.

## Why Interesting
DiSalvo's adversarial/agonistic design framework provides a vocabulary for design that takes power seriously. His DIS 2024 paper "Counting Up: Designing Agonistic Data Collection in the Court Room" uses Mouffe's agonistic political theory as design method. Relevant to Amber's interest in critical computing as political practice.

## Key Works
- *Adversarial Design* (2012, MIT Press) — foundational text
- ["Counting Up: Designing Agonistic Data Collection in the Court Room"](https://doi.org/10.1145/3643834.3661616) (DIS 2024) — with Tran
- "Designing within Capitalism" (DIS 2022) — with Wolf, Asad, Dombrowski
