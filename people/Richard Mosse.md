# Richard Mosse

Irish photographer and filmmaker who uses military/industrial imaging technology — infrared film, thermal cameras, LiDAR — to document humanitarian crises and environmental destruction. His work repurposes surveillance technology for critical witness. Known for: *Infra* (infrared film in Congo conflict zones), *Enclave* (infrared video, Congo), *Heat Maps* (thermal imaging of refugee movements in Europe), *Broken Spectre* (Amazon deforestation). Represented by [[Jack Shainman Gallery]].

## Why Interesting
Mosse repurposes military imaging technology — tools designed for killing — to bear witness to environmental and humanitarian catastrophe. This is exactly the kind of "technology turned against power" move that intersects with Amber's critical computing interests. He uses thermal cameras to make Amazon deforestation viscerally visible. The technological aesthetic becomes political content.

## Key Works
- *Infra* (2010-2011) — infrared film documenting Congo conflict
- *Enclave* (2013) — infrared multi-channel video installation
- *Heat Maps* (2016-2017) — thermal imaging of European refugee crisis
- *Broken Spectre* (2018-2022) — 20m panoramic film of Amazon deforestation, shown at Jack Shainman Tribeca
