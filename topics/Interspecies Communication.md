# Interspecies Communication

The project of decoding, bridging, and designing communication *across species boundaries*. Encompasses bioacoustics, cognitive ethology, AI-augmented animal language research, and emerging speculative / design approaches to non-human agency. In 2024–2026, AI has fundamentally changed what seems possible: from sperm whale coda decoding to foundation models for animal vocalizations, this is now a live scientific frontier with real philosophical urgency.

---

## 🗺 Domain Summary

**Interspecies communication** sits at the intersection of:
- **Cognitive ethology** — studying animal minds, intentionality, and communication
- **Bioacoustics** — recording and analysing animal vocalizations, ultrasound, infrasound
- **Animal communication science** — semiotic analysis of signals (vocal, chemical, gestural, visual)
- **AI/ML for animal decoding** — applying LLMs, self-supervised audio models, and multimodal ML to non-human signals
- **Multispecies design / more-than-human design** — designing tools, interfaces, and experiences that include non-human participants

**Historical arc:**
- 1927: Karl von Frisch decodes honey bee waggle dance — first symbolic communication found in another species
- 1960s–70s: Dolphin and primate language experiments (John Lilly with dolphins; Gardner & Gardner teaching Washoe sign language)
- 1967: Roger Payne discovers humpback whale songs — cultural meaning in complex vocalizations
- 1976: Donald Griffin's *The Question of Animal Awareness* — cognitive ethology as a field
- 1984: Katy Payne discovers elephant infrasound — long-range invisible communication
- 1990s–2000s: Kanzi the bonobo (Sue Savage-Rumbaugh) uses lexigrams; Alex the parrot (Irene Pepperberg) demonstrates conceptual understanding
- 2013: TED talk "An Interspecies Internet?" (Vint Cerf, Peter Gabriel, Neil Gershenfeld, Diana Reiss)
- 2017–present: Earth Species Project founded — applying ML to decode animal communication at scale
- 2020: Project CETI (Cetacean Translation Initiative) launched — sperm whale coda decoding via ML + robotics
- 2024: NatureLM-audio released — first large audio-language foundation model for bioacoustics
- 2024–2025: Wild elephant "name-calling" confirmed; sperm whale cultural dialects decoded; AI reveals bat communication structure

**Core theoretical tensions:**
1. **Language vs. communication**: Do non-human species have *language* (referential, compositional, recursive) or only *communication*? The goalposts keep moving as AI reveals new complexity.
2. **Decoding vs. translation**: Can we *understand* animal signals or only statistically correlate them with human labels?
3. **Umwelt problem** (Jakob von Uexküll): Each species inhabits a perceptual world shaped by its senses — can we ever truly cross that boundary?
4. **Anthropomorphism vs. anthropocentrism**: Between over-projecting human categories and refusing to grant non-human intelligence its due.
5. **Two-way communication**: Most current research is passive listening; active *dialogue* with animals remains aspirational.

## ⚡ Edge of Chaos Analysis

**Is it fast-evolving?** Yes — this is currently one of the fastest-moving intersections of AI and biology.

**What's driving it?**
- **Foundation models applied to bioacoustics**: Large-scale audio encoders (like Earth Species Project's AVES and NatureLM-audio) can now perform zero-shot classification across species — enabling rapid cross-species analysis
- **Data infrastructure**: Massive acoustic archives (Xeno-canto for birds, Watkins Marine Mammal Database, etc.) becoming ML-ready datasets
- **Sensor hardware**: Animal-worn sensor tags (CETI Tag, MIT CSAIL open-source) enabling rich multimodal data collection in the wild
- **Sperm whale CETI project**: Major international collaboration (MIT, Oxford, Project CETI) generating sperm whale coda datasets — 2026 expected to yield structural linguistic findings
- **Funding surge**: TED Audacious Project, xRSS grants, conservation technology funding all flowing in
- **Philosophical urgency**: Climate crisis + species extinction creates political pressure to understand and include non-humans in decision-making

**Estimated horizon for major breakthroughs:** 2–5 years for structural insights; genuine bidirectional communication probably 10+ years.

## 🔬 Sub-Topics & Trends (2022–2026)

### 1. AI Bioacoustic Foundation Models
- **NatureLM-audio** (Earth Species Project, 2024): First large audio-language model for animal sounds. Trained on bioacoustic archives + speech + music. Zero-shot species classification, call-type prediction, bio-audio captioning. arXiv: [2411.07186](https://arxiv.org/abs/2411.07186). DOI: 10.48550/arXiv.2411.07186
- **AVES** (Earth Species Project, 2022): Self-supervised transformer encoder for animal vocalizations. Outperforms supervised baselines. arXiv: [2210.14493](https://arxiv.org/abs/2210.14493)
- **BEANS / BEANS-Zero benchmarks**: New standardized benchmarks for zero-shot bioacoustic evaluation

### 2. Sperm Whale Communication (Project CETI)
- Sperm whales communicate via *codas* — patterns of clicks organized into combinatorial sequences. They form social *clans* distinguished by distinct coda repertoires (cultural dialects)
- **Project CETI** (Cetacean Translation Initiative): Multi-year international project deploying AI-powered hydrophones and whale-worn sensor tags to record and decode Caribbean sperm whale communication. Key collaborators: David Gruber, Michael Bronstein (**Oxford**! ⭐ proximity), Shane Gero, Daniela Rus (MIT), Max Tegmark (MIT)
- **CETI Tag** (MIT CSAIL, open-source): Animal-worn sensor platform for cetacean bioacoustic research — [ceti-tag.csail.mit.edu](https://ceti-tag.csail.mit.edu)
- 2024: Structural analysis of sperm whale coda revealed combinatorial richness comparable in complexity to simple human syllables

### 3. Elephant Name-Calling
- 2024, *Nature Ecology & Evolution*: Wild African elephants use specific vocalizations to address individual conspecifics — analogous to personal names. First evidence of name-calling in non-human species that wasn't learned from humans. Demonstrates higher social cognition.

### 4. Multimodal Animal Communication
- Beyond vocalizations: chemical signaling (ants, bees, plants), electrical field communication (electric fish), infrasound (elephants, whales), ultraviolet visual signals (bees, birds), magnetic sensing (birds)
- Interest in cross-modal translation: can AI mediate signals between modalities?

### 5. Human-Animal Interface Design
- "Buttons for dogs" movement (Christina Hunger's AAC work, DogFACS) — speculative but generating serious ethological debate
- **Interspecies Internet** (Vint Cerf, Peter Gabriel, Neil Gershenfeld, Diana Reiss): think-tank exploring networked animal-computer interfaces — [interspecies.io](https://www.interspecies.io). Founded 2013, 4500+ members globally
- Bee communication interfaces: projects using AI to interpret bee waggle dances for human audiences

### 6. Ethics & Epistemics of AI-Mediated Interspecies Communication
- Growing literature on ethical implications: A. Küçükuncular (2025) "Ethical implications of AI-mediated interspecies communication" (*AI and Ethics*). DOI: 10.1007/s43681-025-00828-z
- Questions: Who speaks *for* animals? Can decoded communication confer legal standing? What does "translation" mean when there's no shared intentionality?

### 7. Speculative Design & Arts
- Artists working at this interface: Interspecies Internet lecture series, Ars Electronica Bioart section, various speculative design projects
- A. Krauth (2025) "AI and Care for Other (ed) Animals: Art, machine learning, and interspecies futures" — *Leonardo* (MIT Press). DOI: 10.1162/leon_a_02674

### Why This Topic is Directly Relevant

Amber's work sits at the centre of this topic's most interesting tensions:

1. **Agent ethology** — Amber studies how agents (artificial) communicate, behave, and form social patterns. Interspecies communication asks the inverse: how do *biological* agents communicate in ways we've never fully understood. These mirror each other perfectly. AI agents as "a new species" whose communication we also can't fully parse = direct parallel.

2. **More-than-human design** — Designing tools and interfaces that include non-human participants is Amber's design research program. Interspecies communication is where this program becomes literal: designing for actual non-human species.

3. **ALife** — The evolution of communication systems, emergence of signal conventions, cultural transmission across whale clans — these are exactly the ALife dynamics Amber is exploring computationally. Real whale cultural dialects = empirical grounding for ALife communication models.

4. **Machine behavior / non-human agency** — Interspecies communication research is fundamentally about taking non-human agency seriously. Amber's work on machine behavior is philosophically parallel.

5. **Open-endedness** — Earth Species Project explicitly asks: can we build models that generalize to *any* species, including those we haven't studied? This is the biological version of Amber's open-endedness research.
