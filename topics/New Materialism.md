# New Materialism

New Materialism is a philosophical movement reorienting how we think about matter, agency, and the world. It rejects representationalism, essentialism, and anthropocentrism — dissolving the old binaries of nature/culture, subject/object, human/nonhuman. Matter is not passive stuff waiting for human minds to give it meaning: it is active, generative, and agential. The term was independently coined by **[[Manuel DeLanda]]** and **[[Rosi Braidotti]]** in the late 1990s, drawing on Deleuze/Guattari and Simondon to forge a materialist ontology centred on process, individuation, and intra-action rather than fixed substances. For Amber, this is a directly adjacent philosophical infrastructure for more-than-human design, machine behavior as ethology, and cosmotechnics — especially the question of what it means for AI systems to *matter*.

## Domain Summary

New Materialism emerged in the 1990s-2000s as a response to the "linguistic turn" in humanities — the idea that language/discourse is the primary lens for understanding social reality. New materialists argued this left actual material forces undertheorized: bodies, technologies, organisms, energies, climates — these things don't just passively receive meaning; they *act*.

**Key theoretical pillars:**

1. **Agential Realism** (Karen Barad): Derived from Niels Bohr's quantum physics. Reality is constituted through *intra-action* — entities don't pre-exist their relations, they emerge through them. The apparatus of observation is part of what's observed. Published in *Meeting the Universe Halfway* (2007, Duke). Hugely influential in STS, feminist theory, education.

2. **Vibrant Matter / Thing-Power** (Jane Bennett): Draws on Spinoza, Deleuze, Latour. Things have vitality — a kind of agency or force of their own. *Vibrant Matter* (2010, Duke) asks: what if we took seriously the actancy of nonhuman things? Food, electricity, trash — all have political effects.

3. **Posthuman Nomadic Subject** (Rosi Braidotti): Combines feminist theory, Deleuze, and Spinoza to argue that the "human" was always a specific (white, male, propertied, European) construction. Posthumanism means embracing a new figuration of subjectivity: nomadic, multiple, transversal. *The Posthuman* (2013, Polity).

4. **Deleuze-Guattari derived materialism** (Manuel DeLanda): Non-organic life, intensive processes, realist ontology (assemblage theory). *Intensive Science and Virtual Philosophy* (2002). More rigorous philosophically; less popular with humanities audiences.

5. **Ecology of Practices** (Isabelle Stengers): Matter and knowledge are entangled in practices. From her cosmopolitics. Emphasizes *slowing down* — not imposing universal frameworks but attending to the specific demands of each practice.

6. **Meshwork / Materials** (Tim Ingold): Against hylomorphism (form imposed on passive matter). Materials have their own properties, flows, tendencies. Building, making, dwelling are *with* materials, not *on* them. *The Life of Lines* (2015). Huge influence in design research.

7. **Feminist STS / situated knowledges** (Donna Haraway): Situated, partial, embodied knowledge. "Making kin, not babies." *Staying with the Trouble* (2016). Speculative fabulation. The tentacular Chthulucene vs the Anthropocene.

**Philosophical lineages:**
- Deleuze & Guattari (Anti-Oedipus, A Thousand Plateaus, What is Philosophy?)
- Spinoza (single substance, modes, affects)
- Whitehead (process philosophy, actual occasions)
- Simondon (individuation, technical objects)
- Marxist materialism (but moving beyond economic determinism)
- Phenomenology (Merleau-Ponty, Heidegger) — but criticized for lingering anthropocentrism

## Edge of Chaos Analysis

**Is this edge of chaos?** YES — but the edge has moved. The founding texts (Barad 2007, Bennett 2010, Braidotti 2013) are now classics being *applied* rather than just theorized. The field is now in an expansive application phase.

**Where is it hot right now (2023-2026):**

1. **Computational/Digital New Materialism**: How do algorithms, data centres, cloud infrastructures have their own material agency? Following Parikka's *A Geology of Media* (2015), researchers are asking what it means for AI to be material. Media materialism meets new materialism. Kittler, Parikka, Starosielski.

2. **More-than-Human Design**: Directly applying new materialist thinking to design practice. Wakkary (*Things We Could Design*, 2021), Lindström & Ståhl, Bardzell. This is the HCI hotspot. CHI, DIS, RTD increasingly publish this work.

3. **Multispecies / Planetary**: Tsing's mushrooms, van Dooren's bird extinctions, Puig de la Bellacasa's "matters of care" — new materialism meeting ecology and multispecies ethics.

4. **Decolonial Turn**: Scholars like Juanita Sundberg, Zoe Todd (Métis), and the Indigenous STS community challenging new materialism's Western bias. Is Barad's quantum physics just another Western universalism? This is a live debate.

5. **AI & Agency**: Applying intra-action theory to machine agency — what does it mean for LLMs to "act"? New materialist HCI researchers are rethinking AI as agential more-than-human systems rather than mere tools.

6. **Care & Repair**: Puig de la Bellacasa's *Matters of Care* (2017) and repair studies (Jackson, Henke) — material practices of maintaining/caring for things.

**What's cooling**: Pure philosophical theory-building (the Barad exegesis machine) is maturing. The field is moving toward empirical-theoretical hybrids.

**Timeline to hot**: Already hot in design/STS. The HCI application wave is 2023-2028. Now is the time to plant a flag.

## Sub-topics & Trends (last 3-5 years)

**1. More-than-Human Computing** (⭐⭐⭐ for Amber)
Directly drawing on Haraway, Barad, and Bennett to redesign computing systems that aren't just human-centered. [[Ron Wakkary]]'s lab, [[Phoebe Sengers]], Anna Vallgårda (ITU Copenhagen). Key venue: DIS, CHI workshops, TEI. Papers at CHI 2023, 2024 on posthumanist HCI.

**2. Matters of Care & Repair**
María Puig de la Bellacasa's *Matters of Care* (2017) has seeded a whole subfield around caring practices, infrastructure maintenance, and repair as political ethics. Connects to Amber's care ethics work.

**3. Feminist Posthumanism**
Braidotti's Posthumanities Hub (Utrecht), Cecilia Åsberg's work at Linköping. *The Posthuman Glossary* (2018, Bloomsbury) is the reference map of the field. Feminist posthumanism is the theoretical backbone of more-than-human design.

**4. Speculative Fabulation / Making Kin**
Haraway's "SF" (speculative fabulation, string figures, science fiction). Becoming-with, sympoiesis. Directly applicable to speculative design. Scholars like Vinciane Despret, Thom van Dooren.

**5. Material Engagement Theory**
Lambros Malafouris's *How Things Shape the Mind* (2013) — extended cognition meets new materialism. Agency distributed across human-material networks. Hot in archaeology and cognitive science.

**6. Geology of Media / Computational Materialism**
[[Jussi Parikka]]'s work on media archaeology and the material substrates of media technologies. Rare earth minerals, data centres, electronic waste. Bridging media theory and new materialism.

**7. Cosmotechnical Pluralism (connection to Yuk Hui)**
[[Cosmotechnics]] (Yuk Hui) and new materialism both reject the universality of "the" nature or "the" technology. Non-Western materialisms (Chinese qi, Indigenous ontologies) as alternative material philosophies. Hui's dialogue with Stengers and Latour is directly relevant here.

3. **Does new materialism have politics?** Andreas Malm's critique: by dissolving the human/nature distinction, do we lose the ability to hold corporations responsible for climate destruction? The political stakes are live.

5. **Can new materialism inform responsible AI governance?** If AI systems are agential intra-actions rather than mere tools, how does this change liability, design ethics, accountability?
