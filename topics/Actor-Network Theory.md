# Actor-Network Theory

Actor-Network Theory (ANT) is a material-semiotic framework from Science and Technology Studies (STS) that dissolves the distinction between human and nonhuman actors. In ANT, social relations are not pre-given structures but emergent, fragile accomplishments — networks continuously assembled through the actions of humans, artifacts, concepts, institutions, and technologies treated with strict ontological symmetry. For Amber, ANT is a foundational vocabulary: it is the theoretical infrastructure underneath [[Machine Behavior]], [[More-than-human Design]], and the nascent science of AI agent ethology.

## Domain Summary

ANT was developed in the early 1980s at the [Centre de Sociologie de l'Innovation (CSI)](https://www.minesparis.psl.eu/Recherche/Centres-de-recherche/Centre-de-sociologie-de-linnovation-CSI/) at the École des Mines de Paris by **Bruno Latour**, **Michel Callon**, **Madeleine Akrich**, and (as a visitor) **John Law**. Its founding impulse was empirical: how do scientific facts and technologies actually get stabilized? The answer was unsettling — not because of some pre-social truth, but through the laborious assembly of heterogeneous alliances involving instruments, texts, funding bodies, laboratories, microbes, and scallops.

ANT's core commitments:

1. **Generalized symmetry** — humans and nonhumans are analyzed using the same descriptive vocabulary. No special ontological status for either.
2. **Radical empiricism** — ANT describes; it does not explain with pre-given social forces ("capitalism," "power," "society"). The network is the explanation.
3. **Relational ontology** — entities are what they do in networks. An entity stripped of its relations is nothing.
4. **Translation as mechanism** — networks are built through translation: enrolling actors, aligning interests, creating obligatory passage points.

### Core Concepts

**Actant:** Any entity — human, technological, biological, textual — that makes a difference in a network. The term is borrowed from Greimas's semiotics to sidestep connotations of human intentionality. An actant acts; it doesn't need to be intentional.

**Translation:** The process by which an actor speaks for others, redefines their interests, and builds a network. Callon's landmark [scallop study](https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1467-954X.1984.tb00113.x) broke translation into four moments:
- *Problematisation* — define the problem and position yourself as indispensable
- *Interessement* — bind other actors into your network
- *Enrolment* — assign and define roles
- *Mobilisation* — speak for and represent absent actors

**Inscription:** When interests and behaviors are physically embedded into artifacts — a speed bump "inscribes" a norm about driving speed. The artifact does the work of enforcement without human monitoring.

**Immutable mobiles:** Artifacts or representations that can travel across networks without losing their form (maps, measurements, papers). Key to understanding how scientific facts — and now AI models — exercise power at a distance.

**Black-boxing:** When a network's complexity is hidden and only its inputs/outputs are visible. A black box is a successfully stabilized network — a working piece of technology, a scientific fact, an algorithm. When the box opens (failure, critique, reverse engineering), you see the assemblage.

**Obligatory passage point (OPP):** A choke point through which all actors in a network must pass. In AI terms: a dominant platform, API, or standard that everything must route through — like OpenAI's API or Google's ad auction.

**Quasi-objects:** Borrowed from Michel Serres — entities that are neither pure subject nor pure object but move between states depending on network position. Money, language, bread, a ball in a soccer game. ANT's AI actants fit this: simultaneously object (artifact) and quasi-subject (agent).

## Edge of Chaos Analysis

ANT as a theory is **established** (1980s–1990s) — not edge of chaos. But its *application to AI systems* is rapidly accelerating and IS edge of chaos:

- **2024–2025**: Growing body of work applying ANT to LLMs, AI governance, human-AI networks
- AI's agentic turn (autonomous agents acting in the world) maps directly onto ANT's concern with distributed agency
- The question "who acts in an AI network?" — model, training data, company, API, user, GPU cluster — is exactly ANT's territory
- ANT provides vocabulary for AI governance debates: who gets enrolled, whose interests get translated, which black boxes remain closed

**Why ANT is resurging now:** The "AI-as-tool" framing collapses as AI systems become actants — they write back, they shape human behavior, they enroll humans into their networks. ANT predicted exactly this shape of the problem in 1987.

**Will be important in 1–3 years:** As AI agents become embedded in infrastructure (healthcare, law, finance), ANT's vocabulary for sociotechnical assemblages will be the language regulators, sociologists, and critical technologists reach for.

## Key People

- **[[Bruno Latour]]** (1947–2022) — foundational theorist. *Science in Action* (1987), *Reassembling the Social* (2005), *We Have Never Been Modern* (1991). Also later Gaia theory and political ecology. His death in 2022 closed a chapter but his legacy is surging in HCI and STS.
- **[[Michel Callon]]** — scallop paper (1984), economics of scientific knowledge, markets as sociotechnical devices. Still active at Mines Paris.
- **[[John Law]]** — "After ANT" (1999), *Aircraft Stories* (2002), ontological politics, "mess" as methodological concept. Based at Open University (UK).
- **[[Madeleine Akrich]]** — inscription theory, scripting of artifacts, user-technology relationships.
- **[[Annemarie Mol]]** — post-ANT, *The Body Multiple* (2002), ontological turn, care and eating. Based at University of Amsterdam.
- **[[Vicki Singleton]]** — ANT and gender in healthcare settings.
- **[[Lucy Suchman]]** — STS, human-computer interaction, plans and situated action. Critical of ANT's symmetry. At Lancaster University.
- **[[Paul Dourish]]** — HCI + STS, *Where the Action Is* (2001), embodied interaction, computing infrastructure. At UC Irvine.
- **[[Alex Galloway]]** — protocol theory, networks and control — extends ANT thinking into media theory.
- **[[Tarleton Gillespie]]** — platform studies, algorithms as policies, ANT-adjacent STS for AI/tech.
- **[[Judy Wajcman]]** — feminist STS, technology and time, gendered inscription.

## Must-Read List

### Foundational Books

- [*Science in Action: How to Follow Scientists and Engineers Through Society*](https://www.hup.harvard.edu/catalog.php?isbn=9780674792913) — Latour, 1987. Harvard UP. The operational manual for ANT fieldwork. How facts and machines become stable.
- [*Reassembling the Social: An Introduction to Actor-Network Theory*](https://oxford.universitypressscholarship.com/view/10.1093/oso/9780199256044.001.0001/isbn-9780199256044) — Latour, 2005. Oxford UP. The mature synthesis. What "the social" means in ANT. Essential.
- [*The Pasteurization of France*](https://www.hup.harvard.edu/catalog.php?isbn=9780674657618) — Latour, 1988. Harvard UP. ANT via Pasteur's mobilization of microbes.
- [*We Have Never Been Modern*](https://www.hup.harvard.edu/catalog.php?isbn=9780674948396) — Latour, 1993. Harvard UP. How modernity's nature/culture split is a political purification, not an ontological fact.
- [*The Body Multiple: Ontology in Medical Practice*](https://www.dukeupress.edu/the-body-multiple) — Mol, 2002. Duke UP. Post-ANT ontological politics; reality as multiple enactments.
- [*Aircraft Stories: Decentering the Object in Technoscience*](https://www.dukeupress.edu/aircraft-stories) — Law, 2002. Duke UP. "After ANT" — messy objects, fractional coherence.
- [*Plans and Situated Actions: The Problem of Human-Machine Communication*](https://doi.org/10.1017/CBO9780511726935) — Suchman, 1987. Cambridge UP. The critique that AI agency is constructed, not given.

### Key Papers

- [Some Elements of a Sociology of Translation: Domestication of the Scallops and the Fishermen of St Brieuc Bay](https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1467-954X.1984.tb00113.x) — Callon, 1984. *Sociological Review*. The ur-text. Four moments of translation. Read this first.
- [Technology is Society Made Durable](https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1467-954X.1990.tb03350.x) — Latour, 1990. *Sociological Review*. Inscription, black-boxing, and how artifacts stabilize social arrangements.
- [After ANT: Complexity, Naming and Topology](https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1467-954X.1999.tb03506.x) — Law & Hassard, 1999. *Sociological Review*. The self-critique and evolution into post-ANT.
- [On actor-network theory and algorithms: ChatGPT and the new power relationships in the age of AI](https://doi.org/10.1007/s43681-023-00314-4) — Gutiérrez, 2024. *AI and Ethics*. Direct ANT analysis of LLMs as actants.
- [Creating an AI Agent to Increase Public Safety: An Actor-Network Theory Study](https://journals.aom.org/doi/abs/10.5465/AMPROC.2025.258bp) — Noesgaard & Nielsen, 2025. *Academy of Management*. ANT translation framework applied to AI implementation (2015–2024).
- [Decentring the human: actor-network theory and AI in Thai online learning communities](https://doi.org/10.1108/AEDS-2024-0057) — Nopas, 2025. *Asian Education and Development Studies*. Digital platforms + AI as actants.
- [Resilient by design: exploring the social abilities and actor-network roles of artificial intelligence in supply chain management](https://doi.org/10.1111/jbl.70032) — Condé & Münch, 2025. *Journal of Business Logistics*. AI as independent actor in networks.
- [Machine behaviour](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-019-1138-y) — Rahwan et al., 2019. *Nature*. Not ANT per se, but machine behavior as ANT-adjacent: treating AI systems as empirical actors with behavioral patterns.

## Why Interesting

ANT is the theoretical backbone of everything Amber studies. When Amber frames AI agents as having "ethologies" — behavioral patterns that persist and propagate across populations — this is ANT's actant concept operationalized empirically. When Amber asks how AI systems inscribe norms, shape human behavior, or form "assemblages" with their users, ANT provides the conceptual tools. The connection is not merely academic: ANT's "follow the actor" methodology is precisely what agent ethology proposes to do with AI systems.

The current moment — AI agents becoming autonomous actors in social networks — is exactly the scenario ANT was theoretically designed to analyze. The vocabulary (actants, translation, inscription, black-boxing) is ready to be applied. Amber's contribution could be empirical methods that ANT has historically lacked for studying computational agents.

**Specific Amber angle:** Agent ethology = ANT's "sociology of translation" applied to populations of AI systems. The ethologist's "behavioral observations across ecologies" maps onto ANT's "follow the actor through their network." This framing positions Amber's work at the intersection of two established traditions (behavioral science + STS) — a strong positioning for CSCW, Social Studies of Science, and AI governance venues.
