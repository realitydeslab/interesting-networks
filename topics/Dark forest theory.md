# Dark Forest Theory

Originally from Liu Cixin's *The Three-Body Problem*: in a universe of scarce resources and unknown others, civilizations hide to survive. Applied to the internet by [[Bogna Konior]] and others.

## Dark Forest Internet
- The open web is a "light forest" — exposed, surveilled, monetized
- People retreat to "dark forests" — group chats, Discord servers, newsletters, private spaces
- Response to platform capitalism and algorithmic manipulation

## Key Texts
- [[Bogna Konior]] — *Dark Forest Theory of the Internet* (Polity, 2025)
- Liu Cixin — *The Three-Body Problem* trilogy
- Yancey Strickler — "The Dark Forest Theory of the Internet" (2019 blog post)

## Connections
- [[Bogna Konior]] — key theorist
- Platform criticism, post-social-media discourse
- Privacy, encryption, decentralization movements

## Why Interesting
Directly from Amber's key inspiration (Konior). Connects speculative fiction (Liu Cixin) to internet theory to design implications. How do you design for dark forest conditions?
