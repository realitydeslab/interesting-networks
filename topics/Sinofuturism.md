# Sinofuturism

Sinofuturism is the concept that China constitutes the foundation for a technologically advanced future, challenging Western hegemony. Originating in Western accelerationist theory (CCRU/Nick Land), it was adopted and transformed by Chinese and diaspora artists, philosophers, and writers to explore technology's relationship with Chinese society through art, science fiction, and philosophy. It is simultaneously a critical art practice, a philosophical framework, and an emerging academic field at the intersection of media theory, postcolonialism, and technoscience.

## Domain Summary
Sinofuturism sits at the intersection of:
- **Accelerationism** (CCRU/Nick Land genealogy) — China as a site of hypercapitalist-communist synthesis
- **Postcolonial theory** — recuperating non-Western technological futures against Orientalist stereotypes
- **Cosmotechnics** (Yuk Hui) — different cosmologies produce different technologies; China's relation to Tao and cosmos produces different technological futures
- **Speculative fiction** — Chinese sci-fi (Liu Cixin, Chen Qiufan) as anticipatory practice
- **Media art** — Lawrence Lek, Cao Fei, Lu Yang as artistic practitioners
- **Critical theory** — Bogna Konior, Benjamin Bratton, Anna Greenspan as theorists

**Historical arc:**
- Nick Land/CCRU (1990s): "Neo-China arrives from the future" — Western accelerationist fascination
- Lawrence Lek (2016): *Sinofuturism (1839–2046 AD)* — the pivotal artwork that crystallizes the concept
- Yuk Hui (2016): *The Question Concerning Technology in China* — philosophical depth
- Bogna Konior (2019–present): darkening/complicating via dark forest metaphor
- 2024–25: Field maturation with edited volumes, critical counter-concepts (Sinopessimism), academic institutionalization
