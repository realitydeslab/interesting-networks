# Cosmotechnics

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Deep research completed. Full intellectual genealogy mapped from Simondon → Stiegler → Hui. Comprehensive survey of communities, venues, and opportunities.

The unification of cosmic and moral orders through technical activities, as theorized by philosopher Yuk Hui. Cosmotechnics challenges the universality of Western technology by proposing that different civilizations develop distinct ways of relating cosmos, technics, and morality — what Hui calls different "cosmotechnics."

## Domain Summary

Cosmotechnics emerges from continental philosophy of technology, specifically responding to Heidegger's "The Question Concerning Technology" (1953). Hui argues that Heidegger's concept of technē, while breaking from instrumental views of technology, remains fundamentally Western and cannot be universally applied.

The core insight: technology is not neutral or universal. Each civilization develops its own cosmotechnics based on its understanding of cosmos, time, and moral order. Chinese cosmotechnics, for example, draws on concepts of qi (氣), dao (道), and the unity of heaven-earth-human (天地人), creating technological thinking fundamentally different from Western enframing (Gestell).

## Key People

### **[[Yuk Hui]]** ⭐⭐⭐
- Professor at Erasmus University Rotterdam & City University of Hong Kong  
- Originator of cosmotechnics concept
- Doctoral student of Bernard Stiegler at Goldsmiths
- Convenor of Research Network for Philosophy and Technology since 2014
- Berggruen Prize juror since 2020
- Recent major lectures: Miki Kiyoshi Memorial Lecture (Kyoto, Jan 2026), Digital Cosmos Symposium (Amsterdam, Nov 2025)

### **[[Gilbert Simondon]]** ⭐⭐⭐
- French philosopher (1924-1989), foundational to technics tradition
- *On the Mode of Existence of Technical Objects* (1958) — key influence on Stiegler and Hui
- Concept of "concretization" and "individuation" central to cosmotechnics genealogy

### **[[Bernard Stiegler]]** ⭐⭐⭐  
- French philosopher (1952-2020), Hui's doctoral supervisor
- *Technics and Time* trilogy (1994-2001) — bridge between Simondon and Hui
- Developed technics as constitutive of human temporality

### **[[Bogna Konior]]** ⭐⭐⭐
- Philosopher at Winchester School of Art
- Co-editor of "Machine Decision is Not Final: China and the History and Future of AI"
- Key figure connecting cosmotechnics to sinofuturism and AI philosophy

### **[[Benjamin Bratton]]** ⭐⭐
- Professor at UC San Diego, director of Strelka Institute
- *The Stack* (2015) — planetary-scale computation
- In dialogue with cosmotechnics through "planetary thinking"

### **[[Pieter Lemmens]]** ⭐⭐
- Philosophy professor at Radboud University  
- Co-editor with Hui on "Cosmotechnics For a Renewed Concept of Technology in the Anthropocene" (2021)
- Key commentator on Simondon-Stiegler-Hui genealogy

## Why Interesting

Cosmotechnics represents a fundamental challenge to technological universalism — the assumption that there is one "correct" way to understand and develop technology. This directly connects to Amber's research interests in several ways:

**Critical Computing:** Provides philosophical framework for questioning the universality of Western computational paradigms and imagining alternatives.

**More-than-human Design:** Offers tools for thinking about technology that doesn't assume human-centered cosmologies, opening space for multispecies and ecological approaches.

**Non-Western Perspectives:** Creates legitimate theoretical space for non-Western approaches to technology, countering Silicon Valley monoculture.

**Edge of Chaos:** The field is rapidly evolving as Chinese technological power grows and demand increases for alternatives to Western tech paradigms. Perfect timing for engagement.
