# Multispecies Design

Design with, for, and alongside multiple species. Extends more-than-human design specifically toward the entangled relations between humans and nonhuman life — animals, plants, fungi, microbes, and increasingly, AI systems. Rooted in multispecies ethnography (Haraway, Tsing, van Dooren) and now becoming a distinct design practice through researchers like Ron Wakkary and the Design+Posthumanism Network. The field asks: what does it mean to design when humans are not the only stakeholders? Who or what has a say in how the built world is made? The question lands differently depending on whether "nonhuman" means a crow, a bacterium, a mycorrhizal network, or an AI agent — and that ambiguity is precisely what makes this territory generative for Amber.

## Domain Summary

Multispecies Design emerged at the intersection of feminist science studies, environmental humanities, and interaction design. Its theoretical foundations lie in three overlapping intellectual traditions:

**1. Feminist Science Studies (Haraway lineage):** Donna Haraway's *The Companion Species Manifesto* (2003) proposed that human-dog co-evolution is a model for understanding how to live with radical difference. The key concept: "companion species" (not "companion animals") — the relation itself is what matters. Her later *Staying with the Trouble* (2016) developed "sympoiesis" (making-together) against autopoiesis (self-making), and proposed the "Chthulucene" as an alternative epoch-label to Anthropocene — one that foregrounds multispecies entanglement rather than human dominance.

**2. Multispecies Ethnography (Tsing lineage):** Anna Tsing's *The Mushroom at the End of the World* (2015) follows matsutake fungi through global supply chains, revealing how "assemblages" — unintentional collaborations between pines, fungi, and humans — make worlds. The key move: studying capitalism and survival from the perspective of what humans cannot control (fungi cannot be cultivated). Tsing's method — "the arts of noticing" — is a systematic approach to attending to more-than-human collaborations.

**3. Environmental Humanities (van Dooren, Rose lineage):** Thom van Dooren's extinction studies (Flight Ways, 2014; The Wake of Crows, 2019; A World in a Shell, 2022) treat extinction not as a statistics problem but as a philosophical and ethical one — what does it mean to lose a way of life? With Deborah Bird Rose (1946–2018), he co-founded "extinction studies" as a field that takes mourning seriously. The design implication: things made to last outlive species, and things made to decay enable multispecies cohabitation.

**Design's entry into this territory** came primarily through Ron Wakkary's *Things We Could Design: For More Than Human-Centered Worlds* (MIT Press, 2021), which translated posthumanist philosophy into design theory and practice. Wakkary's "more-than-human design" became the umbrella concept; multispecies design is its most biologically-specific expression. Key design research has included: compost kits, plant studios, backyard ecologies, insect observation, bee hive interfaces, and biomaterial design — all attempts to make design WITH living systems rather than FOR human users.

**What's driving it:**
- Ecological crisis making anthropocentrism intellectually untenable
- Posthumanist philosophy becoming legible to design researchers
- New materialist and feminist STS frameworks mainstreaming
- Emergence of biomaterials, living systems design, synthetic biology
- The question of AI agency opening multispecies to digital-nonhuman territory

**Will it be hot in 1-3 years?** Yes. The 2025 Routledge textbook and DIS/CHI uptake suggest the field is moving from fringe to mainstream in design research. Amber is at exactly the right moment to enter this conversation — before it becomes predictable.

## Sub-topics & Trends (last 3-5 years)

- **Multispecies justice** — moving from design WITH to design FOR nonhumans, including rights-of-nature frameworks (Josh Gellers, Rights of Nature)
- **Living and biomaterials design** — designing WITH bacteria, fungi, cellulose as active agents rather than passive materials (Lauren Thu, TU Eindhoven; Carolina de Lara)
- **Vermicomposting and soil ecologies** — literally designing into decomposition cycles (Wakkary + Tiffany Wun's Compost Kits, 2025)
- **Insect-centered design** — observation, attunement, and cohabitation with invertebrates (Turner Boxes and Bees, DIS 2023; I.N.S.E.C.T. Summer Camp community)
- **Plant studies in design** — plant communication, sensing, and agency (Natasha Myers' Plant Studies Collaboratory; Wakkary's Plant Studio CHI 2025)
- **Extinction and grief in design** — designing for mourning, care, and loss in multispecies terms (van Dooren lineage)
- **Rights of Nature in design** — design advocacy for nonhuman political representation (Species Embassy Viskan-Borås, Nordic Summit of Species 2025)
- **AI as nonhuman agent** — the emerging edge: if AI systems behave, adapt, and co-evolve, can they be understood within multispecies frameworks? (underdeveloped, ripe for Amber)

## Key People

- **[[Donna Haraway]]** ⭐⭐⭐ — Emeritus Professor, UC Santa Cruz (History of Consciousness & Feminist Studies). The foundational theorist of companion species, sympoiesis, and the Chthulucene. *The Companion Species Manifesto* (2003) reframed human-nonhuman relations as constitutive rather than merely adjacent. *Staying with the Trouble* (2016) is the masterwork. Co-founded [[Multispecies Salon]] with Tsing. 2025 Erasmus Prize laureate (€150,000). All serious multispecies design work cites her.

- **[[Anna Tsing]]** ⭐⭐⭐ — Professor of Anthropology, UC Santa Cruz & Co-director of AURA (Aarhus University). Pioneer of multispecies ethnography. *The Mushroom at the End of the World* (2015) is the most-read text in this field — simultaneously a capitalism critique, a fieldwork guide, and a theory of multispecies "assemblages." Her concept of the "arts of noticing" is directly methodologically applicable. The Feral Atlas (2020, digital) extends this collaboratively.

- **[[Ron Wakkary]]** ⭐⭐⭐ — Professor, SFU (SIAT) & TU Eindhoven (Future Everyday cluster). **The key figure bridging multispecies theory with design practice.** *Things We Could Design: For More Than Human-Centered Worlds* (MIT Press, 2021, Kyoto Design Award) is the foundational design text. His Everyday Design Studio at SFU has produced: plant studios, compost kits, backyard ecology projects, insect observation designs. Co-chair of ACM DIS Steering Committee. Collaborators include Doenja Oogjes, Oscar Tomico, Kristina Andersen, Elisa Giaccardi.

- **Thom van Dooren** — Professor of Environmental Humanities, University of Sydney & Humboldt Fellow, MESH Cologne. Co-founded extinction studies with Deborah Bird Rose. Key texts: [*Flight Ways: Life and Loss at the Edge of Extinction*](http://thomvandooren.org/publications/flight-ways/) (Columbia, 2014), [*The Wake of Crows*](http://thomvandooren.org/wake) (Columbia, 2019), [*A World in a Shell: Snail Stories for a Time of Extinctions*](https://www.thomvandooren.org/a-world-in-a-shell/) (MIT Press, 2022). His approach: philosophy through field research with specific species (crows, vultures, penguins, snails). Current project: *Multispecies Storytelling in Catastrophic Times* (2025 talk series). His co-productions with designers (multimedia essay on regent honeyeater, 2024) show direct design collaboration. Website: <https://www.thomvandooren.org/>

- **Natasha Myers** — Associate Professor of Anthropology, York University; Director, [Plant Studies Collaboratory](http://plantstudies.wordpress.com). Coined "Planthroposcene" (2016) — a proposed alternative epoch centered on plants and plant-human entanglements. Key text: [*Rendering Life Molecular: Models, Modelers, and Excitable Matter*](https://www.dukeupress.edu/rendering-life-molecular) (Duke, 2015). Current project: Seeding Planthroposcenes — documenting affective ecologies between plants and people. Her work in botanical gardens and urban parks grounds abstract theory in specific sites. Website: <https://natashamyers.wordpress.com/>

- **Deborah Bird Rose** (1946–2018) — Environmental humanist, philosopher, Indigenist. Former professor, Macquarie University. Co-founded extinction studies with van Dooren. Key texts: *Wild Dog Dreaming: Love and Extinction* (2011), *Nourishing Terrains* (1996). Her approach drew on Aboriginal Australian knowledge systems (particularly Yarralin cosmologies) to theorize multispecies ethics and reciprocity. Death: November 2018. Her work continues to shape the field through van Dooren's ongoing development of extinction studies.

- **[[Elisa Giaccardi]]** ⭐⭐⭐ — Professor, TU Delft. "Things-as-co-ethnographers," biological-HCI, more-than-human data practices. Key collaborator with Wakkary. Co-author of "The Future of More-Than-Human Design" (Aarhus 2025). Connected to Design+Posthumanism Network.

- **Iohanna Nicenboim** ⭐⭐ — TU Delft. Animal-centered design, AI care, more-than-human design. Active in [[Design+Posthumanism Network]]. Connected to DIS and CHI communities.

## Must-Read List

### Books
- [*The Companion Species Manifesto: Dogs, People, and Significant Otherness*](https://press.uchicago.edu/ucp/books/book/distributed/C/bo3618612.html) (Haraway, 2003) — the foundational text. Short (60 pages), dense, and provocative. Read before anything else in this field.
- [*The Mushroom at the End of the World: On the Possibility of Life in Capitalist Ruins*](https://press.princeton.edu/books/paperback/9780691178325/the-mushroom-at-the-end-the-world) (Tsing, 2015) — the exemplar of multispecies ethnography. Extraordinary writing. Essential.
- [*Staying with the Trouble: Making Kin in the Chthulucene*](https://www.dukeupress.edu/staying-with-the-trouble) (Haraway, 2016) — synthesizes the entire intellectual tradition. Chthulucene, sympoiesis, SF method.
- [*Things We Could Design: For More Than Human-Centered Worlds*](https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262542876/things-we-could-design/) (Wakkary, 2021) — the design translation. Essential for HCI/design researchers.
- [*Arts of Living on a Damaged Planet: Ghosts and Monsters of the Anthropocene*](https://upress.umn.edu/book-division/books/arts-of-living-on-a-damaged-planet) (Tsing, Swanson, Gan, Bubandt, eds., 2017) — anthology covering the full range of multispecies approaches. Anthology: useful for finding relevant essays.
- [*Flight Ways: Life and Loss at the Edge of Extinction*](http://thomvandooren.org/publications/flight-ways/) (van Dooren, 2014) — multispecies ethics through specific species stories (albatrosses, cranes, penguins, vultures, honeybees). Beautiful and rigorous.
- [*More-than-Human Design in Practice*](https://www.routledge.com/More-Than-Human-Design-in-Practice/Rosen-Salovaara-Botero-Sondergaard/p/book/9781032741208) (Rosén, Salovaara, Botero, Søndergaard, eds., Routledge, 2025) — **the field-defining design textbook**. Just published. Co-edited by D+PH Network members.

### Papers
- [Backyard Practices: A Liminal Approach to Designing in More-than-Human Worlds](https://doi.org/10.1145/3706598.3713291) (Wakkary et al., CHI 2025) — multispecies design through backyard ecologies. Current.
- [Turner Boxes and Bees: From Ambivalence to Diffraction](https://doi.org/10.1145/3563657.3596020) (Wakkary, Oogjes, Sakib, Behzad, DIS 2023) — insect observation as design practice.
- [Constituency as a Matter of Practice: Moving a Plant Studio](https://doi.org/10.1145/3706598.3713916) (Wakkary, Tomico, Oogjes, CHI 2025) — plant-human design collaborations.
- [Feminist Posthumanist Design of Menstrual Care for More-than-Human Bodies](https://doi.org/10.1145/3544548.3581507) (Søndergaard & Woytuk, CHI 2023) — posthuman bodies beyond the human/nonhuman binary.
- [Compost Kits: Bridging More-than-Human Theory with Design Practice through Vermicomposting](https://doi.org/10.1145/3757980.3757990) (Wakkary & Wun, MindTrek 2025) — literal decomposition as design material.
- ["The Future of More-Than-Human Design: A Computing Practice in Crisis?"](https://doi.org/10.1145/3737609.3747092) (Wakkary, Barendregt, Berger, Frauenberger, Giaccardi, Nicenboim + 13 others, Aarhus 2025) — 19-author coalition paper interrogating the field.
- [Unruly Edges: Mushrooms as Companion Species](https://doi.org/10.1017/S0266467412000685) (Tsing, 2012, Journal of the Royal Anthropological Institute) — seminal multispecies ethnography paper.

### Journals

## Open Questions

1. **Can AI systems be included in multispecies frameworks?** Most multispecies design concerns biological nonhumans. But if we study AI systems behaviorally — their habits, their ecologies, their co-evolutionary relationships with humans — are they a kind of nonhuman agent? Agent ethology (Amber's concept) might be the bridge concept.

2. **What does design for nonhuman flourishing look like at scale?** Most multispecies design is small-scale (one garden, one lab). How do design principles scale to urban ecologies, infrastructure, or digital environments?

3. **Is "participation" meaningful for nonhumans?** Participatory design assumes participants can represent their interests. Plants and fungi can't vote. What forms of proxy representation or design speculation are ethically adequate?

4. **What's the relationship between multispecies design and indigenous knowledge?** Many indigenous ontologies don't separate the human from the nonhuman. Is multispecies design reinventing something that was never lost in certain traditions? (Tsing's engagement with Indonesian and Japanese forest communities gestures toward this.)

5. **How does multispecies design engage with extinction?** If species are disappearing faster than we can design with them, what does it mean to design for a multispecies world that is becoming less multispecies?

6. **Cosmotechnics and multispecies:** Yuk Hui's cosmotechnics framework (non-Western techno-philosophical traditions) — could it inform a non-Western multispecies design? Chinese traditional medicine and landscape ecology have sophisticated multispecies ontologies.

3. **HoloKit and embodied interaction** — HoloKit enables embodied, spatial computing that could extend human perception toward nonhuman scales (infrared, sonar, mycelial networks, bird migration paths). Designing mixed reality experiences that help humans attune to nonhuman life is a multispecies design practice.

**The Haraway-Tsing-Wakkary lineage** is directly relevant: Haraway's "situated knowledges" grounds the epistemology; Tsing's "arts of noticing" grounds the method; Wakkary's design practice grounds the HCI application. **Research-taste alignment:** This field is weird + rigorous (mushrooms as capitalism critique? fungi-as-design-material?), practice-based (making things, not just theorizing), and engaged with non-Western knowledge systems (Tsing's Japanese and Indonesian fieldwork, van Dooren's engagement with Aboriginal Australian ontologies). It's interdisciplinary at a deep level — not just "applying anthropology to design" but generating genuinely new methods.
