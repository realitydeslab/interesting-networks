# Oblique Strategies

A deck of ~100–150 oracle cards co-created by [[Brian Eno]] and [[Peter Schmidt]] in 1975. Each card carries an oblique aphorism or instruction designed to help artists break through creative blocks by forcing lateral thinking. One of the most influential creative process tools of the 20th century. Available online at <https://oblique-strategies.com/>.

Example cards:
- *"Honour thy error as a hidden intention"*
- *"Use an old idea"*
- *"Do nothing for as long as possible"*
- *"What would your closest friend do?"*
- *"State the problem in words as clearly as possible"*
- *"Emphasize the flaws"*
- *"Be extravagant"*

## Origin
Eno and Schmidt independently arrived at similar practices for their own creative work — Schmidt's "Thoughts Behind The Thoughts," Eno's own aphorisms. They combined efforts when they realized they were doing the same thing. First edition published 1975. Six editions through 2007.

Used extensively during the recording of David Bowie's Berlin Trilogy (Low, Heroes, Lodger) and many other Eno-produced albums. Bowie, Talking Heads, and others have cited them as important tools in the studio.

## Why Interesting
As a designed system for prompting creative cognition under constraints, Oblique Strategies sits at the intersection of art, game design, cognition, and productivity methodology. It's a pre-digital example of what we might now call "prompt engineering for human creativity." The constraint-based creativity approach (constraints as generative) connects to research on computational creativity, generative systems, and design methodology. For design fiction and speculative design: it's a model for creating tools that change how people think, not just what they do.
