# Technoetics

Technoetics is a neologism coined by [[Roy Ascott]] in 1997, fusing *techne* (craft/technology) with *noetics* (the philosophy of mind and consciousness). It names a convergent, transdisciplinary field that explores consciousness and connectivity through digital, telematic, chemical, biological, and spiritual means — embracing both interactive technologies and the creative use of "moistmedia" (wet/biological + digital). The term first appeared in Ascott's 1997 paper "The Technoetic Aesthetic: Art and the Matter of Consciousness" and has since anchored a research programme, doctoral platform, journal, and teaching curriculum centred on the intersection of art, science, and mind.

## Domain Summary

Technoetics operates at the junction of:
- **Cybernetics and telematics** — networked systems as extensions of consciousness
- **Consciousness research** — phenomenology, noetics, altered states, psychoactive technologies
- **Bioart and wet computing** — biological, nano, and genetic media
- **Telematic art** — art through telecommunications networks
- **Syncretism** — drawing from shamanic, spiritual, and non-Western knowledge traditions

Ascott frames Technoetics as post-biological: technologies of consciousness include not just digital/telematic tools but also plant medicines ("vegetal technologies"), genetic interventions, and neuro-interfaces. This distinguishes it from a purely computational aesthetics — it's explicitly interested in expanded states of mind, cosmic connectedness, and the blurring of human/machine/biological boundaries.

The intellectual lineage runs: **Norbert Wiener** (cybernetics) → **Ascott's Groundcourse** (radical pedagogy, 1960s Ealing/Ipswich) → **telematic art** (1970s-80s networked artworks) → **Technoetics** (1997–present). Ascott was directly influenced by Gordon Pask (conversation theory), Stafford Beer, and Gregory Bateson, and situates his work within second-order cybernetics.

## Edge of Chaos Analysis

**Not edge of chaos.** Technoetics as a branded field is primarily associated with Ascott (born 1934, now 91) and has a stable but not rapidly-expanding research community. The journal is active (Vol 23, 2026) and indexed in Clarivate/Scopus, but it remains a niche interdisciplinary venue rather than a hot emerging field. The *ideas* within Technoetics — consciousness + technology, bioart, moistmedia — are alive and resonant with current machine consciousness debates, but the Technoetics *brand* is unlikely to spike. Worth tracking as a lineage and intellectual resource, not as a field to bet on professionally.

## Must-Read List

### Books
- [*Telematic Embrace: Visionary Theories of Art, Technology and Consciousness*](https://www.ucpress.edu/book/9780520218536/telematic-embrace) (Roy Ascott, ed. Edward Shanken, UC Press 2003) — the definitive Ascott anthology; covers cybernetism, telematics, and Technoetics from 1960s to 2000s. **Essential lineage text.**
- [*Technoetic Arts*](https://www.intellectbooks.com/technoetic-arts-a-journal-of-speculative-research) (Roy Ascott, Intellect Books 2003) — foundational statement of Technoetics as practice.
- [*Art and Technology: The New Combine*](https://mitpress.mit.edu/) — context volume (broader media art).

### Papers
- ["The Technoetic Aesthetic: Art and the Matter of Consciousness"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Technoetics) (Roy Ascott, 1997) — **first use of the term**; in: *Consciousness Reframed* Proceedings, University of Wales College, Newport. No online DOI found; source: Wikipedia citation.
- ["Technoetic Aesthetics: 100 Terms and Definitions for the Post-biological Era"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Technoetics) (Roy Ascott, 1997) — companion glossary.
- ["Moistmedia, Technoetics and the Three VRs"](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/265474622_Moistmedia_Technoetics_and_the_Three_VRs) (Roy Ascott, ~2002) — on wet computing and consciousness.

### Journals
- [*Technoetic Arts: A Journal of Speculative Research*](https://www.intellectbooks.com/technoetic-arts-a-journal-of-speculative-research) (Intellect Books) — **the** venue for this field; Vol 23, 2026; indexed Clarivate/Scopus. Online hub: <https://ta.pubpub.org/>

## Connections to Amber's Work

1. **Machine consciousness** — Technoetics is literally defined as "the technology of consciousness." Ascott's concern with how technology extends, transforms, and participates in consciousness directly maps onto Amber's machine consciousness/death philosophy. His framing is art-theoretical; Amber's is HCI/ALife — but the question is the same.

2. **More-than-human design** — Technoetics explicitly includes "vegetal, moist, or linguistic" technologies of consciousness, positioning plants, fungi, and biological media as co-participants in mind. This aligns with Amber's more-than-human design and multispecies perspectives.

3. **Cosmotechnics resonance** — Yuk Hui's cosmotechnics (different techno-logies emerging from different cosmologies) and Ascott's syncretism (drawing on shamanic/indigenous knowledge traditions alongside cybernetics) share a commitment to non-Western and pluralist technoscience. Amber sits at the Yuk Hui / China Academy of Art intersection — Technoetics is a Western-adjacent but China-active parallel discourse.

4. **ALife & wetware** — Ascott's moistmedia concept (bio-digital hybrids, living neurons as art medium) connects to ALife's interest in the boundary between living and non-living systems. Guy Ben-Ary's neuron work (*Revivification*, 2026) is a live instance of this crossover.

5. **Telematic art → agent networks** — Ascott's telematic art practice (distributed networked art, 1980s) is a historical precursor to contemporary thinking about multi-agent systems as creative/social infrastructure.

## Why Interesting

Technoetics is the art world's sustained attempt, since 1997, to hold consciousness and technology together as a single research object — not by reducing one to the other, but by asking how technology *is* a mode of consciousness, and vice versa. For Amber, this is interesting less as a live cutting-edge field (it's established) and more as:

1. **Intellectual lineage** — Ascott is 91 and built this field over 60 years. His work legitimises the territory Amber is entering with machine death and machine consciousness. Citing this lineage is strategic.
2. **China crossover** — Ascott has been deeply embedded in Chinese art-tech institutions (CAFA, SIVA, DeTao). This is a network Amber may already be proximate to through China Academy of Art.
3. **Journal as venue** — *Technoetic Arts* is a real indexed journal (Scopus/Clarivate) that would welcome speculative machine consciousness work. Amber's ERA fellowship themes would fit Vol 24 or 25.
4. **Speculative legitimacy** — Technoetics has kept "consciousness" and "art" together in an academic context for 25+ years, against a tide of computational reductionism. This is a form of intellectual courage Amber should know about and reference.
