# Speculative Computation

Speculative Computation is the philosophical project of treating computation not merely as a technical tool but as a site of ontological, political, and aesthetic inquiry. What *is* computation? What could it become? What forms of life, intelligence, and world-ordering does it make possible — or foreclose? The field sits at the intersection of continental philosophy, philosophy of technology, critical theory, and speculative design. It draws on thinkers from Turing and Wiener through Deleuze/Guattari to Yuk Hui, Beatrice Fazi, and Benjamin Bratton, asking not "how does computation work?" but "what does computation *mean* — and what might it mean otherwise?"

## Domain Summary

Speculative Computation encompasses several overlapping projects:

1. **Philosophy of computation** — treating computation as an object of philosophical scrutiny rather than a neutral substrate. What are the ontological commitments of formal systems? What is left out?

2. **Planetary computation** (Bratton's frame) — computation as global infrastructure, reshaping sovereignty, territory, and life at planetary scale. The "Stack" as an accidental megastructure layering Earth → Cloud → City → Address → Interface → User.

3. **Computational indeterminacy** (Fazi's frame) — pushing back against the idea that computation is fully deterministic. Drawing on Turing, Gödel, and Whitehead to argue computation has genuine immanent potential — not just execution of predefined rules but genuine becoming.

4. **Cosmotechnical computation** (Yuk Hui's frame) — arguing that modernity's computational paradigm is one specific cosmotechnics (Greek/European), and that non-Western traditions (Chinese, Daoist, Confucian) ground different relationships between *techne* and *cosmos*. Multiple modernities = multiple computational ontologies.

5. **Rationalist/AGI philosophy** (Negarestani's frame) — philosophy as a program for constructing intelligence. The "intelligence" question as both descriptive and normative: what should intelligence become? Connects German Idealism to contemporary AI.

6. **Sinofuturism and machine decision** (Konior/Greenspan/Ireland) — how Chinese AI development is not merely a "copy" of Western paradigms but a site of genuine philosophical difference in what intelligence, automation, and decision mean.

## Key People

- **[[Benjamin Bratton]]** — philosopher of technology at UCSD; Director of Antikythera. Coined "planetary computation," "The Stack." Core books: *The Stack* (MIT Press, 2015), *The Terraforming* (Strelka, 2019), *Accept All Cookies* (Berggruen Press, 2025). Visiting researcher at Google's Paradigms of Intelligence group (2024–). Foundational.

- **[[Beatrice Fazi]]** — philosopher at University of Sussex. Author of *Contingent Computation* (Rowman & Littlefield, 2018). Argues for genuine indeterminacy/contingency in computation against reductive determinism. Draws on Whitehead's process philosophy and Turing's halting problem. Key bridge between continental philosophy and theory of computation.

- **[[Yuk Hui]]** — philosopher of technology. Author of *The Question Concerning Technology in China* (Urbanomic, 2016), *Recursivity and Contingency* (Rowman & Littlefield, 2019), *Art and Cosmotechnics* (e-flux/Minnesota, 2021), *Post-Europe* (Urbanomic, 2024). Core thesis: there is no single universalizable technology — different civilizations produce different cosmotechnics. Visiting professor at China Academy of Art (where Amber has taught).

- **[[Reza Negarestani]]** — philosopher, author of *Intelligence and Spirit* (Urbanomic/Sequence Press, 2018). Synthesizes German Idealism, cognitive science, and theoretical computer science into a rationalist philosophy of intelligence. Directs Critical Philosophy programme at [New Centre for Research & Practice](https://www.newcentre.org/). Key figure for the philosophical program behind AI development.

- **Luciana Parisi** — philosopher at Duke; *Contagious Architecture: Computation, Aesthetics, and Space* (MIT Press, 2013). Argues computation has aesthetic dimensions that exceed algorithmic determinism — connects to Fazi's work on indeterminacy.

- **N. Katherine Hayles** — literary theorist; *My Mother Was a Computer* (U Chicago, 2005), *Unthinking* (U Chicago, 2017). Digital humanities perspective on computation's literary/cognitive dimensions.

- **Matteo Pasquinelli** — philosopher at GSAP Berlin; *The Eye of the Master* (Verso, 2023). Materialist genealogy of AI and machine learning — labor, abstraction, and machine intelligence.

- **Bogna Konior** — co-editor of *Machine Decision is Not Final* (Urbanomic, 2024). Specializes in Chinese internet culture, dark ecology, speculative thought.

- **Anna Greenspan** — co-editor of *Machine Decision is Not Final*; NYU Shanghai; specializes in Sinofuturism and temporal cognition.

- **Amy Ireland** — Urbanomic; co-author of *Cute Accelerationism* (2024); researches xenofeminism and accelerationism.

- **Lukáš Likavčan** — *Introduction to Comparative Planetology* (Strelka Press, 2019); research at Antikythera. Connects comparative cosmology to computational infrastructure.
