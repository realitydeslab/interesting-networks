# Dark Forest Theory

Dark Forest Theory originates in Liu Cixin's *Three-Body Problem* trilogy (2006–2010): the universe is a dark forest where every civilization is an armed hunter — because any visible civilization risks being detected and destroyed, all civilizations hide. Applied to the internet by Yancey Strickler (2019) and theorized by Bogna Konior (2025), it describes the retreat from the toxic, surveilled public internet into encrypted, private, invite-only "dark forest" spaces. Konior extends the metaphor to analyze how intelligence — human and artificial — operates under conditions of secrecy, hostility, and concealment.

## Domain Summary

**Three genealogies of Dark Forest Theory:**

### 1. Cosmological (Liu Cixin, 2006–2010)
The *Three-Body Problem* trilogy introduces the Dark Forest as a solution to the Fermi Paradox: why is the universe silent? Because any civilization that reveals itself will be destroyed by another. The "axioms of civilizational sociology": survival is the primary need; civilizations continuously expand; chain-suspicion makes cooperation impossible. Result: hide or be eliminated.

### 2. Internet Theory (Yancey Strickler, 2019 → 2024)
Strickler's 2019 essay applied the metaphor to internet culture: in response to advertising, tracking, trolling, and predatory behaviors, people retreat to "dark forests" — newsletters, podcasts, group chats, small online communities — away from the algorithmic mainstream. Venkatesh Rao coined "cozyweb" as the positive framing (2019). By 2024, Strickler extended to "post-individual" identity formation in dark forest contexts.

### 3. AI/Media Theory (Bogna Konior, 2025)
Konior's book-length treatment (Polity, 2025) moves beyond metaphor to theory: how does intelligence manifest under conditions of secrecy, hostility, and concealment? Applies this to AI systems, digital culture, and the political economy of concealment. Key question: what happens to cognition when its environment rewards hiding?

**Connected concepts:**
- **Cozyweb** (Venkatesh Rao, 2019) — the warm, private internet as positive counterpart
- **Dark patterns** — UI design that exploits users; the dark forest is the user's rational response
- **Encrypted communication** — Signal, Telegram, etc. as dark forest infrastructure
- **Private digital communities** — Discord servers, invite-only forums
- **AI alignment** — concealment as an AI safety problem (deceptive alignment)
- **Fermi Paradox** — cosmological original context
