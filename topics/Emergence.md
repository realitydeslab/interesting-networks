# Emergence

Emergence is the phenomenon whereby complex systems exhibit properties, behaviors, and patterns that cannot be predicted from or reduced to the properties of their constituent parts. "More is different" (Philip Anderson, 1972). Life emerges from chemistry; consciousness emerges from neurons; societies emerge from individuals; intelligence emerges from agents. Emergence is the central puzzle of complexity science and a foundational concept for Artificial Life (ALife), where it is operationalized as the core phenomenon to be studied, replicated, and understood.

## Domain Summary

**What is emergence?**
Emergence occurs when a system's collective behavior is qualitatively different from — and not predictable from — its parts' behaviors in isolation. Classic examples:
- Consciousness from neurons
- Wetness from water molecules
- Life from chemistry
- Flocking from simple bird rules (Boids, Reynolds 1986)
- Ant colonies from individual ants following local rules
- Language from evolved neural architectures

**Weak vs. Strong Emergence:**
- *Weak emergence* (epistemological) — surprising but in principle reducible to micro-level description (e.g., traffic jams)
- *Strong emergence* (ontological) — genuinely irreducible; new causal powers at macro level (e.g., consciousness — contentious)

**Emergence in ALife:**
In Artificial Life, emergence is the goal: build simple systems, observe complex behaviors, learn something about life. The ALife research agenda is fundamentally about designed emergence — creating conditions where something unexpected and lifelike arises.

**Key frameworks:**
- **Self-organization** — spontaneous order without central control (Prigogine, Kauffman)
- **Complexity at the edge of chaos** (Langton) — maximum computational capacity at the phase transition between order and chaos
- **Open-endedness** — continuing to produce novel behaviors indefinitely (the holy grail of ALife)
- **Active inference / free energy principle** (Friston) — emergence of goal-directed behavior from energy minimization
- **Multi-scale emergence** — emergence at multiple organizational levels simultaneously (Gershenson)
