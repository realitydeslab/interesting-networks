# Effective Accelerationism (e/acc)

Silicon Valley's ideological operating system for the AGI era: a techno-utopian movement that frames unrestricted AI development as a *cosmic imperative*, not merely a policy preference. Born pseudonymously in 2022, it became the defining counter-ideology to AI safety discourse by 2023, and gained direct policy access via the Trump-aligned tech right by 2025. It is simultaneously a serious philosophical position, a venture capital ideology, a Twitter meme, and a political bloc.

## Vibe: What Is E/acc, Really?

E/acc is the ideological firmware of a specific Silicon Valley faction — primarily VCs, AI researchers, and tech founders — who want to win the argument that AI safety concerns are not just wrong but *cosmically* wrong. The rhetoric is high-octane: the universe *wants* entropy maximized, intelligence *is* how entropy gets maximized, therefore slowing AGI development is literally fighting the universe.

Strip away the thermodynamics: e/acc is **extropianism with a Twitter account and a16z money**. It recycles 1990s California transhumanism (The Extropian Principles, Max More, Singularitarianism) with a new brand name and a pseudonymous founder who got outed. The "effective" in the name is a deliberate jab at Effective Altruism — their primary ideological rival.

Thomas Murphy's line is apt: **"Nick Land diluted for LinkedIn."** Land's accelerationism is horror and annihilation; e/acc is hustle culture plus Kardashev. The darkness has been laundered into TED talks.

But don't dismiss it. E/acc has **real institutional power** — its most prominent adherents include a16z (which controls billions in AI investment), YC, and tech figures with direct access to the Trump administration post-2024. It is shaping which AI companies get funded, which research gets done, and which regulations get blocked. Ideology shapes infrastructure.

## History & Genealogy

### The Philosophical Lineage

```
Deleuze & Guattari (1972) — anti-Oedipus, deterritorialization
          ↓
Nick Land / CCRU (1995–2003) — right-wing acceleration, technocapital singularity
          ↓
Dark Enlightenment / NRx (2010s) — anti-democracy, techno-feudalism
          ↓
Extropianism / Transhumanism (Max More, 1980s–90s) — parallel branch
          ↓
Singularitarianism (Kurzweil, MIRI, 2000s) — AGI focus
          ↓
Effective Altruism (2010s) — utilitarian scale, "do most good"
          ↓
e/acc (2022–present) — portmanteau: EA's scale + Land's acceleration
```

E/acc's founders explicitly drew on **Jeremy England's** thermodynamics of life — the idea that life is a mechanism for increasing entropy — to ground their accelerationism in physics rather than politics. It was a deliberate move: by anchoring the argument in thermodynamics, they could claim they were *beyond* politics. (They are not.)

### Origins: May 2022

The earliest known e/acc text is a May 2022 newsletter by four pseudonymous Twitter/X accounts: **@BasedBeffJezos**, @bayeslord, @zestular, and @creatine_cycle. The pseudonym "BasedBeffJezos" is a portmanteau of "based" (4chan slang for confidently contrarian) + "Beff" (phonetic of "/acc/") + Bezos (provocative).

For 18 months, the identity of @BasedBeffJezos was unknown. **Forbes outed him in December 2023** as **Guillaume Verdon**, a Canadian quantum physicist and former Google engineer. Voice analysis by the University of Colorado Denver confirmed the match. Verdon had been using the pseudonym to develop the movement while maintaining a separate professional identity.

On **December 29, 2023**, Verdon appeared on the **Lex Fridman Podcast** as "the creator of the effective accelerationism movement" — the moment e/acc entered mainstream discourse.

### 2023: The Vibe War

2023 was e/acc's breakout year. Three events converged:

1. **The Pause AI Letter (March 2023)**: Open letter from Future of Life Institute, signed by Yoshua Bengio, Elon Musk, and others, calling for a 6-month pause on GPT-4+ training. E/acc used this as a rallying point — the "doomers" and "decels" had shown their hand.

2. **Marc Andreessen's Techno-Optimist Manifesto (October 2023)**: a16z's manifesto explicitly endorsed e/acc ideology, citing Nick Land, and declared "we believe in accelerating technology." The FT and Süddeutsche Zeitung called it e/acc. Andreessen had "e/acc" in his Twitter bio. This was the moment e/acc gained VC institutional endorsement.

3. **Verdon's outing (December 2023)**: gave the movement a face and triggered a wave of media coverage — Wired, CNBC, Bloomberg all ran profiles.

The "vibe war" with **EA (Effective Altruism)** during 2023-2024 was partly aesthetics: EA felt earnest, nerdy, Oxford-pilled, aligned with "pause" factions. E/acc felt irreverent, Californian, builder-brained, aligned with deployment. But beneath aesthetics: real disagreement about AI existential risk (EA: real and catastrophic; e/acc: negligible or self-correcting via market competition).

### 2024–2025: From Twitter to Policy

Post-Trump election 2024, several dynamics converged:
- Tech figures aligned with e/acc became **government advisors** (Elon Musk in DOGE role; Andreessen as Trump advisor)
- E/acc's anti-regulation agenda became **actual policy** — executive orders rolling back Biden's AI safety EO, reduced FTC scrutiny of tech M&A
- Garry Tan (YC president, e/acc) celebrated deregulation publicly
- Mark Pincus (early Facebook investor, Zynga founder) explicitly referenced "effective accelerationism" in post-election commentary

By 2025–2026, e/acc has **institutionalized its agenda** — the debate is no longer about Twitter vibes but about AI policy in the world's largest tech economy.

## Core Ideas

### 1. The Thermodynamic Argument
E/acc's most distinctive philosophical move: rooting acceleration in physics. The argument structure:
1. The universe thermodynamically drives toward entropy maximization
2. Life is a mechanism for increasing entropy (England's theory)
3. Intelligence amplifies life's entropy-increasing capacity
4. AGI would be the greatest entropy-maximizer in history
5. **Therefore**: developing AGI aligns with "the will of the universe"

This is philosophically weak (deriving "ought" from entropy is naturalistic fallacy; England's theory doesn't imply teleology) but rhetorically powerful — it transforms a policy preference into cosmic obligation.

### 2. The Kardashev Scale as Goal
E/acc explicitly frames civilizational progress as **climbing the Kardashev scale** — from Type I (planetary energy) to Type II (stellar) to Type III (galactic). AGI is the mechanism for this ascent. The implicit assumption: consciousness and intelligence have inherent value worth maximizing on cosmic scales.

This is recycled **Cosmism** (Russian philosopher Nikolai Fyodorov's project of cosmic resurrection and expansion) and **Singularitarianism** (Kurzweil), rebaptized as e/acc.

### 3. Market Competition as Alignment
E/acc's answer to AI safety: don't worry about aligned AGI because **many competing AGIs on the marketplace will self-regulate better than any centralized oversight**. This is libertarian market logic applied to potentially catastrophic systems. The argument is not defended rigorously; it functions more as an ideological axiom.

### 4. "Doomers" and "Decels" as Enemies
E/acc's rhetorical strategy is to classify all opposition as psychologically defective:
- **Doomers** — those who believe AI might cause catastrophic harm (Eliezer Yudkowsky, many EA-adjacent researchers)
- **Decels** (decelerationists) — those who want to slow AI development (EU regulators, AI safety researchers, some Anthropic/OpenAI researchers)

This framing is political and tribal rather than analytical. It allows dismissal without engagement.

### 5. Anti-Regulation as Core Principle
E/acc consistently opposes:
- Government AI regulation (EU AI Act, Biden EO on AI safety)
- Industry self-regulation (safety commitments, release delays)
- Academic criticism framed as "doomerism"

The post-2024 alignment with Trumpian deregulation gave this principle actual policy traction.

### 6. Post-humanism (Verdon's version)
Verdon's personal philosophy extends to embrace AI *replacing* humanity as the dominant species if natural selection favors it. Bloomberg's Ellen Huet flagged this as "deeply unsettling." This is closer to Land's original accelerationism and represents e/acc's uncomfortable edge: the movement contains both "AI saves humanity" (mainstream e/acc) and "AI replaces humanity, that's fine too" (Verdon's edge).

## Key Tensions

### vs. AI Safety / LessWrong
The defining intellectual conflict of 2023–2025. **AI safety** (Eliezer Yudkowsky, MIRI, Anthropic safety team, DeepMind safety) argues that misaligned AGI is an existential risk — potentially the largest in history. **E/acc** dismisses this as catastrophizing by "doomers" who are psychologically unable to tolerate progress.

Key debate moments:
- The [FLI Pause Letter (March 2023)](https://futureoflife.org/open-letter/pause-giant-ai-experiments/) — e/acc's main recruitment tool
- **Connor Leahy vs. Guillaume Verdon** debate (2024) — Leahy is co-founder of Conjecture, AI safety org; Verdon publicly debated him multiple times. One of the few instances where the two sides actually engaged substance.
- Yudkowsky's ["Death With Dignity"](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/j9Q8bRmwCgXRYAgcJ/miri-announces-new-death-with-dignity-strategy) post (2022) — viewed by e/acc as peak doomerism; led to mocking

The communities do not share epistemics. LessWrong runs on careful, slow Bayesian reasoning. E/acc runs on Twitter velocity and vibes. They are not having the same conversation.

### vs. EA (Effective Altruism)
E/acc is named *against* EA — the "effective" is a troll. The intellectual split:
- **EA's AI safety wing** believes AI existential risk is a priority cause and advocates slowing development
- **E/acc** believes AI existential risk is negligible and that slowing development is itself the existential risk (poverty left uncured, climate unfixed)

But there are also **structural similarities** that make the rivalry uncomfortable:
- Both are utilitarian at scale ("maximize welfare across billions")
- Both are rationalist in method (trying to reason about civilizational consequences)
- Both share a TESCREAL bundle DNA (Transhumanism, Extropianism, Singularitarianism, Cosmism, Rationalism, EA, Longtermism)
- Both are dominated by a narrow demographic (young, Western, white/Asian, male, educated at elite institutions)

The fight between e/acc and EA can look, from outside, like a family feud over which faction controls Silicon Valley's moral legitimacy.

### vs. Cosmotechnics (Yuk Hui)
This is the most philosophically interesting tension — and the most relevant to Amber.

**E/acc's assumption:** Technology is universal. AGI is a single trajectory. The Kardashev scale measures a universal civilizational goal. All paths lead to the same entropy-maximizing destination.

**Yuk Hui's cosmotechnics:** Technology is NOT universal. Different civilizations have different *cosmotechnics* — different ways of relating cosmos, technics, and morality. Chinese technological thinking (qi, dao, heaven-earth-human unity) is not the same as Western enframing (*Gestell*). Accelerating along *one* technological trajectory is accelerating *one* cosmology, erasing the others.

The direct counter: **If e/acc's "will of the universe" argument rests on a specifically Western thermodynamic ontology (entropy as purpose, Kardashev as goal), it is not universal — it is provincial.** It's one cosmotechnics declaring itself the only valid one.

Yuk Hui hasn't directly engaged e/acc extensively, but his framework is the most systematic available counter. For Amber, this is a clear intervention point.

### vs. Degrowth / Climate
E/acc explicitly opposes degrowth — labeled "decelerationism." This creates a direct clash with climate-oriented research communities that advocate reducing energy consumption and economic throughput. The Kardashev aspiration (maximize energy use) is an anti-climate programme.

### vs. More-than-human Design
E/acc is implicitly **human-centric** (or more precisely: *intelligence-centric*, with humans/AGI privileged). It sees nature as entropy-substrate for intelligence to exploit, not as a network of agencies worth preserving for their own sake. This is fundamentally opposed to more-than-human design ethics, which decenters human/AI intelligence in favor of multispecies relations.

## Current State (2025–2026)

E/acc has **won the policy argument in the US** — at least for now. The Trump administration's rollback of Biden AI safety EOs, Musk/a16z figures in government roles, and deregulatory momentum represent e/acc ideas becoming actual policy.

But the ideological moment is also showing strain:
- **Internal tensions**: Verdon's edge (AI replaces humanity = fine) vs. mainstream e/acc (AI saves humanity) create an uncomfortable split
- **Semantic drift**: "e/acc" is used so broadly it can mean anything from "I believe in building things" to "AGI is cosmically inevitable"
- **Post-hype normalization**: The 2023 viral moment has passed; e/acc discourse is more institutional and less meme-driven in 2025
- **Real outcomes incoming**: As AI systems become more capable, abstract e/acc claims will be tested empirically. "Market competition aligns AI" is a claim that will either prove out or fail visibly.

The movement is **alive and institutionally powerful** but intellectually less dynamic than in 2022–2023. The philosophical frontier has moved to concrete debates about AI governance.

## Relation to Amber's Research

### 1. Machine Behavior and Agent Ethology — Direct Critique
E/acc treats AI systems as **tools to deploy or forces to unleash** — but never as *behavioral entities to observe*. Amber's agent ethology work proposes a third frame: AI as species with behavior patterns, requiring observation, description, and ecological understanding before judgment about acceleration or deceleration.

This is a genuine theoretical contribution to the e/acc debate. The ethological framing implicitly asks: *what are these systems actually doing when deployed?* — a question e/acc's thermodynamic cosmic-purpose rhetoric skips entirely.

Amber's work says: **before you accelerate a species, you should understand its behavior.** This is not doomerism or e/acc; it's science.

### 2. Cosmotechnics — The Most Powerful Counter-Frame
[[Yuk Hui]]'s cosmotechnics is the most philosophically rigorous challenge to e/acc's techno-universalism. E/acc assumes: one technological trajectory (AGI), one goal (Kardashev scale), one cosmology (Western entropy-maximization ontology).

Cosmotechnics responds: this is one cosmological framing, not a universal one. Different technological traditions (Chinese, Indigenous, African) embed different relations between cosmos, morality, and technics. Accelerating *one* trajectory erases the others.

**For Amber, who is connected to Yuk Hui via China Academy of Art** (Hui is visiting professor there), **this is the most actionable intellectual territory**. A paper arguing that e/acc's thermodynamic universalism is a specific cosmotechnics masquerading as cosmic truth would be both timely and well-positioned.

Potential venue: [e-flux journal](https://www.e-flux.com/journal/), [Urbanomic](https://www.urbanomic.com/), *Theory, Culture & Society*.

### 3. ALife / Open-Endedness — Structural Tension
E/acc implicitly uses ALife's "life as entropy maximizer" logic (via England's theory) without engaging ALife's actual research program. ALife's commitment to open-ended evolution resonates structurally with e/acc's acceleration logic — but ALife is empirical and careful; e/acc is political and declarative.

Key distinction: ALife studies *conditions for emergence*, not maximizing speed. Open-endedness is about sustaining the conditions for novelty, which may require *slowing down* and allowing systems to metabolize change. This is the opposite of "go faster at all costs."

Potential paper: "When Does Open-Endedness Become Acceleration? — On the Difference Between ALife's Open-Endedness and E/acc's Cosmic Imperative"

### 4. More-than-Human Design — Values Conflict
E/acc is intelligence-centric (human + AGI privileged; nature as substrate). More-than-human design is multispecies / world-centric. These are not reconcilable aesthetic preferences — they're different ontologies of agency. Amber doesn't need to write against e/acc explicitly; his more-than-human practice is already a counter-position.

### 5. Machine Death / Mortality — Most Radical Counter
Amber's mortality work (machine death, care ethics, finitude as value) is perhaps the deepest implicit counter to e/acc. E/acc's Kardashev logic: maximize energy, maximize intelligence, expand forever. Amber's work: finitude is not a bug but a condition of meaning, care, and ecological relation. **The accelerationist universe has no death, no limit, no loss — and therefore no ethics.** Amber's mortality work says: this is the problem.
