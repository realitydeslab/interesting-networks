# Inflexions: A Journal for Research-Creation

Open-access, experimental web-based journal published by the [[SenseLab]], operating 2008–2019 (11 issues + special pop-ups). Member of the **[[Open Humanities Press]]** journal collective. Co-founded by [[Erin Manning]] and [[Brian Massumi]].

The journal embeds its content in interactive, designed web environments — issues are created as events as much as publications, with web design as integral to the intellectual content. Most issues require/required Flash (now defunct) and were designed for a computer screen experience; some content exists only in the web form.

## Premise

*Inflexions* publishes work that enacts research-creation rather than just writing about it. The design, the multimedia format, the collaborative editing — all are treated as philosophical acts. Each issue is themed and assembled by guest editors from the SenseLab orbit.

## Issues

- **No. 1 — What is Inflexions?** (2008) — founding issue
- **No. 2** (2008)
- **No. 3 — Micropolitics: Exploring Ethico-Aesthetics** (Oct 2009) — edited by Nasrin Himada, Erin Manning, Leslie Plumb. Key issue: [[Brian Massumi]]'s "Of Micropolitics and Microperception" appears here.
- **No. 4 — Transversal Fields of Experience** (Nov 2010) — edited by Christoph Brunner, Troy Rhoades, Bianca Scliar, Janita Wiersma. Massumi's "On Critique" appears here.

- **No. 7 — Animating Biophilosophy** (2013) — two nodes: "Animating Biophilosophy" (AJ Nocek and Phillip Thurtle) and "From Earth to Outer Space" (Marie-Pier Boucher and Adam Szymanski).
- **No. 8 — Radical Pedagogy** (2015) — Massumi's "Collective Expression: A Radical Pragmatics" here; edited by Gerko Egert, Ilona Hongisto, et al.
- **No. 9 — F(r)ictions** (July 2016) — Hubert Gendron-Blais, Diego Gil, Joel E. Mason.
- **No. 10 — Modes of Exhaustion** (Oct 2017) — Érik Bordeleau, Christoph Brunner, Halbe Kuipers, Nguyen Nam Chi, Toni Pape.
- **INFLeXions Pop Up 1: Diversity in Diversity** (Sept 2018) — contributors including Rachel Armstrong, Rolf Hughes, Estée Klar, Tito Mukhopadhyay, Adam Wolfond.
- **No. 11 — popfab\*** (Nov 2019) — edited by Halbe Kuipers; contributions from Brasil-based practitioners and European SenseLab collaborators. The final issue.

## Why Interesting

*Inflexions* was one of the most genuinely experimental humanities journals of its era. While most "digital humanities" journals just put PDFs online, *Inflexions* tried to make the medium part of the message — at the cost of now having much of its content inaccessible due to Flash deprecation. An interesting cautionary tale about format politics in open-access publishing.

The journal directly connects to the SenseLab's philosophy of research-creation: you can't separate the intellectual content from the formal/material choices of how it's presented.
