# Inflexions: A Journal for Research-Creation

The most formally experimental humanities journal of its era. *Inflexions* published work that **enacted** research-creation rather than merely describing it — each issue built as an interactive web environment, with design, sound, and interface treated as philosophical acts as much as the writing. Founded by [[SenseLab]] (Concordia University, Montreal), now under [[3 Ecologies Institute]] curatorship. Member of the [[Open Humanities Press]] journal collective. Co-founded by [[Erin Manning]] and [[Brian Massumi]], 2008–2019, 11 issues + 1 Pop Up + 1 Out-of-Series.

ISSN: 1916-5870. Bilingual: English and French. Creative Commons BY-NC-ND 3.0.

## Mission & Premise

From the About page:

> *Inflexions* publishes articles, short texts of various genres including poetry and ficto-theory, images, sound, and other multimedia content. We invite writing and/or other forms of expression actively exploring such issues as: (inter/trans/non)disciplinarity; the emergence of new modes of collaboration; micropolitics and the life and death of institutions; creativity, subjectivity and collectivity in cultural production; the ethics of aesthetics; the aesthetic as ethics.

Each issue has two sections:
- **NODE** — thematically interlinked pieces engaging a specific problematic
- **TANGENTS** — individual contributions that strike off in their own directions, resonating across divergences

Unsolicited submissions were welcome. Peer review: vetted by editorial collective members + international advisory board, with a generous/generative ethos rather than gatekeeping.

The journal **embraced the technical possibilities of the Web** — most early issues required Flash (now deprecated), making them partially or fully inaccessible today. A cautionary tale about format politics and digital preservation in experimental publishing. PDFs of some issues were provided as fallback.

## All Issues (Complete)

### No. 1 — How is Research-Creation? (2008)
- **Edited by:** Alanna Thain, Christoph Brunner, Natasha Prévost
- *Founding issue. Sets the terms for the journal's inquiry.*

### No. 2 — Rhythmic Nexus: the Felt Togetherness of Movement and Thought (2008)
- **Edited by:** Stamatia Portonova, Bianca Scliar, Natasha Prévost
- *Movement, rhythm, togetherness as philosophical problems.*

### No. 3 — Micropolitics: Exploring Ethico-Aesthetics (Oct 2009)
- **Edited by:** Nasrin Himada, Erin Manning, Leslie Plumb
- **Key text:** Brian Massumi, "Of Micropolitics and Microperception"
- *Massumi's micropolitics concept fully developed here. Ethico-aesthetics as the terrain where affect meets politics.*

### No. 4 — Transversal Fields of Experience (Nov 2010)
- **Edited by:** Christoph Brunner, Troy Rhoades, Bianca Scliar, Janita Wiersma
- **Key text:** Brian Massumi, "On Critique"
- *Transversality (Guattari) as mode of interdisciplinary encounter.*

### No. 5 — Gilbert Simondon (March 2012)
- **Edited by:** Marie-Pierre Boucher, Patrick Harrop, Troy Rhoades
- Interface concept design by Ron Simon; web design by Leslie Plumb
- **NODE contributors:** Didier Debaise, Ted Krueger, Thomas Lamarre, Rob Mitchell, Chris Salter, Henning Schmidgen, Marie-Pier Boucher & Patrick Harrop (interview with Rafael Lozano-Hemmer)
- *Simondon's individuation theory as resource for art-science interface. Major Simondon issue in Anglophone context.*

### No. 6 — Arakawa & Gins, a Special Issue (2012)
- **Edited by:** Jondi Keane & Trish Glazebrook
- Web design by Leslie Plumb
- *Tribute and exploration of [Arakawa and Gins](https://www.reversibledestiny.org/)' reversible destiny architecture. Arakawa died May 18, 2010. Issue includes keynotes, tributes, and tangents.*
- **Notable contributors:** Eugene Gendlin, Ken Wark, Erin Manning, Brian Massumi (co-authored tangent with Manning), Helene Frichot, Thomas Lamarre, Don Ihde, George Quasha, Gregg Lambert
- **Key text:** Erin Manning & Brian Massumi, "A Perspective of the Universe" (pp. 450-458)

### Into the Midst (Out-of-Series, 2012) — *Flash only*
- **Edited by:** Erin Manning; Web design: Erin Manning & Leslie Plumb
- Documents the SenseLab's [Into the Midst](http://senselab.ca/wp2/events/into-the-midst/) event (Oct 2012): a 5-day collaborative research-creation workshop in/around the SATosphere (Society for Art and Technology's immersive dome), Montreal
- *First "out of series" issue. Collective authorship, design as chorus.*
- ⚠️ Flash only — essentially inaccessible today

### No. 7 — Animating Biophilosophy (2013)
- **NODE edited by:** A.J. Nocek and Phillip Thurtle
- **TANGENT edited by:** Marie-Pier Boucher and Adam Szymanski
- Web design by Leslie Plumb
- **NODE contributors:** Eugene Thacker ("On Ascensionism"), A.J. Nocek, Henning Schmidgen, Phillip Thurtle, Deborah Levitt, Richard Doyle, Elizabeth Buschmann (video), Stephanie Maxwell (video)
- **TANGENT contributors:** Greg Lynn, Gerko Egert, Toni Pape (on Christian Marclay's The Clock), Adam Szymanski (on Peter Mettler), Adam Zaretsky, Andy Gracie

### No. 8 — Radical Pedagogy (2015)

## Key Texts Published in Inflexions

Across its 11 issues, *Inflexions* published some of the defining texts of affect theory and research-creation methodology:

- **Brian Massumi**, "Of Micropolitics and Microperception" — No. 3 (2009) — one of his clearest expositions of micropolitics
- **Brian Massumi**, "On Critique" — No. 4 (2010) — his response to the "turn against critique" debate
- **Brian Massumi**, "Collective Expression: A Radical Pragmatics" — No. 8 (2015), pp. 59-88 — pragmatist theory of collective action
- **Erin Manning & Brian Massumi**, "A Perspective of the Universe" — No. 6 (2012) — collaborative text on Arakawa/Gins
- **Erin Manning**, "10 Propositions for a Radical Pedagogy" — No. 8 (2015) — concise statement of research-creation pedagogy
- **Eugene Thacker**, "On Ascensionism" — No. 7 (2013) — early text in his dark vitalism trajectory
- **Thomas Lamarre**, "Humans and Machines" — No. 5 (2012) — Simondon + anime + media theory
- **Chris Salter**, "Just Noticeable Difference: Ontogenesis, Performativity and the Perceptual Gap" — No. 5 (2012)

## Intellectual Lineage & Key Concepts

*Inflexions* drew on a specific philosophical toolkit:
- **Gilles Deleuze & Félix Guattari** — transversality, rhizome, affect, micropolitics, molecular/molar distinction
- **Alfred North Whitehead** — process philosophy, events, prehension (quoted on the about page)
- **William James** — pragmatism, experience
- **Henri Bergson** — duration, movement, creative evolution
- **Gilbert Simondon** (No. 5) — individuation, technical objects, phase shifts
- **Shusaku Arakawa & Madeline Gins** (No. 6) — reversible destiny, procedural architecture
- **Félix Guattari** — three ecologies (social/environmental/conceptual) — directly names the successor organization 3 Ecologies
- **The Undercommons** (Harney & Moten) — appears in No. 8 tangent title

## Format & Accessibility Notes

The journal's commitment to the web as medium came at a cost:
- **Issues 1–7 and "Into the Midst"**: Require **Adobe Flash** — now defunct in all major browsers. These issues are largely inaccessible without historical browser emulation.
- **Issues 8–11 and Pop Up 1**: Use JavaScript/HTML5 — still accessible on desktop; mobile access limited for some
- **PDFs available** for issues 10+ as fallback
- The website remains live at [inflexions.org](http://www.inflexions.org)
- The [Internet Archive](https://web.archive.org/web/*/http://www.inflexions.org/) has some captured versions, but Flash content is unplayable

This is one of the most significant digital preservation failures in experimental humanities publishing. The journal's most formally adventurous issues are now effectively inaccessible.

## Successor: 3 Ecologies Project

After *Inflexions* ceased in 2019, the [[SenseLab]] transitioned its extra-university activities into the **[3 Ecologies Project](https://3ecologies.org)** (3E):
- Non-profit registered in Quebec
- "Autonomous organization dedicated to participatory experimentation in research-creation"
- Operates as a "process seed bank" — disseminating seeds of creative practice
- Named after Guattari's three ecologies (social, environmental, conceptual)
- Network of 800+ participants worldwide; active local groups in Montreal, Australia, Brazil, Europe
- Formal partnerships with 24+ universities and art organizations
- All activities open and free of charge
- Special emphasis on neurodiversity, non-normative perceptual worlds

## For Amber

### Why This Journal Matters to Amber

*Inflexions* is the ur-text of **research-creation** as a philosophically serious practice. For Amber's HCI/design work at Oxford, this genealogy is crucial: the journal develops the epistemological infrastructure for claiming that making IS knowing, that design IS philosophy, that the form of a work carries its intellectual content.

### Direct Connections

1. **🔧 Methodology** — The NODE/TANGENT structure (thematic core + resonant tangents) is a model Amber could adapt for workshop or symposium design. It creates productive obliqueness — you learn from the angle, not just the center.

2. **💡 Key concept: Research-Creation** — Inflexions articulates what "research-creation" means better than any other source. This is directly relevant to Amber's practice-based PhD at Oxford. Manning/Massumi offer a philosophical grounding for claims that practice-based work IS research.

3. **🌍 Somaesthetics connection** — Manning's work on the minor gesture, autistic perception (Adam Wolfond, Tito Mukhopadhyay in Pop Up 1), and neurodiversity as philosophical resource connects directly to Amber's interest in embodied computing and somaesthetics. The [[TEI]] community (which Amber knows well) and *Inflexions* share deep roots in phenomenology of the body.

4. **🌍 Simondon** — Issue No. 5 is essential background for anyone working on AI/machine agency. Simondon's *individuation* concept (things emerge through processes, not as pre-formed entities) is directly applicable to understanding machine behavior, artificial life, and open-ended AI systems — all in Amber's wheelhouse.

5. **🌍 Biophilosophy / ALife connection** — Issue No. 7 (Animating Biophilosophy) connects directly to Amber's ALIFE interests. Eugene Thacker's piece, Adam Zaretsky's bioart, and the vitalism thread running through the issue provide philosophical resources for thinking about artificial life as philosophical inquiry.

6. **🤝 Network** — The SenseLab/Inflexions network intersects with multiple communities Amber moves in: [[Erin Manning]] has exhibited at [[Ars Electronica]]; [[Christoph Brunner]] is now at Leuphana (near the European critical media scene); the Simondon + biophilosophy cluster connects to SFI/ALife.

7. **📚 The Flash deprecation story** — Inflexions' inaccessibility is a cautionary tale for Amber's own work on mixed reality and experimental interfaces. The medium IS the message, but the medium can also fail. How to design research artifacts that outlive their technical substrate?

8. **🔮 3 Ecologies Project** — The successor organization is worth watching. Their "process seed bank" model (distributing techniques rather than products) is a design research methodology that echoes Future Observatory's community-as-research-infrastructure model.

### Engagement Paths
- Read the PDF-accessible issues (8-11) first — they're the most recent and most accessible
- Manning's *The Minor Gesture* (Duke UP, 2016) is the best book-length development of Inflexions ideas
- Massumi's *Politics of Affect* (Polity, 2015) is more accessible than *Parables for the Virtual*
- The [3 Ecologies Project](https://3ecologies.org) sometimes runs public events — worth tracking

## Why Interesting

*Inflexions* is rare: a humanities journal that tried to make the form do theoretical work. Most journals are delivery mechanisms for ideas; *Inflexions* tried to make the delivery inseparable from the ideas. That it largely failed (Flash deprecation) is almost as interesting as what it achieved. 

The intellectual lineage — Deleuze-Guattari, Whitehead, Simondon, Bergson — is a coherent and useful toolkit for anyone thinking about process, emergence, and agency. The research-creation concept it developed is the philosophical underpinning for practice-based research in HCI, design, and media arts.

The neurodiversity turn in later issues (Pop Up 1: Tito Mukhopadhyay, Adam Wolfond) is ahead of its time and connects to current conversations about non-normative cognition, accessibility, and what counts as legitimate knowledge production.

The Brasil-heavy final issue signals the SenseLab network's genuine global reach (not just Anglophone) — the Paulista somatic/political scene (Christine Greiner, Fabiane M. Borges) brought into dialogue with Northern European and Canadian researchers.
