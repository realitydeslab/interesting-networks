# Petzel Gallery

Contemporary commercial gallery in New York representing ~47 international artists and estates. Founded by Friedrich Petzel. Two Manhattan locations: primary at **520 W 25th Street** (Chelsea) and secondary at **35 E 67th Street** (Upper East Side). One of the most intellectually serious commercial galleries in NYC — genuinely rigorous about conceptual and critical art, with a multigenerational roster bridging postwar European conceptualism (Maria Lassnig, Hanne Darboven, Asger Jorn) and contemporary critical-tech practice ([[Simon Denny]], Seth Price, Nikita Gale). Known for supporting work that engages with technology, politics, and culture at a genuinely theoretical level, not just aesthetically.

## Programmes
Petzel runs a **"The Viewing Room"** series — short-run exhibitions (usually one week) within the gallery space, spotlighting single works or small focused presentations. Has featured: Grace Weaver, Hannah Wilke, Tschabalala Self, Emily Mae Smith, Ross Bleckner.

No formal residency or fellowship programme. Engagement is primarily through artist representation and exhibition. Gallery participates in **Art Basel**, **TEFAF**, **NADA** art fairs.

## Why Interesting
Petzel is where critical-tech art gets legitimized in the NYC commercial market. The gallery's long-term commitment to Simon Denny (from 2011 to present) demonstrates that platform critique, crypto-culture analysis, and game dungeon aesthetics can sustain a serious commercial art practice. Friedrich Petzel's European background (the roster is heavy with German/Austrian artists) gives the gallery a more theoretically serious character than many New York commercial galleries.

The MUD group show (2024) was the most intellectually ambitious show the gallery has done recently: tracing the genealogy of virtual worlds from BBS and text-game culture to contemporary metaverses, and connecting that history to painting, sculpture, and critical media art. This is the kind of curatorial thinking Amber should be aware of — it operates at the intersection of platform studies, game studies, and contemporary art that Amber's own research inhabits.

**For Amber specifically:** Petzel represents the commercial art world's most credible engagement with the territories Amber researches academically. It's worth tracking because the artists here (Denny, Price, Young, Gale) will likely end up at the same venues, symposia, and festivals that Amber attends.
