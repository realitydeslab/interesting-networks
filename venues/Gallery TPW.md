Artist-run centre and independent gallery in Toronto, Canada. Founded 1977 as Toronto Photographers Workshop, now operating as Gallery TPW. One of Canada's leading artist-run spaces committed to lens-based and contemporary art practices, with a particular focus on underrepresented voices and experimental approaches to image-making. The only organization of its scale in the Greater Toronto Area committed to underrepresented artistic and curatorial practices at the edge of photography.

## Mission & History

- **Founded:** 1977 as Toronto Photographers Workshop
- **Continuous exhibitions since:** 1980
- **Location:** Toronto (artist-run, not affiliated with any university or municipality)
- **Key shift:** 2012–2014 ran "TPW R&D" — a flexible storefront for artistic performance and educational experiments, temporarily stepping away from structured exhibition. This informed their current hybrid model.
- **New home since April 2015:** Integrated performance, sound, movement, and constructed social events into exhibition model
- **Current focus:** Lens-based work expanded to include video, performance, text, sculpture — anything intersecting with image culture

**Mission excerpt:** "Gallery TPW is the only organization of its scale within the GTA committed to supporting underrepresented artistic and curatorial practices that push the boundaries of lens-based work."

**Philosophical commitment:** Audience engagement, self-reflexive institutional inquiry, and transparency. The gallery is unusual in foregrounding how its own choices affect the meaning of art — a kind of institutional criticality.

## Key People

- **[Liz Ikiriko](https://www.gallerytpw.ca/)** — Curator (active 2020–). Curated "a lineage of transgression" editions (2020, 2025, 2026). Strong focus on Black feminist cultural lineages, language, and transgression. Based Toronto.
- **TPW Acquisitions Committee** (est. 1990) — formed to collect work; collection now held at the Image Centre (Toronto Metropolitan University)

## Submission / Engagement Model

Gallery TPW is an artist-run centre — it does **not** have an open call submission system like a festival. Exhibition proposals are typically invited or developed through curatorial relationships. Engagement pathways:

1. **Curatorial relationship** — build relationship with curator Liz Ikiriko or other programmers
2. **TD Vitrine open calls** — the Vitrine programme (2025–27 is currently running) likely runs open calls every 2 years; watch for 2027 cycle
3. **Public programming** — talks, screenings, reading groups are sometimes open to proposals
4. **Institutional network** — TPW is deeply connected to Canadian artist-run centre network; entry via fellow institutions (YYZ, Mercer Union, Plug In ICA)
5. **Write for TPW** — they commission artist writings and critical essays; pitch to program team

**Contact:** info@gallerytpw.ca | 170 St. Helens Avenue, Toronto ON
