# IEEE VIS

The premier annual conference on data visualization and visual analytics, sponsored by IEEE Computer Society and the Visualization and Graphics Technical Community (VGTC). One of the largest and most prestigious venues in the information visualization / visual analytics space. Typically held in October–November in the US.

## Topics

IEEE VIS uses a six-area model (introduced around 2021 reVISe reform):

### Area 1: Theoretical & Empirical
Perception studies, evaluation methodologies, user studies, frameworks. The most "science" of VIS.

### Area 2: Applications
Domain-specific visualization: biomedical, climate, urban, social. Design studies.

### Area 3: Systems & Rendering
GPU rendering, large-scale data, real-time vis, scientific visualization (SciVis).

### Area 4: Representations & Interaction
Novel visual encodings, interaction techniques, storytelling, narrative visualization.
→ **Most relevant to Amber** — includes narrative, expressive, interactive vis.

### Area 5: Data Transformations
ML-based vis, dimensionality reduction, data processing pipelines.

### Area 6: Analysis & Decisions
Visual analytics, decision support, uncertainty, trust in AI-generated views.

### Associated Tracks (beyond papers)
- **Short Papers** — 4-page, late-breaking, tools, evaluations; deadline Apr 30
- **Posters** — work-in-progress, early results, speculative; deadline Jun 24
- **VISAP** — VIS Arts Program: mini-conference + exhibition (papers/pictorials + art show)
- **Workshops** — satellite workshops (e.g., EnergyVis, CityVis)
- **Vis in Practice** — industry applications
- **Doctoral Colloquium**
- **VAST Challenge / SciVis Contest** — annual competitions

### Relevance Assessment

**Short answer:** IEEE VIS is not Amber's primary home — it's primarily an engineering/science conference for data visualization specialists. But **VISAP (the Arts Program) is genuinely exciting and relevant**.

**Where Amber fits:**
1. **VISAP (Arts Program)** ⭐⭐⭐ — This is where Amber belongs at IEEE VIS. VISAP 2025 explicitly called for:
   - "Environmental, eco-critical, and more-than-human data engagements"
   - "Algorithmic storytelling and human–machine collaboration"
   - "Experimental, poetic, and speculative visualizations"
   - "Anti-colonial and decolonial approaches to data"

   VISAP is juried, publishes in IEEE Xplore, and is held as a mini-conference within VIS. This framing maps closely to Amber's practice.

2. **Area 4: Representations & Interaction** — If Amber has spatial visualization work (GravField traces, agent behavior mapping), this could be a home. A design study on visualizing agent behavioral patterns would be a stretch but possible.

3. **Posters** — Low-barrier, non-archival (optional inclusion). Could be used to test whether VIS community responds to agent ethology framing.

### GravField / Agent Visualization Angle

The "vis" angle — spatial flow visualization, real-time field rendering, multi-user shared perception — could be framed as a novel interaction/representation at Area 4 or Short Papers. But the community is highly technical; it would need a rigorous empirical evaluation to be competitive at full papers.
