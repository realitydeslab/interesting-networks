# RTD (Research Through Design)

Intimate, experimental, artefact-first. RTD is the premier practice-based design research conference — the one that decided that showing artifacts is more generative than presenting papers in dark auditoriums. Proceeds via curated **exhibition + round-table discussions** in themed "Rooms of Interest" rather than conventional presentation sessions. At its peak, a formative community for practice-based research. Currently **dormant since 2019** (COVID disrupted the planned 2022 edition; future unclear).

## Submission Format (Historical — RTD 2019)
RTD does **not** use traditional paper formats. Key types:
- **Rooms of Interest submissions** — practice-based artifacts with accompanying documentation/text (not standard papers; the artifact IS the research)
- **Provocations** — invited plenary talks designed to spark debate
- Two-stage review: Stage 1 = broad suitability; Stage 2 = full submission with artifact documentation
- Acceptance rate: ~15% overall (Stage 1 → final), ~64% at Stage 2 (for those who pass Stage 1)
- Paper length: varies; format is more like annotated portfolios than standard papers
