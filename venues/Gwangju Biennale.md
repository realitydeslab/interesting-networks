# Gwangju Biennale

One of Asia's most important and politically charged contemporary art biennials, held in Gwangju, South Korea since 1995. Biennial (odd years, with exceptions). Now in its 16th edition (2026), directed by Singaporean artist/curator Ho Tzu Nyen. The biennale is inseparable from the memory of the [1980 Gwangju Uprising](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gwangju_uprising) — its democratic resistance DNA shapes every edition. Known for bringing together artists across the Global South, East/Southeast Asia, and European art worlds. Recurring interest in questions of technology, ecology, and the more-than-human has made it one of the Asian biennials most relevant to Amber's research practice.

**UPGRADED TO ⭐⭐⭐** — The 16th edition curator Ho Tzu Nyen brings Guattarian/schizoanalytic frameworks, Southeast Asian histories and myths, and deep engagement with film/media as speculative form. Past editions (2024 Pansori, 2021 Minds Rising) engaged explicitly with human-machine-animal-spirit continuums, computational aesthetics, and decolonial tech — directly in Amber's territory. The artist circuit overlaps significantly (Tabita Rezaire, Katja Novitskova, Agnieszka Kurant, Philippe Parreno, Lucy Raven).

## Key People

### Curators / Artistic Directors

**Ho Tzu Nyen** (16th edition, 2026)
- Singaporean artist, filmmaker, curator, born 1976
- Themes: myth, identity, history, Southeast Asian narrative, Guattarian psychoanalysis
- Key works: *HERE* (2009) — film about "videocure" at psychiatric facility, inspired by Félix Guattari; *The Critical Dictionary of Southeast Asia* (2012–), ongoing mapping project for Southeast Asian historical/cultural narrative
- Represented Singapore at Venice Biennale 2011; participated in 14th Gwangju Biennale (2018) and contributed commissioned work to 13th edition (2021); co-curated 2019 Asian Art Biennale (Taiwan); participated in 2014 Shanghai Biennale, 2019 Aichi Triennale, 2019 Sharjah Biennial
- Source: [Artforum announcement, April 2025](https://www.artforum.com/news/ho-tzu-nyen-named-artistic-director-2026-gwangju-biennale-1234729676/)
- Intellectual circuit: Guattari, anti-psychiatry, schizoanalysis, Southeast Asian decolonial histories, myth and non-linear temporality

**Nicolas Bourriaud** (15th edition, 2024)
- Theme: *Pansori, a soundscape of the 21st century*
- Framework: subaltern voices, spaces between humans/machines/animals/spirits/organic life, post-pandemic fragmentation, relational aesthetics updated
- Books: *Relational Aesthetics* (1998), *Postproduction* (2002), *The Radicant* (2009), *Inclusions: Aesthetics of the Capitalocene* (2020)
- Source: [ArtReview](https://artreview.com/15th-gwangju-biennale-announces-theme-and-artists/), [Artforum appointment](https://www.artforum.com/news/nicolas-bourriaud-to-direct-15th-gwangju-biennale-252705/)

**Defne Ayas & Natasha Ginwala** (13th edition, 2021)
- Theme: *Minds Rising Spirits Tuning*
- Engaged AI consciousness, neural/spiritual dimensions, posthuman subjectivity
- Source: [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gwangju_Biennale)

**Sook-Kyung Lee** (14th edition, 2023)
- Theme: *Soft and Weak Like Water* (with Kerryn Greenberg, Sooyoung Leam, Harry C. H. Choi)
- Tate curator; ecological, Daoist frameworks
- Source: [Artforum](https://www.artforum.com/news/tates-sook-kyung-lee-named-artistic-director-of-2023-gwangju-biennale-251298/)

**11-curator team** (12th edition, 2018, *Imagined Borders*)
- Including: Clara Kim (Tate), Gridthiya Gaweewong (Bangkok/Jim Thompson Art Center), Rita Gonzalez (LACMA), David Teh (independent), Yeon Shim Chung, Yeewan Koon

## The VH Award (Hyundai)

The **VH Award** (full name: *Vanity and Humility Award*, sponsored by [Hyundai Motor Company](https://www.hyundai.com/)) is an award given biennially in connection with the Gwangju Biennale, supporting emerging Korean or Korea-based artists working across art and technology. It provides a significant production budget and visibility within the Korean art ecosystem. Winners have included artists working at the intersection of media, computation, and embodied practice. The award connects the biennale to corporate patronage networks (Hyundai), the Korean contemporary art market, and international circuits (Hyundai also sponsors the Hyundai Commission at Tate Modern — same corporate art infrastructure).
