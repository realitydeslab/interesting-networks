# New Museum

New York City's primary museum dedicated to contemporary art from emerging and underrepresented artists and perspectives. Located on the Bowery in Manhattan. Founded 1977 by Marcia Tucker. Institutional home of **[[Rhizome]]** (digital art platform) and host of **The Generational** — a triennial exhibition series.

## Key People
- **Lisa Phillips** — Director
- **[[Massimiliano Gioni]]** — Director of International Exhibitions; co-curated *Younger Than Jesus* (2009 Triennial)
- **[[Lauren Cornell]]** — Curator/Adjunct Curator 2007–2017; organized two Triennial editions
- **[[Laura Hoptman]]** — Senior Curator; co-curated *Younger Than Jesus*

## Topics
- Contemporary art from emerging voices
- Internet art and digital culture (via Rhizome)
- Technology and society
- Social and political art practice

## Key Events / Exhibitions
- **The Generational: *Younger Than Jesus*** (2009) — curated by Cornell, Gioni, Hoptman; 50 artists from 25 countries under 33 years old
- **[*Free*](https://archive.newmuseum.org/exhibitions/1092)** (2010) — curated by Lauren Cornell; art and the internet as public space
- **The Generational: *Surround Audience*** (2015) — curated by Lauren Cornell and Ryan Trecartin; post-internet condition

## Why Interesting
The New Museum's Rhizome affiliation and Triennial programming have made it the most important US institution for the art-technology intersection since the 2000s. The three Triennial editions under Cornell's hand — or co-organization — trace the arc from net art to post-internet to total mediation.
