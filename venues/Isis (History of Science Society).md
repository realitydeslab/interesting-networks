# Isis (History of Science Society)

The flagship journal of the History of Science Society (HSS), published by University of Chicago Press. The oldest and most prestigious journal in history of science — founded 1913. Publishes original research articles, critical essays, and book reviews spanning the full history of science, technology, and medicine.

*Osiris* is its thematic companion journal, publishing annual themed volumes.

## Key People

- [[Stephanie Dick]] — major author; "AfterMath" (2011), "Of Models and Machines" (2015)

## Topics

- History of mathematics, physics, biology, chemistry, medicine
- History of computing and AI
- Feminist, postcolonial, and STS approaches to science history
- Philosophy of science historically situated

## Why Interesting

Publishing in *Isis* is the mark of a serious historian of science. Dick's two *Isis* papers established her reputation in the field. The journal increasingly publishes critical computing history — making it a convergence point for STS scholars interested in AI history.
