## Vibe
LA's most agile non-collecting contemporary art institution. Former name: Santa Monica Museum of Art (SMMoA). Punches way above its weight — 12,700 sq ft, always free, politically engaged, culturally diverse. Under [[Anne Ellegood]] since 2019, it has sharpened its focus on work by artists from underrepresented communities: Indigenous, queer, Latinx, Black. Not a tech-art institution — not really interested in AI or speculative systems — but deeply invested in political and critical practice. The vibe is closer to a kunsthalle with a social justice agenda than a commercial white cube or a media arts center. Think: Chicano muralism meets feminist press meets decolonial theory. Located in Downtown LA's Arts District (not Silver Lake, not Hollywood — the gritty, adjacent-to-skid-row end of downtown).

## History
- **1984:** Founded as Santa Monica Museum of Art (SMMoA) by developer Abby Sher in the Frank Gehry–designed Edgemar complex on Main Street, Santa Monica.
- **1988:** [Thomas Rhoads](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Institute_of_Contemporary_Art,_Los_Angeles) becomes first executive director. Inaugural show: *Art in the Raw* in a former ice warehouse. Begins championing emerging artists via Artist Project series — first solo shows for Jennifer Steinkamp (1989), Allan Sekula (1996).
- **1997–1998:** Moves to [Bergamot Station Arts Center](https://www.bergamotstation.com/) in Santa Monica.
- **2000:** Elsa Longhauser succeeds Rhoads as director. Marks arrival with retrospective of feminist Austrian artist [Valie Export](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/VALIE_EXPORT). Notable shows during Longhauser era: Yoshimoto Nara (2000), Urs Fischer (2002), Mickalene Thomas (2012).
- **2005:** Guest-curated [Semina Culture: Wallace Berman and His Circle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Semina_Culture) — key counterculture history show.
- **2015:** Leaves Santa Monica / Bergamot Station.
- **2016:** Official name change to Institute of Contemporary Art, Los Angeles (ICA LA). Announces relocation to Downtown LA.
- **2017:** Reopens in Downtown Arts District after $5M fundraising campaign. New space: 12,700 sq ft in renovated former manufacturing building designed by [wHY Architecture](https://www.why-site.com/) (Kulapat Yantrasast).
- **2019:** [Anne Ellegood](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anne_Ellegood) named Executive Director (July). Comes from Hammer Museum (senior curator, 2009–2019).
- **2024:** Announces $5M purchase plan for current building, majority from Mohn Family Trust — rare institutional stability move for an LA kunsthalle.

## Key People

### Leadership
- **[[Anne Ellegood]]** — Executive Director (2019–present). Born Portland, OR, c.1966/67. BA Women's Studies, University of Colorado Boulder. MFA/Curatorial Studies, [Center for Curatorial Studies, Bard College](https://www.bard.edu/ccs/) (1998). Career: New Museum (curator, post-Bard), Peter Norton collection (local curator NYC), [Hirshhorn Museum](https://hirshhorn.si.edu/) (curator, 2005–2009), [[Hammer Museum]] (senior curator, 2009–2019). Key projects at Hammer: *Made in L.A.* biennial 2012 and 2018 (co-organized), *Take It or Leave It: Institution, Image, Ideology* (2014, with Johanna Burton — on appropriation art and institutional critique), Jimmie Durham retrospective (2017, controversial re: Cherokee identity). Mentor: [Marcia Tucker](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marcia_Tucker) (New Museum founder). Programming philosophy: politically engaged, focused on underrepresented artists, anti-hierarchical, artist-first.

### Exhibition Cycles
Three exhibition seasons per year (spring/fall/winter), each featuring 3–5 concurrent exhibitions. Mix of:
- Solo commissions by emerging/mid-career artists
- Thematic group shows with strong conceptual framing
- Publisher/press partnerships (Feminist Press, J&L Books, Ooga Booga, GenderFail)

### Learning & Community Programs
- **[Agency of Assets](https://www.theicala.org/en/learning/5-agency-of-assets)** — Youth fellowship: paid summer employment, mentorship, arts leadership for local youth. Annual. Produced *Data (beta)* with York Chang.
- **AR Transmissions** — Augmented reality learning program (one of the rare tech-adjacent initiatives)
- **Community voting programmes** — Has hosted voter registration/voting at ICA LA
- **Publisher partnerships** — Extended collaborations with small presses (Feminist Press, J&L Books, GenderFail, Cardboard House Press, Ooga Booga, Reparations Club) that function as mini-exhibitions/reading rooms

### Programming Philosophy (Ellegood era, 2019–present)
Current mission: "support art that sparks the pleasure of discovery and challenges the way we see and experience the world, ourselves, and each other... committed to upending hierarchies of race, class, gender, and culture."

Strong signals: decolonial practice, Indigenous artists, queer/trans artists, Latinx artists, feminist publishing, performance, community engagement. **Not** a digital art or tech-art space. **Not** interested in AI/ML. **Not** doing speculative design shows. The political critique is present but it's cultural-political (race, colonialism, gender) rather than techno-political (surveillance, algorithmic power, machine behavior).

## Relationship to LA Art Ecosystem
- **vs. [[Hammer Museum]]:** Hammer is university-affiliated (UCLA), has a permanent collection program (*Made in L.A.* biennial), larger budget, more centrist. ICA LA is more radical/experimental, smaller, no collection, more purely kunsthalle. Ellegood came from Hammer but has sharpened ICA LA's politics.
- **vs. LACMA:** LACMA is encyclopedic collection museum, very different scale/mission.
- **vs. MOCA:** Museum of Contemporary Art has a collection, more blue-chip. ICA LA is non-collecting, artist-first.
- **vs. [[LAXART]]:** LAXART is the peer grassroots institution, even more experimental/project-based. Together they form the "adventurous LA contemporary art" bracket.
- **Downtown Arts District location:** Proximity to other arts orgs in that neighborhood.
