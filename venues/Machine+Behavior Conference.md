## Vibe
Small, elite, agenda-setting. ~100-150 attendees. Not a mega-conference — feels like a working meeting where the future of a field gets shaped. Held at [Harnack House](https://www.harnackhaus-berlin.mpg.de/en), the historic Max Planck conference venue in Berlin (where Einstein used to hang out). The intimacy is the point: AI scientists and behavioral scientists in the same room, actually talking.

## Mission & Scope
Born from [[Iyad Rahwan]]'s [seminal 2019 Nature paper](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-019-1138-y) "Machine Behaviour" which argued that AI systems should be studied like organisms — through behavioral observation, not just engineering specs. The conference operationalizes this vision:

- How will AI impact human communication, cooperation, conflict, culture?
- How do we ensure AI safety when human cognitive limitations are the attack vector?
- What can biology, political science, cultural evolution teach us about controlling more powerful intelligence?
- How do human-machine collectives behave at scale?
