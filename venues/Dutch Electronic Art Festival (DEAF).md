# Dutch Electronic Art Festival (DEAF)

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-03-03:** Full deep research by subagent (dr-deaf-festival). Expanded all sections: complete edition history, key people, notable artists, V2_ publications, Amber engagement paths. Confirmed dormant since 2014.
- **2026-02-26:** Stub created during V2_Lab deep research. Last edition: 2014.

Biannual (later irregular) festival organized by [[V2_Lab for the Unstable Media]] in Rotterdam, showcasing crossovers between art, technology and society. One of Europe's foundational media art festivals, running 10 editions from 1994 to 2014. Its forerunner, the *Manifestation for the Unstable Media*, was held annually at V2_'s earlier location in 's-Hertogenbosch from 1986. No new edition announced since 2014 — effectively dormant. V2_ continues as an active institution running residencies, fellowships, and events in DEAF's intellectual spirit.

---

## History & Background

**Forerunner:** [Manifestation for the Unstable Media](https://v2.nl/events/manifestation-for-the-unstable-media) — annual event at V2_ in 's-Hertogenbosch (Den Bosch), 1986–1993. V2_ itself was founded in 1987 in 's-Hertogenbosch, before relocating to Rotterdam in 1994.

**DEAF proper:** Launched 1994 when V2_ moved to Rotterdam. Ran 10 editions over 20 years. Not strictly biennial — some years were skipped (especially between 2007 and 2012 — a 5-year gap). Format combined exhibitions, performances, symposia, workshops, and publications.

**Intellectual DNA:** DEAF was organized as a *festival of ideas*, not just an art show. Each edition had a strong curatorial theme, explored through both artworks and associated publications (the V2_/NAi Publishers book series). The themes track the evolution of media art discourse from the 1990s through the 2010s: from ecological and generative systems, through accidents and networks, to affective computing, things/objects, and critiques of progress ideology.

**Current Status:** No edition since 2014. V2_ has not announced a revival. The organization continues through other formats: Open_Lab, Summersessions, Wintersessions, residencies, fellowships. DEAF's intellectual legacy persists in V2_'s ongoing programming.

**Archive:** V2_ maintains an online archive at [v2.nl/events/deaf](https://v2.nl/events/deaf). Edition archives accessible via Monoskop: [monoskop.org/Dutch_Electronic_Art_Festival](https://monoskop.org/Dutch_Electronic_Art_Festival).

## Thematic Evolution

The themes of DEAF editions trace the discourse arc of European media art and critical technology studies:

- **1994 — Generated Nature:** Generative systems, artificial life, nature/technology. Connected to early ALife discourse. Directly resonant with Amber's interest in ALife and computational creativity.
- **1995 — Interfacing Realities:** Human-computer interface as reality mediation. Early VR, telepresence.
- **1996 — Digital Territories:** Networks, spatial politics of digital culture.
- **1998 — The Art of the Accident:** Glitch, failure, error as aesthetic and epistemic. Paul Virilio's "the accident" as framework. Published as book edited by Joke Brouwer & Arjen Mulder ([V2_/NAi, 1998](https://v2.nl/archive/articles/art-of-the-accident)).
- **2000 — Machine Times:** Temporal politics of technology, machine rhythms. Book published: *Machine Times* (ed. Joke Brouwer & Arjen Mulder, V2_/NAi Publishers, 2000. URL: [v2.nl/archive/articles/machine-times](https://v2.nl/archive/articles/machine-times)).
- **2003 — Data Knitting:** Networked fabric of data, weaving/webs metaphor.
- **2004 — Affective Turbulence:** Emotional and affective computing. AI/technology and emotions.
- **2007 — Interact or Die!:** Participatory systems, interaction as survival. Book published: *Interact or Die!* (ed. Joke Brouwer & Arjen Mulder, V2_/NAi, 2007. URL: [v2.nl/archive/articles/interact-or-die](https://v2.nl/archive/articles/interact-or-die)).
- **2012 — The Power of Things!:** Agency of objects, things that act, posthuman actants. Direct intersection with OOO, speculative realism, and more-than-human design.
- **2014 — The Progress Trap:** Critique of "progress" ideology, tech disruption narratives, sustainability. The most explicitly political edition.

**Arc reading:** From generative aesthetics → networked culture → affective computing → posthuman things → critique of progress. This arc anticipates Amber's territory exactly — especially the pivot toward more-than-human and machine agency themes.

## Key People

### Founders / Artistic Directors
- **[[Alex Adriaansens]]** — V2_ co-founder (with Joke Brouwer and Marleen Stikker), longtime DEAF artistic director. The V2_ Alex Adriaansens Residency is named after him. URL: [v2.nl/people/alex-adriaansens](https://v2.nl/people/alex-adriaansens)
- **[[Joke Brouwer]]** — V2_ co-founder, director for many years, co-editor of the DEAF publication series with Arjen Mulder. Key intellectual architect of DEAF's thematic framework.
- **[[Marleen Stikker]]** — V2_ co-founder (left early; now director of [[Waag Futurelab]] Amsterdam). Pioneer of early internet culture (De Digitale Stad / Digital City Amsterdam). URL: [waag.org](https://waag.org)

### Associated Theorists / Curators
- **[[Arjen Mulder]]** — Writer and theorist, long-term associate at V2_. Co-edited many DEAF publications with Joke Brouwer. Published extensively on biology, perception, and media. URL: [v2.nl/people/arjen-mulder](https://v2.nl/people/arjen-mulder)
- **[[Andreas Broeckmann]]** — Art historian/curator, V2_ Fellow, Berlin. Expert in machine aesthetics and media art history. URL: [v2.nl/people/andreas-broeckmann](https://v2.nl/people/andreas-broeckmann)
- **[[Nat Muller]]** — Independent curator associated with V2_ and Dutch media art scene; now based partly in the Middle East, curating Arab contemporary media art.

## V2_ Publications (DEAF Book Series)

V2_ produced an important series of anthologies alongside DEAF editions, co-published with NAi Publishers (Rotterdam). These remain key texts in media art discourse:

- *The Art of the Accident* (ed. Brouwer, Mulder, Schönwald, V2_/NAi, 1998). URL: [v2.nl/archive/articles/art-of-the-accident](https://v2.nl/archive/articles/art-of-the-accident)
- *Machine Times* (ed. Brouwer & Mulder, V2_/NAi, 2000). URL: [v2.nl/archive/articles/machine-times](https://v2.nl/archive/articles/machine-times)
- *Interact or Die!* (ed. Brouwer & Mulder, V2_/NAi, 2007). URL: [v2.nl/archive/articles/interact-or-die](https://v2.nl/archive/articles/interact-or-die)
- *The Power of Things* (V2_, 2012). URL: [v2.nl/events/deaf2012](https://v2.nl/events/deaf2012)

These anthologies include essays by major figures in media theory, biology, philosophy, and art. They are how V2_ positioned DEAF as intellectual agenda-setting, not just exhibition.

## Why Interesting

DEAF is historically one of the most intellectually rigorous media art festivals in Europe. Unlike Ars Electronica (too large, too tech-positive) or transmediale (too political-media), DEAF occupied a productive middle ground: technically sophisticated art + critical theory + speculative thinking. The thematic trajectory from ALife to more-than-human to progress critique reads like a preview of Amber's research agenda. V2_'s framing of "unstable media" as a philosophical posture — not just a program description — resonates with Amber's core interest in emergence, open-endedness, and non-Western tech philosophy.

The key value here is not DEAF itself (dormant) but the V2_ ecosystem: a community of critical technical artists, curators, and theorists doing serious work at the art-technology interface. Getting into this ecosystem — through residencies, Open_Lab, or the Shifting Cosmologies series — would give Amber access to a European peer network of exactly the right intellectual profile.
