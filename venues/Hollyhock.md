# Hollyhock Leadership Centre

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-03-04:** Deep research completed. Non-profit leadership retreat on Cortes Island BC, on Indigenous lands. Strong ecology and somatic programming; regular host for David Abram. 2026 programs include David Abram's "Between the Human Animal and the Animate Earth" (Aug 2026). Beautiful venue but UK-distant and not a gallery/exhibition context.

## What
A **non-profit leadership centre and retreat facility** on **Cortes Island, British Columbia, Canada** — located on the traditional territories of the Klahoose, Tla'amin, and Homalco Nations, where the Salish Sea meets ancient cedar forests. Operates seasonally (spring through early fall). Founded in the early 1980s, part of the progressive Canadian intentional community and leadership development tradition.

Cortes Island is a remote island accessible only by ferry (from Campbell River, BC — approximately 5 hours from Vancouver).

**Scale:** ~40 bedrooms; small-to-medium programs (10–80 people). Programming runs as multi-day workshops and residential intensives.

## Who Runs It
Non-profit organization. Leadership team not individually named prominently on the website. Known for founding-era connections to the progressive social change movement (Hollyhock has historical ties to figures like Wade Davis and other Canadian ecology/culture figures).

## Focus & Aesthetic
Programming themes:
- **Ecological wisdom and nature connection** — coastal foraging, land/sea relationships, somatic ecology
- **Progressive leadership and social transformation** — climate justice, land conservation, political organizing
- **Somatic and movement practices** — body-based knowing, contemplative approaches
- **Indigenous wisdom and land** — positioned on Indigenous territories, programming reflects land relationships
- **Personal growth and healing** — therapy, contemplative practice, and creative exploration

Aesthetic: slow, embodied, West Coast. Very different from art exhibition venues — this is retreat/seminar infrastructure. No white-cube gallery presence.

## Open Calls / Residencies
No formal residencies or open calls. Programs are led by invited presenters; to present, one would need to be invited by Hollyhock programming staff. Some leadership fellowships may exist — worth checking hollyhock.ca/programs/ each spring.

## Why Interesting to Amber
The primary connection is **David Abram** — the phenomenologist and ecologist whose work on the *Spell of the Sensuous* and perceptual ecology is foundational to more-than-human design and somaesthetic theory. Amber's research on somaesthetic design, more-than-human design, and the animate earth has direct resonance with Abram's frameworks. Hollyhock is the physical space where Abram convenes his most intensive student groups.

Secondary: The West Coast Indigenous land acknowledgment + ecological practice ethos of Hollyhock connects to themes of territory, agency, and more-than-human belonging that run through Amber's work. But this is aspirational rather than programmatic alignment.

**Practical note:** Cortes Island is extremely remote (no car ferry from UK; requires Vancouver + two ferries). Attending would require building it into a Canada trip.

## Rating Assessment
**⭐ — Keep.** The David Abram connection is meaningful philosophically, but Hollyhock is not an art/technology venue, has no CFPs or exhibition program, and is geographically inaccessible without dedicated Canada travel. Worth revisiting if Amber develops a Canada trip or wants to attend an Abram intensive for immersive philosophical grounding. Not a programming target.
