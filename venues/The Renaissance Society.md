# The Renaissance Society

University-affiliated non-profit contemporary art space at the [University of Chicago](https://www.uchicago.edu/). Founded 1915 — one of the oldest institutions in the US dedicated to contemporary art. Known for commissioning ambitious, theoretically-grounded artist projects with intellectual depth. Free admission. Around 3–4 major exhibitions per year, often world or US premieres. Operates as a genuinely independent artist-side institution within the university. Less "white cube" and more "working laboratory for difficult ideas."

## Current & Upcoming Exhibitions (2026)

### 🔴 NOW ON: Leah Ke Yi Zheng — *Change, I Ching (64 Paintings)*
- **Dates:** Jan 10–Apr 12, 2026
- **URL:** [https://renaissancesociety.org/exhibitions/561/leah-ke-yi-zheng-change-i-ching-64-paintings/](https://renaissancesociety.org/exhibitions/561/leah-ke-yi-zheng-change-i-ching-64-paintings/)
- **What:** 64 paintings based on I Ching hexagrams. Practice at intersection of traditional Chinese materiality (silk, hand-built stretchers) and conceptual art methodology.
- **Why Amber:** Explicitly influenced by **Yuk Hui** (Amber's key philosopher for cosmotechnics) and John Cage. East-West philosophical dialogue through materiality. The I Ching as computational logic — hexagrams as combinatorial system, "64 possibilities" as an ALife-adjacent structure.

### *Palomar* (Part 1 + Part 2)
- **Dates:** May 2–Jun 7 (Part 1), Jun 10–Jul 12 (Part 2), 2026
- **URLs:** [Part 1](https://renaissancesociety.org/exhibitions/562/palomar-part-1/) / [Part 2](https://renaissancesociety.org/exhibitions/563/palomar-part-2/)
- **Curated by:** Karsten Lund
- **What:** Group exhibition in two parts. Named after Palomar Observatory + Italo Calvino's novel *Mr. Palomar*. Themes: deep time (the sun took 4.6 billion years to form), non-human temporality ("AI doesn't experience time as a continuous flow"), species extinction (passenger pigeons), cosmic observation, standardized time as technology, the Anthropocene viewed from space.
- **Why Amber:** This is essentially a machine-behavior / more-than-human / cosmotechnics group show without announcing itself as such. The framing of AI and time is exactly Amber's territory — machine temporality, non-human agents, open-endedness.

### Karimah Ashadu — *Tendered*
- **Dates:** Sep 12–Nov 8, 2026
- **URL:** [https://renaissancesociety.org/exhibitions/564/-karimah-ashadu-tendered/](https://renaissancesociety.org/exhibitions/564/-karimah-ashadu-tendered/)
- **What:** U.S. debut of *MUSCLE* — moving image installation about bodybuilders in Lagos within informal economies. The body as site of labor, investment, self-fashioning.

## Institutional Model

- **Founded:** 1915 by University of Chicago faculty
- **Status:** Financially independent nonprofit, independent affiliate of U of C (~5% of budget from university, 95% fundraised)
- **Funders:** [Teiger Foundation](https://teigerfoundation.org/), [Andy Warhol Foundation for Visual Arts](https://warholfoundation.org/), private donors
- **Location:** Cobb Hall, 4th Floor, 5811 South Ellis Avenue, Chicago, IL 60637
- **Hours:** Tue–Fri 10am–5pm, Sat–Sun 12–5pm. Free.
- **Model:** Commissioning + exhibition, no permanent collection. Around 3–4 shows/year. Major solo commissions travel internationally.

## Open Calls / Residencies
**No open artist calls.** The Renaissance Society works exclusively by invitation and commission. There is no open submission process. Artists are invited by the director and curatorial team based on awareness of their practice.

**How artists get in:** Relationship-based. Best path is through shared networks — curators, critics, other artists who have shown there. Myriam Ben Salah's Paris/Kaleidoscope network (European scene) is the key entry point. Camden Art Centre co-production creates a UK institutional bridge.
