## What

Founded by Carla Rapoport in Wales in 2012, the Lumen Prize began as a boutique digital art prize and grew into the leading international award recognizing artists at the intersection of art and technology. Now a 501(c)(3) nonprofit with a two-stage judging process: a 50+ member International Selectors Committee produces a longlist, then a distinguished Jury Panel (from major museums) selects winners. The prize awards annually in November, with winners joining the Lumen Artist Community and gaining access to exhibitions, commissions, and global institutional networks.

The prize has tracked the evolution of digital art from early net art and generative work (2012–2016) through VR/AR (2017–2020), crypto/NFT art (2021–2023), to a 2026 category restructure that emphasizes *systems thinking*, *ecological/social critique*, and *embodied experience* — effectively positioning itself to recognize the most critically engaged art-tech practice rather than mere spectacle.

**The 2026 restructure is significant**: moving from medium-specific categories (Still Image, Moving Image, etc.) to concept-driven ones (Memory & Culture, Systems & Ecologies, Presence & Experience) signals a maturation toward critical and philosophical depth.

## Categories & Prize Structure

**2026 Prize Fund: $30,000 total**

### Three Entry Categories ($5,000 each)

**1. Memory, Culture & Time**
Works engaging past/present/future: memory, ancestry, archives, storytelling, language, technology critique, historical reframing. Inspired by: Sasha Stiles' "Words Beyond Words" (2025 winner), AI Nüshu by Yuqian Sun. *→ Less relevant for Amber.*

**2. Systems & Ecologies** ⭐ *Most relevant for Amber*
Works examining or constructing systems — cultural, ecological, technological, social. Code, data, networks, biological or environmental processes, institutional frameworks. Explicitly includes: "misinformation, machine vision, or human–machine relations." Inspired by: Entangled Others' "Self-Contained" (DNA mutation as image-making), mots' "Deutsch/Nicht Deutsch" (biased AI identity determination), Cumulus/MORAKANA (satellite tracking). *→ **This is Amber's category.** Agent ethology, machine behavior, ALife ecosystems — all eligible.*

**3. Presence & Experience** ⭐ *Potentially relevant*
Embodied, immersive, participatory, interactive — live coding, performance, XR, co-creation. Inspired by: Umweltraum((a)) (Jakob von Uexküll Umwelt concept), CuteCircuit SoundShirt. *→ Could fit Amber's more embodied/performative work.*

### Gold Award: $15,000
One winner across all categories — "best work" overall.

### Student Award: $2,000
Sponsored by HKUST Division of Arts and Machine Creativity. For BA/BFA/MFA/MA/PhD students. Entry limit 300; early application advised.

## Notable Winners (Amber-Relevant)

**ALife & Natural Systems lineage:**
- **[Jon McCormack](https://lumenprize.org/2016-winner/still-image-award-winner-2016)** — *Fifty Sisters* (2016 Still Image) — 50 computer-synthesised plant-forms algorithmically grown via artificial evolution and generative grammars from oil company logos. Canonical ALife-meets-critical-art.
- **[boredomresearch](https://lumenprize.org/2016-winner/moving-image-award-winner-2016)** — 2016 Moving Image — "principles of scientific modelling, inspired by the mechanisms and behaviours of natural systems." Real-time generative natural patterns over extended durations.
- **[Cesar & Lois](https://lumenprize.org/2018-winners/cesar-lois)** — *Degenerative Cultures* (2018) — biological-technological network; living microorganisms + digital networks + AI; replicates logic of *Physarum polycephalum* slime mold intelligence. Direct ALife territory.
- **[Dave Murray-Rust & Rocio von Jungenfeld](https://lumenprize.org/2019-winners/dave-murray-rust-rocio-von-jungenfeld)** — *ichtsuchende* (2019) — "society of biologically inspired, robotic creatures who exchange light as a source of energy and as means of communication." Agent behavior, emergent ecosystems.
- **[Entangled Others](https://lumenprize.org/2025-winners/nature-climate)** — *Self-Contained* (2025 Nature & Climate Winner) — "mutation of digital images as DNA: crossbreeding, mutating, and splicing random compressed fragments." Synthetic DNA as artistic medium. Deep ALife, speculative biology.

**Machine Behavior & AI Critique lineage:**
- **[Mario Klingemann](https://lumenprize.org/2018-winners/mario-klingemann)** — 2018 Gold — chain of GANs generating from stick-figure inputs. One of the first major GAN-art prize wins.
- **[Theresa Reiwer](https://lumenprize.org/2024-lumen-prize-winners/theresa-reiwer)** — 2024 Gold — 8 AIs in group therapy session, navigating moral concepts, exposed flawed datasets. Machine sociality, AI psychology critique.
- **[UchanSun (Yuqian Sun)](https://lumenprize.org/2023-winners/2023-student-award-winner)** — *Hyborg Agency* (2023 Student) — LLM agents in an immersive forest linked to a Discord community; "parallel world for AI agents." Directly adjacent to Amber's agent-ethology work.
- **[mots](https://lumenprize.org/2025-winners/experiential)** — *DEUTSCH / NICHT DEUTSCH* (2025 Experiential) — biased AI models determine if participants are "German" or "Not German." Critical machine behavior, bias, identity.

**More-than-Human / Umwelt lineage:**
- **[Laura Mannelli and Sonia Killmann](https://lumenprize.org/2025-winners/performance-music)** — *Umweltraum((a))* (2025 Performance & Music) — nonlinear immersive sound installation inspired by **Jakob von Uexküll's Umwelt and Umweltraum** concepts; "mutating synthetic ecosystem questioning life, machines, and non-human intelligence." Directly cites the foundational more-than-human theorist. 
- **[Nye Thompson & UBERMORGEN](https://lumenprize.org/2021-winners/gold-award-winner)** — 2021 Gold — "world's first horror movie for and by machines." Emergent Network Organism, CCTV cameras as sensory organs. Machine gaze, nonhuman perspective.
- **[Alexandra Daisy Ginsberg et al.](https://lumenprize.org/2019-winners/alexandra-daisy-ginsberg-dr-christina-agapakis/ginkgo-bioworks-sissel-tolaas)** — *Resurrecting the Sublime* (2019) — biologically resurrecting scents of extinct flowers via Ginkgo Bioworks. More-than-human design at its finest.
- **[Libby Heaney](https://lumenprize.org/2022-winners/bcs-immersive-environment-award-winner)** — *Ent-* (2022) — quantum computing as artistic medium; animations made with quantum computing. Nonhuman computation as aesthetic.

**Speculative/Political Systems:**
- **[CNDSD and IVAN ABREU](https://lumenprize.org/2023-winners/2023-gold-award-winner)** — *AUTOCONSTRUCCION* (2023 Gold) — live-coded audiovisual concert; algorithms narrating speculative architecture and informal housing. Code-as-performance.
- **[Sougwen Chung](https://lumenprize.org/2019-winners/sougwen-chung)** — 2019 Interactive — Human-machine drawing collaboration (DOUG robots). One of the most recognized human-machine creative practice artists.
