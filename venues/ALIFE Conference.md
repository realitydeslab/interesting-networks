# ALIFE Conference

The world's premiere venue for Artificial Life research — where "life-as-it-could-be" is studied, synthesized, and celebrated. Annual. Spans soft robotics, synthetic biology, digital evolution, complex adaptive systems, origins of life, open-ended evolution, and art-AI intersection. Organized under the auspices of [[ISAL]]. Proceedings published by MIT Press as open access.

## Topics

### Hot at ALIFE Right Now
- **Open-Ended Evolution** — how do systems keep generating novelty? The "OEE problem." Ken Stanley, Joel Lehman, Tim Taylor.
- **Embodied / Morphological Intelligence** — body shapes computation; soft robotics; xenobots
- **Origins of Life / Astrobiology** — assembly theory (Sara Imari Walker, Lee Cronin), protocells, wet ALife
- **Digital Evolution** — evolving software, Avida framework, fitness landscapes
- **Synthetic Biology as ALife** — biological parts as computational substrate
- **Emergence of Language, Culture, Social Organization** — from agent interactions
- **AI × ALife** — LLMs as "life-like"? Neural ALife, foundation models as complex adaptive systems
- **Art × ALife** — significant art track, dedicated art keynotes at recent conferences
- **Philosophy of ALife** — consciousness, autopoiesis, minimal cognition, integrated information theory

### Submission Types
- **Full Papers:** 3–8 pages (not incl. refs). New, unpublished work. Peer reviewed. Published by MIT Press as open access proceedings. Oral or poster.
- **Summaries:** Up to 2 pages. Summarize previously published work (not preprints). Not in proceedings. Oral or poster.
- **Late-Breaking Abstracts:** Up to 2 pages. New ideas/work in progress. Reviewed for relevance. Poster only.
- **Journal Fast-Track:** Papers accepted in *Artificial Life* journal (MIT Press) are automatically accepted as oral presentations at ALIFE 2026.

## Why Interesting

ALIFE is one of the few venues where the question "what IS life?" is taken seriously as a scientific AND philosophical question. The community includes not just traditional CS/bio researchers but philosophers, artists, theorists. The art track gives Amber a legitimate entry point. The themes—emergence, open-endedness, self-organization—are deeply aligned with her research on machine behavior and agent ethology. The conference is small enough to be community-like (a few hundred attendees), not a mega-conference. The 2024 theme explicitly celebrated weird and fundamental science, which matches Amber's aesthetic. Connected to [[ISAL]], [[Santa Fe Institute]], [[Cross Labs]], and the broader ALife network.
