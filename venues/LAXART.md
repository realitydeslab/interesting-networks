# LAXART (now The Brick)

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-03-04:** Deep research completed. LAXART has rebranded/reorganized as **The Brick** (the-brick.org). Website is minimal — signups only. Organization appears to be in transition or reduced operations. Very limited public programming visible.

## What
Originally **LAXART** — a Los Angeles-based nonprofit contemporary art organization founded in the early 2000s, known for supporting emerging LA artists through exhibitions, publications, and public programs. A key collaborator with the **Hammer Museum** on *Made in L.A.* (MiLA) biennial. Now operating as **The Brick** (legally: LAXART, dba The Brick), a non-profit 501(c)3 (EIN 20-2911916).

The rebrand to "The Brick" appears to represent a shift in organizational identity, though the exact nature of the change (new programming focus, new venue, or reduced operations) is not clear from the current minimal web presence.

## Who Runs It
- **Lauri Firstenberg** — Director and Chief Curator (co-organized *Made in L.A.* 2012 with Anne Ellegood at the Hammer)
- Current team structure under The Brick rebrand is unclear

## Focus & Aesthetic
Historically: grassroots, artist-centric programming with an emphasis on emerging and mid-career LA artists; exhibition-making that extends beyond gallery walls; feminist and participatory sensibilities; publications as artistic objects. Co-produced *Made in L.A.* LA biennial alongside the Hammer Museum.

## Notable Artists / Past Exhibitions
