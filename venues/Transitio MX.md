# Transitio_MX

**Festival Internacional de Artes Electrónicas y Video** — Latin America's premier biennial of new media art and video, held in Mexico City. Organized by [[Centro Multimedia]] (part of CENART, Centro Nacional de las Artes) under the Mexican Ministry of Culture (formerly CONACULTA). Ran 2005–2015 (7 planned editions, 6 realized). **Transitio_MX07 was cancelled in September 2017 following the devastating Mexico City earthquakes.** No edition has taken place since. The domain (transitiomx.net) has since been squatted by an unrelated site. **As of 2026, the festival appears inactive/discontinued.**

## About the Festival

- **Organizer:** [Centro Multimedia](https://cmm.cenart.gob.mx/), part of [Centro Nacional de las Artes (CENART)](https://www.cenart.gob.mx/)
- **Address:** Río Churubusco No. 79, esq. calz. de Tlalpan, col. Country Club, Coyoacán, Ciudad de México
- **Format:** Biennial. Four central activities: **competition (concurso), exhibition (muestra), symposium, workshops** + live performances.
- **Governance:** A *Consejo de Planeación y Evaluación* (Planning and Evaluation Council) was established in 2004. It included independent artists, directors, cultural managers, and CENART officials. Each edition had an invited international Artistic Director.
- **Language:** Spanish/English bilingual.
- **Character:** Translocal, transgenerational, transmedial. "The show brought together artists from different generations and geographic locations to reaffirm the Festival's translocal, transgenerational, and transmedial spirit."

## Key People

- **[[Joanna Zylinska]]** — Artistic Director, MX05 (Goldsmiths, University of London). The only edition known to have had an internationally prominent critical media theorist as director. Her contribution gave MX05 its theoretical depth and international profile.
- **Grace Quintanilla** — Artistic Director, MX02 (2007). Mexican curator.
- **Álvaro Rodríguez Tirado** — Director of CENART, inaugurated MX05 alongside Zylinska.
- **Xóchitl Córdova** — Subdirectora de Proyectos de Videoarte, Centro Multimedia (active during MX03, 2009).

## MX05 Biomediaciones — Deep Focus (2013)

The most theoretically rich edition. [[Joanna Zylinska]] was invited from Goldsmiths, University of London, to serve as Artistic Director.

**Theme statement (from the festival archive):**
> *"La palabra 'vida' tiene numerosos significados. Para comenzar, es una abstracción filosófica que se refiere a nuestra existencia en el mundo... La vida como tal no existe, siempre se encuentra mediada por el lenguaje, la cultura, la tecnología y la biología."*
> *"The word 'life' has numerous meanings. Life as such does not exist, it is always mediated by language, culture, technology and biology."*

The theme "Biomediations" encapsulated the dynamic interplay of life processes — environmental, social, cellular — and the creative, evolving nature of media itself. Media were conceptualized as alive: composed of technological and biological elements, capable of generating new forms, unprecedented connections, unexpected events.

**Media explored:** still and moving image, video and sound art, data visualization, digital/electronic art, installation, performance, **bioart** (genes, living tissue, blood, viruses, bacteria as artistic material).

**Key philosophical threads:**
- Life as permanently mediated (Zylinska's extended media philosophy)
- Philosophy of life: creation, duration, emergence, process, flow
- Mexican cultural dimensions of life/death mediation
- Bio-politics of the organism
- Posthumanism via biopolitical lens

**Symposium speakers included:**
- [Stelarc](https://stelarc.org/) — cyborg performance, body extensions
- [Eduardo Kac](https://ekac.org/) — bioart, transgenic organisms, GFP Bunny
- Enrique Lefft — Mexican cultural theorist
- Ana María Martínez de la Escalera — philosopher, feminist theory
- Mónica Bakke — philosopher of life/biology/media
- Alberto López Cuenca — art theory and critical practice

**Festival venues:** Laboratorio Arte Alameda, Centro Cultural Digital, Centro Cultural España, SAE Institute, Fonoteca Nacional.

**Inauguration:** September 20, 2013. Plaza de las Artes del CENART. Inaugurated by Álvaro Rodríguez Tirado (CENART director) alongside Joanna Zylinska.

## MX07 ¿Cómo decir nosotrxs? — The Cancelled Edition (2017)

**Theme:** "¿Cómo decir nosotrxs?" (How to say us/ourselves?) — note the gender-neutral "nosotrxs" in the title, a feminist/queer grammatical intervention.
**Sub-themes:** Energía, Sustentabilidad, Medio Ambiente (Energy, Sustainability, Environment)
**Planned dates:** September 23 – October 1, 2017, Centro Nacional de las Artes

**Cancellation:** On September 19, 2017, two catastrophic earthquakes struck Mexico City, killing hundreds and causing massive destruction. The festival website posted:
> *"POR LOS ACONTECIMIENTOS RECIENTES INFORMAMOS A NUESTRA COMUNIDAD QUE EL FESTIVAL TRANSITIO_MX 07, DEBE SER CANCELADO. AGRADECEMOS A TODOS SU APOYO Y SOLIDARIDAD. #FuerzaMéxico ¿#CómoDecirNosotrxs?"*

The festival never resumed after 2017. No MX08 has been announced as of March 2026.

## Why Interesting

Transitio_MX is the historical center of gravity for media art in Latin America. The Biomediations edition (MX05, 2013) under Joanna Zylinska was the festival's theoretical high-water mark — directly engaging biopolitics, posthumanism, and the mediation of life in ways that speak directly to Amber's interests in more-than-human design and machine behavior. The cancelled MX07's theme "¿Cómo decir nosotrxs?" also shows a feminist/queer-inflected attention to collective subjectivity under conditions of environmental crisis — another resonant thread for Amber. While the festival is currently dormant, understanding its history illuminates the Latin American media art ecosystem Amber might one day want to engage.
