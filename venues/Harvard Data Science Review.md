# Harvard Data Science Review

Open-access journal published by MIT Press on behalf of the Harvard Data Science Initiative. Unusually interdisciplinary — sits at the intersection of technical data science and humanities/social science approaches. Covers foundations, methods, applications, ethics, and history of data science.

The "Mining the Past" column — co-edited by [[Stephanie Dick]] — provides historical grounding for data science, asking how computing history should inform present practice.

## Key People

- [[Stephanie Dick]] — co-editor, "Mining the Past" column (history of data science)

## Topics

- Data science methods and foundations
- Ethics and social implications of data
- Historical perspectives on computation and data ("Mining the Past")
- Statistics, probability, machine learning theory

## Why Interesting

One of the few venues where rigorous historical/critical scholarship about computing appears alongside technical data science. The "Mining the Past" column is a model of accessible scholarship connecting history to present debates. Open access via MIT Press.
