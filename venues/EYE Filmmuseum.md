# EYE Filmmuseum

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-27:** Deep research completed. Full venue profile: EYE Art & Film Prize all 11 winners documented, upcoming exhibitions, key people, academic access, network connections, and Amber opportunities.

The Dutch national film museum and archive, housed in a striking angular white building by [Delugan Meissl Associated Architects](https://www.dmaa.at/) (opened 2012), on the north bank of the IJ in Amsterdam. EYE is both a film archive (50,000+ films, oldest dating to 1896) and an active exhibition programme — the premier European venue for work at the intersection of film and visual art. Known internationally for the **EYE Art & Film Prize** (est. 2015), awarded annually to artists whose work crosses the border between cinema and contemporary art. A rare institution that treats the moving image as both archival heritage and cutting-edge artistic medium.

## Recent Editions (Exhibitions)

**2025 — Garrett Bradley: Revolutions** (14 June – 7 September 2025)
- First European solo museum exhibition by the Oscar-nominated US filmmaker. Explores abstraction, archival filmmaking, race, and representation. Works include *America* (2019, multi-channel 35mm) and *AKA* (2019). Accompanied by publication with Rebecca Matalon (Contemporary Arts Museum Houston).

**2024 — Paravel & Castaing-Taylor: Cosmic Realism** (20 January – 20 May 2024)
- First retrospective of Véréna Paravel and Lucien Castaing-Taylor (founders of Harvard's [Sensory Ethnography Lab](https://sel.fas.harvard.edu/)). Seven immersive installations including *Leviathan* (2012, fish-eye GoPro fishing documentary), *De Humani Corporis Fabrica* (2022, inside hospitals/bodies), *somniloquies* (2017, sleep recordings). Non-human, cosmic perspectives. Explicit content warning.

**2023 (Oct) — 2024 (Jan) — Janis Rafa: Feed me. Cheat me. Eat me.**
- Solo exhibition by Greek-Dutch artist (Rijksakademie alumna, PhD Leeds). Films and video installations centered on silent presence of non-humans, interspecies relations, animals in factory farming, late-capitalist exploitation. New works *Landscape Depressions* (2023) premiered at EYE.

**2023 (Jan–June) — Saodat Ismailova: 18,000 Worlds**
- First major retrospective of the Uzbek artist (2022 EYE Prize winner). Central Asian myths, ritual, post-Soviet ecology, spiritual heritage. Includes *Zukhra* (2013, Venice Biennale), *Two Horizons* (2017), *The Haunted* (2017). Accompanied by publication with nai010 publishers.

**2022 — Meriem Bennani, Kahlil Joseph, Karrabing Film Collective: EYE Art & Film Prize (2019–2021)**
- Group exhibition of three recent prize winners. Bennani's AR/3D animated immigration work, Joseph's experimental documentary, Karrabing's indigenous "improvisational realism."

**2018 — Hito Steyerl, Ben Rivers, Wang Bing: EYE Art & Film Prize (2015–2017)**
- Group exhibition of first three prize winners. [[Hito Steyerl]]'s media theory video work, Ben Rivers' experimental ethnographic film, Wang Bing's monumental Chinese documentary.

## EYE Art & Film Prize — Full History

Annual prize (since 2015) awarding €30,000 to an artist/filmmaker building exceptional work at the intersection of visual art and film. Supported by [Ammodo](https://www.ammodo.nl/en) (from 2023); previously by Paddy & Joan Leigh Fermor Arts Fund (2015–2022). Prize includes cash sum for creating new work + solo or joint exhibition at EYE.

**Selection process:** International advisory board nominates → international jury selects winner. Jury chaired by EYE director.

## Why Interesting

EYE is a **taste-setter for art-film intersection** in Europe and globally. Its prize list is a decade-long curatorial statement about what counts as serious practice at the edge of cinema and contemporary art. The pattern is clear: hybrid media, non-Western artists, social/political engagement, non-human perspectives.

**For Amber's research threads:**
- **More-than-human** — Paravel/Castaing-Taylor's SEL methodology, Janis Rafa's interspecies affect, Karrabing's indigenous cosmologies
- **Critical computing** — Meriem Bennani's AR distortions, Chia-Wei Hsu's archaeology + VR, Eye(s) Open AI installation
- **Computational creativity** — moving image as artistic research, film as medium for speculative inquiry
- **Non-Western tech theory** — strong programming of Central Asian, Southeast Asian, indigenous, South American artists
- **Archive as living system** — EYE explicitly frames its colonial collection as a "Living Archive" open to reinterpretation

**The prize is also an annual signal**: checking the EYE Art & Film Prize winner each year is a direct line into which hybrid media practices these top international curators are validating. The 2024 winner (Chia-Wei Hsu) used archaeometry and VR; the 2025 winner (Sohrab Hura) works across photography/film/painting/drawing. The pattern suggests continued openness to computational and hybrid media practices.

**Geographic adjacency:** Amsterdam from London is easy travel. EYE is accessible by foot from Amsterdam Centraal (5-min ferry). Dutch art-tech infrastructure (IFFR, V2_, HeK Basel network) all connect here.
