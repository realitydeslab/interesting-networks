## What

FACT (Foundation for Art and Creative Technology) is a public arts organisation founded in Liverpool in 2003, the city's first new arts building in over 60 years. It combines:
- **Public gallery** — presenting cutting-edge media art, digital art, and immersive installations (free entry)
- **Picturehouse cinema** — arthouse and artist film programming
- **Studio/Lab** — experimental production space (opened 2024) for artist members, researchers, curators, technologists; includes video editing, podcast suites, XR/immersive kit, high-powered computers, motion capture

FACT's origin traces to **Merseyside Moviola** (screening artist films in a disused chapel), which launched **Video Positive** — the UK's international festival of video art — in 1989. That festival introduced Tony Oursler, Mark Wallinger, and Isaac Julien to wider UK audiences. FACT emerged from that lineage in 2003.

Since opening, FACT has commissioned and exhibited: Isaac Julien, Pipilotti Rist, Nam June Paik, Black Audio Film Collective, Superflex, Krzysztof Wodiczko, Agnes Varda, Wu Tsang, Ryoichi Kurokawa, Apichatpong Weerasethakul, Larissa Sansour & Søren Lind, Larry Achiampong & David Blandy, Chila Kumari Singh Burman, Danielle Brathwaite-Shirley, Rebecca Allen, Jenkin van Zyl, [[Jiabao Li]].

FACT is funded by Arts Council England, Liverpool City Council, Culture Liverpool, Bloomberg Philanthropies (Studio/Lab Digital Accelerator), and The Foyle Foundation.

## Past Exhibitions

### 2026 Programme (current/upcoming)
FACT's 2026 programme is explicitly framed around "more-than-human perspectives," AI's creative possibilities/cultural implications, speculative mythologies, and participatory world-building.

**[Can Meeple Escape the Neurophoria?](https://www.fact.co.uk/event/can-meeple-escape-the-neurophoria)** (6 Feb – 26 Apr 2026)
Curated by Milia Xin Bi (2025 Curator-in-Residence). Artists: Vytas Jankauskas, Joseph Wilk, Jan Zuiderveld. Inspired by tabletop gaming dynamics. Investigates evolving human-AI relationships, surveillance, climate anxiety, digital systems. Jan Zuiderveld's works explicitly "create encounters between people and machine-learning algorithms placed inside physical objects, highlighting how we often see certain behaviours as signs of life" — this is literally machine behavior / agent ethology territory. Joseph Wilk: tabletop game as "resistance against the political ideas built into many technologies."

**[Rachel Maclean: They've Got Your Eyes](https://www.fact.co.uk/event/rachel-maclean)** (20 Mar – 16 Aug 2026)
Film created using AI models trained on Maclean's own image and artistic archive. Questions authorship, identity, ego's role in AI development. Draws parallels between AI boom and Victorian-era invention culture. Co-commissioned with Sonica Glasgow, supported by 1646 The Hague, Scottish Government Festivals Expo Fund, Creative Scotland.

**[ONLY SLIME: AFTERLIFE](https://www.fact.co.uk/event/only-slime)** (20 Mar – 16 Aug 2026)
Duo: Toby Pfeil and Claudia Cox. Expands their 2023 computer-game opera into installation. Motion capture, mini-games, 3D characters between fantasy underworlds. Explores creator/player/avatar power dynamics. "What control really looks like inside a system designed by someone else." Supported by Office for Contemporary Art Norway.

**[Sahjan Kooner](https://www.fact.co.uk/event/sahjan-kooner)** (22 May – 16 Aug 2026)
Collaborative with youth clubs in Liverpool and Wigan. Speculative museum futures, decolonial world-building, participatory storytelling as power.

**[Rae-Yen Song 宋瑞渊: •~TUA~• 大眼 •~MAK~•](https://www.fact.co.uk/event/rae-yen-song)** (18 Sep 2026 – 14 Feb 2027)
More-than-human kinship, diasporic mythologies, subaquatic deities, Chinese mythology, ancestral imagination. Science fact-fiction. Co-commissioned with Tramway Glasgow.

**[Uma Breakdown: Earth AD: Low Estate](https://www.fact.co.uk/event/uma-breakdown-earth-ad-low-estate)** (19 Jan – 22 Mar 2026)
Video game about death and love — player is last character alive.
