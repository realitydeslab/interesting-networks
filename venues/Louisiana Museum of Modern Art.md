# Louisiana Museum of Modern Art

One of the most important and ambitious contemporary art museums in Northern Europe — perhaps the world's best museum at combining radical critical practice with popular accessibility. Perched on the Øresund coast at Humlebæk, Denmark (35 km north of Copenhagen), with a dramatic landscape overlooking the strait toward Sweden. Founded 1958 by Knud W. Jensen. ~700,000 visitors annually — one of Scandinavia's most-visited cultural institutions.

Louisiana is not a regional museum that happens to have good art. It is a **curatorial and intellectual project** that consistently produces the most ambitious single-artist retrospectives in Northern Europe, hosts genuinely critical discourse through Louisiana LIVE and Louisiana Channel, and runs long-form architecture/design series that rival any specialist museum. The Forensic Architecture exhibition (2022), The Irreplaceable Human (2023), and the Architecture Connecting series (2024–2029) mark it as a museum willing to take ideas seriously.

**For Amber specifically:** Louisiana is the closest European equivalent to a LACMA + MoMA + institution that programs seriously around human/machine questions, more-than-human design, decolonization of design, climate and biopolitics in architecture. Several recent exhibitions are directly in Amber's research sweet spot.

## Key People

### Leadership
- **Poul Erik Tøjner** — Director. Has led Louisiana since 1999. Quotes from him in collection releases suggest an intellectual orientation: talks about the collection as "DNA strand of the museum" and the importance of "contact with our present and near future." A director with a curatorial vision, not just an administrator.
- **Mathias Ussing Seeberg** — Curator. Led *The Irreplaceable Human* (2023, creativity + AI) and other major exhibitions. This is the person to know for Amber's interests.

### Louisiana LIVE Hosts
- **Anna Ingrisch** — Host since 2018. Journalist background (DR2, Radio24Syv). Hosts the LIVE stage.
- **Marc-Christoph Wagner** — Host. German journalist based in Copenhagen, co-founded Louisiana Channel as "public service channel" for art on the internet.
- **Gunver Lystbæk Vestergård** — Host of Louisiana LIVE Science. Science journalist (Weekendavisen), PhD in science journalism. Physics, astronomy, science history focus.

## Topics & Curatorial Identity

Louisiana's curatorial signature:
1. **American modernism meets critical contemporary practice** — Pop art (Warhol, Rauschenberg, Fahlström, Kogelnik) as anchor; current programming extends this toward Black American artists (Jafa, Basquiat, Firelei Báez), feminist artists, video art
2. **Architecture as inquiry** — Multiple long-running architecture series treating buildings/design as critical practice (Forensic Architecture, biodigital architecture, decolonial architecture, memory/landscape)
3. **Thematic interdisciplinary exhibitions** — Arctic (2013), The Moon (2018), Mother! (2021), The Irreplaceable Human (2023) — big-idea surveys combining art, science, philosophy
4. **Video art and film** — Arthur Jafa (2021), Taryn Simon (2021), Mika Rottenberg (2021), Nan Goldin (2023, 2024), Pipilotti Rist (2019), moving images as medium
5. **Louisiana LIVE** — Live performance, talks, concerts by writers, scientists, artists, debaters. Louisiana LIVE Science track focuses on technology/research/science discourse

### 2023
- **[The Irreplaceable Human – Conditions of Creativity in the Age of AI](https://louisiana.dk/en/exhibition/the-irreplaceable-human/)** (23 Nov 2023 – 1 Apr 2024) — ⚑ *Most relevant to Amber.* 60+ artists, large interdisciplinary survey on creativity, childhood, work, AI. "Stem from a concern about structural imbalances in modern society." Curator: Mathias Ussing Seeberg. Artists included: Chicks on Speed, robotlab, Jeremy Deller, Ryan Gander, Jon Rafman and many others. Six expert films. Amber's concerns precisely.

More-than-human design from an African decolonial perspective. Called "the best architecture exhibitions produced in European museums in the last decade" (World-Architects.com).
- **[Gauri Gill](https://louisiana.dk/en/exhibition/gauri-gill/)** — Major solo; Indian photographer, rural India, collaborations with artisans

## Permanent Collection Highlights
The collection (~3,500 works) is anchored in international modernism + strong Scandinavian holdings:
- **Alexander Calder** — major mobile sculptures in sculpture park
- **Alberto Giacometti** — dedicated Giacometti Gallery
- **Henry Moore** — sculpture park
- **Asger Jorn** — major CoBrA collection
- **Francis Bacon** — major holdings
- **Louise Bourgeois** — works in collection
- **Yayoi Kusama** — *Gleaming Lights of the Souls* permanent installation (South Wing)
- **Andy Warhol, Robert Rauschenberg, Öyvind Fahlström, Kiki Kogelnik** — strong Pop art core

Recent acquisitions (2022–2025): Firelei Báez, Dana Schutz, Alex Da Corte, Richard Prince, Alvaro Barrington, Paul McCarthy & Mike Kelley, Ed Ruscha, Diane Arbus, Gauri Gill, Issy Wood.

## Architecture & Setting
- **Original villa** (1958) — Knud W. Jensen's home, starting point of the museum
- **Expansion wings** — Jørgen Bo and Vilhelm Wohlert (original architects), added organically over decades into cliff/landscape
- **Sculpture Park** — significant outdoor collection, Calder, Moore, Miró, and others; overlooking Øresund
- **South Wing** — major exhibition galleries; Kusama permanent installation
- **North Wing** — collection galleries
- **Concert Hall** — Louisiana LIVE events; 600+ capacity; exceptional acoustics

## Programmes & Series

### Architecture Connecting (2024–2029)
[https://louisiana.dk/en/exhibitions/](https://louisiana.dk/en/exhibitions/) — Long-running architecture series investigating interdisciplinary practice in era of climate crisis and social challenges. 6 exhibitions planned 2024–2029:
- 2024: *Living Structures* (biodigital architecture)
- 2026: *Memoryscapes* (memory/landscape/archaeology of future)
- Next: unknown, but framing includes "city/country" and "nature/culture" antitheses

### The Architect's Studio (ended 2023)
Previous architecture series (ended with Cave_bureau 2023). Featured: Forensic Architecture, Cave_bureau, and others. 5 total exhibitions.

### Louisiana LIVE
[https://louisiana.dk/en/what-is-on/louisiana-live/](https://louisiana.dk/en/what-is-on/louisiana-live/) — Regular live events: authors, scientists, artists, debaters. Louisiana LIVE Science track specifically focuses on research, technology, nature science. Hosted by Anna Ingrisch, Marc-Christoph Wagner (arts), Gunver Lystbæk Vestergård (science). Supported by C.L. Davids Fond og Samling.

### Louisiana Channel
[https://channel.louisiana.dk](https://channel.louisiana.dk) — Non-profit public service website for art/culture content on the internet. Founded and run by Louisiana; weekly video output. Series include:
- *Advice to the Young* (94 videos) — artists advising young artists
- *Facing the Blank Page* (17 videos) — writers on the horror of blank page
- *Artists on One Work* (36 videos) — deep dives into single artworks
- *On Jørn Utzon* (11 videos) — architects on Utzon
- *On Outer Space* (12 videos) — artists on outer space
Marc-Christoph Wagner was a founding voice. **Amber: Louisiana Channel has substantial free online video content — worth browsing as a research resource for interviews with artists.**

### Louisiana Literature
Regular literary programming; authors, readings, conversations.

### Louisiana Learning
Educational programmes for schools and children. Children's Wing is a permanent fixture.

## Fellowships & Programmes for External Researchers
Louisiana does **not** appear to run residencies or fellowships for external researchers/artists in the traditional sense. It is primarily an exhibiting institution. However:
- Guest curator/scholar relationships may exist for specific exhibitions
- Louisiana Channel produces commissioned content
- Louisiana LIVE occasionally features external researchers/scientists
- **No open-call residency program identified** — but worth emailing the curatorial team if seeking collaboration on a relevant exhibition theme

## For Amber

### 🌍 Why Visit
Louisiana is a **once-in-a-trip destination** for anyone in Northern Europe working at art/science/technology intersections. The combination of:
- A world-class permanent collection in a stunning building + landscape
- Genuinely critical curatorial programming (Forensic Architecture, Cave_bureau, The Irreplaceable Human)
- Architecture series that engages biopolitics, decolonization, climate
- Louisiana Channel as a free, high-quality cultural resource

### 🔧 Louisiana as Model
Louisiana's Architecture Connecting series (2024–2029) is a model for **how to frame speculative/critical design inquiry at institutional scale** — not a one-off exhibition but a sustained multi-year investigation. Louisiana does this better than most institutions. Amber should study this curatorial strategy.

Louisiana Channel is a model for **museum-as-media-infrastructure**: they built a free public service platform for cultural content, separate from the museum's ticket revenue. Long-form interviews, artist talks, educational content. Runs on a budget of a regional Danish museum. Ambitious and punching above weight.

### 👋 Visit Opportunity
If Amber is ever in Copenhagen (research visit, conference, transit), Louisiana is 35 min from the city by train. Open Tue–Fri 11:00–22:00 (late!), Sat–Sun 11:00–18:00, closed Monday. Currently showing Basquiat Headstrong + Memoryscapes until May 2026.

## Why ⭐⭐⭐
Louisiana earns the top rating because:
1. It is one of the few European institutions consistently programming at the intersection of art, science, AI, decolonization, and climate without becoming a technology showroom
2. Its Architecture Connecting series is a sustained intellectual project, not a trend grab
3. The Forensic Architecture and Cave_bureau exhibitions demonstrate willingness to show genuinely political, technically ambitious work
4. Louisiana Channel is a serious media infrastructure — free, public service, high-quality — that Amber should be using
5. The permanent collection (Giacometti, Calder, Jorn, Kusama, Bacon) + sculptural landscape is a unique cultural experience available via a day trip from London
