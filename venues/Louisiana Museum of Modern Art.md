# Louisiana Museum of Modern Art

Major museum of modern and contemporary art located in Humlebæk, Denmark, 35 km north of Copenhagen. Founded 1958 by Knud W. Jensen. One of the most-visited art museums in Scandinavia. Situated on a dramatic coastal site overlooking the Øresund strait toward Sweden.

Known for a world-class permanent collection (Alexander Calder, Alberto Giacometti, Francis Bacon, Asger Jorn, Henry Moore), a strong tradition of large solo exhibitions, and an expansive sculpture park. Also runs Louisiana Live — a concert and performance series.

## Key Exhibitions (Selected Recent)
- **"Magnumb"** — [[Arthur Jafa]] (April 21–October 31, 2021). Largest retrospective of Jafa's work to date. New works made for the occasion. Louisiana framed Jafa in the tradition of American Pop art (Warhol, Rauschenberg, Fahlström).
- Major exhibitions of: David Hockney, Ellsworth Kelly, John Baldessari, Marlene Dumas, William Kentridge, Robert Rauschenberg

## Why Interesting
Louisiana punches above its size for a regional museum — it regularly produces the most ambitious single-artist exhibitions in Northern Europe. Its programming combines rigorous critical art practice with popular accessibility. The Jafa show was a model for how to present video art at scale. Worth visiting if in Denmark. No application-oriented programs for external researchers are obvious, but worth monitoring.
