# Transfer Gallery

**"An experimental space that explores simulation and expanded practice."**

Founded in Brooklyn (2013) by Kelani Nichole. Relocated to Los Angeles (2019), then Miami (2021). Currently operates across Miami / NYC / Online. Represents ~28 artists making computer-based, internet-native, and immersive experimental media art. In 2023, at its 10-year mark, TRANSFER paused its traditional gallery program to build the **DATA TRUST** — a decentralized, artist-owned archive and not-for-profit cooperative trust. Backed by Knight Foundation, Filecoin Foundation, Gray Area, and NYU Tandon.

## The DATA TRUST

**URL:** [transfergallery.com/data-trust](https://transfergallery.com/data-trust/)

### What It Is
The DATA TRUST is TRANSFER's central project from 2023 onward — pausing the traditional gallery program to build resilient infrastructure for media art. It combines:
1. **Perpetual Purpose Trust** — legal framework where artworks are held in trust in perpetuity, as a non-profit, with conservation as the sole purpose
2. **Cooperative Value Exchange** — formalizing the non-monetary exchanges (skills, labor, care) that powered TRANSFER's community for 10 years
3. **Decentralized Archive** — peer-to-peer storage using IPFS and decentralized web infrastructure across artists' studio nodes

### Key Features
- **Decentralized storage:** Artists hold their own nodes; work is seeded across the network
- **Smart contracts for governance:** Replacing a board with automated cooperative governance — equity tracked via contribution, not financial investment
- **Artwork equity management:** Using appraisal + conservation (unique to art world) to grow equity in digital works over time
- **Open catalog:** Public-facing catalog (like Artsy but transparent) — pricing visible, loan requests possible, all labor made visible
- **Exit to Community model:** Inspired by [Media Economies Design Lab at CU Boulder](https://www.colorado.edu/lab/medlab/exit-to-community)

## How to Approach / Submit

TRANSFER does **not** have an open call or public submission process. The gallery works through:

1. **Relationship-based invitation** — Kelani has worked with most artists for years before formal representation. The ethos is mutualistic and trust-based ("moving at the speed of trust").
2. **Virtual visit / inquiry** — Email director@transfergallery.com to request a virtual studio visit or introduce work.
3. **DATA TRUST entry point** — The cooperative model means there may be pathways for artists who want to participate in the decentralized archive structure rather than traditional gallery representation. The model is explicitly designed to expand.
4. **FORA consortium** — [fora.media](http://fora.media) — the open consortium around decentralized conservation. This is a community entry point that doesn't require gallery representation.
5. **Institutional commissions** — The TRANSFER Download and VR Commission programs are commissionable by institutions. If Amber is affiliated with an Oxford institution or festival, this is a route.
6. **Gray Area DWeb community** — Both Kelani and Wade are embedded in Gray Area / DWeb networks. These communities are accessible entry points.

## Why Interesting

Transfer Gallery sits at a genuinely rare intersection: **feminist tech critique + decentralized infrastructure + cooperative economics + media art conservation**. This isn't a gallery doing "AI art" shows — it's a 12-year practice of thinking about what it means for artists to own and control their cultural production. The DATA TRUST is the most ambitious artist-owned infrastructure project in the contemporary art world right now.

From research-taste.md: this hits multiple Amber signals simultaneously:
- **More-than-human** — large swath of the roster (Pinar Yoldas, LaTurbo Avedon, Eva Papamargariti)
- **Critical computing** — technology as political act; subversion as curatorial practice
- **Participatory / democratic design** — the cooperative model is structurally participatory
- **Embodied interaction** — VR Commission program, holodeck installation philosophy
- **Edge of chaos** — the DATA TRUST is an ongoing speculative design experiment, actively evolving, explicitly uncertain

The gallery's ethos — iterative, open source, mutualistic, human-scale — maps directly to Amber's maker identity (HoloKit as open-source AR). This isn't a prestige trophy venue; it's a values-aligned community.
