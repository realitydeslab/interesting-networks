# Sónar Barcelona

One of the world's premier festivals dedicated to music, creativity, and technology — since 1994. Split into two complementary events: **Sónar** (the music festival) and **Sónar+D** (the innovation congress and exhibition). Every June in Barcelona. Probably the highest-profile venue globally for serious audiovisual performance art that also maintains intellectual credibility — the rare festival where [[Daito Manabe]] premieres a Google DeepMind collaboration, Ryoji Ikeda fills a hall with data sculptures, and Holly Herndon talks AI composition. The circuit node linking electronic music culture to art-tech research, media art, and speculative technology.

## Overview & History

Founded **2 June 1994** in Barcelona by Robles, Palau, and Caballero as "Festival of Advanced Music and Multimedia Art." First edition at CCCB and Apolo club; 6,000 attendees. Grew rapidly: 28,000 (1997), 53,000 (2000), 80,000 (stabilized), 121,000 (2013, 20th anniversary), 126,000 (2018, 25th anniversary, peak attendance).

**Sónar+D** was formalized in 2013 when Sónar by Day moved to Fira Montjuïc. Prior iterations: a record/technology trade fair started at the very first edition in 1994. The professional/business congress dimension (Networking Day) was added in 2017.

**Format evolution by 2026:**
- Sónar by Day + Sónar by Night → collapsed into one unified festival at Fira Gran Via (no more splitting locations)
- Sónar+D → relocated from CCIB (Barcelona International Convention Centre) to [Llotja de Mar](https://www.llotjademar.cat/en) (historic Gothic building, city center) for 2026
- 2026 is described as a "reboot" — more accessible tickets, standalone Sónar+D day passes

**Notable political context (2025):** Superstruct Entertainment (Sónar parent) was acquired by KKR-led consortium (~2024). BDS activists flagged KKR's alleged ties to Israeli settlement companies. 30-50+ artists cancelled Sónar 2025 in solidarity. This is live political terrain. Sónar 2026 is proceeding; Daito Manabe, Charlotte de Witte, The Prodigy, Skepta, etc. confirmed.

**International footprint:** Festivals in 65 cities across 4 continents over the years. Annual events in Lisbon and Istanbul. Past: Bogotá, Buenos Aires, Hong Kong, Reykjavik, Tokyo. Sónar+D has appeared internationally too.

## Sónar+D — What Is It Exactly?

Sónar+D is Sónar's R&D lab / innovation congress. It runs concurrently to Sónar (2 days in 2026: Thu-Fri, 18-19 June), 10am–8pm each day.

**Four pillars:**
1. **Talks** — keynotes, panels, conversations. Scientists next to musicians next to technologists. Past speakers include: Brian Eno, Björk, Holly Herndon, Alva Noto, Ryuichi Sakamoto, Richie Hawtin, Kode 9, Jean-Michel Jarre, [[Daito Manabe]] (keynote 2019).
2. **Exhibition** (Project Area) — interactive exhibits, prototypes, artworks. Selected via the annual Open Call.
3. **Community** — b2b networking, Delegate Pass holders, industry meetups.
4. **Performance** — AV performances intersecting tech and live electronics.

**Institutions that have participated:** BBC R&D, Spotify, Google Arts & Culture, Universal Music Group, Sony Music, MIT Media Lab, Roland, Native Instruments, Pioneer DJ, HP, Mediapro.

**80% of Sónar+D activities are open to general Sónar ticket holders** (during the 3 Sónar+D days) — low barrier to attendance.

In 2026, Sónar+D will also have a presence at Fira Gran Via (curating digital art experiences within the main festival).

## How to Get Involved in Sónar+D

### As an Exhibitor (Open Call)
Sónar+D runs an **annual Open Call for Exhibitors** (Project Area). The 2026 deadline was **22 February 2026** (now closed). Open Call portal: [sonar.es/en/programme/sonar-d](https://sonar.es/en/programme/sonar-d). 

**What they look for:** Projects, prototypes, or products spanning art, digital creativity, and technology. Suitable for interactive exhibition format.

**Funding available:** Artists/creators/collectives can apply for travel + participation funding to get to Barcelona.

**Curated by:** Cris Checa (Project Area, since 2023). To get on radar: engage with Sónar+D online, submit to Open Call, reach out to Antònia Folguera or Cris Checa directly.

The **2027 cycle** will likely open late 2026 / early 2027. Watch: [sonar.es/en/programme/sonar-d](https://sonar.es/en/programme/sonar-d)

### As a Speaker / Panelist
Talks are **invitation-only** — no public CFP for speakers. The curatorial team (Antònia Folguera + panel of experts) selects based on thematic fit and prominence. Past speakers suggest the bar is high but includes academics, researchers, and artists, not just industry figures.

**Path to invitation:** (1) Get work exhibited via Open Call first; (2) Engage through partner orgs like Ars Electronica or S+T+ARTS; (3) Existing relationships with Sónar's artistic network.

### As a Performer
Performers at Sónar are booked through Sónar's music programming team. AV performances (SonarHall, SonarLab) are curated separately from club DJ bookings. Path: representation by a reputable agency, prior presence in the festival circuit (Ars Electronica, MUTEK, TodaysArt, CTM), or direct commission via Sónar+D's exhibition/performance track.

## Circuit Analysis: The Sónar Node

**Who performs at Sónar?** The computational performance circuit:
- [[Daito Manabe]] / Rhizomatiks
- Ryoji Ikeda (data/sound sculptor)
- Holly Herndon (AI choir, machine voice)
- Alva Noto (Carsten Nicolai) — minimal data aesthetics
- Arca — avant-pop, posthuman body
- Autechre, Squarepusher — algorithmic composition veterans
- Nicolas Jaar, FKA Twigs, Thom Yorke — crossover art-pop
- Jean-Michel Jarre — elder statesman of AV spectacle

**Circuit overlap:**

| Festival | Overlap | Character |
|----------|---------|-----------|
| [[Ars Electronica]] | Very high (direct S+T+ARTS partnership) | More academic/art, Linz |
| [[MUTEK]] | High | More experimental, Montreal/Mexico |
| CTM Festival | High | More critical theory, Berlin |
| TodaysArt | Medium | Smaller, The Hague |
| Transmediale | Medium | More media theory, Berlin |
| Dekmantel | Medium | More purely music, Amsterdam |
| Unsound | Medium | More experimental club, Kraków |
| Roskilde | Low | Mainstream crossover |

Sónar occupies a **unique position**: bigger and more accessible than Ars Electronica / CTM, but more intellectually serious than mainstream dance festivals. The AI & Music S+T+ARTS programme ties it explicitly to the EU creative tech research ecosystem.
