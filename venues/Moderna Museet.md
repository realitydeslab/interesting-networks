# Moderna Museet

Swedish national museum of modern and contemporary art. Two locations: the main museum on Skeppsholmen island in central Stockholm (building by Rafael Moneo, 1998; previously a navy drill hall from 1958) and Moderna Museet Malmö (opened 2009, in the former Rooseum building). Permanent collection of over 130,000 works spanning painting, sculpture, photography, prints, and 400 moving image works. One of Europe's leading modern art institutions; third-most visited museum in Stockholm (~500,000 visitors/year).

## Institutional History

Moderna Museet opened 9 May 1958 on Skeppsholmen, initially in the Exercishuset (former navy drill hall). Director **Pontus Hultén** (1958–1973) transformed it from the outset into a radically interdisciplinary institution:

- 1955: Hultén started an experimental film association at Eriksbergsbiografen, 500 members transferred to Moderna Museet when he became director
- 1958: Opening exhibition — Picasso's *Guernica* — was accompanied by an avant-garde film festival, *"Apropos Eggeling"*, featuring Viking Eggeling's *Diagonal Symphony* (1922)
- 1961: *Movement in Art* exhibition — film integral
- 1963: *The Museum of Our Wishes* — SEK 5M government acquisition of iconic modernist works (Balla, Picabia, Schwitters, de Chirico, etc.)
- 1966: Niki de Saint Phalle and Jean Tinguely's *SHE* — film integral to exhibition concept
- 1968: Andy Warhol — first solo museum exhibition in Europe
- 1980: *The Pleasure Dome* — American experimental film 1939–1979, selected by Jonas Mekas; acquired Stan Brakhage, Robert Breer, Owen Land works; this shaped the moving image collection
- 1993: Theft of six Picassos and two Braques (most recovered)
- 1994–1998: Temporarily moved to Spårvägshallarna while new Moneo building constructed
- 1998: Rafael Moneo building opened (Pontus Hultén Study Gallery later designed by Renzo Piano)
- 2004–2008: Various commissions including Yinka Shonibare, Marysia Lewandowska & Neil Cummings (50th anniversary)
- 2009: Moderna Museet Malmö opened
- 2020: Director Gitte Ørskou devised new institutional manifesto (seven policies for the future)
- **2026: Major restructuring — ArkDes and Public Art Agency incorporated into Moderna Museet**

## Major Institutional Change (2026)
**As of 1 January 2026**, the Swedish government incorporated both:
- **ArkDes** (Swedish Centre for Architecture and Design) — one of the world's largest architecture collections (4M+ drawings, photographs, models)
- **Public Art Agency** (Statens konstråd) — national body for public art commissions

...into Moderna Museet. This creates a new combined cultural authority. During 2026, an interim organisation is in place while a long-term structure is being established. **This is highly significant for design/architecture-adjacent research**: the combined institution now holds modern art, design, architecture, and public art under one roof, at least administratively.

## Collection (Moving Images)
Moderna Museet's moving image collection (~400 works) is historically grounded in:
- American experimental film 1939–1979 (Brakhage, Breer, Owen Land — acquired via *The Pleasure Dome* 1980)
- Nordic video art from 1990s
- Film as interdisciplinary element in exhibitions since the 1950s (Hultén era)
- Recent commissions from artists working with moving images

This makes Moderna Museet a key institutional reference point for anyone researching the genealogy of artists' moving image in Europe.

## For Amber

### Why It Matters
Moderna Museet is primarily a **reference and circuit venue** for Amber, not an immediate engagement target. Key relevance:

1. **Experimental film lineage** — Pontus Hultén's programming (1958–1973) is canonical: Viking Eggeling, *Diagonal Symphony*; *The Pleasure Dome* (Jonas Mekas); Warhol. This is the Scandinavian institutional genealogy of the expanded cinema tradition Amber is tracing.

2. **Moving image collection** — 400 works, including Stan Brakhage, Robert Breer, Owen Land. Relevant for citation and historical grounding of artists' film practices in any academic paper Amber writes on expanded cinema.

3. **Mike Kelley (Ghost and Spirit, 2025)** — Kelley's animism, abjection, and object-subjectivity are directly adjacent to Amber's interests in machine death, non-human agency, and more-than-human design. This exhibition demonstrates Moderna Museet's appetite for these themes.

4. **Lap-See Lam (2025)** — AI-adjacent speculative aesthetics; good signal that the museum programs this area.

5. **ArkDes merger (2026)** — The incorporation of ArkDes means Moderna Museet now also governs Sweden's national design+architecture collection. ArkDes previously organised design research, commissions, and exhibitions in the speculative/critical design space. This merged authority could be relevant for HCI-adjacent design research venues.

6. **Rauschenberg / E.A.T. connection** — The *Monogram* collection piece and ongoing Rauschenberg exhibitions echo the E.A.T. lineage relevant to Amber's technology-art practice (also relevant to ZKM Rauschenberg Residency).

7. **European co-production model** — The Serpentine / Moderna Museet / Reina Sofía / Kunstmuseum Den Haag circuit is the European museum co-production circuit. Understanding this circuit is useful for Amber's long-term institutional strategy.
