# Sundance New Frontier

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-03-01:** Full deep research completed. Venue history, exhibitions 2007–2022, notable artists, curatorial philosophy, network analysis, stubs created for key figures.

The **world's oldest and most prestigious immersive art exhibition** embedded within a major film festival. Founded 2007 at Sundance Film Festival (Park City, Utah), New Frontier is where VR, AR, installation art, and emerging media storytelling get their institutional stamp. Not just a VR showcase — a critically curated art exhibition that launched careers and seeded an industry. The premier US venue at the intersection of immersive technology, documentary storytelling, and social justice art.

## Topics & Curatorial Direction

**Core thematic territories:**
- **VR/AR as empathy machine** — immersive first-person perspective for social justice (early phase)
- **Post-anthropocentric embodiment** — being other creatures, environments, phenomena (2019–)
- **Climate + biosphere** — earthly relations, ecological grief, multispecies futures (strong 2019–2022)
- **Black + Indigenous futures** — centering non-Western narratives and aesthetics
- **Social VR + collective presence** — pandemic-era experiments in online communal experience
- **AI + speculative imagination** — increasingly present post-2022

**Aesthetic signature:**
- Heavy on experiential, non-narrative work
- Documentary impulse + speculative form (Nonny de la Peña's journalism-VR fusion)
- Social justice + critical technology lens
- Feminist and Afrofuturist tendencies (Hyphen-Labs, Shari Frilot's background in queer cinema)
- More artistically ambitious and politically serious than commercial VR showcases (unlike CES or E3)

**What makes it distinct from other immersive venues:**
- Sundance prestige + indie film ecosystem = cultural legitimacy
- Shari Frilot's taste = not just VR, but VR that asks *why* and *for whom*
- Residency program builds artist relationships over multiple years (not just submission/selection)
- MIT partnership = academic discourse layer
- First mover in VR exhibition for film festivals → defined what that could be

## Annual Exhibitions Timeline

### 2007 — Founding: "New Frontier on Main"
First edition. Established the format: art installation + emerging tech + film crossover.
Early participants included Doug Aitken, Jennifer Steinkamp, Aaron Koblin.

### 2012 — The Inflection Point
**Nonny de la Peña** premiered "Hunger in Los Angeles" — a VR journalism piece about a diabetic man collapsing at a food bank. Palmer Luckey (then a teenager, Oculus not yet founded) saw it and it became a key inspiration for the Oculus Rift. A directly causative link between one New Frontier exhibit and the birth of the commercial VR industry.

### 2016 — 10th Anniversary
Largest edition to date: 30+ VR installations. Key works:
- "Double Conscience" — Kahlil Joseph + Kendrick Lamar
- "Stonemilker" VR — Björk + Andrew Thomas Huang (via Vrse.works / Chris Milk)
- "The Blue Encounter" — whale face-to-face encounter
- Also: Notes on Blindness (VR companion), mobile VR documentary slate (20+ downloadable pieces)
Lynette Wallworth's VR residency connected; later showed "Collisions" at Venice, Toronto, etc.

### 2017
- **Hyphen-Labs** — "NeuroSpeculative AfroFeminism" — salon/beauty parlor + speculative Afrofuturist tech. Breakthrough moment for the studio.

### 2018
- **Eliza McNitt** — "SPHERES: Songs of Spacetime" (exec. produced by Darren Aronofsky; narration Jessica Chastain; music by Michael Stein & Kyle Dixon). First VR work to sell for seven figures at Sundance. Co-presented with Fistful of Stars (SXSW 2017).

### 2019
Continued VR/AR expansion. Increasing focus on climate and non-Western perspectives.

### 2020 — Climate Edition
Partnership with MIT Open Documentary Lab (climate focus):
- "Hypha" — become a mushroom doing bioremediation on Earth (VR)
- "Breathe" — body sensors + MR + breath visualization; lead artist Diego Galafassi; climate as embodied experience
- "Animalia Sum" — Bianca Kennedy & The Swan Collective; insects-as-food; absurdist ecological critique
- "Infinitely Yours" — Miwa Matreyek; dance + shadow puppetry + animation; climate grief
- "Anti-Gone" — Theo Triantafyllidis; simultaneous onstage + digital performance; submerged city

### 2021 — Online/Virtual (COVID Edition)
First fully online edition. Experimented with what "immersive" means without physical co-presence:
- "7 Sounds" — Sam Green; binaural spatial audio; live-streamed performance event
- "Beyond the Breakdown" — Tony Patrick, Grace Lee, Lauren Lee McCarthy; AI companion "Serenity" + collective gathering in browser; originated from 2017 New Frontier World Building Residency
- "Weirdo Night" — Jibz Cameron; queer variety show; explored empty comedy clubs as COVID artifact

### 2022 — Metaverse Debates
As Meta (Zuckerberg) declared the metaverse, New Frontier became a critical counterpoint:
- "32 Sounds" — Sam Green; immersive cinematic audio experience with live performance and binaural recording
- **Lynette Wallworth** (first VR residency artist) publicly pushed back against monopolized metaverse: *"Until we find ways of extending access through cost and opportunity in this space, the metaverse is not meta — it's a mono-verse."*
- "We Met in Virtual Reality" (competition, not New Frontier) — Joe Hunting's doc shot in VRChat — created meta-context for New Frontier debates

### 2023 — Hiatus / "Period of Reimagination"
**New Frontier cancelled** for the first time in its history. Sundance cited the challenging period for VR/immersive media: headset saturation, COVID aftermath, and a broader industry reckoning about VR's future. Framed as a "reimagination" rather than permanent end.

## Selection / Submission Process

**Two pathways:**
1. **Open submissions** — Artists can submit work through the standard Sundance submission portal (fees apply, varies by format)
2. **Curated invitations** — Shari Frilot and team actively commission and invite work they've tracked

**New Frontier World Building Residency:**
- Multi-year residency program that incubates works before exhibition
- Example: "Beyond the Breakdown" (2021) originated from the 2017 World Building Residency in Los Angeles
- Application-based; highly competitive; provides development time, resources, community

**What they look for:**
- Immersive, interactive, or new media work that pushes the form
- Social justice, documentary, or humanistic themes — not pure entertainment VR
- Works that cross disciplines: film × technology × art × performance
- Strong critical lens; not just "cool VR" — must have *something to say*

**Typical section size:** 20–30 works across VR, AR, installation, performance, mobile

## Geographic & Network Circuit

**Sundance New Frontier sits at the center of several interlocking circuits:**

**The Immersive Festival Circuit:**
- **Venice VR** (La Biennale di Venezia) — peer European counterpart; artists often show both
- **Tribeca Immersive** — NYC counterpart; also spring timing means some works flow from Sundance
- **SXSW XR** — Austin, March; overlapping artists and themes
- **Sheffield Doc/Fest** — UK; documentary-immersive overlap
- **Hot Docs** (Toronto) — documentary film overlap

**The Art World Circuit:**
- **Ars Electronica** — [[Ars Electronica]] (Linz); many same artists appear in Prix Ars Electronica
- **Transmediale** (Berlin) — critical media art
- **ZKM** (Karlsruhe) — institutional art-tech space
- Works that premiere at New Frontier often travel to gallery and museum exhibitions

**The Film / Hollywood Circuit:**
- Studio and production company presence (Darren Aronofsky exec'd SPHERES)
- IP development scouts
- VR as extension of documentary and narrative film careers

**Geographic base:** Park City, Utah. Remote, winter setting creates an intensive closed-world experience — immersive art feels even more immersive when you're already cut off from regular life by altitude and snow.

## Why Interesting

New Frontier is the **founding institution** of immersive art as a recognized art form. It predates all the VR hype cycles and survived (mostly) the VR winter. Shari Frilot's sustained curatorial vision represents one of the most consistent and ethically serious approaches to technology-art intersection at institutional scale.

For Amber specifically:
- The Nonny de la Peña / Palmer Luckey story is the best-documented case of how a **single speculative artwork accidentally created an industry** — directly relevant to AI/machine behavior research
- The program's consistent centering of **non-Western voices and feminist/Afrofuturist perspectives** connects to Amber's cosmotechnics and sinofuturism interests
- The **MIT Open Documentary Lab partnership** signals it's taken seriously as research-adjacent practice, not just entertainment
- The **World Building Residency** is an application target for Amber's HoloKit/AR work
