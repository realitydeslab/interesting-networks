# E-WERK Luckenwalde

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-27:** Stub created during Lucia Pietroiusti deep research. Hosted *The Shape of a Circle in the Mind of a Fish, Part 6: Love and Lament* (2025) — the first post-Serpentine edition of the series.

An experimental arts and culture venue in Luckenwalde, Brandenburg, Germany — a former industrial power station repurposed as an alternative arts space. Hosted **The Shape of a Circle in the Mind of a Fish, Part 6: Love and Lament** (2025), the first edition of [[Lucia Pietroiusti]]'s long-running interspecies symposia series to take place after her departure from Serpentine Galleries. The venue is known for ecological, alternative, and post-industrial programming.

## Profile
- **Type:** Post-industrial arts/culture space; former electricity generating station
- **Location:** Luckenwalde, Brandenburg (~50km south of Berlin)
- **Character:** Experimental, ecological, alternative to mainstream Berlin circuit

## Key Events
- **The Shape of a Circle in the Mind of a Fish, Part 6: Love and Lament** (2025) — co-hosted with [[Lucia Pietroiusti]], including launch of the *Fish* book

## Key People
- [[Lucia Pietroiusti]] — programming collaborator (Fish Part 6)

## For Amber
- **Alternative institutional model.** Post-industrial arts spaces in Germany (think HAU, Hebbel am Ufer; Zollverein, Essen) have pioneered ecological and alternative institutional models. E-WERK Luckenwalde fits this tradition.
- **Distance from Berlin** — the fact that Pietroiusti chose to take the *Fish* series here (rather than a prestigious Berlin venue) signals something about the kind of intimate, serious, non-commercial programming she values.

## Why Interesting
A venue that can attract Lucia Pietroiusti for the first independent edition of a landmark series is doing something right institutionally. Worth monitoring as a model for ecology-art venues outside major art capitals.
