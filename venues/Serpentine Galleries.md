## Vibe
One of the world's most important contemporary art institutions — and one that has genuinely committed to the art × technology interface at institutional depth. Not corporate futurism, not "AI for art's sake" — Serpentine's tech programming (especially the R&D Platform) treats advanced technologies as genuinely destabilizing forces requiring artistic and philosophical response. The annual Pavilion commissions are among the most prestigious in architecture. The Marathon events are legendary. Location in Hyde Park / Kensington Gardens means free access to 1.2 million visitors per year. Free admission — that matters.

The key insight: **Serpentine operates as a two-track institution** — the exhibition programme (blue chip contemporary art) and the R&D / Arts Technologies arm (genuinely experimental, policy-shaping, network-building). For Amber, it's the second track that matters most.

## History
- **1970:** Serpentine South opens as an art gallery in a former 1934 tea pavilion designed by J. Grey West. Initially showing young British artists, summer only (no heating), later winter exhibitions added.
- **1993–1996:** Diana, Princess of Wales, serves as patron. Ian Hamilton Finlay's permanent installation dedicated to her.
- **2000:** First annual Pavilion commission — Zaha Hadid. Inaugurates what becomes the world's most prestigious temporary architecture programme.
- **2006:** First Interview Marathon (Hans Ulrich Obrist & Rem Koolhaas) — begins the legendary Marathon format.
- **2013:** Serpentine North (originally Serpentine Sackler Gallery) opens — Grade II listed 1805 gunpowder store with Zaha Hadid Architects extension. Renamed 2021 following Sackler name removal.
- **c.2013–2019:** Ben Vickers joins as Curator/CTO — builds the R&D Platform and Arts Technologies strand. This is the period when Serpentine becomes a serious tech-art institution (not just a contemporary art gallery that occasionally shows digital work).
- **2020:** FAE1 (*Art x Advanced Technologies*) published — launches the annual strategic briefing series.
- **2021:** Sackler name removed; gallery renamed Serpentine North.
- **2022:** FAE3 (*Art x Decentralised Tech*) — web3/blockchain/dweb strategies for cultural institutions.
- **2023:** FAE4 (*Art x Public AI*) — mapping AI's impact on creative economy.
- **2025:** FAE5 (*Art x Creative R&D*) — advocates for Creative R&D as core cultural infrastructure.
- **Current leadership:** Bettina Korek (CEO), Hans Ulrich Obrist (Artistic Director).

## Topics & Programme Strands

### Arts Technologies
The institutional technology arm. Three sub-programmes:
- **R&D Platform** — interdisciplinary creative R&D, prototype projects, cultural policy, digital strategy.
- **Future Art Ecosystems (FAE)** — annual strategic briefing series for art × advanced technologies. Policy-shaping, distributed widely.
- **Hivemind** — knowledge game for artists.

### The Marathons
Legendary format: 24-hour events gathering artists, philosophers, scientists, architects, writers for intensive conversations. Format began 2006 (Interview Marathon with Rem Koolhaas). Key marathons include:
- **Manifesto Marathon** (2008) — at a time when artistic manifestos were returning
- **Map Marathon** (2010)
- **Memory Marathon** (2012)
- **Poetry Marathon** (2014)
- **Transformation Marathon** (2015) — 24 hours, 10th anniversary of format. Radio broadcast. Participants: Bruno Latour, Nick Bostrom, Rosi Braidotti, Helen Hester, Lynn Hershman Leeson, Eyal Weizman, Saskia Sassen, Deep Lab, Haunted Machines (Natalie Kane + Tobias Revell), and 100+ others. Curated by Hans Ulrich Obrist, Lucia Pietroiusti, **Ben Vickers**, Claude Adjil.
- **GUEST, GHOST, HOST: MACHINE! Marathon** (c.2018) — machine-themed.
- **Infinite Ecologies Marathon** (2024) — ecology focused.

### Pavilion
Annual summer architectural commission (June–October). **Rules:** architect must not have built in England before. Completed in six months. 3 months on lawn. Hosts Park Nights performance programme inside.

## Why Interesting
Serpentine is the UK's most influential contemporary art institution with a genuine, deep, long-term commitment to art × advanced technologies. Not performative — they built an institutional R&D arm, ran it for a decade, and are now shaping UK cultural-technology policy through FAE. Their network spans art, technology, civic sector, and academia in a way that few institutions achieve. For Amber, it's the local equivalent of a major international art-tech hub — with the massive advantage of being within walking distance (if Amber is in London).

The FAE network is especially important: it's a distributed community of exactly the kind of people Amber should know — artists, curators, technologists, policy people working at the productive edge of art × technology.

The **Transformation Marathon 2015** is a historical marker: Nick Bostrom reading from *Superintelligence* at a Serpentine event, alongside Bruno Latour, Rosi Braidotti, and Helen Hester — this is the institution that can hold those conversations. That intellectual range, at that level, in London. Amber should be in this room.
