Art and science center in Red Hook, Brooklyn, NY. Founded as "Museum of the Working Artist." Runs interdisciplinary residencies, exhibitions, public events, and a Technology Lab. Strong art-science crossover; not just exhibition space but genuine production environment with studios, workshops, and labs.

## Residency Programmes
### Visual Arts Residency
- **Duration:** 5 months
- **Spots:** 12 Visual Arts Residents per year
- **Benefits:** Free studio space, access to wood/metal shops, audio-visual equipment, print labs (risograph, darkroom), bimonthly studio visits by curators and peers, public engagement opportunities, alumni network
- **Open call:** Opens August each year for following year
- **2026 open call:** CLOSED (August 31, 2025 deadline) — 2026 cohort selected
- **Next open call:** ~August 2026 for 2027 residency
- **Juried by:** Alumni residents + field experts
- **URL:** [https://pioneerworks.org/residency/visual-arts](https://pioneerworks.org/residency/visual-arts)

- **Duration:** 1 month
- **Spots:** 12 Music Residents per year
- **Similar benefits to Visual Arts**

## Why Interesting
Pioneer Works is a genuine art-science production environment — not a gallery that puts science on display, but a facility where artists work alongside scientists. The Technology Lab and Science Studios make it meaningfully different from other NYC art centers. The OceanX partnership (2026) demonstrates institutional ambition at the art-science interface. However, it skews toward visual arts and music rather than HCI/research/critical computing.
