# C L E A R I N G

Transatlantic contemporary art gallery with a Belgium-inflected sensibility — international roster, theory-aware programming, and a relaxed confidence in showing painting, sculpture, installation, and video under the same roof. Not a "digital art" or "computational art" gallery, but consistently represents artists who brush against ecology, mythology, speculative biology, postcolonial technology, and non-human worlds. Home to [[Meriem Bennani]], [[Marguerite Humeau]], [[Korakrit Arunanondchai]], and [[Marina Pinsky]] — the roster that matters for Amber.

## Overview / Vibe

C L E A R I N G is the kind of gallery that names its annual programming cycle "SEASONS" and gives each season a title like "YOGA GAGA," "FACE OF THE EARTH," or "TONIGHT AT NOON." That tonal palette — slightly absurdist, cosmopolitan, emotionally serious without being ponderous — tells you everything about the gallery's posture.

Founded in Brussels, expanded to New York (first Brooklyn, then multiple Manhattan spaces, now 260 Bowery in SoHo), and eventually Los Angeles (530 N Western Avenue). The Brussels origin matters: it gives the gallery a distinctly European conceptual fluency that the New York location absorbs but never entirely domesticates. The result is a gallery that moves freely between Belgian/French and American contemporary art scenes — a rare operational advantage.

The exhibition model is high-velocity: multiple simultaneous shows across locations, frequent group exhibitions alongside solo presentations, summer shows in Comporta (Portugal) and Basel (Art Basel week). The "Seasons" framing suggests the whole programme as a single ongoing organism rather than discrete events — curatorially coherent over time.

**What C L E A R I N G is NOT:** It is not an artist-run space, not an experimental non-profit, not a media art platform. It is a commercially serious gallery with a sophisticated taste profile that happens to include some deeply speculative and more-than-human adjacent work alongside a lot of painting.

## Artist Roster

The full roster spans painting, sculpture, video, installation, and performance. Below are artists sorted by relevance to Amber:

### ⭐ Highly Relevant to Amber

**[[Meriem Bennani]]** — Moroccan-American artist based in New York. Video, installation, animation, CGI. Work explores surveillance, diaspora, digital media culture, and non-human narratives. *MISSION TEENS* (2019), *Party on the CAPS* (2019), *Cursed Objects* (2022), *For My Best Family* (Serpentine). Primary gallery. Directly relevant to Amber's interest in machine behavior and more-than-human worldbuilding.

**[[Marguerite Humeau]]** — French artist based in London. Sculpture, sound, installation. Works speculate on the origins of life, evolution, ancient biology, and non-human sensory worlds. Key series: *RIDDLES* (2017, speculative pre-human communication), *MIST* (2019), *ENERGY FLOWS* (2022), *Birth Canal Drawings* (2018). Exhibits at Serpentine, MoMA PS1, Tate. Strong more-than-human/speculative biology thread. Amber should know her work.

**[[Korakrit Arunanondchai]]** — Thai-American artist. Video, performance, painting. Works fuse Buddhist cosmology, synthetic materials, digital technology, and post-human spirituality. The ongoing "Painting with History in a Room Filled with Men with Funny Names" series (started 2013 at C L E A R I N G) is a landmark. Collaborates with Alex Gvojic. Shown at Palais de Tokyo, e-flux, MoMA. Highly relevant to Amber's interest in more-than-human worlds and speculative cosmologies.

**[[Marina Pinsky]]** — Russian-American artist. Works with geometry, optics, materials, and systems. Show titles like *Four Color Theorem* (2020), *POLAR* (2016), *EMERGY* (2022), *Le Rail* (2021) suggest rigorous conceptual underpinning. Works across photography, sculpture, wall works. Not computational art per se but scientifically-inflected and systems-thinking.

### ⭐ Interesting Adjacents

**Neïl Beloufa** — French-Algerian artist. Video and installation. *Pandemic Pandemonium* (Brussels 2022). Political, technology-critical, formally inventive. Not represented but shown.

**Matt Copson** — British artist. Laser animation, video. *Age Of Coming* (2021), *Of Coming Age* (2024, laser animation). Works at the boundary of performance, video, and digital spectacle.

**Sara Flores** — Shipibo-Conibo artist from Peru. Works with Amazonian Kené geometric patterns, plants, and cosmological knowledge systems. *Soi Biri* (2023), *KENÉ* (2022). Non-Western knowledge system × material practice.

**Celia Vasquez Yui** — *The Council of the Mother Spirits of the Animals* (2023). Multispecies cosmology, indigenous Amazonian references. The title alone is Amber-relevant.

**Huma Bhabha** — Pakistani-American sculptor. Works with mythological and postcolonial materials. *WAGES OF FEAR* (2015). Shown at Met Breuer, Venice Biennale.

**Ryan Foerster** — Canadian artist. Photography, video. Works with material degradation, natural processes, analog/digital boundaries. *ZOLTOG99* (2019), *VIDEO CLUB #4* (2021).

**Gabrielė Adomaitytė** — Lithuanian artist. *Chronicler* (2024 LA). Works with data, archival processes, contemporary technology.

### Core Painters (Less Relevant but Gallery Backbone)

Harold Ancart, Sebastian Black, Calvin Marcus, Jean-Marie Appriou, Loïc Raguénès, Daniel Dewar & Grégory Gicquel, Koenraad Dedobbeleer, Daisy Sheff, Shota Nakamura, Hugh Hayden, Adam Alessi, Robert Zehnder, Tomasz Kowalski, Henry Curchod, Zak Kitnick, Aaron Garber-Maikovska.

## Network Position

C L E A R I N G sits at an interesting node in the transatlantic art world:

- **Meriem Bennani** → MoMA, Serpentine, New Museum
- **Huma Bhabha** → Venice Biennale, Met Breuer

The gallery reliably feeds artists into major institutional shows. It's a feeder gallery for Serpentine-level visibility. No direct known connections to ZKM, HeK, or transmediale — this is NOT a media art network gallery. Its adjacent world is IRL contemporary art institutions, not media arts festivals.

**Art fair presence:** Art Basel (Basel), Frieze Art Fair, The Armory Show, Art Brussels. High-market commercial activity.

**No open calls, no residencies, no public programmes** — it's a commercial gallery, not an institution. Access is through representation or institutional invitation.

## Why Interesting for Amber

**Direct interest:** The gallery represents three artists that Amber should genuinely know — Bennani, Humeau, Arunanondchai. These are not peripheral names; they're among the most intellectually serious artists working with speculative biology, digital-spiritual cosmology, and more-than-human worldbuilding in the contemporary art world.

**Indirect interest:** Watching C L E A R I N G's exhibition calendar is a reliable way to track when Bennani, Humeau, and Arunanondchai have new work. Their solo shows here are typically significant moments.

**Honest assessment:** C L E A R I N G is NOT a natural venue for Amber's own work. It's a commercial gallery representing established/mid-career artists. Amber's research-artistic practice would more likely connect with non-commercial institutional frameworks — Serpentine R&D, ZKM, HeK, arts-science residencies. There are no open calls, no artist submissions, no programmatic opportunities. The path to C L E A R I N G runs through: (1) institutional success at non-commercial venues → (2) gallery interest.

**What Amber can learn from the gallery's artist choices:** The gallery is comfortable holding a complex roster where speculative, posthuman, and cosmological work sits alongside figurative painting. This is actually a useful model — it suggests that serious speculative work can exist in commercial frameworks if the quality is high enough.

**Specific artists to follow:**
- [[Marguerite Humeau]] — the closest to Amber's interests; London-based; worth knowing in person. Works with non-human life, ancient biology, speculative futures.
- [[Korakrit Arunanondchai]] — digital-spiritual-cosmological video practice. The "with history..." series is a landmark for anyone thinking about machine behavior and post-human futures.
- [[Meriem Bennani]] — already mapped in Amber's network.

**Brussels connection:** If Amber is ever in Brussels, C L E A R I N G Brussels is worth visiting. European programming tends to be more conceptually adventurous than the New York space.
