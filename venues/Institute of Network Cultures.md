# Institute of Network Cultures (INC)

Amsterdam-based research institute and publisher, founded in 2004 by [[Geert Lovink]] at the Amsterdam University of Applied Sciences (HvA). A critical internet culture hub that brings together researchers, artists, activists, programmers, designers, and students around platform critique, network aesthetics, alternative economies, and urgent publishing. INC is now entering a historic transition — closing its HvA chapter and reinventing as an autonomous NGO/orgnet after Lovink's forced retirement in September 2026.

## Mission & Objectives

INC analyzes and shapes network cultures through events, publications, and online dialogue. Core themes:
- Platform critique and platform brutality
- Urgent/hybrid/digital publishing
- Alternative revenue models and economic experimentation (MoneyLab)
- Critical design and counter-culture making
- Image culture, video culture, Gen Z aesthetics
- Tactical media and activist networks

INC's method: identify emerging critical topics → organize events around key thinkers → publish from those networks → sustain research communities. Publications are Creative Commons (CC-BY-NC-SA 4.0), most free to download.

## Geert Lovink — Current Ideas & Focus (2024–2026)

**Latest book:** [*Platform Brutality*](https://networkcultures.org/geert/2026/01/21/interview-with-geert-lovink-about-platform-brutality/) (Valiz, Amsterdam, 2025)
- Covers the post-COVID "permacrisis" period: wars, climate, inflation, attention collapse, ideophobia
- Central concept: platforms have reached the point where their extractivist logic becomes **direct political violence** (inspired by Achille Mbembe's necropolitics)
- Data collection used to control borders, target civilians; deletion as destruction of communities; trivialisation of violence
- Proposes "Team Human" mass platform exit as political strategy — but critiques individual guilt-trip approaches
- Advocates for "vibe labs," experimentation, tools-not-platforms thinking
- Francesca Bria's [Eurostack](https://eurostack.eu/) as model for tech sovereignty

**Current essay focus (Feb-Mar 2026):**
- "Canada Theory" — geopolitical debates at INC (Feb 28, 2026)
- Wikipedia, paid editors, and AI (Feb 2026)
- Platform brutality podcast appearances
- Art in Permacrisis podcast (with Sepp Eckenhaussen)

**Key concepts in Lovink's corpus:**
- **Organized networks** — networks that self-organize around issues (not around institutions)
- **Unlike Us** — the unity of social media critique and alternatives
- **Tactical media** — media practices as political intervention
- **Net nihilism** — critical engagement with the internet's failures without utopian escape
- **Platform brutality** — the violence embedded in platform logics
- **Urgency publishing** — fast, open-access, practice-embedded publishing as counter to academic slowness

**Context: INC transitioning:** Lovink is retiring Sept 1, 2026 at mandatory age 67. Rather than let INC be absorbed by AI ethics bureaucracy or different institutional agenda, the team is transitioning INC into an autonomous NGO/orgnet. This is a **generative crisis moment** — INC is looking for new coalitions, partners, funding.
