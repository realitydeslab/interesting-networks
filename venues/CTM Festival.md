# CTM Festival

## ⚡ Recent Updates

Annual festival for adventurous music and sound art in Berlin (late January – early February). 27 editions. Originated inside [[transmediale]] in 1999 as "Club Transmediale." Now fully independent under DISK Berlin. The sound/music counterpart to transmediale's visual/theoretical focus. Together they form Berlin's critical media art ecosystem. Strong theory/discourse programme makes it welcoming to researchers alongside musicians.

---

## History

- **1999:** Founded as "Club Transmediale" — nighttime electronic music programming within transmediale, by Marcus Weiser (Rechenzentrum), Lillevan Pobjoy, Timm Ringewaldt, Remaclus Schuurbiers, Jan Rohlf. First edition at Maria am Ostbahnhof, Berlin.
- **2001:** Skipped a year during transmediale funding crisis.
- **~2003:** Began operating more independently; Oliver Baurhenn joined, first public funding obtained.
- **2012:** Formally rebranded as **CTM Festival** (from "Club Transmediale").
- **2014:** CTM Radio Lab launched — yearly commissions for live performance/radio hybrids.
- **2016:** SHAPE platform launched (European artist mobility); CTM X international collaborations begin.
- **2022–2025:** SHAPE+ (second stage with residency focus).
- **2023–2026:** [tekhnē project](https://www.ctm-festival.de/activities/tekhne) — six European organisations exploring emancipatory potential of technology in music/sound art.
- **2024–present:** Goethe-Institut x Somerset House Studios x CTM residency (Germany-based artist → London → CTM).
- **Current:** 10-day event with concerts, installations, panel discussions, screenings, workshops. Run by DISK Berlin (non-profit). Funded by Berlin Senate for Culture.

**Relationship to transmediale:** Sister festival. Joint Vorspiel pre-festival programme (Berlin independent spaces, since 2011). Joint evening events. Shared audiences and critical orientation. Completely separate programming and curation.

## Recent Editions: Themes & Intellectual Currents

| Year | Theme | Key Ideas |
|------|-------|-----------|
| **2022** | **Contact** | Post-pandemic return; modes of encounter and touch; inequality in contact; inter-species and planetary contact; "how" of contact (empathy, respect) |
| **2023** | **Portals** | Music as portal to other realities; world-building; access/exclusion; decolonial sound histories; speculative imagination through sonic practice |
| **2024** | **Sustain** | Endurance, sustainability, survival; music ecosystems; DIY resilience; ecological/social/cultural sustainability; dissonance as antidote; community as resource |
| **2025** | *(no single theme word)* | Polyphony of voices in chaotic realities; key strands: "Resynthesising the Traditional" (critical engagement with heritage); "Stranger AI / Wilding AI" (AI disruption in sound/art); "Affection" (radio commissions); collaboration with Dancecult conference |
| **2026** | **dissonate < > resonate** | Dissonance/resonance as social-political metaphor; ear-witnessing; (embodied) listening as method; dissonance as sincerity and productive impulse; solidarity through resonance; living within contradictory realities |

**Pattern:** CTM themes consistently ask what music *does* to/for society — not just aesthetics. Political, ecological, decolonial, and now explicitly acoustic-political dimensions. Theory is baked into the curatorial DNA.

## Submission / Engagement Opportunities

CTM does **not** run a standard open call for performances — the music programme is curated by invitation. However:

1. **Research Networking Day** — Open submission for scholars/artists to present research. **Best entry point for Amber.** Free to attend; applications likely open mid-autumn for January edition.
2. **Discourse Programme** — Speakers invited by curators (Anita Jóri, Lucia Udvardyová, Taïca Replansky). Contact via info@disk-agency.de or via community overlap.
3. **Goethe-Institut x Somerset House Studios Residency** — Open call typically announced by Goethe-Institut London / Somerset House Studios. Germany-based artist focus (Amber is Oxford-based, may not qualify unless Germany-connected).
4. **A2A Transmission Workshops** — "Artists by artists" — possibly open call component for artist practitioners.
5. **tekhnē network** — Partner organisations may have affiliate programmes.
6. **CTM Magazine** — Accepts commissioned essays and interviews. Taïca Replansky / Lucia Udvardyová are editors.
7. **Vorspiel** — Berlin-based project spaces can apply to host a Vorspiel event (requires Berlin presence).

**How to get in (Discourse):** CTM curators are highly networked in European sound/media art. They respond to: researchers with strong theory-practice hybrid work, people who bridge music + critical theory, non-Western or decolonial perspectives, embodied/ecological sound research. The Discourse programme explicitly welcomes artists who are also researchers.

**Prestige:** High. CTM is the European flagship for critical electronic/experimental music culture. Discourse programme speakers are drawn from top sound studies + media art academic circuits. Being on the Discourse programme is a meaningful credential in this world.

## Why Interesting

CTM is one of the few European festivals where electronic/experimental music, critical theory, and speculative practice consistently cohabitate at a high level. The Discourse programme is not a side event — it's central to the curatorial identity. Speakers include sound studies scholars, artists who theorize, and theorists who make. The themes (Portals, Sustain, Contact, dissonate < > resonate) are philosophically sophisticated and politically engaged — not entertainment wrappers.

The festival's consistent attention to AI in music (2025: "Stranger AI / Wilding AI"), technology critique (tekhnē project), embodied listening, and non-Western perspectives makes it genuinely relevant to Amber's research interests. This is not a venue that just presents music — it *thinks* about music as a way of thinking about everything else. That's Amber's mode too.

Key overlap with Amber: embodied/somaesthetic dimensions of technology; AI as non-human agent; speculative futures through practice; critical theory + art; more-than-human orientation to sound and environment.
