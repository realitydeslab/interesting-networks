# TEI — ACM Conference on Tangible, Embedded and Embodied Interaction

The annual meeting point where physical computing meets design theory, where making is research, and where the body is not a peripheral but the primary interface. TEI is the ACM SIGCHI conference where researchers, designers, artists, and makers converge around one core conviction: that the physical world is not just a canvas for the digital, but its rightful co-conspirator. Smaller and more intimate than CHI (~300-500 attendees), more design-forward and art-integrated than UIST, more hands-dirty than DIS — TEI occupies a genuinely distinctive niche in the HCI universe.

TEI 2026 marks the **20th edition** of the conference — a major milestone — and returns to North America (Chicago, March 8–11, 2026) for the first time in seven years.

## Key People

### Founders & Pioneering Figures

- **[Hiroshi Ishii](https://tangible.media.mit.edu/person/hiroshi-ishii/)** — MIT Media Lab, [Tangible Media Group](https://tangible.media.mit.edu/). The godfather. Coined "Tangible Bits" (1997) and later "Radical Atoms." TEI conference was built substantially on his intellectual foundation. His group's work (inFORM, TRANSFORM, Sandscape, Urp, Topobo, Actuated Workbench) has defined what tangible computing looks like for two decades. Latest vision: **TeleAbsence** — connecting across time via tangible media.
- **Brygg Ullmer** — Louisiana State University. Co-authored the foundational Tangible Bits paper with Ishii (CHI 1997). Developed the token+constraint model and MCRpd framework for TUI taxonomy.
- **[Eva Hornecker](https://eva-hornecker.com/)** — Bauhaus-Universität Weimar. Central theorist of tangible interaction. Developed the **Tangible Interaction Framework** (with Jacob Buur, 2006) covering: tangible manipulation, spatial interaction, embodied facilitation, expressive representation. One of the most cited TEI researchers. Edits the ACM TOCHI journal.
- **Jacob Buur** — University of Southern Denmark. Co-developed tangible interaction frameworks, participatory design integration.

### Somaesthetic / Embodied Interaction Lineage (High Amber Relevance)
- **[[Kia Höök]]** — Stockholm University. Pioneer of **somaesthetic design** — designing for felt bodily experience, not just function. Her book *[Designing with the Body](https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262038669/designing-with-the-body/)* (MIT Press, 2018) is the field's canonical text. Huge figure at TEI and DIS. Direct intellectual overlap with Amber.
- **Ylva Fernaeus** — Stockholm University / KTH. Tangible play, material design for children, digital-physical artifacts. Long-term TEI community member.
- **[Paul Dourish](https://www.ics.uci.edu/~jpd/)** — UC Irvine. Not exclusively a TEI figure but his book *[Where the Action Is: The Foundations of Embodied Interaction](https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262541787/where-the-action-is/)* (MIT Press, 2001) is the theoretical backbone of the whole field. Phenomenology → HCI.

### Shape-Changing / Smart Materials
- **Lining Yao** — CMU Human-Computer Interaction Institute. Morphing materials, bio-hybrid interfaces, programmable matter. Regular TEI presence.
- **Asta Roseway** — Microsoft Research. Wearables, e-textiles, biofeedback.
- **Sean Follmer** — Stanford Human-Computer Interaction Group. Shape-changing interfaces, deformable UIs, haptics.

### Maker Culture / Physical Computing
- **Leah Buechley** — Formerly MIT, now UNM. LilyPad Arduino, e-textiles, maker culture critique. Highly cited in TEI and tangible learning contexts.
- **Michael Eisenberg** — CU Boulder. Computational crafts, Stitchable I/O, HyperGami. Learning through making.

## Topics

### Core Scope
- **Tangible User Interfaces (TUIs)** — physical objects as UI controls; token+constraint models; graspable computation
- **Embedded Interaction** — computing woven into everyday objects, environments, materials
- **Embodied Interaction** — body as primary locus of experience; phenomenological approaches; movement and gesture
- **E-textiles & Wearables** — conductive fabrics, soft circuits, body-worn computation
- **Shape-Changing Interfaces** — deformable displays, actuated surfaces, programmable matter
- **Digital Fabrication & Making** — laser cutting, 3D printing, CNC; maker epistemology
- **Haptics** — tactile feedback, force display, skin-based interaction
- **Physical Computing** — Arduino, microcontrollers, sensors/actuators as research medium
- **Smart Materials** — biomaterials, stimuli-responsive materials, 4D printing
- **Robotic UIs** — social robots, robot-as-interface, drone interaction

### Emerging / Hot at TEI Right Now
- **AI × Tangible** — LLM-driven physical interfaces; embodied agents with physical presence; tangible AI
- **More-Than-Human** — non-human actors in tangible design; animal-computer interaction; posthuman perspectives
- **Post-Anthropocentric Design** — material agency, design-with-nature
- **Climate & Sustainability** — circular design, bio-materials, e-waste critique
- **Somaesthetic Computing** — first-person methods for body-based research; movement as data
- **Augmented Reality as Tangible** — MR overlaid on physical objects; spatial computing + tangibility
- **Speculative / Critical Tangibles** — critical design applied to physical computing artifacts
- **Indigenous / Non-Western Material Practices** — critique of Eurocentric TUI assumptions

### Tracks (Standard TEI Structure)
1. **Full Papers** — 6–10 pages, archival. Core research contributions. ~25–35% acceptance rate.
2. **Pictorials** — image-heavy design research format (pioneered extensively at TEI/DIS). 8-page limit, image-led narrative. Excellent for design-through-making contributions.
3. **Studios** — half- or full-day hands-on workshops. Community-building, skill-sharing, experimental formats.
4. **Demos & Performances** — interactive demonstrations of working systems or live performances integrating technology.
5. **Art Installations** — TEI has a dedicated art track; significant gallery/installation component.
6. **Graduate Symposium / Doctoral Symposium** — for PhD students to present work-in-progress.
7. **Work-in-Progress (WiP)** — 2-page extended abstracts for early work.

## Landmark Papers & Works

### Foundational Theory
- **[Tangible Bits: Towards Seamless Interfaces between People, Bits and Atoms](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/258549.258715)** — Ishii & Ullmer (CHI 1997). The origin document. Graspable UI → Tangible Bits vision. DOI: 10.1145/258549.258715
- **[Where the Action Is: The Foundations of Embodied Interaction](https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262541787/where-the-action-is/)** — Paul Dourish (MIT Press, 2001). Phenomenology applied to computing. THE theoretical spine.
- **[Getting a Grip on Tangible Interaction](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/1124772.1124931)** — Hornecker & Buur (CHI 2006). Structured framework: four themes of tangible interaction. DOI: 10.1145/1124772.1124931
- **[Radical Atoms: Beyond Tangible Bits, Toward Transformable Materials](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/2556288.2557257)** — Ishii et al. (CHI 2012). Vision of computationally programmable matter. DOI: 10.1145/2556288.2557257

### Shape-Changing / Materials
- **[Urp: A Luminous-Tangible Workbench for Urban Planning and Design](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/302979.303114)** — Underkoffler & Ishii (CHI 1999). Tangible urban simulation. DOI: 10.1145/302979.303114
- **[inFORM: Dynamic Physical Affordances and Constraints through Shape and Object Actuation](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/2501988.2502032)** — Follmer, Leithinger, Olwal, Hogge, Ishii (UIST 2013). Shape-display landmark. DOI: 10.1145/2501988.2502032

### Somaesthetic
- **[Designing with the Body: Somaesthetic Interaction Design](https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262038669/designing-with-the-body/)** — Kia Höök (MIT Press, 2018). Canonical somaesthetic design text.
- **[Somaesthetic Appreciation Design](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3290605.3300526)** — Höök et al. (CHI 2019). DOI: 10.1145/3290605.3300526

### Wearables / E-Textiles
- **[A Construction Kit for Electronic Textiles](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/1347390.1347396)** — Buechley et al. (TEI 2008). LilyPad Arduino origin paper. DOI: 10.1145/1347390.1347396

### Maker Culture / Digital Fabrication
- **[Fabricating Ourselves: Augmenting the Human Body through Digital Fabrication](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/2839462.2839469)** — various TEI 2016 work
- **[Critical Making: Conceptual and Material Studies in Technology and Social Life](https://openlibrary.org/works/OL17828374W)** — Garnet Hertz (ed.) — maker culture critical theory

## Acceptance Rates

Approximate (varies year to year):
- **Full Papers:** ~25–35% acceptance rate
- **Pictorials:** ~30–40% acceptance rate (somewhat higher — more craft/design-led, less "hypothesis-testing" pressure)
- **WiP/Notes:** ~40–50%
- **Studios/Workshops:** varies — highly curated

Note: TEI is significantly more selective than it appears because submissions are already pre-filtered by community familiarity. First-time submitters from adjacent communities (CHI, CSCW) sometimes find the format expectations unfamiliar — physical demonstration matters.

## Edge of Chaos Analysis

**Is TEI at the edge? Partially yes, in specific directions.**

TEI itself is an established, stable community (~20 years, ~300-500 attendees, predictable format). But within TEI, several threads are genuinely edge-of-chaos right now:

**AI × Tangible (emerging fast):**
The question of what happens when AI agents get physical bodies is genuinely new. TEI has historically ignored AI/ML as too abstract; now the question of embodied AI, tangible AI interfaces, and physical presence for LLM-powered systems is forcing a reckoning. This is exactly Amber's intersection.

**More-Than-Human / Posthuman Design:**
Building momentum. Critical design + tangible computing + posthumanism is a growing thread. Not yet a dominant mode at TEI — still contrarian enough to be interesting.

**Somaesthetic Computing Evolution:**
Höök's somaesthetic design has been institutionally successful (major CHI papers, MIT Press book). The next generation of somaesthetic work — applying it to MR, to AI agents, to post-human scenarios — is wide open.

**Open Hardware / Global South Making:**
TEI's maker culture has been criticized for being US/European-centric. There's growing interest in indigenous material practices, Global South making traditions, non-Western tangible culture. Amber's China connections + HoloKit's global reach is a differentiator here.

**Verdict:** TEI itself is not a chaotic field — it's well-established. But the specific intersections with Amber's work (embodied AI, somaesthetic MR, more-than-human tangibles) ARE at the edge. TEI is the right venue to *claim* those intersections.
