# Critical Times: Interventions in Global Critical Theory

A peer-reviewed, open-access journal committed to the intellectual and political project of critical theory in its global dimensions. Published by [Duke University Press](https://www.dukeupress.edu/critical-times). Currently on Volume 9 (as of 2026). A project of the [International Consortium of Critical Theory Programs (ICCTP)](https://icctp.net), supported by a grant from the [Andrew W. Mellon Foundation](https://www.mellon.org).

## About the Journal

Critical Times publishes work that calls attention to the ongoing, collective **reinvention of critique**. It foregrounds theory from the southern hemisphere and beyond the Euro-American axis, and in practices of cross-regional intellectual exchange. The journal is explicitly an **intervention** — not just a repository — in critical theory.

**ISSN:** 2578-5370 (print) / 2578-5389 (electronic)  
**Frequency:** 3 issues per year  
**Open access:** Yes — immediate, no APC (article processing charges), Creative Commons BY-NC-ND

## Topics & Editorial Line

**What gets published:**
- Novel theoretical contributions to critical theory, broadly defined
- Work engaging social and political theory, literature, philosophy, art, anthropology, humanities, critical social sciences
- Theory from the Global South / non-Euro-American positions
- Cross-regional intellectual exchange
- Timely political commentary and theoretical response
- Translations of key works not yet in English

**Key intellectual orientations:**
- Post-Frankfurt-School critical theory (not just Habermas/Honneth — also Mbembe, Butler, Spivak)
- Decolonial and postcolonial thought
- Feminist and queer theory
- Black studies and critical race theory
- Chinese and East Asian critical thought ([[Wang Hui]])
- Latin American critical theory
- Political philosophy of precarity, biopolitics, necropolitics

**NOT typically published:**
- Empirical social science without theoretical grounding
- AI/technology papers in the engineering or HCI mode
- Design research (no precedent)
- Machine behavior, agent ethology, ALife

## Article Types & Submission Guidelines

| Type | Length | Review |
|------|--------|--------|
| **Scholarly Essays** | ≤12,000 words incl. abstract (100–150 words), endnotes, bibliography | Double-blind peer review (after editorial pre-review) |
| **Emergences** | 5,000–8,000 words incl. abstract | Editorial review (not blind peer review) |
| **Artistic Interventions** | Visual art contributions | Curated by art editors |
| **Translations** | Previously published, not in English | Editorial review |

**Process for Scholarly Essays:**  
1. Pre-review by senior, associate, and assistant editors  
2. If forwarded → double-blind peer review  
3. Article + readers' reports discussed by full editorial team

## Editorial Line Analysis

Critical Times occupies a distinctive niche: **humanities-anchored critical theory with explicit global/decolonial commitments**. It is:
- More philosophically rigorous than area studies journals
- More politically committed than philosophy journals
- More globally oriented than classic Frankfurt School venues (like *Constellations* or *New German Critique*)
- Peer company: *boundary 2*, *Social Text*, *Postcolonial Studies*, *GLQ*, *differences*

The Mellon Foundation backing + ICCTP network gives it institutional stability. The executive board (Butler, Spivak, Mbembe, Gilroy) signals it as a serious venue in the global humanities.

**Acceptance rate:** Not publicly disclosed; likely selective (~15–25% based on peer profile).

## Technology / AI / Posthumanism Coverage

Critical Times does **not** have a strong track record on AI, technology, or posthumanism per se. However:
- The journal has published on biopolitics, necropolitics (relevant to machine death, mortality)
- Wang Hui's participation connects it to Chinese technology debates and cosmotechnics-adjacent thought
- Achille Mbembe's presence connects it to posthumanism through necropolitics
- The "Emergences" section is more flexible and could accommodate tech-critical interventions
- **[[Yuk Hui]]**'s cosmotechnics framework would be a natural fit here if submitted as critical theory (not as media studies)

No specific articles found on AI, agent ethology, or cosmotechnics yet — this is a **gap and opportunity**.
