# The Shed

NYC's flagship interdisciplinary cultural center — a machine for producing ambitious, boundary-crossing commissions at the intersection of performing arts, visual art, and emerging technology. Opened April 5, 2019, in Hudson Yards. Distinctive retractable shell (the "McCourt") makes the building itself a kinetic artwork. Known for producing artist-led works that break discipline silos. Most relevant to Amber: the 2021 *The Looking Glass* AR exhibition (17 AR works, Acute Art partnership), MIT Media Lab partnership for VR/AI art, and biennial Open Call commissioning program.

## Key People
- **Alex Poots** (Artistic Director, CBE) — Founding artistic director. Previously: Manchester International Festival (founding AD, 2005–2015), Park Avenue Armory (founding AD, 2012–2015), Barbican Centre (1996–2003). Edinburgh-born, music background (trumpet, composition). Known for artist-led, radically interdisciplinary commissions. Stepped down as CEO January 2023; retains Artistic Director role.
- **Meredith "Max" Hodges** (CEO, Oct 2023–) — Joined from Boston Ballet.
- **Emma Enderby** (Chief Curator) — Co-curated *The Looking Glass* AR exhibition with [[Daniel Birnbaum]] (Acute Art / Moderna Museet).
- **Jonathan Tisch** (Board Chairman, 2022–) — Succeeded founding chairman Daniel Doctoroff.
- **Daniel Birnbaum** — (not staff) Guest curator of *The Looking Glass*; former director Moderna Museet, co-founder [[Acute Art]].

## History & Architecture

Conceived from Hudson Yards rezoning plan (~2008), city-owned land preserved for cultural use at 545 W. 30th Street between 10th and 11th Avenues, adjacent to the northern High Line. Name evolved: Culture Shed → Hudson Yards Cultural Shed → simply The Shed (2016).

**Architects:** Diller Scofidio + Renfro (lead) + Rockwell Group (collaborating). Structural/facade/kinetic engineering: Thornton Tomasetti.

**Building facts:**
- 170,000 sq ft total; 40,000 sq ft column-free exhibition space; 25,000 sq ft "museum-quality" space
- 500-seat theater; third-level flexible performance/exhibition space
- Retractable shell ("The McCourt"): 8 million lbs ETFE-clad steel frame, extends to create 16,000 sq ft enclosed space for large-scale performances; opens within 5 minutes via 8 bogie wheels driven by 6 × 15-hp motors
- Building officially renamed the **Bloomberg Building** after Michael Bloomberg donated $75M total

**Funding:** $529M raised for $550M capital campaign. Bloomberg $75M, NYC Capital Grant $75M. Endorsed by MoMA and Guggenheim directors.

## Mission & Programming Model

The Shed is explicitly a **commissioning institution**, not primarily a presenting venue. It commissions new works that couldn't exist elsewhere — requiring the building's flexibility, scale, and production resources. Programming spans:
- **Visual art exhibitions** (large-scale commissions, retrospectives)
- **Performing arts** (concerts, theater, dance)
- **Digital and immersive** (AR, VR, AI art, film)
- **Open Call** — biennial commissioning program for NYC-based emerging artists; proposals reviewed by panel of ~50 critics/professionals

Early institutional partnership: **MIT Media Lab** (2016+) — assisting artists creating works involving VR and AI.

## Notable Programming

### XR / Digital Art
- ***The Looking Glass*** (2021) — 17 AR artworks across Hudson Yards plaza and the High Line. Produced in partnership with [[Acute Art]]. Curated by Emma Enderby + [[Daniel Birnbaum]]. Artists include some of Acute Art's major roster (Jeff Koons, Marina Abramović, Olafur Eliasson, Hito Steyerl, et al. — Acute Art's typical roster). Free and outdoors. Context: post-pandemic reopening of outdoor space. The most ambitious AR public exhibition mounted by a major US institution to that date. Viewable via the Acute Art app.

### Ecological / More-than-Human
- **Agnes Denes: *Absolutes and Intermediates*** — Major retrospective spanning 50+ years. Denes's practice spans Land Art, Computer Art (1970s), environmental activism (Wheatfield, 1982). The retrospective positioned her as central to ecological + computational art lineage.

### Social / Political
- **Open Call** (biennial, launched ~2019, major 2021 edition) — NYC-based emerging artists. 2021 edition focused on diasporic identity, grief, urban landscape; 27 proposals selected, 11 visual artists. Artists including Kenneth Tam, Pauline Shaw, Anne Wu. Commissioning model: artists propose works, panel selects, Shed produces.
- **Howardena Pindell: *Rope/Fire/Water*** (2020) — Post-COVID reopening exhibition. Confronting racism and systemic violence through personal/political lens.
- **Tony Cokes** (2018) — Exhibition on gentrification.
- **Lawrence Weiner** — First permanent plaza commission (2017): "In front of itself" in 12-foot pavers.

### Performance / Interdisciplinary
- **Opening programme** (April 2019):
  - *Soundtrack of America* — Steve McQueen concept; African American music history
  - *Reich Richter Pärt* — Gerhard Richter × Steve Reich × Arvo Pärt
  - *Norma Jeane Baker of Troy* — Anne Carson poem-play; Ben Whishaw + Renée Fleming
  - Trisha Donnelly exhibition
- **Björk's Cornucopia** (May–June 2019) — 9-show run, elaborate stage design, ecological activism
- **Wu Tsang: *MOBY DICK; or, The Whale*** (2022) — Feature-length "silent" film adaptation; Wu Tsang + Moved by the Motion collective. Co-produced with Schauspielhaus Zürich, LUMA Foundation, Superblue, TBA21–Academy, Whitney Museum.
- ***Here We Are*** (2023) — World premiere of Stephen Sondheim's final musical.
- ***Viola's Room*** (Jun–Oct 2025) — Punchdrunk immersive experience; Helena Bonham Carter.
- ***An Ark*** (2026) — Immersive experience; Ian McKellen.

## Fellowships & Programmes

- **[Shed Labs](https://theshed.org/programs/shed-labs)** — Artist development program for early-career artists. Mentorship, resources, access to Shed's production infrastructure. NYC-based focus.
- **Open Call** — Biennial commissioning initiative. Not a traditional open call with fixed deadlines, but a solicitation of proposals every two years. Monitor theshed.org for next cycle. Past cycles: 2019, 2021–22.

## Institutional Circuit

The Shed occupies a peculiar circuit: it's simultaneously:
1. **Major NYC institution** (Bloomberg money, city land, MoMA/Guggenheim endorsements) — but organized around commissions, not collections
2. **Adjacent to Chelsea gallery world** but not a gallery (no commercial function)
3. **Aligned with European festival model** (Alex Poots's Manchester background shows: commissions, premieres, not touring productions)
4. **Tech-art adjacent** (MIT Media Lab partnership, Looking Glass AR) but not a "tech" institution

Peer venues in Poots's circuit: [[Park Avenue Armory]], Manchester International Festival, [[Barbican Art Gallery]] (London). Aesthetic circuit: risk-taking, interdisciplinary, production-heavy.

## Controversies
- **Stephen Ross** (board member, real estate developer) hosted Trump 2020 fundraiser (2019) → artist protests, A.L. Steiner and Zackary Drucker pulled work; Rag & Bone pulled fashion show. Ross left board Dec 2019.
- The Shed is literally built at Hudson Yards — the most aggressive luxury real-estate development in NYC history. Critics: it "artwashes" Hudson Yards. Complicated position.
- Laid off 25% of staff during pandemic.

## Why Relevant to Amber

1. **HoloKit / XR angle**: *The Looking Glass* (2021) is arguably the most ambitious public AR exhibition mounted by a major US institution. 17 works, outdoor, High Line + Hudson Yards. Acute Art as production partner. If Amber ever wants to pitch an XR/HoloKit public art project to a major NYC institution, The Shed is the most obvious candidate. Emma Enderby + Daniel Birnbaum are the curators to know.

2. **Ecological / More-than-human**: Agnes Denes retrospective signals deep interest in art-ecology-technology intersection. Denes's Computer Art (1970s) + Land Art are ancestral to more-than-human computation.

3. **Open Call**: Biennial commissioning for NYC-based emerging artists. Monitor for next cycle. Works across all media. Large-scale, production-supported.

4. **MIT Media Lab partnership**: Formal institutional link to AI and VR art development. Signals The Shed's interest in technology-mediated practice.

5. **Alex Poots / Amber connections**: Poots built his reputation on artist-led, radically interdisciplinary work that borrows from music, visual art, performance. This matches Amber's practice style. Poots's prior work includes Wu Tsang, Agnes Denes, Steve McQueen — all artists whose work touches on posthuman, ecological, or identity-systems themes relevant to Amber's concerns.

6. **Wu Tsang / Moved by the Motion** — queer, posthuman, fluid collective. Amber's themes of machine behavior, ecological/more-than-human, fluid agency resonate with Tsang's approach.
