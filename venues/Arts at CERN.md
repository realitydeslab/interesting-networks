# Arts at CERN

Arts programme of CERN (the European Organization for Nuclear Research / particle physics laboratory). Directed by Monica Bello. Creates "extraordinary opportunities for dialogue and exchange between artists and scientists." Their flagship programme is **Collide International** — residency award organized every 3 years in partnership with a city and cultural organization.

## Collide International Barcelona (2019)
- 228 entries from 49 countries
- Rosa Menkman won with "Shadow Knowledge" — about the fringes of what is enlightened, using shadow as metaphor for un-rendered knowledge
- Honorary mentions: Samoa Rémy (CH), Addie Wagenknecht (AT), Nathan Witt (UK), Gabriella Torres (PR)
- Partnership: City of Barcelona + Institut de Cultura de Barcelona
- Jury: Monica Bello, Oriol Gual (La Capella), Joana Hurtado (Fabra i Coats), Helga Timko (CERN physicist)

## Why Interesting
CERN is the largest scientific instrument ever built. Their art programme creates direct dialogue between artists and particle physicists — people working at the absolute edges of human knowledge and imaging capacity. The connection Rosa Menkman forged here — between image resolution and scientific measurement resolution — is a genuine conceptual bridge, not just institutional prestige.

For Amber: CERN is where human knowledge about the physical universe hits its fundamental limits. The "impossible images" research Rosa developed here is directly relevant to questions about AI's rendering limits.
