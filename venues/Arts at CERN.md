# Arts at CERN

Arts programme of CERN (the European Organization for Nuclear Research) — the world's largest particle physics laboratory, located in Geneva, Switzerland. Since 2012, Arts at CERN has invited over 200 artists to experience fundamental physics research and engage with CERN's scientific community. Directed since May 2025 by **Giulia Bini** (succeeding Mónica Bello after a decade). Three current programme pillars: **Collide** (international), **Resonance** (Geneva-local), and **Connect** (Swiss/international with Pro Helvetia). Over 35 artworks commissioned since 2018, showcased in major museums worldwide.

## Giulia Bini — New Head (May 2025)

**Background:** Italian curator, PhD in art history. Founding head of *Enter the Hyper-Scientific* (artist-in-residence at EPFL Lausanne). Former ZKM Karlsruhe curatorial team. Lectured at HEAD-Geneva. Committee of Experts, Pro Helvetia (2021–2025).

**Curatorial vision:** "I believe in the growing potential and societal impact of the versatile nexus between scientific and artistic research, practice, and thought... Both science and art can sharpen the lenses through which we observe our surroundings — from the smallest to the largest scale."

**Her angle vs. Mónica Bello's:** Bello built the infrastructure (programmes, global network). Bini brings a focus on institutional strategy and *how* techno-scientific discourse redefines artistic practices and exhibition formats. More ZKM-influenced (media art, technology-critical art history).

**Why it matters for Amber:** Bini's EPFL background means she understands computational/tech artists, not just fine arts. Her ZKM background means she values critical art theory + technology. This is Amber's exact zone.

## Mónica Bello — The Founding Vision (2012–2025)

Built Arts at CERN from nothing into one of the most prestigious art-science residencies globally. Key contributions:
- Structured diversity in residencies: diverse locations, career stages, backgrounds
- Launched Art Commissions programme (artworks that outlast residencies)
- Founded CERN Art and Science Summit (annual)
- Built network spanning labs, cultural orgs, creative communities worldwide

**Her curatorial vision (legacy):** Art-science not as illustration — artists as researchers who produce genuine knowledge through their encounter with physics. The residency is about *research*, not decoration. Artists develop proposals around CERN's specific research questions.

## Why Interesting

CERN is where human knowledge about the physical universe hits fundamental limits. The art programme creates genuine dialogue between artists and physicists at the frontier of what can be known. This isn't prestige-hunting — the conceptual territory is real: emergence, observation, measurement limits, scale, time, decay. For Amber's Agent Ethology work, the parallel to particle physics methodology is intellectually serious.
