# imagineNATIVE Film + Media Arts Festival

The world's largest presenter of Indigenous screen content. Annual festival in Toronto (TIFF Lightbox) and online, now running in **June** rather than October (shifted schedule starting 2025). Covers Film + Video, Digital + Interactive, and Audio works by Indigenous artists worldwide. Founded 2000, now in its 26th year. The **Digital + Interactive Award** and **New Artist in Digital + Interactive Award** are the premier recognition for Indigenous digital, interactive, and new media art globally.

- **[Vanessa Racine](https://imaginenative.org)** — 2025 New Artist in Digital+Interactive Award: *orange pekoe*. Emerging digital/interactive artist.

## Topics / Formats

### Primary Tracks
- **Film + Video**: Short and feature narratives, documentaries, experimental film
- **Digital + Interactive**: Web-based, installation, VR/AR, games, interactive experiences, new media — Indigenous artists working in computational and interactive media
- **Audio**: Narrative audio, experimental audio, podcasts

### Recurring Themes
- **Indigenous futurism**: Speculative Indigenous futures, decolonizing time
- **Land and ecology**: Indigenous ecological knowledge, territory, water, animal relations — profoundly more-than-human ontological grounding
- **Language revitalization**: Works in Indigenous languages (dedicated "Indigenous Language Production Award")
- **Intergenerational trauma and healing**
- **After Dark / genre**: Horror, dark fiction (Jeff Barnaby Award)
- **Innovation in storytelling**: Boundary-pushing form (Kent Monkman Award)

## For Amber

**Amber is not Indigenous** — imagineNATIVE requires works by Indigenous artists (directors, producers, writers, designers). This is a hard eligibility requirement. **Amber cannot submit work here.** This is a discovery and contact space, not a submission venue for Amber.

**Why it still matters:**

1. **Cosmotechnics via Indigenous technê:** Indigenous media art is perhaps the richest living example of non-Western technê in the contemporary arts world. *Wilfred Buck's Star Stories* (Indigenous astronomy as interactive experience) — this is cosmology embedded in technological form. Directly relevant to Amber's interest in cosmotechnics (Yuk Hui) and non-Western approaches to technology. The festival is a major site where this work gets made visible.

2. **More-than-human ontologies at the base:** Indigenous worldviews are fundamentally non-anthropocentric — land, water, animals, sky as agents and relatives, not resources. This is a different route to more-than-human design than Western posthumanism. imagineNATIVE showcases works where this ontology is operationalized in interactive and digital form. Relevant to Amber's more-than-human design interests.

3. **Jason Edward Lewis + AbTeC connection:** Lewis (ObX Lab, Concordia) is Amber's strongest entry point. He bridges Indigenous new media, AI poetics, and institutional infrastructure building. His work on "Decolonizing AI" and Indigenous language+technology sits at exact intersection with Amber's agent ethology / machine behavior territory. imagineNATIVE is his home festival.

4. **Discovery of Indigenous digital/interactive artists:** The Digital+Interactive track surfaces artists doing computational and interactive work from non-Western cosmological foundations — which Amber won't find at Ars Electronica or SIGGRAPH. A useful scouting ground.

5. **Indigenous futurism / speculative futures:** Skawennati's *TimeTraveller™* machinima, LaPensée's ecological games — Indigenous speculative design is a distinct tradition from Western speculative design. Adds depth to Amber's speculative design understanding.

## Why Interesting

imagineNATIVE represents something unique: **Indigenous cosmologies operationalized in contemporary digital and interactive form**. This is not tokenism or primitivism — it's a sophisticated computational art practice grounded in distinct ontologies where land, water, animals, and sky are relational agents rather than inert matter.

For Amber, the connection is indirect but real: the festival surfaces the most advanced examples of non-Western technê in interactive media globally. The Digital+Interactive track specifically shows what it looks like when computation is approached from an Anishinaabe, Haudenosaunee, Māori, or Sámi cosmological frame — a radically different design ontology from Western AI/robotics labs.

**Relevance to Amber's specific interests:**
- **Cosmotechnics (Yuk Hui):** Indigenous media art = living practice of non-Western cosmotechnics
- **More-than-human design:** Indigenous new media often encodes non-anthropocentric relationality
- **Speculative design:** Indigenous futurism as a distinct speculative tradition
- **Non-Western technê:** The whole festival is a showcase of this
- **Less direct:** ALife, agent ethology, machine behavior — not the focus
