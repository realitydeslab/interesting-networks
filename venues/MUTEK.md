# MUTEK

International festival of digital creativity and electronic music. Founded 2000 in Montréal. The premier global circuit for audiovisual performance — experimental, technically ambitious, conceptually serious. Now a multi-city network spanning 7 cities across 4 continents. One of the few festival-scale venues that combines live AV performance, critical discourse (Forum MUTEK), and funded residency/lab programmes. The "MU" in MUTEK stands for **mutation**.

## Overview

MUTEK is a **Montréal-based non-profit** dedicated to the dissemination and development of digital creativity in sound, music, and audiovisual art. Founded in 2000, it has presented nearly 1,500 artists worldwide across all editions.

**Mission statement:** Provide a platform for original and visionary artists working in experimental electronic music and digital art. Balance experimental edge with accessibility/discovery for audiences.

**What distinguishes MUTEK:**
- **Multi-city global network** — not a single festival but a federated brand: 7 cities, each with its own identity
- **Forum MUTEK** — a serious professional/critical programme alongside the festival proper. Not just panels — includes funded residency labs, market networking, and critical discourse
- **MUTEK AI Ecologies Lab** — a funded interdisciplinary residency programme for artists working at the AI × sustainability × culture intersection. Genuinely unusual and relevant to Amber
- **Immersive Collection** — MUTEK produces XR (VR/AR) artworks, co-commissioned and distributed on Steam/Viveport, shown internationally
- **Practice-first** — unlike Ars Electronica (more gallery/exhibition) or CTM (more academic), MUTEK is centred on **live performance** as the primary form

## Programming

### Festival Core (Montréal & Satellite Cities)
- **Live A/V performance** — the heart of MUTEK. Artists performing audiovisual sets, often site-specific or using custom systems. Not DJs with visuals — these are composed, technical, often generative works
- **Nocturne series** (Tokyo) — evening-long AV performance programmes, split between experimental and club-oriented
- **Commissions and premieres** — MUTEK regularly premieres new works; each edition has notable Canadian, North American, and international premieres

### Forum MUTEK (separate programme, Montréal, Aug 20–22 alongside festival)
- **"Marketplace for ideas"** — Forum.mutek.org
- 2025 (Edition 11): theme **"Radical Rituals"** — 100+ creators, innovators, and practitioners; three-day programme
- Covers: artistic research, creation, interdisciplinary collaboration, critical approaches to digital creation
- **MUTEK Match** — curated networking event for festival, Forum, and Village Numérique market participants (August 21, 2025)
- **MUTEK Market** — talent buyer / artist industry market. Artists can be listed as "market artists"
- **Incubator** — programme accompanying emerging artists

### MUTEK AI Ecologies Lab (Forum-attached residency)
- **URL:** [forum.mutek.org/en/mutek-ai-ecologies-lab](https://forum.mutek.org/en/mutek-ai-ecologies-lab)
- An **interdisciplinary residency** (6 selected participants) for artists developing AI tools for the cultural sector
- 2025 timetable: Call for projects Jan–Feb, notifications March, virtual lab April, in-person lab June at Concordia/SAT Montreal, presentation at MUTEK Forum August
- Outcomes showcased at Forum through workshops, presentations, knowledge-sharing
- Partners: MUTEK + SAT (Society for Arts and Technology) + Applied AI Institute + Milieux Institute + Abundant Intelligences
- Focus: AI + sustainability + cultural sector. Not just art-for-art's-sake but **environmental responsibility in AI adoption**

### Immersive Collection (XR production)
- Since 2022, MUTEK produces **Extended Reality (XR) artworks** — co-commissioning artists to create VR/AR experiences
- Distributed on Steam, Viveport, and via Astrea (international XR distributor)
- Shown internationally at partner festivals (Ars Electronica, Sónar+D, Raindance London, MUTEK Tokyo, etc.)
- Scope: 2022 collection premiered at Montréal, then toured globally through 2024
- Artists include: France Jobin & Markus Heckmann (*Entanglement XR*), Chloe Alexandra Thompson & Matthew Edwards (*House of Moiré*), Line Katcho (*Immortelle*)

### Village Numérique
- Public-facing installation programme in Montreal city streets. MUTEK received the 2025 Canadian Tourism Awards Innovator of the Year Award for this programme.

## Artist Profile

MUTEK showcases artists working at the intersection of:
- **Generative / algorithmic systems** — artists using custom software, procedural generation
- **Live coding and modular synthesis**
- **Data-driven visual systems** — real-time, responsive, generative
- **AV performance** — not just music + visuals, but tightly integrated audiovisual compositions
- **Extended Reality** — immersive and spatial works

**Artists from the AV performance circuit that have appeared at MUTEK:**
- [[Daito Manabe]] (Rhizomatiks) — *Phase Forms* at MUTEK.JP 2025; lecture at MUTEK.MX 2019
- **France Jobin** — Montréal-based sound/visual artist, Immersive Collection collaborator
- **Markus Heckmann** — visual artist, Derivative TouchDesigner community, Immersive Collection
- **Ricardo Villalobos** — MUTEK Barcelona 2025 (club-oriented end of the spectrum)
- The broader circuit includes: Ryoji Ikeda, Objekt, KMRU, Jlin, Holly Herndon, Arca — MUTEK consistently books the experimental electronic/AV field's key figures

**Overlap with Amber's interests:**
- Artists using **generative and agent-based systems** for live performance
- **XR/immersive** — MUTEK is one of the few festivals actively commissioning and distributing XR works
- **AI Ecologies Lab** is explicitly about AI in art/culture — directly relevant to Amber's computational creativity and ALife interests

### MUTEK Market (Artist Visibility)
- MUTEK Forum runs an industry market where artists can be listed as "market artists" for talent buyers
- This is how many artists get booked into satellite editions globally
- Contact via Forum.mutek.org

### Artist Selection (Festival Performance)
- MUTEK does **not appear to have an open public submission process** for live festival performances — curation is invitation-based
- Path to invitation: be visible on the circuit (Sónar, CTM, Ars Electronica), be listed in the Market, or be introduced by existing MUTEK network

### Incubator Programme
- Forum has an "Incubator: Accompanying tomorrow's artists" programme — emerging artist development
- Check forum.mutek.org for upcoming cohort calls

## Why Interesting

MUTEK sits at the exact intersection of Amber's practice and presentation ambitions:
1. **Computational creativity** is the festival's core aesthetic — generative, algorithmic, AV
2. **More-than-human** — the AI Ecologies Lab explicitly asks about AI in relation to natural ecosystems, not just human creativity
3. **Practice-based** — Amber can show *work*, not just talk about it
4. **XR/immersive** — one of the few festivals that actually commissions and distributes XR
5. **Global network** — a strong MUTEK relationship opens doors in Montréal, Tokyo, Barcelona, Buenos Aires simultaneously
