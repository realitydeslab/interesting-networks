# STRP Festival

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-03-03:** Full deep research. Updated from stub. Corrected frequency (now annual, two events/year, not biennial). Next event: *Soft, Slow and Powerful* 26-28 March 2026. New PROTO space opening April 2026. Young ACT Award is the key application pathway.
- **2026-02-26:** Created stub note via [[Kyle McDonald]] deep research.

Art and technology platform based in Eindhoven, Netherlands. Founded 2006, celebrating 20 years in 2026. Explores "the relationship between people, experimental technologies, art, society and the future." Exhibitions, performances, workshops, education programmes. Eindhoven context: strong design+technology culture (TU/e, Design Academy Eindhoven), home of Philips — making STRP sit at the art/tech/design intersection with a European critical slant.

## Fellowships & Programmes

### 🏆 STRP Young ACT Award
The primary **open call / award** for emerging artists. Key pathway for Amber.
- **What:** For artists transitioning from art school to professional practice; present graduation projects expanded into STRP exhibitions
- **Prize:** Project budget + artistic and production guidance + exhibition at STRP spring event + network access
- **Selection:** Jury = STRP Artistic Director + STRP Young Advisory Board; 3 winners selected per cycle
- **2025 Winners (presenting at Big Energy, Slow Futures, March 2026):**
  - Sara Ballout — *Networks of Necessity* (Lebanese artist, infrastructure collapse, interactive installations, decentralised energy networks)
  - Gjorgji Despodov — *Cache Cleanse: Doom Scroll Rehab* (speculative "provocotypes," digital overconsumption, immersive experience)
  - Ecaterina Grigorieva — *The Sounds We Hear On Land* (glass sculptures, water sounds, audience participation)
- **How to find:** Watch <https://strp.nl/> and their newsletter for annual call announcement
- **Application timing:** Likely announced early-to-mid year for following spring event

## Why Interesting
STRP occupies a distinct niche in the European art+tech landscape: community-focused, Eindhoven-rooted (Design Academy + TU/e + Philips heritage), genuinely critical of technology rather than celebratory. The shift from large biennial → two smaller focused events per year signals a more intimate, curated model. The Young ACT Award shows institutional commitment to supporting emerging artists.

Thematic relevance to Amber: indirect but real. STRP's emphasis on *relational* technology, energy politics, collective presence, and speculative futures resonates with Amber's more-than-human and machine behavior interests. The venue circuit (European media art/design) overlaps with artists Amber should know.

**Circuit fit:** Ars Electronica tier but smaller/more curated. Same circuit as FIBER Amsterdam, GOGBOT Enschede, Dutch Design Week, Transmediale Berlin. Good for building European presence.
