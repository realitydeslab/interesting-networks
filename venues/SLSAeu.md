# SLSAeu (European Society for Literature, Science and the Arts)

## ⚡ Recent Updates

European annual conference of the Society for Literature, Science, and the Arts (SLSA). Founded 2010 as sister organisation to US-based SLSA; first meeting 2012, Riga. Smaller than US SLSA (est. 50–200 participants). Interdisciplinary: humanities, social sciences, arts, science, medicine, engineering, computer science. Moves between European host institutions annually.

---

## Overview

SLSAeu is the European chapter of the international [Society for Literature, Science, and the Arts](http://www.litsciarts.org/) (SLSA). It operates as a formally independent European society (registered in Switzerland), not merely a satellite event. It has its own governance, membership, book series, and elected officers.

**Relationship to SLSA US:**
- Sister organisation, not subordinate
- Liaison officer links both (Carol Colatrella, Georgia Tech, represents SLSA on SLSAeu board)
- Members of US SLSA get reduced SLSAeu membership (€20 vs €30)
- Both share the journal *Configurations* (Johns Hopkins UP)
- Similar intellectual territory; SLSAeu tends to be stronger in European critical theory, posthumanism, and medical humanities

**Membership fees:**
- Individual: €30/year (1 vote)
- Reduced (students, low income, SLSA/BSLS members): €20/year
- Institutional: €150/year
- Benefits: reduced conference fee, 50% discount on Brill series *Experimental Practices*, *Critical Posthumanisms*, *Critical Animal Studies*

**Submission process:**
- Abstract (~250 words) + 50-word bio
- Formats: 20-min paper presentations, discipline-crossing panels (3+ participants from different disciplines)
- Also welcomes art events, artist-scholar panels
- Decisions typically ~4–6 weeks after deadline

## Typical Conference Profile

**Scale:** Estimated 80–200 participants. Smaller and more intimate than US SLSA (~500+). More like a specialist symposium than a mega-conference.

**Disciplines most represented:**
- Literary/cultural studies (dominant)
- Medical humanities
- Science and technology studies (STS)
- Philosophy (continental, posthumanism)
- Feminist science studies
- Art history, bioart
- Environmental humanities

**Less represented (but present):**
- Computer science / HCI
- AI/ML research
- Design

**Submission formats:**
- Individual 20-min papers
- Cross-disciplinary panels (3+ people from different fields, one as moderator)
- Artist-scholar hybrid events
- Art exhibitions (section head: Jens Hauser, Paris-based)

**ECR support:** Active commitment to early-career researchers; bursaries offered at recent editions.

**Publication pipeline:** Papers can feed into *Experimental Practices* yearbook (Brill), *Configurations* journal (Johns Hopkins UP), or *Journal of Literature and Science*.

## For Amber

### Fit Assessment: ⭐⭐ (Good, not essential)

SLSAeu is **not Amber's primary venue** — it's literary studies-first, with humanities/posthumanism as the dominant lens. But it's **geographically accessible** (annual UK/European editions) and increasingly engages with digital/computational questions.

**Strongest alignment:**
- Critical posthumanism (Amber's more-than-human framing)
- Machine death / mortality (→ 2027 Basel "Death & Dying" is a **direct hit**)
- Cognitive assemblages, nonhuman agency (from 2021 Bergen framing)
- Art-science practice (HoloKit, experiential futures)

**Weaker alignment:**
- Agent ethology, ALife, machine behavior as *science* — this community reads these topics through literary/humanities lens, not computational
- HCI methods, system building — not valued here
- Quantitative / empirical approaches

**Submission format recommendation:**

**Who to meet:**

**SLSAeu vs SLSA US for Amber:**
- SLSA US: larger (~500 people), US-based, more diverse (more CS/media theory crossover), Pittsburgh/Atlanta axis

- **Verdict:** SLSAeu is actually better for Amber given Oxford base. Lower travel cost, more networking per interaction.
