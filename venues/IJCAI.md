## For Amber

IJCAI is a **workshop-first venue** for Amber. The main conference track is prestigious but oriented toward ML systems papers. The value is:

1. **Workshop submissions** — find workshops on agent ethics, social simulation, human-AI governance. The COINE workshop series (Coordination, Organisations, Institutions, Norms, Ethics — see [[AAMAS]] for AAMAS 2026 edition) has historically appeared at both AAMAS and IJCAI.
2. **Special Track on Human-Centered AI** — an opening for framing more-than-human work, though this track tends toward classic user-centered AI research.
3. **Special Track on AI for Social Good** — potentially home for Amber's Sovereign/Parasitic Agents work framed as societal impact.
4. **Networking** — a large gathering of AI researchers including the machine behavior and ethics community; valuable for meeting people even without a submission.
5. **Location 2026: Bremen, Germany** — central Europe, accessible from Oxford/London.

**Priority recommendation:** Monitor the workshop list when it goes live (~May/June 2026). If COINE 2026 appears at IJCAI (it sometimes runs there as a joint event), this is a direct target. Otherwise, the AI for Social Good special track is worth watching for a submission angle.

**Rating rationale:** ⭐⭐ not ⭐⭐⭐ because: (a) main track is too technical/ML-heavy, (b) workshop list not confirmed, (c) less focused than AAMAS, ALIFE, or CHI for Amber's specific interests.

## Why Interesting

IJCAI hosts the COINE workshop series (the longest-running venue on AI ethics and norms — 18 AAMAS editions + recurring IJCAI appearances). For machine behavior and AI society work, IJCAI's workshops are a significant community touchpoint. The conference's scale (~1,000+ papers) also means it draws people across the full AI ethics/society landscape, making it valuable for networking even without a submission.
