# ACM SIGGRAPH

**ACM SIGGRAPH** (Special Interest Group on Computer GRAPHics and Interactive Techniques) is the world's premier conference on computer graphics, interactive techniques, and creative digital technology. Founded 1974. Annual attendance ~14,000–17,000 across researchers, practitioners, artists, and technologists. The only major conference that fully bridges rigorous technical computer science and art practice at equivalent depth.

For Amber: SIGGRAPH is where HoloKit-class work (interactive MR), ALife-as-art installations, and machine behavior expressed as creative technology all find their natural home. It is **the** venue for technology that is simultaneously research and art — which is Amber's whole practice.

### Most Relevant Tracks for Amber

**Art Papers** (the most aligned with Amber's practice-based research):
- Peer-reviewed, ~8–15 pages
- Published in [Proceedings of the ACM on Computer Graphics and Interactive Techniques (PACMCGIT)](https://dl.acm.org/journal/pacmcgit) — an ACM journal, high prestige
- Art Papers accept: case studies of realized projects, critical/theoretical essays on creative tech, investigative research using art practice as method
- Double-blind review
- Historically: works from MIT Media Lab, Ars Electronica winners, academic artists

**Art Gallery**:
- Juried exhibition (not peer-reviewed, more like a prestigious art exhibition)
- Physical installation at SIGGRAPH venue
- Work must be interactive and experienced in-person
- Best in Show award
- Historically exhibits: interactive sculptures, responsive environments, AI-generative art, data visualization as art

## For Amber

**Why SIGGRAPH matters for Amber:**

SIGGRAPH occupies a unique position — it's the only major conference where rigorous academic peer review meets gallery-quality art exhibition at equal prestige. CHI is HCI-focused; DIS is design-focused; Ars Electronica is art-exhibition focused but not academic peer-reviewed. SIGGRAPH does all of it simultaneously.
