# SLSA Conference

The annual conference of the [Society for Literature, Science, and the Arts](https://litsciarts.org/) — the premier humanities-facing venue for posthumanism, science studies, bioart, and critical theory of technology. Where Haraway's lineage is most alive, where Friedrich Kittler got a lifetime achievement award, and where scholars work across literature, science, medicine, and art with genuine theoretical ambition. Intimate compared to 4S; more experimental and literary in orientation. Essential venue for anyone working at the edge of STS, posthumanism, and digital culture.

## History

SLSA was founded in **August 1985** at the 17th International Congress of the History of Science, Berkeley, CA, by members who wanted an organization at the intersection of science and humanistic inquiry. Initially called the **Society for Literature and Science (SLS)**, it held its first annual meeting in 1987 (Worcester, MA) as a symposium titled "Literature and Science as Modes of Expression." Annual conferences followed every year from 1988.

The name change to SLSA happened at the **2003 annual meeting** (Austin, TX) — adding "Arts" to the title to acknowledge the visual arts, bioart, sound, and performance that had become central to the community. The **first formal SLSA conference** (as opposed to SLS) was 2004 in Durham, NC.

Key publication: ***Configurations: A Journal of Literature, Science and Technology*** (Johns Hopkins University Press, founded 1993) — the only journal devoted to the study of discourse around theories and practices of science, technology, and medicine.

Current size: "hundreds of participants" at annual conference. A mid-sized specialist conference — far more intimate than 4S (Society for Science & Technology Studies) or MLA.

## How SLSA Differs from 4S/EASST/DRS

| | SLSA | 4S / EASST | DRS |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Core orientation** | Humanistic (literary, artistic, philosophical) | Social science (sociology, ethnography) | Design as discipline |
| **Method preference** | Close reading, cultural theory | Qualitative fieldwork, ethnography | Practice-based research |
| **Posthumanism** | Central framework | Peripheral (STS-focused) | Adjacent (via more-than-human design) |
| **Art/science** | Core (bioart, science-art) | Marginal | Core (research through design) |
| **Scale** | Hundreds | Thousands | Hundreds-low thousands |
| **Vibe** | Literary-theoretical, experimental | Rigorous social science | Design-practice |
| **Haraway** | Central canon | Referenced | Referenced |
| **Where CS papers fit** | Only if humanistic framing | If sociology of technology | If design-led |

SLSA is the conference where a paper about **machine death as literary/philosophical trope** lands better than one presenting empirical data. The frame is always: what does this *mean* culturally, narratively, philosophically?

SLSA is where Amber's theoretical framework (posthumanism, more-than-human, cyborg sociology) meets its home community. The conference draws scholars who have been doing this work since the 1990s — the intellectual ancestors of Amber's current interests. Unlike CHI or FAccT which are design/tech-first, SLSA is humanities-first, which means:

1. **Machine death as philosophy works here** — no need to justify it with user studies
