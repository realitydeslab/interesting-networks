# Zeitschrift für Medienwissenschaft (ZfM)

The flagship peer-reviewed journal for German-language media studies (*Medienwissenschaft*). Published by [transcript Verlag](https://www.transcript-verlag.de/) (Bielefeld), the ZfM is the central publication venue for the German tradition — media archaeology, cultural techniques (*Kulturtechniken*), media philosophy, digital culture. Established 2009. Sustained by the [GfM (Gesellschaft für Medienwissenschaft)](https://gfmedienwissenschaft.de/). Politically engaged: feminist, antiracist, science-critical. Publishes in German primarily; some English accepted.

## Journal Structure

Each issue follows a fixed format:

- **Schwerpunkt** — thematic core, conceptualized by a guest editorial team
- **Extra** — current essays not tied to the main theme (open submissions)
- **Debatte** — theoretical and/or science-political statements
- **Werkzeuge** — reflections on software/hardware tools shaping research and teaching
- **Besprechungen** — thematic cluster reviews of recent publications
- **Laborgespräche** — conversations with research labs and artistic practice spaces
- **Bildstrecke** — visual essay on image archives

**Online presence:**
- **GAAAP_ The Blog** — gender, antiracism, antisemitism critique, antiableism, antikapitalism, postcoloniality
- **Videography** — multimedia blog on the video essay form
- **OMS-Blog** — Open Media Studies discussions
- **Debatten** — ongoing theoretical debates (Wissenschaftsfreiheit, Medien und Bildung, Methoden der Medienwissenschaft)

## Submission Process

1. **Thematic articles:** Respond to open CFPs for each Schwerpunkt (guest editor issues); or propose a Schwerpunkt yourself
2. **Extra articles:** Submit directly at any time — no CFP required; addressed to themes other than the current Schwerpunkt
3. **Review essays, book reviews, conference reports:** Submit to website blogs after prior agreement with the editorial office
4. **Contact:** [redaktion@zfmedienwissenschaft.de](mailto:redaktion@zfmedienwissenschaft.de)
5. **Downloads:** [Styleguide 2025](https://zfmedienwissenschaft.de/sites/default/files/media/allgemein/downloads/ZfM_Styleguide_2025_2.pdf) · [Submission template](https://zfmedienwissenschaft.de/sites/default/files/media/allgemein/downloads/ZfM_Vorlage%20fu%CC%88r%20Beitragseinreichungen_2024_final.dotx) · [Guide for guest editors](https://zfmedienwissenschaft.de/sites/default/files/media/allgemein/downloads/ZfM_Leitfaden%20f%C3%BCr%20SP-Hg._2025.pdf)
6. **Zotero citation style:** Available [via Zotero style repository](https://www.zotero.org/styles/zeitschrift-fur-medienwissenschaft)

**Language note:** The editorial statement is published bilingually (DE/EN), and the journal accepts contributions from international scholars. English articles can appear in the *Extra* section or as part of internationally co-edited *Schwerpunkt* issues, though German is strongly preferred and most peer reviewers work in German.

## The "Perverse Bienen" Article

**"Perverse Bienen: Artificial Life und der Apfel der Erkenntnis"**
Authors: Jan Müggenburg, Ingo Warnke, Thomas Schmickl
Published: ZfM, approx. Heft 18 (~2/2017 or 1/2018)

This is the most directly relevant ZfM article for Amber's work:

- **Jan Müggenburg** — media theorist at [[ICAM Leuphana]], specializes in cybernetics, ALife, and the media history of computing. Key figure bridging German Medienwissenschaft and Artificial Life discourse.
- **Ingo Warnke** — linguist and discourse analyst; contributes a semiotic/language perspective on ALife
- **Thomas Schmickl** — swarm robotics biologist at University of Graz; known for artificial bee colonies and swarm intelligence research

The article positions ALife (artificial bees / swarm simulations) philosophically within the German media-philosophical tradition — reading artificial life through notions of the forbidden, knowledge, sexuality, and epistemology (the "apple of knowledge" = knowledge from transgression). This is a rare example of ZfM directly engaging with ALife as a media-philosophical object, rather than purely culturally.

**Significance for Amber:** Demonstrates that ZfM can publish ALife content when it's framed through media philosophy. Amber's work on agent ethology and machine behavior *could* find a home here if framed as media-philosophical inquiry rather than design research.

## ZfM in the German Medienwissenschaft Ecosystem

ZfM is the house journal of the GfM. Key adjacent journals and how they differ:

| Journal | Publisher | Focus | Language | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **ZfM** | transcript | Cultural media studies, media philosophy | DE (primarily) | Flagship GfM journal |
| **[Digital Culture & Society](https://www.transcript-verlag.de/978-3-8376-4563-3/)** | transcript | Digital sociology / media studies | **EN** | Better entry for English-language work |
| **[Medienwissenschaft: Rezensionen](https://mewire.de/)** | — | Reviews only | DE | Not a research venue |
| **[Zeitschrift für Kulturwissenschaften (ZfK)](https://www.transcript-verlag.de/zeitschriften/zfk)** | transcript | Cultural studies broadly | DE | More adjacent to anthropology |
| **[IMAGE](https://www.gib.uni-tuebingen.de/image)** | GIB | Visual studies, Bildwissenschaft | DE/EN | More visual media focused |

**Key distinction:** ZfM is politically and theoretically oriented (cultural critique, feminist, postcolonial) and more tied to *Kulturwissenschaft* than to empirical or computational media studies. It contrasts with Anglo-American *media studies* by staying closer to Continental philosophy.

**Connection to ICAM Leuphana:** [[ICAM Leuphana]] (Institute for Culture and Aesthetics of Digital Media) faculty — including [[Maren Haffke]], [[Jan Müggenburg]], Claus Pias, Martin Warnke — are major contributors. Leuphana is the de facto institutional home of German digital Medienwissenschaft. ZfM is its journal.

## Key Intellectual Territory

ZfM's core intellectual territory relevant to Amber:

- **Media philosophy** — the conditions of possibility for media (Kittler-tradition, post-Kittler)
- **Kulturtechniken** (Cultural Techniques) — Bernhard Siegert, Thomas Macho, Cornelia Vismann tradition. Media as practice before knowledge.
- **Media archaeology** — Zielinski, Ernst, Huhtamo tradition. Digging for suppressed technologies.
- **Digital cultures** — participatory cultures, platform studies, meme theory
- **Posthumanism / more-than-human** — increasingly present, especially in care/ecology issues
- **Gender, race, disability** — GAAAP_ blog and several Schwerpunkte
- **ALife as media** — rare but possible (see Perverse Bienen)

## For Amber

### 🎯 Venue Fit Assessment

**Overall fit: Medium** — ZfM is not a natural home for Amber's empirical/design work, but IS the right venue for a philosophically framed piece that bridges ALife with German media theory.

**What could fit:**
- A media-philosophical piece on agent ethology positioned within the Kulturtechniken tradition (e.g., "what does it mean for machines to have behaviors? A media-philosophical reading")
- An essay on ALife and cosmotechnics, framing Yuk Hui's work alongside German media theory (ZfM published content adjacent to this)
- A *Laborgespräch* (lab conversation) about RealityDesign Lab's work on machine behavior — this is a lower-stakes entry point than a peer-reviewed article
- A blog piece for GAAAP_ or OMS-Blog if connecting to digital culture / critical AI themes

**What won't fit:**
- CHI/HCI-style empirical studies
- Technical ALife work without critical-philosophical framing
- Design research in the mode of RtD (Research through Design)

### 🌍 Strategic Use

ZfM is less a *publication target* for Amber and more a **reading context**. Understanding what ZfM publishes = understanding how German media theory frames the questions Amber works on. Amber can:
1. **Read** relevant issues to understand the European critical media studies framing of machine behavior
2. **Propose a Schwerpunkt** with a German collaborator (e.g., someone at ICAM Leuphana) — this would let Amber shape a whole issue
3. **Submit an Extra piece** in English or German if she has a media-philosophical argument that doesn't need empirical framing
4. **Use the Laborgespräch format** to introduce HoloKit/Reality Design Lab to the German Medienwissenschaft community — lower stakes, good visibility

## Why Interesting

ZfM is the German media studies journal. It matters to Amber because:

1. **Cosmotechnics connection** — Yuk Hui's work is increasingly cited in European media studies; ZfM is the venue where German media philosophy meets these new questions. An Amber piece connecting ALife to cosmotechnics or non-Western technology theory would be a genuine contribution.

2. **ICAM Leuphana pipeline** — Amber is already adjacent to ICAM faculty (Haffke). This creates a natural pathway to ZfM via co-authorship or Schwerpunkt co-editing.

3. **The "Perverse Bienen" precedent** — ZfM has published directly on ALife before. Amber's work would not be unprecedented.

4. **European legitimacy** — Publishing in ZfM signals engagement with European critical humanities, which diversifies Amber's venue portfolio beyond ACM/IEEE/ALIFE.

5. **Research archaeology value** — Reading ZfM archive = understanding how European media theorists have already thought about machines, behavior, and life. Useful background for positioning Amber's work.
