# Łaźnia Centre for Contemporary Arts (CSW Łaźnia)

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-03-04:** Deep research completed. **Upgraded to ⭐⭐.** Łaźnia is more substantial than the stub suggested — it runs **Studiotopia 2.0** (a major EU art-science project on the Anthropocene/Symbiocene) and has a consistent program of media art, ecology, and speculative science work. Upcoming: Studiotopia 2.0 exhibition "Sensitive Data of the Anthropocene" opens March 13, 2026.

## What
A municipal Centre for Contemporary Art (CSW — Centrum Sztuki Współczesnej) in **Gdansk, Poland**, operating across two venues:
- **Łaźnia 1** — the main historic bathhouse building
- **Łaźnia 2** — a second space in the Nowy Port (New Port) district, with permanent outdoor installations

Founded in the 1990s (one of Poland's leading contemporary art centres). Runs year-round exhibitions, performances, screenings, and public programs. Also participates in European arts networks and EU-funded projects.

## Who Runs It
Municipal cultural institution of the City of Gdansk. The organization collaborates with international partners through projects like Studiotopia. Staff hiring notice was listed Feb 2026 (CSW is actively growing its team).

## Focus & Aesthetic
Contemporary art with strong emphasis on:
- **Art + science** (Studiotopia project — art meets Anthropocene/Symbiocene research)
- Media art and new media
- Ecology and more-than-human perspectives
- Feminist and social justice art
- Local community engagement (outdoor installations in Nowy Port)

Sensibility is engaged, critical, research-oriented — similar to Ars Electronica or HeK Basel, but with a distinctly Eastern European grounding and municipal roots.

## Notable Artists / Past Exhibitions
- **Ionat Zurr & Oron Catts (TC&A)** — *Crude Life* retrospective (2012), first full retrospective of the Tissue Culture & Art Project
- **Bogna Burska** — *Oddychaj* (Breathe), Sept 2025–Mar 2026 (Łaźnia 2)
- **Patricia J. Reis & Dorota Walentynowicz** — *Sensory Mechanisms*, Nov 2025–Feb 2026 (Łaźnia 1)
- **Mariusz Waras & Katarzyna Józefowicz** — permanent outdoor installations in Nowy Port ("Furioza" and "Aglomerat")
- **Studiotopia 2.0: Sensitive Data of the Anthropocene** — opens March 13, 2026 (Łaźnia 1); runs through May 24, 2026
