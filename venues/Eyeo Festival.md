# Eyeo Festival

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Initial deep research. Full note created from official website, documentation archive, Vimeo channel, Medium recap posts, founder essay.
- **2022:** Returned after 2020–21 COVID pause. 10th anniversary edition (June 14–17, 2022, Walker Art Center). Code+Care summit. No 2023+ editions announced as of research date.
- **2019:** Last edition before COVID pause. Themes around surveillance, AI, climate, community tech. Vimeo archive: 40 talks.
- **2020:** Was planned (save-the-date: June 8–11, 2020) but cancelled/postponed due to COVID.

## Vibe
**The TED for creative coders — but warmer, weirder, and more critical.** 600+ attendees, 4 days, one beautiful Midwestern city. Not a typical tech conference: no VC pitches, no industry puff. The curation is notoriously selective — speakers are invited, not submitted — and the result is a community that actually talks to each other. Named *Eyeo* by founder Dave Schroeder as a portmanteau of "eye-opening" and "I/O." Deliberately non-specific to allow evolution.

Held primarily at the **[Walker Art Center](https://walkerart.org/)** in Minneapolis — one of the great US contemporary art institutions — with evening events scattered across the city's industrial venues. The combination of a prestige art institution + Midwestern accessibility (not NYC, not SF) shapes the tone: ambitious but humane. Tagline: *Converge to Inspire.*

The festival is deeply anti-"AI-for-X-without-questioning-X." Even as topics evolved (from raw generative art in 2011 to ML/AI ethics by 2019), the critical, artistic, and humanist lens remained constant. When attendees in 2019 were told "please care a little less about machine learning," the room didn't revolt — it nodded.

## Relationship to Processing / openFrameworks Communities

Eyeo is the *annual heartbeat* of the creative coding community — the one event where the Processing, p5.js, openFrameworks, and adjacent communities physically converge. This is not accidental: the festival was founded partly to celebrate and promote the philosophy of open-source creative coding tools.

**Processing lineage:** Ben Fry and [[Casey Reas]] (Processing co-creators), [[Lauren Lee McCarthy]] (p5.js creator), Daniel Shiffman (The Coding Train), [[Golan Levin]] (ACG/MIT lineage) — all have been Eyeo speakers. The [[Processing Foundation]] is listed as a 2022 partner.

**openFrameworks lineage:** [[Zachary Lieberman]] and Arturo Castro (oF co-creators) + Theo Watson (oF co-creator) all spoke at Eyeo. The Clinic for Open Source Arts (University of Denver) — a major oF-aligned institution — is a 2022 sponsor.

**School for Poetic Computation (SFPC):** Zach Lieberman co-founded SFPC in 2013 alongside Taeyoon Choi, Jen Lowe, and Amit Pitaru — all Eyeo speakers. The overlap is near-total.

**The mechanism:** Eyeo doesn't publish proceedings or have a formal academic structure. Instead it operates as a **network amplifier**: speakers introduce collaborators to audiences, workshops cross-pollinate communities, the Vimeo archive keeps talks circulating. The result is that watching the Eyeo archive (443+ videos) is essentially watching the creative coding world define itself in real time across a decade.

## Topics Covered (by era)

**2011–2013:** Creative coding, generative art, data visualization, physical computing, interactive installation. Raw enthusiasm phase.

**2014–2016:** Data ethics begins appearing. Activism + code. Code+Activism summit 2016. Surveillance, privacy. Social justice enters.

**2017–2019:** AI/ML critique intensifies. Climate. Accessibility and digital divide. Code+Resistance, Code+Libraries summits. Explicitly anti-hype about AI.

**2022 (return):** Code+Care summit — ethics of care in tech practice. AI storytelling. XR. Afrofuturism. Lakota epistemologies. Intersectional feminist tech.

## Program Format (2022 edition as reference)
- **Day 1 (Tuesday):** Pre-festival workshops at Walker Art Center (full-day, ~$329 + materials fee). Simultaneous options (e.g., Data Life Drawing, Interactive ML, Kinetic Sculpture). Code+ Summit at Minneapolis Central Library (un-conference, $10–40, pay-what-you-can). Kick-off keynote + ignite talks (evening, all-attendees).
- **Days 2–3 (Wed–Thu):** Main conference talks at Walker Art Center. ~10–12 speakers per day. Evening events at Minneapolis industrial venues (Machine Shop etc.).
- **Day 4 (Friday):** Final talks, closing keynotes, party.

## Submission / Speaking Process
**There is NO formal call for submissions.** From the official contact page:
> "We don't do a formal call for submissions for talks. Just send us a note and some links about yourself and your work and we'll check it out... We don't respond to inquiries from PR firms working to get speaking gigs for their clients."

**How to get invited:** Direct outreach to Dave Schroeder ([info@eyeofestival.com](mailto:info@eyeofestival.com)) with links to work. Curation is personal and relationship-based. Being in the creative coding community helps enormously — most speakers are already connected to previous Eyeo speakers.

**Workshops:** Similarly invite-only or via direct outreach. Workshop registration fees are ~$329 + materials.

**Scholarships / Fellowships:** Since 2012, Eyeo has offered fellowship tickets (free tickets) to practicing artists and nonprofit workers. "Full ride" diversity scholarships (travel + ticket) offered in 2018 and 2019. Apply via: [info@eyeofestival.com](mailto:info@eyeofestival.com)

**Student Volunteers:** Free attendance in exchange for volunteering. Application deadline historically early March. Application via Wufoo form (check website).

## Why Interesting
Eyeo is where the creative coding community becomes visible to itself. It's where open-source tool-builders, data artists, critical technologists, and speculative designers share a room and realize they're part of something. The festival is on uncertain footing post-2022, but its decade of archive is one of the most valuable records of how art-technology practice evolved through the 2010s — from techno-optimism to critical humanism. Understanding Eyeo is essential for understanding the community that Amber belongs to and benefits from. Even in hiatus, Eyeo's alumni network continues to shape festivals, schools, communities, and institutions across the art-tech world.
