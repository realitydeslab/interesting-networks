# Schinkel Pavillon

Berlin's most rigorous independent contemporary art space. A neoclassical pavilion at Oberwallstraße 1 (near Museum Island), named for Prussian architect Karl Friedrich Schinkel. Founded ~2012, it operates as a Kunstverein-adjacent programme in a two-floor structure: the upper floor for main exhibitions, the lower "Schinkel Klause" (former GDR restaurant) for more labyrinthine shows. No permanent collection — all programming, all the time. The institutional equivalent of a high-stakes journal: small, considered, high-signal.

Site of *Proof of Work* (2018) — the defining European exhibition for blockchain-as-art-critique, where [[terra0]]'s Premna Daemon debuted. Programme covers serious contemporary art (Louise Bourgeois, Philippe Parreno, Thomas Hirschhorn) alongside more speculative/tech-critical programming (Simon Denny, Christopher Kulendran Thomas, DIS). Not a tech venue — a serious art venue that occasionally goes there when the question warrants it.

## *Proof of Work* (2018) — Full Profile

**Dates:** September 8 – December 20/21, 2018
**Curated by:** [[Simon Denny]] (as artist-curator, with distributed selection method)
**Location:** Schinkel Klause (the labyrinthine lower floor — former GDR restaurant)

**Concept:** Explored the cultural explosion triggered by Bitcoin/blockchain since 2009 — alternative governance, peer-to-peer systems, DAOs, crypto as infrastructure for money/computing/organizing. Title from the Bitcoin consensus algorithm: Proof of Work is "a piece of data which is difficult (costly, time-consuming) to produce but easy for others to verify."

**Curatorial method:** Transparent/distributed — a series of creators-as-nodes were invited to propose positions, pushing back on singular curatorial authority. An organizational diagram was produced and exhibited alongside the show.

**Artists and works:**
- **terra0: Premna Daemon** — augmented Premna Microphylla bonsai tree operating as autonomous agent via smart contracts. Debut of this work. See [[terra0]].
- **Distributed Gallery: Chaos Machine**
- **FOAM: Tropical Mining Station** — geospatial blockchain protocol
- **0XΩ (Matt Liston + Avery Singer): Dogewhal**
- **Jonas Lund: Jonas Lund Token (JLT) 1041** — artist-as-token
- **Harm van den Dorpel: Lammer Asbestus / Wyn (Nested Exchange Series)** — generative blockchain art
- **Kei Kreutler: The Pareto Deck (5 of Forks, 2 of Swords)** — tarot meets governance
- **Nick Koppenhagen + Wesley Simon: Decentralized Autonomous Kunstverein** — DAO as art institution
- **Billy Rennekamp: Leather Spheres**
- **Aria Dean: BLACKBOX**
- **Rhea Myers: Secret Artwork (Subject)**
- **Wayne Lloyd: How We Won the War on Socialism**
- **Mark Lombardi** (historical drawings) — conspiracy mapping predating Bitcoin
- **Hayal Pozanti: 63 (average number of days it takes to break a bad habit)**
- **Miljohn Ruperto + Ulrik Heltoft: Voynich Botanical Studies**
- **CryptoKitties display** (Guile Twardowski)

**Context:** Berlin's crypto scene at the time: Crypto Circle (Slack group), Full Node co-working space (Kreuzberg). The show sat at the peak moment of the first crypto art wave, before NFTs, when the critical conversation was about DAOs, ecological smart contracts, distributed governance.

**Publication:** [Proof of Work (2018)](https://simondenny.net/proof-of-work-publication/)

**Legacy:** terra0's Premna Daemon went on to show at 58th Carnegie International, Venice Architecture Biennale (17th), The Shed NYC, Canadian Centre for Architecture, Kunsthalle Zürich. The show is retrospectively seen as the canonical European moment for blockchain art critique.

## Circuit & Connections

Schinkel Pavillon is on the **Berlin serious contemporary art circuit** — comparable in stature to KW Institute for Contemporary Art, Hamburger Bahnhof, Nationalgalerie. But operating independently, without state collection pressure. Its circuit overlaps with:
- **Kunsthalle Zürich** — similar programme, Swiss peer
- **ICA London** — comparable ambition/independence
- **Serpentine Galleries** — bigger budget, same international roster
- **MoMA PS1** — New York equivalent for daring programming
- **Galerie Buchholz** — frequently shares artists (Simon Denny, etc.)

Artists who show at Schinkel Pavillon also show at: Venice Biennale, Documenta, Whitney Biennial. It's not an alternative space — it's Tier 1 European contemporary art with editorial independence.

**Berlin nodes:** Same city as Transmediale, KW, HKW, ZKM (Karlsruhe nearby). For tech-critical art, Berlin is the central node in Europe.

## Why Interesting

Schinkel Pavillon is the proof-of-concept that serious contemporary art institutions can engage with blockchain, DAOs, machine agency, and ecological computation without losing artistic rigour. The *Proof of Work* show (2018) was not a tech showcase — it was a critical interrogation of an emerging infrastructure. That's exactly the mode Amber works in: not "AI for art" but AI as a site of philosophical and political investigation. The venue's consistent quality and its willingness to let a bonsai tree operate as an autonomous economic agent on the Ethereum blockchain — that's the right kind of serious.
