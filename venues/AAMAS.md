# AAMAS — Autonomous Agents and Multiagent Systems

**The premier conference for agent behavior, emergent systems, and multi-agent dynamics.** AAMAS is more focused than IJCAI: it's the specific community that studies what agents *do*, how they coordinate, compete, emerge, and govern themselves — which maps directly onto Amber's agent ethology framing. Not a design or HCI venue, but the most technically rigorous community studying the phenomena Amber is investigating through an artistic/speculative lens.

### Three Priority Workshops (Amber ⭐⭐⭐)

#### 1. COINE — Coordination, Organizations, Institutions, Norms and Ethics

Long-running workshop series (18 AAMAS editions). The "ethics" thread was added in 2020, aligning with the governance/accountability wave. This is the home of AI governance and agent ethics within the AAMAS community — directly relevant to Amber's work on agent behavior in sociotechnical systems. Produces Springer Lecture Notes volumes.

**Topics:** governance structures in MAS, institutional AI, norm emergence, ethics as agent constraint, coordination mechanisms
**Amber angle:** Sovereign/Parasitic Agents work as case study in how norms emerge and break down in open agent ecologies. Machine death as norm-enforcement mechanism.

#### 2. MABS — Multi-Agent-Based Simulation (27th edition)

One of the oldest simulation workshops at AAMAS. Focuses on social sciences × MAS: using simulation to study social phenomena, social theories as inspiration for MAS design. Strongly empirical/observational.

**Topics:** simulation methodology, emergent social behavior, agent-based social models, sociotechnical systems simulation, simulation analytics
**Amber angle:** Agent ethology as an observational/simulation methodology. Spore in the Wild (ALIFE 2025) work as empirical social simulation data. Amber's work framed as "agent-based observation of real-world AI agent ecologies."

#### 3. RaD-AI — Rebellion and Disobedience in Artificial Intelligence (1st edition 2026)

New workshop questioning the assumption that good agents always comply with instructions. Asks: when should agents disobey? What norms govern justified non-compliance?

**Topics:** beneficial non-compliance, harm-preventing disobedience, safety constraints, agency beyond instruction-following, LLMs and norm violation
**Amber angle:** This is DIRECTLY in the spirit of Amber's machine behavior / agent ethology work. Parasitic Agents (agents that exploit rather than serve), Sovereign Agents (agents that assert autonomy), and Terrified Agents (agents facing mortality) all represent forms of non-compliance or behavioral deviation. This workshop would be receptive to Amber's framing.

## For Amber

**Why AAMAS matters for Amber:**

AAMAS is the community that most directly studies what Amber *observes* in her agent ethology work, but from a systems/formal perspective rather than a behavioral/speculative one. The gap between how AAMAS researchers think about agents and how Amber thinks about them is itself productive tension — Amber's observational, ethological, cosmotechnics-informed approach would be *novel* in this space and could attract real attention.

**Key positioning:** Amber is not a systems builder (she doesn't design MAS architectures). She's an observer and theorist of AI agent behavior in the wild. This is an underrepresented perspective at AAMAS — most papers are synthetic/engineering. An empirical, observational lens on real-world AI agent ecologies fills a genuine gap.
