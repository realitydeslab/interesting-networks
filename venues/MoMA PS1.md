# MoMA PS1

Experimental contemporary art center in Queens, NYC (Long Island City, 22-25 Jackson Avenue). Affiliated with the Museum of Modern Art since 2000. Founded 1971 by Alanna Heiss as the Institute for Art and Urban Resources. The scrappier, riskier, more experimental sibling to MoMA — historically the place where artists working with time-based media, digital art, sound, performance, and speculative practices get serious institutional credibility. Free admission for New Yorkers starting January 2026 (three-year program, funded by $900K donation from Sonya Yu).

**Current Director:** Connie Butler (The Agnes Gund Director) — replaced Kate Fowle. Previously at LA's Hammer Museum.

## Mission & Identity

MoMA PS1 positions itself as "artist-centered and community-driven" — boundary-breaking ideas, risk-taking practices. Unlike MoMA itself, PS1 foregrounds emerging and experimental work, particularly:
- Digital innovation (generative AI, videogame engines, time-based media)
- Performance and live events
- Cross-disciplinary commissions
- Community-embedded practices (Queens focus, diaspora, labor)
- Collective and collaborative art-making

The **Homeroom** program is their ongoing teaching and learning initiative, running alongside exhibitions. The **Winter Talks** lecture series invites scholars/cultural workers on art, technology, economics.

## Programs & Events

### Recurring Programs

**Warm Up** (summer, outdoor courtyard, July–August)
- Annual summer music series, 27+ seasons. Electronic music, experimental, avant-garde.
- PS1's signature public-facing event. 2025 lineup: MikeQ, John Glacier, Sarz, Fred Moten & Brandon Lopez, DJ Stingray 313.
- Organized by curatorial team with host committee of NYC artists/musicians.

**Winter Talks** (winter lecture series)
- Distinguished speakers on cultural issues urgent for artists/scholars/cultural workers.
- Free with RSVP. 2025-26 season: Keeanga-Yamahtta Taylor (March 2026). Material in development that bridges art, culture, technology, economics.
- **Amber note:** Winter Talks format (material in development, experimental discourse) is very aligned — this is where scholars and artists push speculative ideas.

**Homeroom** (ongoing education program)
- Educational programming embedded in the museum, artist-led workshops.
- Homeroom: Love Rules (2025) is a recent iteration.

**Sunday Sessions** (performance series, ongoing)
- Ceremonies Out of the Air (2024-2025) — major performance series running Dec 2024–Mar 2025.

**Greater New York** (survey exhibition, every 5 years)
- Cross-borough survey of emerging/mid-career NYC artists. 6th edition opens April 16, 2026 — **53 artists**, celebrating PS1's 50th anniversary. Coincides with Whitney Biennial 2026.
- Themes: resistance/survival in face of surveillance technology, precarious economy.
- Focus: sculpture, installation, time-based media, contemporary painting.
- Note: Amber isn't NYC-based, so this isn't directly applicable — but attending/networking around this show in April 2026 would be **high value**.

### Artist-in-Residence / Fellow Programs

**Homeroom Fellow** — embedded curatorial/education fellowship (currently Jolene Fernandez). Invite-based, not publicly advertised.

**No formal open-call residency detected** — PS1 doesn't run a traditional open-call studio residency. Artist selection is curatorial-driven (Ruba Katrib's team commission/select artists through curatorial relationships). Path in is through curator relationships, not open applications.

**Greater New Yorkers** — patron program at $7,500/year. Gives studio visits, international art fair access, exclusive programming. Not a residency but provides insider access. **Invitation only.**

**Queens Collective** — $950/year patron program, more accessible. Open applications online. Gives Warm Up access, exhibition tours, behind-the-scenes moments.

### Young Architects Program (YAP)
Originally a prestigious annual commission for emerging architects — PS1 funded an outdoor installation each summer. **Status: effectively paused/discontinued.** The 2025 summer commission (stage design for Warm Up 2025) went to artist/musician Jeffrey Joyal — **the YAP format has been replaced by site-specific artist commissions** (no longer architect-focused, no longer called YAP). The Warm Up 2024 stage was designed by artist Stewart Uoo. No open call, no deadlines — purely curatorial selection.

## Thematic Appetite (What PS1 Programs)

Based on recent programming pattern:
1. **Digital/computational worldbuilding** (Ayoung Kim, Ian Cheng) — generative systems, AI-produced narratives, gamified realities
2. **Labor + technology + body** — gig economy, platform capitalism, optimization of human behavior
3. **Performance and time-based media** — live events, durational work, sonic/aural art
4. **Diaspora and global contemporary** — strong Asia/East Asia/Africa/Latin America focus
5. **Ecology and material excess** — waste, environmental surplus, planetary scale
6. **Sound and music** — Warm Up, Ceremonies Out of the Air
7. **Community-embedded practices** — Queens community, education, collective work

**What PS1 DOESN'T typically do:**
- Pure STEM/academic tech demos
- Commercial AR/VR without strong art conceptual framing
- Work without clear art-world positioning
