# Jameel Arts Centre

Dubai's leading contemporary arts institution — and one of the most important non-Western art ecosystems globally. Opened 2018 at Jaddaf Waterfront, backed by Art Jameel (the Jameel family's arts philanthropy arm). Not a state museum, not a commercial gallery — a genuinely independent institution with serious curatorial ambition, deep pockets, and global institutional connections (V&A, The Met, Delfina Foundation). The Art Jameel ecosystem now spans Dubai and Jeddah, runs international prizes, commissions, climate grants, and residencies. A key node in the Gulf Futurism universe and increasingly a site where post-colonial, more-than-human, and ecological art discourse gets produced.

## What It Is

Jameel Arts Centre is an independent contemporary arts institution opened in November 2018 at Jaddaf Waterfront, Dubai, UAE. Founded and supported by [Art Jameel](https://artjameel.org), the organisation established in 2003 as part of [Community Jameel](https://www.cjameel.org/), the Jameel family's philanthropic arm. The founding figure is Fady Jameel (President/Chairman, son of the late Abdul Latif Jameel). Art Director: Antonia Carver (long-standing director, former editor of Art Dubai magazine).

The Centre houses:
- **Multiple gallery spaces** (Gallery 9, Gallery 10, Project Space, Artist Garden)
- **[Jameel Library](https://jameelartscentre.org/learn/library/)** — open-access research library dedicated to artists and cultural movements in the Gulf and beyond
- **Cinema Akil** — Gulf's leading independent cinema, now housed at the Centre
- **Writer's Studio** — dedicated space for arts writing residency
- **Restaurant, shop, outdoor sculpture park** on the Jaddaf waterfront/creek

The Art Jameel Collection has works by 170+ artists and collectives, with a particular focus on West/South Asia and Africa. ~50% are women. Loans active to Venice Biennale, Gwangju Biennale, MoMA PS1, Louisiana Art Museum, Dhaka Art Summit, and more.

**Scale:** This is not a small institution. It has multi-year institutional partnerships with the V&A (London) and the Metropolitan Museum of Art (New York), and a co-directorship model with Delfina Foundation (London).

## Residency / Commission Programs

### Art Jameel Commissions
[https://jameelartscentre.org/programmes/art-jameel-commissions/](https://jameelartscentre.org/programmes/art-jameel-commissions/)

An annual multi-year commissioning programme. Each cycle focuses on a different medium/theme. Has run:
- **Sculpture** (2018)
- **Arts Writing and Research** (2019) — winner: Nadia Christidi (water crises, future scenarios)
- **Digital** (2020, theme: Time) — open call, ~200 submissions; winner: Nadim Choufi (sci-fi film on smart cities & ecological systems). Jury included Jenna Sutela (digital/organic media artist), Ben Vickers (Serpentine CTO). MIT J-WEL mentoring included.
- **Painting and Drawing** (2021 open call)
- Future cycles TBC — **check for new open calls at**: https://jameelartscentre.org/programmes/art-jameel-commissions/

**Status for Amber:** Art Jameel Commissions: Digital is the closest fit. It runs open calls with ~200 submissions and a rigorous jury. The 2020 call sought work questioning digital art, time, and technology through speculative/ecological lenses. Amber's HoloKit + agent ethology work is directly relevant. **Watch for the next digital/technology-themed cycle.**

### Research and Practice Platform
[https://artjameel.org/sustainability/art-jameel-research-and-practice-platform/](https://artjameel.org/sustainability/art-jameel-research-and-practice-platform/)

Small grants for artists and practitioners responding to climate emergency:
- 2020 edition: MENA-based practitioners, £1k-£3k micro-fees (96 allocations from 496 applications)
- 2023-24 edition: UAE-based artists and collectives, AED 3,600–22,000 (~$1,000–$6,000); climate-focused; in partnership with Dubai Culture and Al Quoz Creative Zone

**Status for Amber:** Primarily for MENA/UAE-based practitioners. Less relevant for Amber directly unless collaborating with a UAE artist.

### Anhar: Culture and Climate Platform
[https://artjameel.org/sustainability/anhar-culture-climate-platform/](https://artjameel.org/sustainability/anhar-culture-climate-platform/)

Designed by Art Jameel + British Council for the MENA region. Three tiers:
- **Tier 1:** £1,000–£5,000 to individual artists/collectives on climate projects
- **Tier 2:** £5,000–£10,000 to cultural organisations for decarbonisation
- **Tier 3 (KEY):** £25,000–£50,000 for **UK × MENA collaborations** — "at least a component taking place in a MENA country"

**Status for Amber:** Tier 3 is highly relevant! As an Oxford-based researcher, Amber qualifies as a UK entity and could apply for a UK-MENA collaboration grant. The British Council is involved — potentially accessible through Oxford's existing BC connections. An Anhar Tier 3 collaboration could fund a research-art project bridging Amber's work (agent ethology, more-than-human design) with a MENA artist or institution. **Actively investigate.**

### Delfina Foundation Residency (via partnership)
[https://www.delfinafoundation.com/](https://www.delfinafoundation.com/)

Delfina Foundation (London) is Art Jameel's residency expertise partner. Delfina runs 40+ international residencies/year. Themes include performance, food, public space, politics. ## Jameel Prize

[https://artjameel.org/projects/jameel-prize/](https://artjameel.org/projects/jameel-prize/)

The world's leading award for contemporary art and design **inspired by Islamic tradition**. Organised by the V&A, in partnership with Art Jameel. Prize: £25,000. Runs every 2-3 years.

**History of editions:**
| Edition | Theme | Winner | Year |
|---------|-------|--------|------|
| JP 7 | Moving Images (film, video, digital tech) | Khandakar Ohida (India) | 2024 |
| JP 6 | Poetry to Politics (design, typography, fashion) | Ajlan Gharem (Saudi Arabia) | 2021 |
| JP 5 | (no themed title) | Mehdi Moutashar (Iraq) & Marina Tabassum (Bangladesh) | ~2018 |
| JP 1–4 | Various | Various MENA/South Asian artists | 2009–2015 |

Over 7 editions: 1,700+ applications from 40+ countries; 56 artists exhibited; 18 venues worldwide (V&A → international tour → Hayy Jameel).

**JP7 Moving Images jury:** Morehshin Allahyari (Iranian digital artist), Ajlan Gharem (prev. winner), Sadia Shirazi (curator), Laura U. Marks (academic), Tristram Hunt (V&A director). Curated by Rachel Dedman (V&A's Jameel Curator).

**Is Amber eligible?** The Jameel Prize specifically celebrates work **inspired by Islamic tradition** in contemporary culture. Unless Amber's work engages directly with Islamic visual culture/design tradition, this is likely not the right fit — it requires that specific thematic alignment. However, the Digital edition's focus on "established and emerging digital technologies" opens the door wider. Worth monitoring. The JP7 open call (2023) sought artists using film, video, time-based media, and digital technologies — Amber's XR/AR work (HoloKit) might qualify if framed around cultural heritage or contemporary Islamic design discourse.
