# Haus der Kulturen der Welt (HKW)

Berlin's premier institution for contemporary art and culture from a global, non-Western perspective. "House of World Cultures." Government-funded cultural centre operating at the intersection of art, science, and politics — with an unusually rigorous intellectual programme combining exhibitions, residencies, research projects, and publications. One of the most intellectually serious art institutions in Europe, with a programme philosophy based on "worlds" as plural, processual, and malleable: "The world is not a noun, but a verb: to unworld, to world, and to reworld." Funded by the Federal Government Commissioner for Culture (Beauftragter der Bundesregierung für Kultur und Medien).

## Key People

### Current Leadership
- **[[Bonaventure Soh Bejeng Ndikung]]** ⭐⭐ — Artistic Director since January 2023. Born 1977 in Yaoundé, Cameroon. Remarkable background: PhD in medical biotechnology (Heinrich-Heine-Universität Düsseldorf / TU Berlin), postdoctorate in biophysics (University of Montpellier) — then pivoted to curatorial practice. Founded [SAVVY Contemporary](https://savvy-contemporary.com/), a Berlin project space on the threshold of Western and non-Western art. Curated documenta 14. Programme vision under his leadership emphasises plurality, migrant-situated knowledges, non-Western epistemologies, and the "Tout Moun Se Moun" (Haitian Revolution) maxim of radical human equality — extended to non-human beings.

### Former Leadership (key for historical context)
- **[[Anselm Franke]]** ⭐⭐ — Head of Visual Art and Film, 2013–**2022** (now departed). Curated Animism (2010–2014), Forensis (2014), Whole Earth (2013), Parapolitics (2018), Love and Ethnology (2017–2020). His decade at HKW produced the most directly relevant programmes for Amber's interests. Now working independently. Based in Berlin.
- **Bernd Scherer** — General Director, 2006–2019. The intellectual architect of HKW's interdisciplinary turn. His tenure produced the Anthropocene Project and many landmark programmes.

### Regular Artists/Researchers
- **aaajiao (Xu Wenkai)** ⭐⭐ — Chinese net-art/digital artist. Showed in Global Fascisms (2025). Key figure in Chinese digital art. Worth noting.
- **Shu Lea Cheang** — veteran cyberfeminist, digital media artist. Showed in Global Fascisms.
- **Mimi Ọnụọha** — US-based artist working on data, race, and absence. Showed in Global Fascisms.
- **George E. Lewis** — Columbia University composer/professor. Co-curated Afrodiaspora – Composing While Black (2025/26). Pioneer in algorithmic/AI composition.
- **[Forensic Architecture](https://forensic-architecture.org/)** (Eyal Weizman) — co-produced Forensis exhibition (2014). Core of the investigative aesthetics milieu.
- **Slavs and Tatars** — art collective exploring Eastern European / Central Asian / Islamic intersection. Showed in Tirailleurs (2026).

## Historical Landmark Shows (Highly Relevant to Amber)

These programmes were produced primarily under Bernd Scherer (2006–2019) and Anselm Franke (2013–2022) — HKW's golden era for Amber's direct interests:

- **[Forensis: The Architecture of Public Truth](https://www.hkw.de/en/programme/project/forensis2014.php)** (2014) — Co-produced with Forensic Architecture / Eyal Weizman. Landmark show legitimising investigative, evidence-based aesthetics as art practice. Published as *Forensis* (Sternberg Press, 2014).
- **[The Whole Earth: California and the Disappearance of the Outside](https://www.hkw.de/en/programme/projekte/2013/whole_earth/whole_earth_start.php)** (2013) — Examined early digital utopianism, cybernetics, Stewart Brand's ecosystem — cyberculture as ideology. Edited volume with Diedrich Diederichsen.
- **[Animism](https://www.hkw.de/en/programme/projekte/2012/animism/animism_start.php)** (2010–2014, toured internationally) — Anselm Franke's foundational exhibition on animism as counter to nature/culture divide. Connecting to more-than-human, non-Western epistemologies, and the agency of things. Toured Antwerp, Bern, Vienna, Berlin, Beirut, Seoul, Shanghai. **Directly relevant to Amber's more-than-human + cosmotechnics interests.**
- **[The Anthropocene Project](https://www.hkw.de/en/programme/projekte/bilder/2014/anthropocene_2013_2014/anthropocene_start.php)** (2013–2014) — Major multi-year project on Earth systems, non-human agency, human ecological impact. Co-produced with multiple institutions. **Direct connection to more-than-human and agent-world relations.**
- **[Parapolitics: Cultural Freedom and the Cold War](https://www.hkw.de/en/programme/projekte/2018/parapolitics/parapolitics_start.php)** (2018) — On CIA-funded Cold War cultural infrastructure. Political history of Western cultural institutions.
- **[Love and Ethnology. The Colonial Dialectics of Sensitivity](https://www.hkw.de/en/programme/projekte/2017/love_and_ethnology/love_and_ethnology_start.php)** (2017–2020) — Multi-chapter international project (Lisbon, Dakar, NYC etc.) with Goethe-Institut. On Hubert Fichte, ethnology, colonial imagination.

## Why Interesting

HKW operates at the intellectual register Amber is aiming for — rigorous, politically engaged, globally situated, uncomfortable with disciplinary boundaries. The Animism exhibition (Franke) is arguably the most important art-world framing of the animist/more-than-human turn. The Anthropocene Project positioned HKW as a site for art-science encounter. Even under the current pivot toward decolonial themes, the institutional DNA is exactly right. Additionally, current director Ndikung's background — biotech PhD + biophysicist turned decolonial curator — is itself a remarkable case study of how science and art can be in genuine dialogue.
