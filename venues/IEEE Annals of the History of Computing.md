# IEEE Annals of the History of Computing

The flagship peer-reviewed journal for the history of computing. Published by IEEE Computer Society. Covers hardware, software, systems, organizations, people, and social context of computing's history from Babbage to the present.

Notable for bridging engineering/technical history with social and cultural history of computing — increasingly open to STS and critical approaches.

## Key People

- [[Stephanie Dick]] — editorial board member; 2018 Best Paper Award (with Daniel Volmar, "DLL Hell")

## Topics

- History of computing hardware, software, systems
- Institutional histories (Bell Labs, IBM, PARC, etc.)
- Labor, gender, race in computing history
- Software maintenance and infrastructure
- AI and programming language history

## Why Interesting

The journal where computing historians publish. Also where critical HoC work (feminist, STS, labor-oriented) increasingly appears. Stephanie Dick and colleagues are pushing it toward more politically aware history of computing.
