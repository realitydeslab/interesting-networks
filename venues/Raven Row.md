# Raven Row

Artist-run exhibition space in London's Shoreditch, founded and directed by Alex Sainsbury. One of the most important venues for artists' film screening and expanded cinema events in the UK. Known for rigorous, scholarly programming of historical avant-garde film and expanded cinema alongside contemporary practice. Operates without a fixed collection — each project is commissioned or produced specifically for the space. Receives Arts Council England and other funding.

## Key Events
- **Filmaktion** (4–5 March 2017) — Malcolm Le Grice, Gill Eatherley, and William Raban reconvened the Filmaktion group; staged *Horror Film 2* publicly for the first time since 1973; curated by Mark Webber; in the context of *This Way Out of England: Gallery House in Retrospect* (10 Feb – 26 Mar 2017).

## Key Curators
- **Alex Sainsbury** — founder/director
- **Mark Webber** — guest curator; specialises in expanded cinema and artists' film

## Why Interesting
Raven Row represents a specific institutional model: a privately-funded, non-commercial exhibition space that programs with academic rigour. Their expanded cinema and artists' film programming (including Filmaktion 2017) makes them one of the key venues for understanding the historical legacy of the LFMC and structural-materialist film in contemporary context.
