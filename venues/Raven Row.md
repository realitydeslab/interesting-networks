# Raven Row

One of London's most rigorous artist-run exhibition spaces, entirely privately funded. Occupies two Grade I– and Grade II–listed 18th-century Huguenot silk merchant's houses at 56 & 58 Artillery Lane, Spitalfields (near Liverpool Street). Founded 2009 by Alex Sainsbury — independent collector, curator, and Sainsbury family heir — who set up a charity to run it. Converted by [6a Architects](https://www.6a.co.uk/) into a contemporary gallery behind the preserved historic façades. Free admission always. No permanent collection. Every project is commissioned or produced specifically for the space.

Raven Row occupies a unique position in London's art ecology: it has the financial independence of private philanthropy but the curatorial ambition of a public institution. As Sainsbury puts it: *"We can take risks as there is not the relentlessness you find elsewhere."* This means long-haul scholarly projects, archival digs, expanded cinema restaging events, and niche historical revivals that major institutions can't easily accommodate.

## Institutional Model

- **Funding:** Private philanthropy (Alex Sainsbury personally funds; no reliance on Arts Council or box-office pressure)
- **Legal form:** Registered charity
- **Collection:** None — each show is purpose-made
- **Admission:** Always free
- **Programme pace:** 3–4 major exhibitions per year; occasional screenings and events
- **Curatorial model:** Mix of Sainsbury-curated shows and invited guest curators
- **Building lease:** Long-term (at least 20 years confirmed)
- **Intermission model:** Building was lent to non-profit groups during 2017–2023 hiatus (Piper Keys, Asia-Art-Activism Research Network, London Renters Union, East London Cable)

## Key People
- **Alex Sainsbury** — Founder, director, primary curator and funder. Previously founded Peer (Hoxton, 1998) and 38 Langham Street project space (2001–2003). Has a long record of supporting structural-materialist and conceptual practices.
- **Mark Webber** — Guest curator; specialist in expanded cinema and artists' film; curated the Filmaktion 2017 event and other Raven Row exhibitions.
- **Richard Grayson** — Guest curator (curated *Polytechnic*)

### 2017: Final Show Before Hiatus
- **This Way Out of England: Gallery House in Retrospect** (10 Feb – 26 Mar 2017) — Survey of the famous Gallery House (Cromwell Road, London, 1971–72), a pivotal institution of the British avant-garde. Within this show:
  - **Filmaktion** (4–5 March 2017) — Curated by Mark Webber. Malcolm Le Grice, Gill Eatherley, and William Raban reconvened Filmaktion (the structural film performance group). They staged *Horror Film 2* publicly for the first time since 1973. **Directly relevant to Amber — this is the living LFMC lineage in action.**

### Hiatus 2017–2022
- Space lent to community organisations; Sainsbury confirmed "there was always going to be an intermission"
- Planned to reopen 2020; delayed (likely COVID)

### Reopened January 2023
- **People Make Television** (28 Jan – 26 Mar 2023) — Curated by Alex Sainsbury. More than 100 editions of the BBC2 *Open Door* programme (1973–1980s), which gave members of the public brief control over broadcast. Drew from the BBC Community Programme Unit archive. Grassroots television as expanded media practice, community media history.
- **June Givanni Pan African Cinema Archive** (April 2023) — Exhibition drawn from the collection of Guyanese-born curator June Givanni; archive of Pan-African and African diaspora cinema.
- **Christine Kozlov** (date to confirm from site, ~2023–2024) — Nearly complete retrospective of Kozlov's brief but radical Conceptual Art output; reflections on her collaborations with The Red Krayola, Art & Language, Joan Jonas, Robert Rauschenberg.

## Venue Circuit & Connections
Raven Row sits in the network of:
- **[LUX](https://lux.org.uk/)** — distribution/exhibition body for artists' film in the UK; overlapping audiences and artists
- **[BFI](https://www.bfi.org.uk/)** — British Film Institute; occasional co-presentations
- **Tate Modern** (Tanks) — for expanded cinema events; Raven Row's programme is complementary/specialist
- **Mark Webber** → bridges Raven Row to LUX, LFMC legacy, international expanded cinema circuit
- **Arts Council England** — Raven Row is independent of ACE funding but embedded in the same scene

## No Open Calls / Residencies
Raven Row does **not** operate open calls for artists. Exhibition proposals are initiated by Sainsbury or invited guest curators. There is no public residency programme. Engagement path: build relationship through the scene (screenings, expanded cinema network, Mark Webber connection).

## Why Interesting for Amber

**Raven Row is the institutional embodiment of the Malcolm Le Grice lineage in London.** The Filmaktion 2017 event — restaging *Horror Film 2* (1973) for the first time since it was made — demonstrates exactly what Amber is researching: expanded cinema as live, durational, machine-involved performance. This is not passive film screening; it's apparatus art.

Key connections to Amber's work:

1. **Malcolm Le Grice direct link** — Filmaktion 2017 at Raven Row is the primary documented instance of the Le Grice circle reconvening. Raven Row is the contemporary institutional home of this practice.

2. **Structural film as cosmotechnics** — Raven Row's taste for structural-materialist practices (Le Grice, Rainer, Farocki) maps onto Amber's interest in how machines, media, and bodies configure perception. Farocki's *Harun Farocki* body of work on surveillance/soft cinema especially relevant.

3. **Expanded cinema** — Amber's interest in the Le Grice lineage and expanded cinema finds its contemporary UK anchor here. Raven Row is the space where restaging, reconstruction, and live cinema events happen.

4. **Artist-run infrastructure model** — Raven Row's private philanthropy model (no ACE dependency, no commercial pressure) is a reference model for how Amber might think about the Reality Design Lab's institutional independence.

5. **Mark Webber** — Guest curator at Raven Row for expanded cinema. He is a key person in the UK expanded cinema network; a bridge to Raven Row for Amber.

6. **Yvonne Rainer / Stephen Willats** — Rainer's body/performance/film intersection and Willats's social cybernetics both speak to Amber's more-than-human design and participatory systems concerns.
