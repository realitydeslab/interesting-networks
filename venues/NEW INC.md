# NEW INC

The world's first museum-led cultural incubator. Founded 2014 at the New Museum (NYC) by director Lisa Phillips and deputy director Karen Wong. Now in Year 12, NEW INC supports ~120 creative entrepreneurs annually through professional development, studio access, mentorship, and community. Alumni network of 730+. Members have collectively raised $28.9M and created 686 jobs. Not just a residency — a one-year professional development program at the edge of art, design, science, and tech.

## Topics & Tracks (Year 13)

Five tracks, each with a dedicated Mentor-in-Residence:

### 🎨 Art & Code *(partnership with [Rhizome](https://rhizome.org))*
Computational artmaking, digital preservation, born-digital art. Artists, creative technologists, game designers, sound artists, interaction designers. Focus on artistic practice. Rhizome mentorship opportunities.
**Amber fit: HIGH** — practice-based research with HoloKit, agent ethology as artistic research.

### 🔬 Creative Science
Scientific inquiry in creative practice: speculative design, hybrid ecologies, biotechnology, robotics, food systems, aquaculture, renewable energy. Artists, researchers, food scientists, healers, technologists.
**Amber fit: HIGH** — speculative design, more-than-human, ALife, interspecies design all map directly.

### 🥽 Extended Realities *(partnership with [Onassis ONX](https://www.onx.studio))*
XR, VR, MR, AR, metaverse, immersive sound, generative systems. Priority access to Onassis ONX production studio (390 Broadway). XR producers, choreographers, sound artists, game designers, motion capture, digital worldbuilders.
**Amber fit: VERY HIGH** — HoloKit is literally an open-source MR headset. This is Amber's home territory.

### 🚀 Launchpad
Product-to-market: startups, founders, creatives launching products. Less relevant for Amber's research practice.

### 🏛️ Social Architecture
Design and architecture for community-centered public space. Architects, land artists, urban planners, community organizers. Moderate relevance.

- **Professional development:** Business modeling, fundraising, legal/IP, strategic planning, leadership
- **Mentorship:** Dedicated mentor (one-on-one monthly), mentor matchmaking process, office hours with industry experts
- **Network:** 730+ alumni, 130+ mentors annually
- **DEMO festival:** Annual public festival where members present work — a major platform
- **Onassis ONX access:** (Extended Realities track) Priority access to XR production studio

**Cost:** $75/month × 12 = $900/year + $150 one-time setup fee. Subsidized by institutional support.

## Eligibility Notes

- **International OK:** Does not require NYC residency. Hybrid format — many offerings 10am–6pm ET.
- **PhD students:** ⚠️ "Membership is not open to students or people enrolled in undergraduate or graduate programs. **However, those completing dissertation work as part of a PhD program may be considered.**"

- **Teams allowed:** Up to 4 people; each team member must pay separately.
- **Time commitment:** 4–8 hours/week, Mon–Fri 10am–6pm ET. Significant for someone in Oxford.
