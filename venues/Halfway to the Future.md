# Halfway to the Future (HTTF)

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Initial deep research. Comprehensive note created from two-edition history (2019, 2024).
- **2024 edition:** Oct 21-23, 2024 at Dream Inn, Santa Cruz, CA. Featured Donna Haraway in opening fireside chat with Fred Turner. Amber's paper "EverForest" presented.
- **2019 edition:** Nov 19-20, 2019 at Albert Hall Conference Centre, Nottingham, UK. Founded by Mixed Reality Lab (20th anniversary).

## Vibe

Intimate, reflective, historically-minded. HTTF is the HCI community's "long conversation with itself" — a space to look back at landmark publications and ask: what did we get right, what did we miss, where are we going? Not a progress showcase; more like a seminar where the discipline takes stock. Single-track, strongly themed, structured around core panelists who re-read their own seminal texts. Small enough (~150-200 attendees) that ideas actually circulate — the roundtable format is real, not performative.

The 2024 edition had a distinctly California-humanist feel: opening night at UCSC's Haybarn, Donna Haraway in conversation with Fred Turner tracing the arc from feminist technoculture to multispecies thinking. Situated in Santa Cruz — the counterculture-adjacent UC campus — was intentional. Stuart Reeves (Nottingham) and Katherine Isbister (UCSC) as general chairs bridging the UK-US HCI tradition.

## Format

Each edition is organized around **3-4 thematic panels**. Each panel:
1. **Core voice talk** (30 min) — a prominent figure reflects on one of their own landmark publications
2. **Paper presentations** (short, 5-10 min each) — peer-reviewed papers responding to the theme
3. **Roundtable discussion** — moderated conversation between core panelist and paper authors

Plus: **Interactive forums** (poster + demo session with lightning talks), **Synthesis conversation** at end.

**Publications:** All accepted papers published in ACM Digital Library as part of ACM International Conference Proceedings Series (ICPS). Separate tracks for full papers (up to 6,000 words) and short papers (up to 2,000 words). Also accepts pictorials (up to 8 pages full / 4 pages short). Peer-reviewed double-blind. Papers not selected for panels → poster format.

**Submission:** Via EasyChair. ACM formatting guidelines (LaTeX/Word/Overleaf for papers; InDesign template for pictorials). Anonymization policy follows ACM CHI anonymization guidelines.

## Relationship to HCI Community

HTTF positions itself as a **reflective practice venue** within ACM HCI, distinct from CHI (which is massive, submission-hungry, trend-chasing). HTTF is:
- **Deliberately small** — intimacy enables real intellectual community
- **Historically anchored** — each edition re-reads the field's foundational texts
- **Single-track** — everyone sees everything, no parallel sessions
- **Critical rather than celebratory** — the format forces confrontation with what landmark papers got wrong or missed

**Relationship to ACM CHI:** HTTF proceedings are published in ACM ICPS (the lower-tier ACM proceedings series, separate from CHI TOCHI). However, the *people* at HTTF are the same senior figures who keynote CHI and publish in TOCHI/CSCW. It's less about citations, more about intellectual positioning.

**Relationship to ACM DIS:** [DIS (Designing Interactive Systems)](../venues/ACM DIS.md) is the more design-led venue in the same orbit. There is significant community overlap. HTTF is more reflective/historical; DIS is more empirical/design-practice.

**Who attends:** Senior HCI/design researchers, PhD students from the right labs, international contingent (particularly UK-US-Scandinavia axis). Not industry-heavy. Conceptual rigor expected.
