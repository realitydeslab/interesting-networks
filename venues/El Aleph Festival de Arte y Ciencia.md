# El Aleph Festival de Arte y Ciencia

The most intellectually serious annual **art + science + technology** festival in Latin America — co-produced by **UNAM** (via [[Cátedra Max Aub]]) and held across [[MUAC]] and other UNAM cultural spaces in Mexico City. Named after Borges' "El Aleph" — the point in space that contains all other points — a perfect frame for a festival that refuses disciplinary closure. Not a tech showcase. A critical, transdisciplinary, decolonially-oriented cultural event with genuine international reach. **2026 edition: May 8–17 — an imminent and actionable speaking opportunity for Amber.**

## What

El Aleph Festival de Arte y Ciencia is an **annual multi-day festival** produced by the **[[Cátedra Max Aub]], Transdisciplina en Arte y Tecnología** at UNAM (Universidad Nacional Autónoma de México) — Latin America's largest and most prestigious public university. It runs across UNAM cultural venues in Mexico City, principally [[MUAC]] (Museo Universitario Arte Contemporáneo), the Centro Cultural Universitario (CCU), and Ciudad Universitaria — itself a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

**The Borges reference is intentional and instructive.** In "El Aleph" (1945), Borges describes a point beneath a staircase in Buenos Aires that contains every point in the universe simultaneously. The festival's name frames it as a site of total convergence — science, art, technology, philosophy, ecology, politics — without hierarchy between disciplines. This is not interdisciplinary in the polite, marketing sense. It is genuinely transdisciplinary in the tradition of **Max Aub himself** (the namesake of the Cátedra): the Spanish-Mexican Republican exile who wrote fake biographies of fictional Cubist painters, co-organized Guernica's placement at the 1937 International Exposition, and worked with Luis Buñuel — someone for whom fiction, political commitment, and artistic experimentation were inseparable.

**The Latin American difference:** El Aleph operates from a position that the dominant art-technology festivals (Ars Electronica, transmediale, CTM, ISEA) structurally cannot — it speaks from the Global South, from a university whose mandate is public service, and from a tradition that explicitly names **data colonialism**, **indigenous knowledge systems**, and **non-Western technology theory** as core concerns. Where Ars Electronica celebrates technological innovation, El Aleph asks who is harmed by it and whose knowledge it erases.

The festival format typically includes: **performances, installations, symposia, workshops, films** — spread over 10+ days. It convenes Latin American artists and researchers alongside international guests, producing a genuinely cross-circuit encounter that European art-technology festivals rarely achieve.

The producing organization — [[Cátedra Max Aub]] — also generates publications from each festival edition. The 2024 "Museo digital" forum was published as a free book downloadable from MUAC/UNAM.

## Past Editions

### 2024: "Museo digital. Inteligencias y artificios"
**Forum dates:** March 6–7, 2024. Venue: MUAC Auditorium.
**Co-organizers:** Cátedra Max Aub + [[MUAC]] + Fundación Telefónica Movistar México.

The title pun is deliberate: *artificios* = "artifices" = "tricks/deceptions." A critique of AI is baked into the title.

**Program:**
- **Sara Diamond** (President Emerita, OCAD University, Toronto) — *"Reformar: construyendo alianzas para un conocimiento inclusivo en el siglo XXI"* (Building alliances for inclusive knowledge)
- **Ulises A. Mejias** (SUNY Oswego; co-author of *Data Colonialism*, ~1,670 citations) — *"Respuestas culturales al colonialismo de datos"* (Cultural responses to data colonialism)
- **Panel: "Tecnologías del futuro: memoria, lenguas y conocimientos milenarios en tiempos de la inteligencia artificial"** — moderator: Guillermo García Pérez (MUAC); panelists: Roberto Zariquiey (linguistics, PUCP Lima), Fernanda Pitta (MAC São Paulo), Federico Pérez Villoro (artist-programmer, Mexico City)
- **Case Study: UsuLe — Museo del Oro Precolombino, San José** — Priscilla Molina (Universidad de Costa Rica)
- Presented by Amanda de la Garza (DGAV) and José-Carlos Mariátegui
- Moderated by Tania Aedo (Cátedra Max Aub) and Julio García Murillo (MUAC)

**Published output:** Free book downloadable via MUAC website. Ulises Mejias contributed the chapter "The Museum as a Cultural Response to Data Colonialism."

**Videos:** Available on [MUAC YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/user/muacunam).

Known themes from prior years include: ecology and technology, body-machine relations, social dimensions of computational systems, speculative computation. The festival has run annually for multiple years under the Cátedra Max Aub framework.

**[[Transitio MX]]** (the biennial new media art festival at Centro Multimedia, Mexico City) is a related but distinct circuit — El Aleph is UNAM-based and more academic/symposium-oriented; Transitio MX is Centro Multimedia-based and more festival/exhibition-oriented. They are complementary nodes of the Mexico City art-technology scene.
