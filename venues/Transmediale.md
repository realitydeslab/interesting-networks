Annual festival for art and digital culture in Berlin. Europe's most theoretically rigorous critical media art platform — not a technology showcase but a space for critique. Where critical theory meets digital culture. Held end of January / beginning of February, typically 5 days. Primary venue: [Haus der Kulturen der Welt (HKW)](https://www.hkw.de/) + [silent green Kulturquartier](https://www.silent-green.net/).

## Fellowships & Programmes

### Residency Programs (Apply July–August Each Year)

1. **[Vilém Flusser Residency for Artistic Research](https://transmediale.de/en/news/open-calls-for-residents-2026)**  
   In cooperation with [Universität der Künste Berlin (UdK)](https://www.udk-berlin.de/)  
   Research-based artistic practice; access to Vilém Flusser Archive. Most prestigious route. 
   → **Amber's strongest entry point.**

2. **[transmediale × ARKO Residency](https://transmediale.de/en/news/transmediale-x-arko-residency-2026)**  
   In cooperation with [Arts Council Korea (ARKO)](https://www.arko.or.kr/eng/)  
   For Korean artists or international artists engaging with Korean context.

3. **[Digital Art Residency](https://transmediale.de/en/news/digital-art-residency-2026)**  
   In cooperation with [Pro Helvetia](https://prohelvetia.ch/en/) (Swiss Arts Council).  
   For Swiss-connected artists or digital art practice.

**Residency timeline:** Applications open July, deadline August, residents announced October, active spring–summer, open studio in June, work shown at following festival.

### Vorspiel (Pre-Festival Distributed Programme)
Open call for Berlin-area spaces/artists to run events across city venues (galleries, project spaces). Joint with [[CTM Festival]]. **Lowest barrier entry point.** Application typically September, deadline ~November.

### DARC Research Workshop
Annual academic workshop, partnership with [Digital Aesthetics Research Center (DARC), Aarhus University](https://cc.au.dk/en/research/centers/digital-aesthetics-research-centre/). PhD/researcher track. Published output.

### transmediale/journal
[Online publication](https://transmediale.de/en/journal) — critical writing on themes. Issue #7 published Jan 2025. **Pitch writing here** for intellectual presence.

### Marshall McLuhan Lecture
Annual prestigious public lecture. Registration required. Partnership event at festival.

### 💡 Why the Flusser Connection Matters for Amber
Flusser's argument: cameras (and all technical images) program operators to behave in ways that realize the apparatus's potential. This is a 1983 proto-agent-ethology argument. Machines have behavior; humans adapt to it. The transmediale/UdK partnership literally maintains the archive of this idea — being in this circle is being in the intellectual genealogy of Amber's own research.

## Why Interesting

**Historically formative:** Since 1988, transmediale has been Berlin's home for critical digital culture. The 2006 shift from "media art festival" to "festival for art and digital culture" shows institutional intelligence — they foresaw that digital would become the condition of contemporary life, not just an artistic medium.

**Theoretically aligned:** Flusser's *technical images* → apparatus theory → machine behavior. The Vilém Flusser Archive at UdK Berlin is the archive of ideas Amber's research is deeply connected to. The festival celebrates Flusser's legacy by taking seriously the question: what does technology do to humans? What do humans do to technology? This is exactly Amber's research question from a different angle.

**Currently evolving:** The rotating curatorial model keeps transmediale from calcifying. 2026's Global South turn (Githere + García Sossa) and 2027's open call model signal continued theoretical evolution. Not a static institution — churning with intellectual energy.

**Strategic value:** European credibility. Being a transmediale artist/researcher signals: theoretically serious, politically engaged, internationally connected. For Amber building Oxford-based credibility while remaining globally connected, this is the right European anchor.
