# Fusebox Festival

Austin, TX's flagship interdisciplinary arts festival — a city-wide celebration of live performance, installation, ecological art, and experimental work. Founded 2005 by artists; now operates year-round. **Pivoting to biennial** from 2026 onward. Has explicit Art+Tech programming thread and a meaningful more-than-human/ecological axis. [[Jiabao Li]] and [[Ecocentric Future Lab]] had a landmark 2024 presence here. **Strong signal for Amber.**

## Institutional Profile

**Founded:** 2005 by a group of Austin-based artists who wanted to create robust exchange across art forms and geography, with a focus on live experience and festival-as-place-making.

**Status:** Non-profit arts organization. Address: 2008 Alexander Ave, Austin, TX 78722.

**Scale:** 1,200+ projects, 4,000+ artists supported over 22 years.

**Frequency change:** After 20+ years as an annual festival, Fusebox transitioned to **biennial** format in 2026 — described as "a concentrated burst of art, ideas, and connection."

**Year-round programmes:**
- **Fusebox Presents** — ongoing performance series, partnership with Texas Performing Arts
- **Fusebox Salon Series** — monthly artist salons (~8/year, September–May), member-access
- **Art+Tech Micro-Fest** — dedicated art × technology programming strand (details TBD; appeared in nav)
- **Live in America** — appears to be a touring/international exchange program
- **Local Artist Submission** — open call mechanism for Austin-based artists

**Mission:** "Fusebox collaborates with artists and community groups to share unforgettable live performances, exhibitions, and experiences." Values: gathering, imagination, plurality of perspectives, reciprocal exchange, joy, access, supporting artists.

## Programming Model

Fusebox presents work across: **visual art, film, theater, dance, music, writing, design, and food**. The programming is explicitly described as "genre-defying" and "interdisciplinary." Notable characteristics:

- **Site-specific** — projects in parking garages, baseball fields, building walls, empty lots, bat colonies, rooftops, bars, galleries
- **Live experience as core value** — "the act of gathering and using our imaginations together"
- **Commissioning** — festival actively commissions and premieres new works; pays artists
- **Scale-range** — intimate salons to MacArthur Fellows, Grammy winners, HBO creators
- **Cross-sector** — brings artists, community groups, businesses, civic groups together

## The Contemporary Austin Partnership (HOST, 2024)

**HOST: Fusebox** (April 2024) was a landmark co-production celebrating Fusebox's 20th anniversary. Curated by Robin K. Williams (The Contemporary Austin) and Ron Berry (Fusebox).

The exhibition was structured as a "Fusebox record store" — visitors browse shelves of "records" representing past performances, with screened excerpts and audio interviews via listening stations. A playful archival format for ephemeral performance history.

Featured 20 Fusebox alumni including:
- **[[Jiabao Li]]** (Austin, TX) — bat colony / EchoVision project (see below)
- Action Hero (Bristol, UK)
- Dickie Beau (London, UK)
- Tania El Khoury (NY)
- Manuela Infante (Santiago, Chile)

- Tetsuya Umeda (Osaka)
- Lagartijas Tiradas al Sol (Mexico City)
- Jaamil Olawale Kosoko (Detroit)

**Partnership model:** The Contemporary Austin provides gallery space (Jones Center) + curatorial expertise + museum audience; Fusebox provides artist network + performance curation + programming.

## Jiabao Li / Ecocentric Future Lab — 2024 "Host" Program

[[Jiabao Li]] (UT Austin, Ecocentric Future Lab) created a site-specific ecological performance event within the HOST: Fusebox framework:

- **Location:** Austin's Congress Avenue Bridge (home to 1.5 million Mexican free-tailed bats — the world's largest urban bat colony)
- **Collaborator:** Merlin Tuttle, bat expert
- **Performance:** *Nocturnal Fugue* — live performance at the bridge
- **Exhibition:** *EchoVision* — shown at The Contemporary Austin / Jones Center
- **Scale:** ~500 attendees
- **Concept:** Art-science encounter activating a real ecological phenomenon (the bats) as artistic material; the bridge-to-museum arc moved audiences from raw nature to institutional reflection

**What this signals:** Fusebox actively enables this kind of multispecies, site-based, ecology-driven art that uses scientific partners and public space. This is not incidental — it reflects Fusebox's core interest in "how festivals can live in more meaningful relationship to place."

- **International performance art circuit** — Verdensteatret (Oslo), Tania El Khoury, Action Hero (Bristol), Manuela Infante (Chile), Samson Young (HK) all appeared
- **US experimental performance circuit** — MacArthur Fellows, Robin Frohardt (Herb Alpert Award), Rude Mechs
- **Art × ecology circuit** — Jiabao Li's bat colony piece; Dirty Projectors "Song of the Earth"; Robin Frohardt's parking lot ecosystem
- **Art × tech** — explicit "Art+Tech Micro-Fest" strand; Jiabao Li (Apple, AR/VR, wearables, robotics)
- **Live cinema / performance installation** — Robin Frohardt, Jim Findlay, others

**Prestige level:** High for experimental US performance; mid-tier international (not Venice Biennale, but genuinely respected in experimental circuits). MacArthur Fellows + Herb Alpert winners = institutional credibility.

**Artist profile:** Multi-disciplinary, internationally-networked, with practice-based research sensibility. Not purely gallery art, not purely theater — lives in the in-between.
