# Ljubljana Biennale of Graphic Arts

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-03-04:** Deep research completed. 36th edition (2025, "The Oracle") has surprisingly strong resonance with Amber's interests: autonomous beings, puppet-as-AI, collective intelligence, oracle as speculative practice. MGLC runs annual Critic-in-Residence open call — potential direct engagement path.
- **2026-02-26:** Stub created during [[Bogna Konior]] deep research.

One of the world's oldest biennales (founded 1955, Ljubljana, Slovenia), organized by MGLC (International Centre of Graphic Arts). Originally a printmaking competition, now a full contemporary art biennale with major commissions, multi-site exhibitions, and internationally recognized curatorial programmes. Archive: [bienale.si](https://bienale.si). Works primarily by invitation — no open submission for the main exhibition. Deeply embedded in the Slovenian art-tech ecosystem alongside [[Aksioma]].

## Topics

The biennale has evolved from traditional printmaking into expanded contemporary art. Key thematic threads across recent editions:

- **Autonomous beings / puppet logic**: 36th edition's core metaphor — the Žogica Marogica puppet as question: "Who controls whom? Should beings be operated by strings or hands, or become independent?" Direct ALife resonance.
- **Speculative technology / lost futures**: 34th edition (Iskra Delta) — Yugoslav computing company summoned from the future; speculative tech history; internet culture; emerging technologies as fiction.
- **Anti-colonial / non-aligned networks**: 35th edition — Africa-Yugoslavia solidarity, Nkrumah's Ghana, non-aligned movement, transnational infrastructure. Historical internationalism as model for present.
- **Oracular / divinatory practice**: 36th — the oracle as site of collective dreaming, freedom, and peace-making. Art as speculative commons.
- **Intermedia / expanded printmaking**: Since 2000s, video, installation, performance, digital work fully incorporated.

## Recent Editions

### 36th (2025) — *The Oracle / Orakelj*
- **Artistic director:** [Chus Martínez](https://www.fhnw.ch/en/people/chus-martinez)
- **Dates:** 6 June – 12 October 2025
- **Venues:** MGLC Grad Tivoli, MGLC Švicarija, MGLC Plečnikov Auditorium (Tivoli Park), Moderna galerija (MG+), Mestna galerija Ljubljana
- **Artists:** 32 total, 21 with newly commissioned works
- **Theme:** The oracle as site of collective dreaming and freedom. Art as space where one can "demand freedom and peace." Framed around the Žogica Marogica puppet — a beloved Slovenian character whose creator (Ajša Pengov, 1913–1983) asked: should beings be operated by hands or strings, or become independent? Thematic cluster: gambling technology, bodiless autonomous intelligences, mass education in the digital age, new folklore for collective dreaming. Automata by Silvano Omerzu installed at all venues.
- **Prizes:** Grand Prize: Noor Abed; Research residency prize: Gabi Dao; Special mention: Maria Arnal; Audience: Sinzo Aanza
- **ALife signal:** The Žogica puppet thread — "bodiless autonomous intelligences capable of surpassing humans" / "analogue mass education in the digital age" — directly mirrors Amber's ALife/agent ethology questions.

### 35th (2023/2024) — *From the Void Came the Gifts of the Cosmos*
- **Artistic director:** [Ibrahim Mahama](https://whitecube.com/artists/artist/ibrahim_mahama)
- **Theme:** Non-aligned movement; Africa-Yugoslavia socialist internationalism; Nkrumah's Ghana; empty space as egalitarian potential from which multitudes arise. Decolonial, ecological, infrastructure-focused.
- **Curatorial team:** Exit Frame Collective, Alicia Knock, Selom Koffi Kudjie, Inga Lāce, Beya Othmani, Patrick Nii Okanta
- **Venues:** MGLC Grad Tivoli, Cukrarna, MGLC Švicarija; also integrated Ljubljana autonomous zones PLAC and Krater
- **Public programme:** *Agyina: Warning of the Wise* — film screenings, symposium, performances, cycling tours
- **Historical note:** Biennale archive reveals its 1955 founding included non-aligned African and Asian participants alongside USSR and West — internacionalist from the start.

### 34th (2021) — *Iskra Delta*
- **Theme:** Speculative revival of Iskra Delta, Yugoslav computing company that many believed could have become a global tech power. Set against pandemic, climate anxiety. Themes: hyperpop, memes, political compass, "doomers," speculative technology, lost futures, platform subcultures.
- **Participants:** Regional artists + younger generation; included [[Bogna Konior]] (video essays + catalog texts)
- **Medium:** Multidisciplinary — video, performance, digital, fiction

### 33rd (2019) — *Vice v Lisice* (Handcuffs)
- Theme: constraint, restriction, institutional limits

### 32nd (2017) — *Kriterij rojstva* (Birth Criterion)
- Theme: birth, origin, emergence

## Why Interesting

One of the world's oldest biennales (founded 1955 during the Cold War as Yugoslavia's internationalist gesture), now a major contemporary art event that has quietly evolved into one of Europe's most intellectually interesting biennales — each edition is concept-driven, commission-heavy, and politically engaged. Not a showcase or market event; closer to Documenta in spirit. The 36th edition's exploration of autonomous beings, puppet-automata, and oracle-as-collective-intelligence is the clearest resonance with Amber's work. The Slovenian context (Ljubljana = Aksioma + ŠUM Journal + this biennale) forms a coherent Eastern European critical media art ecosystem worth knowing. Entry via [[Aksioma]] network is the highest-probability path. The MGLC Critic-in-Residence is the most direct open-call opportunity.
