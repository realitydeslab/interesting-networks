The **Society for Social Studies of Science (4S)** annual meeting is the global flagship of Science and Technology Studies. Founded in 1975, 4S is the canonical North American-centered STS umbrella — the place where STS was institutionalized as a field. Every four years it merges with [[EASST Conference]] for a joint megaconference; solo meetings draw ~1500–2000 attendees from across disciplines. Publishes *[Science, Technology, and Human Values](https://journals.sagepub.com/home/sth)* (STHV) with Sage and runs the open-access *[Engaging Science, Technology and Society](https://estsjournal.org/)* (ESTS).

## Topics
4S covers all of STS: laboratories, expertise, infrastructure, policy, race/gender/sexuality, nonhumans, environment, medicine, AI/algorithms, design, futures. Less curated than EASST — broader, more sociological mainstream. Making & Doing track creates space for experimental and practice-based formats.

## 4S vs EASST: Key Differences

| | 4S | EASST |
|---|---|---|
| Base | North America (but global) | Europe |
| Size | 1500–2000 solo | 1000–1500 solo |
| Culture | More sociological mainstream | Slightly more experimental, feminist |
| Geography | Canada/US mostly | Rotating European cities |
| For Amber (UK base) | Travel cost & visa friction | More accessible |
| Joint | 2028 (est.) | — |

From Amber's UK/Oxford position, EASST solo conferences are logistically easier. But 4S has the bigger international network, and the **Making & Doing** format is equally well-developed.

## Submission Formats

1. **Individual presentations** — 15–20 min paper in an organized panel
2. **Organized panels** — convener assembles 3–5 presentations around a theme
3. **Making & Doing** — practice/exhibition/demo format; low-text, embodied, speculative work actively welcomed. Perfect for Amber's design research.
4. **Roundtables** — discussion-focused, no papers
5. **Adjacent gatherings** — workshops, meetings, affiliated events (more experimental)

Making & Doing is particularly interesting for Amber — it's where STS meets design/art practice, and where more-than-human installations, prototypes, and speculative work gets shown alongside papers.
