# Nam June Paik Art Center

South Korean media art institution dedicated to the legacy of [Nam June Paik](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nam_June_Paik) — the Korean-American video art pioneer who invented the genre. Runs a prestigious biennial prize worth $50,000 USD to artists/thinkers amalgamating art and technology. The prize winner list reads like a who's-who of critical tech art: Bruno Latour, Trevor Paglen, Blast Theory, CAMP.

## What

[Nam June Paik Art Center](https://njp.ggcf.kr/en) (백남준아트센터) opened in 2008 in Giheung District, Yongin, Gyeonggi Province, South Korea. It is operated by the [Gyeonggi Cultural Foundation](https://www.ggcf.kr/). The museum hosts both permanent and temporary exhibitions centred on the work of Nam June Paik (1932–2006), who is widely regarded as the pioneer of video art and a foundational figure in art-technology practice.

The museum's permanent collection focuses on Paik's work — his TV sculptures, video installations, and performance art documentation. The curation philosophy resists chronological ordering, inviting visitors to form their own readings.

The Center is also a research and programming hub for the broader field of media art, particularly work that extends Paik's vision: art that uses electronic/digital media, challenges audience relationships, blurs music/performance/visual art boundaries, and asks critical questions about technology.

## The NJP Prize

The **Nam June Paik Art Center Prize** was established in 2009. It is awarded to artists and theorists whose works:
- Amalgamate art and technology
- Pursue new ways of communication
- Interact with audiences in novel ways
- Fuse and conflate music, performance, and visual art

**Prize amount:** $50,000 USD  
**Format:** Biennial (originally annual in 2009-2010, shifted to biennial)  
**Award includes:** Cash prize + solo exhibition at the Center  
**Prize ceremony:** Held in Korea, hosted with the Governor of Gyeonggi Province

**Selection:** A Selection Committee chooses the winner. Criteria emphasize artists who "investigate paths that no other artist has taken" — the exploratory, boundary-dissolving spirit of Paik himself.

No open application process has been publicly documented — this appears to be a nomination/committee-selected prize rather than an open call. The selection skews heavily international, with Korean artists appearing primarily in the inaugural year.

**Pattern analysis:** The prize consistently bridges disciplines — a philosopher (Latour), a geographer-artist (Paglen), a collective (Blast Theory, CAMP), and a choreographer (Ahn). This is NOT a narrow media-art prize — it rewards critical, boundary-crossing work that extends Paik's spirit into unexpected territories.
