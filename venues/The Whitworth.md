# The Whitworth

University of Manchester's civic art gallery, founded 1889 "for the perpetual gratification of the people of Manchester." Extensively renovated 2015. Houses an internationally important collection of 60,000+ works — art, textiles, wallpapers. Under Director **Sook-Kyung Lee** (appointed ~2023), the curatorial direction has sharpened significantly: transnational art histories, decoloniality, ecology, care ethics. Not a tech-art or media-art venue per se, but programmes artists who engage with politics of identity, body, care, and non-Western traditions. Relevant to Amber mainly via the **care ethics / mortality** thread (Still Care, Still Parents) and the **Raqs Media Collective** connection. Manchester is ~2h from Oxford by train — accessible for visits.

## Key People

## Topics & Curatorial Lens

The Whitworth's programme under Sook-Kyung Lee emphasises:
- **Transnational & decolonial art histories** — non-Western perspectives, global modernisms
- **Care ethics & community** — healthcare, bereavement, civic wellbeing
- **Feminist / outsider art** — Romany heritage, folk traditions, disability arts
- **Ecology & nature** — trees, park, environmental themes
- **Cross-community collaboration** — NHS, disability arts charities, community curators
- **Textile & craft** — major collection strength; international textile traditions

Less prominent (or absent): tech-art, digital art, AI, media art, speculative design, ALife.

## Fellowships & Programmes

### Frieze x Deutsche Bank Emerging Curators Fellowship
Currently held by Sophia Allison at the Whitworth. This is a **curatorial** fellowship, not an artist residency. Partnership between Frieze art fair and Deutsche Bank. Eligibility and application process via [Frieze](https://www.frieze.com/). Worth tracking for curators in Amber's network.

### Research Collaborations
The Whitworth accepts research collaborations and curatorial exhibition tour requests via their collections team: [whitworthcollections.manchester.ac.uk](https://whitworthcollections.manchester.ac.uk/collections/using-the-collection/research-support/)

**Important note:** Exhibition programme is planned 3 years in advance by in-house curators; **they do not accept unsolicited exhibition proposals.** Entry point for artists is via gallery representation and curator relationships, not open calls.

### No Open Artist Residency Found
As of March 2026, no standing open-call artist residency programme exists at the Whitworth.

## Why Interesting

The Whitworth is not a tech-art venue and has no digital-art or media-art programme. But under Sook-Kyung Lee, it punches intellectually — her curatorial thinking (decoloniality, ecology, transnational art histories) overlaps with Amber's more-than-human and cosmotechnics territory at a philosophical level.

The **Still Care** / **Still Parents** programme is the most direct resonance: art made with and for healthcare workers dealing with dying and bereavement. This is precisely the terrain of Amber's machine death research — care for the dying, the ethics of attending to death. That the Whitworth did it with NHS midwives is not unlike what Amber could imagine doing with dying machines or systems.

**Raqs Media Collective** link is important: Raqs's *Twilight Language* at the Whitworth in 2017 is the most technologically-inflected, philosophically complex exhibition the Whitworth has hosted in recent memory. That's Amber's adjacent world.

The **Frieze x Deutsche Bank Emerging Curators Fellowship** at the Whitworth is worth tracking for Amber's curatorial network (Danlin Huang, etc.).
