# CogSci Conference (Annual Meeting of the Cognitive Science Society)

The **Annual Meeting of the Cognitive Science Society (CogSci)** is the flagship interdisciplinary conference on mind, brain, computation, and behavior. Held annually since 1979, it draws cognitive psychologists, neuroscientists, linguists, philosophers, AI researchers, and anthropologists. The intellectual home of distributed cognition (Hutchins), extended mind (Clark & Chalmers), predictive processing, and probabilistic models of mind. Papers published in open-access proceedings via [eScholarship](https://escholarship.org/uc/cognitivesciencesociety).

## Topics

CogSci covers the full cognitive science spectrum:
- **Cognitive psychology** — memory, attention, learning, reasoning (dominant)
- **Computational modeling** — Bayesian models, neural networks as cognitive models (awarded explicitly)
- **Developmental cognition** — infant/child learning, theory of mind
- **Language & linguistics** — acquisition, processing, pragmatics, semantics
- **Cognitive neuroscience** — brain imaging, EEG (growing but not dominant)
- **Philosophy of mind** — consciousness, intentionality, extended mind, theory of mind
- **AI & machine cognition** — LLMs as cognitive models, human-AI comparison (rapidly growing)
- **4E cognition** — embodied, embedded, extended, enactive frameworks

## Paper Format

- **Length:** 6 pages max + unlimited references
- **Style:** Two-column
- **Review:** Double-blind
- **Workshops:** 2-page proposals, same deadline as papers; half/full-day formats
- **Symposia:** 2-page proposals; 90-min slot (4×20min talks + discussion)
- **Member abstracts:** 150-word abstracts for CSS members (poster presentation)
- **Submission portal:** [PCS (PrecisionConference)](https://precisionconference.com/~cogsci/)
