# Seven on Seven

Annual conference organized by [[Rhizome]] at the [New Museum](https://www.newmuseum.org/), pairing seven contemporary artists with seven technology innovators and challenging them to develop something new together over the course of one day. Each pair presents the result publicly the following day.

## Background
Co-founded in 2010 by [[Lauren Cornell]] (then Rhizome Executive Director), Fred Benenson, John Borthwick, and Peter Rojas. Deliberately modelled on [Experiments in Art and Technology (E.A.T.)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Experiments_in_Art_and_Technology), the project Billy Klüver and Robert Rauschenberg initiated at Bell Labs in 1967. The title echoes that historical precedent — a structured encounter between art and technology that is unpredictable in outcome.

## Key People
- **[[Lauren Cornell]]** — co-founder; organized during her Rhizome tenure
- Notable artists who participated: Taryn Simon, Evan Roth, Monica Narula, Ryan Trecartin, Tauba Auerbach, Liz Magic Laser, Rashaad Newsome, LaToya Ruby Frazier, Jon Rafman, Stephanie Syjuco
- Notable technologists: Jeff Hammerbacher, Joshua Schachter, Matt Mullenweg, Hilary Mason, Andrew Kortina, David Karp, Aaron Swartz, Peter Rojas

## Notable Outcomes
- **Taryn Simon × Aaron Swartz** → *[Image Atlas](https://archive.rhizome.org/artbase/66618/image-atlas/)* — a search engine comparing image results across different national internet contexts; made the cultural specificity of Google search visible

## Why Interesting
Seven on Seven institutionalised the idea that artists and technologists should collaborate, not just comment on each other's work. The Taryn Simon/Aaron Swartz pairing alone produced a piece that remains relevant to discussions of geopolitics and search algorithms. The conference model itself — public, time-limited, outcome-oriented — has been widely imitated.
