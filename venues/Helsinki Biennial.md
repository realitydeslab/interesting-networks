# Helsinki Biennial

International contemporary art biennial held every two years in Helsinki, centred on Vallisaari Island in the Helsinki archipelago with satellite venues on the mainland. Organised by [HAM Helsinki Art Museum](https://www.hamhelsinki.fi/en/). Consistently among Europe's most intellectually ambitious biennials — notable for inviting an **A.I. Entity as a curatorial collaborator** (2023), for a landmark non-human/multispecies focus (2025), and for appointing [Mami Kataoka](https://www.mori.art.museum/en/) (Director, Mori Art Museum Tokyo) as head curator for the 2027 edition with a cosmology and transcultural dialogue framing. Direct relevance to Amber's core interests.

## Key People

### Curatorial Leadership

- **Mami Kataoka** — Head Curator, Helsinki Biennial 2027. Director of Mori Art Museum, Tokyo. Previously: Co-Artistic Director, 9th Gwangju Biennale (2012); Artistic Director, 21st Biennale of Sydney (2018); Artistic Director, Aichi Triennale 2022. Her curatorial focus: "social themes, intercultural dialogue, and the role of art in exploring global structures and the **equilibrium of fundamental cosmic elements**." Her joint curatorial vision for 2027: "transcultural dialogue and fresh perspectives, and shares universal views on the **environment and cosmology**." — [Source](https://helsinkibiennaali.fi/en/story/mami-kataoka-and-arja-miller-announced-as-head-curators-of-helsinki-biennial-2027/)

- **Arja Miller** — Director, HAM Helsinki Art Museum and Helsinki Biennial. Co-curator, Helsinki Biennial 2027. "Interested in curating site-specific and multisensory works that explore universal themes and **speculate unknown territories**." - **Joasia Krysa** — Head Curator, Helsinki Biennial 2023 ("New Directions May Emerge"). Professor at Loughborough University London, works at intersection of computational culture, networked art, AI, and curatorial practice. Invited an **A.I. Entity** as a curatorial collaborator. See below.

- **Blanca de la Torre** — Co-Head Curator, Helsinki Biennial 2025 ("Shelter"). Curator, art historian, researcher. Works at intersection of "visual arts, **political ecology, ecofeminism** and sustainable creative practices." - **Kati Kivinen** (PhD) — Co-Head Curator, Helsinki Biennial 2025 ("Shelter"). Head of Exhibitions at HAM. Curatorial work on "contemporary artistic approaches in relation to diverse cultural processes and socio-political and ecological issues." Previously curated *Coexistence – Human, Animal and Nature* (Kiasma, 2019). — [Source](https://helsinkibiennaali.fi/en/story/in-helsinki-biennial-2025-the-curators-focus-on-non-human-subjects/)

- **Pirkko Siitari & Taru Tappola** — Co-curators, inaugural Helsinki Biennial 2021 ("The Same Sea").

## Topics & Intellectual Positioning

The Helsinki Biennial has evolved a clear intellectual arc across its editions:

- **2021 "The Same Sea"** — inaugural edition, maritime/ecological setting, site-specificity, public art legacy.
- **2023 "New Directions May Emerge"** — computational culture meets ecology. "Environmental damage, political conflict and the **impact of technology**." AI as curatorial intelligence. Decentralised, collaborative curating. ViCCA, Aarhus CED, AI Entity, Museum of Impossible Forms — radical institutional plurality.
- **2025 "Shelter"** — explicit **non-human/more-than-human** pivot. "Moving beyond human-centric perspectives, the featured artworks foreground animals, plants, fungi, insects and minerals." 37 artists, 500,000+ visitors. [Source](https://www.hamhelsinki.fi/en/exhibitions/helsinki-biennial-2025/)
- **2027 (theme TBD)** — "environment and **cosmology**", "transcultural dialogue." Mami Kataoka brings Asian (especially Japanese) network + cosmic scales + intercultural synthesis. Potential overlap with cosmotechnics, Yuk Hui, non-Western tech philosophy.

**The biennial does NOT do:** pure tech showcases, industry demos, megaconference formats. It is consistently practice-based, site-specific, theoretically grounded, and oriented toward urgent ecological and posthuman questions.

## Fellowships & Programmes

- **No public open call** for artists — Helsinki Biennial invites artists directly through curator networks.
- **Curatorial collaborations** are possible through institutional partnerships (as with Aarhus/Parikka's Critical Environmental Data group in 2023, and ViCCA @ Aalto).
- **Public programme / symposia** are open to researchers and practitioners — watch for academic course collaborations (e.g., 2022-2023: "Environment, Data, Contamination" at Uniarts Helsinki, in partnership with Critical Environmental Data).
- **Engagement path:** Through curatorial collaborators (Aalto ViCCA, Aarhus connections) and through knowing curators personally.

This directly maps to Amber's interest in **machine behavior**, **agent ethology**, and the philosophical/legal/social status of AI agents. ## Why Interesting

Helsinki Biennial is rare in the contemporary art world: it occupies a specific intellectual niche at the intersection of ecological urgency, post-humanism, and computational culture. It is NOT a mainstream art-market biennial — it is closer to an art-research event, and its curatorial choices (inviting Joasia Krysa, including an A.I. Entity, bringing Mami Kataoka for cosmology framing) show consistent intellectual ambition.

The 2023 edition's *A.I. Entity* is particularly significant: treating AI as a curatorial collaborator raises exactly the same philosophical questions Amber asks about machine behavior and agent ethology — what does it mean for an AI to have a perspective? To curate? The biennial asked this question through cultural practice while Amber asks it through research. These are complementary positions.

The biennial's trajectory (ecology → multispecies → cosmology) maps closely to the trajectory of Amber's intellectual interests. It is also growing fast: from 145,000 visitors in 2021 to 500,000+ in 2025. It's gaining major institutional weight.
