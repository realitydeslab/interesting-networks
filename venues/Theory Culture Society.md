# Theory, Culture & Society

The definitive international journal for critical and cultural theory — founded 1982, #1 in Cultural Studies by impact factor (2.810, 2019). Published by [SAGE](https://uk.sagepub.com/) at [Goldsmiths, University of London](https://www.gold.ac.uk/). Works across the borderlines of sociology, cultural studies, humanities, and critical philosophy. Increasingly global in scope, explicitly challenging Western-centred knowledge classifications. The journal where serious continental philosophy meets social analysis, where Baudrillard gets cited alongside Mbembe, where Yuk Hui's cosmotechnics finds its theoretical home.

**Companion journal:** [[Body & Society]] — launched 1995, somaesthetics, embodiment, the body as sociotechnical object.

## Submission Guidelines

**Standard articles:** 7,000–8,000 words. Submit via [SAGE Manuscript Central](https://mc.manuscriptcentral.com/tcs). Full guidelines at [journals.sagepub.com/author-instructions/TCS](https://journals.sagepub.com/author-instructions/TCS).

**Special issues:** 7–9 articles + full introduction (7–9,000 words). Proposal to [tcs@sagepub.co.uk](mailto:tcs@sagepub.co.uk). 24-month timeline from proposal to publication. Proposals require 1–1,500 word rationale, draft article titles/abstracts, and biographical notes.

**Special sections:** 4–6 articles + shorter introduction (5–7,000 words).

**E-special issues:** Separate proposal track — [e-special-issue-proposals](https://www.theoryculturesociety.org/e-special-issue-proposals).

**Website contributions:** Essays, reviews, video abstracts. Lighter-weight entry point to the TCS ecosystem.

## History
Founded **1982** at **Teesside Polytechnic** (North East England) by Mike Featherstone — notable for its inauspicious timing (Thatcherite cuts) and peripheral location. Moved to SAGE 1987; to Nottingham Trent University 1996–2011; to Goldsmiths 2012. **Body & Society** launched 1995 as companion. The **TCS New Encyclopaedia Project** launched 2001, with first volume on "Problematizing Global Knowledge" in 2006.
