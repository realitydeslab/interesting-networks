# Manifesta — European Biennial of Contemporary Art

Nomadic European biennial founded in 1996. Unlike Venice, Documenta, or Kassel, Manifesta moves to a different European city for each edition — making site and European context central to the curatorial concept. Often chooses post-industrial or peripheral European cities rather than established cultural capitals. Has been particularly attentive to geopolitical transitions (Eastern Europe, post-industrial cities) and institutional critique.

## Recent Editions
- **Manifesta 9** (2012, Genk, Belgium) — "The Deep of the Modern" — [[Cuauhtémoc Medina]]. Former coal mine setting; explored industrial capitalism's underground.
- **Manifesta 10** (2014, St. Petersburg, Russia) — curated by Kasper König. Controversial given Russia's anti-gay legislation.
- **Manifesta 11** (2016, Zurich, Switzerland) — curated by Christian Jankowski. Theme: professional work and art worlds.
- **Manifesta 12** (2018, Palermo, Italy) — collective curation. Migration, Mediterranean, colonialism.
- **Manifesta 13** (2020, Marseille, France) — postponed by COVID.
- **Manifesta 14** (2022, Pristina, Kosovo) — curated by Catherine Nichols.

## Why Interesting
Manifesta's nomadic format makes it one of the most interesting laboratories for site-specific European curating. Its choice of peripheral or transitional European cities (Genk post-coal, Pristina post-conflict) creates built-in political stakes. [[Cuauhtémoc Medina]]'s Manifesta 9 was particularly celebrated as one of the theoretically richest editions.
