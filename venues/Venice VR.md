# Venice Immersive (formerly Venice VR)

The world's most prestigious international festival for immersive and XR media — the "Cannes of VR." Part of [La Biennale di Venezia's Venice International Film Festival](https://www.labiennale.org/en/cinema), held annually in late August / early September on the Lido di Venezia. Founded 2017 as **Venice VR**, renamed **Venice Immersive** around 2022 to reflect broadening scope beyond headset-only VR to include all XR: VR, AR, MR, immersive installations, virtual worlds, interactive cinema.

Venice Immersive awards the **Golden Lion for Best VR Experience** (the top prize), the **Special Jury Prize**, and the **Best Experience** award within its competition section. Selection is highly curated: typically ~30 works in Competition + Out of Competition, selected from hundreds of global submissions. Submission is via [La Biennale's official call for entries](https://www.labiennale.org/en/cinema/submission-regulations).

The section sits alongside the main Venice Film Festival — benefiting from the prestige, press corps, and industry presence of one of cinema's Big Three alongside Cannes and Berlin. Location: primarily Lido island, with Venice Immersive works often installed at the **Lazzaretto Vecchio** island (an island quarantine site — historically resonant location for immersive work).

## Key People

- **Alberto Barbera** — Festival Director (since 2011). Overall artistic director of the Venice Film Festival. [Profile](https://www.labiennale.org/en/cinema/2025/director)
- **Liz Rosenthal** — longtime Venice VR curator/programmer who built the section from 2017–2022. Co-founder of [Power to the Pixel](https://powertothepixel.com/). Among the most influential curators in immersive arts globally.
- **Michel Reilhac** — co-curator of Venice VR alongside Rosenthal for several years. Based in Belgium. Former CEO of Arte France Cinéma. One of the founders of the immersive arts ecosystem. Now focused on interactive documentary.
- **Savina Neirotti** — Head of Programme for Biennale College Cinema. Manages the Biennale College Cinema Immersive development lab.
- **Jury members rotate** — for Venice Immersive Competition. In 2025: Jury composition changes each year; typically includes XR artists, curators, and film directors.

## Topics

- **Virtual Reality (6DoF and 360°)** — narrative and experiential VR works
- **Mixed/Augmented Reality** — location-based AR, wearable MR
- **Immersive installations** — multi-user, performative, spatial
- **Virtual Worlds** — social VR, metaverse art
- **Interactive cinema** — user-agency narrative forms
- **XR performance** — live/hybrid XR work
- **Any length** — from short-form experiences to long installations
- All forms of creative expression through XR are eligible

## Fellowships & Programmes

### Biennale College Cinema Immersive (flagship programme)
La Biennale's development lab for immersive work by emerging creators. Selected projects receive:
- Mentorship from international experts (including Festival Director Alberto Barbera and curators)
- Development support
- **World premiere** at Venice Immersive out of competition
- Grant funding

Application process: Call typically opens Jan–April for summer festival. Check [labiennale.org/en/cinema](https://www.labiennale.org/en/cinema) for 2026 call.

Example: *First Virtual Suit* (Kazuki Yuhara, Japan, 2025) was a Biennale College Cinema Immersive work — produced through the lab, world premiered at Venice 2025.

**This is the most direct route for emerging immersive artists to get to Venice.**

### Biennale College Cinema (broader)
The parent programme (for film) has run since 2012 — 3132+ applications total, 45 micro-budget features produced. Immersive track follows similar model. Supported by: Ministero della Cultura, Chanel, Gotham Film & Media Institute, TorinoFilmLab, Creative Europe.

## Why Interesting

Venice Immersive is the single highest-prestige venue in the immersive/XR art world. Unlike technology conferences or XR industry events, this is a film festival — it treats VR/AR/MR as cinema and art, not product demos. The audience includes cinema critics, curators, collectors, and cultural institutions. Winning or being selected here has a different symbolic weight than winning an XR industry award. For Amber's research program — which spans technical (HoloKit), artistic (China Academy of Art), and academic (Oxford HCI) — Venice Immersive is the art-world legitimation pathway. The Biennale College Cinema Immersive programme is the specific development pathway designed for exactly the kind of work Amber does.
