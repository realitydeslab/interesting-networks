# Biennale de l'Image en Mouvement (BIM)

Geneva's flagship moving image biennial — one of the oldest in Europe, and since 2014 fully retooled as a **commissioning machine**: every work is newly made, the Centre produces it all. Not a festival where you submit work. An invitation-only biennial where the curators select a small cohort of artists and commission everything from scratch. The result is unusually coherent, ambitious, and often ahead of the curve. BIM 2024's theme "A Cosmic Movie Camera" pulled in Interspecifics, Lawrence Lek, Jenna Sutela, and Lauren Lee McCarthy — a roster that reads like Amber's ideal peer group.

## Overview / Vibe

BIM was founded in 1985 by André Iten as the "Semaine Internationale de Vidéo" (International Video Week) — one of the first dedicated moving image events in Europe. For its first two decades it was run by the Centre pour l'Image Contemporaine (CIC). In 2009 CAC Genève (Centre d'Art Contemporain Genève, now called simply "le Centre") inherited the biennial mandate. In 2014 the Centre relaunched it as something quite different from the original video festival: a **production-focused biennial** where all works are new commissions, produced and funded by the Centre. No submissions, no retrospectives — just fresh work.

The format is a hybrid: part exhibition, part festival. There's an opening week with performances and events, followed by an exhibition period (works shown in the Centre's main space and city-wide partner venues). The Centre also operates **MIRE**, a long-running audiovisual art integration project that places BIM-produced works in Geneva's public transport infrastructure (Léman Express stations).

BIM sits at the intersection of film, installation, performance, and increasingly digital/AI/non-human media. It has consistently been politically and formally adventurous, with each edition working through a small roster (15-20 artists) rather than a bloated programme. The curatorial model brings in a high-profile external co-curator per edition alongside Andrea Bellini (permanent director/co-curator).

## Key People

- **Andrea Bellini** — Director of CAC Genève; permanent co-curator of every BIM edition since 2014. The institutional spine of the biennial.
- **André Iten** — Founded the original "Semaine Internationale de Vidéo" in 1985.
- **Nora N. Khan** — Co-curator of BIM 2024. Writer, editor, critic working at the intersection of AI, computation, and art theory. One of the most important voices in AI art discourse.

- **Cecilia Alemani** — Co-curator of BIM 2016; later curated Venice Biennale 2022.

## Key Editions

### BIM 2024 — "A Cosmic Movie Camera"
**Co-curators:** Andrea Bellini + Nora N. Khan

Theme anchored in the astrophysics discovery of photon rings around black holes — "infinite light traps" — used as a lens on invisible/unknown forces in image-making at the algorithm age.

**Artists:** Basel Abbas & Ruanne Abou-Rahme, Alfatih, American Artist, Danielle Brathwaite-Shirley, Sheila Chukwulozie, Formafantasma, Aziz Hazara, **Interspecifics**, **Lawrence Lek**, Shuang Li, Diego Marcon, **Lauren Lee McCarthy**, Sahej Rahal, **Jenna Sutela**, Emmanuel Van der Auwera.

Standout works described in the commission: holographic figures, generative biological libraries, AI tribunal simulations, distributed ancestral intelligence games, dramas of artificial beings, genetic exchange laboratories, hallucinated cloud cities.

This edition also launched the Centre's 50th anniversary celebrations, with an architectural design project by Giacomo Castagnola woven into the BIM context.

### BIM 2021 — "Everything But The World"
**Co-curators:** Andrea Bellini + DIS collective

Framed as a "radical pilot season" — interrupting habitual programs to find a way out of human-centric, capitalism-driven narratives. DIS's film *Everything But The World* commissioned specifically for BIM: a non-linear natural history of homo sapiens as a kind of TV pilot.

**Artists:** Emily Allan & Leah Hennessey, Theo Anthony, Riccardo Benassi, Will Benedict & Steffen Jørgensen, Hannah Black & Juliana Huxtable & And Or Forever, DIS, Giulia Essyad, Simon Fujiwara, GRAU, Mandy Harris Williams, Camille Henrot, Sabrina Röthlisberger Belkacem, Akeem Smith, TELFAR.

Works also integrated into MIRE public transport project (Léman Express stations).

### BIM 2018
**Co-curators:** Andrea Bellini + Andrea Lissoni (Tate Modern)

**Artists:** Meriem Bennani, Lawrence Abu Hamdan, Fatima Al Qadiri & Khalid Al Gharaballi, Korakrit Arunanondchai & Alex Govjic, **Ian Cheng**, Tamara Henderson, Kahlil Joseph. Single-channel films: Sarah Abu Abdallah, Neïl Beloufa, Irene Dionisio, James N. Kienitz Wilkins, Tobias Madison, Florent Meng, Bahar Noorizadeh, Eduardo Williams, Leslie Thornton & James Richards. Performances: Ligia Lewis (trilogy co-produced with HAU Berlin), Elysia Crampton, Pan Daijing.

World premiere of Meriem Bennani's *Party on the CAPS* here — before Whitney Biennial. Also Ian Cheng showing in Geneva (his simulation/AI work).

### BIM 2016
**Co-curators:** Andrea Bellini + Cecilia Alemani + Caroline Bourgeois + Elvira Dyangani Ose

27 new works. Themes: gender and body politics (Boychild, Wu Tsang, Pauline Boudry & Renate Lorenz, Phoebe Boswell), resource exploitation (Karimah Ashadu, Bertille Bak, Hicham Berrada, Bodil Furu), anthropological research. Also: Tracey Rose, Cally Spooner, Kerry Tribe, Sophia Al Maria, John M. Armleder & Stephan Eicher.

Opening week 9–13 November 2016, exhibition to 29 January 2017.

### BIM 2014
**Co-curators:** Andrea Bellini + Hans-Ulrich Obrist + Yann Chateigné

First edition of the relaunched BIM. 22 new works commissioned and produced by the Centre. Partners: FMAC, FCAC, HEAD–Genève, Museum of Old and New Art (MONA, Tasmania), Arthub Asia (Shanghai), R4-Ile Seguin (Paris). Strong international collaborative framing from the start.

### Pre-2014 (Historical Phase)
From 1985–2007 the biennial was run by Centre pour l'Image Contemporaine. Artists shown over 30 years include: Bill Viola, Gary Hill, Steina & Woody Vasulka, Robert Filliou, Chris Marker, Guy Debord, Vito Acconci, William Wegman, Bruce Nauman, Chantal Akerman, Rebecca Horn, Jean-Luc Godard, Andy Warhol, Philippe Garrel, Nam June Paik, Laurie Anderson, Artavazd Pelechian, Harun Farocki, Matt Mullican, Anri Sala, Straub/Huillet. A canonical canon of video art history.

CAC Genève took over the mandate in 2009, relaunched with new format in 2014.

## Submission Process

**Not an open call for the main BIM programme.** The biennial is **invitation-only and commission-based**: the Centre selects artists and produces entirely new works for each edition. No submissions accepted for the main exhibition.

However: the Centre does run periodic **APPEL À PROJETS** (open calls) for other programmes — e.g. an "Appel à Projets 2025" with a deadline of 31 August 2024 was active. These may be for residencies, the Dynamo programme, or Project Space — not the biennial itself. Worth monitoring the Centre's website for these ancillary calls.

**To get BIM-commissioned:** The pathway is recognition → being brought to the curators' attention → invitation. Building relationships with the Centre, showing at partner institutions (HAU Berlin, Tate Modern, Serpentine, Fondation Pinault, etc.) would be the route.

## Network Position

BIM sits at the heart of the **European media art / moving image circuit**. Key relationships:

- **Tate Modern** (Andrea Lissoni curated BIM 2018, is now Director of Haus der Kunst Munich)
- **Serpentine Gallery** (Hans-Ulrich Obrist curated BIM 2014)
- **Fondation Pinault** (Caroline Bourgeois curated BIM 2016)
- **HAU Hebbel am Ufer Berlin** — co-produced Ligia Lewis performance at BIM 2018
- **HEAD–Genève** — academic partner (Yann Chateigné, BIM 2014)
- **Venice Biennale** — Cecilia Alemani (BIM 2016) curated Venice 2022
- **Museo Reina Sofía** — Elvira Dyangani Ose (BIM 2016) is now director

BIM is **not** a media/tech festival like transmediale or Ars Electronica. It's firmly in the contemporary art circuit — white cube + cinema hybrid, Geneva's prestigious museum infrastructure. It's closer to a high-end biennial (like Manifesta) than to a media art festival. This is both its strength (real art world weight) and its limitation (less porous to experimental tech practice).

Compared to peers:
- **transmediale** — more media art/theory focus, less commissioning power, Berlin scene
- **IDFA DocLab** — documentary/interactive, Amsterdam, more accessible to application
- **Image en Champs** — niche French moving image, smaller scale
- **Le Fresnoy Panorama** — French school-based, strong but institutional
- **Ars Electronica** — explicitly tech/art, Linz, more open call friendly

BIM's position is unique: the combination of historical prestige (founded 1985), commissioning budget, CAC Genève's institutional weight, and recent shift toward AI/non-human/post-digital themes makes it one of the most interesting places to be commissioned.
