# The Menil Collection

Major private art museum in Houston, Texas. Founded by philanthropists John and Dominique de Menil — [Menil Foundation](https://www.menil.org/about) established 1954; main building opened 1987. 30-acre campus in a residential neighborhood. 25,000+ works: strongest in Surrealism, African art, Byzantine/early Christian, Pacific Islands, and contemporary. Free admission always. Connected to Rice University intellectual community (Timothy Morton, OOO, hyperobjects). The Menil has a distinctive intellectual culture that bridges contemporary art and humanistic thought at the highest level. Not primarily a commissioning venue — a collection museum — but hosts serious public programming.

## Institutional Model

- **Type:** Private collection museum (no commissioning focus, no residency program)
- **Founded:** 1987 (main building); Menil Foundation est. 1954
- **Collection:** 25,000+ works across prehistoric era to present
- **Campus:** 30 acres in residential Houston neighborhood. Buildings: Main Museum, [Menil Drawing Institute](https://www.menil.org/drawing), Cy Twombly Gallery, Dan Flavin Installation at Richmond Hall.
- **Admission:** Always free
- **Hours:** Wed–Sun, 11am–7pm
- **Address:** 1533 Sul Ross Street, Houston, TX

## Fellowships & Programmes

- **Menil Drawing Institute Pre-Doctoral Fellowship** — for doctoral students researching drawing as medium
- **Menil Drawing Institute Research Fellowship** — for established scholars
- **Morgan-Menil Research Fellowship** — joint with Morgan Library & Museum (NY)
- **Andrew W. Mellon Advanced Training Fellowship in Art Conservation** — conservation professionals; rotating specialties; open minimum every 3 years

*Note:* All fellowships are for art historians and conservators, not for artists or HCI/design researchers. Not directly relevant to Amber's profile.

- **Dominique de Menil's legacy** includes the [Rothko Chapel](https://www.rothkochapel.org/) — non-denominational meditation space across the street; major spiritual-arts crossroads.
