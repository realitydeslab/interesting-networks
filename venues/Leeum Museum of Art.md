# Leeum Museum of Art

South Korea's most prestigious private art museum — Samsung Foundation of Culture's flagship cultural institution, located in Hannam-dong, Yongsan District, Seoul. Three buildings by three world-class architects (Mario Botta, Jean Nouvel, Rem Koolhaas), opened 2004. Permanent collection spans 5,000 years of Korean traditional art (36 national treasures) and blue-chip Western contemporary (Hirst, Warhol, Rothko, Yves Klein, Judd, Louise Bourgeois). Shows at Leeum carry the highest institutional prestige in the South Korean and broader East Asian art world. Increasingly showing major international technology/experiential art: **A.A. Murakami** (*Cloudwalkers*, 2022–23) is a key signal of their digital/immersive art direction.

## Institution Profile

**Name:** Leeum Museum of Art (리움미술관)  
**Operator:** Samsung Foundation of Culture (삼성문화재단)  
**Location:** 747-18 Hannam 2-dong, Yongsan-gu, Seoul, South Korea  
**Opened:** October 2004  
**Hours:** Tuesday–Sunday, 10:30 AM–6:00 PM (closed Mondays, New Year, Chuseok)  
**Admission:** 18,000 KRW general; 9,000 KRW youth/seniors  
**Website:** [www.leeumhoam.org/leeum](https://www.leeumhoam.org/leeum)  
**Status:** One of South Korea's top three private art museums

The name "Leeum" combines **Lee** (이, from Samsung founder Lee Byung-chul's family name) with Latin *-eum* (museum suffix). The museum is the primary cultural expression of the Samsung corporate empire's patronage ambitions — conceived to place Korean art and Samsung's cultural prestige on the international stage.

### Architecture — Three Buildings, Three Architects

A defining feature: three internationally renowned architects each designed a distinct building, united by Rem Koolhaas's master plan.

**Museum 1 (M1)** — designed by [Mario Botta](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mario_Botta) (Swiss):
- Houses the permanent collection of **traditional Korean art**
- 36 pieces designated **national treasures**
- Collection includes: celadon and buncheong ceramics, folk and landscape paintings, Buddhist art and manuscripts, 14th-century daggers, crowns, earrings and ornaments
- Architectural form: two large geometric volumes — a reverse cone (penetrating into the ground, forming the basement lobby) and a simple hexahedral structure
- Façade: terra cotta bricks
- Interior: visitors descend through 4 floors (ceramics → swords → jewelry → artifacts)
- Lobby: central meeting point connecting all three buildings underground

**Museum 2 (M2)** — designed by [Jean Nouvel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jean_Nouvel) (French):
- Houses the permanent collection of **contemporary and modern art** — both Korean and international
- Blue-chip permanent holdings: **Damien Hirst**, **Andy Warhol**, **Mark Rothko**, **Yves Klein**, **Donald Judd**
- Structural innovation: post-tension building technique creating completely column-free open exhibition space
- Lower levels face a sunken garden with gabion walls (iron cages filled with unearthed construction rocks) and birch trees + ferns
- This is where major international temporary exhibitions take place

**Samsung Child Education & Culture Center** — designed by [Rem Koolhaas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rem_Koolhaas) / [OMA](http://oma.eu/projects/2004/leeum-museum):
- Also created the master plan for the whole complex
- Three-story glass and steel building; uses pilotis for lighting and framing views of surrounding urban context
- Main entrance to the entire complex via glass structure
- The Black concrete box holds temporary exhibitions
- Landscape by [Petra Blaisse](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Petra_Blaisse) (Inside Outside studio, Amsterdam)

**Sculpture Garden:** Over the parking garage; rectangular strip of gravel and vegetation; has featured [Louise Bourgeois](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Louise_Bourgeois)'s *Maman* sculptures.

**Award:** 2005 Excellence in Design for Civic Architecture, Seoul Metropolitan Government

## Curatorial Focus & Permanent Collection

### Korean Traditional Art (M1)
- 5,000-year span: prehistoric through Joseon Dynasty (1392–1897)
- 36 national treasures — the densest private concentration in South Korea
- Key media: ceramics (celadon, buncheong, white porcelain), metalwork, Buddhist painting and sculpture, literati painting
- Celadon collection considered world-class; comparable to National Museum of Korea

### International Contemporary Art (M2 permanent)
- **Damien Hirst** — natural history and mortality sculptures
- **Andy Warhol** — pop/silkscreen works
- **Mark Rothko** — color field paintings
- **Yves Klein** — monochromes and Anthropometry
- **Donald Judd** — minimalist sculpture
- **Louise Bourgeois** — *Maman* (outdoor sculpture, Sculpture Garden)
- The collection signals classical blue-chip Western modernism + postmodernism — a canonical art-world language

### Korean Contemporary Art (M2 temporary + collection)
Leeum has become a primary institutional champion of Korean contemporary art internationally. Key artists:
- **Lee Bul** (이불) — radical performance, sculpture, installation. 40-year career retrospective currently showing (2025–2026). Work explores: body and social control, human-technology relations, nature/civilization tension. One of Korea's most internationally recognized contemporary artists.
- **Nam June Paik** — video art pioneer (Korean-born, globally recognized)
- Other significant Korean contemporary figures shown in temporary exhibitions

## Technology, Media Art & Ephemeral Tech

Leeum has a growing but selective engagement with technology-based and experiential art. This is not a digital art museum — it's a prestige fine art institution that occasionally commissions or hosts major technology/experiential works at the intersection of art and natural phenomena. Key signals:

### A.A. Murakami — *Cloudwalkers* (2022–2023) ← KEY

**Exhibition:** *Cloudwalkers*  
**Artist:** [[A.A. Murakami]] (Azusa Murakami + Alexander Groves), Tokyo/London  
**Venue:** Leeum Museum of Art, Seoul  
**Dates:** September 2, 2022 – January 8, 2023  
**Context:** Major solo presentation of A.A. Murakami's "Ephemeral Tech" practice — technology that works through fog, plasma, and bubbles rather than screens or LED arrays.

**About the work:** A.A. Murakami's *Cloudwalkers* is rooted in their core practice of making technology yield to physical laws — a moment when digital code hands over to fog or plasma, which then evolves according to physics. The title (*Cloudwalkers*) evokes the duo's recurring interest in cloud, atmosphere, and breath as liminal phenomena that occupy the space between presence and absence, digital and material.

**Why it matters for Leeum:** Hosting A.A. Murakami at this moment (2022) is significant — the duo was simultaneously releasing the *Floating World Genesis* NFT series on Art Blocks × Pace Verso (Dec 2022) and showing *Silent Fall* at the Royal Academy, London (Oct 2022), *Flood* at Tank Shanghai (Nov 2022), and *Wavelength* at Beijing Times Museum (July 2022). *Cloudwalkers* at Leeum was part of a major international surge in their profile, and Leeum positioned itself as the premium Seoul showcase for this wave.

**About A.A. Murakami:** Pioneer duo of "Ephemeral Tech." Works in permanent collections of MoMA New York, Centre Pompidou Paris, and M+ Hong Kong. Represented by Pace Gallery, Superblue, and Pearl Lam Galleries. The Leeum show connects them to Samsung's cultural patronage and the prestigious Hannam-dong art district, a nexus of Seoul's gallery and museum scene.

### Other Technology/Experiential Art at Leeum
- **Lee Bul** — her work extensively uses technology, cyborgism, and digital fabrication in sculpture and installation. Her survey at Leeum (2025–26) is the museum's most comprehensive recent tech-art engagement.
- The Samsung Child Education & Culture Center hosts technology-forward programming for younger audiences — potentially a pipeline for future experimental/digital commissions.

### What Leeum Is NOT (yet)
Leeum is not an institutional equivalent of [teamLab Borderless](https://www.teamlab.art/e/borderless/) or [Ars Electronica](https://ars.electronica.art/). It's a prestige fine art museum that selectively incorporates experiential/ephemeral tech as part of broader contemporary art programming. The curatorial mode is quality over quantity in the digital space.

## Circuit Analysis — What Showing at Leeum Means

**Prestige signal:** A solo or major group show at Leeum is a top-tier institutional endorsement in the East Asian art world. It signals blue-chip status, serious collecting interest, and broadens an artist's market in South Korea and East Asia. For international artists, Leeum is roughly equivalent in prestige terms to a show at Serpentine Galleries (London), Jeu de Paume (Paris), or Hirshhorn (Washington).

**Key international artists who have shown at Leeum:**
- Damien Hirst (permanent collection)
- Louise Bourgeois (permanent collection / Sculpture Garden)
- Mark Rothko (permanent collection)
- Donald Judd (permanent collection)
- Yves Klein (permanent collection)
- Andy Warhol (permanent collection)
- A.A. Murakami (*Cloudwalkers*, 2022–23 — temporary)
- Lee Bul (major survey, 2025–26)

**The circuit:**  
Leeum operates within a specific Seoul art geography. The museum is in Hannam-dong (용산구 한남동) — the premium gallery district of Seoul, also home to Galerie Perrotin Seoul, Kukje Gallery, and numerous blue-chip international galleries that have opened Seoul outposts. **Frieze Seoul** (launched 2022, held at COEX), the annual art fair, has positioned Seoul as a major art market hub, and Leeum sits at the apex of institutional prestige around it.

**Regional partners in the East Asian art-tech circuit:**
- **M+ Museum, Hong Kong** — equivalent prestige institution; A.A. Murakami's *Floating World / Beyond the Horizon* shown here (Aug 2024–Feb 2025). M+ is the primary rival/partner in the East Asian institutional art ecosystem.
- **National Museum of Modern and Contemporary Art (MMCA), Korea** — the national public institution; Leeum is its private-sector complement
- **Miraikan (National Museum of Emerging Science and Innovation), Tokyo** — science/technology museum that also hosts art; A.A. Murakami's *Passage of Ra* shown here (Jan 2024–Sep 2024)
- **Tank Shanghai** — contemporary art center in Shanghai; A.A. Murakami's *Flood* (Nov 2022)
- **Beijing Times Museum** — A.A. Murakami's *Wavelength* (July 2022)
- **Asia Culture Center, Gwangju** — A.A. Murakami's *Scenery of the Emptiness* (Dec 2022–Aug 2023)

## East Asian Art-Tech Circuit

Seoul has emerged as the third major node (alongside Tokyo and Hong Kong/Shanghai) of the East Asian contemporary art world. Leeum anchors the institutional prestige layer in Seoul.

**The Seoul art-tech ecosystem:**
- **Frieze Seoul** (est. 2022) — brings the major international gallery network (Pace, Gagosian, Hauser & Wirth, etc.) to Seoul annually in September
- **Leeum** — top-tier private museum; Samsung's cultural arm
- **LG Art Centre Seoul** — performing arts + visual arts venue; A.A. Murakami has a permanent installation here (*Passage*)
- **Paradise City Art Space** — entertainment/resort complex with significant art commissions; A.A. Murakami's *Space Symphony* (Oct 2022–Mar 2023)
- **Hyundai Motor Studio** — Hyundai's cultural arm; A.A. Murakami's *Under A Flowing Field* (Busan, 2023)
- **Kukje Gallery** — blue-chip Korean gallery representing international and Korean artists

The East Asian art-tech circuit is characterized by:
1. Corporate cultural patronage (Samsung/Leeum, LG/LG Art Centre, Hyundai/Hyundai Motor Studio) — tech conglomerates as art institutions
2. Experiential/immersive art as prestige product (teamLab in Tokyo, A.A. Murakami across the region)
3. East-West bridge: artists with cross-cultural identity (A.A. Murakami: Tokyo/London; Pearl Lam Galleries: HK/Shanghai + Western artists) find natural homes here
4. Cosmotechnics resonance: non-Western technology philosophy (Shinto, Daoist) embedded in the most prestigious tech-art works

## Fellowships, Open Calls & Opportunities

Leeum does **not** have a public residency programme or open submission process for exhibitions. It is a **curated invitation-only** institution — exhibitions are organized by the curatorial team and through gallery/dealer relationships.

**How to get in front of Leeum's curators:**
- Representation by Pace Gallery, Pearl Lam Galleries, Kukje Gallery, or other Leeum-aligned commercial galleries is the primary access route
- Participation in Frieze Seoul or Art Basel Hong Kong creates visibility in the Seoul institutional ecosystem
- Strong M+ or MMCA connections feed into Leeum's curatorial radar
- No public open calls, residency applications, or fellowship programmes identified
