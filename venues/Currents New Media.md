# Currents New Media

Annual international new media art + technology festival in Santa Fe, New Mexico. Run by Parallel Studios (nonprofit), founded 2002 as video art exhibitions, relaunched as citywide annual festival in 2010. Now in its 17th year. Free exhibitions, intimate scale (~6,200 visitors), genuine curation — not an industry showcase. A real art-science venue that accepts robotics, AI, ecology, interspecies work. [[Jiabao Li]] exhibits here repeatedly — strong signal for Amber's territory.

## What It Is

CURRENTS is produced by [Parallel Studios](https://currentsnewmedia.org/about/), a Santa Fe 501(c)(3) nonprofit. It operates year-round — exhibitions, workshops, community programs — with the annual festival as its centerpiece.

**What they show:**
- Interactive new media installations
- AI/artist collaborations
- Robotics, kinetic, and electro-mechanical installations
- Sound installations and spatial audio
- Single channel video and multimedia performance
- VR/XR environments
- Wearables
- Outdoor and site-specific installations
- Digitally generated objects

**Philosophy:** New media as a bridge between art and the general public. Not a collector/gallery market event — genuinely public-facing, free admission, community-oriented. Strong youth program (Youth Media Makers). Artists give lectures and workshops.

**Scale:** Multiple venues in the Railyard Arts District, primary venue El Museo Cultural de Santa Fe. Also Relay Santa Fe (Siler District). International artists alongside US and New Mexico-based artists.

## Programming Focus

The festival has consistent thematic threads across years:

**Ecological / more-than-human** — Jiabao Li's multispecies and interspecies work (recurring exhibitor), garden/wilderness pieces, biosensory wearables, environmental data sonification.

**Machine behavior and systems** — Adversarial networks, flocking behavior (Birds in Flight), self-organizing fields. The 2026 pre-show "Orchestrated Systems" explicitly frames machines as expressive agents that "listen, respond, repeat, drift, or resist control."

**AI ethics and digital life** — "Organising Principles of Experience" (Gabriela Tropia, 2026 selected): AI model tuned to Maya Deren's writings, explores digital resurrection, memory, identity ethics.

**Glitch, friction, slowness** — 2025 symposium: "Art & Technology in a Culture of Speed" — artists who "throw wrenches in the works, force glitches and generate friction to reveal ghosts in the machine." Critical, not celebratory.

**Sound and spatial audio** — Strong presence of spatial sound installations, environmental listening, signal-based work.

**Physics/complexity as art** — Quantum Jungle (2026): 1000+ touch-sensitive springs, Schrödinger's Equation, emergent behavior through physical interaction.
