# Hammer Museum

Contemporary art museum affiliated with UCLA in Westwood, Los Angeles. Free admission (FREE FOR GOOD since June 2025). Known for politically engaged, socially progressive contemporary programming, a strong feminist curatorial identity, and the *Made in L.A.* biennial that tracks the LA emerging art scene. Houses the Billy Wilder Theater (300 seats), 300+ public programs per year, and a distinctive artist residency model that emphasizes freedom over production. The Hammer is the most politically and critically aware major contemporary art institution in LA.

## History & Mission

Founded November 1990 by **Armand Hammer** (industrialist, chairman of Occidental Petroleum) to house his personal art collection — old master and 19th-century European paintings, a major Honoré Daumier collection. Designed by architect Edward Larrabee Barnes, built adjacent to Occidental's HQ in Westwood. Hammer died December 1990, three weeks after opening.

In **1994**, the museum partnered with UCLA, which took over management and operations. UCLA relocated its Wight Art Gallery collections and Grunwald Center for the Graphic Arts to the Hammer. The Franklin D. Murphy Sculpture Garden (on UCLA campus) came under Hammer management.

**Directors:**
- Henry Hopkins (1994–1998) — established UCLA integration
- **Ann Philbin (1999–2024)** — 25-year transformative tenure: built the Hammer Contemporary Collection (4,500+ works), developed the national/international reputation for thematic contemporary art, initiated *Made in L.A.* biennial (2012), oversaw renovation including the Billy Wilder Theater
- **Zoë Ryan (2025–present)** — incoming director, former curator at Art Institute of Chicago. Leading the institution's next chapter.

**Mission:** "Art not only has the power to transport us through aesthetic experience but can also provide significant insight into some of the most pressing cultural, political, and social questions of our time."

**Civic identity:** "FREE FOR GOOD" — free admission to all since June 2025 (previously free; now a formal commitment). Strong commitment to diversity: under Philbin, Hammer was the only LA institution that devoted 50% of solo exhibitions to female artists (2008–2012).

## Made in L.A. Biennial

**What it is:** A biennial devoted exclusively to Los Angeles–based artists. Launched 2012. One of the most significant platforms for identifying who the LA art world considers important before they break internationally. Artists from diverse backgrounds across painting, sculpture, video, installation, performance.

**History:**
- **2012** (inaugural): 60 LA artists across Hammer + LAXART + LA Municipal Art Gallery in Barnsdall Park. Organized by Anne Ellegood (Hammer), Ali Subotnick (Hammer), Lauri Firstenberg, Cesar Garcia, Malik Gaines (LAXART). Showed [[Arthur Jafa]] (visual research archive/binders).
- **2014**: Entire Hammer space; 30+ artists/collectives. Organized by Connie Butler + Michael Ned Holte.
- **2016, 2018, 2020, 2022**: Continued biennial; 2018 organized by Ellegood before her departure.
- **2024: "Acts of Living"** — 39 artists, collectives, and organizations representing a cross-section of LA. Organized by Diana Nawi, Pablo José Ramírez + Luce Curatorial Fellow Ashton Cooper. <https://hammer.ucla.edu/exhibitions/2024/made-in-la-2024-acts-of-living>
- **2026**: Expected next edition. No CFP announced as of March 2026 — watch <https://hammer.ucla.edu/> for announcement.

**Submission process:** *Made in L.A.* does not have an open call/CFP — curators select artists through studio visits, field research, and nomination. Being based in LA is required. Amber is not LA-based, so not directly applicable, but valuable as a signal of who's emerging in LA.

## Fellowships & Programmes

### Luce Curatorial Fellowship
Named after the Henry Luce Foundation. Supports emerging curators working on *Made in L.A.* biennial. Not an open application — nominated/appointed. Ashton Cooper held this role for *MiLA 2024*.

### Artist Residency Program
- **No production obligation** — hallmark of the program. Research and exploration first.
- Variable length (tailored to individual needs).
- Artists encouraged to engage with UCLA's collections, archives, and faculty.
- Hammer staff facilitate connections to LA curators, critics, artists.
- Past residents have produced films, performances, workshops, traveled the county.
- **No regular open call** found — invitational. Monitor <https://hammer.ucla.edu/artist-residencies> for announcements.

### Hammer Projects
~15 per year; laboratory-like, new work; international + local artists. Works are acquired for the Contemporary Collection. These are commissions rather than open calls.

## Circuit & LA Context

The Hammer is at the **civic/political end** of the LA contemporary art spectrum. Its circuit connects to:
- [**ICA LA**](https://theicala.org/) — Anne Ellegood moved there; similar progressive identity; smaller, more experimental
- [**LAXART**](https://laxart.org/) — long-time collaborator on *Made in L.A.*; grassroots LA nonprofit
- [**The Mistake Room**](https://themistakeroom.com/) — LA's most internationally connected contemporary space; overlapping artist circuit
- **Getty** — institutional neighbor; Pacific Standard Time initiative
- **MoCA** — institutional peer, different aesthetic (more market-oriented)
- **UCLA** — through the university partnership: faculty lectures, film archive, student programs

**International connections:** *Made in L.A.* is covered by artforum, Frieze, Art in America; brings international curators to LA to scout. Good visibility but Hammer is primarily a *local* champion, not an international mega-institution.
