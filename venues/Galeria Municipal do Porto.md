# Galeria Municipal do Porto

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-03-04:** Deep research completed. **Upgraded to ⭐⭐.** Curated heavily by **Filipa Ramos** — a key figure in interspecies, more-than-human, and ecology-focused art. Most recent accessible show (*Norte Silvestre e Agreste*, 2023–2024) was deeply aligned with more-than-human ecology: rituals of animals, plants, minerals, and the nonhuman North. Strong Serpentine/General Ecology connection through Filipa Ramos.

## What
A municipal contemporary art gallery in **Porto, Portugal**, operated by the Porto City Council. One of Portugal's most significant municipal art exhibition spaces — programming contemporary art with a curatorial ambition well beyond typical civic gallery scope.

The gallery has distinguished itself through partnerships with international curators and institutions, particularly the **Serpentine's General Ecology Programme** (via Filipa Ramos and Lucia Pietroiusti).

## Who Runs It
Municipal institution of Porto. Curatorial program frequently involves **Filipa Ramos** as curator — making this gallery one of the primary institutional homes for her more-than-human / interspecies / ecology-centered curatorial practice. The gallery also worked with **Juan Luis Toboso** as co-curator.

## Focus & Aesthetic
Contemporary art with strong currents of:
- **More-than-human ecology** — animals, plants, minerals, elements as subjects and agents
- **Interspecies / multispecies thinking** — cosmologies and rituals of nonhuman life
- **Territory and belonging** — landscapes as sites of living knowledge and memory
- **Speculative ecology** — past-present-future of land, sea, and settlement
- **Film, performance, and installation**

Aesthetic sensibility: research-intensive, philosophically grounded, poetic. Comparable sensibility to Serpentine General Ecology, Onassis Foundation, or Kunstverein Braunschweig.

## Notable Artists / Past Exhibitions
- **Fish, Part 5: "The Shape of a Circle in the Dream of a Fish"** (November 26–27, 2022) — the fifth and final Serpentine General Ecology interspecies symposium, co-curated by Lucia Pietroiusti and Filipa Ramos. Live presentations, film screenings, performances.
- **"Norte Silvestre e Agreste" (The Sylvan and Harsh North)** (December 9, 2023–March 10, 2024) — curated by Filipa Ramos and Juan Luis Toboso. Investigated the human and nonhuman inhabitants of the Iberian Northwest: meteorological patterns, myths, rituals of animals, plants, elements, and minerals. Deeply more-than-human in orientation.

## Open Calls / Residencies
No visible open calls at time of research. Programming appears to operate by invitation/curatorial appointment. Worth contacting directly if there is a relevant project, given Filipa Ramos connection.
