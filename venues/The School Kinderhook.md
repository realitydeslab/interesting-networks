# The School, Kinderhook

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-03-04:** Deep research completed. Large-scale exhibition annex operated by Jack Shainman Gallery in upstate New York; focuses on ambitious solo/group shows that can't fit a Chelsea gallery. Strong social justice, diaspora, and identity programming.

## What
A 30,000 sq ft former high school building (completed 1929, dedicated by FDR in 1931) in Kinderhook, NY (Columbia County, upstate New York), converted into an exhibition space by Jack Shainman Gallery. Opened 2014. The historic federal-revival building was renovated by Spanish architect Antonio Torrecillas (1962–2015), retaining its original colonial Dutch details. Sits on five acres with year-round outdoor sculpture. Open by appointment during installations; admission free to the public.

## Who Runs It
Operated by **Jack Shainman Gallery** (New York City). Jack Shainman is director. The space functions as an annex to the Chelsea gallery, enabling artists to realize large-scale and multi-disciplinary projects.

## Focus & Aesthetic
Large-scale, multi-disciplinary, socially engaged contemporary art. The curatorial vision emphasizes creative exploration and cultural exchange — spanning entire career surveys, unexpected cross-generational dialogues, and works that address identity, diaspora, social justice, and environmental themes. The converted school building adds symbolic resonance: former state institution of education repurposed for radical contemporary expression.

## Notable Artists / Past Exhibitions
- **Nick Cave** — inaugural exhibition 2014; nearly fifty Soundsuits and recent works
- **El Anatsui** — five-decade retrospective following Golden Lion for Lifetime Achievement (2015); traveled to Carriageworks Sydney
- **Jean-Michel Basquiat & Andy Warhol** — joint exhibition
- **Group shows** featuring Nina Chanel Abney, David Altmejd, Huma Bhabha, Andrea Bowers, Kerry James Marshall, Andres Serrano, Laurie Simmons, Hank Willis Thomas, Carrie Mae Weems, Lynette Yiadom-Boakye, Margaret Kilgallen, Barry McGee

## Open Calls / Residencies
No open calls or residency program — exhibitions are invitation-only, programmed by Jack Shainman Gallery.

## Why Interesting to Amber
Peripheral relevance only. The School is a compelling institutional model — transforming a large historic public building into infrastructure for ambitious art — with some symbolic resonance around repurposing sites of state education. However, the program focuses primarily on figurative, social justice, and diaspora art rather than Amber's interests in agent ethology, machine behavior, ALife, or speculative design. The venue lacks a technology or more-than-human dimension. **Useful to know as a reference point for institutional models** (large site + ambitious commission model similar to Echigo-Tsumari or E-WERK), but not a programming target.

**⭐ — Keep.** Strong venue and institutional model, but programming is not in Amber's orbit. Commercial gallery satellite, not a CFP/residency context. Revisit only if Jack Shainman Gallery program expands into tech/science/speculative territory.
