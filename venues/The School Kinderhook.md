# The School, Kinderhook

30,000 sq ft exhibition space in a converted 1930s school building in Kinderhook, NY (Columbia County, upstate New York), operated by [[Jack Shainman Gallery]]. Opened 2014. Purpose: showing large-scale, ambitious, multi-disciplinary artworks that don't fit in a Chelsea gallery. Open by appointment during installations. Free to public.

## Why Interesting
The School is a model for how to take a large historic building and use it as infrastructure for ambitious art that commercial galleries can't accommodate. The school → art venue transformation is also symbolically resonant (former place of state education → radical contemporary art). A potential inspiration for Amber's thinking about institutional models and residency-based research.
