# transmediale

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Deep research completed. Full history, leadership, submission paths, and network connections mapped.

Annual festival for art and digital culture in Berlin. Europe's leading platform for critical media theory and technopolitical discourse. More theoretically rigorous and politically engaged than [[Ars Electronica]].

## History & Evolution

**Origins (1988-1997):** Founded as VideoFilmFest in 1988 by Hartmut Horst and video artist Micky Kwella at Berlinale. Created to platform electronic media productions excluded from traditional film festivals. Renamed VideoFest (1989), then transmedia (1997), finally transmediale (1998) to reflect programmatic expansion into multimedia art, internet art, and software art.

**Broeckmann Era (2001-2006):** Art historian and curator Andreas Broeckmann took artistic direction in 2001, establishing Haus der Kulturen der Welt as main venue. Introduced the transmediale Award for "visionary works and projects centering technology-led societies" and extensive media art exhibition format (2002). Secured funding from Kulturstiftung des Bundes (German Federal Cultural Foundation) in 2004.

**Critical Turn (2006-2011):** Shifted focus from "international media art festival" to "festival for art and digital culture" - explicitly including everyday life and technology critique. Stephen Kovats became artistic director (2007), introducing the Vilém Flusser Theory Award in 2008 for "outstanding, media-theoretical and research-based artworks" named after the media theorist.

**Gansing Era (2011-2020):** Media researcher Kristoffer Gansing led curatorial direction, replacing existing awards with residency program for artistic research (2012). Brought "curatorial coherency" and more focused exhibition programming to counter "creative crowd" diffusion.

**Current Phase (2021-present):** Dr Nóra Ó Murchú became artistic director (2021-2024), diversifying programming with expanded curatorial teams. Current curators for 2025: Elise Misao Hunchuck and Ben Evans James. 2026 curators: Neema Githere and Juan Pablo García Sossa.

## Connection to CTM Festival

**Sister Event Relationship:** CTM Festival (music/sound) began as part of transmediale, became independent but maintains close coordination. Both festivals often run simultaneously in Berlin, sharing some venues and audiences. CTM focuses on experimental music/sound art while transmediale centers visual/theoretical work.

**Joint Programming:** Collaborative projects through "Vorspiel" pre-festival program involving network of Berlin galleries, project spaces, venues.

## Connection to HKW

**Institutional Partnership:** Haus der Kulturen der Welt serves as primary venue since 2008. HKW's mission of "strategies for better living together in and with this world" aligns with transmediale's critical approach to technology and society.

**Shared Values:** Both institutions focus on non-Western perspectives, posthuman thinking, critical engagement with contemporary challenges.
