# NEWNOW Festival

German art-technology festival that ran for one edition in 2021 at Zeche Zollverein (Mischanlage der Kokerei Zollverein), Essen. 60 international artists-in-residency over 6 weeks at a decommissioned coal mine — a UNESCO World Heritage Site — confronting the end of fossil fuel civilization through site-specific digital art. Premiered [[Kimchi and Chips]]' *Another Moon*, powered by solar panels over the former colliery. Website (newnow-festival.com) went offline in early 2023 and the domain newnow.eu was separately parked for sale. No subsequent editions found. Appears to be a compelling one-off.

## Program — 2021 Edition (August 27 – October 3, 2021)

**Headline Work:**
- [[Kimchi and Chips]] (Mimi Son & Elliot Woods, KR/UK) — *[Another Moon](https://kimchiandchips.com/works/anothermoon/)* (world premiere). 40 laser projectors powered by solar panels create a virtual second moon visible from up to 1km away. Duration each night determined by the sunshine of the preceding day — embodying a "fast/slow energy" ethic. Explicitly frames the end of the coal era.

**Featured Artists:**
- **Joanie Lemercier** (FR) — works on the industrial heritage of the Ruhr region; explored the impact of strip mining on one of Europe's oldest forests, the [Hambacher Forst](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hambacher_Forst)
- **Stine Deja** (DK) — work on the cultural significance of coal and fire for cohabitation
- **Studio Above&Below** (UK) — virtual sculptures derived from Ruhr Valley surveying data
- **Matthew Herbert** (UK, composer) × Essener Projekt Stone Techno — recording sound underground at the mine; new sounds under Tage. Live at "Immersive Sound Nights"
- **Christian Mio Loclair** (DE, media artist) — rebuilt the Zollverein Mischanlage as a virtual digital exhibition and event space ("Zeche" platform)

**Format:**
- First 3 weeks: residency producing large-scale installations on two floors of the Mischanlage (mixing plant of the coking works)
- "Immersive Sound Nights" (Aug 27 + Sept 17): live music + visual art performances
- Conference "Another End is Possible" (Sept 18–19): interdisciplinary conference on utopian societies on the moon, post-migratory colonies beyond Earth, pathways to a "new now"

**Commissioners & Funders:**
- [Stiftung Zollverein](https://www.zollverein.de/) (organizing institution)
- [RAG Stiftung](https://www.rag-stiftung.de/) — the German post-mining legacy foundation that manages obligations from the end of hard coal mining; co-commissioned *Another Moon*
- [Arts Council Korea](https://www.arko.or.kr/eng/) + [Korea Arts Management Service](https://www.gokams.or.kr/eng/) — supported the Korean artist contingent

| **NEWNOW** (Essen) | One-off | 2021 | ~60 artists | Site-specific, post-industrial, energy-ethics, commissioning |

NEWNOW's distinctive character: **site as argument**. Showing art about the end of fossil fuels *at the former coal mine that powered the Ruhr* is a curatorial statement of unusual clarity. More commission-oriented than submission-based (like Ars Electronica). Closer in spirit to [Artangel](https://www.artangel.org.uk/) than to a festival.

## Why Interesting
1. **Site-as-argument** curatorial intelligence: bringing *Another Moon*'s solar-powered light to a former coal mine made the installation significantly more meaningful. This is the kind of curation Amber's own work benefits from — context as amplifier.
2. **Energy ethics as aesthetic category**: The festival explicitly positioned "slow energy" as an artistic vocabulary. Duration tied to sunshine = ethical constraint made visible. Resonates with Amber's interest in cosmotechnics (technology as cosmological ordering, Yuk Hui) and more-than-human design.
3. **Post-industrial heritage as speculative space**: Zeche Zollverein's UNESCO status + decommissioning make it a charged site for futures thinking. The conference "Another End is Possible" about utopian societies on the moon reflects this perfectly.
4. **Korean-German institutional bridge**: RAG Stiftung + Arts Council Korea as co-commissioners is an unusual funding constellation — not just "art-tech" but cross-cultural energy politics.
5. Even if discontinued, the festival's *model* is instructive: small, commission-heavy, thematically coherent, site-specific. This is closer to what Amber's own practice aspires to produce than a submission-based megafestival.
