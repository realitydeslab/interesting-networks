Independent (scholar-led), online, libre open access journal of peer-reviewed theoretical interventions into all aspects of media and communications. No author fees. No subscription fees. CC-BY licence. Published twice per year. Indexed in DOAJ.

## What It Is

**Media Theory** (MT) is one of the few genuinely independent, non-commercial academic journals in media/technology theory. Owned by the **Media Theory Association** — a nonprofit registered in France, whose members ARE the editorial board — and published by [Trent University Library & Archives](https://www.trentu.ca/), Ontario, Canada. Not owned by Elsevier, Sage, or any commercial publisher.

It is a member of the [Radical Open Access Collective](http://radicaloa.disruptivemedia.org.uk/) — a network of scholar-led, non-commercial open access venues. All articles published CC-BY (from Vol 7.2 / 2023 onwards). Authors retain copyright.

**ISSN:** 2557-826X. Indexed in [DOAJ](https://doaj.org/toc/2557-826X).

**Website:** <https://mediatheoryjournal.org/>

## Editorial Scope

The journal pursues **deprovincialization** — disentangling media theory from a "predictable constellation of industries, disciplines, traditions, and regions." It explicitly:

- Welcomes submissions that critically engage with theoretical frameworks
- Encourages stretching the definition of "media" — philosophy, theories (plural), not just "theory" in the singular
- Interested in theory that illuminates empirical work, not theory for its own sake
- Especially keen on feminist theory, queer theory, critical race theory
- Encourages global south perspectives and emerging scholars
- Open to debates on open access, peer-review, and academic publishing itself

This is **not** a pure media studies journal — it publishes on computation, AI, temporality, materiality, speculative technologies, infrastructure, and algorithmic governance. Strong engagement with continental philosophy (Stiegler, Derrida, McLuhan are in their reference templates).

## Submission Process

- **Format:** Double-blind peer-reviewed
- **Length:** 6,000–8,000 words (including notes and references). Reasonably flexible.
- **Format:** Word (.docx)
- **Referencing:** Harvard (classic Harvard style from 2026 onwards)
- **Abstract:** ~150 words; ~5 keywords; short bio note
- **Submit to:** editors@mediatheoryjournal.org (standard submissions) or direct to guest editors for special issues
- **Special issue proposals:** Send brief outline to editors; decision + feedback within 2 weeks. Encouraged to mix solicited + CFP articles.
- **No fees:** No APCs, no subscription fees
- **Copyright:** Authors retain copyright (CC-BY)
- **Turnaround:** Not stated, but 2 weeks for special issue proposal feedback is quick

## Impact & Prestige

MT is a **niche but respected** venue in media/technology theory. It sits in the:
- Radical Open Access Collective (peer community: Mattering Press, punctum books, etc.)
- International/interdisciplinary tradition of media studies with critical theory inflection

It is NOT indexed in Web of Science or Scopus (typical of radical open access), so it may not count toward metrics-heavy tenure systems. But in the **theory-heavy humanities** and **critical media** world, it is taken seriously. Publishing here signals alignment with scholar-led, anti-commercial academic publishing — itself a political stance.

Key authors who have published here: Beatrice Fazi, N. Katherine Hayles, David Beer, Luciana Parisi, Stamatia Portanova — suggesting a high-quality intellectual community even without impact factor.
