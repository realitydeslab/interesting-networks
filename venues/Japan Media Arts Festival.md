# Japan Media Arts Festival

> ⚠️ **DISCONTINUED** — The Japan Media Arts Festival ran from 1997 to 2022 (25 editions). The 25th anniversary edition in 2022 was the final competition. A retrospective special exhibition was held in Feb 2023 at Warehouse TERRADA in Tokyo. No future editions planned as of 2026.

Premier Japanese government-funded media arts festival that ran for 25 years, recognizing outstanding works across Art, Entertainment, Animation, and Manga. Attracted ~4,000 entries annually (roughly half international) and was one of the most prestigious media arts awards in Asia. Now a historical reference, but the ecosystem it built — artists, institutions, circuits — remains alive.

## Structure & Awards

**Four divisions:**
- **Art Division** — the one most relevant to Amber. New/interactive/digital art, installations, performances, bioart, speculative works. Formerly split into "Non-Interactive" and "Interactive" until mid-2000s.
- **Entertainment Division** — video games, interactive experiences, apps, websites
- **Animation Division** — animated works
- **Manga Division** — comics/manga

**Awards within each division:**
- **Grand Prize** (Grand Prix) — top honor
- **Excellence Award** — 4 per division
- **New Face Award** (since 2011) — emerging artists
- **Social Impact Award** (since 2020) — work with societal significance
- **Jury Selection** — notable works selected without award

**Organizer:** [Agency for Cultural Affairs, Japan](https://www.bunka.go.jp/english/index.html) (government ministry)

**Scale:** 4,000–4,500 entries per year; ~50% international submissions. Open self-submission, no invitation required. No entry fee (government funded). No geographic restriction — genuinely international.

## Key People

- **[[Hiroshi Ishiguro]]** — robotics/android researcher at Osaka University; project *Alter* (2017 Excellence Award, Art Division) — android with artificial life behavior. Major figure in Japanese science-art.
- **[[Takashi Ikegami]]** — ALife researcher at Tokyo University; co-creator of *Alter* (2017) with Ishiguro. Directly relevant to Amber's ALife interests.
- **[[Daito Manabe]]** — Japanese media artist, rhizomatiks; multiple JMAF wins (*particles* 2011 Excellence, *discrete figures* 2019 Excellence). Central figure in Tokyo tech-art scene.
- **[[So Kanno]]** — Japanese media artist; *Senseless Drawing Bot* (2011 New Face), *Lasermice* (2019 Excellence). Robot/machine behavior art.
- **[[Lauren Lee McCarthy]]** ⭐⭐⭐ — American artist; *SOMEONE* (2020 Social Impact Award) — surveillance/smart home performance art. Creator of p5.js. Very relevant to Amber (human-machine social dynamics).
- **[[Sougwen Chung]]** ⭐⭐⭐ — Excellence Award 2016 (*Drawing Operations Unit: Generation 1*). Human-robot collaborative drawing.
- **[[James Bridle]]** — British artist/writer; *Dronestagram* (2013 Excellence, Art Division) — political/drone surveillance art. Very Amber-adjacent (critical technology).
- **[[Ai Hasegawa]]** — Japanese speculative designer; *(Im)possible Baby* (2015 Excellence) — speculative biodesign. Perfect example of the type of work that wins.
- **[[Meiro Koizumi]]** — Japanese video artist; *Prometheus Bound* (2021 Grand Prize, Art Division).
- **[[Ryuichi Sakamoto]]** + Daito Manabe — *Sensing streams* (2014 Excellence) — sensor/nature/data art collaboration.
- **[[Carsten Nicolai]]** — German artist (Alva Noto); *crt mgn* (2013 Grand Prize, Art Division) — generative/systemic art.

**Pattern in Art Division:** The festival consistently rewarded:
1. **Machine/robot behavior** — systems with emergent behavior, drawing bots, AI entities
2. **Bioart** — biological materials, wetware, living systems
3. **Critical/political media** — drone surveillance art, data visualization as critique
4. **Speculative design** — future scenarios involving technology and body
5. **Sound + data systems** — generative audio, sensor-to-sound work
6. **Physical computing / installations** — not pure software; embodied, spatial, haptic

International artists won consistently. Not a Japan-only prize.

## Exhibition & Venue

**Primary venue:** [Miraikan — The National Museum of Emerging Science and Innovation](https://www.miraikan.jst.go.jp/en/), Odaiba, Tokyo. Annual summer/autumn exhibition.

Also shown at:
- [NTT InterCommunication Center (ICC)](https://www.ntticc.or.jp/en/), Tokyo Opera City, Shinjuku
- [The National Art Center, Tokyo](https://www.nact.jp/english/), Roppongi
- Toured regionally within Japan (Kyoto, Nagasaki, Aichi, Okinawa, etc.)
- Toured internationally — Ars Electronica (Linz), Annecy Film Festival, Manga Barcelona, Singapore, China

## International Reach

- Genuinely international — ~50% of entries from abroad in later years
- International artists won Grand Prize and Excellence regularly (Carsten Nicolai/Germany 2013, Bryan Chung/HK 2015, Haythem Zakaria/Tunisia 2018, Adam Brown/USA 2020, Lauren Lee McCarthy/USA 2020 Social Impact, Simon Weckert/Germany 2021 Social Impact)
- Award recognized in international media arts world — listed alongside Ars Electronica, Prix Ars, SIGGRAPH art shows, FILE, etc.
- But primarily cited *within* Japan as the major national media arts honor
- International recognition: moderate-high within media arts community; lower outside it

## Connected Ecosystem (Alive Post-JMAF)

Even though JMAF itself ended, the institutions and communities it connected still operate:

- **[[NTT InterCommunication Center (ICC)]]** — <https://www.ntticc.or.jp/en/> — Tokyo's premier media arts exhibition space. Ongoing programs, residencies, exhibitions. **Highly relevant for Amber.**
- **[[Ars Electronica]]** — JMAF alumni regularly show at Ars Electronica (Linz). The two circuits overlapped extensively.
- **[[rhizomatiks]]** — Daito Manabe's studio. Tokyo's central tech-art production house. Connected to most JMAF winners.
- **[[Media Arts Database (MADB)]]** — <https://mediaarts-db.bunka.go.jp/> — Government archive of all JMAF works. Valuable research resource.
- **[[Agency for Cultural Affairs]]** — Continues other media arts programs even without JMAF. Creator support program survived: <https://creators.j-mediaarts.jp/about-en/>
- **Miraikan** — Continues as science/tech art exhibition venue without JMAF hosting
- **[[Tokyo arts-tech scene]]** — Manabe/rhizomatiks, ICC, teamLab adjacent community remains active

## Fellowships & Programmes (Historical)

## Why Interesting

JMAF was the clearest mirror of what the Japanese art establishment considered valid in media arts over 25 years. Its Art Division winner history is a **canon of the field**: Carsten Nicolai, James Bridle, Daito Manabe, So Kanno, Sougwen Chung, Lauren Lee McCarthy, Hiroshi Ishiguro + Takashi Ikegami (Alter). The overlap with Amber's aesthetic is striking — ALife, human-robot collaboration, bioart, speculative design, critical technology. 

The fact that *Alter* (an android with artificial life behavior, by an ALife researcher) won Excellence in 2017 is particularly notable: Japan's official media arts prize validated ALife-based art practice. This is the exact intersection Amber is pursuing.

That the festival ended is itself a data point — the Japanese government's appetite for this kind of support shifted. Worth understanding why (budget cuts, digital acceleration, format obsolescence?).
