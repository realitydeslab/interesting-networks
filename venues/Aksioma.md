# Aksioma – Institute for Contemporary Art

Ljubljana-based non-profit art institute (founded 2002) that produces, presents and disseminates contemporary art projects focused on critical understanding of society and new technologies. One of Europe's most intellectually sharp media-art institutions — consistently programs artists and thinkers who sit at the intersection of political theory, digital infrastructure critique, and speculative art. Punches far above its size. Strong publishing output, recurring conference series, and an international advisory board that reads like a who's-who of critical tech-art.

## Key Programs

### Tactics & Practice (recurring)
Aksioma's flagship discursive + exhibition programme. Each edition: one or more solo exhibitions + a conference + publications. Editions (numbered):
- **#12 New Extractivism** (2022) — Joana Moll, [[Vladan Joler]], Disnovation.org, Ben Grosser. Themes: data colonialism, algorithmic extraction, platform capitalism.
- **#13** — From COMMONS to NFTs (Felix Stalder et al., 2022–23)
- **#14** — Hyperemployment (2023) — !Mediengruppe Bitnik, Felix Stalder, Silvio Lorusso, Luciana Parisi
- **#16 Are You a Software Update?** (Feb 2025, Kino Šiška) — [[Alberto Toscano]], Alex Quicho, Nora O'Murchú, Noura Tafeche, [[Tega Brain]] & Sam Lavigne, Wassim Z. Alsindi. Theme: software as fascist operating system, algorithmic governance, platform totalitarianism.
- **#17 Becoming Image** (Feb–Jun 2026) — Nina Davies, Felicity Hammond, Martyna Marciniak, Alan Warburton, Simone C Niquille, Sheung Yiu, Marco De Mutiis. Theme: AI image generation, synthetic media, hyperrealism, model collapse.

### PostScriptUM
Aksioma's long-running pamphlet series (2010–2023, now anthologized). Short theoretical essays (10–30 pages) by key thinkers in digital art and culture. See [PostScriptUM Anthology](https://aksioma.org/postscriptum-anthology).

### multithread (new, 2026)
New pocket-format editorial series launched January 2026. Edited by Lea Sande and Ema Maznik Antić. Focus: artistic/critical interventions in online and algorithmic landscapes. Experimental writing, speculative fiction to political theory. 4 issues/year. Afterword by established author for each issue. Debut: [*The Blue Flower Syndrome*](https://aksioma.org/the-blue-flower-syndrome) by Benze De Ream (afterword by Metahaven).

### Project Space
Physical gallery at Komenskega 18, Ljubljana. TUE–FRI 12–18:00. Solo and group exhibitions, usually connected to a Tactics & Practice edition.

## Why Interesting for Amber ⭐⭐⭐

**Strong alignment** — upgrading to ⭐⭐⭐ based on the following:

1. **Software as machine behavior / agent behavior**: The *Are You a Software Update?* programme (2025) is precisely about understanding how algorithmic systems behave in the world — shaping participation, governance, desire. This is machine behavior as political ecology. Amber's work on agent ethology would fit directly.

2. **Becoming Image (2026)**: The current programme (Feb–Jun 2026, ongoing!) is about AI image generation, synthetic media, model collapse, AI hyperrealism — highly relevant to Amber's computational creativity / ALife interests. Artists like Alan Warburton, Simone C Niquille, Nina Davies are in Amber's adjacent space.

3. **Dark forest theory**: *Internet's Dark Forests* by Marta Ceccarelli was published here as a PostScriptUM pamphlet — and dark forest theory appears in Amber's research-taste.md as an emerging interest. Direct overlap.

4. **Swiping Right on God**: Adjacent to Amber's interest in AI × theology × mysticism (via [[Bogna Konior]]).

5. **Technologies That Scale / New Extractivism**: Infrastructure critique + more-than-human implications — connects to Amber's critical computing interests.

6. **Eastern European theoretical positioning**: Non-Western (in the European sense) angle on digital culture, fits Amber's interest in non-Western tech traditions. Ljubljana as a different vantage point than London/Berlin/NYC.

7. **PostScriptUM + multithread publishing**: Possible contribution opportunity — Aksioma publishes short, pointed theoretical texts (10–80pp). Amber's agent ethology work, machine behavior, or more-than-human design could be a strong fit for *multithread* (which explicitly seeks interdisciplinary + speculative writing).
