# MONA — Museum of Old and New Art

Australia's most adventurous contemporary art museum, built by gambling magnate David Walsh in Berriedale, Tasmania. Known for deliberately provocative and challenging art — deliberately positioned as "the subversive adult Disneyland." Architecturally stunning, built underground into a Tasmanian sandstone cliff on a peninsula on the Derwent River. Opened 2011.

MONA doesn't follow mainstream institutional trends — it makes decisions based on Walsh's personal obsessions and a genuine commitment to confronting, disturbing, and illuminating work. This has made it one of the few institutions outside Europe and North America that shapes global contemporary art conversations.

Hosted Simon Denny's *Mine* (2019) — a major installation on extraction, labor, and data capitalism, followed by tours to K21 Düsseldorf and Petzel New York.

## Why Interesting
If you want serious institutional endorsement in the Australian/Pacific region, MONA is it. Much higher prestige than any other Australian museum for contemporary work. Walsh's idiosyncratic taste means work that wouldn't fit mainstream institutional logic can get shown here.
