# MONA — Museum of Old and New Art

The largest privately funded museum in the Southern Hemisphere. Built by professional gambler and art collector [David Walsh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/David_Walsh_(art_collector)) in Berriedale, Tasmania — carved underground into a sandstone cliff on a peninsula of the Derwent River. Opened January 2011. Walsh calls it "a subversive adult Disneyland." Central themes: sex, death, and the transgressive. Not a gallery: an obsession made architectural.

MONA holds 1,900+ works, is architecturally designed by [Fender Katsalidis Architects](https://fkaustralia.com/), and is visited by 300,000+ people annually. Notable for presenting work that doesn't fit mainstream institutional logic: digestive machines, suicide bomber chocolates, reversed Guggenheim descents. The art labels are replaced by a phone app ("the O") — no wall text, only subjective response.

Runs two annual festivals: **Dark MOFO** (June, winter solstice) and **MONA FOMA** (summer, now concluded after 2024).

## Key People

### David Walsh — Owner & Founder
[Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/David_Walsh_(art_collector)). Born 1961, Hobart. Professional gambler who built a mathematical betting syndicate; used winnings to build MONA. "Rabid atheist." Obsessions: death, sex, time, consciousness. Self-educated: briefly studied mathematics/CS at University of Tasmania before dropping out. Published memoir *A Bone of Fact* (2014). Made Officer of the Order of Australia (AO) in 2016 for services to visual arts. MONA was literally built around his collection — including Sidney Nolan's *Snake* mural, which required special accommodation. Walsh is the institution; his taste **is** MONA's curatorial logic.

### Kirsha Kaechele — Co-Owner & Artist
[Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kirsha_Kaechele). Born 1976, California. Married to David Walsh (2014). Founder of KKProjects and Life is Art Foundation. Raised in Guam, Micronesia, and Japan. Informal education via 50+ countries over 7 years, mentored by Biosphere 2 creator John P. Allen, chemist Albert Hofmann, John Perry Barlow, and others. Previously ran an art space in five deteriorating houses in New Orleans. Deeply involved in MONA programming; known for major feminist and participatory works at the museum. **She is arguably the most interesting person at MONA for Amber** — artist + curator with a genuine experimental philosophy.

### Olivier Varenne — Senior Curator
MONA's lead curator. Works alongside Jarrod Rawlins and Emma Pike. Responsible for acquisitions and programming.

### Leigh Carmichael — Former Creative Director, Dark MOFO (2013–2023)
Co-launched Dark MOFO with David Walsh and Brian Ritchie in 2013. Ran festival for a decade. Stepped down 2023, retaining role as director of creative agency **DarkLab** (which manages other cultural projects in Tasmania).

### Chris Twite — Current Creative Director, Dark MOFO (2024–)
Formerly curator at [Sydney Festival](https://www.sydneyfestival.org.au/) and [Falls Festival](https://fallsfestival.com/). Appointed 2023 to lead Dark MOFO's next chapter. First full festival in 2025.

### Brian Ritchie — Founding Curator, MONA FOMA
[Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brian_Ritchie). Violent Femmes bassist. Curated MONA FOMA 2009–2024. Brought experimental music, noise, and avant-garde art to Tasmania for 15 years.

## Topics / Collection Themes
- **Death** — mummified cats, Egyptian antiquities, macabre objects
- **Sex** — explicit works, erotic art from multiple cultures and periods
- **Time** — ancient artefacts juxtaposed with contemporary provocations
- **The body** — digestive systems, flesh, biological process as art
- **Subversion** — work that challenges institutional comfort

## Key Works in Collection
- [*Snake*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Snake_(Nolan)) — Sidney Nolan (1970–72). 1,620 paintings forming a giant Rainbow Serpent mural. MONA was built to accommodate it.
- [*Cloaca Professional*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cloaca_(art)) — Wim Delvoye. Machine replicating human digestive system; excretes daily.
- *On the road to heaven the highway to hell* — Stephen Shanabrook. Suicide bomber remains cast in dark chocolate.
- *Amarna* — [James Turrell](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/James_Turrell) (2015). Permanent light installation.
- *spectra* — [Ryoji Ikeda](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ryoji_Ikeda). 15km-high light column. Permanent installation since Dark MOFO 2013.
- [*Mine*](https://mona.net.au) — [[Simon Denny]] (2019). Major installation on extraction, labor, and data capitalism. Later toured K21 Düsseldorf and Petzel New York.

## Festivals & Programmes

### Dark MOFO
[darkmofo.net.au](https://darkmofo.net.au) — Annual mid-winter festival, June, Hobart. Celebrates the southern winter solstice. Events take place at night. Key recurring elements:
- **Nude Solstice Swim** — 1,000+ people in the River Derwent at dawn on the shortest day
- **Winter Feast** — large communal food event
- **Night Shift / Slow Burn** — late-night dance parties
- Large-scale light and sound installations
- Transgressive performance, noise, metal, avant-garde music
- Attendance: 427,000 in 2023 (record). Supports major Tasmanian economy (>$5.5M in 2023).
- Government funded: Tasmanian Government committed $7.5M 2021–2024.

**Notable Dark MOFO artists/works by year:**
- 2013: Ryoji Ikeda *spectra* (permanent); nude swim inaugural
- 2015: Antony and the Johnsons, Yamantaka Eye
- 2016: Chelsea Wolfe, Tim Hecker
- 2017: Einstürzende Neubauten, Mogwai, Ulver, Grouper
- 2018: Autechre (significant: one of few Southern Hemisphere Autechre appearances)
- 2021: Santiago Sierra *Union Flag* (controversy with First Nations communities; major institutional reckoning)
- 2023: Max Richter *Sleep* (200 cots, overnight); Florentina Holzinger *A Divine Comedy* (transgressive female performance); Chen Tianzhuo *Trance* (durational performance/rave)
- 2025: Chris Twite's first full edition (June 5–21, 2025)

### MONA FOMA (concluded)
[monafoma.net.au](https://web.archive.org/web/20240317213146/https://monafoma.net.au/) — Ran 2009–2024. Summer festival curated by Brian Ritchie. Music, performance, visual art. Featured Eminent Artist in Residence (EAR) programme. **Now concluded.**

### DarkLab
Leigh Carmichael's creative agency, managing broader cultural projects in Tasmania beyond Dark MOFO. Separate from MONA's core institution.

## Fellowships & Programmes

## Why Interesting
MONA is the only major art institution outside Europe/North America that has shaped global contemporary art conversation in the 2010s–2020s. It does this through a single rule: Walsh's personal obsessions determine everything. This is both its weakness (no structural curatorial logic) and its extraordinary strength (no institutional caution).

For Amber's interests: MONA is a site of **embodied provocation** — art about the body's limits, death, sex, time, digestion, consciousness. This connects to Amber's emerging interest in mortality, machine death, and somaesthetic design. Dark MOFO specifically is a festival of **darkness, winter, transgression** — aesthetic territory close to Amber's non-Western tech theory interests and the "dark forest" thread.

MONA also proves the "small over big" principle at a geographic extreme: Hobart, population 240,000, has built the most provocative art institution in the Southern Hemisphere. The lesson is institutional courage + one obsessive backer + geographic isolation as creative advantage.
