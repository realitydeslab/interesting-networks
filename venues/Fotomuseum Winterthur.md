# Fotomuseum Winterthur

**Europe's premier institution for photography theory and critical image discourse.** Founded 1993, based in Winterthur, Switzerland (20 km from Zurich). Not just a photo museum — a theory machine that runs symposia, a research blog, and commissions philosophical essays from major thinkers. Sits at the intersection where photography meets AI imaging, machine vision, archival politics, and posthumanism. **[[Joanna Zylinska]]** presented photography theory events here, which signals that the museum engages directly with nonhuman/posthuman photography at an intellectual level that aligns tightly with Amber's computational/more-than-human work.

## Key People

**Founding/Historical:**
- **George Reinhart** — founder (1993), nephew of Swiss art collector [Oskar Reinhart](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oskar_Reinhart). Photography patron, not a theorist.
- **Urs Stahel** — first director (~1993–2010). Major figure in international photography; built the museum's theoretical reputation. Established the "Still Searching" platform and the museum's critical orientation.
- **Thomas Seelig** — longtime curator / vice director; key intellectual architect. Significantly shaped the museum's programming around photography theory, post-digital photography, and archival politics.

**Current (verify at fotomuseum.ch):**
- **Duncan Forbes** — director (appointed c. 2019–2020). Previously at [Deutsche Börse Photography Foundation](https://www.deutscheboerse.com/dbg-en/about-us/culture/photography-foundation). Background in photography history and theory.
- **Nadine Wietlisbach** — senior curator (verify current role).

**Associated Thinkers (via Still Searching blog and symposia):**
- **[[Joanna Zylinska]]** — King's College London. Nonhuman photography, AI art, posthumanism. Appeared at photography theory events at Fotomuseum. Her books *Nonhuman Photography* (MIT Press, 2017) and *The Perception Machine* (MIT Press, 2023) are exactly the intellectual register this museum operates in.
- **[Geoffrey Batchen](https://www.reading.ac.uk/art/staff/Geoffrey-Batchen)** — photography historian, wrote for Still Searching. *Burning with Desire* (1997), *Each Wild Idea* (2001).
- **[Ariella Azoulay](https://ariellazoulay.com/)** — political philosophy of photography, civil imagination. The "civil contract of photography."
- **[Charlotte Cotton](https://charlottecotton.com/)** — photography curator, formerly ICP and LACMA. Has engaged with the museum.

## Mission & Program

**Three orientations (per museum's own framing):**
1. **Art museum** — photography as contemporary art form; cutting-edge shows
2. **Historical museum** — 19th and 20th century photography masters
3. **Cultural-historical museum** — applied photography (police photography, industrial, medical, fashion, architecture)

This triple mandate is unusual — most photography museums pick one. Fotomuseum Winterthur insists on holding all three together, which means it can talk about Nan Goldin and police surveillance photography in the same institution. This is genuinely critical.

**Collection (~4,000 works):**
Artists include: [Nobuyoshi Araki](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nobuyoshi_Araki), [Lewis Baltz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lewis_Baltz), [Vanessa Beecroft](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vanessa_Beecroft), [Larry Clark](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Larry_Clark), [William Eggleston](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/William_Eggleston), [Hans-Peter Feldmann](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hans-Peter_Feldmann), [Robert Frank](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robert_Frank_(photographer)), [Nan Goldin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nan_Goldin), [Andreas Gursky](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Andreas_Gursky), [Roni Horn](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roni_Horn), [Boris Mikhailov](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boris_Mikhailov_(photographer)), [Thomas Ruff](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thomas_Ruff) — and many others. Strong emphasis on conceptual and post-documentary photography.

## Center of Photography (Joint with Fotostiftung Schweiz)

Since 2003, Fotomuseum Winterthur and **[Fotostiftung Schweiz](https://www.fotostiftung.ch/en/)** have operated as a **joint Center of Photography** on opposite sides of Grüzenstrasse. Shared: library, seminar rooms, collection storage, museum shop, bistro "George." This makes Winterthur a genuine photography hub — two major institutions, shared infrastructure, complementary missions.

- **Exhibition area:** 1,000 m² (FMW alone); 3,000 m² total shared complex
- **Location:** Grüzenstrasse 44, 8400 Winterthur, Switzerland
- **Hours:** Tue–Sun 11:00–18:00, Wed until 20:00. Closed Monday.

**⚠️ RENOVATION NOTE:** Fotomuseum closed June 2023 for major redevelopment/expansion. Planned reopening: spring 2025. As of early 2026, should be open — but verify before planning engagement.

## Still Searching — The Theory Platform

**The most important thing about this museum** (beyond the exhibitions) is **Still Searching**: an online platform/blog where Fotomuseum commissions short essays, notes, and theoretical texts from photographers, curators, artists, and theorists.

- URL: [fotomuseum.ch/en/research/still-searching/](https://www.fotomuseum.ch/en/research/still-searching/)
- Format: Short commissioned texts (not traditional blog posts) — reflections, provocations, arguments
- Topics: photography theory, post-digital image, archive, surveillance, machine vision, algorithmic images, posthumanism
- Who contributes: major figures in photography theory internationally — Geoffrey Batchen, Charlotte Cotton, Ariella Azoulay, and almost certainly Joanna Zylinska
- **Significance:** Being invited to contribute = being recognized as part of the international photography theory discourse. It's a signal of intellectual legitimacy in this field.

**Why this matters for Amber:** Still Searching is a potential entry point. If Amber develops a strong photographic/image-theory angle on computational photography, machine vision, or AI imaging — a Still Searching contribution could be both pathway and validation.

## Past Exhibitions — Relevant to Amber

Past exhibitions that connect to Amber's intellectual interests (not exhaustive):

**Machine vision, AI, computation:**
- **[Trevor Paglen](http://www.paglen.com/)** — surveillance photography, AI image classification, adversarial images. Paglen's "Invisible Images" (2016 essay; subsequent shows) directly addresses how AI systems see. This is canonical for Amber's territory.
- **[Thomas Ruff](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thomas_Ruff)** (in collection) — large-scale machine-processed/digitally manipulated images, JPEG series, ma.r.s. series (NASA satellite images). Questions what photography is in digital era.
- **[Penelope Umbrico](https://www.penelopeumbrico.net/)** — works with aggregated found photography from Flickr, Craigslist. Questions authorship, platforms, collective image-making. Major figure in post-digital photography theory.

**Archives, surveillance, political photography:**
- **[Taryn Simon](https://www.tarynsimon.com/)** — systematic photography of systems (prisons, government archives, classified sites). Forensic methodology.
- **[Harun Farocki](https://www.harunfarocki.de/)** — essay films on images and power; "operational images" concept. Critical to understanding machine vision.
- **[Hito Steyerl](https://www.e-flux.com/journal/author/hito-steyerl/)** — video essays on poor images, circulation, AI. Major figure in contemporary media art.

**Nonhuman, posthuman photography:**
- Exhibitions engaging with what photography is when it's not made for human eyes — satellite imagery, surveillance feeds, algorithmic generation. This is exactly Zylinska's *Nonhuman Photography* territory.

## Venue Circuit Analysis

**What kind of venue is this?**
Fotomuseum Winterthur is part of the "critical photography theory" circuit — distinguished from mainstream photo fairs (Paris Photo, AIPAD) by its intellectual seriousness and theoretical ambition.

**Peer institutions (the circuit):**
- **[C/O Berlin](https://co-berlin.org/en)** — Germany's leading photo exhibition space
- **[Rencontres d'Arles](https://www.rencontres-arles.com/)** — major annual photo festival (southern France)
- **[Foam Amsterdam](https://www.foam.org/)** — photography museum with theoretical program
- **[FOMU Antwerp](https://fotomuseum.be/)** — Belgium's photography museum
- **[The Photographers' Gallery](https://thephotographersgallery.org.uk/)** — London; only major dedicated photography gallery in UK
- **[ICP New York](https://www.icp.org/)** — International Center of Photography
- **[Musée de l'Élysée](https://www.elysee.ch/en/)** — Lausanne, now part of Plateforme 10

**What showing here signals:**
- You are taken seriously in international photography discourse
- You engage with photography as theory, not just aesthetics
- You're in conversation with critical image theory (not just "nice photos")
- European institutional prestige in the photography world
- Can lead to inclusion in museum programming cycles (one show leads to invitations elsewhere in the circuit)

**Who shows here:**
Artists who think about photography's politics, epistemology, and materiality — not just photographers who make beautiful images. Conceptual, political, post-documentary, research-based photography.
