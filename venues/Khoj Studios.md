# Khoj Studios

Delhi-based not-for-profit contemporary arts organization founded 1997. One of South Asia's most important sites for experimental, interdisciplinary, and critical contemporary art practice. Operates international artist residencies, exhibitions, symposia, and community programmes. Strong focus on intersections of technology, ecology, body, and society from a non-Western, Global South perspective. Thematic territory maps directly onto Amber's interests in cosmotechnics, more-than-human design, digital bodies, and art-science. **Upgrading to ⭐⭐⭐ — active institution with immediate relevance and actionable engagement paths.**

## For Amber

### Why Highly Relevant (⭐⭐⭐ justified)

1. **Cosmotechnics, lived**: Khoj is the pre-eminent site for thinking technology from a South Asian / non-Western perspective. Their "Technologies" thematic explicitly engages with *technê* — method, mechanism, device. This is cosmotechnics in practice, not just theory. Yuk Hui's cosmotechnics argument centres on non-Western technological traditions; Khoj embodies this in South Asia.

2. **Bodies, machines, publics**: The entire 2025–2026 strand directly overlaps with Amber's agent ethology and more-than-human design work. Machines not as tools but as participants. AI as affective/intimate infrastructure. The *Are You Human?* exhibition asks questions about organic/machinic porosity that Amber's work on HoloKit + machine behavior also asks.

3. **Digital Futures thematic**: Explicitly about "datafication as knowledge," "internet/digital infrastructural matrix," "technocracy," "surveillance capitalism" — these are adjacent to Amber's critical computing interests. Their take is grounded in Global South power asymmetries (digital inequality in India), which adds a non-Western lens missing from most of Amber's usual circuit.

4. **Science thematic — planetary sensorium**: The language of "planetary sensorium" and "vernacular understandings of science" is close to Amber's somaesthetic design and embodied sensing interests.

5. **International residency — actionable now**: Khoj runs annual international residencies. Amber could apply, or propose a research visit, funded through a bilateral UK-India cultural programme or directly. Being based at Oxford is a strong signal.

6. **Network**: Khoj hosts Chris Salter (who intersects with immersive/HCI arts), Vladan Joler (data infrastructure), Nishant Shah (digital life + affect + India), and collaborated with Raqs Media Collective. These are all figures adjacent to Amber's intellectual world.

7. **Global South positioning**: As someone who moves between China, UK, and US, Amber's research could benefit from a South Asian / Global South critical lens. Khoj is the entry point into the Indian contemporary art-tech scene. Raqs → Khoj → the wider South Asian experimental arts network.

## Why Interesting

Khoj is the rare institution that bridges experimental art practice, critical theory, and social engagement from a position entirely outside the Euro-American art-tech mainstream — while remaining globally connected (Tate Modern, Goethe-Institut, Ars Electronica, Somerset House Studios, PICA Perth are all partners). 

In a world where most "digital art" institutions carry the same European/North American theoretical DNA, Khoj operates from a fundamentally different set of conditions: digital inequality in India, the politics of air and water in South Asia, the body as site of both ecological and algorithmic extraction. This makes their output conceptually fresher and their questions sharper.

For Amber's research on cosmotechnics, more-than-human design, and agent behavior, Khoj provides the South Asian vantage point — a perspective as different from the Euro-American as Chinese cosmotechnics (Yuk Hui's angle), but grounded in colonial/postcolonial technological history rather than Confucian philosophy. The two perspectives complement rather than duplicate each other.

Practically: Khoj is active, well-networked, and genuinely international. There is a path for Amber to engage.
