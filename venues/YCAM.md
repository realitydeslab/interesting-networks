# YCAM — Yamaguchi Center for Arts and Media

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-03-03:** Deep research completed. Upgraded from ⭐⭐ to ⭐⭐⭐. - **2026-02-28:** Stub created via Dumb Type deep research.

Major Japanese media art center in Yamaguchi City. Founded November 2003. Run by the Yamaguchi City Foundation for Cultural Promotion. Designed by architect Arata Isozaki. Current Director: Tadayuki Miwa (since 2020).

Unique institutional model: YCAM employs a permanent in-house R&D team called **YCAM InterLab** (~20 staff — curators, engineers, educators, designers) who co-produce all commissioned works. This is NOT a typical curator-artist model. YCAM commissions artists who then *embed* with the InterLab team for residency-style production periods.

## Mission & Programs

### Core Philosophy
YCAM operates around a "Research & Development (R&D) Project" format. Every project is a long-term program from investigation → experimentation → output (artwork, workshop, software/hardware, papers). Key keywords: **"Open" and "Collaborative".**

### YCAM InterLab
- Internal R&D team of ~20 resident staff
- Functions: media tech research, original work production, open-source publication of results, exchange with external institutions, training of human resources
- Website: [special.ycam.jp/interlab/en/](https://special.ycam.jp/interlab/en/)
- All software/hardware is released open-source

### Key R&D Programs (completed but formative):

**Reactor for Awareness in Motion (RAM)** (2010–2015)
- Joint project with choreographer Yoko Ando
- Goal: develop open-source tools for creating "new body images" in dance/performance
- Published at TEI '16 conference
- Resulted in an open-source motion toolkit for dance/performance
- [Project page](https://www.ycam.jp/en/projects/ram/)

**YCAM Bio Research** (2015–2019)
- Established a wet lab (BioLab) inside an art center — extremely unusual
- Explored: bioart, synthetic biology, environmental DNA, DIY biology
- Projects: "DNA of Forests," "Genome Bento," environmental archive of Japanese gardens
- Collaborated with: SymbioticA (Perth bioscience art lab), contact Gonzo, DBCLS
- Intensive workshop series "YCAM InterLab Camp" with international guests
- Published papers, translated "BioBuilder" (synthetic biology textbook)
- [Project page](https://www.ycam.jp/en/projects/ycam-bio-research/)

**YCAM Sports Research** (2015–2023)
- Created "new sports" using media technology
- Events: Sports Hackathon, Yamaguchi Future Sports Day
- Extended to schools: Sports Hackathon for Kids
- [Project page](https://www.ycam.jp/en/projects/ycam-sports-research/)

**Guest Research Projects** (ongoing series):
- Vol. 1: ProCamToolkit — Kyle McDonald (projection mapping tools) (2011)
- Vol. 2: Duration + ofxTimeline — generative media composition tools (2012)
- Vol. 3: Tools & Algorithms for Computational Design — web-based computational design (2015–2016)

**YCAM Film Factory** (series, 2015–2018) — collaborative film production with directors

**TECHTILE** (joint project with Keio University) — haptic design. Won Good Design Award 2012.

**Korogaru Koen** (2012–) — media technology playground for children. Deployed in Yamaguchi, Sapporo, Tokyo. Won Kids Design Award multiple times.

**RADLOCAL** (2014–2018) — community design platform using media tech to address local Yamaguchi challenges. Intensive workshops (lectures, fieldwork, ideathons).

## Fellowships & Programmes

### Internship Program
- **URL:** [ycam.jp/en/recruit/intern/](https://www.ycam.jp/en/recruit/intern/)
- **Duration:** 1–3 months
- **Target:** Graduate students, young artists, researchers, technicians, coders, designers in new media art/performance
- **Language:** English or Japanese
- **Content:** Assistance in YCAM activities — planning, operation, production, R&D, workshops
- **Cost:** Unpaid — intern covers all expenses (travel, accommodation, living)
- **Apply:** At least 3 months before start date. Send to: intern@ycam.jp
- **Process:** Document screening → online interview with YCAM staff
- **Application docs:** Application form (PDF) + 500-word essay + letter of recommendation + portfolio + passport copy (non-Japanese)
- **Note:** No current open positions for staff. Internship remains open year-round.

### Artist Residency (embedded production)
YCAM does not have a formal "artist residency" open call on its website. Instead, **artists are commissioned by YCAM** to come for production residencies — they embed with the InterLab team to co-produce new works. These commissions are not open-call; they are direct invitations from YCAM curatorial staff.

### Corporate/Research Collaboration
YCAM accepts proposals from corporations and research institutions for joint R&D. This is the path for a researcher like Amber — proposing a research collaboration with the InterLab team.
- Contact: information@ycam.jp

## Why Interesting
YCAM is a model institution: it proved that an art center in a small Japanese city can punch at global weight by combining serious R&D infrastructure with an open-source, collaborative ethos. The InterLab model — permanent technical staff as co-creators, not just support — is a template worth studying. Their trajectory through body/performance (RAM), biotechnology (BioLab), community (RADLOCAL), and sports demonstrates a genuine commitment to edge-of-chaos territories. For Amber, YCAM represents both a **venue to engage** and an **institutional model to learn from**. The Dumb Type and Shiro Takatani connections make this a warm, not cold, network.
