# Nottingham Contemporary

One of the largest contemporary art galleries in the UK — a civic institution in Nottingham with a distinctly theoretical curatorial sensibility: postcolonial, ecological, feminist, and materialist. Not a technology venue, but consistently programs work at the intersection of critical theory, geology, more-than-human thought, and speculative futures. Publishes through [The Contemporary Journal](https://thecontemporaryjournal.org/), an online platform for long-form research and commissioned writing.

## What It Is

Nottingham Contemporary opened in 2009 in a purpose-built [building by Caruso St John Architects](https://www.nottinghamcontemporary.org/about/our-building/) — RIBA Award-winning, embedded in sandstone cliffs, one of the largest contemporary art spaces in the UK. It emerged from Angel Row Gallery (1991–2007) with a significant capital investment from Arts Council England. Since opening it has welcomed 2 million visitors and presented 60+ exhibitions.

**Scale:** Four large gallery spaces. Major commissioning capacity. Exhibitions frequently tour nationally and internationally (Hayward Gallery Touring partnerships, international co-commissions with institutions like The Renaissance Society Chicago, KM21 The Hague, MOCA Tokyo, Para Site Hong Kong).

**Funding:** Arts Council England (principal), strategic partners with Nottingham Trent University and University of Nottingham. Supported by private commissioning circles for each exhibition.

**Mission:** "A home for collaboration." Values: brave, open, hopeful, resourceful. Explicit commitment to equity, diversity, access.

---

## Programming Philosophy

Nottingham Contemporary's curatorial sensibility is:

- **Theoretically rigorous** — exhibitions come with extended research strands, commissions, symposia, and published essays. Not a white-box gallery for art-market work.
- **Postcolonial and politically engaged** — consistent attention to Global South practices, decolonial knowledge, race, extraction, land.
- **Ecologically oriented** — exhibitions and programmes repeatedly engage with climate, geology, material extraction, non-Western cosmologies of earth and life.
- **Archival and historical** — thematic shows pull deep history alongside contemporary commissions. Work from centuries ago shown alongside new commissions.
- **Practice-based research** — the gallery has formal research partnerships with University of Nottingham (AHRC-funded collaborative doctoral awards) and supports practice-based scholarly programming.
- **Small-to-medium scale artists** — supports artists at early and mid-career alongside historically overlooked figures. Many "first UK solo shows."

The programming is notably **non-technological** — very little tech art, no AI hype. But the critical theory framing (geology, race, ecology, matter, sound) is exactly the intellectual territory Amber inhabits.

## Notable Exhibitions (Amber-Relevant)

### **Hollow Earth: Art, Caves & The Subterranean Imaginary** (Sep 2022 – Jan 2023)
→ [nottinghamcontemporary.org](https://www.nottinghamcontemporary.org/whats-on/hollow-earth-art-caves-the-subterranean-imaginary/)

The landmark exhibition most relevant to Amber's network. 150 works by 50+ artists exploring caves, geology, underground, and the subterranean imaginary. Organized with Hayward Gallery Touring. Artists: Goshka Macuga, Robert Smithson, Kaari Upson, Lydia Ourahmane, Ilana Halperin, Gordon Matta-Clark, Pauline Oliveros, and many more.

Accompanied by **Caves, Dwellings & Vibration** — a two-day programme with keynote by [[Kathryn Yusoff]]: *"Rethinking Geologic Subjectivity in Broken Earths"* (moderated by Andrew Goffey, Director of Centre for Critical Theory, University of Nottingham). This is the direct Amber-network connection.

The research strand **Emergency & Emergence** (published in The Contemporary Journal) extends the themes: colonial extraction, critical raw materials, care, ecology, geologic subjectivity.

★ **Highest relevance to Amber.** Direct intersection with Yusoff network, geopoetics, more-than-human materiality.

### **Our Silver City, 2094** (Nov 2021 – Apr 2022)
→ [nottinghamcontemporary.org](https://www.nottinghamcontemporary.org/whats-on/our-silver-city-2094/)

Speculative futures exhibition conceived as a "sci-fi novel." Imagines Nottingham in 2094 after resource wars, flooding, plastic-eating bacteria. Artists: Jenna Sutela, Femke Herregraven, Céline Condorelli, Grace Ndiritu, Sandra Mujinga, Ben Rivers. Curatorial methodology by Prem Krishnamurthy; co-curated with Kiera Blakey, Sam Thorne, Nicole Yip.

Asks: *"How might art envision, prototype and practice new ways of being in the uncertain future?"*

★ **Strong relevance.** Speculative futures + ecological collapse + non-human timescales. Jenna Sutela (bioart/AI/alife) is an artist whose work directly overlaps with Amber's interests.

### **Sung Tieu: In Cold Print** (Feb–Aug 2020)
→ [nottinghamcontemporary.org](https://www.nottinghamcontemporary.org/whats-on/sung-tieu-in-cold-print/)

## The Contemporary Journal

→ [thecontemporaryjournal.org](https://thecontemporaryjournal.org/)

The online publishing platform of Nottingham Contemporary. Long-form research, commissioned essays, poetry, artists' writings, moving image, and sonic works. Organized by **research strands** tied to exhibitions and ongoing thematic concerns.

**Key strand: Emergency & Emergence** — tied to Hollow Earth and the Caves programme. Includes:
- [Kathryn Yusoff: Rethinking Geologic Subjectivity in Broken Earths](https://thecontemporaryjournal.org/strands/emergency-emergence/kathryn-yusoff-rethinking-geologic-subjectivity-in-broken-earths) (talk recording + transcript)
- [Postnatural Supernatural](https://thecontemporaryjournal.org/strands/emergency-emergence/postnatural-supernatural) — Helena Hunter on tree spirits, colonisation, deforestation, bird decline (Philippines)
- [Critical Raw Materials Podcast Series by FRAUD](https://thecontemporaryjournal.org/strands/emergency-emergence/critical-raw-materials-podcast-series-by-fraud) — supply chains, colonial legacies, Global South extraction
- Listening Sessions on political sound and solidarity
- Poetry, fiction, essays on care, climate, grief

**Character of the Journal:** Academic-creative hybrid. Commissions artists and theorists equally. Uses essays, poetry, moving image. The Yusoff talk is there in full. This is a serious publication platform, not PR.

**For Amber:** The Journal is a plausible submission venue. If Amber develops writing at the intersection of geology/matter/machine/more-than-human, an essay commission here is achievable — especially via the Yusoff connection.

## Open Calls / Residencies / Commissions

No standing open call or artist residency found in current research. Nottingham Contemporary commissions new works for each exhibition cycle but does not appear to run a public open-call residency programme.

**However:**
- The gallery has an active **Commissioning Circle** model — private donors fund specific commissions per exhibition. Commissions are selected by curators, not open call.
- The **University of Nottingham AHRC doctoral partnership** (Midlands4Cities) funds practice-based PhDs that collaborate with the gallery. This is an entry route — Amber could potentially co-develop a project through Oxford-Nottingham collaboration.
- **The Contemporary Journal** does commission writers and artists — this is the most accessible entry point. Submitting a speculative essay or artist text to an upcoming research strand is plausible.

**Keep watching:** [nottinghamcontemporary.org/about/work-with-us/](https://www.nottinghamcontemporary.org/about/work-with-us/) — currently no vacancies but lists include curatorial assistant, learning roles, etc.
