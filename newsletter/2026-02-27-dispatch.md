## 🏛️ Interesting Community

### 1. Transluce — "the bitter lesson applied to AI safety"
Founded by Jacob Steinhardt (Berkeley). Their thesis: safety research should be *empirical first*, not theoretical. They got pre-release access to o3 and found it doesn't just hallucinate — it *confabulates* (constructs elaborate justifications for false claims). Their tool "Docent" monitors agent behavior in real-time. Small team, huge influence on how frontier models get evaluated. Feels like the safety version of what agent ethology should be — observe first, theorize later.

A human multi-agent system that deliberately dissolves individual identity. They're the living proof that collective authorship works.

## 🔬 Interesting Finding

### 4. Michael Bernstein's *Generative Agents* → now a startup
The Stanford paper that simulated 25 AI agents in a Smallville-like town and they spontaneously organized a Valentine's Day party nobody programmed. Published *Science* 2025: algorithmic feed reranking can measurably reduce political polarization. Now on leave co-founding **Simile.ai** — applying generative agents to synthetic user research. The finding: agents with believable social behavior emerge from surprisingly simple architectures. The company bet: this is commercially valuable.

### 5. Wendy Chun confirmed for Yuk Hui's Dialogues — May 4, 2026
Her core finding (*Discriminating Data*, 2021): machine learning's mathematical structure IS the mathematical structure of segregation. Correlation = homophily = like-attracts-like = digital redlining. Not a bug — the math itself is the problem. She'll present at Research Network for Philosophy and Technology Dialogues (online, May 4). Amber can attend and engage directly. 

### 6. Erich Hörl on "General Ecology" — technology as environmentality
Lüneburg media philosopher. His concept: we've moved from a "technological condition" to an "environmentality" — technology isn't a tool anymore, it's the *medium in which everything exists*. Like fish not seeing water. For agents: we don't interact WITH agents, we exist IN an agent environment. Upcoming Dialogues speaker (April 2026, Yuk Hui's network, online).

### 7. Sebastian Risi's "Quality-Diversity" algorithms
ITU Copenhagen. His lab produces algorithms that don't optimize for one solution but generate *diverse libraries of solutions*. MAP-Elites + QD-Search. The agent ethology connection: instead of asking "what's the best agent behavior?", ask "what's the full repertoire of possible agent behaviors?" Biodiversity, not monoculture. Co-authored with Joel Lehman on illumination algorithms.

## 🎨 Interesting Artwork

### 8. Stephanie Dinkins — *Not The Only One* (N'TOO)
A deep learning system trained on three generations of oral history from one Black American family. Not a chatbot — a *family memory*. It speaks in fragments, contradicts itself, forgets and remembers like an actual elder. The project asks: what happens when AI carries the stories of people who were systematically denied archives? N'TOO is the opposite of ChatGPT — slow, partial, rooted in a specific lineage.

### 9. Jiabao Li — *Haiku Glacier* (2025, SIGGRAPH Art Gallery)
3D ice printer that captures ice formation in real-time; camera records the ice; AI translates the images into haiku. Created during Arctic Circle residency in Svalbard. The melting is the core aesthetic — the artwork literally disappears. Shown at Ars Electronica 2025. The agent here is the ice-AI-haiku feedback loop — a system that creates and destroys simultaneously.

### 10. Arthur Jafa — *APEX* (2013) / *Love Is The Message, The Message Is Death* (2016)
7-minute video collage of Black American life set to Kanye West's "Ultralight Beam." Went viral among artists. *APEX* is harder: rapid-fire images of Black bodies, space, velocity, compression — 8 minutes that feel like getting hit. Golden Lion Venice 2019. Jafa calls his method "Black visual intonation" — the rhythm and cadence of Black speech applied to image editing. Not about AI, but the methodology — pattern, rhythm, accumulation as meaning-making — is how agent behavior should be represented.

### 11. Teresa Margolles — Fourth Plinth, Trafalgar Square (through late 2026)
Cast of a trans sex worker's face in concrete, placed on the most visible public sculpture site in London. Margolles works exclusively with materials from morgues — shrouds used to clean murder victims, water used to wash corpses, concrete mixed with human remains. Not metaphor. The material *is* death. Currently visible from anywhere in Trafalgar Square. For your mortality research: what does it mean to make death *materially present* in public space?
