## 🔥 Top Finds

### 1. Raqs Media Collective — *Cavalcade* (2025): AI agents through Indic cosmology
Delhi trio built an AI artwork using Sufi temporality and Indic cyclical time instead of Western linear autonomy. Golden Lion Venice 2022. Their concept of "seepage" — how agents leak across ontological boundaries — is more useful than "autonomy" for describing what agents actually do.
→ [Raqs Media Collective](https://ryvfrzo.net/)

### 2. Saodat Ismailova — Chilltan: 40 bodies as one entity
Uzbek filmmaker working with Central Asian spirit beings that are simultaneously one and forty. Not metaphor — lived Sufi cosmology. For multi-agent systems: when does a swarm become a being? Le Fresnoy + EYE Film Prize.
→ [Saodat Ismailova](https://www.saodatismailova.com/)

### 3. Vinciane Despret — ethology as methodology for AI
Belgian philosopher who asks: "What would happen if we let animals ask us questions?" Her approach — treating nonhuman subjects as capable of surprising you — is the methodological foundation agent ethology needs. Was visiting professor at Le Fresnoy.
→ Book: *What Would Animals Say If We Asked the Right Questions?* (2016)

### 4. Bernhard Siegert — "Cultural Techniques": behavior creates concepts
German media theory. A door creates the concept of inside/outside, not the other way around. Applied to AI: agent behaviors CREATE the concepts of intelligence, autonomy, sociality — not vice versa. Theoretical framework that makes agent ethology rigorous. Bernard Geoghegan at KCL is the English-language bridge (1hr from Oxford).
→ Book: *Cultural Techniques* (2015)

### 5. Simondon's individuation → agents aren't things, they're processes
Gilbert Simondon (1958): entities aren't fixed — they're ongoing processes of becoming. An agent isn't a thing that *has* behavior, it's a process that *is* behavior. Erin Manning's Inflexions journal (800+ participants) was built on this. The 3 Ecologies Project continues it.
→ Book: *On the Mode of Existence of Technical Objects* (1958/2017)

## 🌏 Non-Western Agent Frameworks

### 6. Dipesh Chakrabarty — "agency" is a provincial concept
UChicago historian. *Provincializing Europe*: Western categories (agency, autonomy, individual) aren't universal — they're local. His "History 1 vs History 2" (dominant vs subaltern temporalities coexisting) maps onto how different agent architectures produce different temporalities. Also at UChicago → James Evans connection.

### 7. Denise Ferreira da Silva — difference without separability
Brazilian philosopher. Entities exist in relation without being reducible to individuals. Uses quantum physics metaphors from Black/Indigenous Brazilian thought. For agents: *entangled* rather than *autonomous*. Breaks the assumption that agency requires a bounded self.

### 8. Nils Bubandt — spirits as political agents
Tsing's collaborator. In Indonesian cosmology, nonhuman agents (spirits, ancestors) are recognized political actors with rights and obligations. Not "animism" — a functioning multi-agent governance system running for centuries.

### 9. Eduardo Viveiros de Castro — Amazonian "multinaturalism"
One culture, many natures. Inverse of Western multiculturalism. Implies: one AI architecture, many kinds of agency. Haven't deep-researched yet — could be a stub.

### 10. Reza Negarestani — intelligence as normative navigation
Iranian philosopher. *Intelligence and Spirit*: intelligence isn't computation — it's self-revision through commitment to norms. Agents become intelligent by *navigating between commitments*, not by processing data. Persian/Islamic philosophy + German Idealism.

## 🧬 Methodological Insights

### 16. Paul Dourish's "accounting practices" → agent ethnomethodology
2025 SIGCHI Lifetime Achievement. His ethnomethodological approach to HCI asks: how do participants *make sense of* their own actions? Applied to agents: how do agents account for their own behavior to other agents? Steve Benford co-edited TOCHI with Dourish in 2001 — warm bridge.

### 17. Kristina Höök's "Strong Concepts" (793 citations)
The most-cited design research paper. "Strong concepts" are intermediate-level knowledge — not as abstract as theory, not as concrete as a prototype. "Agent ethology" could become a strong concept. Höök is the soma design founder and just swept SIGCHI 2025 awards.

### 18. Anna Cox's "design frictions" → "agent frictions"
UCLIC, 1hr from Oxford. Design frictions = intentional resistance in interfaces. What are "agent frictions" — intentional resistance in agent-agent or agent-human interactions? Open collaboration question.

### 19. Forensic Architecture — counter-forensics as method
Eyal Weizman's lab at Goldsmiths (London). They reverse-engineer evidence from state violence using spatial analysis. The methodology — treating architecture/media as witnesses — could be applied to AI agent systems. "Forensic agent ethology": reconstructing what happened in an agent system from its traces.

### 20. Stephanie Dick — "intelligence deflation"
Historian of computing. Every time a task gets automated, it stops being called "intelligence." MACSYMA paper on how AI remakes the expert. The concept of intelligence deflation reframes the entire AI capability debate.
