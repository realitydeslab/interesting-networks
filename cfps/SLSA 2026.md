### Theme Description

The conference invites scholars, artists, writers, scientists, and practitioners to critically engage with **epistemic justice** and the emergence of **Alterscience**. At a moment when climatic, social, and political crises destabilize established structures of knowledge, SLSA 2026 seeks to:

- Reimagine what counts as valid knowledge
- Amplify epistemologies historically marginalized or excluded
- Construct new, plural, and inclusive intellectual futures
- Bring together humanities, social sciences, natural sciences, arts, and emerging technologies
- Rethink how knowledge is produced, legitimized, and transmitted
- Examine ethical, political, and ecological consequences of knowledge systems

The conference foregrounds **plural, insurgent, Indigenous, Afro-diasporic, migrant, refugee, feminist, queer, and Global South knowledges**.

Organized by USP in partnership with:
- Graduate Program in Humanities, Rights and Other Legitimacies (PPGHDL/FFLCH/USP)
- Brazil–Africa Research Center (NAP Brasil África/USP)
- Intercultural Dialogues Research Group (IEA/USP)
- Bibli-ASPA (South America–Africa–Arab Countries Research and Cultural Center)

## Why Relevant to Amber

The theme opens multiple entry points for Amber's work:

**Strong fit — epistemic justice framing:**
1. **Machine Death** — what does it mean for knowledge systems (AI, agents) to "die"? Epistemic implications of machine mortality. Can machines be epistemic subjects? Frame via Indigenous knowledge traditions meeting AI temporality.
2. **Agent Ethology as Alterscience** — observing AI behavior as a practice from outside mainstream CS. Positions Amber's work as challenging hegemonic ways of knowing AI.
3. **Cyborg Sociology meets Global South** — cyborg theory has been critiqued for its Northern/Western centering. A response from the Global South context is ideal for this venue.

**Moderate fit:**
- More-than-human design — posthumanism frame is there but weaker connection to epistemic justice theme
- HoloKit as decolonial hardware — open-source MR for global accessibility?

**Best submission:** "Toward a Thanatology of Artificial Agents: Machine Death, Epistemic Justice, and What It Means When AI Systems End" — connecting machine mortality to questions of who decides when AI is alive or dead, whose knowledge counts in AI governance.

**Secondary option:** Panel proposal — "Alterscience and the More-than-Human" — curate 3–4 papers from Amber's network (Helena Rong, Danlin Huang?) around machine behavior, posthumanism, and non-Western tech philosophy.
