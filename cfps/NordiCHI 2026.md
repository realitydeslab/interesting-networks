## What

NordiCHI 2026 invites submissions across 10 tracks spanning the full breadth of HCI research. Conference theme is **Pulse — exploring the energy that drives us**: rhythms of life, dynamic and calm, digital and natural. Held in Vaasa, a coastal Finnish city known as the Nordic energy cluster.

**Proceedings:** ACM Digital Library (ICPS series). Most tracks published as full papers; LBW/demos/arts-based published as Extended Abstracts (Open Access, no APC).

In-person only — no hybrid or remote attendance.

## Track Details

### 📝 Research Papers (deadline April 23)
Full and shorter papers across all HCI areas. NordiCHI "has traditionally highlighted human-centred, values-driven work that bridges design inquiry and empirical study, often engaging societal contexts (e.g., health, education, sustainability, fairness), participatory methods, and critical/reflective perspectives." Technical work without HCI contribution is out of scope.
- Abstract + metadata due April 16 (earlier!)
- Dual anonymous review. No rebuttal.
- Chairs: Duncan Brumby (UCL), Teresa Hirzle (Copenhagen)
- CFP: <https://sites.uwasa.fi/nordichi2026/nordichi-2026/submit/papers/>

### ✍️ Critiques (deadline April 23) ← **AMBER'S BEST BET**
Explicitly for work "outside the scope of regular papers." Two submission types:
- **Critical writings** (≤8,000 words): speculative essays, provocations, alternative arguments. Assessed on ability to "critically examine a topic, inspire, and/or spark debate." NOT evidence-based — visionary and speculative writing explicitly welcomed.
- **Critical visualizations** (500-word abstract + documentation): theater, role-playing, live experiences, digital art with critical framing.
- Alternative formats encouraged. Contact chairs for unusual ideas.
- NOT required to use ACM template for visualization submissions.
- Chairs: Marta Lárusdóttir (Reykjavik), Johanna Ylipulli (U Vaasa), Maria Normark (Uppsala)
- CFP: <https://sites.uwasa.fi/nordichi2026/nordichi-2026/submit/critiques/>

**Example accepted critiques (NordiCHI 2024):**
- "UDREDNING: an Extended Reality Artwork for Affectively Engaging with Diagnosing Processes" (Fritsch, Gad). [doi:10.1145/3677045.3685491](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3677045.3685491)
- "Zine for Feminist Design of Reproductive Technologies" (Campo Woytuk et al.). [doi:10.1145/3677045.3685497](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3677045.3685497)

### 🎨 Arts-Based Approaches (deadline April 23)
Exhibition in the **Wasa Innovation Center Catacombs** (former 1800s brewery, subterranean chamber atmosphere). Presented during conference dinner, October 6.
- Accepts: interactive installations, video-based artworks, art-based research methods, performances, theoretical papers on arts-based HCI
- 2026 theme focus: "energy" (sustainability, digital/physical, temporal rhythms)
- Extended abstract 1,500-2,000 words + technical requirements
- **NOT anonymized** (identity of artist is part of the submission)
- No APC — published as Extended Abstract under ACM Open Access
- Chairs: Rebekah Rousi, Johanna Silvennoinen, Anne Scott Wilson
- CFP: <https://sites.uwasa.fi/nordichi2026/nordichi-2026/submit/arts-based-approaches-in-hci/>

### 🗂️ Late-Breaking Work (deadline May 18)
Work-in-progress, preliminary findings, provocations, open questions. Presented as **posters at Monday evening reception** (October 5). ≤5,000 words, double-blind. No APC (Extended Abstract in ACM DL).
- CFP: <https://sites.uwasa.fi/nordichi2026/nordichi-2026/submit/late-breaking-work/>

### 🛠️ Workshops (deadline May 7)
Half-day or full-day. Pre-conference, October 3-4. At Åbo Akademi University campus in Vaasa. "Proposals that engage with other disciplines and traditions, as well as with industry, art, activism, and other practices to advance constructive and critical discussions are particularly welcome." Black box theater room available.
- Notification fast: May 18.
- CFP: <https://sites.uwasa.fi/nordichi2026/nordichi-2026/submit/workshops/>

## Why Relevant

### Amber's Fit: Direct

**1. Critiques ← Machine Thanatology / Terrified Agents**
NordiCHI's Critiques track is designed for *exactly* the kind of work Amber does: machine death as a critical HCI provocation, agent mortality as a design constraint. The track explicitly welcomes "provocative, assertive, or speculative" writing that is "visionary rather than evidence-based." The template examples from 2024 (XR artworks engaging with diagnosing processes, feminist zines for reproductive technologies) show what "critiques" means in practice.

**2. Arts-Based Approaches ← Any of Amber's computational art projects**
Body Oracle, agent-based speculative installations, or new work could be proposed as an arts-based contribution. The Catacombs venue (subterranean chambers, former brewery) is a genuinely atmospheric exhibition space. The session is explicitly designed for "artists, designers, researchers, and practitioners" — Amber straddles all four. 

**3. Research Papers ← Agent Ethology (in-the-wild frame)**
NordiCHI's "Understanding People" track explicitly welcomes "in the wild" studies. Agent Ethology's observational methodology — studying agents as they actually behave in deployment — mirrors the "in the wild" tradition that Yvonne Rogers (the keynote speaker!) pioneered. This is a strong framing angle.

**4. Somaesthetics community (via [[Kia Höök]])**
NordiCHI is where the somaesthetics-in-HCI community gathers. Amber teaches Somaesthetic Realities at China Academy of Art. NordiCHI 2026 would be the natural place to introduce this work to the people who built the concept.
