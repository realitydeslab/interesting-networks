## Scope (Critical for Amber)

CSCW explicitly states papers must focus on **social aspects of technology mediation** — groups, organizations, communities, networks.

**In scope:**
- Social and crowd computing (social media, wikis, collective intelligence, crowdsourcing)
- Critical/historical/ethnographic analyses of sociotechnical systems
- Ethics and policy implications of computing systems
- CSCW systems with emerging technologies (AI, robotics, AR/VR) *when contextualized in social/cooperative framing*
- Theoretical frameworks for social computing

**⚠️ EXPLICITLY OUT OF SCOPE:**
> *"Research on a collaboration between a single user and any number of AI agents"* is out of scope.

This is a critical framing constraint for Amber's work. **Machine behavior as a social phenomenon at collective scale** fits. Single-user-AI interaction does NOT.

**How to frame Amber's work for CSCW:**
- Frame agents as social actors in multi-agent collectives, not as tools for individual users
- Emphasize emergent social structures, norm formation, group dynamics *among* agents
- Connect to CSCW literature on social computing: collective intelligence, crowd behavior, sociotechnical systems
- Agent ethology → studying agents as a social/ecological population (Lorenz tradition applied to AI)
- Machine behavior as social science: how do multi-agent systems behave collectively, what are the social implications?

## Strategic Fit for Amber

**Overall fit: ⭐⭐⭐ — but requires careful framing.**

CSCW is the right venue for Amber's work on **machine behavior as social science** and **agent ethology** — IF framed at the group/social/collective level, not individual user-AI interaction.

### What Works
1. **Agent ethology as social science** — studying AI agent populations using methods from ethology, ecology, sociology. This fits CSCW's empirical/theoretical tracks perfectly.
2. **Multi-agent social systems** — emergent collective behavior, norm formation, trust networks among agents. CSCW has embraced AI/ML/robotics work when it's socialized.
3. **Trustworthy agentic web** — distributed AI infrastructure, collective implications, governance. Connects to CSCW's ethics/policy track.
4. **Decentralized AI as social infrastructure** — protocols, coordination mechanisms, collective intelligence. Perfect CSCW territory.
5. **Machine behavior as observable social phenomenon** — the Rahwan et al. "Machine Behaviour" Nature paper (2019) frames this as a new scientific field; CSCW is where it plays out empirically.

### What Needs Care
- **Avoid:** framing as "how AI helps individual users"
- **Avoid:** purely technical/systems contributions without social context
- **Invoke:** CSCW literature! Must cite CSCW community work (Grudin, Ackerman, Dourish, etc.)
- **Frame:** agents as collective social actors, not individual assistants
