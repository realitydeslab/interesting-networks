# CFP: Fantastic Futures 2026 — Trust in the Loop

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-27:** Note created. Proposals due April 6. ~5 weeks out.

## What
Annual AI4LAM conference, 2026 edition. Theme: **"Trust in the Loop"** — extending "human in the loop" to focus on how LAMs (Libraries, Archives, Museums) ensure AI-enabled services are built on trust, authenticity, and accountability.

**Conference dates:** September 15–17, 2026, Washington DC  
**Co-hosts:** Library of Congress, National Gallery of Art, Smithsonian Institution

**Topics:**
1. **Implementations** — governance, risk, scaling, AI decision-making frameworks
2. **Data** — quality, provenance, MCP-based data management, policies for scraping/training data
3. **People** — user engagement, staff training, community consent, stakeholder management
4. **Models & Infrastructure** — shared infrastructure, open tools, incident reporting, cost management

**Submission types:** Lightning talk, Short paper, Long paper, Panel, Workshop

## Deadline
**⚠️ April 6, 2026** (~5 weeks from note creation)

Submission portal opens in March. Sign up to [AI4LAM email list](https://groups.google.com/g/ai4lam) for portal link.

## Why Relevant
"Trust in the Loop" = trustworthy AI = core Amber territory. Specific hooks:

- **Trustworthy agentic systems in cultural heritage contexts** — how do AI agents behave when processing archival knowledge? What accountability structures are needed?
- **Protocol studies angle** — how do archival protocols (provenance chains, chain of custody, authority records) interact with AI? AI as disruptor of epistemic authority.
- **Machine behavior in high-stakes institutional contexts** — archives are among society's most trust-sensitive institutions.
- **Content authenticity / provenance** — AI-generated content vs. authentic records: how do LAMs detect, label, preserve provenance?

## Amber's Options
- **Lightning talk or short paper** — lowest friction. Could repurpose existing work on trustworthy AI / agent accountability for a GLAM-adjacent audience.
- **Workshop** — higher effort but more influence. A workshop on "Machine Behavior in Archival Contexts" could open collaboration with the GLAM research community.
