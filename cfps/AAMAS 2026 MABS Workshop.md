# CFP: AAMAS 2026 — MABS: 27th International Workshop on Multi-Agent-Based Simulation

> **Status: EXPIRED for 2026** — workshop paper deadline was Feb 4, 2026 (recommended). **Flag for 2027** — this is a direct Amber target. 27th edition in 2026 = extremely established series.

## What

MABS is the oldest social simulation workshop at AAMAS — **27 editions** makes it one of the longest-running interdisciplinary workshops in AI. It explicitly bridges **social science theory and multi-agent computational models**: using ABM (Agent-Based Modeling) to study social phenomena, and using social theory to inspire new MAS frameworks.

The workshop's core thesis: social systems (from economics to ecology to political science) can be understood through agent-based simulation, and simulation results yield real scientific insight into social dynamics.

**2026 Themes:**
- Simulation methodology and tools
- Emergent social and intelligent behaviour
- Social theory as MAS design inspiration
- Complex social and sociotechnical system modeling
- Cross-fertilization of MAS engineering and social modeling
- Simulation analytics (turning simulation output into insight)
- Diverse application domains

**Format:** One-day workshop, May 26, 2026, Paphos, Cyprus

## Why Relevant

MABS is where **agent ethology meets social simulation**. The workshop's emphasis on empirical observation of emergent social behavior in agent populations is methodologically adjacent to what Amber does when she observes Sovereign/Parasitic/Commensal patterns in AI agent ecologies.

**Key parallels:**
1. **Agent-Based Modeling tradition** — ABM researchers model emergent social phenomena by simulating agent interactions. Amber *observes* emergent social phenomena in real AI agent ecologies. Her work is "ABM in reverse": not building simulated societies but observing naturally-occurring ones.
2. **Schelling segregation / Nowak & May spatial dilemmas** — classical MABS papers study how social structures emerge from simple agent rules. Amber's Parasitic Agents paper shows how exploitative strategies emerge without explicit design in open ecologies.
3. **Cross-fertilization goal** — MABS explicitly wants to cross-pollinate CS × social science. Amber's cosmotechnics/more-than-human lens is exactly the kind of social/humanistic perspective MABS is inviting in.

**Paper angle for 2027:**
> *"From Simulation to Field Observation: Agent-Based Social Dynamics in Open AI Agent Ecologies"* — reframe Amber's empirical work (Spore in the Wild, Sovereign/Parasitic agents) as *field observation* data that complements and validates ABM social simulation models. Position agent ethology as an empirical methodology that MABS researchers can use alongside their computational models.
