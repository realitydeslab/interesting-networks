## What Is transmediale?

transmediale is the leading international festival for art and digital culture, held annually in Berlin since 1988. It takes the form of:
- A **festival** (conference-style discourse: lectures, panels, workshops, performances)
- An **exhibition** (curated artworks and installations)
- A **film and video programme**
- Year-round activities: residencies, publications, the transmediale/journal, and the Vorspiel pre-festival programme

Funded as a cultural institution of excellence by the **Kulturstiftung des Bundes** (German Federal Cultural Foundation) since 2004. Organised by **transmediale e.V.** (became independent in 2020, previously under Kulturprojekte Berlin).

Key sibling event: **CTM Festival** (music and sound, co-runs alongside transmediale).

## transmediale 2026 (39th Edition) — What Happened

**Theme:** *By the Mango Belt & Tamarind Road: Compassing, Protocoling, Metaphoring*  
**Dates:** 28 January – 1 February 2026  
**Venues:** silent green Kulturquartier, CANK (Neukölln), Säule (Berghain), transmediale Studio  
**Curators:** Neema Githere and Juan Pablo García Sossa

### Curatorial Vision (tm26)

The festival reimagined the tropics as a network of exchange — "a living recursive carrier net" stretching across latitudes, rhythms, and systems. The theme was structured around three methodological gestures:

- **Compassing** — re-orienting and pacing algo-rhythms; moving with currents rather than seeking fixed optimisation. Netting Groups formed in Pasifika, Swahili Coast, SEA, and Abya Yala, meeting IRL and URL before the Berlin gathering.
- **Metaphoring** — bypassing inherited logics through stories and carriers; re-coding through imagery and relation.
- **Protocoling** — translating situated practices into technological protocols; developing "pocket infrastructures," holding-structures, and hanging-structures for collective use and reciprocity.

**Key intellectual references:**
- Ben Okri, *The Famished Road* (1991): "In the beginning there was a river…"
- Ursula K. Le Guin on technology as "active human interface with the material world"
- Malidoma Somé on non-Cartesian, non-Newtonian parallel technological pathways
- Ubuntu proverb: "If you want to go fast, go alone. If you want to go far, go together."

**Intellectual lineage:** Decolonial tech studies, non-extractive infrastructure, relational protocols, tropical/southern epistemologies, non-Western cosmologies of technology, critical data studies.

**Programme types (tm26):** Lecture-performances, sound sessions, workshops, conversations, screenings, installations, netting groups, evening music/club nights with CTM, Vorspiel pre-festival events.

**Atmosphere:** Hands-on, sensory, community-oriented. Food as relational technology. Screenprinting. A "dance tournament." Collaborative methodologies.

## transmediale 2027 (40th Anniversary) — What We Know

### Festival Curator: Barbara Cueto

Announced **9 February 2026**. Selected through transmediale's annual open call for festival curation (launched in 2025; closed 30.11.2025). Selected by advisory board: **Nora Al-Badri, Asia Bazdyrieva, Gabriele Horn, Jussi Parikka**.

Barbara Cueto is a Berlin-based curator, writer, and researcher. Her practice connects long-term research with public encounters, placing historical lineages of critical technological practice in dialogue with contemporary experimentation.

**Barbara Cueto's vision (direct quote):**
> For the 40th edition, I envision transmediale as a lattice, a structure that provides the scaffolding upon which complexity builds. A lattice is porous, holding by what it connects and by what it leaves open, carrying weight through relation. These apparently blank spaces sustain the political imagination of digital culture, where other futures can be rehearsed rather than predicted. This edition builds a counter-infrastructure that connects practices, stories, and communities across four decades, treating technology not as a fixed system but as a shared space of imagination.

**Advisory board comment:**
> Barbara Cueto presents a consistent approach that reflects transmediale's earlier iterations, while adding new insights into how to celebrate the anniversary festival. She is well attuned to key Berlin communities with the capacity to establish relevant collaborations beyond those already existing. Her proposal contributes to the festival's commitment to creating an inclusive, vibrant, and complex ecology of thought and praxis.

**Key concepts for tm27 festival:** lattice, counter-infrastructure, political imagination, digital culture, four decades of history, shared space of imagination, futures rehearsed not predicted.

### Exhibition Curator: Antonina Stebur

Announced **October 2025** (open call deadline: 6 August 2025). Exhibition will be held at **Akademie der Künste (Hanseatenweg)**, Berlin, opening end of January 2027, running approximately **6 weeks**.

Antonina Stebur is a curator and researcher who explores contemporary art as a tool for infrastructural and political imagination. She is Editor-in-Chief of *AWC Journal* and founder of *Mycelium* [Грыбнiца], a decolonial research lab. Previous work includes *What Are Our Collective Dreams?* (2025, Warsaw), *If Disrupted, It Becomes Tangible* (2023, Vilnius), *Sense of Safety* (2024, Kharkiv). Contributed to documenta 15, Manifesta 14, ZKM, Theatertreffen Berlin.

**Antonina Stebur's vision (direct quote):**
> My curatorial and research practices look at how infrastructures – digital, social, or emotional – shape power, exclusion, and fragile solidarities, but also open spaces for collective imagination. For transmediale 2027, I propose an exhibition that reframes the festival's 40th anniversary through the lens of ruins of infrastructure as a method. Rather than celebrating achievement, it lingers within breakdowns, the militarisation of everyday systems. I would like to rethink transmediale itself as a configuration, not only a site of representation, but as a framework of imagination for the futures we urgently need.

**Advisory board framing of her proposal:**
> The proposed key ideas of failure and infrastructure, ruins and glitches, connect with Berlin's own urban history, thereby situating transmediale's history within these broader contexts. The exhibition concept resonates with transmediale's core themes - criticality, grassroots resistance, and the interrelation of technology and politics - while also adding a bold, imaginative dimension through the invitation to train our "utopian muscles".

**Key concepts for tm27 exhibition:** ruins of infrastructure, failure, breakdown, militarisation of everyday systems, glitches, Berlin urban history, utopian muscles, political imagination.

**Structural note:** Starting 2027, transmediale introduces a more sustainable rhythm with a major exhibition every two years.

## Structure: How transmediale Works

### The Festival (Annual)
- **Discourse programme:** Panels, lectures, conversations, workshops (curated by festival curator + team)
- **Artistic programme:** Performances, lecture-performances, sonic experiments
- **Film/video programme:** Curated screenings
- **Evening programme:** In collaboration with CTM Festival

### The Exhibition (Every Two Years, Starting 2027)
- Curated by separate exhibition curator
- Held at Akademie der Künste or HKW
- Runs several weeks post-festival

### Year-Round
- **transmediale/journal** — online publication; Issue #7 released Jan 2025. Previously published themed issues (e.g., "#scale"). Publishes essays, texts, theoretical pieces.
- **Residencies:** 
  - Vilém Flusser Residency for Artistic Research (in partnership with UdK Berlin)
  - Digital Art Residency (in partnership with various international partners)
  - International residencies (e.g., ARKO/Korea, Pro Helvetia/Switzerland, Chile)
  - Applications typically open July–September for the following year
- **Vorspiel:** Pre-festival programme distributed across Berlin venues. Open call ~Sept–Oct for November deadline.
- **DARC Research Workshop:** Annual PhD/researcher workshop. Open call ~October–November.
- **transmediale Studio:** Year-round project space at silent green Kulturquartier.

## How to Get Involved (Pathways)

transmediale does **not** have a traditional academic CFP. The festival is **invitation-and-curation-based**. Entry points:

1. **Direct contact with curator:** The festival curator (Barbara Cueto for 2027) will be developing the programme. The best route is direct outreach with a compelling proposal aligned to the curatorial vision.

2. **Vorspiel:** Pre-festival distributed programme. Open call expected ~September 2026 (deadline ~November 2026). Independent Berlin-based organisations can apply to host Vorspiel events. Accessible entry point for workshops, screenings, talks.

3. **DARC Research Workshop:** Annual workshop for researchers/PhD students. Expected call ~October–November 2026.

4. **Residencies:** Apply through annual open calls (July–September typically). The Vilém Flusser Residency is specifically for artistic research — well-suited for Amber.

5. **transmediale/journal:** Reach out about contributing essays or commissioned texts.

6. **Cold outreach → warm relationship:** transmediale values long-term community ties. Publishing in the journal, participating in Vorspiel, or attending the festival builds credibility for future curatorial consideration.
