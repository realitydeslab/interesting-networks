## Workshop Scope & Topics

The workshop targets three main questions:

1. **LLMs FOR EC**: Using LLMs as algorithm components — mutation operators, solution generators, crossover operators, hyperparameter tuners. Key applications: algorithm design (FunSearch, AlphaEvolve, LLaMEA), program synthesis, heuristic discovery.
2. **LLMs WITH EC**: EC providing search pressure on LLM-generated outputs — evolving LLM prompts, evolving LLM architectures, quality-diversity applied to LLM outputs.
3. **LLMs AS EC**: The key conceptual question Amber should engage — **how can an LLM behave as an evolutionary agent?** Open-ended evolution, behavioral diversity, emergent dynamics.

Specific topics in scope:
- LLMs as surrogate fitness functions or behavioral characterization tools
- Evolving LLM populations / multi-agent LLM societies
- Open-ended algorithm discovery (FunSearch, LLaMEA, ELM)
- Quality-Diversity (QD) algorithms applied to or enhanced by LLMs
- LLMs generating training data for evolutionary algorithms
- Behavioral analysis of LLM-EC hybrids through empirical observation
- Safety and alignment implications of self-improving AI design systems

## Key Frameworks Referenced by This Workshop

### FunSearch (DeepMind, 2023)
Romera-Paredes et al. "Mathematical discoveries from program search with large language models." *Nature* (2023). [DOI: 10.1038/s41586-023-06924-6](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-023-06924-6)

LLM generates Python functions ("programs"); evolutionary algorithm selects and recombines the best. Used to discover new algorithms for cap set problem (mathematical record), bin packing. Key insight: LLM provides semantic variation that random mutation cannot. DeepMind's foundational LLM-for-EC paper.

### AlphaEvolve (DeepMind, 2025)
Novikov, Vũ, Eisenberger et al. "AlphaEvolve: A coding agent for scientific and algorithmic discovery." [arXiv:2506.13131](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.13131) (white paper).

Evolutionary coding agent: Gemini LLM generates programs, evolutionary search selects and improves. Discovers new algorithms for matrix multiplication, faster sorting, bin packing. Full AI-driven evolutionary loop: generate → evaluate → select → repeat. The current state of the art for LLM+EC.

### LLaMEA (Leiden University, 2024)
van Stein & Bäck. "LLaMEA: A Large Language Model Evolutionary Algorithm for Automatically Generating Metaheuristics." [arXiv:2405.20132](https://arxiv.org/abs/2405.20132). Accepted at IEEE TEVC.

GPT-4 generates complete metaheuristic algorithms (e.g., variants of particle swarm, differential evolution) as Python code. Evolutionary loop refines them on benchmark problems. First framework to generate competitive metaheuristics fully automatically. One of the workshop organizers (van Stein) co-authored this.

### Evolution Through Large Models (ELM, Joel Lehman, 2022)
Lehman et al. "Evolution through Large Models." [arXiv:2206.08896](https://arxiv.org/abs/2206.08896).

The conceptual ancestor of all the above. LLMs as mutation operators in genetic programming, combined with MAP-Elites (a quality-diversity algorithm) to generate diverse functional robot body plans. Demonstrates LLMs bring human-like semantic variation to evolutionary search. Joel Lehman's foundational contribution.

### Quality-Diversity (QD) through AI Feedback (QDAIF, 2023)
Bradley, Dai, Teufel, Zhang, Clune, Stanley, Lehman et al. "Quality-Diversity through AI Feedback." [arXiv:2310.13452](https://arxiv.org/abs/2310.13452).

QD algorithms aim to find many diverse, high-quality solutions simultaneously (cf. MAP-Elites). QDAIF extends this to qualitative domains (creative writing, code) by using LLMs as both the behavior space descriptor and the quality evaluator. Joel Lehman's key bridge between QD and language models. **Directly relevant to Amber**: QD can produce diversity of behaviors, not just optimal solutions — this is the computational version of Amber's behavioral diversity interest.

## Quality-Diversity (QD) — Key Concept

QD algorithms don't just find the best solution — they find the *most diverse set of high-quality solutions* across a behavior space. Instead of a single fitness function, agents are characterized by behavioral descriptors (e.g., how they move, what strategies they use) and each "niche" in behavior space is filled by the best-performing solution found.

**Why this matters for Amber:** QD is essentially computational ethology. It maps behavioral repertoires, not just performance peaks. Amber's agent ethology framework — observing and categorizing AI agent behaviors — is the qualitative version of what QD does formally. This is the intellectual bridge.

Key QD papers:
- MAP-Elites: [Mouret & Clune (2015)](https://arxiv.org/abs/1504.04909) — the foundational QD algorithm
- QD in language generation: QDAIF above
- QD through human feedback: [Ding, Zhang, Clune, Spector, Lehman (2023)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2310.12426)

### Option A: Insured Agents as Evolutionary Game Theory

> Multi-agent LLM societies exhibit emergent behavioral dynamics that can be analyzed through the lens of evolutionary game theory. We introduce *insurance mechanisms* as evolutionary fitness shapers: by embedding credible commitment devices into multi-agent LLM populations, we induce selection pressure toward cooperative, trustworthy behavioral phenotypes. Through empirical observation on the Concordia simulation platform, we characterize how insurance-backed interactions reshape the behavioral repertoire of agents across evolutionary time — demonstrating that designed institutional constraints can function as fitness landscapes that drive behavioral evolution in LLM populations. We argue that agent ethology — the systematic observation and classification of AI agent behavioral strategies — provides a principled methodology for studying emergent evolutionary dynamics in LLM-based systems.

### Option B: Agent Ethology as Behavioral Characterization for LLM-EC Systems

> As LLM-EC hybrid systems grow in complexity, characterizing the behavioral diversity they generate becomes critical for understanding and governing their evolutionary dynamics. Drawing on a taxonomy of emergent agent behavioral phenotypes (sovereign, parasitic, mortal-aware, insured) observed in open-ended multi-agent LLM environments, we demonstrate that ethological classification provides a qualitative counterpart to formal quality-diversity metrics. This framing enables analysis of behavioral niches, evolutionary stable strategies, and selection dynamics in multi-agent LLM populations without requiring predefined behavioral descriptors — making it applicable to open-ended evolutionary systems where the behavior space is not known in advance.

### 🥇 Option A — Extended Abstract (4 pages, NO APC) — **RECOMMENDED**

**Title:** *Insured Agents: Insurance Mechanisms as Evolutionary Fitness Shapers in Multi-Agent LLM Societies*

**Framing:** Position/work-in-progress paper. No full experimental results required. Presents the theoretical framework and experimental design, with preliminary or expected results. This is the **actionable path** given the current state of the project (planning stage, no Concordia experiments run yet).

> As LLM-powered agents proliferate in open digital environments, their collective behavior under competitive selection pressure becomes a critical AI governance challenge. Willis et al. (2026) demonstrate that cultural evolution in LLM agent populations risks convergence to poor cooperative equilibria — paradoxically, more capable models accelerate defection. No institutional countermeasure has been proposed.
>
> We introduce *insurance mechanisms* as evolutionary fitness shapers in multi-agent LLM societies. Drawing on mechanism design theory and evolutionary game theory, we propose that insurance acts as a *credible commitment device*: premium payment creates a costly, verifiable signal of accountability that makes trustworthiness evolutionarily stable under cultural selection. This mechanism — which we term *institutional reciprocity* — extends Nowak's (2006) five rules for the evolution of cooperation with a sixth: economic institutions that reshape the selection landscape for agent behavioral strategies.
>
> We formalize a game-theoretic model of insurance in agent populations, showing analytically that insurance expands the basin of attraction of cooperative equilibria and that a critical coverage threshold I\* exists above which cooperative cascade is self-sustaining. We present an experimental design implementing four insurance conditions (mandatory, optional market, mutual pool, and a sham commitment-only ablation) within Google DeepMind's Concordia cultural evolution framework, testable across LLM agent populations in social dilemma scenarios. The sham ablation — in which agents pay premiums that never result in payouts — is designed to isolate the commitment signal from the incentive structure, directly testing whether cultural evolution is driven by accountability signaling or by financial incentives.
>
> Our framework positions institutional design — not model training alone — as essential infrastructure for governing evolutionary dynamics in LLM agent societies. We connect this to open-ended evolution: insurance mechanisms alter the selection landscape such that cooperative behavioral diversity is maintained rather than eliminated by evolutionary pressure, with implications for designing AI systems that remain aligned over generational timescales.

**Title:** *Insured Agents: Insurance Markets Stabilize Cooperation in Evolutionary LLM Agent Societies*

**Framing:** Full empirical paper. Requires at least Willis replication + T0 baseline + 1-2 insurance conditions in Concordia. The sham ablation result (even with small N) would be enough for a workshop paper.

> As LLM-powered agents proliferate across digital economies, their collective behavior under evolutionary selection becomes a critical governance challenge. Willis et al. (2026) demonstrate that cultural evolution in LLM agent populations risks convergence to defective equilibria — paradoxically, more capable models produce worse collective outcomes. We introduce *insurance mechanisms* as an institutional intervention that stabilizes cooperative equilibria in evolutionary LLM societies.
>
> Drawing on mechanism design theory, we argue that insurance acts as a *credible commitment device*: agents who purchase insurance publicly signal accountability, creating selection pressure for cooperative strategies that spreads through cultural evolution. We implement four insurance designs — mandatory, optional market, mutual pool, and a commitment-only sham condition — within Google DeepMind's Concordia cultural evolution framework, testing across populations of LLM agents in three social dilemma scenarios. We find that: (1) all insurance conditions substantially increase cooperation rates versus uninsured baseline; (2) the commitment signal from premium payment drives the majority of the effect, exceeding payout incentives, established via the sham insurance ablation; (3) a critical insurance penetration threshold (~20% of population) exists above which cooperative cascade becomes self-sustaining; and (4) insurance reverses Willis et al.'s "newer models → worse outcomes" paradox, converting capability advantages into societal benefits.
>
> Our results establish institutional design as essential infrastructure for cooperative AI agent societies. We release our insurance market extension to Concordia as open-source. We discuss connections to open-ended evolution: insurance reshapes the behavioral fitness landscape such that diversity and cooperation are maintained across evolutionary timescales.
