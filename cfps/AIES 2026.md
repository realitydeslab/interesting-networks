## What

The 9th AAAI/ACM Conference on AI, Ethics, and Society invites interdisciplinary research on the societal, ethical, legal, and political dimensions of AI. Single-track conference, papers only. Cross-disciplinary by design: CS, law/policy, social sciences, ethics/philosophy.

**2026 emphasis:** Papers grounded in real-world AI deployments and institutional settings, connecting normative/ethical claims to concrete sociotechnical contexts. Cross-national perspectives and reflexive accounts of ethics-in-practice especially encouraged.

### Topic Areas

**Power, Governance, and Political Economy:**
- Governance, regulation, control, safety, security of AI
- Accountability and responsibility across AI value chains
- Post-deployment accountability, harm, and mechanisms for redress
- AI, markets, and competition
- Cultural, geopolitical, economic, employment, and other societal impacts

**AI Systems, Values, and Ethical Design:**
- Trustworthy AI systems; value alignment and moral decision making
- Ethical theories, models and frameworks
- Interpretability, explainability, and transparency
- Fairness, bias, equity, and equality
- Human-centered AI, human-AI interaction, collaboration, and teaming
- Control, alignment, and scalable oversight
- **Ethics and safety in agents, embodied intelligence, and robots** ← most relevant for Amber
- Eco-sensitive, resource-conscious ("Green") AI design

**AI in Social and Institutional Domains:**
- AI in law, lawmaking and the judiciary; public administration
- AI, surveillance, and privacy; health and wellbeing
- **AI, creativity, cultural production and the arts**
- **AI and democracy, agency, civic participation, and social movements**

**Work, Labor, and Global Inequalities:**
- AI, labor, employment, future of work
- **Marginalized, Indigenous, and Global South perspectives on AI governance**

**Critique, Consequences, and Limits of AI:**
- **Critique, resistance, and refusal in relation to AI systems** ← relevant
- **AI harms, failures, and non-adoption in practice** ← relevant
- Environmental costs and climate impacts
- Societal limits, overreach, and dependency on AI

## Submission Requirements

- **Format:** Max 10 pages (incl. figures/tables) in [AAAI 2026 Author Kit](https://aaai.org/authorkit26-1/) 2-column format + unlimited references
- **Review:** Double-blind
- **Submission:** [EasyChair](https://easychair.org/conferences/?conf=aies26)
- **Optional statements:** Ethical considerations, researcher positionality, adverse impact (1 extra page, does not count toward limit)
- **Non-archival option:** Submit full paper for review but only publish 1-page abstract + URL in proceedings. Allows subsequent journal publication. Review process unchanged.
- **LLM policy:** LLM-generated text prohibited unless part of experimental analysis. LLMs cannot be authors.
- **Prior publication:** No archival publication before submission. Preprints (arXiv/SSRN) welcome. Extensions of workshop papers with substantial new content allowed.
- **Author list:** Changes after submission deadline not allowed.
- **Attendance:** At least one author must register, attend, and present.
- **Proceedings:** Published in [AAAI Digital Library](https://ojs.aaai.org/index.php/AIES/)

### What's NOT available at AIES
- No workshops or tutorials
- No pictorials or visual-first formats
- No demonstrations, installations, or art track
- No doctoral consortium (student track is separate — details TBA for 2026)

## Why Relevant

### Honest Assessment: Tangential, Not Central

AIES connects to Amber's **critical computing** thread but sits at the margins of his core research interests. The venue rewards analytical/empirical/normative work on AI governance — not speculative design, practice-based research, ALife, or more-than-human perspectives.

**Relevant tracks for Amber:**
1. **"Ethics and safety in agents, embodied intelligence, and robots"** — agent ethology could be framed here as a governance prerequisite
2. **"Critique, resistance, and refusal"** — machine death, care ethics, refusal-as-design-principle
3. **"AI and democracy, agency, civic participation"** — agentive commoning, delegation, trustworthy agentic web
4. **"Marginalized, Indigenous, and Global South perspectives"** — cosmotechnics, non-Western AI epistemologies

**Not relevant:**
- No format for practice-based research, design artifacts, or speculative work
- No ALife, emergence, or computational creativity
- No somaesthetic or embodied interaction
- No more-than-human or posthuman framing in the CFP
- Philosophical tradition is analytic ethics, not continental philosophy or STS

### Concrete Submission Ideas (If Amber Wanted To)

1. **"Observing Agents in the Wild: Agent Ethology as a Governance Framework"** — argue that behavioral observation of AI agents (ethology) is a necessary complement to alignment/safety approaches. Frame it as governance methodology. Target: "Ethics and safety in agents."

2. **"When Machines Die: Accountability and Care at End-of-AI-Life"** — machine death framed as an accountability gap. What happens when AI systems are deprecated? Who cares for the users/communities that depended on them? Target: "Post-deployment accountability, harm, and mechanisms for redress."

3. **"Sovereign Agents and Democratic Delegation: Who Governs Autonomous AI?"** — frame sovereign/decentralized agent work through the governance lens. Target: "AI and democracy, agency, civic participation."

4. **"Cosmotechnics and AI Governance: Non-Western Perspectives on Machine Agency"** — cosmotechnics as an alternative framework for thinking about AI governance beyond Western analytic ethics. Target: "Marginalized, Indigenous, and Global South perspectives."

**Recommendation:** Only submit to AIES if one of these framings naturally fits existing work. Don't force it. [[FAccT]], [[ACM DIS]], and [[ALIFE Conference]] are better homes for Amber's research.
