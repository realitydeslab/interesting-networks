## What It Is

The **ISAL Summer School** is an educational programme organized annually by the **International Society for Artificial Life (ISAL)** — typically through its student chapter, **ERA (Emerging Researchers in ALife)**. It runs in conjunction with the annual **ALIFE Conference** and targets PhD students, postdocs, and emerging researchers who want hands-on exposure to artificial life theory, methods, and community.

Past editions feature workshops, tutorials, and talks by leading ALife researchers, covering everything from evolutionary computation and open-endedness to wet ALife, morphogenesis, and the philosophy of life. It is one of the few venues in the world that takes "life as it could be" seriously as a research agenda — bridging biology, computation, philosophy, and art.

For Amber, this is one of the most intellectually aligned venues available. Her advisor Joel Lehman is a leading ALife researcher. Her research on machine behavior, agent ethology, and more-than-human design maps directly onto ALife's core agenda. ALIFE is also the home of the open-endedness research thread — one of the field's most active debates.

### Format Pattern (Based on Past Editions)
ISAL/ALIFE summer schools typically:
- Run 1-2 days before the main conference (pre-conference satellite)
- Include 4-8 tutorial sessions on core ALife methods (evolutionary computation, cellular automata, agent-based simulation, artificial chemistry, etc.)
- Feature invited speakers — often leading ALife researchers who also speak at the main conference
- Target PhD students and postdocs; some open to advanced undergrads
- Include social/networking events to build ERA community
- Are low/no cost for conference registrants

## Strategic Fit for Amber ⭐⭐⭐

**This is one of the most intellectually aligned communities for Amber in existence.**

### Why ALIFE Is Amber's People

1. **Joel Lehman is a pioneer here.** Amber's fellowship advisor invented the concept of novelty search and is one of the founders of open-endedness research — both central to ALife. ALIFE is where Lehman's work lives. Attending means entering Joel's home community.

2. **Machine behavior / agent ethology.** ALife is the origin discipline for studying how agents behave as if alive. Amber's work on agent ethology maps directly. The ALIFE community is experimenting with agents, emergence, and life-like behavior as research.

3. **Machine death.** ALIFE 2025 had an entire workshop on "Mortal Agents in ALIFE: Physical, Psychological, and Social Death in the Machine" — this is Amber's core theme. The ALIFE community is thinking seriously about mortality, finitude, and the ethics of artificial life.

4. **More-than-human design / philosophy of ALife.** The ALIFE community actively welcomes philosophy and art. ALIFE 2026 explicitly includes "Ethics, Existential Risks, and Philosophy of Artificial Life" as a track. Amber's cosmotechnics perspective and more-than-human design work fit perfectly.

5. **Open-endedness.** One of the most active research debates in ALife. Kenneth O. Stanley (who gave ALIFE 2025 keynote) defined open-endedness research. Amber's interest in open-ended evolution connects directly.

6. **HoloKit angle.** ALife has strong interest in embodied, situated, and physical intelligence. HoloKit as a platform for embodied ALife research is a plausible pitch.

7. **ERA = Amber's community.** The Emerging Researchers in ALife student group organizes the summer school and is explicitly for PhD students. Amber can enter as a peer, not just an attendee.

### Why to Attend (Not Just Submit a Paper)

- **Network depth:** ALIFE is small enough (~200-400 attendees) that Amber can meet everyone in her interest area. This is not CHI or NeurIPS. You can talk to every keynote speaker.
- **Waterloo corridor.** Canada's AI/complexity research hub. University of Waterloo hosts the Waterloo Institute for Complexity & Innovation. Connections here matter.
- **ERA community.** Getting involved with ERA (the student chapter) gives Amber a global cohort of ALife PhD students — a natural peer group for machine behavior and agent ethology research.
- **Positioning.** If Amber publishes at ALIFE 2026, she establishes presence in the ALife venue — which would be unusual for an HCC Oxford PhD, but powerful for her specific research agenda.
