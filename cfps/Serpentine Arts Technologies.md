## What

**Serpentine Arts Technologies** is the technology arm of [Serpentine Galleries](https://www.serpentinegalleries.org/) (Kensington Gardens, London), running three interconnected programmes:

### 1. R&D Platform
<https://www.serpentinegalleries.org/arts-technologies/rd-platform/>

An institutional research programme supporting **interdisciplinary creative R&D, prototype projects, cultural policy, and digital strategy**. The Platform:
- Brings together cultural organisations, artists, technologists, policymakers, and the wider art ecosystem
- Supports prototype projects and knowledge exchange
- Advocates for Creative R&D as core cultural infrastructure (as articulated in FAE5)
- **No formal open call** — entry is through the FAE community, relationship-building, and direct proposal to the Arts Technologies team

Research interests (from their site):
- Core values of cultural production in coming decades
- Long-term art × academia × civic × technology relationships
- New cultural infrastructures for a resilient art world
- How art can shape the development of future technologies
- Creative R&D as driver of innovation beyond the cultural sector

### 2. Future Art Ecosystems (FAE)
<https://futureartecosystems.org/>

Annual strategic briefing series + growing community of artists, technologists, policymakers, researchers, and fellow organisations. Five briefings published (2020–2025):
- **FAE1:** *Art x Advanced Technologies* (2020)
- **FAE2:** *Art x Metaverse* (2021)
- **FAE3:** *Art x Decentralised Tech* (2022) — with [[Gary Zhexi Zhang]], [[Victoria Ivanova]]
- **FAE4:** *Art x Public AI* (2023)
- **[FAE5: Art x Creative R&D](https://futureartecosystems.org/briefing/fae5/)** (2025) — advocates Creative R&D as core UK industrial strategy. Features [[Bogna Konior]], Ian Cheng, Lauren Lee McCarthy, Anicka Yi, Danielle Brathwaite-Shirley

FAE has a growing **community** (Telegram, monthly calls) open to anyone aligned with their mission. FAE6 is in preparation.

### 3. Digital Commissions
Commissioned online, app-based, and augmented reality artworks. Notable past commissions:
- **Ian Cheng:** *Bad Corgi* (2015) — iOS simulation/cognition app
- **Jakob Kudsk Steensen:** *The Deep Listener* (2019) — AR ecological walk in Hyde Park (with Google Arts and Culture)
- **Jakob Kudsk Steensen:** *Catharsis* (2020) — virtual old-growth forest
- **Hito Steyerl:** *Actual Reality OS* (2019) — AR app visualising social inequality data
- **Danielle Brathwaite-Shirley:** *THE DELUSION* (2025–26) — multiplayer video game exploring polarisation

No regular open call — invitation-based, but pitching via the Arts Technologies team is possible.
