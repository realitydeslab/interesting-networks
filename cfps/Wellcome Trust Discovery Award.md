## What

The **Wellcome Discovery Award** provides long-term, flexible funding for established researchers and teams to pursue **bold and creative research** that delivers significant shifts in understanding related to human life, health and wellbeing.

- **Funder:** [Wellcome Trust](https://wellcome.org/) — major independent UK charitable foundation (~£36bn endowment)
- **Scheme page:** <https://wellcome.org/research-funding/schemes/wellcome-discovery-awards>
- **Amount:** Average **£3.5 million** (no fixed upper limit, but >£5M gets extra scrutiny)
- **Duration:** Up to **8 years** (shorter for some disciplines; longer if part-time)
- **Frequency:** Twice a year — **upcoming deadline: March 31, 2026**
- **Location:** Lead applicant must be at UK, Republic of Ireland, or eligible LMIC institution
- **Disciplines:** Any — STEM, experimental medicine, humanities and social science, clinical/allied health sciences, public health — as long as it connects to improving human life, health and wellbeing

## Award Details (for planning purposes)

### What Wellcome Funds
- **Bold, creative, curiosity-driven research** — explicitly not incremental
- Research that leads to **significant shifts in understanding** — transformation, not consolidation
- **Any discipline** — including humanities, social science, arts, if connected to human health/wellbeing
- Interdisciplinary approaches strongly welcomed: "Wellcome will particularly welcome applications that bring together different disciplines to tackle problems creatively"
- Applicants must "think boldly and with ambition, asking challenging questions with the potential for real impact"

### Research Costs Covered
- Lead applicant's salary (usually 100%)
- Research staff salaries
- Equipment, consumables, travel
- Publication costs
- Patient and public involvement
- Up to 8 years of funding

### Review Criteria (inferred from Wellcome guidance)
1. **Boldness** — Is this genuinely ambitious and transformative?
2. **Scientific/scholarly quality** — Is the approach rigorous?
3. **Significance** — Potential to shift understanding of human life/health/wellbeing?
4. **Track record** — Does the lead applicant have a demonstrated ability to deliver?
5. **Team strength** — Is the team right for this research?
6. **Value for money** — Is the budget justified?
