## What

The **Wellcome Discovery Award** provides long-term, flexible funding for established researchers and teams to pursue **bold and creative research** that delivers significant shifts in understanding related to human life, health and wellbeing.

- **Funder:** [Wellcome Trust](https://wellcome.org/) — major independent UK charitable foundation (~£36bn endowment)
- **Scheme page:** <https://wellcome.org/research-funding/schemes/wellcome-discovery-awards>
- **Amount:** Average **£3.5 million** (no fixed upper limit; >£5M gets extra scrutiny)
- **Duration:** Up to **8 years** (average 7 years; can be shorter for some disciplines; longer if part-time)
- **Frequency:** **Twice a year** — confirmed upcoming deadline: **March 2026** (exact date in system: March 31, 2026)
- **Location:** Lead applicant must be at UK, Republic of Ireland, or eligible LMIC institution
- **Disciplines:** Any — STEM, experimental medicine, humanities and social science, clinical/allied health sciences, public health — as long as it connects to improving human life, health and wellbeing

### Application Process
Wellcome does **not** use a letter of intent / expression of interest for Discovery Awards. It is a **direct full application** process:

1. **Register on Wellcome's grant management system** (Grants.Wellcome.ac.uk) — requires institutional registration
2. **Discuss with your Research Office** at the administering institution (Oxford Research Services for Oxford-based applicants)
3. **Prepare full application including:**
   - Research proposal (scientific/scholarly case)
   - Lay summary
   - Budget and justification
   - Track record and outputs (lead + all co-applicants)
   - Team composition rationale
   - Diversity and inclusion approach
   - Research environment statement
4. **Institutional sign-off** required — Oxford must countersign
5. **Interview stage** — shortlisted candidates are called for interview (the 50/25/25 weighting applies here)

**Note:** No letter of intent. The full application is the entry point. This makes it critical that any March 31 application is already well underway.

## Review Criteria (Assessment Weightings)

At **interview stage**, applications are assessed on:

### Research Proposal — 50%
- **Bold:** Aims to deliver a significant shift in understanding OR significant advance over existing methods/frameworks/tools
- **Creative:** Novel approach — new concepts, methods, technologies, or existing ideas combined in a new way
- **High quality:** Well-designed, clear, evidence-supported, feasible outputs

### Skills and Experience (all applicants) — 25%
- Research outputs and contributions to research community
- Track record developing team members and researchers
- Leadership and management skills + development plans
- If team: rationale for team approach, team composition (diversity, career stages), contribution of each member

### Research Environment — 25%
- Contribution to organisation's strategic aims
- How environment helps develop research capabilities
- Experience contributing to positive and inclusive research culture
