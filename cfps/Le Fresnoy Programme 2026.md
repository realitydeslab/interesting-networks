## What

Le Fresnoy – Studio national des arts contemporains (Tourcoing, France) accepts applications for its unique 2-year post-graduate programme in contemporary arts. The programme accepts students from **all artistic backgrounds** (visual art, cinema, photography, video, architecture, music, performance, etc.) and **all nationalities**.

**What you get:**
- €8,400 production budget per project (2 projects over 2 years)
- Access to professional equipment: film stages, recording/mixing studios, video editing suites (16mm and 35mm), darkroom, photography studio, carpentry/metalworking/digital workshops
- Mentorship by world-leading visiting artist-professors (no permanent faculty — it changes every year)
- Exhibition of work at the annual Panorama show
- Free tuition for all nationalities (nominal university-style registration fees only)
- International network: works travel to biennials, festivals, major cultural institutions globally
- Optional doctorate in artistic creation pathway with Université de Lille or UQAM (Montréal)

**Year 1:** Film, photography, video, sound creation — experimentation with traditional media in a transdisciplinary logic.

**Year 2:** Digital creation — cutting-edge technologies and contemporary forms of expression.

## Eligibility
- Max age: **35 years** at January 1 of admission year
- Minimum: Baccalaureate + 5 years of certified study, OR Baccalaureate + 7 years of artistic/professional experience
- Open to all nationalities
- Instruction in French (French language skills required, though international students present)

## Why Relevant

Le Fresnoy is the most prestigious post-graduate art-technology programme in France and arguably in Europe for production-focused expanded cinema and new media. It explicitly includes:
- **Digital creation** as the core Year 2 track — XR, generative systems, immersive installation
- **Science partnerships**: IRCAM, CNRS, INRIA, IFREMER as institutional partners — collaborations between art and science research embedded in the curriculum
- Visiting professors include figures directly relevant to Amber: **Justine Emard** (AI + robotics, was visiting professor 2021-22 and 2023-24), **Hicham Berrada** (science + art, visiting 2024-25), **Vinciane Despret** (animal studies + more-than-human philosophy, visiting 2024-25)
- Student work shown at Centre Pompidou, Palais de Tokyo, TBA21, Fondation Hermès — elite institutional circulation

For Amber specifically: the production budget and professional equipment access would enable the kind of ambitious XR/mixed reality/agent-based installation work that's difficult to realise in a pure academic context. The 2-year structure is compatible with Amber's Oxford PhD (sabbatical or parallel track worth exploring).
