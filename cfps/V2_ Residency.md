# V2_ Lab for the Unstable Media — Residency & Fellowship Programs

> "An autonomous zone where experiments and collaborations can take place outside of the constraints of innovation agendas or economic and political imperatives."

[V2_ Lab for the Unstable Media](https://v2.nl/) is one of Europe's oldest and most respected art-technology institutions, based in Rotterdam since 1994 (originally Den Bosch, ~1980s). It occupies a unique position as a critical, independent space for media art, tech-society debate, and political engagement. The "unstable" in its name is a philosophical provocation — it embraces instability, emergence, and process as artistic material.

## What V2_ Does

V2_ runs a spectrum from Think-Tank to Do-Tank: expert meetings, workshops, residencies, publications, conferences, exhibitions. Key programs:

### 1. Alex Adriaansens Residency
**URL:** [v2.nl/pages/the-alex-adriaansens-residency-2](https://v2.nl/pages/the-alex-adriaansens-residency-2)

Named after V2_'s former co-founder/director. **Changed focus significantly in 2025.**

**2025 Focus (new):** For *curators, community organisers, cultural practitioners* in media art and digital cultures — exploring questions about independent institutions, curatorial practice, DIY culture, spaces for encounter. Research topic freely chosen. Access to V2_'s extensive archive.

**Previously (2020–2024):** For established artists/designers exploring the *aesthetic potential of the materiality of technical media*, material basis of digital culture.

**Support:** €7,500 stipend + materials, travel, accommodation. 3-month duration. Workspace at V2_. Optional guidance from V2_ archivist and team. Ends with public presentation or essay.

**Eligibility:** Primarily early-career curators, initiators, community organisers. International welcome. Duos/teams can apply (share grant).

**2025 deadline:** 1 September 2025 — **CLOSED**. 2026 call not yet announced.

**Past residents:**
- 2019: [Martin Howse](https://v2.nl/people/martin-howse/) — *Tiny Mining* (programmer, performer, explorer of physical computation)
- 2021: [Rosa Menkman](https://v2.nl/people/rosa-menkman/) — *Im/Possible Images Reader* (noise artifacts, image resolution)
- 2023/24: [Alessandro Ludovico](https://v2.nl/people/alessandro-ludovico/) — *Distributed Archive & Temporary Library* (artist, writer, Neural editor)
- 2025: [Agustina Andreoletti](https://v2.nl/people/agustina-andreoletti/) — *Open Source Intelligence for Collective Heritage Research* (techno-colonialism, distributed agency); [Katažyna Jankovska](https://v2.nl/people/katazyna-jankovska/) — *Curating Off-Grid* (curator/writer/researcher)

## Why "Unstable Media" Resonates with Amber

The "unstable" framing is philosophically loaded — it's not just a name, it's a research posture. V2_ treats instability, emergence, and process as *artistic material*. This maps onto Amber's work in several ways:

1. **Agent Ethology** → agents are behaviorally unstable, emergent — perfect "unstable media" framing
2. **ALife / Open-Endedness** → open-ended evolution as productive instability
3. **Machine Behavior** → machines behaving unexpectedly, outside human scripts
4. **HoloKit / MR** → mixed reality as unstable perceptual layer (reality destabilized by AR overlay)
5. **More-than-Human Design** → the Shifting Cosmologies XR series at V2_ is *exactly* this territory

V2_ is institutionally committed to "the debate about the influence of technological change on society" — Amber's cosmotechnics work would find deep resonance here.

## Why Interesting

V2_ is one of the few institutions in Europe that treats "unstable media" not as a defect but as a *research posture* — philosophical instability, emergence, and process as artistic substance. This resonates deeply with Amber's work on emergent agent behavior, ALife, machine behavior as a field. The "Shifting Cosmologies" series on more-than-human XR is essentially Amber's intellectual territory rendered as curated event series. The fellowship and residency alumni (Rosa Menkman, Dani Ploeger, Nicolas Maigret, Martin Howse) represent a community of critical-technical artists doing rigorous work at the art-technology interface — Amber's tribe.

**Key reframe for Amber:** The "unstable media" concept maps perfectly onto his research — agents are unstable media, AR/MR layers destabilize reality, ALife systems are productively unstable. Applying to V2_ programs as a researcher investigating "unstable digital life forms" (agents, ALife) would land well.
