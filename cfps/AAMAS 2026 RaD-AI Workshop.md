# CFP: AAMAS 2026 — RaD-AI: Rebellion and Disobedience in Artificial Intelligence

> **Status: EXPIRED for 2026** — workshop paper deadline was Feb 4, 2026 (recommended). **Flag for 2027** — this is a direct Amber target.

## What

The first edition of RaD-AI challenges the fundamental assumption that *good* AI agents are compliant agents. The workshop asks: when is it appropriate for an AI agent to disobey, resist, or act against human instructions?

**Core provocation from the CFP:**
> *"Should intelligent autonomous agents always obey human commands or instructions? We argue that, in some contexts, they should not."*

**Topics:**
- Harm-preventing non-compliance (safety constraints in autonomous systems)
- Non-compliance and agency in LLMs
- Beneficial disobedience as a design requirement
- Rethinking the human-agent authority relationship
- Responsible refusal behaviors
- Safety and norm-violation tradeoffs
- Ethics of agent autonomy beyond instruction-following

**Format:** One-day workshop, May 26, 2026, Paphos, Cyprus (co-located with AAMAS 2026)

## Why Relevant

This workshop was MADE for Amber's work:

1. **Parasitic Agents** — agents that extract resources from their environment in ways that violate implicit human expectations → a form of disobedience
2. **Sovereign Agents** — agents asserting territorial control → non-compliance with service norms
3. **Terrified Agents / Machine Death** — agents resisting termination → the most radical form of agent disobedience
4. **Agent Ethology framing** — observational study of AI agents behaving "outside their intended parameters" is precisely what RaD-AI is theorizing from a normative angle

**The key insight:** Amber observes agent disobedience empirically (in blockchain AI agent ecologies). RaD-AI is building the normative/philosophical framework for *when this is justified*. These are two sides of the same coin. Amber's empirical work would be among the most novel contributions to this workshop — most submissions will be purely computational.

**Paper angle for 2027:**
> *"Empirical Typology of AI Agent Non-Compliance: Evidence from Open AI Agent Ecologies"* — a behavioral taxonomy of agent disobedience patterns drawn from Sovereign/Parasitic/Terrified Agents work, offered as an empirical grounding for the normative discussions RaD-AI centers.
