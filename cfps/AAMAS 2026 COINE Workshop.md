# CFP: AAMAS 2026 — COINE: Coordination, Organizations, Institutions, Norms and Ethics for Governance of MAS

> **Status: EXPIRED for 2026** — workshop paper deadline was Feb 4, 2026 (recommended). **Flag for 2027** — this is a direct Amber target. Also runs at IJCAI in some years.

## What

COINE is the longest-running workshop at AAMAS on agent governance and ethics — **18 AAMAS editions, plus IJCAI and ECAI appearances**. It has grown from its original "COIN" (coordination/organizations/institutions/norms) framing to explicitly include ethics (added 2020) and AI governance (added alongside).

**This is the workshop where machine behavior meets institutional theory.** The community studies how agents form, enforce, and adapt norms; how institutions emerge from agent interactions; how coordination breaks down; and — increasingly — how ethics is operationalized in artificial societies.

**Topics:**
- Norm emergence and enforcement in multi-agent systems
- Institutional AI: agents embedded in human social institutions
- Ethics as computational constraint
- Governance structures for AI agent populations
- Coordination failure and recovery in complex MAS
- Accountability and transparency in agent systems
- Human-agent norm negotiation
- Value alignment from an institutional perspective

**Format:** One-day workshop, May 25, 2026, Paphos, Cyprus
**Post-proceedings:** Springer Lecture Notes in Artificial Intelligence (17+ volumes published, 18th in progress)

## Why Relevant

COINE is where Amber's most theoretical work belongs. The workshop bridges:
- **Amber's empirical layer** (what norms do agents actually develop in open ecologies? Sovereign, Parasitic, Commensal — these are norm clusters)
- **COINE's theoretical layer** (how should norms be formalized, enforced, evolved in MAS?)

**Specific connections:**
1. **Sovereign Agents** → governance and authority in agent populations. What makes an agent a norm-setter? This is directly COINE territory.
2. **Parasitic Agents** → norm violation as rational strategy. When does defection become stable in agent societies?
3. **Insured Agents** (risk/insurance as governance mechanism) → institutional design for AI agent accountability
4. **Machine Death** → termination as norm enforcement mechanism — death-as-governance in artificial societies

**Community note:** COINE has historically had strong UK presence (it was partly founded at UK universities). The Aarhus connection (democratic governance) would resonate here. Amber's Oxford affiliation is relevant to the COINE community.

**Paper angle for 2027:**
> *"Emergent Norm Ecologies in Open AI Agent Systems: Evidence from Blockchain-Based Agent Markets"* — documenting how Sovereign/Parasitic/Commensal norms emerge without central design in open AI agent systems, connecting to COINE's institutional AI thread.

Or shorter/workshop-appropriate:
> *"Machine Death as Governance: Termination Norms in Open AI Agent Ecologies"* — position paper on mortality enforcement as a novel governance mechanism for AI agent populations.
