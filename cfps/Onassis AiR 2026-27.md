## What

[Onassis AiR](https://www.onassis.org/air) (Artistic Research) is the flagship residency program of [Onassis Stegi](https://www.onassis.org/initiatives/onassis-stegi), the cultural center of the Onassis Foundation in Athens. Running since 2019, it has supported ~250 fellows from around the world.

**This is a fully-funded research residency, not a production residency.** No final work required. Process, research, and experimentation are at the core — participants are invited to go deeper into their practice without pressure to show a finished outcome.

**2026–27 cohort:** 30 residencies across September 2026 – July 2027. Each residency is 10 weeks in Athens, Greece.

## Onassis AiR vs. Onassis Stegi vs. Onassis ONX

**Onassis AiR** is a programme run by **Onassis Stegi** (the Athens cultural centre). They are the same institution — AiR is one of Stegi's flagship programmes.

**Onassis ONX** is a separate entity — a global art & advanced technologies platform with studios in New York (Tribeca) and Athens (Onassis Ready). ONX and AiR are **partnered**: from 2026, AiR residents get access to the ONX Athens studio. Completing AiR can be a pathway toward ONX membership.

## Past Residents — What Do They Fund?

In 6 years (2019–2025), ~250 fellows. From public information:

- **Interdisciplinary practice** is the norm — theater, dance, and visual arts dominate numerically but Art & Technology is a named category
- **Research-led rather than production-led** — most residents use the time for R&D, not to produce a specific work
- **International but with Greek connection** — some residents develop Greek-specific projects, but it's not required
- **Artists who later showed through Plásmata** public art series include participants working on AI, ecology, digital bodies — overlapping with ONX's thematic areas

Onassis ONX (the art-tech arm) has supported: [[Jiabao Li]] (interspecies/AI/HoloKit), Theo Triantafyllidis (feral digital ecologies), Danielle Brathwaite-Shirley (interactive video game art), Cameron Kostopoulos (haptic VR), Miriam Simun (AI + ecology). This gives a sense of the *broader Onassis taste* — speculative, embodied, post-human, and formally ambitious.

The **Art & Advanced Technologies** track at AiR likely funds work similar to ONX's portfolio: XR, AI, embodied computation, computational creativity, emerging tech × critical practice.
