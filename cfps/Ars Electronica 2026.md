## Overview

Ars Electronica is one of the world's most prestigious festivals at the intersection of art, technology, and society. Founded 1979, the Prix Ars Electronica (est. 1987) is the longest-running media art competition, widely considered the "Oscar of digital art." Past participants and winners include major AI, ALife, bio-art, and interactive art figures.

The 2026 theme **"Future Begins"** invites artistic exploration of how we collectively shape futures — resonating with speculative design, agent-based futures, and more-than-human co-creation. The festival spans exhibitions, concerts, talks, workshops, and guided tours across multiple venues in Linz.

## Prestige & Acceptance Rates

Ars Electronica is widely regarded as one of the most prestigious media art events globally:
- Prix Ars Electronica receives **~3,000–4,000 submissions per year** globally
- ~1 Golden Nica + ~2 Awards of Distinction + ~12 Honorary Mentions per category
- Acceptance to any recognition: **~0.5%** for Golden Nica, ~5% for any recognition
- It functions as a "who's who" of media art — winners include Joseph Weizenbaum, Christa Sommerer, Lynn Muldoon, Random International, TeamLab, Memo Akten, Lauren Lee McCarthy, Forensic Architecture
- The Festival itself attracts ~100,000 visitors across the week
