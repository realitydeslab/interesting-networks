## What

[Eyebeam's 2026 Residency](https://eyebeam.org/open-call-2026/) under the season theme **"Speculating on Plurality"** — a 12-week physical residency for emerging NYC-based artists to develop work-in-progress. The prompt: *What is required to move us towards a pluralistic commons — a space that holds our multitudes and their potentials towards shared futures?*

This is Eyebeam's **first physical NYC residency since the pandemic**. Six artists will be selected.

**Hosted at:** [NYU Tandon @ The Yard](https://engineering.nyu.edu/research/nyu-tandon-yard), Brooklyn Navy Yard — a 14,000 sq ft emerging media production and research facility.

## What They Fund

- **$4,000 stipend** (+ additional funds for activations)
- Daily studio access 9 AM–9 PM, shared studio + dedicated desk
- Option for NYU graduate assistant technical support: 3D Design, **AR/VR development**, Motion Capture, **XR production**, TouchDesigner, Coding, Web Development, Videography, Unreal Engine, and more
- Mentorship with Eyebeam alumni
- Opportunities with partner organizations and visiting professionals

## Duration

**12 weeks** — April 1 to June 30, 2026. In-person, NYC.

## Eligibility

**Hard requirements:**

1. **Must reside in New York City (5 boroughs)** — active use of studio space required
2. **Emerging artist** — practicing professionally for no more than 10 years
3. **Individual applicant** (not a group/org)
4. **NOT currently enrolled in any full-time degree program**
5. **NOT employed as full-time faculty at a higher learning institution**

## Application Requirements

Four-part application via Submittable:

1. **Eligibility affirmation**
2. **Applicant info** — name, location, DOB, email, 200-word bio, CV/Resume
3. **Application materials:**
   - Statement of Purpose (300 words): current work/thinking, creative influences, how residency supports your progress
   - Three short-answer questions (300 words each):
     - How do you plan to engage with peers in the cohort?
     - How have you centered care, community, and solidarity in your practice?
     - How do you interpret the residency's themes and how do they show up in your practice?
   - Two work samples (at least one complete; one can be WIP)
4. **Demographic survey** (optional)

## Season Theme: Speculating on Plurality

Central prompt: *"What is required to move us towards a pluralistic commons — a space that holds our multitudes and their potentials towards shared futures?"*

Key questions:
- Are we sharing realities in an era of digital connectivity?
- In an era of technofeudalism, can we escape technology's deployment in state surveillance?
- Do popular technologies impulse towards creating sameness?

Eyebeam seeks artists engaging with: **digital rights**, **surveillance critique**, **pluralism**, **commons**, **speculative design**, **technology ethics**, **emerging media**, **open-source**, **AR/VR/XR**, **generative AI critique**, and more.
