## What

**[Akademie Schloss Solitude](https://www.akademie-solitude.de/)** is one of Europe's most prestigious interdisciplinary artist-in-residence programs, operating since 1990 out of a baroque palace on the forested hills outside Stuttgart. It awards **50–60 international fellowships every two years** to artists, scholars, and cultural practitioners across all disciplines. Unlike deliverable-based programs, the fellowship is explicitly **open-ended in terms of results** — the point is concentrated work, not output.

The Akademie occupies two side wings of the historic Schloss Solitude. Fellows live and work in the same building — 41 studios, each with living area, working area, small kitchen, and private bathroom. The communal structure fosters serendipitous interdisciplinary contact daily.

### Fellowship Tracks
- **Visual** — Fine Arts & Film/Video
- **Aural & Physical** — Music/Sound & Performing Arts
- **Spatial** — Architecture & Design
- **Textual** — Literature & Language
- **Digital** — New Media Art & Digital Art ← **AMBER'S PRIMARY TRACK**
  > *"Artistic practice engaging with digital, algorithmic and networked technologies, gaming, (live) coding, hacking, open-source culture, digital communities, and post-digital aesthetics."*
- **Scientific** — Humanities & Applied Sciences ← **ALSO ELIGIBLE** (current focus: philosophy, media studies, sociology, cultural studies, political science, law, life sciences, economics)
- **Societal/Communal-based Work** — newer track (from 2020)

### Eligibility Rules
- International applicants welcome, no nationality restrictions
- **2–10 years post-education professional experience** (guideline, not strict rule)
- PhD candidates are explicitly eligible — enrolled students without PhD status typically are not
- Collectives of up to 2 people may apply (must have worked together ≥2 years, 6-month only)
- English or German proficiency required
- Full-time residency in Stuttgart required — no remote options
- No full professors; no students without PhD status
- Partners may live in studio (their costs not covered); family-friendly studios available (limited)

## Fellowship Details

### What's Provided
- **Stipend:** €1,300/month to cover living expenses
- **Studio:** Fully furnished live/work studio (41 total) with living area, kitchen, private bathroom
- **Utilities:** Electricity, heating, water included
- **Travel:** One-time reimbursement for arrival and departure
- **Workshops:** Wood, metal, video editing/VR studios available on-site
- **Library:** On-site library access
- **Health insurance:** Covered for non-EU residents
- **Project funding:** Available, depending on budget — no guarantee, apply separately
- **Community:** ~50–60 international fellows across all disciplines simultaneously

### Duration
- **Individuals:** 6 or 9 months (Akademie management determines ratio)
- **Collectives:** 6 months only
- **Scientific track:** 6 months only
- Start time is flexible within 2 years of jury results announcement
- Full-time residency — part-time employment (e.g., teaching) must be paused or significantly reduced

### What's Expected
- Main residence in Stuttgart for entire fellowship duration
- Active participation in Akademie community and events
- Openness to interdisciplinary exchange
- Local networking in Stuttgart and the region
- Contributing to Solitude Blog, Journal, or other publications (encouraged, not mandated)

## Application Process

### Three-Stage Structure

**Stage 1: Open Short Application (online)**
- Online form: personal information, sphere of practice, motivation statement
- For spheres with very high application volumes (Digital is likely one), Solitude conducts an **anonymous random selection procedure** for fair pre-selection — supervised by a notary, **no AI used**
- This lottery stage filters applications before full review

**Stage 2: Full Application (online)**
- Admitted applicants submit a full application with deadline on the platform
- Likely includes: portfolio, project statement, CV, work samples/links
- Specific word counts not published, but typical for this format: 500-1000 words statement

**Stage 3: Jury Selection**
- **2 independent international jurors** per discipline in the Digital track
- Jury composition published alongside results announcement
- Selection based on qualitative, professional, interdisciplinary criteria

### Jury Criteria (Official)
1. **Depth of content** — artistic or scholarly substance of portfolio/project
2. **Social and contemporary relevance** of the work
3. **Openness** to new forms of thought and expression
4. **Career stage** — in phase of consolidating practice, not beginning, not established
5. **Readiness** for interdisciplinary exchange and regional networking

### Competitiveness Assessment
- Digital track is one of the more competitive spheres (hence the pre-selection lottery)
- Total applications likely 1,000–2,000+ per cycle across all tracks
- Digital track: estimated 100–300+ applications for approximately **3–5 fellowship slots**
- Implied success rate: **1–5%** in Digital track
- The lottery pre-selection means luck is a genuine factor at Stage 1 → Stage 2 transition
- Once in Stage 2, the full application is evaluated on merit

## Web Residencies (Separate Program)

[Web Residencies](https://www.akademie-solitude.de/en/fellowship/special-fellowships/web-residencies/) are a distinct, **exclusively online** program running since 2016:

- **Format:** 4–8 weeks, exclusively online
- **Stipend:** €1,200 per residency
- **Frequency:** Multiple calls per year (1–3), curated by invited experts or collectives
- **Scale:** 4–6 people/collectives per call
- **Past collaboration:** [ZKM | Center for Art and Media](https://zkm.de/) co-curated 9 calls with Solitude (2017–2020)
- **Production prize:** Annual HASH prize (€8,000) for outstanding project — awarded 2018, 2020; currently appears inactive

**Web Residency vs. Main Fellowship:**
| Feature | Web Residency | Main Fellowship |
|---------|--------------|-----------------|
| Duration | 4–8 weeks | 6–9 months |
| Location | Fully online | Stuttgart, on-site |
| Stipend | €1,200 | €1,300/month |
| Studio | No | Yes, furnished |
| Community | Virtual | Live-in |
| Frequency | Multiple/year | Every 2 years |
| Competition | Lower | Very high |

**Strategic use:** A Web Residency would build relationship with Solitude's Digital track curators and community **before** applying for the main fellowship. Monitor [solitude.de](https://www.akademie-solitude.de) for new Web Residency calls.

## The Fellowship Experience (What It's Actually Like)

Based on available documentation and the institution's self-presentation:

**Physical setup:** You live and work in a baroque wing of a palace on a forest hill. Studios combine living and working space — genuinely live/work, not separate apartment + studio. Monthly dinners happen in the communal space. Stuttgart city center is ~20 minutes away.

**Community density:** With 50–60 fellows at any one time, the community is large enough for genuine interdisciplinary serendipity but small enough to know everyone. The mix spans visual artists, composers, choreographers, writers, philosophers, coders — this is Solitude's core value proposition.

**Freedom:** Explicitly no deliverables. The Akademie does not ask for exhibitions, publications, or reports. What you produce is yours. This is rare and valuable — most residencies demand output.

**Pressure vs. isolation:** The isolation of the palace setting and the freedom of no deliverables can be either productive or disorienting. Reports from fellows (via published interviews) consistently describe it as *intense focus* punctuated by community events. The "Solitude" in the name is intentional.

**Stuttgart:** The city has good infrastructure, is not Berlin (less nightlife, more liveable), and has a strong design/engineering culture (Mercedes-Benz, Bosch, Porsche nearby). The art scene is smaller but genuine. The Staatsgalerie Stuttgart and Kunstmuseum Stuttgart are significant institutions.

**Language:** English sufficiency for the fellowship, though German helps for regional networking. Many fellows don't speak German.
