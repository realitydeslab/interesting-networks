## Why Relevant (Amber-specific)

### 🚨 The VISAP angle is the whole story here

The VIS Arts Program (**VISAP**) is a separate mini-conference and exhibition embedded within IEEE VIS. It consistently accepts work that is:
- Speculative, poetic, experimental vis
- AI as creative collaborator
- Environmental / ecological / more-than-human
- Community-driven, participatory data practices
- Decolonial or activist framings of data

VISAP 2025 (Vienna) theme: **"Collective Care"** — examples from the call:
> *"From speculative design to AI co-authorship, from community-centered storytelling to environmental data engagement... Environmental, eco-critical, and more-than-human data engagements... AI as collaborator in envisioning critical, thoughtful, future-oriented ideations."*

This language maps directly to Amber's practice. VISAP papers are indexed in IEEE Xplore (legitimate academic publication). VISAP exhibition accepts interactive artworks.

**VISAP 2026 theme:** Not yet announced (Arts Program Chairs listed in 2026 committee but names TBD). Watch [visap.net](https://visap.net) from May 2026.

### Possible Amber submissions to VISAP 2026
1. **Agent Ethology as Data Art** — visualizing behavioral traces of AI agents as an ecological ethogram. Frame HoloKit/GravField as making agent behavior *perceptible*.
2. **Terrified Agents as VISAP Exhibition piece** — an interactive installation surfacing machine death anxiety through real-time data visualization.
3. **Pictorial on more-than-human data practices** — how do we visualize non-human behavioral data ethically? Frames Amber's methodology.
4. **GravField as spatial vis** — innovative real-time spatial field visualization in MR headset; if reframed as "representation of collective behavior," could work at Area 4 or VISAP.

### Poster Track (lower barrier, June 24 deadline)
Posters are non-archival (optional inclusion), juried (not peer-reviewed rigorously), and explicitly welcome:
- "Provoking perspectives"
- "Work-in-progress presented prior to full publication"
- "New angles on existing work"

A poster on **agent behavioral visualization** (e.g., what does it look like to build an ethogram for AI?) could test whether the VIS community resonates with this framing — and get Amber's face into the VIS community.
