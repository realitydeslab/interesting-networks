## What

[SymbioticA](https://www.symbiotica.uwa.edu.au/) was the world's first artistic research laboratory embedded in a biological science department. Founded in 2000 at the University of Western Australia (Perth), it enabled artists, researchers, designers, architects, and humanities scholars to engage in wet biology practices — tissue culture, synthetic biology, tissue engineering — not merely to comment on them, but to use them as artistic medium.

Its name derives from "science + symbiosis + art." The lab ran three overlapping programmes:
1. **Residency** — visiting researchers from around the world conduct hands-on bioart research
2. **Academic** — Masters of Biological Arts (by coursework and by research), PhD by research
3. **Workshops, exhibitions, symposia** — regular public events

> ⚠️ **IMPORTANT**: In October 2022, UWA gave SymbioticA 10 working days to justify their funding. A petition collected 11,000+ signatures. Outcome: unclear. The website is now archived, and SymbioticA does not appear in UWA's current research entities list. The lab may be **defunct or operating in severely reduced capacity as of 2026.**

## What They Fund / Eligibility (Historical)

When active, the programme was:
- **Open to:** artists, scientists, designers, architects, humanities scholars, performance artists, science fiction writers, political theorists
- **No prior biology experience required** — training provided
- **Funded:** access to biological labs (Level 2 biolab), mentorship, scientific/technical training, expert connections
- **Duration:** variable (weeks to months); "more than 100 residents" over 25 years
- **Geography of past residents:** Australia, USA, UK, New Zealand, India, Portugal, Canada, Singapore, Netherlands, Norway, France, Japan, Denmark, China, Argentina

## Key Works (TC&A Project)

The lab's signature research arm — TC&A Project — produced:
- [**Victimless Leather**](https://tcaproject.net/portfolio/victimless-leather/) (2004) — jacket grown from cell cultures without killing animals. Exhibited MoMA NY 2008; museum ended exhibition by "killing" the living piece.
- [**Pig Wings**](https://tcaproject.net/portfolio/pigs-wings/) (2000–01) — pig bone tissue grown in wing shapes; ethics of inter-species organ transplantation
- [**Disembodied Cuisine**](https://tcaproject.net/portfolio/disembodied-cuisine/) (2003) — cultured frog muscle for food, eaten by dinner guests
- [**Semi-Living Worry Dolls**](https://tcaproject.net/portfolio/worry-dolls/) (2001) — tissue-grown dolls, with Guy Ben-Ary
- [**Biomess**](https://tcaproject.net/portfolio/biomess/) (2018) — Art Gallery of Western Australia; living systems and mess
- [**Sunlight, Soil & Shit (De)Cycle**](https://sunlightsoilshit.systems/) (2022) — sustainable food / AgTech critique, with Steve Berrick

## Why Relevant / Intellectual Framing

SymbioticA invented the concept of the **"semi-living"** — cells and tissues isolated from organisms and coerced to grow in predetermined shapes. Neither fully alive nor fully artificial. This is philosophically adjacent to:
- Amber's interest in **more-than-human design** — what obligations do we have to non-human entities?
- Amber's **ALife** work — what constitutes "life"? Where does life begin and end?
- **Machine behavior** — how do non-human entities behave, and what ethics apply?
- **Mortality** (Amber's philosophical core) — TC&A Project work is explicitly about death, killing, and the fragility of life (the MoMA exhibit was "killed" by the museum)

The key intellectual question SymbioticA asks: *What does it mean to create life in the lab? What are the ethical and cultural implications?* This is adjacent to Amber's question: *What does it mean to create artificial agents? What are the ethical obligations we have toward them?*
