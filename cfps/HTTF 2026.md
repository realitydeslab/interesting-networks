## What Is HTTF?

**Halfway to the Future (HTTF)** is a small, single-track academic symposium in the ACM HCI tradition — designed as a reflective, historically-anchored counterpoint to CHI's massive scale. It convenes ~150-200 HCI and design researchers to examine seminal past work, take stock of the field, and project forward.

The format: 3-5 **thematic panels**, each anchored by a **core panelist** who reflects on one of their own landmark publications. Short peer-reviewed papers respond to each theme. All accepted work published in ACM Digital Library (ACM ICPS). Single track — everyone hears everything.

**Founded by:** Robb Mitchell and the Mixed Reality Lab at the University of Nottingham. The 2019 inaugural edition marked the MRL's 20th anniversary. **Steve Benford** — Amber knows him — was a **keynote panelist at HTTF 2019**, reflecting on his Blasttheory mixed reality performance work. He is one of the founding intellectual community's core figures.

**Cadence:** Roughly quinquennial (2019, 2024). Not annual. This is intentional — the rarity preserves the event's weight and intimacy.

## Submission Format (from 2024 edition)

When HTTF runs, it accepts:
- **Full papers:** up to 6,000 words (ACM formatting)
- **Short papers:** up to 2,000 words
- **Pictorials:** up to 8 pages full / 4 pages short (InDesign template)
- **Interactive Forums:** poster + demo format (lightning talks)
- All work peer-reviewed, double-blind
- Submission via EasyChair
- Published in ACM Digital Library (ACM ICPS)

**Typical deadlines (based on 2024):**
- Paper submission: ~June (for October symposium)
- Early bird registration: ~September
- Symposium: October-November
