## What

The [Creative Capital Award](https://creative-capital.org/) is one of the most prestigious artist grants in the United States. Founded in 1999, it supports individual artists creating innovative new work across all disciplines. As of 2026, Creative Capital has awarded $55M to 1,119 artists.

**Two grant types (as of 2026):**

1. **Creative Capital Award** — Up to **$50,000 unrestricted** project grant for creation of new work + professional development services + community-building opportunities. 49 awards in 2026.

2. **State of the Art Prize** *(new in 2026)* — **$10,000 unrestricted** grant, one artist per US state/territory (53 awards). Inaugural prize in 2026 for Creative Capital's 25th anniversary.

**Disciplines supported:**
- Visual Arts
- Film
- Dance
- Theater
- Music / Jazz
- Literature
- **Technology** ← explicitly listed
- Multidisciplinary
- Socially engaged forms in all disciplines

**Scale (2026 cycle):** 4,546 applications → 109 awardees (extremely competitive; ~2.4% acceptance rate).

**Professional development services include:** artist lab courses, community, advisory support — designed to help artists build sustainable practices beyond the grant.

## Eligibility

**Key constraint: US-based residency required.**

The 2026 program explicitly supported "artists residing in all 50 states and territories." All 2026 Creative Capital Award recipients are listed with US cities. The program is described as a "national, open call grant program."

**Exception/ambiguity:** One 2026 Literature awardee (Alex Marzano-Lesnevich) is listed as "Vancouver, British Columbia" — suggesting some flexibility may exist, or the artist has dual US/Canada presence. Worth watching.
