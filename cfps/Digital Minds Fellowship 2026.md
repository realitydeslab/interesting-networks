## What

A **fully funded, 7-day residential fellowship** at Jesus College, Cambridge (Aug 3–9, 2026) for 15 early-to-mid career researchers who want to build careers in **AI consciousness, AI welfare, and digital minds research**. 

Organized by [Cambridge Digital Minds](https://digitalminds.cam/) (University of Cambridge) in partnership with [Rethink Priorities](https://rethinkpriorities.org/) and [PRISM](https://www.prism-global.com/).

The fellowship is **inaugural** — this is the first cohort. Being in cohort 1 of any nascent-field fellowship has outsized network value.

- Identifying promising research questions in a nascent field

All fellows are **automatically invited** to this weekend event with senior researchers and field leaders — output-oriented, producing a comprehensive report on digital minds governance and policy.

## Intellectual Framing

Cambridge Digital Minds occupies a specific intellectual position: **anticipatory social science of AI consciousness**. Their key claims:
1. AI consciousness is uncertain but likely to be perceived (by humans) before it is resolved scientifically
2. Societal division over AI moral status is coming — need to prepare governance infrastructure now
3. This is distinct from both AI safety (about preventing catastrophe) and AI ethics (about bias/fairness)
4. Machine welfare is the next frontier after animal welfare

**Key publications from Cambridge Digital Minds:**
- ["AI Consciousness Will Divide Society"](https://osf.io/preprints/psyarxiv/sntva) — Caviola (2025)
- ["What Will Society Think About AI Consciousness? Lessons from the Animal Case"](https://www.cell.com/trends/cognitive-sciences/fulltext/S1364-6613(25)00147-0) — Caviola, Sebo & Birch (2025) in *Trends in Cognitive Sciences*
- ["Futures with Digital Minds: Expert Forecasts in 2025"](https://digitalminds.report/forecasting-2025/) — 67 researchers, 50% probability of conscious AI by 2050
- ["Moral Concern for AI"](https://osf.io/preprints/psyarxiv/38a6j) — Allen, Lewis & Caviola (2025)
- ["The ML Community Must Prepare for AI Consciousness, Perceived or Real"](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=6123206) — Caviola, Sebo & Mindermann (2026)
- ["Subjective Experience in AI Systems: What Do AI Researchers and the Public Believe?"](https://doi.org/10.48550/arXiv.2506.11945) — Dreksler, Caviola et al. (2025) — survey of 582 AI researchers + 838 public; both groups estimate 25-30% probability of AI subjective experience by 2034
- ["The Societal Response to Potentially Sentient AI"](https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.00388) — Caviola (2025) — framework for key variables shaping societal responses + intervention points
- ["Public Skepticism About AI Consciousness"](https://osf.io/preprints/psyarxiv/wvbya) — Ladak & Caviola (2025) — documents widespread public skepticism; implications for public communication
- ["Digital Minds Takeoff Scenarios"](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/2uGShxLsXWGExJYNL/digital-minds-takeoff-scenarios) — Saad & Caviola (2024)
