## Workshop Focus

> Biological systems self-organise from simple components into complex organisms. This workshop explores how evolutionary algorithms can guide self-organising systems — from Neural CAs to cultural evolution models — toward open-ended, robust, and creative behaviour.

Directly maps to: agent ethology (emergent collective behavior), ALife (Lenia, CAs), open-endedness (core Amber/Lehman interest), machine behavior (artificial life as ethology subject).

>
> Frame Amber's Sovereign/Parasitic agents work as a case study in cultural evolution and emergent self-organisation. The paper would argue that autonomous AI agents, when deployed in open environments, develop emergent power structures (sovereign, parasitic, commensal) analogous to evolutionary dynamics in spatial social dilemmas — without any centralised controller.
