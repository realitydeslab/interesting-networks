## Submission Types

### Full Papers (⭐ Best route for Amber)
- 3–8 pages, NOT including references or acknowledgements
- New, unpublished work
- Peer reviewed as self-contained work
- **Published by MIT Press as open access conference proceedings**
- Assigned oral or poster presentation
- **This is what Amber should aim for**

### Summaries (💡 Low-bar route if Amber has published work)
- Up to 2 pages, NOT including references
- Must summarize a **previously published, peer-reviewed** work (NOT preprints — those should be submitted as full papers)
- NOT included in proceedings (publicly available but no DOI)
- Peer-reviewed for quality and relevance
- Assigned oral or poster (full papers get priority for oral)

### Late-Breaking Abstracts (🏃 Last resort, work-in-progress)
- Up to 2 pages, NOT including references
- New ideas and work in progress
- Reviewed for relevance and quality
- NOT included in proceedings
- Poster presentation only
- **Deadline: July 20, 2026** — much more time!

### Journal Fast-Track (⭐ Premium route)
- Papers accepted in *Artificial Life* journal (MIT Press) are **automatically accepted as oral presentations** at ALIFE 2026
- Relevant authors receive specific invitations
- Online presenters showing journal-accepted papers can attend their session **for free**
- Regular registration still required for in-person attendance

## Why Relevant to Amber

**ALIFE 2026 is the most immediately actionable opportunity for Amber in the ALife space.** Here's why:

### Strategic Reasons
1. **Directly aligned community** — ALIFE is where machine behavior, agent ethology, and artificial life intersect. Amber's work is ALife whether she labels it that way or not.
2. **Art track is legitimate** — ALIFE has dedicated art keynotes (ALIFE 2025: Nao Tokui, Takashi Ikegami as Art Keynote panelists). Not a sideline.
3. **Small enough to matter** — ALIFE is a few hundred researchers, not thousands. Being present = being known.
4. **MIT Press proceedings** — legitimate academic publication, open access, well-indexed.
5. **Waterloo, Canada** — accessible from UK (vs. last year's Kyoto or Copenhagen).
6. **32 days to deadline** — tight but achievable for a 3-8 page paper.

### Paper Ideas for Amber
1. **Agent Ethology as ALife** — frame the machinebehavior.science agenda through ALife lens. "Life" as a behavioral pattern, not substrate. Connects machine behavior → ALife.
2. **Parasitic Agents** — parasitism as ALife phenomenon: one artificial agent evolving to exploit another's resources. Directly maps to ALife concepts (symbiosis, co-evolution, emergence).
3. **Social Organization Emergence** — how do norms, trust, or exclusion behaviors emerge from agent interactions? "Emergence of Signaling, Interaction, Cooperation" — exact topic match.
4. **ALife-based Art** — submit to the art track. Describe an artwork (installation, generative piece) that uses ALife principles. This is a legitimate submission type at ALIFE.
5. **Summary submission** — if Amber has existing published work from CHI, DIS, etc., a 2-page summary could get her a presentation slot with minimal new writing.

### Even If Paper Isn't Ready
- **Late-breaking abstract (July 20)** — very low bar; work in progress is explicitly OK; poster at the conference
- **Just attend** — the conference itself is valuable; ~500 researchers, multiple art-adjacent events, hackathon
