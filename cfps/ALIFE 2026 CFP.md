## Submission Types

### Full Papers (⭐ Best route for Amber)
- **3–8 pages**, NOT including references or acknowledgements
- New, unpublished work
- Peer reviewed as self-contained work
- **Published by MIT Press as open access conference proceedings**
- Assigned oral or poster presentation
- **Templates:** [Overleaf](https://www.overleaf.com/read/tzwbbdfxqdrw#3ddbfa) | [LaTeX](https://drive.google.com/file/d/16XmT031NVqm34rU7SPArwSJRRvIbRM3i/view) | [MS-Word](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1RYP1lEvA0bHo3TxwcX2Zs8nct0llA_lW/edit)
- **Submit via:** <https://2026.alife.org/> (SUBMIT button on homepage)

### Summaries (💡 Low-bar if Amber has published work)
- **Up to 2 pages**, NOT including references
- Must summarize a **previously published, peer-reviewed** work (NOT preprints — those should be full papers)
- NOT included in proceedings (publicly available but no DOI)
- Peer-reviewed for quality and relevance
- Assigned oral or poster (full papers get oral priority)

### Late-Breaking Abstracts (🏃 Last resort, work-in-progress)
- **Up to 2 pages**, NOT including references
- New ideas and work in progress explicitly OK
- NOT included in proceedings
- Poster presentation only
- **Deadline: July 20, 2026** — much more time

### Journal Fast-Track (⭐ Premium route)
- Papers accepted in *Artificial Life* journal (MIT Press) → **automatically accepted as oral presentations** at ALIFE 2026
- Online presenters showing journal-accepted papers can attend their session for free

## Review Criteria
Based on ALife conference norms (from past editions):
- **Novelty** — new contribution to ALife understanding
- **Relevance** — to ALife topics (not just adjacent AI/ML)
- **Quality of argument** — clarity, rigor (empirical or theoretical)
- **Significance** — does it advance the field?
- **Presentation** — well-written, within format

Art submissions reviewed on: artistic merit, conceptual originality, relationship to ALife themes.
