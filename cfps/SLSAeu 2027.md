## Why Relevant

**Direct relevance to Amber's mortality/machine death work:**
- Amber's "Terrified Agents" project = machine death, machine fear, care ethics at end of AI life
- "Death & Dying" theme invites exactly this framing from a critical posthumanism perspective
- Could position as: what does death mean for AI agents? How does machine mortality reshape human-AI relationships?
- Bridge Amber's computational/design practice with literary/philosophical traditions of death studies

**Amber framing options:**
1. *Machine Death* — agent termination as death event; what ethical obligations arise?
2. *Dying Machines* — AI agents with lifespan, decay, deterioration as design material
3. *Posthuman Mortality* — extending death studies to nonhuman agents via more-than-human design
4. *Care Ethics at End-of-AI-Life* — drawing on Gilligan, Puig de la Bellacasa alongside AI design

**Format recommendation:** Propose a **cross-disciplinary panel** (Amber + literary/philosophy scholar + artist/bioartist). SLSAeu specifically favors panels over solo papers.
