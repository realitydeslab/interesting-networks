## Why Relevant

ICML workshops are one of the most important community-building mechanisms in ML — they define emerging fields and attract the core ML community. Unlike primary venues (CHI, CSCW, FAccT), ICML workshops reach a mass ML audience. That's the opportunity: Amber's work on machine behavior, agent ethology, sovereign agents, and AI governance can reach ML researchers who haven't yet engaged with these framings.

**What's special about ICML workshops:**
- Papers typically 4–8 pages, **non-archival** by default (can submit elsewhere afterward = zero risk)
- Organizers grant 5 complimentary full conference registrations — co-organizers get in free
- Mix of invited talks + contributed papers + posters + panel discussions
- Workshop papers sometimes appear in PMLR proceedings (archival if opted into by workshop)
- Accept rate varies by workshop, typically 30–50% (vs. 26–28% for main track)

## 📋 ICML 2026 New Features — Key for Amber

### Position Papers Track (New at ICML 2026)
**[Full CFP](https://icml.cc/Conferences/2026/CallForPositionPapers)**

ICML 2026 introduced a dedicated **Position Papers** track — papers that argue for a viewpoint about what *should* be done, rather than reporting completed work. This is **ideal for Amber's conceptual/philosophical work**.

Review criteria (different from main track):
- Does the paper clearly state a position?
- Does it inspire constructive community discussion?
- Does it have a "Call to Action"?
- Does it address credible alternative views?

Format requirements:
- Title must state the position: `"Position: [claim]"`
- Must include "Alternative Views" section
- Must include "Call to Action" section

**Eligible topics include:**
- Ethical considerations in ML research and deployment
- How to improve the beneficial impact of ML
- Regulation of ML technology
- What next-gen ML researchers need to know

⚠️ **ICML 2026 deadline CLOSED (Jan 28, 2026).** But this is **a major opportunity for ICML 2027** — start drafting position papers now.

Example titles Amber could write:
- `"Position: ML Agents Should Be Studied as Organisms, Not Programs"`
- `"Position: Machine Behavior Requires Ethology, Not Only Alignment"`
- `"Position: Sovereign AI Agents Need a New Legal and Moral Framework"`
- `"Position: Cosmotechnics Must Inform AI Development Beyond WEIRD Assumptions"`

### Other ICML 2026 New Features
- **Paper Assistant Tool (PAT)** by Google — experimental AI review assistance
- **Self-ranking in reviews** (Weijie Su / Integrity Chair) — reviewers self-assess their reviews
- **New integrity policies** — stricter conflict-of-interest, reviewer accountability

### Is ICML the Right Venue?

**Yes for workshops, cautiously for main track.**

ICML's core community is ML methodology — algorithms, systems, theory. Amber's work is interdisciplinary. The right entry point is:

1. **Workshops** (March 20 onwards) — find the right one, submit a non-archival workshop paper
