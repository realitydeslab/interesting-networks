## Why Relevant

RO-MAN is not Amber's core venue, but there are tangential connections:

1. **Machine behavior** — how robots express behavior, communicate, and engage with humans overlaps with agent ethology
2. **More-than-human design** — designing HRI that treats robots as genuine agents, not tools
3. **Japan context** — Kitakyushu conference; strong Japanese HRI culture (robot companions, social robots) intersects with East Asian technology philosophy
4. **Late Breaking Reports** — 2-page format, ideal for preliminary work or work-in-progress

**Weak fit:** RO-MAN is primarily an engineering conference. Papers focus on robot manipulation, gesture recognition, emotion detection. The critical/philosophical angle Amber brings may feel out of place without strong empirical HRI component.

**Stronger fit would be:** Papers on how humans perceive robot agency, interpret robot "death" or failure, or attribute intentionality — these have clear RO-MAN angle.
