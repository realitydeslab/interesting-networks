## The Conference

### Theme & Vision

The 2026 theme — "Realizing Human-Robot Symbiosis with AI" — emphasizes robots as collaborators and teammates rather than replacements. The conference explicitly asks: how can robots complement rather than substitute humans? "Hybrid intelligence where robots and humans together can be more capable, effective, productive, efficient, and enjoyable." This is operationally focused but has philosophical room.

Importantly, the **[FAQ](https://ro-man2026.org/faq/)** explicitly states: *"Papers will not be rejected solely on the grounds that they are not highly technical. The acceptance criteria strongly emphasize the paper's quality and its contribution to the community."* — Position and perspective papers are eligible.

### Scale & Community

RO-MAN is the major IEEE annual conference on HRI, running since 1992. It draws ~300-500 attendees. Community breakdown: academic robotics labs (majority), some HCI researchers, occasional industry presence. Japanese robotics community is well-represented given the geographic rotation. The steering committee shows the conference has deep roots in Europe, North America, and East Asia.

### Acceptance Rates

Not published officially. Based on community knowledge: ~30–40% acceptance rate for regular papers. LBRs have higher acceptance, as they are lower-stakes (2-page, poster presentation).

## Late Breaking Reports (LBR) — KEY TRACK

**Deadline: June 12, 2026 (Friday) | Notification: June 26, 2026**

LBR is the most realistic and actionable track for Amber. Key details:

### Format
- **Page limit:** 2 pages (IEEE two-column format, based on RO-MAN convention; exact format TBD on LBR submission page when it opens)
- **Presentation:** Interactive/poster session (designated interactive sessions — not full oral)
- **Proceedings:** Published in IEEE Xplore (counts as citable publication)
- **Submission system:** [PaperPlaza (PaperCept)](https://ras.papercept.net/)
- **Review:** Lower bar than regular papers; community review; typically 50-70% acceptance

### What Gets Accepted
Based on RO-MAN LBR history:
- **Work-in-progress** — "recent innovative applications and work-in-progress reports"
- Preliminary experimental results with a robot or HRI setup
- Novel concepts/positions backed by initial observations
- Design explorations (concept + motivation + early prototype)
- Cross-disciplinary framings not yet fully developed

The key bar is: **novel idea + interesting preliminary evidence or clear argument**. Not a full paper. Not speculation without grounding.

## RO-MAN History & Community

### Community Profile
- **Who attends:** Academic HRI labs (majority), robotics engineers, some cognitive scientists and HCI researchers, occasional artists/designers
- **Industry presence:** Limited — more than CHI but less than CES. Social robotics companies (Furhat, PAL, IROBOT) occasionally present
- **Philosophy/critical track:** Weak by default, but growing. Topics like "Philosophical Issues in Human-Robot Coexistence" have been accepted. Position papers are explicitly allowed (per FAQ). The HREI special session in 2026 is a rare critical-design entry point.
- **Japanese robotics culture:** Deeply present. Masahiro Mori's "uncanny valley" hypothesis (1970) is foundational; Hiroshi Ishiguro's android work recurs at RO-MAN. This cultural context is significant for Amber's cosmotechnics angle — Japanese philosophy of technology (robots as companions in Buddhist cosmology, PARO at hospices) maps closely to Yuk Hui's project.
