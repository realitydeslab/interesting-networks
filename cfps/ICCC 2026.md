## Why Relevant

ICCC sits at the crossroads of:
- **AI systems as autonomous agents** — machine creativity framed through emergence, behavior, autonomy
- **Interdisciplinary perspectives** — philosophy, sociology, design, arts
- **Social models of creativity** — multi-agent dynamics, social creativity, idea diffusion
- **Human-machine co-creativity** — designing with AI entities

Amber's angles:
- **Agent ethology framed as machine creativity** — agents exhibiting creative-seeming behavior; etho-method lens on what AI "does" autonomously
- **ALife as creative computation** — living systems as generative
- **Machine death/mortality** — creativity requires stakes; what happens to creative output when the machine dies?
- **More-than-human co-creativity** — designing WITH AI agents not just FOR humans

However: Amber is not primarily a CC researcher. Risk of not fitting the community's expectations. Would need to frame explicitly in CC terms (not just agent behavior or design).
