## What

**NeurIPS** (Conference on Neural Information Processing Systems) is the premier ML/AI conference (~15,000 attendees). Workshops run on the first two days — at NeurIPS 2025 (San Diego), workshops were December 6–7, with main conference December 8–13.

**NeurIPS 2026** is confirmed for **Sydney, Australia, 6–12 December 2026** — source: [neurips.cc](https://neurips.cc/) homepage announcement.

Workshops are half-day or full-day (7–9 hours) satellite events proposable by the community. They're an informal, agenda-setting venue — the official CFP says: *"Good workshops have helped to crystallize common problems and emerging scientific paradigms, explicitly contrast competing frameworks, and clarify essential questions."* That's a direct match to Amber's approach.

## Workshop Proposal Process

From the [NeurIPS 2025 Guidance for Workshop Proposals](https://neurips.cc/Conferences/2025/CallForWorkshopsGuidance):

1. **Read the guidance** — there's a detailed template and philosophy document
2. **Form a team** — multi-institution organizer teams are expected; solo proposals rarely accepted
3. **Submit via OpenReview** — proposals are peer-reviewed by the workshop chairs
4. **Key selection criteria:** Does the topic serve an "appreciable fraction" of the NeurIPS community? Does it complement (not duplicate) main conference? Does it build community?
5. **Community building** — NeurIPS explicitly expects workshops to include community-forming elements, not just paper sessions

**Proposing a workshop at NeurIPS is hard for Amber right now:**
- NeurIPS community is primarily ML engineers/researchers — Amber's design/humanities framing needs ML co-organizers to be credible
- Better path: submit a paper to an existing aligned workshop, establish presence, then propose in 2027–2028
