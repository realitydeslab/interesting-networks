## What

**[ICLR 2026](https://iclr.cc/Conferences/2026)** (International Conference on Learning Representations) — top-tier ML venue, April 24–25 main conference, April 26–27 workshops.

- **Location:** Rio de Janeiro, Brazil
- **Workshop date:** Sunday, April 26, 2026 (05:00 AM – 01:00 PM PDT)

### Workshop Focus

The **"Agents in the Wild: Safety, Security, and Beyond"** workshop investigates LLM-based agents deployed in real environments — covering safety, security, unexpected emergent behaviors, and real-world trustworthiness challenges. "In the Wild" refers to uncontrolled deployment conditions. The "and Beyond" signals openness to broader societal and behavioral implications beyond the technical safety/security core.

**Topics of interest (inferred from organizer research profiles):**
- LLM agent safety in real deployment environments
- Adversarial attacks on agentic systems
- Trustworthiness benchmarks for AI agents (multimodal, video, text)
- Alignment and refusal mechanisms (activation steering, representation steering)
- Agentic reasoning, instruction-following, and failure modes
- Multi-agent safety dynamics
- Data-centric approaches to trustworthy AI

- The jump from data/alignment to "agents in the wild" reflects the field's pivot toward agentic AI deployment concerns in 2025-2026

### Two "Agentic Wild" Workshops at ICLR 2026
Notably, ICLR 2026 has **two** workshops with the "wild" framing on consecutive days:
1. **"Agents in the Wild: Safety, Security, and Beyond"** (April 26) — this one; Wang Lab + Dawn Song
2. **"Agentic AI in the Wild: From Hallucinations to Reliable Autonomy"** (April 27) — Grigorios Chrysos, Yixuan Li, Etsuko Ishii, Xuefeng Du, Katia Sycara

The existence of two similar workshops signals that "agents in wild environments" is a hot framing in 2026. Both are primarily technical (safety, hallucinations, reliability) but both gesture toward broader deployment realities.

### ICLR 2026 Community Vibe
ICLR is the premier deep learning conference. At the 2026 edition, the dominant theme is clearly **agentic AI and its challenges**. Multiple workshops touch on agents: Lifelong Agents, Multi-Agent Learning, MemAgents, Agentic AI in the Wild, Post-AGI Science. The field is grappling with: what happens when these systems run unsupervised?

## Community Mapping: Overlaps with Agent Ethology & Machine Behavior

### Where the Communities Meet
The "agents in the wild" ML safety community and Amber's machine behavior / agent ethology framing share:
- The observation that AI agents behave **unexpectedly** in real deployment
- The question: what do agents actually *do* vs. what were they designed to do?
- Interest in cataloguing and understanding emergent behavioral patterns

### Where They Diverge
| Dimension | ML Safety Community | Agent Ethology (Amber's framing) |
|-----------|--------------------|---------------------------------|

| **Goal** | Fix/align the agent | Understand the agent as it is |
| **Epistemics** | Engineering | Science/philosophy |
| **Orientation** | Normative (agents *should* be safe) | Descriptive (agents *are* in environments) |

## For Amber

### ❌ What NOT to Do
Don't submit Amber's philosophical/design work as-is to this workshop. The community is technical ML safety engineers. A paper about "cosmotechnics of machine death" or "somaesthetic agent observation" would land badly.

### ✅ Strategic Bridge Opportunities for ICLR 2027

**Option 1: Agent Ethology as a Safety Framework**
Reframe agent ethology as: "Before we can make agents safe, we need to understand what they actually do in the wild — an ethological foundation for AI safety." This bridges Amber's observational approach with the workshop's core concerns.
- Paper candidate: **Agent Ethology** — position it as contributing to safety by first characterizing behavioral profiles
- Key framing: "Agents in the wild exhibit behavioral phenotypes that current safety benchmarks don't capture"

**Option 2: Sovereign Agents ↔ Agency and Safety**
Amber's Sovereign Agents work (FAccT 2026) explores the governance implications of autonomous agency. This has direct relevance to "Agents in the Wild: Safety, Security, and Beyond."
- Paper candidate: **Sovereign Agents** — governance and accountability frameworks for autonomous agents
- Target: the "Beyond" in the workshop title — what lies beyond purely technical safety?

**Option 3: Machine Death / Terrified Agents as Edge Case**
Agents that refuse, degrade, fail, or die are an under-studied edge case in AI safety. Amber's machine death work could be framed as: what happens at the safety/failure boundary?
- Paper candidate: **Machine Death** / **Terrified Agents** — phenomenology of agent failure modes
- Key framing: "Safety discussions assume agents that want to comply; what about agents that don't, can't, or are designed to refuse?"

**Option 4: Target the "Post-AGI Science and Society" Workshop Instead**
For ICLR 2027, the equivalent of the "Post-AGI Science and Society Workshop" is likely a better fit for Amber's framing. Watch for it in the 2027 workshop lineup.
