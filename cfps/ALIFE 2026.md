## Submission Tracks

### Full Papers (3–8 pages, excl. references)
- New, unpublished work
- Peer reviewed as self-contained
- Published in MIT Press open-access proceedings (with DOIs)
- Assigned oral or poster presentation
- Priority for oral presentations over Summaries

### Summaries (max 2 pages)
- Summarizes previously published, peer-reviewed work (not preprints — those go as Full Papers)
- NOT included in proceedings; no DOI
- Oral or poster presentation

### Late-Breaking Abstracts (max 2 pages, excl. references)
- New ideas, work in progress
- Not in proceedings
- Poster presentation only
- Reviewed for relevance + quality

### Journal Fast-Track
- Papers accepted in the *Artificial Life* journal (MIT Press) are **automatically accepted** as oral presentations at ALIFE 2026
- Authors still must register for in-person attendance (online presenters may attend their session free)

## Conference Profile & Community

ALIFE is the world's premier artificial life conference, running since 1987. It is the flagship event of the International Society for Artificial Life (ISAL). The community spans:

- **Evolutionary computation / evolutionary robotics** (EC, morphogenetic robotics)
- **Open-endedness** research (Kenneth Stanley, Joel Lehman's home turf)
- **Agent-based modeling** and emergence
- **Synthetic / wet biology**
- **Cognitive science, embodied cognition**
- **ALife-based art** (strong art track)
- **Philosophy of biology and life**
- Increasing interest in **AI safety adjacent** topics (ethics, existential risk)

Attendance: typically 200–400 researchers. Acceptance rate: ~50% historically (mix of full papers and posters). More selective for oral presentations.

## For Amber — Strategic Fit Analysis

### Why ALIFE is Amber's Home Venue

1. **Joel Lehman** (Amber's ERA supervisor) is one of the fathers of open-endedness and a defining figure in ALife — submitting to ALIFE connects directly to Amber's advisor's core community.

2. **Machine behavior / agent ethology** — ALIFE's "Emergence of Signaling, Social Organization, and Culture" and "Minimal, Behaviour-based Robotics" tracks map directly.

3. **Machine death / mortality** — "Ethics, Existential Risks, and Philosophy of Artificial Life" + "Autopoiesis, Self/Other" tracks. Machine death as a philosophical ALife concept is novel and provocative — excellent fit.

4. **Open-endedness** — Core ALife topic. Kenneth Stanley keynoted ALIFE 2025. Amber's ERA fellowship with Joel Lehman on open-endedness makes this essential territory.

5. **Art/Design track** — "ALife-based Art and Theatre" explicitly listed. Speculative design and machine mortality could be presented as art + research crossover.

6. **More-than-human design / cosmotechnics** — "Ethics, Philosophy of ALife" and "Narrative/Social Intelligence" tracks accommodate this.

### Recommended Submission Strategy

**Priority 1 — Full Paper (deadline March 30)**
- Topic: Agent ethology as a framework for understanding machine behavior in ALife systems, OR machine death/mortality as an ALife design constraint
- Pitch: "What can ALife teach us about how agents die? And what can machine death teach us about open-ended evolution?"
- Connects: open-endedness (Lehman), agent behavior (ethology), mortality (existential risk / philosophy track)
- Length: 4–6 pages is typical sweet spot

**Priority 2 — Late-Breaking Abstract (deadline July 20)**
- Lower stakes, poster format — good for very new/speculative work
- Can present work-in-progress on insured agents, machine death, sovereign agents

**Priority 3 — Art Track submission**
- If any speculative design or installation piece is ready

### The Pitch (for Full Paper)

> **"Thanatogenesis: Machine Death as a Principle for Open-Ended Artificial Life"**
> How programmed mortality shapes agent behavior, drives evolutionary transitions, and enables richer ecological dynamics in ALife systems. Drawing on agent ethology (observational study of artificial agent behavior), we examine how death-as-design creates adaptive pressures that static systems lack.

Or alternatively:

> **"Agent Ethology: An Observational Framework for Studying Artificial Agent Behavior in the Wild"**
> Proposing a methodology borrowed from ethology (behavioral biology) to systematically study how AI agents behave in complex, open-ended environments.
