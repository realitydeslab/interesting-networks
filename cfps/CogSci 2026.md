## Why Relevant

CogSci is the premier interdisciplinary venue for mind-related research. Relevant to Amber's work because:

1. **Machine cognition** — CogSci increasingly includes AI cognition, comparing machine and biological intelligence
2. **Embodied cognition** — relevant to Amber's somaesthetic and more-than-human design work
3. **Active inference / predictive processing** — theoretical framework bridging biology and AI that appears in ALife work
4. **Animal cognition comparisons** — useful bridge to agent ethology framing

**Honest fit assessment:** CogSci is primarily about human and animal cognition, not AI. Papers tend to be empirical, psychologically grounded. Amber's work is philosophical/design-oriented. The fit is tangential unless she has an empirical component comparing machine and human behavior.

**Better fit would be:** A paper on how people perceive AI agents as having beliefs/intentions/agency — this is classic CogSci territory that connects to Amber's agent ethology frame.
