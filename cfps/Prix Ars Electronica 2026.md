## What

The **Prix Ars Electronica** is the world's longest-running and most prestigious media art competition — widely called the "Oscar of digital art." Established 1987 by Hannes Leopoldseder, it receives ~3,000–4,000 submissions annually from ~100 nations. Winners receive the **Golden Nica** (statuette + €10,000 + prominent festival appearance at [Ars Electronica Festival](https://ars.electronica.art/festival/en/) in Linz, Austria).

**One submission → three competitions:** A single submission automatically enters the Prix Ars Electronica, and can also be considered for the [S+T+ARTS Prize](https://starts-prize.aec.at/en/) (European Commission, €20,000 per winner) and other partnered awards.

## 2026 Categories

### ✅ Interactive Art+ — **PRIMARY TARGET FOR AMBER**
- URL: https://ars.electronica.art/prix/en/categories/interactive-art/
- Dedicated to artistic research in all forms: installations, performances, emerging engagement models
- Focus on interaction methods that drive **transformative paradigms** — influencing people, reshaping behaviors, sparking meaningful change
- Explicitly welcomes: "interaction designs that envision futures where humans and machines coexist autonomously, and works that expand the ways communication can unfold — among humans as well as between human and non-human entities"
- Accepts: installations, performances, audience participation, network projects, AI/ML, **VR/AR/MR/XR**, telepresence, innovative interfaces, robotics, software, apps
- Prizes: 1 Golden Nica (€10,000 + statuette), 2 Awards of Distinction, up to 12 Honorary Mentions
- Past Golden Nica winners:
  - **2024:** [Nosukaay](https://calls.ars.electronica.art/2025/prix/winners/15487/) / Diane Cescutti — participatory performance
  - **2022:** [Bi0film.net: Resist like bacteria](https://ars.electronica.art/prix/en/) / Jung Hsu, Natalia Rivera — biofilm-inspired resistance network
  - **2020:** [SOMEONE](https://lauren-mccarthy.com/SOMEONE) / Lauren Lee McCarthy — AI home surveillance/care installation
  - **2016:** [Can you hear me?](https://wachter-jud.net/can-you-hear-me/) / Christoph Wachter, Mathias Jud — rogue WiFi in conflict zones
  - **2014:** [Loophole for All](https://paolocirio.net/work/loophole-for-all/) / Paolo Cirio — tax haven data leaks

## Submission Requirements

|-------|-------------|

| **Project description** | Within **2,000 characters** — artistic concept, interaction design, technical realization |
| **Artist portrait + bio** | Required |
| **Additional material** | Optional: images, PDF, drawings |
| **AI disclosure** | Optional but encouraged: what AI used, how |

| **Work must be realized** | Jury needs documentation to judge — fully speculative/unrealized work excluded |
| **Submission fee** | **No fee** |
| **Submission portal** | https://ars.electronica.art/prix/en/ |

## Prestige & Acceptance Rates

- ~3,987 submissions from 98 nations (2025)
- 1 Golden Nica per category (~0.025%)
- 2 Awards of Distinction per category (~0.05%)
- Up to 12 Honorary Mentions per category (~0.3%)
- "Any recognition" acceptance rate: ~0.35–0.5% per submission
- Winners include: Joseph Weizenbaum, Christa Sommerer, TeamLab, Memo Akten, Lauren Lee McCarthy, Forensic Architecture, Random International
- Festival draws ~100,000 visitors across 5 days in Linz

**Prestige/effort ratio:** Very high. The submission materials (3-min video + 2000-char description) are moderate effort for very high return — if you already have documentation of a realized work.
