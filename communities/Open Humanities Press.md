# Open Humanities Press

An international, **scholar-led open access publishing collective** founded in **2006** by [[Gary Hall]], [[Sigi Jöttkandt]], Paul Ashton, and David Ottina. Not a conventional publisher with a business model — OHP is a volunteer-run, not-for-profit Community Interest Company (registered London, Companies Act 2006, No. 8481225), legally structured to serve a public benefit: making critical thought free.

OHP embodies the **Radical Open Access** philosophy: not OA as compliance checkbox or APC-funded business model, but as a **political commitment** — a challenge to the commercialisation and enclosure of scholarly knowledge. All content is published under **Creative Commons Attribution 4.0** licence. No cookies, no user tracking. Hosted by [Ibiblio](https://www.ibiblio.org/) at UNC Chapel Hill.

The three current **Directors** are:
- **[[Gary Hall]]** — co-founder, media philosopher, Professor at Coventry University
- **[[Sigi Jöttkandt]]** — co-founder, UNSW Sydney; Lacanian-inflected literary studies
- **[[Joanna Zylinska]]** — Director, Professor at King's College London; media philosophy + AI art

## Intellectual Mission

OHP's self-description: *"A grassroots response to the crisis in scholarly publishing in the humanities."* Its founding principles are Access, Scholarship, Diversity, and Transparency. All publications are peer-reviewed (OHP maintains that radical OA does not mean abandoning scholarly rigour — it means decoupling rigour from commercial gatekeeping).

The site is deliberately minimal — no analytics, no institutional branding theatre, no prestige performance. It is an **infrastructure for ideas**, not an imprint for status.

What distinguishes OHP from other open-access publishers:
1. **Volunteer scholar-run** — no paid staff; the work is done by the community
2. **No APCs** — authors don't pay; readers don't pay; the system runs on scholarly labour
3. **Radical, not compliant** — founding member of the [[Radical Open Access Collective]] (2015), which critiques even "gold" OA as often reproducing commercial logic
4. **Experimentalism** — the Liquid Books, Living Books, Combinatorial Books series actively rethink the book as form
5. **Genuine pluralism** — the board includes Alain Badiou, Donna Haraway, Graham Harman, N. Katherine Hayles, Bruno Latour, Gayatri Chakravorty Spivak, Ngũgĩ wa Thiong'o — not a narrow school

## Book Series (11 Active)

### 1. MEDIA : ART : WRITE : NOW
**Editor:** [[Joanna Zylinska]] (King's College London)

The flagship series for Amber's research territory. Short, urgent texts (between essay and monograph) on technology-based arts and critical digital practice. Series description: *"to recalibrate how we see, hear and feel in the contemporary mediated environment."*

**Advisory Board:** [[Hito Steyerl]], [[Eduardo Kac]], [[Oron Catts]] & [[Ionat Zurr]] (SymbioticA), [[Cornelia Sollfrank]], [[Mark Amerika]], Morehshin Allahyari, Kristoffer Gansing (Royal Danish Academy), [[Nishant Shah]] (CUHK), Kate Mondloch (Oregon), Esther Leslie (Birkbeck), Ryszard Kluszczyński (Łódź), Kenneth Goldsmith (Penn), Eduardo Kac (SAIC), [[Katrina Sluis]] (ANU), Asbjørn Grønstad (Bergen), Benjamin Mayer-Foulkes (17: Institute of Critical Studies, Mexico)

**Published Titles:**

- *Masked Media: What It Means to Be Human in the Age of Artificial Creative Intelligence* — [[Gary Hall]] (2025) — co-written with AI assemblage; published under Collective Conditions for Re-Use licence; challenges liberal humanist authorship

### 2. Critical Climate Change
**Editors:** Tom Cohen (University at Albany) & Claire Colebrook (Penn State)

Seminal series addressing the Anthropocene not as political messaging but as ontological event. Scope: *"material agencies that impact on biomass and energy, erased borders and microbial invention, geological and nanographic time, and extinction events."* Premised on the idea that standard critical languages are inadequate to the situation.

**Advisory Board:** Rosi Braidotti (Utrecht), Eduardo Cadava (Princeton), Timothy Clark (Durham), Timothy Morton (Rice), J. Hillis Miller (UC Irvine), Isabelle Stengers (Brussels)

**Key Titles:**
- *In Catastrophic Times: Resisting the Coming Barbarism* — [[Isabelle Stengers]] — her urgent political manifesto
- *Minimal Ethics for the Anthropocene* — [[Joanna Zylinska]] — ethics scaled to planetary crisis; free OA
- *Death of the PostHuman: Essays on Extinction Vol. 1* — Claire Colebrook — posthumanism's logical endpoint
- *Art in the Anthropocene* — Heather Davis & Etienne Turpin (eds.)
- *Architecture in the Anthropocene* — Etienne Turpin (ed.)
- *The Chernobyl Herbarium* — Michael Marder & Anaïs Tondeur — nuclear landscapes and plant philosophy
- *Twilight of the Anthropocene Idols* — Tom Cohen, Claire Colebrook & J. Hillis Miller

### 3. CCC2 Irreversibility
**Editors:** Tom Cohen & Claire Colebrook

The "second phase" of Critical Climate Change — after the tipping points have tipped. Premise: we are now in an era of *managed extinction events* and the speculative window of "Anthropocene talk" has closed. Also runs a sub-series: **The Nethercene: Ecocide and Inscription**, which interrogates AI as another "singularity" racing against ecological collapse.

**Advisory Board includes:** [[Yuk Hui]] (Leuphana), Nick Mirzoeff (NYU), Daniel Ross (Ars Industrialis), Oxana Timofeeva (European University at St Petersburg)

**Key Titles:**
- *The Neganthropocene* — [[Bernard Stiegler]] — his late political ecology, drawing from Derrida and Simondon
- *Nanjing Lectures (2016-2019)* — [[Bernard Stiegler]]
- *Bifurcate: There Is No Alternative* — [[Bernard Stiegler]] & the Internation Collective — final call for a different economy
- *hyposubjects: On Becoming Human* — [[Timothy Morton]] & Dominic Boyer — ecological subjectivity
- *Anthropocene Back Loop: Experimentation in Unsafe Operating Space* — Stephanie Wakefield
- *Ecocide and Inscription vol. 1 Black Ops* — Tom Cohen — "hyper-blackness" beyond racial binaries

### 4. New Metaphysics
**Editors:** Graham Harman (UC Santa Cruz / European Graduate School) & Bruno Latour (Sciences-Po) †

The series that launched Object-Oriented Ontology (OOO) as a publishing tradition. Series manifesto: against *"nail-filing analytic critique and the continental reverence for dusty textual monuments"* — in favour of *"the spirit of the intellectual gambler."* Harman describes it as seeking a new metaphysical "sound" from any nation. Still actively publishing.

**Key Titles:**
- *Realist Magic: Objects, Ontology, Causality* — [[Timothy Morton]] — OOO theory of causality; second ed. now published
- *The Democracy of Objects* — Levi R. Bryant — flat ontology
- *New Materialism: Interviews & Cartographies* — Rick Dolphijn & Iris van der Tuin (eds.) — landmark interviews with Haraway, Barad, DeLanda, Braidotti
- *Being Up For Grabs* — Hilan Bensusan — metaphysics of indeterminacy
- *Machine Sensation* — Tessa G. Leach

**Editor:** Andrew Murphie (UNSW)

**Advisory Board:** Erin Manning (Concordia), Brian Massumi (Montréal), Anna Munster (UNSW), Brett Neilson (Western Sydney), Ned Rossiter (Western Sydney), Adrian Mackenzie (Lancaster), Matthew Fuller (Goldsmiths), Nathaniel Tkacz (Warwick)

### 7. Low Latencies
**Editors:** Brett Neilson & Ned Rossiter (Western Sydney University)

Political economy of speed, logistics, computation. Series title taken from high-frequency trading — low latency as the optimisation of speed to outrun competition. Also registers "a certain melancholia that infects the circuits of the social brain." Advisory includes [[Shannon Mattern]] (New School), [[Jussi Parikka]] (Aarhus + Prague), Sandro Mezzadra (Bologna).

**Titles:** *Contagion Design*, *Data Farms*, *Logistical Worlds* series (Kolkata, Valparaiso)

### 8. Immediations
**Editor:** SenseLab (Erin Manning, Anna Munster, Bodil Marie Stavning Thomsen)

Philosophy of affect, co-composition, relational practice. Whitehead-inflected. **Note: Series has moved to punctum books under the [3Ecologies imprint](https://punctumbooks.com/imprints/3ecologies-books/).** Still appears in OHP catalogue.

**Key Titles:** *Immediation I & II* (Manning, Munster, Thomsen), *The Principle of Unrest* (Brian Massumi), *Nocturnal Fabulations*, *Ways of Following*

### 9. Liquid and Living Books
**Editor:** [[Gary Hall]] (+ Clare Birchall, Janneke Adema)

A cluster of three experimental sub-series:
- **Liquid Books** — open editing: readers invited to annotate, remix, rewrite. "Frozen" versions of most interesting results published as new volumes
- **Living Books About Life** — JISC-funded; curated OA books bridging humanities and sciences; topics included bioethics, e-waste, energy, pharmacology; co-produced with Coventry University, Goldsmiths, University of Kent
- **Combinatorial Books: Gathering Flowers** — creative remix of OHP back catalogue using CC licences + open authoring tools; developed with the [COPIM project](https://copim.pub/)

## Radical Open Access Collective (ROAC)

Founded 2015 by OHP together with other scholar-led presses. Members include:
- **Open Book Publishers** (Cambridge)
- **Mattering Press**
- **Mayfly Books**
- **meson press** (Leuphana)
- **punctum books** — also host for the Immediations series' 3Ecologies imprint
- **re.press**

The ROAC distinguishes "radical OA" from mainstream OA: opposed to Article Processing Charges (APCs) which simply move the paywall from reader to author. Advocates for collectively maintained, community-controlled infrastructure. Gary Hall published *Publishing Activism within/without a Toxic University* (OHP, 2024) — a collaborative essay using Surrealist cadavre exquis method, by ROAC members, reflecting on publishing activism inside/against the neoliberal university.

## What Makes OHP Distinctive

1. **No commercial logic at all.** Not a foundation-funded press pretending to be radical. Not a "gold OA" press charging APCs. Genuinely volunteer-run.

2. **Form experimentation.** Liquid Books (open editing), Living Books (living/updatable), Combinatorial Books (creative remix) — the book form is questioned, not just the business model.

3. **Series autonomy.** Each series has independent editors and review processes. OHP is a federation, not a monolith. The New Metaphysics series (Harman) and the Critical Climate Change series (Cohen/Colebrook) have almost nothing stylistically in common — and that's intentional.

4. **Theoretical ambition.** The editorial board and series advisory boards are not PR lists — they are active intellectuals who set the tone. This is not a repository but a curatorial project.

5. **Short-form books.** The MEDIA:ART:WRITE:NOW series specifically chose the format *between essay and monograph* — urgent, writable in months not years, accessible not exhaustive. This is a deliberate counter to the 400-page theory brick.

6. **Interconnection.** OHP, Radical OA Collective, Culture Machine journal, Fibreculture community, SenseLab, COPIM project — all interlocking; Hall and Zylinska are nodes in all of them simultaneously.

## For Amber

**Primary Value: Discovery Channel**

The MEDIA:ART:WRITE:NOW series is essentially a curated feed of exactly the kind of ideas that matter here — AI art, computational arts, glitch, posthuman photography, mycology-as-media, geological cinema. Everything is free to download. Start here, read everything in the series.

**Secondary Value: Publishing Platform**

If Amber develops writing at the intersection of AI, art, and critical theory, OHP is the natural publisher. The MEDIA:ART:WRITE:NOW series specifically publishes short, urgent texts (not 400-page monographs). Contact Joanna Zylinska directly (joanna.zylinska@kcl.ac.uk). The series takes experimental, practice-adjacent writing.

**Tertiary Value: Political Model**

OHP is a working example of how to build scholarly publishing infrastructure outside commercial logic. For anyone thinking about how knowledge production should work in an AI age, this is a live experiment — 20 years of experience, sustained by volunteer labour and political commitment.
