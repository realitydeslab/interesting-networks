# NYU Shanghai IMA (Interactive Media Arts)

Academic community of artists, thinkers, and makers at [NYU Shanghai](https://shanghai.nyu.edu/). Dedicated to cultivating cutting-edge theory and practice within media arts. Fosters interdisciplinary research, critical and artistic exploration, experimentation, and collaboration. Trains future generations of artists and scholars reshaping the landscape of emerging media.

**What makes it unique:** IMA sits at the intersection of Western media theory and Chinese technoculture, housed in Shanghai — one of the world's most rapidly mutating cities. It's not just a media arts department; it's a node where continental philosophy, accelerationism (via [[CCRU]] alumni), East Asian intellectual traditions, and hands-on making converge. The [Center for AI and Culture (AIC)](https://aic.shanghai.nyu.edu) gives it a research arm that punches far above a typical undergraduate department's weight.

**The vibe:** Theory-heavy but practice-oriented. Faculty include CCRU founding members, Princeton-trained media theorists, Ars Electronica exhibitors, and maker-educators. The student body is international (Sino-American university). Courses range from "After Us: Posthuman Media" and "Writing with AI: Philosophy and Practice" to "Machine Learning for Artists and Designers" and "Bio-Inspired Robotic Systems." The intellectual density is unusual for an arts department — closer to a hybrid of NYU ITP + a philosophy department + a fabrication lab.

## Center for AI and Culture (AIC)

<https://aic.shanghai.nyu.edu>

Directed by [[Anna Greenspan]]. Investigates the philosophical and cultural implications of AI as it transforms the contemporary world. Makes full use of its Shanghai location to research the cutting edge of emerging planetary media.

**Research approach:** Draws on philosophy (analytic and continental traditions), media theory, history of science and computation, cultural and artistic practice, as well as empirical research and production. Aims to think widely across cultures and disciplines.

## IMA Artist in Residency Programme

<https://ima.shanghai.nyu.edu/artists-residency>

**Year-long programme.** Residents join as visiting faculty, work on self-directed interdisciplinary research, teach one course per semester. Access to FabLab, Wood Shop, Emerging Media Studio, Lighting Studio, Research Studio. Awarded a research fund. Expected to involve students across the university. Final outcomes: workshops, lectures, exhibitions.

**Inaugural residents ('23–'24):** Dennis de Bel and Lei Ban.
