# NYU Shanghai IMA (Interactive Media Arts)

Academic community of artists, thinkers, and makers. Interdisciplinary media arts with strong theory backbone. Shanghai location gives it unique access to Chinese tech culture and art scenes.

## Key Faculty
- [[Bogna Konior]] — media theory, AI & culture, dark forest theory, AI × mysticism
- **Anna Greenspan** — contemporary global media, co-editor with Bratton & Konior on China AI book
- **Junnan Chen** — media theory, feminist theory, cybernetic aesthetics, Cold War Japan, extraterrestrial imaginaries, philosophy of time. Also filmmaker.
- **Marcela Godoy** — area head, media arts
- **Stavros Didakis** — interactive media
- **Gottfried Haider** — creative tech
- **Leon Eckert** — creative coding
- **J.H. Moon** — algorithmic animation, interaction design

## Connections
- [[Bogna Konior]] — faculty, connects to [[Antikythera]], [[Benjamin Bratton]], e-flux
- Anna Greenspan — connects to Urbanomic, China AI discourse
- Junnan Chen — connects to cybernetic aesthetics, East Asian studies, film festivals
- AI & Culture Research Center — housed here
- NYU ITP (New York) — sister programme

## Why Interesting
The Shanghai location + faculty mix creates a unique node: Western media theory meets Chinese tech culture. Bogna and Anna's China AI book project is a signal. Junnan Chen's cybernetic aesthetics + Japan + feminist theory is very Amber. The whole department sits at art × tech × critical theory × Asia, which is a rare and valuable intersection.
