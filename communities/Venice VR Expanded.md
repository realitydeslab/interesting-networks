# Venice VR Expanded

The immersive media section of the **Venice Film Festival** (Mostra Internazionale d'Arte Cinematografica), the world's oldest film festival. Launched as "Venice VR" in 2017, expanded to "VR Expanded" with outdoor/hybrid installations from 2021. Presents VR, AR, and installation-based works in competition alongside narrative and documentary cinema — giving immersive art cinema's most prestigious institutional legitimacy.

## Key Context
- **Part of the Venice Film Festival** (Biennale di Venezia), which runs August–September
- **One of the "big three" for immersive art**: alongside [[Sundance New Frontier]] (January, Park City) and [[IDFA DocLab]] (November, Amsterdam)
- Works that premiere at [[Sundance New Frontier]] often travel to Venice VR for European premiere
- [[Lynette Wallworth]]'s *Greenland Melting* was among the first VR films at Venice (2017)
- **Jury awards** given in Competition and Best Experience categories

## Submission
- Annual open call; typically opens in early spring (Feb–March) for August/September festival
- Monitor: <https://www.labiennale.org/en/cinema/submissions>

## Why Interesting for Amber
Second major step in the Sundance → Venice → museum/global circuit. A work that premieres at New Frontier would typically then target Venice VR. Getting into Venice VR alone is sufficient to be taken seriously by the museum acquisition circuit. For AR works using HoloKit: Venice VR has increasingly accepted non-headset installations.
