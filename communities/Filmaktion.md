# Filmaktion

A loose-knit group of experimental British filmmakers who worked and performed together in the early 1970s, all core members of the [[London Film-Makers Co-operative (LFMC)]]. Endorsed "a more active, participatory experience of cinema" — re-imagined film projection as a live event. Rejected conventional cinemas in favour of art venues and warehouses. Their improvisational and participatory approach made their work effectively performance-based: film as a live, embodied, immersive event.

*Note: Tate identifies all four as artists "associated with Filmaktion." The 2012 Tate Modern event featured Eatherley, Le Grice, and Raban. Nicolson's membership is confirmed by Tate's Expanded Cinema art term.*

## Approach
- Film as live event, not screened object
- Projection as performance medium — the projector, screen, and audience space as one integrated environment
- Improvisational: works performed differently in each space
- Rejection of the conventional cinema auditorium: works shown in art venues, warehouses, galleries
- All four members were also active in the LFMC — Filmaktion is the performance/expanded-cinema strand of LFMC practice

## Connected Institutions
- **[[London Film-Makers Co-operative (LFMC)]]** — all four members were LFMC members; Filmaktion is a tendency within the LFMC, not a separate organisation
- **[[LUX]]** — current distributor for Filmaktion-associated works

## Why Interesting
Filmaktion is the term that names the expanded/performance cinema tendency within British structural film — the practice of treating the projection event as a live performance, not a screened documentary record. It is directly relevant to any interest in the history of participatory/immersive installation, performance art intersecting with cinema, and the institutional history of experimental film in the UK.
