# LAS Art Foundation

Berlin-based non-profit art foundation at the intersection of art, science and technology. One of the most ambitious commissioning institutions in Europe for artist-led explorations of AI, quantum computing, ecology and interspecies futures. Hosts major exhibitions at iconic Berlin venues (Kraftwerk Berlin, Halle am Berghain) and commissions large-scale works that push boundaries between artistic practice and cutting-edge science. Championed [[terra0]]'s [[Autonomous Forest]] — a living DAO forest on Ethereum — as a flagship commission. Won the S+T+ARTS Grand Prize: Innovation Collaboration from the European Commission.

## Mission & Objectives
LAS Art Foundation (originally **Light Art Space**, rebranded April 2023) was founded in 2019 by transport entrepreneur **Jan Fischer** and art historian **Dr Bettina Kames**. Their mission: to be "a new type of arts organisation — one that interrogates our present and gives form to future imaginaries."

Key thematic programmes:
- **Sensing Quantum** — philosophical and artistic possibilities of quantum technologies (won S+T+ARTS Grand Prize)
- **Interspecies Future** — how planetary-scale technologies benefit human and non-human worlds
- **Artificial Intelligence** — how AI reshapes social, economic, and cultural structures
- **World-Building** — artists' imaginations as tools for exploring social structures
- **LAS Online** — internet-based and web-native artworks

LAS explicitly positions itself as *post-Anthropocentric* — "humankind doesn't see itself at the centre, but as part of an interconnected whole." Practically: they commission works for pollinators (Ginsberg), give forests DAO governance (terra0), explore quantum indeterminacy as philosophy (Huyghe).

## Key Publications & Output

### *Interspecies Future: A Primer* (2024)
[Published by LAS Art Foundation and Distanz Verlag](https://www.las-art.foundation/explore/publication-interspecies-future-a-primer). Edited by K Allado-McDowell, Julia Kaganskiy, Bettina Kames, Corinne Mynatt. 60+ contributors including:
- Artists: Jakob Kudsk Steensen, Anicka Yi, Jenna Sutela, Alexandra Daisy Ginsberg, Josèfa Ntjam, [[terra0]], James Bridle, Tomás Saraceno
- Scientists: Karen Bakker, Daniel Chamovitz, Mirjam Knörschild, Dorion Sagan, Tom Mustill
- Philosophers: Sue Donaldson, Tyson Yunkaporta, Vinciane Despret, Benjamin Bratton
- AR chapters by Wang & Söderström

This book is a landmark text for the interspecies/more-than-human art-science space. Originated from a July 2022 symposium at Museum für Naturkunde Berlin, chaired by K Allado-McDowell, Bettina Kames, Johannes Vogel, J. M. Ledgard.

## Key Contributions
- **Pioneering quantum art** — Sensing Quantum programme won S+T+ARTS Grand Prize: Innovation Collaboration from EU. Two major quantum commissions (Laure Prouvost 2025, Pierre Huyghe 2026).
- **Interspecies art at scale** — *Pollinator Pathmaker* is the first Continental European edition of Ginsberg's living sculpture. 7,000+ plants for pollinators.
- **DAO governance as art** — Commissioned *Autonomous Forest*: first German woodland managed by a DAO, merging Ethereum smart contracts with German civil law (Verein).
- **Landmark publication** — *Interspecies Future: A Primer* (2024) is the field's foundational text. 60+ contributors.
- **Venice Biennale presence** — Co-commissioning Natasha Tontey for Venice Biennale 2026 (with Amos Rex Helsinki).
- **Institutional model** — demonstrates how a private foundation can take genuine intellectual/artistic risk with emerging science.
