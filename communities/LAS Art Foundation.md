# LAS Art Foundation

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-03-01:** Deep research completed. Upgraded to ⭐⭐⭐ — direct relevance to Amber's more-than-human, AI, ALife, and speculative design interests. Key institution connecting Amber's Berlin network.
- **2026-03-01:** Stub created. Discovered during Interdependence (podcast) deep research.

Berlin-based non-profit arts foundation (formerly Light Art Space) commissioning ambitious, technology-engaged art at the intersections of AI, quantum computing, interspecies futures, ecology, and blockchain. Founded 2019 by Jan Fischer and Dr Bettina Kames. Operates at Halle am Berghain and Kraftwerk Berlin. Won S+T+ARTS Grand Prize from the European Commission (2025). The most institutionally serious art-tech commissioning foundation in Berlin — not a gallery, not a festival, but a full-spectrum R&D and programming organization with an explicit post-Anthropocene philosophical position.

## Mission & Objectives

From their [Charter](https://www.las-art.foundation/las-charter):

> "LAS Art Foundation is a new type of arts organisation — one that looks deep into our present and gives form to future imaginaries."

LAS investigates: artificial intelligence, quantum computing, ecology, biotechnology, Web3/blockchain, interspecies relations. Programme includes: newly commissioned installations, performances, educational programming, publications, and research projects.

**Core philosophical stance:** "LAS is led by a vision of the future whereby humankind doesn't see itself at the centre, but as part of an interconnected whole." Explicitly post-Anthropocene, interspecies, more-than-human. This is not decorative rhetoric — it shapes commissions.

**Programme thematic pillars:**
- **Sensing Quantum** — philosophical and artistic possibilities of quantum technologies
- **Interspecies Future** — planetary-scale, more-than-human futures
- **Artificial Intelligence** — social/cultural/economic AI reshaping
- **World-Building** — artists' world-building as social imaginary
- **Web3 / Blockchain** — decentralized governance and ownership experiments

## Programme History (Exhibitions & Commissions)

### 2026
- **Pierre Huyghe: [Liminals](https://www.las-art.foundation/programme/pierre-huyghe)** (Jan 23 – Mar 8, 2026) — Halle am Berghain. Large-scale quantum-AI film environment. Co-commissioned with Hartwig Art Foundation. The central work of the Sensing Quantum programme. Used 100-qubit Pasqal quantum computer (Forschungszentrum Jülich) to simulate matter oscillation and generate sound. Consulted with quantum physicist Tommaso Calarco and philosopher Tobias Rees. A "faceless, human-like figure" in a realm outside time and space — machine consciousness / liminal ontology.
- **Natasha Tontey: [The Phantom Combatants and the Metabolism of Disobedient Organs](https://www.las-art.foundation/programme/natasha-tontey-the-phantom-combatants-and-the-metabolism-of-disobedient-organs)** (May 9 – Oct 25, 2026) — Ateneo Veneto di Scienze, Lettere ed Arti, Venice. Co-commissioned with [Amos Rex](https://www.amosrex.fi/) (Helsinki). At Venice Biennale (61st International Art Exhibition). Multimedia installation: 1950s Indonesian female resistance fighter, bodily transformation, surveillance subversion, Minahasan ancestral ritual. Tontey is Minahasan, from Sulawesi.

### 2025
- **Laure Prouvost: WE FELT A STAR DYING** (Spring 2025) — Halle am Berghain. Part of Sensing Quantum programme. The commission that earned S+T+ARTS Grand Prize.
- **terra0: [Autonomous Forest](https://www.las-art.foundation/programme/terra0-autonomous-forest)** (from Nov 6, 2025) — Locations in Berlin and online. First German woodland managed by a DAO (Verein + Ethereum blockchain). NFT sale enables worldwide holders to acquire artwork and membership. Ongoing.

### 2024
- **Danielle Brathwaite-Shirley: THE SOUL STATION** (Summer 2024) — Halle am Berghain. Collaborative story-driven game installation with time loop, moral dilemmas, Black and Queer character focus. Curator: Dr Boris Magrini.
- **Josèfa Ntjam: swell of spæc(i)es** (2024) — interspecies video and installation work.
- **Alexandra Daisy Ginsberg: Pollinator Pathmaker LAS Edition** — culinary residency with Chelsea Turowsky (May–Aug 2024). Four tasting menus corresponding to seasonal changes in the pollinator garden.
- **Publication:** [*Interspecies Future: A Primer*](https://www.las-art.foundation/explore/publication-interspecies-future-a-primer) (April 2024) — Edited by K Allado-McDowell, Julia Kaganskiy, Bettina Kames, Corinne Mynatt. Published by LAS Art Foundation and Distanz Verlag. 60+ contributors: Jakob Kudsk Steensen, Anicka Yi, Jenna Sutela, Alexandra Daisy Ginsberg, Josèfa Ntjam, terra0, James Bridle, Tomás Saraceno + scientists Karen Bakker, Daniel Chamovitz, Dorion Sagan, Tom Mustill, Kristin Andrews + philosophers Sue Donaldson, Tyson Yunkaporta, Vinciane Despret, Benjamin Bratton. Each chapter opens with an AR animation by Wang & Söderström.
- **Online programme:** "To Fracture Time Is to Create an Otherwise" (June 2024 – March 2026)

### 2023
- **Alexandra Daisy Ginsberg: [Pollinator Pathmaker](https://www.las-art.foundation/programme/pollinator-pathmaker)** (June 20, 2023 – Nov 1, 2026) — Museum für Naturkunde Berlin. 722m² living sculpture with 7,000+ plants from 80 varieties. First Pollinator Pathmaker edition in Continental Europe. Algorithm-generated planting scheme optimized for local pollinator species.
- **Lawrence Lek: NOX** (2023) — Speculative near-future AI beings: self-driving vehicles Enigma-76 and Vanguard-3181. Weaves cinematic formats, gaming, locative sound, Eastern/Western philosophies, smart city processes. "NOX stands for Nonhuman Excellence." Public programme: expert talks on smart city design (CityLAB Berlin), AI training (DFKI's Prof Dr Wolfgang Wahlster), etc.
- **Marianna Simnett: Gorgon** (2023) — HAU Berlin. AI-fueled work connected to Herndon/Dryhurst network.

### 2022
- **Robert Irwin: Luminescence** (2022) — Kraftwerk Berlin. Light and Space movement legend in industrial Berlin site.

### 2021
- **Jakob Kudsk Steensen: Berl-Berl** (July 10 – Sep 26, 2021) — Halle am Berghain. Immersive installation: ecology and mythology of Berlin's origins as wetland 10,000 years ago. Macro photogrammetry of Berlin-Brandenburg wetlands. Sound by Matt McCorkle and [[Arca]] (musician). Museum für Naturkunde Berlin specimens integrated.
- **Ian Cheng: Artist Talk** (Sep 19, 2021) — Luma Westbau.

### 2019 (Launch)
- **Refik Anadol: Latent Being** (Nov 23, 2019 – Jan 5, 2020) — Kraftwerk Berlin. LAS's first exhibition. Site-specific AI data painting installation. Data about city, architecture, visitors transformed into hallucinogenic AI paintings on LED walls.

LAS is one of a handful of institutions worldwide that commission art at the exact intersection where Amber works: AI agency, more-than-human futures, posthuman systems, ecological entanglement, non-Western cosmotechnics (Natasha Tontey / Minahasan ritual), and speculative temporalities. Unlike festivals (transmediale, Ars Electronica), LAS produces sustained multi-year commissions with genuine R&D phases — they embed artists with quantum physicists. This is the institutional model Amber should study and eventually pitch to. The Sensing Quantum programme's S+T+ARTS Grand Prize signals EU-level legitimacy. The terra0 Autonomous Forest is the most interesting blockchain-governance-as-art project in current European scene. Most importantly: LAS operates at Halle am Berghain — the most culturally charged large venue in Berlin — and brings those audiences into contact with serious philosophical and scientific ideas. That's the convergence zone Amber's work needs.
