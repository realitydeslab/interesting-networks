# Leuphana Institute for Advanced Studies in Culture and Society (LIAS)

The [Leuphana Institute for Advanced Studies in Culture and Society (LIAS)](https://www.leuphana.de/en/research-centers/lias.html) is a research fellowship institute at [Leuphana University Lüneburg](https://www.leuphana.de/en/), co-directed by [[Erich Hörl]] and art historian [[Susanne Leeb]]. Founded April 2022, housed in the Daniel Libeskind-designed Central Building, LIAS hosts international humanities, social science, and cultural studies researchers for 3–12 month fellowships. Distinguished by deliberate postcolonial, Global South-oriented programming and grounding in Hörl's "general ecology" theory of technological environmentality. Think Wissenschaftskolleg Berlin but with critical-theoretical and non-Eurocentric commitments.

## Mission & Objectives

LIAS was established to bring international researchers together in extended dialogue — not the conference sprint model, but the **residency model** (one year for regular fellows). Its overarching programme is titled **"Culture and Society in Transformation"** and organised around four intellectual mottos:

1. **Thinking Across Hemispheres** — decolonial, multiperspectival, non-Eurocentric knowledge production
2. **Critique and Imagination** — critical diagnosis of the present + opening alternative possibilities
3. **Potentialisation** — revealing hidden possibilities against "there is no alternative" discourse
4. **Co-Existence in a Globalized World** — global entanglement, polycrisis, cohabitation

Funded initially through 2027 by the "Spitzenforschung für Niedersachsen" (SPRUNG) programme; from 2027 by "Zukunft. Niedersachsen." Phase 2 (from 2027) will centre on **"transformative knowledge"** — how processes of change (cybernetisation, decolonisation, globalisation, climate) get framed as technical problems, and what genuinely transformative knowledge looks like instead.

## Intellectual Lineage

LIAS sits at the intersection of several European critical theory traditions:

1. **German Medienwissenschaft** — Kittler, cybernetics, cultural techniques (Kulturtechniken). ICAM's historical specialisation. Claus Pias, Jan Müggenburg.
2. **French Philosophy of Technology** — Simondon, Stiegler, Derrida, Foucault, Deleuze/Guattari. Hörl's primary lineage. The concept of *pharmacology* (Stiegler), *individuation* (Simondon), *general organology*.
3. **General Ecology (Hörl)** — The proposition that we now live in a *technosphere* replacing nature as the encompassing milieu. All modes of existence are now technologically conditioned. Political question: how to develop critical ecological thinking for this condition.
4. **Postcolonial/Decolonial Theory** — Strong Global South emphasis: UWC partnership, Premesh Lalu, Radha D'Souza, various African fellows. Actively de-provincializing European critical theory.
5. **Feminist/Postcapitalist Theory** — Katherine Gibson's community economies and *A Postcapitalist Politics*. Care ethics, commons, more-than-human economies.
6. **Frankfurt School** — Alex Demirović, "Cultures of Critique" doctoral programme. Critical theory tradition preserved and updated.

## Why Interesting

LIAS represents the best of European advanced-study culture: genuinely long-form intellectual engagement (12 months, not 3 days), explicit decolonial commitments, and grounded in one of the most relevant philosophical traditions for Amber's work (general ecology, philosophy of technology, Stiegler/Simondon). The combination of Hörl's theoretical program, the practitioner fellows (Moreschi, Oliveira), and the opening toward multispecies/more-than-human thinking (Bertúa) makes this an unusually good fit for Amber. It's not a tech institute — but that's the point. LIAS is where Amber's technical work gets **theorized**.
