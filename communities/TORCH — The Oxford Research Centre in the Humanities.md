TORCH is Oxford's flagship interdisciplinary humanities research centre, now housed in the spectacular new Schwarzman Centre for the Humanities. It brings together researchers from across all humanities divisions at Oxford — English, music, theology, art, archaeology, philosophy, classics, area studies — to create interdisciplinary conversations. With the new building, it has become a physical nexus for humanities activity at Oxford.

---

## Mission & Objectives

TORCH's mission is to promote, support, and develop interdisciplinary research in the humanities at Oxford. It:
- Facilitates cross-faculty collaboration through research networks, reading groups, and networking events
- Hosts public events bridging academic humanities and wider audiences
- Provides a physical home for humanities interdisciplinarity in the Schwarzman Centre
- Supports early-career researchers through writing groups, networking, and community-building
- Reports into the Humanities Research Committee of the Humanities Division

The Schwarzman Centre itself is a cultural venue with performance spaces, exhibition spaces, seminar rooms, a library, recording facilities, and meeting rooms — designed to host conferences, performances, exhibitions, and research events.

## The Schwarzman Centre for the Humanities

The Stephen A. Schwarzman Centre for the Humanities is Oxford's newest major building, located in the Radcliffe Observatory Quarter on Woodstock Road. Key features:
- **Performance spaces** — for concerts, theatre, film screenings
- **Exhibition spaces** — for visual art and installations
- **Seminar rooms** — regular academic events
- **Recording facilities** — professional recording studio
- **Libraries and collections** — humanities research resources
- **Café and social spaces** — informal gathering
- **Venue hire** — conferences, corporate events, special events

Website: [schwarzmancentre.ox.ac.uk](https://www.schwarzmancentre.ox.ac.uk/)

The **Institute for Ethics in AI** is headquartered in the Schwarzman Centre, making it a hub for AI ethics + humanities intersection.
