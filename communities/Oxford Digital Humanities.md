# Oxford Digital Humanities

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-03-12:** Initial deep research completed. Profile created with DHOxSS 2026 confirmed, network structure documented.
- **DHOxSS 2026 confirmed:** 24–28 August 2026, St Anne's College, Oxford. Registration opens March 2026. Bursary applications already closed.

A university-wide network (not a formal research centre) coordinating Digital Humanities activity at the University of Oxford. Unlike CDH Cambridge (which is a dedicated research centre with its own staff, programmes, and physical space), Oxford DH is a **distributed network** — a coordination layer connecting DH practitioners across faculties, libraries, and institutes. The main operational presence is through the **Centre for Digital Scholarship** within the Bodleian Libraries.

**Key distinction from Cambridge CDH:** Oxford DH has no dedicated MPhil or PhD in Digital Humanities. No permanent director of "Digital Humanities" per se. No equivalent of CDH's Data Schools or Methods Fellows programme. The intellectual energy is distributed across existing departments — particularly [[Oxford Internet Institute (OII)]], the [Oxford e-Research Centre](http://www.oerc.ox.ac.uk/), and the Bodleian Libraries.

## Mission & Objectives

The Digital Humanities at Oxford network exists to:
- Connect researchers, projects, and support services across the University
- Provide training and support for DH methods
- Maintain the [People & Projects directory](https://digital.humanities.ox.ac.uk/people-projects) (current Oxford staff and students doing DH work)
- Organise the annual Digital Humanities at Oxford Summer School (DHOxSS)
- Facilitate access to support services for digital research projects

Oxford's DH claim: "The University of Oxford is world-renowned for its scholarship in the Digital Humanities" — but the activity is embedded in departments rather than centralised.

## Key Contributions

## Why Interesting

Oxford DH is a ⭐⭐ rather than ⭐⭐⭐ because:

**Strengths:**
- Amber's home university — low barrier to engagement
- DHOxSS is a genuinely world-class summer school
- Access to Bodleian Libraries digital scholarship resources
- Joint DPhil supervision pathway

**Limitations:**
- Network, not a centre — less coherent intellectual community than Cambridge CDH
- No dedicated DH degree programmes (MPhil, PhD)
- Events page is empty — less actively programming
- Less critically engaged than Cambridge CDH (more tools-and-techniques, less critical theory)
- Website feels somewhat dated — some links still reference 2021 online-only format

**For Amber:** The main value is DHOxSS (attend or propose workshop), the mailing list (stay informed), and the directory (be discoverable). For critical DH engagement, Cambridge CDH is the better bet. But since Oxford is Amber's base, maintaining awareness of Oxford DH activity is worthwhile.
