# Cátedra Max Aub — Transdisciplina en Arte y Tecnología

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-27:** Stub created during MUAC deep research.

**Cátedra Max Aub, Transdisciplina en Arte y Tecnología** at UNAM — a research unit at the intersection of art and technology within Mexico's national university. Named after Max Aub, the Spanish-Mexican writer who bridged literature and politics.

Coordinator: **Tania Aedo** — regularly co-programs with [[MUAC]] and the El Aleph Festival. The cátedra represents UNAM's formal commitment to art-technology transdisciplinarity and is the academic bridge between MUAC's programming and the broader UNAM research infrastructure.

## Key People
- **Tania Aedo** — Coordinator. Appears in MUAC's digital/technology programming (2024 "Museo digital" forum). Positioned at the nexus of art-technology-decoloniality in Mexican academic context.

## Mission
Transdisciplinary research and programming at the art-technology intersection from a critical/political perspective. In 2024, focused on data colonialism and cultural responses to AI/digital infrastructure.
