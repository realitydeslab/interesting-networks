# STEIM (Studio for Electro-Instrumental Music)

Experimental music technology institution based in Amsterdam, Netherlands. Founded 1969. **Closed 2019** after losing Dutch government funding. One of the most important institutions in European experimental electronic music and instrument design for 50 years.

## Why Interesting
STEIM was where serious electronic instrument makers and experimental musicians worked for decades. The community it produced — electronic instrument designers, sonic artists, experimental composers — is scattered across European design-research institutions. [[Kristina Andersen]] was based there for 14 years. STEIM alumni represent a practitioner network that bridges art, technology, craft, and sonic performance in ways rarely found in academic institutions.

## Key People
- **[[Kristina Andersen]]** — 14 years as core member; ran MA between STEIM and Sonology 2011–17
- Michel Waisvisz — legendary co-founder figure; inventor of The Hands
- Sensorband, Laetitia Sonami — major artists associated with STEIM

## Key Connections
- **[[Institute of Sonology]]** (Royal Conservatoire, The Hague) — ran combined MA 2011–17 with Kristina
- Dutch music technology ecosystem

## Status
Closed 2019. Legacy continues through alumni network.
