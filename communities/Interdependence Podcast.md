Podcast co-hosted by [[Holly Herndon]] and [[Mat Dryhurst]] from their Berlin studio. Launched April 30, 2020 ([announced via Pitchfork](https://pitchfork.com/news/holly-herndon-launches-new-podcast-with-mat-dryhurst/)). The defining long-form conversation show at the intersection of AI ethics, creative practice, decentralized internet, and more-than-human futures. A key gathering point for the Berlin-based critical tech-art intelligentsia. Based on Simplecast; hosted at [interdependence.fm](https://interdependence.fm/).

## Mission & Objectives

Interdependence is built around the conviction that technology shapes culture, and culture should shape technology — but only if artists, thinkers, and practitioners are at the table. Holly and Mat interview people working at the **frontier of integrating arts and technology**, specifically those asking uncomfortable ethical and philosophical questions about AI, blockchain, digital rights, and creative sovereignty.

The podcast explicitly frames itself against both techno-utopianism and techno-pessimism. Each episode is an extended intellectual conversation (60-90 min) between hosts and guest. Named for the concept of **interdependence** — artists, technologists, and communities are mutually entangled, and new tech paradigms require new collective governance frameworks. This intellectual environment directly spawned [[Spawning AI]] and the [[Holly+]] protocol.

**Active period:** April 2020 – September 2023 (sitemap last updated 2023-09-10). Status as of 2026 unclear — may have wound down or shifted format.

## Key Intellectual Themes

Consistent threads across all episodes:

1. **Artist sovereignty in the AI era** — platforming those building consent/governance frameworks for creative labor (Spawning, Holly+, UMAW, haveibeentrained.com)
2. **More-than-human intelligence** — serious engagement with non-human cognition, cybernetics, animal/machine sensing (Bridle, K Allado-McDowell, interspecies communication)
3. **Decentralized / Web3 infrastructure for culture** — blockchain, DAOs, NFTs as potential tools for artist governance, not just speculation (De Filippi, Harm van den Dorpel, Zora, Nina)
4. **AI aesthetics and language** — need for new critical vocabulary to understand ML's impact on art (patten, Anil Bawa-Cavia, Adverb)
5. **Permaweird / ambient crisis** — tracking cultural mood of technological acceleration without premature crisis declarations (Rao)
6. **Extitutional forms** — communities existing outside and between institutions (De Filippi's Extitutional Theory)
7. **Music industry futures** — Penny Fractions, UMAW, streaming critique, alternative music economies
8. **Non-Western tech futures** — Sinofuturism (Lawrence Lek), ancestral technologies (Debit/Mayan AI music), Indigenous AI protocols, Senyawa's transnational network
9. **Critical cold war of technology** — CIA/CCF cultural funding history, information technology as geopolitical battleground (Saunders, Skrubej)
