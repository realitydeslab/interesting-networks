# Forensic Architecture

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Initial deep research. Full profile created from Wikipedia, Guardian, Right Livelihood Award, and Eyal Weizman biographical sources.

**The most politically consequential art-research agency of our era.** A multidisciplinary research group based at [[Goldsmiths Visual Cultures]], University of London, founded 2010 by [[Eyal Weizman]]. Uses architectural analysis, 3D modelling, open-source media, remote sensing, witness testimony, and photogrammetry to investigate state violence, human rights violations, and environmental destruction. Their investigations have been presented to the UN, the ICC, Amnesty International, and parliamentary inquiries. Named to the 2018 Turner Prize shortlist; received the Right Livelihood Award 2024.

**The intellectual concept:** "investigative aesthetics" — the claim that aesthetic analysis is not decorative but evidentiary. Visual form as a form of truth production. Forensic architecture as counter-forensics: turning the state's investigative apparatus back onto the state.

## Mission & Objectives

FA develops new evidentiary techniques and undertakes advanced architectural and media research with and on behalf of communities affected by state violence. It operates on the principle that **truth requires tools** — and that those tools can be aesthetic, spatial, and computational.

**Counter-forensics:** FA explicitly frames its work as "counter-forensics" — lacking the state's powers of crime scene access, FA must reverse-engineer events from open-source materials. The state controls forensics; FA uses the same tools to expose the state's own crimes.

**Three spaces of operation:** field → laboratory → forum. The forum includes not just courts but art galleries, media, citizens' tribunals, and parliamentary hearings. Art exhibitions are themselves evidence presentations.

**The long duration of the split second:** As Weizman says — "We believe that looking at micro details can open a doorway to understanding broader historical, political and cultural contexts." Each investigation begins with a specific incident but opens onto structural patterns of power.

**Working principle:** Will not take commissions from governments or corporations. Works exclusively with civil society, NGOs, human rights lawyers, and affected communities.

## History & Founding

**1990s:** Eyal Weizman, while studying architecture at the [Architectural Association](https://www.aaschool.ac.uk/) in London, volunteers with the Palestinian Ministry of Planning. Discovers that maps and spatial representation are tools of occupation — Palestinians denied access to accurate maps. Produces in 2002 the first accurate map of Israeli settlements in the West Bank: "counter-cartography."

**2002:** Weizman's settlement exhibition in Berlin is censored by the Israel Association of United Architects — catalogues destroyed. The censorship makes him famous. He then exhibits at the Israeli Pavilion at Venice Architecture Biennale.

**~2007:** Weizman joins [[Goldsmiths Visual Cultures]] as Professor of Spatial and Visual Cultures. Founds the Centre for Research Architecture within the department. 

**2010:** Forensic Architecture formally established as a research project within the Centre for Research Architecture, Goldsmiths. First investigation: the killing of Bassem Abu Rahma in Bil'in — presented to the Supreme Court of Israel for human rights lawyer [Michael Sfard](https://www.haaretz.com/2014-01-20/ty-article/michael-sfard-a-lawyer-who-takes-israel-to-court-for-palestinians/0000017f-dbad-df9c-a17f-dbff13070000).

**2011:** European Research Council funding (first grant). Forensic Oceanography sub-team formed — investigating migration deaths across the Mediterranean.

**2012:** White phosphorus report to UN Convention on Certain Conventional Weapons; Israel admits use for the first time. "Where the Drones Strike" investigation with SITU Studio and [Bureau of Investigative Journalism](https://www.thebureauinvestigates.com/).

**2013:** Second ERC grant — Pattrn platform for data aggregation and visualisation.

**2014:** Gaza Platform (with Amnesty International) — interactive map of 2014 Israeli attacks. Exhibition at [Haus der Kulturen der Welt](https://www.hkw.de/), Berlin.

**2015:** Black Friday investigation — Rafah bombing analysis using bomb cloud shapes from mobile phone footage. Exposed the Hannibal Directive (Israeli military policy authorizing killing of own soldiers to prevent capture) — leads to its discontinuation.

**2016:** Third ERC grant. Saydnaya Prison investigation — reconstructed a secret Syrian detention facility through "ear witnessing": detainees blindfolded for years, only spatial sound memories available. Digital model built from sonic testimony.

**2017:** NSU investigation — German intelligence agent at scene of neo-Nazi murder in Kassel. FA reconstructs the event at full scale, demonstrates agent could not have failed to notice the killing. Shown at documenta 14. Investigation referenced in parliamentary inquiries and NSU trial in Munich.

**2017:** Prix Ars Electronica, European Cultural Foundation Princess Margriet Award.

**2018:** Turner Prize nomination — shortlisted for work relating to the killing of Yacoub Abu Al-Qia'an in Umm al-Hiran (Israeli police killing of a Bedouin citizen during demolitions). Exhibition at Tate Britain. Lost to Charlotte Prodger. ICA London solo show: "Counter Investigations."

**2019:** Whitney Biennial — video exposing Whitney trustee Warren Kanders' company manufacturing tear gas used against protesters worldwide. Kanders resigns from Whitney board. FA then withdraws from the Biennial in solidarity with other artists.

**2020:** Eyal Weizman's US visa waiver revoked — algorithm flags him as security threat, likely due to FA's investigations.

**2021:** Peabody Award for Eyal Weizman. Mark Duggan police shooting investigation. Cloud Studies at Whitworth Art Gallery, Manchester — censored when Manchester University initially removes Palestinian solidarity statement, then reverses after public pressure.

**2024 (October):** "A Cartography of Genocide" — 827-page report on Israel's campaign in Gaza since October 2023, with interactive map, concludes "systematic, organised, intended to destroy conditions of life."

**2024:** [Right Livelihood Award](https://rightlivelihood.org/the-change-makers/find-a-laureate/forensic-architecture/) — "for pioneering digital forensic methods to ensure justice and accountability for victims and survivors of human and environmental rights violations." Over 100 investigations completed since founding.

## Key Investigations & Projects

### Most Landmark Cases
1. **Black Friday / Rafah** (2015) — Analysed bomb clouds in mobile phone footage to locate and map 2,000 Israeli strikes on Rafah. Exposed the Hannibal Directive; led to its discontinuation. [Investigation](https://forensic-architecture.org/investigation/the-bombing-of-rafah).
2. **Saydnaya Prison, Syria** (2016) — Reconstructed secret detention facility through "ear witnessing" — detainees' sonic memories of a space they were kept blindfolded. Built 3D model from sound. [Investigation](https://forensic-architecture.org/investigation/saydnaya). 
3. **NSU / Halit Yozgat, Kassel** (2017) — Full-scale physical reconstruction of internet cafe where neo-Nazi murdered Yozgat while German spy was present. Proved spy could not have missed the killing. Shown at documenta 14. [Investigation](https://forensic-architecture.org/investigation/nsu-investigation-the-murder-of-halit-yozgat).
4. **Gaza Platform** (2014, with Amnesty) — Interactive map of all strikes in 2014 Gaza conflict. [Platform](https://gazaplatform.amnesty.org/).
5. **Iguala 43 Students, Mexico** (2017) — Reconstruction of disappearance/murder of 43 student teachers. Commissioned by IACHR.
6. **Grenfell Tower** (2017) — Investigation into the fire that killed 72 in London. Analysed cladding ignition.
7. **Whitney Biennial / Tear Gas** (2019) — Exposed trustee Warren Kanders' company manufacturing tear gas. Kanders resigned. FA then withdrew in solidarity.
8. **A Cartography of Genocide** (2024) — 827-page report + interactive map of Gaza campaign since October 2023. [Gaza Map](https://gaza.forensic-architecture.org/).
9. **Forensic Oceanography** (2011–ongoing) — Mediterranean migration deaths. [Left-to-Die Boat case](https://forensic-architecture.org/investigation/the-left-to-die-boat) (73 migrants left adrift 2 weeks in NATO surveillance area).
10. **White Phosphorus / Operation Cast Lead** (2012) — Report to UN led Israel to admit use for first time; IDF announced discontinuation in populated areas.

## Key Contributions

**To human rights practice:**
- Demonstrated open-source investigation (OSINT + spatial analysis) as viable evidentiary standard for international courts
- Gaza Platform pioneered interactive mapping of conflict evidence
- Saydnaya demonstrated that sonic testimony can produce legally usable spatial reconstructions
- Black Friday analysis led to real policy change (Hannibal Directive discontinued)
- White phosphorus report led to Israeli admission + policy change

**To art and theory:**
- "Investigative aesthetics" — a new field claiming aesthetic analysis as truth production, not just representation
- "Counter-forensics" — a methodology for civil society to challenge state narratives using state's own tools
- Third-generation institutional critique — the museum as a forum for evidence presentation, not just art display
- Forensic Oceanography — a whole new sub-discipline of migration research

**To architecture:**
- Transformed what "architecture" means — from buildings to the spatial analysis of events
- Proved architects can serve as expert witnesses in courts and international bodies
- Built a global model for architecture practice that refuses state commissions
