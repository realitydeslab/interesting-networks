# Forensic Architecture

Interdisciplinary research agency based at Goldsmiths, University of London. Founded and directed by architect Eyal Weizman. Investigates state violence, crimes against civilians, human rights abuses — using architecture, machine learning, 3D modeling, satellite data, social media, and witness testimony as investigative tools.

Exhibited at Louisiana Museum of Modern Art (2022), Documenta (Kassel), Venice Architecture Biennale, and many others.

## Why Interesting
Forensic Architecture is one of the most intellectually rigorous examples of computation as political tool in practice. They use machine learning and spatial reconstruction not as art but as *evidence* — for human rights cases, genocide investigations, police brutality documentation. "Not only people but all matter has a memory." This directly intersects Amber's interests in machine behavior, AI as social infrastructure, and critical computing.

Eyal Weizman is UK-based (Goldsmiths, London). Amber is Oxford-based. **Potential proximity connection.**
