# Forensic Architecture

Multidisciplinary research agency based at [[Goldsmiths Visual Cultures]], University of London. Founded 2010 by [[Eyal Weizman]] within the Centre for Research Architecture. Uses architectural techniques, computation, open-source media analysis, remote sensing, and visual evidence to investigate state violence and human rights violations.

Interdisciplinary team: architects, scholars, artists, filmmakers, software developers, investigative journalists, archaeologists, lawyers, scientists. Works with international prosecutors, Amnesty International, UN bodies, ICC, civil society groups.

**Key recognitions:** Prix Ars Electronica (2017), Turner Prize nomination (2018, shown at Tate Britain), Peabody Award (2021 for Eyal Weizman).

**Key concept:** "investigative aesthetics" — aesthetic analysis as a form of evidence production and political intervention. Co-developed in *Investigative Aesthetics* (Verso, 2021, Weizman + Matthew Fuller).

## Why Interesting
Forensic Architecture is the most consequential example of art-research having direct political impact: their investigations have been presented to the UN, international courts, Amnesty International. For Amber, their methodology of combining computation, visual analysis, and political commitment is a model for what "critical computing" could mean in practice.
