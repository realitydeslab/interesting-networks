# Forensic Architecture

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Full deep research completed. Upgraded from stub to ⭐⭐⭐ community profile with complete history, investigations, team, exhibitions, partnerships, and awards.

Multidisciplinary research agency based at [[Goldsmiths Visual Cultures]], University of London. The most politically consequential art-research institution of the 2010s–2020s. Uses architectural techniques, computation, open-source media analysis, remote sensing, 3D modeling, and spatial analysis to investigate state violence and human rights violations. Oscillates deliberately between art galleries and courts of law — the same work functions as Turner Prize–nominated art AND as evidence at the International Court of Justice.

## Mission & Objectives
Founded 2010 by [[Eyal Weizman]] within the [Centre for Research Architecture](https://www.gold.ac.uk/pg/ma-research-architecture/) at Goldsmiths. Developed as a response to:
- The urbanization of warfare
- Erosion of trust in state-produced evidence
- Proliferation of open-source media (smartphone footage, satellite imagery)
- The need for civil society to have independent evidence-production capacity

**Core concept: "Counter-forensics"** — turning the state's forensic gaze back onto the state. Instead of the state doing forensics on citizens, FA does forensics on state violence.

**Theoretical framework:** FA operates across three spaces: the **field** (where events occur), the **laboratory** (where evidence is analyzed), and the **forum** (where evidence is presented). "Forums" include not only courts but also museums, art galleries, citizens' tribunals, newspapers, and the media. This forum-multiplication strategy is FA's signature.

Funded by three major [European Research Council (ERC)](https://erc.europa.eu/) grants (2011, 2013, 2016) plus human rights grants. The ERC grants provided the institutional stability to grow from a research project into a permanent agency.

## Key Publications & Output

### Investigations (selected landmark cases)
- **Killing of Bassem Abu Rahma, Bil'in** (2010) — FA's first investigation. Presented to Israeli Supreme Court.
- **Left-to-Die Boat** (2012) — 73 migrants left drifting in NATO surveillance area. Via Forensic Oceanography.
- **White Phosphorus in Gaza** (2012) — presented to UN. Led Israel to admit use and discontinue in populated areas.
- **Where the Drones Strike** (2012) — with SITU Studio and Bureau of Investigative Journalism. For UN Special Rapporteur.
- **Gaza Platform** (2014–2015) — interactive map of Israeli attacks, with Amnesty International.
- **Rafah: Black Friday** (2015) — with Amnesty International. Exposed the Hannibal Directive.
- **Saydnaya Prison** (2016) — reconstructed Syrian torture prison through "ear witnessing." Methodology breakthrough.
- **77sqm_9:26min (NSU/Halit Yozgat)** (2017) — German intelligence agent at neo-Nazi murder scene. Shown at documenta 14. Referenced in German parliamentary inquiries.
- **The Long Duration of a Split Second (Umm al-Hiran)** (2017–2018) — Turner Prize 2018 nomination piece.
- **El Junquito Raid** (2018) — with [Bellingcat](https://www.bellingcat.com/) and Venezuelan journalists. Investigated extrajudicial killings.
- **Triple-Chaser** (2019) — Whitney Biennial. Led to Warren Kanders' resignation from Whitney board.
- **Cloud Studies** (2020) — environmental forensics. ZKM Karlsruhe, Whitworth Manchester.
- **A Cartography of Genocide** (2024) — 827-page report on Israel's campaign in Gaza. Used in South Africa's genocide case at the ICJ.

### Books
- *[FORENSIS: The Architecture of Public Truth](https://www.sternberg-press.com/)* (Sternberg Press, 2014) — edited by Weizman
- *[Forensic Architecture: Violence at the Threshold of Detectability](https://mitpress.mit.edu/books/forensic-architecture)* (Zone Books/MIT Press, 2017) — the definitive methodology text
- *[Investigative Aesthetics](https://www.versobooks.com/books/3832-investigative-aesthetics)* (Verso, 2021) — with [[Matthew Fuller]], extending the framework
- *[The Police Shooting of Mark Duggan](https://cabinetmagazine.org/)* (Cabinet Books / ICA London, 2021)

### Software/Platforms
- **Pattrn** — multimedia data-aggregation and visualization platform. Enables anonymous collation of first-hand reports. Used in Gaza Platform. Also used by [ACLED](https://acleddata.com/).

### Methodologies
- **3D spatial reconstruction** from smartphone footage and open-source imagery
- **"Ear witnessing"** — reconstructing architecture from auditory memories of blindfolded/traumatized witnesses
- **Bomb cloud analysis** — locating strikes by analyzing explosion clouds in footage
- **Photogrammetry and LiDAR scanning** of conflict sites
- **Ground-penetrating radar** for archaeological evidence
- **Witness-aided digital modeling** — using 3D models to stimulate memory recall in traumatized witnesses

## Key Contributions
1. **Invented "counter-forensics" as a practice** — gave civil society its own forensic capacity independent of state resources
2. **Demonstrated that aesthetic analysis IS political evidence** — the Turner Prize + ICJ dual validation
3. **Pioneered open-source investigation for human rights** — before Bellingcat became famous, FA was using similar methods in an art-architecture context
4. **Created a replicable model** — Forensic Oceanography, Lawrence Abu Hamdan's Earshot, and others have spun off from FA's framework
5. **Forced the art world to reckon with political evidence** — the Turner Prize nomination and Whitney Biennial (Kanders resignation) changed what counts as contemporary art
6. **Produced evidence used in international courts** — the ICJ, ICC, Israeli Supreme Court, German parliamentary inquiries

## Why Interesting
Forensic Architecture is the proof that "critical computing" and "art-science" are not just academic categories — they can change the world. FA's investigations have:
- Led Israel to discontinue white phosphorus use in populated areas
- Exposed the Hannibal Directive, leading to its discontinuation
- Forced the resignation of a Whitney Museum board member
- Been referenced in German parliamentary inquiries into neo-Nazi murders
- Been used as evidence in the ICJ genocide case against Israel

No other art-research institution can claim this level of real-world political impact. For Amber, FA represents the gold standard for what "practice-based research" can achieve when computation, aesthetics, and political commitment align.
