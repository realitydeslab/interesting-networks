# Creative AI Lab

A research programme and partnership between the **Department of Digital Humanities at King's College London** and **[[Serpentine Galleries]]** (Arts Technologies team), ongoing since July 2020. One of the most prominent institutional intersections of contemporary art and AI research in the UK.

## Mission
Investigates:
- How AI/ML tools can support artistic practices
- How artistic and curatorial practices are changing due to AI/ML
- Creative AI/ML as a critical practice
- The aesthetics of AI/ML

The lab positions cultural institutions, artists, engineers and researchers as the core constituency — not tech companies.

## Activities
- Produces **Creative AI Tools & Resources Database** (creative-ai.org) — tools for artists, designers, engineers, curators
- Research workshops, public discussions, writing sessions
- **R&D Labyrinths** — online tutorials
- Position papers (2023 Creative AI Position Paper: "Toward a C5 Model in Creative AI")

## Why Interesting for Amber
The Creative AI Lab is a model of how a cultural institution (gallery) and a university can co-produce critical AI research without ceding to tech industry capture. The partnership structure, the emphasis on aesthetics and politics, and the strong feminist/critical theory grounding (via Zylinska, Bunz) makes this a relevant institutional case study for thinking about how to position AI art research.

Also: Amber is in London orbit → the Lab hosts events at Serpentine Galleries (Kensington Gardens).
