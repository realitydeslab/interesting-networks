# Dumb Type

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Stub created via NTT ICC deep research.
- **2022:** Japan Pavilion at Venice Biennale 59th International Art Exhibition — new installation "2022" with Ryuichi Sakamoto as new member.
- **2023:** Japan Pavilion in Tokyo: "Dumb Type, 2022: remap" at Artizon Museum; *AUDIO VISUAL LIVE 2022: remix* at NTT ICC.

Kyoto-based collective founded in **1984** by artists from diverse backgrounds: visual art, music, video, dance, design, computer programming. Open-ended creative model: no fixed director, changing roster of members, collaborative exploration of performance and installation.

Key members include: **Ryoji Ikeda** (composer, joined 1994), **Shiro Takatani** (visual direction), Teiji Furuhashi (founder, deceased 1995), BuBu de la Madeleine, Tomari Hiromasa.

## Why Interesting
Foundational Japanese performance-technology collective bridging performance, video, sound, and digital technology. Dumb Type is the origin point of the serious Japanese media art tradition that [[Ryoji Ikeda]], [[Daito Manabe]], and others emerged from or were influenced by. Their open collaborative model (no fixed director) is an interesting institutional form.
