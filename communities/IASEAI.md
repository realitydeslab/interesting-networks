# International Association for Safe and Ethical AI (IASEAI)

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-03-14:** Initial deep research. Created note with full profile from website scrape, leadership, affiliates, and conference history.
- **2026-02-24:** IASEAI'26 conference held at UNESCO House, Paris. 1,300+ attendees from 99+ countries. Second annual conference.

A global, membership-based, independent 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization founded to address the risks and opportunities associated with rapid advances in AI. Pronounced "eye-see-eye." Brings together experts from academia, policy, civil society, industry, and beyond to promote research, shape policy, and build understanding around safe and ethical AI. Headquartered in the US; conferences held at prestigious Paris venues.

## Mission & Objectives
**Mission:** To ensure that AI systems operate safely and ethically, benefiting all of humanity.

**Core belief:** AI can be beneficial, but current systems are being developed without appropriate safeguards. As these systems become more capable and more directly involved in critical social and economic functions, it is essential to provide assurances that they will operate safely and benefit humanity.

**Four pillars:**
1. **Community** — annual conference, workshops, 2,000+ members from 65+ countries, 130+ affiliate organizations
2. **Research** — promoting technical and sociotechnical approaches to safe/ethical AI; seed grants for new research centers; commissioned research for policy support; awards and fellowships
3. **Policy** — specific policy analyses on standards, regulation, international cooperation, and research funding; elected Council sets directions; working groups of experts
4. **Education** — building awareness about AI risks and mitigation; course development, educational content, expert speakers bureau, webinars

## History & Founding
**November 2023:** [AI Safety Summit at Bletchley Park](https://www.gov.uk/government/topical-events/ai-safety-summit-2023) promises intergovernmental cooperation on AI safety. Subsequent developments (weakening EU AI Act, US-China rivalry) threaten to undermine cooperation.

**May 2024:** Steering committee formed to explore creating an international organization for safe and ethical AI. Chaired by [[Stuart Russell]], including [[Yoshua Bengio]], [[Kate Crawford]], [[Alondra Nelson]], [[Max Tegmark]], [[Andrew Yao]], [[Jason Matheny]] (RAND CEO), [[James Manyika]] (Google), [[Gillian Hadfield]], [[Eric Horvitz]], [[Aza Raskin]], [[Francesca Rossi]], [[Bart Selman]], [[Marietje Schaake]], [[Ya-Qin Zhang]]. [[Mark Nitzberg]] agreed to serve as interim executive director.

**November 2024:** Formally incorporated as a nonprofit, with [[Amir Banifatemi]], [[Mark Nitzberg]], and [[Stuart Russell]] as officers. Initial funding from the [Beneficial AI Foundation](https://beneficialai.org/).

**February 2025:** [First conference (IASEAI'25)](https://www.iaseai.org/our-programs/iaseai25) at OECD Headquarters, Paris. ~700 in-person attendees from 65 countries, 1,400+ online. Featured [[Geoffrey Hinton]], [[Yoshua Bengio]], [[Kate Crawford]], [[Stuart Russell]], [[Joseph Stiglitz]], [[Maria Ressa]], [[Max Tegmark]]. Concluded with [10 critical action items](https://www.iaseai.org/our-programs/call-to-action).

**February 2026:** [Second conference (IASEAI'26)](https://www.iaseai.org/our-programs/iaseai26) at UNESCO House, Paris. 1,300+ attendees from 99+ countries. By invitation only.

**Current transition:** Moving to new governance structure — Board of Directors with fiduciary responsibility; President and Council (elections forthcoming); Advisory Board; appointed Executive Director. Also finalizing national/regional chapter policies.
