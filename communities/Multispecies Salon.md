# Multispecies Salon

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-03-02:** Expanded stub with additional context from Multispecies Design deep research.
- **2026-02-26:** Stub created during Anna Tsing deep research.

Annual gathering and informal intellectual community at UC Santa Cruz, co-founded by [[Donna Haraway]] and [[Anna Tsing]]. One of the key communities that incubated *Staying with the Trouble* (Haraway, 2016) and the broader multispecies ethnography movement. Brings together artists, scientists, anthropologists, philosophers, and designers around shared questions about more-than-human worlds.

## Mission & Objectives
Not a formal institution — more a recurring interdisciplinary salon and conversation. The Salon functions as a creative incubator for ideas that cross the art-science-humanities divide around multispecies questions. Companion species, extinction, entanglement, ecological ethics. Format: talks, workshops, collective discussion, sometimes exhibitions and publications.

The Salon model: gather a small, high-quality interdisciplinary group around a shared intellectual problem rather than a shared discipline. The Haraway-Tsing axis gives it coherence without imposing a fixed agenda.

## Key People
- **[[Donna Haraway]]** — Co-founder. UC Santa Cruz, History of Consciousness.
- **[[Anna Tsing]]** — Co-founder. UC Santa Cruz, Anthropology.
- Various artists, scientists, scholars at UCSC and invited guests — names not consistently documented publicly.

## Key Publications & Output
The Multispecies Salon has produced or incubated:
- *[Multispecies Salon](https://www.dukeupress.edu/multispecies-salon)* (edited volume, Duke University Press, 2014) — ed. Eben Kirksey. Essays from salon participants. Artists, activists, biologists, anthropologists. Key text for the community's intellectual output.
- Conceptual infrastructure for *Staying with the Trouble* (Haraway, 2016) — Haraway credits the salon as a key intellectual community.
- Influenced the arts of living on a damaged planet tradition (Tsing et al., 2017).

## For Amber
- **Model for community-building:** The Salon's format — intimate, interdisciplinary, recurring — is a model for how to build intellectual community around emerging questions. Not a conference, not a journal, not a lab.
- **Art-science integration:** Bridges scientific rigor (Tsing's ethnography, biological participants) with artistic practice without reducing either side. A template for how Amber's work could situate itself.
- **Lineage:** Understanding the Salon clarifies why Haraway and Tsing's ideas feel so intellectually integrated — they were co-developed in this context.
- **The design connection:** Eben Kirksey (the editor of the *Multispecies Salon* volume) and others have taken multispecies thinking into design-adjacent territories. Worth tracing.

## Why Interesting
The Salon is the originating community for much of contemporary multispecies thinking. It's where "multispecies" became a recognizable intellectual position rather than just a description. The model (small, intimate, interdisciplinary, recurring) is worth understanding as a community-building strategy for Amber's own research environment.
