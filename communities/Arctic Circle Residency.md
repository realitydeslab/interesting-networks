# Arctic Circle Residency

An expeditionary art-science residency aboard a sailing vessel in the high Arctic. For ~3 weeks each year, a self-selected cohort of artists, scientists, architects, and educators live and work together on a traditionally rigged schooner sailing the Svalbard Archipelago, 10° latitude from the North Pole. The extreme environment is the medium. Alumni include Venice Biennale pavilion artists, Guggenheim fellows, and figures working at the edge of ecological and more-than-human practice.

## Mission & Objectives
> "The Arctic Circle is a nexus where art intersects science, architecture, education, and activism — an incubator for thought and experimentation for artists and innovators who seek out and foster areas of collaboration to engage in the central issues of our time."

Founded in 2009 by **Aaron T. O'Connor** (Director). The model: bring together people from radically different disciplines and put them in extreme confinement — Arctic conditions, a small ship, no WiFi, polar bear country — and see what emerges. Aaron's framing: "Perhaps the fact that we're in the Arctic doesn't matter. It may be more important to put a group of artists and scientists together in such close confinement, in tough conditions, just to see what happens."

The residency is explicitly **not** a retreat. It's fieldwork. Participants pursue personal projects onboard and ashore, while the expedition structure creates unavoidable collision with collaborators from other disciplines. The extreme environment disrupts normal working methods; you can't retreat to your studio.

## Structure & Application
- **Frequency:** Annual; two expeditions (Spring + Autumn/Summer), each ~16 days
- **Vessel:** Traditionally rigged schooner (historically M/S Noorderlicht and Tall Ship Antigua), sailing the Svalbard Archipelago
- **Location:** International territory of Svalbard, Arctic Ocean — 10° latitude from the North Pole
- **Participant fee:** ~$5,800 USD (partial fellowships available)
- **Disciplines accepted:** Contemporary artists (all disciplines), scientists, architects, educators, innovators
- **Application:** Via mailing list ONLY — no public open call. Join the mailing list at [thearcticcircle.org](https://thearcticcircle.org/) to receive calls for applications
- **Size:** ~20–30 participants per expedition
- **2026 expeditions:** Spring May 14–30, Summer July 14–30 (Svalbard)

## Key Contributions
1. **Self-selection filter** — the ~$5,800 fee + remote logistics creates a cohort of people genuinely committed to art-science intersection. No casual participants.
2. **Interdisciplinary pressure** — 3 weeks on a small boat, no escape from collaboration. Forces genuine exchange across disciplines.
3. **Climate art lineage** — Arctic Circle alumni consistently produce work about ecological crisis, glacial retreat, sea ice loss — but from a place of having *been there*, not observing from afar.
4. **Global alumni network** — 100s of alumni across visual art, music, writing, architecture, science. The annual exhibit programme creates ongoing visibility.

## Events & Programmes
- **Annual expeditions:** Two per year, ~May and ~September/October (dates vary), ~16 days each. Svalbard Archipelago.
