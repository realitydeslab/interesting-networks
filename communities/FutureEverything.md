# FutureEverything

Manchester-based art, technology and society organization (est. 1995) undergoing a radical existential transformation — pivoting from digital culture festival to a **Nature Directed** organization that formally appointed Nature to its Board of Directors (December 2025). Now exploring technology through the lens of multispecies justice, ecological repair, and more-than-human governance. Facing financial crisis as of early 2026, with closure threatened for April 2026 if core funding isn't secured.

## Mission & Objectives

FutureEverything operates at the intersection of art, technology, and society. Founded 1995 as a digital music/arts festival, it has continually reinvented itself. Current mission: **"Imagine and catalyse just relations with technologies that increasingly pervade our lives."**

Their **Nature Directed** framework (2024–2025) is a radical organizational experiment:
- Formally appointed Nature to the Board of Directors (first UK CIC to do so)
- Amended Articles of Association to give Nature legal standing
- Appointed a human "Nature Director" (Dani Admiss) as proxy for Nature's interests
- Created "Webs of Justice" — a declaration of responsibilities to Nature, recited at all key meetings
- Working with [Lawyers for Nature](https://www.lawyersfornature.com/) on the legal architecture
- Influenced by Odawa Anishinaabe attorney Ariel Clark on values-as-doing-words (kinship, leadership, accountability)
- Frames technology use through multispecies justice and ecological restoration

## Why Interesting

FutureEverything has made a radical, genuinely weird bet: that Nature should have formal legal standing in organizational governance. This is not rhetorical — they amended their Articles of Association and appointed a human proxy. This is exactly Amber's territory:

1. **More-than-human design** — FutureEverything is living proof that more-than-human theory can be institutionally enacted, not just theorized. They went from "we talk about more-than-human" to "nature is literally on our board." Radical.

2. **Speculative institutions** — They're doing speculative governance, not just speculative design. What does it mean to run an organization *with* Nature? This is cosmotechnics in practice — technology relations restructured through a non-Western/ecological episteme.

3. **Critical Computing** — The Composting Futures event (with UAL Critical Climate Computing) and their whole framing of technology as potentially extractive-vs-regenerative directly maps onto Amber's critical computing interests.

4. **Art-science bridging** — 30 years of commissions (Kimchi and Chips, Di Mainstone, Maya Chowdhry, Vicky Clarke, Flora Yin-Wong) demonstrating sustained, serious practice at the art-technology-society intersection.

5. **Interspecies communication** — Their Harvard presentation explicitly frames AI as potentially "bypassing" genuine interspecies communication through extractive logic. This connects to Amber's interest in machine behavior and more-than-human design.

6. **MENA programme** — [Digital] Transmissions shows genuine North-South global connectivity in digital arts, not just Western-centric networks.

**Caveat:** The organization may close in April 2026 due to financial crisis. This makes engagement urgent — either they close (in which case track the diaspora of people and ideas) or they survive and need allies.
