# FAccT — ACM Conference on Fairness, Accountability, and Transparency

Annual conference on fairness, accountability, and transparency in sociotechnical systems. Published by ACM. One of the most important venues for critical AI research — sits at the intersection of CS, social science, law, and policy.

**Why significant:** This is where the [Stochastic Parrots](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3442188.3445922) paper was published (2021). Also where much of the work on algorithmic bias, data documentation, AI governance, and tech critique appears. Connects the academic critical AI community (Gebru, Bender, Mitchell, Hanna) with policy, law, and civil society.

**Circuit:** Closely connected to [[DAIR Institute]], [[AI Now Institute]], [[Algorithmic Justice League]], [[Kate Crawford]]'s world, [[Joy Buolamwini]]'s world. The venue where "AI ethics as structural critique" (not just principles/checklists) became academically legitimate.

**Amber connection:** FAccT is a plausible venue for work on machine behavior, sovereign agents, dissociative agents — especially with a critical/structural framing.

## Key Papers Published Here
- Stochastic Parrots (2021) — Bender, Gebru, McMillan-Major, Mitchell
- Datasheets for Datasets — Gebru et al.
- Various work by Kate Crawford, Meredith Whittaker, Rediet Abebe
