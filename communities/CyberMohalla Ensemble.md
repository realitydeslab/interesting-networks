# CyberMohalla Ensemble

Delhi-based collective of "unorthodox proletarian urbanists" — practitioners embedded in the city's margins. Long-term collaborators with [[Raqs Media Collective]] over more than a decade; their worker-diary text features prominently in Raqs's 2011 work *Strikes at Time* (Centre Pompidou), which weaves their writing with Jacques Rancière's *The Nights of Labour*.

CyberMohalla (literally "cyber neighborhood") was a practice initiated by [[Sarai]] embedding media labs in working-class neighborhoods of Delhi, producing alternative urban theory from below. The Ensemble is the practitioner collective that emerged from this.
