# Four Corners Film Workshop

East London's longest-running radical film and photography organisation, founded 1973 in Bethnal Green. A half-century of socially-engaged, politically-conscious media arts practice — from co-operative filmmaking and feminist distribution to community darkrooms and free industry training. Now a registered charity at 121 Roman Road, combining gallery, archive, facilities hire, and access programmes.

## Mission & Objectives
"Four Corners believes in film and photography for all." Building on almost 50 years of radical, socially-engaged approaches to photography and film. Direct work to tackle access barriers in the creative industries: free/subsidised training, paid placements, mentoring, community darkrooms, affordable gallery hire, co-working space for creatives.

Three pillars:
1. **Community access** — free training for underrepresented people, Tower Hamlets focus
2. **Artist development** — support for emerging and mid-career artists
3. **Cultural programming** — gallery exhibitions, screenings, talks, archive projects

## History

### Formation (1973)
Four Corners was formed in 1973 by four young filmmakers — **Joanna Davis**, **Mary Pat Leece**, **Ronald (Ron) Peck**, and **Wilfried Thust** — after meeting at the [London Film School](https://www.lfs.org.uk/). They set up at **113 Roman Road**, Bethnal Green, creating a cinema and production workshop to bring films and filmmaking to previously excluded communities. The name "Four Corners" reflects the four founding members.

### Channel 4 Workshop Period (1980s)
Provisionally franchised under the Channel Four Workshop Agreement in the 1980s — one of a small number of regional workshops with guaranteed funding. This enabled production of films including:
- **[Nighthawks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nighthawks_(1978_film))** (Ron Peck/Paul Hallam, 1978) — Britain's first explicitly gay feature film, premiered at Edinburgh Film Festival. Produced by Four Corners Films under budget of £66,000. Cast includes Derek Jarman.
- **Bred and Born** — Channel 4 documentary
- **Hang On A Minute** — short film
- **Is That It?** — short film
- **A Kind of English** (Ruhul Amin, 1986, Channel 4) — first Bangladeshi feature film made in Britain

### Post-Channel 4: Production & Training Resource (1990s–2000s)
After C4 funding ceased mid-1980s, Four Corners became a production and training resource — supporting BAFTA and Turner Prize nominees alongside local artists. Monthly screenings in a small raked cinema at 113 Roman Road. From the 1990s, increasingly recognised for high-quality, free industry training for unemployed people from diverse, non-traditional backgrounds.

### Relocation and Expansion (2003–2007)
In 2003 Four Corners was granted **119–121 Roman Road** by Arts Council England — the space previously occupied by **Camerawork** (formerly the Half Moon Photography Workshop), a complementary radical photography organisation. After a £1m build project, Four Corners' new centre opened in **2007**, providing gallery, training rooms, film facilities, darkrooms, studio, and workspace.

### Camerawork Connection
The **Half Moon Photography Workshop** began in 1972 as a darkroom co-operative and gallery space, later moving to 119–121 Roman Road and publishing **[Camerawork magazine](https://www.fourcornersfilm.co.uk/four-corners-archive)** — a radical left photography journal running 1976–1985. When Camerawork closed, Four Corners took over the physical space and eventually absorbed the archive. The two organisations were neighbouring radical collectives in Bethnal Green through the 1970s and 1980s.

## Key Publications & Output

### Camerawork Magazine
[*Camerawork*](https://www.fourcornersfilm.co.uk/four-corners-archive) — radical photography journal published by Half Moon Photography Workshop, 1976–1985. A complete set is in the Four Corners digital archive. Covered socialist, feminist, and community photography. Key publication of the British radical photography movement.

### Films Produced
- [*Nighthawks*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nighthawks_(1978_film)) (Ron Peck/Paul Hallam, 1978) — first gay British feature
- *A Kind of English* (Ruhul Amin, 1986) — first Bangladeshi British feature
- *Bred and Born*, *Hang On A Minute*, *Is That It?* — Channel 4 Workshop productions

### Photography Commissions
- *Working Lives* (2024–2025) — Commission-winning photographers Bella Denise, Karolina Raczynski, Jade Sweeting exploring work, labour and care.

### Training
High-quality, free industry training for unemployed people from diverse/non-traditional backgrounds — model running since the 1990s. Specialist skills training, mentoring, paid placements. Collaborates with ScreenSkills. New opportunities announced via mailing list.

### Archive Programme
**[Four Corners Archive](https://www.fourcornersfilm.co.uk/four-corners-archive)** — Digital archive of Four Corners films and Half Moon Photography Workshop material (1972–1987). Includes:
- Four Corners' own films
- Half Moon Photography Workshop touring exhibitions
- Complete run of Camerawork magazine
- Growing oral history interviews and donated material
Archive project started 2016 (Heritage Lottery Fund grant); launched summer 2018 with exhibition and events. Physical archive held at [Bishopsgate Institute](https://www.bishopsgate.org.uk/).

## Why Interesting
Four Corners sits at the intersection of Amber's interests in **artist-run institutions**, **collective models**, **feminist/radical practice**, and **participatory community engagement**. It demonstrates what happens when radical 1970s collectives survive into the present: they inevitably institutionalise, but Four Corners has maintained a meaningful commitment to access and community through its charity structure. The archive is a primary source for understanding the experimental film and photography scene that produced figures like Lis Rhodes. The Nighthawks / A Kind of English legacy shows the collective's role in British queer and diaspora media history. 

For Amber's interest in more-than-human design and experimental media, this is a genealogical node — understanding what came before the digital art spaces. The artist-run workshop model (collective decision-making, shared equipment, community training) is a precursor to many current models of creative infrastructure.
