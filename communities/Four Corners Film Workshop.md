# Four Corners Film Workshop

East London artist-run film and photography collective with roots in the 1970s. Originally **Four Corners** (workshop) and **Camerawork** (gallery), operating as neighbouring radical collectives in Bethnal Green. Now operating as [Four Corners](https://www.fourcornersfilm.co.uk/), a registered charity focussed on creative media access, education, and empowerment.

## Mission & Objectives
Socially-engaged film and photography practice. Access to arts for underrepresented communities. Training projects, artist development, cultural programming. "Four Corners believes in film and photography for all."

Almost 50 years of "radical, socially-engaged approaches to photography and film."

## Key People (known connections)
- **[[Lis Rhodes]]** — Member of Four Corners Film Workshop (listed on luxonline.org.uk biography).

## Why Interesting
Four Corners is one of the longest-running radical media arts organisations in the UK. Its roots in the 1970s East London scene connect it to the broader network of artist-run workshops and collectives (alongside the LFMC, Circles, etc.). Still active as a community resource. The connection to Rhodes places it firmly in the feminist experimental film network of 1970s–80s London.
