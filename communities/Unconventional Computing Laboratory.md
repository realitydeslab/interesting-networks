# Unconventional Computing Laboratory

Research lab at the University of the West of England (UWE), Bristol, directed by [[Andrew Adamatzky]]. The leading academic centre for non-standard computing substrates globally. Works with slime mould, fungi, chemical reaction-diffusion systems, soldier crabs, billiard balls. Publishes the *International Journal of Unconventional Computing*.

## Key People
- [[Andrew Adamatzky]] — Director; slime mould, chemical, fungal computing

## Topics
- Biological computing (slime mould, fungi, bacteria)
- Chemical computing (reaction-diffusion logic)
- Cellular automata
- Unconventional AI and intelligence

## Events & Programmes
- Hosts the *International Journal of Unconventional Computing* (open to submissions)
- Connected to UCNC (Unconventional Computation and Natural Computation) conference

## Why Interesting
UWE Bristol is accessible from Oxford. Adamatzky's lab is genuinely weird and genuinely rigorous — the combination Amber responds to. Slime mould computing, fungal computing, and chemical computing are all forms of more-than-human intelligence that raise exactly the questions Amber works on: what is behavior, what is cognition, who is a computational agent?
