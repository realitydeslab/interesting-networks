# Oceanic Humanities for the Global South

A platform evolving an oceanic humanities approach appropriate to the Global South. Co-directed by [[Isabel Hofmeyr]] and [[Charne Lavery]] out of [[WISER]] (Wits University, Johannesburg). Operates at the intersection of environmental crisis + decolonial critique + ocean studies. Recognises both environmental catastrophe and global inequality as legacies of empire.

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-28:** Stub created via Isabel Hofmeyr deep research.

## Mission & Objectives
Rising sea levels as visible sign of climate change require new research and writing styles: an oceanic humanities. The platform engages with:
- Both human and non-human aspects of the ocean
- Both the depth and surface of the seas
- Scientific and sacred concepts of water
- Critical ocean studies + postcolonial understandings

Records, showcases, and connects research, writing, artistic production, and public humanities work combining critical ocean studies with postcolonial understandings.
