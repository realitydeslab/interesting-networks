# Regen Network

Blockchain platform and distributed network — the "Operating System for Ecological Regeneration." Provides digital public infrastructure for registering regenerative projects, issuing science-backed ecological credits, and governing the ecological commons. [[Regen Foundation]] is its nonprofit arm.

## Key People
- **[[Austin Wade Smith]]** — Executive Director of Regen Foundation (nonprofit arm)
- **[[Kei Kreutler]]** — Board of Regen Foundation
- **Regen Network Development, PBC** — Tech arm; builds digital tools for managing ecological commons

## Topics
- **Ecological credits** — Peer-reviewed, blockchain-backed ecological assets (carbon, biodiversity, water quality, social co-benefits)
- **$REGEN token** — Native token; Tokenomics Working Group incentivizes regenerative outcomes
- **Registry infrastructure** — Anyone can register projects, create claims, direct capital
- **Open science** — Open protocols for measurement and verification

## Network Participants
- **ecometric Ltd.** — Soil carbon credits with AI-driven measurement
- **Fundación Pachamama** — Indigenous-led regenerative credits in Latin America
- **Amazon Sacred Headwaters Alliance** — Ancestral stewardship credits for 86M acres of rainforest
- **Terrasos** — Biodiversity and habitat banking
- **SeaTrees** — Ocean ecosystem restoration (kelp, mangroves)
- **ERA Brazil** — Amazon restoration, biodiversity crediting
- **Kulshan Carbon Trust** — Biochar creation credits
- **Virridy** — Watershed and green infrastructure credits

## Key Infrastructure
- **Regen App** (app.regen.network) — Marketplace for ecological credits; blockchain verification without wallet required
- **Community Staking DAO** — Community governance over network direction

## Why Interesting
Regen Network is the technical substrate under Regen Foundation's more ambitious governance experiments. The combination of blockchain infrastructure + nonprofit community building + ecological institution design is a real attempt at building commons-based ecological economy. Key case study in how "crypto for good" can be practically grounded in scientific rigor and community governance.
