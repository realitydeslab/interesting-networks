# Biofilia – Base for Biological Arts

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-28:** Full deep research. Expanded from stub. History, research projects, key people, collaborators, advisory board, connected institutions.
- **2026-02-26:** Created stub during Ionat Zurr deep research.

**The Nordic region's first biological art laboratory — a transdisciplinary platform for art, life sciences, and technology at Aalto University.** Launched in 2012 under Aalto ARTS (School of Arts, Design and Architecture), Biofilia was founded in explicit partnership with [[SymbioticA]] at the University of Western Australia. It is the institutional proof that the SymbioticA model — artists embedded inside a functioning wet biology lab at a research university — can be successfully exported internationally. Biofilia is also distinctive as the only fully equipped biological lab operated by an art school and housed inside an *electrical engineering building*, creating an unusual institutional topology.

## Mission & Objectives

Biofilia offers a platform and infrastructure for transdisciplinary research and education aimed at creating cultural discussion and innovation around topics related to the manipulation of life and biological processes — at practical, theoretical, philosophical, and ethical levels. It provides artists, researchers, students, and scholars with the ability to engage with the life sciences within an artistic and cultural context, making creative and critical links between biosciences, engineering, and the arts.

The programme consists of research projects alongside a series of courses, lectures, and hands-on workshops in the laboratory and in natural environments exploring interfaces between biosciences and art.

## History

**2011–2013:** Biofilia laboratory and programme was set up by a team consisting of Professor **Helena Sederholm**, project manager **Ulla Taipale** ([capsula.org.es](http://capsula.org.es)) and laboratory manager **Marika Hellman**, in close cooperation with **[[Ionat Zurr]]** and **[[Oron Catts]]** (founders of [[SymbioticA]], UWA). The lab interiors were designed by architect [Samu Viitanen](http://samuviitanen.com/).

**June 2011:** Memorandum of Understanding signed between University of Western Australia and Aalto University, formalizing the institutional collaboration.

**August 2012:** Biofilia officially launched under Aalto ARTS. From this date, SymbioticA Director Oron Catts and Academic Co-ordinator Dr. Ionat Zurr begin working in collaboration with Pia Lindman and Aalto ARTS to consult and contribute to Biofilia's development. Both hold Visiting Professor positions.

**2014 onwards:** Biofilia integrated into the new **Master Programme in Visual Culture and Contemporary Art** at Aalto ARTS, headed by **Professor Pia Lindman**. Marika Hellman continues as Laboratory Manager. The research programme continues with resident artists and international collaborations.

**Current status:** Biofilia's dedicated website (biofilia.aalto.fi) is no longer live as of 2026 — the domain redirects to Aalto's main site. Its research was folded into Aalto ARTS more broadly. The [[Bioart Society]] in Helsinki continues as the main Finnish bio-art institution, with SOLU as its programme space.

## Research Projects

### Crude Matter
**Chief investigators:** Oron Catts and Ionat Zurr
Artistic meditation on "the substrate as the main context for life." Loosely based on the Golem story (Hebrew: *golem* = "crude, unshaped matter"), exploring alchemical transformation of materials into substrates that support and act on life. Critiques the logic that privileges DNA information over the context in which life operates. Will grow and kill life over a range of materials and forms; draws on experiments showing stem cell fate is determined by substrate stiffness.

Project involves extracting matter from two symbolic sites: the **banks of the Vltava (Moldau) river, Prague** (Golem origin) and a **1942 WWII German Junker 88 crash site in far northern Finland**. Matter sent to material science labs worldwide for analysis; biocompatible substances extracted for tissue scaffolds. Initial tissue: amphibian cells (representing regeneration and the land-water interface).

Supported by Aalto University (Biofilia), SymbioticA (UWA), University of Helsinki Kilpisjärvi Biological Station, and the [Finnish Bioart Society](https://bioartsociety.fi/).

### Melliferopolis – Honeybees in Urban Environments
Transdisciplinary collaborative project at the art-bioscience intersection, investigating:
- Role of honeybees in urban natural processes
- Honey and bees as bio-indicators and environmental monitors
- Implications of bees appearing in cities and interactions with human citizens

Carried out 2012–2013 as a Biofilia research project. From 2013 continued as independent project under the **Melliferopolis** umbrella. Initiative by artist and researcher **Christina Stadlbauer**. [melliferopolis.net](http://www.melliferopolis.net/)

### Animating Semi-Living — Muscle Actuators as Cultural Evocative Objects
Interdisciplinary project examining shifting perceptions of "the living" through tissue-engineered muscle actuators in custom bioreactors. Furthers posthumanist discourses on movement as indicator for life with agency; suggests new modes of inquiry of material life.

Uses skeletal muscle (from rodent primary cell lines) as actuators. Two objectives:
1. Exploring muscle tissue constructs through art and design discourses
2. Initial research into growth and use of muscle tissue as "motors" that generate movement

**Collaborators:** Dr. Ionat Zurr, Oron Catts, Dr. Chris Salter, Dr. Jonas Rubenson, Dr. Stuart Hodgettes. Funded by the **Australian Research Council**.

### Climate Whirl Project
Combining eco-physiological and artistic research. 2012–2013 collaboration between:
- Division of Atmospheric Sciences (Dept. Physics) and Dept. Forest Sciences, University of Helsinki
- Biofilia, Aalto University

Goal: use novel art-science methods to communicate complex multilevel research from the **SMEAR II station at Hyytiälä Forestry Field Station** to wider audiences. 2013: Call for artists; **Agnes Meyer-Brandis** selected for 2-month residency. Results shown August 2014 as participatory workshop and artwork. Funded by [Kone Foundation](https://koneensaatio.fi/).

### Drosophila titanus (Andy Gracie)
Artist Andy Gracie continued his ongoing project at Biofilia: attempting the impossible task of breeding a species of *Drosophila* fit for survival on Saturn's moon Titan, with scientific rigour. New experiments for conditioning, acclimatization, and selection; development of a new incubator/simulator apparatus. Parallel work with drosophila S2 cell lines; investigating artificial cell differentiation through hormones and imaginal discs. Strong theoretical focus on drosophila as living metaphor for the human body as defined by scientific practice.

## For Amber

### Why Interesting
Biofilia is a concrete institutional proof-of-concept for the SymbioticA model in a European (Nordic) context. For Amber:

1. **More-than-human design**: The lab explicitly materializes more-than-human relations — muscle actuators, bee ecologies, tissue scaffolding, drosophila breeding for alien environments. All highly relevant to Amber's interests.

2. **Art-science institution building**: Shows how artists can build genuine research infrastructure inside an engineering university — and how international collaboration (UWA–Aalto MOU) can seed such institutions.

3. **Nordic bio-art scene**: Entry point into the Finnish/Nordic bioart community, which is active and connected (Bioart Society, SOLU, Ars Bioarctica residency, Kilpisjärvi Biological Station).

4. **ALife resonances**: The Drosophila titanus project (breeding for Titan) and the Animating Semi-Living project (muscle tissue as motor with agency) directly engage ALife themes — agency, life boundary, speculative evolution, non-terrestrial life.

5. **Key advisory board figures**: Peter Galison (Harvard History of Science) and Krzysztof Wodiczko (Harvard GSD) — distinguished and well-networked advisory voices. Galison in particular bridges STS and philosophy of science.

## Why Interesting

Biofilia matters as a **case study in institutional technology transfer** — specifically, the export of the SymbioticA model to a new geography. The MOU between UWA and Aalto University (2011) is an unusual institutional mechanism: a research-art laboratory collaboration between universities on opposite ends of the planet. The fact that this worked — that Biofilia ran a real programme for several years, attracted international residents, ran genuine research projects — shows that the biological arts model is portable.

For Amber, Biofilia is relevant as:
1. **Model for institution-building** — how to establish a bio-art lab inside an engineering-focused university
2. **Nordic art-science ecosystem** — entry into the Finnish/Nordic scene, which has produced unique cultural conditions (extreme environments as artistic material, ecological urgency, strong public arts infrastructure)
3. **ALife-adjacent research** — the Drosophila titanus project in particular is directly ALife: speculative evolution for non-terrestrial survival, using living organisms as research medium. The Animating Semi-Living project touches tissue robotics and agency.
4. **Advisory board network** — Peter Galison (history of science), Krzysztof Wodiczko (interventionist public art), Erich Berger (bioart) — high-quality intellectual neighbourhood.
