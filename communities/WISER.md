# WISER — Wits Institute for Social and Economic Research

Africa's leading interdisciplinary humanities research institute. Based at the University of the Witwatersrand, Johannesburg. 25+ years of generating cutting-edge scholarship on: necropolitics, hydrocolonialism, biometric capitalism, African aesthetics, African intellectual history, Johannesburg as postcolonial metropolis, and much more.

## Key People
- **[[Achille Mbembe]]** — research professor (primary base)
- **[[Sarah Nuttall]]** — professor; directed the institute 2013-2023
- **Isabel Hofmeyr** — literary scholar, co-convenes research forums with Nuttall

## Mission & Objectives
Interdisciplinary scholarship in the African humanities; collaborative research and publication; critical public humanities practice for and from the Global South; intellectual community for postgraduate students, writers in residence, and international visitors.

## Key Contributions
- The WISER Print Seminar series — ongoing research presentations open to public
- Online research forums: Heated Conversations, Breathing In: Air and Atmospheres, Plant Lives (Nuttall + Hofmeyr)
- Major book series with Duke University Press and other publishers
- The intellectual home of necropolitics as a live research programme
