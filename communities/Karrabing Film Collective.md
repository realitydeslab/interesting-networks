# Karrabing Film Collective

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-03-01:** Deep research completed. Expanded all sections — filmography, exhibitions, theory, members, Amber relevance.
- **2026-02-27:** Stub created during EYE Filmmuseum deep research. EYE Art & Film Prize winner 2021.

Indigenous media collective and grassroots activist organisation founded in 2008, based in the Northern Territory, Australia. Over 50 members (grown from ~30), all but one Indigenous stakeholders for the land, ages from newborn to elder. Self-described grassroots activist collective creating films and video installations as **political self-organization, cultural survival, and ancestral obligations**. The word "karrabing" means "low tide" in Emmiyengal — a form of collectivity outside government-imposed structures of clanship or land ownership.

## Mission & Objectives

- Keep members' own lands strong by strengthening family, environmental, and totemic relations
- Create films that represent their lives, land, and culture from the inside — not through outsider ethnographic gaze
- Challenge Australian government land and welfare policies through satirical and political films
- Develop "improvisational realism" as an artistic/political methodology
- Maintain ancestral heritage sites through the **Karrabing Art Residency for Ancestors (KARA)** program
- Bend Western mapping technologies (GIS, GPS) to reflect a world connected through ancestral agencies (**KMAP** project)
- Model an integrated economic and ecological model: cultural heritage management + art production + sea ranger programs

## The "Improvisational Realism" Methodology

Their films are shot on handheld cameras and phones, featuring collective members as actors in improvised scenarios drawn from their actual lived experiences with government and corporate entities. Stories blend:
- Fictional and documentary
- Past and present
- Indigenous mythology and contemporary political reality
- Satire and genuine political urgency
- Near-future speculative scenarios (toxic capitalism, zombie whites, ancestral beings)

The result bypasses the fiction/documentary binary entirely. "Improvisational realism" as a concept is a genuine contribution to film theory — not just a description of their process but a **critique of both Hollywood narrative and traditional ethnographic documentary**. Their nonlinear narratives jump freely in time, weaving cultural memory, place, and ancestry.

## Organisational Structure

- Incorporated as **Karrabing Indigenous Corporation** — Not-for-Profit, Tax Exempt, Gift Receiving corporation under the Office of the Registrar of Indigenous Corporations
- **Not** a conventional art collective with agent/gallery representation in the traditional sense
- Operates through self-organised family and community relationships
- Explicitly refuses external production protocols

## Projects & Programmes

### KARA — Karrabing Art Residency for Ancestors
[karrabing.info/karrabing-art-residency](https://karrabing.info/karrabing-art-residency)
Residency for Ancestors at the southern side of Anson Bay, NT. Partners: **Serpentine, London**; **Aboriginal Areas Protection Authority**; **Indigenous Language and Arts Program**. Goals:
- Endowment for protection of totemic/historical ecology in Cape Ford region
- Arts and Sea Rangers programme to monitor ecological and ancestral health
- 4-5 residences including one for art residencies and curatorial conversations
An "open air gallery whose primary audiences are their ancestral totems and relatives" — a radical inversion of the gallery concept.

### KMAP — Karrabing Mapping the Ancestral Present
[karrabing.info/mapping](https://karrabing.info/mapping)
Working with governmental and non-governmental agencies to reconceptualise spiritual, ecological, and historical connections to their lands. Bends Western GIS/GPS mapping to reflect a world connected through **ancestral agencies of totems**. Currently focused on historical rock fish traps and fish totems of their coastal region.

## Key Theoretical Contributions

**"Improvisational realism"** — their methodology as cinematic theory. Not ethnographic documentary (which always carries the colonial gaze), not fiction (which has no political obligation to truth), but something third: a practice of political self-representation that uses drama to explore real structural conditions.

**"Karrabing" as concept** — low tide as time of exposure, vulnerability, and also richness (what's revealed when the tide goes out). A collectivity that doesn't depend on government-approved land ownership or clan structures — an alternative model of Indigenous organisation.

**"Ancestral present"** — Karrabing films don't use a linear temporal framework. Ancestors, totems, colonial history, and near-future speculation all coexist in a single temporal field. This is not mysticism but a different ontological model of time.

**Geontopower (via Povinelli)** — Povinelli's theoretical framework developed through collaboration with Karrabing: power operates through the distinction between Life and Nonlife (geology vs. biology). *Geontologies* (Duke UP, 2016) uses Karrabing's practice as a key site of thought. This is directly relevant to more-than-human theory.

**Between Gaia and Ground** (2021, Povinelli et al. with Karrabing) — collaborative publication developing these ideas.

## Venue Circuit Analysis

Karrabing operates in a very specific circuit, reflecting their position as Indigenous practitioners working both within and against major institutions:

**1. Major Biennial Circuit** — Gwangju, Shanghai, Sydney, Jakarta, Biennale circuit. This legitimises them within the global contemporary art world while using those platforms to project their political message.

**2. Film Festival Circuit** — Berlinale, Melbourne IFF, IFFR Rotterdam, MoMA PS1. Positions them as film artists, not just "Indigenous art" — forces engagement on cinematic terms.

**3. UK/European Major Institutions** — Tate Modern, Centre Pompidou, Serpentine, EYE. These are advocacy partnerships as much as exhibitions.

**4. Australian + Pacific** — IMA Brisbane, AGNSW Sydney, Art Gallery NSW. Home circuit.

**5. Commission-Based Research Partners** — Wexner Center (Ohio), Dazibao (Montréal). These are specifically interested in their practice-as-research.

Notable: **they do NOT show in commercial galleries**, and explicitly refuse conventional art market representation. Their institutional relationships are strategic alliances.

## For Amber

- **🌍 Key theoretical reference** — Karrabing + Povinelli's *geontologies* / "geontopower" is one of the most important frameworks for **more-than-human theory** from an Indigenous perspective. This is not peripheral to Amber's work — it's a direct intellectual ancestor of thinking about non-human agency, life/nonlife distinctions, and ontological politics. Read *Geontologies* (Duke UP, 2016).

- **🌍 Methodological reference** — "Improvisational realism" and "ancestral present" are concepts that could directly inform how Amber thinks about AI-generated narratives, speculative design, and the fiction/reality binary in his own work. Their approach to nonlinear temporal representation is directly analogous to design fiction methodologies.

- **🤝 Serpentine connection — actionable** — Karrabing's partnership with Serpentine (London) via Back to Earth and KARA means there's a shared Serpentine network. - **🤝 Superflux** — the speculative design studio [Superflux](https://superflux.in/) showed at the same Back to Earth exhibition. Superflux is London-based, works on speculative futures + more-than-human futures. This is a potential Amber connection worth investigating.

- **📚 Via Povinelli** — Elizabeth Povinelli is the key intellectual intermediary. Her work on geontologies, geontopower, and late liberalism is essential reading for anyone working in more-than-human design, critical AI studies, and ontological politics. Create a [[Elizabeth Povinelli]] note.

- **🔮 KARA Residency model** — Their Art Residency for Ancestors is a provocative inversion of the art residency concept (primary audience = ancestral totems, not humans). This is a useful conceptual provocation for thinking about post-human-centric design.

- **🌍 Indigenous ontology as counterpoint** — Karrabing's work is the most rigorous example of an Indigenous ontological framework being deployed as critique of both state power AND Western art/film convention simultaneously. For Amber's work on more-than-human design, having this as an explicit reference point would strengthen the theoretical grounding.

## Why Interesting

Karrabing sits at a genuine intersection of several of Amber's core threads:

1. **More-than-human** — their "ancestral present" framework treats totems, land, and ancestors as agents with ongoing demands on the living. This is indigenous ontology as more-than-human theory.

2. **Critical computing (sideways)** — their KMAP project bends GIS/GPS to ancestral ontology. They are explicitly hacking Western mapping tech. Not computational art, but a form of critical media practice.

3. **Art-science** — their collaboration with Povinelli produces genuine theoretical work, not just artistic statement. The Karrabing method has been theorised, published, and cited in critical theory.

4. **Participatory** — their model of collective organisation (Karrabing Indigenous Corporation) is a working alternative to both state and market models.

5. **Posthuman / post-anthropocentric** — their films regularly feature non-human actors (totems, spirits, mermaids) as political agents, not metaphors.

**Amber's research on cosmotechnics/Yuk Hui territory**: Karrabing's critique of Western land and governance — and their bending of Western tech to non-Western ontologies — is a Southern-hemisphere parallel to Yuk Hui's cosmotechnics argument. Not East Asian, but structurally homologous: indigenous tech vs. Western univocal technics.
