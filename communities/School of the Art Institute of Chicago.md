# School of the Art Institute of Chicago (SAIC)

One of the oldest and most influential art schools in the US — and arguably the most serious institutional home for art-technology-biology intersection. Founded 1866. Ranked #2 Fine Arts in US (U.S. News). Home to [[Eduardo Kac]], the Visiting Artists Program (est. 1868), and the Art & Technology Studies department that invented the field. Attached at the hip to the Art Institute of Chicago museum — students have one of the world's great art collections as a resource. Vibe: rigorously interdisciplinary, politically conscious, not afraid of bio art, scent, circuits, or synthetic biology.

## Mission & Objectives
SAIC believes that meaning and making are inseparable. As a professional art school integrated with a major art museum, it educates artists, designers, and scholars who are "driven by ideas — our responses to the worlds of art and science, to social justice issues, to personal identity and community." Its graduate program is ranked #2 Fine Arts in the US. The institution explicitly values:
- Interdisciplinary crossing — free to explore outside your department
- Rigorous critique — critique week as major pedagogical method
- Practice-based knowing — making as epistemology

In 2024, fiber artist and academic administrator **Jiseon Lee Isbara** became president, succeeding Elissa Tenny.

## Key Departments & Programs

### [Art & Technology Studies (ATSP)](https://www.saic.edu/atsp)
The flagship department. BFA and MFA. One of the oldest art-technology programmes in the US. Teaches art-technology as a critical and aesthetic practice, not just tool use. Current faculty teach circuits, bio art, computer vision, kinetics, olfactory art, robotics. The department takes seriously the question: *what can art do with technology that technology cannot do for itself?*

### [Visual and Critical Studies (VCS)](https://www.saic.edu/visual-critical-studies)
BA and MA. "Concerned with the study of visual media across many different disciplines." Hybrid research practices — overlap between studio and scholarly production. Students with backgrounds in visual arts, social sciences, critical theory, philosophy, performance, social justice. Strong pipeline to museum studies, arts administration, academic humanities.

## Key Faculty

### [[Eduardo Kac]]
Professor, Art & Technology Studies. Pioneer of **bio art**, **transgenic art**, and **telepresence art**. Created *GFP Bunny* (2000) — genetically engineered fluorescent rabbit — and *Time Capsule* (1997), *Genesis* (1999). SAIC's anchor in the intersection of art, biotechnology, and life sciences. One of the most cited figures in art-science globally.

### Douglas Rosman
Faculty, Art & Technology Studies. Teaches *Introduction to Computer Vision* — covers ML body tracking, real-time object detection, p5.js, TouchDesigner. Critically frames automated mass surveillance alongside interactive installations. Practice at the intersection of creative coding and critical tech.

### Andrew H. Scarpelli
Faculty, Art & Technology Studies. Teaches *Synthetic Futures: Biodesign Challenge* — a studio/science hybrid course where students enter the [Biodesign Challenge](https://www.biodesignchallenge.org/) competition. Covers genetic engineering theory, bacterial cultures, DNA extraction, PCR, gel electrophoresis — situated within cultural, ethical, and artistic contexts. The rare place where art students do actual wet lab biology.

### Joseph Michael Kramer
Faculty, Art & Technology Studies. Teaches the core intro course *Art and Technology Practices* — provides interdisciplinary grounding in electronic media, perception, inter-media composition. Gateway into the department.

### Dan Miller
Faculty, Art & Technology Studies. Teaches *Mechanisms, Movement & Meaning* (kinetics, motors, motor control) and *Air, Fluid and Actuators* (pneumatics, inflatables, Arduino). Connects to Ars Electronica, Rhizome, Bitforms gallery ecosystem.

### Bonnie Han Jones
Faculty, Art & Technology Studies. Teaches *Introduction to Sound Practices* and *Hardware Hacking* — soldering, contact mics, electromagnetic sniffing, circuit bending. Hands-on, materialist approach to technology as creative medium.

### Brett Ian Balogh
Faculty, Art & Technology Studies. Teaches *Analog Systems* — explicitly positioned as stepping "outside the shadow of AI and mass computation." Circuits as creative tools for sound, light, motion. Includes study of Sandin Image Processor.

### Judy Malloy
Faculty, Art & Technology Studies. Teaches *Women Artists in Cyberspace* — computing history through women pioneers (Katherine Johnson, Lynn Conway, Margaret Hamilton) through to contemporary cyberartists (Shu Lea Cheang, Micha Cardenas, Lynn Hershman Leeson). Feminist historiography of tech.

### Tedd Neenan
Faculty, Art & Technology Studies. Teaches *Olfactory Art* — scent as expressive medium, using the ATS Perfume Organ and hydro-distillation. One of the only dedicated courses in olfactory art at any art school globally.

### Lou Mallozzi
Faculty, Art & Technology Studies. Teaches *Sound and Image* — post-production, sound design, compositional strategies for sound-image relationships.

### Gregory Mowery
Faculty, Art & Technology Studies. Teaches *Neon Techniques* and *Shaping Light* — neon tube bending, light design, LED/cold cathode. References Bruce Nauman, Keith Sonnier, Joseph Kosuth.

## Key Publications & Output

### [Video Data Bank (VDB)](https://www.vdb.org/)
Founded at SAIC in 1976. The leading US resource for video art. 6,000+ titles by 600+ video artists — including early performance documentation, media art, video poetry. Available for research and study at SAIC. Connected to the video art canon (Nam June Paik, Joan Jonas, etc.)

### [F Newsmagazine](https://fnewsmagazine.com/)
SAIC's student-run newspaper. Monthly, 12,000-copy print run distributed throughout Chicago. Student journalism, criticism, exhibition coverage.

### [Joan Flasch Artists' Book Collection](https://www.saic.edu/libraries-special-collections/john-m-flaxman-library/special-collections/joan-flasch-artists-book-collection)
11,000+ artists' publications — books, zines, digital works, audio recordings. Dating back to 1960s. Unparalleled for research on artists' publishing practices.

### [John M. Flaxman Library](https://www.saic.edu/libraries-special-collections/john-m-flaxman-library)
Hundreds of thousands of books, magazines, films, sound recordings on contemporary and historic art and design.

### ExTV
Student-run time-arts platform, broadcasts throughout SAIC campus. Video art, performance documentation.

## Why Interesting

**For Amber's research, SAIC matters on multiple vectors:**

1. **Institutional home of bio art** — [[Eduardo Kac]] coined the term and still teaches here. ATSP is where "what does biology mean as an artistic medium?" is taken seriously as a curriculum question — not just a visiting lecture.

2. **Art-technology with critical theory** — Unlike many tech-art programmes that default to cool demo culture, SAIC's VCS and ATSP courses consistently ask: *what are the power structures here? who benefits?* The Analog Systems course explicitly critiques AI computation dominance. The Computer Vision course teaches surveillance critique alongside body tracking.

3. **Speculative science as art practice** — Synthetic Futures: Biodesign Challenge, Olfactory Art, Eduardo Kac's transgenic work — these are not speculative fiction but speculative making. This is exactly Amber's mode.

4. **Visiting Artists Program** — Has hosted Hito Steyerl, Trevor Paglen, Stephanie Dinkins, Wu Tsang — the exact intellectual community Amber should be part of. Being invited to SAIC VAP would signal membership in this network.

5. **Chicago node** — UChicago (James Evans, Amber's key person), SAIC, and the broader Chicago arts scene form a connected ecosystem. Visiting one is a reason to visit all.
