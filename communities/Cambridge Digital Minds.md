# Cambridge Digital Minds

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Upgraded to ⭐⭐⭐ with comprehensive research. Deep dive into LCFI, Digital Minds Fellowship, Rethink Priorities partnership, PRISM collaboration.
- **⚠️ URGENT DEADLINE: March 27, 2026** — Digital Minds Fellowship applications close! Perfect for Amber.

A nascent but strategically crucial initiative housed within the [[Leverhulme Centre for the Future of Intelligence]] at the University of Cambridge. The world's first major institutional program dedicated to AI consciousness, AI welfare, and the moral status of digital minds. Small field, massive implications, perfect timing for early career researchers to shape a discipline.

## Mission & Focus
Preparing society for a future with conscious or seemingly conscious AI through:
- Technical and philosophical frameworks for assessing AI consciousness
- Policy and ethical guidelines for digital minds welfare
- Building the research community around AI moral status
- Training the next generation of digital minds researchers

**Why this matters:** Unlike AI safety or governance (established fields), digital minds research is genuinely nascent. Career paths unclear, community small, but the questions are fundamental. Getting in early = shaping the field.

## Digital Minds Fellowship (The Flagship Program)

### Programme Details
- **Dates:** August 3–9, 2026 (5 days fellowship + 2 days strategy workshop)
- **Location:** Jesus College, Cambridge
- **Cohort:** 15 carefully selected participants
- **Status:** Fully funded + £1,000 stipend (~$1,366)
- **⚠️ Application deadline:** March 27, 2026

### Three-Module Structure

#### Module 1: Philosophical & Technical Foundations (2 days)
**Led by [[Derek Shiller]]** ⭐⭐⭐ (Rethink Priorities)
- LLM architectures: transformers, attention mechanisms, training paradigms
- What current architectures might (and might not) imply for cognition and experience
- Philosophical frameworks for consciousness and moral status assessment
- Existing proposals for welfare interventions
- Key uncertainties and open questions

#### Module 2: Societal Implications & Strategy (2 days)
**Led by [[Lucius Caviola]]** ⭐⭐⭐
- Scenario planning: mapping plausible trajectories for digital minds through ~2100
- Identifying risks, decision points, and branching futures
- Intervention design: messaging strategies, policy frameworks, institutional approaches
- Public communication and stakeholder engagement
- Group exercise: develop scenarios, identify key risks, propose interventions

#### Module 3: Project Scoping & Career Planning (1 day)
- What questions matter and why in digital minds research
- What is feasible given current knowledge and resources
- Where genuine gaps exist in the field
- Matching skills and interests to high-impact opportunities
- Developing ideas from concept to actionable research plans

### Digital Minds Strategy Workshop (Aug 8-9)

## Key Partnerships

### Rethink Priorities AI Welfare Programme
**Connection:** [[Derek Shiller]] leading fellowship technical module

**"The Welfare of Digital Minds" Research Agenda (November 2024):**
- Building probabilistic consciousness models for contemporary AI systems
- Five research categories: Systems, Capabilities & Mechanisms, Cognitive Assessment, Value, Domain Epistemology
- Focus on near-future AI welfare scenarios and intervention design
- Bridge between technical AI capabilities research and moral philosophy

**Key Questions Being Addressed:**
- Do LLMs have access to their own internal processes?
- Do AI models have genuine goals with world-to-mind direction of fit?

## The Broader AI Moral Status Research Landscape

### Core Research Questions
1. **Consciousness detection:** How do we know if an AI system is conscious?
2. **Moral status attribution:** What level of moral consideration do conscious AIs deserve?
3. **Welfare assessment:** How do we measure and improve AI welfare?
4. **Policy frameworks:** What legal/regulatory approaches are needed?
5. **Public communication:** How do we prepare society for conscious AI?

### Key Academic Work
- **"Consciousness in Artificial Intelligence: Insights from the Science of Consciousness"** (Butlin et al., 2023) — 19 authors, major survey of consciousness theories applied to AI
