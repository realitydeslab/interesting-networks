# IRI (Institut de recherche et d'innovation)

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Full deep research completed. Expanded from stub to gold standard profile with full history, key people (Scientific College), research programs, theoretical frameworks, Centre Pompidou connection, and Amber-specific analysis.

Research institute founded in April 2006 at the [Centre Pompidou](https://www.centrepompidou.fr/) in Paris by philosopher [[Bernard Stiegler]], to anticipate, accompany, and analyze cultural, scientific, and economic mutations induced by digital technologies. Unique institution bridging continental philosophy (Stiegler's technics/pharmacology framework), museum practice, and practical digital research. In 2008 became an autonomous research association with multiple institutional members. Continues post-Stiegler under a Scientific and Industrial College (Collège Scientifique et Industriel) in close collaboration with [[Collectif Internation]] and [[Association Épokhè]].

## Mission & Objectives

**Core mission:** "Develop new critical contributive devices and research the epistemological, cultural and political stakes of digital technology." — Founded to "anticipate, accompany, and analyze the mutations of cultural, scientific and economic activities induced by digital technologies."

**Philosophical foundation:** Rooted in [[Bernard Stiegler]]'s philosophy of technics — every technical object is a *pharmakon* (Greek: simultaneously poison and remedy). Technology destroys existing forms of knowledge and attention while creating new possibilities. IRI's task is to develop the *care* (soin) required to navigate this double-bind. Not anti-technology, not technologically naive: engaged, critical, and constructive.

**Two central concepts:**
1. **Organology of knowledge** (*organologie des savoirs*) — the co-individuation of human organs, technical organs, and social organizations. Knowledge is always organized through technical media; changing the media changes the knowledge.
2. **The figure of the amateur** (*la figure de l'amateur*) — the engaged, passionate non-professional who takes care of something for its own sake, not for economic return. Digital technologies both threaten (proletarianization of amateurs into passive consumers) and enable (collaborative tools for amateur knowledge production) this figure.

**Current research axes (post-Stiegler):**
- [Parentalité, Soin et Numérique](https://tac93.fr/fr/axes/parentalite/) — parenting, care, and digital technology (esp. screen exposure in children)
- [Design de la contribution urbaine](https://tac93.fr/fr/activities/eco/) — contributive urban design, ecological local currencies
- [Économie de la contribution](https://tac93.fr/fr/axes/economie/) — contributive economy beyond consumerism
- Savoirs et technologies — knowledge and technologies

## History & Founding

**1987–88:** Bernard Stiegler (then freshly out of prison, PhD student under Derrida) co-commissions exhibition *Mémoires du futur: bibliothèques et technologies* at Centre Pompidou with Catherine Counot. First institutional connection to the museum.

**1 January 2006:** Stiegler becomes Director of the Department of Cultural Development at Centre Georges-Pompidou. Gains institutional position to build new research structure.

**April 2006:** IRI formally created at Stiegler's initiative, housed within Centre Pompidou. Mission: research into digital culture and its consequences; develop new "critical contributive devices."

**2007:** First *Entretiens du Nouveau Monde Industriel* (ENMI) conference, co-organized with Cap Digital and Les Ateliers (ENSCI). Theme: "Design and Bottom-Up Innovation." Annual event at Centre Pompidou becomes flagship event.

**August 2008:** IRI acquires autonomous association status (*association de recherche autonome*). Founding administrative members: [Centre Pompidou](https://www.centrepompidou.fr/), [Centre de Culture Contemporaine de Barcelone (CCCB)](https://www.cccb.org/), [Microsoft France](https://www.microsoft.com/fr-fr/), [Institut Mines-Telecom](https://www.imt.fr/), [Strate École de Design](https://www.strate.design/), [École Supérieure d'Art du Pays Basque](https://esapyrenees.fr/), [CY École de Design](https://www.cy-ad.fr/), [Épokhè/Ars Industrialis](https://epokhe.world/), [Caisse des Dépôts](https://www.caissedesdepots.fr/), [Fondation Orange](https://www.fondationorange.com/). Also included: [Goldsmiths College](https://www.gold.ac.uk/), [Bell Labs](https://www.bell-labs.com/), [France Télévisions](https://www.france.tv/), [Tokyo University](https://www.u-tokyo.ac.jp/en/).

**2010:** [[Bernard Stiegler]] opens [pharmakon.fr](https://pharmakon.fr/) philosophy school in Épineuil-le-Fleuriel, running in parallel as the theoretical engine for IRI research. Three-track model: public courses, doctoral seminars (by videoconference), summer academies.

**2012:** [Digital Studies](https://digital-studies.org/) international network established — IRI's major international research network, connecting institutions globally around questions of digital epistemology.

**May 2016:** [Territoire Apprenant Contributif (TAC)](https://tac93.fr/) project initiated in Plaine Commune (Seine-Saint-Denis, Paris suburbs). Major long-term territorial research project — applying contributive economy theory in specific geographic community.

**5 August 2020:** Bernard Stiegler dies by suicide. IRI transitions to collective leadership under Collège Scientifique et Industriel, continuing his research programs.

**Post-2020:** IRI continues in close collaboration with [[Collectif Internation]] and [[Association Épokhè]]. Current president: **Vincent Puig** (Épokhè – Ars Industrialis). TAC project remains active (latest activities include FOSDEM 2024 presentation, educational programs in Seine-Saint-Denis schools).

**Note:** Centre Pompidou closed March 2025 for major renovation until 2030. IRI's institutional status during this period requires monitoring.

- **Entretiens du Nouveau Monde Industriel (ENMI)** — Annual flagship event at Centre Pompidou (2007–2019+), co-organized with [Cap Digital](https://www.capdigital.com/) and [Les Ateliers (ENSCI)](https://www.ensci.com/):

  - 2011: Technology Trust

  - 2018: *L'intelligence des villes et la nouvelle révolution urbaine* ("Intelligence of Cities and the New Urban Revolution")

### Software Tools
- **TimeLine annotation software** — collaborative annotation and analysis platform for temporal objects (films, audio recordings). Allows critics, analysts, and amateurs to "map" films via graphical timeline with multimedia comments. Implements Stiegler's theory of "temporal objects" and attention.
- **Annotation/controversy platform** — allows audiences at events to annotate talks in real-time (agreement/disagreement/question/reference), then visualize collective positions on timeline synchronized with video recording. Early prototype of social reading technology.

## Key Contributions

**Theoretical contributions:**
1. **Pharmacology of digital technology** — systematic framework for analyzing digital tech as simultaneously destructive and generative, requiring care and critique rather than simple acceptance or rejection.
2. **Amateur figure in digital culture** — recovered and theorized the *amateur* (the passionate non-professional) as crucial for cultural vitality against proletarianization. Directly relevant to participatory design, open source, maker culture.
3. **General organology** — framework for analyzing co-evolution of human organs (nervous system), technical organs (tools, media), and social organizations. Predates "sociotechnical systems" and is more philosophically rigorous.
4. **Critical contributive devices** — practical concept for tools that enable critical engagement rather than passive consumption. Implemented in TimeLine software.
5. **Contributive economy theory** — alternative to both capitalism and traditional socialism, based on contribution and care rather than consumption. Implemented in TAC and ECO projects.

**Practical contributions:**
- Pioneered annotation tools for cultural analysis (2006–2012) before this was standard in digital humanities
- Created new model of philosophy-museum collaboration through institutional embedding at Pompidou
- TAC project: one of few sustained experiments applying philosophical theory to territorial/community development

## Why Interesting

IRI sits at an unusual intersection: it is simultaneously a **philosophical institution** (rooted in the most rigorous Continental philosophy of technology), a **museum research center** (inside Centre Pompidou, engaged with cultural practice), a **digital humanities lab** (building actual software tools), and a **territorial social project** (the TAC experiment in Seine-Saint-Denis). This multi-layered institutional identity is rare and generative.

For Amber specifically: the pharmacological framework is one of the most sophisticated available for thinking about AI and attention. The amateur figure resonates with Amber's interest in participatory and democratic design. The general organology concept is a powerful tool for analyzing how technical systems co-evolve with human capacities and social organizations.

The Stiegler legacy network also connects directly to Amber's existing interests: [[Yuk Hui]] is Stiegler's direct intellectual heir; [[Gilbert Simondon]] is Stiegler's key precursor; and the IRI tradition feeds directly into contemporary debates about algorithmic governmentality ([[Antoinette Rouvroy]]), digital sovereignty, and alternative economic models.

IRI is **edge of chaos adjacent**: the Stiegler-founded institutions are in productive post-death reconfiguration, the ideas are being taken up and extended by a new generation of scholars (Yuk Hui, Anaïs Nony, Maël Montévil), and the pharmacological framework is becoming newly relevant as AI ethics debates need more sophisticated conceptual tools.
