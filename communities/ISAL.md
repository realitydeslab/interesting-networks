# International Society for Artificial Life (ISAL)

The learned society and institutional home of the Artificial Life community. Non-profit, international, democratic. Founded May 2001. Governs the annual ALIFE conference, publishes *Artificial Life* journal (MIT Press), maintains the ALife Encyclopedia, and runs ISAL Awards. The gateway organization for anyone entering the ALife field. Interdisciplinary community spanning CS, biology, philosophy, robotics, art, and complex systems.

## Mission & Objectives

**Mission:** To promote research and education in Artificial Life through:
1. Creating a supportive and inclusive community focused on ethical and effective scholarship in ALife
2. Increasing the external impact, accessibility, and relevance of ALife scholarship

**Vision:** For ALife to be widely known and valued as a highly interdisciplinary, impactful, and welcoming area of scholarship.

**Core Values:**
- Public good — contribute to human society and the biosphere
- Inclusive and supportive community — everyone welcome
- Strong community — support communication across diverse fields
- Strong scholarship — scientific rigor + high ethical standards

**Scope:** ISAL explicitly values interdisciplinary and transdisciplinary research, and prioritizes being viable for early career scholars.

## Key Publications & Output

### *Artificial Life* Journal (MIT Press)
- **Website:** <https://direct.mit.edu/artl>
- The flagship journal of the ALife field. Published by MIT Press.
- Papers accepted in the journal are **automatically accepted as oral presentations** at the ALIFE conference
- ISAL members get full electronic access to entire archive (back to first issue)
- Open access conference proceedings published annually
- **Strategy:** Publishing in *Artificial Life* = guaranteed ALIFE conference oral slot

### ALife Encyclopedia
- **Website:** <https://alife.org/encyclopedia/>
- Community-written educational resource on ALife topics
- ISAL members can contribute/edit entries
- Current entries include: Neural Networks (Dec 2025 update), and growing
- Good way to establish expertise and get involved as a new member

### ISAL Newsletter
- <https://alife.org/category/newsletter/>

### Conference Proceedings
- MIT Press open access proceedings from every annual conference
- <https://direct.mit.edu/isal> for full archive

## ISAL Awards (Annual)

Awarded each year at the ALIFE conference. Nominations open to all ALife community members.

### Award Categories
1. **Lifetime Achievement** — sustained career contributions
2. **Distinguished Early-Career Investigator** — researcher within ~10 years of PhD
3. **Education and Outreach** — public understanding of ALife
4. **Exceptional Service** — service to the ISAL community
5. **Outstanding Publication of the Decade** — best paper from ~10 years ago (rotates)
6. **Outstanding Publication** — best recent paper (not always awarded)
7. **Outstanding Student Publication** — best paper with student first author
8. **Outstanding Reviewer, Artificial Life Journal** — peer review excellence

## Why Interesting

ISAL is not just a professional society — it's the institutional backbone of a community asking genuinely wild questions about what life is. The ALife field bridges CS, biology, philosophy, and art in a way that few other communities do. The awards history reveals the values: Takashi Ikegami (art × ALife) won Lifetime Achievement; Craig Reynolds (Boids — flocking behavior algorithm from 1987!) won Lifetime Achievement; Cross Labs (art-science institute) won Education & Outreach. The community explicitly values weirdness, art, and fundamental questions over applied work. This is Amber's people. The international board (Japan heavy, UK, USA, Nordic) reflects a genuinely global community. The annual conference alternates geographies. For Amber's work on machine behavior, agent ethology, parasitic agents — this community would find it interesting and validate it as ALife.
