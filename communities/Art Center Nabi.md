# Art Center Nabi 아트센터나비

Korea's first and most prominent new media / digital art institution. Founded 2000 in Seoul by **Soh Yeong Roh** (노소영), daughter of former South Korean President Roh Tae-woo, and initially housed in — and funded by — **SK Group**'s headquarters building. This chaebol-backed origin is the key to understanding Nabi: it had institutional legitimacy and resources from the start, letting it take risks on emerging media art at a moment when no Korean infrastructure existed for it. Twenty-five years on, it is the anchor of the Korean media art ecosystem and hosted the **30th ISEA** in Seoul in 2025.

## Mission & Objectives
Three C's: **Critique, Creation, Community**. Operating at the intersection of art, science, and technology, Nabi:
- Produces and commissions new media/digital artworks
- Runs international artist residencies (partnered with V2_ Rotterdam and others)
- Maintains education programmes (NABI SCHOOL)
- Runs E.I.Lab (Emotional Intelligence Lab) — a creator/maker lab embedded within the institution
- Archives Korean media art history (25-year archive, "Beyond the Wall")
- Connects Korean artists to the global media art circuit (ISEA, Ars Electronica, etc.)

The philosophical north star: **Nam June Paik**'s "Humanizing Technology." Nabi sees itself as carrying forward the legacy of Korea's greatest media artist into the 21st century.

## Key Contributions
- Established Korea's first dedicated new media art institution (2000) — there was no infrastructure before Nabi
- **INDAF 2010** (Incheon International Digital Art Festival) — brought Korea into the global media art festival circuit
- **ISEA 2025** in Seoul — the 30th ISEA, one of the world's oldest and most prestigious electronic art symposia, brought to Korea for the first time
- **E.I.Lab** — embedded creator lab for Korean media artists working with HCI, AI, and robotics
- **Nam June Paik homage** (2014) — 30-year anniversary of "Good Morning, Mr. Orwell" satellite performance, recreated in Busan
- International residency model: partnership with [[V2_Lab for the Unstable Media]] (Rotterdam) to bring global media artists to Seoul

## Why Interesting
Nabi is interesting as an **institutional model** as much as a programme. How does a major media art institution get built in a country with no prior infrastructure? Answer: chaebol patronage (SK Group) + political capital (Roh family) + genuine intellectual mission (Nam June Paik legacy). This combination produced something real and lasting. 25 years later, Nabi can host an ISEA.

For Amber specifically: Korea is an underexplored node in the Asia-Pacific circuit. Most Western-facing researchers know Japan (YCAM, ICC) but not Korea. Nabi is the entry point. The ISEA 2025 connection gives Amber a warm entry.

The **AI art / computational creativity** thread (Why Future Still needs Us, E.I.Lab, residency programme) is directly aligned with Amber's computational creativity interests. [[Maurice Benayoun]]'s participation connects to the French-Chinese-global media art circuit, and [[Gene Kogan]]'s participation connects to the ML-art-generative space.
