# Jack Shainman Gallery

New York powerhouse gallery (Chelsea + Tribeca + The School in Kinderhook NY) dedicated to socially engaged art by multicultural artists — especially artists of color from Africa, the African diaspora, Latin America, and the Middle East. Founded 1984 by Jack Shainman and Claude Simard. Known for long-term, committed artist relationships, introducing international artists to American audiences, and building careers over decades. A landmark institution in contemporary art that bridges social justice, diaspora identity, and formal artistic excellence.

## Mission & Objectives

Jack Shainman Gallery champions artists who have "achieved mastery of their creative disciplines and are among the most compelling and influential contributors to culture today." Over four decades, the gallery's focus has been:

- **Introducing international artists to American audiences** — first New York exhibitions of Nick Cave, Hayv Kahraman, Kerry James Marshall, Meleko Mokgosi, Richard Mosse, Toyin Ojih Odutola, Hank Willis Thomas, Lynette Yiadom-Boakye, among others.
- **Long-term artist development** — gallerist as long-form partner rather than short-term market player
- **Social and cultural engagement** — roster of artists who "engage in the social and cultural issues of their time"
- **Multicultural representation** — deliberate focus on Africa, East Asia, Latin America, Middle East, alongside US artists of color

## Key People

### Jack Shainman (Founder/Director)
- Grew up in Williamstown, Massachusetts; began collecting as a young man at Williams College
- Co-founded gallery 1984 in Washington DC with Claude Simard
- Currently married to [[Carlos Vega]] (painter, also represented by the gallery) who manages logistical and creative aspects of the gallery
- Known for relentless artist loyalty — Nick Cave has been with the gallery since 2005

### Claude Simard (1956–2014, Co-founder)
- Shainman's then-partner and creative collaborator
- Died in 2014, same year The School opened — a bittersweet milestone
- His memory honored on the About page: "In loving memory of our dear friend and co-founder"

### Gloria Vega Martín (Architect)
- Shainman and Vega's niece, based in Málaga
- Oversaw renovation and preservation of the Clock Tower Building (46 Lafayette St)
- Notable: architect was a family member — speaks to the personal, intimate model of gallery building

## Locations & Venues

### 513 West 20th Street, Chelsea (flagship since 1997)
The original New York home. Pristine white walls, conventional commercial gallery format. Still active.

### 46 Lafayette Street, Tribeca — The Clock Tower Building
- **20,000 sq ft** in the historic 1898 Clock Tower Building (108 Leonard), an Italian Renaissance Revival former New York Life Insurance Company HQ
- Designed by Stephen Decatur Hatch, completed by McKim, Mead & White
- **Soft opened January 2024** with Richard Mosse's *Broken Spectre* (Amazon deforestation film)
- **Officially opened January 2025** with Nick Cave's inaugural solo show
- Acquired for reportedly **$20M**, ~$4M renovations; renovated by architect Gloria Vega Martín
- 29ft coffered ceilings, arched windows, marble columns — radically different aesthetic from Chelsea
- Plans: 4 major long-term exhibitions per year

### The School, 25 Broad Street, Kinderhook, NY
- **30,000 sq ft** converted school building upstate New York (Columbia County)
- Opened **2014** — same year Claude Simard died
- Purpose-built for large-scale, ambitious, multi-disciplinary projects that can't fit in Chelsea
- Open by appointment during installation
- Has hosted controversial public art: Nick Cave's 160-ft work *Truth Be Told* (First Amendment dispute with local zoning board — zoning board ruled unanimously it was protected speech)

## Ideas & Themes

### Social Justice Through Formal Excellence
The gallery's thesis: the best political art is made by the best formal artists. Kerry James Marshall's paintings are extraordinary technically — the politics emerge from that mastery, not despite it. The gallery doesn't compromise on craft.

### Diaspora & Identity
Heavy focus on Afrodiaspora (El Anatsui, Marshall, Hendricks, Parks, Ringgold), but also Iraqi diaspora (Kahraman), Cuban diaspora (Capote), Palestinian (Adi Nes), Pueblo Indigenous (Rose B. Simpson). The common thread: artists who make work about where they come from and what has been done to their people.

### Long-Form Gallerist-Artist Partnership as Ethics
In an era of art market flipping and short attention spans, Shainman is conspicuous for 20+ year relationships with artists. This is institutional ethics made visible: loyalty as practice.

### Expanding the Institution Into Nature/History
The School and Clock Tower moves are both about finding spaces with HISTORY and SCALE that match the ambition of the work. The School is in a building that educated generations of upstate NY children. The Clock Tower was built by one of the most powerful companies of the Gilded Age. Shainman consistently selects historically loaded architectures.

### Bioregional Art
Richard Mosse's Amazon work, environmental themes — the gallery is expanding beyond racial justice into ecological justice, particularly through artists using technology (thermal cameras, infrared film) to document environmental crisis.
