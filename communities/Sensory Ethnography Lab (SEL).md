# Sensory Ethnography Lab (SEL)

Harvard University lab founded by Lucien Castaing-Taylor in 2006. Produces experimental documentary films that challenge anthropocentric perception through immersive, multisensory approaches. Works often blur the boundary between cinema, anthropology, and art.

## Key Works
- *Sweetgrass* (2009) — sheep herding in Montana; animal-human-land
- *Leviathan* (2012) — fishing trawler, GoPro-based; nominated for multiple awards
- *Manakamana* (2013) — cable car, Nepal
- *Caniba* (2017) — Japanese cannibal; extreme perception
- *De Humani Corporis Fabrica* (2022) — inside the human body; hospitals; extraordinary

## Key People
- [[Lucien Castaing-Taylor]] — founder
- Véréna Paravel — co-director of *Leviathan*, *Caniba*, *De Humani Corporis Fabrica*

## Why Interesting
SEL's methodology — embodied, multisensory, beyond-human-perspective filmmaking — directly resonates with more-than-human design, somaesthetic research, and posthuman aesthetics. The lab proves that ethnographic rigor and radical aesthetics can coexist. A model for Amber's research approach: studying non-human worlds through careful attention.

Connected to [[European Graduate School (EGS)]] via Castaing-Taylor's teaching.
