# Power Station of Art (PSA) 上海当代艺术博物馆

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Stub created during Chinese Art-Tech Scene deep research.

China's first state-run museum dedicated to contemporary art. Shanghai's premier public contemporary art institution. Opened 2012 in a former power station (converted from Expo 2010 Pavilion of the Future, on the Huangpu River). Director: Li Xu. Deputy Director: Gong Yan. Home to the **Shanghai Biennale** since 2012.

## Background
- Converted power station building: industrial heritage aesthetic resonates with its position as a public institution in a formerly industrial waterfront
- Cost $64 million to convert, funded by Shanghai government
- On site of 2010 World Expo, left bank of Huangpu River
- China's first non-private contemporary art museum
- Opened with Centre Pompidou show: "Electric Fields, Surrealism and Beyond"

## Shanghai Biennale
Founded 1996 by Fang Zengxian (then Shanghai Art Museum director). Moved to PSA from 2012. China's most established biennale, with each edition having distinct curatorial framing. Key event for Chinese and international artists.

## Key Artists/Exhibitions
- Zhang Peili, Cao Fei, Gary Zhexi Zhang, Sougwen Chung, and many others have shown here
- Regular site for both Chinese and international contemporary art

## For Amber
PSA / Shanghai Biennale is the most significant public contemporary art venue in Shanghai. For any institutional engagement with the Chinese art establishment, PSA is a key target. The Shanghai Biennale open calls and curator networks are important contacts.

## Why Interesting
Only state-run contemporary art museum in mainland China. The fact that it's a former power station signals China's ambition to repurpose industrial heritage for cultural use, parallel to Tate Modern's Bankside power station transformation. The Shanghai Biennale is the most important biennale in China.
