The world's most famous interdisciplinary research sandbox. Founded 1985 by Nicholas Negroponte and Jerome Wiesner at MIT, the Media Lab operates at the intersection of technology, science, art, and design. It does not observe traditional disciplinary boundaries — instead it maps territory: sensing, computing, music, cities, robots, brains, materials. Post-Epstein, it has been rebuilding toward more explicit public-interest and ethical mandates.

## Mission & Objectives
**Vision:** "Invent a better and more just future — by, with, and for all."

**Mission:** "To create transformative technologies, experiences, and systems that enable people to reimagine and redesign their lives."

**Core model:** Over 90 corporate members (at $250K+/year each) fund research themes rather than specific projects. Members get IP access without royalties for 2 years after disclosure. Research groups are led by faculty who also advise MAS (Media Arts & Sciences) graduate students. Admission is <5% — highly competitive.

**Annual budget:** ~$75-80 million.
**Location:** MIT campus, Cambridge MA — Building E15 (Wiesner Building, I. M. Pei) + E14 (Fumihiko Maki, 2009 expansion).

## Key Research Groups (Relevant to Amber)

### [Tangible Media Group](https://tangible.media.mit.edu/) — Hiroshi Ishii
**The single most relevant group to HoloKit.** Pursues vision of **Tangible Bits & Radical Atoms** — giving physical form to digital information, seamlessly coupling the digital and physical worlds. Current new vision: **TeleAbsence** — connecting people across time (to one's younger self, lost loved ones, even the afterlife). This is deeply adjacent to Amber's machine death work.

Key concepts:
- *Tangible Bits* (1997): CHI '97 paper that founded TUI (Tangible User Interfaces) as a field. DOI: [10.1145/258549.258715](https://doi.org/10.1145/258549.258715)
- *Radical Atoms* (2012): hypothetical materials that change form and properties computationally — like pixels, but in atoms. Theme of [Ars Electronica 2016](https://ars.electronica.art/radicalatoms/en/theme/).
- *Shape Displays*: programmable matter, shape-changing UIs
- *TeleAbsence*: connecting across time, afterlife contact

Hiroshi Ishii — ACM Fellow (2022), SIGCHI Lifetime Research Award (2019). Previously CSCW research lead at NTT Japan (1988–1994). Educated at Hokkaido University.

### [Fluid Interfaces](https://www.media.mit.edu/groups/fluid-interfaces/overview/) — Pattie Maes
Research at the intersection of HCI and AI. Combines psychology, AI, HCI, and neuroscience to design systems for: motivation, attention, memory, learning, creativity, critical thinking, reasoning, communication, sleep, emotion regulation. Always-on devices (phones, wearables) reimagined not as information delivery but as **cognitive augmentation**.

Key themes: personalized context-aware systems, brain-computer interfaces, wearable sensors, machine learning for psychology "in the wild."

Pattie Maes — Germeshausen Professor of Media Arts and Sciences. Pioneered **Software Agents** in the 1990s (precursor to today's agent paradigms). Received ACM Lifetime Achievement Award in HCI (2025). Co-founder of Firefly Networks (→ Microsoft), Open Ratings (→ Dun & Bradstreet), [Tulip Co](https://tulip.co/). Worked with Marvin Minsky and Rodney Brooks at MIT AI Lab before joining Media Lab. Editor of 4 books, 500+ peer-reviewed articles. Belgian (PhD: Vrije Universiteit Brussel).

### [Personal Robots](https://www.media.mit.edu/groups/personal-robots/overview/) — Cynthia Breazeal
Social robots, virtual agents, and AR characters that interact using language, affective cues, and nonverbal behavior. Key question: "What is the new type of relationship between people and intelligent, human-friendly machines?" Emphasis on:
- Long-term human-robot interaction
- Emotional and social persuasion
- AI Literacy for children and adults
- Participatory co-design
- Health, happiness, creativity applications

Cynthia Breazeal — founder of the field of **social robotics**. Created Kismet (expressive robot face, 1990s). Author of [*Designing Sociable Robots*](https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262025102/) (MIT Press, 2002). Co-founder of [Jibo](https://www.jibo.com/) (social robot startup). Strong focus on ethical development, responsible AI.

**For Amber:** The framing of "emergence of a new type of relationship between people and intelligent machines" maps directly to Amber's agent ethology work. Breazeal's robots are studied ecologically — in the wild, over long time — which connects to Amber's methodology.

### [Opera of the Future](https://www.media.mit.edu/groups/opera-of-the-future/overview/) — Tod Machover
Technologies for music and human-nonhuman expression. Key projects:
- **[Hyperinstruments](https://www.media.mit.edu/projects/hyperinstruments/overview/)** — enhanced performance expressivity (Guitar Hero grew from here)
- **[Hyperscore](https://www.media.mit.edu/projects/hyperscore/overview/)** — musical creativity for young people via lines and colors
- **[Brain Opera](https://www.media.mit.edu/projects/brain-opera-vienna/overview/)** (1996) — audience-interactive
- **[Death and the Powers](https://www.media.mit.edu/projects/death-and-the-powers-redefining-opera/overview/)** (2010) — robotic opera, Pulitzer Prize finalist. A robot world inherits a human legacy. Themes: machine consciousness, death, succession.
- **VALIS** (1987; revised 2023) — AI-infused opera
- **[City Symphonies](https://www.media.mit.edu/projects/city-symphonies-massive-musical-collaboration/overview/)** — collective sonic creation for Seoul (2027) and LA (2028)
- **The Overstory** (2028) — opera based on Richard Powers' Pulitzer novel about humans and trees. **Directly more-than-human design.** Premieres Boston Lyric Opera early 2028.
- **BSO Commission** (2026) — Live AI interaction, "future of democracy." Premieres Symphony Hall October 2026.

Tod Machover — Muriel R. Cooper Professor of Music and Media. Also Faculty Director of MIT Media Lab (since ~2025). Officier de l'Ordre des Arts et Lettres (France). Composer-in-Residence Lucerne Festival 2015. Called "musical visionary" by NYT. Published "AI and Musical Discovery" (MIT Press, 2024) and "Composing the Future of Health" (Viking Random House, 2024).

**For Amber:** *Death and the Powers* and *The Overstory* are extraordinary connection points — machine death, non-human worlds, the ethics of what happens when machines die or when non-human beings have agency. Machover would be a fascinating conversation partner.

### [Responsive Environments](http://resenv.media.mit.edu) — Joseph Paradiso
Sensor substrates and networks that augment and mediate human experience. Research spans:
- Wearable systems, smart buildings, environmental sensing
- **Sensor representation in virtual and augmented reality** (directly relevant to HoloKit)
- Sensor-based inference, IoT foundations
- Energy harvesting, power management
- Space missions

### [Lifelong Kindergarten](https://www.media.mit.edu/groups/lifelong-kindergarten/overview/) — Mitchel Resnick
Innovative technologies and communities for creative learning. Created **[Scratch](https://scratch.mit.edu/)** (block-based programming language, millions of users globally; [DOI: 10.1145/1592761.1592779](https://doi.org/10.1145/1592761.1592779)). Focus on constructionist learning — learning by making, especially for marginalized youth.

### [City Science](https://www.media.mit.edu/groups/city-science/overview/) — Kent Larson
Urban architecture, mobility, and city simulation. Key sub-projects:
- *Changing Places* — responsive live/work spaces
- *[CityScope](https://www.media.mit.edu/projects/cityscope/overview/)* — urban modeling, simulation, prediction
- *Mobility on Demand* — sustainable personal electric vehicles
- *City Science Network* — global network of urban innovation labs

### [Synthetic Neurobiology](https://www.media.mit.edu/groups/synthetic-neurobiology/overview/) — Ed Boyden
Neuroscience tools for brain circuit mapping, neural dynamics recording/control, and therapeutic development. **Expansion microscopy** — making brains transparent and expandable for nanoscale imaging. Tools distributed freely to scientific community. Less directly relevant to Amber, but methodologically interesting for studying behavioral/neurological phenomena in living systems.

## Epstein Controversy & Lab Evolution

In August 2019, The New Yorker's Ronan Farrow revealed that Director Joi Ito and development director Peter Cohen had deliberately obscured $525,000 in donations from Jeffrey Epstein — despite Epstein being internally flagged as "Disqualified" as a donor. - $100K (2013) — Joi Ito's discretion
- $300K (2013–2014) — Joscha Bach research
- $225K — Seth Lloyd research
- **$125K — Neri Oxman research**
- Various other amounts

Additional: [Bill Gates donated $2.2M through Epstein](https://www.bostonglobe.com/2021/05/10/nation/melinda-gates-began-meeting-with-divorce-lawyers-2019-when-reports-bills-ties-jeffrey-epstein-surfaced-according-report/) (per Boston Globe).

MIT released full Goodwin Procter fact-finding report in January 2020: [factfindingjan2020.mit.edu](http://factfindingjan2020.mit.edu/files/MIT-report.pdf).

### Post-Epstein Evolution
The lab has undergone significant governance reform since 2019:
1. **New leadership pipeline** — From tech-entrepreneur director (Ito) → aerospace/NASA (Newman) → public-interest/FCC policy (Rosenworcel). Signals shift toward governance legitimacy and public accountability.
2. **Dual leadership model** — Splitting "public face" (Rosenworcel) and "academic/research" (Machover) roles acknowledges both outreach and intellectual rigor are needed.
3. **Neri Oxman departure** — The lab's most visible "more-than-human design" voice departed, leaving a gap Amber could potentially fill.
4. **"40 for 40" drive** — Anniversary focused on AI ethics, sustainability, creativity — not just technological output.
5. **Research reorientation** — More explicit emphasis on social impact, diversity/equity/inclusion, civic technology (Deb Roy's CCC, Rosenworcel's policy background).
6. **Saudi funding controversy** — MiSK (Mohammed bin Salman's foundation) was a member company until 2018. Combined with Epstein, the lab has had to rebuild its ethics reputation from scratch.

**Assessment:** The lab is in genuine transition. Still prestigious, still producing important work. But the ethos is shifting from "magical playground for elites" toward "responsible innovation for society." Amber's critical lens on technology aligns better with the post-2019 lab than the pre-2019 one.

### Why Interesting
MIT Media Lab is the most prestigious single institution in the world for the kind of work Amber does. The overlaps are stunning:

1. **HoloKit ↔ Tangible Media / Responsive Environments** — Hiroshi Ishii's Tangible Bits/Radical Atoms is the canonical theoretical framework for physical-digital interaction. 2. **Agent Ethology ↔ Personal Robots** — Breazeal's group studies robots as social agents in the wild over long-term encounters. 3. **Machine Death ↔ Opera of the Future** — Machover's *Death and the Powers* (robotic opera, Pulitzer finalist) explicitly explores machine death and the question of what happens when a human's essence is transferred to machines that then must outlive them.
