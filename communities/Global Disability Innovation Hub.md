# Global Disability Innovation Hub (GDI Hub)

World's first assistive technology (AT) research hub, co-located at UCL and Queen Elizabeth Olympic Park (East London). The only institution globally with WHO Collaborating Centre designation for Assistive Technology. Operational in 60+ countries, reaching 64 million people since 2016 through a portfolio of £50M+ in projects with 150+ partners. Dual structure: UCL-led Academic Research Centre (ARC) and a non-profit Community Interest Company (CIC). Born from the London 2012 Paralympic Legacy programme.

## Mission & Objectives
GDI Hub accelerates disability innovation for global justice — improving access to assistive technology for the 1 billion+ people who need it but don't have it (90% of those who need AT in LMICs lack access). Their six strategic goals (2025-2030):
1. **AI as Assistive Technology** — harness AI to expand AT access
2. **Gender & Disability** — intersectional inclusion
3. **Inclusive & Climate-Resilient Cities** — disability-inclusive urban planning
4. **Local Production** — manufacturing AT in LMICs
5. Global health equity and system strengthening
6. Policy mainstreaming (WHO, World Bank, UN CRPD frameworks)

Their approach: "Include, Inquire, Incubate, Invest, Integrate, Impart" — covering research, policy, ventures, and education.

## Key Contributions
- **WHO Collaborating Centre designation** — only institution globally with this for AT (2021). Enables formal WHO policy influence.
- **AT2030 programme** — £50M+ flagship, reached 64M people in 60+ countries, 150+ partners. The world's largest systematic effort to test what works for AT access in LMICs.
- **World's first MSc Disability, Design & Innovation** — cross-institutional (UCL + Loughborough University London + London College of Fashion).
- **Innovate Now** — Africa's first AT accelerator (21 ventures across 4 cycles).
- **Attvaran** — Asia's first AT accelerator (India, with IIT Delhi + IIT Madras).
- **Centre for Digital Language Inclusion (CDLI)** — open-source ASR for non-standard speech in African languages (Google.org funded); addresses the 2,000+ African languages with no speech recognition.
- **#WeThe15 Movement** — founding partner; global campaign for disability rights visibility.

## Why Interesting
Three reasons this institution matters for Amber's orbit:

1. **Policy-academic power model.** GDI Hub shows how a university research centre can acquire genuine WHO-level institutional authority. They didn't just publish — they became the official global knowledge node for AT. This is the model Amber should study for how to position research centers as infrastructure, not just publications.

2. **Non-Western HCI frontier.** The CDLI (African speech AI), Kenya video accessibility research, and LMICs focus make GDI Hub one of the few HCI-adjacent institutions doing serious empirical work outside WEIRD (Western, Educated, Industrialized, Rich, Democratic) contexts. This intersects with Amber's cosmotechnics interests and global research practice at Reality Design Lab.

3. **Embodied + AT convergence.** [[Nadia Bianchi-Berthouze]]'s affective computing + body movement work at UCL Interaction Centre — one of GDI Hub's academic cores — is directly adjacent to Amber's somaesthetic design work. The intersection of embodied HCI and disability is underexplored, and GDI Hub is the institutional home for it.
