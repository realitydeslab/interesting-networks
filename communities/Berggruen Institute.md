Think tank and philosophical institute founded in Los Angeles in 2010 by billionaire investor-philanthropist Nicolas Berggruen and journalist Nathan Gardels. Mission: "ideas for shaping human progress" — governance innovation, East-West philosophical dialogue, philosophical engagement with technological transformation. Endowment of $1 billion (2024). Operations in LA, Beijing (Peking University), and Venice (Casa dei Tre Oci).

**Key distinction from typical think tanks:** Berggruen Institute seriously invests in philosophy and culture as essential to governance and long-term human flourishing — not just as window dressing, but as its central program. The $1M Berggruen Prize is designed to be a "Nobel Prize for Philosophy."

---

## Mission & Objectives

Founded 2010 with the belief that good governance and long-term thinking require serious engagement with philosophy and culture. Three broad areas:

1. **Philosophy & Culture** — the Prize, fellowship program, Noema Magazine (née WorldPost), and Berggruen Press
2. **Governance Innovation** — 21st Century Council (ex-heads of state), Council on the Future of Europe, Think Long Committee for California
3. **East-West Dialogue** — Berggruen China Center at Peking University ($25.5M investment, 2018); fellowships bridging Chinese and Western thought

**New direction (2020s):** Growing focus on AI, computation, and technology governance. The [[Antikythera]] program (directed by [[Benjamin Bratton]]) is the most intellectually ambitious example.

## Key Programs & Centers

### Philosophy and Culture Center (est. 2015)
- **Berggruen Prize for Philosophy & Culture** — annual $1M prize. Independent jury. Awarded at New York Public Library in October. Designed as "Nobel Prize for Philosophy."
- **Fellowship Program** — collaborations with USC, Harvard, Stanford, Oxford, and Peking University. Fellows work on philosophy-governance intersections.
- **Berggruen Press** — publishing arm. Publishes books including Antikythera series, Bratton's work, philosophy-adjacent titles.
- **Noema Magazine** — online/print magazine covering technology, philosophy, governance, geopolitics, culture. Originally founded as The WorldPost (2014, joint with HuffPost; 2017, with Washington Post). Renamed Noema 2020. Editor-in-chief: Nathan Gardels. Topics: Technology & the Human, Future of Capitalism, Philosophy & Culture, Climate Crisis, Geopolitics & Deglobalization, Future of Democracy, Digital Society. Free, open access. URL: [noemamag.com](https://www.noemamag.com/).
- **Studio B** — production division. Directed by Alex Gardels and Nathalia Ramos. Produces salon series (Eric Schmidt + Ashton Kutcher on AI; Yuval Noah Harari + Joseph Gordon-Levitt). Podcast: *Futurology*. Works with Academy Museum of Motion Pictures on cinema programming.

### [[Antikythera]] Program (est. 2022)
Berggruen's most intellectually ambitious program. R&D institute for Planetary Computation and the Evolution of Intelligence. Directed by [[Benjamin Bratton]]. Five research areas: Planetary Computation, Synthetic Intelligence, Recursive Simulations, Hemispherical Stacks, Planetary Sapience. Produces: annual research studios, Antikythera Journal (MIT Press), book series (MIT Press), Venice Biennale exhibitions, salons, public lectures. Partners: Google DeepMind, Cambridge CSER. → **See [[Antikythera]] for full detail.**

### Berggruen China Center (est. 2018)
Interdisciplinary academic research institute at Peking University, founded with $25.5 million from Berggruen Institute. Runs fellowship program; hosts symposia and conferences bridging Chinese and Western scholarship. URL: part of berggruen.org structure.

### Governance Programs
- **21st Century Council** (est. 2011) — former heads of state, global business leaders, intellectuals. Past members: Gordon Brown (UK PM), Ernesto Zedillo (Mexican President), Fernando Henrique Cardoso (Brazilian President). Met with Xi Jinping and Chinese officials.
- **Council on the Future of Europe** — policy + public debate on European integration. The council's 2013 proposal eventually became part of EU's Investment Plan for Europe (€315 billion).
- **Think Long Committee for California** — bipartisan state-level governance reform. Published *A Blueprint to Renew California* (2011). Supported multiple California ballot initiatives.

### Physical Infrastructure
- **Los Angeles HQ** — primary office
- **Scholars' Campus / Monteverdi** (planned) — 447-acre residential research headquarters in Santa Monica Mountains (near Getty Center). Acquired land for $45M in 2014. Designed by [Herzog & de Meuron](https://www.herzogdemeuron.com/), with Gensler as executive architect. Ambitious vision of a philosopher-kings retreat.
- **Casa dei Tre Oci, Venice** — neo-Gothic building (1913) on Giudecca island, acquired 2021. European headquarters and cultural center.
- **Beijing Center** — at Peking University campus.

## Why Interesting

Berggruen is unusual in the think tank landscape: it takes philosophy seriously as the driver of governance, not as decoration. The $1B endowment means it has genuine staying power. The combination of Yuk Hui as Prize jury chair, Antikythera as the in-house philosophy-of-computation program, and Noema Magazine as publishing platform creates a rare ecosystem where cosmotechnics, East-West dialogue, planetary computation, and governance thinking all coexist.

For Amber specifically: this is the institutional context in which [[Yuk Hui]]'s work has most prestige, and in which [[Benjamin Bratton]]'s speculative methodology gets funded. Two of Amber's most important intellectual reference points operate within this ecosystem.

The Berggruen Prize winner list also functions as a philosophical reading list: thinkers of justice, identity, ethics, care — not pure technologists. This humanist center of gravity is what distinguishes Berggruen from pure AI governance institutes.
