# Hanart Forum

Hong Kong-based discursive platform for contemporary thought and artistic practices. Founded 2022. Situated in a restored 1920s Edwardian-colonial row house in Happy Valley, Hong Kong. The institutional home of **Hanart Press** and co-organizer of the landmark *Cybernetics for the 21st Century* lecture series with [[Yuk Hui]] and the [[Research Network for Philosophy and Technology]]. One of the most concentrated nodes connecting Chinese and global philosophy-of-technology discourse in the Asia-Pacific region.

## Mission & Objectives

Hanart Forum was established in 2022 as a **discursive platform for contemporary thought** at the intersection of philosophy and art. It also serves as a site for **diverse artistic practices**, with programming that explicitly links Chinese and global intellectual traditions. The physical location — a heritage Edwardian-colonial building from 1920 in Happy Valley, Hong Kong — functions as both a cultural statement and a practical venue for in-person gatherings.

The Forum's programming spans:
- Lecture and seminar series (online and in-person)
- Publishing (via **Hanart Press**)
- Video dissemination (YouTube and Bilibili)
- International research collaborations

The name "漢雅" (Hanart) plays on the Chinese character 漢 (Han, Chinese) and 雅 (ya, elegant/refined), signaling a deliberate positioning at the junction of Chinese intellectual heritage and Western contemporary philosophy.

## Key Contributions

1. **Institutionalizing cosmotechnics discourse in Hong Kong** — provides a local anchor for Yuk Hui's global network in the Asia-Pacific region
2. **Multilingual dissemination** — YouTube + Bilibili strategy reaches both Western and Chinese-speaking audiences simultaneously
3. **Open-access philosophy publishing** — Hanart Press distributes *Cybernetics for the 21st Century* as a free PDF, democratizing access to high-level philosophy-of-technology discourse
4. **Cross-civilizational dialogue platform** — explicitly bridges Chinese and European philosophical traditions, unlike most Western philosophy-of-technology venues

## Why Interesting

Hanart Forum is a **rare institutional artifact**: a small, independent, intellectually serious platform that has managed to convene figures like Katherine Hayles, Luciana Parisi, Bruce Sterling, Wendy Chun, and Mark Coeckelbergh under a single series — all orbiting the cosmotechnics / cybernetics / philosophy-of-technology axis that is central to Amber's research. Its strategic positioning between Chinese and European philosophical traditions (Hanart Press, Bilibili + YouTube, partnerships spanning Rotterdam–Hong Kong–Tokyo–Rio) makes it a significant node in Amber's extended intellectual network. The open-access publishing commitment and free online events lower the barrier to engagement significantly.

The Forum also represents an interesting institutional *model*: a philosophy salon / press / event space hybrid, occupying a heritage building — part Ars Industrialis, part residency program, part micro-publisher. Worth studying as a model for what a research-adjacent cultural institution can look like.
