MIT's flagship interdisciplinary center bridging art, science, and technology. Joint initiative of the Office of the Provost, School of Architecture & Planning (SA+P), and School of Humanities, Arts & Social Sciences (SHASS). Founded to create "new opportunities for art, science, and technology to thrive as interrelated, mutually-informing modes of exploration, knowledge, and discovery."

## Mission & Objectives

CAST's core mission is to foster cross-disciplinary collaboration between artists and MIT scientists/engineers at the **research and development phase** — not just exhibition or illustration. Artists are embedded in cutting-edge MIT labs where scientists are "open to artists' speculative and hands-on way of working."

Four pillars:
1. **Visiting Artists** — embed internationally acclaimed artists in MIT research for extended residencies
2. **Faculty Grants** — fund cross-disciplinary creative-research projects by MIT faculty
3. **Cross-Disciplinary Classes** — integrate arts into MIT's core curriculum
4. **Public Output** — symposia, performances (MIT Sounding, MIT Performing), publications, exhibitions

CAST explicitly prioritizes proposals focused on **racial and social justice** and amplifying **BIPOC artistic voices**. Applications: [castgrants@mit.edu](mailto:castgrants@mit.edu)

## Key Contributions

1. **The Ida Ely Rubin Artist in Residence programme** — One of the few artist residency programmes globally that explicitly places artists *inside* functioning research labs. Artists work with specific faculty/researchers for extended periods, co-creating projects that genuinely advance both art and science. Model: artist as research collaborator, not artist-in-lab-for-inspiration.

2. **The Dasha Zhukova Distinguished Visiting Artist position** — 2-year sustained residency (launched Fall 2016) allowing deep, long-form research development. First sustained multi-year art-science residency of this type at a major research university.

3. **CAST-MIT Press book pipeline** — Symposia reliably become MIT Press books (*Active Matter*, *Being Material*). Creates a model where live art-science encounter becomes canonical academic text.

4. **Cross-disciplinary class funding** — $30k-$50k grants for MIT faculty to develop new credit-bearing courses integrating arts. Funds guest artists, materials, development. Creates permanent art-science curriculum rather than one-off events.

5. **Unfolding Intelligence (2021)** — One of the earliest significant institutional art-AI symposia at a major research university. Featured generative art, Traditional Ecological Knowledge + AI, AI bias, algorithmic oppression. Now a landmark reference.

6. **BIPOC-centered programming** — Explicit CAST policy to prioritize racial/social justice proposals and BIPOC artist voices. More visible in recent (2019-2025) programming.

## Events & Programmes

### Funding Available (for MIT-affiliated collaborators)
- **Cross-Disciplinary Class Development Fund:** $30k–$50k. For MIT faculty/lecturers to develop new credit-bearing courses integrating arts.
- **Faculty Grant:** up to $30k. For MIT full-time faculty to develop creative/research projects.
- **Fay Chandler Creativity Grant:** up to $7,500. For instructional staff, for projects presented publicly within a year.
- **Global Exhibition and Performance Fund:** up to $25k. For MIT faculty with work appearing at a major global venue.
- **Visiting Artist Grant:** $5k–$40k. For faculty or program directors collaborating with visiting artists. Must include student engagement and public presentation.
- **Artists in the Classroom micro-grants:** $250-300/visit. For inviting practicing artists as guest speakers.

*Note: CAST does **not** accept self-nominations by artists for the visiting artist residency. Must be proposed by MIT faculty/program directors.*

## Why Interesting

MIT CAST is the most well-resourced, most institutionally embedded, and arguably most intellectually serious art-science programme in the world — operating at the intersection of STEM's best research and globally acclaimed artistic practice. What distinguishes it from peer institutions:

1. **Lab-embedded residencies** — Not a studio in an art building but actual placement in active research labs (necstlab, CSAIL, biology, aeronautics). The science collaboration is the artwork.

2. **MIT Press publication pipeline** — Symposia reliably produce canonical texts. *Active Matter* and *Being Material* are now standard references in programmable materials / biodesign discourse.

3. **Unfolding Intelligence precedent** — CAST already has a major AI+art symposium in its history, directly relevant to Amber's machine behavior / agent ethology work. The 2021 symposium is a model and reference point.

4. **The BIPOC-centered turn** — Post-2020 CAST explicitly centers racial justice and BIPOC voices in its grant criteria. This makes it more than just a technical/aesthetic program; it's engaged with the social and political dimensions of technology in ways that align with Amber's critical perspective.

5. **Global reach** — Artists like Tomás Saraceno, Anicka Yi, Es Devlin, Trevor Paglen have all passed through CAST. Being associated with CAST places an artist in a particular international circuit.

For Amber specifically: CAST is the prototype for what a "critical spatial computing + embodied interaction" programme could look like at Oxford. Studying CAST's model — how it structures grants, residencies, publications, symposia — is a lesson in institution-building for art-science at research universities.
