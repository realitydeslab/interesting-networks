# ZKM | Hertzlab

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Initial deep research. Comprehensive note written with full history, research themes, infrastructure, programmes, key people, and Amber-specific opportunities. The Hertzlab is ZKM's operational research heart — the place where residencies happen, music technology is built, and interdisciplinary production actually occurs. Distinct from the museum/exhibition arm. Primary entry point for practice-based artists.

---

The research and production platform of [[ZKM Center for Art and Media Karlsruhe]]. Established **2017**, merging the former **Institute for Visual Media** (founded 1992) and **Institute for Music and Acoustics** (IMA, founded 1989 as ZKM's first research unit). Named for [Heinrich Hertz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heinrich_Hertz) (1857–1894), the physicist who proved electromagnetic waves exist through famous spark experiments at Karlsruhe Technical University (now KIT) in 1886 — founder of radio technology, wireless transmission of information.

A transdisciplinary lab operating at the interface of media arts, science, and society. Studios, ateliers, hackspaces. Regular **Open Hertzlab** public sessions. The lab where ~600 guest artists since 1989 have actually worked — making it not just a curatorial front but an active production site.

---

## Mission & Objectives

The Hertzlab frames its work around big social questions asked through artistic means: *"What will our world look like 100 years from now? How do we design human relationships when the chatbot does the flirting for us? Do robots have a right to work-life balance? Do we need a Turing Test for humans?"*

Three core functions:
1. **Artistic-technological research** — tinkering, programming, building, learning together
2. **Curation and supervision of global residency program** — proposal-based guest artist programme (600+ artists since 1989)
3. **Concerts, showcases, performances** — presenting artistic research produced at the lab and elsewhere

**Not a museum.** The Hertzlab is the production engine that makes ZKM more than a gallery — it's why ZKM is a "centre" (center + producer), not just an exhibition venue.

---

## Six Research Themes

All Hertzlab projects orient around these six frameworks:

1. **Human-machine relations** — AI, chatbots, automation, robotics, the agency of artificial systems
2. **Ecology / environment** — climate, natural systems, nonhuman agency, planetary computation
3. **Future narratives** — non-European, non-US storytelling traditions; decolonial futures; global media art
4. **Embodied interaction** — body, movement, senses, somatic computing, kinesthesia
5. **Ethics of technology** — power, responsibility, rights of machines and humans, surveillance, access
6. **Media archaeology and digital preservation** — conserving ephemeral digital works, maintaining cultural memory

These themes are not departments — they're organising questions that allow a single residency to sit at multiple intersections.

**[Alistair Hudson](https://zkm.de/en/persons/alistair-hudson)** — Scientific-Artistic Chairman, ZKM, since April 2023. Oversees Hertzlab as part of ZKM's research arm. Previous: Manchester Art Gallery and The Whitworth (2018–2022), Grizedale Arts, MIMA. "Useful museum" philosophy. Co-heads [Asociación de Arte Útil](https://www.arte-util.org/) with Tania Bruguera. See: [[Alistair Hudson]] stub.

**[Margit Rosen](https://zkm.de/en/persons/margit-rosen)** — Head of Collections, Archives & Research since 2016. Key person for research-based residencies and academic collaborations. Teaches MediaArtHistories Master's (Danube University Krems), HfG Karlsruhe, CAFA Beijing.

**[Peter Weibel](https://zkm.de/en/persons/peter-weibel)** (1944–2023) — ZKM Director 1999–2023. Under Weibel, the Hertzlab's predecessor institutes became theoretically ambitious; he brought Bruno Latour, Siegfried Zielinski, and international media theorists to Karlsruhe. The theoretical depth of Hertzlab residencies owes much to Weibel's vision of art as cognitive act. See: [[Peter Weibel]] stub.

**Heinrich Klotz** (1935–1999) — ZKM Founding Director 1989–1999. Architecture historian. Founded the Institute for Music and Acoustics (1989) as ZKM's first operational unit — the seed from which Hertzlab grew. Coined: *"The ZKM is a center and not a museum"* — the Hertzlab embodies this.

**The Hertzlab technical team** — software engineers, acousticians, media technologists who support guest artists. Unnamed but essential: they build the tools (Zirkonium spatial sound software, etc.) that artists use.

## Infrastructure: What's Actually There

The Hertzlab has purpose-built research/production infrastructure — unusual in the media art world:

### Sound / Music Studios
- **The Sound Dome** (Klangdom) — 43 Meyersound loudspeakers suspended 3-dimensionally in a cube; elliptical ring system + 4 floor speakers; 40-channel polyphonic spatial audio; world-class facility for electroacoustic composition and acoustic research. Public concert hall AND research lab.
- **The Cube** — The visual landmark of ZKM; free-standing acoustic recording studio with rubber-buffered steel girders, double walls, high-isolation doors; excellent room acoustics. Heart of musical-artistic production.
- **5 Music studios with octaphonic loudspeaker systems** — for composition and spatial audio work
- **2 Recording studios** — professional-grade
- **Studio modelled on the Klangdom** — dome of 23 loudspeakers; research and rehearsal space

### Visual / Interactive Media
- **Panorama Lab** — immersive image and spatial sound presentation; one of the extraordinary presentation venues ZKM developed for spatial phenomena
- **4 Media technology studios** — equipped for sensor technology, image recognition, VR, AR, AI
- **Rapid Prototyping Lab** — making, building, hardware
- **Hackspaces and ateliers** — informal open-lab spaces

### Software Tools (Open Source)
- **[Zirkonium](https://zkm.de/en/software-development)** — spatial sound control software for composing music in 2D/3D loudspeaker systems; intuitive graphical interface for spatial movement of sounds; free macOS download since v3.4 (2019); used to control the Sound Dome
- **ZirkPad** — iPad app for remote control of Zirkonium; free download
- Further software developed in cooperation with guest artists (custom tools for specific works)

**Note:** This infrastructure is accessible to guest artists — not just ZKM employees. Having access to the Sound Dome + Klangdom for electroacoustic work is genuinely rare internationally.

## Key Publications & Output

The Hertzlab doesn't have its own journal, but its research feeds into ZKM's broader publication programme (570+ publications since 1989):

- **[*BioMedia: The Age of Media with Life-like Behavior*](https://zkm.de/en/publications/biomedia)** (2023) — the defining theoretical statement emerging from Hertzlab-adjacent research; Weibel coined the "BioMedia" concept from Hertzlab's work with generative/adaptive art
- **[*Christa Sommerer & Laurent Mignonneau: The Artwork as a Living System*](https://zkm.de/en/publications/christa-sommerer-laurent-mignonneau)** (2022) — 30 years of alife art, much of it produced with Hertzlab support
- Giga-Hertz Award catalogues (annual since 2007) — documenting electroacoustic music productions
- **Media Art Net** ([medienkunstnetz.de](https://www.medienkunstnetz.de/)) — digital publication documenting media art history; uses worldwide for study; developed with Hertzlab involvement

## Key Contributions to Media Art

The Hertzlab and its predecessor institutes (IMA + IVM) have contributed:

1. **Spatial audio as art medium** — the Sound Dome and Klangdom made possible compositions that couldn't exist without the space; Hertzlab has hosted hundreds of world premieres of electroacoustic works
2. **Software tools for artists** — Zirkonium is used globally; the Hertzlab software development tradition bridges art and engineering tool-making
3. **Giga-Hertz Award** — since 2007, one of the main annual prizes in electronic/electroacoustic music; shaped careers and commissioned works (16 editions by 2023)
4. **Production residencies** — 600+ guest artists since 1989; works produced here enter permanent exhibition or tour internationally
5. **ZKM/IRCAM partnership** — linking two of the world's top music-technology research institutions
6. **Media archaeology tools** — Laboratory for Antiquated Video Systems (shared with ZKM collections) preserves technology-based works that would otherwise be unplayable
7. **Open Hertzlab events** — monthly public access to research in progress; model for transparent research institution

- Running since 1989 (through predecessor institutes)
- 600+ guest artists to date; international
- Support: technical, organizational, financial
- Covers all genres: electronic music, visual media, software art, interactive installation, performance, film, games, scientific research on electronic arts
- No fixed annual call — proposals submitted to info@zkm.de; also periodic open calls on the website
- Duration: variable (days to months); terms negotiated per project
- Access: Hertzlab studios, Sound Dome, Cube, Panorama Lab, software tools
- **This is the main entry point for Amber**

**[Giga-Hertz Award & Production Residency](https://zkm.de/en/projects/giga-hertz-award)**
- Joint with [SWR Experimentalstudio Freiburg](https://www.swr.de/swrkultur/musik-und-theater/swr-experimentalstudio/)
- Annual since 2007; 16th edition 2023
- **Giga-Hertz Grand Prize**: €10,000 lifetime achievement (jury-nominated, no open call)
- **2× Giga-Hertz Production Awards**: €5,000 each — **OPEN CALL** — for sound artists and composers of electronic, electroacoustic, or mixed music; winners do a production residency at ZKM Hertzlab or SWR Experimental Studio
- **Giga-Hertz PopExperimental Advancement Award**: €5,000 — since 2022 — for experimental electronic pop and club music (with Popakademie Baden-Württemberg)
- **Total prize fund: €25,000**
- **Application process:** 2 steps — (1) submit reference work ≤5 years old, ≤20 min, clearly electronic means; (2) project exposé for new work to be realized as world premiere at following year's ceremony
- Residency location: ZKM Hertzlab (Karlsruhe) or SWR Experimental Studio (Freiburg) — decided by jury
- URL: <https://zkm.de/en/projects/giga-hertz-award>

**[Joint ZKM | IRCAM Residency](https://zkm.de/en/open-calls)**
- Partnership with [IRCAM](https://www.ircam.fr/) (Paris) — world-leading acoustic/music research institute; directed by Frank Madlener
- Project-related; varies in duration and theme; focus on acoustics/sound + visual media + software development
- Check [open-calls page](https://zkm.de/en/open-calls) for current availability

**[Artists at Risk Residency](https://artistsatrisk.org/)**
- For artists threatened by war/state repression; not open call
- Partners: Artists at Risk initiative, Goethe-Institut, German Foreign Office, Martin Roth Initiative

## For Amber

### Why Interesting
Hertzlab is **the operational research heart** of Europe's most important media art institution. Not the museum/gallery face of ZKM, but the actual lab where work gets made. For Amber's practice — computational creativity, embodied interaction, AI+art, posthuman questions — the six Hertzlab themes are a direct fit. The lab's combination of rare infrastructure (Sound Dome, Panorama Lab, Rapid Prototyping) + residency support + international network makes it uniquely valuable.

**The Weibel legacy:** Peter Weibel's 24-year directorship established ZKM/Hertzlab as a place that takes ideas seriously — not just "cool art made with computers" but art as epistemological research, as cognitive act. That intellectual seriousness is still embedded in the institution, even as Hudson's "useful museum" pivot adds new dimensions.

**BioMedia alignment:** The Hertzlab's work on generative systems, adaptive robots, and artificial life — culminating in the *BioMedia* thesis — is directly connected to Amber's alife and machine behavior interests. **Open entry points:** The Open Hertzlab monthly events are free and public — lowest-friction entry point to meet the team. The guest artist programme is proposal-based (not competition), making it more accessible than many prestigious residencies.

## Recent Events That Matter

**2017** — Hertzlab formally constituted by merging Institute for Visual Media and Institute for Music and Acoustics; the naming honors Heinrich Hertz and marks a new phase of ZKM's research identity.

**2022** — *Christa Sommerer & Laurent Mignonneau: The Artwork as a Living System* retrospective at ZKM — 30 years of alife art, much produced with Hertzlab. Defining show for the Hertzlab's alife-art tradition.

**2022** — **Giga-Hertz PopExperimental Advancement Award** added as new category — signals opening of Hertzlab's music prize toward experimental pop/club/electronic music beyond academic electroacoustics. Broader reach.

**2023 (March)** — **Peter Weibel dies** (aged 78). 24-year era ends. Weibel's theoretical legacy (BioMedia, Latour collaborations, media theory) is embedded in Hertzlab DNA but the institution is now redefining itself.

**2023 (April)** — **Alistair Hudson** takes over as Scientific-Artistic Chairman. "Useful museum" philosophy. Hertzlab will need to navigate between Weibel's theory-heavy legacy and Hudson's social transformation agenda. The 2024–2026 *Fellow Travellers* programme is the flagship — watch how it changes Hertzlab's residency culture.

**2023** — *BioMedia* exhibition — 60+ artists, generative systems, adaptive robots, AI art — the most ambitious ZKM show yet on art exhibiting life-like behavior. Major theoretical moment for what Hertzlab represents.

**2025** — *Electronic Petting Zoo* installed in ZKM foyer (Nov 2025 – Aug 2026): 10 interactive robot guinea pigs responding to touch and voice. Hertzlab human-machine-relations theme made tangible and accessible.

**2026 (March 25)** — *Connection Machine CM-2* + conference "Envisioning AI: Legacy and Impact of the Connection Machine" — AI prehistory exhibition in the foyer; convening AI historians, artists, researchers. **Amber should attend this.**

**2026 (July 4)** — *Silver Egg: The Eros of Media Art* opens — 30 international positions on eros/desire/media/ecology; robotics, kinetics, sound, light, touch. Posthuman + embodied + erotic mode. **The most conceptually aligned upcoming ZKM exhibition to Amber's interests.**

## Why Interesting

**ZKM/Hertzlab is where European media art theory and practice converge.** Not just a museum with nice exhibitions but a *production machine* — the lab where works are made, where artists spend months building, where software tools are developed that other artists worldwide use.

**The lineage matters:** Hertzlab descends from the Institute for Music and Acoustics (1989), which is where ZKM first operationalized Klotz's "digital Bauhaus" vision. The Sound Dome, Cube, and spatial audio tradition represent 35+ years of accumulated tool-building for artists — you can't replicate this infrastructure overnight.

**Theoretical ambition** (Weibel legacy) + **social purpose** (Hudson's useful museum) + **technical depth** (Sound Dome, software tools) = unusual combination. Most institutions are one of these things; ZKM/Hertzlab tries to be all three simultaneously.

**For Amber specifically:** The six research themes (human-machine, ecology, future narratives, embodied, ethics, media archaeology) are essentially a taxonomy of Amber's interests. The Hertzlab is one of the few institutional spaces where computational creativity + posthuman thinking + critical computing + embodied interaction are all legible and welcome simultaneously.

**The BioMedia thesis** — that 21st century media will increasingly exhibit life-like behavior — is the most useful frame ZKM has generated for Amber's alife + machine behavior interests. Being in institutional dialogue with ZKM/Hertzlab means being in dialogue with the institution that named this moment.

**Non-UK, non-US:** ZKM/Hertzlab represents a distinctly European, distinctly German tradition — rigorous, theoretically serious, state-funded (Baden-Württemberg + City of Karlsruhe), long-term. It doesn't run on venture capital or philanthropic fashion cycles. That institutional durability is increasingly rare and valuable.
