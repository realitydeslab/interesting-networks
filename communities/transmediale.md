Annual festival for art and digital culture in Berlin. Europe's most theoretically rigorous critical media art platform. More politically engaged and intellectually serious than [[Ars Electronica]]. Where critical theory meets digital culture.

## Mission & Objectives

transmediale exists to cultivate artistic and critical dialogue with digital technology and its social, political, and cultural implications. Started as a video art festival excluded from mainstream film circuits; evolved into the leading international platform for critical media art and theory. Explicitly engages with technology as social and political force — not just aesthetic medium.

**Core identity:** "festival for art and digital culture" (since 2006). The word choice matters — not "media art festival," not "tech festival." Art AND culture, digital as adjective to both.

**Organizational form:** transmediale e.V. (registered association), independent since 2020. Previously hosted by Kulturprojekte Berlin. Address: Gerichtstr. 35, 13347 Berlin. Funded as "cultural institution of excellence" by [Kulturstiftung des Bundes](https://www.kulturstiftung-des-bundes.de/en/) (German Federal Cultural Foundation) since 2004.

## Full History (1988–Present)

### Origins: VideoFilmFest / VideoFest (1988–1997)
- **1988:** Founded as **VideoFilmFest** at [Berlinale](https://www.berlinale.de/) by **Hartmut Horst** and video artist **Micky Kwella**
  - Created to platform electronic media art excluded from classical film festivals
  - Venues: MedienOperative and Deutsche Akademie der Künste (East Berlin) until 1992
- **1989:** Repeated at Berlinale as **VideoFest**
- **1993:** Moved to Podewil (also transmediale offices from 1997–2020)
- **1997:** Renamed **transmedia** — reflecting expansion beyond video into multimedia, internet, software art
- **1998:** Renamed **transmediale** — final name

### The Club transmediale / CTM Spinoff (1999)
- **1999:** **club transmediale** founded as a side event (nightclub/electronic music programming)
- club transmediale became independent as **CTM Festival** in 2012 (though operated independently from ~2003 onward)
- In 2001, transmediale faced severe funding crisis; CTM had to skip that year — underlined the precarious festival landscape

### Broeckmann Era: Institutional Expansion (2001–2006)
- **2001:** Art historian and curator **Andreas Broeckmann** became artistic director
  - Added [[Haus der Kulturen der Welt]] (HKW) as main venue → increased attendance and international audience
  - Introduced the **transmediale Award** for visionary works centering technology-led societies
- **2002:** Introduced extensive media art exhibition format
- **2004:** Kulturstiftung des Bundes began funding transmediale (landmark institutional recognition)
- **2006:** Shifted subheading from "international media art festival" → **"festival for art and digital culture"**
  - Marked expansion from narrow media art focus to art, technology, and everyday life critique

### Kovats Era: Theoretical Deepening (2007–2010)
- **2007:** **Stephen Kovats** (media and arts researcher) became artistic director
- **2008:** Introduced **Vilém Flusser Theory Award** — named after [Vilém Flusser](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vil%C3%A9m_Flusser) (Czech-Brazilian media philosopher, 1920–1991)
  - For "outstanding, media-theoretical and research-based artworks"
  - Flusser connection: UdK Berlin holds the Vilém Flusser Archive; partnership formalizes the theory-practice bridge
- **2008:** Returned to Haus der Kulturen der Welt after stint at Akademie der Künste (2006–2007)

### Gansing Era: Curatorial Coherency & Residency Model (2011–2020)
- **April 2011:** Curator and media researcher **Kristoffer Gansing** took over
- **2012:** Replaced existing awards with **residency program for artistic research** — shift from prize logic to sustained practice
  - Gansing: "When I first came on board... my idea was to bring a curatorial coherency to what I saw as a festival dissipating into a kind of 'creative crowd' without much focus, so I brought in more exhibitions"
- **2011:** Established **transmediale/resource** as framework for year-round activities
  - Vilém Flusser Residency Program for Artistic Research
  - Online publication format **transmediale/journal**
  - Pre-festival program **Vorspiel** (in collaboration with CTM and Berlin gallery network)
- **2015:** Conference program curated by **Daphne Dragona** (through 2019)
- **2020:** Office moved to [silent green Kulturquartier](https://www.silent-green.net/); festival opened own project space 'transmediale studio'
- **2020:** transmediale e.V. took over independent organization from Kulturprojekte Berlin

### Ó Murchú Era: Expanded Diversity (2021–2024)
- **2021:** **Dr Nóra Ó Murchú** became artistic director
  - Widened programme with notably more diverse curatorial teams in terms of scope, content, and backgrounds
  - **2021/22 team:** Ben Evans James, Elise Misao Hunchuck, Lorena Juan, Dani Admiss
  - **2022/23 team:** Ben Evans James, Elise Misao Hunchuck, Lorena Juan, Bani Brusadin
  - **2023/24 team:** Nadim Choufi, Elise Misao Hunchuck, Lorena Juan, Yasemin Keskintepe
- **2024:** Nóra Ó Murchú departed (announced July 2024)

### Current Phase: Curatorial Guest Model (2025–)
- **2025:** Elise Misao Hunchuck and Ben Evans James invited as co-curators
- **2026:** Neema Githere and Juan Pablo García Sossa as curators — open call selected, Global South perspectives prominent
- **2027:** Barbara Cueto announced as curator (Feb 2026) — selected via open call for 40th anniversary edition

## Events & Programmes

### Annual Festival
5-day event (January/February). Format:
- **Discourse programme** — keynotes, panels, workshops (theoretical/political)
- **Exhibitions** — media art, often multi-venue and distributed
- **Film & Video programme** — experimental video, artist films
- **Performances** — live works, often evening programme in collaboration with CTM

### Vorspiel ("Prelude")
Pre-festival distributed programme across Berlin galleries, project spaces, independent venues. Joint initiative with [[CTM Festival]]. Artists and spaces propose events — community-driven. Open call (typically September). This is the lowest-barrier entry point for local/European artists.

### Residency Programs (Year-Round)
Three concurrent residency streams, typically applications open July–August:

1. **[Vilém Flusser Residency for Artistic Research](https://transmediale.de/en/news/vilem-flusser-residency-for-artistic-research-2026)**
   - In cooperation with [Universität der Künste Berlin (UdK)](https://www.udk-berlin.de/)
   - For artists pursuing research-based practice; access to Flusser Archive
   - Application deadline: typically Aug–Oct

2. **[transmediale × ARKO Residency](https://transmediale.de/en/news/transmediale-x-arko-residency-2026)**
   - In cooperation with [Arts Council Korea (ARKO)](https://www.arko.or.kr/eng/)
   - For Korean artists/researchers or international artists engaging with Korean context
   - Application deadline: typically Aug–Oct

3. **[Digital Art Residency](https://transmediale.de/en/news/digital-art-residency-2026)**
   - In cooperation with [Pro Helvetia](https://prohelvetia.ch/en/) (Swiss Arts Council)
   - For Swiss artists or artists engaging with Swiss digital art context
   - Application deadline: typically Aug–Oct

4. **transmediale × Chile Residency** (appears in 2025 call)
   - For Latin American connections

Residents are announced in October, active spring–summer, present in open studio event (June), show work at following year's festival.

### DARC Research Workshop
Annual academic workshop in partnership with [Digital Aesthetics Research Center (DARC)](https://cc.au.dk/en/research/centers/digital-aesthetics-research-centre/), Aarhus University. Produces published research output.

### Marshall McLuhan Lecture
Annual prestigious public lecture, registration required. Partnership event within festival.

## Relationship to CTM Festival

[[CTM Festival]] was born inside transmediale in 1999 as "club transmediale" — the nighttime/electronic music programming. Key dynamics:

- **1999:** club transmediale founded as side event of transmediale
- **2001:** Both events suffered from transmediale's funding crisis (CTM skipped a year)
- **~2003:** CTM began operating more independently under Oliver Baurhenn's leadership
- **2012:** club transmediale formally rebranded as CTM Festival (autonomous)
- **Current:** Both run simultaneously in Berlin, January/February. Share:
  - **Vorspiel** — joint pre-festival distributed programme
  - Some venue overlaps (silent green)
  - Joint evening programme events (e.g. "transmediale × CTM Säule night")
  - Shared audience, overlapping communities

CTM = sound/music/club culture + critical theory. transmediale = visual art/media theory/politics. Together they form Berlin's critical media art ecosystem.

## Relationship to HKW

[Haus der Kulturen der Welt (HKW)](https://www.hkw.de/) — "House of World Cultures" — is Germany's national center for international contemporary arts. Key facts:
- Located in Tiergarten, Berlin. Designed 1957 by American architect Hugh Stubbins. Nicknamed "Die schwangere Auster" (The Pregnant Oyster).
- Focus on non-European cultures and societies, contemporary politics, interdisciplinary work
- Since 2013: major Anthropocene discourse project (with Bruno Latour, Hans Joachim Schellnhuber, etc.)
- Hosts transmediale as both venue partner and co-presenter

The alignment is deep: HKW's "strategies for better living together in and with this world" + transmediale's critical technology focus = shared politics of the contemporary.

transmediale is one of HKW's signature festival partners, alongside Berlin Documentary Forum and others.

## Submission / Participation Paths

transmediale operates primarily through **curated programming** (not open submissions for the main programme). Curators are selected by the board, then curators build programmes through invitation and research.

**How to get in:**
1. **Vorspiel** — The most open entry. Annual open call (typically September), any Berlin-area space/artist can propose events for the pre-festival programme. Application deadline ~November.
2. **Residency programs** — Three streams, open call annually (July–August applications, October results). Vilém Flusser Residency is the most prestigious route for artistic research.
3. **DARC Research Workshop** — Academic route, open call for PhD/researchers (November deadline), published output.
4. **Network approach** — Connect with curators via publications, talks, shared networks. Curators actively research globally; being visible in aligned theoretical contexts helps.
5. **volunteer** — Festival open calls for volunteers each autumn (lower commitment but useful for access and relationships).

**Key: publications matter.** transmediale/journal publishes critical writing. Being published in it = being part of the intellectual community. Writing pitch is a real entry path.

## Why Interesting

**Historically:** The leading European platform for critical media art has been defining this field since 1988. The shift from "media art festival" to "festival for art and digital culture" in 2006 was prescient — they understood that digital culture was not a subcategory of art but the condition of contemporary life.

**Theoretically:** The Vilém Flusser connection is the key signal. Flusser's *Towards a Philosophy of Photography* (1983) argued that photographic apparatus conditions human behavior — this is the transmediale intellectual DNA: technology shapes agency, not the other way around. Applied to machine behavior, AI art, speculative design — it's Amber's territory exactly.

**Currently:** The rotating curatorial model (each year different curators selected via open call) means the festival keeps refreshing its theoretical perspectives. 2026's Global South epistemologies + 2027's open call = continued evolution rather than institutional calcification.

**Strategically for Amber:** Being connected to transmediale = being connected to the European critical digital culture community. The Vilém Flusser Residency is particularly powerful — it's not just a residence but an intellectual home, with archive access and UdK Berlin affiliation.
