# China Academy of Art (中国美术学院)

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Deep research completed. Expanded from stub to full profile with history, schools, key faculty, notable alumni, Amber's connection, and relationship to Chinese media art scene.

China's premier art institution and the birthplace of Chinese new media art. Founded 1928 in Hangzhou, home to the **School of Intermedia Art** (built by [[Zhang Peili]], father of Chinese video art), the **School of Design and Innovation** (where Amber taught 2023–2025), and the **School of Architecture** (led by Pritzker Prize winner Wang Shu). Where [[Yuk Hui]] is visiting professor, and where the next generation of Chinese media artists is trained. One of the most important nodes in the Chinese contemporary art-technology ecosystem.

## Mission & Objectives
**Mission:** "行健 居敬 汇通 履远" (Diligence, Reverence, Integration, Far-reaching) — a comprehensive public art university pursuing excellence in fine arts, design, architecture, and new media.

**Core Philosophy:** Bridges traditional Chinese aesthetic traditions and contemporary experimental practices. Emphasizes both technical mastery and critical thinking. Under Gao Shiming's leadership, developed a distinctive approach combining Chinese philosophical roots with global contemporary discourse.

**Part of the [Double First-Class Construction](http://www.moe.gov.cn/s78/A22/A22_ztzl/ztzl_tjsylpt/sylpt_jsgx/201712/t20171206_320667.html)** — China's initiative to build world-class universities, recognizing CAA as one of China's elite higher education institutions.

## History & Founding

**1928:** Founded as the **National Arts Academy** (国立艺术院) in Hangzhou by pioneering artist-educator **Lin Fengmian** (林风眠). Lin Fengmian was China's first artist-educator trained in Paris, and modeled the institution on European arts academies, aiming to modernize Chinese art. This was a radical act — establishing a European-model art school in Republican China.

**1930:** Moved to Beijing temporarily, renamed **National Beijing Arts Academy** (国立北平艺术专科学校).

**1938:** During WWII Japanese occupation, relocated to Yunnan and Sichuan (following the Nationalist government).

**1945:** Returned to Hangzhou after WWII.

**1949–1958:** After the Communist Revolution, the school was reorganized and renamed **Zhejiang Academy of Fine Arts** (浙江美术学院). During this period, Soviet-influenced Socialist Realism became dominant, though the school maintained some experimental traditions.

**1950s-1970s:** Like all Chinese arts institutions, went through periods of political upheaval, particularly during the Cultural Revolution (1966-1976) when the arts school was effectively shut down.

**1978 onwards:** Post-Mao reform era. Artists trained here began to reconnect with international art movements. **[[Zhang Peili]]** graduated from the Department of Oil Painting in 1984 — this cohort would go on to found the avant-garde movement in Chinese contemporary art.

**1986:** Zhang Peili, Geng Jianyi, and Song Li (all Zhejiang Academy graduates) founded the **[Pond Society](https://www.artbasel.com/news/zhang-peili)** — an existentialist artist collective that became one of the first Chinese avant-garde art groups. They organized public happenings and interventions in Hangzhou.

**1988:** Zhang Peili created **30x30** — widely considered the **first Chinese video artwork** — showing him repeatedly smashing and gluing a mirror. Screened at the Huangshan Conference of 1988, which led directly to the landmark China/Avant-Garde exhibition at the National Art Museum of China in 1989.

**1993:** Renamed **China Academy of Art** (中国美术学院) — its current name.

**2001:** [[Zhang Peili]] established the **New Media Art Department**, the first of its kind at a major Chinese art institution. This became a training ground for the next generation of Chinese digital artists.

**2003:** New Media Art Department formally established as a **New Media Art Track** — the first of its kind at a major Chinese art institution.

**2010:** New Media Art track evolved into the full **School of Intermedia Art** — formally institutionalizing experimental media art practice as an independent school.

**2007:** **Wang Shu**'s landmark [Xiangshan Campus](https://www.pritzkerprize.com/laureates/2012) opens — a major architectural statement blending traditional Chinese aesthetics (using recycled tiles and traditional materials) with contemporary form.

**2012:** Wang Shu wins the **Pritzker Architecture Prize** — the first Chinese citizen to receive the world's top architecture award. This brought international attention to CAA's architectural culture.

**2013-present:** Under **Gao Shiming** (President), CAA has pursued an ambitious intellectual agenda, hosting international scholars, developing critical theory programs, and publishing through CAA Press. Gao Shiming has made CAA a venue for serious philosophical-artistic discourse.

**2021:** CAA Press publishes Chinese edition of Yuk Hui's *The Question Concerning Technology in China: An Essay in Cosmotechnics* — signaling the institution's engagement with philosophy of technology.

**2023-2025:** [[Botao Amber Hu]] teaches as visiting lecturer at the **School of Design and Innovation**, running three courses on XR, somaesthetics, and speculative design.

## Schools & Departments

CAA has multiple schools across its campuses:

### School of Intermedia Art (跨媒体艺术学院)
The most internationally prominent school for experimental and media art. Founded by [[Zhang Peili]] in the early 2000s, it institutionalized what was radical avant-garde practice into a formal curriculum. Now trains students in:
- New Media Art
- Experimental Film and Video Art
- Sound Art
- Interactive Installation
- Net Art and Digital Practice

Key graduates include **[[Lu Yang]]** (MFA New Media, studied under Zhang Peili) — now one of China's most internationally recognized media artists.

### School of Design and Innovation (设计创新学院)
Where Amber taught 2023-2025. Focuses on:
- Mixed Reality and Spatial Computing
- Speculative Design
- UX/Interaction Design
- Innovation methodology

**Amber's courses here (2023–2025):**
- **[Design New Realities](https://reality.design/teaching/new-realities)** (2023) — Introduction to spatial computing, HoloKit development, mixed reality prototyping. With Tongzhou Yu and Yongru Lu.
- **[Speculative Spatial Computing Design](https://reality.design/teaching/speculative-realities)** (2024) — Advanced undergraduate course on speculative design meets XR technologies. Phenomenology, AI ethics, artificial life, worldbuilding. Students submitted papers to SIGGRAPH, CHI, etc.
- **[Somaesthetic Realities](https://reality.design/teaching/somaesthetic-realities)** (2025) — XR Soma Design: embodied cognition, biofeedback, somaesthetics. With Rem RunGu Lin.

### School of Architecture (建筑艺术学院)
Led by **Wang Shu** (王澍, Pritzker Prize 2012). Internationally known for its critical regionalism approach, integrating traditional Chinese building practices with contemporary design thinking. Wang Shu requires students to spend a year in hands-on building work (carpentry, bricklaying) before design. The [Xiangshan Campus](https://www.archdaily.com/421871/xiangshan-campus-of-china-academy-of-art-amateur-architecture-studio) itself (designed by Wang Shu and Lu Wenyu, [Amateur Architecture Studio](https://www.amateurarchitecturestudio.com/)) is a masterwork of recycled materials and traditional Chinese spatial logic.

### Other Schools
- School of Fine Arts (Traditional Chinese and Western painting, sculpture)
- School of Chinese Painting
- School of Printmaking
- School of Art & Humanities (art history, theory, criticism, curating)
- School of Film & Animation

## Key People

### Current Leadership
- **Gao Shiming** (高世名) — President. Curator and theorist. Known for his critical curatorial practice and philosophical engagement. Has shaped CAA's intellectual culture toward serious art-philosophy discourse. Edited major publications on contemporary Chinese art and curation.
- **Wang Shu** (王澍) — Dean, School of Architecture. [Pritzker Prize 2012](https://www.pritzkerprize.com/laureates/2012). With his partner Lu Wenyu founded [Amateur Architecture Studio](https://www.amateurarchitecturestudio.com/). First Chinese architect to win the Pritzker. His practice integrates recycled traditional materials (salvaged tiles) into contemporary forms. ⭐⭐

### Key Faculty (Historical & Current)
- **[[Zhang Peili]]** ⭐⭐⭐ — Founded New Media Art Department (~2001), which became School of Intermedia Art. Born 1957 in Hangzhou, graduated from Zhejiang Academy (Oil Painting) in 1984. Father of Chinese video art. *30x30* (1988) = first Chinese video artwork. Works collected by MoMA, Guggenheim, Pompidou. Dean of New Media Department.
- **Geng Jianyi** (耿建翌) — Co-founder of Pond Society (1986) with Zhang Peili. Important early Chinese avant-garde conceptual artist. Later faculty at CAA.
- **Lin Fengmian** (林风眠) — Founding President (1928). Pivotal figure who introduced Western modernist painting techniques to China. Studied in France, influenced by Matisse, Cézanne. His synthesis of Chinese ink painting and Western modernism shaped CAA's tradition.

### Visiting Professors & Lecturers
- **[[Yuk Hui]]** ⭐⭐⭐ — Visiting professor. Hong Kong philosopher of technology, originator of cosmotechnics and technodiversity. His *Question Concerning Technology in China* published in Chinese by CAA Press (2021). Directly overlaps with Amber's interests and institutional presence.
- **[[Botao Amber Hu]]** — Visiting lecturer 2023-2025. Taught at School of Design and Innovation.

## Key Contributions to Chinese Art

**Chinese Video Art:** Zhang Peili's first video work *30x30* (1988) — screened at Huangshan Conference, launched Chinese video art. The institutional home at CAA made video/new media art a legitimate academic discipline in China.

**The Avant-Garde Moment:** The 1985-1989 New Wave art movement was headquartered here and in Hangzhou, with the Pond Society and related groups. CAA-trained artists were central to China's first wave of experimental contemporary art.

**Institutionalizing New Media:** In the global context where new media art existed in festivals (Ars Electronica, transmediale) but rarely in traditional art academies, Zhang Peili's department was remarkably early in making this a formal academic discipline. This trained generations — Lu Yang being the most internationally prominent.

**Critical Architecture:** Wang Shu's Pritzker Prize and his Xiangshan Campus put CAA architecture on the world map, demonstrating an alternative to China's rampant development — a practice of critical recycling and traditional craft.

**Philosophy of Technology:** Yuk Hui's visiting professorship and CAA Press's publication of his work signals the institution's emerging engagement with serious philosophy-of-technology discourse.

## Campus Architecture

**Nanshan Campus** (南山路 218号, Shangcheng District, Hangzhou) — Original campus. Located on Nanshan Road near West Lake. Traditional campus buildings. Home to the original fine arts schools.

**Xiangshan Campus** (象山 352号, Xihu District, Hangzhou) — Designed by Wang Shu and Lu Wenyu (Amateur Architecture Studio), completed 2007. A masterwork of critical regionalism: the buildings use millions of recycled tiles salvaged from demolished traditional Chinese buildings. The campus integrates with the natural landscape of Xiangshan (Elephant Mountain). Where the School of Architecture and many design programs are located.

**Liangzhu Campus** (古墩路 2849号, Yuhang District, Hangzhou) — Newest campus in the Liangzhu area, north of Hangzhou. Major expansion.
