# MATS Program (ML Alignment Theory Scholars)

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Deep research completed. Created comprehensive note covering history, program structure, tracks, alumni outcomes, and extensive mentor network analysis.

The world's premier fellowship program for emerging AI alignment researchers. A 12-week intensive research experience connecting talented individuals with top mentors in AI safety, interpretability, governance, and security. Known for producing an extraordinary concentration of AI safety leaders - 10% of alumni have founded AI safety organizations, 80% now work in alignment.

## Mission & Objectives
**Mission:** Find and train talented individuals for reducing risks from unaligned artificial intelligence. Transform ambitious researchers from diverse backgrounds into effective AI safety contributors through intensive mentorship, research experience, and community building.

**Core Philosophy:** AI alignment is the world's most urgent and talent-constrained problem. Ambitious researchers from varied backgrounds can meaningfully contribute to alignment research with proper training, logistics, and community support. Focus on producing researchers who launch long-term careers in AI safety.

**Research Approach:** Hands-on research using machine learning experiments across five tracks: empirical safety research, policy & strategy, theory, technical governance, and compute infrastructure. Heavy emphasis on mentorship from leading researchers in industry and academia.

## History & Founding
**Original Name:** Stanford Existential Risk Initiative (SERI) MATS Program - began as Stanford-affiliated initiative before becoming independent.

**Evolution to Independence:** Became fully independent 501(c)(3) public charity (EIN: 99-0648563) called MATS Research, though maintains collaborative relationships with academic institutions.

**Program Structure Development:** Started with smaller cohorts, evolved into current format with 12-week main program plus optional 6+ month London-based extension program. 

**Geographic Expansion:** Originally Berkeley-based, expanded to include London locations in response to European AI safety ecosystem growth and regulatory developments.

**Scale Growth:** Summer 2026 program represents largest to date: 120 fellows and 100 mentors, demonstrating massive scaling while maintaining quality.

## Program Structure

### 12-Week Fellowship Format
- **Location:** Primary Berkeley location + London option
- **Stipend:** $15,000 from AI Safety Support for living expenses
- **Compute Budget:** $12,000 in compute resources for experiments
- **Workspace:** Dedicated office space in Berkeley and London
- **Housing & Meals:** Private housing provided, catered lunches and dinners

### Research Tracks & Streams
**Empirical Track:** Hands-on ML experiments on model safety
- Interpretability (Neel Nanda, Arthur Conmy, Lee Sharkey streams)
- Control & monitoring (Buck Shlegeris, Ethan Perez, Sam Marks streams)
- Model organisms & scheming (multiple mentors)

**Policy & Strategy Track:** Strategic dynamics and governance
- AI forecasting and macrostrategy 
- International coordination and institutional design
- Regulatory proposals and implementation

**Theory Track:** Mathematical and philosophical foundations
- Agent foundations and decision theory (Abram Demski stream)
- Singular learning theory applications (Timaeus stream)
- Formal approaches to alignment

**Technical Governance Track:** Policy-technology interface
- Compliance protocols and evaluation standards
- Compute governance and proliferation
- Verification and enforcement mechanisms

**Compute Infrastructure Track:** Hardware-level AI security
- Side-channel analysis and cluster security
- Physical-layer verification
- Infrastructure monitoring and securing development

### Application Process
- **Application Period:** December 16 - January 18 annually
- **Additional Evaluations:** Late January through March (coding tests, work samples, interviews)
- **Final Offers:** Late March/early April
- **Program Dates:** June through August, with September extension phase
