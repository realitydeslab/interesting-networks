# 17, Instituto de Estudios Críticos (17, Institute for Critical Studies)

A **graduate school and cultural institution** in Mexico City, directed by **Benjamin Mayer Foulkes**. Occupies a unique position: it is simultaneously a postgraduate institution offering MA and PhD programs, a publisher (editorial 17), and a venue for critical theory symposia. Named provocatively — "17" refers to the 17 theses of Louis Althusser, and more broadly to the idea of criticism that cannot be contained in a single discipline. Runs an annual international colloquium series that brings together Latin American, European, and North American critical thinkers.

Gary Hall has visited and keynoted since 2009 — one of the most sustained long-term relationships in his intellectual career. The 17 Institute also produced Liquid Books Vol.10 (*Eco-catástrofe y deconstrucción*), a collectively-produced OA liquid book in Spanish on decolonial environmental criticism.

## Key People
- **Benjamin Mayer Foulkes** — founder and director; German-Mexican intellectual, publisher
- **[[Gary Hall]]** — recurring keynote speaker since 2009; 8+ talks; co-produced Liquid Books Vol.10
- **[[Gabriela Méndez Cota]]** ⭐⭐ — professor at Goldsmiths; ran the MA seminar that produced Liquid Books Vol.10 at 17 Institute; key translator of Hall's work into Spanish
- **Leonidas Donskis** — European Parliament member for Lithuania; appeared in January 2014 XVI International Symposium
- Various Latin American and European critical theorists (rotating)

## Topics
- Continental philosophy (Derrida, Stiegler, Foucault, Althusser)
- Postcolonial and decolonial theory
- Experimental publishing and knowledge commons
- Digital humanities and open access
- Environmental criticism and eco-catastrophe
- Economics and political theory

- Published proceedings and books through editorial 17

## Why Interesting
17 is one of the most intellectually serious small institutions in Latin America and models what a scholar-led institution can be: it runs on intellectual commitment, not endowment. Its commitment to open access publishing in Spanish is important for making critical theory accessible in the Global South. The 17-Hall relationship is a model for North-South intellectual collaboration that goes beyond keynoting: it produced OA books in Spanish, ongoing exchanges, and genuine intellectual development in both directions. For Amber: 17 is a window into Mexican and Latin American critical theory, which is a seriously underserved network in the European/North American academic scene.
