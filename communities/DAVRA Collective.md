# DAVRA Collective

Central Asian contemporary art research collective founded by [[Saodat Ismailova]] in Tashkent in 2021. First pan-Central Asian contemporary art institution dedicated to researching, documenting and disseminating the culture and knowledge of Central Asia. The name "DAVRA" refers to a circular gathering or assembly — the form in which traditional knowledge is shared.

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-27:** Stub created during Saodat Ismailova deep research.

## Mission & Objectives
Research, documentation and public dissemination of Central Asian cultural knowledge — traditional practices, spiritual traditions, oral histories, contemporary art — that risk erasure through colonial and post-Soviet pressures.

## Key People
- **[[Saodat Ismailova]]** — founder. Uzbek filmmaker and artist.
- 18 artists from Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan, Tajikistan, and Uzbekistan (members not individually named in public sources).

## Key Contributions
- **documenta fifteen (2022)** — participated as lumbung member. Ran a 40-day public programme alongside Ismailova's *Chilltan* installation in the Fridericianum basement, Kassel. Activities included music, clay sculpture, film. Brought Central Asian cultural practices into the world's most visible art event.
- **EYE Filmmuseum** — Eye Film Player programmed three curated DAVRA programmes presenting three generations of Central Asian artists, curated by Dilda Ramazan, alongside the *18,000 Worlds* retrospective.

## Events & Programmes

- Eye Film Player, Amsterdam (2023) — curated film programmes

## Why Interesting
DAVRA is a model for collective research infrastructure building from a non-Western position. Rather than waiting for Western institutions to curate Central Asian art, Ismailova created the institution that could do it on its own terms. This is exactly the "communities as research infrastructure" pattern that interests Amber (via Future Observatory model). 

The lumbung connection also ties DAVRA into ruangrupa's solidarity-based art-world critique — a pan-Global South alternative to the commercial gallery circuit.
