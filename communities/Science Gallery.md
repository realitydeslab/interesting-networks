# Science Gallery (International Network)

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Stub created during SymbioticA deep research.

International network of galleries and research institutions at the intersection of art and science, targeting youth audiences (15-25). Founded 2008 at Trinity College Dublin (Science Gallery Dublin). Now a global network with locations in London (King's College London), Bengaluru, Melbourne, Atlanta, Trento, Venice, Detroit.

**Mission:** "Collide art, science and society." Focus on open-ended, provocation-based exhibitions that invite participation and debate rather than deliver settled knowledge.

**Relationship to SymbioticA:** Co-produced *Visceral: The Living Art Experiment* with [[SymbioticA]] — a major exhibition exploring what constitutes living, scientific truths, and the ethics of life manipulation.

**Model:** Exhibition themes are open calls — artists, scientists, designers, and researchers propose works around a central provocation. Strong curatorial vision; youth-focused; designed to spark controversy rather than educate passively.

## Why Interesting
Key venue for art-science work at the public-facing level. Has exhibited bio-art, ALife, AI-art. Open call model is accessible to artists at various career stages. London location (Science Gallery London at King's College) directly relevant to Amber.
