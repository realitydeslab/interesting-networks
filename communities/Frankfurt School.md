# Frankfurt School / Institut für Sozialforschung

The most influential tradition of critical social theory in the 20th century. Founded 1923 at Goethe University Frankfurt. Key figures: Max Horkheimer, Theodor Adorno, Herbert Marcuse, Erich Fromm, Walter Benjamin (associated), Jürgen Habermas (second generation), [[Alexander Kluge]] and [[Oskar Negt]] (third generation). Known for: *Dialectic of Enlightenment* (Adorno/Horkheimer, 1944), *Minima Moralia* (Adorno, 1951), *The Structural Transformation of the Public Sphere* (Habermas, 1962). The tradition that most rigorously analyzed how Enlightenment rationality becomes an instrument of domination — foundational for critical computing, platform studies, and AI ethics.
