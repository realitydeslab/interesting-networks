# School for Poetic Computation (SFPC)

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-27:** Deep research completed ⭐⭐. Expanded founders, leadership, programs, sessions, faculty, board. New stubs created: [[Taeyoon Choi]], [[American Artist]].
- **2026-02-26:** Created stub note. Discovered through [[Golan Levin]] research (workshop organizer) and [[Zachary Lieberman]] research (co-founder).

Hybrid school/residency/research group in New York City, founded 2013 by [[Zachary Lieberman]], Taeyoon Choi, Amit Pitaru, and Jen Lowe. Runs intensive online and in-person programs that approach computation from an artistic, critical, and poetic angle — explicitly NOT a tech school. "A school where technical and artistic education collide and collude." Small cohorts, intensive immersion, strong community. Motto: **"More poems, less demos."**

Since 2021, radically restructured around BIPOC, queer, disabled, and feminist leadership after a public reckoning with the original founders' handling of BLM concerns. Now run by a collective of stewards/co-directors centered on abolitionist, Black feminist, and transformative justice principles.

---

## Mission & Objectives

> "School for Poetic Computation (SFPC) is an experimental school in New York and online founded in 2013. SFPC facilitates the study of art, code, hardware, and critical theory through lenses of decolonization and transformative justice. SFPC offers programs grounded in solidarity across social differences and empowerment through a deeper technical and political understanding of tools towards building just communities."

Explicitly designed as a platform for people who are Black, Indigenous, of color, trans, gender non-conforming, queer, disabled, survivors, from low-income backgrounds, and oppressed to feel empowered that their ideas are important, necessary, and central.

## Key Contributions

- **Model for artist-run technical education** — demonstrated that rigorous coding + hardware education can be explicitly political and feminist. Widely cited as inspiration for similar initiatives.
- **Critical computing lineage** — SFPC alumni span a specific "critical tech + abolitionist" corridor; graduates teaching at NYU ITP, Parsons, Cooper Union, RISD.
- **2021 transformation** — publicly navigated a political transition (BLM → steward model) and documented it openly; now a model for other institutions undergoing similar reckonings.
- **NYU ITP pipeline** — many SFPC instructors are affiliated with NYU's Interactive Telecommunications Program (ITP); strong overlap.
- **Processing/openFrameworks community** — early SFPC was a hub for these creative coding ecosystems.

## Philosophy & Approach

SFPC's unique formula:
1. **"Poetic" not "practical"** — computation as medium for meaning-making, not productivity
2. **Small cohort, no credentials** — no degrees, no job placement promises; creates intimacy and trust
3. **Faculty as practitioners** — all instructors are working artists, not professional educators
4. **Critical and political** — courses examine power structures IN technology, not just how to use technology
5. **Abolitionist pedagogy** — explicitly informed by prison abolition, Black feminism, transformative justice (post-2021 especially)
6. **Multiple modalities** — code + theory + hardware + performance all equally valid

The Spring 2026 curatorial statement captures current ethos: "While corporate tech scales its threats, we look to the past to understand what we want the present to be: the internet as a site that once held room for taste, play, and self-expression beyond commodification."

## Why Interesting

SFPC is the **closest institutional parallel to Amber's own practice**: technically rigorous but explicitly political and poetic. After its 2021 restructuring, SFPC doubled down on abolitionist, queer, feminist, and decolonial currents — making it one of the rare spaces where computation is treated as a *site of political struggle* rather than neutral tooling.

The "Agential Worldbuilding" course (Fall 2024) and the "Rhythmanalytics" course (Spring 2026, with DeForrest Brown Jr.) show that SFPC is gravitating toward exactly Amber's territory: agency, machine behavior, and the politics of rhythmic/computational time. The board includes [[American Artist]] and [[Tega Brain]] — two of the most rigorous critical tech practitioners working today.
