Switzerland's leading institution for media art and digital culture. Founded 2011 in Basel, HeK has become one of Europe's premier venues for art at the intersection of technology, society, and ecology. Deeply embedded in the Art Basel ecosystem, with its own permanent collection of "born-digital" works and a growing portfolio of international residency and research partnerships.

---

## Mission & Objectives

HeK (House of Electronic Arts) is dedicated to **digital culture and the new art forms of the Information Age**. Since 2011, the institution has been central to creative and critical discourse on the aesthetic, socio-political and economic effects of media technologies.

As an interdisciplinary museum, HeK operates as a **turntable for artistic events** spanning visual arts, music, theatre, dance, performance and design. It's not just a gallery — it's a research and mediation platform.

Three core operating areas:
1. **Exhibition programme** — solo and group shows, primarily media/digital/electronic art
2. **Collection** — unique focus on "born-digital" works (software, net art, interactive installations); custodian of Swiss Federal Art Collection digital works
3. **Education & community** — workshops, events, Pax Art Awards, online programmes, Mesh Festival

Key institutional position: **sole Swiss centre of excellence for media art**. HeK holds permanent loans of digital works from the Swiss Federal Art Collection and Kunstkredit Basel-Stadt, cementing its role as the national authority.

**Location:** Freilager-Platz 9, CH-4142 Münchenstein / Basel (own building since November 2014, renovation funded by Christoph Merian Foundation and Ernst Göhner Foundation). Open extra hours during Art Basel Week (daily 10AM–6PM).

**Predecessor institutions:** Forum für neue Medien Plug.in and Shift Festival der elektronischen Künste.

## Key Contributions

1. **Swiss anchor for media art** — The sole Swiss institution with a mandate to collect, preserve and exhibit digital/media art. Custodian for Swiss Federal Art Collection digital works.
2. **Born-digital collection methodology** — Pioneered institutional strategies for preserving net art, software works, interactive installations without physical manifestation. Collaboration with conservators and programmers globally.
3. **Pax Art Awards** — Annual cash prizes (CHF 30,000 main + 2 development prizes) for Swiss media artists since 2018. Real financial support that enables artists to produce new work.
4. **Regionale network** — Part of a trinational group exhibition spanning 20 institutions across Germany, France and Switzerland. Open call, focused on the Upper Rhine tri-border region.
5. **Mesh Festival** — Co-organizes this festival for art and technology on the Freilager-Platz.
6. **International residency research** — SPATIAL AFFAIRS programme linking Basel/Bangalore/San Sebastián for XR residencies.
7. **Pandemic online innovation** — HEK Net Works (2020) and Net Encounters (2021) commissioned online artworks during COVID, supporting artists financially.

## Events & Programmes

### Permanent / Annual Programmes

**[Pax Art Awards](https://hek.ch/en/projects/pax-art-awards)** (since 2018, annually)
Swiss media art prize. Main prize CHF 30,000 for established artist; 2 development prizes for emerging artists. Artists are *nominated* (not self-applying). Jury: Stefanie Marlene Wenger, Carmen Weisskopf, Luc Meier, Sabine Himmelsbach + Art Foundation Pax board members. Each year's winners exhibit new works at HeK the following year. **Not directly applicable for Amber (Swiss artists only)** but signals what HeK values.

**[Regionale](https://hek.ch/en/program)** (annual, Nov–Jan)
Trinational group exhibition across 20 institutions in DE/FR/CH. Open call for artists in the Upper Rhine tri-border region. Regionale 26 runs 30.11.2025–04.01.2026. Note: requires regional ties.

**[HEK Net Works](https://hek.ch/en/projects/hek-net-works)** (since March 2020, ongoing)
Commissioned net-based artworks for HeK's digital channels. Monthly feature. Artists create specifically for HeK's social media or website. Lower barrier of entry — digital, international.

**[Mesh Festival](https://meshfestival.ch/)** (biennial)
Festival for art and technology, co-located at Freilager-Platz. Theme of next edition (Oct 14–18, 2026): "urban actions | spatial utopias" — spatial impacts of digital technologies, immersive public space, AR/VR, smart city critiques. Open to international artists.

**Friends of HEK (FOH)**
Tokenized membership / DAO model. First museum to adopt tokenized membership. Experimental community around blockchain and digital art ownership.

### Residency / Research Programmes

**[SPATIAL AFFAIRS: Extended Reality Experiences](https://hek.ch/en/projects/spatial-affairs)**
Two-year joint residency/creation programme.
- **Partners:** [[MAP — Museum of Art and Photography]], Bangalore (India); [Tabakalera](https://www.tabakalera.eus/) — International Centre for Contemporary Culture, Donostia/San Sebastián (Spain)
- **Technology partners:** [iart](https://iart.ch/) (media architecture, Basel/Switzerland); [metaLAB (at) Basel](https://metalab.github.io/) (Switzerland); TEKNIKER technology centre, Eibar (Spain)
- **What:** Artists develop XR prototypes through residencies at all three venues. Uses AIRE platform (iart's XR engine, location tracking, hybrid real-time experiences). TEKNIKER supports collaborative robots, machine vision, AI, XR in physical spaces.
- **For whom:** Artists with XR (extended reality) experience or clear potential; interest in performance or installation with technology; research-embedded practice.
- **Selection:** Open call. One artist per institution per cycle.
- **Amber relevance:** HIGH — XR, performance, physical-digital entanglement. Apply through open call.

## Collection

HeK's collection focuses on **"born-digital" art** — art created and located in the digital medium, often without physical manifestation:
- Software-based works
- Net art
- Interactive installations
- Videos
- Site-specific digital commissions

Scope: Swiss artists from mid-1990s to present, with some historical acquisitions from the 1990s.

Key sources: trinational research project *Digitale Medienkunst am Oberrhein* (2010–2012); Digital Art Collection (Annette Schindler and Reinhard Storz); Christoph Merian Foundation; Freiwilliger Museumsverein Basel (FMB); Swiss Federal Art Collection (permanent loan); Kunstkredit Basel-Stadt.

HeK maintains an online collection: [hek.ch/en/collection](https://hek.ch/en/collection). Also cares for digital works from Swiss Federal Art Collection and Kunstkredit Basel-Stadt.
