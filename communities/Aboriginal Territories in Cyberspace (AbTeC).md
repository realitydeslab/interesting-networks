# Aboriginal Territories in Cyberspace (AbTeC)

Aboriginally-determined research-creation studio lab co-founded in 2005 by [[Jason Edward Lewis]] and [[Skawennati]] at Concordia University. Mission: ensure Indigenous presence in websites, online environments, video games, and virtual worlds.

Rooted in **CyberPowWow** (late 1990s–early 2000s) — the world's first online gallery and chat space for contemporary Indigenous art, organized by Skawennati. The realization that "even on the Internet, Native people need a self-determined place to call home" became AbTeC's founding principle.

## What They Do
- Artwork, writing, lectures, workshops, residencies, exhibitions
- **Skins Workshops** — teaching Indigenous youth to make video games and machinima from community stories
- **TimeTraveller™** — Skawennati's machinima series exploring Haudenosaunee futures in Second Life
- Parent organization of the **Initiative for Indigenous Futures (IIF)**
- Host institution for the **Abundant Intelligences** research program

## Key People
- **[[Jason Edward Lewis]]** — co-founder, co-director
- **[[Skawennati]]** — co-founder, co-director (Kanien'kehá:ka/Mohawk artist)
- **Mikhel Proulx** — researcher

## Connection to Amber
Foundational lab for thinking about Indigenous sovereignty in digital/computational spaces. The cyberspace-as-territory framing is directly relevant to Amber's interests in decentralized AI and the politics of digital infrastructure.
