# Global AI Narratives (GAIN)

Interdisciplinary academic research project (2018–2022) hosted at the [[Leverhulme Centre for the Future of Intelligence (LCFI)]] at the University of Cambridge. The most comprehensive cross-cultural AI narratives project ever conducted: 20 workshops across five continents, hundreds of regional scholars, and two Oxford University Press anthologies. The core argument — that different cultures see AI through radically different lenses shaped by religion, philosophy, literature, and cinema — is now canonical in AI ethics discourse.

## Mission & Objectives

GAIN was built around a simple but powerful observation: **public perceptions of AI are shaped by cultural narratives, and those narratives are not universal**. The Terminator-style existential threat is an Anglophone cinematic phenomenon. Japanese AI is shaped by Doraemon and Buddhist notions of non-dualism. Chinese AI by Taoist cosmology, wuxia mythology, and a CCP development imperative. Indian AI by caste politics and multilingual literary traditions. Arabic AI by Quranic metaphysics. The stories a culture tells about intelligent machines shape what it builds, how it governs it, and what it fears.

Three stated goals:
1. Understand how different cultures perceive AI risks and benefits, and what shapes those perceptions.
2. Create a genuinely global community of AI narratives scholars.
3. Foster interdisciplinary research from underrepresented regions and groups.

## Legacy: AI Narratives & Justice Programme

After GAIN concluded in 2022, the work continued and expanded into LCFI's [AI: Narratives and Justice Programme](https://www.lcfi.ac.uk/research/programme/ai-narratives-and-justice), which now includes multiple active projects:

- [**Desirable Digitalisation**](https://www.lcfi.ac.uk/research/project/desirable-digitalisiation) — intersectional/anthropological perspectives on AI design for justice
- [**Centre for Drones and Culture**](https://www.lcfi.ac.uk/research/project/drones-and-culture) — socio-political imaginaries of drone technology
- [**Imaginaries of Immortality in the Age of AI**](https://www.lcfi.ac.uk/research/project/imaginaries-of-immortality-in-the-age-of-ai) — deadbots, griefbots, posthumous avatars
- [**History of AI**](https://www.lcfi.ac.uk/research/project/history-ai) — social, political, technological history of intelligent systems
- [**Gender and AI**](https://www.lcfi.ac.uk/research/project/ai-and-gender) — feminist/queer approaches; critical race, postcolonial, crip theory
- [**Decolonising AI**](https://www.lcfi.ac.uk/research/project/decolonising-ai) — developer diversity and dataset bias
- [**AI Narratives**](https://www.lcfi.ac.uk/research/project/ai-narratives) — how we talk about AI shapes its development

## Why Interesting
GAIN established the scholarly infrastructure for cross-cultural AI narrative research — the framework, the global network, the workshop methodology, the OUP publication track. The key theoretical move: **narrative shapes governance**. What a culture imagines AI to be determines what it will allow AI to do. This is the missing layer in most AI ethics work, which assumes a universal observer. GAIN proved there is no such thing.
