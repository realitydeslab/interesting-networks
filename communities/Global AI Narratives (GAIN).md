# Global AI Narratives (GAIN)

Interdisciplinary academic project (2018–2022) within the [[Leverhulme Centre for the Future of Intelligence (LCFI)]] at Cambridge. Led by [[Kanta Dihal]] (Project Lead) and [[Stephen Cave]] (Executive Director). Ran 20+ workshops across five continents to map how different cultures — Japan, China, India, MENA, Africa, Latin America, Russia, Germany, Central/Eastern Europe, Singapore, and more — imagine AI differently from the dominant Anglophone (Terminator/I, Robot) narrative. Resulted in *[Imagining AI: How the World Sees Intelligent Machines](https://global.oup.com/academic/product/imagining-ai-9780198959557)* (OUP, 2023).

## Mission & Objectives
- Understand how different cultures perceive AI risks and benefits (beyond Western Anglophone defaults)
- Create a global community of AI narratives researchers
- Foster interdisciplinary research from underrepresented regions

Key insight: Japanese AI (Doraemon-style friendly robots) vs. Western AI (Terminator-style existential threat) are not the same thing culturally — and these narrative differences shape governance expectations, public trust, and development priorities.

## Why Interesting
The most comprehensive cross-cultural AI narratives project ever conducted. The basic insight — that the stories different cultures tell about AI shape how they develop, regulate, and experience AI — is now canonical. The GAIN global network is a ready-made infrastructure for anyone doing cross-cultural AI research.
