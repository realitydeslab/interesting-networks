# Stanford HCI Group

Stanford's flagship human-computer interaction research group. One of the most prolific and influential HCI labs in the world. Birthplace of many foundational HCI ideas (Terry Winograd, the WIMP interface, d.school spin-off). Currently at the intersection of HCI, AI, and social computing. Michael Bernstein's lab is the center of gravity for the group's current agenda.

## Key People
- **[[Michael Bernstein]]** ⭐⭐⭐ — Social computing, generative agents, crowd computing. Bass University Fellow. Currently on leave at Simile.ai.
- **James Landay** — HCI systems, health tech, design tools. Former group director.

## Why Interesting
The group defines what "responsible social AI" means in the United States. Unlike European HCI (more critical theory, participatory) or industry labs (more capability-focused), Stanford HCI occupies a distinctive niche: high technical rigor + strong social impact orientation. The Generative Agents paper came from here. The Flash Teams labor research came from here. The Street-Level Algorithms critical computing paper came from here. Understanding this group = understanding the dominant US paradigm for human-centered AI.

Key connection: [[Michael Bernstein]] is the intellectual center of the group's current agenda on LLM social simulation, connecting to [[Joon Sung Park]] (Generative Agents) and [[Percy Liang]] (foundation models).
