# Black Audio Film Collective

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Stub created during The Otolith Group deep research.

British Black arts collective active 1982–1998. Co-founded by **[[John Akomfrah]]**, Edward George, Lina Gopaul, Avril Johnson, Reece Augury, Trevor Mathison, and Claire Joseph. Foundational to British Black cultural politics and the essay film tradition in the UK. Their practice combined documentary, found footage, archival material, and spoken word into politically engaged film essays. Key works include *Handsworth Songs* (1986, BAFTA-winning), *Testament* (1988), and *Seven Songs for Malcolm X* (1993). The Otolith Group's book *The Ghosts of Songs: The Film Art of the Black Audio Film Collective* (Liverpool University Press, 2007) is the major scholarly account of their work — edited and introduced by [[Kodwo Eshun]].

## Why Interesting
Historical root of the essay film tradition in British Black art. Direct ancestor of [[The Otolith Group]]'s practice. Their archival methods, spoken word aesthetics, and political commitment established a template that continues to influence film-based art practice. [[John Akomfrah]] went on to be an advisory board member for The Otolith Group, maintaining the lineage.

## Key People
- [[John Akomfrah]] — co-founder
- [[Kodwo Eshun]] — major scholar of their work; author of *The Ghosts of Songs*
