Founded in 2007 by philanthropist Delfina Entrecanales. London's **largest international artist residency programme** (6–8 residents at any one time, up to 3 months). Based near Buckingham Palace at 29–31 Catherine Place, central London. Non-political, non-grant-making foundation.

---

## Mission & Objectives

- Facilitate mobility of cultural practitioners through access to learning, resources and networks
- Foster mutual understanding via artistic practice and exchange
- Strengthen international collaboration and partnership
- Encourage critical and engaging practices in the arts
- Promote contemporary visual culture, particularly under-represented practices

Since 2014 expansion, hosts 6–8 residents simultaneously. International reach with deep relationships in Middle East, North Africa and South Asia. Past international residencies (now London-only): São Paulo, Halabja, Damascus, Granada, Bethlehem, Muscat, Dubai, Beirut, Cordoba, Ramallah.

## Thematic Programmes

Delfina's residency programme is primarily **thematic** — seasons are organised around recurring programme themes:

### 1. [science_technology_society (STS)](https://www.delfinafoundation.com/programmes/science-technology-society/)
The most relevant programme for Amber. Begun autumn 2019. Supported in partnership with [Gaia Art Foundation](http://www.gaiaartfoundation.org) (since 2017). Explores interdisciplinary approaches that "consider, intervene in, and speculate on the world and its possible futures."

**Season 1 — Autumn 2019**
Theme: environmental data; digitally captured lived experience; technology and social structures.
- International residents: Salik Ansari, Burak Arikan, Kyriaki Goni, Jeppe Ugelvig, Jaro Varga, Victoria Scott
- UK associates: Matterlurgy, Nick Laessing, [[Gary Zhexi Zhang]]
- Public events: "Trust Issues: A roundtable on art, decentralisation and emergent technocultures" (Oct 2019); AIR_MORPHOLOGIES workshop; Hydrogen utopia? workshop; Adventures in Non-Ordinary Reality workshop

**Season 2 — Autumn 2020**
Theme: cultural perspectives on technology and sciences; reimagining our relationship in a vulnerable ecosystem. Topics: ecological footprint of AI, indigenous robotics, Maori relationship with night sky, Black speculative fiction, data feminism, dreamt vs virtual reality.
- International residents: Rosalie Yu, Rut Karin Zettergren, Sara Garzón
- UK associates: Louise Beer, Sonya Dyer, [[Anna Ridler]]
- Public events: Worldmaking Practices: A Take on the Future; Dreaming the Technological workshop; Photographic Knitting Club

**Season 3 — Winter 2025**
Theme: emergent technologies × mental well-being; reimagining mental health support, justice, and pride. "Mad knowledge."
- International residents: Abdul Halik Azeez, Anna Costa e Silva, Aracha Cholitgul, Dennis Dizon, Doreen Chan, Ali Akbar Mehta, Simon Speiser, Gabriella Hirst
- UK associates: Caroline Sinders & Romy Gad el Rab, **[[Xin Liu]]**, Micha Frazer-Carroll

### 2. [The Politics of Food](https://www.delfinafoundation.com/programmes/politics-of-food/)
Interrogating cultures of food, production, consumption, distribution.

### 3. [Collecting as Practice](https://www.delfinafoundation.com/programmes/collecting-as-practice/)
Exploring philosophy, psychology and politics of collecting.

### 4. [Performance as Process](https://www.delfinafoundation.com/programmes/performance-as-process/)
Experimenting with performativity as a way of approaching and understanding the world.

## Why Interesting

Delfina's STS programme is one of the few institutional spaces in the UK that explicitly supports **speculative, critical, and interdisciplinary** practice at the intersection of art, science and technology — without being an academic institution. It's adjacent to but distinct from university research: it offers **time, space, community, and public platform**, not grant funding or tenure.

The STS alumni list reads like a who's who of Amber's interest space: Anna Ridler (ML art), Gary Zhexi Zhang (technocultures), Xin Liu (biotech × space × MIT), Caroline Sinders (feminist AI). The seasonal structure means each cohort is a curated community of like-minded practitioners who meet, collaborate, and become lifelong peers.

For Amber specifically: London proximity makes this extremely actionable. The STS programme brief ("consider, intervene in, and speculate on the world and its possible futures") could have been written for Amber's practice. This is a **realistic near-term residency target**.
