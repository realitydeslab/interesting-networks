# Blue Marble Space

US-based international non-profit promoting cooperative space exploration, examining life as a planetary process, and enabling a sustainable future on Earth — with a distinct emphasis on science communication, early-career mentorship, and transdisciplinary inquiry including moral philosophy.

## Mission & Objectives

> "We are a US-based international non-profit that promotes cooperative exploration of space, examines life as a planetary process, and enables a sustainable future on Earth. Our mission is to cultivate scientific innovation and entrepreneurship by engaging with lifelong learners."

Blue Marble Space (BMS) operates as an umbrella organization for several distinct initiatives. Their approach is notable for: (1) geographically distributed research model with very low overhead (~15% vs. typical universities), (2) international inclusivity — most programs are open to non-US participants, and (3) explicit commitment to science communication as integral to research careers, not a side activity.

The organization takes its name and spirit from the famous NASA "Blue Marble" photograph (Earth from space), using it as a symbol of planetary unity.

## Key Initiatives

### 🔬 [Blue Marble Space Institute of Science (BMSIS)](https://bmsis.org/)
The research arm. A distributed institute of scientists working on:
- Origins of life and astrobiology
- Planetary habitability and atmospheric dynamics
- Co-evolution of Earth and life
- Climate change and sustainable development
- Human spaceflight and space exploration
- **Science communication and moral philosophy** (notable inclusions!)
- Complex systems and data science

Scientists affiliate as Research Scientists or Science Communication & Education specialists. Open for affiliation to PhDs and select Masters-level scholars. [Apply here](https://docs.google.com/forms/d/e/1FAIpQLSfQnkcN2pNIVnxsFnx5AXBTzPzKW-81lKpneKahWJdjaw4y4Q/viewform).

### 🎓 [Young Scientist Program (YSP)](https://bmsis.org/ysp/)
"The premier online internship on Earth for those interested in Earth and space science, science communication, and moral philosophy." Annual online program (June 1 – August 31) for undergraduates and post-bachelors. Pairs Research Associates with BMSIS scientists on funded and unfunded projects. Has a formal partnership with NASA Ames Research Center Space Biosciences Division. Entirely remote; open internationally.

### 🎓 [Visiting Scholars Program](https://bmsis.org/visitingscholars/)
For graduate students and professionals in research, science communication, or education who wish to work with a BMSIS affiliate. More advanced than YSP.

### 🌍 [One Flag in Space](https://www.oneflaginspace.org/)
Campaign to promote the Earth Flag (featuring the Blue Marble image) as a symbol of international unity in space exploration. Collects global demonstrations of its use; advocates for displaying it on astronaut suits and space equipment.

### 📡 [SAGANet](https://www.saganet.org/)
Public science communication platform — see [[SAGANet]].

### 📰 [Explainables](https://www.explainables.org/)
Science communication initiative focused on making research accessible.

### 🎓 [Ad Astra Academy](http://adastra.world/)
Educational program — specifics not documented online; likely K-12 science education.

### 🥗 [SALAD](http://saladproject.org/)
Science communication project.

### 🌍 [Earthling's Hub](https://earthlingshub.org/)
Community hub for the broader BMS community.

### 📬 [BMS Insider Newsletter](https://insider.bmsis.org/)
Regular updates on astrobiology, Earth/space sciences, and BMS opportunities.

## Key Publications & Output

BMSIS scientists publish in peer-reviewed academic journals across:
- Astrobiology (journal)
- Origins of Life and Evolution of Biospheres
- International Journal of Astrobiology
- Nature (via collaborators like Sara Walker)

The organization also produces public science communication through SAGANet, BMS Insider newsletter, and Explainables.

## Key Contributions

1. **Distributed research model** — demonstrated that legitimate scientific research can be conducted by a geographically distributed, internationally diverse team with minimal overhead
2. **YSP internship** — trained hundreds of early-career scientists internationally; adopted by NASA Ames
3. **Science communication integration** — made EPO (Education and Public Outreach) a required component of all research activities, not optional
4. **Moral philosophy in space science** — unusual inclusion of philosophical/ethical inquiry alongside hard science
5. **One Flag in Space** — international symbolic campaign for space cooperation

## Why Interesting

Blue Marble Space sits at an unusual intersection: rigorous scientific research + explicit moral philosophy + science communication as core value + global/distributed model. Most research institutes don't include "moral philosophy" as an explicit research area alongside hard science. The distributed, low-overhead model (15% vs. universities' 40–60%) is genuinely innovative for academic research.

For Amber's interests, the main pull is: (1) Sara Walker's board presence connects to assembly theory and life-as-information, which has ALife implications; (2) the few people working at art+science + philosophy intersections within this space community are unusual outliers worth knowing; (3) the model of science communication as integral to research (not bolt-on) resonates with Amber's practice-based approach.

However, the bulk of BMSIS work is traditional astrobiology/space science — planetary habitability, prebiotic chemistry, Mars missions — which is outside Amber's immediate research territory. The connection is through specific individuals and the philosophical framing, not the core mission.
