Podcast co-hosted by [[Holly Herndon]] and [[Mat Dryhurst]] from their Berlin studio. Launched April 30, 2020 (announced via [Pitchfork](https://pitchfork.com/news/holly-herndon-launches-new-podcast-with-mat-dryhurst/)). The defining long-form conversation show at the intersection of AI ethics, creative practice, decentralized internet, and more-than-human futures. A key gathering point for the Berlin-based critical tech-art intelligentsia.

## Mission & Objectives

Interdependence is built around the conviction that technology shapes culture, and culture should shape technology — but only if artists, thinkers, and practitioners are at the table. Holly and Mat interview people working at the **frontier of integrating arts and technology**, specifically those asking uncomfortable ethical and philosophical questions about AI, blockchain, digital rights, and creative sovereignty.

The podcast explicitly frames itself against both techno-utopianism and techno-pessimism — it wants substantive engagement from people who actually build, make, and think critically. Each episode is essentially an extended intellectual conversation (60-90 minutes) between the hosts and a guest.

Named for the concept of **interdependence** — the idea that artists, technologists, and communities are mutually entangled, and that new tech paradigms require new collective governance frameworks. This is the intellectual environment that spawned [[Spawning AI]] and the [[Holly+]] protocol.

## Key Intellectual Themes

The podcast has developed a coherent intellectual framework across its episodes:

1. **Artist sovereignty in the AI era** — the podcast explicitly platforms those building new consent/governance frameworks for creative labor (Spawning, Holly+, haveibeentrained.com)
2. **More-than-human intelligence** — engaging seriously with non-human cognition, cybernetics, animal sensing (Bridle, K Allado-McDowell)
3. **Decentralized / Web3 infrastructure for culture** — blockchain, DAOs, NFTs as potential tools for artist governance, not just speculation (De Filippi, Harm van den Dorpel)
4. **AI aesthetics and language** — the need for new critical vocabulary to understand machine learning's impact on art (patten, Anil)
5. **Permaweird / ambient crisis** — tracking the cultural mood of technological acceleration without declaring crisis prematurely (Rao)
6. **Extitutional forms** — communities that exist outside and between institutions (De Filippi's Extitutional Theory)
