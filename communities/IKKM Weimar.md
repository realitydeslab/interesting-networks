# IKKM Weimar — Internationales Kolleg für Kulturtechnikforschung und Medienphilosophie

**Status: CLOSED (April 2008 – March 2020).** The International Research Institute for Cultural Techniques and Media Philosophy was the premier German humanities research institute for **Kulturtechnik** (cultural techniques) and **Medienphilosophie** (media philosophy) — operating for twelve years at [Bauhaus-Universität Weimar](https://www.uni-weimar.de/). It hosted nearly every significant figure in German media theory over its 12-year life, producing the Kulturtechnik paradigm's most important institutional fruit. Now archived; website at [ikkm-weimar.de](https://www.ikkm-weimar.de/) still accessible.

## Background

The IKKM was founded in April 2008 by media theorists [[Bernhard Siegert]] (chair for Theory and History of Cultural Techniques, Bauhaus-Universität Weimar) and Lorenz Engell (chair for Media Aesthetics). It was one of **ten Käte Hamburger Kollegs** — a prestigious program of the German **Federal Ministry of Education and Research (BMBF)** designed to create internationally connected humanities research hubs, not DFG-funded as often assumed.

The institute was physically housed in the **Palais Dürckheim** in Weimar, a building designed by Belgian Art Nouveau architect Henry van de Velde (who also founded the Weimar Art School that preceded the Bauhaus). This location was symbolic — van de Velde's architecture as itself a cultural technique.

The IKKM operated on the **fellow principle**: each year hosting up to ten internationally renowned scholars (Senior Fellows) freed from their home institutions for 1–2 semesters, plus junior Research Fellows and Short-Term Fellows. Two twelve-year research programmes organized the work.

## Research Programmes

### Programme 1: Cultural Techniques (2008–2014)
Focused on the relationship between humans and things under high-technological conditions, drawing on Agency Theory, Actor-Network-Theory (ANT), Science & Technology Studies (STS), and art anthropology.

Six annual research themes:
- **2008/09:** Hominization and Anthropotechnologies — the Making of Humans
- **2009/10:** Referentialization and Ontogenesis — the Making of Things
- **2010/11:** Semiosis — the Transformation of Objects into Signs
- **2011/12:** Localization — the Production of Sites
- **2012/13:** Synchronization — the Production of the Present
- **2013/14:** Historization — the Production of the Past

### Programme 2: Operative Ontologies (2014–2020)
Switched from *relations* to *operations* — investigating how media-technical operations (not merely networks of actants) constitute different "modes of existence." Drew heavily on Souriau, Haudricourt, Viveiros de Castro, Descola, and Latour.

Six annual research themes organized into three pairs:
- **I. Öffnen und Schließen (Opening and Closing):**
  - 2014/15: Rahmen/Nähen (Framing/Sewing)
  - 2015/16: Erscheinen/Verschwinden (Appearing/Disappearing) [implied]
- **II. Koppeln und Ablösen (Coupling and Releasing):**
  - 2016/17: Verdichten/Streuen (Condensing/Dispersing)
  - 2017/18: Zeigen/Verursachen (Showing/Causing) [implied]
- **III. Wiederholen und Reflektieren (Repeating and Reflecting):**
  - 2018/19: Reproduzieren/Vervielfältigen (Reproducing/Multiplying)
  - 2019/20: Rückwenden/(Selbst-)Reflektieren (Turning Back/Self-Reflecting)

## Key Publications

### Journal: Zeitschrift für Medien- und Kulturforschung (ZfMK)
The IKKM's primary publication organ — a peer-reviewed bi-annual journal for media and cultural research. Each issue themed around IKKM annual topics. Published in German with English summaries. Available via Felix Meiner Verlag and Hamburg University Press.

### IKKM BOOKS (Sternberg Press / Wilhelm Fink)
A book series publishing Fellows' research developed during residency. Key titles include scholarship that defined the cultural techniques turn. Earlier volumes via Sternberg Press, later through Fink/transcript. Examples:
- Works by Siegert on *Cultural Techniques* (expanded as Fordham UP edition)
- Engell on philosophy of television
- Fellows' monographs on their period-specific research topics

### IKKM Lectures (Video/Audio Archive)
Recordings of public lectures by fellows and invited speakers — archived on IKKM website (still accessible as of 2026). A trove for understanding the intellectual trajectory of the institute across 12 years.

## The Kulturtechnik Paradigm

The IKKM was the institutional home of **Kulturtechnik** (*cultural techniques*) as a research paradigm — distinct from both Anglo-American STS and from traditional Kittlerian media archaeology. Key features:

1. **Ontological priority of practices over symbols:** Cultural techniques (writing, reading, counting, drawing, coding, mapping) *produce* the distinctions they seem to merely express (human/animal, sign/thing, inside/outside). Not tools but constitutive operations.

2. **Beyond agency theory:** Went further than ANT (Latour) by asking not just "who acts?" but "how do operations constitute modes of being?" The operative ontology turn (Program 2) addressed this directly.

3. **Material/technical basis of culture:** All cultural distinctions are made through material operations — filing, indexing, sorting, addressing, transmitting — that have their own logics.

4. **Key genealogy:** Leroi-Gourhan → Mauss → Leroi-Gourhan → Foucault → Kittler → Siegert/Engell. Also: Cassirer (symbolic forms), Warburg (image survival), and French anthropology (Haudricourt, Mauss's techniques of the body).

5. **The "mediality" of all operations:** Distinguishing human from animal, inside from outside, presence from absence — all these are mediated distinctions. The medium is not a channel but a constitutive operation.

Scholars associated with the paradigm beyond IKKM founders: Sybille Krämer (FU Berlin/Leuphana), Horst Bredekamp (Humboldt, "Bildakt"), Thomas Macho (Humboldt), Erhard Schüttpelz (Siegen), Cornelia Vismann (legal techniques, deceased 2010), Markus Krajewski (Basel), Geoffrey Winthrop-Young (UBC).

## Why Interesting (for Amber)

The IKKM is **closed** — but its intellectual legacy is essential context for anyone working at the intersection of media philosophy, technology, and cultural theory in a European register. Key reasons to understand it:

1. **Genealogy of concepts Amber uses:** Cultural techniques, operative ontology, media as constitutive (not instrumental) — these concepts are directly relevant to more-than-human design, agent ethology, and protocol studies.

2. **The Kulturtechnik → Cosmotechnics bridge:** Yuk Hui's project of diversifying technology philosophy explicitly builds on the Kulturtechnik tradition while decolonizing it. Understanding IKKM is necessary for understanding where Yuk Hui is coming from.

3. **People network:** Many IKKM fellows (Erich Hörl, Joseph Vogl, Brian Larkin) are in Amber's extended intellectual orbit already.

4. **German-French synthesis:** IKKM systematically brought German media theory into dialogue with French theory (Latour, Descola, Viveiros de Castro) in ways that anticipated STS-philosophy convergences important for Amber's work.

5. **Fellowship model precedent:** The IKKM fellow model — senior scholars freed for 1–2 semesters to pursue a single project in community — is exemplary. Understanding what it produced is useful for thinking about what kinds of institutional structures enable the best humanistic research.

**Note on fellowship applications:** The IKKM is **closed** as of 2020. No new applications possible. Watch for successor programs in the Käte Hamburger Kolleg network.
