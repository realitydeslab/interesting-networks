# IKKM Weimar — Internationales Kolleg für Kulturtechnikforschung und Medienphilosophie

The International Research Institute for Cultural Techniques and Media Philosophy at [Bauhaus-Universität Weimar](https://www.uni-weimar.de/). The premier German research institute for **Kulturtechnik** (cultural techniques) and **Medienphilosophie** (media philosophy) — the intersection of philosophy, history of science, media studies, and the theory of technical artefacts. Founded 2009, DFG-funded. One of the most prestigious fellows programmes in German humanities.

## Background

The IKKM was established as part of the German Excellence Initiative to consolidate a distinctly German approach to cultural techniques (Kulturtechniken) — the historical and philosophical study of how technical practices (writing, reading, counting, drawing, coding) constitute culture. The Kulturtechnik paradigm emerged from the post-Kittler media theory environment and is associated with scholars like Sybille Krämer, Horst Bredekamp, Bernhard Siegert, and Lorenz Engell.

The IKKM runs a prestigious **fellowship programme** for established international researchers (Senior Fellows) and offers postdoc and doctoral positions. Fellowship periods are typically 3-12 months.

## Key Fellows (sample)

- **Lorenz Engell** — media aesthetics (resident at Weimar)
- **Bernhard Siegert** — cultural techniques (resident at Weimar)
- **Joseph Vogl** — media history and theory
- Many international fellows from France, UK, US

## Key Concepts
- **Kulturtechnik** (cultural techniques) — reading, writing, counting, drawing as constitutive of cultural order (not just reflective of it)
- **Medienphilosophie** — philosophy of media as philosophy proper
- Connection to the Weimar art/design tradition (Bauhaus) and to the Goethe-era Weimar classical heritage

## Why Interesting
