# BioFi Project

Framework and movement for **Bioregional Finance** — organizing capital flows around bioregional regeneration rather than political/economic boundaries. Published *The BioFi Book* (2024). Launched by Samantha Power (former World Bank sustainable finance consultant; no relation to the diplomat).

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-27:** Stub created from Regen Foundation deep research.

## Key People
- **Samantha Power** — Director. Former World Bank sustainable finance consultant.

## Topics
- **Bioregional Finance Facilities (BFFs)** — Grassroots financial institutions emerging from bioregional organizing; make community projects legible to larger financial systems
- **Bioregionalism** — Organizing social/economic life around watersheds and ecosystem boundaries rather than political borders
- **Multi-capital** — Beyond financial capital; integrating ecological, social, cultural capital
- **Mechanism design** — Eco-credit, complementary currency, and other financial instruments for bioregional ends

## Key Publication
- **[*The BioFi Book*](https://www.biofi.earth/biofi-book)** (2024) — Framework for prioritizing ecosystem regeneration in economics; introduces financial instruments; case studies

## Ecosystem Connections
- **[[Regen Foundation]]** — Closely allied; Austin Wade Smith participates in BioFi events
- **Regenerate Cascadia** — BFF prototype in Cascadia bioregion
- **Salmon Returns** — Finance ecosystem for Salmon Nation bioregion
- **Kwaxala** — First Nation's reverse logging/Centree eco-credit initiative
- **Amazon Sacred Headwaters Alliance** — Partner

## Events
- **[Cascadia BioFi Conference](https://www.cascadiabiofi.org/)** (inaugural spring 2025, Georgetown Steam Plant, Seattle) — 200 attendees; featured Austin Wade Smith, Regenerate Cascadia, Kwaxala, Salmon Returns

## Why Interesting
BioFi is one of the most concrete attempts to build new financial infrastructure that makes ecosystems legible as economic participants. The field bridges crypto/DAO technology with Indigenous sovereignty, bioregional organizing, and regenerative agriculture. Companion ecosystem to Regen Foundation's more-than-human governance work. Featured in [Noema Magazine](https://www.noemamag.com/inside-the-push-to-rebuild-society-around-ecosystems/).
