# BioFi Project

Framework and movement for **Bioregional Finance** — organizing capital flows around bioregional regeneration rather than political/economic boundaries. Published *The BioFi Book* (June 2024), which became an international bestseller and was translated into Spanish. Fiscally sponsored project of BFI (501(c)(3)).

## Mission & Objectives

BioFi proposes **Bioregional Financing Facilities (BFFs)** as a new institutional template for connecting capital pools with regenerative projects organized around ecological boundaries (watersheds, biodiversity zones) rather than political ones. The project:

1. **Decentralizes financial resource governance** — pushing capital allocation decisions to place-based communities
2. **Designs project portfolios for systemic change** — portfolios of regenerative initiatives, not single projects
3. **Transitions to regenerative economy** — beyond "closing the nature finance gap" (which risks replicating extractive finance with green labels)

The core insight: regenerative projects led by people with deep relationships to place are often **illegible to capital holders** — too small, too informal, too relational. BFFs are the connective tissue to make them legible without colonizing them.

Four BFF templates proposed:
- **Bioregional Trust** — grant-making/holding structures
- **Bioregional Venture Studio** — incubating regenerative enterprises
- **Bioregional Investment Company** — equity/debt investment in regenerative businesses
- **Bioregional Bank** — full banking infrastructure for a bioregion

## Key Contributions

- **Theoretical framework**: First comprehensive articulation of "Bioregional Financing Facilities" as a scalable institutional template (distinct from ad hoc regenerative finance)
- **Template library**: Four distinct BFF templates (Trust, Venture Studio, Investment Company, Bank) with detailed implementation guidance
- **Community of practice**: Active global CoP with Google Calendar and Luma calendar linking bioregionalists, finance professionals, Indigenous leaders, and DAO/crypto practitioners
- **Cascadia BioFi Conference** (spring 2025): First dedicated bioregional finance conference; bridged Indigenous sovereignty, conventional impact finance, and crypto/web3 communities

## Core Concepts

- **Bioregionalism** — Organizing social/economic life around watersheds and ecosystem boundaries rather than political borders. Emerged 1970s (Peter Berg, Planet Drum Foundation); waned ~2000; reviving 2020s with tech infrastructure
- **Multi-capital** — Beyond financial capital; integrating ecological, social, cultural, and "living" capital
- **Mechanism design for regeneration** — Eco-credit, complementary currency, quadratic voting, conviction voting, tokenized ecosystems
- **Illegibility problem** — Small, informal, relational regenerative projects are structurally excluded from capital; BFFs solve the translation problem
- **"Great wealth transfer"** — Baby boomer wealth redistribution as a key opportunity window for redirecting capital to bioregional ends

## Why Interesting

BioFi is one of the most concrete attempts to build financial infrastructure that treats ecosystems as participants rather than resources. It bridges:
1. **Bioregional organizing** (1970s ecological counterculture) + **web3/DAO governance** (2020s decentralized finance) + **Indigenous sovereignty**
2. **Legibility problem as mechanism design challenge** — how to make informal, relational, place-based projects legible to capital without colonizing them
3. **"Aikido finance"** — not fighting extractive capitalism but building parallel institutions that make the old model obsolete (Fuller's "build a new model" strategy)

This connects to Amber's interest in protocol design, more-than-human governance, and the question of how non-human actors gain standing in social/economic systems.
