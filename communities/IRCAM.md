# IRCAM (Institut de Recherche et Coordination Acoustique/Musique)

One of the world's largest public research centers dedicated to musical creation and scientific research. Founded 1977 by Pierre Boulez at Centre Pompidou, Paris. Over 160 collaborators. Directed by Frank Madlener. Hosts UMR9912 STMS research lab (IRCAM-CNRS-Sorbonne University).

Work at intersection of acoustics, electronic music composition, real-time signal processing, AI, and artistic creation. Sister institution to ZKM in the European media-art research landscape — joint residency programme with [[Hertzlab ZKM]].

## Why Interesting
Joint residency partner with ZKM Hertzlab — path to both institutions simultaneously. IRCAM's tradition of composer-technologist-scientist collaboration is directly relevant to Amber's computational creativity work. The STMS lab (Signal, Systems, and Acoustics) does cutting-edge research on music AI, machine listening, interactive performance. Not just sound: real-time computer systems for interactive installations.

## Programmes & Entry Points
- **Joint ZKM | IRCAM Residency** — with [[Hertzlab ZKM]]; proposal-based; acoustics + visual media + software
- **ManiFeste** — annual festival of contemporary music + digital arts; open to artists and academics
- **Cursus** — annual composition + computer music training (1-year intensive): very competitive, prestigious; for composers using electronics
- **Research Grants** — for academic-artistic research at IRCAM/STMS lab
- URL for opportunities: <https://www.ircam.fr/>
