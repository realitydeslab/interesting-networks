# IRCAM (Institut de Recherche et Coordination Acoustique/Musique)

The world's preeminent public research center for musical creation and scientific research. Founded 1977 by Pierre Boulez at the Centre Pompidou, Paris. Over 160 collaborators. Directed by **Frank Madlener**. Hosts the UMR9912 **STMS lab** (Science and Technology of Music and Sound — joint with CNRS, Sorbonne University, French Ministry of Culture). The model institution where composer-technologist-scientist collaboration was institutionalized and legitimized globally. If there's a canon of art-science institutional architecture, IRCAM wrote it.

## Mission & Objectives

IRCAM exists at the intersection of **scientific research**, **technological development**, and **contemporary music creation**. The founding insight: specific problems in contemporary composition generate original scientific advances that extend far beyond music. And conversely, science feeds musical expression. This bidirectional loop — composition as research, research as composition — is IRCAM's operating principle.

Three research axes frame everything:
1. **The Sound Workshop** — signal processing, analysis-synthesis, acoustics
2. **The Musical Body** — embodied interaction, gesture, movement, perception
3. **Creative Dynamics** — AI, machine learning, generative systems, musical representations

## Key Software / Technological Contributions

IRCAM has produced several foundational music technology tools, many now industry/field standards:
- **Max/MSP** (co-developed; licensed to Cycling '74) — visual programming language for interactive music/media. Ubiquitous in media art worldwide.
- **OpenMusic** — computer-aided composition environment
- **Spat~** — spatial audio rendering (widely used in installation art and concert halls)
- **SuperVP** — phase vocoder, high-quality audio transformation
- **Audiosculpt** — audio analysis-resynthesis
- **IRCAM Amplify** — commercial/tech transfer arm; licenses audio AI to industry
- **ANTESCOFO** — score following system for live electronics-musician interaction

## Events & Programmes

### ManiFeste Festival
Annual festival (May–June, Paris). "A festival of contemporary music and live performance — a manifesto for learned, desirable, popular art." Combines concerts, installations, academic events. 2025: May 23 – June 28. 2026 edition incoming.
URL: [https://manifeste.ircam.fr/](https://manifeste.ircam.fr/)

### ManiFeste-2026 Festival Academy
New tracks for 2026:
- **In Situ Dance Film Composition** — site-specific, cross-disciplinary
- **In Situ Electronic Composition**
- **Solo and Electronics** — performer + live electronics
- **ULYSSES Ensemble** — European ensemble-in-residence network
Application URL: [https://www.ircam.fr/](https://www.ircam.fr/appels-candidatures/) (check annually)

### Cursus — Composition and Computer Music
Annual 1-year intensive programme. The most prestigious computer music composition training in the world. Composers work with IRCAM's tools and researchers. Very competitive, international. Application: see [IRCAM calls for applications](https://www.ircam.fr/).

### Master ATIAM (Acoustics, Signal Processing, Computer Science applied to Music)
Joint Master between Sorbonne Université and Télécom Paris. Research-oriented. The academic pipeline into STMS lab.

### AIMove Master
Interdisciplinary master program linking AI, movement/dance, and music. Directly addresses the body-AI intersection.

### Sound Design Master's Program
Professional training in spatial audio, interactive sound design.

### Forum Vertigo
Innovative art-science-technology processes; annual symposium on intersections of arts, sciences, technologies and society. Closely related to IRCAM's R&D transfer and dissemination. URL: [https://forumvertigo.ircam.fr/](https://forumvertigo.ircam.fr/)

### STMS Seminars
- **"Why Write Music?"** — annual seminar series on the aesthetics and politics of contemporary composition
- **Les Rencontres de STMS** / **Les Mercredis de STMS** — regular research seminars, open to visitors

### Residency Programme — Artistic Research
IRCAM hosts composers and artists in studio; a dedicated "Artistic Residencies Blog" documents the process. Residencies are proposal-based.

### Joint ZKM | IRCAM Residency (via Hertzlab ZKM)
[[Hertzlab ZKM]] and IRCAM run a joint residency program for artist-researchers working across sound, visual media, and software. Entry via either institution. Proposal-based. See [[Hertzlab ZKM]] for more detail on the ZKM side. This is the most direct dual entry point — one residency, two institutions (Paris + Karlsruhe).
