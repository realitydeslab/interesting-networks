# NEW INC

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Full deep research. **Y13 Open Call is LIVE NOW** — closes early March 2026. Amber should seriously consider applying to Creative Science or Extended Realities track. New purpose-built space opening in expanded New Museum building (OMA design) on March 21, 2026.

The world's first museum-led cultural incubator. NEW INC is where serious art-tech practitioners go to stop being just practitioners and start building sustainable practices — with a real studio, 130+ mentors, and a cohort of 50-60 peers working across the same weird intersection of art, code, science, and design. The vibe is: experimental but functional; interdisciplinary but structured; critical but not anti-market. This is not a traditional residency — it's more like a co-working space plus MBA for cultural workers who refuse to fit into existing categories.

---

## Mission & Objectives

"The future of new media, art, design, science, and technology where access to mentorship, space, and resources create pathways for experimentation."

Conceived as the New Museum's commitment to new art and new ideas beyond gallery walls. NEW INC runs a values-driven yearlong membership program: workspace + mentorship + peer community + professional development, all in one. The goal is sustainable practices, not just interesting projects. As of 2026 it is in its 12th year.

**Founded 2014** by [Lisa Phillips](https://www.newmuseum.org/staff/lisa-phillips/) (New Museum's Toby Devan Lewis Director) and Karen Wong (then Deputy Director). First of its kind — museum as incubator host rather than passive exhibitor of finished work.

**Scale (as of 2026):**
- 730+ alumni total across 12 years
- ~50–64 new members per year
- 130+ mentors annually
- $28.9M raised by NEW INC members
- 384 businesses created or retained
- 686 jobs created to date
- Total member database: 577 people across Years 1–12

## Program Structure

### How It Works
- **Duration:** Yearlong, September–August
- **Format:** Hybrid/opt-in. Members not required to be NYC-based full-time; programming is mostly hybrid
- **Engagement:** 4–8 hours/week participation (events, workshops, mentor sessions)
- **Workspace:** In-person workspace in New Museum's new purpose-built space (opening March 21, 2026 in the OMA-designed expansion at 235 Bowery). Media Lab + Fabrication Lab (3D printers, laser cutters)
- **Cost:** $75/month + $150 setup fee (subsidies available for BIPOC, queer, trans, nonbinary, disabled members)
- **Teams:** Accept individuals and teams up to 4

### Member Tracks (Year 12, 2025–26)
Each track has a dedicated Mentor-in-Residence and partner programming:
1. **Art & Code** — partnership with [[Rhizome]]
2. **Creative Science** — art-science intersections
3. **Extended Realities** — XR/immersive media, partnership with [[Onassis ONX]]
4. **Cooperative Studies** — (renamed **Launchpad** for Y13)
5. **Social Architecture** — community, space, social design

### Year 13 Tracks (2026–27)
- Art & Code (Rhizome)
- Creative Science
- Extended Realities (Onassis ONX)
- **Launchpad** (new name, replacing Cooperative Studies)
- Social Architecture

### Track Categories (Historical)
Past track names include: Art & Code, Collective Abundance, Community, Creative Experiments, Creative Science, Cultural Futures, E.A.T., Extended Realities, Future Memory, Hybrid Practice, Ideas for the City, Immersive Experiences, Museum Technology, Social Architecture, Social Impact, XR: Bodies in Space. The track system started around Y7 and has evolved each year.

### What Members Receive
- **Workspace:** In-person space at NEW INC's offices (dedicated desks at additional cost)
- **Dedicated mentor:** One mentor assigned to member/team for the year
- **Track Mentor-in-Residence:** Subject-matter specialist for the full cohort
- **Professional development:** Full year of tailored programming
- **Track partner access:** Special programming from Rhizome, Onassis ONX, etc.
- **Community:** Members-only virtual + in-person events
- **Network:** Access to 130+ mentor pool for office hours

## Key Contributions

NEW INC has made several significant contributions to the art-tech world:

1. **The museum-incubator model** — Created the template for cultural institutions to host creative incubators. Since replicated by others globally, but NEW INC remains the original and most influential.

2. **Onassis ONX (co-founded 2020)** — Co-founded [Onassis ONX](https://www.onx.studio/) with the Onassis Foundation as a dedicated XR accelerator/studio. Now one of the most important XR production hubs for artists globally, with studios in NYC and Athens. Members and fellows work at intersection of AI, immersive media, spatial computing, gaming.

3. **Cross-sector mentorship infrastructure** — Built a network of 130+ mentors/year who bridge art, tech, business, academia — rare in both the art world and startup ecosystem.

4. **Creative Science track** — Pioneering track that explicitly supports art-science researchers, providing institutional home for a practice type that typically falls between academic and art world funding structures.

5. **Alumni network density** — With 730+ alumni over 12 years, has produced a significant cohort of mid-career art-tech practitioners who now hold institutional positions globally.

6. **Hybrid/non-NYC-exclusive model** — Made a major art-tech program geographically accessible without full relocation requirement.

## Why Interesting

NEW INC sits at a rare intersection: **institutional legitimacy** (New Museum's 50-year track record) + **operational infrastructure** (real space, real mentors, real community) + **genuine tolerance for experimental work** that doesn't fit commercial or academic categories.

For Amber's research taste profile (from research-taste.md), NEW INC is interesting on multiple axes:

**Art-Science bridging:** The Creative Science track is one of the few programmatic homes for exactly what Amber does — using artistic methods to investigate scientific phenomena (XR + embodied cognition + biological systems). This is rare institutional support for a practice that typically gets rejected by both art grants (too scientific) and science grants (too artistic).

**XR/Immersive + critical lens:** Extended Realities track isn't just "let's make VR" — it's XR with curatorial and critical grounding. The Onassis ONX partnership brings Greek/European conceptual tradition. This aligns with Amber's interest in XR as philosophical/phenomenological medium, not just technical novelty.

**Networks of practice:** With 730+ alumni across 12 years, NEW INC has become a generative node for the art-tech field. Being in this network means knowing people building the next decade's institutions, festivals, labs. The cohort model means Amber would have 50-60 serious peers working at her same intersection.

**Mentor access:** 130+ mentors/year from across tech, art, architecture, academia. This is unusually cross-sector — not just art world OR tech industry but genuinely both. Figures like Fiona Raby (design futures) and Dana Karwas (Yale CCAM) represent exactly the senior practitioners Amber should know.

**NYC as platform:** Physical presence in NYC remains uniquely important for the art-tech world. NEW INC provides a home base without requiring full relocation. For someone with Amber's international profile, this is exactly the right kind of NYC foothold.

**Historical moment:** The new OMA-designed building opening March 2026 marks a significant institutional upgrade. This is NOT a declining institution — it's growing. Being in Y13 means being part of the first cohort in the new space.
