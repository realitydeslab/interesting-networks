# HKU School of Computing and Data Science

Brand-new school (est. July 1, 2024) at the University of Hong Kong, formed by merging the Department of Computer Science, Department of Statistics and Actuarial Science, and a newly created AI department. Directed by **Prof. Yi Ma** (Chair of AI). ~60 faculty, ~800 postgraduate students, 15 academic programmes. The most ambitious CS-AI-Stats integration in Hong Kong, with a dual-city structure — HK + a new Shanghai campus in Zhangjiang Hi-Tech Park. Strategic significance: HKU CDS sits at the intersection of HK international finance culture and Mainland China tech infrastructure, actively recruiting globally via the [HKU-100 IDS Scholars programme](https://datascience.hku.hk/).

Affiliated research arm: [HKU Musketeers Foundation Institute of Data Science (HKU IDS)](https://datascience.hku.hk/) — a separate institute housing interdisciplinary scholars co-hosted with Philosophy, Psychology, Urban Planning, etc. This is where the interesting people are.

## Mission & Objectives

HKU CDS was formed to respond to the growing importance of computing and AI in the 21st century. Its stated ambition: to combine theoretical rigor (Statistics, Algorithms, Quantum Computing) with applied AI impact and industry proximity — especially via the Shanghai campus embedded in Zhangjiang, China's answer to Silicon Valley. The Musketeers Foundation IDS arm operates as a cross-disciplinary attractor — recruiting scholars who bridge CS with Philosophy, Psychology, Urban Studies, Law, etc.

Key strategic facts:
- Ranked in [QS World Rankings in Data Science & AI](https://www.topuniversities.com/)
- HKU-100 Initiative — aggressive global recruitment of IDS scholars
- Shanghai campus creates formal HK-mainland bridge (unique in the Hong Kong university landscape)
- New AI Literacy course (SCDS1001) rolled out to *all* HKU undergrads — signals institution-wide AI normalization agenda

## Why Interesting

HKU CDS is interesting not because it's already doing critical/cultural AI work — it largely isn't — but because of what it *will become*. It's a brand new institution with:
1. **Significant resources** (HKU-100 recruitment, Musketeers Foundation endowment)
2. **A genuine interdisciplinary IDS arm** that has already recruited a philosopher (Babic) and urban planner (Kirkley)
3. **A Shanghai campus** in the middle of China's tech-industrial heartland — fieldwork site for machine behavior research
4. **A director (Yi Ma)** who built ShanghaiTech and knows how to create institutions that bridge China and the West
5. **Structural gap**: almost no one in the school is doing critical AI, machine behavior as science, or cyborg sociology — which means there's space to bring something that doesn't exist yet

The window is now. HKU CDS is in identity formation. An early engagement with Babic, or a visiting role at the Shanghai campus, could shape what the school becomes as it builds humanistic AI capacity.
