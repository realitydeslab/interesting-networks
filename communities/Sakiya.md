# Sakiya – Art Science Agriculture

Artist residency and experimental institution in Ram, near Ramallah, Palestine. Brings together artists, scientists, and farmers to think through land, ecology, and sovereignty. One of the most distinctive residency programs globally because of its political-ecological focus: the land itself is the site of inquiry.

## Notable Fellows
- [[Agnes Cameron]] — artist fellow (2019); produced Permaculture Network simulation
- [[Gary Zhexi Zhang]] — artist fellow (2019); later held Berggruen Institute fellowship

## Why Interesting
Sakiya is not just a residency — it's a political practice. Working on Palestinian land means every ecological question is also a question of sovereignty, dispossession, and survival. Artists who come here have to reckon with what "land data" means under occupation. This produced some of the most politically serious simulation work in recent years (Cameron/Zhang's Permaculture Network).

Berggruen Institute held a fellowship at Sakiya (Gary Zhexi Zhang). Medialab Prado also has ties via the fud project. Sakiya sits at the intersection of Palestinian cultural infrastructure, international art-science programs, and ecological thinking — an unusually rich node.
