# Stanford RegLab

The Regulation, Evaluation, and Governance Lab at Stanford Law School. Directed by Daniel E. Ho (William Benjamin Scott and Luna M. Scott Professor of Law, also Professor of Political Science and CS by courtesy). Focuses on high-impact demonstration projects of data science and machine learning in public policy — specifically: how do you evaluate whether regulations are effective, and how do AI/ML systems intersect with administrative law?

## Why Discovered
Emerged from CRFM Stanford deep research. Daniel E. Ho sits on the FMTI advisory board and co-advises postdocs at CRFM (notably A. Feder Cooper). RegLab represents the law/governance side of CRFM's policy infrastructure — the institution that translates CRFM's transparency work into legal and regulatory frameworks.

## Key Figure
**Daniel E. Ho** — Director. National AI Advisory Committee (NAIAC), advising the White House on AI policy. Senior Advisor on Responsible AI at US Department of Labor. Special Advisor to ABA Task Force on Law and AI. FMTI advisory board. Scholarship on administrative law, regulatory policy, antidiscrimination.
