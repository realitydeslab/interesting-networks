# Dark Mountain Project

Arts and cultural community responding to ecological collapse. Writers, artists, and practitioners working at the intersection of ecological crisis, storytelling, and cultural transformation. Based in UK (founded 2009). Produces an annual journal (*Dark Mountain*) and runs gatherings.

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-03-03:** Stub created during Rewilding deep research. Identified as potentially interesting arts-ecology community for Amber.

## Mission & Objectives
Founded by writers Paul Kingsnorth and Dougald Hine, Dark Mountain rejected "green" optimism and asked: what do we do with art, culture, and meaning when ecological collapse is underway? Produces uncivilized writing — literature that moves beyond the myth of human progress.

## Key People
- **Paul Kingsnorth** — novelist, founder. Moved to Ireland, increasingly spiritual/Orthodox Christian direction. Website: [paulkingsnorth.net](https://paulkingsnorth.net/)
- **Dougald Hine** — co-founder. Lectures, facilitates, writes on collapse and community.

## Key Publications & Output
- [*Dark Mountain* journal](https://dark-mountain.net/shop/) — annual anthology, art + writing
- Online essays and interviews

## Events & Programmes
- Annual gatherings in UK/Europe
- Writing workshops

## For Amber
- **Why interesting:** One of the few UK communities where serious artists and thinkers work with ecological collapse as their central material — not advocacy but cultural processing. Rewilding + ALife + mortality themes all present implicitly.
- **How to engage:** Read the journal; attend a gathering; submit writing or visual work.
- **Proximity:** UK-based, periodic gatherings.
- **Tone:** Somewhat melancholic/uncivilized aesthetic — different from Amber's speculative optimism, but interesting friction.

## Why Interesting
Dark Mountain does what Amber's research gestures toward: takes ecological entanglement and multispecies thinking into art practice, without the techno-optimism that dominates the HCI/design field. A grounding community for more-than-human thinking.
