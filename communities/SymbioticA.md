# SymbioticA

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Full deep research. Expanded from stub to gold standard. History, programs, key people, exhibitions, current institutional status.
- **2022 (Oct):** UWA issued 10-day notice to SymbioticA to "justify continued funding." Global outcry: 11,000-signature petition, testimonials from Orlan, Eduardo Kac, Kira O'Reilly and dozens of major artists and scientists. UWA ultimately reversed the decision.
- **2022 (post):** SymbioticA relocated from School of Human Sciences to being **hosted by the Art Gallery of Western Australia** (AGWA). Oron Catts is now also Academic Lead/Director of UWA's Institute of Advanced Studies.
- **2025 (Feb):** Research output still active — *Ex Utero* exhibition (Ionat Zurr, with C. Millett and C. White).

**The world's first and most influential biological arts research laboratory.** A one-of-a-kind institution: artists doing actual wet biology inside a university science department, not representing it. Founded 2000. Site of global firsts in cultured meat, lab-grown leather, neural organoid art. After surviving a defunding attempt in 2022, it continues to operate — now with more institutional visibility than ever.

## Mission & Objectives

**Mission:** To facilitate hands-on, curiosity-based artistic research into the life sciences. "Artists actively use the tools and technologies of science not just to comment about them, but also to explore their possibilities." 

**Name etymology:** Science + Symbiosis + Art — the three-way mutualism that defines the lab's practice.

**Core philosophy:** Unlike art-science collaborations where artists and scientists work in parallel, SymbioticA places artists *inside* scientific laboratories, training them in actual experimental technique. Residents learn sterile technique, tissue culture, cell biology, neuroscience. The resulting work is not art *about* science but art *made from* science.

**Approach to research:** Curiosity-based, non-utilitarian, philosophically motivated. Free from the deliverable-driven culture of grant science, while still complying with ethics regulations. This creates a unique space: the rigor of scientific method without the instrumental pressure of application.

## History & Founding

**1996:** [[Oron Catts]] and [[Ionat Zurr]] begin working as artists-in-residence in the School of Anatomy and Human Biology at UWA, and at the [Lions Eye Institute](https://www.lei.org.au/), Perth. Their **Tissue Culture & Art Project (TC&A)** is established — early explorations growing tissue over polymer scaffolds as artistic material.

**1998–1999:** TC&A work gains international attention. Catts and Zurr, alongside biologist **Professor Miranda Grounds** (muscle repair research) and neuroscientist **Professor Stuart Bunt** (spinal cord injury research), begin planning a permanent laboratory facility.

**2000:** **SymbioticA officially founded** — "The Art & Science Collaborative Research Laboratory" within the School of Anatomy and Human Biology, UWA. The co-founders are Catts, Grounds, and Bunt. The lab is embedded in scientific infrastructure (Level 2 bio lab), not an art faculty. This architectural decision — placing the art lab inside the science department — is strategically decisive.

**2000–2001:** Catts and Zurr depart for a [Harvard Medical School](https://hms.harvard.edu/) residency at the Tissue Engineering and Organ Fabrication Laboratory (Massachusetts General Hospital, under Joseph Vacanti). This enables the **Pig Wings** and **Stone Age of Biology** projects — the first works requiring actual tissue engineering infrastructure at scale.

**2002:** First major curated exhibition: **BIOFEEL: Art and Biology**, Biennale of Electronic Arts Perth ([BEAP](https://www.beap.org/)), curated by Oron Catts. Establishes SymbioticA as a curatorial as well as research institution.

**2003:** **[[Disembodied Cuisine]]** performed at *L'Art Biotech*, [Le Lieu Unique](https://www.lalieuunique.com/), Nantes — the world's first public performance of consuming lab-grown tissue. Catts and Zurr grow frog skeletal muscle and serve it to gallery visitors, while the living frog that provided the biopsy remains alive in a tank.

**2004:** **BioDifference: The Political Ecology Exhibition**, BEAP 2004 — curated by Catts and Zurr. Explores biological art and interconnectivity of living systems.

**2007:** SymbioticA wins the **inaugural [Prix Ars Electronica Golden Nica for Hybrid Arts](https://ars.electronica.art/prix/en/)** — an unprecedented institutional award (the whole lab, not an individual work). This defines "Hybrid Arts" as a canonical category in media art. Oron Catts curates a **SymbioticA Showcase** at Ars Electronica that year as a result.

**2007:** **Still, Living** — major bio-art exhibition curated by [Jens Hauser](http://www.labo-lhybride.org/en/), produced by SymbioticA for BEAP.

**2008:** SymbioticA becomes the **Centre of Excellence in Biological Arts**. WA Premier's Science Award.

**2009–2010:** Ongoing international reach: Catts and Zurr take visiting professorships at [Royal College of Art](https://www.rca.ac.uk/) (London, Design Interactions programme).

**2010:** SymbioticA named a **Research Centre within UWA**, cementing its institutional standing.

**2012–2013:** Catts and Zurr establish **Biofilia – Base for Biological Arts** at [Aalto University](https://www.aalto.fi/), Helsinki — proof that the SymbioticA model can be exported internationally. First biological art lab in the Nordic region.

**2013:** **Semipermeable (+)** at [Powerhouse Museum](https://www.maas.museum/powerhouse/), Sydney — curated by Catts. Retrospective of SymbioticA projects from 2000.

**2015:** **DeMonstrable** at Lawrence Wilson Art Gallery, UWA — opened by [Paola Antonelli](https://www.moma.org/research-and-learning/departments/curatorial-affairs/paola-antonelli) (MoMA). The 20th anniversary of the Earmouse — a nexus of bio-art history.

**2015:** **Futile Labor** at John Curtin Gallery, Perth — with Ionat Zurr, Chris Salter, Oron Catts.

**2018:** Major Perth exhibition season — **Biomess** at Art Gallery of WA; **HyperPrometheus** at Perth Institute of Contemporary Arts (PICA); **The Mess We're In** at Old Customs House, Fremantle.

**2019:** Catts and Zurr invited guest artists at **[Arts at CERN](https://arts.cern/)**, Geneva.

**2022 (Feb):** **Sunlight, Soil & Shit (De)Cycle (3SDC)** at PS Gallery, Fremantle — collaboration with Steve Berrick. Contest food systems, AgTech critique. Symposium on contestable food futures.

**2022 (Oct):** UWA issues defunding notice. Global petition reaches 11,000 signatures. Letters of support from Orlan, Kira O'Reilly, Critical Art Ensemble, Eduardo Kac, and hundreds more. SymbioticA survives — relocated to partnership with Art Gallery of Western Australia.

**2025 (Feb):** *Ex Utero* (Ionat Zurr, with C. Millett and C. White) — ongoing active research output.

### Theoretical Contributions
- **The "Semi-Living" concept** — philosophical category for entities that are simultaneously biological (living cells) and constructed (polymer scaffolds, artificial environments). Not alive in a conventional sense, not dead, not robotic. Forces new ethics of care.
- **"Neolifism"** — critique of the way "life" is instrumentalized as raw material and rhetorical tool in biotechnology marketing.
- **"Contestable futures"** — methodological concept: using art to materialize futures that science is creating but not ethically examining. Cultured meat, victimless leather, chimeric organisms.

## Key Contributions

**SymbioticA's world firsts:**
1. **First university-based biological arts research laboratory** (2000) — the model that inspired dozens of subsequent programmes worldwide.
2. **First lab-grown meat performance** — Disembodied Cuisine (2003, Nantes): cultured frog steak consumed in a gallery, a decade before Silicon Valley started funding "cellular agriculture."
3. **First tissue-engineered clothing** — Victimless Leather (2004): a stitchless jacket grown from mouse stem cells. Exhibited at MoMA (2008).
4. **First neural organoid art** — Guy Ben-Ary's cellF (2015): living neurons as a musical instrument.
5. **First biological arts Master's programme** — MBiolArts, UWA, instigated by Ionat Zurr.
6. **Field-naming:** Won the inaugural Prix Ars Electronica Golden Nica for Hybrid Arts (2007) — the award that named and legitimized "biological hybrid arts" as a category.

**Intellectual contributions:**
- The "Semi-Living" as a philosophical concept that precedes and enriches current debates about AI consciousness, synthetic biology, and lab-grown meat ethics.
- Demonstrating that artistic research can produce *actual* scientific and philosophical novelty, not just commentary.
- Proving that a small lab in Perth, Western Australia can be globally influential through sheer conceptual rigor and institutional networking.

## For Amber

### Why Interesting
**SymbioticA is the most relevant existing institution for several of Amber's research threads at once:**

1. **More-than-human design**: The Semi-Living entities are literally the most concrete realization of more-than-human design anyone has produced — living, non-human, aesthetically intentional. Catts and Zurr invented the philosophical vocabulary *before* more-than-human became a design discourse term.

2. **Bioethics as design**: SymbioticA approaches ethics not as external constraint but as the material of the work. Every piece IS an ethical argument materialized. This is the version of design ethics that has philosophical rigor.

3. **Art-science integration**: The strongest existing model of genuine artist-in-laboratory practice. Not collaboration from a distance but embedded, skilled co-participation.

4. **Posthuman / speculative practice**: Every project at SymbioticA runs a speculative scenario (what if meat could be grown without killing? What if leather were alive?) and then materializes it. This is speculative design executed with biological rigor.

5. **ALife connection**: Guy Ben-Ary's cellF and MEART, the neural organoid work — these sit directly at the intersection of biological and artificial life. SymbioticA touches ALIFE research through actual living neural networks used as artistic systems.

6. **[[Lu Yang]] connection**: Lu Yang was a SymbioticA resident — direct bridge into Amber's existing network.

## Why Interesting

**The canonical institution of biological arts.** SymbioticA is to bio-art what [[Santa Fe Institute]] is to complexity science: the founding institution, the laboratory model, the community hub, and the intellectual generator. Every major figure working in biological art has either come through SymbioticA or been shaped by its existence.

**Relevance to Amber:**

1. **More-than-human**: The Semi-Living entities are the most philosophically rigorous materialization of more-than-human design anywhere. SymbioticA didn't just discuss non-human life — it made art from it, grew it, displayed it, killed it (by accident), and forced institutions to deal with the ethical implications.

2. **Speculative design ante litteram**: Victimless Leather, cultured frog steak, lab-grown pig wings — these are all speculative design scenarios materialized as wet biology, executed a decade before Dunne & Raby formalized speculative design in London.

3. **Critical institutions model**: SymbioticA is the strongest existing proof that an arts institution embedded in a science department can generate genuine critical and intellectual novelty. This is a model Amber should understand deeply.

4. **ALife adjacency**: The neural organoid work (cellF, MEART), the interest in agency and movement in semi-living material, and SymbioticA's relationship with the ALIFE community make this a key node for Amber's ALife interests.

5. **The 2022 defunding attempt as signal**: The fact that 11,000 people signed a petition to save SymbioticA shows how large and committed its international community is. This is a community with genuine loyalty and sense of shared mission. Worth being part of.

**Edge of chaos assessment:** The biological arts field is NOT fast-moving in the digital sense — tissue culture is slow. But institutionally, it is at an inflection point: the SymbioticA model is being copied (Biofilia, various Science Gallery bio-art labs), cellular agriculture has become billion-dollar industry (vindicating the aesthetic provocation), and the ethics of "semi-living" entities is becoming a live political question. The field SymbioticA built is entering mainstream relevance.
