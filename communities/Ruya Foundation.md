# Ruya Foundation

Baghdad-based cultural foundation founded by historian and cultural entrepreneur **Tamara Chalabi**. Mission: to support contemporary art in Iraq and facilitate international artistic exchange with Iraqi artists and communities. One of the most important art organizations working in a conflict/post-conflict context, using cultural production as a form of historical documentation and social reconstruction.

## Key People
- **Tamara Chalabi** — founder/chair; historian (Harvard-trained); daughter of Iraqi politician Ahmed Chalabi; based between London and Baghdad. Author of *The Shia of Samarra*. Longtime dialogue partner with [[Francis Alÿs]] on the intersection of art and reconciliation in Iraq.
- **Parwar Tariq** — executive producer; managed production of *Sandlines*.

## Key Projects & Collaborations
- **[Sandlines, the Story of History](https://francisalys.com/sandlines/)** (2018–2020) — co-produced with [[Francis Alÿs]] and [[Julien Devaux]]. Children near Mosul reenact a century of Iraqi history. Premiered January 2020; 16 festivals, 10 countries.
- **Iraqi Pavilion, 57th Venice Biennale** (2017) — facilitated [[Francis Alÿs]]'s presentation *as If* (Mosul). Alÿs had visited the Mosul front line with a Kurdish battalion in November 2016, facilitated by Ruya.
- **Baghdad artist workshop** (February 2016) — Ruya facilitated Alÿs's first Iraq trip, including a workshop for artists in Baghdad and visits to refugee camps in northern Iraq.
- Annual exhibitions, commissions, and cultural programming in Iraq.

## Why Interesting
Ruya Foundation is doing what almost no other cultural institution does: supporting serious contemporary art production *from within* a conflict and post-conflict context. They don't bring international artists in to observe; they build long-term relationships that produce works of genuine political depth — *Sandlines* being the exemplary case. Their Venice presence (Iraqi Pavilion) makes them a node connecting the Baghdad cultural scene to the global contemporary art world.

Tamara Chalabi's specific position — Iraqi-British cultural diplomat and historian operating through art — is an unusual and powerful one. The conversation she published with [[Francis Alÿs]] (2017) is one of the most honest documents in contemporary art writing about the ethics of artistic intervention in conflict zones.
