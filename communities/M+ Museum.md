# M+ Museum

> Asia's most consequential new art institution. Opened 2021 in Hong Kong's West Kowloon Cultural District, M+ is a once-in-a-generation museum built to rival Tate Modern, MoMA, and Centre Pompidou — with a distinctly Asian axis. 6,421+ works, 2.6 million visitors in 2024, and the most sophisticated moving image programme in the region.

## Mission & Objectives

The name derives from "museum and more" — coined by founding director Lars Nittve to signal deliberate departure from the traditional fine art museum model. M+ exhibits twentieth- and twenty-first-century visual culture across four disciplines:

1. **Visual Art** — post-war and contemporary; international with strong Asian axis
2. **Design & Architecture** — Hong Kong design, Asian modernism, post-colonial nation-building
3. **Moving Image** — video art, film, animation, digital media; treated as equal to fine art
4. **Hong Kong Visual Culture** — unique institutional commitment to HK's own visual history

The HK$5.9 billion institution is intended to rival Tate Modern, MoMA, and Centre Pompidou in breadth and importance. In practice, its differentiator is not scale but perspective: it views all of world art from the position of Asia, and particularly from Hong Kong's unique position as the hinge between Chinese and international culture.

Owner: **[West Kowloon Cultural District Authority (WKCDA)](https://www.westkowloon.hk/)**, a statutory body of the Hong Kong SAR government.

**Architecture**: Designed by **Herzog & de Meuron** with **Farrells** (won 2013 competition). The building has an inverted-T profile — horizontal exhibition slab lifted off the ground for pedestrian flow underneath; tower above with lounges, gardens, offices. The LED-integrated facade serves as a public art display screen visible across Victoria Harbour. Underground, tunnels from the Airport Railway run beneath the site, creating unique subterranean exhibition potential.

## Key Contributions

1. **The M+ Sigg Collection** — 1,510 works of contemporary Chinese art, the most comprehensive public collection globally. Includes politically sensitive work (Ai Weiwei, Liu Heung Shing's Tiananmen photographs) that cannot be shown in mainland China. This is M+'s defining geopolitical statement.

2. **Defining Moving Image as Museum Discipline** — M+ treats moving image, video art, and digital media as coequal with painting and sculpture, not auxiliary. Pauline Yao's programme positions HK as the global center for thinking about Asian moving image in a museum context.

3. **Tehching Hsieh Archive** — Most comprehensive collection of Hsieh's performance documentation of any public institution (acquired 2013). One of the defining acts of M+'s commitment to performance/time-based work.

4. **Archigram Archive** — Entire archive of the iconic British radical architecture collective, acquired 2019. The most politically charged architecture acquisition of recent years (there were attempts to block the sale to overseas buyer).

5. **Hong Kong Visual Culture as Category** — M+ institutionally defined "Hong Kong visual culture" as a distinct curatorial category; the collection includes neon signs, graffiti (Tsang Tsou Choi/King of Kowloon), vernacular objects, and urban documentation as art.

6. **Venice Biennale Representation** — Since 2018, M+ has curated Hong Kong's national pavilion at Venice, launching local artists to international prominence:
   - 2018: Samson Young (*Songs for Disaster Relief World Tour*) — first Sigg Prize winner
   - 2020: Shirley Tse (*Stakes and Holders*)
   - 2022: Angela Su (*Arise*) — biological/posthuman imagery
   - 2024: Trevor Yeung (*Courtyard of Attachments*) — aquatic ecology/attachment

7. **Sigg Prize** — The most significant prize for contemporary Chinese art (biennial, Greater China + diaspora). First awarded 2019 to Samson Young.

8. **Institutional Non-Compliance** — The decision to keep politically sensitive Ai Weiwei works (despite NSL pressure) and only partially comply with pro-Beijing demands was a defining institutional statement. Remains the most watched censorship case in Asian museum history.

9. **M+ Facade as Public Art Screen** — The LED-integrated building facade functions as a public art display visible across Victoria Harbour. Unique in the global museum landscape — architecture as broadcast medium.

10. **International Co-productions** — M+ is the only Asian museum that co-produces major exhibitions at the same level as ZKM Karlsruhe, Haus der Kunst München, and MOT Tokyo. This peer-to-peer model (rather than travelling shows) is a significant institutional statement.

### Sigg Prize
**[Sigg Prize](https://www.mplus.org.hk/en/sigg-prize/)** — biennial award for contemporary Chinese art (Greater China + diaspora). Founded by Uli Sigg. M+ administers the prize. Open call for nominations, international jury. Previous winners:

### Sinofuturism Angle
The **Shanshui: Echoes and Signals** exhibition is M+'s most explicit sinofuturism-adjacent programme — it frames digital media and computational art within the classical Chinese shanshui landscape tradition. This is not speculative sinofuturism (à la Lawrence Lek) but institutional sinofuturism: the museum itself is performing the remix. Artists like Liu Chuang and Tatsuo Miyajima in this context suggests M+ is ready for more computational/digital work in the Chinese landscape/philosophy frame.

The **Sigg Prize** circuit (Samson Young's sound art, Angela Su's posthuman biology) also shows M+ collecting and awarding work that is technologically experimental and culturally grounded in Greater China without being nationalist.

## Recent Events That Matter

**2021: Opening under NSL shadow** — M+ opened November 12, 2021, two months after the Hong Kong National Security Law was in full force. Pre-opening, pro-Beijing politicians attacked Ai Weiwei works in the collection. M+ removed an image of the Ai Weiwei work from its website but retained the work. The degree to which M+ can maintain curatorial independence under political pressure is the defining ongoing story. So far: the Sigg Collection is intact, the politically sensitive works are retained, and the programming shows no obvious deference to Beijing. But the soft self-censorship on Ai Weiwei (removing the website image) is noted.

**2022: Haegue Yang acquisition** — *Sonic Rescue Ropes* acquired — significant signal that M+ is building its global contemporary art collection beyond the Chinese axis.

**2023/2024: Venice Biennale expansion** — Trevor Yeung's 2024 Venice presentation (*Courtyard of Attachments*) was one of the most discussed national pavilions. Olivia Chow's curatorial approach — working with fishless aquariums, water agency, ecological attachment — brought a sophisticated post-human/ecological register to HK's representation. This is the model for what M+ considers exhibition-level contemporary art.

**2024: Making It Matters with Anna Ridler** — The inclusion of Anna Ridler (who uses GANs trained on tulip datasets; her *Myriad (Tulips)* is a key work in machine learning art) in a permanent collection display confirms M+ is actively collecting computational/AI art — not just as technology but as art historical gesture.

**2025: Manifesto of Spring (ZKM co-production)** — The most significant circuit-linking event. Curated by Kelly Li with ZKM Karlsruhe and ACC Gwangju, this exhibition formally established M+ as a co-equal institutional partner in the European media art network, not just a recipient of travelling shows.

**2025: Sakamoto + Carsten Nicolai double** — The decision to open two simultaneous exhibitions (Sakamoto retrospective with Shiro Takatani collaboration + Carsten Nicolai/Alva Noto works) is a strong programmatic statement: M+ is placing music/sound/moving image at the center of its 2026 programme. This is the Japanese avant-garde (Onkyo, EAI, Raster-Noton) through a Hong Kong institutional lens.

**2026: Lee Bul retrospective** — Lee Bul: From 1998 to Now is the first major retrospective of Lee Bul's work in Asia. Lee Bul's posthuman cyborg bodies, architectural sublime, and techno-utopian critique are exactly where M+'s interests converge.

## Why Interesting

M+ is not just a museum. It is the **institutional argument** that Asia — and specifically Chinese visual culture — belongs at the center of global contemporary art discourse, not the periphery.

**For the digital/media art field:**
- Pauline Yao's Moving Image programme is the most sophisticated curatorial framework for Asian media art in any public institution
- The Shanshui exhibition frames computational media and digital art within Chinese philosophical/aesthetic tradition — a move that validates digital practice as culturally rooted, not culturally rootless technology
- The Anna Ridler + Harold Cohen + John Maeda constellation in *Making It Matters* shows M+ building an art-historical account of computation that includes AI/ML art as genuine art history

**For the Chinese art ecosystem:**
- The Sigg Collection + Sigg Prize form the most important institutional node for contemporary Chinese art outside mainland China — and the only node that can hold politically sensitive work
