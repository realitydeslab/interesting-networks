# M+ Museum

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Stub created during Chinese Art-Tech Scene deep research.

Asia's most important new contemporary art museum. Opened November 12, 2021 in the West Kowloon Cultural District, Hong Kong. Designed by Herzog & de Meuron. 2,612,691 visitors in 2024. The institution Hong Kong spent decades building to rival Tate Modern, MoMA, and Centre Pompidou.

## Background
- Part of the $5.9 billion HK West Kowloon Cultural District development
- Director: **Suhanya Raffel** (CEO)
- Chief Curator: **Doryun Chong**
- Curators include: Pi Li (visual art), Pauline J. Yao (moving image), Shirley Surya (design and architecture), Ikko Yokoyama (design and architecture)
- Collection: 6,421+ works (2021); 20th and 21st century visual culture encompassing visual art, design and architecture, moving image, and Hong Kong visual culture

## Collection Focus
- **Visual Art** — 20th–21st century, with strong Asian and particularly Chinese component
- **Design & Architecture** — Including from Yung-Ho Chang and Asian design traditions
- **Moving Image** — Significant focus; relevant for Chinese media/video art
- **Hong Kong Visual Culture** — Unique institutional commitment to Hong Kong's own visual history

## Key Curatorial Names
- **Pauline J. Yao** — Curator of moving image. Strong expertise in Chinese and Asian media art.
- **Pi Li** — Visual art curator. Former Serpentine curatorsir. Expert in Chinese contemporary art.

## Key Communities
- **[[Chinese Art-Tech Scene]]** — Major institutional node; collects and exhibits Chinese media art
- **[[K11 Art Foundation]]** — Adrian Cheng connection (West Kowloon development)
- **West Kowloon Cultural District Authority** — Owner

## For Amber
M+ is the most significant new institutional node in the Asian art ecosystem. Its moving image collection is directly relevant to Chinese media art (which is Amber's institutional context). For major institutional exhibitions of XR/spatial computing work in Hong Kong, M+ would be the dream venue. Pauline J. Yao (moving image curator) and Pi Li (visual art curator) are key contacts.

## Why Interesting
The most ambitious new art museum in Asia. Its combination of visual art, design, and moving image with a strong commitment to Asian cultural production (particularly Chinese and Hong Kong work) makes it uniquely positioned to validate media art practice from the Chinese context. Opening in 2021 — just as Chinese media art is achieving maximum international recognition — positions M+ at the center of the next decade's Asian art narrative.
