## Mission & Objectives

The Design+Posthumanism Network (D+PH) is a **self-organized, non-hierarchical research community** exploring the intersections of design research and posthumanism. Born on 24 October 2018 in Gothenburg, Sweden, it emerged from the Scandinavian design research tradition (primarily HDK-Valand) and has grown into an international network spanning Scandinavia, Germany, UK, Netherlands, Portugal, and beyond.

The network operates on three registers:
1. **Theoretical** — bringing posthuman philosophy (Haraway, Barad, Braidotti) into design research
2. **Methodological** — developing concrete tools and methods for more-than-human design practice
3. **Community** — connecting early-career researchers, designers, artists, and practitioners working at the edge of posthumanism

Its core proposition: design can no longer be exclusively human-centered. The network moves from humanist "user-centered design" toward **more-than-human design** — design with, for, and as part of multispecies worlds. It explicitly decouples itself from transhumanist enhancement fantasies, instead embracing critical posthumanism, ecological entanglement, and the agency of non-human things.

In June 2024, the network formally established an **8-person board** to coordinate activities. It has published a [More-than-Human Design Manifesto](https://designandposthumanism.org/2024/05/14/a-more-than-human-design-manifesto/) and appeared in [ACM Interactions Magazine](https://dl.acm.org/doi/pdf/10.1145/3736709) (2025).

## Key Contributions

1. **Field Infrastructure** — the only dedicated international network specifically for more-than-human design research. The community glue for a generation of researchers who felt orphaned from mainstream HCD conferences.

2. **Method Library** — the ongoing [Tools & Methods series](https://designandposthumanism.org/2026/01/15/tools-methods-workshop-sessions-2026/) is building a living repository of more-than-human design methods: Shroom Cards, Tidal Time, Affective Pedagogy, POM (Planetary Operating Model), Ocean as Method, etc.

3. **The Stitches Series** — ongoing micro-interview series (15+ interviews published) connecting members, building community memory, and creating an accessible archive of perspectives. The "Stitches" format: members interview each other informally.

4. **Annual I.N.S.E.C.T. Summer Camp** — 4+ years of practice-based, residential, multispecies research gathering. Unique methodology: co-creation, embodiment, focus on local ecosystem, companion species (Haraway-inspired Camille Stories method). Sliding scale fees (€300–600). Open call annually.

5. **Doctoral dissertation pipeline** — at least 5 D+PH members have defended PhDs on posthumanist design themes 2023–2025: Erik Sandelin, Petra Lilja, Sebastian Gatz, Moniek Driesse, Burak Taşdizen. The network functions as a peer support structure for early-career researchers navigating an unconventional dissertation space.

6. **Confluence of European Water Bodies** — active participation in this grassroots multi-stakeholder network representing 25+ European water bodies (lakes, rivers, seas, glaciers). Brings design practice into rights-of-nature activism.
