# NYPL Cullman Center for Scholars and Writers

A year-long residential fellowship at the New York Public Library for writers, journalists, and scholars working on non-fiction projects requiring deep archival research. Based at the NYPL's Stephen A. Schwarzman Building (Fifth Avenue and 42nd Street, Manhattan). Fellows have private studies and access to NYPL's entire research collection.

## Fellows Program

Up to 15 fellows per year. Open to writers and scholars (US and international). Stipend ~$75,000 for the academic year. Applications typically open in the spring for the following academic year; deadline usually in late October/early November.

**Application URL:** [nypl.org/research/collections/fellowships-grants/cullman](https://www.nypl.org/research/collections/fellowships-grants/cullman)

## Why Interesting
One of the most prestigious non-fiction writing fellowships in the US. The NYPL's research collections are unparalleled — strong for American history, Black history, immigration, and culture. If Amber has a book project with major archival dimensions, this is worth considering.
