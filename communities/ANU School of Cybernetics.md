# ANU School of Cybernetics

Australia's premier institution for a 21st-century cybernetics — treating cybernetics as a living intellectual tradition that fuses systems theory, anthropology, engineering, design, governance, and the arts. Located in the College of Systems and Society at the Australian National University in Canberra. Founded 2021 by Distinguished Professor Genevieve Bell, who went on to become ANU Vice-Chancellor in January 2024. One of the very few institutions in the world that positions cybernetics as a serious platform for AI ethics, safety, and sociotechnical futures research — not just a historical footnote.

## Mission & Objectives

The School is on a mission to establish cybernetics as a critical toolkit for navigating major societal transformations — through education, research, policy development, and art-science collaboration.

Key intellectual anchors:
- **Cyber-physical systems (CPS)** as the primary object of study — not "AI" as a buzzword, but the systems where data, networks, algorithms, ML, and edge computing converge
- **Transdisciplinarity** from the ground up — CS + anthropology + law + design + philosophy + ecology, always in dialogue
- **The human/environmental loop** — cybernetics forces you to include the social and ecological in the technical system design
- **Indigenous knowledge systems** as legitimate epistemological frameworks for technology design
- Systems that last: the Brewarrina fish traps (35,000 years of use) are a recurring reference in their theory of long-lasting technical systems

Core framing (via Mary Catherine Bateson, daughter of Gregory Bateson + Margaret Mead): "The tragedy of the cybernetic revolution...has been the neglect of the systems theory side of it. We chose marketable gadgets in preference to a deeper understanding of the world we live in."

[Profile](https://cybernetics.anu.edu.au/people/genevieve-bell/) | Distinguished Professor, Florence Violet McKenzie Chair

- ⭐⭐ — figure worth tracking. Rare: anthropologist-turned-VC who built a whole school around cybernetics. The intellectual architecture she built here is her most enduring contribution.

### Dr Ambelin Kwaymullina — Cybernetic Imagination Resident (2023)
Palyku writer, artist and academic. Practitioner of Indigenous Futurisms (term coined by Anishinaabe academic Grace Dillon). Her 2023 residency applied Indigenous futurist thinking to AI through acrylic painting series. Key argument: in Indigenous knowledge systems, there is no "artificial" intelligence — intelligence is relational and embedded in Country. Powerful counter-epistemology to Western AI discourse.

## Key Programs & Education

### [Master of Applied Cybernetics](https://cybernetics.anu.edu.au/people/)
- World's first graduate program for ensuring AI-enabled cyber-physical systems are safe, sustainable, and responsible.
- One-year postgraduate program. Launched 2019 as the "3A Institute" prototype.
- Students come from engineering, CS, design ethnography, STS, philosophy, history, business strategy.
- Funded by: Department of Defence, Commonwealth Bank of Australia, and others.

### PhD Program in Cybernetics
- Convenored by Dr Ben Swift.
- Interdisciplinary. Sits in College of Systems and Society.

### Learning Experiences (LX)
- Short programs for individuals and organizations.
- Includes executive programs like "Thinking Beyond AI" (Microsoft partnership).
- AI standard training (AS ISO/IEC 42001:2023) — Australia's world-first AI standard online course.

### [Cybernetic Imagination Residency](https://cybernetics.anu.edu.au/projects/tomorrows-pasts/)
- Annual residency for artists, storytellers, thinkers, creatives.
- Focus: credible, hopeful, unexpected stories about the future that instigate positive change.
- Past residents: Ambelin Kwaymullina (2023), briggsnpledger (2025), Lynette Wallworth (2022–ongoing).
- This is the key entry point for Amber to connect with the school.

### [Cybernetic Studio](https://cybernetics.anu.edu.au/cybernetic-studio/)
- Physical/conceptual space led by Dr Ben Swift.
- 2025/2026 theme: Human-Scale AI — making AI perceptible at human sensory scale.
- Brings together artists, engineers, computer scientists, environmentalists to prototype cybernetic systems.
- Roots in early cybernetics material practice: Wiener's hearing glove, Grey Walter's tortoises, Ashby's homeostat, Beer and Pask's fungoid whisper systems.

## Why Interesting

The ANU School of Cybernetics is doing something genuinely rare: it treats cybernetics not as a historical artifact but as a living toolkit for navigating AI, CPS, and social transformation. Most institutions either:
1. Talk about AI ethics without systems theory (hollow ethics theater), or
2. Do systems theory without art/culture (sterile engineering)

This school does both — and adds Indigenous knowledge systems as a third epistemological framework. The Cybernetic Imagination Residency gives artists genuine institutional support alongside researchers. Ben Swift's Human-Scale AI project is philosophically adjacent to everything Amber cares about: making invisible computation visible, connecting machines to embodied human experience.
