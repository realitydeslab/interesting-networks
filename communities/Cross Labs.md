# Cross Labs

Kyoto-based research institute pursuing the mathematical foundations of all intelligent processes — natural and artificial. Founded in 2019 as the fundamental research arm of [Cross Compass Ltd.](https://www.cross-compass.com/), a Japanese manufacturing AI company. Small (~10–15 researchers), interdisciplinary, and punching wildly above its weight. Organized ALIFE 2025, runs annual workshops with world-class speakers, publishes IMI Journal, and runs the Cross Labs ALife Summer School. Vibe: intimate intellectual hub where ALife, consciousness, philosophy, and AI collide.

**Address:** 302, Kikusan Bldg., 112 Machigashiracho, Nakagyo Ward, Kyoto 604-8206, Japan ([Google Maps](https://www.google.fr/maps/place/Cross+Labs/@35.008814,135.7546114,17z))

## Mission & Objectives
"Uncover the mathematical bases of all intelligent processes, observable both in nature and in artificial environments." Bridges industrial and academic research, promoting fundamental and applied science connected to technological needs. Key insight: Cross Compass (the parent) does manufacturing AI (computer vision, anomaly detection for factories); Cross Labs does fundamental research on intelligence, life, and consciousness. The arrangement lets researchers pursue deep theoretical questions while funded by industry — a rare and valuable model.

## Research Themes
1. **Open-Endedness** — Algorithms that keep learning forever, like biological evolution. Creating machines that generate novelty without new data.
2. **Theory of Agency** — Mathematical frameworks for understanding how goals emerge in agents. Moving beyond deep learning to the next AI paradigms.
3. **Collective Learning** — Societies of AI agents that learn from each other, parallelize computation, invent new collective mechanisms.
4. **Neuroscience of Intelligence** — Translating biological processes from diverse forms of intelligence into fundamental mathematical principles.

  - 2025: [New Human Interfaces](https://www.crosslabs.org/new-human-interfaces) — embodied cognition, HCI. Keynotes: Pattie Maes (MIT), Daniel Helman.
  - 2024: [After Babel](https://www.crosslabs.org/after-babel-the-quest-for-universal-communication) — universal communication, cross-species language. Panelists: Wolfram, Levin, Zenil, Gershenson, Schwitzgebel.
  - 2023: [Creativity Unleashed](https://www.crosslabs.org/creativity-unleashed) — computational creativity. Speakers: Bongard, Cheney, Soros, Taylor, Lehman, Colton, Sha Xin Wei.

The result: a small team in Kyoto doing research that spans origins of life, consciousness, swarm intelligence, and AI art — and organizing the world's premiere ALife conference.
