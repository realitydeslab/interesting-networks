# SenseLab

**Research-creation collective** based in Montreal, founded and directed by [[Erin Manning]] in 2004. Co-created with [[Brian Massumi]]. Operates at the intersection of philosophy, art, movement, and activist practice. Affiliated with **Hexagram: Institute for Research/Creation in Media Arts and Technology** at Concordia University.

SenseLab's premise: bodies think. Thought happens through movement and sensation, not just cognition. The lab produces knowledge *through* aesthetic and choreographic experience, not merely *about* it.

## What SenseLab Does

- **Events of radical pedagogy** — collectively designed experiences that are simultaneously philosophical and choreographic. Not lectures; participants are expected to move, make, and compose together. These events last multiple days and involve practitioners from movement arts, philosophy, disability activism, queer theory, architecture, and media.
- **Research-creation** — as Manning and Massumi define it, this is a two-way transduction between language-based philosophical concepts and non-language-based modes of experience. Distinct from "art illustrating theory" or "theory explaining art."
- **Collective writing and editing** — through the Immediations book series and the Inflexions journal.
- **Neurodiversity advocacy** — Manning's alliance with autistic thinkers (Tito Rajarshi Mukhopadhyay, Adam Wolfond) is embedded in SenseLab practice. Neurodiversity as epistemic diversity.

## Key Events

- **Into the Midst** (2012) — five-day collaborative research-creation workshop at the SATosphere (Society for Art and Technology's immersive dome environment). Documented in a special issue of *Inflexions*.
- **Movements of Thought / Knots of Thought / Nœuds de la Pensée** — ongoing workshop series integrating movement research and philosophical inquiry.
- Workshops at Zürcher Hochschule der Künste (Zurich University of the Arts).

## Outputs

- **[[Inflexions: A Journal for Research-Creation]]** — SenseLab's open-access web journal (2008–2019, 11 issues). Member of [[Open Humanities Press]] journal collective.
- **Immediations book series** — co-edited by SenseLab; at [[Open Humanities Press]] (2015–2018), then at [[punctum books]] / 3Ecologies (2018–present). Series directors: [[Erin Manning]], [[Brian Massumi]], Andrew Goodman, Andrew Murphie, Toni Pape.
- **[3ecologies.org](https://3ecologies.org)** — ongoing web platform.
- Multiple SSHRC (Social Sciences and Humanities Research Council of Canada) research grants.

## Why Interesting

SenseLab is possibly the most interesting research collective in the humanities right now for anyone thinking about how knowledge can be produced otherwise. It refuses the standard academic division between thinking-and-talking and making-and-moving. The fact that it has persisted for 20+ years, produced a sustained book series, and maintained a genuine collective (rather than degenerating into a brand for a single PI) is remarkable.

The neurodiversity dimension is significant: SenseLab is one of the few theory institutions that has genuinely integrated autistic practitioners into its epistemological core — not as research subjects but as co-theorists.
