# e-flux

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Initial deep research. Full profile written from Wikipedia, official website, and associated pages. e-flux is active, publishing weekly content, launched e-flux Criticism (2023) and e-flux Index quarterly print (2024). Anton Vidokle curated 14th Shanghai Biennale "Cosmos Cinema" (2023).

## Vibe
e-flux is the **connective tissue of the international contemporary art world**. Began as an email announcement platform in 1998 — when email itself was new — and transformed over 25 years into the most influential platform for art theory on the internet. Reading e-flux journal = reading what the art world's most serious thinkers are thinking. Not a gallery, not a school, not a museum — something harder to define: a platform, an institution, a publishing house, an artist project, and a curatorial entity all at once. It is where [[Hito Steyerl]]'s "In Defense of the Poor Image" became canonical. That one essay, published 2009, has shaped how an entire generation thinks about images. There is no equivalent.

150,000+ readers globally. Used by MoMA, Tate, Centre Pompidou, documenta, Venice Biennale, Frieze, and virtually every major institution in contemporary art. If you publish here, you're in the conversation.

## Mission & Objectives
- Distribute press releases and announcements for exhibitions, events, and programmes globally (the original mission, still running)
- Publish critical essays on art, technology, politics, architecture, and culture (e-flux journal, launched 2008)
- Produce and present original art projects, exhibitions, and symposia
- Host screenings, lectures, and events in Brooklyn (e-flux Screening Room)
- Provide a free video archive and film platform (e-flux Film)
- Connect art institutions with global audiences

## Key Publications & Output

### e-flux journal (2008–present)
- **URL:** [e-flux.com/journal](https://www.e-flux.com/journal/)
- Monthly online publication. Launched by Aranda, Vidokle, and Brian Kuan Wood.
- Topics: art theory, cultural politics, technology, critical theory, film, history.
- Publishes essays, commissioned texts, translations.
- Most cited/canonical essays include:
  - [Hito Steyerl, "In Defense of the Poor Image" (2009)](https://www.e-flux.com/journal/10/61362/in-defense-of-the-poor-image/) — probably the most cited essay in contemporary art theory
  - Boris Groys's extensive body of writing on the avant-garde
  - Multiple essays by Vidokle on art, labor, and cosmism
- **Over 140 issues published (2008–2026)**
- 10th anniversary (2019): conferences in Rotterdam, Paris, Berlin, New York

## Key Contributions to the Field
1. **Invented the art email announcement** — e-flux email newsletters are the industry standard for how art institutions communicate with the global art community
2. **Institutionalized online art theory** — e-flux journal made serious theory publishing native to the web, not a secondary dump
3. **Hito Steyerl's "Poor Image"** — published an essay that defined how an era thinks about digital images and circulation
4. **The artist-as-publisher model** — demonstrated that artists can run influential intellectual institutions, not just make objects
5. **Russian Cosmism revival** — Vidokle and Boris Groys made cosmism a live intellectual current in contemporary art through e-flux
6. **Alternative economies for art** — Pawnshop, Time/Bank demonstrated art as economic critique in action

## Submission Process
e-flux journal is **invitation-only / commission-based** — essays are commissioned, not submitted via open call. To get in: you need to be known to the editors (Aranda, Vidokle, Wood) or be proposed by a known contributor.

e-flux **announcements** are paid/institutional — institutions pay a fee to send announcements to the list. Rates: contact e-flux directly.

e-flux **Architecture**, **Notes**, **Criticism**: also primarily commissioned/invitation-based.

**Practical pathways:**
1. Be introduced by a current contributor (strongest path)
2. Develop a relationship at events (Screening Room, lectures)
3. Get published in adjacent venues (Afterall, Frieze, October) and become known
4. Pitch directly via email to the section editors (journal@e-flux.com, architecture@e-flux.com)

## Why Interesting

It has defined the discourse through which the art world processes technology, politics, labor, race, ecology, and philosophy for 25+ years. It operates at the exact intersection of Amber's interests: critical computing, posthuman theory, speculative design, non-Western techno-philosophy. The journal's emphasis on essays that are rigorously theoretical but practice-linked is precisely the kind of thinking Amber does and produces.

More practically: if you are serious about being a voice in art-technology discourse, e-flux is the venue. Not one of many — THE venue. Bogna Konior's placement there tells you everything you need to know about who publishes here.
