# Future of Life Institute (FLI)

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-03-01:** Full deep research completed. Expanded all sections with comprehensive detail.
- **2026-02-28:** Stub created during CSER deep research.

Non-profit research institute and advocacy organization founded March 2014. Mission: steering transformative technology towards benefiting life and away from extreme large-scale risks. Headquarters: Campbell, California, with offices in Brussels, London, and Washington D.C. One of the most influential organizations in the AI safety and existential risk ecosystem.

---

## Mission & Objectives

FLI's stated mission is to "steer transformative technology towards benefiting life and away from extreme large-scale risks." Their philosophy centers on the existential risk from advanced AI (AGI/superintelligence), but also addresses biotechnology, nuclear weapons, and global catastrophic risk more broadly.

**Core belief:** "The way powerful technology is developed and used will be the most important factor in determining the prospects for the future of life."

**Focus Areas:**
1. **Artificial Intelligence** — safety, alignment, governance, preventing catastrophic misuse or loss of control
2. **Biotechnology** — biosecurity, pandemic preparedness, risks of engineered pathogens
3. **Nuclear Weapons** — disarmament advocacy, preventing nuclear winter

**Work Programs:**
- **Policy & Research** — advocacy at UN, EU, U.S. government; producing policy papers
- **Futures** — foresight projects exploring long-range scenarios (run by [Emilia Javorsky](https://futureoflife.org/our-people/emilia-javorsky/))
- **Communications** — public education, media, podcast, documentary film
- **Grantmaking** — peer-reviewed grants to AI safety and existential risk researchers worldwide

## Key Publications & Output

### Landmark Open Letters
- **[2015 Puerto Rico AI Open Letter](https://futureoflife.org/misc/open_letter)** — Signed by Stephen Hawking, Elon Musk, Stuart Russell, hundreds of AI researchers; called for AI safety research. The catalytic document that put "AI safety" on the mainstream map.
- **[2017 Asilomar AI Principles](https://futureoflife.org/open-letter/ai-principles/)** — 23 principles for beneficial AI governance; signed by thousands of AI researchers and industry figures; named after the historic Asilomar Conference on Recombinant DNA (1975). One of the earliest and most cited AI governance frameworks. State of California formally endorsed in 2018.
- **[2018 Lethal Autonomous Weapons Pledge](https://futureoflife.org/open-letter/lethal-autonomous-weapons-pledge/)** — Now signed by 5000+ individuals and organizations; "the decision to take a human life should never be delegated to a machine." Signatories include Elon Musk, Demis Hassabis, Shane Legg, Mustafa Suleyman.
- **[2023 Pause Giant AI Experiments Open Letter](https://futureoflife.org/open-letter/pause-giant-ai-experiments/)** — Called for 6-month moratorium on AI systems more powerful than GPT-4; 30,000+ signatories including Elon Musk, Steve Wozniak, Yoshua Bengio, Yuval Noah Harari; generated massive global debate.
- **[2025 Statement on Superintelligence](https://futureoflife.org/statement-on-superintelligence/)** — Called for prohibition on superintelligence development until provably safe; signatories include Nobel laureates Geoffrey Hinton, Daron Acemoglu, Yoshua Bengio, Frank Wilczek, Steve Wozniak, Prince Harry and Meghan, Stephen Fry, Joseph Gordon-Levitt, Will.i.am, Yuval Noah Harari.

### Policy Papers (Recent)
- **[Control Inversion](https://futureoflife.org/document/control-inversion/)** (November 2025) — Analysis of AI governance risks
- **[AI Safety Index: Winter 2025](https://futureoflife.org/document/ai-safety-index-winter-2025/)** — Comparative index evaluating major AI labs on safety practices
- **[Embedded Off-Switches for AI Compute](https://futureoflife.org/document/embedded-off-switches/)** (September 2025)

## Key Contributions

1. **Catalyzing AI safety as a research field** — The 2015 Puerto Rico conference and open letter, backed by Musk donation ($10M to FLI for grants), legitimized AI safety as a serious scientific concern at a time when it was considered fringe.
2. **2015 Grant Program** — World's first peer-reviewed AI safety grants program. $6.5M to 37 researchers. Seeded much of what became the AI safety research ecosystem (MIRI, CHAI, future researchers who went to Anthropic/OpenAI safety teams).
3. **Asilomar AI Principles (2017)** — Among the most cited AI governance frameworks globally; influenced subsequent efforts by IEEE, EU, OECD.
4. **Lethal Autonomous Weapons campaign** — Slaughterbots film + UN advocacy; FLI at the UN (Geneva, 2021); major campaign against killer robots.
5. **Nuclear Weapons Treaty support** — Mobilized 3,700 scientists in 100 countries (30 Nobel Laureates) for the UN Treaty on the Prohibition of Nuclear Weapons (2017).
6. **2023 AI Pause Letter** — Forced a global public reckoning with AI speed. Even if the pause didn't happen, the letter changed the political conversation and contributed to UK AI Safety Summit (Bletchley Park, 2023) happening.
7. **EU AI Act influence** — Successfully advocated for including general-purpose AI systems (like GPT-4) in the EU AI Act's scope.
8. **Future of Life Award** (annual since 2017) — Celebrates unsung heroes who averted catastrophe. First recipient: Vasili Arkhipov (prevented nuclear war during Cuban Missile Crisis). Amplifies the narrative of human agency in averting disaster.
9. **AI Existential Safety Community** — A peer community for faculty and AI researchers; provides travel grants and networking for AI safety researchers.
