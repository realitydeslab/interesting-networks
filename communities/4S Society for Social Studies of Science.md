# 4S — Society for Social Studies of Science

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Stub created during Donna Haraway deep research.

The primary disciplinary home for Science & Technology Studies (STS). Annual international conference; the J.D. Bernal Prize and Ludwik Fleck Prize are its highest honors (both won by Donna Haraway). Interdisciplinary community of scholars studying science and technology as social, political, and cultural phenomena.

## Mission & Objectives
Studies how science and technology are shaped by social, cultural, economic, and political forces — and vice versa.

## Key People
- **[[Donna Haraway]]** ⭐⭐⭐ — J.D. Bernal Prize 2000, Ludwik Fleck Prize 1999
- **[[Lucy Suchman]]** ⭐⭐⭐ — J.D. Bernal Prize 2014, President 2016–2017. The HCI-to-STS crossover figure.
- **[[Bruno Latour]]** ⭐⭐⭐ — Major contributor; actor-network theory; Bernal Prize 2013
- **[[Isabelle Stengers]]** ⭐⭐⭐ — Cosmopolitics; frequent presence
- **Sheila Jasanoff** — Harvard; science/democracy; major figure
- **Langdon Winner** — *Do Artifacts Have Politics?* — foundational
- **Andrew Pickering** — *The Mangle of Practice*

## Key Awards
- **[J.D. Bernal Prize](https://www.4sonline.org/prizes)** — highest honor; lifetime achievement
- **[Ludwik Fleck Prize](https://www.4sonline.org/prizes)** — best book in STS

## Events & Programmes
- **Annual Meeting** — major international STS conference. Jointly held with EASST (European Association for the Study of Science and Technology) in even years.
- Travel grants and fellowships available

## For Amber
- STS is the community that gives theoretical grounding to Amber's critical computing and machine behavior work. Situated knowledges, actor-networks, feminist technology studies — all from 4S.
- The **4S Annual Meeting** is a key venue for publishing critical work about AI/ML from an STS perspective.
- The **Fleck and Bernal prizes** tell you who the important people are in this field.

## Why Interesting
