The canonical disciplinary home for Science & Technology Studies (STS). Founded in 1975, now with 3,000+ scholars attending its annual meeting. The community that produced Haraway's cyborg, Latour's actor-networks, Suchman's situated action, and feminist technoscience — all pillars of Amber's intellectual genealogy. If Amber publishes critically about AI and machine behavior, this is the community most likely to take it seriously.

---

## Mission & Objectives

4S is a non-profit scholarly association devoted to understanding how science and technology are shaped by — and in turn reshape — social, political, cultural, and economic forces. It is interdisciplinary by design: its conferences and publications draw from sociology, anthropology, history, philosophy, political science, computer science, and beyond.

**Charter:** Drafted 1975. First president: [Robert K. Merton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robert_K._Merton) (the founding sociologist of science).

**Governance:** Nine-person council + president. Biennial election cycle. Current president: **Anne Pollock** (2023–25; gender and health STS scholar at King's College London — UK-based, potentially connectable for Amber).

**Member base:** Worldwide. Over 3,000 attendees at the 2024 Amsterdam meeting (joint with EASST).

## Key People

### Founders & Origin
- **[Robert K. Merton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robert_K._Merton)** — Founding president 1975–76. Sociologist of science; developed "norms of science" (CUDOS), the Matthew Effect, ethos of science. Established the intellectual framework for studying science *as* a social institution.

### Past Presidents (Notable for Amber)
- **[[Donna Haraway]]** — Council member 1995–97. Bernal Prize 2000. *A Cyborg Manifesto*, *Staying with the Trouble*. The feminist technoscience anchor. ⭐⭐⭐
- **[[Bruno Latour]]** — President 2004–05. Bernal Prize 1992/2013. Actor-network theory, *Reassembling the Social*, *We Have Never Been Modern*. The ANT anchor. ⭐⭐⭐
- **[[Lucy Suchman]]** — President 2016–17. Bernal Prize 2014. *Plans and Situated Actions* (1987) — the foundational HCI-to-STS crossover text on human-machine communication. Direct precursor to Amber's work on machine behavior. ⭐⭐⭐
- **Kim Fortun** — President 2018–19. Ethnography + environmental hazards + platform studies.
- **Joan Fujimura** — President 2020–21 (filling Alondra Nelson's vacancy). Bernal Prize 2023. Genomics + race + sociology of science.
- **Alondra Nelson** — President-elect 2020–21, resigned for Biden admin (OSTP). STS + race + technology + policy. Important bridge figure.
- **Emma Kowal** — President 2022–23. Indigenous genetics + postcolonial STS.
- **Anne Pollock** — President 2023–25. Gender, race, pharmaceuticals, global health. At King's College London. ⭐ (London-based!)

### Council Members of High Relevance to Amber
- **[Noortje Marres](https://warwick.ac.uk/fac/soc/ces/people/nmarres/)** (council 2019–21) — Digital methods, STS of data, issue mapping. At Warwick. The digital STS figure.
- **[Natasha Myers](https://natashamyers.ca/)** (council 2010–12) — Feminist plant studies, more-than-human, "planthropocene." Strong overlap with Amber's more-than-human thread.
- **[Maria Puig de la Bellacasa](https://mariapuigdelabellacasa.wordpress.com/)** (council 2010–12) — *Matters of Care* (2017); feminist STS, posthumanism, care ethics. Direct connection to Amber's ethics-of-care angle. ⭐⭐
- **[Michelle Murphy](https://michellemurphy.net/)** (council 2007–09) — Chemosphere, feminist STS, economization of life. Fleck prize winner.

### Major Intellectual Figures in the 4S Orbit
- **[Sheila Jasanoff](https://www.hks.harvard.edu/faculty/sheila-jasanoff)** — Harvard Kennedy School. Science + law + democracy + co-production. Bernal Prize 2004. Key for policy-adjacent STS work.
- **[Karin Knorr Cetina](https://sociology.uchicago.edu/directory/karin-knorr-cetina)** — Epistemic cultures; *Epistemic Cultures* (1999). How different sciences know differently. President 1996–97.
- **[Andrew Pickering](https://sociology.exeter.ac.uk/staff/pickering/)** — *The Mangle of Practice* (1995); dance of agency between humans and nonhumans.
- **[Isabelle Stengers](https://www.ulb.be/fr/isabelle-stengers)** — Cosmopolitics; "ecologies of practice"; Whitehead revival. Fleck Prize 2013. ⭐⭐⭐
- **[Susan Leigh Star](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Susan_leigh_star)** — President 2006–07. *Sorting Things Out* (with Bowker). Boundary objects. Information infrastructure as political.
- **[Langdon Winner](https://www.langdonwinner.com/)** — "Do Artifacts Have Politics?" (1980). The foundational political philosophy of technology text. Bernal Prize 2020.
- **[Thom van Dooren](https://thomvandooren.org/)** — *Flight Ways* (2014), *The Wake of Crows* (2019). Multispecies ethics, extinction studies. Fleck Prize 2021. Strong more-than-human thread. ⭐⭐
- **[Geoffrey Bowker](https://ics.uci.edu/faculty/profiles/view_faculty.php?ucinetid=gbowker)** — *Sorting Things Out*, *Memory Practices in the Sciences*. Classification + information infrastructure. Bernal Prize 2024.
- **[Annemarie Mol](https://www.uva.nl/profiel/m/o/a.mol/a.mol.html)** — *The Body Multiple* (2002). Ontological politics, more-than-human medicine. Fleck Prize 2004. Bernal Prize 2024. ⭐⭐⭐

## Key Publications & Output

### Primary Journals
1. **[Science, Technology, & Human Values (ST&HV)](https://journals.sagepub.com/home/sth)** — Quarterly, peer-reviewed. Published since 1967 (originally *Newsletter on Science, Technology, & Human Values*, becoming a journal in 1976). Published via SAGE. Impact factor ~3.16. The flagship STS journal. Current editorial team: Timothy Neale (Deakin), Courtney Addison (Victoria Univ. Wellington), Matthew Kearnes (UNSW), Kari Lancaster (UNSW). Previous editors include Susan Leigh Star, Geoffrey Bowker, Ulrike Felt — all major figures. The top publication target if Amber wants STS traction.

2. **[Engaging Science, Technology, and Society (ESTS)](https://estsjournal.org/)** — Open access, diamond model. Online-only. 2020–2025 editorial collective (transnational). More experimental, political, accessible. Final 2020–25 editorial collective editorial published Dec 2025. New editorial team taking over 2026. Good venue for more radical/speculative STS work.

3. **[4S Backchannels](https://4sonline.org/news_manager.php)** — Member blog. Recent posts include "Holding the Line: Values Drift, AI Anomia, and the Craft of Accountable Leadership" (Jan 2026) — directly relevant to AI governance. Active community commentary.

## Key Contributions

STS (and 4S specifically) produced most of the intellectual tools Amber's research relies on:

- **Actor-Network Theory** (Latour, Callon, Law) — nonhumans as actants; agency distributed across networks of humans and things
- **Feminist Technoscience** (Haraway, Suchman, Murphy) — situated knowledges, cyborg theory, care ethics in tech
- **Social Construction of Technology (SCOT)** (Bijker, Pinch) — artifacts as socially shaped, interpretive flexibility
- **Boundary Objects** (Star, Griesemer) — objects that bridge different communities of practice
- **Epistemic Cultures** (Knorr Cetina) — how different disciplines "know" differently
- **Co-production** (Jasanoff) — science and social order produce each other
- **More-than-human studies** (Haraway, Mol, van Dooren, Myers) — decentering the human in knowledge-making
- **Ontological Politics** (Mol) — different enactments of the same object = multiple ontologies
- **Matters of Care** (Puig de la Bellacasa) — care as a political and epistemic stance toward the more-than-human

### Prizes & Awards
- **[J.D. Bernal Prize](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Desmond_Bernal_Prize)** — Highest lifetime achievement honor. Annual. Recent winners: Geoffrey Bowker & Annemarie Mol (2024), Joan Fujimura & Warwick Anderson (2023), Arie Rip & Troy Duster (2022). The Bernal Prize list is essentially a canon of STS — reading it backwards is a history of the field.
- **[Ludwik Fleck Prize](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ludwik_Fleck_Prize)** — Best book in STS. Annual. 2025 winner: Lisa Yin Han, *Deepwater Alchemy*. 2024: Shannon Cramm, *Unmaking the Bomb*. 2021 winner Thom van Dooren (*The Wake of Crows*) directly relevant to Amber's more-than-human interests.
- **[Rachel Carson Prize](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rachel_Carson_Prize_(academic_book_prize))** — Book of "social or political relevance." 2025: Emily Yates-Doerr, *Mal-Nutrition*. Recent winners include STS work on race, extraction, environment.
- **[Nicholas C. Mullins Award](https://www.4sonline.org/)** — For outstanding graduate student scholarship in STS. Important for PhD students.

### Travel & Membership Support
- Travel grants for early career scholars / scholars from the Global South. Check 4sonline.org for current offerings.
- Membership includes journal subscriptions (ST&HV via SAGE, ESTS open access).

## For Amber

**Why interesting:**
4S is the discipline-home for everyone in Amber's intellectual lineage: Haraway, Latour, Suchman, Stengers, Mol, Puig de la Bellacasa. If Amber wants to position critical work about AI/machine behavior within a tradition that has theoretical depth (rather than just HCI or CS), 4S is the venue.

**How can Amber engage:**
- **Submit to the Annual Meeting** — panels and papers accepted on STS of AI, machine behavior, more-than-human computation. Very welcoming of interdisciplinary and practice-based work. Strong precedent for design-research papers if framed with STS vocabulary.
- **Publish in ESTS** — open access, more experimental than ST&HV, more politically engaged. A good first STS publication target.
- **Publish in ST&HV** — harder but higher impact. Amber's HCI + critical AI + feminist technoscience angle would fit if framed rigorously.
- **Engage with Backchannels** — the 4S blog welcomes member contributions. A lower-stakes entry point.

**Opening opportunities:**
- 4S does not run residencies directly, but many 4S-adjacent communities do (e.g., at Goldsmiths, Lancaster, MIT STS). Connections through 4S → residency opportunities.
- The **Mullins Award** is relevant if Amber is still in early PhD stage.

**Who should Amber know:**
- **Noortje Marres** (Warwick) — digital methods + STS of data. Adjacent to Amber's agentic/networked AI work.
- **Natasha Myers** — planthropocene, more-than-human. At York University but frequent Europe visitor.
- **Maria Puig de la Bellacasa** — at Leicester (UK!). *Matters of Care* is mandatory reading for Amber's ethics-of-care angle toward machines.
- **Thom van Dooren** — multispecies ethics + extinction. UNSW-based but globally connected.
- **Anne Pollock** — King's College London. Current 4S president. London-based; potential connection.

**Key publications to be part of:**

**Intellectual framing tip for Amber:** STS scholars respond to vocabulary — "situated knowledges," "ontological politics," "co-production," "matters of care," "boundary objects." Amber's existing work maps onto these concepts readily. The key move is to explicitly invoke STS theory while presenting empirical/design work.

## Why Interesting

4S is the theoretical backbone for everything Amber is building. Amber's work sits at the intersection of:
- **Machine behavior as science** (4S has a long tradition of studying AI/computing as sociotechnical phenomena)
- **More-than-human design** (Haraway, Mol, van Dooren, Myers all in the 4S canon)
- **Feminist technoscience** (the political backbone for why any of this matters)
- **Cyborg sociology** (literally invented at 4S — Haraway's Bernal Prize was for the Cyborg Manifesto)

The practical reason 4S matters: when Amber publishes critical work about AI, machine behavior, or agent ethology, the 4S community provides a ready-made audience that already has the vocabulary to understand it — and the prestige metrics (ST&HV, Bernal Prize, ESTS) to validate it institutionally. CS conferences won't give Amber this; 4S will.

The deeper reason: the intellectual questions 4S has been asking since 1975 ("whose knowledge?" "what counts as agency?" "how do artifacts have politics?") are exactly the questions Amber's research is asking about AI agents. Amber is doing STS work, whether or not they call it that.
