# General Ecology (Serpentine)

[Serpentine Ecologies](https://www.serpentinegalleries.org/ecologies/) — the flagship ecology research programme at [[Serpentine Galleries]], London. Founded in 2018 by [[Lucia Pietroiusti]]. One of the most sustained experiments in ecology-as-curatorial-methodology at a major contemporary art institution globally. Ran 2018–2024, evolving from "General Ecology project" into "Ecologies" (a formal department). Continued by Serpentine's Ecologies team after Lucia's departure in 2024.

## Mission & Objectives
General Ecology set out to explore ecological thinking across art, science, politics, and philosophy — not just "art about climate" but ecology as a framework for rethinking how institutions operate, how knowledge is produced, and how communities gather. Key questions: What does it mean to take non-human life seriously? How do we dissolve anthropocentric frameworks? What can art do that science alone cannot?

## Key Projects & Outputs
- **The Shape of a Circle in the Mind of a Fish** — 5-edition symposium series (2018–2022) on interspecies consciousness, co-curated with Filipa Ramos
- **PLANTSEX** (2019) — event + MAL Journal special issue on botany and eroticism
- **Back to Earth** (2020–2022) — climate emergency art programme
- **140 Artists' Ideas for Planet Earth** (Penguin Press, 2022) — book
- **Infinite Ecologies Marathon: The Prelude** (October 2023)
- **Infinite Ecologies Marathon** (July–September 2024)

## For Amber
- **Why interesting:** The General Ecology model — using long-running community events to generate ecological knowledge — is a template for how Amber could build a sustained research programme around more-than-human / machine behavior. The series model is directly applicable.
- **How to engage:** Serpentine Ecologies continues to run events; attending builds this community relationship.

## Why Interesting
The most institutionally successful example of ecology-as-research-methodology in the UK contemporary art world. The Fish series set a standard for how interdisciplinary art-science inquiry can be sustained over multiple years.
