Major EA/longtermist philanthropic advisory organisation focused on catastrophic and existential risk. Runs bespoke donor advising, three public funds (FAIF, NWPF, ECF), and curated retreats that convene philanthropists, policymakers, academics, and AI safety researchers. London office makes this a live, proximate node for Amber.

## Mission & Objectives

Longview describes its mission as harnessing the power of philanthropy to protect our future. They offer donor education, grant recommendations, grant-making infrastructure, and follow-up outcome reporting — all free of charge — for donors giving over $1M/year. For smaller donors, public funds (the [Emerging Challenges Fund](https://www.longview.org/fund/emerging-challenges-fund/)) are accessible from any amount.

**Core focus areas:**
1. **Frontier AI safety** — technical AI safety research, AI governance (US, EU, China), capability evaluations, digital sentience
2. **Nuclear weapons policy** — opposing destabilising systems, reducing deterrence failure risk, preventing inadvertent escalation
3. **Biosecurity** — engineered pandemic prevention, bioweapon screening (e.g. SecureDNA), far-UVC clean-air technology

**Values:** truth-seeking, far-sighted, risk-neutral, impartial across present and future generations; evidence-driven grantmaking process with two-stage grant investigation and counterfactual reasoning.

## Funds

### [Frontier AI Fund (FAIF)](https://www.longview.org/fund/frontier-ai-fund/)
Private discretionary fund. In its inaugural nine months (December 2024 – September 2025):
- **Raised:** $20 million
- **Disbursed:** $11.1 million to **18 organisations**
- **Focus areas:** AI safety research, AI engineering, field-building, communications, advocacy
- **Access:** Disbursement report shared with donors giving $100K+. Contact: [simran@longview.org](mailto:simran@longview.org)

### [Nuclear Weapons Policy Fund (NWPF)](https://www.longview.org/fund/nuclear-weapons-policy-fund/)
Managed with support from [Giving What We Can](https://www.givingwhatwecan.org/). Focus: countering new arms race (US, Russia, China), opposing destabilising nuclear systems, mitigating deterrence failure. Philanthropic funding in this space has been cut in half to under $40M; NWPF positions itself as the disciplined, prioritised counterweight.

**Featured NWPF grants:**
- [Carnegie Endowment for International Peace](https://carnegieendowment.org/) — escalation pathway research
- [Nuclear Information Project / Federation of American Scientists](https://fas.org/) — nuclear arsenal monitoring
- [Council on Strategic Risks](https://councilonstrategicrisks.org/) — avoiding inadvertent nuclear escalation

### [Emerging Challenges Fund (ECF)](https://www.longview.org/fund/emerging-challenges-fund/)
Public-facing pooled fund (anyone can donate). 2000+ donors. Managed with support from Giving What We Can. Additional test for ECF grantees: legible theory of impact + benefit from diverse funding sources (independence from industry actors).

**2025 ECF grantees (thematic areas):**
- Frontier AI governance in US and Europe (government capacity building, talent pipelines)
- US-China AI arms control dialogues
- AI capability evaluations and misuse research
- Broader societal implications of rapid AI progress

**Featured ECF grants:**
- [Interpretability at Harvard University](https://www.harvard.edu/) — mechanistic interpretability research (locating concepts/facts within neural networks)
- [Blueprint Biosecurity](https://www.blueprintbiosecurity.org/) — far-UVC clean air technology safety research
- Carnegie Endowment for International Peace — nuclear escalation pathway workshops
