# Aarhus University Research on the Anthropocene (AURA)

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Full deep research completed. Merged stub notes, filled all sections with primary sources. Confirmed AURA concluded in 2018 but its intellectual lineage continues through BLUE project and Bubandt's ongoing work. Key legacies: *Arts of Living on a Damaged Planet* (2017) and *Feral Atlas* (2020 digital publication).

Major European research center for Anthropocene studies, hosted at Aarhus University, Denmark. Funded 2013–2018 by the Danish National Research Foundation's Niels Bohr Professorship programme (approx. $5M USD). Established around Chinese-American anthropologist **Anna Tsing**'s appointment as Niels Bohr Professor — one of Denmark's most prestigious research appointments. AURA pioneered transdisciplinary Anthropocene studies combining anthropology, biology, and philosophy, and coined the central methodology of **multispecies ethnography**: studying humans and non-humans (mushrooms, salmon, insects, fungi) as co-constituted life forms shaping each other through damaged landscapes.

## Mission & Objectives

**Mission statement:** "To open up a novel and truly trans-disciplinary field of research into the Anthropocene which is needed to understand the kinds of lives that are made and the futures that are possible in the ruined, re-wilded, and unintended landscapes of the Anthropocene."

**Core research program:** "Living in the Anthropocene: Discovering the Potential of Unintended Design on Anthropogenic Landscapes" — examining what Tsing calls "unintentional design": human projects that produce landscapes we did not intend, and the non-human life forms that flourish within those ruins.

**Methodological signature:** Trans-disciplinary collaboration between:
- Anthropology (fieldwork-based ethnography)
- Biology (ecology, mycology, botany)
- Philosophy (ontology, STS, feminist theory)
- Environmental humanities

**Duration:** September 1, 2013 – December 31, 2018 (5 years)

**Funder:** [Danish National Research Foundation](https://dg.dk/en/) — via the Niels Bohr Professorship programme, which brings senior international scholars to Denmark to host research programs "characterized by novelty, creativity, and excellence"

## History & Founding

**2010:** Anna Tsing spends six months at Aarhus University on a Guggenheim Fellowship. Nils Bubandt is already professor there. Initial collaboration begins.

**2013 June:** Tsing awarded one of six $5M Niels Bohr Professorships by the Danish National Research Foundation. [Announcement from UCSC News](https://news.ucsc.edu/2013/06/tsing-danish-professorship/). She becomes one of the first US anthropologists to receive a Danish national research professorship.

**2013 September 1:** AURA officially opens. Program: conferences and fellowships for graduate students, postdocs, and visiting scholars. Tsing will split time between Aarhus and her home institution UC Santa Cruz.

**2014:** Tsing spends academic year 2014-15 in full residency at Aarhus (Denmark's largest university with 43,600 students, founded 1928, located ~120 miles west of Copenhagen).

**2014 May (UCSC kickoff):** Conference "Imagining Life and Death in Difficult Times" at UCSC with science fiction author Ursula K. Le Guin as featured participant. Also launches the "Bateson Initiative" honoring Gregory Bateson.

**2013–2018:** AURA hosts:
- Guest Professorship Programme (13 international scholars)
- Visiting PhD student programme (5+ visiting researchers)
- Regular workshops and conferences
- Working Paper series (3+ volumes)
- Field research sites, including former Danish brown-coal mine at Søby Brunkulslejer

**2017:** *Arts of Living on a Damaged Planet* published by University of Minnesota Press — AURA's flagship collective edited volume.

**2018:** *Feral Dynamics of Post-Industrial Ruin* special issue of *Journal of Ethnobiology* published — focused on Søby brown-coal mine. Tsing, Bubandt, Latour, Stengers roundtable in *Ethnos*.

**December 31, 2018:** AURA concludes its DNRF-funded phase.

**2020:** *Feral Atlas: The More-than-Human Anthropocene* published as interactive digital atlas (Stanford University Press Digital) — AURA's most technically innovative legacy output.

**2021–present:** Legacy continues via **BLUE: Multispecies Ethnographies of Oceans in Crisis** (PI: Nils Bubandt, funded by Danish Independent Research Fund) and **Centre for Environmental Humanities** at AU.

## Key Publications & Output

### Flagship Books

**[*Arts of Living on a Damaged Planet: Ghosts and Monsters of the Anthropocene*](https://www.upress.umn.edu/9781517902377/arts-of-living-on-a-damaged-planet/)** (2017)
- Editors: Anna Tsing, Heather Swanson, Elaine Gan, Nils Bubandt
- University of Minnesota Press
- Two-sided volume: "Ghosts" (hauntings of damaged ecologies) + "Monsters" (nonhuman entanglements). Includes essays from scientists, writers, artists.
- Contributors include Deborah Bird Rose, Scott Gilbert, Donna Haraway
- Reviews from Isabelle Stengers, Peter Galison (Harvard), Michael Hadfield (U Hawaii). *Shelf Awareness*: "Mandatory reading." *Glasgow Review of Books*: "Convincing case for relevance of 'hard science' to art and politics."
- One of the defining texts of multispecies studies and the environmental humanities.

**[*Feral Atlas: The More-than-Human Anthropocene*](https://feralatlas.supdigital.org/)** (2020)
- Editors: Anna Tsing, Jennifer Deger, Alder Keleman Saxena, Feifei Zhou
- Stanford University Press Digital publication (interactive web atlas)
- Mapping "feral" entities — non-human life forms and phenomena unleashed by human infrastructures that then escape human control (invasive species, toxic chemicals, feral domesticated animals, nuclear radiation)
- 79 contributors across disciplines including artists, scientists, anthropologists, geographers
- Innovative digital format: networked essays + visual mapping + sound + image
- Winner of multiple awards; recognized as a landmark in digital humanities publishing

### Key Field Site: Søby Brunkulslejer
A former brown-coal (lignite) mine in Jutland, Denmark — used as central empirical case study. The post-industrial ruin shows "feral dynamics": abandoned mine now rewilded, revealing how human industrial action creates unexpected multispecies landscapes. Documented across multiple AURA papers and the *Journal of Ethnobiology* special issue.

## Key Contributions
1. **Institutionalized multispecies ethnography** — Transformed it from a methodological suggestion into a funded research program with dedicated journal issues, working papers, and flagship volume
2. **Coined the "Patchy Anthropocene"** — Against the totalizing macro-narrative of "the Anthropocene," AURA emphasized patchiness: heterogeneous zones of damage, recovery, abandonment, and unexpected flourishing
3. **Feral ecology framework** — "Feral" as concept for entities unleashed by human infrastructure that then escape control — invasive species, chemicals, animals — and shape landscapes unpredictably
4. **Arts of Living** — The phrase itself, as methodology: attending to forms of life that persist, adapt, and create livability within ruins rather than mourning pristine nature
5. **Transatlantic institutional bridge** — AURA connected European environmental humanities (Danish universities, Rachel Carson Center) with US West Coast multispecies studies (UCSC, Stanford, UC Berkeley)
6. **Innovative publication formats** — *Feral Atlas* as landmark digital scholarship, pushing beyond conventional academic monograph

## Why Interesting
AURA is the **institutional origin point of multispecies environmental humanities** in Europe — the tradition Amber is already orbiting through more-than-human design, posthuman computing, and AI ecology discourses. Understanding AURA means understanding where today's vocabulary comes from: "feral," "patchy Anthropocene," "arts of living," "unintentional design."

The unique structure — a US scholar (Tsing) institutionally embedded in Denmark — created a transatlantic network that now connects UCSC, Stanford, Danish universities, and the Rachel Carson Center in Munich. This network is still active and is the European wing of the more-than-human intellectual tradition.

For Amber specifically: the concept of **"feral dynamics"** — human-designed systems that escape their intended function and reshape their environment unpredictably — is directly applicable to AI systems, algorithmic ecologies, and post-human design. AURA's frameworks provide the vocabulary for Amber's research interests that goes beyond the techno-centric discourse.
