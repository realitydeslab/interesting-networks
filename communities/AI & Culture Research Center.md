# AI & Culture Research Center (AIC)

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Full deep research. Comprehensive profile with all faculty, affiliates, publications, events, and Amber-relevance analysis.
- **2026-02-26:** Discovered through [[Bogna Konior]] research. Created stub note.

NYU Shanghai's Center for AI and Culture investigates the philosophical and cultural implications of artificial intelligence as it suffuses and transforms the contemporary world. Closely affiliated with [[NYU Shanghai IMA]], AIC benefits from an extensive network of scholars, artists, and practitioners. The Center makes full use of its location in cosmopolitan Shanghai at the premier Sino-American university to research the cutting edge of emerging planetary media. Directed by [[Anna Greenspan]] (CCRU founding member) with [[Bogna Konior]] as core media theory faculty — this is the most theoretically serious AI + culture institution operating from within China.

---

## Mission & Objectives

Investigates AI as it suffuses and transforms the contemporary world. Research draws on:
- Philosophy (analytic and continental traditions)
- Media theory
- History of science and computation broadly conceived
- Cultural and artistic practice
- Empirical research and production

Research clusters: AI consciousness and cognition, AI personalities and unexpected behaviors, evolutionary dynamics in biological and synthetic systems, ethics and value alignment, human-AI relations, posthuman and alien intelligence.

**Reading Group — AI & Humanities:** Regular sessions bringing together scholars from Media Studies, Computer Science, Linguistics, and Philosophy. Topics: machine consciousness, AI behaviors, ethics/value alignment, human-AI relations, posthuman intelligence.

## Key Contributions

1. **Critical AI theory from a non-Western standpoint** — The only major academic center producing serious philosophical-critical work on AI explicitly from within and about China's own AI development context.

2. ***Machine Decision is Not Final*** — A landmark interdisciplinary volume that does what almost no other AI publication attempts: treats China not as a threat or an imitation but as a distinct intelligence tradition with different foundational assumptions about what "artificial" and "intelligent" mean.

3. **CCRU lineage into Chinese context** — Anna Greenspan bringing her Cybernetic Culture Research Unit intellectual formation into Shanghai is theoretically remarkable — accelerationist/speculative realist genealogy meets Chinese technoculture.

4. **Bogna Konior's dark forest framework** — A new conceptual vocabulary for understanding internet behavior and AI development through the lens of Liu Cixin's cosmic sociology. Poised to become influential.

5. **IMA Artists in Residency** — Year-long research residency for artists/researchers as visiting faculty at NYU Shanghai.

6. **Reading group infrastructure** — Regular "AI & Humanities" reading group bridging CS, Media Studies, Linguistics, Philosophy.

## Why Interesting

The AIC is uniquely positioned at the intersection of **three things that rarely come together:**

1. **Theoretical seriousness** — Anna Greenspan's CCRU formation, Bogna Konior's dark forest theory, external affiliates Gary Zhexi Zhang and Amy Ireland: this is a philosophically sophisticated operation, not an industry AI ethics center.

2. **Chinese situatedness** — Actually *in* Shanghai, actually engaging with Chinese AI discourse from inside, not as external observers. The *Machine Decision* volume is the clearest articulation of why this matters: China doesn't just have different AI labs, it has different foundational concepts of what "artificial" and "intelligent" mean.

3. **Art-practice integration** — The IMA connection means the AIC's theoretical work is continuously grounded in media arts practice. Courses like "Writing with AI" and "Embodied Creative AI" are not theory-only — they're making things.

For Amber's research taste profile: **cosmotechnics** (Yuk Hui is referenced throughout); **sinofuturism** (the whole project); **critical computing** (structural not checklist); **machine behavior** (reading group focus); **more-than-human** (Coccia, Pisters events). This is five-out-of-five alignment.

The AIC is the institutional instantiation of the intellectual tradition that Bogna Konior represents. If Amber is drawn to Bogna, she should be drawn to the AIC as the community infrastructure around that kind of thought.
