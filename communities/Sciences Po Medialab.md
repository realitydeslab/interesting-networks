## Mission & Objectives
The [Sciences Po Medialab](https://medialab.sciencespo.fr/) is a research laboratory at Sciences Po Paris focused on the application of digital methods to the social sciences. Founded and originally directed by [[Bruno Latour]] (2006–2017), it developed the pedagogy of **controversy mapping** (Forccast project) — a technique for teaching students to analyze complex sociotechnical controversies through digital network analysis.

After Latour's retirement (2017) and death (2022), the lab continues under new leadership, working on:
- Digital methods for social science
- Controversy mapping
- Data visualization
- Network analysis of public debates

The lab developed or adapted tools including **Hyphe** (web crawler for mapping online communities), **Gephi** (network visualization, though independent), and other digital mapping tools. The Forccast project trained a generation of researchers in STS-informed digital analysis.

## Key People
- **[[Bruno Latour]]** — founder and first Scientific Director (2006–2017)
- [Dominique Cardon](https://medialab.sciencespo.fr/equipe/dominique-cardon/) — sociologist of the internet; current prominent member
- [Mathieu Jacomy](https://medialab.sciencespo.fr/equipe/mathieu-jacomy/) — developer; created Gephi and other network tools

## Key Contributions
- **Controversy mapping pedagogy** — a method for teaching STS analysis through digital mapping of public controversies
- **Forccast project** — EU-funded training programme for controversy mapping
- **Hyphe** — open-source web crawler designed for social science network mapping
