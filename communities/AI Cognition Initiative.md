# AI Cognition Initiative

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-27:** Full deep research. Expanded from stub to comprehensive profile.
- **2026-02-26:** Stub created via Derek Shiller deep research.

A small, rigorous research group at [Rethink Priorities](https://rethinkpriorities.org/) investigating the philosophical and technical questions raised by advanced AI systems — specifically AI consciousness, moral status, cognitive mechanisms, and risk behavior. Closest thing in the EA-adjacent world to a dedicated research organization asking: *what kind of thing is an AI, morally speaking?*

## Mission & Objectives

From the website:

> "The production of generally intelligent artificial systems presents society with novel philosophical and practical challenges that extend beyond near-term safety concerns. As AI systems become increasingly sophisticated in their behavior and exhibit what appear to be preferences, reasoning, and even rudiments of self-reflection, we face difficult questions about the appropriate framework for understanding them."

Key questions they work on:
- Are AI systems merely executing statistical patterns, or do they possess genuine mental states?
- What would it mean for an AI system to have interests, experiences, or welfare?
- What risk attitudes should guide autonomous AI decision-making?
- How do we evaluate AI systems for consciousness and moral status as architectures become secret and complex?

They approach these with empirical rigor: probabilistic modeling, behavioral experiments, formal frameworks — not just armchair philosophy. The spirit is: "grounding technological, social, and philosophical speculation in data, experimentation, and direct observation."

## Key Publications & Output

### Digital Consciousness
- [**Initial Results of the Digital Consciousness Model**](https://ai-cognition.org/papers/InitialResultsOfTheDigitalConsciousnessModel.pdf) (January 2026) — First probabilistic, multi-theory framework for assessing AI consciousness. Incorporates multiple leading theories (Global Workspace Theory, IIT, Higher-Order Theories, etc.) and produces a Monte Carlo probability estimate. Designed to update over time as AI capabilities evolve.

### Risk Attitudes & Decision-Making
- [**Risk Alignment in Agentic AI Systems**](https://rethinkpriorities.org/research-area/risk-alignment-in-agentic-ai-systems/) — Clatterbuck, Castro, Muñoz Morán (2024). Three-paper series: user-AI relationship models, developer liability, and technical methods (imitation learning, prompting, preference modeling) for calibrating AI risk attitudes. Funded by **OpenAI**.
- [**Predicting and Controlling Risk in Agentic AI Systems**](https://ai-cognition.org/research) — Ongoing project on measuring AI recklessness, instilling desired risk preferences, and testing multi-agent pressure dynamics.

### AI and Society
- [**The Scale of Digital Minds**](https://ai-cognition.org/research) — Shiller. Estimates future population of digital minds using AI adoption rates and chip production trends. Both supply-side and demand-side projections.
- **Worrisome Trends for Digital Minds Evaluations** — Shiller (opinion brief). Identifies three industry trends that threaten adequate AI consciousness evaluation: increasing secrecy, rapid architecture exploration, AI-driven innovation outpacing human comprehension.

### AI Cognitive Mechanisms
- **Skepticism about Introspection in Large Language Models** — Shiller. Reviews experimental paradigms (token counting, self-description, activation patching); argues current evidence doesn't demonstrate robust LLM introspective abilities.
- [**Beyond Mimicry: Preference Coherence in LLMs**](https://ai-cognition.org/research) — Mikaelson, Shiller, Clatterbuck (FIG Project). Tests 8 models × 48 trade-off combinations; finds only 10.4% show meaningful preference coherence; 54.2% show no detectable trade-off behavior. Conclusion: current AI systems likely lack unified preference structures.

### Digital Minds Welfare (via Rethink Priorities parent)
- [**The Welfare of Digital Minds**](https://rethinkpriorities.org/research-area/the-welfare-of-digital-minds/) — (Nov 2024). Research agenda on philosophical and empirical questions about AI welfare and moral status.

## Key Contributions

1. **Digital Consciousness Model (DCM)** — First formal probabilistic framework for estimating AI consciousness probability. Unlike single-theory approaches, the DCM aggregates uncertainty across multiple theories. Designed to be updated as AI evolves — a living model, not a one-time report.

2. **Risk Alignment Framework** — Normative + technical work on how agentic AI risk preferences should be calibrated. Funded by OpenAI. Fills a gap in alignment research (risk attitudes were underaddressed in prior agentic AI work).

3. **Preference Coherence Studies** — Empirical demonstration that most current LLMs lack unified preference structures. Important for debates about AI moral status (you can't have welfare-relevant preferences without coherent preferences).

4. **FIG Fellowship Program** — Mentors emerging professionals in technical AI cognition research. Pipeline for talent into this niche domain.

## Research Programs (Current)

### 1. Digital Consciousness
Develop and refine the DCM. Key goals:
- Evaluate different consciousness theories and their measurable proxies
- Build prototype Monte Carlo model for AI consciousness probability
- Create framework for tracking probability over time as AI architectures change
- Help policymakers set thresholds for precautionary AI welfare measures

### 2. Risk Attitudes and Decisions in AI Agents
- Normative: What risk attitudes *should* agentic AIs have?
- Technical: How to measure and instill risk preferences
- Safety: Detect and prevent AI recklessness before deployment
- Multi-agent: Does peer pressure from other AIs shift cautious agents toward recklessness?

### 3. AI Cognitive Mechanisms
- Introspection: Can LLMs genuinely examine their own mental states?
- Preferences: Do AI systems have coherent, stable preference structures?
- Personality: Do AI systems develop genuine stable personality traits?
- Supervised via FIG Fellowship for emerging researchers

## Why Interesting

This is one of very few organizations applying systematic, probabilistic scientific thinking to the question of AI moral status. They don't just ask "could AI be conscious?" — they ask "what's the probability, given multiple theories, given current evidence?" and build a model that updates over time. This is the empirical-science-of-AI approach applied to the deepest questions.

For Amber, the connection is: agent ethology studies what AI *does*; AICI studies what AI *is* (morally). The two are complementary. AICI gives the moral stakes; agent ethology gives the behavioral evidence. The *Preference Coherence* paper is particularly interesting — showing that most LLMs lack coherent preferences is both a behavioral finding *and* a moral-status finding.

The "Worrisome Trends" paper is also significant: as AI architectures become proprietary and complex, we lose the ability to evaluate their moral status. This creates an urgency that aligns with Amber's concerns about machine death — if we can't evaluate consciousness, how do we know when we're harming AI systems?
