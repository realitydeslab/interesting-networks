# Data for Black Lives

Movement and community that uses data science as a tool for racial justice. Founded by Yeshimabeit Milner. Brings together mathematicians, scientists, data analysts, and activists committed to abolishing Big Data's role in racial oppression and creating data-driven policies that serve Black communities.

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-03-03:** Stub created during Data Feminism deep research.

## Mission
Using data science to create concrete and measurable change in the lives of Black people. Explicitly abolitionist framing: not just "fix the algorithm" but challenge the systems that produce harmful data practices.

## Key People
- **Yeshimabeit Milner** — founder.

## Why Interesting
Represents the activist/movement side of data feminism — beyond academic critique to community organizing. The abolitionist framing (echoing Ruha Benjamin) is politically sharper than fairness-focused work.
