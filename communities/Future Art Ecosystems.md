# Future Art Ecosystems (FAE)

Annual strategic briefing series produced by [Serpentine Arts Technologies](https://www.serpentinegalleries.org/) in collaboration with [Rival Strategy](https://rival.tools/). The authoritative policy-shaping publication for practitioners and organisations working at art × advanced technology (AxAT). Published since 2020. Five editions to date. Has grown into a live community of artists, technologists, policymakers, and researchers, with a Telegram group and monthly community calls.

**Mission:** Building 21st-century cultural infrastructure for the public good, focused on four areas: Advanced Production Capabilities, Protocols for Organisational Interoperability, New Ownership + Distribution Models, New Systems of Measurement.

## Mission & Objectives

FAE positions itself as the field's annual strategic compass — not a journal, not a manifesto, but a **briefing**: concepts, language, and arguments for ecosystem actors to integrate into operational agendas. The briefings target:
- Cultural organisations building AxAT infrastructure
- Policymakers shaping R&D definitions and innovation frameworks
- Artists navigating the intersection of practice, technology, and institution
- Technologists and funders understanding the arts as R&D actors

Core conceptual frame: "AxAT" (Art and Advanced Technologies) as a distinct category requiring dedicated infrastructure — advanced production capabilities, protocols for interoperability, new ownership/distribution models, and new measurement systems.

**Policy ambition:** FAE briefings are submitted to UK government consultations and shape funding programmes. FAE5 contributed directly to the UK Industrial Strategy, and proposes legislation for Creative R&D recognition under DCMS and DSIT.

### FAE5 Structure (Chapters)
1. **Preface** — Victoria Ivanova framing
2. **Introduction** — creative R&D as distinct AxAT category
3. **Creative R&D** — defining the concept, challenging OECD Frascati Manual exclusion of arts from R&D frameworks, case for expansion
4. **Artist** — how artists function as creative R&D agents (case studies: Lauren Lee McCarthy, Danielle Brathwaite-Shirley, Operator, liam Young, Alice Bucknell)
5. **Infrastructure** — AxAT organisational models: RSC, Serpentine Arts Technologies, NEW INC, 221A, Design Museum Future Observatory
6. **Proposals** — concrete policy asks to UK government (see below)
7. **Postface** — Hans Ulrich Obrist + Bettina Korek

### FAE5 Policy Proposals
1. **Establish Cross-Departmental Entity** — jointly overseen by DCMS and DSIT dedicated to Creative R&D
2. **Broaden DSIT's Definition of R&D** — explicitly include arts, humanities, and social sciences (HMRC currently excludes them)
3. **Adopt Ecosystem Measurement Models** — new metrics capturing innovation value of cultural sector
4. **(Further proposals likely — only first 3 captured in research)*
