# Design Academy Eindhoven

One of Europe's most prestigious and intellectually rigorous design schools, in Eindhoven, Netherlands (De Witte Dame building; moving to Microlab on Strijp-S by 2027). ~700 students. QS World University Rankings 2024: **joint 9th globally in Art & Design** (82.2/100). Known for a conceptual, critical, and socially engaged approach to design well beyond commercial practice.

Five MA programmes (Contextual Design, Critical Inquiry Lab, Geo—Design, Information Design, Social Design) consistently produce graduates who become leading figures in speculative/critical design and related fields.

## 🏗️ Institutional Context

- **Founded:** 1947 (as Academie voor Industriële Vormgeving); renamed DAE in 1999
- **Location:** De Witte Dame, Eindhoven. Moving to **Microlab on Strijp-S** (Eindhoven) by 2027
- **Outgoing CD:** Joseph Grima (Space Caviar co-founder; served 2× 4-year terms)
- **Incoming Creative Director:** [[Ramon Amaro]] (from Sept 2025) — previously Senior Researcher in Digital Culture at Nieuwe Instituut, Rotterdam; founded -1 (intersectional space for design/architecture/technology experimentation there); author of *The Black Technical Object* (Sternberg Press, 2023) on race and ML
- **Annual event:** GraduateShow during **Dutch Design Week** (October, Eindhoven) — one of Europe's most-watched design events
- **Research department:** Professorships and Research Innovation & Advancement (RIA) team; contact: research@designacademy.nl

## 📚 MA Programmes (Deep Dive)

### 1. Critical Inquiry Lab (CIL)
- **Page:** designacademy.nl/page/408/critical-inquiry-lab
- **Head:** [[Patricia Reed]]
- **Former name:** Design Curating and Writing
- **Philosophy:** Transdisciplinary; research-based design as vehicle for public activation. Explicitly uses "human/non-human relational terms" — ecosocial justice, intergenerational thinking, planetary condition. Design as a "relay" of external making and internal self-examination. Hope as verb, not attitude ("cunning hope").
- **Amber relevance:** **High.** Most explicitly more-than-human of all DAE programmes. Patricia Reed's framing of "ecosocial justice" + "human/non-human relational terms" maps directly onto Amber's more-than-human thread.
- **Tutors 2025-2026:** Merve Bedir, Danae Io (danaep.com), Matthew C. Wilson, Aiwen Yin, Kris Dittel, Gijs de Boer, Louis Braddock Clarke, Juan Arturo Garcia, Lodovica Guarnieri, Nirit Peled, Karoline Świeżyński, Saskia van Stein, Yağmur Uçkunkaya
- **Recent past tutors:** Patricia Reed, Gabriel A. Maher, Silvio Lorusso, Gascia Ouzounian, Iris van der Tuin & Nanna Verhoeff

### 2. Geo—Design
- **Page:** designacademy.nl/page/5809/geo%E2%80%94design
- **Founded:** 2020 (youngest programme)
- **Heads:** [[Metahaven]] (Vinca Kruk & Daniel van der Velden) — artist collective doing filmmaking, design, writing; working on film about AI and poetry (*The Feeling Sonnets*); new book on the future of the beholder's share in art (Verso, Spring 2025)
- **Former heads:** Formafantasma (Andrea Trimarchi & Simone Farresin) — led department to its initial "critical materiality" stance
- **Philosophy:** Designing with the Earth — ecology, geology, geopolitics, biology, physics, cognitive science. Field research + interdisciplinary dialogues. Moving image as a research tool. Art & science as adjacent practices.
- **Curriculum highlights:** Critical Materiality, Earth Practice, Cinematic Moving Image, Investigative Moving Image, Art & Science, Glaciology, Hypertext/Coding/Worldbuilding, 3D Worldbuilding, Ecology studios
- **Amber relevance:** **Medium-high.** Planetary/more-than-human design, art-science, speculative materiality. Metahaven's interest in AI and cinema relevant to Amber's research aesthetics.
- **Tutors 2024-2025:** Bahar Noorizadeh (investigative moving image), Ali Eslami (hypertext cognitive models), Carlo Lorenzetti (earth practice), Elizabeth Case (glaciology), Agustina Woodgate (graduation studio), Vibeke Mascini (art & science), Margarida Mendes (thesis/fieldwork), Gabriel Maher (graduation studio), Meredith Root-Bernstein (ecology), Merve Bedir (thesis)

### 3. Information Design
- **Page:** designacademy.nl/page/5827/information-design
- **Head:** Marco Ferrari (co-founder of Studio Folder, studiofolder.it)
- **Former head:** Joost Grootens (2010–2022)
- **Philosophy:** Politics and agency of information; rejects authorial "data storytelling" in favour of critical, collective, open-source practices. Addresses entangled relationships between social injustices, extraction, racial discrimination, spatial violence, and climate emergency. Strives for open repositories and non-extractive data practices.
- **Research Labs:** Parametric Truth Lab (Simone Niquille) + Roots Lab (Julian Oliver) — run as quasi-scientific/activist labs embedded in design curriculum
- **Amber relevance:** **Medium-high.** Closest programme to critical computing in the DAE. Julian Oliver (Roots Lab) is a canonical critical engineer. Anna Engelhardt (decolonialhacker.org) brings decolonial + technical critique.
- **Core Tutors 2024-2025:** Matteo Azzi & Giorgio Uboldi (Calibro), Eugene Yiu Nam Cheung (decolonialhacker.org), Sophie Dyer, Anna Engelhardt, Jannetje in 't Veld, Toon Koehorst, Silvio Lorusso, Simone Niquille, Julian Oliver, Arthur Roeloffzen, Vera Sacchetti, The Rodina
- **Guest Tutors:** Galo Canizares, Chris Lee, Noam Youngrak Son, Sanjana Varghese, Quadrature, RGBdog
- **Other current tutors:** [[Lukáš Likavčan]] (teaches here per earlier research)

### 4. Contextual Design
- **Page:** designacademy.nl/page/405/contextual-design
- **Philosophy:** Design as cultural critique within multiple simultaneous contexts — systems of power, colonialism, ecological destruction, spatial/social/historical contexts. Feminist + ecological theory as theoretical foundation. Emphasizes imagination, narrative, authorship.
- **Reading list:** bell hooks, Audre Lorde, Sara Ahmed, Nirmal Puwar, T.J. Demos, Adrienne Maree Brown & Walidah Imarisha, Rosi Braidotti (*Post Human Knowledge*), Silvia Frederici, Claudia Mareis & Nina Paim (*Design Struggles*)
- **Current/recent tutors:** Formafantasma (Simone Farresin & Andrea Trimarchi), Sonja Bäumel, Anna Aagaard Jensen, Katharina Mischer & Thomas Traxler, Kostas Lambridis, Damir Khairat, Tamar Shafrir, Aram Lee, Aurelie Hoegy, Minji Choi
- **Amber relevance:** Medium. Strong feminist/ecological lens, Braidotti posthumanism on reading list. Somewhat less technically engaged than CIL or InfoDesign.

### 5. Social Design
- **Page:** designacademy.nl/page/5839/social-design
- **Founded:** 2012
- **Philosophy:** Material as mediator/activator/disruptor; proactive ethics; storytelling and collective imagination; collaboration as core practice. Critical of design's colonial/Eurocentric legacy. "Designing not-yets."
- **Tutors/heads:** Daniel Parnitzke, Kuang-Yi Ku, Rawan Khater, Basse Stittgen, Audrey Large, Laura Deschl, Tanguy Pitavy, Emma Bereau, Daniela Tokashiki Kunigami, Ginevra Petrozzi, Femke Hoppenbrouwer
- **Amber relevance:** Low-medium. Ethics-driven but less technically critical. The *storying* framing is interesting for speculative design.

## 💡 Why Interesting (for Amber)

DAE is the closest thing Europe has to a **critical/speculative design institution that consistently punches above its weight**. Several specific reasons why it matters now:

1. **Ramon Amaro's appointment** (Sept 2025) is a significant signal. He's a critical computing theorist (race + ML) taking over a major design school. This could shift DAE's intellectual direction toward critical AI and computational culture — exactly Amber's territory.

2. **CIL's more-than-human framing** under Patricia Reed is the most explicit "more-than-human design" pedagogy in Europe at the moment. If Amber wants to teach or lecture here, CIL is the natural home.

3. **Lukáš + Bogna connections** mean Amber has warm entry points. These are people who can facilitate invitations.
