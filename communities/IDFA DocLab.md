# IDFA DocLab

One of the world's most important platforms for interactive documentary art, immersive non-fiction, and experimental media storytelling. The new media strand of [IDFA](https://www.idfa.nl/) (International Documentary Film Festival Amsterdam — the world's largest documentary film festival), DocLab has been curating the intersection of documentary ethics and interactive/immersive technology since 2007. Located in Amsterdam, held annually in November.

## Mission & Objectives

IDFA DocLab's mission is to explore the art of interactive and immersive non-fiction storytelling. Since 2007, it has developed into one of the leading international platforms at the intersection of:
- Documentary ethics and practice
- Interactive art and technology
- XR/VR/AR experiences
- Installation art with non-fiction roots
- Data art and computational storytelling
- Live performance and audience participation

DocLab's distinctive position: it is embedded in a **cinema/documentary context** rather than a pure art-tech or games context. This gives it a unique curatorial lens — asking what interactive media *owes* to documentary's ethical commitments to reality, truth, and witness.

Key curatorial concept: **"phenomenal friction"** — the productive discomfort between a viewer's expectations of documentary truth and the strange affordances of interactive/immersive media.

## Key Contributions

1. **Legitimized interactive documentary as an art form** — by embedding it in a prestigious documentary film festival, giving it cultural capital that art-tech circuits couldn't provide
2. **The DocLab R&D Programme** — year-round research programme with MIT Open Documentary Lab, using the festival as a living lab
3. **Created the field vocabulary** — "interactive non-fiction," "immersive documentary," "phenomenal friction" are partially DocLab coinages
4. **Championed ethical questions** — persistently asking what interactive/VR media owes to documentary's commitments to truth and representation
5. **Cross-sector bridge** — connects documentary filmmakers with new media artists, tech developers, and data scientists
6. **DocLab Forum** — three-day co-production/co-financing market, supporting interactive projects in development

## Events & Programmes

### Annual Exhibition
- ~30 interactive installations and VR/AR experiences
- 11 days duration (during IDFA, November, Amsterdam)
- Held at multiple venues including [Flemish Cultural Center de Brakke Grond](https://www.brakke-grond.nl/)
- Features both competition works and invited projects
- Open to public; on-site reservations for VR experiences

### Competitions
- **IDFA DocLab Award for Digital Storytelling** — web docs, apps, data art, audio, interactive journalism
- **IDFA DocLab Immersive Non-Fiction Award** — VR, AR, installation, live performance, fulldome
- **Special Jury Awards for Creative Technology** — technical innovation

### Live Cinema Events
One-off interactive evenings during the festival period. Past formats:
- Immersive audience experiments (Anagram's *A Glitch in Time*, Moniker's *Crowd Assessment*)
- Musical cinema experiences (*Bear 71 Live*, *#Alleman*, *Dreams*)
- Interactive dinners (*Eat | Tech | Kitchen*, *A Dinner with Frankenstein AI*)
- Theatre performances (Ontroerend Goed's *£¥€$*)
- World premieres (Shared Individual, *{The And} Live*, *This American Life* with Ira Glass)

### Interactive Conference (DocLab Conference)
- One-day conference during IDFA
- Artists, thinkers from art, technology, science, documentary
- Past speakers: Rem Koolhaas, Ira Glass, Errol Morris, Sophia al Maria, William Uricchio, Jonathan Harris, Marleen Stikker

### IDFA DocLab R&D Program
- Year-round research programme
- In collaboration with [MIT Open Documentary Lab](https://odl.mit.edu/) (Cambridge, MA)
- Uses IDFA festival as a living lab
- Focus on specific research questions from the field each year
- Targets artists, developers, scientists, entrepreneurs in interactive/immersive content

### IDFA DocLab Forum
- Part of the larger [IDFA Forum](https://professionals.idfa.nl/) co-production market
- Three-day co-production and co-financing market for interactive projects
- Projects at all production stages — development, co-production, finishing
- Partners find new collaborators, receive in-depth feedback

## Why Interesting

DocLab is where documentary ethics meets interactive art — a rare platform that takes both seriously. Unlike pure art-tech venues (Ars Electronica, transmediale) it maintains documentary's commitment to real people and real situations. Unlike pure film festivals, it genuinely champions experimental form and technology.

For Amber specifically:
1. **Agent ethology connection**: Works like *Agence* (AI agents in VR) and *Artificial: Room One* (one-on-one AI conversation) preview exactly the kinds of questions Amber is asking about machine behavior — but in an experiential/artistic rather than academic register
2. **HoloKit / AR opportunity**: DocLab actively looks for AR and location-based experiences — HoloKit-based work would be distinctive in this context
3. **Non-fiction dimension**: Amber's work often has a documentary quality (the China Academy of Art student performances, the field studies of agent behavior) that could frame interactive/XR work for DocLab submission
4. **Network bridge**: DocLab connects documentary filmmakers, tech artists, and scientists — exactly the kind of interdisciplinary junction that Amber inhabits
5. **MIT connection**: DocLab's R&D partnership with MIT Open Documentary Lab, and speakers like William Uricchio, suggests institutional alignment with Amber's academic trajectory
