# Forge Project

Native-led nonprofit organization with a lending collection of Indigenous art, located in New York state. Executive Director and Chief Curator: **[[Candice Hopkins]]** (CCS Bard alumna). Mandate: cultivate and advance Indigenous leadership in arts and culture. Partners with CCS Bard to offer the **Forge Scholarship for Indigenous Art** — full tuition coverage + stipend for CCS Bard MA students focusing on Indigenous art, advised by the Fellow in Indigenous Art History and Curatorial Studies.

## Mission & Objectives
Advancing Indigenous art leadership through a lending collection, educational programs, and institutional partnerships. Brings Native-led perspectives into the mainstream contemporary art world.

## Key People
- **[[Candice Hopkins]]** — Executive Director and Chief Curator

## Events & Programmes
- **Forge Scholarship for Indigenous Art** (at CCS Bard) — Full tuition + stipend for CCS Bard MA students focusing on Indigenous art. Students are advised by the Forge Fellow in Indigenous Art History and Curatorial Studies. Part of a larger Bard College endowment gift. <https://ccs.bard.edu/school/application-information>
- CCS courses hosted at Forge Project as part of the Scholarship program

## Connected Institutions
- [[Center for Curatorial Studies Bard]] — formal partnership via Forge Scholarship and advising
- [[Bard College]] — broader institutional relationship (Center for Indigenous Studies)

## Why Interesting
The Forge Project is an example of how Indigenous-led institutions are reshaping the contemporary art world from within. Its partnership with CCS Bard shows how even elite curatorial education is being transformed by Indigenous perspectives on land, sovereignty, and art history.
