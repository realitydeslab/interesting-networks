# Forge Project

Native-led nonprofit arts organization on the unceded homelands of the Moh-He-Con-Nuck (Stockbridge-Munsee Band of Mohicans) in Taghkanic, NY (Hudson River Valley). Forge is a platform for Indigenous cultural leadership: a fellowship, lending art collection of 176+ contemporary Indigenous works, publishing imprint, convenings, and institutional partnerships. Buildings designed by Ai Weiwei — the only residential structures he's designed in the US. **Executive Director and Chief Curator: [[Candice Hopkins]] (Carcross/Tagish First Nation / Tlingit).** Note: website is [forgeproject.com](https://forgeproject.com/) (not .org, which is now a parked domain).

## Mission & Objectives

Forge Project's mandate is to cultivate and advance Indigenous leadership in arts and culture. It operates through:
- A **fellowship** program ($25,000/fellow, 3-week residency) for Indigenous artists, scholars, organizers, educators, and cultural workers
- A **lending collection** of 176+ artworks by contemporary Indigenous artists (available for institutional loans)
- **Convenings** — fully funded gatherings for Native people (art criticism, land research, community)
- **Publishing** — own imprint (Dancing Foxes Press partnership)
- **Partnerships** — CCS Bard, Stockbridge-Munsee Band, Sky High Farm, Musée des beaux-arts de Montréal

**Key phrase (self-description):** "decolonial arts and culture initiative." Forge positions itself as redistributing wealth to Indigenous cultural workers and as a platform for Indigenous sovereignty — not just a gallery or residency, but a model of governance.

## Key Contributions

1. **First major Native-led lending collection** of contemporary Indigenous art in the US
2. **Indigenous governance model** — ISC sits above board, ensuring Native leadership at all levels; first major example of this org structure in arts
3. **Forge Scholarship** — brings Indigenous curatorial studies to CCS Bard (elite curatorial MA), funding full Indigenous students
4. **Redistribution model** — describes itself as wealth redistribution infrastructure, not just philanthropy
5. **MOU with Stockbridge-Munsee** — diplomatic relations with the nation on whose lands they operate; model for institutional land accountability
6. **Publishing imprint** — first major anthology on Native performance art since 1969

## Why Interesting

### Decolonial institutional model
Forge is one of the most sophisticated attempts to build Indigenous-governed arts infrastructure in North America. The ISC-above-board governance model, the MOU with Stockbridge-Munsee, and the explicit framing around wealth redistribution and Indigenous sovereignty make it a live experiment in decolonial institution-building — not just in rhetoric but in legal structure.

### Relevance to Amber's research
- **More-than-human design:** Indigenous perspectives on land, sovereignty, and non-human relations are foundational to more-than-human thinking. Forge explicitly works with Indigenous ontologies where land has agency.
- **Decolonial AI (via Kite):** 2023 Fellow [[Kite (Suzanne Kite)]] (Oglála Lakȟóta) is one of the few Indigenous artists working on AI/ML from Indigenous ontological frameworks (Lakota AI Lab at Bard, PhD at Concordia on Lakota ontology + AI). This is a direct connection to Amber's decolonial AI interests.
- **Sovereignty as design principle:** Forge's "visual sovereignty," "narrative sovereignty," and "Indigenous futurity" framing aligns with how Amber thinks about sovereign agents and self-determination in AI.
- **Performance as knowledge:** Forge centers performance art as the origin of contemporary Indigenous art. This matches Amber's interest in embodied, practice-based approaches.
- **Model institutions:** Forge shows how an institution can be built around non-Western epistemology while engaging elite Western art world infrastructure (CCS Bard, Venice, documenta).
