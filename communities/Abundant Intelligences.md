# Abundant Intelligences

Six-year (2023–2029) international research program reimagining how to conceptualize and design AI based on Indigenous Knowledge systems. Co-directed by [[Jason Edward Lewis]] (Concordia, Montreal) and Prof. Hēmi Whaanga (Massey University, NZ). 48 co-investigators and collaborators at 13 universities/research institutes and 8 community-based organizations across Canada, US, and New Zealand.

## Core Argument
Indigenous epistemologies provide robust frameworks for AI that are *optimized for abundance rather than scarcity*, integrate technology into existing lifeways, and support flourishing of future generations. The goal is not to "diversify" AI — it's to build *better AI* by drawing on knowledge systems that have always maintained protocols for non-human relations.

## Three Axes
- **Integrations** — synthesizing Indigenous Knowledge practices and frameworks with mainstream AI research methods (focus: Years 1–2)
- **Imaginaries** — developing future imaginaries and speculative designs of Indigenous AI (focus: Years 3–4)
- **Intelligences** — applying IK perspectives to technical AI challenges including NLP, storytelling, social-neuro AI (focus: Years 5–6)

## Founding Pods
- **Niitsitapi/Kanien'kehá:ka Pod** — Lethbridge, AB
- **Ka Hawai'i Pae Aina Pod** — University of Hawai'i
- **Aotearoa Pod** — Wellington + Palmerston North + Kaitaia (NZ)

## Connection to Amber
The most ambitious existing research program working on Indigenous AI design. Directly relevant to Amber's interests in trustworthy AI, agent ethics, and more-than-human frameworks. The "Imaginaries" axis (speculative design of Indigenous AI) is a natural dialogue partner for Amber's speculative design work.
