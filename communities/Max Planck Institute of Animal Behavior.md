# Max Planck Institute of Animal Behavior (MPI-AB)

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-03-06:** Deep research completed. Full profile created. ICARUS resumed operations in orbit (Nov 2025). Iain Couzin received the Hector Science Award (Jan 2026). "When Minds Align" paper in *Nature Communications* reveals neural basis of flocking. Capuchin abduction study (cultural traditions) published in *Current Biology*.

The world's premier institute for studying animal behavior across scales — from individual neurons to planetary migration — and the closest biological ground truth for what agent ethology seeks to do with AI systems. Based in Konstanz and Radolfzell, Germany, co-embedded with the Centre for the Advanced Study of Collective Behaviour (CASCB) at the University of Konstanz, a DFG Cluster of Excellence (2019–2027).

## Mission & Objectives

**Mission:** To understand animal behavior at all levels of biological organization — from molecules to ecosystems — using cutting-edge quantitative methods including high-throughput tracking, machine vision, AI-powered behavioral analysis, and global sensor networks.

**Core Themes:**
1. **Collective behavior** — how simple individual interactions produce complex emergent group behavior
2. **Movement ecology & migration** — how animals navigate the globe, and the global consequences
3. **Animal societies** — how social structures evolve, how groups coordinate and make decisions
4. **Behavioral evolution** — what drives the evolution of complex behavior, from neurobiology to ecology

**Philosophical stance:** Animal behavior is a lens for understanding biological complexity at every scale. Methodologically, MPI-AB leads in *computational ethology* — bringing ML, computer vision, and AI into the study of behavior in the wild.

**Institutional twin:** The [CASCB](https://www.exc.uni-konstanz.de/collective-behaviour/) (Centre for the Advanced Study of Collective Behaviour) at University of Konstanz is a DFG Cluster of Excellence co-directed by Couzin, providing a joint intellectual home for collective behavior science. CASCB funds postdocs, runs seminars (including robotics/AI-adjacent ScyPhy seminars), and hosts an active graduate community.

1. **Decentralized decision-making without leaders** — mathematical framework for how collective decisions emerge from local interactions (Couzin 2005)
2. **Allocentric flocking** — ring attractor neural framework; collective motion emerges from individual navigation, not rule-following (2025)
3. **ICARUS** — first planetary-scale animal observatory from space; global biodiversity sensor network (Wikelski)
4. **Movebank** — world's largest animal tracking database: 9.8B locations, 1,633 taxa (open to researchers worldwide)

7. **GPS-based collective decision studies in wild primates** — quantifying democratic movement decisions in baboons (Strandburg-Peshkin 2015)
8. **VR for animals** — immersive virtual reality environments for studying fish behavior in controlled conditions (Couzin lab, University of Konstanz)

## Key Infrastructure & Technology

**Movebank** ([movebank.org](https://www.movebank.org/)) — Free, open animal tracking archive hosted by MPI-AB. As of Feb 2026: 9.8B animal locations, 8.4B sensor measurements, 9,755 studies, 1,633 taxa, 5,094 data owners. Long-term funded by Max Planck Society and University of Konstanz.

**ICARUS** ([icarus.mpg.de](https://www.icarus.mpg.de/en)) — International Cooperation for Animal Research Using Space. Miniature sensors on animals worldwide → satellite receiver → real-time global data. Initially on ISS (2020), now CubeSat (SpaceX Transporter-15, Nov 2025). Next-gen: ICARUS 2.0 with constellation of satellites. Enables near-real-time monitoring of animal locations, behavior, health, and environment worldwide.

**High-precision multi-animal tracking** — the Couzin lab has built custom tracking systems that follow thousands of individuals simultaneously in controlled environments and nature. Combined with machine learning for automated behavioral classification.

**VR for animals ("The Matrix for Fish")** — Immersive virtual reality systems placing fish in computer-generated social environments to study perception and collective behavior under controlled conditions. URL: [https://www.uni-konstanz.de/en/university/news-and-media/current-announcements/news-in-detail/die-matrix-fuer-fische/](https://www.uni-konstanz.de/en/university/news-and-media/current-announcements/news-in-detail/die-matrix-fuer-fische/)

**Camera trap arrays** — used by Crofoot and Barrett groups. On Jicarón Island (Panama), 85+ motion-triggered cameras tracked the origin and spread of capuchin behavioral traditions over 15 months.
