## Mission & Objectives

CSER is an interdisciplinary research centre within the [Institute for Technology and Humanity](https://www.cser.ac.uk/) at the **University of Cambridge**, dedicated to the study and mitigation of existential and global catastrophic risks. Founded in 2012 by Huw Price, Martin Rees, and Jaan Tallinn, CSER operates with an explicit impact mandate: not just to understand risk, but to mitigate it.

The centre brings together technologists, academics, and policy-makers to:
- Foster a worldwide community focused on catastrophic risk
- Produce original research and policy expertise
- Influence governance at national and international levels
- Develop the field of "existential risk studies" as a rigorous academic discipline

CSER is housed at **16 Mill Lane, Cambridge, CB2 1SB** and is part of the broader Cambridge ecosystem including the [Leverhulme Centre for the Future of Intelligence (LCFI)](https://lcfi.ac.uk/) (a CSER spinoff).

## Research Themes

CSER organises research around six themes:

### 1. [Risks from Artificial Intelligence and Advanced Technologies](https://www.cser.ac.uk/work/research-themes/risks-from-artificial-intelligence/)
Near- and long-term AI safety and governance; cybersecurity; AI and nuclear weapons; AI race rhetoric; cross-cultural AI governance (including China). Key people: Seán Ó hÉigeartaigh, Seth Baum, Shahar Avin.

### 2. [Biology, Biotechnology and Global Catastrophic Risks](https://www.cser.ac.uk/work/research-themes/biology-biotechnology-and-global-catastrophic-risks/)
Pandemic preparedness, biosecurity governance, synthetic biology risks, cyberbiosecurity (intersection of cyber and bio risks), military biotechnology. Key people: Catherine Rhodes.

### 3. [Global Systemic and Environmental Risk](https://www.cser.ac.uk/work/research-themes/global-systemic-and-environmental-risk/)
Climate change tail risks, ecosystem collapse, "polycrisis," volcanic eruption risks, systemic risk modelling. Collaborating with UN ASRA, UNDRR, G20. Key people: Lara Mani, Lalitha Sundaram.

### 4. [Governance and Global Justice](https://www.cser.ac.uk/work/research-themes/governance-and-global-justice/)
How inequality, corruption, anti-Black racism, and structural discrimination drive global risk. Postcolonial and anti-racist critical perspectives. Who participates in x-risk governance? Key people: SJ Beard, Luke Kemp.

### 5. [A Science of Global Risk](https://www.cser.ac.uk/work/research-projects/a-science-of-global-risk/)
Developing rigorous methodologies for studying low-probability/high-impact events; horizon scanning, concept modelling, logic trees, futures studies. Funded by [Templeton World Charity Foundation](https://www.templetonworldcharity.org/). Key people: SJ Beard, Catherine Rhodes, Paul Ingram, Luke Kemp.

### 6. [Managing Extreme Technological Risks](https://www.cser.ac.uk/work/research-projects/managing-extreme-technological-risks/)
Foundational work on identifying and evaluating extreme technological risks; encouraging responsible innovation culture.
