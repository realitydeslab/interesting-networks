# Leverhulme Centre for the Future of Intelligence (LCFI / CFI)

The world's leading interdisciplinary research centre on the ethics and future of AI. Launched in 2016 with a Stephen Hawking speech ("likely to be either the best or worst thing ever to happen to humanity"). Housed at Cambridge, with spokes at Imperial College London and UC Berkeley. Now part of the Institute for Technology and Humanity (ITH) umbrella alongside CSER and CHIA.

## Mission & Objectives
Highly interdisciplinary AI research exploring the nature, ethics, and impact of AI in the near, mid-, and long-term. Research spans:
- Algorithmic transparency
- Nature of intelligence
- Automated warfare
- Consciousness and moral status
- Social AI
- Feminist AI
- AI-amplified injustice
- Global and pluriversal design
- AI and democracy/geopolitics/natural environment

## Research Programmes
1. **Kinds of Intelligence** ([link](https://www.lcfi.ac.uk/research/programme/kinds-of-intelligence)) — Co-dir: Henry Shevlin, Marta Halina. Nature of intelligence across biological, artificial, and hybrid systems.
2. **AI: Narratives and Justice** ([link](https://www.lcfi.ac.uk/research/programme/ai-narratives-and-justice)) — Co-dir: Cave, Ganesh, Drage. Cultural contexts shaping AI; race, gender, cognitive justice.
3. **AI: Trust and Society** — Dir: Adrian Weller. Algorithmic transparency, societal impact.
4. **AI: Futures and Responsibility (AI:FAR)** ([ai-far.org](https://www.ai-far.org/)) — Dir: Seán Ó hÉigeartaigh. Long-term governance and x-risk.
5. **Design, Participation, and Praxis** — AI creation processes.
