# Library Stack

A digital library and small-press publisher operating at the intersection of art, technology, design, architecture, and critical theory. Founded as Library Stack LLC (US), it functions as both a subscription-based institutional library platform and an active co-publisher of niche, high-signal texts. The collection spans eBooks, podcasts, journals, experimental fonts, software, exhibition materials, streaming video, and artist writings — material "not indexed in any other library database or academic collection." Carbon emissions offset via Climeworks.

Think of it as the **Semiotext(e) for the AI-critical art world** — but as infrastructure, not just a press. Selected by a circle of collaborating editors. Institutional subscribers include art schools, museums, and university libraries. The platform integrates with teaching software (Canvas, Moodle, Blackboard) and supports inter-library loan.

## What It Actually Is

Library Stack is **platform + publisher**:
1. **Platform**: Institutional library subscription service. Academic and museum libraries get ebook lending, file access, catalog integration, ILL, and teaching tools. Exhibition platforms get "branch libraries" connecting local publics to global circuits.
2. **Publisher**: Directly co-publishes projects with institutional partners (CSM/UAL, Onassis Foundation, Singapore Art Museum, NN Contemporary Art, etc.)
3. **Aggregator**: Distributes material from independent publishers including End of Medium, Inventory Press, Open Humanities Press, Onassis Foundation, Glass Bead, Serpentine — that otherwise live in silos.

The collection taxonomy (subjects, keywords, formats) reveals the curatorial intelligence: subjects like "Nonhuman Agency," "Haunting Algorithms," "Machine Sensing," "Human Sensing," "Technofeminism," "Dark Ecology" — this is not a general library.

## Why Interesting for Amber

**Library Stack is the cultural infrastructure for the field Amber works in.** Not a venue for showing work — a library for the intellectual community doing critical AI, nonhuman agency, machine behavior work. Several specific connections:

1. **Version Space** = the closest existing model to what Amber could participate in or inspire. Conversations between artists and researchers on AI cognition, indigenous approaches, machine behavior. If Amber's agent ethology work matures, this is the natural home for an essay/interview.

2. **"Synthetic Cognition"** framing in *Chimeras* = cousin concept to agent ethology. Both try to de-anthropocentrize AI from the inside out — not just "AI bias" critique but fundamental ontological reframing.

3. **Proof of Personhood** = *personhood* as a contested category in the AI age — directly speaks to Amber's machine death / machine consciousness axis.

4. **London connections**: CSM/UAL (TEXT-TO-SPEECH), Serpentine R&D — Amber is Oxford-based, this is very accessible.

5. **Publishing opportunity**: Library Stack co-publishes. For Amber's more essay-length critical AI writing (less academic, more art-world legibility), this is a natural venue — especially for work intersecting with agent ethology, machine behavior, or more-than-human AI.

6. **Institutional circulation**: Material from Library Stack circulates through museum and university libraries globally. High cultural capital, low circulation = Amber's preferred intellectual register.

## Caveats

- Library Stack doesn't list its full team/editorial board publicly — curatorial selection feels deliberate but opaque.
- Subscription-based platform; full access requires institutional affiliation.
- The "Singapore / London axis" mentioned in original stub was a simplification — it's more accurately New York-based LLC with London academic partnerships and Singapore as a co-publisher for one volume.
