CRASSH is one of the largest humanities institutes in the world, established in 2001, with a global reputation for interdisciplinary excellence. Based at the Alison Richard Building (7 West Road, Cambridge CB3 9DP), it promotes research innovation across arts, humanities, and social sciences — and critically, forges connections with science subjects. It runs fellowship schemes, research networks, events funding, writing weeks, and a massive programme of public events.

## Mission & Objectives

CRASSH's mission is to promote and support research innovation in the arts, humanities, and social sciences. It:
- Equips researchers to design and lead ambitious interdisciplinary projects
- Champions research that experiments with creative forms of communication
- Explores inclusive forms of collaboration with wide range of practitioners
- Engages with new publics in the UK and across the world
- Provides a space for critical thinking about the ethics and politics of research

Key principles:
- **Interdisciplinarity** — Not just within humanities, but bridging to sciences
- **Creative engagement** — Performance, film, art, making activities alongside academic work
- **Global reach** — International fellowship schemes, worldwide network
- **Diverse formats** — reading groups, workshops, symposia, performances, exhibitions, writing weeks

## Key Contributions

1. **Infrastructure for interdisciplinary humanities** — CRASSH is the Cambridge equivalent of TORCH at Oxford. It provides the organizational, financial, and physical infrastructure for cross-disciplinary humanities research.
2. **Accessible funding for early-career researchers** — The Research Networks and Events funding are designed for postdocs and PhD students, not just established professors. Low barriers, high support.
3. **Creative-academic fusion** — CRASSH explicitly encourages performances, exhibitions, films, and creative initiatives alongside academic research. This is rare institutional support for practice-based research.
4. **Global fellowship network** — The visiting fellowship scheme creates international traffic through Cambridge humanities.
