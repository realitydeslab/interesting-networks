# Antikythera

An R&D institute for Planetary Computation and the Evolution of Intelligence. Founded 2022, based at the [[Berggruen Institute]], directed by philosopher [[Benjamin Bratton]]. Antikythera is best described as a school of thought — building conceptual infrastructure (studios, journal, MIT Press book series, salons, exhibitions) for a new philosophy of computation that refuses to merely apply old concepts to new technology, instead inventing new ones. Takes its name from the Antikythera mechanism (200 BCE), the first known artificial computer — a planetary navigation and prediction device.

## Mission & Objectives

**Core mission:** Rethink the past, present, and future of planetary computation. Develop outputs spanning foresight, speculation, scenarios, and company-ready ideas.

**Key distinction from other AI think tanks:** Antikythera explicitly starts from technology and works *toward* new philosophy, rather than applying inherited philosophical frameworks to technology. As they put it: not "what would Kant make of driverless cars?" but "what new concepts does computation force us to invent?"

**Five Research Areas:**
1. **Planetary Computation** — computation as global infrastructure, geological force, and epistemological medium. How computation doesn't just relay information but generates it.
2. **Synthetic Intelligence** — machine intelligence beyond anthropomorphic models. AI as existential technology (in Stanisław Lem's sense). Large foundational models, natural/artificial language, models of mind, animal cognition, urban automation, machine sensing, global history of machine intelligence beyond Western narratives.
3. **Recursive Simulations** — theory of simulations in science, politics, identity, and cognition. How simulation technologies shape perception, action, and reality through recursion and reflexivity.
4. **Hemispherical Stacks** — multipolarization of planetary computation. Computation geopolitics: chip wars, data sovereignty, competing hemispheres of computational influence (US vs China vs EU). Cloud platforms evolving into quasi-states, states evolving into cloud platforms. [Full project: hemstacks.antikythera.org]
5. **Planetary Sapience** — evolution of intelligence at planetary scale, the "Noocene" — how intelligence comes to grasp its own evolution. Includes astrobiology perspective (biosignatures, technosignatures, substrate-agnostic ecology).

**Upcoming Research:**
- **Agentworld** — society co-populated with billions of AGI-level agents. New forms of synthetic social relations. Psychological dimensions of agent doubles.
- **Convergent Artificialization** — comparative and cross-cultural understanding of "the artificial." Evolutionary theory vs. cultural definitions across traditions.

## Key Contributions

**Conceptual innovations Antikythera has contributed to the discourse:**

1. **Planetary Computation** — computation not as tool or algorithm but as planetary infrastructure that generates knowledge and reorganizes sovereignty. The Stack's six-layer model (Earth, Cloud, City, Address, Interface, User) as analytical framework.

2. **Noocene** — the geological epoch defined by planetary-scale intelligence grasping its own evolution, and the paradox that its success may undermine its future conditions.

3. **Hemispherical Stacks** — computational geopolitics framing: instead of a single global internet, competing hemispheres of computational infrastructure, each with different epistemic and governance cultures.

4. **Synthetic Intelligence** (vs. Artificial Intelligence) — a vocabulary shift: "synthetic" escapes the "artificial" = deceptive/fake connotation and positions AI as a genuine evolution of intelligence, not an imitation of human thought.

5. **Convergent Artificialization** — the artificial is not the invention of one culture; altering ecological niches is a universal adaptive strategy, suggesting the artificial is intrinsic to life.

6. **Allocentrism** — philosophical position: instead of anthropocentrism or ecocentrism, a general account of intelligence that includes but is not centered on humans.

7. **Agentworld** — sociotechnical analysis of a world co-populated with billions of AGI-level agents; how new forms of synthetic social relations emerge.

8. **Studio Culture for Computation** — adapting the architecture studio model (speculative, projective, first-principles thinking) to the software/hardware infrastructures of planetary computation.

## Events & Programmes

### Research Studios
The core programme format. Architecture-school-style design studios applied to computation. "Projective models" — speculative, experimental research with autonomy from direct professional application. Output: papers, propositions, films, published in the journal. 

Each studio focuses on a research theme from the five areas. Examples: **Cognitive Infrastructures**, **Hemispherical Stacks**, **Agentworld**, **Convergent Artificialization**.

**Participation:** Studio Researchers and Affiliate Researchers join annually. Roles appear to be by invitation/nomination, not open call — though the Substack newsletter ([antikythera.substack.com](https://antikythera.substack.com)) is the primary channel for announcements. No public application page found as of 2026-02-26.

### Antikythera Salons
Private/semi-public convenings of "polymaths and versalists." Technologists, researchers, philosophers, designers, policymakers, artists, architects, engineers. Support ideation, prospect development, understanding applications. Location-agnostic.

## Why Interesting

Antikythera sits at the most productive edge of chaos in contemporary theory: the moment when AI has clearly outpaced inherited philosophical frameworks, and new concepts are urgently needed.

**For Amber specifically:**
- It's the only institution that simultaneously takes computation seriously as philosophy AND refuses to accept Western European frameworks as the only lens
- The cosmotechnics connection is explicit: *Machine Decision is Not Final* is a direct intervention into what happens when you take seriously that different cultures have different philosophies of the artificial and of intelligence
- The studio methodology — speculation as design practice — aligns with Amber's interest in practice-based knowing
- The scale of thinking (planetary, geological, civilizational) gives Amber a framework for her own research at scales beyond the interaction or device
- Antikythera is naming the discourse that Amber's own work will eventually need to situate itself within: what does it mean to design for/with/against planetary computation?

**The institutional model is itself interesting** — Antikythera proves that serious speculative philosophy can operate at the intersection of academia (MIT Press, UCSD), industry (Google DeepMind), philanthropy (Berggruen), and design (Venice Biennale) simultaneously. This is a career architecture model worth studying.

**The Yuk Hui connection:** While Yuk Hui himself doesn't appear on Antikythera's affiliate lists, his cosmotechnics framework is a clear intellectual parallel/interlocutor — the question of whether "technology" is culturally universal or plural, whether Chinese philosophy has a different ontology of the artificial. *Machine Decision is Not Final* and Hui's *Recursivity and Contingency* are in conversation, even when not explicitly citing each other. The shared territory: computation, multiple philosophical traditions, what comes after Western metaphysics of technology.
