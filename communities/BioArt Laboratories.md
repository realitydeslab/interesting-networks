# BioArt Laboratories

Eindhoven-based foundation (est. 2011) functioning as a gateway between science, nature, technology, and creativity. Founded by artist-entrepreneur [[Jalila Essaïdi]] on the strength of her iconic "Bulletproof Skin" project, it is Europe's most prominent dedicated **bioart** infrastructure: hybrid wet-lab + atelier, talent incubation, and a 15-year run co-organizing the **BAD Award** (Bio Art & Design Award) with [[MU Hybrid Arthouse]]. Now operating from a reclaimed WWII German military bunker complex in Eindhoven's Strijp district — the "BioArt Village" — surrounded by forest.

## Why Interesting

BioArt Laboratories is **the** institutional node in the European bioart ecosystem for artists working with living matter. Its relevance to Amber's interests:

1. **More-than-human design / multispecies** — The lab's entire mission is dissolving the human/nonhuman boundary through collaboration with living systems (bacteria, fungi, slime mold, plants). This maps directly onto Amber's more-than-human design research.
2. **ALife-adjacent** — Key BAD Award projects have explored bio-algorithmic governance (Michael Sedbon's *CMD*, 2019 — a bio-computational ecosystem where bacterial colonies governed by political AI compete for light resources), and living systems as computational substrates. Jalila Essaïdi's bulletproof skin is essentially ALife + bioengineering.
3. **Symbiocene framing** — BioArt Laboratories uses the term "Symbiocene" extensively (post-Anthropocene epoch defined by symbiosis), and has built "Symbiocene Forest" installations repeatedly at Dutch Design Week. This resonates with Amber's open-endedness and post-Anthropocene interests.
4. **Eindhoven cluster** — Core of the Eindhoven design-science-tech cluster: BioArt Labs + [[MU Hybrid Arthouse]] + [[Design Academy Eindhoven]] + [[Dutch Design Foundation]] + STRP festival. Understanding this geography is useful.
5. **Talent pipeline** — The Talent Pressure Cooker (annual) is where emerging bioart-adjacent designers incubate. Worth tracking for discovering next-generation more-than-human practitioners.

**Caveat:** BioArt Laboratories is more focused on *wet biology* (biomaterials, living matter) than on computational ALife, machine behavior, or cyborg sociology. It's at the soft-matter end of the more-than-human spectrum. The BAD Award is now paused, reducing its active programmatic output.

## History

- **2011:** Founded by [[Jalila Essaïdi]] in Eindhoven. Born from her "Bulletproof Skin" project (2010 BAD Award winner), which bioengineered human skin with spider silk to stop a slow-speed bullet. Essaïdi used her experience and biotech network to open the infrastructure to other talent.
- **2014:** Physical foothold established in Eindhoven, Noord Brabant.
- **2016:** Relocated from 750m² lab in Strijp-T to the "Deutsche Woningen" — a WWII German military bunker complex built for officers, disguised as a traditional Brabant farmstead in Heimatstil. Now the BioArt Village: 1.5 hectare plot of mixed forest, hybrid lab, ateliers.
- **~2010–2024:** Co-organized the BAD Award with MU Hybrid Arthouse annually (15 editions total).
- **2025:** BAD Award paused for redesign.

## Location

**BioArt Village** — Oirschotsedijk 14-10, Eindhoven. In the forested green lobe between Strijp-S creative district and future Brainport Industries Campus. Five reinforced buildings in Heimatstil, 1.5 hectares. 10 min by bike from city centre, 10 min from Eindhoven Airport.

## Programs

### 🧫 Hybrid Laboratory
One-of-a-kind facility that doubles as biochemical research station AND traditional atelier:
- Biochemical analyses, biomaterial manipulation and cultivation
- Techniques: quantitative inorganic analysis, material research, microscopy, microbiological quantification
- **Materialenbank** — living library of bio-based materials including: Gecko Glue, Slime mold, Luminous bacteria (stored at conditions ranging from -80°C to forest environments)

### 🌱 Talent Pressure Cooker (Annual)
Flagship talent development program. International open call for artists/designers/scientists proposing promising projects using biomaterials. Selected talents receive:
- Access to the hybrid laboratory and Materialenbank
- Professional guidance transforming idea/prototype into concept/product
- Showcase at **Dutch Design Week** in Eindhoven
- Profile in BioArt Labs' network
- Past themes: "Chilling in a Box" (2015), "The Essence of Things" (2016), "Metamorphosis" (2017), "Anthropocene" (2018), "Symbiocene" (ongoing)

### 🏆 BAD Award (Bio Art & Design Award) — *Paused 2025*
Co-organized with [[MU Hybrid Arthouse]], [[Dutch Design Foundation]], [[Next Nature]], and [[NWO]] (Netherlands Organisation for Scientific Research):
- €25,000 production grant
- Artist-scientist collaboration pairs Dutch researchers with international artists
- 15 editions, 2010–2024
- Exhibition at MU and BioArt Laboratories during Dutch Design Week
- **2025:** Discontinued for redesign. MU statement: *"The BAD network is cracking and in its current form is proving insufficiently resilient to outside influences."*

### 🔬 Crosslabs / Education
Crossdisciplinary workshops for students (Fontys, Radboud, etc.):
- Crosslab 1: Starting the Symbiocene
- Crosslab 2: Bombs for Biodiversity
- Crosslab 3: Parasites and Paper
- Crosslab 4: Bioplastics
- Special sessions: Designing with Slime Mould, Plant Cloning, Cyanotype

### 🔥 Fire Pit Debates
Public debates on urgent societal topics. Past themes: Nostalgotopia, Living Materials, Trust in Science, Coexistence, Cybernetic Forest, Responsible Innovation.

### 🌳 Symbiocene Forest
Annual installation at Dutch Design Week — a forest environment exploring post-Anthropocene, symbiosis-based futures. Long-running program.

## Key People

Also creator of Mestic® (cow manure → cellulose). Won H&M Global Change Award 2017 for Mestic. CEO of Inspidere B.V. (biotech company). Pioneer of bioart as entrepreneurship.
