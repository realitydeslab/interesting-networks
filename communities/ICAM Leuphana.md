# ICAM — Institute for Culture and Aesthetics of Digital Media, Leuphana

The Institute for Culture and Aesthetics of Digital Media ([ICAM](https://www.leuphana.de/en/institutes/icam.html)) at [Leuphana University Lüneburg](https://www.leuphana.de/en/). One of the most important centres for media studies and philosophy of technology in Germany. Home institute of Erich Hörl (general ecology, disruption theory) and connected to a strong PhD programme and Digital Culture Research Lab.

## Background

Leuphana University Lüneburg is notable for its distinctive interdisciplinary structure and its intellectual culture that bridges continental theory, empirical research, and critical studies. The ICAM specifically researches the conditions, effects, and aesthetics of digital media, exploring how digital media shape the signs and objects they process, and how cultural techniques shape the use of digital media.

The ICAM is associated with the **Digital Culture Research Lab (DCRL)** (empirical digital cultures research) and the **DFG-Graduiertenkolleg "Kulturen der Kritik"** (doctoral training in cultures of critique).

## Why Interesting
The ICAM is the institutional home of some of the most theoretically ambitious German media philosophy work. The proximity of Hörl's general ecology project with empirical digital cultures research (DCRL) is unusual — most German media philosophy institutes are more purely theoretical. The "Kulturen der Kritik" doctoral programme is one of the few German doctoral programmes explicitly organized around critique as a practice — interesting for Amber.
