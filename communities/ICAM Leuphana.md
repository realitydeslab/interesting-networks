# ICAM — Institute for Culture and Aesthetics of Digital Media, Leuphana

The [Institute for Culture and Aesthetics of Digital Media (ICAM)](https://www.leuphana.de/en/institutes/icam.html) at [Leuphana University Lüneburg](https://www.leuphana.de/) is one of the most theoretically sophisticated centres for media philosophy and digital culture studies in Europe. It is the home of Erich Hörl's **general ecology** project, the MECS (Medienkulturen der Computersimulation / Media Cultures of Computer Simulation) DFG research group, and a thriving doctoral ecosystem built around critique, cultural technique, and the aesthetics of technical media. ICAM bridges continental European media theory with empirical digital culture research in a way that few other European institutes do.

## Mission & Objectives

ICAM researches the **conditions, effects, and aesthetics of digital media** — exploring how technical media shape cultural life and how cultural techniques shape the development and use of digital systems. The institute maintains a strong commitment to:

- **Media philosophy and cultural theory** (Kittler-lineage but expanded)
- **Media archaeology** — historical reconstruction of technical conditions
- **General ecology** (Hörl) — posthuman, more-than-human, technologically-conditioned existence
- **Cultural techniques** (*Kulturtechniken*) — practices that precede and condition media
- **Computer simulation** as media culture (formerly MECS programme)
- **Sound studies**, acoustic ecology, and media aesthetics
- **Critical theory** of digital cultures (scoring, data politics, verticality)

The institute is also the home of the **Leuphana Centre for Digital Cultures (CDC)**, which coordinates digital cultures research across the university.

*The intellectual anchor of ICAM. The most internationally connected figure.*
- **Core project:** [General Ecology](https://www.bloomsbury.com/us/general-ecology-9781350065468/) — a post-anthropocentric framework for understanding how technical systems, living organisms, and cultural formations co-constitute shared environments. - **Key concepts:** General ecology, disruption, technological condition, sense, milieu, more-than-human
- **Trajectory:** Earlier work engaged Simondon, Heidegger, Stiegler. Now builds a major synthetic framework for post-anthropocentric life with technology.
- **Relevance to Amber:** Hörl's general ecology is the European theoretical cousin of Amber's more-than-human design / ALife interests. Hörl thinks seriously about what it means for non-human entities to have environments and ecologies — directly feeds into questions of machine death, care ethics, artificial life.

## Key Concepts for Amber

### General Ecology (Hörl)
The central theoretical project of ICAM. Hörl argues that we now inhabit a **general ecology** — a condition of planetary technological saturation where the distinction between natural and artificial ecology collapses. Drawing on Simondon's individuation, Heidegger's technology, Guattari's three ecologies, and Stiegler's tertiary retention, Hörl proposes that *sense* itself has become technological. This is a post-anthropocentric theory of environment: it positions non-human technical systems as co-constituents of shared ecologies of sensing, action, and meaning.

**For Amber:** This directly frames what Amber does — designing in a more-than-human ecology where AI agents, sensors, and humans co-constitute environments. Hörl's framework provides the European theoretical scaffolding for Amber's empirical and design-practice work. "Machine death" and "care ethics" in Amber's work are implicitly operating within a general ecological frame.

### Kulturtechniken (Cultural Techniques)
A methodological tradition centered at ICAM and Humboldt University Berlin. Analyzes practices (reading, writing, counting, thresholding) that precede and condition media systems. Key figures: Bernhard Siegert, Cornelia Vismann, Thomas Macho.

### Media Archaeology
History of technical media that doesn't take technological progress narratives at face value. ICAM is part of the German media archaeology tradition (Friedrich Kittler → Leuphana lineage). Müggenburg's BCL work and Haffke's keyboard work exemplify this.

### Computer Simulation as Culture (MECS Legacy)
Pias/Warnke's MECS group established simulation not just as a scientific tool but as a **cultural form** — with its own aesthetics, epistemology, and politics. Key question: when simulation becomes the way we know reality, what happens to empirical observation?

## Why Interesting

ICAM is the clearest European theoretical institution working at the intersection of **posthuman/more-than-human philosophy**, **media history**, **cybernetics**, and **critical digital culture**. This is precisely Amber's theoretical orbit. The specific strengths:

- Hörl's general ecology = the best European framework for thinking what Amber thinks
