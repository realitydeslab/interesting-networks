An online "parallel academia" — a Licensed Proprietary School (LPS) authorized by the State of Michigan offering graduate-level certificate programs and open-enrollment seminars in critical philosophy, arts, humanities, and sciences. Self-described as a network of researchers and practitioners building knowledge "alongside, within, and between existing disciplines, institutions and technologies." Home to Reza Negarestani, the &&& journal, and a globally distributed community of post-institutional thinkers.

## Mission & Objectives

The New Centre describes itself as an instrument of **"parallel academia"** — not replacing traditional university degrees but **complementing, intensifying, and accelerating** them. The mission is threefold:

- **Accelerate academia** — bring together researchers across disciplines at the graduate level, outside institutional constraints
- **Ecologize knowledge** — make connections that siloed academic institutions cannot, enabling distributed research networks
- **Shape the future** — operate democratically through annual member voting, accountable to the global intellectual community rather than to accreditation bureaucracies

Founded in **2014** by **Mohammad Salemy** as a co-founder. Licensed by the State of Michigan as a Licensed Proprietary School (LPS) since inception. Legally based in **Jersey City, NJ**. Fully **online** — all seminars via Zoom. The institution operates partly through Salemy's studio practice. Since the COVID pandemic, The New Centre has also offered institutional moderating services and platform consulting to universities transitioning online.

What makes it distinct from traditional academia:
- No prerequisites for enrollment — anyone can join any seminar
- No disciplinary gatekeeping — seminars cross philosophy, biology, AI, art, politics freely
- Instructors are well-known scholars **not** attached to degree programs
- Affordable and globally accessible — Global South applicants get 50% automatic discount
- The degree function is replaced by publication — certificate students must publish an essay or complete an institutional project
- Community is as much the product as the curriculum

### Reza Negarestani — *Philosopher & Key Instructor*
Iranian-born philosopher. Author of *Cyclonopedia* (re.press, 2008) — the founding text of "theory-fiction" and a key node in speculative realism/dark thought. Author of *Intelligence and Spirit* (MIT Press/Urbanomic, 2018) — his mature systematic philosophy. Current project: **rationalist universalism** — the evolution of reason from modern knowledge to contemporary philosophies of AI and intelligence. Has lectured at numerous international universities. Deeply connected to Urbanomic publishing. At The New Centre, runs seminars on rationalism, the Enlightenment, philosophy of mind, and AI. Most recently: *Enlightenment Reloaded* (Feb-March 2026) — tracing Descartes, Spinoza, algorithmic thought from al-Khwarizmi to contemporary AI critique.

Not simply an accelerationist — his later work explicitly critiques Land-style accelerationism and moves toward a **rationalist universalism** that sees reason and intelligence as emancipatory tools.

[Profile →](https://thenewcentre.org/people/reza-negarestani/)

### Anna Longo — *Instructor (Critical Philosophy, AI)*
French philosopher. Recent seminar: *Lyotard and Non-Human Language in the Age of LMMs* (March-April 2026) — examining what LLMs reveal about language, using Lyotard's *Le Différend* and *Les Immatériaux* (Centre Pompidou, 1985). Brings French theory into direct contact with contemporary AI.

### Rasmus Haukedal — *Instructor (Biology, Cognitive Science)*
Recent seminar: *The Organisation of Nature: Trends in Theoretical Biology and Cognitive Science* — tracing dialectical principles in evolutionary biology, enactivism, and philosophy of mind. A crossover between Hegel, Lewontin, and contemporary cognitive science.

## Intellectual Connections

### Accelerationism & Dark Theory
The New Centre's roots are tangled with late Warwick CCRU lineage (Nick Land, CCRU) via Negarestani's *Cyclonopedia*. However, Negarestani has explicitly moved **away** from Land-style accelerationism — his later work (*Intelligence and Spirit*) is a systematic rationalism that critiques the irrationalism of accelerationist thought. The New Centre is now better understood as a **post-accelerationist rationalist** space — taking the theoretical seriousness seriously without the reactionary politics.

### Inhumanism & Speculative Philosophy
Negarestani's concept of **inhumanism** — the idea that reason itself demands we revise what it means to be human — is central to the Critical Philosophy programme. This connects to:
- Speculative realism (Meillassoux, Brassier, etc.)
- Laboria Cuboniks / Xenofeminism (overlapping circles — accelerating away from biological essentialism)
- Urbanomic publishing (Robin Mackay's house for speculative philosophy)
- Continental philosophy of mind and AI (Stiegler, Simondon, etc.)

### Iranian Intellectual Diaspora
The New Centre has become increasingly prominent as a platform for **Iranian intellectual diaspora** voices — particularly around the 2022 *Woman, Life, Freedom* uprising and continuing into 2026. &&& journal is currently a major hub for this.
