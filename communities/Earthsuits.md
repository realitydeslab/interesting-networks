# Earthsuits R&D Platform

An independent R&D platform and ongoing research project co-curated by [[Lukáš Likavčan]] and [[Stephanie Sherman]], hosted at UAL Central Saint Martins (CSM) as part of the Digital Innovation Season. Earthsuits investigates the histories and futures of wearables — garments, gear, interfaces, materials — as planetary technologies that support navigation, communication, and adaptation on a changing Earth. The platform draws on comparative planetology, speculative design, environmental humanities, fashion theory, and STS to reimagine clothing and wearable technologies as climate adaptation infrastructure.

## Mission & Objectives

Earthsuits explores clothing as **the first human technology** — predating fire and stone tools — and the paradigmatic interface between body and planet. Key intellectual moves:

1. **Clothing as planetary technology**: Following McLuhan's "extensions of the human body," clothes are understood as exosomatic prostheses that enabled Homo sapiens to migrate across all Earth climate zones.
2. **Comparative planetology lens**: Likavčan's question — *"For what Earth do we design?"* — positions the wearable as a response to Earth's specific atmospheric conditions, just as spacesuits respond to the void of outer space.
3. **Fashion as protocol**: A pivot from fashion as identity expression to fashion as operational system for equitable climate adaptation — "Universal Basic Luxury."
4. **Metabolic/atmospheric perspective**: Wearables as membranes and permeable interfaces between body-metabolism and planetary-metabolism, drawing on Sloterdijk's atmospheric philosophy and Tripaldi's *Parallel Minds*.
5. **Humans as planetary sensors**: Earthsuits envisions wearables turning humans into sensing nodes feeding anonymized climate and health data back to planetary informatics infrastructure.

The platform generates: written genealogies, user and case studies, creative briefs, prototypes of devices and environments, dynamic presentations, capsule collections, fashion shows, and multimedia works. Philosophy and practice will be compiled in a **print and digital open-access publication**.

Earthsuits explicitly invites: industry partners, academic partners, and NGOs for sponsored curriculum projects and special events.

## Key Contributions & Theoretical Framework

Earthsuits' distinctive theoretical contribution is the **triple framing**:
1. **Genealogical**: Clothing as ancestral technology → spacesuit as modern apex → climate earthsuit as necessary next step
2. **Metabolic**: Wearables as permeable membranes in a planetary metabolism system (not just personal items)
3. **Political**: Fashion as protocol for Universal Basic Luxury → equitable redistribution of material resources via refashioned supply chains

This sits at the **intersection** of: fashion theory, STS, environmental humanities, philosophy of technology, speculative design, architecture, and climate adaptation — all coherent with Likavčan's comparative planetology.
