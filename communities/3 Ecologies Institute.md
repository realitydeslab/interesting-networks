# 3 Ecologies Institute (3e)

**Extra-university research-creation environment** rooted in land, philosophy, and collective practice. Founded by [[Erin Manning]] as the direct successor to the SenseLab's decade-long *Immediations* project (2012–2019). Registered as a non-profit in Québec; operates from a land base in Sainte-Anne-du-Lac, Upper Laurentians (~2 hours north of Montréal). Fundamentally anti-institutional in structure: no tuition, no gatekeeping by credentials, no conventional grant dependency. A **processual gift economy**.

The "3 ecologies" are drawn from Félix Guattari's *The Three Ecologies* (1989):
1. **Social ecology** — how we live and organize collectively
2. **Environmental ecology** — how we inhabit and are inhabited by land, more-than-human worlds
3. **Mental/conceptual ecology** — how thought forms, what knowledge we cultivate and for whom

These three are **transversal** — you cannot address one without the others. 3e attempts to be a collective form of life operating at all three registers simultaneously, on actual land.

## Mission & Objectives

3e exists to create a **permanent, non-institutional infrastructure** for experimental research-creation that is:
- **Neurodiverse** — designed from the start to be welcoming to autistic and otherwise non-neurotypical participants. Not accommodating neurodiversity as an afterthought; centering it structurally.
- **Post-capitalist** — operating on a processual gift economy. No tuition, no conventional exchange logic. Resources (time, skill, labour, knowledge) circulate as gifts rather than commodities.
- **Land-based** — rooted in a real piece of land being taken permanently out of the property market ("giving the land back to itself"). The goal: land preserved in perpetuity for experimental collective research.
- **Extra-university** — explicitly not a university lab or department. Draws from university networks but is not dependent on them. A model of intellectual life outside institutional structures.
- **Process-oriented** — the methodology is process philosophy (Whitehead, Bergson, Deleuze-Guattari). Not outcomes-first. The event is itself the research.

**The gift economy model in practice:**
- No tuition fees. Participants contribute financially according to capacity.
- Contributions-in-kind (labour, skills) valued equally to money.
- Maple syrup sales, Patreon subscriptions, and occasional artwork donations provide operating funds.
- The guest house at the land base is rented on short-term markets when not in use for 3e events (generating income), removed from the market during residencies.
- Large donations (e.g., land fund contributions via the "100 Acres" artwork at ~£2,000/piece) support land acquisition.
- Events are largely self-organizing through "pop-up propositions" initiated by participants.

**"Pop-up propositions":**
3e's signature event format. There is no set programme. Participants arrive with a shared reading/concept. Each day, anyone can propose an activity (a walk, a practice, a discussion, a making). The programme emerges collectively. This is not chaos — it's a specific technique for generating emergent collectivity.

**Anarchiving:**
3e archives its events not through conventional documentation (photographs, videos, reports) but through an *anarchive* — a non-representational process of keeping material traces alive in a living system. The 3E anarchive is hosted at [fuit.es](https://fuit.es/) (enter token "murmurations," select 3E, explore "portals").

## Key Contributions

1. **Model for post-capitalist research infrastructure**: Perhaps the most worked-out attempt in the humanities/arts to build a non-market, non-institutional model for sustained collective intellectual life. Unlike STS collectives (still university-dependent) or art collectives (still market-dependent), 3e is trying to be genuinely extra-university AND gift-economic.

2. **Research-creation as methodology**: 3e/SenseLab codified "research-creation" not as "art + research" but as a specific practice where making IS theorizing — the artwork or event is not illustrating a concept, the concept emerges from the doing. Inflexions (2008–2019) was the journal of record for this. The 2008 inaugural issue "How is Research-Creation?" is the canonical founding document of this methodology in Canadian art theory.

3. **Anarchiving**: The concept of the anarchive (non-representational collective memory) is 3e's/SenseLab's original theoretical and practical contribution. Not a documentation archive; a living process that keeps event-traces active without fixing them into representation. Now implemented at fuit.es.

4. **Process Seed Bank / alter-economy speculation**: 8 working papers (2016–2019) developing a post-blockchain qualitative economy. First serious attempt to think what a DAO might look like that uses affective intensities (not just tokens) as its unit of value. Partnership with ECSA.

5. **Land-based praxis**: The 3e land is not a retreat center or an artist residency — it is an attempt to enact a different relationship to land (from land belonging to us → us belonging to the land). Maple syrup production, ceramics with local clay, forest foraging, and hügelkultur permaculture are not recreational add-ons; they are the research.

6. **Neurodiversity as structural principle**: 3e is one of very few intellectual communities that has tried to build neurodiversity into its fundamental operational structure — not as accommodation policy but as generative orientation. The "minor gesture" concept (from Manning's book) is central: attending to movements that escape normative legibility.

7. **The pop-up proposition format**: A concrete, exportable technique for self-organizing collective intellectual events. Anyone can propose an activity; the day's programme assembles from these; no hierarchy of expertise governs what matters.

## Recent Events That Matter

1. **Rémi Roy's death and memorial fund (August/January 2025–2026)** — The loss of 3e's primary local collaborator reveals both the fragility and the real rootedness of the project. Rémi embodied what 3e is attempting: indigenous ecological knowledge, subsistence farming, and genuine land relationship. The fund in his memory is also a continuation of his work.

2. **"Giving the Land Back to Itself" — Perceptual Ferment 2025** — The concept that land should no longer "belong" to 3e but that 3e participants should belong to the land is a significant philosophical-legal move. Combined with the "100 Acres" fundraising artwork and the land expansion, this marks a shift from 3e as "research project with land" to 3e as "land-based institution working toward permanent commons."

3. **Law 97 Quebec Forest Response (June 2025)** — 3e's public engagement with Quebec's deforestation legislation shows that the land-based work is not apolitical. The network is mobilizing around forest defense.

4. **Spring Rolling Residency 2025 — New Format** — The 3-month rolling residency (organized by Mie Natius) produced the most maple syrup ever (265 L), had 30+ participants, and demonstrated a new format for sustained community engagement. The 3-month format is replicable.

5. **Cephalopod Dreams Alter-Economies 2024** — The restarting of discussions on decentralized finance and collective decision-making (with readings of Graeber and Jodi Melamed on racial capitalism) marks 3e entering "a new phase" — possibly toward more formal alter-economic implementation.

## Why Interesting

3e is one of the very few attempts in contemporary humanities/arts to build a **genuinely post-capitalist, post-institutional research infrastructure** — not as a slogan but as an operational practice. Most alternatives either remain tethered to university grants (critical theory labs), depend on market-adjacent art-world philanthropy (residencies), or are too small to sustain intellectual momentum. 3e is:

- **Actually running for 6+ years** with land, events, a publishing imprint, and a global participant network
- **Not dependent on any single institution** — extra-university by design
- **Philosophically rigorous** — grounded in Whitehead-Bergson-Deleuze-Guattari process philosophy, not just vibes
- **Producing theory AND practice** — the events are research, the books emerge from events, the land is the philosophy
- **Engaged with neurodiversity** in a way almost no research community is: structurally, not just rhetorically
- **Scaling globally** without becoming institutional — 800+ participants, local groups in Montréal, Australia, Brazil, Europe, all networked loosely through reading groups and events

For anyone building alternative research infrastructures, experimental knowledge networks, or thinking about what "post-university" intellectual life might actually look like, 3e is the most interesting working example currently active.

The **fuit.es anarchive** (token: murmurations) is also worth experiencing as an artwork/concept — it's a genuinely novel form of collective memory that doesn't reduce events to documentation.

The **Process Seed Bank** (the speculative DAO/alter-economy work) is ahead of almost everything else in thinking about what a qualitative, affective, non-transactional economic infrastructure for creative communities might look like.
