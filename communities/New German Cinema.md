# New German Cinema (Neuer Deutscher Film)

West German film movement, 1962–1982. Launched by the [Oberhausen Manifesto](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oberhausen_Manifesto) (28 February 1962). Key directors: [[Alexander Kluge]], Rainer Werner Fassbinder, Werner Herzog, Wim Wenders, Volker Schlöndorff, Margarethe von Trotta, [[Edgar Reitz]], Helma Sanders-Brahms, Werner Schroeter. Influenced by French New Wave and Italian Neorealism. Collective distribution via Filmverlag der Autoren (1971). Characterized by political relevance, personal authorship, formal experimentation, low budgets. Kluge's institutional work (Oberhausen Manifesto moderation, HfG Ulm film institute, legal advocacy for public film funding) created the institutional conditions the movement needed to exist.
