# European Graduate School (EGS)

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-28:** Deep research completed. Full profile assembled from EGS/PACT website, 2026 schedule, public lecture archive, admissions, strategic plan. Rating upgraded to ⭐⭐⭐.
- **2026-02-27:** Stub created during Graham Harman deep research.

Unusual private graduate school — the world's most concentrated assembly of continental philosophers, critical theorists, filmmakers, and artists, gathered for intensive residential seminars. Two divisions: **PACT** (Philosophy, Art & Critical Thought) and **AHS** (Arts, Health & Society). Not a conventional university — the experience is about direct intellectual encounter with world-leading thinkers, not curriculum delivery. Established in the Swiss Alps in 1994; now holds sessions in Saas-Fee (Switzerland), Valletta (Malta), Bergamo (Italy), and online. Wholly tuition-funded. No endowment, no state subsidies.

## Mission & Objectives
EGS seeks to intervene in a global context where education is increasingly structured by instrumental ends that favor technical expertise and frustrate fundamental questioning. It keeps alive free inquiry, creative practice, and intellectual exchange of the highest level directed to the most pressing issues of our time.

The PACT Division (formerly "Media & Communication") covers: philosophy, art, critical thought, architecture, film, literature, digital design, music, psychoanalysis, and political thought.

The AHS Division focuses on expressive arts — coaching, therapy, education, conflict transformation, peacebuilding — founded under Paolo Knill, who pioneered expressive arts therapy.

**Organizational structure:** Non-profit EGSF (European Graduate School Foundation), co-founded by the State of Wallis, the municipality of Saas-Fee, and EGIS (European Foundation of Interdisciplinary Studies, Zürich). Since 2016 recognized as a voluntary organization in Malta; since 2017, "Friends of The EGS Inc." in New York for fundraising. Low-residency format: MA (12 seminars + thesis) and PhD (12 seminars + 2-3 years dissertation). EU-accredited degrees. Also: certificate programs, post-doctoral study, single-course attendance, auditing ($350/seminar).

**Strategic Plan 2025–2030:** Four pillars — Organizational Effectiveness, Academic Program Development, Student Experience, Institutional Advocacy. [Full plan](http://egs.edu/wp-content/uploads/2026/01/EGS-Strategic-Plan_2025-2030.pdf).

## Key Contributions
1. **The public lecture archive** — freely available via YouTube. Hundreds of hours of Derrida, Baudrillard, Virilio, Butler, Žižek, Malabou, Harman, Negarestani, Moten — this is an extraordinary intellectual resource.
2. **Continental philosophy + media art bridge** — one of few institutions systematically bringing continental philosophy together with filmmakers, digital artists, composers.
3. **Low-residency model** — demonstrates that serious graduate education can happen outside the standard university structure; inspired by Humboldtian German university seminar tradition, delivered in Alpine context.
4. **Saas-Fee as intellectual geography** — the remote Alpine village as a forcing function for intellectual intensity; isolation + concentration.

## Key Ideas Circulating at EGS (2024–2026)
- **Catherine Malabou's AI critique:** Can AI be a Kantian "a priori synthetic structure"? (Dec 2025 lecture). AI and technofascism — how critics unknowingly reproduce Heideggerian frameworks (2026 seminar). Philosophy after AI: "if conceptual synthesis and inference are increasingly delegated to technical systems, what becomes of philosophical critique?"
- **Fred Moten & Stefano Harney:** The undercommons, Black study, logistics, study as fugitive practice
- **Mladen Dolar on Alienation:** Marx/Hegel/Freud/Lacan — can we find emancipation *within* alienation? In dialogue with Todd McGowan's *Embracing Alienation* (2024)
- **Achille Mbembe:** Necropolitics to substantive democracy
- **Christoph Menke:** Form of liberation — "a form that, as Marx said of the political-legal form of the Commune, is not oppressive but under which liberation takes place"
- **Elie During & Emmanuel Alloa:** Perspectivism vs. relativism — from Nietzsche/Deleuze/Latour/Viveiros de Castro to physics. Relationist ontology for a pluriverse.
- **Lucien Castaing-Taylor:** Art/anthropology/aesthetics — Harvard Sensory Ethnography Lab's major works (Sweetgrass, Leviathan, De Humani Corporis Fabrica)
