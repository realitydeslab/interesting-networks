# Interact Fellowship

A fellowship of technologists committed to intentional impact — "summer camp for nerds" who ask not just *what can we build* but *what should we build*. Founded 2012, voluntarily run by former fellows. Annual cohort + occasional residency program. Backed by a16z, Sequoia, Founders Fund, Bain Capital, NEA, Lux Capital, Thrive Capital — but the culture is conspicuously non-VC, non-transactional, and philosophically grounded.

## Mission & Objectives

Interact believes:
1. Technology is not an industry; it's a discipline changing every industry.
2. People don't do great things alone — progress is achieved together.
3. To better the world, we must also better ourselves.

The fellowship creates a **non-instrumental space** for ambitious young technologists — explicitly designed to escape the VC/startup/clout culture. The "Moral Philosophies of Technology Circle" is perhaps the most distinctive signal: reading groups on Heidegger, debating what technology *is* and what it *should do*. This is Silicon Valley's philosophical underground.

The residency program (inaugural 2022, Brooklyn) expanded the age window for mid-career technologists (25+), with dedicated physical space for independent work.

**Key structural insight:** Interact is voluntarily organized by former fellows — it has no professional staff, no institutional agenda. The culture is self-reproducing.

## Key Contributions

- **Pipeline for philosophical technologists** — the fellowship selects people who ask "what should we do?" not just "what can we build?" This has produced: AI governance researchers (Saffron Huang/CIP), embodied computing researchers (Omar/Folk Computer), art × science practitioners (Wendi Yan), and cultural critics (Jasmine Sun).
- **Moral Philosophies of Technology Circle** — structured reading/discussion groups on the philosophy of technology. Saffron Huang calls this "incredibly formative." Topics: Heidegger, what technology *is*, implications for practice.
- **Non-VC networking model** — backs its fellows financially (fully-sponsored retreats) without equity, without agenda. Creates high-trust bonds that function as "couchsurfing network all over the world" per Jasmine Sun.
- **Summer Residency (2022, Brooklyn)** — provided physical space + cohort structure for independent projects. Omar Rizwan used it to develop what became Folk Computer. Saffron Huang used it to start CIP.

## Events & Programmes

- **Annual Fellowship Retreats** — twice a year, fully sponsored. Venues: Lake Tahoe (multiple years), Austin, Miami, Brooklyn residency. Cohort-based structure with collective activities.
- **Interact Residency** (launched 2022) — summer program in New York for mid-career technologists. Physical shared workspace + community. Opened the age window beyond the traditional under-25 cohort.
- **Moral Philosophies of Technology Circle** — internal study groups. Saffron Huang co-ran with Maran, Matthew, Jasmine, Tammy, Anna.
