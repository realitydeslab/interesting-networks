# Whitney Independent Study Program (ISP)

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-27:** Full deep research completed. 2025–26 year is **paused** — Director search underway; program resumes 2026. Gregg Bordowitz appointed director in 2023; he is himself an ISP alum (1985–86). Sharon Hayes and Andrea Fraser currently faculty. 2024–25 cohort listed.
- **2026-02-27:** Stub created via American Artist deep research.

The most prestigious artist-theorist training program in the US art world, hosted at the [Whitney Museum of American Art](https://whitney.org/). Founded 1968. Three tracks: Studio, Critical Studies, and Curatorial. The intellectual engine is critical theory (psychoanalysis, Marxism, feminism, postcolonialism, institutional critique). Alumni disproportionately shape US curatorial and artistic discourse for the next generation. **Known above all for producing artists who are simultaneously rigorous theorists.** The ISP is the node connecting US critical art practice to the European critical theory tradition — and increasingly to Black Studies, queer theory, and abolitionist frameworks. Alumni include [[American Artist]], [[Andrea Fraser]], [[Glenn Ligon]], [[Gregg Bordowitz]], and many leading curators and critics.

## Mission & Objectives

The ISP describes itself as "an experimental study community dedicated to fostering critical thinking, cross-disciplinary scholarship and writing, and multimedia artistic practices." The program "encourages the theoretical and critical study of artistic practices" through seminars, reading groups, workshops, screenings, performances, poetry readings, and studio visits.

**Three tracks:**
- **Studio Program** (15 participants/year): Weitzen Family Fellows. Access to Lichtenstein Studio + 24/7 facility access. May present work (finished or unfinished) at year-end exhibition.
- **Critical Studies Program** (6 participants/year): Helena Rubinstein Fellows. Individual scholarly research + writing projects; present at a public symposium in May.
- **Curatorial Program** (4 participants/year): Helena Rubinstein Fellows. Collaborate to produce an actual exhibition — select artworks, arrange loans, design installation, write catalogue essays.

**Total cohort:** 25 participants per year.

**Non-degree granting. No tuition. No application fees.** One-time relocation stipend for Studio participants (Weitzen Family Fellowship).

**Application:** Via SlideRoom, January–February annually. Requires: CV, statement (1500 words), 2 letters of recommendation. Critical Studies + Curatorial tracks also require writing sample (15pp) and research/exhibition proposal.

**Visa:** Whitney can sponsor J-1 "Trainee" visas for international participants.

**Credit:** Most cooperating schools grant 12–16 credits for ISP participation.

## Key Publications & Output

- **ISP 40th Anniversary Book** (2008): [PDF available](https://whitneymedia.org/assets/generic_file/142/40yrsISPbook.pdf) — history of the program, key texts, participant works.
- **Critical Studies public symposium** (annual, May): Research presentations by fellows — often the first public airing of emerging critical art theory.
- **Curatorial exhibition** (annual): Produced by Curatorial fellows; substantive catalogue essays.
- Connected to *[October](https://www.mitpressjournals.org/loi/octo)* journal — Gregg Bordowitz (ISP alum) published there; the ISP intellectual tradition overlaps closely with the journal's critical theory orientation (Rosalind Krauss, Douglas Crimp circles).

## Key Contributions

1. **Critical artist-theorist formation**: ISP graduates are unusually hybrid — rigorous theorists who make art, and artists who write and teach theory. This produces people who shape field discourse across multiple registers simultaneously.
2. **Institutional critique tradition in the US**: Andrea Fraser, Renée Green, and others who formed the US wing of institutional critique largely passed through or were shaped by ISP.
3. **Black Studies + art integration**: American Artist (2017), Glenn Ligon (1985), and others have used ISP as the site where Black Studies theoretical frameworks became integrated with contemporary art practice.
4. **AIDS activism + cultural theory intersection**: Gregg Bordowitz and the ISP cohort of the 1980s connected queer theory, AIDS activism, and video art in a way that shaped activist art for decades.
5. **Curator formation**: The Curatorial track has produced a significant portion of the generation of socially-engaged, theoretically-grounded curators now running major US institutions.

## Events & Programmes

- **Weekly seminars**: Tuesday and Thursday, 3–5pm. Visiting artists, theorists, critics — the core of ISP intellectual life.
- **Reading groups** and film screenings (throughout year)
- **Studio visits**: Participants visit each other's studios and receive visits from faculty and guests.
- **Critical Studies Public Symposium** (May): Public presentations of fellows' research.
- **Studio Program Year-End Exhibition** (May/June): Participants present work individually or collaboratively.
- **Curatorial Exhibition**: Produced during the year, open to public.
- **Application period**: January–February annually (currently closed; program paused 2025–26).

**2025–26 Status: PAUSED.** The Whitney is conducting a Director search in collaboration with alumni and supporters. Program expected to resume in the 2026–27 academic year.

## Why Interesting

ISP is not primarily interesting because of the Whitney brand — it's interesting because of the **specific production of hybrid artist-theorist subjects** it has generated across 50+ years. The ISP intellectual tradition is distinctly Marxist-feminist-psychoanalytic (the Frankfurt School + Screen Theory + October magazine tradition) applied to contemporary art practice. This produces a very specific kind of practitioner: someone who uses art to theorize social conditions, and who grounds aesthetic choices in critical theory.

For Amber's research taste, ISP matters in two ways:
1. **Genealogically**: Understanding where critical-theory-inflected art comes from in the US — who produces the practitioners that Amber finds most interesting (American Artist is the most direct example).
2. **As a model**: ISP's structure — intensive seminar-based community, no degree, tuition-free, small cohort, theory-plus-practice — is a model for what intensive critical intellectual formation can look like outside the traditional university. This is increasingly relevant as traditional MFA programs become expensive and credential-oriented.

The ISP's current pause creates a rare moment: the community is reorganizing, old alumni are re-engaging, and the program's future theoretical direction is genuinely open.
