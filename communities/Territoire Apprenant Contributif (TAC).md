# Territoire Apprenant Contributif (TAC)

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Created stub during [[IRI (Institut de recherche et d'innovation)]] deep research. Active research program as of 2024.

Long-term contributive research program initiated in May 2016 by [[IRI (Institut de recherche et d'innovation)]] in **Plaine Commune** (Seine-Saint-Denis, Paris suburbs). Reunites residents, professionals, and researchers to cultivate new forms of knowledge within an economy supporting sustainable bifurcation. Direct institutional experiment implementing [[Bernard Stiegler]]'s contributive economy theory in a specific geographic community.

## Mission & Objectives
"Cultivate new forms of knowledge in the context of an economy that supports contribution towards a sustainable bifurcation." Operate at intersection of:
- Community knowledge production
- Contributive (non-consumptive) economics
- Philosophy-in-practice (Stiegler's pharmacological framework applied locally)
- Territorial development

## Research Axes
- **[Parentalité, Soin et Numérique](https://tac93.fr/fr/axes/parentalite/)** — parenting, care, and digital technology; studying effects of screen overexposure in children
- **[Design de la contribution urbaine](https://tac93.fr/fr/activities/eco/)** — contributive urban design; digital urbanity beyond "smart city"
- **[Économie de la contribution](https://tac93.fr/fr/axes/economie/)** — contributive economy; knowledge as condition of contribution
- **Savoirs et technologies** — knowledge and technologies

## Active Projects (as of 2024)
- **En Veille** — parent association against screen overexposure in children
- **Urbanités numériques en jeux** — urban game design with schools across Seine-Saint-Denis
- **Ateliers Clinique Contributive** — contributive research on screen overexposure
- **Comité ECO** — local ecological currency connecting residents with sustainable initiatives
- **Raisonnons nos écrans** — parents and researchers working on "reasoned screen practices"
- **ContribAlim** — contributive creation of recipes for collective cooking
- **FOSDEM 2024 presentation** — engagement with open-source/digital communities

## For Amber
**Why interesting:** One of the few sustained experiments connecting sophisticated philosophy of technology to actual community practice and territorial governance. Unlike most theory-in-practice projects, TAC has operated for 8+ years with genuine community involvement. The combination of contributive economy theory, participatory design, and local governance is directly relevant to Amber's interests in participatory and democratic design.

**How can Amber engage:** Research visit to Plaine Commune; collaboration on design-of-contribution-systems; connection through IRI network.

**What to learn:** TAC is a model for how to operationalize philosophy — not just write about contributive economy but build the institutional structures to test it. Amber could study this as a case study in design-as-theory-in-practice.

## Why Interesting
Rare long-term experiment applying continental philosophy to territorial/community development. Operates at intersection of participatory design, philosophy of technology, and local governance. Shows what it looks like when theory becomes practice at community scale.
