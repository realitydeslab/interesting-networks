# Cave_bureau

Nairobi-based architecture and design studio founded by Kabage Karanja and Stella Mutegi. Works at the intersection of architecture, decolonization, ecology, and African cultural heritage. Uses Kenya's volcanic cave systems as conceptual anchor for an "Anthropocene Museum" — framing caves as geothermal energy sources, as archives of human evolution, and as design inspiration.

Framing: "Decolonization takes precedence over decarbonization." Critical positioning that reorders the usual hierarchy of sustainability discourse.

Exhibited at Louisiana Museum of Modern Art (2023) in *The Architect's Studio* series. Called "the best architecture exhibitions produced in European museums in the last decade" by World-Architects.com.

## Why Interesting
Cave_bureau is one of the clearest examples of more-than-human design from a non-Western, specifically African decolonial perspective. Their "Cow Corridor" project (restoring Masai migratory trails through Nairobi) is precisely the kind of multispecies, more-than-human intervention that Amber's research is interested in. They use 3D scanning and digital tools as instruments of cultural recovery, not techno-solutionism.
