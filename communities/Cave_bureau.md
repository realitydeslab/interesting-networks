# Cave_bureau

Nairobi-based bureau of architects and researchers charting "architecture as a deep time earth practice of social and ecological repair." Founded 2014 by Kabage Karanja and Stella Mutegi — both practicing architects, spelunkers, and academics who literally descend into Kenya's volcanic caves to do their research. Their central project: the **Anthropocene Museum** (2019–ongoing), a roaming, nomadic museum that has toured from a Smithsonian courtyard in New York to a working slaughterhouse in Sharjah to a flagship solo show at Louisiana Museum of Modern Art. In 2025 they curated the British Pavilion at the Venice Architecture Biennale.

Core thesis: caves are the origin of architecture, geothermal energy extraction is a new form of colonial violence, and the Anthropocene cannot be addressed without addressing colonialism first. Signature line: **"Decolonization takes precedence over decarbonization."**

## Mission & Objectives

Cave_bureau describes itself as "charting explorations into architecture as a deep time earth practice of social and ecological repair." Their work:

- Maps, 3D-scans, and translates Kenya's volcanic cave systems into architectural installations and proposals
- Confronts the colonial and neoliberal dimensions of the Anthropocene — arguing that environmental degradation and colonial violence are the same crisis
- Develops what they call **"reverse futurism"**: combining ancestral indigenous knowledge with contemporary architecture to propose alternative futures
- Runs the **Anthropocene Museum** as a roaming, nomadic, non-building museum that can occupy any space — a slaughterhouse, a dome, a Smithsonian courtyard — and turn it into a site of trauma, resistance, and healing
- Actively engages with Maasai communities on real infrastructure projects (Cow Corridors, water harvesting, geothermal resistance)
- Bridges academia and practice: teaching globally, lecturing at Yale, Columbia, and African architecture schools

## Key Contributions

1. **Anthropocene Museum** — a new museum typology: roaming, site-sensitive, non-building, non-institutional. Now 10+ editions spanning 4 continents. The museum "doesn't own a building" — it occupies found spaces and transforms them.
2. **Cave as architectural origin myth** — the thesis that the lava tubes of the Great Rift Valley are the ur-architecture, predating (and equal to) the Pantheon by 1.7 million years. The Baboon Parliament cave on Mt Suswa has the same proportions as Rome's Pantheon, which they demonstrate through drawing and model.
3. **3D scanning as decolonial act** — using photogrammetry and laser scanning to create precise architectural records of caves associated with slavery (Shimoni), Mau Mau resistance (Mbai), and geological heritage (Mt Suswa), before they're lost to development.
4. **Cow Corridors** — a concrete infrastructure proposal to restore Maasai grazing routes through Nairobi — legally banned since British colonial partition. Specific: network of routes along road/rail reserves, MMRS (Maasai Migratory Reservoir Structures) as geological markers.
5. **Reverse futurism** — their coined concept: combining pre-colonial indigenous knowledge with contemporary architecture to generate futures that are "beyond carbon neutral" by returning to them. Contrasts with "green futurism" that ignores who pays.
6. **The BRIT** — imagined institution: Benevolent Reparations Institute, proposed in AM 4.0. A creative mechanism to attract return of stolen wealth through the arts. Not yet real, but a designed proposition.

## Venue Circuit Analysis

Cave_bureau operates across a very specific set of venues:

**Type 1: Elite European art-architecture circuit**
- Louisiana Museum of Modern Art (Denmark's top museum for contemporary art — the *Architect's Studio* series is their most prestigious architecture programme)
- Venice Architecture Biennale (first Kenyan participants ever; now curating British Pavilion)
- Sharjah Architecture Triennial (Gulf's most significant architecture biennale, curated from global south)
- Prada Frames / Salone del Mobile, Milan (high-profile, industry-adjacent)

**Type 2: US academic/museum circuit**
- Cooper Hewitt, Smithsonian Design Museum, NYC (design museum, not just art — strategic)
- Columbia University GSAPP (graduate school, teaching practice)
- Yale School of Architecture (visiting professorships)

**Type 3: Their own invented circuit (the cave itself)**
- Mount Suswa, Great Rift Valley (the ur-site — primary research site)
- Shimoni Caves, Indian Ocean coast (slave trade sites)
- Mbai Cave, Kiambu County (Mau Mau resistance site)
- Old Sharjah Slaughterhouse (non-museum → museum conversion)

**What this reveals:** Cave_bureau deliberately works in non-museum spaces to "expand the bounds of museology." Their circuit alternates between legitimacy-building in prestige European/American institutions and return to the African landscape that grounds their practice. They use the European stage to amplify African voices.

## Why Interesting

Cave_bureau is one of the most important studios working at the intersection of Amber's core interests:

1. **More-than-human design from non-Western perspective** — The Cow Corridors project literally restores multispecies infrastructure (human-cattle-land) erased by colonialism. This is more-than-human design as political act, not aesthetic preference.

2. **Deep time as design framework** — Their use of geological time (cave systems 1.7 million years old, Anthropocene as epoch, Pleistocene eruptions) aligns with Amber's interest in more-than-human temporalities and the long now.

3. **Decolonial tech critique** — Their position on geothermal energy (green energy that still displaces Maasai) is exactly the "AI for X without questioning X" critique from research-taste.md, applied to climate tech.

4. **Museum as research infrastructure** — The Anthropocene Museum is a model for how a research programme can generate its own institutional form. No building needed. This is directly relevant to Amber's thinking about how research can become its own platform.

5. **Africa as source (not destination)** — Their framing: caves are the origin of all architecture; Kenya's Rift Valley is the cradle of humankind. This inverts the usual Western → Global South direction of knowledge transfer. Amber's interest in non-Western tech theory maps directly.

6. **3D scanning as decolonial act** — Digital tools used not for techno-solutionism but for cultural recovery and preservation. Contrasts with usual Silicon Valley "technology solves everything" narrative.
