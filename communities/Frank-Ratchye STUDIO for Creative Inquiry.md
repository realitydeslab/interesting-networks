# Frank-Ratchye STUDIO for Creative Inquiry

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-28:** Full deep research completed. Upgraded to ⭐⭐⭐. Mapped all grants, current leadership, key projects, engagement paths.
- **2026-02-26:** Created stub note. Discovered through [[Golan Levin]] research.

CMU's laboratory for "atypical, trans-disciplinary, and inter-institutional research at the intersections of arts, science, technology and culture." One of the most significant and long-running art-technology labs in the US academic system. Explicitly **anti-disciplinary** — not interdisciplinary (between disciplines) but against disciplinary thinking altogether. Runs a sprawling ecosystem of grants, residencies, speaker series, and open-source projects. The XRTC Creative Research Grant (2023+) specifically funds XR work — directly relevant to Amber/HoloKit.

## Mission & Objectives

> "A laboratory for atypical, trans-disciplinary, and inter-institutional research at the intersections of arts, science, technology and culture."

The STUDIO describes itself as "a venue, a classroom, a laboratory, and a commons." Its physical space (1800 sq ft) operates as a deterritorialized "Third Space" — a drop-in collaborative workspace for CMU students seeking relief from disciplinary compartmentalization. Committed to: open-source skill sharing, risk-taking, creative redistribution of resources, and non-extractive community partnerships in Pittsburgh.

## History

- **1985:** Founded as the **Center for Art and Technology** at CMU.
- **1989:** Reimagined as the **STUDIO for Creative Inquiry** by College of Fine Arts Dean **Lowry Burgess** and School of Art Head **Bryan Rogers**. The founding team also included Wendy Plesniak and Bill Rodgers.
- **1991:** Associate Director **Marge Myers** joins (serves until 2015). She was instrumental in the STUDIO's administrative continuity for 24 years.
- **2009:** **Golan Levin** becomes director/co-director. His era (2009–2023) defines the lab's global identity in computational art, open-source culture, and creative coding.
- **2012:** Renamed **Frank-Ratchye STUDIO for Creative Inquiry** after CMU alumni **Ed Frank** and **Sarah Ratchye** whose support established the Frank-Ratchye Further Fund (FRFF).
- **2023:** Golan Levin steps down, becomes Director Emeritus. **Nica Ross** becomes current Director.

## Key Contributions (Landmark Projects)

- **[Free Universal Construction Kit](https://studioforcreativeinquiry.org/project/free-universal-construction-kit)** (Levin + Shawn Sims, 2012) — ~80 3D-printable adapter bricks enabling interoperability between 10 children's toys (Lego, K'Nex, Lincoln Logs, etc.). In MoMA permanent collection. Canonical open-source / interoperability statement.
- **[Conflict Kitchen](https://studioforcreativeinquiry.org/project/conflict-kitchen)** (Jon Rubin + Dawn Weleski) — Takeout restaurant serving only cuisine from countries the US is in conflict with. Pittsburgh landmark. Iran, Afghanistan, Venezuela, Cuba, North Korea, Palestine editions. Art as civic infrastructure.
- **[Warhol Digital Recovery Project](https://studioforcreativeinquiry.org/project/warhol-data-recovery)** (2014, with Cory Arcangel + Andy Warhol Museum + CMU Computer Club) — Recovered Andy Warhol's unknown digital artworks from 1985 Amiga floppy disks. Major cultural rescue operation.
- **[Terrapattern](https://studioforcreativeinquiry.org/project/terrapattern)** (2016, Levin + team) — Visual search tool for satellite imagery using ML. Search by visual similarity across satellite maps.
- **[NeoLucida](https://www.neolucida.com/)** (2013, Levin + Pablo Garcia) — Affordable, open-source camera lucida for artists. Kickstarter success.
- **[Deep Lab](https://studioforcreativeinquiry.org/project/deep-lab-2)** — Feminist cybersecurity research collective. Convened women coders/artists to examine surveillance, privacy, and security culture.
- **[Moon Ark](https://studioforcreativeinquiry.org/project/moonark)** — Artistic payload delivered to the moon.
- **[Stateful LSTM / ml5.js (Memo Akten Residency)](https://studioforcreativeinquiry.org/project/stateful-lstm-and-charrnns-memo-akten-artist-residency)** (2019) — ML tools and research with Memo Akten.
- **[PEmbroider](https://studioforcreativeinquiry.org/project/pembroider)** (2020, Levin + Lingdong Huang + Tatyana Mustakos) — Embroidery library for Processing.
- **[p5.js Contributors' Conferences](https://studioforcreativeinquiry.org/project/p5jscon)** — Hosted the global creative coding community.
- **[Workhorse Queen](https://studioforcreativeinquiry.org/project/workhorse-queen)** — More recent STUDIO project.

## Events & Programmes

### Grants (STUDIO-administered, open to CMU-affiliated)

1. **[Frank-Ratchye Further Fund (FRFF)](https://studioforcreativeinquiry.org/frff-grants-microgrants)** — The core internal grant. Two tiers:
   - *Microgrants*: rolling applications, up to $500, director decision
   - *Full Grants*: $501–$5,000, juried, two deadlines/year (Fall ~Sep, Spring ~Feb)
   - Any CMU faculty/staff/student eligible. Projects must be "thinking at the edges" of discipline intersections.
   - [Apply for Microgrant](https://cfa.slideroom.com/#/permalink/program/85183/rjaUfDMpjU) | [Apply for Full Grant](https://cfa.slideroom.com/#/permalink/program/85948/vWCzsBo1Wn)

2. **[XRTC Creative Research Grant](https://studioforcreativeinquiry.org/xrtc-funded-projects)** (2023+) — $1,000–$6,000 for XR creative research. Collaboration with Extended Reality Technology Center + Askwith Kenner Global Languages and Cultures Room. Final works may be presented at [Sona Immersive Storytelling Festival](https://www.sonafestival.com/). **Highly relevant to Amber / HoloKit work.**

3. **[CS+X Grant](https://studioforcreativeinquiry.org/csx-projects)** (2024+) — $1k–$7k for CS + arts/humanities interdisciplinary projects. Collaboration with CMU School of Computer Science.

4. **[Steiner Visitor Invitation Grant](https://studioforcreativeinquiry.org/steiner-visitor-invitation-grants)** — Faculty/staff/students can nominate and invite a guest lecturer to visit their classroom. Limited support for in-person visits. **Amber could be invited this way.**

5. **[FUCKERY Award](https://studioforcreativeinquiry.org/fuckery-award-projects)** (Moore Family Fund for Underwriting Creativity, Knowledge, Experimentation, Research and Yumminess) — Anti-disciplinary undergraduate research award. Awarded at CMU's Meeting of the Minds undergraduate conference.

6. **[Maddy Varner Mastication Grant](https://studioforcreativeinquiry.org/maddy-varner-mastication-grant-for-time-and-or-teeth-based-work)** — $500 for "work that chews the cud" (time-based/teeth-based works). First recipient spent 700+ hours training squirrels to act in domestic scenes.

### Sylvia and David Steiner Speaker Series
The flagship public programming series. Brings creative practitioners who "push boundaries, defy definition, and demonstrate excellence in all aspects of the arts." Any CMU community member can nominate a guest. Strongly encourages women, BIPOC, LGBTQIA+ practitioners. Results in annual Steiner Book PDF (free download). Past speakers across fields: visual art, music, architecture, code, performance.

## Why Interesting

The STUDIO is one of only a handful of US academic institutions that has sustained genuinely anti-disciplinary research for 35+ years inside a major research university. Most "interdisciplinary" labs are interdisciplinary in name only — the STUDIO has the receipts: p5.js (worldwide creative coding), Conflict Kitchen (art as civic life), Free Universal Construction Kit (MoMA), Warhol floppy disk recovery (cultural preservation via code). The Levin era proved that a small lab inside a university can produce globally influential open-source culture, not just papers.

For Amber specifically: this is the model for what Reality Design Lab could become. The XRTC grant is an immediate concrete engagement path. The Steiner visiting artist program is the high-prestige entry. And Golan Levin — already in the graph — is the human bridge.
