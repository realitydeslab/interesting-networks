# Frank-Ratchye STUDIO for Creative Inquiry

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Created stub note. Discovered through **[[Golan Levin]]** research — he directed it 2009–2023.

CMU's laboratory for "atypical, trans-disciplinary, and inter-institutional research at the intersections of arts, science, technology and culture." Founded 1990 by Lowry Burgess. One of the most significant art-technology labs in the US academic system. Has supported hundreds of projects in computational art, creative engineering, and critical making.

## Mission
"A laboratory for atypical, trans-disciplinary, and inter-institutional research at the intersections of arts, science, technology and culture." Explicitly anti-disciplinary — it exists to fund and host work that doesn't fit existing academic categories.

## Key People
- **[[Golan Levin]]** — Director/Co-Director 2009–2023. His presence shaped the lab's identity in computational art.
- **Lowry Burgess** — Founding director (1990)
- **Marge Myers** — Associate Director 1991–2015

## Key Projects (via Levin era)
- Free Universal Construction Kit (Levin + Sims, 2012) — MoMA collection
- Terrapattern (2016) — satellite imagery ML tool
- NeoLucida (2013) — affordable camera lucida
- Many visiting artist projects

## For Amber
- **Visiting artist/research opportunities** — the STUDIO takes visiting artists for projects
- **Model for institution-building** — if Amber ever wants to build a lab, this is a case study in how to run a 30-year interdisciplinary research unit inside a university
- **The Steiner Book/Speaker Series** — annual publication and speaker series; engaging channel

## Why Interesting
One of the few academic labs explicitly positioned as "anti-disciplinary" — not interdisciplinary (between disciplines) but against disciplinary thinking altogether. This gives it flexibility to support genuinely weird projects. The Levin directorship (2009–2023) made it a hub for computational art, open-source culture, and critical making.
