# AI4LAM

**AI for Libraries, Archives, and Museums** — an international, participatory community of practice advancing AI in the GLAM (Galleries, Libraries, Archives, Museums) sector. Founded 2018, organized around the annual **Fantastic Futures** conference and monthly community calls. The key nexus where AI research meets cultural heritage institutions, copyright, provenance, and stewardship ethics.

## Mission & Objectives

> "ai4lam is a collaborative framework for libraries, archives and museums to organize, share and elevate their knowledge about and use of artificial intelligence. Individually we are slow and isolated; collectively we can go faster and farther."

Core focus areas:
- Ethical AI implementation in cultural heritage institutions
- AI-assisted collection digitisation, cataloguing, metadata
- Multimodal access to archive materials (OCR, speech-to-text, NER)
- Shared infrastructure, open tools, benchmark datasets
- Governance frameworks for AI in public institutions
- Sustainability and long-term resource management
- Human-centred design and staff/community engagement

## History

- **2018:** [National Library of Norway](http://www.nb.no) and [Stanford University Libraries](http://library.stanford.edu) sign a [Memorandum of Understanding](https://drive.google.com/file/d/1yiYm_6aThG4vqX4SMb_GoY8_s1bvNs66/view) to create open AI collaboration — inspired by the [IIIF (International Image Interoperability Framework)](https://iiif.io) model.
- **2018:** First **Fantastic Futures** conference, Oslo (National Library of Norway).
- **2019:** Second Fantastic Futures, Stanford. AI4LAM network formally constituted. Secretariat of 5 orgs established.
- **2020:** Monthly community calls launched (April), open to all.
- **2021:** LIBER-co-hosted workshop series "Applying and deploying AI in GLAMS" (5 workshops).
- **2025:** British Library hosts FF2025 "AI Everywhere, All at Once" — 150 proposals, 80 accepted.
- **2025-01:** Dr. Ines Vodopivec joins as Secretary General.
- **2026:** FF2026 "Trust in the Loop" — DC, Sep 15-17, co-hosted by Library of Congress, National Gallery of Art, Smithsonian.

## Key Contributions

- Created the international coordination layer for AI in GLAM that didn't exist before (analogous to IIIF for image interoperability).
- Hosts the only global practitioner-researcher conference bridging AI research and cultural heritage institutions (Fantastic Futures).
- Produced open benchmark datasets and shared infrastructure proposals for LAM AI workflows.
- Advocacy for ethical, sustainable, human-centred AI in public memory institutions.
- Made NER, OCR, speech-to-text, and multimodal search standard practice in archives.

## Why Interesting

AI4LAM sits at an underexplored intersection: **institutional conservatism + AI disruption**. Libraries, archives, museums are among society's most trusted institutions — guardians of cultural memory, epistemic authority, and provenance. When AI enters these spaces, it challenges the very foundations of archival trust: authenticity, accountability, chain of custody.

This is not "AI for X" without questioning X. AI4LAM's 2026 "Trust in the Loop" theme explicitly frames AI as a challenge to institutional trust — exactly the kind of critical, non-naive AI framing that connects to Amber's interests in machine behavior, trustworthy AI, and protocol studies.

The IIIF-inspired coordination model (open standards, shared infrastructure, institutional MoUs) is also a compelling example of how a distributed community of practice can move conservatively-minded institutions — relevant to anyone thinking about AI governance and protocol design at institutional scale.

Amber's connection via [[Daniel Chávez Heras]] gives her a warm intro; Giles Bergel at Oxford gives her a local anchor. This is a community worth engaging with in 2026.
