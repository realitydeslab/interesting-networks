# Instituto de Investigaciones Estéticas (IIE-UNAM)

Research institute at UNAM (Universidad Nacional Autónoma de México) dedicated to art history, aesthetics, and visual culture. Founded 1935. One of the oldest and most prestigious art history research institutes in the Americas, with historical emphasis on pre-Columbian and colonial Mexican art. In recent decades has expanded to include contemporary art — [[Cuauhtémoc Medina]] is the primary researcher in this area.

## Research Areas
- Pre-Columbian art and architecture
- Colonial Mexican art
- 19th-century Mexican art
- Contemporary art (newer focus)
- Art conservation and diagnostics

## Key People
- **[[Cuauhtémoc Medina]]** ⭐⭐⭐ — Senior researcher (Investigador Titular B) since 1993. Research areas: contemporary art in Latin America, politically informed art, curatorial studies.
- **[[Olivier Debroise]]** ⭐⭐ — (deceased 2008) Former researcher; co-edited *La era de la discrepancia* with Medina.

## Publications
- *Anales del Instituto de Investigaciones Estéticas* — long-running journal (since 1937)
- Various monographs on Mexican and Latin American art

## Why Interesting
The institutional home of Mexico's most rigorous contemporary art historical scholarship. Its dual role as academic research institute and curator-producing institution (Medina runs both IIE and MUAC) is a distinctive model.
