# CRFM Stanford

## Center for Research on Foundation Models

An interdisciplinary initiative at Stanford Institute for Human-Centered AI (HAI) focused on making fundamental advances in the study, development, and deployment of foundation models. Founded August 2021, co-launched with the landmark "On the Opportunities and Risks of Foundation Models" report — essentially the conceptual founding document for the entire foundation model research field.

## What It Does

Four pillars:
1. **Technical foundations** — data, systems, architectures, training, adaptation, evaluation, interpretability
2. **Beneficial applications** — domain-specific foundation models for law, music, robotics, biomedicine
3. **Societal impact** — transparency, supply chains, openness, copyright, privacy, systemic risk
4. **AI policy** — direct government engagement across multiple jurisdictions

Spans 10+ departments at Stanford. Not just CS — law, medicine, education, social science, economics.

## Key Projects

- **[HELM](https://crfm.stanford.edu/helm/)** — Holistic Evaluation of Language Models: live leaderboard, 30+ models, 42 scenarios, 7 metric dimensions
- **[FMTI](https://crfm.stanford.edu/fmti/)** — Foundation Model Transparency Index: 100-indicator transparency framework, scores OpenAI/Google/Meta/Anthropic etc.
- **[Ecosystem Graphs](https://crfm.stanford.edu/ecosystem-graphs/)** — maps AI supply chain: what data/compute/models flow into what applications
- **Foundation Models paper** (arXiv:2108.07258) — the defining conceptual framework
- **California Report on Frontier AI Policy** (2025) — direct policy input

## Why Interesting
CRFM is the academic counterweight to closed AI development. They coined the term, built the benchmarks, and are building policy frameworks — trying to create the institutional infrastructure for foundation model governance before corporate power consolidates fully. The open science work (Marin) is particularly notable as a political commitment, not just a technical one.
