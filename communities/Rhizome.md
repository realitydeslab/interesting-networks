# Rhizome

The premier nonprofit organization dedicated to born-digital art and net art. Affiliated with the New Museum, New York. Founded 1996 by Mark Tribe as an email list for digital culture; became a key institution for commissioning, exhibiting, and preserving art engaged with technology. Now operates as the institutional home for digital art preservation, criticism, and commissioning — with platforms like **ArtBase** (archive of net art) and **Webrecorder** (tools for web archiving). **[[Lauren Cornell]]** was Executive Director 2005–2012 before moving to New Museum and then CCS Bard.

## Mission & Objectives
"Rhizome supports born-digital art and culture through commissions, preservation, criticism, and community engagement." Unique mission: treats digital art as culturally significant and works to preserve it (technically difficult given the ephemeral nature of software, browsers, and web technologies).

## Key People
- **[[Lauren Cornell]]** — Executive Director 2005–2012 (now at CCS Bard)
- **Mark Tribe** — Founder (1996)
- **Michael Connor** — Artistic Director (current)

## Key Contributions
- **ArtBase** — archive of 2,000+ net artworks: <https://rhizome.org/art/artbase/>
- **Seven on Seven** — annual conference pairing artists with technologists (one day to make something new together); co-founded by Lauren Cornell; held at New Museum
- **Webrecorder** — open-source tools for capturing and replaying web-based artworks: <https://webrecorder.net/>
- Critical writing platform on digital culture and art
- Commissions program for new digital art

## Events & Programmes
- **Seven on Seven** (annual, New Museum, NYC) — artists + technologists paired for 24-hour collaborations; produces unexpected creative results. <https://rhizome.org/sevenonseven/>
- Public programming, talks, and online events
- **Microgrants** and commissions for digital artists

## Why Interesting
Rhizome is the institutional infrastructure of internet/digital art. It's where the history of net art lives (ArtBase), where new digital art gets commissioned, and where the best critical writing on art + technology gets published. Anyone seriously working at the art+technology intersection should know Rhizome deeply. Seven on Seven is especially interesting as a model for productive artist-technologist collaboration that doesn't just result in "tech demos."
