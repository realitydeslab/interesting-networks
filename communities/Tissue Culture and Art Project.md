# Tissue Culture & Art Project (TC&A)

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Stub created during SymbioticA deep research. See [[Oron Catts]] and [[Ionat Zurr]] notes for full detail on individual works.

Pioneering biological art collaboration established 1996 by [[Oron Catts]] and [[Ionat Zurr]], based at [[SymbioticA]], University of Western Australia. First to systematically use tissue engineering as an artistic medium. Created the "Semi-Living" as a philosophical category: entities grown from living cells but existing in a grey zone between life and artifact, requiring human technological intervention to survive.

**The TC&A Project IS the research and artistic output** of Catts and Zurr as a collaborative pair; SymbioticA is the institutional home that houses and enables it.

## Key Works (chronological)
- *Stage 1 & 2* (1996) — earliest tissue-over-scaffold experiments
- *Semi-Living Steak* (2000) — lab-grown frog steak; first cultured food artwork
- *Pigs Wings* (2000–01, with [[Guy Ben-Ary]]) — pig bone tissue wings; at Harvard; **MoMA permanent collection**
- *Semi-Living Worry Dolls* (2001, with [[Guy Ben-Ary]]) — first tissue sculptures in gallery context
- *Disembodied Cuisine* (2003) — frog steak consumed in gallery; first cultured meat performance globally
- *Extra Ear ¼ Scale* (2003, with [[Guy Ben-Ary]] & [[Stelarc]]) — human cell ear; **MoMA**
- *Victimless Leather* (2004) — mouse-cell jacket; **exhibited MoMA 2008** (museum killed cells)
- *NoArk* (2007) — taxonomy and extinction
- *Neolifism / Neolife* (2010) — curation/critique of "life" as raw material
- *Biomess* (2018) — Art Gallery of WA
- *Crossing Kingdoms* (2018) — mammalian/yeast synthetic cell collaboration
- *Compostcubator series* (2016–2019) — compost as cell incubator
- *Sunlight, Soil & Shit (De)Cycle* (2022) — contestable food systems

## Institutional Collections
Works held by: [MoMA NY](https://www.moma.org/), Mori Art Museum (Tokyo), NGV (Melbourne), GoMA (Brisbane), National Art Museum of China (Beijing).

## Why Interesting
**The founding practice of biological arts.** Catts & Zurr invented the field, named the key concepts ("Semi-Living"), executed the decisive world firsts (cultured meat, cultured clothing), and built the institutional infrastructure ([[SymbioticA]]) that made it all possible. Everything in contemporary bio-art descends from or is in dialogue with TC&A.
