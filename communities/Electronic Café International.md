# Electronic Café International

Founded by [[Kit Galloway & Sherrie Rabinowitz]] in Santa Monica, California. One of the first networked art spaces — a café, networking centre, performance and workshop space, and art hub combining telematic technologies (video, fax, slow-scan TV, computer networking) with community space. Initially developed for the 1984 LA Olympics (connecting five culturally diverse LA restaurants); became a permanent institution.

## Why Interesting
Pioneered the idea of a physical community space organized around networked telematic connectivity — essentially a pre-internet internet café with an art program. The community-network hybrid model they developed is a precursor to hybrid physical-digital spaces now common in media art. Historical reference for thinking about how networked spaces constitute community.
