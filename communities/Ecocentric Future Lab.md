# Ecocentric Future Lab

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-28:** Deep research completed. Full profile covering all projects, key people, venue circuit, publications, and Amber's collaboration record.
- **2026-02-26:** Initial stub created.

A university research-art lab at UT Austin directed by [[Jiabao Li]], dedicated to radical shifts in how humans perceive, relate to, and design with non-human intelligences. "Moving beyond human-centric and profit-driven approaches, our lab explores a multi-species, eco-centric worldview that encourages collaboration and empathy with non-human intelligences." The lab works with actual animals and biological processes — spiders, cats, dogs, grackles, squid, bats, mice, crickets, plankton, menstrual stem cells — not as metaphors but as collaborators.

## Mission & Objectives

The lab articulates the Anthropocene challenge clearly: "Human-centric thinking is causing us to damage our planet and other forms of life in ways that can't be repaired… our challenge for the next decade will be to reimagine how we live through the lens of the environment; to shift our perspectives from human-centered, EGO-centric, profit-driven post-industrialist thinking to a multi-species, ECO-centric worldview."

This is not speculative abstraction — the lab operationalizes this through:
- **Animal-computer interaction frameworks** — students work directly with spiders, cats, dogs, grackles
- **Biomaterial innovation** — e-waste repurposing, bioplastics, novel materials
- **Animal enrichment design** — real interventions for captive animals in Costa Rica
- **Voxel-based multi-material 3D printing** — inspired by natural heterogeneity
- **AI for animal behavior/communication** — bat vocalizations, squid camouflage, plankton mobility
- **Eco-phenomenological mixed reality** — using AR/VR to embody non-human perceptual worlds

The foundational philosophical move: "Numerous types of intelligence exist beyond human intelligence — distributed intelligence of octopuses, swarm intelligence of ants, bees, and birds, collective intelligence of mycelium, microbial intelligence. Co-creating with non-human species helps us probe different intelligences, shift perspectives, and interrogate ourselves."

### Key Projects

**[Nocturnal Fugue + EchoVision](https://www.jiabaoli.org/nocturnal-fugue)** (2024–2025)  
Jiabao Li, Matt McCorkle, Botao Amber Hu. Inspired by Thomas Nagel's "What Is it Like to Be a Bat?" — two-part work: *Nocturnal Fugue* is an immersive soundscape using AI-discovered bat social calls (isolation, sleeping, kissing, mating, feeding, grooming, fighting) transformed into spatial sound, projection mapping, haptic transducers. *EchoVision* is a mixed reality installation using a custom bat-shaped HoloKit headset to simulate bat echolocation. User vocalizes, interprets echoes with MR visualization. Co-produced with Onassis Foundation. Premiered at The Contemporary Austin under Congress Avenue Bridge (the largest urban bat colony in the world — 1.5M bats), with bat expert Merlin Tuttle. Appeared on Xinhua News, Forbes.

**[CryoScapes](https://www.jiabaoli.org/cryoscapes)** (2025–2026)  
Jiabao Li, Ziyuan Jiang, Kuan-Ju Wu, [[Yasuaki Kakehi]]. 3D ice printer capturing ice formation; melting as core aesthetic. Haiku-generating AI interprets ice formations in real time. Born during Arctic Circle Residency in Svalbard. Site-specific — each exhibition location's humidity, temperature, air quality co-creates the work. "A self-destroying machine." Collaboration with xlab, U. Tokyo.

**[Squid Map](https://www.jiabaoli.org/squid-map)** (2022–2025)  
Lab residency at Kewalo Marine Biology Lab, U. Hawaii. Hawaiian bobtail squid placed in tank with sand shaped into country outlines; squid rearranges sand, ignoring human-made borders. Gyotaku prints with squid ink (funeral for squids who died in lab). Personal meditation on immigration (Jiabao as Chinese immigrant). [NEW INC], Serpentine Gallery.

**[Chthulucene](https://www.jiabaoli.org/chthulucene)** (2022–2024)  
Imagines post-human world where octopuses inherit Earth after sea levels rise and humans face extinction. Contact improvisation + AI style transfer between octopuses and dancers. References Donna Haraway's Chthulucene concept. [NEW INC], Serpentine Gallery. Special thanks: Donna Haraway, Fiona Raby, Margaret McFall-Ngai, Paola Antonelli.

**[Squeeker: The Mouse Coach](https://www.jiabaoli.org/mouse-coach)** (2023–2024)  
Mouse-controlled running app (Apple App Store + Google Play). Smart running wheel triggers notifications when mouse runs; human matches mouse's daily half-marathon, mouse gets treat. [IDFA DocLab](https://www.idfa.nl/en/film/5e56524e-6675-4203-b51f-914ad9ee5df6/squeeker:-the-mouse-coach/) premiere, Amsterdam. Questions ethics of mice in scientific labs.

**[ANIMO: WildLife Priority Mode](https://www.jiabaoli.org/animo)** (2023–2025)  
2 billion wild animals killed by vehicles annually (10× the human road death toll). Installation: carpets made from roadkill animal photos; autonomous Roomba navigates between them. Proposes AI wildlife detection for self-driving cars. DESIGNART TOKYO 2024.

**[Wings Against the Veil of Night](https://www.jiabaoli.org/wings-against-the-veil-of-light)** (2024)  
Butoh performance about cricket chirping, mating, light pollution. Custom wings from zippers + rulers allow dancers to mimic cricket chirping sounds. Uses Hijikata's bug score. Light pollution causing male crickets to chirp at wrong times, disconnecting from females.

**[Sentient Clit: The Pussification of BioTech](https://www.jiabaoli.org/sentient-clit)** (2024)  
With WhiteFeather Hunter. 3D bioprinted clitorises from menstrual stem cells. Challenges male-dominated biotech. Used OnlyFans as distribution strategy. Exploratorium "Sexplorations."

**[The Long Fall](https://www.jiabaoli.org/long-fall)** (2025)  
Immersive audiovisual journey with plankton + Earth's carbon cycle. Uses Stanford Prakash Lab's PlanktonScope and Gravity Machine datasets. Live bioluminescent dinoflagellate tank as living instrument.

**[The Elephant in the Room](https://www.jiabaoli.org/elephant-in-the-room)** (2024)  
Legal action against Wild Elephant Valley (Yunnan) for elephant abuse. Interactive 3D model of scarred elephant, AR placement, viral Elephant Song video. Art as collective political action.

## Why Interesting

The Ecocentric Future Lab is one of the few places in the world that successfully institutionalizes the full art × science × more-than-human stack within an academic context. Most "speculative design" labs theorize about animals; this lab works with actual bats, squids, mice, crickets, and menstrual stem cells. Most "bioart" labs exhibit at niche venues; this lab publishes at CHI and exhibits at Ars Electronica. 

For Amber specifically: this lab is already his deepest collaborative relationship. Jiabao Li and Amber are co-first authors on 6+ papers — EchoVision runs on HoloKit hardware. This isn't a future opportunity; it's an ongoing partnership producing peer-reviewed research and internationally-exhibited art simultaneously.

The lab's venue circuit analysis reveals it's successfully occupying an unusual triple space: **academic HCI** (CHI, SIGGRAPH, IEEE VR), **major art institutions** (MoMA, Venice Biennale, Ars Electronica, Onassis), and **documentary/interactive media** (IDFA DocLab, Sheffield DocFest, SXSW XR). Very few labs can claim all three.

The connection to Amber's work is specific and deep: eco-phenomenological mixed reality (their term) + HoloKit hardware + CHI/IEEE VR publications = Amber's research contribution is structurally embedded in this lab's identity.
