# Heated Conversations Forum

Online research forum convened by [[Sarah Nuttall]] and [[Isabel Hofmeyr]] at [[WISER]] in 2023. First in a trilogy of 6-month forums generating new work from the Global South on elemental themes. Now reaching publication as book/special issue.

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-28:** Stub created via Sarah Nuttall deep research.

## Mission & Objectives
Considered both: (1) a literally heating planet — climate, heat, thermal politics; and (2) overheated pedagogies — the crisis conditions of teaching and knowledge production in the Global South. Open-subscription online format, widely subscribed.

## Key People
- [[Sarah Nuttall]] — co-convener
- [[Isabel Hofmeyr]] — co-convener

## Topics
- Thermal politics and climate crisis
- Overheated pedagogies in the Global South
- Heat as material and political category
- Elemental humanities approach to temperature

## Why Interesting
The "overheated pedagogies" concept — which treats the crisis of Global South universities as itself a climatic condition — is a model for thinking about overheated computing infrastructure: servers, data centers, AI training as thermal events.
