# Foundational Questions Institute (FQXi)

A private grant-making institute and think-tank that funds high-risk, high-reward foundational science — questions at the edge of physics and cosmology that conventional institutions won't touch. Founded 2006 by **Max Tegmark** (MIT) and **Anthony Aguirre** (UC Santa Cruz). Over $29 million awarded since founding. Think: the place where rigorous physicists seriously investigate consciousness, time, agency, information-as-fuel, and life in the universe.

**Mission:** "FQxI catalyzes research and outreach primarily on questions at the foundations of physics and cosmology. FQxI supports high-risk, high-reward science—in particular new frontiers and innovative ideas integral to a deep understanding of physical reality that lie beyond the remit of conventional institutional support and funding."

## Mission & Objectives

FQXi's six goals:
1. **Expand scientific purview** — fund questions outside conventional grant sources
2. **Redress incrementalism** — establish "islands of understanding" through flexible high-risk funding
3. **Create meaningful convenings** — bring together foundational researchers across disciplines
4. **Make science equitable** — address marginalization in foundations of science
5. **Improve scientific practices** — research, reimagine, and prototype new scientific institutions
6. **Increase public understanding** — disseminate discoveries and worldview implications

Operates via the **FQxI Fund**, a donor-advised fund at the **Silicon Valley Community Foundation**, or directly. Funds theorists, experimenters, and outreach specialists worldwide.

## Key Contributions

1. **Institutionalizing "weird physics"** — gave legitimacy and funding to questions (consciousness, time's arrow, quantum foundations, information biology) that mainstream physics ignored
2. **Information-theoretic approaches to life** — funded Walker, Crutchfield, and others pushing information as foundational to biology/consciousness
3. **Agency and intelligence as physics** — before "AI" was fashionable, FQXi was funding serious physics of agency and intelligence (2018–2019 RFPs)
4. **Essay contests as intellectual commons** — created a genre of rigorous-but-accessible philosophical-physics essays (It From Bit, Digital vs Analog) that became citation-worthy artifacts
5. **Bridging philosophy and physics** — funded philosophy of physics (Craig Callender, Dean Rickles) and outreach (NOVA) alongside hard science

## Why Interesting

FQXi is the **institutional backbone** for the exact questions Amber is asking — but coming from a physics/cosmology angle rather than ALife/HCI. The cross-pollination potential is immense:

1. **Same questions, different languages** — FQXi physicists and Amber's ALife community are asking identical questions (What is life? What is intelligence? What is information? What is an observer?) but using different vocabularies. Amber is positioned to bridge these.
