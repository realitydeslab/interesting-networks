# Bartlett School of Architecture (UCL)

World's top-ranked architecture school (#1 QS 2025, #1 Guardian 2026), part of UCL's Bartlett Faculty of the Built Environment. ~1,700 students and 400+ staff across architecture, design, and computation. Known for a *unit system* — semi-autonomous design studios each running their own research agenda, which gives the school unusual intellectual diversity. Under current director Prof. Amy Kulper (since Sep 2022), the school has explicitly committed to speculative, computational, and bio-integrated design alongside a post-scandal push towards radical pedagogy and spatial justice.

## Why Interesting
For Amber's work: the Bartlett is a site where **architecture increasingly overlaps with agent-based design, living systems, game engines, and computational worldbuilding** — all adjacent to his research interests. UCL proximity matters: Karl Friston's [Wellcome Centre for Human Neuroimaging](https://www.fil.ion.ucl.ac.uk/) is at UCL, and the school is actively expanding into AI-related research. Three specific programs are directly relevant:
- **Bio-Integrated Design** — architecture + living systems + synthetic biology. Direct overlap with more-than-human design.
- **Architectural Computation MSc** — algorithms, AI, generative design.
- **Cinematic and Videogame Architecture MArch** — uses game engines (Unreal, Unity) to design and inhabit speculative architectures.

The school's **CRUNCH lecture series** is also a publicly accessible venue for encountering practitioners at the architecture–society–technology frontier in London.

## Institutional Context
- Part of **Bartlett Faculty of the Built Environment** — the UK's largest multidisciplinary built environment faculty. ~3,500 students, 300 admin staff.
- The broader Bartlett includes: School of Architecture, School of Planning, Centre for Advanced Spatial Analysis, Development Planning Unit, School of Sustainable Construction, Institute of Environmental Design and Engineering, Energy Institute, Institute of Sustainable Resources.
- **UCL connection** — the same university as Karl Friston (Wellcome Centre for Human Neuroimaging). Not an explicit collaboration but the institutional proximity matters.
- Validated by **RIBA** (Royal Institute of British Architects).

## Institutional History & Controversy
- Founded 1841 — first architecture chair in UK. Named "Bartlett" in 1919 after Sir Herbert Bartlett.
- 2021–2022: major sexism/racism/bullying scandal exposed. 70+ testimonies. Director Bob Sheil resigned. UCL publicly apologised June 2022. Howlett Brown Report (119 pages) identified "boys' club" culture, power/protectionism, fear of speaking out.
- Post-scandal reform: new director Amy Kulper, Shared Governance, commitment to spatial justice, decolonisation, decarbonisation.
- Feb 2026: Staff complained to Health & Safety Executive (HSE) about excessive workloads and stress — some staff say the "mandate of change" from the report has been used to impose top-down agendas.
