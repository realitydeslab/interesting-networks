# NRx (Neoreactionary) Network / Dark Enlightenment

The informal online community of bloggers and political theorists that coalesced around **Curtis Yarvin** (Mencius Moldbug) and was named by **[[Nick Land]]** as the "Dark Enlightenment" in his 2012 essay of the same name.

## What It Is

An anti-democratic, anti-egalitarian reactionary political movement. Key positions:
- Liberal democratic institutions are a "Cathedral" — a secular religion suppressing truth
- Democracy should be replaced by CEO-run competitive city-states
- Scientific racism is suppressed truth the Cathedral refuses to acknowledge
- Technological singularity requires abolishing humanist politics
- Capital, not democracy, should be the organizing force of society

Yarvin began constructing the framework in 2007-08. Land named and extended it in 2012. The movement has been described as "the most significant political theory within the alt-right" and as providing philosophical foundations for considerable amounts of alt-right activity.

## Online Venues

- *Unqualified Reservations* (Yarvin's founding blog)
- *Gray Mirror* (Yarvin's current Substack)
- *Xenosystems* (Land's blog, 2004-2022, now defunct)
- Social Matter forum
- Thermidor Magazine
- Hestia Society

## Silicon Valley Connection

Despite or because of its anti-democratic stance, NRx has found significant sympathy in Silicon Valley:
- **Peter Thiel** — invested in Yarvin's company Tlon; considered "fully enlightened" by Yarvin
- **Marc Andreessen** — quoted Yarvin, called him a "friend"
- **JD Vance** — cited Yarvin as an influence; connected to Thiel
- **Steve Bannon** — read Yarvin's work

The appeal seems to be that NRx provides intellectual justification for techno-libertarian rule by capital against democratic accountability — exactly what Silicon Valley's venture capital class would find appealing.

## Why In Graph

This community exists as **context for understanding [[Nick Land]]'s Dark Enlightenment phase**. Understanding what NRx is and why it gained traction in Silicon Valley is useful for understanding:
- The political valence of accelerationist thought
- Why Land's colleagues (Fisher, Brassier, Grant, Goodman) disowned him
- The live danger within posthumanist/accelerationist frameworks when not anchored by humanist politics
