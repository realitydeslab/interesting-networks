# Inflexions: A Journal for Research-Creation

**Open-access, multimedia peer journal** produced by the [[SenseLab]] at Concordia University (Montréal), now archived under [3ecologies.org](https://3ecologies.org) curatorship. Published 11 issues + 1 pop-up (2008–2019). The canonical journal of **research-creation methodology** in Canada and arguably the world. ISSN 1916-5870. Member of [Open Humanities Press](http://openhumanitiespress.org/) and [DOAJ](http://www.doaj.org/).

The founding issue ("How is Research-Creation?", 2008) remains the key text defining research-creation as a distinct methodological practice. The journal itself *is* research-creation: web-native, multimedia, non-text-only, process-driven. After ceasing, the [[3 Ecologies Institute]] / Immediations book series at [[punctum books]] became the successor publication vehicle.

## Mission & Objectives

Inflexions defines itself as promoting **experimental practices combining research and creation** to foster symbiotic links between philosophical inquiry, technological innovation, artistic production, and social/political engagement. From their [about page](http://www.inflexions.org/about.html):

> *"We invite writing and/or other forms of expression actively exploring such issues as: (inter/trans/non) disciplinarity; the emergence of new modes of collaboration; micropolitics and the life and death of institutions; creativity, subjectivity and collectivity in cultural production; the ethics of aesthetics; the aesthetic as ethics."*

The journal embraces the technical possibilities of the web — Flash-based interactive issues, sound, multimedia, PDF layers, nonlinear navigation. Each issue has a **NODE** (thematically interlinked conceptual cluster) and **TANGENTS** (individual divergent contributions that resonate across their differences).

Philosophical anchors: **Whitehead** (process philosophy), **Deleuze/Guattari** (affect, transversality, micropolitics), **Simondon** (individuation, technics), **Bergson** (duration, movement). The epigraph from Whitehead: *"A thought is a tremendous mode of excitement. Like a stone thrown into a pond it disturbs the whole surface of our being."*

### Issue Highlights

**No.1 — "How is Research-Creation?"** (2008): The founding manifesto. Still the most-cited text defining research-creation as a methodological practice in Canadian and international art academia. The question is answered by *being* research-creation — the journal format is itself the argument.

**No.5 — "Gilbert Simondon"**: A full issue on Simondon's philosophy of individuation, technics, and transduction. Crucial reading for anyone working in cosmotechnics (Yuk Hui) or the philosophy of technology and life. Brunner, Boucher, Rhoades.

**No.7 — "Animating Biophilosophy"**: Explores the intersection of biological thinking, life, and philosophy. NODE edited by Nocek and Thurtle: "The work collected here generate nomadic moments within biological thinking about life. Animating Biophilosophy, encouraging thought to move, sense, respond." TANGENT: "From Earth to Outer Space" — embodied art/activist practices exploring zones of (in)habitability.

**No.8 — "Radical Pedagogies"**: Includes Brian Massumi's "Collective Expression: A Radical Pragmatics" (pp. 59-88) and Erin Manning's "10 Propositions for a Radical Pedagogy, or How to Rethink Value" (pp. 202-210). Key for understanding the SenseLab pedagogy of research-creation.

**No.10 — "Modes of Exhaustion"**: Edited with an "Introduction: For an Ethology of Exhaustion" (Brunner, Kuipers, Pape). **The word ethology appears explicitly** — directly connecting to the intellectual lineage Amber is drawing from. Guattarian ethology as a way of thinking about exhaustion, sustainability, and indigenous solidarities.

**pOp up 1 — "Diversity in Diversity"**: Embraces neurodiversity (Adam Wolfond, Tito Mukhopadhyay — nonspeaking autistic writers); coordinated by SpaZe (a neurodivergent collective). Directly shapes 3E's later emphasis on welcoming neurodiversity.

## Key Contributions

1. **Institutionalizing research-creation methodology** — made research-creation legible as a practice to Canadian and international funding bodies (SSHRC), academia, and the art world
2. **Web-native scholarly publishing** — each issue built as multimedia experience, not PDF supplement. Flash-based, interactive, designed as artistic-philosophical objects
3. **Theorizing affect, process, transversality** — established key vocabulary (from Deleuze/Guattari/Simondon/Whitehead) now used across performance studies, HCI, design research
4. **Ethology as method** — No.10's "Ethology of Exhaustion" explicitly uses ethological framing for cultural/political analysis; direct lineage to Massumi and Manning's broader project
5. **Neurodiversity inclusion** — pOp up 1 and 3E's subsequent work; shaped the field's understanding of access and neurodivergent knowledges
6. **Open Humanities Press** — institutional infrastructure for open-access critical theory publishing; Inflexions helped establish this network

## Why Interesting

Inflexions sits at the intersection of Amber's core interests in multiple ways:

1. **More-than-human + embodied practice:** The journal theorizes sensing bodies in movement, affect, the emergence of collective experience — all aligned with Amber's interest in somaesthetic design and more-than-human interaction.

2. **Process philosophy lineage:** Whitehead, Deleuze, Simondon — the same thinkers Yuk Hui (who teaches at China Academy of Art where Amber lectures) draws on. This is a shared intellectual inheritance.

3. **Research-creation as methodology:** This is what Amber DOES. HoloKit is research-creation. The speculative AR installations are research-creation. Inflexions is the venue that legitimized this mode academically.

4. **Ethology connection:** The explicit use of "ethology" as an analytic framework (No.10's "Ethology of Exhaustion") predates but legitimizes Amber's "agent ethology" framing. Direct intellectual genealogy.

5. **Multimedia/speculative publishing:** The journal experimented with what academic publishing could be — Flash, interactive, web-native — resonating with Amber's interest in how research can be presented non-conventionally.

6. **Radical pedagogy:** Amber teaches speculative design practices. Manning's pedagogy is a direct model.
