# Complexity Science Hub (CSH)

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-03-11:** Initial deep research. Full profile with all research topics, 80+ resident scientists, 60+ external faculty, education programs, Art & Science program, and Amber-relevant connections mapped.

Europe's leading research center for the study of complex systems. Founded 2015 in Vienna, Austria. ~70+ resident researchers. CSH translates massive datasets into understanding of interconnected systems — from healthcare and supply chains to social dynamics and digital ecosystems. Transdisciplinary by design: "to solve problems that cannot be addressed successfully in any other way." Vienna location, strong SFI overlap, and direct relevance to agent-based modeling, collective behavior, and social simulation make this a high-priority node.

## Mission & Objectives

**Mission:** Create a profound scientific understanding of complex systems to make smart predictions about how systems respond to change and steer them in directions that benefit all of society.

**Approach:** Transdisciplinary research combining physics, mathematics, computational modeling, data science, and network science with questions from medicine, economics, ecology, and social sciences. Focus on real-world data at massive scale.

**Founded:** 2015 in Vienna, Austria. Grown from founding initiative to 70+ researchers. President: **[[Stefan Thurner]]** (co-founder).

**Structure:** Three overarching research clusters:
1. **Societies Under Stress** — crises, systemic risk, resilience
2. **Societies in Transition** — digital transformation, innovation, economic evolution
3. **Metabolism of Societies** — sustainability, resource flows, urban systems

## Research Topics

CSH organizes research into 16 distinct topic areas. Those most relevant to Amber's interests are flagged with 🎯:

### 🎯 Collective Minds
How collectives — from small groups to entire societies — adapt beliefs, decisions, and organizational structures. Computational models grounded in real-world data on cognition and sociality. Studies collective adaptation, belief formation, opinion dynamics. **Led by [[Mirta Galesic]] and [[Henrik Olsson]].**
- URL: <https://csh.ac.at/research/research-topic/collective-minds/>

### 🎯 Social Complexity & Collapse
Cliodynamics — history as science. Uses massive [Seshat: Global History Databank](https://seshatdatabank.info/) to test theories about the evolution and breakdown of complex human societies. **Agent-based modeling** combined with cultural evolution theories. **Led by [[Peter Turchin]].**
- URL: <https://csh.ac.at/research/research-topic/social-complexity-collapse/>

### 🎯 Foundations of Complex Systems
New mathematics and statistics for networked dynamical systems. Co-evolutionary systems, collective phenomena, systemic risks. Closely tied to information theory and statistical physics of complex systems.
- URL: <https://csh.ac.at/research/research-topic/foundations/>

### 🎯 AI & Machine Learning
Trustworthy ML for complex problems. Explores AI from statistical physics and information theory perspectives. Uses LLMs and NLP for analyzing social dynamics. Aims to understand **"future trajectories of human and AI collectives."**
- URL: <https://csh.ac.at/research/research-topic/ai-and-machine-learning/>

### 🎯 Algorithmic Fairness
Biases in algorithms and social networks. Agent-based modeling of inequality dynamics. Network structure effects on ranking/recommender algorithms. **Led by [[Fariba Karimi]].**
- URL: <https://csh.ac.at/research/research-topic/algorithmic-fairness/>

## Events & Programmes

### 🎓 PostDoc Program
Fully funded postdoctoral fellowships for early-career researchers. EUR 66,532/year salary + health insurance + pension. Initial 1-year commitment with extension possible. Priority areas: key technology sectors, supply chain intelligence, systemic risk, **applied AI**, sustainable cities, social cohesion, socio-ecological modeling. Support for applying for MSCA, FWF ESPRIT, and other external grants.
- URL: <https://csh.ac.at/education/postdoc-program/>

### 🎓 Graduate Program (Digital Innovation School)
Fully funded PhD program. EUR 39,004/year salary + EUR 3,000 travel allowance + health insurance. 3-year program with optional extension. Students produce at least 3 first-author research papers. Funded by Austrian Federal Chancellery and Ministry for Women, Science & Research. English is working language; no German required.
- URL: <https://csh.ac.at/education/graduate-program/>
- FAQ: <https://csh.ac.at/dis-faq/>

### 🎯 Visiting Researchers Program
Research visits of **3–12 months**, can begin any time. Visiting researchers are integrated into a CSH host's research group, present their work to the community. In-person in Vienna. **Open rolling applications.**
