# Oxford Reasoning with Machines Lab (OxRML)

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-03-09:** Deep research completed. Full community note with 12 team members, key publications, connections to Amber's work mapped.

A research lab at the [Oxford Internet Institute (OII)](https://www.oii.ox.ac.uk/), University of Oxford, led by [Professor Adam Mahdi](https://www.oii.ox.ac.uk/people/profiles/adam-mahdi/). **Amber's neighbour lab at OII.** The lab conducts rigorous, mathematically-grounded empirical research on how large language models reason, solve complex problems, and interact with humans — with direct focus on evaluation science, AI safety, agentic systems, and human-AI interaction. Active, fast-moving, publishing at top venues (NeurIPS, ICML, ACL, EMNLP, Nature).

## Mission & Objectives

**Mission:** To advance understanding of how large language models process information, solve tasks, and interact with humans in real-world contexts. Combining mathematical rigour with empirical investigation.

**Core philosophy:** The lab sits at the intersection of technical AI research and social/policy relevance. They don't just build — they evaluate, critique, and understand. The emphasis on *construct validity*, *measurement science*, and *sociotechnical evaluation* sets them apart from pure engineering labs.

**Four research pillars:**
1. **Benchmarks & Evaluation** — science of LLM evaluation, construct validity, measurement frameworks
2. **Agentic AI for Science** — automating scientific process with reliable, transparent agents
3. **AI Safety** — harms evaluation, misalignment in agentic systems, technical mitigations, governance
4. **Human-AI Interaction** — large-scale empirical studies on how humans use AI in real settings

## Key Publications & Output

### Landmark Papers (2024-2026)
1. **[Clinical knowledge in LLMs does not translate to human interactions](https://arxiv.org/search/?query=clinical+knowledge+LLMs+human+interactions+Bean&searchtype=all)** — Bean, Payne, Parsons, Kirk, Mahdi et al. (Apr 2025). *Nature* family. 1,300-participant study showing LLMs ace medical exams but fail in real clinical interaction. Landmark human-AI interaction result.

2. **[Measuring what Matters: Construct Validity in Large Language Model Benchmarks](https://arxiv.org/search/?query=Measuring+Matters+Construct+Validity+LLM+Benchmarks+Bean+Kearns&searchtype=all)** — Bean, Kearns, Romanou, Hafner, Mayne, Korgul, Mahdi et al. (Nov 2025). 29-expert-reviewer systematic study of LLM benchmark validity. Foundational evaluation science.

3. **[It's a TRAP! Task-Redirecting Agent Persuasion Benchmark for Web Agents](https://arxiv.org/search/?query=TRAP+Task+Redirecting+Agent+Persuasion+Korgul&searchtype=all)** — Korgul, Yang, Drohomirecki, Mahdi et al. (Dec 2025). Benchmark for prompt injection attacks on web agents — agentic safety evaluation.

4. **[LINGOLY-TOO: Disentangling Reasoning from Knowledge](https://arxiv.org/search/?query=LINGOLY-TOO+Disentangling+Reasoning+Knowledge+Khouja+Korgul&searchtype=all)** — Khouja, Korgul, Mayne, Kearns, Bean, Mahdi (Mar 2025, updated Mar 2026). Novel benchmark countering reasoning shortcuts via orthographic obfuscation.

5. **[A Positive Case for Faithfulness: LLM Self-Explanations Help Predict Model Behavior](https://arxiv.org/search/?query=Positive+Case+Faithfulness+LLM+Self-Explanations+Mayne+Mahdi&searchtype=all)** — Mayne, Kang, Gould, Mahdi et al. (Feb 2026). Defense of faithfulness metrics; shows explanations have genuine predictive value.

6. **[Agentic Reinforcement Learning for Search is Unsafe](https://arxiv.org/search/?query=Agentic+Reinforcement+Learning+Search+Unsafe+Yang+Mahdi&searchtype=all)** — Yang, Padarha, Lee, Mahdi (Oct 2025). RL-trained search models inherit refusal inconsistently — agentic safety analysis.

7. **[How Does DPO Reduce Toxicity? A Mechanistic Neuron-Level Analysis](https://arxiv.org/search/?query=DPO+Reduce+Toxicity+Mechanistic+Neuron+Yang+Mahdi&searchtype=all)** — Yang, Sondej, Mayne, Mahdi (Nov 2024, updated Jun 2025). Mechanistic interpretability of safety fine-tuning.

8. **[LLMs Don't Know Their Own Decision Boundaries](https://arxiv.org/search/?query=LLMs+Decision+Boundaries+Counterfactual+Explanations+Mayne+Kearns&searchtype=all)** — Mayne, Kearns, Yang, Bean, Mahdi (Sep 2025). Self-generated counterfactual explanations are unreliable — XAI critique.

9. **[Large language models can help boost food production, but be mindful of their risks](https://arxiv.org/search/?query=large+language+models+food+production+risks+De+Clercq&searchtype=all)** — De Clercq, Nehring, Mayne, Mahdi (Mar 2024). LLMs in agriculture — risk-aware perspective piece.

10. **[Do Large Language Models have Shared Weaknesses in Medical Question Answering?](https://arxiv.org/search/?query=LLMs+Shared+Weaknesses+Medical+Question+Bean+Korgul+Mahdi&searchtype=all)** — Bean, Korgul, Krones, McCraith, Mahdi (Oct 2023). Foundational medical LLM evaluation study.
