# Alan Turing Institute

The UK's national institute for data science and artificial intelligence. Founded 2015 by five universities (Cambridge, Edinburgh, Oxford, UCL, Warwick) and EPSRC. Headquartered at the British Library, London. Sits at the centre of the UK's AI research and policy ecosystem — bridging academia, government, and industry on questions of AI safety, ethics, and governance. Most relevant to Amber: the Ethics and Responsible Innovation group and the Centre for Emerging Technology and Security (CETaS).

## Mission & Objectives
> "To make great leaps in data science research in order to change the world for the better."

The Turing's mandate spans:
- **Research excellence** — fundamental and applied AI/data science
- **Driving impact** — applied to real-world problems in science, government, health, security
- **Pioneering a new science** — training the next generation, shaping policy discourse

Core research areas:
1. **Fundamental AI** — foundation models, LLMs, principles underpinning AI
2. **Robotics & Autonomous Systems** — unique challenges of deployment
3. **AI Safety and Security** — contributions to international safety governance
4. **Ethics and Responsible Innovation** — AI ethics, governance frameworks, policy co-creation
5. **Regulation and Governance** — international AI governance landscape
6. **Public Policy** — evidence-based AI policy for the public sector

## Why Interesting

The Alan Turing Institute is the institutional spine of UK AI policy and the primary academic bridge to government on AI ethics, safety, and governance. It's directly relevant to Amber's research on trustworthy AI and agentic systems — not because the Turing is doing radical or speculative work (it isn't), but because it's where UK AI governance happens. David Leslie's group is the closest thing in the UK academy to rigorous process-based AI ethics in practice. For Amber's ambitions to influence AI policy and work on trustworthy agentic systems at scale, the Turing is a key institutional node to know and be known by. Oxford's founding member status and LASR partnership create a ready-made access pathway.

The Turing isn't Amber's aesthetic home — it's quite mainstream and policy-facing. But it's where policy gets made, and Amber's more philosophical/design research can gain legitimacy and reach through that channel.
