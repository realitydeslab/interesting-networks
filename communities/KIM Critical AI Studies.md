# KIM — Critical AI Studies (HfG Karlsruhe)

Research group founded by [[Matteo Pasquinelli]] at the [University of Arts and Design Karlsruhe (HfG)](https://www.hfg-karlsruhe.de/en/) in 2018. One of the first dedicated Critical AI Studies research groups in Europe. Active 2018–2023; website now functions as archive. On Pasquinelli's move to Ca' Foscari Venice, the group's work continues under two successor projects: the EU-funded [AIMODELS ERC project](https://pric.unive.it/projects/ai-models/home) (2024–2028) and the Volkswagen Stiftung [AI Forensics](https://ai-forensics.github.io/) project (2022–2025). The international mailing list [allmodels.ai](https://allmodels.ai) continues as the community's connective tissue.

## Mission & Objectives

KIM's mission was to study artificial intelligence from perspectives rooted in:
- **Media philosophy** (German media theory tradition — Kittler, Siegert, etc.)
- **Political economy** (Italian autonomism/operaismo — Negri, Lazzarato)
- **Feminist and decolonial theory** (Federici, Harding, Fox Keller, Benjamin)
- **History of science and technology** (STS, historical epistemology)

Core thesis: AI is not an autonomous "intelligent" system but a **knowledge instrument** — a Nooscope — that compresses, distorts, and extracts cultural heritage and labour into statistical models. AI must be analyzed in terms of political economy, labour relations, and epistemic colonialism, not just technical bias.

The [allmodels.ai](https://allmodels.ai) manifesto captures this in seven theses:
- *All models are political* — ML models are a new Kulturtechnik of social governance
- *All models are generative* — replacing causation with automated correlation
- *All models are human* — hidden labour of the Global South underpins AI
- *All models break* — "breaking the model" is the experimental method of critical AI studies
- *All models require interpretation* — interpretability is humanist and political, not just technical

## Key Publications & Output

- **[The Nooscope Manifested](https://nooscope.ai/)** (Pasquinelli & Joler, 2020/2021) — landmark visual essay/diagram-manifesto mapping machine learning as knowledge instrument. Published in *AI & Society* (Springer, 2022). Translated into 5+ languages. Shown at Gwangju Biennale. Available as long-form text and as a diagram poster. DOI: [10.1007/s00146-021-01317-5](https://doi.org/10.1007/s00146-021-01317-5)
- **[The Eye of the Master: A Social History of Artificial Intelligence](https://www.matteopasquinelli.com/the-eye-of-the-master/)** (Pasquinelli, Verso, 2023) — book; won the Deutscher Memorial Prize 2024. Translated into 14+ languages (Arabic, Chinese, French, German, Greek, Italian, Russian, Spanish, Turkish and more).
- **[UMBAU journal](https://umbau.hfg-karlsruhe.de/)** — HfG research journal; hosted KIM publications.
- **[AI Forensics project](https://ai-forensics.github.io/)** — multi-institution research project (HfG Karlsruhe, Universität Kassel, Durham University, Cambridge University, UC Santa Barbara). Volkswagen Stiftung funded, 2022–2025. Three workstreams:
  1. *Vectors for Workers: Models of Automation and Autonomy in the Long AI Century*
  2. *Matters of Explanation: Rethinking Explainability with Tangible, Embodied, Material Interactions*
  3. *Art x AI: Who Makes, Who Owns, Who Decides?*
- **[allmodels.ai](https://allmodels.ai)** — international mailing list of critical AI studies, initiated July 2020. "Alternative format" born from a pandemic-canceled workshop series. Community-building infrastructure connecting scholars, artists, coders, activists.
- **[Alleys of Your Mind: Augmented Intelligence and Its Traumas](https://meson.press/books/alleys-of-your-mind/)** (ed. Pasquinelli, Meson Press, 2015) — edited anthology; open access.

## Key Contributions

1. **The Nooscope diagram** — a viral, diagram-as-manifesto artifact that circulates across both academic and art contexts. Model for how Amber might produce research artifacts.
2. **Naming Critical AI Studies as a field** — KIM helped crystallize "Critical AI Studies" as a distinct intellectual formation, with its own methods (historical epistemology, political economy, STS) distinct from AI ethics or AI safety.
3. **allmodels.ai community** — ongoing infrastructure connecting interdisciplinary AI critics globally.
4. **Art school as critical theory hub** — demonstrated that an art school (HfG) can produce globally circulating critical theory.
5. **Successor projects** — AIMODELS and AI Forensics continue the agenda with institutional and ERC backing.

## For Amber

- **Why interesting:** KIM is the European art-school-rooted Critical AI Studies group doing the most theoretically rigorous non-ethics-checklist work. Their approach — historical epistemology + political economy + feminist theory + art practice — is exactly the intellectual formation Amber should be in conversation with. The *Nooscope* is a model artifact: paper + diagram + exhibition piece + teaching tool simultaneously. This is precisely the kind of hybrid research object Amber's work aspires to.

## Why Interesting

KIM showed how an art-school-adjacent research group can produce work that circulates globally in both critical theory AND media art circles simultaneously. For Amber's research program — bridging Alife, machine behavior, more-than-human design, and critical theory — this is the exact model of how to position a research group. The Nooscope is the prototype artifact: it's a paper, a diagram, an exhibition piece, and a teaching tool at once. And the community infrastructure (allmodels.ai) shows how to build a field around a research group without institutional resources.

The intellectual lineage (German media theory + Italian autonomism + feminist epistemology + STS) is also the right lineage for Amber's work — none of the Anglo-American AI ethics discourse, all of the critical theory.
