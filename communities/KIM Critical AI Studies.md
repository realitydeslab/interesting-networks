# KIM — Critical AI Studies (HfG Karlsruhe)

Research group founded by [[Matteo Pasquinelli]] at the [University of Arts and Design Karlsruhe (HfG)](https://www.hfg-karlsruhe.de/en/) in 2018. One of the first dedicated Critical AI Studies research groups in Europe. Active 2018–2023; now coordinated for the AI Forensics project even after Pasquinelli moved to Ca' Foscari.

## Key Output
- [**The Nooscope Manifested**](https://kim.hfg-karlsruhe.de/nooscope.ai) (2020) — landmark visual essay/diagram-manifesto mapping AI as knowledge instrument. Co-authored Pasquinelli & Joler. Published as AI & Society paper (2021). Translated into 5+ languages. Shown in Gwangju Biennale.
- [**UMBAU journal**](https://umbau.hfg-karlsruhe.de/) — HfG research journal; hosted KIM publications.
- **AI Forensics project** ([ai-forensics.github.io](https://ai-forensics.github.io)) — Volkswagen Stiftung funded, 2022–2025. Multi-institution: HfG Karlsruhe, Universität Kassel, Durham University, Cambridge University, UC Santa Barbara. Topics: Vectors for Workers, Matters of Explanation, Art x AI.

## Mission
Critical study of artificial intelligence from media philosophy, political economy, and art-science perspectives. Grounded in the German media theory tradition and Italian operaismo/autonomist theory.

## Events & Programmes
- Hosted symposia on theories of automation, AI and work, AI and culture
- Historical Materialism conference talks (2023)
- Connected to HfG's broader critical theory curriculum

## For Amber
- **Why interesting**: One of the only European Critical AI Studies groups doing rigorously theoretical work (not just ethics checklists). The Nooscope method — diagram-manifesto — is directly relevant to Amber's own visualization of agent behavior.
- **How to engage**: Via [[Matteo Pasquinelli]] at Ca' Foscari. AI Forensics project has final publication forthcoming (2025). KIM website archives all publications and events.
- **Research intersection**: "Art x AI: Who Makes, Who Owns, Who Decides?" workstream directly relevant to Amber's work on machine behavior and agent ethology.

## Why Interesting
KIM showed how an art-school-adjacent research group can produce work that circulates globally in critical theory AND media art circles simultaneously. The Nooscope is the model artifact: it's a paper, a diagram, an exhibition piece, and a teaching tool at once.
