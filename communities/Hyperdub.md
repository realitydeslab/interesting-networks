# Hyperdub

Electronic music record label founded by Steve Goodman (Kode9) in London in 2004 — one of the most theoretically sophisticated labels in contemporary electronic music. The crucial organisational bridge between [[Cybernetic Culture Research Unit (CCRU)|CCRU]] theory-fiction and the living practice of sound culture: hauntology, sonic warfare, afrofuturism, and more-than-human politics all crystallise here in music form.

## Mission & Objectives

Hyperdub began in 1999 as a **webzine** blending forward-thinking UK dance music with theoretical writing. In 2004 it became a record label. Its first release: "Sine of the Dub," a collaboration between Kode9 and The Spaceape — a minimal cover of Prince's "Sign O' the Times" reworked to sound like "a man on his deathbed."

The label's core ethos: **theory and sound are not separate**. Goodman's PhD (University of Warwick, 1999) on "Turbulence: A Cartography of Postmodern Violence" feeds directly into the music. Hyperdub is what happens when CCRU ideas stop being text and become bass frequencies.

RBMA described Hyperdub as ["one of the UK's most celebrated underground labels."](https://www.redbull.com/gb-en/under-the-covers-hyperdub) It established the dubstep genre, then moved into UK funky, footwork, IDM, and art-electronic music — always with intellectual seriousness and curatorial boldness.

Key institutional genealogy:
- **University of Warwick** → CCRU → Kode9 + Mark Fisher
- **Katasonix** (1999 label co-run with Mark Fisher) → **Hyperdub** (2004)
- **Hyperdub webzine** (1999) → **Hyperdub Records** (2004)
- **Flatlines sublabel** (launched for posthumous Mark Fisher audio work)

## Key People

### Founder

**[[Steve Goodman]] (Kode9)** ⭐⭐⭐  
Born 1973, Glasgow. PhD in Philosophy, University of Warwick (1999). Thesis: *Turbulence: A Cartography of Postmodern Violence*. CCRU member at Warwick alongside Mark Fisher. Moved to London 1999. Taught at University of East London (media production, MA Sonic Culture) until 2014. Author of *[Sonic Warfare: Sound, Affect, and the Ecology of Fear](https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262516556/)* (MIT Press, 2009). Also coined the term **"sinofuturism"** while at the CCRU — making him a crucial theoretical node for Amber's cosmotechnics interests. Sinofuturism explores China not as future-backward but as accelerationist attractor.

### Core Artists (complete roster)

**Burial** (William Emmanuel Bevan) ⭐⭐⭐  
South London. First artist signed to Hyperdub (2005). Debut album *[Burial](https://hyperdub.net/products/burial-burial)* (2006) — Wire album of year. *[Untrue](https://hyperdub.net/products/burial-untrue)* (2007) — landmark of electronic music. Collaborators include Four Tet, Massive Attack, Thom Yorke, The Bug, Zomby. Remained largely anonymous until 2008. Key figure in Mark Fisher's hauntology theory — "the ghostly textures of rave, the vocal samples from soul and jungle, give Burial's music its hauntological quality, invoking a past that never was."

**[[Mark Fisher]]** — though not a Hyperdub artist per se, was Kode9's co-founder of Katasonix. The Flatlines sublabel released his posthumous audio work *[On Vanishing Land](https://hyperdub.net/)* (with Justin Barton).

**Fatima Al Qadiri** ⭐⭐⭐  
Born 1981, Dakar (Kuwaiti diplomat family). NYU linguistics degree. Conceptual artist + musician. Key albums on Hyperdub: *[Asiatisch](https://www.discogs.com/master/661534)* (2014), *[Brute](https://www.discogs.com/master/978773)* (2016), *[Medieval Femme](https://www.discogs.com/master/2197040)* (2021), *Shaneera* (2017), *Gumar* (2023). Also member of GCC collective (exhibited MoMA PS1, Whitney, Fridericianum). Sister: visual artist Monira Al Qadiri. Her music on Beyoncé's Renaissance World Tour. Major node in Gulf/Arabic experimental music scene.

**Loraine James** ⭐⭐  
Born 1995, Enfield, North London. IDM/experimental. Signed Hyperdub 2019. Album *[For You and I](https://lorainejames.bandcamp.com/album/for-you-and-i)* (2019) topped Quietus/DJ Mag end-of-year. Also releases as Whatever the Weather (ambient). *[Reflection](https://lorainejames.bandcamp.com/album/reflection)* (2021). Julius Eastman tribute album (Phantom Limb, 2022). CTM Festival collaborations with Isabel Lewis. Queer artist from working-class North London — significant voice in UK electronic landscape.

**Dean Blunt** ⭐⭐  
Nigerian-British, from Hackney. Art-pop provocateur. Releases on Hyperdub as Babyfather and Dean Blunt (also Hype Williams, with Inga Copeland, pre-Hyperdub). Category-refusing, mystery-cultivating. Contemporary art + music crossover figure.

**Inga Copeland** ⭐⭐  
Russian-Estonian artist (also known as Lolina). Part of Hype Williams duo with Dean Blunt. Experimental electronic. Released solo on Hyperdub. Post-Hype Williams solo work under Lolina name.

1. **Founded the dubstep genre as a viable artistic form** — not just a club style but a medium for hauntological and theoretical expression
2. **Institutionalised theory-music practice** — demonstrated that a label can be a philosophical project
3. **Coined "sinofuturism"** — Kode9 coined the term at CCRU; now a major theoretical framework touching Amber's cosmotechnics interests
4. **Preserved and developed CCRU legacy** through music — the most accessible entry point to CCRU thought
5. **Launched *On Vanishing Land*** — the primary posthumous output of Mark Fisher's audio practice
6. **Built a diasporic roster** — South African, Angolan, Palestinian-American, Kuwaiti, Japanese artists within a single label ecosystem
7. **Sustained The Wire's discourse** — consistently the most Wire-covered UK label for 20 years

## Key Theoretical Concepts (for Amber's use)

### Hauntology
Borrowed from Derrida, developed by Mark Fisher through Burial's music. The present haunted by pasts that never fully arrived — failed futures of rave, jungle, the welfare state. Electronic music as the sonic form of hauntology. Burial's chopped vocal samples = ghosts of rave.

### Sonic Warfare / Bass Materialism
Kode9's *Sonic Warfare* (MIT Press, 2009): sound as affective weapon, not just communication. "Unsound" — the vibrational potential beneath audibility. Sound can induce dread, panic, love, trance. Music as force field, not signal. Connects to affect theory (Massumi, Spinoza) and Deleuze/Guattari.

### Sinofuturism
Kode9 coined "sinofuturism" at CCRU, later developed by Lawrence Lek (CGI film), Dani Admiss, and others. China as the site of an accelerationist experiment: high-speed capitalism + collectivist infrastructure + non-Western modernity. Not a celebration but an analysis. Extremely relevant to Amber's cosmotechnics work.

### Hyperstition
CCRU concept (Nick Land primary, but shared): ideas that make themselves real through circulation. Fictions that become facts. The entire Hyperdub project is a hyperstition machine — what if dubstep was a philosophy? Answer: it became one.

### The Hardcore Continuum
Term by Simon Reynolds; adopted by Kode9. The unbroken line of UK dance music: rave → jungle → drum'n'bass → 2-step garage → grime → dubstep → UK funky → footwork → UK bass. Hyperdub is the primary institutional carrier of this genealogy.

### Afrofuturism → Sonic Afrofuturism
Kode9 studied afrofuturism at Warwick. Burial's South London urban soundscapes. Cooly G and the UK funky connection. DJ Rashad and Chicago footwork. The label consistently draws from Black Atlantic sonic traditions.

## Why Interesting

Hyperdub is the best-documented case study in the 21st century of a cultural institution that takes theory seriously as *production practice* — not as window dressing but as the actual generative engine. When Kode9 signs Burial, it's because dubstep materialises CCRU ideas about temporal fragmentation and sonic affect. When Fatima Al Qadiri releases *Asiatisch*, she's conducting conceptual art through music distribution. When Nazar releases *Demilitarize*, it's sonic testimony to colonial violence.

For Amber: this is a model for how non-academic institutions can carry and transmit critical theory. It also sits at the intersection of:
- **CCRU/accelerationism** (core genealogy)
- **Sinofuturism** (coined by Kode9)
- **Hauntology** (Burial + Mark Fisher)
- **Diasporic futurism** (Gulf, South Africa, Angola, Philippines)
- **Sonic/embodied experience** as philosophical methodology
- **Machine death / posthumous work** (Flatlines/On Vanishing Land)

All of these are live Amber concerns. The label is a portal into an enormous intellectual-creative network.
