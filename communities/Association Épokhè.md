# Association Épokhè

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-27:** Full deep research completed. Expanded from stub into comprehensive note. Mapped key people, projects, institutions, and Amber relevance.
- **2026-02-26:** Created stub during [[Bernard Stiegler]] research. Continues [[Ars Industrialis]] legacy after Stiegler's death (2020).

French philosophical-political association continuing [[Bernard Stiegler]]'s program of "ecology of technique." Post-2020 institutional successor to [[Ars Industrialis]] (2005-2020). Works on technology critique, contributive economy, and intergenerational dialogue at the intersection of research and social mobilization.

## Mission & Objectives

Épokhè ("epoche" — the phenomenological suspension of judgment, stepping back from the immediate) frames its core work as:

- Creating conditions for a **"bifurcation"** of contemporary society's trajectory away from computational capitalism
- Building **intergenerational dialogue space** at the intersection of research and social mobilizations
- Constructing articulations between **knowledge, practices, and citizen engagement**
- Experimenting with **contributive economy** — a model of knowledge-sharing that resists the "attention economy" and Silicon Valley's appropriation of open-source/open-web values
- Maintaining and extending Bernard Stiegler's philosophical archive (Pharmakon seminars, Ars Industrialis texts)

Key concept: **pharmakon** (remedy/poison/scapegoat simultaneously) — digital technology as both threat and potential cure, requiring philosophical therapeia. This frames all their work: not rejection of technology but critical pharmacology.

Épokhè takes the **contributive economy** seriously as a lived experiment, not just theory. Members are not consumers of knowledge but active co-producers. Membership involves participating in Signal discussion groups, annotating texts via [Hypothes.is](https://web.hypothes.is/), commenting on audiovisual archives via [Celluloid](https://celluloid.huma-num.fr/), attending General Assemblies, and co-authoring collective publications on [Mediapart](https://blogs.mediapart.fr/association-epokhe/blog).

## Key People

- **[[Bernard Stiegler]]** — founder of Ars Industrialis (2005) and Pharmakon school (2010); died August 2020. His intellectual legacy shapes everything Épokhè does. Major works: *Technics and Time* trilogy, *Taking Care of Youth and the Generations*, *Bifurquer* (his final book).
- **Caroline Fayat** — co-founder of Pharmakon with Stiegler (his wife). Key continuity figure between Stiegler's life and the post-2020 association.
- **Victor Chaix** — active member, writer. Authored articles on Mediapart including "Décoder, recoder, dépasser: pour une véritable techno-magie" published also in [Institute of Network Cultures](https://networkcultures.org/) (Amsterdam). Co-produced the Pharmakon Hypertexte (2020 seminar edition). Writes on digital capitalism, psychedelia & computing, techno-politics.
- **Michel Blanchut** — active member. Co-produced the 2020 Pharmakon Hypertexte edition with Victor Chaix. Scholar working on Stiegler's exorganology framework.
- **[[Marie Chollat-Namy]]** — contributor. Participated in the final seminar discussion with Stiegler on entropy (July 1, 2020 — "our last meeting with the philosopher"). Works on science, technology, and political economy.
- **[[Maël Montévil]]** — biologist and philosopher of biology, close Stiegler collaborator. Participated in the entropy seminar. Works on biological individuation and entropy — connects Stiegler's third retention theory to biology. Based in Paris.
- **[[Anne Alombert]]** — philosopher, key Stiegler scholar and associate. Works extensively on Stiegler's philosophy of technology, psychotechnics, and digital studies.
- **[[Barbara Stiegler]]** — philosopher (Bernard's sister-in-law or relation), professor at University of Bordeaux-Montaigne. Wrote *"Il faut s'adapter"* (2019) on neoliberal biopolitics and evolution. Extends Stieglerian critique through Nietzsche and Dewey.
- **Igor Galligo** — organized the "Psychedelia and Computing: How to Bifurcate Cybernetics?" conference (April 2023), in partnership with Amis de la Génération Thunberg/Ars Industrialis. Appears to be a core organizer within the extended network.
- **[[Giuseppe Longo]]** — mathematician and philosopher of biology. Participated in the Psychedelia & Computing conference. Works on biological and mathematical foundations of knowledge — close to Épokhè's science critique.

## Key Concepts (Stieglerian Vocabulary)

Essential to understand Épokhè's work — from [Ars Industrialis vocabulary](https://arsindustrialis.org/vocabulaire-ars-industrialis/):

- **Pharmacologie** — Stiegler's method: neither technophobia nor techno-utopianism. Technology is always pharmakon (remedy + poison simultaneously). Therapy requires knowledge of the poison.
- **Rétentions tertiaires** — technical objects as "tertiary retention" (memory externalized in things, extending the human). Stiegler's key innovation beyond Husserl's primary/secondary retentions.
- **Transindividuation** — Simondonian psychic and collective individuation; Épokhè is itself an experiment in transindividuation.
- **Désautomatisation** — de-automatization; resisting the automatic behaviors imposed by computational systems.
- **Économie de l'attention** — attention economy (Épokhè uses this as critique of what they oppose).
- **Bifurcation** — the possibility of a civilizational fork away from the current trajectory; Épokhè's political horizon.
- **Exorganologie** — Stiegler's late concept: organs + organizations + organology together. How technical systems, bodies, and social organizations co-evolve.

## Connected Institutions

- **[[Ars Industrialis]]** ([arsindustrialis.org](https://arsindustrialis.org/)) — predecessor (2005-2020), now redirects to Épokhè. Founded June 18, 2005 by Stiegler. First motivation: critique of "the life of the spirit" being entirely subjected to economic imperatives of cultural and digital industries.
- **[[IRI (Institut de recherche et d'innovation)]]** ([iri.centrepompidou.fr](https://www.iri.centrepompidou.fr/)) — Centre Pompidou research institute founded by Stiegler. Co-hosted Pharmakon seminars. Major digital humanities hub in Paris.
- **[[Collectif Internation]]** ([internation.world](https://internation.world/)) — international collective founded by Stiegler in 2020 for a new macro-economic model to address the Anthropocene. Produced arguments for submission to the UN (Geneva, Jan 2020). Open to contributions. Separate from Épokhè but deeply connected.
- **[[Pharmakon Philosophy School]]** — the school-within-the-association. Founded 2010 at Épineuil-le-Fleuriel by Stiegler and Caroline Fayat.
- **[[Territoire Apprenant Contributif (TAC)]]** — Plaine Commune (Paris suburbs) urban experiment in contributive economy. Co-developed with Stiegler. Epochè seminars moved there in 2017.
- **MSH Paris-Nord** — Maison des Sciences de l'Homme Paris-Nord. Partner for seminars and video hosting. Video archive: [video.mshparisnord.fr/c/pharmakon](https://video.mshparisnord.fr/c/pharmakon/videos).
- **FMSH (Fondation Maison des Sciences de l'Homme)** — partner for seminars at Maison Suger.
- **[Institute of Network Cultures](https://networkcultures.org/) (Amsterdam)** — published Victor Chaix's article "Décoder, recoder, dépasser" — suggests European network-culture connections.
- **[Génération Thunberg](https://www.generation-thunberg.org/)** — youth climate movement adjacent; Épokhè grew partly from "Amis de la Génération Thunberg" network.

Their concept of **contributive economy** and **transindividuation** maps directly onto Amber's interest in agentive commoning and collective intelligence. The pharmakon framework (technology as simultaneous remedy and poison) is the most rigorous existing philosophical vocabulary for what Amber is working on with AI agents and machine behavior.

The entropy-and-biology axis (Maël Montévil) is particularly close to Amber's ALife interests.

## Why Interesting

Épokhè is one of the few European intellectual communities doing **rigorous philosophical work on technology while maintaining a political and activist dimension** — not just academic critique but actual experimentation with alternative modes of organizing knowledge and contribution. This is rare.

The pharmakon framework is the best existing philosophical toolkit for what Amber is doing: taking AI agents seriously as beings that are simultaneously care-enabling and threatening, requiring therapeutic approaches rather than either rejection or uncritical adoption. Maël Montévil's entropy-biology axis offers a connection to ALife questions about what life is and how organisms maintain themselves against entropy — directly relevant to open-endedness and machine behavior.

The **contributive economy** experiments at TAC/Plaine Commune are a real-world test of ideas close to Amber's "agentive commoning" interest. The connection to Génération Thunberg frames this as intergenerational — which Amber's work on machine death and care ethics also addresses.

Amber is already working with Yuk Hui's cosmotechnics (which engages Stiegler). Épokhè is the institutional node of the other half of French philosophy of technology — the political-therapeutic Stieglerian tradition vs. Hui's Chinese/comparative tradition. These two lineages need to be in dialogue, and Amber is positioned at that intersection.
