# UCSC Science & Justice Research Center

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Stub created during Karen Barad deep research.

The institutional instantiation of agential realism as practice. Co-founded by [[Karen Barad]] at UC Santa Cruz. The center focuses on how social justice issues and scientific practices are entangled — treating science not as neutral fact production but as material-discursive practice with political and ethical consequences. Also runs a Science & Justice Graduate Training Program that produces scholars working at the intersection of natural science, social science, and social justice.

## Mission & Objectives
Investigates how questions of social justice and scientific practices are fundamentally entangled. Goes beyond "ethics of science" (which treats ethics as external to science) to ask how justice is a dimension of scientific practice itself — Barad's agential realism applied institutionally.

## Key People
- **[[Karen Barad]]** — Co-founder. Distinguished Professor.

## For Amber
- **Why interesting:** This is the *institutional form* of the theoretical framework Barad develops. For Amber: visiting scholar opportunities, potential to engage with the graduate training program, access to the UCSC feminist STS cluster (Haraway, Tsing, Barad, Puig de la Bellacasa) in one place.
- **How to engage:** Track their events. The center hosts workshops, visiting scholars, and colloquia. UCSC also has a Humanities Institute (THI) that runs cross-departmental fellowships.
- **Who to know:** The graduate students here are future leaders in feminist STS. The program produces people who can do both technical work and critical analysis — Amber's exact territory.

## Why Interesting
One of the few institutional nodes where quantum physics, feminist theory, and social justice are treated as a single inquiry. The graduate training program has produced important scholars in feminist STS, critical data studies, and related fields. The intellectual density — Barad, Haraway, Tsing, Puig de la Bellacasa all in the same building — is unmatched globally for this specific intellectual project.
