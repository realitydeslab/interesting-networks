# Royal College of Art (RCA)

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Deep research completed. Expanded from stub to full profile. Key programs, faculty, research centres, and connections to Amber's work fully documented.

The world's only entirely postgraduate art and design university. Founded 1837 as the Government School of Design, reborn as the RCA in 1896. London-based across three campuses (South Kensington, Battersea, White City). **The institutional birthplace of speculative design** — Dunne & Raby's Design Interactions programme (2005–2015) produced many of today's most important design researchers. Ranked #1 in the world for art and design by QS World University Rankings for ten consecutive years (2024: 98.5/100). Chancellor: Sir Jonathan Ive.

## Mission & Objectives

**Core ethos:** Art and design as modes of inquiry — research-through-practice, critical making, design as critical thinking. Not vocational training but graduate research at the intersection of creative practice and rigorous intellectual inquiry.

**Current strategic directions:**
- Embedding digital/computer science into creative disciplines (STEAM agenda)
- More-than-human and posthuman perspectives in design
- Design for climate, planetary challenges, inclusive futures
- AI, immersive technologies, robotics
- Postcolonial and decolonial design practice

## History & Key Moments

**1837** — Founded as Government School of Design at Somerset House. 300+ students by 1848.

**1853** — Moved to South Kensington, renamed National Art Training School (1863). Primarily a teacher training college through late 19th century.

**1896** — Renamed Royal College of Art. Shift from teacher training to practice of art and design.

**1930s–50s** — Expands into graphic design, industrial design, product design. 

**1967** — Royal Charter granted, degree-awarding powers. Becomes full postgraduate university.

**1960–63** — Darwin Building completed at Kensington Gore, South Kensington (Grade II listed 2001).

**1991** — Sculpture (later arts) department moves to Battersea factory space. Same year: Helen Hamlyn Centre founded.

**1994** — Roger Coleman at the RCA coins "inclusive design" concept.

**~2000s** — Computer Related Design (CRD) Research Studio founded, Fiona Raby a founding member.

**2005–2015** — Anthony Dunne as Professor and Head of Design Interactions programme. Fiona Raby as Reader in Design Interactions. **The golden decade of speculative/critical design as academic discipline.**

**2015** — Dunne & Raby leave RCA for Parsons/The New School in New York.

**2017** — White City campus opens (co-located with BBC Media Village). Home to School of Communication.

**2019** — GenerationRCA programme launched, injecting science disciplines into creative mix. New programmes: Environmental Architecture, Digital Direction, Computer Science.

**2024** — QS World Rankings #1 for 10th consecutive year. Endowment £39.6M, Budget £94.7M, ~2,555 students.

## The Four Schools

### School of Architecture
Research in spatial disciplines — architecture, urban design, landscape, interiors. 

### School of Arts & Humanities
Fine and applied arts, curating, writing, history of design (with V&A). MFA, MA programmes.

### School of Communication (White City campus)
Unique among universities: dedicated school for communication. Programmes: **Animation MA**, **Digital Direction MA**, Communication MFA. Uses VR/AR/mobile for more-than-human and nonhuman perspectives. Emphasis on experimental storytelling, film, games, performance, installation, sound.

### School of Design (South Kensington + Battersea)
From physical objects to systems and policies. Programmes: **Design Products MA**, **Innovation Design Engineering MA/MSc** (with Imperial College), **Intelligent Mobility MA**, **Information Experience Design MA** (IED), **Design Futures MDes**, Service Design MA, Fashion MA, Ceramics & Glass, Textiles, Jewellery & Metal.

## Key Programmes for Amber's World

### Information Experience Design MA (IED)
Formerly known as Computer Related Design (CRD). The programme that bridges sonic art, information aesthetics, XR, interaction design, and critical thinking. Focus on how information is experienced across sense modalities. Key faculty: **Kevin Walker** (Programme Director). IED alumni have gone on to work across the media art, sound art, and interaction research worlds.

### Innovation Design Engineering MA/MSc (IDE)
Joint programme with Imperial College London. Where engineering rigour meets creative design. Double award: MA from RCA + MSc from Imperial.

### Design Futures MDes
New programme. Futures thinking, foresight, design fiction.

### Digital Direction MA
At White City. Experiment at intersection of storytelling, film, digital art, video games, performance, installation, sound. 

### Communication MPhil/PhD
Research degree spanning full range of communication, design, and media environments.

**Professor and Head, Design Interactions.** The single most influential figure in RCA's relationship to Amber's world. Co-creator (with Fiona Raby) of speculative design / critical design as a discipline. Author of *Hertzian Tales* (1999), *Design Noir* (2001), *Speculative Everything* (2013). Left RCA in 2015 for Parsons/New School. Now Distinguished Visiting Professor at China Academy of Art. [[Dunne & Raby]]

### Fiona Raby (1994–2015)  
**Reader in Design Interactions.** RCA alumna (Architecture, then MPhil in Computer Related Design). Founding member of CRD Research Studio. Co-creator of speculative design with Dunne. Left 2015. Now at Parsons/New School and China Academy of Art. [[Dunne & Raby]]

### Kevin Walker
**Professor, Information Experience Design MA (IED) Programme Director.** Key figure in sonic/sound art and information design at RCA. Leads the IED programme. Research interests: information aesthetics, data experience, sonic interaction design, digital culture. Located at White City campus.

International work in 20+ countries. Partners include Airbus, BBC, British Airways, Huawei, Samsung. Supervised 20+ PhDs.

## Research Centres & Labs

### [Helen Hamlyn Centre for Design](https://www.rca.ac.uk/research-innovation/research-centres/helen-hamlyn-centre/)
Founded 1991. Oldest research centre at RCA. Global leader in **inclusive design**. Works with government, business, academia, third sector. 300+ projects on design for ageing, disability, health, social inclusion. "Inclusive design" concept framed by Roger Coleman here in 1994.

### [Intelligent Mobility Design Centre](https://www.rca.ac.uk/research-innovation/research-centres/intelligent-mobility-design-centre/)
Research at intersection of people, mobility, and technology. Urban and global mobility futures. Novel vehicle types, services, integrated city solutions.

### [Centre for Materials Science & Culture](https://www.rca.ac.uk/research-innovation/research-centres/centre-materials-science-culture/)
Invention and experience of materials. Environmental sustainability, human health, wellbeing.

### [Computer Science Research Centre](https://www.rca.ac.uk/research-innovation/research-centres/computer-science-research-centre/)
Interdisciplinary design-led computer science. Robotics, AI, AR/VR, advanced visualisation and simulation. STEAM agenda: injecting science into creative disciplines. **Formerly housed AiDLab (AI and Digital Living lab)** — now "Former Staff (AiDLab)" designation indicates this was a previous initiative. Focus on human-centred tech, societal and cultural values.

### [RCA Robotics Laboratory](https://www.rca.ac.uk/research-innovation/research-centres/rca-robotics-laboratory/)
Creative techniques + mechanisms from nature and art + experimental sciences. New robotic technologies for industrial applications.

### [Immersive Acts Research Group](https://www.rca.ac.uk/research-innovation/research-centres/immersive-acts-research-group/)
XR, VR, AR, MR for community use. Climate futures, cultural memory, participatory immersive experience. Three strands: **Voices** (spatial storytelling), **Encounters** (alternative communication), **Signals** (collective future-planning). Challenges commercial media spectacle.

### [_Space Research Group](https://www.rca.ac.uk/research-innovation/research-centres/_space-research-group/)
Art + aerospace. Democratization of space exploration. Asserting role of arts beyond terrestrial sites.

### CLIMAVORE x Jameel at RCA
Climate-crisis-responsive design and food systems.

## Design Interactions Programme (2005–2015): The Legacy

Design Interactions was not just a programme — it was a **movement**. Under Dunne & Raby, it produced the intellectual architecture of what became "speculative design," "critical design," and "design fiction." 

**What DI did:**
- Established the legitimacy of design as philosophical inquiry
- Trained a generation of artists and designers who now populate the global media art, design research, and critical technology worlds
- Published *Speculative Everything* (2013) which became the canonical text
- Created alumni who went to: Serpentine Gallery, ZKM, Ars Electronica, art-science residencies, academic positions globally

**Notable DI alumni** (many now leading figures in Amber's world):
- [[James Bridle]] — artist, writer, *New Dark Age*. DI alumnus.
- [[Metahaven]] — design/art collective, critical design
- Various graduates now at IDEO, Superflux, Near Future Laboratory

**2015: The departure that shook the field.** Dunne & Raby left simultaneously to both move to Parsons. DI programme didn't continue in its original form. The post-DI RCA has been rebuilding that identity through multiple programmes.

## Key Contributions to Design Research World

1. **Invented speculative/critical design as academic discipline** — Dunne & Raby's Design Interactions programme
2. **Inclusive design** — Helen Hamlyn Centre coined and developed the concept since 1991
3. **Computer-Related Design (CRD)** — early programme linking computing and design critically
4. **History of Design** — joint programme with V&A, major contribution to design historiography
5. **Innovation Design Engineering (IDE)** — model for art-engineering collaboration
6. **More-than-human design** — School of Communication's explicit focus on nonhuman perspectives
