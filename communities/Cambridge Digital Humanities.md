An interdisciplinary research centre at the University of Cambridge, established in 2017. Homed in the School of Arts and Humanities, shared with the School of Humanities and Social Sciences, hosted administratively by [CRASSH](http://www.crassh.cam.ac.uk/). Located at **16 Mill Lane, Cambridge CB2 1RX** — walking distance from ERA's Cambridge base. CDH is one of the most vibrant and critically engaged DH centres in the UK, with a strong emphasis on critical theory, social justice, algorithmic politics, and creative practice alongside technical methods.

**What makes CDH distinctive:** This is NOT a "tools and techniques" centre. CDH defines Digital Humanities expansively — encompassing critical media theorisation, algorithmic justice, automation anxiety, digital media arts, science fiction and futures, maker-spaces, and the social/cultural implications of AI. The critical lens aligns strongly with Amber's work on agent ethology and trustworthy agentic web.

## Mission & Objectives

CDH defines Digital Humanities as encompassing:
- Cultural, social, and epistemic impacts of digital technologies (data, everyday life, literary/cultural forms, medium studies)
- Issues of technocratic rationality, power, and justice
- Digital research methodologies and computational humanities
- Digital libraries and archival-based research
- Digital performance and exhibition (including cultural institutions)
- Research on emerging technologies — notably AI, face recognition, automatic writing, social/cultural implications of robotics

CDH collaborates across the University — with [Cambridge University Library](http://www.lib.cam.ac.uk/), [Cambridge University Press](https://www.cambridge.org/), the [University's museums](https://www.museums.cam.ac.uk/), and multiple faculties. International partnerships in Europe and the US, with a stated priority to develop deeper Global South links.

The DH research community at Cambridge extends across **four of the University's six Schools**, along with the UL, Fitzwilliam Museum, and Museum of Archaeology and Anthropology.
