# Urbanomic

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-03-12:** Deep research completed. Expanded from stub to full profile with complete catalogue, Collapse journal volumes, events history, intellectual lineage, and Amber relevance analysis.
- **2026-03-04:** Stub created during Miguel Abreu Gallery deep research.

A UK-based publisher and arts organization headquartered in The Old Lemonade Factory, Falmouth, Cornwall. Founded and directed by [[Robin Mackay]], Urbanomic is the central node of the speculative philosophy / accelerationism / xenofeminism / cosmotechnics publishing ecosystem. It operates at the intersection of philosophy, art, and science, proposing what Mackay calls an "interrupted relay" in which thinkers offer conceptual resources for reflection on artists' practice, and artists develop and synthesise them in unforeseen ways.

Urbanomic is the intellectual home of the theoretical lineage that feeds directly into Amber's research — from speculative realism through accelerationism to cosmotechnics, from posthumanism to more-than-human design and machine behavior.

## Mission & Objectives

From their [About page](https://www.urbanomic.com/about/):

- **Advocate for philosophy as creative practice** — insisting on the continuing cultural importance of philosophical thinking beyond academia
- **Support interdisciplinary research** — addressing crucial issues that don't fall under any single discipline
- **Present research publicly** — through publications, events, and varied media
- **Engender interdisciplinary production** — creating productive collisions between philosophy, science, and art

**Core Question:** "What space is left for new, exploratory thinking that reaches beyond the confines of professional disciplines?" Urbanomic contends that philosophy has retreated into academic isolation, the sciences have become ever more specialized, and the arts — while providing space for philosophical questions — rarely pursue them explicitly and purposefully.

## Key Contributions

### Intellectual Lineage: Speculative Realism → Accelerationism → Cosmotechnics

Urbanomic was instrumental in catalysing and disseminating several major philosophical movements:

1. **Speculative Realism** (2007–): Collapse II was the first publication to explore speculative realism. Collapse III published the Goldsmiths workshop transcript ([[Ray Brassier]], [[Iain Hamilton Grant]], [[Graham Harman]], [[Quentin Meillassoux]]). This single editorial decision effectively launched an international philosophical movement.

2. **Accelerationism** (2014–): *#Accelerate* (ed. Mackay & Avanessian) provided the definitive genealogy — from Marx's Prometheanism through Deleuze/Guattari, 90s UK cyberculture (CCRU, [[Nick Land]], Sadie Plant), to the left-accelerationism of [[Nick Srnicek]] & [[Alex Williams]]. Urbanomic also published CCRU's collected writings.

3. **Cosmotechnics** (2016–): Publication of [[Yuk Hui]]'s *The Question Concerning Technology in China* introduced the concept of cosmotechnics to Anglophone philosophy — arguing that technology is not universal but culturally-specific, tied to different cosmological traditions. This directly feeds Amber's research on more-than-human design and machine behavior across cultural contexts.

4. **Xenofeminism / Post-Accelerationism** (2018–): Through [[Amy Ireland]], Maya B. Kronic, and the *Cute Accelerationism* project, Urbanomic published the playful, gender-swarming evolution of accelerationist thought.

5. **Cross-Cultural AI Discourse** (2020s): *Machine Decision Is Not Final* (ed. Bratton, Konior, Greenspan, Ireland) — a uniquely-positioned volume on AI through China as a touchstone, exploring cultural difference in intelligence, 人工智能 (rengong zhineng), and non-Western AI imaginaries. Contributors include [[Bogna Konior]], [[Lawrence Lek]], Xia Jia, Chen Qiufan.

### The CCRU Connection

Urbanomic has a deep lineage connection to the **Cybernetic Culture Research Unit** (CCRU, University of Warwick, 1990s), the legendary theory-fiction collective co-founded by [[Nick Land]] and Sadie Plant. Robin Mackay was a CCRU participant. Urbanomic published *CCRU Recursed* (comprehensive archive), *Writings 1997–2003*, and *Digital Hyperstition*, preserving and extending the CCRU legacy. The ***collapse* zine (1995–1996, ed. Mackay with Robert O'Toole) predates the Collapse journal and connects directly to the CCRU milieu.
