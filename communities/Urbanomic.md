# Urbanomic

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Deep research completed. Expanded from stub to full profile. Full Collapse journal catalogued, book catalog documented, key authors mapped, Sequence Press partnership detailed, submission policy confirmed, based: Falmouth confirmed.

**The premier English-language publisher of speculative philosophy, accelerationist theory, and art-science crossover thought.** Founded by Robin Mackay in Falmouth, Cornwall, the press operates as both publisher and arts organization — an "interrupted relay" between philosophers, artists, and scientists that refuses to subordinate philosophical inquiry to academic norms. Incubated speculative realism as a movement, published the CCRU archive, and now anchors the global network around contemporary accelerationism, cosmotechnics, and posthuman thought.

## Mission & Objectives

Urbanomic's founding rationale: philosophy has retreated into academic isolation; the sciences have become inaccessible; the arts air philosophical questions without pursuing them rigorously. Urbanomic proposes an **"interrupted relay"** model: thinkers offer conceptual resources to artists, artists synthesize them in unforeseen ways, stimulating an unpredictable cycle of "research and development" not subordinated to academic norms.

**Core aims:**
- Advocate for philosophical thinking as creative practice
- Support research that crosses disciplines — no single field owns the question
- Present research results to the public in varied media
- Engender interdisciplinary thinking and production

**Legal structure:** Urbanomic Media Ltd, a limited company incorporated in England and Wales (Company No. 8928468). Registered Office: 1, The Old Lemonade Factory, Windsor Quarry, Falmouth TR11 3EX, United Kingdom.

## History

- **c.2005–2006:** Robin Mackay founds Urbanomic in Falmouth, Cornwall. First major output: *Collapse* Vol. I (Number). The journal is informal, limited edition, circulating among a small network.
- **2007:** *Collapse* Vol. II publishes the first sustained engagement with **speculative realism** as a named movement — the most important theoretical intervention in contemporary continental philosophy. The accompanying Speculative Realism Workshop at Goldsmiths (27 Apr 2007) features Brassier, Grant, Harman, Meillassoux.
- **2007–2008:** *Collapse* Vols. III and IV extend the journal's scope into Deleuzian studies and "concept horror" (Lovecraft, weird fiction).
- **2009–2014:** *Collapse* Vols. V–VIII published. Topics: Copernican deanthropomorphization, geophilosophy, culinary materialism, risk/contingency. Simultaneously, Urbanomic begins publishing standalone books (Mono series, Redactions series).
- **2011:** *Fanged Noumena: Collected Writings 1987–2007* — Nick Land's collected essays, edited by Mackay and Brassier — becomes the definitive text of accelerationist theory. Republishes long-unavailable and previously unpublished texts.
- **2012:** Co-publishing partnership established with **[[Sequence Press]]** (New York City) for major philosophical monographs. First major co-publication: Meillassoux's *The Number and the Siren*.
- **2014:** *#Accelerate: The Accelerationist Reader* — landmark anthology, edited by Mackay and Armen Avanessian, that names the accelerationist movement and traces its genealogy from Marx through CCRU to Srnicek/Williams.
- **2015:** *CCRU Writings 1997–2003* published by Urbanomic/Time Spiral Press — definitive CCRU archive.
- **2018:** *Intelligence and Spirit* by Reza Negarestani (Urbanomic/Sequence Press) — a 700-page fusion of German Idealism and Artificial General Intelligence theory. Negarestani's major work.
- **2020s:** Expanded into cosmotechnics (Yuk Hui), posthuman feminism (Amy Ireland, Maya B. Kronic), art-theory (Éric Alliez, Florian Hecker), AI discourse (Machine Decision is Not Final). Global readership across philosophy, art, and speculative fiction communities.

## Key Contributions

**Speculative Realism as movement:** Collapse II (2007) + the Goldsmiths workshop transcript in Collapse III (2007) effectively launched speculative realism as a named philosophical movement — bringing together Brassier, Grant, Harman, and Meillassoux in the same publication space for the first time.

**Accelerationism as genre/theory:** *#Accelerate* (2014) named and canonized the accelerationist genealogy. *Fanged Noumena* made Nick Land accessible. The combined effect established Urbanomic as the archive of the accelerationist tradition.

**CCRU archive:** Published the only comprehensive collection of CCRU writings, preserving texts that had circulated only as photocopies and email attachments.

**Cosmotechnics:** Yuk Hui's Urbanomic books (*The Question Concerning Technology in China*, *Post-Europe*) established cosmotechnics as a recognized field of philosophy of technology.

**Theory-fiction genre:** Urbanomic consistently publishes at the border of fiction and philosophy, extending the CCRU theory-fiction mode into contemporary practice.

**AI philosophy:** *Intelligence and Spirit* (Negarestani, 2018) and *Machine Decision is Not Final* (2025) represent among the most philosophically sophisticated treatments of AI available in English-language publishing.
