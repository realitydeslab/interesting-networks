# Center for Human-Compatible AI (CHAI)

AI safety research center at UC Berkeley, founded by [[Stuart Russell]]. Focus on inverse reinforcement learning (IRL) and the "assistance game" framework — AI systems that are uncertain about human preferences and defer to humans to resolve that uncertainty. Complementary to [[Center for AI Safety (CAIS)]]'s empirical/benchmark approach. Key affiliated researchers include Dylan Hadfield-Menell (MIT), Anca Dragan (Berkeley), and others.
