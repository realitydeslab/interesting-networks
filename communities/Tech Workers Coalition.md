# Tech Workers Coalition

Grassroots labor organization for tech industry workers. Founded 2014 in Silicon Valley. Grew significantly after the [2018 Google Walkout](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2018_Google_walkouts) (which [[Meredith Whittaker]] co-organized). Operates as a non-union workers' organization with chapters across major tech cities including San Francisco, New York, Seattle, London, and elsewhere. Focuses on collective action, worker power, and holding tech companies accountable for ethical and labor violations.

## Mission & Objectives
Unite all workers in the tech industry under a common banner: contractors, temps, vendors, and full-time employees. Fight for worker power at every company, not just through traditional union organizing. Focus on ethics in tech — using collective action to prevent tech's use in surveillance, military applications, immigration enforcement, and discriminatory systems.

## Key Events
- **[2018 Google Walkout](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2018_Google_walkouts)** — over 20,000 Google employees globally walked out to protest sexual harassment culture; co-organized by [[Meredith Whittaker]], Celie O'Neil-Hart and others. Key catalyst for tech labor organizing. Led to Google not renewing Project Maven military AI contract.
- **Project Maven protests** — Google workers petitioned against AI work for the US military drone program.
- **AI ethics council protests** — workers demanded removal of Heritage Foundation's Kay Coles James from Google's external AI ethics board.
