# F.A.T. Lab (Free Art and Technology Lab)

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Created stub note via [[Kyle McDonald]] deep research.

Guerrilla art/technology collective. Known for legally dubious, politically pointed, technically sophisticated interventions in culture and technology. Founded ~2007. The collective's motto: "making things for maximum public effect." Operates from the tradition of tactical media, culture jamming, and free software. Known for releasing open-source projects, provocations, and events.

## Key People
- **[[Kyle McDonald]]** — member. *People Staring at Computers*, *Going Public*, *subpixel*, *ScratchML*
- **[[Zach Lieberman]]** — member
- **Theo Watson** — member
- **James Powderly** — co-founder; also of the EyeWriter project

## Key Works
- **EyeWriter** — DIY eye-tracking for graffiti artists with paralysis
- **Kyle McDonald's *People Staring at Computers*** — hacked Apple Store webcams; Secret Service raid
- **F.A.T. Gold** — retrospective at Eyebeam, NYC (2013)
- Multiple browser extensions, physical interventions, social media hacks

## Why Interesting
FAT Lab represents a tradition of **tactical media** that uses technical skill for political/cultural intervention, with humor and provocation rather than earnest critique. Their methodology — build working systems that expose power — influenced a generation. The collective's willingness to accept legal risk for artistic effect is distinctive.
