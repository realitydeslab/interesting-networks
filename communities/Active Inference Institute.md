# Active Inference Institute

Open-science non-profit hub for the Active Inference (AI) and Free Energy Principle (FEP) community. Radically open and interdisciplinary — "no financial barriers, no required background." Quarterly Roundtables, an annual Applied Symposium, multiple learning streams, internships, fellowships, and a sprawling Discord/YouTube ecosystem. The closest thing to a home institution for anyone working at the intersection of cognition, life, agency, and AI from a first-principles perspective.

## Mission & Objectives

The Active Inference Institute aims to make the Active Inference framework — and the broader Free Energy Principle — **accessible, rigorous, and applicable** across every domain of inquiry: neuroscience, AI, biology, philosophy, social science, economics, ecology, and more.

Founded as "Team Comm" in 2020 and formally incorporated as a nonprofit in Delaware (2022), the Institute positions itself as a **facilitating organization** rather than a primary research producer — it builds the ecosystem infrastructure (courses, journals, events, tools, communities) that makes research and application of active inference possible for a global, interdisciplinary audience.

**Three-scale strategy:**
1. **Participant scale** — personalized educational pathways (internships, textbook groups, fellowships, mentorships)
2. **Institute scale** — organizational learning, grant-seeking, event hosting, publications, open-source tools
3. **Ecosystem scale** — public video content, ontology engineering, open access journal, cross-community partnerships

**Organizational units:**
- **Administrative** — operations, governance, finance
- **EduActive (Education)** — courses, textbook groups, internships, learning streams
- **ReInference (Research)** — publications, symposia, active inference ontology, open-source software

**Core commitments:** Open science, open access, no paywalls, all backgrounds welcome.

## Why Interesting

Active Inference / Free Energy Principle is arguably the most ambitious unified theory in cognitive/life science: one mathematical framework — variational free energy minimization — proposed to explain perception, action, learning, evolution, morphogenesis, sociality, and even quantum mechanics. Whether or not it succeeds in this ambition, the *research community* it has generated is extraordinary: mathematicians, philosophers, neuroscientists, AI researchers, biologists, artists, organizational theorists — all trying to speak the same formal language.

The Active Inference Institute is that community's **operational center**: radically open, genuinely interdisciplinary, and committed to open science. For Amber, the relevance is threefold:
1. **Conceptual** — active inference gives formal language for embodied, ecological, multi-agent behavior — exactly what Amber's work addresses.
2. **Community** — a genuinely open community without gatekeeping, where Amber's art/design-theory perspective would be welcome, not marginal.
3. **Strategic** — the field is on the **edge of chaos**: fast-evolving, not yet mainstream, but increasingly influential. Getting in early, publishing in the AII ecosystem, and appearing at the symposium would be high-leverage moves.
