# YCAM — Yamaguchi Center for Arts and Media

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Deep research completed. Expanded from stub to full profile with history, InterLab, BioLab, key commissions, residency/internship programs, key people, and Japan media art context.

Art center in Yamaguchi City, Japan, open since November 2003. One of the world's most technically sophisticated and committed art centers — not a venue that hosts traveling shows, but a **factory of original works** produced through long-term R&D residencies with international artists. Their in-house team (YCAM InterLab) co-creates everything, and releases all tools as open source. The Yamaguchi location is a deliberate signal: not Tokyo, not Osaka — artists who come here are serious. More than 10 million visitors since 2003. Designed by Arata Isozaki.

## Mission & Objectives
**"Creating together, learning together"** — YCAM exists at the intersection of art, media technology, and community development. Three axes:
1. **Art Expression** — production and exhibition of original works incorporating media technology (installation, dance/theatre, film)
2. **Education** — workshops and programs connecting local citizens to media technology and artistic expression
3. **Community** — linking media technology knowledge to regional challenges and community resources in Yamaguchi

**Core philosophy:** Not a collection institution. YCAM does not hold an art collection. Every exhibition is either produced at YCAM or commissioned specifically for it. Research and development (R&D) is the organizing principle — from investigation through experimentation to artwork, workshop programs, hardware/software development, and academic paper publication.

**"Open" and "Collaborative":** These are YCAM's explicit keywords. Every R&D project involves local citizens, external experts, and the InterLab working together. All software and hardware outputs are released as open source.

## History & Founding
**1988:** Ministry of Posts and Telecommunications announces concept for information zone in Yamaguchi City.

**1989:** Fourth Yamaguchi City Comprehensive Plan positions a "Cultural Exchange Plaza" as a key development.

**1993:** Finalization of the Yamaguchi Media and Culture City Basic Plan.

**1996:** 
- Establishment of Yamaguchi City Foundation for Cultural Promotion
- Architectural proposals: agreement with [Arata Isozaki Atelier](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arata_Isozaki) in November 1996

**1996–2001:** Planning process involving extensive public consultation, citizens' review, and 16 local research group sessions.

**2001:** Design finalized; name decided — "Yamaguchi Center for Arts and Media." Construction begins August 2001.

**November 1, 2003:** YCAM opens. Immediately begins producing and commissioning original works. First work: [*Amodal Suspension*](https://www.ycam.jp/en/archive/works/amodal-suspension/) by Rafael Lozano-Hemmer — 20 searchlights above Yamaguchi responding to internet messages sent from 28 art centers in 15 countries worldwide.

**2004:** 1,000,000 visitors reached (within first year).

**2005:** Toru Yamamoto appointed General Director.

**2007:** 
- Akeo Adachi appointed Director
- *LIFE – fluid, invisible, inaudible…* by Ryuichi Sakamoto + Shiro Takatani premieres at YCAM

**2008:** YCAM wins Public Buildings Association's highest Public Buildings Award (Cultural Facilities section, from Ministry of Land, Infrastructure, Transport and Tourism). Studio A selected as one of "Top 100 Excellent Halls" by Sound Engineers and Artists Society of Japan.

**2010:** *Desire of Codes* by Seiko Mikami opens — later wins Grand Prize at Japan Media Arts Festival (2012).

**2011:** Yamaguchi City Foundation for Cultural Promotion becomes a Public Interest Incorporated Foundation.

**2012:** 
- *THE END* — world's first vocaloid opera (Keiichiro Shibuya + Toshiki Okada) premieres at YCAM; tours to Paris Théâtre du Châtelet, Holland Festival, Georgian National Opera, Matadero Madrid
- *TECHTILE* (YCAM + Keio University) wins Good Design Award

**2013:** YCAM 10th Anniversary Program "Art and Environment for Tomorrow" under artistic direction of [Ryuichi Sakamoto](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ryuichi_Sakamoto). ~300,000 visitors over 8 months.

**2014:** 8,000,000 visitors total. *Korogaru Koen* wins Good Design Award + Kids Design Award.

**2018:** Hajime Masuda appointed Director.

**2019:** YCAM BioLab launches — wet biology laboratory integrated into the art center, for art-science experiments using biotechnology, eDNA, molecular biology.

**2020:** Tadayuki Miwa appointed Director. COVID adaptations. YCAM OpenLab 2020 "Understanding Living Culture 2" paper published on future of labs post-COVID.

**2022:** *Unlearning Language* by Lauren Lee McCarthy + Kyle McDonald — experiential performance exploring AI vs. human linguistic capacity. Archive exhibition, talks with Surya Mattu, Taeyoon Choi, Dominique Chen, Danja Vasiliev.

**2024:** ~445,311 visitors (most recent annual figure).

## YCAM InterLab
The beating heart of YCAM. An internal R&D team of approximately 20 resident staff with backgrounds spanning curation, education, engineering, and design. Unlike a typical museum, the InterLab **actively co-creates** works alongside visiting artists — not just production support but genuine creative and technical collaboration.

**Functions:**
1. Research into media technology applications (including deliberate "misusage" and unexpected possibilities)
2. Production of original works and educational programs
3. Publication of R&D results (papers at CHI, SIGGRAPH, TEI, UIST, Ars Electronica proceedings, etc.)
4. Exchange with educational institutions, research institutes, businesses in Japan and abroad
5. Human resource development (internships, training programs)

**Open source philosophy:** All software and hardware developed through YCAM projects is released as open source for free use by third parties.

**Key InterLab Staff (past and present):**
- **Kazunao ABE** — Curator/Artistic Director. Born 1960 Nagano. Studied aesthetics at Tokyo University of Fine Arts. Previously co-curator at [Canon Artlab](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Canon_Inc.) (1990–2001), where he produced landmark interactive art (Teiji Furuhashi's *LOVERS*, Seiko Mikami's *Molecular Informatics*, Knowbotic Research's *IO_DENCIES*, Carsten Nicolai + Marko Peljhan's *polar*). Joined the planning group for YCAM in the late 1990s, became a founding member in 2001. Chief curator and artistic director overseeing all YCAM-curated programs since 2003.
- **Keina KONNO** — Engineer/researcher, appears across multiple YCAM productions
- **Richi OWAKI** — Engineer/researcher (newClear), skinslides (2009 Jury Selection, Japan Media Arts Festival)
- **Akiko TAKESHITA** — Staff researcher
- **Tsubasa NISHI** — Staff

## YCAM BioLab
A wet biology laboratory established within YCAM circa 2019 — rare for an art center. Enables genuine bioart and art-science research using biotechnology, eDNA metabarcoding, and molecular biology.

**Documented projects:**
- **eDNA metabarcoding of water streams in Japanese gardens** (Kyoto) — environmental DNA analysis using biological samples from traditional garden environments (paper published May 2023)
- **"Incomplete Niwa Archives – anechoic chamber version"** (2025) — continues the BioLab's focus on environment and ecological listening
- **DNA of Forests** field guide (2019 paper at iBOL 2019, Trondheim)

**Significance:** The BioLab makes YCAM a node in the bioart world connecting to communities like [[SymbioticA]] and [[Biofilia Base for Biological Arts]], but approached from a community/environmental science angle rather than pure provocation.

## Key Contributions
1. **[RAM Dance Toolkit](https://github.com/YCAMInterlab/RAMDanceToolkit)** (2013) — Open-source toolkit for interactive dance performance using motion capture + visual programming. Co-created with Yoko Ando. Widely used in dance tech worldwide.
2. **[ProCamToolkit](https://github.com/kylemcdonald/ProCamToolkit)** (2012, Kyle McDonald) — Projector-camera calibration tools. Essential open source contribution to creative coding community.
3. **Open-source R&D outputs** — Software and hardware from every major R&D project released for free use. Includes ofxEpilog (openFrameworks addon for laser cutters), body tracking tools, and more.
4. **TECHTILE** — Haptic interface toolkit (with Keio University), Good Design Award 2012.
5. **Korogaru Koen Park** (2012) — Interactive playground exploring media-embedded environments for children. Multiple iterations in Yamaguchi, Sapporo, Tokyo. Kids Design Award (2014), Good Design Award (2014).
6. **Amodal Suspension** (2003) — Landmark participatory work connecting 28 art centers globally on YCAM's opening night. Established YCAM immediately as world-class institution.
7. **THE END** (2012) — World's first vocaloid opera; internationally touring.
8. **BioLab** — Rare example of wet biology laboratory integrated into an art center; eDNA metabarcoding for environmental research.
9. **29 academic papers** published at CHI, SIGGRAPH, TEI, UIST, ACM Digital Library, iBOL and other international conferences/journals. YCAM publishes original research — unusual for an art center.

## Events & Programmes

### Artist Residencies
YCAM hosts international artist residencies — the central mechanism through which original works are produced. Artists live and work at YCAM for extended periods, collaborating with InterLab staff.
- **Format:** Long-term (typically weeks to months), outcome-focused — exhibition + open-source release
- **Who's been:** Kyle McDonald, Lauren Lee McCarthy, Seiko Mikami, Rafael Lozano-Hemmer, Yoko Ando, Keiichiro Shibuya, Fujiko Nakaya, Daito Manabe/Rhizomatiks, and many others

### Internship Programme
[YCAM Internship](https://www.ycam.jp/en/recruit/intern/) — formal 1–3 month internship for graduate students, young artists, researchers, technicians, coders, designers with backgrounds in new media art/performance.
- **Target:** Strong performance in fields related to new media art/performance
- **Language:** English or Japanese required
- **Schedule:** 5 days/week, 10:00–19:00
- **Cost:** Self-funded (no stipend — all expenses by intern)
- **Application:** 3 months advance notice; screening via documents + online interview
- **Apply to:** intern@ycam.jp

### YCAM OpenLab
Regular open lab events bringing researchers from around the world to discuss shared challenges. "Understanding Living Culture" series. Paused/adapted during COVID (2020 paper published in peer-reviewed journal).

### YCAM BioLab Programme
Wet biology lab for artists and researchers using biotechnology. Environmental DNA analysis, eDNA metabarcoding, molecular biology applications in art/ecology contexts.

### Regular Programming
- Exhibitions (Studio A, Studio B)
- Cinema (Studio C, 100 seats) — Bakuon Film Festival (immersive audio-visual)
- Children's workshops
- YCAM Film Factory — film production program
- Public talks, symposia, lectures alongside each major commission

## For Amber

YCAM is the strongest institutional model in the Japanese media art ecosystem — and arguably one of the strongest in the world for **practice-based, R&D-centered art-technology production**. It's not a gallery, museum, or festival — it's a *research laboratory* that happens to produce art, open-source tools, workshops, and papers simultaneously. ## Japan Media Art Scene Context

YCAM occupies a distinct position in the Japanese media art ecosystem — **neither Tokyo-centric nor mainstream**. Key context:

**The Tokyo institutions:**
- **[[NTT InterCommunication Center (ICC)]]** — NTT-funded, Shinjuku. The prestige venue for international media art in Tokyo. YCAM works tour to ICC regularly (*Desire of Codes*, *THE END*, Keiichiro Shibuya).
- **Rhizomatiks/[[Daito Manabe]]** — The commercial-art crossover flagship. Deeply integrated with YCAM through multiple collaborations.
- **Canon Artlab** (1990–2001, now closed) — Predecessor institution; Kazunao ABE came directly from here. Its legacy lives in YCAM's founding vision.
- **[IAMAS](https://www.iamas.ac.jp/)** — Institute of Advanced Media Arts and Sciences, Ogaki (Gifu Prefecture). Graduate school of media arts. YCAM and IAMAS are complementary: IAMAS trains practitioners, YCAM commissions them.
- **[[ATR Media Integration and Communications Research Laboratories]]** — Corporate research lab; intersects with YCAM through media technology research.

**YCAM's unique position:**
- Located in Yamaguchi (not Tokyo) — deliberate self-selection effect
- City-funded but internationally connected — unusual hybrid
- R&D model makes it more like a research institute than gallery
- Open-source philosophy makes it a node in global creative coding ecosystem (openFrameworks community)
- The Japan Media Arts Festival (national competition) regularly recognizes YCAM works — institutional legitimacy in Japanese art system

**International circuits YCAM plugs into:**
- Ars Electronica (Linz) — multiple YCAM works exhibited
- transmediale (Berlin)
- SIGGRAPH, CHI, TEI, UIST — through InterLab papers
- Holland Festival, Bunkamura Orchard Hall, Théâtre du Châtelet — through touring productions
- iBOL (International Barcode of Life Conference) — through BioLab work

## Why Interesting
YCAM is the living argument that **the most rigorous, world-class media art happens outside the major art market cities, when an institution decides to commit fully to production over presentation.** The Yamaguchi location is a strategic choice: it creates a community that self-selects for serious engagement. Artists who come to YCAM come for the work, not the networking scene.

For Amber specifically: YCAM is the clearest model of **research-as-making** in the Japanese art-tech world. The InterLab publishes papers at CHI and SIGGRAPH, builds open-source tools used worldwide, and produces works that tour internationally — all simultaneously, from the same process. This is exactly the kind of institutional model that could inspire or host Amber's practice-based research.

**The BioLab turn** (post-2019) signals YCAM evolving toward exactly the "more-than-human" territory that Amber's research inhabits: environmental DNA, ecological sensing, biological data as artistic and scientific material.

**The 2022 critical computing turn** (*Unlearning Language*, Critical Engineering Working Group) shows YCAM capable of politically engaged programming — not just technical showcase.
