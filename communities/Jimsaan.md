# Jimsaan

Dakar-based literary publishing house, co-founded by [[Felwine Sarr]], [[Boubacar Boris Diop]], and Nafissatou Dia. First major Senegalese-owned literary publisher with international distribution. Founded as an epistemic sovereignty project: giving African writers a home that doesn't go through Parisian gatekeepers.

## Mission
To publish African literature from a Dakar base — not a Paris or Montreal one. To build African publishing infrastructure rather than exporting manuscripts to European houses.

## Key People
- **[[Felwine Sarr]]** — co-founder
- **Boubacar Boris Diop** — co-founder. Author of *Murambi* (Rwanda genocide novel, written in Wolof/Kinyarwanda)
- **Nafissatou Dia** — co-founder

## Why Interesting
Infrastructure as intellectual sovereignty. The model (build your own publisher; don't wait for recognition from elsewhere) is relevant to any researcher thinking about alternative publication venues or community-owned knowledge commons.
