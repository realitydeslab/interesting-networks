# Hyphen-Labs

Afrofuturist, international art-tech collective. Members include Ashley Baccus-Clark, Carmen Aguilar y Wedge, Ece Tankal, Nitzan Bartov, and JB Rubinovitz. Known for **NeuroSpeculative AfroFeminism (NSAF)** — a transmedia exploration of Black women in technology, society, and culture. Uses fashion, cosmetics, and neurocognitive research as entry points to probe privacy, transparency, identity, and perception.

## Why Interesting
Discovered via [[Adam Harvey]] deep research — Hyphen-Labs commissioned HyperFace from Harvey for their NeuroSpeculative AfroFeminism project at Sundance Film Festival 2017. They represent an intersection of Afrofuturism × speculative design × art-science × feminist tech critique that is highly aligned with Amber's taste profile. Operate in the critical computing / feminist technoscience space.

## Key Works
- **NeuroSpeculative AfroFeminism** (2017) — shown at Sundance Film Festival. Includes HyperFace (designed by Adam Harvey), speculative beauty products, and neuroscience experiments exploring Black women's experiences in VR.
