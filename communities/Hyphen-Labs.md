International collective of women of color working at the intersection of technology, art, science, and speculative futures. Founded 2014. Based in New York and Barcelona. Defined by **NeuroSpeculative AfroFeminism (NSAF)** — the signal work of Afrofuturist VR art.

## Mission & Objectives

Hyphen-Labs uses art, technology, and speculative design to explore and critique systems of power — particularly how they affect women of color. They create:
- **Speculative design objects** — products that don't exist yet but should (or shouldn't)
- **Immersive VR experiences** — embodied explorations of race, identity, and perception
- **Neuroscience research** — studying the cognitive/physiological impact of VR representation on bias
- **Large-scale public installations** — commissioned experiences on social issues

Their motto is building a **fluid international network** of women working in fabrication labs, engineering, architecture, and art — deliberately counteracting the monoculture of those spaces.

They serve dual roles: **self-directed artistic practice** + **commissioned experiential design studio** (represented by production company [m ss ng p eces](https://mssngpeces.com/)).

## Key Projects

### 1. NeuroSpeculative AfroFeminism (NSAF) — 2017 ⭐⭐⭐
[hyphen-labs.com/NSAF](https://hyphen-labs.com/NSAF) | [Tribeca 2017 page](https://tribecafilm.com/films/neurospeculative-afrofeminism-2017)

**The defining work.** Three-part digital narrative: speculative product design + VR experience + neuroscience research.

**The conceptual frame:** Set in a future USA after the First Amendment is abolished. Black women form a band of resistance through a neurological technology that collects "communal memories and knowledge." A beauty salon (named Brooks' Salon, after poet Gwendolyn Brooks) as underground rebel network.

**Part 1 — Speculative Products:**
- Sunblock for dark skin tones (multiverse travel ready)
- **ScatterViz visor** — dichroic reflective tech to deflect aggressive gazes from police/aggressors; visor protects wearer while mirroring aggressor's face back at them
- **Earrings with embedded cameras** (in collaboration with Michelle Cortese) — recording micro/macro aggressions subtly
- **HyperFace scarf** (designed by [[Adam Harvey]]) — countersurveillance fabric that confuses facial recognition cameras
- **Octavia Electrodes** — transcranial extensions for brain optimization (named after Octavia Butler)

**Part 2 — VR Experience:**
- You are placed in the body of a Black woman avatar at Brooks' Salon
- Neurocosmetology lab where Black women pioneer brain optimization / neuromodulation
- Octavia Electrodes braided into hair; transcranial stimulation transports you to alternate realm

**Part 3 — Neuroscience Research:**
- Explores neurological and physiological impact of showing images of empowered Black women in VR
- Studies how immersive embodiment in a person of color's avatar reduces implicit bias
- Collaborating with MIT labs: Imagination, Computation and Expression; Synthetic Neurobiology; Center for Civic Media

### 2. Prescribed to Death / "Stop Everyday Killers" — 2018 🏆
[hyphen-labs.com/Everyday-Painkillers](https://hyphen-labs.com/Everyday-Painkillers)

Collaboration with **National Safety Council** + **Energy BBDO** (directed by Tucker Walsh; production via m ss ng p eces).

Public installation: 22,000 pills carved with human faces lining a black wall in a darkly lit room — memorializing those killed by opioid addiction. Every 24 minutes, a new pill is automatically carved.

## Key Ideas & Intellectual Framework

**NeuroSpeculative AfroFeminism** as concept combines:
- **Afrofuturism** (Black cultural production imagining emancipated futures)
- **Speculative design** (design fiction — objects from possible futures)
- **Feminist technoscience** (Donna Haraway tradition — situated knowledge, cyborg politics)
- **Neuroscience** — empirical research angle; VR as tool to study and modify implicit bias
- **Transhumanism** — cognitive enhancement, brain-computer interface, neuromodulation

**The neurocosmetology lab** is a powerful metaphor: the Black hair salon as community hub → retrofitted as a space of radical scientific experimentation → upending who gets to be the pioneer of neurotechnology.

**Counter-surveillance fashion** (ScatterViz, HyperFace) — fashion as armor, privacy as design material, the body as contested site for surveillance technology.

**Designing for "edge cases"** — they explicitly critique how design "edge cases" (people of color, women) are structurally excluded from technology design processes. They make the edge case the center.

**Ancestral trauma + speculative futures** — Ashley Baccus-Clark: "disrupting ancestral trauma by living fully in the future."
