# IMMA (Irish Museum of Modern Art)

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-03-01:** Deep research completed. Full profile written — residency programme, staff directory, key programmes, international partnerships documented.
- **2026-02-26:** Stub created during The Otolith Group deep research.

Ireland's national institution for modern and contemporary art. Located at Royal Hospital Kilmainham, Dublin 8 — a 17th-century baroque building whose original stables are now converted artist studios. Director: **Annie Fletcher** (since March 2019), formerly Chief Curator at Van Abbemuseum, Eindhoven. Under Fletcher, IMMA has pivoted toward politically committed, research-based, ecologically conscious contemporary art — hosting interdisciplinary events like the *Earth Rising* festival and maintaining international residency exchanges.

## Mission & Objectives

IMMA is Ireland's leading national institution for the collection and presentation of modern and contemporary art. Founded 1990 by the Government of Ireland, opened May 25, 1991. Holds the **Irish National Collection** — now 4,500+ artworks emphasizing post-1940 work by Irish and international artists, including Lucian Freud, Marina Abramović, Louise Bourgeois, Joseph Cornell, Robert Rauschenberg, Sol LeWitt, and Lawrence Weiner.

Core mandate:
- **Collection:** maintain and grow the National Collection
- **Exhibitions:** Irish and international contemporary art commissions and touring shows
- **Residency:** onsite artist-in-residence programme (since 1995) using studios in the Royal Hospital grounds
- **Engagement:** pioneering arts/health, dementia-inclusive, and community programmes
- **Research:** interdisciplinary and institutional research partnerships

## Key Programmes & Events

### Residency Programme
[https://imma.ie/residency-programme/](https://imma.ie/residency-programme/)

IMMA's residency programme dates to **1995**. Studios housed in the converted original stables of the Royal Hospital — the historic site itself is a productive context for resident artists. The programme supports both emerging and established practitioners. Two strands:
- **Open Call** — international open calls held regularly; subscribe to mailing list for notifications
- **Invited** — curated invitations aligned with IMMA's own project programming

Residents are encouraged to engage with IMMA's public through studio access, workshops, and events. Past residents listed in [Artist Directory](https://imma.ie/artists/?category=resident) from 2004 onward; archive 1995–2004 available separately.

**International Residency Partners:**
- [Sweetwater Foundation](https://sweetwaterfoundation.com/) & [Hyde Park Art Centre](https://www.hydeparkart.org/) (Chicago, USA)
- [Fremantle Arts Centre](https://www.fac.org.au/) (Australia)

### Earth Rising Festival
[https://imma.ie/whats-on/earth-rising/](https://imma.ie/whats-on/earth-rising/)

Annual festival at IMMA on art, ecology, and planetary crises. Launched **September 2023** (inaugural). Brings together voices from arts, science, academia, design, law, media, research, and education.

**2025 (September 12–14):** Theme — *Staying with the Trouble*, inspired by Donna Haraway's influential text. Contributors included climate filmmakers, biodiversity educators, traditional musicians, animation, and community performers. Programming includes outdoor cinema, biodiversity tours of the Royal Hospital grounds, workshops, and live music.

**Significance:** The Haraway framing places IMMA squarely in the more-than-human / posthuman discourse — a rare move for a national museum. Bridges ecological thinking with aesthetic practice.

### IMMA Horizons (Arts & Ageing / Dementia-Inclusive)
[https://imma.ie/learn-engage/imma-horizons/](https://imma.ie/learn-engage/imma-horizons/)

Engagement programme for older people, pioneered since IMMA's founding in 1991. Notable firsts:
- **2012:** Co-founded the **[Azure Network](https://azurenetwork.ie/)** with Age & Opportunity, Alzheimer Society of Ireland, and Butler Gallery — trains museums nationwide in dementia-inclusive programming
- **2017:** Partnership with Mercers Institute for Successful Ageing (arts/health crossover between hospital and museum contexts)
- **2018/19:** Dedicated fellowship to research and evaluate dementia-inclusive programming
- **2020:** Creative Ireland-funded COVID response programme for older people (online events + printed resources for residential homes)

### Hennessy Art Fund for IMMA Collection
Launched 2016 as response to austerity-era funding cuts (acquisitions budget effectively eliminated 2011–2016). Multi-year corporate partnership with Hennessy to acquire work by emerging/mid-career Irish and Irish-based artists. Acquired works by: Kevin Atherton, David Beattie, Rhona Byrne, Dennis McNulty (2016); Ciarán Murphy, Maireád McClean, Mark Garry, Yuri Pattison, Eimear Walshe (2017+).

**Yuri Pattison** acquisition is notable — Pattison works with digital infrastructure, data centers, and speculative futures. His work in the IMMA collection signals an interest in digital/computational art practices.

### IMMAxDean Artist Collaboration
Partnership with [Dean Art Studios](https://deanartstudios.com/) (Chatham Row, Dublin city center) gifting 4 studios to IMMA. Recent resident includes **Thaís Muniz** (Brazilian artist, 2022). Extends IMMA's residency footprint into Dublin's cultural core.
