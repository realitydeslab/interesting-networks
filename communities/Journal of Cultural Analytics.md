# Journal of Cultural Analytics

The flagship peer-reviewed open-access journal for cultural analytics — computational and quantitative approaches to cultural artifacts. Since 2016, *JCA* has served as the foundational publishing venue for a field at the intersection of the humanities, social sciences, and computer science. Published by the [Center for Digital Humanities, Princeton University](https://cdh.princeton.edu/). ISSN: 2371-4549.

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-03-01:** Initial deep research. Full editorial board, recent issues, special issues, and submission guidelines documented.

## Mission & Objectives

JCA publishes peer-reviewed articles combining **computational insight with grounding in a humanistic field**. Their core editorial stance (as of Vol. 10, 2025):
- Articles must make **thought-provoking, original arguments** about how culture works at larger scales than traditional research
- Theoretical sophistication + methodological innovation are **not enough alone** — must create new knowledge about cultural objects
- Bridge between humanities, computational social sciences, and information sciences — primary audience is in the humanities
- **Open access** + data/code deposit required for peer review

**Aims and Scope questions JCA cares about:**
1. Can computational/quantitative approaches teach us something new about our datafied present?
2. Can we theorize data-driven research and analytic practices?
3. Can humanities scholars play a central role in creating shared standards for computational methods?
4. Can we merge critical data studies, critical archival studies, and computational humanities with traditional humanist methodologies?

## Key Contributions

JCA has been the **central publishing venue** for cultural analytics since 2016:
- Established computational humanities as a field with its own journal
- Enforces open access + data/code deposit — a model for reproducible humanities research
- Regularly bridges literary studies, art history, film studies, music, and social media analysis
- Featured foundational papers in distant reading, sentiment analysis, network analysis of culture

## Events & Programmes

- **Submission types:** Research articles, data essays (publishing datasets + methodology), special issues
- **Review:** Double-anonymous peer review
- **Submission portal:** <https://culturalanalytics.org/submit/start/>
- **Style:** MLA style, Word or LaTeX. Anonymized for double-blind review. No preprint deposit before review (unusual policy — note this!)
- **Newsletter/email list:** <http://eepurl.com/c7hK6n>
- No associated conference, residency, or fellowship programme — purely a publishing venue
