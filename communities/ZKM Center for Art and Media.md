# ZKM | Center for Art and Media Karlsruhe

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Full deep research completed. Leadership transition from Peter Weibel (d. 2023) to Alistair Hudson (since April 2023) is a major turn — from media theory heavyweight to "useful museum" participatory practice. Key 2026 exhibitions: *Silver Egg (The Eros of Media Art)* (July) with 30 international positions on eros/media/ecology; *Connection Machine CM-2* (March) on AI prehistory. Hertzlab residencies ongoing — Giga-Hertz Production Awards €5k each with studio residency; ZKM/IRCAM joint residency.

---

The world's largest media art institution, housed in a former munitions factory (312m, 10 atria) in Karlsruhe, Germany. Founded 1989, opened in current location 1997. Part museum, part production lab, part archive — 12,000+ works in collection, 600+ guest artists, 570+ publications. Under Peter Weibel (1999–2023) it defined the theory of media art; under Alistair Hudson (2023–) it pivots toward "useful art" and social transformation. Europe's essential venue for art-technology-society questions.

---

## Mission & Objectives

Founded by Heinrich Klotz (1989–1999) on a Bauhaus model: art and technology must compete with and enrich each other. The original mission (Klotz 1992): ZKM as a location for all forms of contemporary art, bringing together artists, scholars, and scientists across disciplines; preserving cultural heritage with special focus on digital art conservation; operating at the interface of art and science.

Under **Peter Weibel** (1999–2023), ZKM became the global theoretical center for media art. Weibel edited foundational volumes, curated landmark shows (*Iconoclash*, *Making Things Public*, *BioMedia*), and ran Ars Electronica as artistic director 1986–1995 before ZKM.

Under **Alistair Hudson** (April 2023–present), the turn is toward "useful museum" — art as social infrastructure, participatory processes, art-as-tool-to-change-the-world. The flagship programme is now *Fellow Travellers* (2024–2026), a multi-year platform for art as social transformation. This is a significant ideological shift worth tracking.

Six core research themes of the **Hertzlab** (research/production arm):
1. Human-machine relations (AI, chatbots, automation)
2. Ecology and environment (climate, natural systems)
3. Future narratives (non-European/US storytelling models)
4. Embodied interaction (body, movement, senses)
5. Ethics of technology (power, responsibility, rights)
6. Media archaeology and restoration (preserving digital heritage)

### Key Historical Figures

**[Peter Weibel](https://zkm.de/en/persons/peter-weibel)** ⭐⭐⭐ (1944–2023) — Born Odessa. Studied literature, medicine, logic, philosophy, film in Paris and Vienna. Artistic director of Ars Electronica 1986–1995. Founded Institute of New Media at Städelschule Frankfurt (1989). Professor University of Applied Arts Vienna 1984–2017. ZKM director 1999–2023. Curated: *Iconoclash* (2002), *Making Things Public* (2005), *BioMedia* (2023) among dozens of landmark shows. Published foundational texts on media art theory. Artist of the collection. Recipient: Oskar-Kokoschka-Preis, Austrian Cross of Honor for Science and Art. See: [*Peter Weibel: Art as an Act of Cognition*](https://zkm.de/en/publications/peter-weibel-art-as-an-act-of-cognition) (ZKM, 2024) — posthumous monograph.

**[Heinrich Klotz](https://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heinrich_Klotz)** (1935–1999) — Founding director. Architecture historian. Founded ZKM's original Bauhaus-inspired vision. Died in office; Weibel succeeded him.

## Key Publications & Output

ZKM has published **570+ publications** since 1989. Many are landmark texts in media art history.

### Essential Texts for Amber:
- **[*BioMedia: The Age of Media with Life-like Behavior*](https://zkm.de/en/publications/biomedia)** (Weibel, ed.; Spector Books, 2023) — defines "biomimetic media" — art made with inorganic material exhibiting organic features. 60+ artists. Essays by Weibel, Samuel Bianchini, Emanuele Quinz, Ingeborg Reichle. **Essential for Amber** — connects media art + alife + posthuman.
- **[*Fellow Travellers: Art as a Tool to Change the World*](https://zkm.de/en/publications/fellow-travellers-art-as-a-tool-to-change-the-world)** (3 vols., 2025; ed. Alistair Hudson with Daniel Pies) — the manifesto of the Hudson era. Vol. 1: A User's Manual; Vol. 2: Fellowship & Solidarity; Vol. 3: Assembling Grounds / Practices of Coexistence.
- **[*Beyond Matter, Within Space*](https://zkm.de/en/publications/beyond-matter-within-space)** (2023) — curatorial techniques for virtual reality / spatial art. Highly relevant for practice.
- **[*Christa Sommerer & Laurent Mignonneau: The Artwork as a Living System*](https://zkm.de/en/publications/christa-sommerer-laurent-mignonneau)** (2022) — 30 years of artificial life art.
- **[*Writing the History of the Future*](https://zkm.de/en/publications/writing-the-history-of-the-future)** (2022) — singular ZKM media art collection catalog.
- **[*Peter Weibel: Art as an Act of Cognition*](https://zkm.de/en/publications/peter-weibel-art-as-an-act-of-cognition)** (ZKM, 2024) — posthumous monograph on Weibel's theory.
- ***Enzyklopädie der Medien*** (ongoing, ZKM): Band 5 (2023): Politik und Medien; Band 6 (2024): Theorie und Medien — monumental German-language media encyclopedia.
- **[*Iconoclash: Beyond Image Wars in Science, Religion and Art*](https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262523127/)** (MIT Press, 2002; Latour & Weibel, eds.) — landmark. Connects science studies, religion, art — Bruno Latour × Weibel co-edited.
- **[*Making Things Public: Atmospheres of Democracy*](https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262122795/)** (MIT Press, 2005; Latour & Weibel, eds.) — another landmark. Democracy × public space × art.

## Key Contributions

What ZKM has given the world:
1. **Defined the field of media art** — gave it institutional legitimacy, collection, archive, theory
2. **Largest media art collection** — ~12,000 works, largest computer-based art collection globally
3. **Pioneer in digital preservation** — Laboratory for Antiquated Video Systems; rescued degrading collections across Europe and US
4. **International Digital Electro-acoustic Music Archive (IDEAMA)** — unique global resource
5. **BioMedia concept** — Weibel coined the term; defined next paradigm beyond video/interactive art
6. **Public access** — free admission to many exhibitions, educational programming, open labs
7. **Artists at Risk programme** — residencies for artists from conflict zones (partnered with Artists at Risk, Goethe-Institut, German Foreign Office)
8. **570+ publications** — many are defining texts; ZKM books co-published with MIT Press, Spector Books, major academic publishers

## Why Interesting

ZKM is the **institutional anchor** for the entire European media art ecosystem. It's where the theory was built (Weibel + Latour collab = *Iconoclash*, *Making Things Public*), where artists were produced (600+ residencies), and where the debate about what media art IS has been fought.

For Amber specifically:

**BioMedia thesis** — Weibel's concept that 21st century media will exhibit life-like behavior (not Bio Art but biomimetic media) is the most useful theoretical frame ZKM has generated for Amber's interests. The exhibition showed: Christa Sommerer & Laurent Mignonneau (alife), adaptive robots, generative AI art — all in one frame. This is Amber's intersection.

**Posthuman tradition** — ZKM's collection includes Stelarc, Lynn Hershman Leeson, Jeffrey Shaw — the core posthuman art canon. Being in institutional dialogue with ZKM means being in dialogue with this lineage.

**Computational creativity** — ZKM has always treated computation as an art medium, not just a tool. The early computer art (Waldemar Cordeiro, Vera Molnár) through to current AI installations forms a continuous genealogy that ZKM maintains.

**Critical computing** — Weibel's work on media critique (how media shapes perception, politics, power) is a direct precursor to Amber's critical computing thread.

**Art-science** — ZKM actively runs joint projects with universities (KIT, HfG), archives scientific instruments (Connection Machine), and frames art as epistemological research.

**The eros angle** — The *Silver Egg* exhibition (July 2026) is remarkable: it explicitly connects Eros to media art, mobilizing Bataille, Kristeva, Sontag, Audre Lorde, Nancy alongside 30 artists. This is exactly the erotic-creative-epistemic mode that Bogna Konior explores, now being institutionalized at ZKM. Amber should see this.

**Non-US, non-UK** — ZKM represents a European tradition (with German rigor, Karlsruhe's physics heritage — Hertz discovered EM waves here) that is genuinely different from the Anglo-American media art scene. Important alternative lineage for Amber to know.
