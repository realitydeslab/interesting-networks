Geneva University of Art and Design (Haute école d'art et de design) — Swiss art school with a distinctive critical-theoretical orientation. Known for the **CCC Research Master** (Critical Curatorial Cybermedia), the **Media Design Master** (interaction design + AI + ecology), and a publishing/journal ecosystem that amplifies critical art-design research across Europe.

## Mission & Objectives
HEAD Geneva sits at the intersection of art practice, design, and critical theory. Unlike technically-oriented design schools, HEAD foregrounds **decolonial, feminist, and queer theory** as active pedagogical frameworks. The CCC program explicitly describes its approach through "decolonial, intersectional feminist and queer theories" — making it unusually politically explicit for a Swiss art school. The new campus signals institutional expansion; the school teaches over 800 students across bachelor, master, and continuing education programs.

## Key Programs

### Master CCC — Critical Curatorial Cybermedia
[https://www.hesge.ch/head/formations-recherche/master-en-arts-visuels-ccc-programme-recherche](https://www.hesge.ch/head/formations-recherche/master-en-arts-visuels-ccc-programme-recherche)

The flagship research-based master in Visual Arts. Describes itself as investigating "the porosity and transversality between artistic and curatorial practices" and "forms of public sharing, knowledge production, circulation and dissemination generated by research through art means." Oriented simultaneously toward the art scene, academia, and civil society. Annual **CCC Public Seminar** focuses on one theme per year (2023/24: "Research (F)acts" — aesthetic inquiry scenarios in artistic/curatorial research practices).

**Faculty:**
- **Federica Martini** — Program head. PhD curator and art historian. Research on exhibition history and geopolitics, aesthetic gestures vs. industrial histories, (in)visibility in contemporary art. Was guest researcher at TrAIN (Transnational Art, Identity and Nation) at UAL London 2022. Author of *Just Another Exhibition: Stories and Politics of Biennials* (postmedia books, 2011).
- **Gene Ray** — Associate Professor, teaches Critical Studies seminar since 2008. Writes on aesthetics of the sublime, politics of memory, at intersections of art, critical theory, and trauma studies. Author of *Terror and the Sublime in Art and Critical Theory*. Led "The Anthropocene Atlas of Geneva" online collective research project.
- **Anne-Julie Raccoursier** — Teaching team

**Guest Interveners (recent):** [[Kodwo Eshun]], Basim Magdy, Çağla E. Aykaç, Cecilia Moya Rivera, Olivia Alexandra Fahmy, Julie Enckell, Nadia Radwan

### Master Media Design
[https://www.hesge.ch/head/formations-recherche/master-en-media-design](https://www.hesge.ch/head/formations-recherche/master-en-media-design)

Framed explicitly as "interaction design in the era of artificial intelligence and ecological transition." Students create interactive interfaces, digital narratives, games, immersive experiences, and interactive installations. The program teaches creative coding, AI, VR, AR, and game engines. Notably human-centered AND ecologically responsible — described as "socially and ecologically responsible" design. Three core themes: (1) evolution of human-machine interaction / "talking to robots" via machine learning; (2) interaction design with limited resources (eco-design); (3) games and narratives.

### Master Visual Knowledge
[https://www.hesge.ch/head/formations-recherche/master-visual-knowledge](https://www.hesge.ch/head/formations-recherche/master-visual-knowledge)

Graphic design master with a critical/visual studies angle. Explores "political, social, ethical and aesthetic issues of images" through visual studies. Tracks include: data web and data design, design in space, interaction and experience, mapping, schematic and diagrammatic drawing, anthropological visual analysis.

### Research Institute — IRAD
**Institut de recherche en art et en design (IRAD)** — HEAD's formal research unit. Publishes in journals such as JAR (Journal for Artistic Research), Parse Journal, e-flux journal. Engaged with art-science intersections and art practice as research methodology.

## Why Interesting
HEAD Geneva is a rare European art school where **critical theory, practice-based research, and technical AI/interaction design coexist** with an explicit feminist-decolonial methodological commitment. The CCC program's connection to [[Kodwo Eshun]] makes it a node in the Afrofuturist/speculative theory European circuit. The Media Design program's focus on AI + ecological transition aligns closely with Amber's more-than-human design research. The institutional model — small Swiss art school, international guest network, in-house journal, publishing arm — is an interesting infrastructure template for practice-as-research. HEAD sits at the Francophone-Germanophone-Anglophone intersection in Europe, making it a useful bridge node for Amber's international presence.
