# KYOTO Design Lab (D-lab)

Cross-disciplinary research and design platform at **Kyoto Institute of Technology (KIT)**, Japan. Founded 2014 under the mission **"Innovation by Design"** — operates as an incubator where architecture, design, digital fabrication, AI, and urban research intersect. Runs international workshops, residency programmes, and collaborations with universities worldwide (ETH Zurich, Princeton, Aalto, SUTD). Home to Japan's largest university design fabrication facility.

Part of KIT's **Center for the Possible Futures (CPF)** — the broader umbrella research institute.

## Structure

Two main arms:
1. **KYOTO Design Lab** — the research, exhibition, and international collaboration hub (lectures, residencies, projects)
2. **Design Factory** — Japan's largest university fabrication space (completed 2018): Digital Factory, Wood Factory, Metal Factory, Textile Factory, Biomaterial Factory, Photo Studio. Factory head: Associate Professor **Kazutoshi Tsuda**.

Also maintains a **Tokyo Gallery** at Arts Chiyoda 3331, 千代田区外神田 (Akihabara area), for preview exhibitions.

## Venue Circuit & Exhibition Practice

D-lab occupies a distinctive position: it is **embedded in Japan's traditional-cultural capital** (Kyoto's 1,200-year heritage, tea ceremony, Noh, Nishiki Market) while maintaining **strong international academic ties** (ETH, Princeton, Aalto). This is not just branding — KIT literally sits at the periphery of Kyoto, using that marginality as innovation engine (their own framing).

The lecture programme is excellent: in 2025-26 alone they hosted Riken Yamamoto (Pritzker winner), Pia Fricker (more-than-human design), Gilles Retsin (automated architecture), Daniela Mitterberger (XR), Emily Pugh (Getty), Christiane Sauer (textiles), Herzog & de Meuron, and Francisco/Manuel Aires Mateus (Portuguese architects). This is a world-class roster — clearly D-lab has strong international curation muscle.

## Intellectual Character

D-lab is not a speculative design lab in the RCA/Dunne-and-Raby sense. It's closer to:
- **Computational architecture + digital fabrication** (main technical axis)
- **Urban research** (KYOTO Shaping the Future biennale, urban dietary landscape)
- **Craft × digital** (textiles, biomaterials, kerf-bending, clay + AI)
- **More-than-human design** (Pia Fricker, XR garden, speculative interfaces)
- **Art + science** (SIGGRAPH, Ai Hasegawa, posthuman craft)

The Biomaterial Factory is notable — growing mycelium leather is research-active here. The posthuman craft angle in "Generative Tectonics" (code, clay, intent collaborate) is directly relevant to post-human making.
