# Nam June Paik Art Center

Museum dedicated to the legacy of Nam June Paik (백남준), the Korean-American artist widely considered the father of video art. Located in Gwacheon, south of Seoul. Peer institution to [[Art Center Nabi]], which explicitly carries forward Paik's "Humanizing Technology" philosophy.

## Vibe
Scholarly-artistic preservation and extension of Nam June Paik's legacy. Runs exhibitions, residencies, and research on video art, media art, and the intersection of art/technology that Paik pioneered.

## Key People
- **Nam June Paik** (1932–2006) — the subject/inspiration. Korean-American video artist. Coined "information superhighway." Key works: TV Garden, Good Morning Mr. Orwell (1984 satellite broadcast).

## Topics
- Video art history and practice
- Media art, new media
- Television, broadcast, satellite art
- Korea's contribution to global media art

## Events & Programmes
- Permanent collection of Paik works
- International residency programme
- Biennial exhibitions

## Why Interesting
Any serious engagement with Korean media art requires understanding the Paik lineage. [[Art Center Nabi]] traces its philosophical lineage through Paik; the Nam June Paik Art Center is the institutional custodian. For Amber: the satellite/telematic art Paik pioneered (Good Morning Mr. Orwell, 1984) is conceptually adjacent to Amber's interests in networked computation, media ecology, and more-than-human systems.
