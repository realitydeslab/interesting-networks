# Earshot

The first not-for-profit agency dedicated solely to sound and acoustic analysis for open-source investigation and human rights. Founded 2023 by [[Lawrence Abu Hamdan]]. Operates from [[Forensic Architecture]] offices in London but works remotely across Beirut and London.

## Key People
- [[Lawrence Abu Hamdan]] — founder, lead investigator
- Caline Matar — audio researcher, second-in-command (Beirut)
- Fabio Cervi — audio investigator, trained architect and musician (London)

## Topics
- Forensic audio analysis
- Open-source intelligence (OSINT) for human rights
- Sound as evidence in legal/advocacy contexts
- Deepfake/audio manipulation detection
- Conflict documentation through acoustic analysis

## Events & Programmes
- Investigations published as reports and media collaborations (Washington Post, Al Jazeera, ITV)
- Public presentations at art institutions (MoMA, Tate, Hangar Barcelona)

## Key Investigations
- Hind Rajab killing (Gaza, 2024) — commissioned by Washington Post
- Al-Ahli Hospital explosion (Gaza, 2023-24) — Doppler analysis
- Reuters journalists attack (southern Lebanon, 2023)
- Nahel Merzouk killing (Nanterre, France, 2023)

## Why Interesting
Earshot is the institutional model for what happens when an artist-researcher proves a methodology over a decade and then formalizes it into infrastructure. It's the sonic counterpart to [[Forensic Architecture]] — smaller, more focused, and at the cutting edge of audio-as-evidence at a moment when AI-generated audio makes this work more urgent than ever.
