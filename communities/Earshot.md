# Earshot

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-28:** Deep research completed. Upgraded to ⭐⭐⭐. Comprehensive profile written from New Yorker profile (July 2024), Brooklyn Rail interview (June 2023), Forensic Architecture documentation. Multiple high-profile Gaza/Lebanon investigations ongoing.
- **2026-02-26:** Initial stub created.

The first not-for-profit agency dedicated solely to audio analysis for human rights investigations and open-source intelligence. Founded in 2023 by [[Lawrence Abu Hamdan]] — a Turner Prize–winning artist-researcher who spent twelve years at [[Forensic Architecture]] proving the methodology — Earshot is the institutional crystallization of a decade of acoustic counter-forensics. Small, remote, and precisely focused: three people, global reach, and an investigative track record that already includes the most prominent atrocity investigations of 2023–2024.

## Mission & Objectives

Earshot describes itself as the first not-for-profit agency devoted solely to audio analysis for human rights advocacy, journalism, and legal cases. It conducts forensic sound analysis — acoustic ballistics, Doppler analysis, spectrographic fingerprinting, deepfake detection — on behalf of NGOs, media organizations, and advocacy groups. It does not exhibit its work as art: that distinction is held by Abu Hamdan's parallel practice. Earshot exists purely in the space of investigation, advocacy, and journalism.

The founding impetus came from the volume of requests Abu Hamdan was receiving as an individual researcher — from journalists, activists, NGOs — whose cases hinged on audio evidence but who had nowhere institutional to turn. As he told the Brooklyn Rail in 2023: *"Since I started doing my own forensic audio investigations in 2011, our cognizance about audio — what it can do for a story, what we can learn from it — has increased dramatically."*

The broader political context: AI-generated audio is making audio authentication more urgent than ever, while states increasingly rely on contested audio as evidence in asylum, criminal, and war-crimes contexts. Earshot positions itself as the civil society counter-weight.

## Key People

### [[Lawrence Abu Hamdan]] — Founder & Lead Investigator
Born 1985 in Amman, Jordan. Son of a Lebanese (Druze) father and British mother. Grew up in Yorkshire. Background in punk/DIY music scene (band: Cleckhuddersfax). BA Sonic Arts, Middlesex University. PhD Forensic Sound Analysis, Goldsmiths University of London, 2017. First cohort of Forensic Architecture researchers (2009). Turner Prize winner (shared, 2019). Nam June Paik Award, Edvard Munch Art Award. MoMA and Guggenheim collections. Called himself a "private ear" — conducts audio investigations for human rights while simultaneously making art from the same material. The investigative work and the art are strictly separate; the art is exhibited, the forensic work is not.

Key insight: *"These are small but essential technical questions — a single jigsaw piece within a much larger puzzle that many different eyes and ears are trying to piece together."*

### Caline Matar — Audio Researcher / Second-in-Command
Based in Beirut. Works remotely. Leads audio research and analysis. Central to the Nahel Merzouk investigation — identified that "pousse-toi" was likely a police officer instructing a colleague to move before the shot, rather than a command to the victim, which would imply premeditation.

### Fabio Cervi — Audio Investigator
Trained architect and musician. Based in London. Also came from the Forensic Architecture orbit. Specializes in spatial audio analysis — creating visual representations of sound data to animate drone movements, map acoustic signatures to spatial positions. Worked on the Reuters journalists attack (Lebanon, October 2023).

## Key Publications & Output

Earshot's output takes two primary forms: (1) research contributions embedded in media investigations (Washington Post, Al Jazeera, ITV), and (2) reports submitted to advocacy organizations and legal bodies. Crucially, Abu Hamdan has said Earshot will never exhibit its work as art — a principled distinction that preserves the forensic integrity of the findings.

Key publications:
- **Hind Rajab Investigation (2024)** — [Washington Post](https://www.washingtonpost.com/) and later Al Jazeera documentary "The Night Won't End" (June 2024). Audio ballistic analysis of gunshots in a phone call, CCTV recordings. Found IDF tanks were likely present and had fired at the family. Concluded the shooter was 13–23 metres away.
- **Al-Ahli Hospital Explosion (October 2023)** — Doppler analysis of two videos taken near the hospital, casting doubt on IDF claim that a misfired Palestinian rocket caused the explosion. (Later expanded by Forensic Architecture's full investigation.)
- **Reuters Journalists Attack, South Lebanon (October 13, 2023)** — Cell-phone video and videographer footage analysis. Found a drone could be heard for 46 minutes prior to the attack, circling journalists at least 11 times. Implication: IDF had sufficient information to identify journalists as civilians.
- **Nahel Merzouk Killing, Nanterre (June 2023)** — Analysis of dialogue in a police shooting. Re-created the incident using Abu Hamdan's own car. Concluded "pousse-toi" was an officer instructing a colleague to clear the shot, suggesting the killing was predetermined.
- **Hospital Nurse Video Authentication (January 2024)** — Al Jazeera segment. Abu Hamdan analyzed a viral video of a supposed nurse in Gaza claiming Hamas was attacking a hospital. Determined the sound of explosions had been added post-production: *"The way those explosions resounded were not consistent with the way her voice was resonating in that room."*

## Key Contributions

- **Institutionalizing acoustic counter-forensics.** Earshot is the first dedicated not-for-profit for this. Before it, acoustic analysis in human rights depended on Abu Hamdan as an individual researcher — a single point of failure.
- **Training pipeline.** Abu Hamdan explicitly founded Earshot so the methodology could be taught and distributed: *"Working on that sort of thing consistently over the years means this is something I can now train other people to do."*
- **Doppler ballistics as standard.** Applying Doppler analysis to contested conflict video to determine direction and origin of munitions — a technique Earshot has deployed in multiple major investigations.
- **Deepfake audio detection.** Building methodology for verifying audio authenticity at a moment when AI-generated audio is proliferating.
- **Collaboration model.** Earshot operates from within Forensic Architecture's London offices and regularly collaborates with FA on joint investigations, creating a combined visual-spatial + acoustic analytical capacity.

## For Amber

**Why interesting:** Earshot represents one of the clearest models in the world for how an artist-researcher can transition from individual practice to institutional infrastructure — formalizing a methodology into an NGO that operates at the intersection of art, science, journalism, and law. This is directly relevant to Amber's world: Reality Design Lab does something structurally analogous (using creative/design practice to probe questions that matter in ethics and governance), and Amber is at an institutional inflection point as a PhD student building toward a lab of his own.

**Thematic resonance:** The deeper thematic connection is about *evidence and witnessing in an age of mediated violence*. Earshot's work on acoustic testimony, earwitness accounts, and the politics of listening maps directly onto Amber's interests in machine behavior, more-than-human sensing, and the epistemic status of non-human evidence. What does it mean for a sound (rather than an image or text) to be evidence? What counts as witnessing? These are exactly the questions Amber's work on agent ethology and machine behavior engage.

**Methodological inspiration:** Earshot's research design — start with artistic intuition (the "earwitness inventory"), develop a methodology through cases, then formalize into infrastructure — is a model Amber could follow. The transition from individual practice to institutional model is one Amber should study closely.

## Why Interesting

Earshot is the sonic counterpart to [[Forensic Architecture]] — smaller, more focused, and operating at the cutting edge of audio-as-evidence at precisely the moment when AI-generated audio makes this work both more urgent and more contested. But it's not just the investigative work that makes Earshot interesting. It's the career architecture:

Lawrence Abu Hamdan spent twelve years building a solo practice that was simultaneously artistic and forensic — using the art world as a space to develop and test ideas, and the investigative work as a space to prove them. When the methodology was mature and the demand was clear, he formalized it into an institution. The institution then gave the methodology a life beyond one person.

For Amber, who is also building at the intersection of art practice and research infrastructure, this is a model worth studying closely. The key moves: (1) prove the methodology through cases over many years; (2) build the artistic/conceptual frame in parallel, which creates a different kind of credibility than academic publication alone; (3) when demand exceeds individual capacity, institutionalize — but maintain the distinction between the artistic and the investigative so neither contaminates the other.

The deeper resonance: Earshot is doing to sound what Forensic Architecture did to space — turning an aesthetic practice into an evidentiary one, and building the institutional infrastructure to make it count in the world. Amber is, in some sense, trying to do something analogous for agent behavior and machine ethics.
