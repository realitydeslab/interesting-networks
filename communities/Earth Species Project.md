An AI nonprofit dedicated to decoding animal communication using machine learning — the world's first serious institutional effort to build "language models for nature." Founded in San Francisco, operating globally through field-biologist partnerships.

## Mission & Objectives

**Mission:** Use AI and machine learning to decode animal communication, breaking the interspecies understanding barrier to foster a more empathetic and effective relationship between humans and the rest of the natural world.

**Core hypothesis:** The same representational structures that underlie human language also appear in animal communication. AI, trained on both, can discover shared patterns across the Tree of Life — enabling *translation without knowing the language*.

**Operating model:** Virtuous research cycle: AI accelerates bioacoustic discovery → discoveries improve AI models. Open science by default: all major models and benchmarks are open-source. Works exclusively with field biologists, never with captive-only systems (with limited exceptions for ethics-controlled vocalization generation studies).

**Roadmap to Decode:** ESP publicly frames its work as a staged program:
1. Build foundational AI tools and benchmarks (BEANS, AVES, NatureLM)
2. Map vocal repertoires of key species (crows, whales, elephants, spiders)
3. Decode meaning: link vocalizations to behavior, context, social dynamics
4. Generate novel vocalizations as probes for playback experiments
5. Achieve bidirectional communication across species

## Key Publications & Papers

### Flagship / Foundation
- **NatureLM-audio** (2024). Robinson et al. *The first large audio-language model for bioacoustics.* arXiv. DOI: <https://doi.org/10.48550/arXiv.2411.07186>  
  World's first bioacoustic audio-language model. Trained on Xeno-canto, iNaturalist, Watkins Marine Mammal Sound Database, and Animal Sound Archive data combined with human speech and music. Achieves SOTA zero-shot classification; novel emergent abilities (individual counting, distress call detection, cross-species transfer). Connected to LLaMA 3.1-8B. Open-source weights and training code released.

- **AVES: Animal Vocalization Encoder based on Self-Supervision** (2022). Hagiwara et al. arXiv. DOI: <https://doi.org/10.48550/arXiv.2210.14493>  
  Self-supervised transformer model for animal vocalization encoding. Outperforms supervised baselines across classification and detection tasks.

- **BEANS: The Benchmark of Animal Sounds** (2022). Hagiwara et al. arXiv. DOI: <https://doi.org/10.48550/arXiv.2210.12300>  
  First standardized ML benchmark for bioacoustics. 12 datasets, birds/mammals/anurans/insects, classification + detection tasks. GitHub: <https://github.com/earthspecies/beans>

- **BEANS-Zero** — Zero-shot extension of BEANS. arXiv: <https://arxiv.org/abs/2411.07186> (included in NatureLM-audio paper and <https://github.com/earthspecies/beans-zero>)

- **What Matters for Bioacoustic Encoding** (2025). arXiv: <https://arxiv.org/abs/2508.11845>  
  Large-scale empirical study of 19 models across 26 datasets. Key finding: 2-stage training (self-supervised pretraining + supervised post-training) on mixed bioacoustic + general audio is optimal.

- **BioCPPNet: automatic bioacoustic source separation with deep neural networks** (2021). *Scientific Reports*. DOI: <https://doi.org/10.1038/s41598-021-02790-2>  
  Bioacoustic cocktail party problem — separating overlapping individual animal vocalizations.

- **Biodenoising** (2024). Miron, Keen, Liu, Hoffman, Hagiwara, Pietquin, Effenberger, Cusimano. ICASSP 2025. arXiv: <https://arxiv.org/pdf/2410.03427>  
  Novel vocalization denoising without requiring clean training data, by adapting human speech enhancement models.

- **Biologger carrion crow study** (2025). ESP + University of León. *Animal Cognition*. URL: <https://link.springer.com/epdf/10.1007/s10071-025-02018-0>  
  127,000 vocalizations captured with MiniDTAG biologgers on cooperatively breeding crows. Voxaboxen used for classification.

- **Zebra finch communication preprint** (2026). *bioRxiv.* URL: <https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.64898/2026.02.12.705387v1>  
  Interactive playback experiment with captive zebra finches; generative vocalization models.

## Key Projects & Species

### Current Active Species Research
- **Hawaiian crows (Corvus hawaiiensis)** — extinct in wild (2002); studying vocal repertoire change in captive breeding populations. With Prof. Christian Rutz (St Andrews).
- **Carrion crows (Corvus corone)** — cooperatively breeding populations in northern Spain. With Profs. Daniela Canestrari and Vittorio Baglione. Biologger study capturing 127,000 vocalizations.
- **Beluga whales** — ongoing communication research.
- **Elephants (Loxodonta africana)** — vocalization separation and repertoire work.
- **Zebra finches (Taeniopygia guttata)** — generative vocalization playback at McGill University (captive population, Dr. Logan James).
- **Humpback whales (Megaptera novaeangliae)** — generative call synthesis (Jen-Yu Liu).
- **Jumping spiders** — acoustic/vibration communication research.
- **Frogs (FrogID partnership)** — Australian Museum's citizen science project; NatureLM-audio evaluated on 1.3M frog recordings with near-perfect accuracy distinguishing frog sounds from other taxa.

### Generative Vocalization Experiment
Jen-Yu Liu is developing models to generate novel vocalizations with controlled meaning — for interactive playback experiments. Ethics protocol: only captive populations initially, strict welfare standards. This is a key "Roadmap to Decode" milestone: if you can generate vocalizations that change animal behavior, you've moved from listening to speaking.

## Relationship to Project CETI

**Project CETI** ([projectceti.org](https://www.projectceti.org/)) and Earth Species Project are the two most prominent AI-meets-animal-communication initiatives, but they operate separately with different strategies:

| | ESP | Project CETI |
|-|-----|--------------|
| **Scope** | Tree of Life (all species) | Sperm whales only |
| **Approach** | Foundation models; generalizable across taxa | Sperm whale-specific dataset collection + ML |
| **Field tech** | Partners with biologists using biologgers, microphones | Custom drone fleets, hydrophone arrays in Dominica |
| **Data collection** | Uses existing archives + partner field data | Building own massive dataset from scratch |
| **AI strategy** | Open-source foundation models | More internal / research focus |
| **Funding** | Private philanthropy, Bezos Earth Fund suspected | TED Audacious Project ($33M+ reported) |

**Key distinction:** CETI is collecting a single enormous dataset from one species; ESP is building generalizable AI that works across thousands of species. The approaches are complementary. ESP's NatureLM-audio includes Watkins Marine Mammal Sound Database (sperm whale codas) in its training data, so there is indirect technical overlap.

**Aza Raskin** is publicly associated with both communities' broader discourse — the "decode animal communication" thesis he champions at ESP rhymes with CETI's sperm whale focus, and he participates in the same intellectual circles (TED, WIRED, The New Yorker).

## For Amber

### Why Interesting
ESP is the mirror image of Amber's agent ethology project — and that mirror structure is intellectually generative.

**The mirror:** ESP applies ML to study animals the way ethologists study animals — observing behavior, classifying calls, mapping repertoires, discovering social structure. Amber studies AI agents the way ethologists study animals — observing behavior, mapping "communication," tracking social dynamics in multi-agent systems. The methodological pipeline is *identical*. The substrate is different (biological vs. digital agents). This is not just analogy — it's transferable methodology.

**The bidirectional question:** If NatureLM-audio can decode shared representational structures across species (crow → whale → spider), can the same approach decode the "communication" between AI agents? Do LLM-to-LLM API calls, token streams, and chain-of-thought outputs constitute a "language" with detectable structure? This is a research question Amber is uniquely positioned to ask.

**More-than-human design:** ESP's Roadmap to Decode is literally a more-than-human design project — designing tools, protocols, and experiments to enable non-human communication to be legible to humans. Amber's design practice around more-than-human systems and posthuman computing connects directly.

**Cosmotechnics resonance:** The question of whether Western AI models can decode non-Western animal cognition and communication systems — or whether they merely impose human-speech-trained representations onto radically different semiotic systems — is a cosmotechnics question. Does NatureLM-audio "understand" crow communication, or project human language structure onto it? This is Amber's territory.

**Aza Raskin × ERA fellowship:** Raskin's dual role as AI risk communicator (Center for Humane Technology) and interspecies communication researcher is precisely the kind of AI safety adjacent work that the ERA fellowship engages. His "Your Undivided Attention" podcast regularly features AI risk thinkers (Yoshua Bengio, Sam Altman critics, alignment researchers). Amber's ERA work on existential risk from AI agents has a natural conversation partner here.
