# dis assembly

Toronto-based neurodiverse arts collective and "co-lab" founded in 2016 by [[Adam Wolfond]] and [[Estée Klar]]. Located at Artscape Youngplace. Explicitly adopts an "anti-mission" — refusing fixed goals as neurotypical impositions on process. Works at the intersection of art, philosophy, disability studies, critical theory, and mutual care.

**Key distinction:** Not an arts program, not a therapy service, not rehabilitation. A milieu for neurodiverse people to create and think together on their own terms. Inspired by new materialisms, affect theory, queer and radical disability studies.

## Why Interesting
A concrete instantiation of what alternative knowledge communities look like when they genuinely decenter normative structures. The "anti-mission" and resistance to operationalization is a methodological provocation relevant to participatory design. Model of mutual care over hierarchical support.
