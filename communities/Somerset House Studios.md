# Somerset House Studios

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-03-12:** Deep research completed. Expanded from stub to full profile. Upgraded from ⭐⭐ to ⭐⭐⭐ — multiple directly relevant residencies (Creative Technologies Fellowship, n-Space Fellowship, UNESCO AI Ethics Residency), London-based and accessible, strong art-technology-society mission with more-than-human governance themes.

A space for experimentation in the centre of London connecting artists, makers, and thinkers with audiences. Up to **70 artists are resident at any one time** for 1–7 years, alongside shorter-term national and international residency programmes. The Studios develops cross-disciplinary projects, powers **Channel** (Somerset House's online art platform), and runs multiple AI/tech-specific residency and fellowship programmes highly relevant to Amber.

## Mission & Objectives

**Core Mission:** Support artists across disciplines to push bold ideas, engage with urgent issues, and experiment with new technologies. Cross-disciplinary, process-oriented, community-driven.

**Who They Are:** Part of Somerset House (Registered Charity No. 1063640), self-described "Home of Cultural Innovators" on the Strand in central London. The Studios is the artist community within the broader Somerset House cultural complex.

**Community Focus:** Cross-disciplinary — music, visual art, performance, writing, and practitioners experimenting with evolved/advanced technologies. Particularly interested in artists who are "genre-curious, collaborative and want to push the boundaries of practice beyond these silos." Primarily London-based artists at various career stages.

## Events & Programmes

### 🔥 n-Space — Art × Technology Interdisciplinary Programme
**The most relevant programme for Amber.** A new space at Somerset House Studios for interdisciplinary experimentation across art and technology. 18-month fellowship programme.

**Structure:**
- **Phase 1:** Collaborative knowledge exchange workshops and experimental design methods, led by UAL's Creative Computing Institute
- **Phase 2:** Artist-led provocations by [[Alexandra Daisy Ginsberg]], Rashaad Newsome, and Sarah Friend
- **Phase 3:** Network mapping and iterative archive by Goldsmiths Computing
- **Public programme:** April 2026 onwards
- **Final showcase:** March 2027

**Partners:**
- **UAL Creative Computing Institute (CCI)** — Experimental Infrastructures research hub: <https://www.arts.ac.uk/creative-computing-institute/>

- **Abandon Normal Devices (AND)** — Radical arts organisation, North of England

**Theme:** Governance in human and more-than-human collaboration. Directly relevant to Amber's work.

### Creative Technologies Fellowship (with UAL CCI)
Annual fellowship for artists using computational systems for critical practice on social/environmental questions. Running in parallel with the academic year.

**What's included:**
- £7,500 artist fee + £7,500 production budget
- Shared studio at Somerset House + workspace/labs at UAL
- Research mentorship from CCI (Creative ML, Robotics, XR, Philosophy of Technology, Critical Design)
- Curatorial support for commission at Somerset House
- Access to project space, rehearsal space, recording studio
- Participation in public outcomes at CCI

**Research areas:**
1. **Creativity, Machine Learning and AI** — Creative uses of foundational 21st-century technology
2. **Human Computer Interaction** — Behavior with emerging technologies
3. **Platforms, Big Data and Digital Citizenship** — Data, platforms, society, equality

URL: <https://www.somersethouse.org.uk/somerset-house-studios/residencies-and-opportunities/creative-technologies-fellowship>

## Why Interesting

Somerset House Studios is London's primary nexus for art-technology-society experimentation. The n-Space programme specifically — with its focus on more-than-human governance and its partnership between computing departments and art organisations — represents exactly the institutional infrastructure Amber's research needs. Unlike traditional academic residencies, the Studios operates at the speed and openness of art practice, making it ideal for speculative, provocative work on machine consciousness, agent ethology, and more-than-human futures.

The Studios is also a **networking amplifier** — 70 artists in residence at any time, plus international programme alumni, create a dense creative community. Being in central London (the Strand) makes it the highest-density creative-tech node accessible to Amber.
