# DFG-Graduiertenkolleg "Kulturen der Kritik" (Cultures of Critique)

**Status: FINISHED (Oct 2016 → Sep 2025).** Nine years of DFG-funded doctoral training in critical theory, cultural studies, media critique, and art criticism at [Leuphana University Lüneburg](https://www.leuphana.de/en/). The programme produced three generations of doctoral candidates and a substantial body of publications, primarily through Diaphanes. Its intellectual output — and the alumni network it created — continues to matter even in the programme's absence.

## Background

The *Graduiertenkolleg* (Research Training Group) format is the DFG's flagship intensive doctoral programme model — 4.5-year funding cycles, with Leuphana's running for a full 9 years (two phases). "Kulturen der Kritik" asked: what is critique? Not as a single method but as a historically and culturally variable *practice* — what counts as critique in different contexts, how critique is institutionalized, what forms and media it uses, and what effects it has.

The programme was organized around three interlocking research areas:
1. **Art Critique** — critical writing about art as bourgeois public sphere instrument; feminist intervention; critique of representation; contemporary artistic practice
2. **Media Critique** — media's role in blocking/enabling access to phenomena; digitalization and new forms of critical subjectivity; algorithms and diagrams replacing classical cultural techniques
3. **Social Critique** — collective and networked forms of protest and critique; Femen as exemplary case of critique depending on representation

The central theoretical claim: *critical practices cannot be separated from the forms and media of their representation.* Representation constitutes the object as it appears in critique and shapes its social effects.

The **second phase** (2021–2025) expanded the frame by asking what happens when critique operates under conditions of *globality*: postcolonial, decolonial, and transcultural perspectives challenging Enlightenment-rooted assumptions about critique. Which agents, expectations, and criteria determine what counts as a critical act?

## Why Interesting

This programme was the most important institutional node for German critical humanities doctoral training at the intersection of media theory, art criticism, and Continental philosophy in the 2016–2025 period. Leuphana under Pias/Hörl/Söntgen emerged as a distinctive alternative to Frankfurt/Berlin — not Frankfurt School critique but something more media-ecological, more attentive to forms, and increasingly postcolonially informed.

The alumni network from three generations now disperses into European and global academia. The programme's refusal to treat critique as a single method — instead studying "cultures" of critique, plural, historically situated — anticipates the kind of situated, practice-grounded scholarship Amber is interested in.

The key thread connecting to Amber's work: **Bélen Prado's dissertation on "Automated Patterns of Culture: Philosophy and Machine Learning"** (completed 2023, under Hörl) directly engages AI/ML through the programme's critical framework. And the programme's insistence that critical practices shape their objects through form and media is structurally consonant with Amber's interest in how agents shape their environments.
