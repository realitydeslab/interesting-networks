# DFG-Graduiertenkolleg "Kulturen der Kritik" (Cultures of Critique)

A DFG-funded (German Research Foundation) doctoral research training group (*Graduiertenkolleg*) at [Leuphana University Lüneburg](https://www.leuphana.de/en/), investigating "cultures of critique" — the historical, theoretical, and practical conditions of critical thought and practice across culture, media, and society. Erich Hörl is Deputy Speaker (*stellvertretender Sprecher*).

## Background

The *Graduiertenkolleg* (Research Training Group) format is the DFG's flagship doctoral programme model — intensive, thematically focused, with regular colloquia, visiting speakers, and joint workshops. "Kulturen der Kritik" focuses on critique not as a single method but as a set of culturally and historically variable practices: what counts as critique in different contexts, how critique is institutionalized, and what critique can do.

Given Hörl's involvement, the programme's intellectual orientation likely combines:
- Critical theory (Frankfurt School tradition)
- Media critique and digital cultures
- Ecological and post-anthropocentric critiques
- Feminist and postcolonial critique

## Why Interesting
The "Kulturen der Kritik" programme represents the intellectual ecosystem that Hörl is cultivating at Leuphana. The doctoral students produced through this programme will shape the next generation of German-language critical media studies and philosophy of technology. The programme also attracts visiting speakers and sometimes accepts visiting doctoral students or scholars.
