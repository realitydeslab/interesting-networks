# Afrotectopia

A social institution (est. 2018, NYC) at the nexus of art, design, technology, culture, and pedagogy — centering Black creativity, radical imagination, and liberatory futures. Founded by [[Ari Melenciano]]. Evolved from an annual festival/conference to an international fellowship bridging connections across the Black and Pan-African diaspora.

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-27:** Initial deep research. Full profile created.
- **~2024:** Published *Black Metal* — a limited-edition art book (500 copies) produced with NYU ITP + MIT Space Exploration Initiative + Ford Foundation. Focus on space travel, sci-fi, speculative design, Black culture, spirituality.

## Mission & Objectives

Afrotectopia's mission is to center Black creative, intellectual, and technological imagination — particularly at the intersection of art, design, science, and technology through a Black and Afrocentric lens. Key philosophical commitments:

- **Technology is not synonymous with digitality** — technology as "extension of sentient capability" (not just human capability)
- **Community over individualism** — every project involves every fellow, privileging collectivity
- **Open-source pedagogy** — research and tools made freely accessible for others to build on
- **Blackness is not a monolith** — intentional diversity of cultural identities within the Black/Pan-African diaspora
- **Omni-specialization** — futures must be designed through comprehensive, multi-domain practice

This is a community that thinks about futures through a critical Black lens — which means it intersects with speculative design, experiential futures, and more-than-human frameworks in a deeply non-Western way.

## Key Contributions

1. **Festival/Conference (2018–present)** — Annual gathering in New York City bringing together Black technologists, artists, designers, researchers. Helped seed a critical Black art-tech scene in NYC.
2. **Imagineer Fellowship (2020)** — Inaugural interdisciplinary research fellowship for 10 Black innovators globally. 6-week program with open public sessions (Imaginariums). Covered: culturally relevant pedagogy, Black radical technoculture, Black futures manifesto, future cities.
3. **International Fellowship (ongoing)** — Evolved from NYC-based festival to an international fellowship bridging the Pan-African diaspora
4. **Space Incubator (2021–2023)** — Incubator in partnership with NYU ITP and MIT Space Exploration Initiative. 4 artists imagined a new world off-planet, resulting in *Black Metal*.
5. **Radical pedagogy** — Ari teaches at NYU ITP, Pratt, Hunter, The New School — disseminating Afrotectopian frameworks into mainstream art-tech education.

## Why Interesting

Afrotectopia is a rare community that does critical computing / speculative futures work from a Black radical imagination framework without being merely "AI ethics" or "representation in tech." Their philosophical move — technology as extension of *sentient* (not just human) capability — is a more-than-human move. This directly intersects Amber's interests:

1. **More-than-human design** — they've already expanded "sentient" beyond human; good dialogue with ALife and machine behavior
2. **Speculative design / experiential futures** — core to the fellowship and publication work
3. **Community infrastructure** — Afrotectopia as a model for how to build critical art-tech community (relevant to Amber's own institutional building)
4. **Non-Western perspectives** — Afrocentric lens on technology challenges Western-centric assumptions; complements Amber's sinofuturism and cosmotechnics interests
5. **Space + futures** — *Black Metal* explicitly engages space travel and sci-fi speculation; resonates with Amber's ALife and open-endedness interests

Afrotectopia is the answer to "what would critical computing look like if it was centered in Black radical imagination rather than European post-humanism?"
