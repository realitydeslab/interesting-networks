# Naropa Institute (Naropa University)

Founded 1974 in Boulder, Colorado by Tibetan Buddhist teacher Chögyam Trungpa Rinpoche. The first Buddhist-inspired university in North America. Home of the Jack Kerouac School of Disembodied Poetics (co-founded by Allen Ginsberg and Anne Waldman). A gathering point in the 1970s for countercultural intellectuals: [[Gregory Bateson]], William S. Burroughs, Pema Chödrön, Ram Dass, Gary Snyder, Diane di Prima. Emphasis on contemplative education, embodied learning, and arts-based practice.

## Why Interesting
Naropa represents the synthesis of contemplative practice, experimental poetics, and systems thinking in 1970s American counterculture. This is where Buddhist mindfulness practice first entered American intellectual life at scale — decades before Jon Kabat-Zinn and MBSR. The embodied, contemplative approach to knowing is relevant to somaesthetic design.
