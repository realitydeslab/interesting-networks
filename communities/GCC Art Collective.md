# GCC Art Collective

Gulf art collective founded 2013 by Monira Al Qadiri; named after the Gulf Cooperation Council (the political-economic consortium of Arabian Gulf nations). Members: Monira Al Qadiri, [[Fatima Al Qadiri]], [[Sophia Al-Maria]], [[Amal Khalaf]], Khalid Al Gharaballi, Nanu Al Hamad, Aziz Al Qatami, Barrak Alzaid. Work critically engages Gulf state aesthetics, diplomatic language, and consumer culture — Gulf Futurism translated into collective institutional critique.

## Mission & Objectives
Collective art practice addressing Gulf state governance, diplomatic spectacle, and the aesthetic language of Gulf modernity. The name puns on the geopolitical body, reclaiming its acronym for critical art.

## Why Interesting
The GCC collective is the institutional expression of Gulf Futurism as collective practice. Their work at MoMA PS1 and major biennials places Gulf critical art at the center of global contemporary art discourse.
