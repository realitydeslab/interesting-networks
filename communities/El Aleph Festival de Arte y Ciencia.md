# El Aleph Festival de Arte y Ciencia

Annual art-and-science festival in Mexico City, produced by **UNAM's Coordinación de Difusión Cultural** (Cultura UNAM). Founded 2017, now in its 9th edition. **~80 activities across 20 venues, ~170 international guests from 11 countries** — consistently one of the largest transdisciplinary art-science gatherings in Latin America. Free admission.

**Named after Borges's "El Aleph"** — the story of a point in space that contains all other points simultaneously, from which one can see everything in the universe. The name is not decorative: it frames the festival's core ambition, to hold all disciplines in a single point of attention without hierarchy.

## Mission & Objectives

El Aleph's core premise: **"Solo en la intersección de campos de estudio, lenguajes y miradas es posible acceder a una comprensión más rica del presente."** (Only at the intersection of fields of study, languages, and perspectives can we access a richer understanding of the present.)

The festival creates **horizontal dialogue** between physics, neuroscience, astrobiología, philosophy, music, dance, theater, film, and visual art. It does not treat science as material for art, nor art as illustration for science — it stages genuine encounters between practitioners from both worlds, with critical theory and philosophy as the third element.

Each edition has a **central theme** that cuts across disciplines:
- **2017** — "La física moderna y otras fronteras" (Modern physics and other frontiers) — *inaugural edition*
- **2018** — "Las fronteras del cerebro y la percepción" (Frontiers of the brain and perception)
- **2019** — "Las ciencias de la complejidad" / "El todo es más que la suma de sus partes" (Complexity sciences / "The whole is more than the sum of its parts") — 45+ activities
- **2020/2021** — Pandemic-inflected editions (COVID, medicine, life-at-limits)
- **2022/2023** — AI, other intelligences, digital politics
- **2024** — Related to Cátedra Max Aub's focus on data colonialism and computational ecology
- **2025 (9th edition)** — **"Redes"** (Networks) — May 8–18, 80+ activities, 20 venues, 170 guests from 11 countries. Exploring networks: social, quantum, poetic, biological, digital. CERN connection prominent.
- **2026 (10th edition)** — May 8–17. Theme TBA.

## Key People

### José Gordon — Artistic Curator
Mexican novelist, essayist, science journalist, TV producer. **Creator of the series *Imaginantes*** (winner at New York Film Festival). Since 2007, hosts and directs **"La Oveja Eléctrica"** (*The Electric Sheep*) on Canal 22 — Mexico's leading science-and-thought TV program. **Premio Nacional de Periodismo 2013** (National Journalism Prize). Author of *El inconcebible universo*, *El Libro del destino*, *Gato encerrado. Fronteras del cerebro*. Also directs **Colisionador de Ideas** (with Luis Cabrera Ruiz) — an audiovisual project bridging frontier science and poetry. Characterized by Augusto Monterroso as "dignifying cultural television." Gordon is the festival's intellectual engine — his choice of themes and guests shapes its canon.

### Juan Ayala — Festival Director (2025)
Secretario Técnico de Planeación y Programación at UNAM. Quoted in 2025 inauguration: "El Aleph is precisely a living, collective, and unique project that can only happen at UNAM, given the synergy and collaboration of its scientific and artistic communities with each other and with international collaborative networks."

### Rosa Beltrán Álvarez — Coordinadora de Difusión Cultural, UNAM
Senior institutional head overseeing the festival. Literary intellectual and cultural administrator. In 2025 inauguration speech: "El Aleph takes its name from a Borges story that dreams of a point where all points are integrated. That metaphor offers a model to appreciate the visible and invisible networks that unite us."

### Tania Aedo — Co-organizer via [[Cátedra Max Aub]]
Coordinator of Cátedra Max Aub, Transdisciplina en Arte y Tecnología (UNAM). The Cátedra is a close institutional partner of the festival; Tania Aedo is the primary contact for the academic/critical programming strand. See [[Tania Aedo]] and [[Cátedra Max Aub]] notes.

### Luis Cabrera Ruiz — Colisionador de Ideas co-director
Co-creator (with José Gordon) of the "Colisionador de Ideas" audiovisual project that opens each festival. Bridges poetry, science, and visual thinking.

## Format & Scale

- **Duration**: ~10 days (May each year; 2025: May 8–18, 2026: May 8–17)
- **Scale**: 80+ activities, 20+ venues, 170+ guests from 10+ countries
- **Activities**: Keynote lectures, panel discussions ("conversatorios"), dance performances, theater, concerts, film screenings, exhibitions, workshops, open-air fora
- **Admission**: Free ("entrada libre") — deliberately open access, not ticketed
- **Broadcast**: **TV UNAM** transmits key events live — extending reach beyond the campus
- **Open air**: Since ~2025, the **Foro de la Fuente** (fountain area at CCU) is used as an outdoor public space for talks and performances — deliberately bringing discussions outside traditional auditoria

- **[Centro Cultural Universitario (CCU)](https://www.cultura.unam.mx/)** — main hub; Sala Miguel Covarrubias, Sala Nezahualcóyotl, Sala Carlos Chávez, Foro de la Fuente

- **[Casa del Lago UNAM](https://casadellago.unam.mx/)** — art space in Chapultepec Park; used for Dream House installation
- **[Museo Universitario del Chopo](https://chopo.unam.mx/)** — alternative arts museum in Colonia Santa María la Ribera
- **[Facultad de Ciencias](https://www.fciencias.unam.mx/)** — Auditorio Carlos Graef
- **Facultad de Arquitectura** — Teatro Estefanía Chávez Barragán
- **Facultad de Filosofía y Letras** — Aula Magna
- **[Casa Universitaria del Libro](https://libros.unam.mx/)** — Librería García Terrés
- **Casa de las Humanidades, UNAM**
- **Piso 16, Laboratorio de Iniciativas Culturales UNAM**
- **Foro, Centro Universitario de Teatro (CUT)**

This city-wide distribution is deliberate: by using venues across the UNAM "campus" (which includes sites across Mexico City), the festival occupies the entire cultural infrastructure of the university and makes each venue a site of transdisciplinary encounter.

## Why Interesting

**The Latin American art-science circuit's flagship.** El Aleph is to Mexico City what Ars Electronica is to Linz or transmediale is to Berlin — but with a distinctly different character: more humanistic, more UNAM-inflected (i.e., more politically engaged, more Global South-situated), more free and public-oriented.

**The UNAM institutional context matters deeply.** UNAM is the largest university in Latin America, with an explicitly humanist, anti-fascist, socially-committed tradition. The festival inherits this. Unlike tech-industry-adjacent festivals, El Aleph is organized through academic infrastructure that has a critical, decolonial, and philosophical orientation — notably via the [[Cátedra Max Aub]] connection.

**Non-European node in the global art-science network.** The Edinburgh Science Festival connection (2025) shows El Aleph is building an international festival-to-festival network — but from a Global South position. It's not a recipient of European cultural export; it's an originating site of encounter.

**Thematic sophistication.** Year after year, the theme is chosen not for buzzword-value but for philosophical richness: "complexity" (not "AI"), "networks" (not "connectivity"), "modern physics" (not "STEM"). The Borges framing is real — this is a festival curated by people who read.

**Dream House (2025)** — bringing La Monte Young and Jung Hee Choi's 1960s sound installation to Mexico for the first time is a significant curation. This is not safe art programming. This is a commitment to serious experimental art as a mode of perceptual inquiry, placed alongside cutting-edge science. That's a strong aesthetic/intellectual statement.
