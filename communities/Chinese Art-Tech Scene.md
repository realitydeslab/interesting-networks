# Chinese Art-Tech Scene

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Full deep research completed. Comprehensive rewrite covering history, institutions, people, events, publications, global connections, and Amber's position within the scene.

The Chinese art-technology intersection is not a single scene but a **layered ecosystem** stretching across Beijing, Shanghai, Hangzhou, Shenzhen, and Hong Kong — each city with its own institutional character, aesthetic politics, and global connections. The scene's genealogy runs from the **'85 New Wave** (八五新潮) of the mid-1980s → **Chinese video art** (Zhang Peili's *30x30*, 1988) → **institutionalized new media art** (CAA School of Intermedia Art, 2000s) → the current moment of globalized digital/AI/speculative practice. It is simultaneously China's most internationally connected art scene and a hotbed of thinking that is irreducible to Western frameworks.

**Amber is not an outsider observing this scene — she taught inside it (CAA, 2023–2025) and is deeply embedded through multiple nodes.** This note maps the terrain she already partly inhabits.

## The Genealogy: From '85 New Wave to Now

### Act I: The '85 New Wave (1985–1989)
The immediate backdrop to everything that followed. In the reform-era opening of Deng Xiaoping's China, young artists across the country discovered Western contemporary art (via reproductions, limited publications, and occasional exhibitions) and responded with a wave of experimental work. The movement was organized by critics **Gao Minglu** (高名潞) and **Li Xianting** (栗宪庭) and culminated in the landmark **China/Avant-Garde** exhibition (中国现代艺术展) at the National Art Museum of China in Beijing, February 1989 — famous for a "No U-Turn" sign at its entrance and shut down twice by authorities.

Key groups included the **[[Pond Society]]** (池社, 1986) — the Hangzhou collective co-founded by **[[Zhang Peili]]**, Geng Jianyi, and Song Ling — whose cool, existentialist conceptualism set a tone distinct from the emotionalism of other '85 New Wave groups. The Pond Society's influence on CAA and on Chinese new media art cannot be overstated.

Other '85 New Wave tendencies included the Xiamen Dada group (**Huang Yong Ping**), the rationalist painters of Beijing, and hundreds of provincial groups responding to the sudden exposure to Western modernism.

### Act II: Chinese Video Art (1988–2000)
The founding gesture: **[[Zhang Peili]]** created **[30x30](https://www.artic.edu/exhibitions/2595/zhang-peili-record-repeat)** (1988) — 180 minutes of mirror-smashing and re-gluing — China's first video artwork. Screened at the Huangshan Conference 1988 that organized the China/Avant-Garde exhibition. This was radical: video was technically illegal as an art medium in China at the time.

Zhang's peer **Wang Gongxin** (王功新) began making video work in Beijing from the mid-1990s, working on questions of surveillance, intimacy, and mediated presence. Together they established video as a legitimate Chinese art form, despite — or through — its difficulty and institutional resistance.

**Qiu Zhijie** (邱志杰) emerged as a key artist-theorist-curator bridging video, performance, installation, and critical writing. Later became a significant figure at CAA and in curating major Chinese biennials.

### Act III: Institutionalization (2000–2015)
The decisive institutional move: **[[Zhang Peili]]** founded the **New Media Art Department** at [[China Academy of Art]] in the early 2000s — the first at any major Chinese art academy. This grew into the **School of Intermedia Art** (跨媒体艺术学院), training the next generation. His most prominent student: **[[Lu Yang]]** (MFA New Media, CAA), now one of China's most internationally visible media artists.

**[[Cao Fei]]** (曹斐, b. 1978, Guangzhou) emerged from the Guangzhou Academy of Fine Arts and Pearl River Delta factory culture, developing multimedia work on labor, digital life, and speculative urbanism. Her *RMB City* (2008–2011, in Second Life) became one of the most discussed Chinese digital artworks internationally, and her *Asia One* (2018) — a speculative doc about fully automated logistics — is one of the most powerful films about labor and technology made anywhere. Represented by Sprüth Magers and Vitamin Creative Space. Major retrospective at UCCA 2021.

In this period, **[[UCCA Center for Contemporary Art]]** (Beijing, opened 2007) became the country's leading independent contemporary art institution, bringing major Chinese and international artists to Beijing's 798 Art Zone. Its opening show — "'85 New Wave: Birth of Chinese Contemporary Art" — self-consciously connected the new institution to the historical avant-garde.

**Chronus Art Center (CAC)** was founded in Shanghai in 2013 by entrepreneur Dillion Zhang, independent curator **Li Zhenhua**, and artist **Hu Jieming** — becoming China's first nonprofit dedicated specifically to media art. Under the artistic direction of **Zhang Ga** (since 2015), CAC has produced internationally significant exhibitions and developed programs with ZKM Karlsruhe, HeK Basel, and the Leonardo/ISAST network.

### Act IV: Current Scene (2015–Now)
The current scene is characterized by:
- **International market integration** — Chinese artists consistently at Venice Biennale, Art Basel, major Western museums
- **Institutional proliferation** — Power Station of Art, M+, UCCA Edge, Rockbund, many private museums
- **Critical technology discourse** — Rise of curatorial voices like **[[Iris Long]]** developing specifically Chinese frameworks for infrastructure studies, speculative computation, and critical AI
- **Sinofuturism** — **[[Lawrence Lek]]**'s theoretical framework (though diaspora-based) has been widely adopted; artists in China and the diaspora develop versions of what it means for China/Chinese to be conflated with AI and futures
- **Science fiction crossover** — **[[Chen Qiufan]]** and the broader Chinese sci-fi boom (**Liu Cixin**, **Hao Jingfang**) increasingly intersect with the visual arts scene
- **Philosophy of technology** — **[[Yuk Hui]]**'s cosmotechnics and technodiversity frameworks are widely read in Chinese art schools and increasingly inform curatorial practice
- **NYU Shanghai** — **[[Anna Greenspan]]** (AIC Director) and **[[Bogna Konior]]** create a node of accelerationist-adjacent media theory in Shanghai, with ties to the global Bratton/Antikythera network

## Key Institutions

### By Geography

#### Beijing
**[[UCCA Center for Contemporary Art]]** ⭐⭐⭐  
The premier independent contemporary art institution in mainland China. Located in the 798 Art Zone, a former East German-designed military electronics factory complex in Dashanzi, Chaoyang District. Founded 2007 by Belgian collectors Guy and Myriam Ullens, now Chinese/international patron-owned. Director: **Philip Tinari**. Has presented 200+ exhibitions including major solo shows of Cao Fei, Qiu Zhijie, Xu Bing, Zhang Huan, as well as international figures (Olafur Eliasson, Tino Sehgal, Matthew Barney). Expanded to UCCA Dune (Beidaihe, 2018) and UCCA Edge (Shanghai, 2021). Tinari co-curated the landmark "Art and China after 1989: Theater of the World" at the Guggenheim (2017).

**798 Art Zone** (798艺术区)  
The de facto center of Beijing's contemporary art world. Former Sino-Soviet-era factory complex now housing UCCA, 100+ galleries, studios, restaurants. Where Beijing's art world has its physical presence. Also hosts the annual **Beijing Design Week**. Something of a tourist zone now, but remains the physical home of the scene.

**Vitamin Creative Space** ⭐⭐  
Beijing (and Guangzhou) gallery that has represented **[[Cao Fei]]** since her early career. One of the key galleries for experimental and digital art practice in China. The support system through which Cao Fei's international career was built.

**Central Academy of Fine Arts (CAFA)** (中央美术学院)  
Beijing's premier fine arts academy. Site of the **Leonardo Art, Science and Technology Lecture Series** partnership with Chronus Art Center. CAFA's media art programs are a Beijing counterpart to CAA's Hangzhou-based School of Intermedia Art.

#### Shanghai
**Chronus Art Center (CAC)** (新时线媒体艺术中心) ⭐⭐⭐  
China's first nonprofit art organization dedicated to media art. Founded 2013. Location: The Point, 14th floor, East Building, No. 102 Qinjiang Road, Xuhui District, Shanghai. Artistic Director: **Zhang Ga** (since 2015). Key institutional history:
- Founded by entrepreneur Dillion Zhang, curator Li Zhenhua, artist Hu Jieming
- Pioneering exhibitions: Extra Time (Raqs Media Collective); Jeffrey Shaw & Hu Jieming Duo Solo; Thomas Feuerstein's *Psychoprosa*; Chris Salter's *Haptic Field*; Terike Haapoja's *Closed Circuit – Open Duration*
- Thematic shows: *Folklore of the Cyber World* (Chinese Pavilion parallel project, Venice 2015); *unREAL: The Algorithmic Present* (with HeK Basel); *Datumsoria: The Return of the Real* (with ZKM Karlsruhe); *Three Rooms* (China/South Korea/Germany)
- Programs: Leonardo Art, Science and Technology Lecture Series (with Leonardo/ISAST and CAFA); residency/fellowship programs
- **Vital node** connecting Chinese media art to international media art festival circuit (ZKM, HeK, Ars Electronica world)

**Power Station of Art (PSA)** (上海当代艺术博物馆) ⭐⭐⭐  
Shanghai's first state-run contemporary art museum. Opened 2012 in a converted former power station on the Huangpu River (Expo 2010 site). Director: Li Xu. Hosts the **Shanghai Biennale** (since 2012). China's first public contemporary art museum. Has presented work by major Chinese and international artists, including Zhang Peili, Gary Zhexi Zhang, and others. The PSA / Shanghai Biennale is the most established and most visited major contemporary art event in mainland China.

**Rockbund Art Museum** (外滩美术馆) ⭐⭐  
Private nonprofit museum in a historic 1933 Royal Asiatic Society building near the Bund, Huangpu District, Shanghai. Initiator: Adrian Cheng (K11/New World Group). Known for ambitious programming with emerging international and Chinese artists. **Gary Zhexi Zhang** held a Strategic Initiatives R&D Fellowship here (2022–23). Part of the Rockbund heritage district development.

**NYU Shanghai IMA** ⭐⭐  
Interactive Media Arts program at NYU Shanghai. Key faculty: **[[Bogna Konior]]** (media theory), **[[Anna Greenspan]]** (AIC Director). **Center for AI and Culture (AIC)** — directed by Greenspan, with external affiliates including Chen Qiufan, Gary Zhexi Zhang, Lukáš Likavčan, Amy Ireland. A node connecting the Shanghai art-tech scene to the broader Bratton/Antikythera/Urbanomic intellectual world.

**West Bund Art District** (西岸)  
Major ongoing development of former industrial land along the Huangpu River in Xuhui District, Shanghai. Now houses **West Bund Art & Design** (major annual art fair), Long Museum, Museum of Art Pudong (MAP), various galleries. Transforming into Shanghai's primary contemporary art zone, analogous to what the South Bank is in London.

**DeTao Masters Academy (DTMA)** ⭐⭐  
Shanghai-based educational institution at the Shanghai Institute of Visual Arts (SIVA). Imports international "Masters" to teach advanced design, art, and technology in China. **Roy Ascott** is the DeTao Master of Technoetic Arts (appointed 2012), running the DeTao Technoetic Arts Studio from 2014. The Planetary Collegium's DeTao-Node Shanghai is hosted here. A bridge between Western art-tech-consciousness research and China's education infrastructure.

#### Hangzhou
**[[China Academy of Art (CAA)]]** (中国美术学院) ⭐⭐⭐  
Founded 1928 in Hangzhou, China's premier art institution. Key schools for the art-tech scene:
- **School of Intermedia Art** (跨媒体艺术学院) — Founded by [[Zhang Peili]], who created the New Media Art Department in the early 2000s. Trains generations of Chinese media artists. Key alumni: [[Lu Yang]], and many others across Chinese digital art. The genealogical origin of Chinese institutionalized new media art.
- **School of Design and Innovation** — Where **[[Botao Amber Hu]]** taught 2023–2025 (three courses: Design New Realities, Speculative Spatial Computing Design, Somaesthetic Realities). **Amber's Chinese institutional home.**
- **School of Architecture** — Led by Wang Shu (Pritzker Prize 2012).
CAA President **Gao Shiming** (高世名) has shaped the institution toward serious critical art-philosophy discourse. **[[Yuk Hui]]** is visiting professor. CAA Press published the Chinese edition of Yuk Hui's *Question Concerning Technology in China* (2021).

#### Shenzhen & Pearl River Delta
**Shenzhen & Hong Kong Bi-City Biennale of Urbanism/Architecture (UABB)** (深港城市建筑双城双年展) ⭐⭐  
Held since 2005. The world's only biennale dedicated to urbanism and architecture. Shenzhen is the appropriate host — China's first special economic zone, a city that grew from a fishing village to 17 million people in 40 years. The UABB consistently engages with technology, urban futures, and architecture's intersection with social transformation. **[[Chen Qiufan]]** participated in the 2019 edition. An important site for thinking about how technology reshapes the urban and social fabric at Chinese speed.

**OCAT (OCT Contemporary Art Terminal)** (华侨城当代艺术中心)  
Contemporary art institution network founded by OCT (Overseas Chinese Town) real estate group. Locations in Shenzhen, Shanghai, Xi'an. Focuses on experimental, interdisciplinary work. Part of how major Chinese real estate development companies support contemporary art as a legitimizing cultural gesture.

**Guangzhou Academy of Fine Arts (GZAFA)** (广州美术学院)  
Where **[[Cao Fei]]** studied (not CAA — common confusion). Important art academy in the Pearl River Delta region. The Pearl River Delta's factory culture and proximity to Hong Kong shaped a distinctive aesthetic sensibility in artists who came through here.

#### Hong Kong
**M+** ⭐⭐⭐  
Opened November 2021 in West Kowloon Cultural District. Director: Suhanya Raffel. Chief Curator: Doryun Chong. Collection: 20th-21st century visual art, design, architecture, moving image focused on Asia. Designed by Herzog & de Meuron. Immediately became one of the most important Asian art institutions. Has collected extensively in the new media and digital art area. Represents the Hong Kong establishment's ambition to rival Tate Modern, MoMA, Pompidou.

**K11 Art Foundation** ⭐⭐  
Founded by Adrian Cheng (New World Development). Private foundation focused on supporting Chinese contemporary art and arts education. Supports Chinese artists at Venice Biennale, major international exhibitions. Has collected work by **[[Lu Yang]]**. Also runs K11 MUSEA (cultural retail) in Hong Kong and Shanghai. Part of a new model of Chinese luxury-philanthropy that has become a major force in Chinese contemporary art funding.

**Para Site** (para-site.art)  
Hong Kong's leading independent art space for contemporary art. A crucial non-commercial venue that has published important critical writing on art from the Asia-Pacific region. **Gary Zhexi Zhang** co-curated an exhibition there ("The Weather In-Between: Monsoons, Markets and Other Derivative Atmospheres").

- **Geng Jianyi** (耿建翌, 1962–2017) ⭐⭐ — Co-founder of Pond Society. Conceptual artist. *The Second State* (1987). Died 2017. Worth own note.

## Events Circuit

### Biennials & Fairs
**Shanghai Biennale** ⭐⭐⭐  
China's most established contemporary art biennale. Founded 1996 by Fang Zengxian. At Power Station of Art since 2012. Thematically oriented — each edition has distinct curatorial framing. Has engaged with themes of technology, climate, ecology, and Chinese modernization. Key exhibition context for major Chinese and international artists.

**Shenzhen & Hong Kong Bi-City Biennale of Urbanism/Architecture (UABB)** ⭐⭐  
Since 2005. Urbanism, architecture, technology. Shenzhen's rapid growth as core subject.

**Art021** ⭐⭐  
Shanghai contemporary art fair. Major site for Chinese and international galleries. November each year.

**West Bund Art & Design** ⭐⭐  
Shanghai art fair, concurrent with Art021. The two fairs together make Shanghai Art Week a major international art market event.

**Art Basel Hong Kong** ⭐⭐⭐  
Major international art fair in Hong Kong. Entry point into Asian art market for international galleries. Combined with local art week activities.

### Festivals & Conferences
**SYSTEM Festival** (系统节 or 系统艺术节)  
Beijing-based art-technology festival. One of the key venues for digital and interactive art practice in Beijing. Brings together Chinese and international artists, curators, and technologists. Part of the developing ecosystem of art-tech events in China.

**Chronus Art Center annual program** ⭐⭐⭐  
Not a single festival but a continuous programming cycle that functions as the primary annual showcase for media art in Shanghai. CAC's exhibitions, residencies, workshops, and Leonardo lectures constitute the most significant ongoing art-tech programming in China.

**Beijing Design Week** ⭐⭐  
Annual design festival in Beijing. Technology and design intersection. Includes events at 798 Art Zone.

**China International Import Expo (CIIE) Cultural Programs**  
Shanghai's major international trade fair includes cultural programming with technology and art intersection. Less focused than art-specific events but represents scale of ambition.

## Global Connections & Circuits

### How Chinese Art-Tech Connects Globally
**Museum canonization circuit:** The lineage Zhang Peili → Lu Yang → others tracks the path of Chinese new media art into Western museum collections. MoMA acquired *Water: Standard Version from Cihai Dictionary* and others by Zhang Peili. Guggenheim, Pompidou, Art Institute of Chicago followed. Lu Yang and Cao Fei are now regularly in major Western museum shows.

**Venice Biennale:** The most important legitimizing event. Zhang Peili (three times), Lu Yang (Venice 2015), Cao Fei (multiple), and others. China's national pavilion has become a major statement each edition. The Chinese Pavilion at Venice 2015 included CAC's *Folklore of the Cyber World* as a parallel project.

**Ars Electronica:** Key connection point between Chinese media art and European media art festival circuit. CAC has programmed in connection with Ars Electronica; Chinese artists regularly show at Ars Electronica festivals.

**ZKM Karlsruhe:** Deep partnership with Chronus Art Center (*Datumsoria: The Return of the Real* co-presented). ZKM has also shown Chinese media art extensively.

**transmediale:** Berlin festival regularly includes Chinese artists in its technology-critique programming.

**Leonardo/ISAST:** The CAC-CAFA collaboration on the Leonardo Art, Science and Technology Lecture Series is a direct institutional bridge to the world's most important art-science journal network.

**British Council:** Gary Zhexi Zhang's 100-page report (2025) was commissioned by British Council, marking the UK's institutional interest in mapping the Chinese art-tech scene.

**Antikythera (Benjamin Bratton):** Chen Qiufan is an affiliate. The Bratton-adjacent network (Greenspan, Konior, Machine Decision is Not Final) is developing significant transnational connections between Western tech-philosophy and Chinese artists/institutions.

### Diaspora Networks
The scene has a significant diaspora component:
- **Lawrence Lek** (London) — Coined "Sinofuturism"; works internationally but engages with Chinese identity and technology
- **Sougwen Chung** (London) — Canadian-Chinese, robotics/drawing collaboration
- **Ian Cheng** (New York) — Chinese-American, ALife and simulation art
- **Gary Zhexi Zhang** (London) — Chinese-British, art-tech research and criticism
- **Yuk Hui** (multiple institutions) — Hong Kong philosopher at the center of cosmotechnics

These diaspora figures are often the interfaces between Chinese and international circuits — more mobile, more legible to Western institutions, but maintaining crucial connections to mainland practice and discourse.

### The Sinofuturism Question
"Sinofuturism" (coined by Lawrence Lek's 2016 video essay) has become a contested but productive concept: the observation that China/Chinese is already conflated with AI, robotics, mass production, and algorithmic futures in the Western imagination — and the art/theory question of what to do with that conflation. Yuk Hui pushes back against flat Sinofuturism by insisting on *cosmotechnics* — that there are multiple technological traditions rooted in different cosmological frameworks, and that Chinese technology cannot be reduced to either catching up with or mirroring Western modernity. The tension between these positions animates much of the best current work.

## Why Interesting

For Amber, this is not an abstract "interesting scene" — it is her institutional context. She taught here. She is building here. Her students are here. The philosophical traditions she draws on (cosmotechnics, somaesthetics) have deep roots here or active interlocutors here. The questions the scene is asking — about China's unique position in global technology development, about whether Chinese technological tradition offers different frameworks for understanding computation, about what it means to develop critical art practice inside one of the world's most surveilled and most dynamic technological cultures — are Amber's questions too.

The scene is also one of the most financially significant in the global contemporary art market (Chinese collectors are among the most important globally), one of the most institutionally ambitious (M+, PSA, UCCA represent world-class institutions), and one of the most intellectually interesting (Yuk Hui, Anna Greenspan, and Iris Long are developing frameworks unavailable elsewhere).

**Quality signals:** MoMA, Guggenheim, Pompidou, Pritzker Prize, Venice Biennale — the Chinese art-tech scene has already achieved the highest levels of Western institutional recognition. This is not peripheral practice waiting to be discovered; it is a mature scene with its own genealogy, quality standards, and global connections.
