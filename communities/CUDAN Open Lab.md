# CUDAN Open Lab

**Cultural Data Analytics Open Lab** at Tallinn University, Estonia. One of the most serious European computational humanities research environments — combining network science, machine learning, computational social science, and information design with art history, film studies, cultural semiotics, and digital humanities. Founded via an EU Horizon 2020 ERA Chair grant (€2.5M + €200k from Estonian Research Council, Grant No. 810961), running June 2020–August 2024. Now continuing 2024–2027 via Tallinn University Research Fund.

The CUDAN model was a deliberate experiment in **intentional academic mixing** — bringing together 12 researchers (6 postdoc Senior Fellows + 6 predoc Junior Fellows) across wildly different disciplines, then forcing cross-pollination. Result: **69 publications involving 103 co-authors** across the project's 4-year duration.

## Mission & Objectives

CUDAN coins and operationalizes **Cultural Data Analytics** (CUDAN) — a novel analytical approach at the intersection of:

1. **Machine learning for images/AV**: Analyzing visual material over historical timescales; detecting objects in artwork; style analysis via embeddings ("artificial neural science")
2. **Linguistic topic modelling + bipartite networks**: Mapping corpora structure and evolution; "palaeontology of memes"
3. **Temporal multilayer network analysis**: Cultural knowledge graphs; "network multiplicity" patterns
4. **Embed-everything approach**: Unifying multimedia/structured/unstructured data in a "multidimensional fluid-dynamics of meaning"
5. **Science-of-science methods**: Mapping achievements and limits of Cultural Data Analytics itself
6. **User experience design for academic mixing**: Studying and optimizing how multidisciplinary labs actually work

The lab frames itself as operating like a **multidisciplinary science lab** — rare in humanities, which tend toward solo scholarship. The ERA Chair research philosophy was high-risk/high-gain: deliberately crossing disciplinary boundaries ERC panels don't support.

## Key Publications & Outputs

### Landmark Papers
- [**"A Network Framework of Cultural History"**](https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/science.1247768) — Schich et al., *Science* 2014 (pre-CUDAN but foundational). 150K+ people birth-death data mapped cultural gravity. Nature video "Charting Culture" went viral.
- [**"Quantifying the global film festival circuit"**](https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pone.0297404) — Zemaityte et al., *PLOS ONE* 2024. Full data + code released. Maps the entire global film festival network.
- [**"Compression ensembles quantify aesthetic complexity"**](https://doi.org/10.1140/epjds/s13688-023-00397-3) — Karjus et al., *EPJ Data Science* 2023. Novel method using compression algorithms to measure aesthetic complexity in art history.
- [**"A framework for the analysis of historical newsreels"**](https://doi.org/10.1057/s41599-024-02886-w) — Oiva et al., *HASS Comms* 2024. Full team collaboration; computational methods for AV history.
- [**"Soviet View of the World"**](https://doi.org/10.22148/001c.118495) — Oiva et al., *Journal of Cultural Analytics* 2024. Long-term visual patterns in Soviet newsreels 1945-1992.
- [**"Perceived gendered self-representation on Tinder"**](https://doi.org/10.1057/s41599-024-03801-z) — Asadchy et al., *HASS Comms* 2024. ML applied to platform gender studies.
- [**"fruit-SALAD dataset"**](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41597-025-04529-4) — Ohm et al., *Scientific Data* 2025. Style-aligned artwork dataset for embedding similarity research.
- [**"Evolving linguistic divergence on polarizing social media"**](https://doi.org/10.1057/s41599-024-02922-9) — Karjus & Cuskley, *HASS Comms* 2024.

## Why Interesting

**For Amber specifically**: CUDAN is doing something rare — using rigorous computational methods to understand culture, arts, and media as systems. This resonates with Amber's interests in machine behavior, social simulation, and more-than-human design. The network science + art history synthesis that Schich pioneered is an intellectual cousin to Amber's agent ethology agenda (studying how agents behave in cultural/social systems).

**Broader significance**: CUDAN proves that European humanities institutions can run science labs properly — with open-source outputs, rigorous methodology, and high-profile publications. The "intentional academic mixing" model is itself a contribution to knowledge about how to do interdisciplinary work.

**The Tallinn angle**: Estonia is a genuinely interesting place for this work — a small nation with massive digital infrastructure (e-Estonia), post-Soviet context that CUDAN researchers engage with directly (the Soviet newsreel work), and an art/tech scene that doesn't get enough attention. The lab is a non-obvious node on the global cultural analytics map.

**Active seminar (open!)**: The weekly seminar series is one of the best ongoing intellectual forums in computational humanities — featuring figures like Karl Friston, Iyad Rahwan, Albert-László Barabási. Amber should subscribe/attend.
