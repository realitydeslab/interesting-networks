# Digital Democracies Institute (DDI)

Research institute at [Simon Fraser University](https://www.sfu.ca/) founded and directed by [[Wendy Chun]]. Investigates the intersection of **digital media, democracy, misinformation, and algorithmic discrimination**. Interdisciplinary and explicitly critical: combines computational methods with cultural studies, critical theory, and social justice frameworks. The institute has ~20 researchers (faculty fellows, postdocs, PhDs, data scientists, staff) and runs 5+ active research projects.

## Mission & Objectives
"We investigate the impacts of mis- and disinformation on society." More broadly: to understand how digital media shapes democracy, with a particular focus on algorithmic discrimination, polarization, and the erosion of epistemic commons.

## Active Research Projects
1. **[Data Fluencies](https://digitaldemocracies.org/data-fluencies/)** — arts/humanities + data science for understanding misinformation. Partners include SSRC (USA), York University, Canterbury University, USC, Emerson, Kentucky.
2. **Beyond Verification** — algorithmic authenticity, polarizing trust, disinformation platforms
3. **From Hate to Agonism** — hate speech, online conflict, agonistic democracy
4. **[BRIDGE Research Consortium](https://digitaldemocracies.org/research/bridge-research-consortium/)** — vaccine misinformation, Canada's Immuno-Engineering Hub
5. **[Imaginative Methods Lab](https://digitaldemocracies.org/research/imaginative-methods/)** — experimental research methods, practice-based interdisciplinary inquiry
6. **First Languages AI Reality (FLAIR)** — Indigenous language AI

## Why Interesting
DDI is the rare institute that simultaneously does: empirical data science (Albrecht, Canute, Vincent), critical theory (Chun, Toscano), history of computing (Dick), and international collaborative research (Data Fluencies). The combination is unusual and productive. Founded by one of the most important critical digital studies scholars alive.
