# Critical Times

*Critical Times: Interventions in Global Critical Theory* — open-access, peer-reviewed journal published by [Duke University Press](https://www.dukeupress.edu/critical-times), launched 2018. A project of the **International Consortium of Critical Theory Programs (ICCTP)**, based at UC Berkeley and funded by the Andrew W. Mellon Foundation. Now in Volume 9 (2026), publishing 3 issues per year.

Explicitly positions itself as the global counterpart to *Critical Inquiry* (Chicago) and *Theory, Culture & Society* — but with deliberate emphasis on theory from the **Global South**, the **southern hemisphere**, and **cross-regional intellectual exchange** beyond Euro-American academia. Publishes scholarly essays, interviews, dialogues, dispatches, **visual art**, and various forms of critical reflection. Fully open access (Creative Commons BY-NC-ND); no APCs; authors retain copyright.

## Mission & Objectives

Critical Times is committed to the intellectual and political project of **critical theory** as a globally distributed, collectively reinvented practice — not a European export. Key commitments:

- **Southern theory** — centering voices from Africa, Latin America, Asia, Middle East, and the Caribbean
- **Cross-regional dialogue** — fostering exchange across traditions that rarely speak to each other
- **Form diversity** — includes artistic interventions alongside scholarly essays
- **Open knowledge** — no paywalls, no APCs; institutional openness as political practice

The journal consciously names itself "critical times" — the present as a period demanding urgent theoretical intervention.

## Key Contributions

Critical Times has established itself as the **premier venue for global critical theory** outside the US/European mainstream. Significant because:

- **Wang Hui on China's modernity** — reframes Chinese intellectual history as theoretically generative rather than derivative of Western categories
- **Mbembe on necropolitics** — extends Foucault's biopolitics concept into African colonial contexts
- **Cross-regional comparatism** — enables theoretical dialogue between Latin American decolonial thought and South Asian subaltern studies
- **Artistic Interventions** — legitimizes visual and artistic practice as theoretical production, not just illustration
- **Emergences section** — rapid-response criticism; can address contemporary events (pandemic, algorithmic governance, climate) without waiting for full peer review
