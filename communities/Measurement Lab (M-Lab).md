# Measurement Lab (M-Lab)

[Measurement Lab (M-Lab)](https://www.measurementlab.net/) is an open, globally distributed network measurement platform providing the world's largest source of open data on internet performance. Co-founded by [[Meredith Whittaker]] at Google, in partnership with New America Foundation's Open Technology Institute, the PlanetLab Consortium, Google, and academic researchers. Data is publicly available under open licenses.

## Mission
Provide open, verifiable, science-grade measurements of internet performance globally. Counter the ISP practice of reporting self-serving network performance data by creating independent, auditable, open measurements that anyone can use.

## Why Interesting
M-Lab is an example of critical infrastructure as argument — Whittaker built it as a response to the political problem of ISPs controlling data about their own network performance. The design principle ("measure independently, release openly") is a form of structural critique embodied in infrastructure. Connects to Amber's interest in how technical architectures embody political values.
