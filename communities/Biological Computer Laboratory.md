# Biological Computer Laboratory (UIUC, 1958–1975)

Research lab founded by [[Heinz von Foerster]] at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign. The institutional home of **second-order cybernetics** — the study of observing systems, self-organization, and the role of the observer in the observed system. Active 1958–1975. Produced some of the most radical thinking in cybernetics, including work by [[Gordon Pask]], Humberto Maturana, and Francisco Varela.

## Key People
- **[[Heinz von Foerster]]** — founder and director
- **[[Gordon Pask]]** — conversation theory, cybernetics of education
- **Humberto Maturana** — developed autopoiesis here (with Varela); *Autopoiesis and Cognition* (1980)
- **Francisco Varela** — autopoiesis, embodied cognition, consciousness studies; later *The Embodied Mind* with Maturana & Thompson

## Why Interesting
The BCL is where second-order cybernetics was forged: the shift from "how do systems work?" to "how do we know how systems work?" (observer-included systems). This is philosophically foundational to complexity science, constructivism, and the posthumanist turn. Maturana and Varela's autopoiesis — the theory that living systems are self-producing circular processes — was developed here and became essential background for ALife research.
