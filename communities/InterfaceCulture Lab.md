# Interface Cultures Lab

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-03-01:** Deep research completed. Major update: Manuela Naveau appointed Professor for Interface Cultures / Critical Data, December 1, 2025. Website migrated from interface.ufg.ac.at to kunstuni-linz.at.
- **2026-02-26:** Stub created via Christa Sommerer & Laurent Mignonneau deep research.

Master's degree program and teaching lab at the [University of Arts Linz (Kunstuniversität Linz)](https://www.kunstuni-linz.at/), Austria, formerly Universität für künstlerische und industrielle Gestaltung (UFG). Founded 2004 by [[Christa Sommerer]] and [[Laurent Mignonneau]], who ran it together until recently. As of December 1, 2025, [[Manuela Naveau]] is the new full Professor for Interface Cultures / Critical Data — a generational handover with a clear ideological shift toward critical data studies and feminist AI. Sommerer and Mignonneau remain on the team.

The program sits at the intersection of interactive art, interface design, critical data, and participatory practice. Its motto: **"All life is interface."** The ALife lineage from Sommerer/Mignonneau is still very present but is now crossbred with critical theory, ecological computing, and feminist tech critique under Naveau.

Located at Domgasse 1, Linz (lecture room at Hauptplatz 23) — literally a few minutes from the [[Ars Electronica]] Center. Annual student exhibition at the Ars Electronica Festival since 2005 is one of the most important institutional relationships in European media art education.

## Program Structure

**MA Interface Cultures** — 2 years / 120 ECTS. Located at Kunstuniversität Linz.

**Core modules:**
- Interface Technologies I
- Interactive Art and Media Studies
- Interface Labor I + Advanced Interface Labor (studio/prototyping)
- Free Electives
- Colloquium / Master Thesis
- Practical Work + Theoretical Work

**Focus areas:** VR/AR · interactive art · sound art · game design · sustainable IT · HCI research · interaction design · critical data

**Lab:** Physical prototyping space at Domgasse 1. Philosophy of the lab: "narratives of entanglement, collaboration, and co-becoming." Methodologies respond to "ecological and economic collapse, toxic accumulation and technological alienation." Not escapism — getting in trouble productively.

## Research

### Critical Data Research Group
The department's primary research initiative, built by Manuela Naveau and Alexander Wöran. Investigates the relationship between artists and data systems — how artistic works reveal how digitalization works (and fails). Bridges digital art, big/small data, machine learning, ethics, feminist philosophy, and political commitments.

**Key activities:**
- **Feminist AI Lectures** — ongoing lecture series, archived at [mediaspace.ufg.at](https://mediaspace.ufg.at/channel/Feminist+AI+Lecture+Series/441141)
- **Critical Data On Tour** — research posters + artistic interventions in public space, in collaboration with WIP Festival, CYENS Centre Cyprus (2024). With Vladan Joler.
- **Guest talk partnership with SKKU (Sungkyunkwan University, Korea), Fernando Velázquez, Julia Kloiber** — shown at Ars Electronica

**Publications:**

## Ideas & Themes

**Philosophical core:** Interface as ontological category — "All life is interface." Not just GUI design. Interface as the zone where bodies, machines, data, and environments meet, conflict, and co-become.

**The Sommerer/Mignonneau ALife tradition:** Living systems as art. Generative life-as-art from the 1990s forward (LP Interactive Plant Growing, 1993). This lineage is the DNA of the program.

**The Naveau turn:** Arriving after 20 years of the Sommerer/Mignonneau aesthetic, Naveau brings critical data studies, feminist AI critique, and the question of who these systems serve and who they exclude. Where Sommerer/Mignonneau asked "what can computation do?" Naveau asks "who does computation benefit, and at what cost?"

**Permacomputing thread:** January 2026 guest talk "For a Permacomputing Perspective in Creative Computing" signals growing engagement with low-power/ecological computing aesthetics. Natural extension of sustainable IT focus.

**Lab philosophy:** "narratives of entanglement, collaboration, and co-becoming." Responding to ecological and economic collapse, not by escaping into utopian fiction, but by "enjoying the challenge of getting in trouble." A praxis-first ethos.

**Linz as media art ecosystem:** The city has Ars Electronica Center, Ars Electronica Festival, OK Linz, KAPU venue, Stadtwerkstatt — a dense infrastructure for media art. Interface Cultures students don't just train in an academic context, they participate in a live scene.

The Critical Data Research Group's work on feminist AI is relevant to Amber's interest in machine behavior and ethics. The ALife tradition is a direct intellectual ancestor of Agent Ethology.
