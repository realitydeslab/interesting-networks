# Ida B. Wells Just Data Lab

Critical data research lab at Princeton University, founded by [[Ruha Benjamin]] in 2018. Named after Ida B. Wells — the 19th-century investigative journalist who used data to document lynching. The lab brings together students, educators, activists, and artists to "rethink and retool data for justice." Runs Artist-in-Residence program, Tech Freedom School, and mentors student researchers.

## Events & Programmes
- **Artist-in-Residence** — year-long residency for artists working on data justice projects
- **Tech Freedom School** — summer initiative for Black college students working on AI bias; profiled by Word in Black
- **Critical + Creative Career Fair** — connects students to justice-oriented tech careers
- **Civics of Technology Conference** — annual conference; JDL presents research

## Why Interesting
One of the few academic labs that treats art as a primary research method for data justice questions. The Artist-in-Residence model is particularly interesting — [[Mimi Onuoha]] and Chanika Svetvilas are serious artists, not just outreach fodder. The Ida B. Wells naming strategy is also smart: it historicizes the project (data journalism as investigative justice tool goes back to 1890s) and gives it political legitimacy beyond "tech ethics."

For Amber: the model of an academic lab with artist residencies could be adapted for machine behavior / agentic systems research at Oxford.
