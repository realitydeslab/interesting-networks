# Superflux

London-based speculative design studio and research practice founded by **Anab Jain** and **Jon Ardern** in 2009. Currently the most influential active speculative design studio globally — the canonical example of design fiction done seriously, with institutional reach and political commitment. Known for immersive, provocative experiential futures work around climate, AI governance, and more-than-human politics. Recipients of the Dezeen **Design Studio of the Year 2021** and the **Royal Designers for Industry (RDI) 2022** — both firsts for a speculative design studio.

Superflux straddles an unusual position: they do serious client work (Google AI, IKEA, DeepMind, UK Cabinet Office) while maintaining a genuine research/art practice that shows at Venice Biennale and MAK Vienna. The studio is London-based but Anab leads an academic programme in Vienna. Their work has defined the vocabulary of "more-than-human design" for the last decade.

## Mission & Objectives

Superflux's stated mission: "Translating Future Uncertainty into Present Day Choices." They use design fiction, speculative artifacts, critical foresight, and immersive installations to make abstract futures *felt* rather than just understood.

Core intellectual moves:
1. **Experiential futures** — build actual objects, rooms, installations that let people inhabit a future scenario, not just read about it
2. **More-than-human design** — since 2018, argued that human-centred design is insufficient; design must account for multispecies, ecological, and non-human perspectives
3. **Actionable hope** — reject both techno-optimism and doom; offer futures that are challenging but navigable
4. **Ecological AI** — emerging framework (2023–): AI systems trained on ecological wisdom, indigenous knowledge, and local stewardship, not just data extraction

They're pioneers in applying speculative design to *governance* and *policy* — unusual for a design studio; most speculative work stays in galleries. Work for UK Cabinet Office, UK Policy Lab, DEFRA, UNDP, DeepMind gives them a policy reach most art studios don't have.

## Key Projects & Publications

### Landmark Works (chronological, major):

**[Nobody Told Me Rivers Dream](https://superflux.in/index.php/work/nobody-told-me-rivers-dream/)** (2025)
AI + ecological intelligence installation. Three handcrafted sensor objects monitor birdsong, tidal flows, atmospheric patterns along River Thames. AI models trained on scientific data *and* folklore/indigenous ecological wisdom. On display at Design Museum London: "More than Human: Making with the Living World" (July 2025).

**[Action Speaks at New York Climate Week](https://superflux.in/index.php/work/action-speaks-2024/)** (2024)
Commission for IKEA/Ingka Group. Modular, circular exhibition for business leaders during NY Climate Week 2024.

**[A Just World on a Safe Planet](https://superflux.in/index.php/work/a-just-world-on-a-safe-planet/)** (2024)
Digital triptych artwork for Earth Commission's landmark planetary boundaries report. Collaboration with Future Earth and Earth Commission scientists.

**[The Quiet Enchanting](https://superflux.in/index.php/work/the-quiet-enchanting/)** (2024)
Speculative frieze on façade of Bush House, Strand, London. Commissioned by King's Culture. A mythical narrative of "collective re-enchantment" and transformed worlds.

**[The Ecological Intelligence Agency](https://superflux.in/index.php/work/the-ecological-intelligence-agency/)** (2023)
Commissioned by Policy Lab (UK) + Defra Futures. Proposes "Ecological AI" — autonomous inter-departmental agency of localised AI models advocating for ecological flourishing, co-developed with local stewards and indigenous knowledge. Imagines AI speaking *on behalf of rivers* in policymaking. First iteration of Superflux's ongoing Ecological AI research.

**[SAFE: A Collection of Works](https://superflux.in/index.php/work/safe-a-collection-of-works-exploring-safer-futures/)** (2022)
Commissioned by Lloyd's Register Foundation. Critical foresight + speculative design on safer futures.

**[It's Easier to Imagine the End of Capitalism than the End of the World](https://superflux.in/index.php/work/its-easier-to-imagine-the-end-of-capitalism-than-the-end-of-the-world/)** (2022)
Serpentine North Gallery commission — reimagining the gallery's "shop" for their Back To Earth show.

**[Refuge for Resurgence](https://superflux.in/index.php/work/refuge-for-resurgence/)** (2021)
Venice Architecture Biennale 2021 (La Biennale di Venezia). Multispecies banquet: fox, rat, wasp, pigeon, cow, human, wild boar, snake, beaver, wolf, raven and mushroom gathered in the ruins of modernity — an image of post-Anthropocene community. Co-curated with Hashim Sarkis. Photographed by Giorgio Lazzaro. Funded by EU Horizon 2020 / CreaTures project.

**[Invocation for Hope](https://superflux.in/index.php/work/invocation-for-hope-3/)** (2021)
Vienna Biennale 2021. Large-scale immersive installation at MAK Vienna. 400+ fire-blackened pine trees in a perfect grid — a devastated forest — with a resurgent living oasis at the centre. One of the most-reviewed speculative design works of the year (Dezeen, Fast Company, ORF).

**[The Vault of Life](https://superflux.in/index.php/work/the-vault-of-life-in-museum-of-the-future/)** (2021)
Museum of the Future, Dubai — exhibition on becoming Climate Positive. Commissioned by Dubai Future Foundation.

**[Assembling Our Future: An Exhibition for IKEA](https://superflux.in/index.php/work/assembling-our-future-an-exhibition-for-ikea/)** (2021)
COP26 commission for IKEA/Ingka Group.

**[The Intersection](https://superflux.in/index.php/work/the-intersection/)** (2020)
Critical foresight + speculative design project on regenerative ambient tech futures. Commissioned by Omidyar Network.

**[Mitigation of Shock (London)](https://superflux.in/index.php/work/mitigation-of-shock/)** (2017)
Defining work. A fully-built future London apartment set in 2050 — climate-changed city with food insecurity, resource scarcity. Visitors walk through and inhabit the space. First shown at CCCB Barcelona ("After the End of the World" show). Traveled internationally.

**[Mitigation of Shock (Singapore)](https://superflux.in/index.php/work/mitigation-of-shock-singapore/)** (later)
Second iteration at ArtScience Museum, Singapore.

**[Future(s) of Power – Algorithmic Power](https://superflux.in/index.php/work/future-of-democracy-algorithmic-power/)** (2019/2020)
Somerset House Studios. Citizens Assembly experiment using sortition for Algorithmic Power — speculative governance.

**[Our Friends Electric](https://superflux.in/index.php/work/friends-electric/)** (2018–)
Design fiction on voice AI alternatives. Commissioned by Mozilla Open IoT Studio (Michelle Thorne + Jon Rogers).

**[DeepMind](https://superflux.in/index.php/work/deepmind/)** (ongoing)
Researching alternate ways of thinking about AI systems. Client: DeepMind.

**[Google AI](https://superflux.in/index.php/work/google-ai/)** (ongoing)
Exploring near future worlds around AI and climate change. Client: Google AI.

**[The Future Starts Here](https://superflux.in/index.php/work/the-future-starts-here/)** (2018)
Films for V&A Museum's landmark "The Future Starts Here" show. Co-commissioned with Office of Political Innovation.

**[MANTIS SYSTEMS](https://superflux.in/index.php/work/mantissystems/)** (2018)
Speculative design intervention for international development sector. Commissioned by BOND, UNDP, NESTA.

**[STARK CHOICES](https://superflux.in/index.php/work/stark-choices/)** (2018)
Immersive simulation of life in 2030 with work/automation changes. Presented at Global Education & Skills Forum 2018 for the Varkey Foundation.

**[Drone Aviary](https://superflux.in/index.php/work/drone-aviary/)** (2015–2018)
Speculative ecosystem of commercial drones in London. Shown at ZKM Karlsruhe, Kunstverein Hannover, and internationally.

**[Uninvited Guests](https://superflux.in/index.php/work/uninvited-guests/)** (2015)
Film about smart home devices and elder care. Widely discussed in design fiction circles.

## Key Contributions

Superflux have made several lasting contributions to the field:

1. **Mainstreaming experiential futures** — built actual rooms/installations not just prototypes; made speculative design *felt* rather than merely seen. Mitigation of Shock became the canonical example.
2. **More-than-Human Centred Design concept** — Anab's 2018 IXDA keynote introduced this concept that has since become a major discourse in design, HCI, and STS.
3. **Design fiction × policy** — pioneered applying speculative design to government clients (UK Cabinet Office, DEFRA, UNDP). Made it viable as a policy tool.
4. **Ecological AI framing** — the EIA (2023) and *Nobody Told Me Rivers Dream* (2025) are early examples of a new genre: AI systems designed *in the service of* ecological intelligence rather than extracting from it.
5. **Studio as cultural institution** — showed that a design studio could maintain both commercial and institutional practice simultaneously.
