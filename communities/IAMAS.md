# IAMAS — Institute of Advanced Media Arts and Sciences

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Deep research completed. Expanded from stub to full profile with complete history, faculty profiles, alumni network, Sommerer connection, YCAM relationship, and strategic analysis.

Japan's most intensely serious graduate institution for art × technology. Founded 1996 in Ogaki, Gifu Prefecture — emphatically not Tokyo. The geographic choice is a policy statement: the school selects for commitment over convenience. Over 25+ years it has produced Japan's most technically sophisticated and artistically rigorous media artists, including the [[Daito Manabe]]–[[Motoi Ishibashi]] axis that became [[Rhizomatiks]]. The alumni network IS a significant proportion of Japan's advanced creative technology scene. [[Christa Sommerer and Laurent Mignonneau]] were Associate Professors here 1997–99 and 2001–04, transferring the entire tradition of European ALife art into Japan before returning to Linz.

## Mission & Objectives
**Mission:** Foster leaders of industrial and artistic culture who bridge media arts, sciences, and technology. Graduate-only. Small cohort (capacity ~20 students per year). Research-focused. Non-departmental — students work in research fields and projects rather than disciplines.

**Core Philosophy:** A single graduate school, a single major. No undergraduate, no adjacent programs. This is structural seriousness: every student is here to do original research. The institution explicitly positions itself as a "unique educational and research institution" — the phrase captures both the modesty and the ambition.

**Pedagogical Approach:** From 2001 to 2012, students worked within a "studio system" (initially 4 studios, then 5). From 2012, reorganized into a "research field system," then into the current "project system" (from 2014). Each reorganization reflects a move toward more fluid, cross-disciplinary collaboration organized around problems rather than techniques.

**RCIC (Research Center for Industrial Cultures):** Established in 2001 as the Cultural Media Center, reorganized in 2010 with a mission to "promote local industries" alongside artistic practice. IAMAS sees itself as an anchor institution for regional development in Gifu.

## History & Founding

**1996:** Gifu Prefecture establishes the **Gifu Prefectural International Academy of Media Arts and Sciences (IAMAS)** as a European-type vocational school. Positioned as part of the [Softopia Japan](https://www.softopia.or.jp/) complex — Gifu's technology park initiative. Two departments from the start: Art and Media Laboratory Department (graduate-level, producing media masters) and Multimedia Studio Department (training information specialists). **[[Itsuo Sakane]]** appointed first Academy President. Artist-in-residence program launched. The logo was designed by Yasuhito Nagahara.

**1997:** Student dormitory (RIST) completed. **[[Christa Sommerer and Laurent Mignonneau]]** join as Associate Professors — bringing the Vienna/Frankfurt/NTT ICC tradition of ALife art directly into IAMAS.

**2000:** New school building completed in Ryoukecho. Four-course multimedia studio laboratory system introduced.

**2001:** **Institute of Advanced Media Arts and Sciences** formally established as a graduate university — one major, one graduate school. The Cultural Media Center (CMC) established as subsidiary organization for publishing and exhibitions. The IAMAS acronym deliberately captures both schools: "Institute of Advanced Media Arts and Sciences" and "International Academy of Media Arts and Sciences."

**2002:** Multimedia Laboratory Department of the original vocational school closed. **[[Daito Manabe]]** and **[[Motoi Ishibashi]]** are among the early cohorts of the new graduate program — they meet at IAMAS and later co-found Rhizomatiks (2006). Manabe began part-time lecturing elsewhere from 2004, suggesting he completed his IAMAS program ~2002–2004.

**2003:** Tadashi Yokoyama appointed 2nd President.

**2005:** Exchange student agreement with the [University of Art and Design Linz](https://www.kunstuni-linz.at/) — cementing the Sommerer connection institutionally.

**2006:** 10th anniversary. Studio system expanded from 4 to 5 studios.

**2009:** Atsuhito Sekiguchi appointed 3rd President. Artist-in-residence system discontinued.

**2010:** CMC reorganized into Research Center for Industrial Cultures (RCIC).

**2011:** International Academy of Media Arts and Sciences (the original 1996 vocational school) shut down per Gifu Prefecture "Action Plan" — ending its 16-year history. Agreement with National Institute of Technology Gifu College.

**2012:** Media Creation Department reorganized from studio system to research field system. 

**2013:** Shigeki Yoshida appointed 4th President.

**2014:** School relocates to **Softopia Japan complex** (permanent address: 4-1-7 Kagano, Ogaki-shi, Gifu 503-0006). Research field system reorganized into Project system.

**2017:** 20th anniversary. **[[Masahiro Miwa]]** appointed 5th President.

**2021:** Doctoral program established. **Nobuya Suzuki** appointed 6th President (current).

**2026:** 24th graduating cohort (IAMAS2026).

- **Matsui Shigeru** — Dean, Professor. Poetry and art criticism. Researches contemporary art experiences from the context of "boring" in 1960s American and Japanese art scenes.
- **Akabane Kyo** — Director of RCIC, Professor. Interaction design. Develops workshops for media creation; spatiotemporal archive for interactive art using 3D scanning.
- **Kobayashi Shigeru** — Library Director, Professor. Philosophy of Technology. Author of "Prototyping Lab 2nd Edition." General Director of **[Ogaki Mini Maker Faire](https://ogaki.minimaker.jp/)** since 2014 — a biennial maker festival that brings IAMAS directly into the city's public life.

## Key Connections

### IAMAS → Rhizomatiks Pipeline
The most famous IAMAS output. Manabe and Ishibashi met at IAMAS and co-founded [Rhizomatiks](https://rhizomatiks.com/) in 2006 after graduating. Rhizomatiks became Japan's premier creative technology collective. This pipeline is the clearest example of IAMAS's function as infrastructure for Japan's creative technology ecosystem.

### IAMAS ↔ ATR (Advanced Telecommunications Research)
[[ATR Media Integration and Communications Research Laboratories]] in Kyoto was Sommerer & Mignonneau's primary institutional base during their IAMAS period. ATR-IAMAS represents a hardware of Japan's art-technology ecosystem: telecom research and arts education in close collaboration.

### IAMAS ↔ Ars Electronica
Itsuo Sakane (IAMAS founding president) was a key organizer of Ars Electronica's "Interactive Art" section. Sommerer's Golden Nica (1994) preceded her IAMAS period. The institutional relationship is deep.

### IAMAS ↔ University of Art and Design Linz
Formal exchange agreement since 2005. This formalizes the Sommerer connection — she teaches at Linz while IAMAS students can study there and vice versa.

### IAMAS ↔ YCAM
[[YCAM]] (Yamaguchi Center for Arts and Media, founded 2003) and IAMAS are the two most technically serious art institutions in Japan, both deliberately located outside Tokyo. They represent complementary models: IAMAS is educational infrastructure (graduate school, 25+ years of alumni), YCAM is production infrastructure (R&D center, commissions, residencies). The Japanese media art scene flows between them. Faculty from IAMAS have collaborated with YCAM and the networks overlap substantially.

### IAMAS ↔ NTT ICC
[NTT InterCommunication Center (ICC)](https://www.ntticc.or.jp/en/) in Tokyo has been a key exhibition and residency site for IAMAS-connected artists (Sommerer, Manabe, etc.) for decades.

### Softopia Japan Ecosystem
IAMAS is embedded in [Softopia Japan](https://www.softopia.or.jp/), Gifu Prefecture's technology park. Since relocating in 2014, IAMAS literally operates within the technology park. The RCIC's mission of "promoting local industries" reflects this institutional integration — IAMAS is simultaneously a cutting-edge art school and a regional economic development tool.

## Key Publications & Output
- **IAMAS2020+ / IAMAS2024 etc.** — Annual graduation exhibitions (February each year) with archive films on IAMAS Channel.
- **Cultural Media Center publications** — Exhibition catalogs and research publications from 2001–2010.
- Student and faculty research is published through academic journals and conferences in media arts, HCI, and information engineering.
- **The IAMAS tradition emphasizes output as process, not product** — the studio/project system is designed to produce researchers and practitioners, not primarily publications.

## Events & Programmes

### Annual Graduation Exhibition (IAMAS20XX)
Every February. Each graduating cohort presents its thesis projects. These are public events open to application. IAMAS2024 archive available on IAMAS Channel. **IAMAS2026 (24th cohort) announced for February 2026.**

### Open House / School Information Sessions
For prospective students. Held several times per year. Can be attended remotely.

### Ogaki Mini Maker Faire
Biennial maker festival held in Ogaki City, Gifu, organized by IAMAS Library Director Kobayashi Shigeru. [https://ogaki.minimaker.jp/](https://ogaki.minimaker.jp/)

### Admission
- **Master's Program:** Annual intake ~20 students. Entrance exam (portfolio, interview). Research Student Program available. Student Exchange Program with Linz.
- **Doctoral Program:** Established 2021. Small cohort.
- School fees: Japanese public university rates (not expensive by global standards).

## For Amber

### Why Interesting
**The primary institutional node for Japanese media art.** IAMAS produced the artists Amber already follows (Manabe, Ishibashi). It's also the Japanese site of the ALife art tradition she traces through Sommerer. Understanding IAMAS means understanding how Japan's art-technology scene was built institutionally — a model worth knowing.

**The IAMAS model:** Small cohort + serious technical training + non-Tokyo = self-selected community of highly committed artists. The intentional regionalism is interesting for someone thinking about how institutions shape artistic practice.

**Masahiro Miwa:** Former president, Honorary Professor, composer working at intersection of algorithmic composition and media art. Understudied figure in Western circles.

**Yukiko Shikata:** Part-time lecturer at IAMAS, one of Japan's most important media art curators and theorists. Key bridge figure between Japanese and international media art discourse.

## Why Interesting

**Japan's most important art-technology graduate school.** IAMAS has operated for nearly 30 years, consistently producing the people who define Japanese media art. Its alumni include the founders of Rhizomatiks and the institutional home for Sommerer & Mignonneau's most productive period. It's the institutional infrastructure behind everything that looks like "Japanese media art."

**The non-Tokyo model.** IAMAS's deliberate location in Ogaki (not Tokyo, not Osaka) means it has consistently attracted students who prioritize the work over career positioning. This self-selection process has produced remarkably high-quality alumni. The model is worth understanding.

**European connection.** The Sommerer link and the Linz exchange agreement mean IAMAS is not insular — it's a node in the global European-Japanese art-technology network.

**For Amber specifically:** Understanding IAMAS contextualizes Manabe (who she already follows), explains where Japanese media art comes from institutionally, and opens potential routes into Japan's creative technology scene.
