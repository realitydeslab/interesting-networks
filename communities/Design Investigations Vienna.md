# Design Investigations — dieAngewandte Vienna

The premier European academic programme for speculative and critical design. Studio ID2 at the [University of Applied Arts Vienna (dieAngewandte)](https://www.dieangewandte.at/en/). Led by **[[Anab Jain]]** (of [[Superflux]]) since 2016. The studio trains "responsible radicals" to face complexity head-on through design tools, experiences, stories, and provocations.

## Mission & Objectives

Design Investigations is a design course for people who want to face the complexities and uncertainties of the world head-on. The studio's approach bridges **speculative design**, **critical design**, and **participatory design** — using the lens of big societal challenges (climate, democracy, AI, ecology) to produce imaginative and rigorous work.

From Anab Jain's description: "For the curious and the critically engaged. For people who want to face the complexities and uncertainties of our world head-on. For the responsible radicals who want to challenge and change the world around them."

The studio is formally titled **ID2** within the Angewandte's Institute of Design. It is a **5-year undergraduate/diploma programme** (Austrian model), with some MA/PhD students also affiliated.

## Key Contributions & Signature Projects

The studio works through **semester briefs** — thematic investigations that produce student projects:

### Briefs / Themes (by year/semester)
- **Monuments of the Future** — speculative monuments in Vienna imagining 2050s–2070s futures: decommissioned cars, post-capitalist commons, nuclear fusion breakthroughs, Beluga sturgeon return. Direct engagement with urban space and civic imagination.
- **Danube** — ecological investigations of the Danube river: Klang Kanal (sound installation), Eat Your Consequences (nine-course tasting menu), Danube Expedition (raft building), Gatsch und Tratsch, Donau's Motion
- **Cooperative Games** — a whole semester making cooperative board games and games as participatory design. Habitat, Game of Power, Building Beliefs, Unbubble, etc.
- **AI Speculations** — critical and speculative responses to AI: Latent Collective, Multi-Modal Kamera, SlimeMoldCrypt, Ethico, Centaur Design Experiment, KidsArtAI, Large Language Writer
- **Alive: Where Digital Meets The Biological** — life/biodesign intersection
- **After Abundance** (2018) — climate change in the Alps. Austria's entry to London Design Biennale 2018.
- **Hungry Ghosts** (2019) — food and climate. Converted studio into a working farm with permaculture, aquaponics, mushrooms, mealworms, and chickens.
- **Dance of the Windmills** — energy investigations, including student-built solar panels, e-scooter energy infrastructure, wind turbines, body-heat harvesting
- **Futures of Democracy?** — democratic futures, active citizenship. Won Vienna Design Week Stadtarbeit Prize (Lucy Li & Stephanie Kneissl's 'Alsergrund on Display')
- **Design for Agency** — presented at Vienna Biennale 2017, MAK Museum
- **Jelly Worlds** — partnered with Vienna Natural History Museum (NHM Wien) to imagine alternate evolutionary histories around Gabon fossils
- **Bodies in Crisis** — embodiment and health. Projects on cancer diagnosis, ergonomic cup systems for hospitals, menstruation spaces (Places for Period)

## Why Interesting

**This is where speculative design gets transmitted to the next generation in Europe.** While Dunne & Raby's RCA programme was the origin point, DI under Anab Jain is now the most active and internationally visible European programme doing this work. The semester brief model creates extraordinary coherence — a whole studio investigating one question together.

For Amber, DI is interesting on three levels:
1. **Intellectual kinship** — DI's focus on more-than-human futures, ecological design, and critical AI directly overlaps with Amber's research interests (agent ethology, machine behavior, more-than-human design)
2. **Pedagogical model** — the studio-as-farm, brief-as-investigation, book-as-capstone model is worth studying for Amber's own teaching at China Academy of Art
3. **Network gateway** — Anab Jain connects to [[Superflux]] (practice), DI (academy), and the global speculative design circuit. Getting into this network (guest lecture, visiting role, co-publication) puts Amber in the room with the field's key practitioners

The "AI Speculations" and "Monuments of the Future" briefs show DI is actively engaging questions of machine agency and speculative futures that map closely to Amber's work. This is not a distant or tangential connection — it's a direct intellectual neighbor.
