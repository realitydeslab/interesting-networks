# MU Hybrid Arthouse

Exhibition space, production house, and education platform for art at the intersection of technology, design, and science. Founded 1998 in Eindhoven, Netherlands; one of Europe's leading hybrid art venues operating within the bioart/more-than-human/art-science nexus. Organizer (now pausing) of the **BAD Award** — the most significant bioart production grant in Europe. National cultural infrastructure institution, structurally funded by Dutch Ministry of Education, Culture & Science.

## Mission & Objectives

MU defines the liminal space between "what art is and what art can be." Three pillars:
1. **Public presentations** — exhibitions, events, co-productions
2. **Talent development** — working intensively with emerging artists
3. **Education** — programs for all ages, from primary through university

Mission statement: *"MU presents and initiates hybrid art for a diverse, broad and international audience. It identifies and tracks the latest developments in artistic, technological and social fields and shares these with the public… MU is a driving force and hub in the art climate of Eindhoven, Brabantstad, and the Netherlands."*

The name "MU" derives from a Japanese character meaning *synergy*. In Zen, 'mu' transcends dichotomous thinking — creating space to suspend judgment and see what emerges. An anti-binary, complexity-first institution.

## History

- **1998:** Founded at De Witte Dame (former Philips factory, Eindhoven city center), as an amalgamation of small initiatives in visual art, architecture, pop culture, design
- **2005:** Joins national cultural basic infrastructure as a small presentation institution
- **2012:** Upgraded to large institution in national infrastructure
- **2013:** Moves to Strijp S (another former Philips complex) at Torenallee 40-06, 5617 BD Eindhoven — the creative/technology district of the city
- **~2010:** Begins BAD Award program (biological art & design)
- **2025:** BAD Award paused; major restructuring of art-science collaboration model

## BAD Award — Bio Art & Design Award

The flagship programme, now paused. Key facts:
- €25,000 production grant for artist-scientist collaborations
- Competition for international artists working with Dutch research institutions
- 15 editions: 2010–2024 (roughly)
- **2025: discontinued** — MU statement: *"The BAD network is cracking and in its current form is proving insufficiently resilient to outside influences."* Redesign planned for 2026.
- Partners: [BioArt Laboratories](https://bioartlab.com/), [Dutch Design Foundation](https://www.dutchdesignfoundation.com/), [Next Nature](https://www.nextnature.net/), [NWO](https://www.nwo.nl/)

## Key Ideas & Intellectual Territory

1. **Hybrid art** — art that exists in multiple registers simultaneously: biological, technological, computational, conceptual, social. The "hybrid" is not multimedia — it's a structural condition.
2. **More-than-human** — MU's 2024-25 "Sensing Otherness" directly uses the language; multispecies attention, non-human communication, planetary entanglement
3. **Art-science collaboration** — the BAD Award's model of pairing artists with Dutch researchers is a production methodology, not just a theme. Creates 3+ month residencies with institutional scientific partners.
4. **Open, non-binary thinking** — the Zen "mu" philosophy: complexity as starting point, refusing easy categorization, postponing judgment
5. **Quantum/uncertainty** — current focus extends from bioart toward fundamental physics as a frame for uncertainty, probability, entanglement as social/artistic concepts
6. **Education as institutional pillar** — not an afterthought; MU runs structured programs from primary school through university

## Why Interesting

MU is the institutionalized European answer to the question: "where does serious art-science happen?" Its BAD Award model — pairing artists with Dutch universities to co-produce new works — is a template that generated 40+ artist-scientist collaborations over 15 years. The 2024-25 "Sensing Otherness" exhibition is essentially a more-than-human sensing program with scientific grounding. This is Amber's territory: post-anthropocentric art practices with scientific rigor.

The discontinuation of the BAD Award in 2025 is important to track — it signals funding pressure on the art-science model in Europe. MU is explicitly recalibrating. This opens space for new kinds of engagement.

From research-taste.md: MU operates at the exact intersection of "interdisciplinary over pure," "practice-based," and "critically engaged." The bioart lineage connects directly to more-than-human design, which is a core Amber thread. The quantum turn in the current exhibition signals an interesting expansion.
