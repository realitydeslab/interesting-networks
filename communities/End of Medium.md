# End of Medium

A small, high-signal digital press publishing "discursive digital projects on topics within design, computing, economics, cultural theory and language." Named after Unicode control character U+0019 (END OF MEDIUM) — which marks the material end of a data transmission — and the URL itself *is* that character rendered as a string (`u0019.com`). Editorial wit embedded in the infrastructure.

Distributes exclusively as eBooks via [Apple Books](https://books.apple.com/) and [Library Stack](https://www.librarystack.org/publisher/end-of-medium/). No physical editions. All 2025. Operated with deliberate obscurity (website minimalist, no team listed, domain cryptic). Feels less like a press than a curatorial project with ISBN numbers.

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-03-02:** Initial deep research. Full catalog mapped. Key people identified. URL corrected (endofmedium.com is unrelated; true URL is u0019.com). Note updated with complete picture.

## Mission & Objectives

"End of Medium publishes discursive digital projects on topics within design, computing, economics, cultural theory and language. Drawing from the eponymous Unicode symbol marking the material end of a transmission, End of Medium seeks new paths for publishing and distribution within the visual fields."

The name is both media-theoretical and self-aware: a press named after the signal that a medium has *ended* — a recursive gesture at the limits of publishing itself. Digital-only distribution is deliberate: they're not just making ebooks but rethinking what distribution means in the post-print moment.

## Key Contributions

1. **Digital-only publishing as a curatorial stance** — Not just a business model. Refusing print is a position about distribution, access, and what "medium" means now.
2. **High-quality editorial partnerships** — Singapore Art Museum, NN Contemporary Art (UK), Arts Council England. This is not self-publishing; it's institutional co-publication with deliberate curatorial selection.
3. **The Capital Dialogues format** — Artist/thinker in conversation with expert translators of Marx's *Capital*. Elegant frame for injecting political economy back into art discourse.
4. **INTERPRT's forensic lexicon** — A working toolkit, not just theory. Ecocide vocabulary + computational method = directly actionable for practitioners.
5. **ISBN strategy** — The sequence suggests planned series, not one-off publications. End of Medium is operating as a coherent publisher, not an occasional project.

## Why Interesting

End of Medium is operating at the same register as Library Stack: small, selective, high-signal, digitally-native. The Unicode URL is a perfect example of their ethos — the medium is the message, literally. They've published four titles in 2025, all excellent, all on themes directly relevant to Amber's work (AI critique, environmental forensics, political economy of art/technology, machine personhood). Not a community to "join" — a publisher to pitch or publish with.
