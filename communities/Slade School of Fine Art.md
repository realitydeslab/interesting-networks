# Slade School of Fine Art

One of the UK's most prestigious fine art schools, part of **[University College London (UCL)](https://www.ucl.ac.uk/)**. Located in Bloomsbury, London. Founded 1871. Produces painters, sculptors, filmmakers, and performance artists. Notable for its emphasis on fine art practice within a research university context. Part-time teaching here has been a long-term commitment for several major British artists.

## Mission & Objectives
Fine art education: painting, sculpture, film/video, printmaking, graduate fine art (MFA, PhD). Research culture through UCL affiliation. One of the longest continuously operating art schools in the UK.

## Key People (known connections)
- **[[Lis Rhodes]]** — Part-time lecturer since 1978. 40+ continuous years. Her presence is itself a form of feminist experimental film pedagogy at the heart of a major British institution.

## Why Interesting
The Slade's location within UCL (a major research university) makes it an unusual space for fine art practice — simultaneously embedded in academic culture and maintaining strong studio-practice traditions. The fact that Lis Rhodes has taught here for 40+ years without it being her "main" affiliation makes it a significant node.
