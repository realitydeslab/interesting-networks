# Harvard Society of Fellows

Three-year cross-disciplinary fellowship at Harvard for exceptional early-career scholars. One of the most prestigious fellowships in North American academia — Junior Fellows are selected for originality and promise across all fields. The fellowship explicitly values work that crosses disciplinary boundaries. Past and present fellows include some of the most influential humanists, scientists, and social scientists of the 20th–21st centuries.

Fellows pursue independent research without formal obligations, supported by a stipend and Harvard resources. Weekly dinners bring Junior Fellows into direct intellectual contact with senior scholars across fields.

## Why Interesting

An intellectual genealogy marker. When someone has been a Harvard Society of Fellows Junior Fellow, it signals: (1) elite cross-disciplinary training, (2) independent scholarly ambition, (3) recognized as exceptional early-career. Many of the most interesting critical theorists and historians passed through this fellowship.

The Society deliberately creates scholars who don't fit neatly into departments — exactly the profile of people who generate interesting work at disciplinary borders.
