# Black in AI

**Non-profit AI affinity group and advocacy organization** co-founded by computer scientists [[Timnit Gebru]] and [[Rediet Abebe]] in 2017. Started as a workshop at NeurIPS; formalized as 501(c)(3) in 2018. Aims to shift the power dynamic across the AI ecosystem — broadening the range of voices contributing to AI development, deployment, and regulation.

The origin story is a formative moment in critical AI history: at the 2016 NeurIPS conference in Barcelona, Gebru counted just **six Black people** out of 5,500 attendees — including herself. She and Abebe co-founded Black in AI the following year to ensure that number would never be so low again. To date, the organization has granted more than **$2 million** to support 400+ AI practitioners to attend major conferences.

## Mission & Objectives

> "We aim to shift the power dynamic across the AI Ecosystem to broaden the range of voices contributing to the development, deployment, and regulation of AI technology."

Four pillars:
1. **Community Engagement** — Professional Membership, General Events, Conferences & Socials, Ecosystem Support
2. **Education & Career Programs** — Emerging Leaders in AI Grad Prep Program; Research Travel Grant Program; BlackAIR Summer Research Program; Postdoc Bridge Program
3. **Initiatives** — Civil Society & Policy; Research & Advocacy; Innovation & Entrepreneurship
4. **Partnerships** — Mission-aligned collaborations with industry, foundations, and peer organizations

Scale (as of 2025): **117+ countries** with BAI members; **1,200+ students supported**; **6,300+ community members & allies**.

## Key Programs

### Research Travel Grant Program
Core program since founding. Grants to Black AI researchers to attend major conferences (NeurIPS, ICML, AAAI, FAccT, etc.). More than $2M distributed since 2017. Addresses structural barrier: visa costs, flight costs, conference fees are prohibitive for researchers from Africa and Global South.

### Emerging Leaders in AI Grad Prep Program (Academic Program)
- Resources for Black junior researchers applying to grad school, navigating grad programs, transitioning to postgraduate employment
- Online education sessions; application fee scholarships; peer + senior mentorship pairs
- Crowdsourced resources simplifying application process
- Research into structural barriers facing Black grad school applicants (e.g., GRE bias against low-income applicants)
- Served 200+ potential grad program candidates in 2021 admissions season alone

### BlackAIR Summer Research Program
Two summer research tracks launched in 2021:
1. Undergraduate internship track
2. Unconstrained research mentorship track — including a track focused explicitly on empowering Black women's AI research projects

### Postdoc Bridge Program
Supporting transitions from PhD to postdoctoral and employment positions.

### Startup & Entrepreneurship Initiative
Program connecting Black tech startups with investors. Innovation & Entrepreneurship is one of three core initiative pillars.

## Key Contributions

1. **Structural diagnosis** — Gebru's 2016 NeurIPS headcount became a canonical moment demonstrating how whiteness is baked into the AI research elite, enabling structural critique backed by lived data.

2. **Conference visa advocacy** — The 2018 and 2019 visa crises (dozens of African academics denied Canadian visas for NeurIPS) surfaced the global inequality of AI conference access. Black in AI's advocacy around visa support became a model for other affinity groups.

3. **Affinity group model** — Inspired and supported formation of [Queer in AI](https://queerinai.com/), [Widening NLP](https://www.winlp.org/), [LatinX in AI (LXAI)](https://www.latinxinai.org/), [Indigenous Protocol and Artificial Intelligence](https://www.indigenous-ai.net/), and others — a whole ecosystem of affinity-based diversity organizations within ML.

4. **Google accountability** — The 2021 solidarity statement refusing Google funding forced a public reckoning with Big Tech's treatment of Black AI researchers, amplifying the DAIR Institute's founding narrative.

5. **Academic pipeline work** — Systematic research into structural barriers (GRE cost and bias, conference access cost, mentorship gaps) translating advocacy into policy recommendations for institutions.

## Why Interesting

Black in AI sits at the intersection of three things Amber cares about:

1. **Structural critique of AI** — The organization was born from a count. Not a theory, not a manifesto — a count. Six Black people at 5,500. That's empirical sociology as activism. It connects to Amber's interest in how structural observation becomes collective action.

2. **Alternative AI institution-building** — The Gebru firing → BAI solidarity → DAIR Institute sequence is a case study in how critical researchers exit corporate structures and build independent infrastructure. DAIR is the institutional result of community solidarity organized through BAI. This is directly relevant to Amber's interests in decentralized AI and trustworthy agentic research.

3. **Global South and African AI** — BAI's visa crisis (2018, 2019) put African AI researchers on the global map as a legitimate constituency being structurally excluded. The planned 2020 Addis Ababa conference (cancelled due to COVID) signals a genuine commitment to African-centered AI community-building that connects to Amber's cosmotechnics and non-Western perspectives interests.
