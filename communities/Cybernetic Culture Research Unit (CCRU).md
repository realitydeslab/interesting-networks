# Cybernetic Culture Research Unit (CCRU)

Experimental cultural theory collective at University of Warwick (1995-early 2000s). Developed influential "theory-fiction" bridging philosophy, cyberpunk, and occultism. Crucial precursor to contemporary accelerationism. Founded by Sadie Plant, later led by Nick Land.

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Created stub during Mark Fisher deep research. Key connection in accelerationist genealogy.

## Mission & Objectives

Interdisciplinary collective exploring intersection of cybernetics, culture, capitalism, and fiction. Listed interests as "cinema, complexity, currencies, dance music, e-cash, encryption, feminism, fiction, images, inorganic life, jungle, markets, matrices, microbiotics, multimedia, networks, numbers, perception, replication, sex, simulation, sound, telecommunications, textiles, texts, trade, video, virtuality, war."

Developed concept of "hyperstition" - self-fulfilling prophecies that reshape reality through cultural circulation.
