# Community Futures Lab

Afrofuturist community centre in the Sharswood-Blumberg neighbourhood of North Philadelphia, opened June 2016 by [[Black Quantum Futurism]] (Rasheedah Phillips and Camae Ayewa). Designed as active resistance to the neighbourhood's rapid gentrification. Hosts workshops, oral history documentation, teach-ins, a speculative futures library, and facilitates community members to record both their memories *and* their visions of the future.

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-27:** Stub created as part of BQF deep research.
- **2023:** Expanded as "Community Futures: STL Lab" for Counterpublic, St. Louis (Apr–Jul 2023) — the model was exported to another city.

## What It Does
- **Oral history** — documenting stories and histories before gentrification erases them
- **Oral futures** — a BQF innovation: asking community members to record *future visions* for the neighbourhood
- [Quantum Event Mapping](https://futureslab.community/) — temporal maps of the neighbourhood that layer memory events with projected futures
- **Workshops and teach-ins** — around temporality, housing rights, speculative fiction
- **Library** — speculative fiction, Afrofuturism, quantum theory, housing justice
- **Chicago version** (2019) — Community Futures Lab Chicago, Chicago Cultural Center / Chicago Architecture Biennial 2019
- **STL Lab** (2023) — Slower Than Light Shrine + Community Futures: STL Lab, Counterpublic, St. Louis

## Why Interesting for Amber
The Oral Futures Booth methodology is a direct instance of **participatory speculative design**: community members co-create futures rather than simply reporting needs. This is a practice-based form of democratic futures-making. The Quantum Event Map is also an unusual spatial-temporal design artifact — a map that documents time rather than space.
