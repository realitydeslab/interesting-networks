# Mediamatic

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-03-02:** Deep research completed. Full profile created — history, Living Labs, Biotoop programs, Zoöp model, residency programme, key people, and connections to Amber's work.
- **2026-02-26:** Stub created during V2_Lab deep research.

Amsterdam-based interdisciplinary art centre dedicated to cross-disciplinary research and experimental practice at the intersection of art, biology, and ecology. Founded in 1983 by Willem Velthoven. Since 2015 based at **Mediamatic Biotoop** (Dijksgracht 6, Amsterdam) — a greenhouse-and-canal complex that is simultaneously a biolab, fermentation facility, restaurant, urban farm, and residency space. Known for its signature programs: the Aromathèque (sensory/olfactory art), Aquatoop (aquatic ecology design), Biotoop (bio-based materials lab), and a rolling artist residency. Recently adopted the **Zoöp** organizational model — formally integrating non-human interests into decision-making. One of Amsterdam's most distinctive and irreverent art-science institutions, operating since 1983 with a DIY, experimental ethos.

## Mission & Objectives

**Core mission:** Challenge conventional boundaries across art, science, and culture through exhibitions, workshops, and long-term research. Bring together artists, designers, and experts to explore biology, ecology, and material experimentation as forms of knowing.

**The Biotoop model (since 2015):** A Living Lab approach — the institution's physical space is itself the experiment. Urban farming, fermentation, mycelium construction, aquaponics, hydroponics, and anthroponics (using human urine as fertilizer) are not just metaphors but actual ongoing practices. The "lab" is always open to visitors.

**The Open City Monastery (2025–ongoing):** Mediamatic's current core concept — a space for rest, reflection, and collective experiment. Inspired by monastic rhythms (archiving, gardening, fermenting, experimenting), explicitly open to "human and more-than-human" life. Free exhibitions; radical accessibility.

**Zoöp model (adopted 2025):** Mediamatic joined the [Zoönomic Institute](https://www.zoonomic.institute/) network, appointing a "Speaker for the Living" (Jolanda Verspagen) to give non-human ecosystems a formal voice in organizational decisions. This is governance as more-than-human practice.

## History

- **1983:** Founded by **Willem Velthoven** (also founder of design studio Mediamatic and the magazine *Mediamatic*). The magazine (1985–1999) was a key platform for early European digital culture theory.
- **1985–1999:** Published *Mediamatic Magazine* — an influential publication on digital culture, net art, and electronic arts. Ran alongside the Dutch digital art scene.
- **2000s–2010s:** The organization evolved from magazine/digital culture focus toward bioart, material culture, and participatory programming. Multiple Amsterdam locations — Mediamatic Supermarket, Post CS building, Mediamatic Bank (2008), Van Gendthallen (2012–2015).
- **2011:** Exhibition *Piece de Résistance* — protest against Dutch cultural budget cuts. Active political actor in arts funding debates.
- **2012:** Began "designing with organisms" — the Mushroom Paradise exhibition at Van Gendthallen marks the shift to bioart and living materials.
- **2015:** Moved to current **Biotoop** location at Dijksgracht 6, Amsterdam. First aquaponics greenhouse installed.
- **2020:** Pigeon Towers project with artist **Arne Hendriks** — mycelium-based architecture towers, multispecies coexistence experiment.
- **2025:** *Rites of Play / Open City Monastery* launch — new programmatic frame for Mediamatic's next chapter.
- **2025:** Formally adopted Zoöp model — non-human speaker integrated into governance.
- **2026:** *Hydrocommons* and *Anatomy of a Material* (Enora Cressan) — current residency-driven exhibitions.

## Key Programs & Output

### Active Programs (2026)
- **[Aromathèque](https://www.mediamatic.net/en/page/391783/aromath%C3%A8que)** — Monthly sensory art program curated by Frank Bloem. Themes: mayonnaise, masochism, speculative fiction, cheese. "Lower senses" as philosophical terrain. Interactive, participatory, deliberately weird.
- **[Aquatoop](https://www.mediamatic.net/en/page/392655/aquatoop)** — Designing structures for water and aquatic inhabitants. Linked to Amsterdam's 200km quay repair project.
- **[Mediamatic Biotoop](https://www.mediamatic.net/en/en/projects)** — The bio-lab: mycelium, fermentation, beer, waste-as-material, mushrooms. Fungal materials for design, science, and art.
- **[Hydroponics Greenhouse](https://www.mediamatic.net/en/page/74034/hydroponics-greenhouse)** — Urban farming lab. Evolved: aquaponics (2012) → anthroponics (human urine fertilization) → hydroponics (2024). Still runs aquaponics workshops.
- **[Pigeon Towers](https://www.mediamatic.net/en/page/379736/pigeon-towers)** — Mycelium-Waste-Pigeon towers (with Arne Hendriks, 2020–ongoing). Multi-species architecture; 100 Years of Learning masterclass series.
- **[Zoöp](https://www.mediamatic.net/en/projects/zoop)** — Organizational model. "Speaker for the Living" sits on the team, advocates for ecosystem interests. Governance as more-than-human practice.
- **[Meet-a-Maker](https://www.mediamatic.net/en/page/381754/meet-a-maker)** — 30-minute 1-on-1 sessions with artists-in-residence. Greenhouse by the canal. Open conversation about art, process, materials.
- **[Open City Monastery / Rites of Play](https://www.mediamatic.net/en/page/392163/rites-of-play)** (2025–ongoing) — Monastic experiment. Free exhibitions. Archiving, gardening, fermenting, experimenting. Explicit more-than-human framing.

## Key Contributions

1. **Bioart as daily practice** — Not just as gallery exhibition but as institutional metabolism. The food they serve, the plants they grow, the mycelium they build with are the work.
2. **Sensory expansion** — The Aromathèque treats olfaction, taste, and touch as undervalued, politically charged domains. "Lower senses" as feminist/critical territory.
3. **More-than-human governance** — The Zoöp model is a concrete organizational experiment in giving non-human actors formal institutional voice. Not art *about* this — actually doing it.
4. **Urban ecology as art-science** — Hydroponics, anthroponics, aquaponics, pigeon towers: a continuous experimental relationship with urban ecology.
5. **Participatory DIY ethos** — Workshops, Meet-a-Maker, Open City Monastery: the institution teaches rather than just shows. Self-sufficient living skills as cultural practice.

## Why Interesting

Mediamatic is rare: an institution that has sustained experimental practice for 40+ years without becoming academic or calcified. The pivot from digital culture magazine (1985–1999) to bioart lab (2012–) to more-than-human organization (2025–) shows genuine intellectual metabolism — they follow what's alive, not what's safe.

The **Zoöp adoption** is the most interesting recent move — it's not art *about* more-than-human governance but an actual structural experiment. A "Speaker for the Living" has formal standing in the organization. This is governance design as speculative practice.

The **Biotoop** model — where the institution is itself the living experiment — aligns with Amber's practice-based research ethos. The aquaponics → anthroponics → hydroponics evolution shows the institution actually learning from and changing based on its experiments. That's ALife thinking applied to institutional form.

Mediamatic is also a **venue circuit** — major European media artists and designers pass through (Frank Bloem, Arne Hendriks, Rosa Vrij, etc.). Being a resident or presenter here places you in a meaningful Amsterdam network connected to V2_, Design Academy Eindhoven, transmediale, and broader European art-science world.
