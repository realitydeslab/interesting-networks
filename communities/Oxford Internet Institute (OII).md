# Oxford Internet Institute (OII)

The Internet research department of the University of Oxford. Multidisciplinary social science research centre studying the societal impacts of the internet, AI, and emerging technologies. Founded 2001. Based at Oxford, relocating to the [Stephen A. Schwarzman Centre](https://www.schwarzmancentre.ox.ac.uk/) in late 2025/2026. Exceptionally unusual in genuinely bridging law, philosophy, social science, and computer science.

**This is Amber's home department.** He is a DPhil student in the Human Centred Computing programme, supervised by Max Van Kleek.

## Mission & Objectives

OII's mission: understand the transformation of social, economic, and political activity online. Research draws on multiple disciplines — essential for tackling the major challenges of the 21st century (AI ethics, digital politics, data governance, platform economics, information security, digital wellbeing).

Research organised into **8 broad thematic areas** and **12 active research groups**.

### 8 Research Areas
1. [Digital Economies](https://www.oii.ox.ac.uk/research/research-areas/digital-economies/)

## Research Groups (All 12)

### 1. [Governance of Emerging Technologies (GET)](https://www.oii.ox.ac.uk/research/research-groups/governance-of-emerging-technologies-group-get/) ⭐⭐⭐
**Leads:** [[Sandra Wachter]], [[Brent Mittelstadt]], Chris Russell
**Focus:** Legal, ethical, social aspects of AI, ML, emerging information technologies.
**Three core themes:**
- Accountability and Explainability — open, understandable, accountable AI
- Data and Inferences — privacy in the age of inferential analytics
- Bias and Fairness — identify, assess, minimise harmful biases

**Key outputs:**
- Counterfactual explanations method — implemented by Google, IBM, Vodafone, Accenture
- "Right to reasonable inferences" — cited in GDPR commentaries and EU Parliament
- Conditional demographic disparity (fairness measure) — influenced EU AI Act, implemented by Amazon

**Funders:** Alan Turing Institute, British Academy, EPSRC, Luminate Group, Miami Foundation, NHS England, Sloan Foundation, Wellcome Trust

**Why important for Amber:** Closest institutional neighbour to machine behavior accountability. GET is explicitly open to collaborators on AI governance. Amber's agent ethology work maps directly onto their accountability/explainability themes.

### 2. [Synthetic Society Lab](https://www.oii.ox.ac.uk/research/research-groups/synthetic-society-lab/) ⭐⭐
**Lead:** [Luc Rocher](https://www.oii.ox.ac.uk/people/profiles/luc-rocher/)
**Focus:** Human-centred computing. Data and algorithms impact on society. Three pillars:
- Privacy-enhancing technologies for researcher data access
- Investigating algorithms in online platforms
- Evaluating human-machine interactions

Independent, evidence-based research. Making digital power visible. Public interest technology.

### 3. [Mind and Emerging Technologies Lab (METL)](https://www.oii.ox.ac.uk/research/research-groups/mind-and-emerging-technologies-lab-metl/)
**Lead:** Andrew Przybylski
**Focus:** Social media, video games, AI → human behaviour, mental health, wellbeing. Open science + rigorous methods. Policy engagement with UK, US, Europe. Partners with tech companies.

### 4. [Reasoning with Machines AI Lab](https://www.oii.ox.ac.uk/research/research-groups/reasoning-with-machines-ai-lab/)
**Lead:** Adam Mahdi
**Focus:** Benchmarking and evaluating LLMs. HCI + mechanistic interpretability. Human-AI reasoning interactions, transparency. Healthcare AI applications.

### 5. [Governing in the Age of AI](https://www.oii.ox.ac.uk/research/research-groups/governing-in-the-age-of-ai/)
**Focus:** Science of governing AI. Two dimensions: (1) Governing WITH AI (policy decisions, public services), (2) Governing AI (regulation, international alignment, implementation). Multidisciplinary.
**External collaborators:** Alan Turing Institute (Cosmina Dorobantu as co-lead on Public Policy Programme)
**Visiting Professor:** [Judy Wajcman](https://www.oii.ox.ac.uk/people/profiles/judy-wajcman/) — Anthony Giddens Professor of Sociology, LSE. Technology + gender + time.

### 6. [Subversive Technologies Lab](https://www.oii.ox.ac.uk/research/research-groups/subversive-technologies-lab/)
**Lead:** Joss Wright
**Focus:** Technologies that exert, resist, or subvert information control. Internet censorship, surveillance, digital rights, online wildlife crime.
**External collaborators:** [The Citizen Lab (University of Toronto)](https://citizenlab.ca/), UK FCDO, [OONI](https://ooni.org/), Access Now, Open Technology Fund, UNEP WCMC, TRAFFIC.

### 7. [Digital Ethics and Defence Technologies](https://www.oii.ox.ac.uk/research/research-groups/digital-ethics-and-defence-technologies/)
