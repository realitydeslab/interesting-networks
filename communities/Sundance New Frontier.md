# Sundance New Frontier

Sundance Film Festival's experimental art × technology program. The venue where Nonny de la Peña premiered *Hunger in Los Angeles* in 2012 (first VR documentary at a major film festival), catalyzing the modern VR industry. Annual program at the Sundance Film Festival (Park City, Utah, January).

## Why Interesting
New Frontier is the most important venue for experimental narrative at the intersection of cinema, technology, and art. VR, AR, AI-generated film, interactive installation — all premiered here before going mainstream. De la Peña used it to legitimize VR journalism; others used it to legitimize volumetric film, interactive documentary, generative storytelling.

Key premieres: *Hunger in Los Angeles* (2012), *Project Syria* (2015), *Kiya* (2016), *Out of Exile* (2017), many others.

## For Amber
Could be a venue for HoloKit-based immersive experiences or agent ethology artwork. Highly selective but open to art-tech hybrid practices. Applications open annually around August/September.
