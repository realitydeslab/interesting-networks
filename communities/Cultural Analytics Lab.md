# Cultural Analytics Lab

Research lab at the CUNY Graduate Center, New York, founded by [[Lev Manovich]]. Pioneered the computational analysis and large-scale visualization of **massive cultural datasets** — from 2.3 million Instagram photos to 4,535 Time Magazine covers to 1 million manga pages. Originally founded in 2007 as the **Software Studies Initiative** at UC San Diego (UCSD / Calit2); moved to CUNY Graduate Center in 2013; renamed Cultural Analytics Lab ~2016. The lab invented the term and practice of **"cultural analytics"** as a discipline: applying data science and computer vision to humanities questions at a scale previously impossible.

## Mission & Objectives

The Cultural Analytics Lab uses **data science, data visualization, and large-scale computational methods** to analyze cultural trends across history. The core claim: by applying tools from computer vision, machine learning, and data visualization to cultural archives at massive scale, patterns become visible that no individual human researcher could perceive. This is not just "digitizing the humanities" — it's a new epistemological stance toward culture itself.

Key methodological pillars:
- **ImagePlot** — custom open-source software for visualizing large image collections (thousands to millions of images)
- **Style Space** — method for comparing and tracking visual style evolution across image sets
- **Social media image analysis** — pioneered computational analysis of Instagram, Twitter image data as cultural data
- **Computer vision applied to art history** — analyzing painting, photography, manga, film collections at scale

The lab was the first to systematically treat Instagram photographs, YouTube videos, and social media data as *cultural archives* deserving humanistic analysis.

## Key Contributions

1. **Invented the term "cultural analytics"** (2005–2007) as a distinct field, now established as a discipline with its own journal.
2. **ImagePlot software** — open-source tool enabling any researcher to visualize large image collections. Democratized large-scale visual cultural research.
3. **Pioneered social media as cultural archive** — Phototrails (2013), Selfiecity (2014), and subsequent projects established methodological precedents for computational analysis of Instagram and Twitter image data.
4. **Art history at scale** — demonstrated that computational methods reveal real patterns in art history invisible to traditional scholarship (manga evolution, Time covers 1923–2009, Soviet photography magazines 1926–1991).
5. **Art-science synthesis** — Manovich is simultaneously a scholar, a software developer, and an active artist. The lab has always operated at the art-science boundary.

## Why Interesting

The Cultural Analytics Lab represents the most rigorous and sustained attempt to apply computational methods to cultural data at scale. They invented the field, built the tools, and produced the canonical publications. For Amber:

1. **Methodological model**: "cultural analytics" as a discipline demonstrates exactly how you turn a methodological approach (data + visualization + humanistic interpretation) into a field with its own journal, textbook, and institutional presence. This is directly replicable for "Agent Ethology."

2. **The scale move**: Manovich's insight that *scale reveals patterns invisible to traditional scholarship* is deeply relevant to ALife — running millions of agent interactions to study behavior requires the same epistemological shift as analyzing a million manga pages to study visual culture.

3. **Art-science bridge**: The lab consistently operates where computation meets culture meets artistic practice. Manovich's dual identity as theorist + artist is rare and worth studying.

4. **AI pivot**: Manovich has been thinking rigorously about AI aesthetics, creativity, and agency since at least 2018. His framework in *Artificial Aesthetics* (2024) is one of the most sophisticated non-hype treatments of GenAI in art discourse. It's required reading for anyone working on machine creativity, which overlaps with Amber's machine behavior / ALife interests.

5. **Institutional template**: The lab → journal → textbook → field pipeline is a masterclass in academic field-building that Amber should study as she develops Agent Ethology, machine behavior, and related research programs.
