# Stanford HAI — Institute for Human-Centered Artificial Intelligence

Stanford's interdisciplinary AI research and governance institute. Founded 2019 by John Etchemendy and Fei-Fei Li. Brings together CS researchers with humanists, social scientists, lawyers, and policy experts to shape how AI develops. Has major funding and significant influence on US AI policy discourse.

## Key People
- **Fei-Fei Li** — Co-Director (founding). Computer vision pioneer (ImageNet). Professor of CS.
- **John Etchemendy** — Co-Director (founding). Former Stanford Provost. Philosophy.
- **[[Michael Bernstein]]** ⭐⭐⭐ — Senior Fellow. His social computing and generative agents research is central to HAI's agenda.
- **[[Percy Liang]]** ⭐⭐⭐ — Senior Member. Director of CRFM (Center for Research on Foundation Models).
- **Jeremy Bailenson** — Virtual reality and presence research.
- **Eric Horvitz** — Microsoft Research Chief Scientific Officer, affiliated.

## Why Interesting
Stanford HAI is where US AI governance thinking crystallizes before reaching Washington. Unlike purely technical AI institutes, HAI's mandate is explicitly about society — which means it's the right venue for research that bridges Amber's interests in machine behavior, social simulation, and critical computing. [[Michael Bernstein]] is a Senior Fellow here; [[Percy Liang]] runs CRFM from within HAI. The Science 2025 paper on algorithmic polarization came from this network.
