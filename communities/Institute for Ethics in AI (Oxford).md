# Institute for Ethics in AI (Oxford)

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-03-12:** Initial deep research. Full profile created.
- **2025-2026:** Institute headquartered in the new **Schwarzman Centre for the Humanities**, Radcliffe Observatory Quarter, Oxford.
- **2024:** HAI Lab (Human-Centered AI Lab) founded within the Institute by Philipp Koralus as inaugural McCord Professor of Philosophy and AI.
- **Joel Lehman** — Amber's ERA advisor — is Senior Research Associate and Cosmos Fellow at HAI Lab.
- **Ryan Othniel Kearns** — MSc candidate at OII, Cosmos Fellow at HAI Lab. Stanford CS + Philosophy background.

The Oxford Institute for Ethics in AI brings together world-leading philosophers, humanities scholars, and STEM researchers with leaders in tech, business, government, and civil society. It is part of the **Philosophy Faculty** and headquartered in the Schwarzman Centre. It combines rigorous philosophical inquiry with interdisciplinary collaboration to shape AI ethics and governance.

**Critical for Amber:** This is not a peripheral community — it is the intellectual home of Amber's ERA advisor (Joel Lehman at HAI Lab), and its research themes (reason, agency, autonomy, human-AI flourishing) directly overlap with Amber's work on agent ethology, trustworthy agentic web, and machine behavior.

---

## Mission & Objectives

The Institute offers independent, informed perspectives on AI ethics to shape innovation and enhance global public debate. Key research themes include:
- AI and humanity: how human values shape AI design, governance, and use
- Philosophy of AI: nature of reason, agency, and autonomy in artificial systems
- AI governance and regulation
- Privacy, surveillance, and digital rights
- Children's agency in the age of AI
- AI and human rights (health, social care)
- Criminal law and AI

The **HAI Lab** (Human-Centered AI Lab) within the Institute focuses specifically on:
- Bridging philosophy and AI on reason, agency, autonomy, and decentralization
- Building a "philosophy-to-code pipeline" for open-source code
- Bringing together philosophers and technologists for human flourishing
- The explicit framing: "Socrates the philosopher in dialogue with Hephaestus the master craftsman"
