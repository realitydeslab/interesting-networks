# Science Fiction World (科幻世界)

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Expanded stub during [[Liu Cixin]] deep research. Added full context, Galaxy Award history, key people network.

China's flagship science fiction magazine, based in **Chengdu, Sichuan Province**. Founded 1979 (as *Sci-Fi Sea*, rebranded to *Science Fiction World* in 1991). The central institution of Chinese SF culture — the magazine that discovered, published, and created the canon of Chinese science fiction writers. Serialized [[Liu Cixin]]'s *The Three-Body Problem* (2006–2007). Runs the **Galaxy Award** (银河奖), voted by readers, China's most prestigious SF honor.

## Key People
- **[[Liu Cixin]]** — serialized *Three-Body Problem* here 2006–2007; won Galaxy Award 9 times
- **[[Chen Qiufan]]** — early career breakthrough stories published here
- **[[Xia Jia]]** — published multiple stories here including early Galaxy Award wins
- Han Song (韩松) — regular contributor, dark side of Chinese SF
- Wang Jinkang (王晋康) — veteran contributor, bioethics-focused SF
- **Editors** — historically influential in shaping Chinese SF taste; Tan Kai (谭楷) is a legendary former editor

## The Galaxy Award (银河奖)
Reader-voted award, run annually by *Science Fiction World*. [[Liu Cixin]] won 9 times. [[Xia Jia]] won 8 times. [[Chen Qiufan]] is a multi-winner. The award is the domestic measure of SF excellence in China — predates and runs parallel to the international Hugo circuit.

## Why Interesting
*Science Fiction World* is the infrastructure of Chinese SF. Without it — specifically its serialization of *Three-Body Problem* and its patronage of the Galaxy Award — the explosion of Chinese SF into global culture may not have happened. Understanding the magazine means understanding how a national SF community is built and maintained by a single institutional node. For Amber: the sinofuturism thread runs through this institution.

## For Amber
- **Who should Amber know?** The editors of *Science Fiction World* are key gatekeepers for Chinese SF culture. The magazine's Chengdu location means it connects to the broader Chinese tech/creative scene.
- **Connection to Galaxy Award circuit** — understanding who has won the Galaxy Award is a map of the Chinese SF landscape
