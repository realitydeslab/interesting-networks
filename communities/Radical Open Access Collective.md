# Radical Open Access Collective

A community of independent presses and journals united by a commitment to open access as a political project, not just a distribution mechanism. Founded around 2015. Associated with [[Open Humanities Press]] and [[Joanna Zylinska]] (member) and [[Gary Hall]] (key figure). The "radical" signals that this is not just OA pragmatics — it's a challenge to commercial publishing and intellectual property regimes.

## Mission
Open access to scholarship as a commons, not a service. Against APCs (Article Processing Charges) that simply move the paywall from reader to author. Advocates for collectively maintained, community-controlled publishing infrastructure.

## Key People
- [[Gary Hall]] — founding figure, Coventry University
- [[Joanna Zylinska]] — member
- [[Open Humanities Press]] — central institutional node

## Why Interesting
For Amber: their model of scholar-run, community-funded publishing is an alternative infrastructure to mainstream academic publishing. If Amber wants to publish research in a politically aligned way that also maximises reach, this collective is the relevant community.
