# Debt Collective

The first debtors' union in US history. Founded by [[Astra Taylor]], Thomas Gokey, and others out of Occupy Wall Street's Strike Debt campaign (2012). Organizes debtors collectively to dispute, resist, and abolish predatory debt — student, medical, and consumer. Operates a digital debt dispute toolkit (the first tool that made debt disputes collectively actionable) and has organized the first student debt strike in the US since the 1970s (Corinthian Colleges, 2015). Model of participatory design-meets-organizing: a digital tool embedded in a social movement.

## Mission & Objectives
Fighting predatory debt through collective organizing. "You are not a loan." Key insight: debt is not personal failure but a political structure — and can be resisted collectively, like labor organizing.

## Key People
- **[[Astra Taylor]]** — co-founder, intellectual architect, public voice
- **Thomas Gokey** — co-founder, artist and organizer
- **Rolling Jubilee** (precursor campaign) — bought and cancelled $32M in distressed debt publicly

## Key Contributions
- First US debtors' union (organizational model)
- First student debt strike since 1970s (Corinthian Colleges debtors, 2015) — sparked broader student debt cancellation movement
- Digital debt dispute toolkit — made collective debt dispute actionable
- Rolling Jubilee — bought $700K in distressed debt, cancelled $32M publicly to demonstrate debt's arbitrary nature
- *Can't Pay, Won't Pay* (Haymarket Books, 2020) — manifesto

## For Amber
- Model of **participatory design + social movement organizing** — the dispute toolkit is a designed artifact that only works within a collective action framework
- The Shuttleworth Foundation fellowship (held by Astra Taylor) was explicitly for the Debt Collective's technology work — a model for funding organizing-oriented design

## Why Interesting
Demonstrates that digital tools can be more than apps — they can be components of collective action. The dispute toolkit isn't valuable as software; it's valuable as part of a union structure. This is what participatory design looks like at the level of political economy.
