# CCRU (Cybernetic Culture Research Unit)

Experimental cultural theory collective, formed 1995 at University of Warwick. Dissolved early 2000s. Produced "theory-fiction" incorporating philosophy, cyberpunk, and occultism. Precursor to accelerationism and speculative realism.

## Key People
- [[Sadie Plant]] — co-founder
- [[Nick Land]] — co-founder
- [[Mark Fisher]] — core member (k-punk)
- [[Kodwo Eshun]] — associated
- [[Steve Goodman]] (Kode9) — member, coined "sinofuturism"
- Iain Hamilton Grant — member (Schelling scholar)

## Key Concepts
- **Hyperstition** — self-fulfilling prophecies through esoteric cybernetic principles
- **Theory-fiction** — hybrid of theory and fiction
- **Sinofuturism** — coined by Kode9
- Influenced by Deleuze & Guattari, Lovecraft, William Gibson, Burroughs

## What It Spawned
- [[Urbanomic]] / Collapse journal — Robin Mackay (CCRU adjacent)
- [[Hyperdub]] — Kode9's label
- Zero Books / Repeater Books — Mark Fisher helped found
- The accelerationism discourse (both left and right)
- Speculative realism movement
- Goldsmiths Visual Cultures department cluster

## Why Interesting
Origin node for an enormous amount of contemporary theory. The CCRU → accelerationism → Bratton → Antikythera genealogy is direct. Also connects to Amber's interest in AI × mysticism (CCRU engaged with occultism and numerology).
