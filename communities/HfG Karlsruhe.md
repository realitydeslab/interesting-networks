# HfG Karlsruhe (Hochschule für Gestaltung / Karlsruhe University of Arts and Design)

**Staatliche Hochschule für Gestaltung Karlsruhe** — a state art college founded in 1992. ~400 students. Located in a former ammunition factory (Hallenbau A) shared with [[ZKM Center for Art and Media]]. Conceived as an **"electronic Bauhaus"** from the start.

## Overview

HfG Karlsruhe sits at the intersection of media art, design practice, and critical theory. Founded alongside [[ZKM Center for Art and Media]] in 1989–1992 by Heinrich Klotz (influential German art historian), it was explicitly designed to link art education, media research, and exhibition in a single institution — the first of its kind in Europe. The ambition: relate traditional arts to media technology and electronic manufacturing processes.

Key structural feature: **time-limited professorships** in practical departments, ensuring fresh artistic input each cycle. Permanent faculty provide continuity; visiting/time-limited professors rotate. This creates unusual dynamism in what gets taught.

Unlike most art schools, HfG still awards **Diplom and Magister degrees** (not Bachelor/Master), rejecting the modularization of the Bologna process. The school sees this as protecting the integrity of project-based, non-modular learning.

## History & Intellectual Lineage

- **Founded April 15, 1992** as a "Reformhochschule" (reform college) alongside ZKM
- **Heinrich Klotz** (founder, rector 1992–1999): art historian, curator of Deutsches Architekturmuseum. Died 1999. The original visionary.
- **Gunter Rambow** (acting rector 1999–2001): visual communication professor
- **Peter Sloterdijk** (rector 2001–2015): philosopher of "bubbles/spheres," gave HfG its philosophical identity
- **Siegfried Zielinski** (rector 2016–2018): media archaeologist, author of *Deep Time of the Media*; resigned citing reform ideas were "not enforceable"
- **Johan F. Hartle** (acting rector 2018–2019): art philosopher
- **Sami Khatib** (current acting rector as of 2025): political and social aesthetics, Benjamin scholar

Intellectual lineage tracks through: **Bauhaus → Ulm School (HfG Ulm) → HfG Karlsruhe**. The Frankfurt Critical Theory tradition (via Sloterdijk, then others) merged with media art practice.

Parallel international siblings:
- Studio Le Fresnoy (France, 1991–97) — [[Le Fresnoy]]
- IAMAS (Japan, Ogaki-shi) — [[IAMAS]]
- MIT Media Lab (US, older, different model)

## Departments / Study Programs

### Media Art (Medienkunst) — core department
- **Digital Art/InfoArt**: data visualization, telematic phenomena, real-time 3D, public space
- **Film**: documentary, experimental, auteur film; strong film theory
- **Photography**: extended photography, research-based, archival practice
- **Moving Images**: video installation, post-production aesthetics
- **Sound**: electroacoustic music, radio play, sound installation; independent label *ichiigai* (founded 2003)
- **3D/VR/AI Lab**: "Expanded 3Digital Cinema Laboratory" — 3D, VR, artificial intelligence in art; co-hosted BEYOND festival with ZKM

### Art Research & Media Philosophy (Kunstwissenschaft und Medienphilosophie) — theoretical dept.
- Systematic (not historical) approach to contemporary art and aesthetics
- Media philosophy, political aesthetics, digital aesthetics
- Cooperation with KIT and University of Heidelberg
- Awards: Magister and Dr. phil. degrees

### Exhibition Design & Scenography (Ausstellungsdesign und Szenografie)
- Exhibition design + curatorial studies + dramaturgy/scenography
- Strong link to Badisches Staatstheater Karlsruhe
- "Black Forest Institute of Art" referenced as excursion partner
- Collaboration with ABK Stuttgart

### Communication Design (Kommunikationsdesign)
- Diploma; transdisciplinary project-based

### Product Design (Produktdesign)
- Diploma; experimental product/design research

## Theoretical Orientation / Key Ideas

HfG's intellectual DNA across different eras:

**1992–1999 (Klotz era):** "Electronic Bauhaus" — unite art, craft, technology under one roof. Media art as the new medium. Adjacent to French post-structuralism (Lyotard's *Les Immatériaux* was an explicit reference point).

**2001–2015 (Sloterdijk era):** German idealist + spheres philosophy applied to space, media, design. Boris Groys' communist aesthetics + media philosophy. Byung-Chul Han's cultural diagnosis (burnout, transparency, surveillance). Neo-Marxist media theory.

**2016–present:** Critical theory + media philosophy + political aesthetics (Khatib, Sheikh). Digital image theory (Bruhn, Zschocke). AI and labor theory (Pasquinelli — ERC-funded, major figure). Postcolonial/decolonial media practice (César, McCarty). More-than-human/ecology (Jordan, Kriemann). Mixed/extended realities (Eifler, new 3D lab).

**Current intellectual preoccupations:**
- Digital image as application / habit (Bruhn's DFG project)
- AI social history and labor theory of machine intelligence (Pasquinelli legacy)
- Anticolonial cinema and archival practice (César)
- More-than-human relations, ecology, robots (Jordan)
- Feminist media, radio, energy politics (McCarty)
- Mixed realities and representation politics (Eifler)
- Critical theory from Global South (Khatib)

## Why Interesting for Amber

**Strong alignment with research taste:**

1. **More-than-Human / Ecological** — Anne Duk Hee Jordan's work (marine organisms + robots, "artificial stupidity") is almost exactly the kind of more-than-human practice Amber studies. Her HfG professorship is new and active.

2. **AI Theory with Critical Depth** — Matteo Pasquinelli (former HfG) is one of the most intellectually serious AI critics working today. *The Eye of the Master* (2023) is a labor theory of AI. His ERC grant from HfG is significant. Connecting to his legacy/current work would strengthen Amber's AI-critical positioning.

3. **Media Philosophy at the Intersection of Practice** — The KuPhi department does things like "Dirty Computer – An Excursion" and student projects on "Memes as Digital Folklore." This is not dry theory — it's theory in the field.

4. **Charlotte Eifler (Mixed Realities)** — new professor for Mixed Realities, works on queer archives + XR + representation politics. Amber's HoloKit connects to XR — potential for collaboration or dialogue.

5. **Speculative/Futures Design track** — "Future of Life" summer program, BEYOND Festival, Driving the Human initiative. HfG has a well-established tradition of speculative design as method.

6. **Filipa César** — anticolonial + archival + performance cinema. Very contemporary. Her decolonial approach to archiving + media connects to themes in Amber's STS and machine behavior work.

7. **ZKM co-location** — visiting HfG = visiting ZKM. These are inseparable. The combined infrastructure (library, studios, Lichthöfe, exhibition halls) is rare in Germany.

8. **German pathway** — Amber has connections to HKW Berlin (overlapping circuit), China Academy of Art. HfG is the German art-tech school with the strongest theoretical tradition. Worth building presence here.
