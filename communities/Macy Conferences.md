# Macy Conferences (1941–1960)

The founding event of cybernetics. A series of interdisciplinary conferences funded by the [Josiah Macy Jr. Foundation](https://www.josiahmacyfoundation.org/) and directed by Frank Fremont-Smith, held in New York 1941–1960. Brought together mathematicians, engineers, neurophysiologists, social scientists, and anthropologists to develop a unified science of circular causation and feedback. Produced cybernetics, information theory, cognitive science, and systems biology. Historical — no longer active.

## Key People
- **[[Gregory Bateson]]** — represented social and behavioral sciences; core group member for all 19 years
- **Norbert Wiener** — mathematician; coined "cybernetics"; *Cybernetics* (1948)
- **John von Neumann** — computation; cellular automata
- **Warren McCulloch** — neurophysiologist; neural net paper (1943) with Walter Pitts
- **Claude Shannon** — information theory
- **[[Heinz von Foerster]]** — physicist; later developed second-order cybernetics
- **Margaret Mead** — anthropologist; attended many sessions

## Why Interesting
The Macy Conferences are the most successful experiment in organized interdisciplinary synthesis in 20th century science. The model — a small core group from radically different disciplines, publishing verbatim transcripts, sustained over 19 years — produced entirely new fields. Understanding this model is useful for thinking about how to organize productive intellectual communities in AI and complex systems today.
