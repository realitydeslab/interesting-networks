# NTT InterCommunication Center (ICC)

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Stub created via Christa Sommerer & Laurent Mignonneau deep research.

Japan's premier media art center, operated by [NTT](https://www.ntt.co.jp/en/) in Tokyo. Opened in 1997 (previewed from 1991). ICC commissions, produces, and collects media artworks at the intersection of art, science, and telecommunications. In the 1990s–2000s it was arguably the world's best-resourced media art center — NTT's telecommunications budget funded levels of production impossible elsewhere. [[Christa Sommerer and Laurent Mignonneau]] were Artists-in-Residence here 1994–95; their *Life Spacies* and *Lift Space* are in the collection. Many of the defining works of digital/interactive art were first shown or supported here.

## Permanent Collection Highlights
- *Lift Space* — Sommerer & Mignonneau
- Various landmark interactive/net art works from 1990s–2000s

## Why Interesting
Historical anchor for understanding why Japan was the global center for art-technology work in the 1990s. ICC's model — telecommunications company funding advanced art production as a form of cultural R&D — has few parallels. Worth understanding for historical context and for the artists whose careers were shaped here.
