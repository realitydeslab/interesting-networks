# Acute Art

VR/AR art production company turning established contemporary artists' first forays into immersive digital space. Think of them as the production house that gives artists like Marina Abramović, Olafur Eliasson, and Cao Fei the technical scaffolding to make their first VR/AR works — without dumbing down the art. Founded 2017, Stockholm. Over 800,000 app downloads and 200+ projects.

## Mission & Objectives
Creating high-quality VR/AR/XR artworks in collaboration with leading contemporary artists. Their model: provide cutting-edge technical expertise (AR, VR, multiplayer, geospatial tech, real-time meshing, AI) while letting the artist retain full creative vision. The result is not tech demos — it's serious conceptual art in new media. Artists do their first VR/AR works through Acute Art.

**Core offering:**
- Artist collaboration: from ideation to launch
- Technology platform: custom XR, multiplayer features, geospatial, ARCore, AI
- Distribution: free Acute Art app (iOS + Android, 800,000+ downloads)
- Institutional partnerships: helps artists place work in major museums

## Key People
- **[[Daniel Birnbaum]]** — Creative Director & in-house curator. Joined January 2019. Former Director of [Moderna Museet](https://www.modernamuseet.se/stockholm/en/), Stockholm (decade-long tenure), and Director of the [53rd Venice Biennale](https://www.labiennale.org/) (2009). His curatorial prestige is the bridge between Acute Art and the highest levels of the contemporary art world. Without Birnbaum, this would be a tech company; with him, it's a credible art institution.
- **Rodrigo Marques** — CTO. Develops custom XR experiences, optimizes production using both new research and existing technologies.

## Key Contributions
- **Legitimizing VR/AR as a serious art medium** — by working with artists already embedded in the contemporary art canon (Abramović, Kapoor, Eliasson), Acute Art shifts VR from tech demo to cultural artifact
- **"First VR/AR work" production** — dozens of artists made their debut immersive work through Acute Art
- **Public AR access model** — the free Acute Art app democratizes access to AR art globally; 800,000+ downloads
- **Institutional partnerships** — placed work in Venice Biennale, Serpentine, The Shed, National Gallery Singapore, Leeum Museum, ArtScience Museum
- **Multispecies art** — Saraceno's *Webs of Life* is a rare case of AR as ecological activism and more-than-human practice

## For Amber
- **Why interesting:** Acute Art is building exactly what Amber's HoloKit enables — but from the production/curation side rather than the open-source tool side. Understanding their model shows what's possible when serious art-world institutions adopt MR. Amber builds the open hardware; Acute Art shows how it can become institutional.
- **Platform overlap:** HoloKit (Amber's tool) and Acute Art's app are two different visions of how AR art reaches audiences. Acute Art = closed, high-production, curator-led. HoloKit = open-source, maker-led. Understanding their model clarifies what Amber's alternative offers.
- **More-than-human angle:** Saraceno's *Webs of Life* is a perfect example of AR used for multispecies activism — exactly what Amber's more-than-human design research is about. This collaboration shows AR/VR can be a more-than-human design medium, not just a spectacle medium.
- **Asian art-tech circuit:** Cao Fei, Lu Yang (China Academy of Art graduate!), Ho Tzu Nyen — all artists Amber might encounter through the Chinese art-tech scene. Acute Art is a hub for this community.
- **Potential collaboration:** Amber's Reality Design Lab could propose a collaboration with Acute Art for a more-than-human themed VR/AR project. The Saraceno precedent (art + ecology + tech) is the model.
- **Daniel Birnbaum connection:** Birnbaum's move from Moderna Museet/Venice Biennale to Acute Art is a signal. Major curators are taking VR/AR seriously as art. This legitimizes the space Amber is working in.
- **Who should Amber know?** [[Daniel Birnbaum]] — the curatorial mind behind their art-world credibility. Also: Emma Enderby (Chief Curator, The Shed) who co-curated The Looking Glass; Cecilia Alemani (Director of High Line Art, also directed Venice Biennale 2022).
- **Key publications to be part of:** Acute Art's app is a distribution platform. If Amber does HoloKit-based AR art, Acute Art could be a distribution/exhibition partner.

## Why Interesting
Acute Art sits at the exact intersection Amber inhabits: high-end art × immersive technology. They've figured out the production model for VR/AR art at institutional scale. Three things make them more than just a tech studio:

1. **The Birnbaum factor** — bringing a Venice Biennale director inside ensures their work is judged by art world standards, not tech standards
2. **The Saraceno signal** — *Webs of Life* shows they're willing to use AR for more-than-human, ecological, multispecies purposes — not just spectacle
3. **The Asia axis** — Cao Fei, Lu Yang (China Academy of Art!), Ho Tzu Nyen — they're one of the few Western art-tech platforms actively building with leading Asian artists

For Amber specifically: they're the institutional model that validates what HoloKit makes possible. Understanding them is understanding where the VR/AR art world is going from the curator/institutional direction.
