# HAI Lab — The Laboratory for Human-Centered AI (Oxford)

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-03-12:** Initial deep research. Full profile created.
- **2024:** HAI Lab founded by Philipp Koralus as part of the Institute for Ethics in AI. Koralus became inaugural McCord Professor of Philosophy and AI.
- **Joel Lehman** joined as Senior Research Associate and Cosmos Fellow — previously OpenAI, Uber AI Labs, University of Copenhagen. **Amber's ERA advisor.**
- **Ryan Kearns** joined as Cosmos Fellow while doing MSc at OII.

HAI Lab is a new research laboratory within Oxford's Institute for Ethics in AI, founded in 2024 by Philipp Koralus. It operates at the intersection of philosophy and AI, with an explicit "philosophy-to-code pipeline" — turning philosophical concepts about reason, agency, and autonomy into working open-source code. Based in the Schwarzman Centre for the Humanities.

**CRITICAL FOR AMBER:** Joel Lehman (Amber's ERA advisor) is Senior Research Associate here. Ryan Kearns (Stanford CS+Philosophy, now OII MSc) is a direct peer. The lab's research agenda — reason, agency, autonomy, decentralization, open-ended machine learning — maps directly onto Amber's interests.

---

## Mission & Objectives

1. **Bridge philosophy and AI** — focusing on reason, agency, autonomy, and decentralization
2. **Bring together philosophers and technologists** for human flourishing
3. **Build a philosophy-to-code pipeline** for open-source code

Core thesis: "We must not only know to build, but build to know." Philosophical concepts relevant to AI increasingly require computational experiments to evaluate. HAI Lab creates the institutional structure for this.

## Philosophy-to-Code Pipeline

HAI Lab's distinctive contribution is making the "philosophy-to-code pipeline" explicit:

**Historical precedents they cite:**
- Bertrand Russell's work on mathematical knowledge → Turing's universal computer
- Saul Kripke's work on necessity → impact in computer science
- Michael Bratman's practical reason → intelligent agent design (via Mike Wooldridge)
- Helen Nissenbaum's work on privacy → computational implementations
- Ruth Chang's work on choices → decision-making systems
- David Marr, Phil Johnson-Laird, Joshua Tenenbaum — cognitive science continuous with philosophy

**The claim:** There is a productive pipeline from philosophical concepts through computational implementation to open-source code. HAI Lab aims to be the institutional home for this pipeline.
