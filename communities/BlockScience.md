# BlockScience

Complex systems engineering, R&D, and analytics firm founded by Michael Zargham (PhD Complex Systems). Builds rigorous simulation-based infrastructure for decentralized sociotechnical systems — from DAO governance and tokenomic design to knowledge organization networks. Their core ethos: treat social and digital systems with the same mathematical rigor as physical engineering. Not a crypto hype shop; closer to a cross between a SFI-style complexity science lab and a policy engineering firm with a web3 application focus.

## Mission & Objectives

From their blog: *"Complex systems engineering, R&D, and analytics firm. Goal is to combine academic-grade research with advanced mathematical and computational engineering to design safe and resilient socio-technical systems."*

They work with for-profit, non-profit, academic, and government clients, and contribute to open-source research. Key domains:
- **Token Engineering** — mathematical design and simulation of tokenomic systems before deployment
- **Mechanism Design** — incentive structures for DAOs, public goods funding, decentralized governance
- **Complex Systems Simulation** — agent-based modeling using their own cadCAD framework
- **Knowledge Infrastructure** — latest work on decentralized knowledge organization (KOI-net)
- **Sociotechnical Safety** — engineering safety into decentralized systems at design time

Their framing: "systems engineering for the digital-physical boundary" — neither pure crypto nor pure CS theory, but the engineering discipline that doesn't yet have a name.

## Key Publications & Output

### Foundational Theory
- [**Generalized Dynamical Systems**](https://doi.org/10.57938/e8d456ea-d975-4111-ac41-052ce73cb0cc) (Zargham & Shorish, 2022) — the theoretical basis for all their tooling. Treats complex systems as typed compositional specifications. Mathematical foundation for their whole approach.

### Software Tools
- **[cadCAD](https://cadcad.org/)** ([GitHub](https://github.com/cadCAD-org/cadCAD)) — Complex Adaptive Dynamics Computer-Aided Design. Python framework for complex systems simulation with Monte Carlo, A/B testing, parameter sweeping. Their most widely adopted open-source output. Used by Ocean Protocol, Commons Stack, Token Engineering community.
- **[MSML](https://github.com/BlockScience/MSML)** — Math Spec Markup Language. Standardizes mathematical specifications as JSON. Generates Obsidian vaults showing every component and its dependencies. Active (Feb 2026).
- **[gds-core](https://github.com/BlockScience/gds-core)** — Generalized Dynamical Systems monorepo (2026). Six packages: `gds-framework`, `gds-viz`, `gds-games` (compositional game theory), `gds-stockflow`, `gds-control`, `gds-examples`. Very active (updated Feb 28, 2026).
- **[KOI-net](https://github.com/BlockScience/koi-net)** — Knowledge Organization Infrastructure Network protocol. Defines communication patterns for heterogeneous networks of knowledge-processing agents. Each node can autonomously input, process, output knowledge. "Fractal-like" — a network of nodes appears as a single node to the outside. This is their emerging next-phase work (Feb 2026).
- **[RID-lib](https://github.com/BlockScience/rid-lib)** — Reference Identifiers protocol, used within KOI-net for identifying knowledge objects.

## Key Contributions

What they've given the world:

1. **cadCAD** — practically created the "token engineering" field as a discipline with simulation-first methodology. Now used by dozens of web3 projects to validate tokenomics before deployment.
2. **Augmented Bonding Curves (ABC)** — co-designed with Commons Stack. A primary issuance AMM for sustainable DAO funding. Deployed by Token Engineering Commons (TEC) in 2021; $TEC held value relative to ETH while comparable small-cap DAOs collapsed.
3. **Conviction Voting** — governance mechanism enabling continuous preference signaling (vs. one-shot voting). Applied in Commons Stack / Token Engineering Commons.
4. **Generalized Dynamical Systems (GDS)** — theoretical framework for compositional system specification. Now being built into software (gds-core, 2026).
5. **KOI-net protocol** — open protocol for decentralized knowledge coordination networks. Potentially significant for agentic web infrastructure.
6. **MSML** — standardizes how complex systems are specified and documented, enabling non-developers to understand models.

## Why Interesting

BlockScience sits in a genuinely unusual position: **rigorous simulation science applied to the design of decentralized human coordination systems**. This is not AI hype; it's closer to operations research meets political economy meets software engineering.

The Amber angle: Amber studies *how agents behave in the wild* — agent ethology, machine behavior as science. BlockScience studies *how to engineer systems so agents behave well* — mechanism design, simulation-before-deployment. These are complementary epistemologies. Behavioral observation (Amber) and engineering specification (BlockScience) produce different knowledge that's incomplete without the other.

Their newest work — **KOI-net** — is particularly relevant. It's building decentralized infrastructure for networks of knowledge-processing agents. This is essentially trying to engineer the substrate that Amber might want to study ethologically. The researchers who build the cage and the researchers who study the animals' behavior rarely talk. They should.

Their humanities presence (Ilan's literature + AI dissertation, Amber Case's calm tech, Elinor Rennie's cooperation sociology) distinguishes them from pure crypto engineering shops. They're asking the same questions from the below.

**From research-taste.md:** Maps to "Critical Computing" (computing as political act, structural critique embedded in incentive design), "Machine Behavior as Science" (cadCAD models agent behavior computationally), and "Participatory & Democratic Design" (ABC and Conviction Voting as mechanism design for democratic coordination).
