# Arts at CERN

The arts programme of [CERN](https://home.cern/) — the European Organization for Nuclear Research, Geneva. Arguably the world's most prestigious art-science residency platform: artists work directly inside particle physics infrastructure — the Large Hadron Collider, ATLAS and CMS detectors, the Antimatter Factory, underground tunnels — and develop projects in genuine dialogue with scientists at the frontier of fundamental physics. Founded around 2011/2012 as a formal programme, it has since placed over 100+ artists at the laboratory and released its first major publication in 2025. Contact: [info.arts@cern.ch](mailto:info.arts@cern.ch) | [@arts.cern](https://www.instagram.com/artscern/) on Instagram | [Vimeo](https://vimeo.com/artscern)

## Mission & Objectives

Arts at CERN creates sustained dialogue between contemporary artists and particle physicists at the world's foremost fundamental science laboratory. Its mission goes beyond typical art-science crossover: artists are embedded in CERN's actual research environment, given access to experiments, scientists, engineers, and technicians, and given time to genuinely engage with open questions at the frontier of physics — dark matter, antimatter, the nature of time, quantum phenomena, the structure of space. The programme actively resists decorative or illustrative uses of science; instead it asks: **what happens to artistic practice when it encounters science that genuinely doesn't know its answers?**

The programme operates under CERN's broader "inspire and educate" mandate and is funded partly through the [CERN & Society Foundation](https://donate.cernandsocietyfoundation.cern/ArtsatCERN/~my-donation), which accepts donations and manages corporate and foundation partnerships.

## Key Contributions

1. **The most institutionally embedded art-science programme in particle physics** — artists have genuine access to experiments, underground tunnels, computing clusters, and CERN's 10,000+ scientist community.

2. **Invented the dual-city residency model** — Collide is dual-location (CERN + partner city), ensuring artists work across institutional and geographic contexts, not just within one lab bubble.

3. **Created a new intellectual circuit** — Arts at CERN alumni appear consistently at Venice Biennale, Ars Electronica, Transmediale, Documenta, institutional exhibitions globally. The programme is a de facto credentialing institution.

4. **Opened fundamental physics to cultural production** — subjects like CPT symmetry, antimatter, dark matter, quantum non-locality, particle decay, and spacetime have entered contemporary art via this programme in rigorous rather than metaphorical ways.

5. **The *In the Spaces Between* book (2025)** — first documented synthesis of a decade of art-physics dialogue, establishing an intellectual canon.

6. **Expanded science into global South via Connect** — partnerships in Chile (ESO/ALMA), India (ICTS-TIFR), Argentina (Pierre Auger Observatory), South Africa (SAAO/SARAO) create a transnational network of science-art exchange.

## Events & Programmes

### 1. Collide 🏆
**[arts.cern/collide](https://arts.cern/collide/)** — Flagship residency, annual open call, international.
- **Format:** 2-month dual residency (1 month at CERN Geneva + 1 month at partner European cultural institution)
- **Stipend:** CHF 15,000 production stipend + travel + accommodation
- **Eligibility:** Any country, 18+, 5+ years professional experience, any creative discipline
- **Selection:** Jury of cultural experts, scientists, institution representatives
- **Current partner (2023–2025):** [Copenhagen Contemporary](https://copenhagencontemporary.org/) (Copenhagen, Denmark) — *Collide Copenhagen*
- **Previous partners:**
  - Ars Electronica, Linz (2012–2015)
  - FACT Liverpool (2016–2018)
  - Barcelona Institute of Culture / Hangar Centre for Art Research (2019–2021)
- **Next phase:** New 3-year partnership starting 2026 (TBC)
- **Historical scale:** 564 submissions from 79 countries (2020 edition); 388 from 75 countries (2021)

### 2. Connect 🌍
**[arts.cern/connect](https://arts.cern/connect/)** — Dual residency with global science labs, in partnership with **Pro Helvetia** (Swiss cultural foundation).
- **Format:** 2 dual residencies/year — one Swiss artist + one artist from partner country; both hosted at CERN and at a partner scientific institution
- **Current scientific partners:**
  - [ICTS-TIFR](https://www.icts.res.in/) (Bengaluru, India)
  - [ESO](https://www.eso.org/public/) + [ALMA Observatory](https://www.almaobservatory.org/) (Atacama Desert, Chile)
  - [Pierre Auger Observatory](https://www.auger.org/) (Mendoza, Argentina)
- **Former partners:** South African Astronomical Observatory (SAAO) + South African Radio Astronomy Observatory (SARAO)
- **Extended to 2028**
- **Sixth edition (2024):** Robin Meier Wiratunga (Switzerland) + Vimala Pons (partner country TBC)
- *Previously called Simetría Award (for Chile-specific editions)*

### 3. Resonance 🏙️
**[arts.cern/resonance](https://arts.cern/resonance/)** — Annual residency for **Geneva-based artists**, in partnership with City of Geneva and Republic and Canton of Geneva.
- **Format:** 2-month fully-funded residency at CERN
- **Timeline:** Annual open call, 2024–2027 framework; ~83+ applications received for 2nd edition
- **Jury:** Giulia Bini (Arts at CERN) + City/Canton cultural advisors + CERN scientist + Barbier-Mueller Museum director
- **1st edition (2024-25):** Winner TBC (programme launched Nov 2024)
- **2nd edition (2025-26):** Monika Emmanuelle Kazi — project *Crystalline Memories* (futurology, diasporic memory, CLOUD and CAST experiments)
- **3rd edition:** Special edition for 2026 (announced)
- **Partners:** Sophie Sallin (City of Geneva Cultural Advisor), Thierry Apothéloz (President, State Council of Geneva)

### 4. Guest Artist Programme 🔭
Invited artists for short stays at CERN to research, investigate, and be inspired. Not competitive — curated invitations. Past participants include Raqs Media Collective, Mika Rottenberg, Oron Catts & Ionat Zurr, Armin Linke, and Honorary Mention recipients from Collide.

### 5. Accelerate (former/suspended)
Country-specific, shorter residency programme (was available 2014-c.2018). Julijonas Urbonas participated via the Lithuanian programme (Rupert Centre, 2016). Tania Candiani via Mexico. Appears to have been folded into Connect.

### 6. Art Commissions
**[arts.cern/art-commissions](https://arts.cern/art-commissions/)** — commissions artworks directly for CERN spaces, particularly the Science Gateway (opened 2023). Multiple artists have been commissioned for permanent or semi-permanent works within CERN's campus.

### 7. Science Gateway Exhibition Programme
The [CERN Science Gateway](https://sciencegateway.cern/) (opened October 2023, designed by Renzo Piano Building Workshop) adjacent to the CERN campus in Meyrin, Geneva, serves as a public-facing cultural venue. Arts at CERN uses it for exhibitions and events, giving artwork a public venue beyond the lab itself.

## Why Interesting

**Arts at CERN is the frontier version of art-science.** Most art-science programmes are paternalistic — "scientists explain their work to artists, who make it pretty." Arts at CERN inverts this: artists are in genuine uncertainty alongside scientists who also don't know their answers. The LHC doesn't know what it will find. The artists don't know what they'll make. Both are inside the same productive unknowing.

The programme produces a kind of art that engages with physics at a level of rigor rare in the art world: Black Quantum Futurism studying CPT symmetry to rethink time; Semiconductor spending months understanding particle detectors; Ryoji Ikeda translating LHC data into acoustic experience at inhuman scales. This is not metaphor — this is genuinely using the conceptual tools of physics to extend art practice.

The intellectual circuit it has built over 13+ years is remarkable: alumni appear consistently at Venice Biennale, Ars Electronica, Documenta, Transmediale, and institutional exhibitions globally. It operates as a hidden credentialing institution for ambitious art-science practice.

For Amber specifically: the questions CERN physicists are asking (what is observation, can symmetry be violated, what is the relationship between information and physical reality) are the same questions that animate Amber's work on collective agency, emergence, and the relationship between data systems and physical worlds. There is an unexplored intellectual territory between particle physics and AI/agent research that Arts at CERN could open.
