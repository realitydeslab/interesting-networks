# Arts at CERN

The art programme of [CERN](https://home.cern/) — the European Organization for Nuclear Research, Geneva. One of the most prestigious and selective art-science residency programmes globally. Places artists directly inside CERN's scientific infrastructure — the Large Hadron Collider, particle detectors, research labs — where they encounter physics, particle theory, and the philosophical edges of scientific knowledge. Unlike most art-science programmes that simulate laboratory proximity, Arts at CERN is literally inside the lab.

## Key Programmes
- **Collide** — flagship residency. 2-month residency at CERN with a scientific mentor. One of the most selective art residencies in the world. Open internationally.
- **Accelerate** — shorter programme, country-specific.
- **Guest Artist** — invited artists for specific projects.

## Key People
- [[Oron Catts]] and [[Ionat Zurr]] — invited guest artists 2019. Their bio-art practice connected to CERN's questions about the fundamental nature of matter.

## Why Interesting

The most rigorous version of the "artist-in-lab" model: not just access to scientists but to the actual infrastructure of particle physics research. The Collide residency is exceptionally selective and puts artists at the genuine frontier of scientific inquiry. Previous artists have made work engaging with dark matter, time, particle decay, antimatter. Very different from biology-focused art-science residencies.

Relevant to Amber because it represents the art-science integration model at its most institutionally ambitious — and because TC&A's presence in 2019 signals the overlap between bio-art and particle physics communities at the frontier of philosophical questioning about life, matter, and agency.
