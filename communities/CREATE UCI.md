# CREATE — Steckler Center for Responsible, Ethical, and Accessible Technology (UCI)

A critical computing research center at UC Irvine's [Donald Bren School of ICS](https://www.ics.uci.edu/), established 2020 through a gift from Vincent (BS '80) and Amanda Steckler. Directed by [[Paul Dourish]] — one of the most significant HCI scholars alive, whose career bridges ethnomethodology, feminist epistemology, STS, and decolonial critique. CREATE is what "responsible technology" looks like when it's grounded in the STS tradition rather than sanitized AI ethics checklists. The center brings together informatics, CS, STS, law, anthropology, media studies, sociology, philosophy, political science, and economics.

## Mission & Objectives

CREATE asks:
- What barriers exist to equitable participation in technological futures, and how can they be dismantled?
- What principles should guide the design of technologies and platforms?
- How should technological processes and infrastructures be reconfigured to place justice and care at the center?
- How do we promote human rights and social values through technology design?

Their goals: amplify UCI's research in these areas; provide a forum for engagement among researchers, practitioners, policy-makers, and civic society participants.

## Why Interesting

CREATE is notable for several reasons relative to Amber's interests:

1. **Dourish's intellectual lineage** — embodied interaction → data imaginaries → decolonial critique. This is the richest critical computing tradition in US HCI. *Where the Action Is* is still cited in every HCI course and CREATE extends that legacy institutionally.

2. **STS-grounded ethics** — not AI safety, not ethics checklists. Ethnographic, feminist, decolonial. This is where structural critique meets design practice.

3. **Data justice + participatory design** — Crooks, Pei, Horgan all doing work that intersects with Amber's agentive commoning + participatory design interests.

4. **Bowker connection** — *Sorting Things Out* (with Susan Leigh Star) is infrastructure studies canon. Directly relevant to Amber's interest in how AI agents organize and classify the world.

5. **Carl DiSalvo** — *Design as Democratic Inquiry* is a key text for practice-based democratic design research. His experimental civics projects are model for what Amber's participatory + speculative work could become.

Amber should track CREATE primarily through its people rather than the institution directly — Dourish, Bowker, DiSalvo are the nodes.
