# DATAS Residency

International art residency programme (2025–26) exploring digital sovereignty, AI governance, and algorithmic power from a Central and Eastern European (CEE) and Southern Caucasian perspective. Full name: **DATAS – The Data and the Sovereign**. Funded by the European Union's [Creative Europe](https://culture.ec.europa.eu/creative-europe) programme. Transnational partnership across Slovenia, Germany, Ukraine, Czech Republic, and Estonia.

## Mission & Objectives

DATAS posits that computation and automation are reshaping global political dynamics — challenging established sovereignties and necessitating a fundamental rethinking of politics. In a post-surveillance information society, where algorithmic influence and AI technologies shape individual and national decisions, the programme asks: *what does sovereignty mean when data is power?*

Core questions the residency explores:
- How do digital infrastructures, AI, and algorithmic governance impact personal and political sovereignty?
- How can art foster awareness and drive shifts in the relationship between technology and power?
- What does technological self-determination look like for nations with post-socialist histories?
- How do we counter extractivist tech treating people as "users and commodities"?

The programme deliberately holds space for **diverse approaches and multiple perspectives** rather than seeking a single answer — inviting artists to treat information technologies as integral to society, not mere tools.

## Programme Format & Eligibility

**Eligibility (2025):**  
Artists must originate from, reside in, or have citizenship of one of 20 countries: Albania, Armenia, Bosnia and Herzegovina, Bulgaria, Croatia, Czech Republic, Estonia, Georgia, Hungary, Kosovo, Latvia, Lithuania, Montenegro, North Macedonia, Poland, Romania, Serbia, Slovakia, Slovenia, Ukraine.

**Format:**
- 8 production residencies total (2 per location)
- Duration: 2–3 months
- Fully funded: €1,000/month stipend + up to €3,000 production costs + travel + accommodation

**Locations:**
| Location | Duration | Focus |
|----------|----------|-------|
| Projekt Atol, Ljubljana | 2 months, Aug–Dec 2025 | AI & network infrastructures, fab-lab |
| MeetFactory, Prague | Aug–Oct 2025 | Multidisciplinary, theatre/gallery context |
| Tallinn Art Hall | Aug–Sep & Oct–Nov 2025 | Startup ecosystem, digitalisation |
| Izolyatsia, Ukraine (online) | 2 months, Aug–Oct 2025 | Digital media, data art, creative coding |

**Output:** Pop-up presentations at host institutions → final group show at Galerie Rudolfinum, Prague (2026).

## Why Interesting

DATAS exemplifies a non-Western but non-Asian approach to critical computing: it emerges from post-socialist, post-authoritarian contexts where questions of technological sovereignty are not abstract — they're about national survival (Ukraine's Izolyatsia doing an *online* residency because of the war is poignant). The expert cast — Bogna Konior, Vladan Joler, Tiziana Terranova — represents the most rigorous critical AI thinkers in the European art-theory scene. The programme's refusal to seek a "single answer" to sovereignty questions is sophisticated. 

For Amber, this is a **mirror circuit**: a community doing with CEE digital politics what Amber does with ALife, more-than-human design, and Sinofuturism. The productive tension between these perspectives could yield significant cross-pollination.
