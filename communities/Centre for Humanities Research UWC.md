# Centre for Humanities Research (CHR), University of the Western Cape

The Centre for Humanities Research (CHR) at the University of the Western Cape (UWC), Cape Town, South Africa, is one of the most important decolonial humanities research institutes in the world. Founded by [[Premesh Lalu]] (founding director) and colleagues, it operates at the intersection of postcolonial theory, African thought, visual culture, archives, and the philosophy of the university.

## Key Figures
- **[[Premesh Lalu]]** — founding director. Postcolonial historiography, university theory, "becoming technical of the human."
- **Patricia Hayes** — NRF SARChI Chair in Visual History and Theory. Colonial photography, documentary, visual archives.
- **Maurits van Bever Donker** — Academic Coordinator. Co-editor *Remains of the Social*.
- **Ross Truscott** — Next Generation Scholar. Psychoanalysis, theory.
- **[[Erich Hörl]]** — Extraordinary Professor (from November 2024). Philosophy of technics, general ecology.

## Key Programmes
- **DST-NRF Flagship Programme in Critical Thought in African Humanities** — major national programme, co-funded with Mellon Foundation
- **Communicating the Humanities project** — convened by Premesh Lalu
- **Next Generation Scholars programme** — early-career humanities scholars

## Key Publications
- *Remains of the Social: Desiring the Post-Apartheid* (Wits UP, 2017)
- *Love and Revolution in the Twentieth-Century Colonial and Postcolonial World* (Palgrave, 2021)
- Various issues of *Kronos: South African History Journal*

## Why Interesting
CHR is the institutional embodiment of Lalu's argument that the humanities can be renewed from the postcolonial periphery. It has managed to attract major international intellectual figures (Erich Hörl, Homi Bhabha's collaboration with Lalu) while remaining anchored in the specific political context of post-apartheid South Africa.
