# diaphanes

Basel/Berlin-based academic publisher specializing in German-language philosophy, media theory, and post-structuralist thought. The primary publisher for the German media theory tradition and its global interlocutors. Key press for works by [[Joseph Vogl]], [[Bernhard Siegert]], [[Friedrich Balke]], and [[Alexander Kluge]]. Also publishes significant translations (French theory, etc.) and produces English-language volumes.

## Key People
- **[[Joseph Vogl]]** — primary author; most of his major works
- **[[Alexander Kluge]]** — *Soll und Haben* (2009) with Vogl
- **[[Bernhard Siegert]]** — Kulturtechnik series
- **[[Friedrich Balke]]** — media theory

## Topics
- German media theory (Kulturtechnikforschung)
- Post-structuralism, continental philosophy
- Literary theory, aesthetics
- Political philosophy (sovereignty, biopolitics)
- Media history and archaeology

## Why Interesting
To know diaphanes is to know the intellectual infrastructure of German media theory. The books they publish often reach English translation 5–10 years later (if at all). Reading diaphanes catalogs = reading the future of Anglo-American theory.
