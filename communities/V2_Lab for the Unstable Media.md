# V2_ Lab for the Unstable Media

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Deep research completed. Full profile created — history, DEAF festival, key people, publications, residencies, and connection to Amber's work.

Europe's longest-running interdisciplinary lab for art and media technology, founded in 1981 in The Hague and relocated to Rotterdam in 1994. Built on the radical concept of "Unstable Media" — art that deliberately inhabits uncertain, evolving technological territories. V2_ presents, produces, archives, and publishes work at the intersection of art, technology and society. Home of the Dutch Electronic Art Festival (DEAF), one of the foundational European media art festivals. 40+ years of sustained critical engagement with technology — from interactive video and robotics (1980s) to bioart and AI (2020s).

## Mission & Objectives

**Core Mission:** V2_ creates a context in which issues regarding the social impact of technology are explored through critical dialogue, artistic reflection, and practice-oriented research. It provides a platform where artists, scientists, software and hardware developers, researchers, and theorists from various disciplines can share their findings and insights.

**The "Unstable Media" Concept (1987):** V2_'s founding theoretical position, articulated in the **Manifesto for Unstable Media** issued in 1987. The manifesto was intended to "ruffle the feathers of the visual arts" — arguing that art should engage with the instability, processuality, and emergence inherent in new media technologies rather than treating them as neutral tools. Art should be as unstable as the media it uses. This positioning defined V2_ as a critical-theoretical institution, not merely an exhibition venue.

**Practice-Research Synthesis:** V2_ operates across presentation, production, archiving, and publication — maintaining one of the most significant archives of media art documentation in Europe (the V2_ online archive of works, events, and people).

## History

### 1981 — Origin as Squat Collective
V2_ was founded in 1981 as an artist collective in a **squat at Vughterstraat 234 in The Hague** (the building's address, "V2_", giving the organization its name). The founding members — including **Alex Adriaansens** and **Joke Brouwer** — named it a multimedia center. The squat hosted concerts, performances, and experiments with analogue media: TVs, loudspeakers, Super-8 film. Exhibition spaces for paintings, installations, and machine art. Bands like **Sonic Youth**, **Einstürzende Neubauten**, and **Laibach** performed at V2_ in the early days. A DIY period combining music, video, sound, and visual arts.

### 1986-1987 — The Unstable Media Turn
Mid-1980s brought a turning point. V2_ began transforming from a multimedia organisation to a **center for art and media technology**. Dissatisfaction with the prevailing art practice led to the **Manifesto for Unstable Media** in 1987. The organization became formally known as the **V2_Institute for the Unstable Media**.

### 1987-1993 — Manifestations for the Unstable Media
V2_ organized annual **Manifestations for the Unstable Media** at its location in 's-Hertogenbosch. These pioneering festivals:
- Focused on new technology and electronic media in audiovisual arts
- Showed interactive video and sound installations
- Experimented with robotics and computer-generated electronic music and digital imagery
- Stimulated debate on art, theory, technology and society

**Manifestation Editions:**
- **1987** (27 Nov - 6 Dec) — First Manifestation
- **1988** (5-12 Nov)
- **1990** (17-25 Nov)
- **1992** (26 Sep - 4 Oct)
- **1993** (30 Sep - 9 Oct) — "The Body in Ruin"

### 1994 — Move to Rotterdam
V2_ moved to its **current location in central Rotterdam**, transitioning to focus on art in electronic networks and communications media, including the World Wide Web. During this period, artists began exploring digital techniques, virtual reality, and 3D projections. The Manifestation evolved into the **Dutch Electronic Art Festival (DEAF)**, becoming biannual.

### 1998 — V2_Lab Opens
The opening of **V2_Lab** in May 1998 — an international media laboratory — extended V2_'s practices toward **art production, research, and knowledge exchange**. The lab became a workspace for national and international artists, scientists, and technicians to collaborate on electronic art projects and technical research, including hardware development and open source software.

### 2018 — Alex Adriaansens Dies
Founding director **Alex Adriaansens** (14 February 1953 – 30 December 2018) passed away after more than three decades leading V2_. Leonardo/MIT Press published an obituary by Andreas Broeckmann.

### 2021 — 40th Anniversary
V2_ celebrated its 40th anniversary, publishing the book *[40 Years of V2_](https://v2.nl/)* documenting four decades of unstable media practice.

### 2024 — Michel van Dartel Departs
**Michel van Dartel**, who served as director of events and research professor, was affiliated with V2_Lab between **2005-2024** before transitioning fully to CARADT (Avans). As of 2024, the organization continues under new leadership.

### Test_Lab
V2_ runs **Test_Lab** — an ongoing event series providing a platform for artists, researchers, and technologists to present and test works-in-progress. Test_Lab functions as a **public laboratory** where experiments are shown before they are finished, embracing failure and process as part of the presentation. Multiple editions per year, each with a specific thematic focus (robotics, sound, interfaces, biology, etc.). One of the first institutional frameworks to treat testing and prototyping as valid artistic formats — influential model for participatory labs.

### Artist Residencies
V2_ has organized residencies for artists working at the intersection of art and technology since the opening of V2_Lab (1998). Residency formats:
- **Production residencies** — Long-term research and development of new works
- **Thematic residencies** — Focused around specific technical or conceptual areas
- The lab provides technical expertise, equipment, and international network connections
- National and international artists, scientists, and technicians collaborate
