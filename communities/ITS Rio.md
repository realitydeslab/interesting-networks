# ITS Rio (Instituto de Tecnologia e Sociedade)

[Institute for Technology and Society, Rio de Janeiro](https://itsrio.org/en/). Brazilian research organization working on technology governance, digital rights, and technology policy from a Global South perspective. Founded by [[Ronaldo Lemos]] and co-founders including [[Carlos Affonso Souza]]. Best known internationally for creating the **Marco Civil da Internet** — Brazil's Civil Rights Framework for the Internet. Currently co-hosting the **Dialogues on Philosophy and Technology 2025–26** series alongside [[Erasmus Institute for Philosophy and Technology]], [[Research Network for Philosophy and Technology]], and [[Hanart Forum]], initiated by [[Yuk Hui]]. ITS Rio is also developing the concept of **technodiversity** as a political-philosophical challenge to Silicon Valley universalism — essentially the applied governance version of Yuk Hui's cosmotechnics.

## Mission & Objectives

ITS Rio's mission is to **ensure that Brazil and the Global South respond creatively and appropriately to the opportunities provided by technology in the digital age**, and that the potential benefits are broadly shared. Through research and partnerships, ITS Rio:

- Analyzes the legal, social, economic and cultural dimensions of technology
- Advocates for public policies and private practices protecting privacy, freedom of expression and access to knowledge
- Offers education, training, and fellowship opportunities in tech policy
- Strengthens **Brazil, Latin America, and Global South voices** in international debates on technology, internet governance, and regulation

ITS Rio is in **special consultative status with the UN Economic and Social Council (ECOSOC)** — giving it formal status in UN processes. It is a non-profit, independent organization. Address: R. da Assembléia, 10, 40º andar, sala 4011, Centro, Rio de Janeiro.

## Key Publications & Output

- [**Marco Civil da Internet**](https://itsrio.org/en/) (2014) — Brazil's landmark Civil Rights Framework for the Internet. Drafted and advocated by ITS Rio's founders. Protects privacy, net neutrality, and freedom of expression online. This is ITS Rio's signature achievement — globally influential as a model for internet rights legislation.

- [**Whitepaper: Technodiversity**](https://itsrio.org/en/publicacoes/whitepaper-technodiversity/) (2024) — ITS Rio is building a global network to challenge the dominant narrative of technology as a universal force. "Think of it like biodiversity but for technology." The whitepaper argues for culturally-grounded AI and technology development against Silicon Valley's "one-size-fits-all" approach. Directly resonant with Yuk Hui's cosmotechnics.

- [**AI Safety and Opportunities Institute for Brazil**](https://itsrio.org/en/publicacoes/an-ai-safety-and-opportunities-institute-for-brazil-a-design-of-features-functions-and-potential-challenges/) (2025) — Report designing a national AI safety infrastructure for Brazil.

- [**Plurality, Technology and the Future of Democracy**](https://itsrio.org/en/publicacoes/report-plurality-technology-and-the-future-of-democracy/) — Report on tech and democratic governance.

- [**AI20: Artificial Intelligence in a Global Context**](https://itsrio.org/en/publicacoes/report-ai20-artificial-intelligence-in-a-global-context/) — Global AI policy report.

- [**From Cash to Code**](https://itsrio.org/pt/publicacoes/from-cash-to-code/) (2021) — Report on financial digitization.

- [**Legal Transformations in the Context of Artificial Intelligence**](https://itsrio.org/en/publicacoes/legal-transformations-in-the-context-of-artificial-intelligence-dialogues-from-the-digital-law-graduate-program/) — Dialogues from the Digital Law Graduate Program.

## Key Contributions

1. **Marco Civil da Internet** — Brazil's internet constitution; internationally cited as a model for rights-based internet governance. ITS Rio's team created it.
2. **Global Policy Fellowship** — bringing international researchers to Rio to engage with Brazilian tech governance; 10 editions, 57 fellows from around the world.
3. **Technodiversity as concept** — institutionalizing Yuk Hui–adjacent thinking at the policy level; challenging technological monoculture with pluralist alternative.
4. **Digital Law Graduate Program** — post-graduate certification program in digital law (partnership with UERJ), training next generation of Brazilian tech lawyers.
5. **ECOSOC status** — formal standing in UN processes, amplifying Global South perspectives at international level.
6. **Varandas ITS** — public lecture series bringing international thinkers to Rio and online; named "Varandas" (verandas/porches) — the informal social space of Brazilian culture.

### ITS Global Policy Fellowship
[Program page](https://itsrio.org/en/projetos/its-global-policy-fellowship-2/)

ITS Rio's flagship program for international researchers. **10th edition** (as of 2025), **57 fellows** from countries around the world. Annual open call.

Fellows work with the team that created the **Marco Civil da Internet** and choose one of four research areas:
1. **Rights and Technology** — content moderation, data protection, AI, privacy, intellectual property, digital identity, connectivity
2. **GovTech** — new development models, distributed technologies, digital public infrastructures (DPI)
3. **Democracy and Technology** — disinformation, civic engagement, AI & climate, online participation, future of work, technodiversity
