# Ignota Books

London-based experimental publisher and cultural programme co-founded in 2018 by [[Ben Vickers]] and Sarah Shin. Operates at the intersection of occult practice, ecology, philosophy, feminist spirituality, and experimental art. The closest thing to a publisher with a genuine cosmological programme: every book, event, and season is part of a coherent attempt to build new frameworks for navigating uncertain futures. Closer to Urbanomic in intellectual seriousness, but with an esoteric, body-centred, feminist-ecological register that Urbanomic doesn't have. Uniquely bridges the occult-mystical tradition with hard science (Lynn Margulis, Pauline Oliveros), AI (K Allado-McDowell), and contemporary art.

## Mission & Objectives

"Building a library of books that can help us navigate an uncertain future." Founded in 2018 by Sarah Shin and Ben Vickers. Ignota publishes books and runs events that draw on **sacred and esoteric traditions, ecology science, experimental music, feminist philosophy, and contemporary art** — not as kitsch mysticism but as serious epistemological alternatives to dominant Western rationalism. The name "Ignota" references Hildegard of Bingen's invented language *Lingua Ignota* ("unknown language") — a medieval signal for alternative knowledge systems, secret codes, and other ways of knowing.

Their model is a **total cultural programme**, not just a publisher:
- Books with editorial coherence
- Seasonal event programmes around each publication
- Annual *Ignota Diary* (occult planner)
- *The Mountain* membership portal (audio content: poetry, music, weird tech, ritual)
- Collaborations with institutions across London and beyond

## Key Publications & Output

### Books
- **[*Pharmako-AI*](https://ignota.org/collections/frontpage/products/pharmako-ai)** (K Allado-McDowell, 2021) — The first book co-written with GPT-3. Animist cybernetics, Peruvian shamanism meets AI. **Seminal text** in AI × mysticism literature. Established Ignota as the publisher for the AI-spirituality edge.
- **[*Air Age Blueprint*](https://ignota.org/products/air-age-blueprint)** (K Allado-McDowell, 2022) — Sequel, also co-written with GPT-3. Human-machine poesis, entheogenic futurism, technological animism.
- **[*Quantum Listening*](https://ignota.org/products/quantum-listening)** (Pauline Oliveros, 2022) — Foreword by Laurie Anderson, introduction by IONE, illustrations by Aura Satz. Manifesto for Deep Listening as activism and radical social matrix transformation. Bridging avant-garde music with spiritual practice.
- **[*Seeds*](https://ignota.org/products/seeds)** (ed. Sarah Shin, Jay Drinkall, Marleen Boschen, 2022) — Companion to Memory Garden project at Biennale Gherdëina, Italy. Plant rituals + ecological practice + Lynn Margulis + Etel Adnan.
- **[*Altered States*](https://ignota.org/products/altered-states)** (2022) — Poetry anthology around altered states of consciousness.
- **[*Gaia and Philosophy*](https://ignota.org/products/gaia-and-philosophy)** (Lynn Margulis + Dorion Sagan, 2023) — Illustrations by Anicka Yi. New intro by Dorion Sagan. Gaia theory fusing science, mathematics, philosophy, ecology, mythology. Challenges Western anthropocentrism via symbiotic biology.
- **Ignota Diary** (annual, created by Sarah Shin and Ben Vickers) — Occult planner filled with magical/sacred dates worldwide, astrological navigation, sonic meditations by Oliveros, seed bombs by Jenna Sutela, rituals by adrienne maree brown etc. A community-building artefact as much as a book.

### Event Seasons (Thematic Multi-Event Programmes)
- **QUANTUM LISTENING season** (2022–2023) — Events at Reference Point, Cafe OTO, Nottingham Contemporary, Gray Area (San Francisco). Around Deep Listening, sound consciousness, meditation as activism.
- **Worlding series** (2022) — Six talks by Reza Negarestani, Amelia Winger-Bearskin, Alice Bucknell, John Tresch, Flavie Audi, SJ Anderson. Speculative cosmologies, worldbuilding, ontology.
- **Gaia Season: A Body in the Form of a Planet** (2023) — Events at Science Gallery London, Camden Arts Centre, Reference Point, Unsound Festival (Krakow). Around *Gaia and Philosophy* launch. Multiple events including film screening (*Symbiotic Earth*), workshops, panel discussions.
- **Ignota Gathering: The Spiral** (October 2023, St James's Church Piccadilly, London) — 5th birthday celebration. Full day/evening of performances, dialogues. With Federico Campagna, Jennifer Higgie, Lawrence Lek, Tai Shani, Shumon Basar, NYX, Lucinda Chua. Supported by ICA London.

### The Mountain (Membership Portal)
Platform for aural exploration: poetry, music, "weird tech," ritual and practice. Members-only audio content. Community infrastructure beyond books.

## Key Contributions

1. **Establishing the AI × mysticism × animism publishing niche** — Pharmako-AI (2021) arrived before anyone else was seriously thinking about AI as spiritual partner. Ignota recognized this before the AI hype cycle.
2. **Archiving and disseminating radical feminist esoteric knowledge** — Pauline Oliveros (Deep Listening), Ursula K. Le Guin, Hildegard of Bingen, Leonora Carrington, Zora Neale Hurston, Octavia Butler — treated as serious intellectual ancestors.
3. **Creating seasonal cultural infrastructure** — Not just publishing but sustained event programmes that build communities around ideas over months.
4. **Bridging science and esotericism** — Lynn Margulis's Gaia theory, Merlin Sheldrake's mycology, quantum physics → presented alongside occult traditions as complementary epistemologies, not opposites.
