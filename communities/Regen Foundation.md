# Regen Foundation

Nonprofit building "ecological institutions" and "animist organizations" — governance and financial infrastructure where rivers, forests, and entire biomes hold funds, make decisions, and participate in protocols of civic care. Nonprofit arm of [[Regen Network]], a blockchain platform for ecological regeneration. Based in Northfield, MA. Founded 2019. 2–10 people, highly efficient.

The core proposition: digital addressing systems make it technically possible for non-humans to be endpoints in digital networks. A forest can have a cryptographic identity. A river can hold communal flood-resilience funds. A wetland can be a governance body. Regen Foundation builds the legal, economic, and technical scaffolding to make this real — not as speculation but as implemented institution design.

## Topics
- **Ecological institutions** — governance frameworks where living systems (forests, rivers, wetlands) gain layered legibility across law, economics, and technology
- **Animist organizations** — organizations treating the Earth as participant, not backdrop
- **Bioregional governance** — coordinating across watershed- and landscape-scale rather than by political borders
- **ReFi (Regenerative Finance)** — financial systems and instruments that prioritize regeneration of ecosystems
- **Rights of Nature / Legal personhood** — giving non-humans legal standing as precondition for economic and governance participation
- **DAOs as commons infrastructure** — blockchains as "addressing" technologies that make it possible to represent trees, watersheds in systems of valuation
- **Indigenous governance protocols** — consent, consensus, witnessing as ancestral precedents for modern institutional design
- **Living covenants** — DAOs encoded as public infrastructure inherently incentivizing stewardship

## Key Publication

## Why Interesting

This is the most practically-implemented "more-than-human governance" project in the interesting-networks graph. They are not doing speculative design fiction — they are:
- Building actual legal personhood infrastructure for rivers and forests
- Deploying blockchain identity/wallets for non-human entities
- Piloting governance where wetlands hold funds and make allocation decisions
- Creating a physical book that itself enacts its principles (gift economy, not commodity)

**The concept pivot:** Regen Foundation reframes the digital/nature separation as false — "The world always already was an internet." Computation, signal processing, and institutional organization are patterns latent in ecological systems long before digital tech. This is not "nature-inspired computing" but the inverse: recognizing that living systems ARE already institutions, and building tools that let us see and work with that.

**The Audrey Tang connection** is a strong signal: Tang endorsed not just the ideas but the governance model. Taiwan's experiment with digital democracy (pol.is, vTaiwan) is the closest precedent to what Regen Foundation is attempting at ecological scale.

**Sara Horowitz connection** is also key: the Freelancers Union/Mutualist Society angle brings a labor-organizing, commoning lineage into what could otherwise be purely a crypto-tech story. This frames Regen Foundation's work as continuous with mutual aid traditions, not a departure from them.

**The IDK (Institution Development Kit)** is the most interesting output: a physical card game for building governance systems. This design choice — ritual + protocol, tearable and playable — intentionally bridges the world of ceremony and the world of smart contracts. It's applied governance design as material culture.

Amber's specific interests perfectly map to Regen Foundation's work:
- **More-than-human** — they're literally building governance systems where non-humans vote, hold funds, own IP
- **Critical computing** — they critique how "legibility systems" commodify ecosystems while using those systems to extend rights
- **Posthuman** — the concept of the "animist organization" directly echoes posthuman theory
- **Participatory design** — IDK workshops are participatory institution design at community scale
- **Climate** — entire project is climate infrastructure at institutional level
