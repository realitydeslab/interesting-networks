# Signal Foundation

Nonprofit organization that builds and operates [Signal](https://signal.org/) — the gold-standard end-to-end encrypted messaging application. Founded in 2018 by [Brian Acton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brian_Acton) (WhatsApp co-founder) with a $50M seed donation. Signal Protocol underlies WhatsApp's encryption and is the canonical approach to encrypted messaging. Used by hundreds of millions of people globally, with strong adoption among journalists, activists, dissidents, and privacy-conscious individuals.

**President:** [[Meredith Whittaker]] (since October 2022)  
**Executive Chairman:** Brian Acton  
**Original creator:** [Moxie Marlinspike](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Moxie_Marlinspike)

## Mission & Objectives
Develop and maintain private communication tools. Operate as a nonprofit, directly accountable to users — not advertisers or investors. Explicitly rejects the surveillance business model. Funded primarily through donations (primarily Brian Acton's ongoing support and growing small-donor base).

## Key People

- **[Brian Acton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brian_Acton)** — Executive Chairman; co-founder of WhatsApp; Signal's largest funder.

## Key Contributions
- **Signal Protocol** — the gold-standard cryptographic protocol for encrypted messaging. Open source, auditable. Used by WhatsApp, Google Messages, and many others.
- **Sealed sender** — metadata protection ensuring Signal cannot determine who is sending messages to whom.
- **Open source** — all Signal code is publicly auditable on [GitHub](https://github.com/signalapp).
- **Disappearing messages, note-to-self, payments** — privacy-first features pioneered by Signal.
- **Stories** — ephemeral messaging, built to meet user needs (especially Southeast Asia, South America) without surveillance.

## Why Interesting
Signal is the clearest existing proof-of-concept that privacy-protecting technology can be usable, popular, and nonprofitably operated. It represents a direct argument against "surveillance capitalism is inevitable." As a case study in alternative tech institution building, it's one of the most important organizations in tech.

From a critical computing perspective: Signal demonstrates that the technical and institutional choices embedded in tech architecture are political choices — choosing not to collect data IS a political act.
