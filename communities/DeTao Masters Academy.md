# DeTao Masters Academy (DTMA / 德稻大师)

Shanghai-based private educational institution and subsidiary of DeTao Group (上海德稻集群文化创意产业（集团）有限公司). Gathers 100+ global industry experts and academic leaders from 24 countries as named "Masters" — internationally recognized practitioners appointed to transmit expertise, lead studios, and design curricula for Chinese students and institutions. Core model: import Western/global intellectual capital into China's art-tech-design education infrastructure. Six domains: **design, fashion, arts, business, animation, film**. Partners with 67+ Chinese universities. **[[Roy Ascott]]** is DeTao Master of Technoetic Arts (2012–), and his **DeTao Technoetic Arts Studio** at SIVA is the most internationally significant output of DTMA for Amber's network.

## Mission & Objectives

DTMA was founded circa 2009–2010 as a subsidiary of DeTao Group to create a new model of elite design and creative education in China. The model:

- **"Masters" system**: Recruit internationally recognized practitioners as named "DeTao Masters" — not just visiting lecturers but branded intellectual authorities who establish named studios, design curricula, mentor students and faculty
- **Six domains**: Design, Fashion, Arts, Business, Animation & Film
- **O+O pedagogy**: DeTao's proprietary "Online + Offline" teaching model with dual-tutor interactive teaching (one international master + one Chinese co-facilitator)
- **University partnerships**: Not a standalone university — works through partnerships with 67 Chinese higher education institutions, 4 national government agencies, and 12 institutional units to embed Masters-designed curricula into existing degree programs
- **Joint experimental programs**: e.g., the "SIVA·德稻实验班" — a joint undergraduate experimental cohort with Shanghai Institute of Visual Arts (SIVA) offering degree-level education under Masters supervision
- **Master Lectures**: High-end public lecture series (300+ lectures held) — brand-building events featuring Masters speaking to broader creative industry audiences
- **Mission statement**: "Committed to providing international expertise in a Chinese cultural context to enhance creativity, innovation and business opportunities"

DTMA is not a traditional academy: it is an **education product company** — generating revenue by selling curricula, co-running programs, and providing educational consultancy to Chinese universities and institutions, while the "Masters" brand creates prestige and legitimacy.

The model gives Western expertise a Chinese-legible prestige framework (master-apprentice tradition recoded for global creative economy).

## Why Interesting

DTMA is interesting primarily as **institutional context for Roy Ascott** — his actual working base since 2014. Understanding DTMA explains HOW Ascott operates in China: not as a university professor but as a branded "Master" with his own named studio and curriculum, embedded within SIVA's degree structure through a commercial education company. This is a specific Chinese model of intellectual import that differs from Western residency or visiting professor models.

Secondarily, DTMA is interesting as a case study in **how China absorbed Western art-tech pedagogy in the 2010s** — through commercial education intermediaries that recruited prestige names, built campus infrastructure (NBBJ), ran lecture series, and embedded curricula into university programs. The Planetary Collegium node is the most academically credible output; the rest of DTMA's "Masters" program is more aligned with Chinese design industry upskilling than with Amber's research interests.

The institution's apparent decline (defunct websites, reduced web presence) after ~2016 may reflect the broader contraction of the "Western expertise import" model as Chinese design education developed its own prestige institutions.
