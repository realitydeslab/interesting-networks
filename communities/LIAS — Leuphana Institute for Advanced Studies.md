# LIAS — Leuphana Institute for Advanced Studies in Culture and Society

The [Leuphana Institute for Advanced Studies in Culture and Society (LIAS)](https://www.leuphana.de/en/research-centers/lias.html) at [Leuphana University Lüneburg](https://www.leuphana.de/en/) is a research institute for visiting international fellows in the humanities, cultural studies, and social sciences. Founded in 2022 and co-directed by [[Erich Hörl]] and [[Susanne Leeb]], it operates out of the iconic Daniel Libeskind-designed Central Building. LIAS is conceptually grounded in [[ICAM Leuphana]]'s theoretical tradition — general ecology, critique of the technological condition, the politics of knowledge — while opening this out to the full spectrum of the humanities and social sciences, with a distinctive emphasis on **Global South dialogue** and **transformative knowledge**. Think Wissenschaftskolleg Berlin but with an explicitly postcolonial and critical-theoretical orientation.

## What Is LIAS?

Founded in April 2022, LIAS is Leuphana's answer to the major European advanced institutes (Wissenschaftskolleg Berlin, ZiF Bielefeld, IAS Princeton) but with a distinctive orientation: **critical and imaginative humanities research on social transformation**, with deliberate emphasis on non-Eurocentric voices. It is funded through 2027 by the "Spitzenforschung für Niedersachsen" (SPRUNG) programme, and from 2027 by the "Zukunft. Niedersachsen" initiative.

LIAS is part of a broader research ecosystem at Leuphana that includes:
- [[ICAM Leuphana]] — Institute for Culture and Aesthetics of Digital Media (parent intellectual milieu)
- **Center for Digital Cultures (CDC)**
- **Center for Critical Studies (CCS)**
- **Center for the Study of Democracy (ZDEMO)**
- **DFG Research Training Group "Cultures of Critique"** — doctoral programme co-run by Hörl, Söntgen, and Beyes

LIAS currently has a thematic focus on **"Culture and Society in Transformation"** structured around four mottos:
1. **Thinking Across Hemispheres** — decolonial, multiperspectival, non-Eurocentric
2. **Critique and Imagination** — diagnosis of the present + opening new possibilities
3. **Potentialisation** — revealing hidden possibilities against inevitability discourse
4. **Co-Existence in a Globalized World** — global entanglement and polycrisis

### Phase 2 (from 2027)
LIAS's second funding phase centres on **"transformative knowledge"** — the historical and political conditions under which processes of change (cybernetisation, decolonisation, globalisation, climate challenge) have been framed as technical problems, and how genuinely transformative knowledge resists that reduction.

## Intellectual Lineage

LIAS sits at the intersection of several European critical theory traditions:

1. **German Media Theory (Medienwissenschaft)** — Kittler, cybernetics, cultural techniques. ICAM's historical specialisation.
2. **French Continental Philosophy of Technology** — Simondon, Stiegler, Nancy, Foucault, Deleuze/Guattari. Hörl's primary lineage.
3. **Postcolonial/Decolonial Theory** — The Global South emphasis, Premesh Lalu's Cape Town connection, Radha D'Souza, Laban Ayiro. Actively de-provincializing European theory.
4. **Feminist/Postcapitalist Theory** — Katherine Gibson (Gibson-Graham), care ethics, commons, more-than-human economies.
5. **Frankfurt School / Critical Theory** — Alex Demirović, "Cultures of Critique" doctoral programme.

**Key concept:** **General Ecology** (Hörl) — the idea that we now live in a technosphere that replaces nature as the encompassing milieu. Every form of life, every mode of existence, every mode of participation is now shaped by technological environments. The political question is how to develop a critical ecology of this condition rather than simply celebrating or condemning it.

## Why Interesting for Amber

LIAS is the most important European advanced-studies fellowship for Amber's research trajectory, for several reasons:

1. **Hörl's general ecology = Amber's theoretical home.** The concept of general ecology (technosphere, milieu, environmentality) directly maps onto Amber's more-than-human design, agent ethology, and machine behavior research. Simondon + ecology + AI = the exact intersection.

2. **ALife/more-than-human + critical theory crossover.** LIAS fellows like Bruno Moreschi (AI training data critique), Pedro Oliveira (sonic epistemologies), Paula Bertúa (multispecies imagination) suggest LIAS welcomes researchers doing exactly this kind of practice-based + theoretically grounded AI/more-than-human work.

3. **Global South emphasis matches Amber's non-Western tech theory interest.** The "Thinking Across Hemispheres" strand is where Eastern European and Chinese techno-philosophical traditions could slot in — Amber's interest in cosmotechnics and Yuk Hui's project would resonate here.

4. **Konkrete Verbindung:** Amber knows the ICAM ecosystem (Hörl, Müggenburg, Warnke). A LIAS fellowship would be the formalized version of that intellectual engagement — 12 months in Lüneburg, embedded in the colloquium, producing major work.

5. **Artist Fellowship.** LIAS awards an annual Artist Fellowship for 3-12 months to visual artists, writers, filmmakers, sound artists. Amber's practice-based AR/MR design work could fit here. The Kunstraum connection would amplify this.
