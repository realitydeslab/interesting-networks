# AI Now Institute

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Deep research completed. Expanded from stub to full profile with history, key people, annual reports, policy impact, and connections to Amber's interests.

The most influential independent research institute in the world focused on the social and political implications of artificial intelligence. Founded in 2017 by [[Kate Crawford]] and [[Meredith Whittaker]] in partnership with NYU — first university institute focused on social implications of AI, and the first AI institute founded and led by women. Now fully independent of universities and refuses all tech industry funding. Publishes annual AI "Landscape Reports" that set the terms for public discourse on AI accountability.

## Mission & Objectives
**Mission:** Produce diagnosis and policy research on artificial intelligence — specifically to redirect away from the current trajectory of unbridled commercial surveillance, consolidation of power in very few companies, and a lack of public accountability.

**Core philosophy:** AI's integration into social domains — health care, education, criminal justice — is being treated as a purely technical problem. AI Now's role is to treat these as social problems first, and bring in domain experts in sociology, law, and history to study the implications. AI as a political and economic force, not just a technology.

**Current approach:** Identify and capitalize on actionable policy windows; advance narratives that chart a long-term strategy for the field; catalyze energy toward action in close partnership with a broad coalition of allies including civil rights organizations, labor groups, and community organizers.

**Independence rule:** As of mid-2022, AI Now does not accept funding from corporate donors, including tech companies. Funded entirely by foundations. This is a structural commitment to credibility — they hold accountable precisely those who would otherwise fund them.

## History & Founding

**2016:** A symposium spearheaded by the Obama White House Office of Science and Technology Policy (OSTP) focused on near-term implications of AI in social domains: inequality, labor, ethics, and healthcare. Led by [Meredith Whittaker](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Meredith_Whittaker) (founder of Google's Open Research Group) and [Kate Crawford](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kate_Crawford) (principal researcher at Microsoft Research). This is the origin event — AI Now grew from this.

**November 2017:** AI Now holds a second symposium on AI and social issues and publicly launches the **AI Now Institute** in partnership with [New York University Tandon School of Engineering](https://engineering.nyu.edu/) and [NYU Center for Data Science](https://cds.nyu.edu/). Claimed as the first university research institute focused on the social implications of AI and the first AI institute founded and led by women.

**2017 (same year):** Publishes the first [AI Now Report](https://ainowinstitute.org/publication/ai-now-2017-report-2) — calls for an end to "black box" algorithms in core social domains (criminal justice, healthcare, welfare, education). Major media coverage, shapes the emerging AI accountability discourse.

**April 2018:** Releases the first [Algorithmic Impact Assessment (AIA)](https://ainowinstitute.org/aiareport2018.pdf) framework — proposing governments assess AI use in public agencies similar to environmental impact assessments, requiring public disclosure and external expert evaluation.

**2019:** Amba Kak joins as Director of Global Policy.

**2021:** AI Now leadership (Whittaker and Kak) appointed as Senior Advisors on AI to FTC Chair [Lina Khan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lina_Khan) — direct pathway from research to regulatory enforcement.

**Mid-2022:** Fully independent of NYU. Adopts strict no-corporate-funding policy. Amba Kak becomes Executive Director.

**2022:** Meredith Whittaker departs to become President of the [Signal Foundation](https://signalfoundation.org/) but retains Chief Advisor role.

**2023:** Annual "Landscape Report" argues meaningful tech reform must focus on addressing concentrated power in the tech industry — a structural critique beyond individual AI ethics failures.

**2024:** Awarded Global AI Policy Leader in civil society organization category. Amba Kak named TIME100 AI (100 most influential in AI).

**2025 (June):** Publishes [*Artificial Power: AI Now 2025 Landscape Report*](https://ainowinstitute.org/2025-landscape) — puts forward actionable strategy for the public to reclaim agency over AI, moves from diagnosis to action.

## Key Publications & Output

### Annual Landscape Reports (Flagship)
A series of major reports mapping the state of AI and its social implications. Each sets the terms for that year's AI policy discourse. All free to download.

- **[AI Now 2025 Landscape: *Artificial Power*](https://ainowinstitute.org/2025-landscape)** (June 2025) — "In the aftermath of the 'AI boom,' examines how push to integrate AI products everywhere grants AI companies power that goes far beyond their deep pockets." Moves from diagnosis to action. Authors: Kate Brennan, Amba Kak, Sarah Myers West.

- **[AI Now 2023 Landscape](https://ainowinstitute.org/2023-landscape)** — Argues meaningful reform of tech sector must focus on addressing concentrated power in the tech industry. Widely covered in Vox, tech press.

- **[AI Now 2017 Report](https://ainowinstitute.org/publication/ai-now-2017-report-2)** (2017) — First major report. Called for end to "black box" algorithms in criminal justice, healthcare, welfare, education. "Current framings of AI ethics are failing." Ten strategic recommendations. Covered by Wired.

### Policy Papers & Frameworks
- **[Algorithmic Impact Assessments](https://ainowinstitute.org/aiareport2018.pdf)** (2018) — Framework for government assessment of AI in public agencies, analogous to environmental impact assessments.
- **Regulating Biometrics** — Edited collection on global approaches to biometric surveillance regulation (ed. Amba Kak)
- Research on algorithmic accountability, surveillance, labor, and democracy published with ACLU, Data & Society, Ada Lovelace Institute

### Congressional Testimonies
- Meredith Whittaker — testified on "AI: Societal and Ethical Implications" (House Science Committee, 2019) and on facial recognition (House Oversight, 2020)
- Amba Kak — testified on AI and data privacy (House, 2023) and Senate Commerce Committee (2024)
- Alli Finn (Partnership & Strategy Lead) — testified at Philadelphia City Council (2025)
- Amba Kak — gave remarks before UN General Assembly on AI Governance (September 2025)

## Key Research Areas (Current)
1. **Concentrated tech power** — How AI accelerates monopolization; antitrust and market structure
2. **AI and labor** — Job displacement, worker surveillance, AI in workplaces
3. **Algorithmic accountability** — Bias, discrimination, impact assessment frameworks
4. **AI and surveillance** — Facial recognition, biometrics, state and commercial surveillance
5. **AI and warfare** — Military AI, weapons systems, safety failures in high-stakes AI
6. **AI and energy infrastructure** — Data centers, nuclear power, environmental cost
7. **AI governance and regulation** — Policy frameworks, international coordination
8. **AI market dynamics** — Tech "bubble," investor hype vs. public interest

## Key Contributions
- **Invented the "AI accountability" discourse** — before AI Now, critical AI research was scattered across law, STS, and CS departments. AI Now named and organized the field.
- **Algorithmic Impact Assessment** — the 2018 AIA framework influenced policy discussions globally, including EU AI Act design.
- **Annual Landscape Report format** — established the genre of the critical AI annual report, now widely imitated.
- **FTC advisory role** — direct bridge between research and enforcement. Whittaker and Kak serving as Lina Khan's AI advisors was unprecedented for an independent research institute.
- **No-corporate-funding model** — demonstrated that rigorous AI policy research could exist without tech industry money, maintaining credibility to critique the same industry.
- **Kate Crawford's FATE/FAccT lineage** — the FAccT conference (primary academic venue for AI accountability research) grew from the FATE group co-founded by Crawford at Microsoft Research. AI Now and FAccT share intellectual DNA.

## Why Interesting
**The structural critics.** In a landscape flooded with AI ethics consultants, AI safety labs funded by tech companies, and compliance frameworks written by the industry itself, AI Now is the institution that says: this is a power problem, not a technology problem. That distinction matters enormously.

**For Amber's critical computing thread:** AI Now embodies "computing as political act" — they don't just study AI's social effects, they actively work to reshape policy and build coalitions for structural change. This is the most consequential version of what critical computing can be.

**For Amber's feminist thread:** Co-founded by women, first AI institute led by women, explicitly feminist in its orientation to power, labor, and surveillance. Crawford, Whittaker, Kak, West — this is a female-led intellectual infrastructure in a male-dominated field.

**The FAccT genealogy:** Understanding AI Now means understanding FAccT (the academic conference), the FATE group (Microsoft Research), Kate Crawford's intellectual trajectory, and the Knowing Machines project. These form a coherent intellectual ecosystem that Amber should map.

**Edge of chaos:** AI Now's policy research responds to real-world events in near-real-time. Their news page tracks what's happening *right now* with AI power, not what happened in academic publishing cycles. This is live intellectual territory.
