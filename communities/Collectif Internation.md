# Collectif Internation

Transdisciplinary collective of 52 "politicised researchers" — scientists, economists, philosophers, anthropologists, artists, designers, engineers, lawyers, and activists — co-founded in September 2018 by philosopher **[[Bernard Stiegler]]** and curator **Hans Ulrich Obrist** at the Serpentine Galleries' Work Marathon in London. Their mission: to propose a new macro-economic model to address the Anthropocene crisis, submitted to the United Nations on the centenary of the League of Nations (January 2020, Geneva). Post-Stiegler (died 2020), the collective continues his late political philosophy work through the posthumous *Bifurcate* (2021) and connected networks.

## Mission & Objectives

The Internation Collective operates on a single audacious thesis: **the Anthropocene is an Entropocene** — the contemporary period is characterized by a massive increase of entropy in all its forms: physical (thermodynamic), biological (biodiversity loss), and informational (post-truth, cognitive disruption). The dominant macro-economic models, grounded in Newtonian mechanics, are incapable of accounting for or reducing this entropy. Therefore, a new economic paradigm is required: one that actively generates **negentropy** (in the sense of Schrödinger) and **anti-entropy** (in the sense of Bailly, Longo and Montévil).

Key theoretical commitments:
- **Locality vs. internationalism**: Drawing on anthropologist Marcel Mauss's 1920 distinction — the local cannot be dissolved in the global. The concept of "internation" means nations cooperating in networks **without erasing their local dimensions**.
- **Contributory research**: New research methods bringing together diverse academic fields with **territorial actors** — local communities, cities, regions — to co-create sustainable economic models that can be reproduced elsewhere.
- **Negentropic economies**: Economic models designed to increase biological, informational, and thermodynamic negentropy rather than accelerate its depletion.

The collective positions itself as a response to UN Secretary-General António Guterres's calls on climate change, arguing that the "lack of political will" is actually a symptom of theoretical disorientation — there is no adequate conceptual framework to understand the challenges.

## The "Internation" Concept

The concept of *internation* was coined by the French anthropologist **Marcel Mauss** in 1920, in the context of the creation of the League of Nations. Mauss's key insight: genuine internationalism should **not** erase national/local specificity. An *internation* is not a supranational authority but a dynamic **agreement between localities** (biological niches, cities, nations, the biosphere) to cooperate while maintaining their particular character.

The Internation Collective reinterprets this for the Anthropocene:
- Localities must be understood at multiple scales: biological niches, cities/nations, the Earth as biospheric locality
- The Anthropocene crisis is partly a crisis of locality — extremist politics exploit the repression of local identity by globalization
- "Inclusive multilateralism" (Guterres's own phrase) should take the form of territorial laboratories: local experimentations that can be evaluated and reproduced at scale

This is philosophically distinct from both **nationalism** (which fetishizes local at the expense of global) and **globalism** (which dissolves local into universal). The internation is a **third way**: federated localities that cooperate without homogenizing.

## Key Publications & Output

- **[*Bifurcate: 'There is No Alternative'*](https://www.openbookpublishers.com/books/10.11647/obp.0277)** (2021, Open Book Publishers) — The collective's central manifesto, edited and led by Stiegler before his death, published posthumously. Argues that humanity must "bifurcate" from its current entropic trajectory. There is no incremental reform possible — a radical break is required. Co-authored by the collective. Available open access. This is Stiegler's last major political-philosophical project.
- **"Arguments on Transition"** (2019–2020) — Work-in-progress chapters submitted to the UN, organized around 9 open thematic groups. Available at [internation.world/arguments-on-transition](https://internation.world/arguments-on-transition/)
- **Letter to António Guterres** (Nov 11, 2019) — Co-signed by Hans Ulrich Obrist and Bernard Stiegler on behalf of the 52-member collective. Formally submitted the Internation framework to the UN Secretary-General. Articulates the collective's theoretical framework and calls for territorial laboratory initiatives.

## Key People

- **[[Bernard Stiegler]]** ⭐⭐⭐ — co-founder. France's most influential philosopher of technology (1952–2020). PhD from EHESS under Jacques Derrida. Founder of [IRI](https://www.iri.centrepompidou.fr/) (Institut de recherche et d'innovation, Centre Georges-Pompidou), [Ars Industrialis](http://arsindustrialis.org/), and pharmakon.fr school. Key works: *Technics and Time* (3 vols), *Symbolic Misery*, *The Neganthropocene*, *Bifurcate*. Mentor to **[[Yuk Hui]]** (cosmotechnics). Died by suicide Aug 5, 2020.
- **Hans Ulrich Obrist** ⭐⭐ — co-founder. Artistic Director of [Serpentine Galleries](https://www.serpentinegalleries.org/), London. One of the world's most influential curators. Launched the Internation project through the Serpentine's Work Marathon. Key node linking avant-garde art with philosophy, science, and politics.
- **[[Yuk Hui]]** ⭐⭐⭐ — doctoral student of Stiegler at EHESS. Philosopher of technology, cosmotechnics, and Chinese philosophy of technology. Now independent/Hong Kong. Key figure continuing Stiegler's legacy with his own framework. Author of *Recursivity and Contingency*, *Cosmotechnics as Cosmopolitics*, *Art and Cosmotechnics*. (see separate note)
- **Caroline Stiegler** — Stiegler's wife and former lawyer. Co-founder of Ars Industrialis. Involved in posthumous curation of Stiegler's work and Internation continuation.
- **Barbara Stiegler** — Bernard's daughter, philosopher at Université Bordeaux-Montaigne. Author of *Il faut s'adapter* (2019), critiquing neoliberal adaptation discourse. Part of Stiegler's philosophical network.
- **Marc Crépon** — philosopher, Ars Industrialis co-founder. ENS Paris. Collaborator on political philosophy.
- **Catherine Perret** — philosopher, Ars Industrialis co-founder. Works on aesthetics and memory.

The collective also comprised (from the 52-member roster): **scientists, mathematicians, legal scholars, economists, anthropologists, sociologists, doctors, artists, engineers, business leaders, activists, and designers** — 52 members in total from around the world.

## Key Contributions

1. **Reviving Mauss's "Internation"** — rescued a buried 1920 concept and made it relevant to 21st century governance debates around Anthropocene and climate change
2. **Entropocene thesis** — naming the Anthropocene as fundamentally a thermodynamic, biological, and informational entropy crisis; connecting Schrödinger's negentropy to economics
3. **Contributory research model** — proposed a methodology combining academic research with territorial actors; distinct from both pure theory and pure activism
4. **Submission to the UN** — unusually direct act of philosopher-researchers entering formal international governance discourse (rare crossover between continental philosophy and UN diplomacy)
5. ***Bifurcate* manifesto** — provides a radical political-philosophical alternative to incrementalism; argues TINA (There Is No Alternative, Thatcher) must itself be bifurcated from

## Why Interesting

Three reasons this is ⭐⭐⭐:

1. **Posthumous legacy as live network**: Stiegler is the Continental philosophical tradition's most serious engager with digital technology, time, and political economy. His late work on Internation and contributory economy is where philosophy, technology, and existential risk converge. This is exactly Amber's territory.

2. **Cosmotechnics bridge**: The Stiegler → Yuk Hui lineage is one of the most important in contemporary philosophy of technology. Yuk Hui's cosmotechnics (non-Western technic pluralism) developed partly through friction with Stiegler. Amber's China Academy of Art connection makes this a live, close network — not a distant archival interest.

3. **The "bifurcate" move**: The intellectual provocation that there is no alternative — that the only genuine response to the Anthropocene is a radical break, not incremental optimization — resonates with Amber's interests in existential risk (ERA fellowship) and non-accelerationist tech futures. The Internation is explicitly anti-"AI for good" optimism — it demands structural transformation.

The Internation should be understood as **the political wing of Stiegler's pharmacological philosophy** — the attempt to translate the theory of technology as pharmakon (poison/remedy) into institutional practice and UN diplomacy.
