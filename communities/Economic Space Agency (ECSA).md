# Economic Space Agency (ECSA)

A post-capitalist economic technology cooperative building peer-to-peer protocols for programmable economic spaces — infrastructure for communities to design their own economies beyond the price monoculture of capitalism. Part think-tank, part protocol lab, part art-theory collective. ECSA's premise: the economy is a communications medium, and like the internet, it can be redesigned from below.

## Origin: Robin Hood Minor Asset Management

ECSA has deep roots in [[Robin Hood Minor Asset Management]] — a decentralized hedge fund and artistic/financial experiment established January 2012 by [[Akseli Virtanen]], Tere Vadén, and others in Finland. Robin Hood used algorithmic mimicry of Wall Street trades to extract value from financial markets and redistribute it to cultural/political projects — essentially open-sourcing finance as an art practice.

- **09/2012** — Robin Hood presented at **Kassel Documenta(13)**, famously antagonizing the art world
- **09/2013** — $100k grant from **Kone Foundation** for development
- **12/2013** — Crypto-equity concept developed
- **06/2014** — Robin Hood team moves to California; begins collaborating with Bruce Initiative for Rethinking Capitalism/UCSC (Robert Meister, Benjamin Lozano) and the Volatility Working Group in NYC (Benjamin Lee, Robert Wosnitzer, Dick Bryan, Jonathan Beller)

## Key Concepts

ECSA constructs a whole new vocabulary for post-capitalist economic infrastructure:

- **Post-capitalist economic media** — economy as a communications protocol, redesignable from below like the internet
- **Economic grammar** — shared protocol for agents to transact, stake, and express value across different communities
- **Network derivative** — a financial instrument that prices exposure to network performance, enabling communities to share risk without centralized ownership
- **Social derivative** — derivatives used to create different socialities (vs. financial derivatives used for profit extraction)
- **Big Put** — the ECSA token; a "put" on the fragility of capitalism itself; shorting capitalism by building a competing network
- **Econaut / Econautics** — navigating the new economic space; analogized to Renaissance scientists inventing instruments to measure a newly-opened reality
- **dEX** — distributed exchange protocol; partition-resistant, scalable P2P exchange of any tokenized information
- **dCredit** — distributed credit protocol; agents issue collateralized credit enabling netted clearing on network ledgers
- **dStake** — distributed stakeholding protocol
- **dPerformance** — distributed performance index protocol
- **Agency (composite agent)** — a collective agent: network of agents appearing as a coherent whole without central decision-making; a many-to-many contractual relationship via network derivative
- **Collateralization of affect** — using financial instruments to collateralize non-monetary values; "collateralization of affect produces community"
- **Decolonised finance** — reorienting financial protocols so communities rule their own value flows; unmaking the naturalized dominance of profit-as-only-measure

## Organizational Design

ECSA is itself designed as a **social derivative**: a collective risk-generating and risk-arbitraging practice structured to make flows of stakes and risks intertwined. The organizational protocol allows both open/flexible engagement and tighter sub-teams coordinated by peer-to-peer stakeholding.

Legal structure: ECSA Inc. (California) + Economic Space Foundation (Zug, Switzerland, est. August 2017).
