# Kadist

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-03-01:** Full deep research completed. Expanded from stub with full mission, key people, programs, commissions, and connections.
- **2026-02-27:** Stub created via American Artist deep research.

International non-profit contemporary art foundation operating across five continents — commissioning, collecting, and presenting politically engaged art from underrepresented regions. A key institutional node at the intersection of critical computing, climate art, post-colonial media, and speculative practice. Their commission programmes (especially "Ways of Reading") have produced landmark critical technology artworks.

## Mission & Objectives

[KADIST](https://kadist.org/) believes contemporary artists make an important contribution to a progressive society through their artwork, which often addresses key issues relevant to the present day. Dedicated to exhibiting artists from more than one hundred countries represented in its collection, KADIST affirms contemporary art's role within social discourse and facilitates new connections across cultures.

Key pillars:
- **Collection** — 2,200+ artworks by 1,400+ artists from underrepresented regions globally. Artists are nominated by a worldwide advisor network and selected through a democratic process. The collection serves as a research tool and starting point for programming.
- **Exhibitions** — Hubs in Paris (19bis/21 rue des Trois-Frères, 18th arr.) and San Francisco/Americas (formerly). Also programs in Asia (China, Southeast Asia), Latin America, and Europe.
- **Commissions** — Multi-year thematic programmes commissioning new works (e.g., "Ways of Reading" 2018-2021, which produced American Artist's *Shaper of God*).
- **Residencies** — Résidence Tallard in Paris (Ella Marder, Program & Development Director). Specific open-call details not currently listed on the website.
- **KADIST.tv** — Streaming platform for moving-image works from the collection. Free access.
- **AFIELD** — Separate but connected fellowship programme for socially-engaged artists (founded by Sandra Terdjman, KADIST co-founder). Annual $10,000 fellowships. Independent organization since 2018.

## Key Contributions

- **Ways of Reading** (2018-2021, San Francisco, curated by Adam Kleinman) — A 3-year programme of seminars, exhibitions, and commissions addressing data, AI, and critical information literacy. Co-produced with [e-flux](https://www.e-flux.com/). Participants included:
  - **American Artist** (*Shaper of God*, 2021 — research machine on Octavia Butler, Black diaspora, Altadena)
  - **James Bridle** — writer/artist/technologist
  - **Kate Crawford** — Distinguished Research Professor, NYU (AI + power)
  - **Trevor Paglen** — artist/researcher (surveillance, AI imaging)
  - **Laura Kurgan** — Columbia GSAPP, Center for Spatial Research (mapping/data ethics)
  - **Anti-Eviction Mapping Project** — data-visualization collective
  - **Kameelah Janan Rasheed** — artist/learner (language, time, place)
  - **Gala Porras-Kim** — artist (archaeology, space, psychic regression)
  - Supported by Swiss Arts Council Pro Helvetia

- **Extended Views** (2025-2026, Copenhagen, with Simian) — Film programme from KADIST collection shown at artist-run space Simian. Themes: perception, ritual, materiality, sound, time.

- **Blind Thread** (2025, with Pivô São Paulo) — Streaming screening programme inspired by Donna Haraway's *Staying with the Trouble*. Artists including Bahar Noorizadeh (Iran), Bo Wang & Pan Lu (China), Mona Benyamin (Palestine). Latin American, Iranian, Chinese, Palestinian works on water ecologies, kinship, speculative narratives.

- **de montañas submarinas el fuego hace islas** (2023-2024, curated by Yina Jiménez Suriel) — Exhibition, film programme, and reader on Afro-Caribbean and Indigenous ecologies. Co-shown at MCAD Manila.

1. **Critical technology art** — The *Ways of Reading* programme brought together James Bridle, Kate Crawford, Trevor Paglen, American Artist. This is the institutional home for artists doing what Amber does as a researcher — taking AI/data seriously as a socio-political phenomenon.

5. **Commissions as research infrastructure** — KADIST treats commissions not as purchases but as research processes. This is exactly the "residency-based research (art × tech × place)" model that research-taste.md flags as an emerging interest.
