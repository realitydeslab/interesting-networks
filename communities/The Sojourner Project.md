# The Sojourner Project

Mobile Black Studies academy initiated by the [[Practicing Refusal Collective]] — an international Black feminist forum of artists and scholars. Named for Sojourner Truth. Structured to intentionally exceed the literal and figurative walls of the university by convening transnational and diasporic gatherings in which conversations, workshops, and art activations create multi-directional encounters with histories of struggle and practices of refusal.

## About
Each convening responds to the cultural, intellectual, political, and social landscapes of African and African diasporic localities through collaborative engagements between Practicing Refusal Collective members and resident artists, scholars, community and cultural workers.

Goals: dialogue on national and regional inflections of anti-Blackness (including black-on-black violence and Afrophobia), the resulting formations of black precarity and fungibility, and the possibilities for creating alternative futures.

## Convenings
- **Paris 2018** — Columbia Global Center / Columbia Institute for Ideas and Imagination at Reid Hall. Dialogues on Black Precarity, Fungibility and Futurity. Panel discussions, film screening, poetry reading, collaborative working groups. 300+ participants. Funded by Barnard Center for Research on Women, Columbia Center for the Study of Social Difference, Institute for Gender Studies, Institute for Ideas and Imagination.
- **South Africa 2020** (originally Durban + Johannesburg) — COVID pivot to online format. Community-based Black Studies curriculum designed with Nontsikelelo Mutiti. Virtual activations 2020–2021.
