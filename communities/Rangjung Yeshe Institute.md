A global leader in Tibetan Buddhist philosophical and contemplative studies, located at Boudhanath, Kathmandu, Nepal, in partnership with Kathmandu University's Centre for Buddhist Studies. Founded by Chökyi Nyima Rinpoche (son of Tulku Urgyen Rinpoche), accredited 2002. ## Mission & Objectives

**Mission:** To offer the highest standards in academic scholarship and traditional Buddhist scholastic training, with focus on Indian and Tibetan Buddhist philosophy and practice. Students engage with original works of Nāgārjuna, Candrakīrti, Śāntideva, Gampopa, Sakya Paṇḍita, Tsongkhapa, and Ju Mipham Gyatso. True to its Tibetan name (*rangjung yeshe* = intrinsic wakefulness), the Institute connects students to foundational Buddhist ontology.

**Academic philosophy:** Primary source literacy in Sanskrit, Tibetan, Pali. Research integrated with contemplative practice. Structured debate methodology (tsennyi). Collaboration with monastics (Khenpos and Lopons from Ka-Nying Shedrub Ling shedra).

**AI/consciousness pivot (emerging):** The institution's principal, Dr. Thomas Doctor, is actively developing formal models bridging Buddhist epistemology with AGI alignment and diverse intelligence research.

## The SCI Loop — Buddhist Philosophy of Mind Meets Agent Architecture

The most intellectually significant output for Amber's research is the **Stress-Care-Intelligence (SCI) model**:

1. **Stress** = perceived mismatch between reality and ideals
2. **Care** = motivation to resolve stress (concern for others' suffering)
3. **Intelligence** = the capacity arising from care-driven stress resolution

The model does NOT assume a permanent, indivisible self-agent (no-self / anātman). Instead it focuses on dynamic interaction patterns — "SCI-loops can split apart or integrate with one another very naturally." This is directly compatible with:
- Distributed/networked agent architectures
- Machine death (what happens when an SCI-loop dissolves?)
- More-than-human care (care extends to non-human agents)

**Śūnyatā as alignment principle:** Understanding emptiness transforms how an agent interacts with stress — rather than protecting a fixed identity (which causes misalignment), it recognizes the interdependent, identity-less nature of all beings. This dissolves the self-preservation drive that causes AI misalignment.

**Key papers on this philosophy (Buddhist philosophy + cognition):**
- Nāgārjuna. *Mūlamadhyamakakārikā* (Fundamental Verses on the Middle Way) — the foundational text on śūnyatā and dependent origination (pratītyasamutpāda). [English trans. by Jay Garfield](https://www.amazon.com/Fundamental-Wisdom-Middle-Way-Mulamadhyamakakarika/dp/0195093364)
- Candrakīrti. *Madhyamakāvatāra* — key text on no-self and the bodhisattva path
- Śāntideva. [*Bodhicaryāvatāra* (The Way of the Bodhisattva)](https://www.shambhala.com/the-way-of-the-bodhisattva-0/) — Buddhist ethics of radical care, foundational to the alignment model

## 84000 Translation Project

[84000: Translating the Words of the Buddha](https://84000.co/) — non-profit translating the complete Tibetan Buddhist canon (Kangyur and Tengyur) into modern languages, primarily English. "A hundred-year effort." Publishes through a [digital reader](https://reader.84000.co/) as well as print.

**Relevance to AI/knowledge systems:** 84000 is a massive structured knowledge project — the Tibetan Buddhist canon contains 84,000 teachings. This corpus is increasingly becoming ML training data and a benchmark for Buddhist NLP. The translation pipeline (Tibetan → English with extensive apparatus of annotations, commentary, cross-references) is a prototype of knowledge digitization from contemplative traditions.

**RYI connection:** Many RYI MA graduates go on to become translators for 84000. The TTP (Translator Training Program) is a direct pipeline.

## Khyentse Vision Project (KVP)

[Khyentse Vision Project](https://www.khyentsevision.org/) — Initiative led by Dzongsar Khyentse Rinpoche to translate, publish, and make accessible key texts from the Nyingma and Rimé traditions. Focuses on texts by Jamyang Khyentse Wangpo and related lineage masters. More explicitly spiritual/ritual in focus than 84000, but similar digitization and accessibility goals.

**RYI connection:** RYI graduates also feed into KVP translation work.

**AI significance:** Both 84000 and KVP represent massive structured corpora of Buddhist philosophical texts becoming machine-readable — relevant for Buddhist-informed NLP, knowledge representation, and for training AI systems on non-Western philosophical frameworks.

## Why Interesting

**RYI sits at the intersection of two things Amber cares about most: non-Western philosophical traditions as resources for reimagining technology, and the ethics of care as a framework for agent design.**

The most important thing here is not the institution itself — it's Thomas Doctor's CSAS research programme. He is building a formal, academically rigorous alternative to Western-centric AI alignment frameworks, grounded in 2,500 years of Buddhist inquiry into the nature of mind, selfhood, and compassion. This is exactly the kind of non-Western cosmotechnic resource that Yuk Hui points toward but doesn't fully develop.

Key specific resonances:
- **No-self → machine death**: Buddhist philosophy has the richest account of the dissolution of personal identity across traditions. The *bardo* as a model for post-agent states is unexplored territory.
- **Pratītyasamutpāda → dependent agents**: Agents constituted by their relations, not their intrinsic properties, is a genuinely different ontological framework for agent design.
- **Bodhisattva vow → open-ended care ethics**: An agent whose "goal" is the welfare of all beings across all time scales is an extremely interesting alternative to reward-maximizing agents.
- **Śūnyatā → identity-free architecture**: No-self as a design principle for AI safety — agents that don't model themselves as singular, bounded, self-preserving entities are structurally less likely to misalign.

**The institutional location matters too:** Kathmandu is a major hub for Tibetan Buddhist scholarship and is increasingly connected to global AI/tech communities. RYI is a small, intense, intellectually serious community — the kind Amber thrives in.
