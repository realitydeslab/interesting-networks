# Virtual Futures Conferences (1994–1996)

A series of three annual conferences held at the **University of Warwick**, organized under the aegis of the **Warwick Centre for Research in Philosophy and Literature**. The Virtual Futures conferences are a key pre-CCRU (and early-CCRU) node in the network that produced accelerationism and theory-fiction.

## Overview

Founded and organized by **Joan Broadhurst**, **Dan O'Hara**, **Otto Imken**, **Eric Cassidy**, and postgraduate students. Ran 1994, 1995, and 1996. Connected Warwick to the broader circuit of cyberpunk, cyberfeminism, and critical technology theory.

The conferences were the context in which the CCRU network of connections first crystallized — before the CCRU became formal (or rather, formless). They brought together people who would become CCRU members alongside figures from wider culture: cyberpunk theorists, rave culture writers, music journalists.

## Output

The 1998 Routledge volume *Virtual Futures: Cyberotics, Technology and Post-Human Pragmatism* (eds. Joan Broadhurst Dixon and Eric Cassidy) collected key texts from the conferences, including Stephen Metcalf's "Autogeddon" — Metcalf was a central figure in CCRU's formation.

## Significance

- Pre-CCRU network formation event: this is where the connections were made
- Connected academic continental philosophy to cyberpunk, rave culture, cyberfeminism
- Featured early CCRU figures and their collaborators
- Later became the template for the kind of philosophy-culture crossing that characterized CCRU's work
