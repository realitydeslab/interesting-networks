# AI Accountability Lab (AIAL)

A justice-first AI research lab embedded in Trinity College Dublin, founded by Dr. [[Abeba Birhane]] in November 2024. Distinct from most "AI ethics" groups by its structural critique — it starts from the premise that AI technologies are inherently entangled with capitalism and oppressive power structures, not that they're neutral tools that can be made ethical with better design. The lab audits AI systems, produces policy-ready evidence, and actively allies with civil society and rights groups to assert accountability. One of the few serious European critical-AI labs doing empirical work with genuine political edge.

## Mission & Objectives

The AIAL is dedicated to ensuring the wider AI ecology — from research and product development to regulation — centres public interest, particularly the most marginalised and disenfranchised. Its core commitments:

- **Challenge and dismantle harmful technologies** — through rigorous audits and empirical evidence
- **Inform evidence-driven policies** — active policy translation of research findings
- **Hold responsible bodies accountable** — working with civil society, rights groups, and investigative journalism
- **Pave the way for just and equitable AI** — grounded in structural critique, not cosmetic ethics

Importantly, the AIAL explicitly frames current AI stagnation as rooted in "extractive technological ecologies and oppressive capitalist structures" — a level of structural honesty rare in academic AI ethics. Research scope runs from large-scale field-wide audits (the AI field itself, regulatory processes) down to specific model/dataset evaluations.

## Key Contributions

The AIAL positions itself as one of the few European labs that:
1. Does **empirical audits** (not just normative ethics frameworks)
2. Grounds audits in **theories of justice** (not just technical fairness metrics)
3. Explicitly names **capitalism and power structures** as root causes
4. Maintains **active civil society alliances** alongside academic output
5. Engages **regulatory processes** directly (AI Act, UN Advisory Body)

The lab fills a gap: between activist civil society groups (like Access Now, ICCL) and academic fairness/ML research (FAccT community). It tries to be rigorous enough for Nature and politically sharp enough to matter in policy debates.

## Why Interesting

The AIAL is one of the best examples of a **justice-first empirical AI lab** in Europe. It sits at the exact intersection of Amber's critical computing and machine behavior threads: it asks "what does AI *do* to the world?" not "how do we make AI ethics checklists?" It refuses the technosolutionist framing. Its structural critique (capitalism, power, extractive tech ecologies) is the same register Amber is working in with Sovereign Agents and Dissociative Agents.

The lab is also a model for how to build a small, sharply positioned research group that punches above its weight: one PI, a handful of people, strong funder alignment, active civil society partnerships, Nature-level publication ambition, and direct policy engagement. Compare to DAIR and AI Now — the same structural approach, but in Dublin, creating a European node in this accountability network.

Birhane's insistence that "critique is service" (parallel to peer review strengthening science) is a philosophically sophisticated position that gives the lab a clear identity distinct from both activist advocacy and academic fence-sitting.
