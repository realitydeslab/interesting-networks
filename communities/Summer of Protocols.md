# Summer of Protocols (SoP)

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Deep research completed. Full profile written from comprehensive website scrape, alumni directory, and key participant research.
- **2025 (SoP25):** Third season underway. Focus on education — 11 university professors developing curricula, modules, and mini courses. Protocol School runs September 2025 online. Theme: "Accelerating Order."
- **2025:** Bridge Atlas salon series launched — podcast + Devconnect Buenos Aires event (November 22, 2025), hosted by Christine Kim, pairing Ethereum Foundation members with SoP alumni.
- **2025:** Special Interest Groups launched: Formal Protocol Theory (led by Venkatesh Rao), Memory Research Group (led by [[Kei Kreutler]]), Protocols for Business, Protocol Fiction.
- **2024 (SoP24):** Six two-person teams given case study grants. In-person events in Singapore, Healdsburg CA, Chiang Mai Thailand. PILL micro-grants for artists/designers. Protocol Symposium debut.
- **2023 (SoP23):** Inaugural season. 12 core researchers + 18 affiliate researchers. In-person retreat in Seattle. Outputs: Protocol Reader (358 pages) and ProtocolKit (3-ring binder, 400+ pages, 512 copies distributed worldwide).

A seasonal research program studying protocols — the rules, standards, and social agreements that structure human and technical coordination. **Amber (Botao Hu) was a researcher in SoP24.** The organizing hunch: that there are universal principles underlying protocols across fields as disparate as internet architecture, coal mining, diplomacy, and emergency medicine. ~$2.5 million invested to date. 70+ alumni worldwide. 100+ items of research output.

## Mission & Objectives

**Mission:** To broaden and accelerate the study of protocols as an emerging cross-disciplinary field — "Protocol Studies." The program treats protocol not merely as a technical term (TCP/IP, HTTP) but as a fundamental organizing concept for human coordination across all domains.

**Founding impulse (late 2022):** The word "protocol" was suddenly everywhere — triggered by Twitter's ownership chaos and the mass migration toward "protocol" alternatives like ActivityPub/Mastodon. The Ethereum Foundation, which maintains a foundational blockchain protocol, commissioned the program to understand protocols more deeply: their history, social implications, governance, and universal principles.

**Core philosophy:** Open, global participation. Decentralized operations. Everything free and open-access. CC+ licensed outputs. The "Fellowship of the Kit" framing — 512 Protocol Kits mailed worldwide to seed a movement, aspiring to do for protocols what Arduinos did for making or the Whole Earth Catalog did for tech culture.

**Three season progression:**
1. **SoP23:** Theoretical foundations — what is a protocol? 12 core + 18 affiliate researchers. 18-week program.
2. **SoP24:** Application and case studies — six 2-person teams studying real-world protocols. PILL micro-grants for artists. Protocol Symposium.
3. **SoP25:** Education — curriculum development, scene-building, technical foundations. Protocol School. Teaching fellows. Formal Protocol Theory track.
