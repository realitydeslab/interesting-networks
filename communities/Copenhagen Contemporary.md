# Copenhagen Contemporary

International contemporary arts centre in Copenhagen — one of the few European institutions genuinely committed to ambitious, large-scale installation art at the intersection of art, science, and technology. Located in a converted industrial hall on **Refshaleøen**, a former shipyard island that is also home to Noma. Known for spectacular scale and technically demanding exhibitions. Board includes a noma CEO and a Space Invaders digital design pioneer — signals cultural ambition beyond typical art institutions.

## Mission & Objectives
Copenhagen Contemporary (CC) is Copenhagen's international art centre showing installation art created by established artists and emerging talents. The Foundation was established **December 2, 2014**. CC creates collaborative partnerships across cultural genres, locally and internationally, with particular emphasis on:
- Large-scale installation art
- Art-science collaborations
- Technology's relationship to humanity and culture
- Non-Western perspectives (pan-Asian, Sinofuturist threads)
- Ambitious productions that push technical and physical limits

## Events & Programmes

### Collide Copenhagen (Arts at CERN partnership, 2023–2025)
**URL:** [copenhagencontemporary.org/en/event/collide-copenhagen-2023/](https://copenhagencontemporary.org/en/event/collide-copenhagen-2023/)

Annual residency award for artists with interest in science and technology:
- 1 month residency at CERN (Geneva) + 1 month at Copenhagen Contemporary
- Themes: artificial intelligence, vast datasets modelling, quantum technologies, philosophical/ethical interpretations of science
- Open to artists from any country, 18+, with 5+ years professional experience
- Artists selected for 2023–2025 exhibited together in *Soft Robots* (2025–2026)
- The 2023–2025 cycle has concluded; future cycles TBD

**Eligibility criteria (from open call):** artists interested in cultural significance of scientific research; art-science dialogue; implications of advanced technologies (AI, quantum, big data); collaborative work across disciplinary boundaries; new models of social enquiry.
