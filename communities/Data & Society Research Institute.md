# Data & Society Research Institute

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-03-12:** Deep research completed. Expanded from stub to full profile. Maintained at ⭐⭐ — excellent critical AI/tech institute but NYC-based with limited direct engagement paths for Amber. Key intellectual community for field awareness but not an immediate action node.

An independent nonprofit research institute in New York City focused on the social, cultural, and ethical implications of data-centric and automated technologies. Founded in **2014** by **[[danah boyd]]** (Microsoft Research Principal Researcher and visiting professor at Georgetown University). Data & Society produces original empirical research, expert commentary, policy briefs, and public events at the intersection of AI, labor, health, human rights, platforms, media, climate, and surveillance.

## Mission & Objectives

**Mission:** Address the most urgent challenges raised by data and automation in society. Data & Society studies how data-driven technologies reshape labor, health, media, and civil liberties — with a focus on **who benefits, who's harmed, and what accountability looks like**.

**Approach:**
- Empirical research grounded in qualitative and quantitative methods
- Policy engagement — briefs, testimony, op-eds
- Public scholarship — blogs (Points), events, media commentary
- Community building — fellows, researchers, affiliated scholars

**Key Research Areas (from library):**
1. **AI** — governance, accountability, fairness, safety
2. **Labor** — automation's impact on workers, gig economy, AI literacy
3. **Health** — AI in healthcare, mental health tech, algorithmic diagnosis
4. **Human Rights** — surveillance, policing, discrimination
5. **Platforms** — social media, content moderation, digital citizenship
6. **Media** — disinformation, manipulation, media literacy
7. **Climate** — data centres, environmental impact of AI
8. **Policy** — regulation, governance frameworks
9. **Accountability** — auditing, transparency, oversight
10. **Surveillance & Privacy** — facial recognition, tracking, data collection
11. **Disinformation & Manipulation** — information warfare, deepfakes
12. **Automation** — AI-driven decision-making, algorithmic systems
