# UC Santa Cruz — History of Consciousness

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Stub created during Donna Haraway deep research.

The most radically interdisciplinary humanities PhD program in the United States. Since 1965, "HistCon" operates at the intersection of humanities, social sciences, arts, and sciences — "problem-driven research" rather than discipline-driven. Home to an extraordinary array of canonical thinkers. Donna Haraway's base for 40 years.

## Mission & Objectives
"History of Consciousness fosters problem-driven research vital to new forms of knowledge and intelligibility and to meet the challenges of imagining new modes of scholarship for the arts, social sciences, and humanities." Studies at the intersection of race, sexuality, gender; global capitalism and cultural processes; psychoanalytic and semiotic theories; science and technology studies; theories of religion; social movements; literary studies and poetics.

## Key People (Faculty, past and present)

### Canonical/Historical
- **[[Donna Haraway]]** ⭐⭐⭐ — Emeritus. First tenured feminist theory professor in the US. Cyborg theory, multispecies thinking. (1980–retirement)
- **Angela Davis** ⭐⭐⭐ — Political philosopher, prison abolition, civil rights. Transformative presence.
- **Fredric Jameson** — Literary/cultural theorist. *Postmodernism, or The Cultural Logic of Late Capitalism*.
- **Hayden White** — Historian of narrative. *Metahistory*. First department chair.
- **James Clifford** — Anthropology of culture. *The Predicament of Culture*.
- **Teresa de Lauretis** — Feminist film theory, queer theory. (d. 2026)
- **Norman O. Brown** — Life Against Death, Love's Body. Joined 1968.
- **Gayatri Chakravorty Spivak** — Postcolonial theory. Has taught in the program.
- **Herbert Marcuse** — Frankfurt School. Has taught in the program.
- **Huey P. Newton** — Co-founder Black Panther Party. HistCon PhD 1980.

### Current Faculty (as of late 2023)
- **Karen Barad** ⭐⭐⭐ — Quantum feminist physics. *Meeting the Universe Halfway*. "Agential realism." Extends Haraway's feminist STS into physics.
- **Banu Bargu** — Political theory, biopolitics.
- **Chris Connery** — Cultural studies, global humanities.
- **Robert Meister** — Political theory.
- **Eric Porter** — Music, cultural history.
- **Massimiliano Tomba** — Political philosophy, Marx.
- **Robert Nichols** — Political theory, indigenous politics.
- **Dimitris Papadopoulos** — Multispecies politics, science studies.
- **Maria Puig de la Bellacasa** — Feminist STS, care ethics, soils. *Matters of Care* (2017). [See also [[María Puig de la Bellacasa]] note]

## Why Interesting
HistCon is **the** institutional proof that the most radical interdisciplinary work happens when you organize around *problems* not disciplines. It's Amber's conceptual ancestors in a single department: Haraway, Davis, Barad, Puig de la Bellacasa. The alumni include a Black Panther co-founder and a design fiction pioneer (Bleecker). This is what "edge of chaos" institutional design looks like.
