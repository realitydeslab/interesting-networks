# Glass Bead Journal

Online theory journal and research platform at the intersection of art, science, and philosophy. Based in Paris and London. Produces long-form academic essays with a strong interest in speculative philosophy, feminist theory, AI/computation as philosophical question, and the politics of abstraction. Notable for commissioning unusual hybrid work between art/design and rigorous philosophy. Published the original XF Manifesto (Xenofeminism: A Politics for Alienation by Laboria Cuboniks). Independent, non-profit, operating since 2014.

## Mission & Objectives

Glass Bead is a **research platform** and a **journal** concerned with transfers of knowledge across art, science, and philosophy, as well as with their practical and political dimensions. Its core proposition: art can function as a mode of rational thought — not merely affective or representational, but genuinely contributing to how we think and act on the world. The name and concept are drawn from Hermann Hesse's *The Glass Bead Game* (1943), which imagines a society entirely dedicated to synthesizing all forms of knowledge through a game — Glass Bead appropriates this image as both aspiration and critique.

Each journal issue explores a **"site"** — defined broadly as a conceptual territory, geopolitical locality, fictional entity, musical expression, or material formation — that functions as a vehicle for traversing multiple disciplinary regions and scales of abstraction.

Research activities include:
- Private and public **events** (workshops, conferences)
- **Audio Research Program** (recordings of talks and special interviews)
- **Research Platform** where participants share current research

**Founded:** 2012 (initiated), 2014 (officially launched via events in New York and Paris)
**Location:** Paris and London

## Journal Issues ("Sites")

### [Site 0. Castalia: the Game of Ends and Means](https://www.glass-bead.org/issue/site-0/) (2016)
The inaugural issue uses Hesse's fictional Castalia as a starting point for rethinking art as rational thought. Argues for morphing the Castalian model — its claim to universal knowledge — to account for the catastrophic predicament of contemporary art. Topics: transdisciplinarity, reason, science-art nexus.

### [Site 1. Logic Gate: the Politics of the Artifactual Mind](https://www.glass-bead.org/journal/site-2-dark-room-somatic-reason-and-synthetic-eros/) (2017)
Launched at ZKM – Center for Art and Media, Karlsruhe (November 2017). Focuses on the fabric of reason itself — how it is being altered by AI. Central thesis: mind has always been artifactual; the externalization of cognition in machines is thus not a rupture but an acceleration of this condition. Politics of AI as the central question.

**Launch event speakers (ZKM, Nov 2017):**
- [Nina Power](https://www.glass-bead.org/contributor/nina-power/) — philosopher, *Revisiting the Human*
- [Matteo Pasquinelli](https://www.glass-bead.org/contributor/matteo-pasquinelli/) — *Machines That Morph Logic* (neural nets as statistical induction, algorithmic bias)
- [T'ai Smith](https://www.glass-bead.org/contributor/tai-smith/) — art historian, *AI's Fashion Mirror Stage*

### [Site 2. Dark Room: Somatic Reason and Synthetic Eros](https://www.glass-bead.org/journal/site-2-dark-room-somatic-reason-and-synthetic-eros/) (2019)
Produced in the framework of **Okayama Art Summit 2019** ("IF THE SNAKE," curated by Pierre Huyghe). Pivots from Sites 0–1's focus on reason to embodied thought — the body, sexuality, and their political dimensions. Explores sex robotics, algorithms and intimacy, cruising architectures, feminist cinema (starting from Kazuo Hara's *Extreme Private Eros: Love Song 1974*). Contends that contemporary upheavals in love/sex/reproduction are crucial transformative vectors for transdisciplinary reason. Published serially across the duration of the triennial.

**Key contributors:**
- [Patricia Reed](https://www.glass-bead.org/article/freedom-and-fiction/) — *Freedom and Fiction* (on episteme, freedom, fictional construction of worlds)
- [Anna Longo](https://www.glass-bead.org/contributor/anna-longo/) — *Probability, Risk and Uncertainty*
- Futoshi Hoshino — *On the Sublime, or the Terror of Philosophy*
- Damjan Jovanovic & Casey Rehm — *Translation, Correlation, Simulation* (AI + 3D object correlation platform)

## Why Interesting

Glass Bead sits at a **rare intersection** of continental philosophy rigor + art practice + political theory + computation, without sliding into either pure activism or pure techno-utopianism. The "site" concept is a genuinely productive intellectual device — not just a theme but an epistemic methodology. The XF Manifesto's publication here is the strongest signal of the journal's positioning: it's the journal that gave xenofeminism its canonical text.

In Amber's taste terms: **weird + rigorous** ✓, **creative practice asking philosophical questions** ✓, **cross-disciplinary without being diffuse** ✓. The journal is slow (3 issues in 10 years) but each is substantive — the opposite of the publish-or-perish machine. The Okayama Art Summit tie-in (Pierre Huyghe curation) shows the journal moves in curatorial art world circuits as well as theory circuits — bridging two different prestige economies.

The editorial team's backgrounds (Goldsmiths Cultural Studies, Goldsmiths Art History, EHESS Political Philosophy, ECAL, ENS) reflect the specific Paris–London critical theory corridor that includes Amber's broader network.
