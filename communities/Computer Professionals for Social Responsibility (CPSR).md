# Computer Professionals for Social Responsibility (CPSR)

One of the earliest organizations to link professional technical computing work to political and social responsibility. Founded in 1981 in the Bay Area, largely by researchers from Stanford and Xerox PARC. Co-founded by [[Lucy Suchman]] who served on the Board of Directors from 1982–1990. CPSR was a precursor to many later critical computing organizations (ACM Ethics, FAccT, AI Now, etc.).

## Why Interesting
Historical node: CPSR was the original critical computing organization, founded by the same generation of researchers (PARC, Stanford) who were inventing personal computing. Its existence shows that critical voices were present at computing's origin, not just recently. [[Lucy Suchman]]'s co-founding role at CPSR (alongside her 22 years at PARC) is evidence that critical computing and technical practice were always intertwined in her work. For Amber: useful historical context for how the critical computing tradition began.
