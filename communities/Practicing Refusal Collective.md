# Practicing Refusal Collective

International Black feminist forum dedicated to initiating new dialogues on Blackness, anti-Black violence, and Black futurity in the 21st century. Formed in 2015 by **Tina Campt** (Columbia/Barnard) and **Saidiya Hartman** (Columbia). 16 members from multiple disciplines of the humanities — 2 Columbia faculty, 4 Barnard faculty, and 10 additional members from 9 universities in the US and Canada.

## About
The Collective's point of departure is a set of overlapping interests in theorizing Black agency in the face of contemporary circumstances of imperilled Blackness. Their framework: **"practice of refusal"** — refusing the current status quo as liveable, refusing to accept Black precarity as inevitable, refusing to embrace the terms of diminished subjecthood. Developed the concept of **"black fungibility"** (the state in which Black bodies are treated as disposable and expendable).

Launched the **[[The Sojourner Project]]** in 2018 — a mobile Black Studies academy hosting transnational gatherings in Africa and its diaspora.
