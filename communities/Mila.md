# Mila — Quebec AI Institute

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-03-07:** Deep research completed. Full profile with GFlowNets, virtual cells, AI safety pivot, and connection mapping to Amber's work.

The world's largest academic AI research institute (~1000 researchers, 100+ faculty), founded in Montreal in 1993 by Yoshua Bengio. Birthplace of deep learning as a discipline. Now pivoting hard into AI safety, AI for science, and virtual cell biology. If SFI is the temple of complexity, Mila is the temple of deep learning — and increasingly, the place where deep learning meets life science and existential risk.

## Mission & Objectives

**Core mission:** Advance AI research for the benefit of humanity, with emphasis on deep learning, reinforcement learning, generative models, and increasingly: AI safety and AI for science (biology, climate, healthcare).

**Key shift (2023–present):** Scientific Director Yoshua Bengio has publicly pivoted from capability research to AI safety and existential risk. Mila's research portfolio now includes active AI alignment work, not just applications.

**Part of Pan-Canadian AI Strategy** alongside [Amii](https://www.amii.ca/) (Alberta) and [Vector Institute](https://vectorinstitute.ai/) (Toronto). Funded by federal and Quebec provincial government, CIFAR, and industry partners.

## History

- **1993:** Founded by Yoshua Bengio as Laboratoire d'informatique des systèmes adaptatifs (LISA) at Université de Montréal
- **2001:** Parallel founding of Reasoning and Learning Lab (RL-Lab) at McGill University
- **2017:** Restructured as partnership of UdeM, McGill, École Polytechnique de Montréal, HEC Montréal. Renamed "Mila"
- **2018:** Turing Award won by Bengio, LeCun, Hinton — "the Godfathers of Deep Learning"
- **2019:** New $6.5M building in Little Italy, Montreal (1000 sq ft facility)
- **2023:** Bengio's public AI safety pivot; co-authored [Statement on AI Risk](https://www.safe.ai/statement-on-ai-risk) alongside major AI safety figures

## Key Research Areas

### 1. Deep Learning & Generative Models
Foundational. GANs (Goodfellow), VAEs, diffusion models, flow matching — Mila was first or early on most of these.

### 2. Reinforcement Learning
Strong RL group (Pineau, Precup, Bellemare). From theory (temporal difference) to applications (video games, robotics, healthcare).

### 3. GFlowNets — Open-Ended Scientific Discovery
- Introduced by Bengio et al. 2021 ([arXiv:2106.04399](https://arxiv.org/abs/2106.04399))
- **Key insight:** Train generative models to sample proportional to reward, not just maximize reward. Produces diversity of high-reward candidates — crucial for drug discovery, scientific hypothesis generation.
- Connects to ALife open-endedness: GFlowNets are essentially open-ended search in combinatorial spaces
- Full theoretical treatment: [GFlowNet Foundations](https://arxiv.org/abs/2111.09266) (arXiv:2111.09266)
- Applied to: drug discovery, protein design, causal discovery, NLP

### 4. AI for Biology & Virtual Cells
- **Jian Tang's DeepGraphLearning Lab** is leading Mila's push into computational biology
- Drug discovery platform: [TorchDrug](https://torchdrug.ai/)
- Virtual cell modeling: PerturbDiff (Feb 2026) — see [[Virtual Cells]] topic note
- Single-cell genomics, protein design, multiomics

### 5. AI Safety
- Bengio signed [Center for AI Safety's Statement on AI Risk](https://www.safe.ai/statement-on-ai-risk) (May 2023)
- Testified to Canadian Parliament and UN on AI risk
- Designing Scientist AI: non-agentic, Bayesian, honest chains-of-thought as latent variables
- Mila has active AI alignment research programme
