## Mission & Scope

ISEA International is the world's premier nomadic media art symposium, combining academic conference, artist talks, and large-scale exhibitions in a single annual event. Founded in **1990** in the Netherlands (originally as *Inter-Society for the Electronic Arts*), renamed ISEA International around 2009. HQ hosted by the [University for the Creative Arts](https://www.uca.ac.uk/), Farnham, UK.

> "Fostering interdisciplinary academic discourse and exchange among culturally diverse organisations and individuals working with art, science and technology."

The symposium travels to a different country every year, ensuring global reach and shifting cultural lens. It occupies a **unique position** in the media art ecosystem: more academically rigorous than [[Ars Electronica]], more artistically experimental than SIGGRAPH, more media-art-focused than CHI/DIS. Proceedings are published and peer-reviewed. Artists are juried. The combination is unusual and productive.

**Annual attendance:** ~800-1,400 international participants.

## Submission Process
ISEA uses **annual competitive open calls** with parallel tracks. Typical timeline: CFP opens ~June/July, deadlines ~October-November of prior year, symposium ~May-June.

### Academic Track
- **Full papers** (5-8 pages, ISEA template) — double-blind peer review by ≥2 IPC members. Accepted → published in proceedings + oral presentation.
- **Short papers** (2-4 pages) — double-blind. Accepted → proceedings + presentation.
- **Panels** (2-4 page proposal, 3-5 participants) — double-blind. Accepted → 90-min moderated session.
- **Posters/Demos** (≤2 page abstract) — published in proceedings, poster display.
- **Institutional Presentations** (≤2 pages) — curatorial/organizational/NGO track, 10-min slot.

### Artistic Track
- **Performance/Exhibition/Screening** — submit CV/portfolio + 2-4 page proposal (artist bio, thematic statement, artwork proposal, images, technical requirements, video doc). Juried by art juries; artistic directors make final decisions.
- **Workshops** — educational/participatory formats.
- **Artist Talks** — can be submitted directly, or failed art proposals may be redirected here.

**Key:** Rejected art proposals may be accepted as artist talks. This creates a "second chance" mechanism. Rejected papers may convert to posters.

**EasyChair** used for online submissions.

## Key People in ISEA History

**[Roger Malina](https://utdallas.edu/arttech/)** — ISEA's intellectual godfather. Astrophysicist, editor of [*Leonardo*](https://www.leonardo.info/) journal (MIT Press), co-founder of ISEA in early 1990s. Lives at the intersection of art and science. Professor at UT Dallas. Arguably the most important person in the academic media art world.

**[Wim van der Plas](https://nl.linkedin.com/in/wim-van-der-plas)** — Long-time archive curator, worked on ISEA archives from 2013. Netherlands-based. Has maintained institutional memory of the organization.

**[Bonnie Mitchell](https://bonniemitchell.net)** — ISEA Archives lead since ~2013. Bowling Green State University. ISEA Archives Chair. Critical infrastructure.

**[Soh Yeong Roh](https://nabi.or.kr)** — General Chair ISEA 2025. Director of [[Art Center Nabi]], Korea's first media art institution. Key figure in Asian media art scene.

**[Yunchul Kim](https://yunchulkim.net)** — Korean artist, ISEA 2025 keynote. Known for "mattereality," crossmorphing, transmattering. Works with magnetic fluids, particle physics, complex materials as artistic media. ⭐⭐⭐ candidate.

**[Nam June Paik](https://njpart.kr/)** (historical) — Korean-American artist, inventor of video art. Inspired the "ISEA in Seoul" framing; Art Center Nabi was founded partly in his spirit. Died 2006.

## Why Interesting
ISEA is the singular venue at the exact intersection of Amber's dual identity: **academic researcher + media artist**. The nomadic format creates a global community that doesn't cluster in one city or country — especially valuable for someone who moves between Oxford, London, Shanghai, and elsewhere.
