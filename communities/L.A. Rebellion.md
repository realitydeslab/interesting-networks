# L.A. Rebellion

The L.A. Rebellion (sometimes "Los Angeles School of Black Filmmakers") refers to the generation of African and African-American filmmakers who studied at the UCLA Film School from the late 1960s through the late 1990s, creating an alternative Black cinema against Hollywood conventions. Influenced by Italian neorealism, Latin American Third Cinema, African cinema, and the French New Wave — and by the post-Watts Riots political moment.

## Key Figures
- [[Charles Burnett]] — *Killer of Sheep* (1978)
- [[Haile Gerima]] — *Bush Mama* (1979), *Sankofa* (1993)
- [[Julie Dash]] ⭐⭐⭐ — *Illusions* (1982), *Daughters of the Dust* (1991)
- Billy Woodberry — *Bless Their Little Hearts* (1984)
- Zeinabu irene Davis — director of documentary *Spirits of Rebellion* about the movement
- Alile Sharon Larkin
- Larry Clark (filmmaker, b. 1948 — not the photographer)

## Scholars/Mentors
- Teshome Gabriel — film scholar, UCLA, mentor
- Clyde Taylor — coined the term "L.A. Rebellion"
- Elyseo J. Taylor — only Black instructor at UCLA film school, early 1970s
- Allyson Nadia Field — *L.A. Rebellion: Creating a New Black Cinema* (book, UC Press, 2015)

## Archive & Legacy
- **UCLA Film & Television Archive** holds and preserves the films; major retrospective 2011 ("L.A. Rebellion: Creating a New Black Cinema"), funded by Getty Foundation / Pacific Standard Time.
- 7+ films selected for National Film Registry: *Killer of Sheep* (1990), *Daughters of the Dust* (2004), *Bless Their Little Hearts* (2013), *To Sleep with Anger* (2017), *Illusions* (2020), *Bush Mama* (2022), *Compensation* (2024)
- Documentary: *Spirits of Rebellion: Black Cinema at UCLA* (dir. Zeinabu irene Davis)

## Relevance
Foundational movement for understanding the aesthetics of [[Arthur Jafa]], [[Julie Dash]], and independent Black cinema. The movement represents a systematic attempt to build a non-Hollywood film grammar rooted in African diasporic experience — relevant to Amber's interest in how cultural communities create alternative epistemologies and institutional infrastructures.
