# lumbung (documenta fifteen ecosystem)

The organizational and curatorial model of [documenta fifteen](https://documenta-fifteen.de/) (2022, Kassel), curated by Indonesian collective [[ruangrupa]]. *Lumbung* is an Indonesian word for a communal rice barn — a shared resource managed collectively. ruangrupa applied this as the conceptual framework for organizing documenta fifteen: participating artists were not solo exhibitors but *lumbung members*, sharing resources, time, and visibility within a commons.

Key principles:
- **Radical collectivity** — no solo stars; collectives over individuals
- **Solidarity economy** — fair distribution of attention, resources, platform
- **Non-Western organizational epistemology** — traditional Indonesian resource-sharing as art-world governance model
- **Process over product** — 100-day duration as lived community, not installation showcase

Lumbung members included [[DAVRA Collective]] (Central Asia), [[Karrabing Film Collective]] (Australia), and ~60 other collectives globally — the most geographically diverse documenta in history. Also the most politically contested (the Antisemitism controversy significantly overshadowed the programmatic achievements).

## Why Interesting

Lumbung is the most significant attempt to apply non-Western organizational epistemology to a major Western art institution. Whether it "worked" is debated, but the framework itself is generative: what does collective resource governance look like in a cultural context? How does solidarity-based distribution differ from market-based? These questions map directly onto Amber's interest in agentive commoning and decentralized AI governance.
