# TNEG

Film production collective / creative triad. Founded by [[Arthur Jafa]], [Malik Hassan Sayeed](https://www.instagram.com/mhsayeed/) (cinematographer), and Elissa Blount Moorhead (curator/producer). The name comes from "The Negative" — the space of inversion, of what hasn't yet been printed into the positive image. Based in Los Angeles.

Their defining mantra: **"How do you make Black cinema with the power, beauty, and alienation of Black music?"**

## Members
- **[[Arthur Jafa]]** — Director, visual artist. The conceptual engine.
- **Malik Hassan Sayeed** — Cinematographer. Met Jafa on the set of *Malcolm X* (1992). The first filmmaker who "truly heard" Jafa. Together they developed Black Visual Intonation as a practical methodology. Also a sought-after commercial DP (music videos, campaigns).
- **Elissa Blount Moorhead** — Curator, producer. The contextualizing intelligence. Ensures the work has critical framing and institutional positioning.

## Key Works Emerging from TNEG
- *APEX* (2013–14) — Sayeed and Kahlil Joseph took Jafa's image archive and placed it on a timeline; this became the work. The TNEG environment was the incubator.
- *Love Is the Message, the Message Is Death* (2016) — the breakthrough work, developed within the TNEG context
- *akingdoncomethas* (2018) — 2-hour film
- Multiple music videos and film projects

## Why Interesting
TNEG is a model for a particular kind of creative structure: not a solo artist with assistants, not a large studio, but a tight triad with distinct complementary functions (maker, technician, contextualizer). The triad provides a critical reader before the work meets the public while maintaining intimacy and shared vision. This structure is rare — most production collectives either fragment or blur roles. TNEG has maintained distinct roles for 30+ years.
