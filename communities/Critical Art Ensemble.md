# Critical Art Ensemble (CAE)

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Stub created during SymbioticA deep research.

US-based tactical media and bioart collective founded in the 1980s. Members include Steve Kurtz, Beverly Schlee, Steve Barnes, Dorian Burr, Hana Iverson. Works at the intersection of art, critical theory, and political activism. Pioneered "tactical media" — using media tools against the media industry's interests.

**Bio-art work:** Genetically modified organisms, biotech critique, "free range grain" actions against GMO agriculture, wetware performance. Key work: *Molecular Invasion* (critiquing corporate biotech/Monsanto). *Marching Plague* (biological warfare critique).

**Notable incident:** In 2004, **Steve Kurtz** called 911 when his wife died of heart failure. Paramedics found biotech equipment in his home (part of his art practice). FBI investigated under bioterrorism statutes. Federal case lasted 4 years before charges were finally dropped. Case became major cause célèbre in art-science community.

Former [[SymbioticA]] resident. Connected to the tactical media, critical biotech, and DIY bio communities internationally.

## Why Interesting
Defining example of critical practice at the intersection of art, biology, and political action. Their FBI case is a landmark in the history of bio-art and free expression. Connected to the SymbioticA alumni network and the broader tactical media scene (V2_, transmediale, etc.).
