# Mahindra Humanities Center

Harvard's flagship interdisciplinary humanities centre. Barker Center, 12 Quincy Street, Cambridge MA 02138. Tel: 617-495-0738. Named in 2011 for [Anand Mahindra](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anand_Mahindra) (Harvard College '77, Visual and Environmental Studies concentrator), in honor of his mother Indira. Founded in 1984 as the Center for Literary Studies → Humanities Center (1996) → Mahindra Humanities Center (2011). Directed by **[[Homi K. Bhabha]]** (Anne F. Rothenberg Professor of the Humanities). One of the most important nodes of postcolonial, critical, and interdisciplinary humanities thought in North America.

## Mission & Objectives
Cross-disciplinary humanities research at Harvard. Organises major lecture series, fellowships, seminars, and conferences. Core commitment: bringing non-Western, postcolonial, and humanistic perspectives into elite American academia. Under Bhabha's directorship (since 2001), consistently centered decolonial thought, hybridity, and the politics of representation.

The Center explicitly values:
- Humanistic approaches to climate/environment (current major focus 2025-26)
- Arts and humanities as sites of political imagination (Abolition series)
- Interdisciplinary doctoral and postdoctoral training (Dissertation Fellowships, Cornell School of Theory)
- Global arts and the "State of the Arts" (Hauser Forum)
- Human values and ethics (Tanner Lectures)
- Psychedelics, consciousness, and culture (new 2025-26 series)

Book project: *Arctic Forms: Literature, Art, Politics* — the Arctic in the Anthropocene, cartography, the sublime, Cold War rhetoric. Published on post-Soviet environmentalism, trauma/war/digital art. From Estonia.

- **Lama Elsharif** — Historian of early modern and modern Middle East and North Africa. Ottoman North Africa, Mediterranean history, intersections of environmental crises and political economy.

- **Cana McGhee** — PhD candidate, Historical Musicology, Harvard. Environmental studies + history of science applied to music. Questions of listenership and musicality.

- **Julia Mead** — Environmental historian of modern Eastern Europe. Energy, gender, and labor. PhD University of Chicago 2025. Dissertation: *Lights Out: Mining Masculinity and Energy in Late Soviet Russia*.

## Key Contributions
- The Norton Lectures (since 1925, 2009-present under Mahindra): the most prestigious arts/humanities lecture series in North America. Format: 6 public lectures by an extraordinary practitioner. Past: John Cage, Toni Morrison, Laurie Anderson, Agnès Varda, Wim Wenders.
- Tanner Lectures on Human Values — Harvard node of a 9-university consortium on human values/ethics.
- Postdoctoral Fellowship pipeline → training postcolonial + environmental humanities scholars.
- Interdisciplinary Dissertation Fellowships → supporting Harvard PhD students at dissertation stage.
- Cornell School of Theory → intensive theory training for graduate students.
- Psychedelics in Society & Culture (new) → humanities/social science framing of psychedelic research.
