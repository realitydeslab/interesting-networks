# Mahindra Humanities Center

Harvard's flagship interdisciplinary humanities centre. Based at Barker Center, 12 Quincy Street, Cambridge MA. Named for Indian industrialist Anand Mahindra's gift to Harvard. Directed by [[Homi K. Bhabha]] (Anne F. Rothenberg Professor).

## Mission
Cross-disciplinary humanities research at Harvard. Organises major lecture series, fellowships, seminars, and conferences across the humanities.

## Key People
- **[[Homi K. Bhabha]]** — Director. Anne F. Rothenberg Professor of the Humanities, Harvard.

## Key Programmes
- **[Norton Lectures](https://mahindrahumanities.harvard.edu/norton-lectures)** — Harvard's preeminent arts/humanities lecture series (endowed 1925). Past lecturers: T.S. Eliot, Borges, Bernstein, Milosz, John Cage, Nadine Gordimer, Orhan Pamuk, William Kentridge, Herbie Hancock, Toni Morrison, Frederick Wiseman, Wim Wenders, Agnès Varda, Laurie Anderson, Viet Thanh Nguyen.
- **[Tanner Lectures](https://mahindrahumanities.harvard.edu/tanner-lectures)** — Annual lectures on human values (nine universities)
- **Abolition series** — humanities approach to abolitionist movements
- **Environment Forum** — arts/humanities reframing human/nature relations
- **Hauser Forum** (Rita E. Hauser) — "The State of the Arts" globally
- **Urban Conversations** — humanistic + spatial investigations
- **Writers Speak** — contemporary writers in conversation
- **Postdoctoral Fellows Seminar**
- **Cornell School of Theory** — theory training for graduate students
- **[Postdoctoral Fellowships](https://mahindrahumanities.harvard.edu/fellowships)**
- **Interdisciplinary Dissertation Fellowships**
