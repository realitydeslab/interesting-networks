# Tangible Media Group

Research group at MIT Media Lab founded by [[Hiroshi Ishii]] in 1995. Pursues the vision of Tangible Bits & Radical Atoms — "seamlessly coupling the dual worlds of bits and atoms by giving dynamic physical form to digital information and computation." Currently extending into TeleAbsence — connecting across time to past selves and deceased loved ones.

## Key People
- [[Hiroshi Ishii]] — founder and director
- [[Lining Yao]] — Ph.D. '17, now CMU
- [[Sean Follmer]] — Ph.D. '15, now Stanford
- [[Xiao Xiao]] — MirrorFugue, TeleAbsence
- [[Kia Höök]] — collaborator (OmniFiber)

## Topics
Tangible User Interfaces, shape-changing interfaces, programmable matter, 4D fabrication, bio-hybrid computing, morphing textiles, digital memory, telepresence, TeleAbsence.

## Events & Programmes
- Regularly presents at ACM CHI, UIST, SIGGRAPH, TEI
- Regular exhibitions at [[Ars Electronica]], Cooper Hewitt, MIT Museum
- Group produces ~60 alumni, many of whom are now HCI faculty worldwide

## Why Interesting
The Tangible Media Group is the institutional home of embodied computing — the tradition that says computing should be felt, not just seen. Their TeleAbsence research (2024–) is now directly engaging mortality, memory, and connection to deceased loved ones — territory that connects to Amber's interests. The group also serves as a professor-factory: alumni lead HCI labs at CMU, Stanford, CU Boulder, UChicago, establishing a field-level infrastructure.
