# TEI Community

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-27:** Deep research completed. Full profile with steering committee, conference history, key people, somaesthetics connections, and Amber's engagement path.
- **2026-02-26:** Created stub during Kia Höök research. TEI 2026 in Chicago March 8-11, theme "Tide + Tied".

The **ACM International Conference on Tangible, Embedded and Embodied Interaction (TEI)** is the premier annual research forum where computing meets the physical body — tangible interfaces, embodied cognition, somaesthetics, shape-changing UIs, and material computation. Founded from Hiroshi Ishii and Brygg Ullmer's 1997 "Tangible Bits" CHI paper, the conference is where engineers become artists and artists become engineers. TEI 2026 is the 20th edition — happening right now (March 8–11, Chicago). Intimate (~150–350 attendees), interdisciplinary, and unusually hospitable to practice-based and arts-led research.

---

## Mission & Objectives
TEI "addresses issues of human-computer interaction, novel tools and technologies, interactive art, and user experience. The work presented at TEI has a strong focus on how computing can bridge atoms and bits into cohesive interactive systems." Its intimate single-track format is by design — a deliberate choice to encourage genuine cross-disciplinary dialogue rather than siloed parallel sessions. Disciplines represented: engineering, interaction design, computer science, product design, media studies, arts.

The conference is sponsored by ACM SIGCHI and has grown from a CHI workshop spinoff (2007) to a fully independent conference series of 20 editions. Acceptance rate is consistently ~25–35% for papers.

**Core intellectual commitments:**
- Physical-digital integration — "bridging atoms and bits"
- Embodied cognition informing design
- Research-through-design and practice-based methodology
- Art × science synthesis (Ishii's motto: "Be Artistic and Analytic. Be Poetic and Pragmatic.")
- Somaesthetics — body as a site of design knowledge (via Kia Höök's KTH lineage)

## Key Publications & Output
- **TEI Conference Proceedings** (ACM Digital Library) — annual, since 2007. All indexed, open access via ACM OpenTOC.
- **Pictorials** — TEI pioneered a distinct "Pictorials" paper format: full-colour, image-heavy research contributions combining visual design work with academic rigor. Rare in ACM conferences; modeled on design research practice. Ideal for Amber's practice-based work.
- **Work in Progress (WiP)** — shorter contributions, lower barrier to entry.
- **Studios** — hands-on workshops at the conference. Facilitate methods transmission.
- **Arts Track** — curated art installations and performances. Part of the official proceedings.

Key texts informing the TEI intellectual tradition:
- Ishii & Ullmer, "Tangible Bits" CHI 1997 — founding paper
- Hornecker & Buur, "Getting a Grip on Tangible Interaction" CHI 2006 — framework paper (4 themes: tangible manipulation, spatial interaction, embodied facilitation, expressive representation)
- Dourish, *Where the Action Is* (2004) — philosophical grounding
- Höök, *Designing with the Body* (2018) — somaesthetics methodology
- Ishii, "Radical Atoms" TEI 2012 — next-generation vision

## Key Contributions
- **Established the Pictorials format** in ACM conferences, enabling visual and design-led research to be valued alongside text-heavy papers.
- **Legitimized somaesthetics as an HCI methodology** — the conference has been a key venue where Kia Höök's soma design work has been presented and debated.
- **Art-technology synthesis** — the Arts & Performance track creates space for work that doesn't fit in purely scientific frames.
- **Small-conference model as premium** — deliberately stayed small (~150–350 attendees) while CHI ballooned. Creates an unusually tight-knit community.
- **Physical computing infrastructure** — reacTIVision (Kaltenbrunner), TUIO protocol, and other tangible interaction tools came out of TEI community work.
- **Spawned the "shape-changing interfaces" research thread** — Radical Atoms work from MIT expanded into a recognizable sub-community at TEI.

## Why Interesting
TEI sits at the exact intersection of every core thread in Amber's research taste: **embodied interaction** (soma, body, movement), **more-than-human design** (agency of materials, non-human actors), **practice-based methods** (Pictorials, Studios, Arts track), and **speculative design** (Art & Performance). Unlike CHI, which has become sprawling and impersonal (~4,000 attendees), TEI's 150–350 person size means every conversation matters and community memory is strong. The Pictorials format uniquely rewards Amber's making-based research. And the somaesthetics thread — from Kia Höök through to Lian Loke and others — provides the intellectual vocabulary Amber needs to articulate the body-centered dimension of her XR and more-than-human design work.

Crucially, TEI hasn't been fully captured by the "AI for X" trend — it still foregrounds physicality, materiality, and the body as primary sites of knowledge production. That makes it a productive counter-community to the scale-obsessed AI mainstream Amber is critiquing.
