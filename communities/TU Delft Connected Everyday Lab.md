# TU Delft Connected Everyday Lab

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Initial discovery through Elisa Giaccardi research.

Research lab at TU Delft's Faculty of Industrial Design Engineering, founded and led by [[Elisa Giaccardi]]. Focus on more-than-human design approaches, connected objects, and posthuman design research.

## Mission & Objectives
Developing design methodologies that account for multispecies worlds and non-anthropocentric perspectives. Research spans from theoretical frameworks to practical applications including biological-HCI and living system interfaces.

## Key People
- **[[Elisa Giaccardi]]** ⭐⭐⭐ — Founder and leader. Professor of Interactive Media Design.
- **[[Iohanna Nicenboim]]** ⭐⭐ — Researcher focusing on more-than-human perspectives in interaction design.
- **[[Seowoo Nam]]** ⭐⭐ — Researcher working on diffractive interfaces and forest ecosystem data.
- **[[Nazli Cila]]** ⭐⭐ — AI agents and more-than-human interactions researcher.
- **[[Mugdha Patil]]** ⭐⭐ — PhD researcher on decolonial design approaches.
- **Jiwei Zhou** — PhD student working on living materials and biological-HCI.

## Key Publications & Output
- Diffractive interfaces for forest ecosystem data (DIS 2025)
- Living cyanobacterial interfaces for domestic air purification (CHI 2024)
- Decolonial design theory and hauntological approaches (CoDesign 2024)
- Research on connected objects, IoT design, and everyday practice

## Key Contributions
- "Things as co-ethnographers" concept — objects as research participants
- Biological-HCI research with living systems (cyanobacteria, microbes)
- Feminist new materialism applied to interface design
- Decolonial perspectives on AI and speculative design
