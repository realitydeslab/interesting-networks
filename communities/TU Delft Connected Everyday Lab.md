# TU Delft Connected Everyday Lab

A leading European research lab at the edge of design, ecology, and more-than-human thinking. Not just researching posthuman theory — actually building it into interfaces, living organisms, and connected everyday objects. The lab turns Karen Barad and Donna Haraway into CHI papers and biological artefacts.

## Mission & Objectives

The Connected Everyday Lab investigates how things — digital, biological, material — can be designed *with* rather than *for* people and more-than-human stakeholders. Research spans from theoretical frameworks (feminist new materialism, posthumanism) to hands-on practice (biological interfaces, IoT traces, speculative design artifacts). 

Core conviction: the relationship between people and connected objects is ethically loaded, ecologically entangled, and deeply undertheorized. The lab does the theory AND the practice.

Three thematic pillars:
1. **More-than-human design** — decenter the human, account for ecological and biological agencies
2. **Connected objects / IoT** — traces, data, ethics of networked things
3. **Biological & material HCI** — living systems as interface materials (cyanobacteria, forests, microbes)

## Key Publications & Output

### Landmark Papers
- **[Diffractive Interfaces: Facilitating Agential Cuts in Forest Data Across More-than-human Scales](https://doi.org/10.1145/3715336.3735404)** (DIS 2025, Giaccardi, Nam, Nicenboim) — Design pictorial applying Karen Barad's diffraction and agential cuts to urban forest simulation interfaces. Challenges anthropocentric agent-based models by designing interfaces that reveal relational forest ecologies. Presented at DIS 2025, Madeira.
- **[Living with Cyanobacteria: Exploring Materiality in Caring for Microbes in Everyday Life](https://research.tudelft.nl/en/publications/5898b8ef-e796-4f58-bc96-5a3bb86eee0f)** (CHI 2024, Zhou, Doubrovski, Giaccardi, Karana) — Color-changing, pliable, adhesive cyanobacterial artefact for domestic air purification. Participants live with it for 2 weeks. Studies mutualistic care in human-microbe interactions. Explores materiality (labour, knowledge, exploration) in caring for living artefacts.
- **[In conversation with ghosts: Towards a hauntological approach to decolonial design for/with AI practices](https://research.tudelft.nl/en/publications/be4a1816-1e91-412d-95fa-a4f7ddf76394)** (CoDesign 2024, Patil, Cila, Redström, Giaccardi) — Applies hauntology (Derrida/Spivak) to decolonial AI design research. Published in CoDesign. Rare paper applying postcolonial theory and philosophy to design practice.
- **[The Future of More-Than-Human Design: A Computing Practice in Crisis?](https://doi.org/10.1145/3737609.3747092)** (Aarhus 2025 workshop, Nicenboim, Giaccardi, Barendregt, Redström + large coalition) — Coalition workshop paper at 6th Decennial Aarhus Conference "Computing X Crisis". Argues it's time to move MTH design from niche/speculation to mainstream practice.
- **[Designing Human-Robot Ecologies: Beyond Utilitarian Relations](https://research.tudelft.nl/en/publications/04a587ea-c508-4c8c-8a58-10d4ee4ff61e)** (2025, Nazli Cila) — Book/monograph on robot design beyond usefulness. Proposes "human-robot ecologies" framing.
- **[Regenerative Material Ecologies in HCI](https://research.tudelft.nl/en/publications/c14d47d3-fc65-4cfe-b72c-dca176b8ba2c)** (2025, Nicenboim, Karana, McQuillan, Devendorf, Kakehi, Bell, Speed, Oogjes, Yao + more) — Large coalition paper on regenerative materials in HCI. Bridges biological/living materials with sustainability and HCI.
- **[Does AI need designing? Exploring Design in Clinical AI](https://research.tudelft.nl/en/publications/a523f9f9-a543-430a-a0e1-16c9f8b8beb4)** (DRS 2024, Ranjbar, Raijmakers, Lloyd, Giaccardi, Ben Allouch) — Questions what design can and should contribute to clinical AI systems.
- **[Dramatic Things: Investigating Value Conflicts in Smart Home through Enactment and Co-speculation](https://research.tudelft.nl/en/publications/3588e0a7-a8b8-4d72-9643-20e1c6c4ea44)** (2025, Cila, Lupetti, Siebert, Van Grunsven) — Using dramatic enactment and speculative methods to surface value conflicts in smart home devices.

### Ongoing Project Platforms
- **[Thing Tank](http://thingtank.org/)** — Internationally funded research platform. Combines field studies, object instrumentation, and machine learning to "listen to what material artefacts have to tell" about shared use, reuse, and deviant repurpose. Harvests this data for ideation, prototyping, and business development.
- **[PACT / Things as Citizens](https://thingsascitizens.org/)** — Explores new partnerships between people and things in the smart city. Framing objects as citizens with political standing.
- **IoT Manifesto** (project) — 10 principles for responsible and ethical IoT design. Created during Holly Robbins' PhD.
- **Mizu** — Connected sink using traces of use as design approach.
- **Chiocciola** — Concept for using material traces as rich communication medium with/through connected objects.
- **Predictive Materialities** — Speculative design concepts for artefacts as co-performers of practice; explores "openness and closure" in object self-reinvention.

### Methodological Contributions
- **Thing Ethnography** — Giaccardi's methodology: objects as co-ethnographers and research participants (not just subjects of study). Key paper: Giaccardi et al. 2016.
- **Diffractive Design** — applying Barad's diffraction (not reflection) as design method for more-than-human interfaces
- **Noticing Entanglements** (Iohanna Nicenboim) — podcast + methodology for guiding designers to expand from user-centered to more-than-human centered design
- **Hauntological Design** — applying hauntology to decolonial AI design practice

## Why Interesting

The CEL is where **posthuman theory meets empirical practice** in Europe. Most labs either do theory (philosophy of technology) or practice (HCI engineering). The CEL does both — Karen Barad's agential realism becomes a CHI paper about cyanobacterial artefacts; Derrida's hauntology becomes a CoDesign article on AI practices.

For Amber specifically:
1. **More-than-human design** is Amber's central research thread — the CEL is the leading European lab in this space
2. **IoT × agency** — the lab's Thing Tank and PACT work on things-as-citizens directly parallels thinking about AI agents as social actors
3. **Biological HCI** — cyanobacteria interfaces point toward a future where computing is living — relevant to Amber's machine consciousness / machine mortality interests
4. **Network access** — getting to know this lab means access to the European DIS/CHI more-than-human design community

The Aarhus 2025 workshop paper is a signal: the lab is **declaring a movement from theory to practice**. This is exactly the edge-of-chaos moment — the field is crystallizing, new coalitions are forming (see the large author list on Regenerative Material Ecologies). Amber should be in this coalition.
