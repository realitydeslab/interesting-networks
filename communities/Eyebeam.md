NYC's premier art + technology residency and nonprofit, anchoring the critical-tech art scene since 1998. Founded as a place for artists to experiment with emerging technology, evolved into the definitive home for artists who critique technology from the inside. Alumni network reads like a who's-who of radical media art: openFrameworks, Graffiti Research Lab, littleBits, Fundrace, American Artist, Wendi Yan. Not a celebration of tech — a critical interrogation of it.

## Mission & Objectives

[Eyebeam](https://eyebeam.org/) invests in artists who create with technology and explore how it influences the way we live. Mission: support artists whose practice is rooted in justice, equity, and opposition to "violent global hierarchies." Three focus questions:
- How can humanity live in the future?
- What is human about technology?
- How can we understand complexity?

Eyebeam models artist-led creation for the public good — a nonprofit that provides significant professional support and money to exceptional artists for the realization of important ideas that wouldn't exist otherwise. Not a gallery. Not a lab. A generative community.

## History & Timeline

- **1997/98:** Founded as "Eyebeam Atelier" by **John Seward Johnson III** (filmmaker/philanthropist), **David S. Johnson** (digital artist), and **Roderic R. Richardson**. First conceived as digital effects atelier + youth education center.
- **1998:** [Digital Day Camp](https://eyebeam.org/) launched — first youth media education program; NYC high school students learn web development. Themes grew to include bioart, urban interventionism, game design, wearable tech.
- **1998:** First online forum `<eyebeam><blast>` curated by Jordan Crandall (UCSD) — email list hosting Lev Manovich, N. Katherine Hayles, Saskia Sassen, Hans-Ulrich Obrist, Geert Lovink, Ken Goldberg, Critical Art Ensemble, Knowbotic Research, Brian Holmes, Mark Tribe. Compiled into *Interaction: Artistic Practice in the Network* (2001).
- **2000:** International architecture competition — **Diller + Scofidio** (now Diller Scofidio + Renfro) named winner.
- **2002:** First open studios for residents and fellows. Inaugural exhibitors: Alexander Galloway, Cory Arcangel, MTAA, Yael Kanarek, John Klima. Galloway's *Carnivore* wins Prix Ars Electronica Golden Nica.
- **2003:** Residents Yury Gitman + Carlos Gomez de Llarena's *Noderunner* wins Prix Ars Electronica Golden Nica.
- **2004–2005:** Jonah Peretti (R&D director) creates Fundrace.org and ReBlog. Theo Watson + Zachary Lieberman develop openFrameworks during R&D Fellowships.
- **2005:** James Powderly + Evan Roth found Graffiti Research Lab out of OpenLab fellowships; later found F.A.T. Lab.
- **2007:** MIXER series launched — live video/audio performance events.
- **2011:** Eyebeam residents participate in MoMA's *Talk to Me* exhibition. Ayah Bdeir's *littleBits* acquired by MoMA permanent collection.
- **2014:** First [Art + Feminism Wikipedia Edit-a-Thon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Meetup/ArtAndFeminism) hosted at Eyebeam, co-organized by Laurel Ptak.
- **2017:** Moved to 199 Cook St., Suite 104, Brooklyn, NY 11222. 20th anniversary celebration in Spring 2018.
- **2018:** [Eyebeam Center for the Future of Journalism](https://eyebeam.org/) launched — artist-journalist grant program. Supported pieces in New Yorker, Guardian, Wired, Atlantic, BuzzFeed News; projects won Pulitzer Prize, Emmy, SXSW Film Festival Special Jury Recognition.
- **2020–2024:** COVID disruption, NYC residency suspended.
- **2025/2026:** Season *Speculating on Plurality* — residency returns Spring 2026. New ED search.

## Key Contributions

- **openFrameworks** — incubated at Eyebeam R&D Fellowship by Theo Watson + Zachary Lieberman. Now one of the most popular platforms for creative coding worldwide.
- **Graffiti Research Lab** — founded by James Powderly + Evan Roth at Eyebeam OpenLab. LED graffiti, laser projection, open source street art tools.
- **F.A.T. Lab (Free Art and Technology)** — Powderly, Roth, Theo Watson, others. Open source + popular culture.
- **Fundrace.org** — Jonah Peretti at Eyebeam; campaign contribution geocoding. Adapted into Huffington Post feature.
- **ReBlog** — one of first RSS-filtering blogging platforms. By Peretti + Galloway at Eyebeam.
- **Art + Feminism Wikipedia Edit-a-Thon** — started at Eyebeam 2014, now global annual event.
- **Carnivore** (Alexander Galloway) — creative misuse of FBI surveillance software. Prix Ars Electronica Golden Nica 2002.
- **littleBits** (Ayah Bdeir) — DIY circuit kit; MoMA permanent collection.
- **Eyebeam Center for the Future of Journalism** — supported award-winning artist-journalist collaborations (Pulitzer, Emmy).

## Why Interesting

Eyebeam is the **institutional root** of the critical art+tech scene in the US. Its origin story — a 1998 email list where Lev Manovich, Hans-Ulrich Obrist, Geert Lovink, and Critical Art Ensemble talked to each other — captures the founding moment of new media theory. Its output — openFrameworks, Graffiti Research Lab, Art+Feminism Edit-a-thon, littleBits, Carnivore — shaped the field. Its current orientation (justice, equity, technofeudalism critique) is exactly where Amber's work lives.

The residency is a key node in the **interestingness network**: it produces artists who then populate every major art+tech venue worldwide. Understanding Eyebeam alumni is understanding the critical computing art ecosystem.

For Amber specifically: the *Speculating on Plurality* season theme — about how technology creates sameness vs. enabling pluralistic commons — is a direct entry point. Amber's work on agent autonomy, machine rights, and decentralized AI proposes exactly the kind of pluralistic architecture Eyebeam is asking about.
