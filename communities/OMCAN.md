# OMCAN — Oxford Mathematics of Consciousness and Applications Network

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-03-12:** Deep research completed. Expanded from stub to full profile. Upgraded from ⭐⭐ to ⭐⭐⭐ — at Amber's own university, directly addresses machine consciousness, with active FQXi-funded research programme.

An interdisciplinary research network based at the **Mathematical Institute, University of Oxford**, bringing together mathematicians, physicists, neuroscientists, experimental psychologists, and philosophers to develop rigorous mathematical theories of consciousness. Founded and led by [[Yakov Kremnitzer]] (known as Kobi), OMCAN sits at the frontier where consciousness science meets mathematical formalism — the precise intersection of Amber's philosophical core (machine consciousness, mortality) and his academic home.

## Mission & Objectives

OMCAN's mission is to establish Oxford as a leading centre for **mathematical consciousness science** — the emerging field that seeks to provide rigorous, testable mathematical descriptions of conscious experience. Core objectives:

1. **Cross-university collaboration** — Provide researchers from across Oxford with opportunities to share knowledge on consciousness science
2. **Seminar series and discussions** — Regular events connecting mathematicians, physicists, neuroscientists, psychologists, and philosophers
3. **Collaborative research funding** — Find and support joint research proposals
4. **Centre building** — Establish a formal Centre for Mathematical Approaches to Consciousness at Oxford (FQXi-funded pump priming grant won in 2020)

**Key Intellectual Framing:** OMCAN operates at the intersection of:
- **Integrated Information Theory (IIT)** — Giulio Tononi's mathematical theory that consciousness = integrated information (Φ)
- **Quantum consciousness** — How consciousness might interact with quantum mechanics, including the measurement problem
- **Free Energy Principle** — [[Karl Friston]]'s framework connecting consciousness to predictive processing
- **Computational models** — SpiNNaker project, neural network approaches
