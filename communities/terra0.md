# terra0

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-03-01:** Deep research completed. Expanded from stub to full entry. ⭐⭐⭐ rating confirmed — one of the most directly relevant art collectives to Amber's research on machine behavior, autonomous agents, more-than-human governance, ALife, and decentralized AI.

Berlin-based art collective (founded 2015) exploring how ecosystems can become economic agents. Their foundational question: *Can an augmented forest own and utilize itself?* Working at the intersection of blockchain technology, ecological theory, and legal personhood, terra0 builds "hybrid ecosystems" — living systems augmented with smart contracts that give them economic agency, ownership, and autonomous governance. Inspired by Richard Brautigan's 1967 poem "All Watched Over by Machines of Loving Grace," which imagined a cybernetic ecology where mammals and computers coexist in "mutually programming harmony."

## Mission & Objectives

terra0 raises questions around:
- **Autonomy and agency** in culture and law — what does it mean for a non-human system to "decide"?
- **Novel distributions of ownership** — who or what can own land, capital, natural resources?
- **Regard for the natural world** within and beyond market capitalism — can ecological systems participate in, and thereby subvert, economic systems?

Their method: augment ecosystems with sensors, smart contracts, and token-based protocols so the ecosystem becomes an economic actor. Each project explores a different facet of this core question, moving from whitepaper speculation (2016) to permanent public sculpture (2022) to actual DAO-governed forest (2025).

**Not utopian tech boosterism** — terra0 is explicitly critical. Their work acknowledges the paradox: to gain sovereignty within capitalism, the forest must first participate in capitalism. The self-utilizing forest sells logging rights to buy its own freedom — a deliberate provocation about the limits of market-based solutions to ecological crisis.

## Key Projects (Chronological)

### [terra0 white paper](https://terra0.org/assets/pdf/terra0_white_paper_2016.pdf) (2016)
The founding document. Proposed a self-utilizing forest which, augmented with sensors and smart contracts, sells logging licenses and accumulates capital — ultimately buying itself and achieving autonomy. Seminal conceptual framework: the non-human ecological system as economic agent using capitalism's own tools against itself. Published/exhibited in *Artists Re:Thinking the Blockchain* (Torque Torque, 2017). Directly cited in Avery Review essay on "Forest Governmentality."

### [Flowertokens](https://flowertokens.terra0.org) (2018)
Early attempt at creating a combined crypto-collectible physical asset. Tokenization and verification of natural commodities (flowers). First shown at [[Trust Berlin]] — one of Berlin's key art-philosophy spaces — during Proof of Work exhibition period. Louis Center and Georgia Hansford wrote the frontend.

### [Premna Daemon](https://medium.com/@terra0/premna-daemon-an-introduction-via-a-history-of-autonomy-in-the-cryptosphere-3cee15e92fe2) (2018)
Augmented Premna Microphylla (bonsai tree species) given autonomous economic agency. Developed for *Proof of Work* exhibition at [[Schinkel Pavillon]], Berlin, curated by Simon Denny. Traces a history of autonomy in cypherpunk, Bitcoin, and Ethereum communities alongside science fiction (Daemon by Daniel Suarez, Accelerando by Charles Stross, Vitalik Buterin's DAO writing). Key conceptual move: situating blockchain-based autonomous agents within longer histories of cybernetic and SF imagination.

### [Two Degrees](https://terra0.medium.com/two-degrees-nft-5af1263bfcc) (2021)
NFT depicting a LiDAR scan of a German forest with an oracle smart contract storing NASA's average annual temperature data. When global temperature rise crosses 2°C, anyone can burn the token. Premiered at **Sotheby's Natively Digital: A Curated NFT Sale** (June 2021) — one of the first major auction house NFT events. Conceptually: art as contingent claim, the artwork's existence literally staked on climate outcomes. Sophisticated critique using DeFi derivatives logic.

### [Seed Capital](https://terra0.medium.com/wen-growth-an-introduction-to-seed-capital-8d4a9df93789) (2022)
Sculpture rethinking economic and ecological relationships between viewers, institution, and artwork. Exhibited at Art Dubai 2022, curated by [[Fingerprints DAO]].

### [A tree; a corporation; a person](https://www.fastcompany.com/90738287/this-tree-owns-itself-and-is-fighting-for-its-own-survival) (2022 — permanent)
Public sculpture testing limits of legal personhood in the US. A tree incorporated as a legal entity, installed as a permanent public sculpture at **Carnegie Museum of Art, Pittsburgh** (from Sep 25, 2022). Directly engages US legal frameworks for non-human personhood. FastCompany coverage: "This Tree Owns Itself and Is Fighting for Its Own Survival."

### [Conditional Power](https://conditionalpower.terra0.org) (2024–ongoing)
A series of works exploring the connection between global environmental changes and the art market. Ongoing, in development.

### [Autonomous Forest](https://autonomousforest.org) (2025–)
terra0's most ambitious and materially realized project. Commissioned by [[LAS Art Foundation]].

**Structure:** A German woodland managed via:
- **Verein** (German non-profit association) as the legal entity that holds land title
- **Ethereum DAO** for collective governance
- **NFTs** (0.08–0.1 ETH) grant voting rights in forest governance; staking NFTs grants Verein membership
- **Custom geohash grid** maps physical land parcels to blockchain tokens
- **Smart contracts** enable transparent, decentralized decision-making about forest management

**Mission statement:** "Who owns the means of Regeneration?" — the Autonomous Forest is dedicated to acquiring and collectively managing forest areas through decentralized platforms where members make decisions collaboratively.

**Why it matters:** This is not a simulation or a whitepaper. It is a real forest, legally owned by a DAO-governed non-profit, where NFT-holding members vote on land management decisions. The 2016 whitepaper has become real governance infrastructure.

## Key Ideas / Conceptual Framework

### 1. The Autonomy Paradox
terra0's foundational insight: to escape the capitalist property regime, a non-human entity must first participate in it. The forest must sell logging rights to accumulate capital to buy itself. This is not naivety — it's a deliberate demonstration of the contradictions in using market mechanisms for emancipatory ends. The paradox is the work.

### 2. Smart Contracts as Ecological Governance
Smart contracts are not just financial instruments — they are governance infrastructure. terra0 treats Ethereum's smart contracts as a legal-technical layer that can replace or augment traditional property law. The "oracle" pattern (connecting off-chain data to on-chain logic) enables ecosystems to respond to real-world conditions automatically.

### 3. Hybrid Ecosystems
terra0's key term: ecosystems that are simultaneously biological and computational. The forest is not controlled by the blockchain; it is augmented by it. The DAO doesn't replace the forest's own processes — it creates a new layer of social/economic organization around them.

### 4. Non-Human Legal Personhood
From Flowertokens to "A tree; a corporation; a person," terra0 systematically probes how legal personhood can be extended to non-humans — not through rights-of-nature activism alone, but through practical legal engineering (incorporating trees, establishing Vereins, using DAOs).

### 5. Art as Infrastructure
terra0 operates in the gap between artwork and institution. Autonomous Forest is not just a sculpture — it is a functioning non-profit and DAO that will outlast any exhibition. This is "art as infrastructure": building actual governance systems through artistic practice.

### 6. Cypherpunk Heritage
terra0 explicitly situates itself within the cypherpunk tradition — the idea that cryptography and distributed systems can create new political realities outside state control. This connects to Vitalik Buterin's early DAO thinking, which was itself inspired by SF (Daniel Suarez's *Daemon*) and Marxist critique.

## Venue Circuit Analysis

terra0 moves through **four overlapping circuits**:

### 1. Institutional European Art Circuit (prestige)
Carnegie International, Venice Architecture Biennale (German Pavilion), Athens Biennale, Kunsthalle Zürich, Haus der Kunst München, Deichtorhallen Hamburg, Zeppelin Museum — these are large institutional shows that confer legitimacy and reach. terra0 appears in this circuit as an established critical voice on blockchain + ecology.

### 2. Berlin Tech-Philosophy Underground (origin + home)
Schinkel Pavillon, Trust Berlin, Transmediale, KW Institute, Spektrum, B-Part, Haus der Statistik — these are the spaces where terra0's ideas were first tested and refined. Small, curated, intellectually serious. Trust Berlin in particular is a recurring home — Paul Seidler specifically uses it for early-stage work.

### 3. Global Blockchain/Crypto-Art Circuit (medium)
Art Dubai, Sotheby's Natively Digital, NFT Paris, Hyundai Motorstudio (Beijing), Kate Vass Galerie (Zürich), NEORT++ (Tokyo) — the crypto art market. terra0 appears here but maintains critical distance; they use the market as a site of investigation, not uncritical participation.

CAFA Art Museum (Beijing), Chronus Art Center (Shanghai), Pivô (São Paulo), NuMu (Guatemala City) — deliberate global expansion beyond the European-American axis. Significant that they showed in China (2020, 2022) — connects to the Chinese art-tech scene through [[Chronus Art Center]].
