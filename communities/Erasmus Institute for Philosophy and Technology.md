# Erasmus Institute for Philosophy and Technology (IPT)

Founded in 2025, housed within the [Erasmus School of Philosophy (ESPhil)](https://www.eur.nl/en/esphil) at Erasmus University Rotterdam. Directed by [[Yuk Hui]], Chair of Human Conditions. The institutional European base for the [[Research Network for Philosophy and Technology]] and the global cosmotechnics research agenda. Recently awarded a €1.5M NWO Vici grant (Feb 2026) — one of the Netherlands' most prestigious research grants. This is the primary institutional home of some of the most rigorous philosophy-of-technology work happening right now, bridging continental European philosophy with non-Western (Chinese, Japanese, Brazilian) perspectives.

## Mission & Objectives

The IPT is founded on the premise that "philosophy and technology are inseparable." Its mandate is double: not only to interpret technology philosophically, but to seek a "technological interpretation of philosophy" — acknowledging that technological questions have been central to philosophical thought since its inception. 

Core research themes:
- **Computation and Automation** — historical and philosophical analysis of AI beyond current technical debates
- **Technology and Planetarization** — how technologies reshape the planet and require new political frameworks
- **Technodiversity** — resisting homogenization, pluralizing technology to incorporate diverse forms of knowledge

The IPT explicitly bridges **engineering, artistic practice, and philosophical inquiry** — rare for a philosophy institute. It aims to be a "global hub for critical reflection" distinct from both pure policy advocacy and pure analytic AI ethics.

Philosophically rooted in continental tradition: phenomenology, poststructuralism, critical theory, philosophical anthropology — but expanded to include non-Western (Chinese, Japanese) philosophy of technology. This is the Yuk Hui signature: taking Simondon, Stiegler, Heidegger seriously while asking what a non-Western philosophy of technology would look like.

## NWO Vici Grant: "Technodiversity: A New Lens on AI" (2026)

[Press release](https://www.eur.nl/en/esphil/news/professor-yuk-hui-awarded-nwo-vici-grant-research-future-ai-and-technodiversity) — Awarded Feb 26, 2026. **€1.5 million euros**, one of Netherlands' most prestigious research grants.

Three pillars:
1. **Epistemological history of AI** — reconstruct AI's intellectual history beyond current technical debates
2. **Critique of political economy of automation** — how modern AI differs from 19th-century industrial machines
3. **Technodiversity investigation** — AI situated in history of philosophy AND reconsidered outside Western thought; multiplicity of cultural/philosophical approaches to technology

This grant will anchor IPT's core research themes and fund new PhDs and postdocs (likely to be announced soon — **watch for open positions**).

## ESPhil Research Framework (4 Programmes)

1. **Human Conditions** (Yuk Hui's home) — subjectivity, AI, climate, feminism, anticolonialism, post-truth, consciousness, agency
2. **The Making of Modernity** — Renaissance to Enlightenment intellectual history; Descartes, Spinoza, Locke, Leibniz
3. **Colliding Worldviews** — quantum mechanics, rival conceptions of life, truth decay, livability
4. **Philosophy of Economics (EIPE)** — world-leading centre for philosophy of economics; rationality, methodology, ethics of economics

Part-Time PhD Programme also available — unusual offering that could interest practitioners.

## Why Interesting

**This is the most rigorous institutional home for non-Western philosophy of technology in Europe.** Unlike AI ethics institutes (which focus on policy/safety) or STS programs (which do sociology of tech), IPT is doing genuine continental philosophy of technology with a commitment to cosmotechnics — the idea that technology is always embedded in a cosmology, and that different civilizations have different technologies because they have different cosmologies.

For Amber: the cosmotechnics framework is directly relevant to thinking about why HoloKit or agentic AI might look different when developed from a Chinese philosophical tradition vs. a Silicon Valley rationalist one. This isn't just Amber's background — it's a live intellectual problem that IPT is working on with serious resources.

The Stiegler connection matters too: Stiegler's pharmacology (technology as both poison and cure), his concept of tertiary retention (digitally externalized memory), and his political economy of attention are all directly relevant to Amber's work on parasitic agents, machine behavior, and more-than-human design.

The NWO Vici grant signals this is a fast-moving, well-resourced institute with momentum. The combination of Yuk Hui's ArtReview Power 100 ranking AND major research funding is rare — it suggests genuine cross-domain influence.
