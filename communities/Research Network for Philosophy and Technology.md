# Research Network for Philosophy and Technology
*技術與哲學研究網絡*

The premier international network for continental and non-Western philosophy of technology. Founded by [[Yuk Hui]] in 2014, it functions simultaneously as a global symposium series, a publisher (via the *Technophany* journal), and the institutional backbone for the cosmotechnics intellectual movement. The network actively insists that there is not one but many philosophies of technology — and that Western modernity does not exhaust the question of what technology means. Among the most intellectually ambitious networks at the intersection of philosophy, AI, cybernetics, art, and critical theory.

## Mission & Objectives

The Research Network for Philosophy and Technology, [established in 2014](https://philosophyandtechnology.network/), is a project to **rethink the relation between philosophy and technology, and the future of this relation from global and historical perspective**. It hosts:

- The academic journal *Technophany — A Journal for Philosophy and Technology* (co-published with [Radboud University Press](https://www.radbouduniversitypress.nl/))
- The *Dialogues on Philosophy and Technology* lecture/seminar series
- The annual *Bernard Stiegler Memorial Lecture* (each August 5th, anniversary of Stiegler's death)
- Symposia, book launches, workshops, and interdisciplinary events worldwide

The network's central wager: technology is not universal but *cosmotechnically plural*. The task of philosophy is not to ratify Western techno-modernity but to think the plurality of technical relations to cosmos and morality across civilizations, and to open up a space for **technodiversity** against the monoculture of global Silicon Valley capitalism. This theoretical agenda is also political: challenging the universalist pretension of Heideggerian technology critique and Silicon Valley teleology simultaneously.

The network operates bilingually (English and Chinese), with a significant Asia-Pacific dimension. Events are regularly held in Hong Kong, mainland China, Taiwan, and Rotterdam, as well as online. The network's website is at [philosophyandtechnology.network](https://philosophyandtechnology.network/) and social presence at [@philotechne](https://twitter.com/philotechne) (Twitter/X) and [YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/channel/UCaLE95M4BamLJwvwUQhL9MQ).

## History & Founding

- **2014** — Founded by [[Yuk Hui]]. Originally an informal intellectual project around his *Question Concerning Technology in China* (Urbanomic/MIT Press, 2016). At this time Hui was based at Leuphana University Lüneburg (Germany).
- **2016** — Publication of *On the Existence of Digital Objects* (U of Minnesota Press) and *The Question Concerning Technology in China* (Urbanomic) — the founding texts of the cosmotechnics paradigm that the network institutionalizes.
- **2019** — Hui moves to City University of Hong Kong (School of Creative Media). The network becomes partially anchored at CityU.
- **2020** — Website launched (philosophyandtechnology.network). Hui publishes *Art and Cosmotechnics* (U of Minnesota Press / e-flux, 2021).
- **2021** — *Technophany* journal launches its inaugural issue (*Futuros Locales / Local Futures* — Latin American cosmotechnics). Dialogues seminar series begins (initiated by Cosmotechnics/Critical AI research project, supported by CityU).
- **2023** — *Technophany* partners with [Radboud University Press](https://www.radbouduniversitypress.nl/) for professional open-access publishing. *Technophany* indexed by [DOAJ](https://doaj.org/) (2024).
- **2024** — Network collaborates with [Hanart Forum](http://hanartforum.org/) (Hong Kong) for the *Cybernetics for the 21st Century Part II* lecture series; also partners with Erasmus School of Philosophy and East Asian institutions.
- **2025** — Hui joins **Erasmus University Rotterdam** as Chair of Human Conditions (Erasmus School of Philosophy). The **Erasmus Institute for Philosophy and Technology** is founded — a new research institute that becomes the institutional home for the network in Europe. First in-person event: Idealism Summer Seminar (July 2, 2025, Erasmus University Rotterdam).

### Scope
The journal publishes "philosophical and historical studies of technologies and the technosciences." Characteristic topics include: cosmotechnics, technodiversity, philosophy of AI and computation, ecology and technosphere, Continental philosophy of technology (Heidegger, Stiegler, Simondon), non-Western philosophy of technology, political philosophy of technology, aesthetics and technology. Articles in English and French. No publication fee.

## Theoretical Debates & Key Themes

### Cosmotechnics vs. Universal Technology
The network's founding debate: against Heidegger's claim that technology represents a Western destiny that other cultures can only receive, Hui argues that each civilization has its own *cosmotechnics* — its own relation between techne, cosmos, and morality. The debate is ongoing: critics ask whether cosmotechnics reproduces culturalism; proponents argue it opens genuine technodiversity.

### Stiegler's Legacy: Pharmacology, Psychopolitics, Noeticopolitics
The network consistently engages Bernard Stiegler's thinking around technology as pharmakon (both remedy and poison), tertiary retention (technical memory), and the proletarianization of attention. After his death in 2020, these themes are being extended by Pieter Lemmens, Daniel Ross, Anne Alombert, and others in directions including: digital media and attention economy, COVID-19 and institutional trust, ecological thought.

### AI, Computation, and Non-Western Thought
A growing cluster around computational creativity (Technophany Vol. 3 No. 2), AI aesthetics (Anna Longo's work), and the question of whether AI can be thought from non-Western frameworks. The Cybernetics II series (2024) explicitly addressed AI through the lens of cybernetics history, asking what happens to freedom, democracy, and creativity in a cybernetic world.

### Entropies and the Anthropocene
The *Entropies* special issue (2024) and the *Biosphere/Technosphere in the Entropocene* seminar series represent the network's engagement with ecological collapse, thermodynamics, and the Anthropocene. The concept of *entropocene* frames our ecological crisis as fundamentally about entropy dissipation.

### German Idealism and Technology
Hegel's 200th anniversary of the *Philosophy of Right* (2021 workshop) and the Idealism Summer Seminar (2025) reflect an ongoing engagement with Kant, Hegel, Schelling in relation to technology, nature, and politics. This is not academic historicism — it's asking whether idealist resources can be recuperated for thinking AI, ecology, and geopolitics.

## Why Interesting

This network occupies a unique intellectual position: it is both **theoretically rigorous** (philosophy by professional philosophers) and **genuinely global** (not just lip service to non-Western thought, but actual structural engagement). The Bernard Stiegler Memorial Lectures, the Cybernetics project, and the Technophany journal all signal a network that is thinking long-term about what philosophy of technology even means when you take seriously that modernity is not universal.

For Amber specifically, this is the single most relevant academic network outside of HCI/design. The question of whether and how design can be a cosmotechnical practice — whether design pluralism is possible — is exactly what this network theorizes. Amber's design/AI practice gives her something to offer back: the phenomenology of actually *doing* design work with AI, which most network members theorize but don't practice.

The network is also **strategically well-positioned** for the coming AI reckoning: as Silicon Valley AI increasingly faces a counter-discourse about AI alignment, AI rights, and AI's cultural dimensions, Hui's cosmotechnics is a rigorous philosophical framework for that debate. The network's interdisciplinary reach (including art institutions, science fiction writers, economists) makes it a serious player in that conversation.
