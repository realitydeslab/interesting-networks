# La Colonie

A decolonial cultural space founded by artist [[Kader Attia]] and restaurateur Zico Selloum in Paris (2016–2020). Operating simultaneously as a café/bar, agora, laboratory, and exhibition space, La Colonie was one of the most politically explicit and intellectually rigorous artist-run cultural infrastructures in contemporary Europe. Located in a former textile factory in the 10th arrondissement near Gare du Nord — a deliberate choice for its historical association with Algerian and African migrant workers. Closed permanently in March 2020 due to the COVID-19 pandemic.

## Mission & Objectives

La Colonie's self-description (from the archived website, translated): *"La Colonie challenges amnesic and harmful postures. It is a convivial space that engages, in full independence, the construction sites of living and thinking together. By implementing this project, artist Kader Attia intends to pose in the present the questions of decolonization of peoples as well as knowledge, behaviors and practices."*

The project was built on a **triple ambition**:

1. **Decolonization of knowledge** — breaking down the boundaries between university knowledge and popular, non-Western, marginalized forms of knowing. Explicitly anti-elitist, anti-institutional.
2. **Social defragmentation** — creating a space where all identities and histories could meet, "especially those of minorities." The goal was literal: combat the social fragmentation of contemporary French (and European) society.
3. **Archiving the present** — La Colonie explicitly aimed to document and archive the knowledge produced there, making it freely available.

### Architecture as Argument
The physical space embodied these principles:
- **Ground floor (200m²):** The café/bar — the social commons. Open, accessible, funded through bar revenue (the financial model that proved vulnerable to COVID).
- **First floor (mezzanine):** Dedicated to knowledge-sharing in motion: public dialogues, conferences, readings, workshops, writing workshops. Isolated from street noise but not from the bar — a deliberate architectural integration.
- **Second floor:** Dedicated to art in non-museum, non-institutional contexts — conceptual, informal, performative.

The three-floor vertical structure was designed for "fluidity between different spaces," so that "life, thought, engagement and artistic practices interact in synergy."

### Financial Model
La Colonie was funded primarily through the café/bar operation — an unusual self-sustaining economic model for cultural infrastructure. This was both a political statement (no institutional dependence) and ultimately its fatal vulnerability (when COVID closed the bar in March 2020, there was no fallback revenue).

## Key People

- **[[Kader Attia]]** — Co-founder, artist. Franco-Algerian. His concept of *repair* (*réparation*) as philosophical and aesthetic practice was central to the space's intellectual DNA. After La Colonie closed, he curated the [12th Berlin Biennale for Contemporary Art (2022)](https://bb12.berlinbiennale.de/). Awards: [Prix Marcel Duchamp (2016)](https://www.centrepompidou.fr/fr/programme/agenda/evenement/cczflgy), [Joan Miró Prize (2018)](https://www.fundaciomiro-bcn.org/en/foundation/joan-miro-prize/).
- **Zico Selloum** — Co-founder, restaurateur. Managed the bar/café component that financially sustained the space. His hospitality expertise made the "agora" function real rather than aspirational.
- **[Samia Henni](https://www.samiahenni.com/)** — Historian and exhibition-maker (architecture, colonialism, military space). Brought her traveling exhibition *Discreet Violence: Architecture and the French War in Algeria* (2018) to La Colonie — the exhibition had already shown at ETH Zurich, the New Institute Rotterdam, and Archive Kabinett Berlin. One of the sharpest minds on French colonial spatial violence.
- **[iLiana Fokianaki](https://statecraftinstitute.net/)** — Curator, writer, founder of [State of Concept Athens](https://stateofconcept.org/). Curated *The Trials of Justice* (May–June 2019) — an exhibition-as-tribunal investigating justice systems and their colonial legacies.
- **[Joanna Warsza](https://www.joannawarsza.pl/)** — Curator and dramaturge, Berlin-based. Curated *Opaque to Herself: Poland and Postcolonialism* (Nov 2019–Jan 2020), La Colonie's final major exhibition. Her work examined Eastern European postcolonial complications — often overlooked in predominantly Western/Southern frameworks of decoloniality.

## Key Exhibitions & Programme

La Colonie hosted a dense, multi-format programme: exhibitions, public dialogues, workshops, screenings, lectures, concerts. Below are the documented exhibitions:

| Exhibition | Date | Curator/Context |
|---|---|---|
| *Opaque to Herself: Poland and Postcolonialism* | Nov 2019–Jan 2020 | Curated by Joanna Warsza — examined Polish colonial histories and blind spots |

| *Return of Fordlandia, an Open-Air Archive* | Nov 2018 | Henry Ford's failed Amazon rubber colony as colonial archetype |

| *CARACAS RESET* | May 2018 | Venezuelan urban/political context |

Beyond exhibitions, La Colonie hosted:
- **Regular public dialogues** with activists, academics, artists, militants
- **Workshops** with universities, collectives, research groups
- **Screenings** and concerts
- A function as **meeting point** for decolonial organizers across disciplines

## Key Publications & Output

La Colonie's explicit goal was to **archive and freely distribute** the knowledge produced there. The space positioned itself as a living archive of postcolonial thought in action. However, the COVID closure cut short what was becoming a significant intellectual documentation project.

Key affiliated publications and reading:
- **[*The Repair from Occident to Extra-Occidental Cultures*](https://www.thegreenbox.net/en/books/repair-occident-extra-occidental-cultures)** (Kader Attia, The Green Box, 2013) — the theoretical foundation underlying La Colonie's intellectual framework
- **[*The White West: Fascism, Unreason, and the Paradox of Modernity*](https://mitpress.mit.edu/9783956795336/the-white-west/)** (ed. Kader Attia, MIT Press, 2024) — the post-La Colonie book, consolidating its intellectual lineage
- **[*Architecture of Counterrevolution*](https://verlag.gta.arch.ethz.ch/en/gta:book_978-3-85676-376-3)** (Samia Henni, gta Verlag, 2017) — the book behind the *Discreet Violence* exhibition
- **[*Discreet Violence: Architecture and the French War in Algeria*](https://www.gta.arch.ethz.ch/ausstellungen/discreet.violence)** (Samia Henni, exhibition catalog/catalogue raisonné)

## Key Contributions

What La Colonie gave to the world:

1. **A model of artist-run decolonial infrastructure** that was explicitly anti-institutional — it rejected state funding dependency in favour of a bar-based self-sustaining economy.
2. **Proof of concept for the decolonial agora** — that a space can function simultaneously as café, commons, intellectual forum, and gallery without sacrificing any of these modes.
3. **A platform for transnational decolonial discourse** — connecting postcolonial conversations in France, Algeria, West Africa, Eastern Europe (the Poland/postcolonialism show was unusual), and beyond.
4. **The opening date as political gesture** — opening on October 17, the anniversary of the [Paris Massacre of 1961](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paris_massacre_of_1961), when Paris police killed hundreds of Algerian protesters, was itself a theorization of space and memory.
5. **Geographic positioning as argument** — near Gare du Nord, in a neighborhood populated by African and Asian migrants, was not aesthetic choice but political declaration.

## Connected Institutions & Ecosystem

La Colonie existed within and contributed to a broader ecosystem:

### Paris Decolonial Scene
- **[Lafayette Anticipations](https://www.lafayetteanticipations.com/en)** — Galeries Lafayette Foundation, Marais district. More institutionally mainstream but adjacent space.
- **[Palais de Tokyo](https://www.palaisdetokyo.com/)** — occasionally programs postcolonial work but remains more mainstream art world.
- **[[Institut du monde arabe]]** — older institutional form; La Colonie was explicitly positioned against this institutionalism.
- **[CAPC musée d'art contemporain de Bordeaux](https://www.capc-bordeaux.fr/)** — showed Kader Attia's work but represents the institutional mode La Colonie critiqued.

### International Ecosystem
- **[[Manifesta 13]]** (Marseille, 2020) — Samia Henni's work appeared here; the peripatetic European biennial as decolonial vehicle.
- **[[12th Berlin Biennale]]** (2022) — Kader Attia as curator, the most direct continuation of La Colonie's intellectual programme at the largest scale.
- **[State of Concept Athens](https://stateofconcept.org/)** — iLiana Fokianaki's space in Athens, a sister project in spirit: small, independent, politically explicit.

### Direct Legacy
La Colonie had no formal successor. Its closure was mourned as a significant loss to Parisian cultural life. However:
- Kader Attia's curation of the **12th Berlin Biennale** (2022) can be read as La Colonie's spirit relocated to institutional scale — many of the same intellectual concerns.
- The **La Colonie model** (artist-run, bar-funded, decolonial, anti-institutional) has influenced subsequent debates about cultural infrastructure in France.
- Several of La Colonie's recurring collaborators continued their work in other spaces: Samia Henni through academic institutions and touring exhibitions; iLiana Fokianaki through State of Concept Athens.

## Why Interesting

La Colonie is directly relevant to several of Amber's intellectual preoccupations:

1. **Communities as research infrastructure** — La Colonie was exactly this: a space where the community *was* the research. It didn't produce research about decolonialism; it *enacted* decolonial knowledge production. This is the Future Observatory model but for postcolonial theory.
2. **Institutional outsiders doing insider things** — La Colonie hosted academic knowledge (Samia Henni, the ETH Zurich research) while deliberately operating outside institutional channels. The friction was generative.
3. **Space as argument** — the location near Gare du Nord, the opening date, the financial model, the three-floor architecture — every design decision was also a theoretical position. This is what speculative/critical design looks like at the infrastructural scale.
4. **Decolonial thought as live practice** — La Colonie is a model for how theory gets enacted, not just discussed. The contrast between this and academic decolonial discourse is instructive.
5. **Financial precarity as design flaw** — the COVID closure reveals a design flaw worth studying: radical independence (no state funding) created vulnerability. The lesson is relevant to any community Amber might help build.
