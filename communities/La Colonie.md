# La Colonie

Decolonial cultural space founded by artist [[Kader Attia]] and restaurateur Zico Selloum in Paris (2016–2020). Located in a former textile factory in the 10th arrondissement near Gare du Nord — the location was deliberate, chosen for its historical association with Algerian migrant workers.

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-28:** Stub created from Kader Attia deep research.
- **2020:** Permanently closed due to COVID-19 pandemic (March 2020).
- **2016:** Opened October 17, 2016 — the 55th anniversary of the [Paris Massacre of 1961](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paris_massacre_of_1961).

## Mission & Objectives
Forum for workshops, lectures, readings, screenings, and art exhibitions with a focus on marginalized artists, thinkers, and activists whose work addressed colonialism, racism, and cultural appropriation. Functioned simultaneously as café/agora and intellectual space.

## Why Interesting
A model for what artist-founded decolonial cultural infrastructure looks like: interdisciplinary, community-centered, politically explicit, and financially precarious (dependent on bar/café revenue — hence the COVID closure). The intentionality of the location (near Gare du Nord, near Algerian migrant history) is a lesson in how space embodies argument.
