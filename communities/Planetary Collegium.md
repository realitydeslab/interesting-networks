# Planetary Collegium

The world's most important transdisciplinary research network for art, science, technology, and consciousness. Founded by **[[Roy Ascott]]** in 1994 as CAiiA (Centre for Advanced Inquiry in the Interactive Arts) at University of Wales, Newport. Home of the Technoetic Arts. Over 80 PhDs granted. The institutional ancestor of the entire art+technology+consciousness research field.

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Created stub during [[Eduardo Kac]] deep research.
- **2026-02-26:** Substantially expanded during [[Roy Ascott]] full deep research.

## Mission & Objectives
Explores consciousness and connectivity at the intersection of art, technology, and science through **transdisciplinary inquiry and critical discourse**, with special reference to **technoetic** research. Seeks to reflect social, technological, and spiritual aspirations of an emerging planetary society. Combines face-to-face association with trans-cultural telematic community. Produces new language, structures, behaviors, and insights into mind, matter, and human identity.

## For Amber
**🎓 Fellowship/PhD** — The model for what a transdisciplinary art-consciousness PhD program can be. Genealogically connected to every serious consciousness-technology artist researcher working today.
**🤝 Network** — Alumni work across bioart, interactive art, technoetics, somatics, net art. If you know the Planetary Collegium PhD network, you know who built the field.
**💡 Institutional Model** — CAiiA/Planetary Collegium shows how to create research infrastructure around a field that doesn't yet exist. The founding gesture: identify the edge, name it, institutionalize it.
**🌍 Shanghai** — The DeTao-Node Shanghai is Roy Ascott's current base. A direct line from the Collegium to the Chinese art-tech scene.

## Why Interesting
**Field Origin**: This is where the intellectual DNA for the entire art+technology+consciousness research field was encoded. Before there was "media art" as a PhD subject, there was CAiiA. Before there was "bioart," Eduardo Kac was writing his thesis here.

**Planetary Thinking**: The name says it. The Collegium operates across nations, cultures, and disciplines. The node structure (Plymouth hub, Trento, Lucerne, Shanghai) enacts the planetary consciousness idea it theorizes.

**Alumni Impact**: The PhD graduates went on to define new fields (bioart, transgenic art, metadesign, transhumanist art) rather than inhabit existing ones. The institution reproduces field-makers.
