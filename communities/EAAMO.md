# EAAMO — Equity and Access in Algorithms, Mechanisms, and Optimization

A global community of 3,000+ researchers, practitioners, and policy-makers from 150+ institutions in 50 countries, working at the intersection of algorithms, mechanism design, and social equity. Born from a 2016 reading group, now an ACM conference + year-round working groups infrastructure. More technical and economics-oriented than FAccT; the venue for CS theory × equity. Governed by a distributed directorship spanning six continents — a deliberately grassroots movement, not a top-down institution.

**Vibe:** Rigorous but socially committed. Bridges economists, CS theorists, policymakers, and community practitioners. Closer to EC (Economics and Computation) than to CHI or FAccT. Strong Global South orientation.

## Mission & Objectives

**Mission:** Place the perspectives and interests of marginalized groups at the foundation of algorithmic and resource allocation systems.

**Founded:** 2016 as Mechanism Design for Social Good (MD4SG) by [Rediet Abebe](https://www.redietabebe.com/) and [Kira Goldner](https://www.kiragoldner.com/). Grew to include [Irene Lo](https://sites.google.com/view/irene-lo) (Stanford) and [Ana-Andreea Stoica](https://sf.is.mpg.de/person/astoica) (MPI-SWS / Columbia). First workshop at Economics and Computation (EC) 2017. First ACM EAAMO conference 2021 (online). First in-person 2022 at George Mason University.

**Four goals:**
1. **Build Community** — EAAMO Bridges (semester-long biweekly working groups) to scaffold new research networks
2. **Bridge Research and Practice** — tutorials at FAccT, EC, WINE; deployment-focused research
3. **Prioritize Outreach** — Summer of Science (pairs indigenous Mexican students with EAAMO researchers); Harvard ReCompute collaboration
4. **Innovate Infrastructure** — Faculty Network for junior faculty; Doctoral Consortium for PhD students; ACM Proceedings for multidisciplinary work that doesn't fit elsewhere

**Funders:** [ACM SIGAI](https://sigai.acm.org/), [ACM SIGecom](https://www.sigecom.org/), [Alfred P. Sloan Foundation](https://sloan.org/), [MacArthur Foundation](https://www.macfound.org/), [Schmidt Futures](https://schmidtfutures.com/), [Institute for New Economic Thinking](https://www.ineteconomics.org/), [PIT Cases / Cornell Tech PiTech](https://tech.cornell.edu/programs/masters-programs/jacobs-technion-cornell-dual-ms/cornell-tech-campus/pitech/).

## Why Interesting

EAAMO is FAccT's more technically rigorous sibling. Where FAccT attracts HCI/law/ethics researchers, EAAMO attracts CS theorists and economists who want to prove equity results rather than just argue for them. For Amber, who operates in design+speculative+ethics space, EAAMO represents a technical depth worth knowing — especially as Sovereign Agents and machine behavior questions increasingly intersect with resource allocation, access, and governance.

The global orientation (50 countries, directors on six continents, Summer of Science in Mexico, San Luis Potosí hosting) makes it genuinely different from other computing conferences. It's trying to build a global grassroots movement, not just another US/European academic conference.

The Cesar Hidalgo connection — moral judgment of humans vs. machines — is the clearest intellectual bridge to Amber's work. EAAMO's colloquium is worth monitoring as a free lecture series.
