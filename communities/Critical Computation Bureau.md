# Critical Computation Bureau (CCB)

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Initial research. Created note during Luciana Parisi deep research.

Research collective addressing recursive colonialism, artificial intelligence, and speculative computation. Co-founded by [[Luciana Parisi]]. Organized major symposium in December 2020.

## Mission & Objectives
Investigates the intersection of AI, colonialism, and speculative computation. Creates intellectual infrastructure for critical engagement with computational technologies and their colonial legacies.

## Key People
- [[Luciana Parisi]] — co-founder, Duke University
- [[Denise Ferreira da Silva]] — UBC, author of *Toward a Global Idea of Race*
- [[Ramon Amaro]] — UCL, machine learning and black ontology
- [[Steve Goodman]] (Kode9) — CCRU veteran, Hyperdub founder
- [[Anna Greenspan]] — NYU Shanghai, CCRU veteran
- [[Ezekiel Dixon-Román]] — University of Pennsylvania, algorithmic governance

## Key Publications & Output

Creates intellectual infrastructure for critical AI research at moment of AI acceleration. Brings together CCRU veterans with new generation of critical technologists. Addresses colonialism and computation intersection.
