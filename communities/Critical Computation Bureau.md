# Critical Computation Bureau (CCB)

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Full deep research. Complete membership mapped, symposium schedule reconstructed session-by-session, manifesto analysed, intellectual framework documented. Corrected founding member names (Oana Pârvan, Brian D'Aquino — not Alexandra/Valentina as previously noted).

Research collective and symposium-organizing body addressing **recursive colonialism, artificial intelligence, and speculative computation**. Organized the *Recursive Colonialism, Artificial Intelligence and Speculative Computation* (RCAISC) symposium in December 2020. Globally situated across the US, UK, and Southern Italy, with networks spanning multiple continents. Produces theory at the intersection of critical race studies, philosophy of technology, and computational media.

---

## Mission & Objectives

From their self-description: "a collective of researchers, artists, and writers working at the intersection of technology and culture, computer science and information theory, aesthetics and politics... engaged in interventions in the technopolitics of racial capitalism and its recursive regeneration."

CCB borrows methods from: philosophy of technology, Black Studies, political theory, computer science and information theory, media aesthetics, cultural and digital media theories. Their mode: "Mixing soundz and screenings with concepts, functions and percepts" — theory + sonic + visual practice as a unified approach.

**Core intellectual project:** Demonstrate that colonialism operates recursively in computational systems — not as a past event but as a self-reproducing loop that regenerates through AI, algorithmic governance, and platform capitalism.

## The Concept: Recursive Colonialism

From the [CCB Manifesto](https://recursivecolonialism.com/manifest/):

**Recursivity as colonial logic:** In computation, recursion means an instruction set calls itself repeatedly (a function that invokes itself). CCB argues colonialism operates the same way — it does not end but loops back on itself, regenerating at each iteration through new forms: surveillance, borders, predictive policing, algorithmic governance.

**Key concepts defined in the manifesto:**

1. **Recursive algorithms → recursive epistemologies**: "Whenever one of the steps within a procedure calls back the procedure, cybernetic feedback control comes to channel the mundane tasks of communication as a Droste effect of knowledge. Recursivity is epistemology. It is the function that entangles cosmogonies within colonial epistemologies."

2. **Necroentropic epistemologies**: Drawing from Gregory Bateson (recursive epistemologies) and Sylvia Wynter (sociogenic principle), CCB argues that "recursive racializing epistemology responds to contingencies through negentropic/necroentropic feedbacks — an exponential extraction of blackness."

3. **Autopoiesis as conservatism**: Colonial knowledge systems are autopoietic (self-organizing, self-preserving across scales). "Autopoiesis is a motor that sets in motion the preservation of myths or cosmogonies across time and space."

4. **Allo-poiesis as resistance**: Against autopoiesis, CCB proposes *allo-poiesis* — making something other than oneself. "Epistemology must return to the allo-poetic condition of knowledge — the condition of the other/different/alien/dispossessed/improper knowledge-without-programming."

5. **Black Allo-Poethics**: Drawing from Denise Ferreira da Silva — "an expansion of the natural sciences starting with cosmo-computations of alien kinds." Not inclusion in existing systems but epistemological abolition. "Machine instrumentality clashes with machine poetics."

6. **Cosmo-computations**: Alternative computational cosmologies drawing from non-Western traditions, diasporic knowledge, afrofuturism. Against the universalism of Western technoscience.

7. **SF Capital** (after Mark Fisher): Science Fiction Capital — the predictive logic of AI toward futures that target "futurities of diasporic movements by designing zones of perpetual war."

**Key formulation**: "The abolition of the racial algorithm has a long history, a cosmogonic multiplicity of histories running on the other side of recursivity and contingency... On the externalities of the racial algorithms there is speculative computation."

## Key Contributions

1. **Conceptual synthesis**: Created the "recursive colonialism" framework linking computational theory (recursion, feedback loops, autopoiesis) to critical race studies — a genuinely new conceptual contribution.

2. **Intellectual infrastructure**: The December 2020 symposium convened a genuinely global network (Naples, London, Delhi, Vancouver, Shanghai, NYC, LA, Berlin) during COVID, proving virtual international theory-making at scale.

3. **Cross-tradition synthesis**: Brought together CCRU legacy (Parisi, Goodman, Greenspan) with Black Studies (Ferreira da Silva, Amaro, Achiampong), postcolonial theory (Chambers, Terranova, Pârvan), global south perspectives (Sundaram, Knoetze, Toya), and feminist political theory (Puar, Tazzioli).

4. **Sound + theory model**: Combined academic dialogues with DJ sets (Kode9, Shannen SP) and film screenings — refusing the academic/art divide.

5. **Glossary as research infrastructure**: The online glossary maps the intellectual field for CCB's project, creating a citeable, shareable map of the conceptual terrain.
