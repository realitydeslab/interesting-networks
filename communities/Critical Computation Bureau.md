# Critical Computation Bureau (CCB)

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Updated with full CCB membership (founding members vs. symposium participants). Corrected: da Silva is a key participant, not a founding member. Founding members confirmed: Parisi, Dixon-Román, Terranova, Pârvan, D'Aquino.
- **2026-02-26:** Initial research. Created note during Luciana Parisi deep research.

Research collective addressing recursive colonialism, artificial intelligence, and speculative computation. Co-founded by [[Luciana Parisi]]. Organized major symposium in December 2020.

## Mission & Objectives
Investigates the intersection of AI, colonialism, and speculative computation. Creates intellectual infrastructure for critical engagement with computational technologies and their colonial legacies.

## Key People
**Founding Members (CCB core):**
- [[Luciana Parisi]] — co-founder, Professor at Duke University (Program in Literature, Computational Media Art and Culture)
- [[Ezekiel Dixon-Román]] — co-founder, University of Pennsylvania (algorithmic governance, quantification in education)
- Tiziana Terranova — co-founder, University of Naples 'L'Orientale' (network culture, digital media)
- Oana Pârvan — co-founder (cultural studies, Romanian theorist, symposium coordinator)
- Brian D'Aquino — co-founder (researcher/DJ, Goldsmiths, online coordinator)
