# Gasworks

A leading South London non-profit arts organisation founded in 1994. Working at the intersection of UK and international contemporary art, Gasworks has supported over 600 artists from 80+ countries. Runs exhibitions, residencies (20/year), subsidised studios, and community participation projects. The London hub of the global [Triangle Network](https://www.trianglenetwork.org/), part of Arts Council England's National Portfolio, and a member of [Common Practice](http://www.commonpractice.org.uk/) and [Plus Tate](https://www.tate.org.uk/about-us/national-international-local/plus-tate). Registered charity (Triangle Arts Trust, Charity no. 326411, Company no. 1646688).

**Address:** 155 Vauxhall Street, London SE11 5RH | **Admission:** Free | **Hours:** Wed–Sun 12–6pm (during exhibitions only)

## Mission & Objectives

Gasworks commissions and produces exhibitions by emerging and underrepresented artists, and hosts residencies enabling artists from around the world to research and develop new work. It provides subsidised studios for London-based artists and co-designs participation projects for local children, young people and families together with community partners. Their ethos: **Gasworks is a home for artists** — committed to building long-term relationships with all artists they work with.

Residencies are **non-prescriptive and process-based** — artists can develop projects in response to their new environment or conduct research benefiting from London's resources. There is no mandate to produce a finished work. This research-first model is rare and makes it particularly attractive for artists at early career stages.

## Key Contributions

1. **600+ artist careers supported** across 80+ countries since 1994 — extraordinary track record for a small institution
2. **Decolonial model of residency** — no prescriptive output, process-based, research-friendly; challenges the "produce and perform" logic of most residencies
3. **Triangle Network hub** — Gasworks is the beating heart of a global alternative art infrastructure (not Tate, not Frieze — genuinely independent)
4. **Genuine community governance** — Participation Advisory Board gives real power to LGBTQ+ African and Latin American community groups in South London
5. **Artist development pathways** — studio → residency → exhibition sequence can launch careers
6. **Free admission** — genuinely accessible institution in a high-rent city
7. **The Otolith Group connection** — associated with Turner Prize-nominated experimental film/theory practice

## Why Interesting

Gasworks operates in the same territory as Amber's research but from the arts infrastructure side. Their residency model — **non-prescriptive, process-based, research-first** — is philosophically aligned with the kind of open-ended, non-instrumental inquiry that characterises Amber's best work (ALife, more-than-human, machine behavior).

The Triangle Network connection makes this not just a local institution but a gateway to a genuinely global alternative art circuit — one that bypasses the usual Frieze/Venice/Art Basel axis. For a researcher interested in non-Western futures, cosmotechnics, and alternative techno-social arrangements, this circuit is worth knowing.

The community governance model (Participation Advisory Board with real LGBTQ+ African and Latin American community voices) shows an institution trying to embody its politics — rare and worth engaging.

Finally: free admission, open studios, and a strong artist development track record make this an *accessible* node, not a gatekept one. Low friction to begin engaging.
