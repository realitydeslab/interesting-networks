# Haus der Kulturen der Welt

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-03-01:** Full deep research completed. Director confirmed as Bonaventure Soh Bejeng Ndikung (since Jan 2023). Updated mission language, key programmes, and connections.
- **2026-02-26:** Stub created during transmediale deep research. Key venue and institutional partner.

Germany's national center for the presentation and discussion of international contemporary arts, with a special focus on non-European cultures and societies. Located in Berlin's Tiergarten park in the architecturally striking former Kongresshalle (1957, architect Hugh Stubbins), nicknamed **Die schwangere Auster** ("The Pregnant Oyster") by Berliners. A federally funded "lighthouse of culture," one of Germany's most important publicly funded contemporary arts institutions. Has hosted [[transmediale]] since 2008.

## Mission & Objectives

HKW is guided by "strategies of how to live and better inhabit this world together." Under Ndikung's leadership since 2023, the mission has been reformulated around several key axes:

- **Pluralism of worlds**: HKW proposes that "'Welt' (world) is not a noun, but a verb: to unworld, to world, and to reworld." This ontological framing resonates directly with posthuman and more-than-human theory.
- **Migrant-situated knowledges**: Programme is anchored around Berlin's 170-nation diaspora community as co-makers, not subjects.
- **More-than-human ethics**: The About statement explicitly includes "respect for living and non-living beings" and acknowledges "animal, plant, fungi, single and multicellular species, as well as non-living species that, too, are entitled to their spaces." This is directly aligned with posthuman philosophy.
- **Emancipatory maxim**: "Tout Moun Se Moun" (from the Haitian Revolution, 1791–1804) — every person is a human being.
- **Anti-hate stance**: Named exclusions include ableism, ageism, antisemitism, homophobia, Islamophobia, racism, sexism, transphobia, xenophobia.

This is notably more philosophically articulated than the previous (Scherer) era's mission language.

## Key People

### Current Director
- **[Bonaventure Soh Bejeng Ndikung](https://www.hkw.de/)** (Director since Jan 2023) — Cameroonian-born curator and writer, born 1977 in Yaoundé. Holds a doctorate in **medical biotechnology** (Heinrich-Heine-Universität Düsseldorf / TU Berlin) and a postdoctorate in **biophysics** (University of Montpellier). Founder and director of [SAVVY Contemporary](https://savvy-contemporary.com/) (Berlin independent project space). Guest professor at Städelschule Frankfurt (2017-2019). Professor for Spatial Strategies MA at Weißensee Academy of Art Berlin. Part of curatorial team for **documenta 14** (Adam Szymczyk, 2017). Curated Finnish pavilion, 58th Venice Biennale (2019). Chief curator of **2025 São Paulo Art Biennial**. This background (science + decolonial art + biopolitics) is unusual and deeply interesting.

## Key Contributions

- **The Anthropocene Project** (2013–2014, extended) — landmark interdisciplinary programme with philosophers, scientists, artists interrogating the Anthropocene concept. Involved Bruno Latour, Hans Joachim Schellnhuber, and many others. Set the template for how art-science institutions tackle planetary crisis. Preceded and influenced much subsequent critical work in this area.
- **Home of transmediale** (since 2008) — primary venue for Europe's most important critical digital culture festival. [[transmediale]]
- **Forecast mentorship programme** — international mentorship programme for emerging artists/thinkers, run from HKW.
- **Berlin Documentary Forum** — biennial; documentary as critical practice.
- **heimaten** (2024–2027) — 4-year programme on plural democracy, migration, and German identity through music, film, literature. Co-curated with Max Czollek and Ibou Diop.
- **Afrodiaspora – Composing While Black** (2025/26) — partnership with Deutsches Symphonie-Orchester Berlin; four centuries of Black composers across three continents. Co-curated with George E. Lewis (Columbia).
- **Tirailleurs: Trials and Tribulations** (exhibition, 21 Mar – 14 Jun 2026) — over 30 international artists, 14 new commissions; about African soldiers who liberated Europe from Nazism. Research partnerships with [Raw Material Company](https://rawmaterialcompany.org/) (Dakar), [Alice Yard](https://aliceyard.blogspot.com/) (Port of Spain), Cinémathèque de Tanger, and others.

## Why Interesting

HKW is the most philosophically ambitious mainstream cultural institution in Germany. Under Bernd Scherer (pre-2023), it hosted the landmark Anthropocene Project and established a rigorous practice of art-science dialogue. Under Bonaventure Soh Bejeng Ndikung (from 2023), this ambition has been reoriented through a decolonial, pluriverse lens — now explicitly naming "non-living beings" in its mission, invoking the Haitian Revolution, and centering migrant epistemologies.

For Amber's research interests, HKW sits at the intersection of:
- **More-than-human**: Institutional mission now explicitly names non-living beings
- **Media archaeology / critical computing**: transmediale partnership keeps this alive
- **Posthuman**: Anthropocene Project legacy; ongoing engagement with planetary scale
- **Cosmotechnics**: HKW's "plurality of worlds" framing resonates with Yuk Hui's multiplicity of technics

The Ndikung appointment is worth watching: a biotech scientist turned decolonial curator running Germany's federal arts institution is unusual. His 2025 São Paulo Biennial chief curatorship means HKW's conceptual framework is being exported to the global biennial circuit.
