# Google Artists + Machine Intelligence (AMI)

Google's flagship program embedding artists inside AI research — not as PR window-dressing, but as genuine co-investigators working alongside engineers and researchers to realize projects with machine intelligence. Founded by [[Blaise Agüera y Arcas]] in 2016, AMI has run residencies, artist grants, and faculty research awards, producing some of the defining works of the AI art canon. Program appears to have gone quiet after 2021; last Medium activity was December 2021.

## Events & Programmes

### Music, Art & Machine Intelligence (MAMI) 2016
Joint AMI + [[Google Magenta]] conference, June 1 2016, San Francisco (~80 attendees, 29 presenters). The foundational gathering of the AI art community before the term "AI art" existed as mainstream discourse. Speakers included:
- Blaise Agüera y Arcas, Douglas Eck, Samy Bengio, Martin Wattenberg, Carolina Pantofaru (all Google)
- [[Ian Cheng]] — real-time simulation art (ALife-adjacent)
- Rebecca Fiebrink — Wekinator interactive ML tool
- Hod Lipson (Columbia) — robotic painting
- Gil Weinberg (Georgia Tech) — prosthetic musical arm
- Jason Yosinski, Emily Denton (NYU) — neural net visualization and GANs
- Aaron Courville (Univ. Montreal) — VAEs and GANs
- Timothy Morton (Rice University) — philosophy/ecology
- Memo Akten, Ross Goodwin, Sheldon Brown — AMI residents

### AMI Residencies (2016–2018)
Embedded artists in Google working directly with engineers and ML researchers. Public exhibitions at [[Gray Area]], San Francisco (first major AMI public show, 2016).

### Artist Grants (2019–2020)
Six artists, each receiving a grant + Google creative technologist collaboration, culminating in artworks shown on Google Arts & Culture and at institutions including Philadelphia Museum of Art and Fondazione Sandretto Re Rebaudengo.

### Research Awards (2020–2021)
Annual open call for faculty proposals, $20K unrestricted + Google researcher partnership. Last cycle announced December 2021. No 2022 posts on Medium — program may be on hiatus or wound down.

## Status & Assessment (2026)
AMI's Medium blog went silent after December 2021. The program was never officially discontinued but appears absorbed/superseded by Google's broader generative AI pivot (Imagen, Gemini, etc.). The original model — artists in genuine research residency — hasn't been formally revived as of early 2026. Twitter (@artwithMI) still active but infrequent.

**What made AMI distinctive:** Unlike most corporate "art + tech" programs that produce PR demos, AMI gave artists actual access to cutting-edge ML research infrastructure and direct engineer collaboration. The MAMI 2016 conference predated the AI art mainstream by 3+ years.

## Why Interesting

**Historical significance:** AMI is arguably the founding institutional node of the AI art field — the place where the first generation of AI artists (Memo Akten, Anna Ridler, Allison Parrish, Martine Syms, Mario Klingemann) got serious ML research access and produced landmark works.

**Network value:** AMI alumni form the core of the global AI art circuit. Understanding AMI = understanding the genealogy of every major AI art figure active today.

**Model worth studying:** The residency model (artists + engineers, genuine collaboration, not PR) is rare and replicable. Amber's work on more-than-human design and agent behavior would fit the AMI model perfectly.

**Kenric Allado-McDowell** specifically: The program's literary/philosophical wing (Pharmako-AI, Atlas of Anomalous AI) points toward exactly the kind of speculative AI inquiry that intersects with Amber's machine behavior and consciousness interests.

**[[Anab Jain]]** connection: AMI funded Design Investigations Vienna's collaborative world-building with AI work — directly adjacent to Amber's speculative design interests.

**[[Ian Cheng]]** connection: His MAMI 2016 appearance with real-time ALife simulations is the clearest historical precedent for Amber's agent ethology / machine behavior work.
