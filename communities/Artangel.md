# Artangel

London-based independent producer and commissioner of site-specific, durational, and ambitious art works that wouldn't be produced by a conventional gallery or museum. Founded 1985. Co-directed by James Lingwood and Michael Morris. One of the most significant commissioning organizations in contemporary art: Artangel doesn't curate shows — it enables works that exist outside the gallery system, often in unexpected locations across London and beyond.

## Key People
- **James Lingwood** — co-director (with Michael Morris). A central figure in the London contemporary art scene for 40 years. Deep working relationship with [[Francis Alÿs]].
- **Michael Morris** — co-director.
- **Richard Harbison** — writer; wrote the text *Incompleteness* for the *Seven Walks* catalogue.
- **Orlando Gough** — composer; collaborated with [[Francis Alÿs]] on *Seven Walks* sound dimension.

## Key Commissioned Works
- **[Seven Walks](https://www.artangel.org.uk/project/seven-walks/)** — [[Francis Alÿs]], 2005. Walks across London: *Guards*, *Nightwatch*, *Railings*, and a city-wide rumour. An architecture of urban ritual and sound.
- **The Waste Land** — T.S. Eliot read by Fiona Shaw (1995)
- **The Unconditional** — Richard Long (1991)
- **Nacht und Nebel** — K Foundation Burn a Million Quid (1994)
- **The Cremaster Cycle** (partial) — Matthew Barney
- **Massaker** — Walid Raad / Atlas Group
- **Every Good Boy Deserves Favour** — Tom Stoppard / André Previn
- **Prison Breaking** — Jeremy Deller
- Regular commissions with major artists including Janet Cardiff, Francis Alÿs, Roger Hiorns, Phyllida Barlow, and others.

## The Artangel Collection
Maintains a collection of moving image works available for loan to institutions: includes *Nightwatch*, *Guards*, and *Railings* from Alÿs's *Seven Walks*. Wall-based works from *Seven Walks* are in the Tate Collection.

## Model
Artangel's model is distinctive: they work with artists on a single ambitious project, providing full production support. Projects may take years to develop. The organization operates outside the gallery and museum system, occupying the gap between public art and institutional exhibition. Their commissioning model supports work that is too large, too site-specific, or too durational for conventional venues. Results are often temporarily public, then documented and collected.

## Why Interesting
Artangel is one of the most important answers to the question: "what kind of institution enables ambitious, non-commercial art?" Their track record over 40 years — commissioning works that have become canonical while remaining outside the gallery market — is a model worth studying for any artist or researcher thinking about institutional design. The working relationship with [[Francis Alÿs]] (*Seven Walks*, 2005; the London rumour project) is a model of how a commissioning body and an artist can co-create within a city's existing fabric.
