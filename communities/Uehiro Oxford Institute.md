The Uehiro Oxford Institute is one of the world's premier centres for practical ethics, housed within Oxford's Philosophy Faculty. It addresses the most pressing ethical questions of the day — from AI and digital ethics to disorders of consciousness, from end-of-life care to synthetic biology. The Institute holds **The Parfit Library** (named after Derek Parfit, the greatest moral philosopher of his generation who was based at Oxford).

**For Amber:** Research topics directly relevant include AI and Digital Ethics, Disorders of Consciousness (machine consciousness angle), Enhancement, and the Philosophical Moral Psychology Lab. The Parfit connection is significant — Parfit's work on personal identity and what matters in survival has deep implications for questions about machine personhood and moral status.

## Mission & Objectives

The Institute brings philosophical expertise to bear on practical ethical questions. Key research areas:
- **AI and Digital Ethics** — central research theme
- **Disorders of Consciousness** — directly relevant to machine consciousness questions
- **Enhancement** (human enhancement, cognitive enhancement)
- **Neuroethics**
- **End-of-Life Care** and euthanasia
- **Ethics of Behavioral Influence and Prediction**
- **Epistemic Autonomy** — relevant to agent autonomy research
- **Well-being in the Digital World**
- **Predictive Technologies, Ethics, and the Future of Insurance**
- **Counterfactual Assessment and Valuation for Awareness Architecture (CAVAA)**
- **The Philosophical Moral Psychology Lab**

The Institute also runs public philosophy programmes:
- **DARCA** — philosophical engagement
- **Practical Ethics and Responsibility Competition (PERC)** — schools outreach
- **Uehiro Oxford Prize in Practical Ethics**
- **Bitesize Ethics Programme** — public engagement
- **MSt in Practical Ethics** — graduate teaching
