# Long Now Foundation

San Francisco-based nonprofit (founded 1996) dedicated to fostering long-term thinking and responsibility in the context of the next 10,000 years. Counteracts what they call "faster/cheaper" short-termism ("now now") with the perspective of "the long now." 

**Founders / Board**: [[Brian Eno]] (coined the phrase "the long now"), Danny Hillis (inventor, conceived the Clock), Stewart Brand (*Whole Earth Catalog*), Kevin Kelly (*Wired* co-founder), Doug Carlston, Peter Schwartz, Paul Saffo.

## Key Projects
- **[10,000 Year Clock](https://longnow.org/clock/)** — mechanical clock being built inside a mountain in the Nevada desert, funded largely by Jeff Bezos ($42M). Designed to tick once a year, chime once a century. Symbol of civilizational timescale thinking.
- **[Rosetta Project](https://rosettaproject.org/)** — archive of documentation for all human languages; a linguistic time capsule.
- **[SALT Seminars](https://longnow.org/seminars/)** (Seminars About Long-term Thinking) — ongoing public lecture series. Inaugural lecture by Brian Eno (2003): "The Big Here and Long Now."
- **[The Interval](https://theinterval.org/)** — their bar/cafe/library in Fort Mason, San Francisco. A physical gathering space for the long-now community.
- **[Revive & Restore](https://reviverestore.org/)** — affiliated project on de-extinction.
- **[Manual for Civilization](https://longnow.org/manual/)** — curated library of ~3,500 books to reboot civilization.

## Why Interesting
The Long Now Foundation is a rare institution that thinks at civilizational timescale — ecology, extinction, cultural longevity. Their connection to both Silicon Valley (Bezos, Hillis) and the counterculture/ecology tradition (Brand, Kelly) makes them an unusual bridge. Their SALT seminars are a quality intellectual public event series. For Amber's interests: the "long now" framing is relevant to ecological thinking, more-than-human design, and the critique of short-termism in tech culture.
