# Long Now Foundation

San Francisco-based nonprofit (founded 01996) dedicated to fostering long-term thinking and responsibility "within the context of the next and last 10,000 years." Counteracts what they call "faster/cheaper" short-termism with the perspective of "the long now." Founded by Stewart Brand, Danny Hillis, and Brian Eno. Produces audacious projects (the 10,000-year Clock, Rosetta Project, Revive & Restore), runs a public lecture series (Long Now Talks, 400+ speakers since 2003), publishes a print journal (Pace Layers), and hosts a bar/cafe/library (The Interval) in Fort Mason, San Francisco. Their Council includes [[Benjamin Bratton]], [[Sara Imari Walker]], K Allado-McDowell, and Katie Paterson — making them a serious intellectual hub, not just a futurist salon.

## Mission & Objectives

> "Civilizations with longer nows look after things better." — Brian Eno

Long Now's core thesis: modern civilization suffers from pathological short-termism — driven by tech acceleration, market short-horizons, election cycles, personal distraction. They offer mechanism and myth for the long view.

Key concepts they've pioneered:
- **"The Long Now"** — 10,000-year timeframe centered on the present, coined by Brian Eno
- **"The Big Here"** — "here" means the whole Earth, not just your city/country
- **Pace Layers framework** (Stewart Brand) — civilizations have layers moving at different speeds: Fashion → Commerce → Infrastructure → Governance → Culture → Nature. Fast layers get attention; slow layers have all the power.
- **"Digital Dark Age"** — digital storage is easy; digital preservation over centuries is not. Major concern about losing civilization's accumulated knowledge.
- **"Good ancestors"** — their ethical imperative: "Everyone in this world would understand themselves as someone else's ancestor."

## Key Projects

### [10,000-Year Clock](https://longnow.org/clock/)
An immense mechanical monument being built inside a mountain in West Texas (privately funded by Jeff Bezos, ~$42M). Hundreds of feet tall. Engineered to require minimal maintenance, powered by sunlight. Ticks once a year, chimes once a century. The chime generator (designed with Brian Eno's help) can produce 3.5 million unique sequences — one for each day of 10,000 years. You have to hike to it, like a pilgrimage.

**Why it matters:** Not just a symbol. The Clock is a thought experiment made physical. As Kevin Kelly writes: "If a Clock can keep going for ten millennia, shouldn't we make sure our civilization does as well?" Forces thinking at civilizational timescale. Connects to deep time, engineering humility, intergenerational responsibility.

**For Amber:** The Clock is a form of "open-endedness" manifested as infrastructure. Hillis's insight that "the real problem is people" (if something becomes unimportant, it gets scrapped; if too important, it gets destroyed) — this is essentially a theory of machine survival and civilizational memory. Resonates with Amber's research on machine death, mortality, and open-endedness.

### [Rosetta Project](https://rosettaproject.org/)
Global collaboration of language specialists and native speakers building a publicly accessible digital library of human languages. Produced the **Rosetta Disk** — a nickel disc the size of a hand, microscopically etched with 13,000+ pages of documentation in 1,500+ human languages, readable at 650X magnification. A linguistic time capsule. "Building an archive of ALL documented human languages."

**Why it matters:** Directly confronts the Digital Dark Age. Analog, non-digital preservation of language data that could survive millennia without electricity. Also model for preserving diversity of thought.

### [Revive & Restore](https://reviverestore.org/)
Genetic rescue for endangered and extinct species. Founded by **Ryan Phelan** and Stewart Brand. Projects include:
- Black-footed ferret (strategic cloning, cells from 1988)
- Woolly mammoth (with Colossal Biosciences)
- Przewalski's horse (successful cloning 2023)
- Passenger pigeon de-extinction
- Advanced Coral Toolkit (gene editing for coral resilience)
- Wild Genomes (genomic sequencing for conservation)
- Stem Cell Technologies for wildlife

**For Amber:** This is ALife with actual biological stakes. De-extinction using CRISPR and synthetic biology = artificial life entering the evolutionary record. Raises profound questions about what "natural" means, agency of extinct organisms, machine-biological hybrids. Ryan Phelan is worth a note.

### [Nevada Bristlecone Preserve](https://longnow.org/bristlecone/)
Long Now's land stewardship project protecting ancient bristlecone pines — some of the oldest living organisms on Earth (up to 5,000 years old). A physical site for long-term thinking.

### Manual for Civilization
A curated library of ~3,500 books that could reboot civilization. Discontinued as separate programme; lives on as a community-curated list.
