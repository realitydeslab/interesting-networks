# London Film-Makers' Co-operative (LFMC)

The foundational institution of British avant-garde and structural-materialist film. Founded 1966 by Stephen Dwoskin and Malcolm Le Grice, inspired by Jonas Mekas's Film-Makers' Cooperative in New York. Operated on a worksharing model: production facilities, distribution, and exhibition all integrated. Hosted the most politically and formally radical British film practice from the mid-1960s through the 1990s. Successor organisation: [LUX](https://lux.org.uk/).

## Mission & Objectives
A non-hierarchical cooperative for experimental filmmakers to make, show, and distribute their own work. Provided access to 16mm production facilities at a time when film equipment was otherwise inaccessible to artists. Screened films that had no other venue. Distributed work internationally through film hire.

## Key People
- **Malcolm Le Grice** — Co-founder. Structural/Materialist filmmaker and theorist. Made *Berlin Horse* (1970).
- **Stephen Dwoskin** — Co-founder. American filmmaker living in London.
- **Peter Gidal** — Theorist of Structural/Materialist film. Author of *Materialist Film* (Routledge). Edited the key anthology.
- **[[Lis Rhodes]]** — Cinema Curator 1975–76. Co-founder of Circles.
- **Annabel Nicolson** — Made film performance works (e.g. *Reel Time*, 1973). Co-founded Circles.
- **Sally Potter** — Early films made at LFMC in the early 1970s.
- **William Raban** — Structural filmmaker and later video artist.
- **Roger Hammond** — Key member.
- **Gill Eatherley** — Filmmaker, member.

## Key Publications & Output
- *Perspectives on British Avant-Garde Film* — catalogue for Arts Council touring programme
- *Film as Film* — Arts Council exhibition catalogue (1979), which included "Women and the Formal Film" statement by feminist LFMC members
- Films distributed: now through [LUX](https://lux.org.uk/)

## Key Contributions
- The British structural-materialist film tradition: treating film as physical substrate rather than transparent window
- Training ground for a generation of British experimental filmmakers
- Feminist schism of 1979 → founding of [[Circles]]: when women artists withdrew from the Arts Council *Film as Film* exhibition, they founded the first feminist film distribution network in the UK

## Connected Institutions
- **[[LUX]]** — successor organisation (merges of LFMC distribution arm + London Video Arts)
- **[[Circles]] / [[Cinenova]]** — feminist split-off, founded 1979
- **[[Four Corners Film Workshop]]** — allied East London radical collective

## Why Interesting
The LFMC is the generative institution of British experimental film. Understanding it means understanding where Rhodes, Potter, Le Grice, Nicolson all came from. It's also a model of artist-run infrastructure that succeeded for 30+ years. The feminist schism of 1979 is one of the most productive institutional ruptures in British art history.
