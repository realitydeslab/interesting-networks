Independent research institute founded by [[Timnit Gebru]] in December 2021, after being fired from Google for co-authoring the Stochastic Parrots paper. Operates across 7+ time zones, 18+ countries, 20+ languages. Community-funded (not industry), explicitly rejecting both corporate and traditional academic constraints. Represents the clearest model globally for **independent critical AI research** that couples accountability with imagination.

## Mission & Objectives
"We cut through the AI hype. We ground our research in community. We imagine alternative futures."

DAIR operates on four research pillars:
1. **[Data for Change](https://www.dair-institute.org/categories/data-for-change/)** — Quantitative and qualitative methodologies equipping historically marginalized groups with data to drive change. (4 active projects)
2. **[The Real Harms of AI Systems](https://www.dair-institute.org/categories/the-real-harms-of-ai-systems/)** — Cutting through AI hype to expose real harms. (6 active projects)
3. **[Alternative Tech Futures](https://www.dair-institute.org/categories/alternative-tech-futures/)** — Envisioning and creating new technological futures centered on care, safety, and possibility. (4 active projects)
4. **[Governance Frameworks for AI Systems](https://www.dair-institute.org/categories/governance-frameworks-for-ai-systems/)** — Guidelines and frameworks for building and deploying AI systems without theft and exploitation. (3 active projects)

The organization explicitly rejects academic burnout and supports researchers having full lives outside work — a structural commitment rare in AI research. It is community-funded, not beholden to tech industry, government, or traditional academic power structures.

## Key Projects & Publications

### The Real Harms of AI Systems
- **[Mystery AI Hype Theater 3000](https://www.dair-institute.org/projects/mystery-ai-hype-theater-3000/)** — Podcast/livestream by Emily Bender + Alex Hanna. 250K+ downloads, 170 countries. Twice-monthly on Twitch (Mondays). Newsletter on [Buttondown](https://buttondown.com/maiht3k). Also on [Buzzsprout](https://www.buzzsprout.com/2126417). Running since 2022.
- **[Worker Surveillance and Wage Theft / Driven Down](https://www.dair-institute.org/projects/driven-down/)** (2024) — Report on Amazon's use of surveillance technology to steal wages, intensify working conditions, evade responsibility. Based on in-depth interviews and direct organizing experience.
- **[AI-Fueled Inequities](https://www.dair-institute.org/projects/ai-fueled-inequities/)** — Research on exploited data workers, refugee/gig worker conscription into AI training, surveillance.
- **[Social media content moderation failures in Ethiopia/Eritrea genocide](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3706598.3714010)** — ACM CHI 2025 paper, 3 years in the making. Led by Meron Estefanos.

### Alternative Tech Futures
- **[Possible Futures Blog Series](https://www.dair-institute.org/projects/possible-futures-blog-series/)** — Speculative near-future writing. Companion to zine workshops. De-centers dominant narratives about technology.
- **[Possible Futures Workshop Series](https://www.dair-institute.org/projects/possible-futures-workshops/)** — Online and in-person workshops producing zines (erasure poetry, collages, mixed media). Online Twitch events + in-person (Seattle, Sep 2025). [Zine library](https://zines.dair-institute.org/).
- **[Sovereign Language Technologies](https://www.dair-institute.org/projects/sovereign-language-technologies/)** — Community-centered design for East African language NLP, enabling indigenous communities to create online knowledge repositories. Collaborates with [Whose Knowledge?](https://whoseknowledge.org/).

### Governance & Labor

- **[The AI Con](https://thecon.ai/)** (Emily Bender & Alex Hanna, May 2025) — Book: "How to Fight Big Tech's Hype and Create the Future We Want." Sharp take-down of AI hype across political economy, philosophy, labor. Published MIT Press area.

## Why Interesting
DAIR is the organizational embodiment of Amber's "Critical Computing" thread — computing as political act, structural critique over ethics checklists. Three things make it exceptional:

1. **The founding story** — Born from Google's suppression of the Stochastic Parrots paper. DAIR exists because corporate AI suppressed critical AI research. That origin embeds a permanent structural critique.

2. **The "Possible Futures" track** — This is where DAIR touches Amber's speculative design interest. They treat imagination as a research methodology (zines, erasure poetry, speculative blog pieces). It's the only major AI ethics org doing this.

3. **The worker-centered empiricism** — Research is genuinely embedded in Kenya, Ethiopia, Venezuela. It's participatory, not extractive. This maps to Amber's "Participatory & Democratic Design" thread.

DAIR and AI Now are the two poles of independent critical AI. DAIR is more community-rooted, globally distributed, and imagination-forward. AI Now is more policy-focused. Together they define the space Amber should know.
