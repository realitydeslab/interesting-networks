# Les Ateliers de la Pensée de Dakar

The premier intellectual forum for African critical theory. Biennial gathering in Dakar, Senegal, convening African and diasporic intellectuals to think African futures without Europe as the center. Co-founded by [[Achille Mbembe]] and [[Felwine Sarr]].

## Mission
To create a space for African thinkers — on the continent and in the diaspora — to engage in open, creative, rigorous philosophical exchange about the African condition and its global implications. Explicitly anti-colonial in its framing: the thinking happens from Africa, not about Africa from elsewhere.

## Key People
- **[[Achille Mbembe]]** — co-founder
- **[[Felwine Sarr]]** — co-founder

## Why Interesting
This is where African critical theory is made. The concepts that circulate in global critical theory — necropolitics, Afropolitanism, decolonial futures — are tested and developed here first. Any scholar working on African AI ethics, decolonial computing, or Global South perspectives should track this forum.
