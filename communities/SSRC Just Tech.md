# SSRC Just Tech

Fellowship and publishing platform at the Social Science Research Council (SSRC) focused on the intersection of technology and society. Funds independent researchers studying how tech systems affect justice, labor, surveillance, and power.

## Key People
- **[[Adrienne Williams]]** — Just Tech Fellow. Worker surveillance and AI data labor.
- **[[Milagros Miceli]]** — Fellow. Data annotation labor, AI supply chains.
- Other fellows working on: AI in Asia (Kriangsak Teerakowitkajorn), platform labor, surveillance tech, AI governance.

## Topics
- AI labor exploitation and data work
- Worker surveillance and wage theft
- AI governance and regulation
- Surveillance technology
- Platforms and infrastructure
- Race, AI, and criminal justice

## Events & Programmes
- **Just Tech Fellowship** — Annual cohort of independent researchers. Fellows conduct research, publish through Just Tech platform, disseminate to policymakers and advocates.
- **Just Tech Newsletter** — Monthly updates from the community, upcoming events, opportunities. <https://just-tech.ssrc.org>
- **Just Tech Articles** — Open-access essays, interviews, and reports. <https://just-tech.ssrc.org/articles/>

## Why Interesting
Just Tech is one of the few fellowship programs explicitly designed to bridge academic AI ethics research and policy/advocacy change. Fellows are expected to disseminate to non-academic audiences. Connects researchers working on AI labor (Williams, Miceli) with a policy-adjacent platform. The SSRC imprimatur adds legitimacy without the constraints of a single university. Worth tracking for fellowship opportunities (if Amber ever wants to fund independent critical computing research).
