# UCCA Center for Contemporary Art (UCCA) 尤伦斯当代艺术中心

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Stub created during Chinese Art-Tech Scene deep research.

China's leading independent contemporary art institution. Located at the heart of the **798 Art Zone** in Beijing. Founded 2007 by Belgian collectors Guy and Myriam Ullens; now Chinese/international patron-owned. More than 1 million visitors per year. Ranked 55th most visited art museum worldwide (2020).

## Background
- Opening exhibition: "'85 New Wave: Birth of Chinese Contemporary Art" (2007, curated by Fei Dawei) — first institutional presentation in PRC of the Chinese avant-garde
- Restructured 2017: separated non-profit (UCCA Foundation) and commercial operations (UCCA Enterprises)
- Expanded to UCCA Dune (seaside, Beidaihe, 2018; designed by OPEN Architecture) and UCCA Edge (Shanghai, 2021; designed by SO-IL)

## Director
**Philip Tinari** — American-born art historian, based in Beijing since early 2000s. Co-curated Guggenheim's "Art and China after 1989: Theater of the World" (2017, with Alexandra Munroe and Hou Hanru) — most comprehensive institutional survey of Chinese art in US.

## Key Exhibitions (selected, art-tech relevant)
- Solo: Cao Fei, Qiu Zhijie, Xu Bing, Wang Jianwei, Zhang Huan, Lu Yang, Birdhead
- International: Olafur Eliasson, Tino Sehgal, Tatsuo Miyajima, Korakrit Arunanondchai
- Major surveys: "On|Off: China's Young Artists" (2013), "The New Normal" (2017)
