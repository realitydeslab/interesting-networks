# Chronus Art Center (CAC) 新时线媒体艺术中心

## ⚡ Recent Updates
- **2026-02-26:** Stub created during Chinese Art-Tech Scene deep research.

China's first nonprofit art organization dedicated to the presentation, research, creation, and scholarship of **media art**. Located at The Point, 14th floor, East Building, No. 102 Qinjiang Road, Xuhui District, Shanghai.

## Background
Founded 2013 by entrepreneur **Dillion Zhang**, independent curator **Li Zhenhua**, and artist **Hu Jieming**. Under the artistic direction of **Zhang Ga** since 2015 — a professor of media art and curator with international network spanning ZKM, Ars Electronica, and Leonardo/ISAST.

## Programs
- Exhibitions (international and Chinese media artists)
- Residency-oriented fellowships
- Lectures and workshops
- Archiving and publishing initiatives
- **Leonardo Art, Science and Technology Lecture Series** — in collaboration with Leonardo/ISAST and CAFA (Central Academy of Fine Arts, Beijing)

## Key Exhibitions (selected)
- *Extra Time* with Raqs Media Collective
- Jeffrey Shaw & Hu Jieming Duo Solo
- *Psychoprosa* — Thomas Feuerstein (Austria)
- *Haptic Field* — Chris Salter (USA)
- *Closed Circuit – Open Duration* — Terike Haapoja (Finland)
- *Folklore of the Cyber World* — parallel project to Chinese Pavilion, Venice Biennale 2015
- *Three Rooms* — touring, young media artists from China, South Korea, Germany
- *unREAL: The Algorithmic Present* — co-presented with HeK Basel
- *Datumsoria: The Return of the Real* — co-presented with ZKM Karlsruhe

## Key People

## For Amber
**The gateway institution** for media art in Shanghai. If Amber is doing media art work that connects to the international media art discourse (ZKM, Leonardo, HeK), CAC is the key Chinese partner. Residency program could be very relevant. Zhang Ga knows the international scene well.

## Why Interesting
Only nonprofit in mainland China dedicated exclusively to media art. Has built genuine international partnerships (ZKM, HeK) that connect Chinese media art to global discourse rather than treating it as a separate national tradition.
