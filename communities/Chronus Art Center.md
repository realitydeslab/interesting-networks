# Chronus Art Center (CAC) 新时线媒体艺术中心

**China's first and only nonprofit dedicated exclusively to media art.** The indispensable bridge between Chinese media art and the global ZKM/Ars Electronica/Leonardo/ISAST circuit. Now in its second decade, CAC is pushing beyond exhibitions into publishing (CAC Books), deep residency research (R&C @CAC Project), and international touring — while maintaining a coherent posthumanist intellectual framework.

## Mission & Objectives

Founded 2013, Chronus Art Center (CAC) is China's first nonprofit organization dedicated to the presentation, **research/creation**, and scholarship of **media art**. CAC creates a multifaceted platform for discourse, production, and dissemination of media art in a global context — bridging China to the international art-technology circuit.

CAC's intellectual framework, developed under Artistic Director **Zhang Ga**, spans six interlocking research threads (articulated in their 2023 10th Anniversary Archive Exhibition):

1. **Machines, robotics, machinic assemblages** — machine aesthetics, bodies and machines, cybernetics, algorithms, machine autonomy
2. **Moving images, VR/AR/XR, hybrid reality** — immersion, embodiment, simulation, existential technologies
3. **Code, data, net.art, internet, blockchain** — critical computation, digital infrastructure, network theory, open source
4. **Bioart, biopolitics, biological processes** — life science × art, kinship, bioengineering, microbiopolitics
5. **AI, algorithms, agentiality, emergence** — symbiosis, biomedia, machinic intelligence, AI posthumanism
6. **Umwelt, energy, ecology, Anthropocene** — more-than-human, New Materialism, cosmopolitics, planetary thinking

Overarching orientation: **posthumanism, new materialism, entanglement ontology**. CAC is theoretically literate — exhibitions are grounded in Thacker, Simondon, Hayles, Stengers, Haraway, Barad, Deleuze.

## Key Contributions

1. **Establishing media art as a serious institutional practice in China** — CAC is the only nonprofit of its kind on the mainland. For a decade it has created infrastructure (exhibitions, education, publications, fellowships) that didn't exist in China.
2. **Connecting Chinese media art to the global circuit** — CAC is China's partner in the ZKM/HeK/Leonardo/Rhizome/Art Center Nabi network. Without CAC, Chinese media art would be far more isolated.
3. **Theoretically rigorous programming** — exhibitions are grounded in posthumanism, new materialism, and contemporary media theory, not just "tech art spectacle." Thacker, Simondon, Haraway, Barad are invoked with meaning.
4. **We=Link platform** — a COVID-era online exhibition platform that became lasting infrastructure; demonstrated how media art institutions can operate online-natively.
5. **R&C @CAC Project** (2024–) — a residency-to-exhibition model with a clear research framework (Bio-Geo-Symbio). Each cohort has a real thesis, not just residency outputs.
6. **CAC Books** — the first significant bilingual (Chinese-English) media art publishing platform from mainland China.
7. **The A&T Programme** — bridging mainstream Chinese contemporary art (Yan Lei, Yin Xiuzhen, Liu Xiaodong) with technology, addressing a gap between the two worlds.

## Why Interesting

1. **The only institution of its kind in China.** No other mainland Chinese organization operates at CAC's level of theoretical seriousness in media art. It's the necessary gateway.
2. **International circuit integration.** CAC is inside the ZKM/HeK/Rhizome/Nabi/V2_/Arts at CERN network — the serious global media art circuit. This makes it a de facto Chinese embassy in that world.
3. **Coherent posthumanist intellectual project.** Most "media art" institutions elsewhere are eclectic; CAC has a defined theoretical orientation (new materialism, posthumanism, entanglement ontology) that generates research, not just spectacle.
4. **The R&C @CAC Project is methodologically interesting.** The Bio-Geo-Symbio framework is a genuine research proposition, not just a curatorial brand. The mix of artists (an LA-based computational artist probing Polynesian navigation, an Austrian eco-critical researcher, a Chinese media professor) shows real intellectual ambition.
5. **The extractivism turn** is timely. "Farming Instead of Mining" and *LLMscape* directly address AI's resource footprint — exactly what's becoming the central debate in critical computing and art.
6. **CAC Books** positions them as intellectual producers, not just exhibitors. A bilingual (Chinese-English) media art anthology is genuinely rare.
7. **The A&T Programme** — connecting Yan Lei, Yin Xiuzhen, Liu Xiaodong (major contemporary Chinese artists) with technology is a culturally interesting move: it imports media art methodology into the mainstream Chinese contemporary art world.
8. **Zhang Ga is a crucial connector.** His dual position as NYU Shanghai professor and CAC artistic director, combined with his relationships across ZKM, Ars Electronica, Leonardo/ISAST, and Rhizome, makes him one of the most strategically positioned individuals in the global-Chinese art-tech field. Anyone wanting to build a presence in that circuit should know him.
