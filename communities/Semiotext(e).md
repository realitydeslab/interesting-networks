# Semiotext(e)

The radical theory press that brought French theory to America — and then stayed radical while others became academic. Founded in 1974 at Columbia University by Sylvère Lotringer (1938–2021), originally as a journal, then as a press. Known for two key series: **Native Agents** (fiction and autobiographical writing, especially queer and trans; edited by Chris Kraus) and **Intervention** (political theory and manifestos). A press where philosophy, theory, memoir, and fiction are not clearly distinct. Currently distributed by MIT Press. The connective tissue between Paris '68, New York punk, queer theory, and contemporary autotheory.

## Key People
- **Sylvère Lotringer** (1938–2021) — founder; organized key anti-psychiatric conferences; personal relationship with many French thinkers; brought Baudrillard, Guattari, Virilio to US audiences
- **Chris Kraus** — editor of Native Agents series; author of *I Love Dick* (1997); major figure in autotheory; ⭐⭐⭐ worth researching
- **[Hedi El Kholti](https://semiotexte.com/)** — publisher since Lotringer's death

- *Hatred of Capitalism* (Kraus & Lotringer, eds.) — anthology
- *[I'm Very into You](https://semiotexte.com/?p=829)* ([[McKenzie Wark]] + Kathy Acker) — email correspondence 1995-96
- *[Reverse Cowgirl](https://semiotexte.com/?p=1147)* (McKenzie Wark) — autofictional trans memoir
- Works by Kathy Acker, Dodie Bellamy, Dennis Cooper, Paul Preciado, Andrea Long Chu, Torrey Peters

## Why Interesting
Semiotext(e)'s Native Agents series is the most important platform for queer/trans autotheory today. It's where theory is memoir and memoir is theory — the formal innovation that [[McKenzie Wark]], [[Kathy Acker]], and others practice is legitimated (and distributed) through this press. If you want to understand where critical theory intersects with trans experience, queer sexuality, and experimental form, Semiotext(e) is the node.
