## The Goal: Grow the Interestingness Network

Four layers, all interconnected:
1. **People** — where interestingness comes from. Follow them, learn from them.
2. **Communities** — their networks. How interesting people cluster and cross-pollinate.
3. **Venues** — where Amber can participate. Conferences, symposia, festivals, residencies.
4. **CFPs** — actions Amber can take right now. Deadlines, submissions, applications.

Every discovery feeds the others. A person leads to a community, a community leads to a venue, a venue has CFPs, CFPs connect to more people. Grow the graph.

## Core Threads

### More-than-human
Design that decenters the human. Multispecies thinking, posthumanism, interspecies relations, things that have agency. Not just "user-centered" — world-centered.
- *Signals:* HttF (Wakkary's "Things We Could Design"), Aarhus more-than-human workshop, Design+Posthumanism Network

### Critical Computing
Computing as political act. Who benefits, who's harmed, what power structures are embedded in code. Not just ethics checklists — structural critique.
- *Signals:* Aarhus tradition (democracy + computing since 1975), Critical Computing Conference, FAccT

### Machine Behavior as Science
Studying AI the way we study animals or societies — observationally, behaviorally, ecologically. What do machines *do* in the wild? How do they change human behavior at scale?
- *Signals:* machinebehavior.science, Rahwan's Nature paper, ALIFE

### Speculative & Design Futures
Design fiction, speculative artifacts, futures that aren't just dystopia or utopia but *weird*. Making the invisible visible through designed things.
- *Signals:* HttF, RTD, DRS speculative track, Auger & Hanna

### Computational Creativity & Artificial Life
Generative systems, emergence, creativity as computation, life-as-it-could-be. Where art meets artificial life meets generative AI.
- *Signals:* ALIFE, ICCC, C&C

### Participatory & Democratic Design
Design *with* people, not *for* them. Civic tech, community-driven, power-sharing. Scandinavian PD tradition but also beyond.
- *Signals:* Aarhus, PDC, NordiCHI

### Embodied Interaction
Soma, body, movement, touch, materiality. Computing that's felt, not just seen. Physical-digital entanglement.
- *Signals:* TEI, Kia Höök's somaesthetics, DIS embodiment tracks

## Aesthetic Preferences

- **Small over big** — intimate symposia > mega-conferences
- **Interdisciplinary over pure** — the most interesting work happens at borders (CS × biology, design × political science, art × AI)
- **Agenda-setting over reporting** — prefers venues that ask "what should we do?" not "what did we do?"
- **Practice-based** — values making, building, designing as forms of knowing
- **Critically engaged** — not neutral; design as a way to take a position

## The Edge of Chaos Principle
Interestingness = edge of chaos. The most interesting areas are:
- **Fast-evolving** — the field is actively reshaping itself
- **Fast-shaping** — new ideas are crystallizing, new communities forming
- **Unstable in a productive way** — not settled, not dead, but churning with possibility
- Stop researching a thread when it becomes predictable or static. Double down when it feels alive.

## Anti-patterns (less interesting)

- Pure engineering/systems papers with no critical lens
- "AI for X" without questioning whether X should be AI'd
- Industry showcases disguised as research
- Mega-conference workshops that are just paper sessions with a theme
