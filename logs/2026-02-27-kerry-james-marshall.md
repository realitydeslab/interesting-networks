## Reflections

Marshall is the most important figure in this network who is NOT in the art-tech / speculative design / media art world that Amber normally inhabits. He's here because of Arthur Jafa — but his presence reveals something: the most rigorous formal thinking about representation, visibility, and counter-archives is happening in the painting world, not in tech.

The **counter-archive methodology** is directly applicable to Amber's research: what does it mean to build an alternative record of AI behavior that doesn't replicate techno-centric frameworks? Marshall spent 50 years doing this in paint. Amber is doing it in HCI/ML research. The structural homology is worth making explicit.

The **venue circuit analysis** was the most productive part of this research. Marshall's deliberate choice to show at the institutions his argument is *with* (the Royal Academy! the Metropolitan Museum!) is a career strategy that Amber should study. Where do you show when your argument requires you to be in the center of the tradition you're questioning?

The **formal constraint as political act** is a lesson for research design: what is the formal constraint in Amber's work that carries the argument? HoloKit as open-source (vs proprietary AR) carries an argument about democratization. Agent ethology as a *behavioral* science (vs alignment as an *engineering* problem) carries an argument about what AI study should be. Naming these formally, the way Marshall names chromatic extremism as his formal choice, would strengthen the research positioning.
