## Key Discoveries

### Intellectual framework
- The Al-Beruni / Ibn Sina exchange (999 CE): Raqs repeatedly cite this as a philosophical origin point — two scholars across 250 miles debating whether there are other solar systems. This is the kind of non-Western cosmological speculation they weave into every timescale. It's the framework that makes *Cavalcade*'s AI section legible — AI enters an ancient conversation about infinity and plurality of worlds.
- Kabir's doha form as a computational structure: permutations and combinations of words creating multiple meanings. The doha is a form of combinatorial poetics — this is how Raqs thinks about language as a machine.
- CyberMohalla Ensemble: deeper than expected — a decade-long dialogue with Delhi's marginal urban practitioners. Their worker diary appears in *Strikes at Time* at Centre Pompidou. This is Raqs's version of situated, embedded practice.

### Recent work signal (2024–2025)
- *Cavalcade* (2025) is the major signal: 40-minute film described as "cosmotechnical carnival" that explicitly uses AI agents to generate print works. Shown at Neubauer Collegium (University of Chicago) — serious intellectual venue — with Dipesh Chakravarty present.
- Sustained climate work: *Blood Moon* (time/moon/body), *Thirst/Trishna* (Wellcome Collection, step-wells, water scarcity), *Na-Bam* (immeasurable water depth). Climate as epistemological problem, not just political one.
- *Wayfaring Ways To Be* (Hanart TZ, Hong Kong 2025): suggests new East Asian gallery relationships developing.

### Historical depth
- *The Untold Intimacy of Digits* (2011): This work traces Aadhaar/UID directly to the 1858 colonial fingerprinting of Raj Konai. The handprint is in the Francis Galton Collection at UCL. This is foundational biometric critique, made as art in 2011 — before STS scholars took it up.
- *Coronation Park* (2015): 8 sculptures of defaced imperial statuary, based on the actual Coronation Park near Delhi. Shown at Venice Biennale 2015. The *Bending Man* sculpture entered the Whitworth's permanent collection.
- *The Necessity of Eternity*: Al-Beruni/Ibn Sina correspondence about whether there are other solar systems. Sharjah Biennale 13.

### Golden Lion
- Won the Venice Biennale Golden Lion (2022) for lifetime contribution. This is the highest institutional honor in contemporary art. It confirms their ⭐⭐⭐ status definitively.
