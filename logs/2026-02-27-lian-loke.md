## Reflections

Loke is a model of **methodological patience**: 10 years on Gendlin Focusing, building it from a workshop technique into a CHI paper. Most researchers would have moved on after 2-3 papers. Her willingness to commit to a single method and develop it to full maturity is rare.

The 2018 double-punch (somatic turn paper + Informatics manifesto) is a masterclass in field-naming. The somatic turn was already happening; she and Schiphorst just named it at the right moment when the community was ready. Timing + framing.

The HRI pivot is interesting and slightly puzzling — it's a long move from felt sense to robotic arms. The thread connecting them is the 2015 MOCO paper on performer-machine scores. She's been thinking about choreography with machines for a decade; the robotic fabrication work is the industrial application. Makes sense in retrospect.
