## Key Discoveries

### The Chilltan Concept
The most important conceptual discovery: chilltan are Central Asian spirit beings — shapeshifters of no fixed gender, derived from Persian "40 bodies" (*chil* = 40, *tan* = body). They take the form of animals, humans, natural phenomena. This concept has been present in Ismailova's work since *40 Days of Silence* (2014) and became the centerpiece of her documenta fifteen installation. 

**Why this matters for Amber:** This is a non-Western concept of multiplicity and non-fixed identity that has direct relevance to thinking about AI entities. It's the exact terrain Bogna Konior points toward but as lived cultural practice rather than theory.

### DAVRA as Infrastructure Model
Ismailova founded DAVRA in 2021 not just as a curatorial concept but as a genuine research institution — 18 artists from four Central Asian countries, running a 40-day programme at documenta. This is the "communities as research infrastructure" model that interests Amber. She didn't wait for Western institutions to curate Central Asian art; she built the infrastructure.

### The Dual Venice+Documenta Achievement (2022)
Being selected for both Venice Biennale *and* documenta in the same year is described as "exceptionally rare." This is the dual legitimation pattern — she maintained credibility in both the film world (Berlinale) and the visual art world (Venice, documenta) simultaneously.

### Pirelli HangarBicocca Survey (2024)
The most recent major show (*A Seed Under Our Tongue*, Sep 2024 – Jan 2025) is her first Italian institutional survey — new commissioned works including a newly edited film (*Arslanbob*) and sculptures (golden seed *Amanat*, resin cave *The Mountain Our Bodies Emptied*). Accompanied by a comprehensive monograph edited by Roberta Tenconi. This is the phase of retrospective institutionalization.

### The "Transmission" Frame
The concept of transmission — of knowledge, stories, memories, landscapes — is now her central conceptual framework. The phrase "we are responsible for the seven generations before us and the seven generations to come after us" is a native oral tradition from Central Asia that she uses as methodology, not decoration.
