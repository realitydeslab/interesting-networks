## Key Findings

**Profile:** Joy Buolamwini is the "conscience of the AI revolution" (Fortune 2019). Rhodes Scholar at Oxford (Jesus College) — relevant proximity to Amber. PhD MIT Media Lab 2022 supervised by Ethan Zuckerman. Founder of Algorithmic Justice League.

**Core intellectual contribution:** The concept of the **"coded gaze"** — how AI systems embed the perspectives of their creators and exclude those not in the room. Her Gender Shades paper (2018, with Timnit Gebru) is the founding document of the AI bias research field, and was presented at the inaugural FAccT conference. Error rates discovered: 0.8% for lighter-skinned men vs. up to 47% for darker-skinned women in commercial facial recognition.

**Art-science synthesis:** She is a true poet-scientist. Uses spoken word, documentary film, gallery exhibitions, and academic papers as simultaneous channels. "AI, Ain't I A Woman?" and "Voicing Erasure" are artistic works that ARE the research, not just communication of it.

**Policy reach:** Testified before US Congress 2019; advised Biden on Executive Order 14110 on AI (2023); Board of NAACP Legal Defense Fund (2025).

**Venue circuit:** Academic (FAccT, MIT, Harvard) + Art institutions (Barbican London, Ars Electronica, Ford Foundation Gallery, Apexart) + Policy/media (TED, Netflix, TIME, Fortune). A classic triangulation strategy.
