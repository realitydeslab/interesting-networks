## Key Findings

### Louisiana Is More Than a Museum
Louisiana is a **cultural media infrastructure** — museum + live events + public service streaming platform (Louisiana Channel). The Channel has 94 *Advice to the Young* videos, artist interviews, series on outer space, Jørn Utzon, etc. This is a free resource Amber should be using.

### 4 Exhibitions Directly in Amber's Research Territory
1. **Forensic Architecture – Witnesses** (2022) — ML + spatial analysis as human rights investigation. Eyal Weizman at Goldsmiths, London. UK-adjacent. ⭐⭐⭐ person/community.
2. **Cave_bureau – The Anthropocene Museum** (2023) — Decolonial more-than-human design from Nairobi. "Cow Corridor" = multispecies design in action. ⭐⭐ community.
3. **The Irreplaceable Human** (2023) — Creativity + AI + childhood + labor. 60+ artists. Curator: Mathias Ussing Seeberg. Amber's most direct curatorial interest match.
4. **Memoryscapes** (2026, current) — DnA_Design (Xu Tiantian, Beijing) + ATTA (Tsuyoshi Tane, Paris). Archaeology of the future. Cosmotechnics + landscape memory + Chinese design theory. On now through May 2026 — Amber could see this on a Copenhagen visit.

### Architecture Connecting Series (2024–2029) as Curatorial Model
Louisiana's 5-year architecture series is a model for how to sustain an institutional inquiry rather than doing one-off themed shows. Each exhibition builds on the last. Worth studying as a curatorial strategy Amber could pitch to other institutions.

### Proximity: Copenhagen is 2h from London
LSM is 35 min from Copenhagen city center by train. If Amber ever goes to Denmark for any reason, this is worth a day trip. Open until 22:00 on weekdays.

## Reflections

Louisiana punches far above its weight as a regional Scandinavian museum. The key insight: they treat exhibitions as intellectual projects, not art shows. *The Irreplaceable Human* had a curator articulating "structural imbalances in modern society" as the motivation. *Cave_bureau* explicitly challenged the climate discourse's hierarchy. *Forensic Architecture* used ML as a tool for bearing witness. These aren't typical museum moves.

The Architecture Connecting series (2024–2029) is particularly notable — a museum committing to a 5-year sustained inquiry rather than annual themed shows. That's confidence in ideas over novelty.

For Amber: this museum is doing what Amber's research wants to do — critically, rigorously, publicly, accessibly. Worth monitoring its programming as a signal of what themes are becoming institutionally legible in Europe.
