## Key Discoveries

### New Vision: TeleAbsence (2024–)
The most important finding: Ishii's newest vision is directly about connecting across time to the deceased. The TeleAbsence paper (PRESENCE 2025) uses the Portuguese concept "saudade" as its emotional core. Five design principles: presence of absence, illusory communication, materiality of memory, traces of reflection, remote time. **This is direct intellectual overlap with Amber's mortality/machine death research.**

### ReMirrorFugue (CHI 2025)
Xiao Xiao's MirrorFugue work has evolved into a systematic study of emotional experience when connecting to decade-old piano recordings — including recordings of deceased pianists. 28-participant study found "strong presence of past pianists" and "sense of time travel." This is rare empirical work on grief + computing.

### The "Anemoia Device" (NeurIPS 2025 Creative AI Track)
New work: tangible system using LLM + olfactory translation to generate "synthetic memories" from archival photographs. Nostalgia for a time never experienced. Very adjacent to Amber's interest in AI-mediated memory and the artificiality of digital experience.

### Radical Atoms as Ars Electronica Theme 2016
The entire Ars Electronica 2016 festival was themed after Ishii's research program. 3-year permanent installation at Ars Electronica Center. This level of art-institution validation for HCI research is extremely rare and speaks to Ishii's deliberate art-science bridging strategy.

### Professor-Factory Scale
Alumni now at CMU (Lining Yao), Stanford (Sean Follmer), CU Boulder (Daniel Leithinger), UChicago (Ken Nakagaki). This is a field-generation, not just a lab.

## Patterns Noticed

### Vision Paper as Infrastructure
Ishii's career is built on a small number of vision papers (Tangible Bits 1997, Radical Atoms 2012, TeleAbsence 2024) that each seed 5-10 years of work by the community. Each paper: declares a field, names it memorably, defines an enemy (Painted Bits, rigid atoms), and invites others to fill the territory. The citation impact is enormous because the papers are cited by everyone working in the space.

### The 7-Year Repositioning Cycle
1997 → 2012 → 2024: roughly every 12 years a new major vision, but each built on the previous. This is slower than the common academic hamster wheel, but produces deeper impact. The trick: each new vision extends rather than abandons the previous one (TUIs → programmable matter → temporal presence).

### Art Venue as Primary Output
Most HCI researchers use art venues as PR. Ishii uses them as primary research validation. His work at Ars Electronica (3-year permanent installation!), Cooper Hewitt, Pompidou, V&A is not promotional — it's peer review by a different community. This makes his work resilient to HCI trends.

### TeleAbsence Arrival Timing
TeleAbsence launches just as AI grief tech (digital immortality, AI-powered chatbots of deceased people) is exploding. This is deliberate positioning — Ishii's framework offers a *counter-model* to synthetic/generative representations of the dead (TeleAbsence explicitly avoids these, emphasizing traces and presence without fake utterances). Thoughtful and principled.
