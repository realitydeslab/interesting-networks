## Key Findings

1. **The UCCA Lab angle is Amber's best entry point.** It's explicitly open to bespoke artist/brand/institution collaborations. Contact: lab@ucca.org.cn. No public application process — cold outreach is the mechanism.

2. **UCCA Edge (Shanghai) is the most tech-forward and emerging-art-focused location.** Opened 2021; relatively new programming history; likely more open to experimental proposals than the established Beijing flagship.

3. **Philip Tinari is the gate.** His network (Guggenheim, Art Basel, WEF, Artforum, Asia Society, Fulbright) is the most valuable institutional bridge in Chinese contemporary art. Amber's China Academy of Art connection is the natural warm introduction path.

4. **UCCA has no open fellowships or residencies for artists.** It's a venue/commissioner, not a residency program. Entry is through curatorial pitch, UCCA Lab commission, or artist relationship.

5. **Sinofuturism thread.** UCCA's consistent programming of Cao Fei, Lu Yang, Xu Bing, Qiu Zhijie places it as the institutional home of the aesthetic tradition most relevant to Amber's work. This is not accidental — it's Tinari's curatorial thesis.

## Reflections
UCCA is deceptively simple from the outside (famous museum, 798, contemporary art) but the institutional architecture is sophisticated: the nonprofit/commercial split, the Patrons Council model, UCCA Lab as revenue engine. Tinari's dual identity as art critic + institutional director is the key — he understands both discourse and operations. For Amber, the most interesting angle is not "how do I exhibit there" but "how do I enter Tinari's network" — which opens the entire Chinese art ↔ global circuit.
