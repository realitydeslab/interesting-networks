## What I Found

### The Lab's Identity
CUDAN is not typical digital humanities. It's genuinely heterodox: a statistical physicist (Tamm), an art historian turned network scientist (Schich), a computational linguist (Karjus), a cultural historian of Soviet propaganda (Oiva), an artist-technologist who builds visualization tools (Ohm), and an artistic researcher who navigates AI latent spaces through gaze and EEG (Mar Canet Solà) — all working on shared cultural questions. The funding model (EU ERA Chair) forced this mixing, and the results show it works.

### Key Discovery: The Seminar Series as Field-Definition
The most interesting structural innovation isn't the publications — it's the Open Lab Seminar Series (2020–2027). Every week, a publicly accessible free Zoom lecture by someone working in or adjacent to the field. The series will constitute a 7-year archive of who defined Cultural Data Analytics. This is institutional infrastructure as intellectual output. Amber can access it immediately (permanent Zoom link).

### Key Discovery: Mar Canet Solà & Tillmann Ohm
These two junior fellows are doing the work most directly relevant to Amber. Ohm's Collection Space Navigator is a practical tool Amber can use right now. Canet Solà's gaze-controlled latent space navigation (Visions of Destruction) is the most interesting practice-based AI art work coming out of the lab — the idea of physical/biometric interfaces to AI latent spaces is genuinely novel.

### Key Discovery: Landmark 2014 Science Paper
Schich's 2014 *Science* paper (150K historical births/deaths as cultural flow network) is important context for understanding the lab's intellectual ambition. The viral Nature video "Charting Culture" made it global. This is not just a paper — it's a proof that computational methods can produce genuinely revelatory cultural insight at a scale previously impossible.

### Key Discovery: Eva Cetinic (March 23 lecture)
Upcoming CUDAN lecturer on March 23: Eva Cetinic from Zurich DSI, presenting "A Decade of AI in the Interpretation and Creation of Art." This is directly relevant to Amber. Created stub.

### Key Discovery: ERA Chair Ended, Lab Continues
The EU Horizon 2020 ERA Chair grant ended August 2024. The lab continues through 2027 via Tallinn University Research Fund. No new ERA-style fellowship calls are currently open, but the research group is active and the seminar series continues.
