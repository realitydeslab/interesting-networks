## Reflections

SFPC's 2021 political transformation is the most interesting thing about it — it went from a beloved creative coding school into an explicitly abolitionist, feminist, BIPOC-centered institution. This is not PR; it's reflected in the actual course offerings (abolitionist memory work, solidarity infrastructures, platform critique). The board composition (American Artist, Tega Brain, Celine Wong Katzman at Rhizome) signals where the institution's intellectual heart is.

**Key insight:** The SFPC-to-NYU-ITP corridor is the backbone of critical creative tech in North America. Amber's work belongs in this conversation. A workshop proposal (Agent Ethology as artistic practice, or Machine Death as poetic form) would be a natural fit.

**American Artist is a ⭐⭐⭐ discovery** — doing the most rigorous Black radical critique of AI/tech systems in contemporary art. This is a person Amber should know.
