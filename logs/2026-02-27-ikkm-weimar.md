## Key Findings

### Critical Corrections to Existing Stub
1. **NOT DFG-funded** — was BMBF-funded (Federal Ministry of Education and Research) as one of 10 Käte Hamburger Kollegs
2. **NOT founded 2009** — founded April 2008
3. **CLOSED** — officially closed March 2020 (ran exactly 12 years)

### Research Programmes Documented
- Programme 1 "Cultural Techniques" (2008–2014): six annual themes tracing making of humans, things, signs, sites, the present, the past
- Programme 2 "Operative Ontologies" (2014–2020): shift from relations to operations; six themes in three pairs (framing/sewing, condensing/dispersing, reproducing/reflecting)

## Reflections

The IKKM is a fascinating case of an institutional form: a 12-year funded intensive fellowship programme that successfully generated a coherent intellectual paradigm (Kulturtechnik). Its closure is significant — it marks the end of the BMBF Käte Hamburger Kolleg funding era for this program, and the dispersal of its intellectual community (Siegert stays at Weimar; Engell retired; Hörl moved to Leuphana ICAM; etc.).

For Amber: the Kulturtechnik paradigm is the German-language version of the more-than-human turn, but with more emphasis on historical specificity and material operations. The shift from "cultural techniques" (Program 1) to "operative ontologies" (Program 2) tracks a wider theoretical development toward ontological approaches (Viveiros de Castro, Descola, Latour's *modes of existence*) — which is where Amber's interest in cosmotechnics and machine behavior sits.
