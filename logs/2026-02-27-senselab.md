## Key Findings

1. **SenseLab (2004–~2019) is now fully transitioned to 3 Ecologies Institute.** The original website is down. The work continues through 3e (land-based), the Immediations/3Ecologies book series (punctum books), and the Thought in the Act series (Duke UP).

2. **Inflexions (2008–2019) is 11 full issues of open-access research-creation theory.** The founding issue "How is Research-Creation?" is the canonical text. All issues involve motion, interactivity (Flash-based issues now require browsers with Flash capability) — the journal *enacted* research-creation in its form.

3. **The intellectual heritage is very specific:** Whitehead + Bergson + Simondon + Deleuze-Guattari + Spinoza + Peirce + James. This is not vague "process philosophy" — it's a specific constellation assembled over 20 years by Manning and Massumi. Anyone wanting to engage seriously needs to read at least: Massumi's *Parables for the Virtual* (2002) and Manning's *The Minor Gesture* (2016).

4. **Neurodiversity is not incidental — it is central.** The philosophical argument is that non-normative perception reveals the structure of experience more clearly than normative perception does. This is a research methodology claim, not just a political one.

5. **The global network is substantial**: 800+ participants in SenseLab/3e network worldwide; active groups in Montreal, Australia, Brazil, and multiple European locations.

6. **The ECSA (Economic Space Agency) collaboration** produced 8 working papers (2016–2019) on post-blockchain qualitative economics. This is the most speculative and forward-looking part of the SenseLab legacy — a serious attempt to imagine economic infrastructure for affect-based value.
