## Key Discoveries

### 1. Leadership Transition
Alain Fleischer (founder, 1997–2024) has retired. Isabelle Gaudefroy (Fondation Cartier) took over in February 2026. This is significant: new leadership after 27 years = moment of openness to new connections and visitors. **Window of opportunity** for Amber to establish a relationship with the institution now.

### 2. Application Deadline Imminent
The 2026 student intake application deadline is **11 March 2026** — two weeks away. This is too soon for Amber to apply as a student, but worth noting for the network: any artist connections who might benefit from Le Fresnoy should be told immediately.

### 3. The EYE Prize Pattern
Two consecutive EYE Art & Film Prize winners (2022: Saodat Ismailova; 2024: Chia-Wei Hsu) are both Le Fresnoy alumni. This is not coincidental. The school specifically attracts and develops artists from non-Western backgrounds working with technical media while retaining cultural specificity. This is the exact profile of work that Amber finds most interesting.

### 4. Véréna Paravel / Harvard SEL Connection
Véréna Paravel (Sensory Ethnography Lab, Harvard) was a Panorama 26 guest. SEL is one of the most important communities for non-anthropocentric media practice. This Le Fresnoy connection creates a bridge to Harvard SEL that's worth noting for Amber. Created stub: `people/Véréna Paravel.md`.

### 5. Cécile B. Evans
Major figure for Amber's interests in agent/machine agency — American-Belgian artist in MoMA, Whitney, Centre Pompidou collections, Serpentine-commissioned. Was Le Fresnoy visiting artist-professor 2023. Created stub: `people/Cécile B. Evans.md`.

### 6. Justine Emard
French AI+robotics artist, AIST (Japan) collaboration. Panorama 26 participant. One of the most rigorous practitioners in art+AI+robotics in France. Under-represented in Amber's network so far. Created stub: `people/Justine Emard.md`.

### 7. Chinese Students Pipeline
Panorama 25 and 26 both include Chinese students (Chongyan Liu, Bohao Liu, Xiyue Hu, Xing Xiao). Le Fresnoy has strong East Asian representation. Amber's China Academy of Art connections could be leveraged to connect.

## Reflections
Le Fresnoy is structurally fascinating: it has no permanent teaching faculty. Every year is different. This makes it inherently dependent on its networks — the visiting artist-professors define the institution's intellectual direction as much as any policy. This is why the transition from Fleischer to Gaudefroy matters: the visiting artists she invites will reflect her taste and networks (Fondation Cartier → contemporary French+international art; Jérôme Bel → performance; Ishigami → architecture-technology). Amber's profile (XR, AI agents, non-Western epistemology, practice-based research) could potentially interest both the Cartier circuit and the art+technology circuit.

The production model — every student makes actual work with 8,400€ budget — is exactly the "making as knowing" epistemology that Amber's research values. This is a place where Amber could both learn from and contribute to the model.
