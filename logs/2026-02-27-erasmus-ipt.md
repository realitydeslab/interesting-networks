## What I Found

This institute is more important than the stub suggested. Three major findings:

### 1. NWO Vici Grant (Feb 26, 2026 — yesterday!)
Just announced: €1.5M research grant from Dutch Research Council for Yuk Hui's "Technodiversity: A New Lens on AI" project. This is a major funding event that will:
- Anchor IPT's research themes
- Fund new PhD/postdoc positions (watch for job postings)
- Signal Dutch government investment in philosophy of AI (rare)

### 2. Kant Machine (published Feb 17, 2026)
New Bloomsbury book directly bridging Kant and AI. Reads Kant alongside Turing, Gödel, Wiener, Minsky, McCarthy, Hinton, Bengio, LeCun. This is exactly the kind of historical-philosophical framing of AI that's rare in current discourse. Essential reading for Amber's AI philosophy interests.

### 3. ArtReview Power 100 #40 (Dec 2025)
Yuk Hui ranked among world's most influential figures shaping contemporary art. This is significant because it positions IPT at intersection of art world and philosophy — not just academic. Explains why IPT co-hosts with Hanart Forum and why art figures like [[Wendi Yan]] orbit cosmotechnics ideas.
