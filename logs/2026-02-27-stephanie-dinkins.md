## Key Findings

**The core insight:** Dinkins isn't just making AI art — she's building counter-infrastructure. N'TOO is trained on oral history from her own family specifically because mainstream NLP datasets are, in her words, "violences against blackness." This is a practice-based critique that most art-about-AI doesn't achieve.

**The Whitney purchase (2019)** is the signal: permanent collection acquisition at the Whitney early in her AI practice trajectory. This locked in canonical status.

**The double fellowship moment (2021):** Knight Arts+Tech + United States Artist in the same year. Both $50K unrestricted. This gave her the financial freedom to build without market pressure.

**The triple 2023 moment:** LG-Guggenheim inaugural award + Time 100 AI + Schmidt AI2050 Senior Fellow — all in 2023. She crossed from "important art figure" to "recognized AI influencer." The inaugural LG-Guggenheim is particularly notable: being the first to define what a new award means is a power position.

**The Ruha Benjamin connection** is the most intellectually interesting thing I found. Dinkins explicitly quotes Benjamin in her bio. They're building the same field from different directions: Benjamin provides the "New Jim Code" theory, Dinkins builds the counter-code. Benjamin should be ⭐⭐⭐ — she's the theoretical bedrock for a significant chunk of critical computing.
