## What I Found

### Person
Bernhard Siegert (b. 1959, Bremen) is the central architect of the **Kulturtechniken** (cultural techniques) paradigm in German media theory. Spent 24 years as Chair for Theory and History of Cultural Techniques at Bauhaus-Universität Weimar (2001–2025, now emeritus). Co-founded the **IKKM** (Internationales Kolleg für Kulturtechnikforschung und Medienphilosophie) with Lorenz Engell in 2008; co-directed it until 2020.

### The Paradigm
Cultural techniques theory: the claim that operations (writing, counting, mapping, drawing grids, crossing thresholds) *precede* and *constitute* the concepts (text, number, territory, inside/outside) they supposedly express. Media don't represent a pre-existing reality — they articulate and constitute it. This is operationalism applied to the history of culture.

### The Books
The key English text is *Cultural Techniques: Grids, Filters, Doors, and Other Articulations of the Real* (Fordham UP, 2015). The new major work is *Final Frontiers: Eine Medienarchäologie des Meeres* (Brill/Fink, 2024) — Open Access, 570pp, on the ocean as the "final frontier" of human habitat and media.

### The Network
The Weimar/IKKM ecosystem is the center. Key figures: Lorenz Engell (co-director), Joseph Vogl (co-editor), Cornelia Vismann (colleague, deceased), Erhard Schüttpelz (media anthropology), Markus Krajewski (paper/bureaucracy), Geoffrey Winthrop-Young (English translator/advocate). Parallel Berlin HZK track: Sybille Krämer, Thomas Macho, Horst Bredekamp.

Anglo-American reception centered on: *Grey Room* (MIT Press), *Theory, Culture & Society* 30:6 (2013 special issue), Fordham UP (publisher).

### The Venue Circuit
Purely academic humanities circuit. German: Fink, diaphanes, Brinkmann&Bose, Merve. International: Grey Room, Theory Culture Society, Fordham UP, Radical Philosophy. No art/tech crossover venues.

## For Amber: Top Insights

1. **Read *Cultural Techniques* (Fordham, 2015)** — the key English text. Start with "Doors" and "The Map is the Territory" (both Open Access links in the note).

2. **Final Frontiers (2024) is Open Access** — Siegert's most recent major work on the ocean as media archaeology is freely available at Brill. Directly resonates with more-than-human, planetary-scale thinking.

3. **Bernard Dionysius Geoghegan is at King's College London** — proximity! His work bridges cybernetics → cultural techniques, which is the arc from information theory to Amber's interests in agent behavior and AI ethics.

4. **Cultural techniques theory = the philosophical grounding Amber's work needs** — the operationalist claim (operations constitute concepts) provides rigorous philosophical vocabulary for: how agents constitute their behavioral worlds, how protocols constitute social orders, how reward functions constitute value systems.

5. **IKKM model** — a small, focused, high-output institution with a house journal + book series + fellowship program. A model for how to build Reality Design Lab's theoretical infrastructure.
