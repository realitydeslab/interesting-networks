## Reflections

Dick's work is quietly essential. She provides historical evidence for critiques that critical theorists often make speculatively — showing from the archives how automation of intelligence has always also been a politics of intelligence. The MACSYMA paper ("Coded Conduct") is a masterpiece — showing how documentation itself was a behavioral script to reshape how mathematicians thought.

The trajectory from "AfterMath" (2011) to "How AI Sets Limits" (2024) is a coherent intellectual project: understanding what we lose when we formalize intelligence. The latest work on logic and the occult is unexpected and potentially explosive — if she follows that thread.

Strong connection to [[Wendy Chun]] and the DDI. Dick provides the historical depth Chun's theoretical framework calls for.

The Harvard Society of Fellows is a strong genealogical marker — places her in the elite cross-disciplinary humanist tradition at Harvard (History of Science PhD + Society of Fellows = serious grounding).

**For Amber:** Her "intelligence deflation" thesis is directly useful — the argument that AI progress is better read as a shrinking of the concept of intelligence. Relevant to machine behavior research, to questions about what AI "actually does" vs. what we say it does. Also: the MACSYMA case study (how expert systems reshape expert minds) is the historical precedent for present-day concerns about AI changing human cognition and practice.
