## Research Taste Notes

Höök's career is the best single example in this knowledge graph of **philosophical pivot as career strategy** — taking a dense philosophical framework and making it into a methodology that generates 20+ years of research and community. The sequence (deep philosophy → methodology → book → community → lifetime recognition) is more explicit and traceable than almost any other researcher in the graph.

The 2025 SIGCHI awards sweep is remarkable: it wasn't just Höök who won — it was her entire lab. When a PI builds a community that generates Lifetime Service awards AND multiple outstanding young researcher recognitions in the same year, that's the vindication of a community-building strategy, not just individual excellence.

The most counterintuitive finding: **"Strong Concepts" (793 citations) is her most-cited paper, not the 2018 book (731)**. The methodology paper beats the field-declaration book. This suggests that HCI researchers adopted her knowledge framework (how to think about design research contributions) even more broadly than they adopted soma design itself. She built intellectual infrastructure for the whole field, not just her subfield.
