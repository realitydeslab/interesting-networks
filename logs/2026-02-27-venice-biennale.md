## What I Found

### The Posthumous 2026 Edition is Significant
Koyo Kouoh (1967–2025) was appointed as 2026 Art Director, died May 10, 2025 — exactly one day after the 61st Biennale was to open. La Biennale decided to proceed with "In Minor Keys" exactly as she conceived it. This is itself a curatorial-political gesture: an institution honoring the posthumous vision of an African curator who was reshaping how Western art institutions handle African contemporary art. Worth following closely.

### The Decolonial Arc 2015–2026
Three consecutive Art Biennales have centered non-Western perspectives:
- 2015: Okwui Enwezor (first African director) — Das Kapital as score
- 2024: Adriano Pedrosa (first LatAm director) — Global South art histories
- 2026: Koyo Kouoh (posthumous) — "In Minor Keys" = subaltern voices

This is a decade-long institutional shift, not a trend. The art world's dominant curatorial institutions (Venice, documenta, etc.) have moved decisively toward decolonial framing. This is the same intellectual trajectory as Yuk Hui, cosmotechnics, non-Western tech-philosophy — which are Amber's interests.

### The 2025 Architecture Biennale is Amber's Most Direct Overlap
Carlo Ratti's "Intelligens. Natural. Artificial. Collective." (2025) was explicitly about the intersection of AI, natural intelligence, and collective intelligence — 750+ participants across architecture, coders, farmers, philosophers, climate scientists. This is Amber's research territory. Unfortunately it already ran (May–Nov 2025) but the documentation/catalogue is worth pursuing.
