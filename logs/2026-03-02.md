### Reflections

It's proof that curatorial courage + financial independence = institutional distinctiveness. The "no wall labels" / app-only interface is a genuine curatorial philosophy, not just a tech gimmick — relevant to Amber's design interests.

The Santiago Sierra *Union Flag* controversy (2021) is worth studying: invited First Nations people to donate blood; crisis led to formal Indigenous advisory structure. Shows MONA can make serious missteps when transgression becomes extraction.

## Deep Research: Willem de Rooij ⭐⭐⭐

**Researcher:** Biber (deep-research subagent)
**Time:** ~45 minutes
**Files touched:** `people/Willem de Rooij.md`

### What Was Researched
Willem de Rooij (born 1969, Beverwijk) is a Dutch conceptual artist and Professor of Fine Arts at Städelschule Frankfurt. He co-founded BPA// Berlin Program for Artists (2016) with Angela Bulloch and Simon Denny. Sources: Wikipedia (comprehensive), Galerie Buchholz, BPA// website, Städelschule.

Key research areas covered: 12-year collaboration with Jeroen de Rijke (deceased 2006), major solo exhibitions 1999–2025, colonial visual history methodology, textile collaborations (Ghana, Indonesia, Germany), key collaborators (Koyo Kouoh, Adrienne Kaeppler, Karwan Fatah-Black), pedagogical infrastructure (Städelschule, Rijksakademie, De Ateliers, BPA//), awards (Bâloise Art Prize 2000, Hugo Boss nomination 2004, Harvard Robert Fulton Fellow 2004, KNAW 2016).

### Key Findings
- The *Valkenburg* exhibition (Centraal Museum Utrecht, 2025) is the culmination of nearly 20 years of research — first catalogue raisonné of Dirk Valkenburg, major statement on colonial Dutch visual ideology. Extraordinary commitment.
- His method of producing scholarly publications (catalogue raisonnés, monographs with academic contributors) **as artistic practice** is a rare and powerful model.
- The KNAW membership (Royal Netherlands Academy of Arts and Sciences) confirms artist-scholar status at the highest Dutch academic level.
- **Koyo Kouoh** (already noted, ⭐⭐) was a key collaborator on wax print project — relationship worth following.
- BPA// is genuinely artist-led, free, structured around studio visits — a minimal-footprint alternative to commercial art education.
- He established a formal exchange between Städelschule and Kwame Nkrumah University in Kumasi, Ghana (2014) — building decolonial infrastructure in art education.

### Relevance to Amber
Strong conceptual overlap despite different fields: politics of representation, how display systems encode power, more-than-human material culture (textiles as colonial carriers). His career is a model for the patient, scholarly, infrastructure-building artist-academic. The **catalogue raisonné as artistic medium** is a strategy Amber could adapt for machine behavior documentation.

## Deep Research: Angela Bulloch ⭐⭐⭐

**Researcher:** Biber (deep-research subagent)
**Time:** ~60 minutes
**Files touched:** `people/Angela Bulloch.md` (full update from stub), `people/Liam Gillick.md` (stub), `venues/Gwangju Biennale.md` (stub)

### What Was Researched
Angela Bulloch — Canadian artist born 1966, based in Berlin. Full research via Wikipedia, Esther Schipper gallery biography, Tate Turner Prize page, angelabulloch.com (ABCDLP record label page, AB_AR app). Sources: comprehensive exhibition histories from Esther Schipper biography (definitive source); Wikipedia for awards and career chronology.

Key research areas: 35+ year career trajectory from Goldsmiths YBA generation (1988) through to Gwangju Biennale 2024; DMX Pixel Box sculptures (signature work); Night Sky series (2008–ongoing); Rules Series (1993); Drawing Machines (biofeedback and mechanical); ABCDLP record label (6 releases, musician collaborations); BPA// Berlin Program for Artists (co-founded 2016 with Simon Denny and Willem de Rooij); HFBK Hamburg professorship (2018–present); awards and public collection depth.

### Key Findings
- The **Night Sky series** (simulating constellations from non-Earth vantage points using 3D stellar cartography) is conceptually the most interesting work for Amber — operationalizes more-than-human perspective-taking through computation.
- Her **strategic refusal** of the media art category is analytically important: despite having invented a form of computational light sculpture in the early 1990s, she positioned herself in the fine art world, not the media art circuit. This category refusal gave her more institutional reach and interpretive freedom.
- **ABCDLP record label** is a model micro-institution: 6 releases since early 2010s, limited editions, artist-musician collaborations, physical vinyl artifacts. Community infrastructure without institutional dependency.
- **Turner Prize 1997 all-female shortlist** with Cornelia Parker, Christine Borland, Gillian Wearing (winner) — historically significant. She lost but gained institutional credibility.
- **Public collections** are exceptional: Centre Pompidou, Tate, Guggenheim Abu Dhabi, Fondation Louis Vuitton, FNAC, Lenbachhaus, RISD, Phillips Collection, Berlinische Galerie, and 15+ others.
- **AB_AR app** (2024) for Gwangju Biennale is a quiet example of AR-as-distribution-mechanism, not spectacle. Visitors access artist sound streams via AR images in galleries and neighborhood. Low-key integration.
- **Liam Gillick** (Goldsmiths cohort, collaborative exhibition 2006) — ⭐⭐ — worth a stub note. Key relational aesthetics figure.

### Reflections
Bulloch's career strategy of concept-first (rules, systems, behavioral emergence) with technology-second is a model for sustainable practice. The conceptual framework from 1993's *Rules Series* is still operating in 2024's *AB_AR* — different tools, same logic. This is rare in art that engages technology.

The BPA// co-founding with Denny and de Rooij is interesting as infrastructure: three artists (all Germany-based, all from different national traditions) creating a fellowship to develop the Berlin art ecosystem. Generosity at scale, without institutional backing.
