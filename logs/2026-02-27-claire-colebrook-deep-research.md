### Hypatia Interview (2025)
Published Fall 2025 — a dialogue on "Ecology, Extinction, and Posthumanism" conducted during COVID (August 2020). Key insight from the abstract: Colebrook argues the ethical demands of climate change **cannot be addressed to a particular subject or "we"** — because the "we" is precisely what's in question. The human as language-being is the problem, not the solution. Only the "death of the ethical and political subject" can provide alternate modes of survival. This is consistent with her whole trajectory.

## Reflections

### On the Intellectual Position
Colebrook occupies a philosophically rare and uncomfortable position: she is the **internal critic of posthumanism**. Her work emerges from continental philosophy, Deleuzian theory, and deconstruction — but she uses those tools to critique the consoling moves that posthumanism keeps making. "Life" as a response to anthropocentrism? Still anthropocentric — it's just human values transferred to a larger category. "Cyborg feminism"? Still assumes some continuity of the subject. "Make kin"? Who defines kinship?

This is the de Man move applied to posthumanism: the text cannot totalize itself, and posthumanism cannot escape the normative frame it is supposed to overcome. Only if you take extinction seriously — as a real philosophical possibility, not just a risk to be managed — does the inquiry become genuinely rigorous.

### On the Relationship to Amber's Territory
The connection to art practice is less direct than some network nodes (she's a philosopher/literary critic, not an artist). But several angles:

1. **Postapocalyptic cinema** — *Who Would You Kill to Save the World?* engages directly with speculative fiction and dystopian film. Any artist working with postapocalyptic imagery should read this.
2. **The OHP books are FREE** — *Death of the PostHuman* and *Sex After Life* are open access. These are genuinely essential texts for anyone thinking about posthumanism in art contexts.
3. **The Critical Climate Change series** — the series contains *Art in the Anthropocene* (Davis & Turpin) which is directly relevant to Amber's territory
4. **The "no consoling outside" argument** — useful critical tool for evaluating AI art that claims to be posthumanist or post-anthropocentric. Does it reproduce human values in AI vocabulary?
