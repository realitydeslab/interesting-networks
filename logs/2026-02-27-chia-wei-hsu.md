## Key Discoveries

### 1. Le Fresnoy → Consecutive EYE Prize Winners
Both [[Saodat Ismailova]] (2022) and [[Chia-Wei Hsu]] (2024) trained at Le Fresnoy. This is a strong institutional signal. Le Fresnoy is an underexplored node in Amber's network — it produces artists who bring non-Western epistemologies into technical media practice, exactly the intersection Amber cares about. **Created Le Fresnoy stub.**

### 2. Archaeometric Method
Hsu's term "archaeometry" (scientific analysis of archaeological materials, applied to cultural history) is a distinctive intellectual move. It positions his work as forensic/scientific rather than merely narrative. This has resonance with Amber's interest in AI as archaeology/reconstruction.

### 3. KMT remnant soldiers as subject community
The forgotten Kuomintang soldiers who remained in the Golden Triangle (Thailand/Burma border) after 1949 are an extraordinary subject: stateless, erased from official Taiwanese and Chinese history, living in permanent liminality. Hsu worked WITH them (not just on them) to create *An Actor from Golden Triangle* — co-authorship with the marginalised.

### 4. EYE Advisory Board includes Apichatpong Weerasethakul
Apichatpong nominated Hsu for the prize. Apichatpong is a ⭐⭐⭐ person already in the network. The advisory board connection creates a bridge between Thai cinematic tradition (spirit/memory/sleep) and Taiwanese archaeometric practice.

### 5. ISCP Residency 2010
Early career New York residency funded by Taiwan's Ministry of Culture. This is the standard infrastructure pathway for Taiwanese artists to access the US art market. Worth noting as a model.

### 6. Dual-validation strategy
Taiwan validation first (Taishin Grand Prize 2017) → European validation (EYE Prize 2024). Seven year gap. Patient, specialist, depth-first strategy. Instructive for Amber.
