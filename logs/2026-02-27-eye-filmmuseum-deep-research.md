## Researcher Notes

EYE is underrated in the art-tech community because it's known primarily as a *film* institution, not a media art one. But its prize list and programming reveal it as one of the sharpest curatorial instruments for art-film-technology crossover in Europe. The advisory board alone (Apichatpong Weerasethakul, Rajendra Roy, Andrea Lissoni, Hoor Al Qasimi) is an extraordinary network.

The shift from PJLF Arts Fund to Ammodo (2023) suggests the prize is now better funded and the Dutch arts infrastructure is more involved — potentially increasing EYE's influence in the Netherlands/EU art world.

Chia-Wei Hsu (2024 winner) is the single most interesting discovery from this research for Amber's purposes: VR + archaeometry + erased Cold War histories = a direct peer and inspiration for AI + archive + non-Western cosmologies work.
