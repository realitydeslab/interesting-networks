## Key Findings
1. **Repair as central concept**: Not just an aesthetic — a genuine philosophical framework. Repair produces difference (not restoration). This is his core thesis, developed across 25+ years of work.
2. **Awards**: Prix Marcel Duchamp (2016) and Joan Miró Prize (2018) are the key legitimating moments. Used these to open La Colonie (2016) — simultaneous prize-winning and institution-building.
3. **La Colonie** (2016–2020): Artist-founded decolonial cultural space in Paris. Closed due to COVID. Location was deliberately chosen (near Gare du Nord, near Algerian migrant history). A model for artist-led infrastructure.
4. **Berlin Biennale 12 (2022)**: Curated by Attia — the move from artist to institutional shaper.
5. **Permanent collections**: MoMA, Tate, Centre Pompidou, Guggenheim, Sharjah Art Foundation, ICA Boston. Deeply embedded in Western institutional art infrastructure.
6. ***The White West* (MIT Press, 2024)**: Edited theoretical volume on Western fascism and modernity — shows him operating as intellectual editor, not just practitioner.

## Reflections
Attia is a rigorous example of how a single sustained concept can generate a career. His patience with *réparation* as a concept is unusual in contemporary art's trend toward diversification. The La Colonie model — precarious, politically explicit, community-oriented — is instructive and sad in its COVID ending.

The repair concept connects surprisingly well to Amber's machine behavior research: what does it mean for an AI system to "repair" damage? Does it restore, or does it transform (and what's the politics of that transformation)?
