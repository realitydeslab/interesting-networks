## What I Found

### The Engineering-to-Theory Pipeline Is the Key Biographical Fact
Hardt started as an engineer at Swarthmore (1978–83), not as a humanist. He worked on solar energy for developing countries, joined the Sanctuary Movement, helped bring computers to the University of El Salvador. His path from engineering → Central American solidarity → comparative literature PhD → political philosopher explains the unusual structural quality of *Empire*: it thinks in systems and flows, not just texts.

### The Negri Meeting Origin Story
Hardt began translating Negri's *The Savage Anomaly* to make contact with him. They first met in Paris, summer 1986 — to discuss translation difficulties. That meeting lasted 40 years and produced six books. "How to Write with Four Hands" (2013, *Genre*) is his reflection on what collaborative writing actually means.

### Theory Out of Bounds Series (1993–2007) Is His Most Durable Infrastructure
Co-edited with Brian Massumi and Sandra Buckley at U Minnesota Press. Published Agamben, Guattari, Virilio, Lyotard, Stengers, Badiou, Deleuze, Cary Wolfe, Chela Sandoval, William Connolly, Alphonso Lingis. For ~15 years, this was the primary channel through which European critical theory reached English-language readers. This was field-building, not just publishing.

### "Affective Labor" (1999) Is Now More Relevant Than Ever
The *boundary 2* paper defined a concept now applied to: platform labor, data annotation, RLHF workers, care workers, influencers. It should be cited in any political-economic analysis of AI labor.

### A Global War Regime (2024, NLR Sidecar, with Sandro Mezzadra)
The most important recent short piece. Argues: we are in a period of war-as-governance; the military and economic have become inextricable; Ukraine/Gaza/Indo-Pacific are logistics conflicts as much as sovereignty conflicts. Proposes "desertion" as the appropriate political response — collective withdrawal. Directly relevant to AI militarization debates.

### Sandro Mezzadra Is the New Key Collaborator
With Negri's death (December 2023), Mezzadra is now Hardt's most active co-thinker. His background in migration theory, border studies, and logistics makes him an interesting complement. *The Rest and the West* (Verso, forthcoming, with Brett Neilson) is the next major text to watch.

### The Subversive Festival Circuit (Zagreb)
Hardt is a documented regular at this Croatian political-intellectual festival. Unusual for a US-based theorist to maintain such strong Eastern European ties. Connects him to the Balkan Forum, World Social Forum network, and a distinctly non-Anglophone left.
