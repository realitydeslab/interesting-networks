## Key Discoveries

### Methodology is More Developed Than Expected
The "situated testimony" method using 3D models to help trauma survivors recover memories is genuinely novel cognitive science in practice — not just visualization. The Saydnaya Prison "ear-witnessing" investigation is the most compelling example: rebuilding a prison from acoustic memory alone.

### Scale
100+ investigations in 31+ countries. 6 months to 2 years per investigation. This is not just an art project — it's a serious research institution with documented real-world impact (Israel abandoned the Hannibal Directive, Golden Dawn banned, etc.)

### The Right Livelihood Award (2024)
Major validation. The award citation explicitly mentions "new methodologies combining technology with human rights advocacy" — exactly what they've built over 15 years.

### FA's Influence on Mainstream Media
The NYT, Washington Post, Economist, and BBC now have in-house forensic investigation teams using FA's methods. This is lasting methodological impact.

### Forensis (Berlin, 2021)
A new independently-organized sister agency founded by former FA researchers — this is a good sign that the model is viable and replicable.

### The Warren Kanders Episode (2019 Whitney Biennial)
FA's investigation of Whitney trustee Kanders' arms company (producing tear gas) led to him resigning from the board. This is the art-world version of their counter-forensic power: using the museum as forum.
